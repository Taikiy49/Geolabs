PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Sunset Beach Shoreline Improvements project site is located near the existing
Sunset Beach Support Park in the Sunset Beach area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided it is proposed to construct a viewing platform and
passenger loading zone with an associated asphaltic concrete bicycle path bikepath at
Sunset Beach We understand that the viewing platform will be a wooden platform structure
supported on rock walls The passenger loading zone area will be paved with asphaltic
concrete In general we anticipate that the viewing platform will be a lightly loaded structure
We also anticipate that the vehicle loading will consist primarily of passenger vehicles and
pick up trucks The proposed passenger loading zone area was relatively open and level We
anticipate that site grading on order of 1 to 4 feet of cuts and fills will be required to achieve
the design finished grades for the proposed construction

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Sunset Beach area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
proposed viewing platform and passenger loading zone site is situated on the makai side of
Kamehameha Highway across from the existing Sunset Beach Support Park The project site
is located between Kamehameha Highway and Sunset Beach as shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
The area of the viewing platform was generally covered by Hau bushes and the
passenger loading zone was occupied by temporary parking stalls A near vertical slope was
observed on the makai side of the Hau bushes In addition significant roots of the Hau
bushes were present on the slopes
An existing residence was noted on the northern corner of the site In general the site
is relatively level and open The ground surface at the site ranges from about Elevation 17
to 23 feet Mean Sea Level MSL An asphaltic concrete paved bikepath traverses the site
from the southwest to the northeast

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling three borings designated as
Boring Nos 1 through 3 with a 3 inch inside diameter ID hand auger Each boring was
advanced to a depth of about 10 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the hand auger borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Detailed descriptions of
the materials encountered during our field exploration are presented on the Logs of Borings
Plates 4 through 6
in general our field exploration encountered about 1 to 1 5 feet of fill material consisting
of dense dark brown silty sands with gravel and traces of roots Below the surface fills
medium dense tannish brown beach sand with traces of coral debris and shell fragments
were encountered The beach sand deposits extended to the maximum depth explored of
approximately 10 feet below the existing ground surface Groundwater was not encountered
in the borings at the time of our field exploration It should be noted that groundwater levels
are subject to change due to seasonal precipitation and other factors
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
As previously indicated our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally
underlain by medium dense beach sand deposits overlain by a thin layer of fill material Based
on the generally competent subsoil conditions encountered at the project site we believe that
the new rock wall supported on shallow foundations bearing on the re compacted on site
materials may be used to support the proposed viewing platform for the subject project Based
on the sandy soil conditions encountered in the borings and the Hau bushes present at the
site special attention should be given to the embedment depths of the footings and site
preparation requirements Detailed discussion and our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this letter report
Foundations
In general we believe that the new rock walls supported on shallow continuous footings
will likely be used to support the proposed viewing platform anticipated for the subject project
Based on our field exploration an allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per
square foot psf may be used for the design of shallow foundations bearing on the
re compacted on site soils or select granular fill material This bearing value is for dead plus
live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused
by wind or seismic forces

Due to the erodible nature of the beach sand deposits erosion control and proper
embedment of the foundation for the rock walls should be given special attention In general
we recommend that the foundations be embedded a minimum of 3 feet below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Thickened edges of the concrete walkway slabs adjacent to unpaved
areas should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grades
Due to the sloping terrain at the project site we recommend that foundations be
embedded deep enough to obtain a minimum 6 foot setback distance measured
horizontally from the outside edge of the footing to the face of the slope Continuous
footings running parallel to the direction of the slope should be constructed in stepped
footings
In general footings should bear on firm and unyielding subgrades The exposed
subgrades at the bottom of the footing excavations should be re compacted to at least
90 percent relative compaction to provide a relatively firm and unyielding bearing surface If
soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they should
be over excavated to expose firm materials The over excavation should be backfilled with
granular material compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the bottom
of footing may be extended down to the underlying competent material Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below an imaginary 45 degree plane extending upward from the bottom edge of
the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary
to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to
reduce the potential for appreciable foundation settlement If foundations are designed and
constructed in accordance with our recommendations we estimate total settlements to be less
than 1 inch with differential settlements on the order of about 0 5 inches
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by frictional resistance developed
between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of friction of 0 35
may be used for footings bearing on the re compacted on site soils or select granular fill
materials Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent
fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the
soils around the footings are well compacted or undisturbed Unless covered by pavements
or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
Subgrade preparation and footing excavations should be observed by a
representative of Geolabs to confirm the foundation bearing conditions and the required
embedment depths

Retaining structures should be well drained to reduce the build up of hydrostatic
pressures A typical drainage system would consist of a 12 inch wide zone of permeable
material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation immediately around a
perforated pipe perforations down at the base of the retaining structure discharging to an
appropriate outlet or weepholes As an alternative a prefabricated drainage product such as
MiraDrain or EnkaDrain may be used instead of the drainage material The prefabricated
drainage product should also be hydraulically connected to a perforated pipe at the base of
the retaining structures
Backfill behind the permeable drainage zone should consist of granular fill materials
less than 3 inches in maximum dimension The excavated on site granular soils may be
used as a source of backfill materials Unless covered by concrete slabs the upper
12 inches of backfill should consist of relatively impervious materials to reduce the potential
for significant water infiltration behind the retaining structures
Site Preparation
Based on our field observations and the information provided we anticipate that
minimal site grading work on the order of about 1 to 4 feet of cuts and fills will be required for
construction of the viewing platform and passenger loading zone at the subject site In
general the area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed
at the on set of earthwork Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should
be removed and disposed of off site
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas
designated to receive fill should be over excavated to expose firm natural material and the
resulting excavation should be backfilled with compacted granular fill material The excavated
soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site or used in landscaping areas
if appropriate
After clearing and grubbing areas designated to receive fills should be scarified to a
depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is
the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
The asphaltic concrete passenger loading zone and or the new bikepath subgrades
should be kept moist and free of shrinkage cracks until covered by base course or concrete
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after preparation of the subgrade we recommend that the
soil be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks Saturation and subsequent yielding of the
exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation
of the soft areas and replacement with compacted granular fill material

In general the on site sandy soils generated from the excavations may be re used as
a source of fill materials If imported fill materials will be necessary the imported fill materials
should consist of non expansive granular materials such as crushed coral basalt or cinder
sand The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than
3 inches in maximum dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles
passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more
and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with
ASTM D 1883
Imported fill materials should be tested for compliance with the recommendations
presented herein prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use Imported fill materials
should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction
Pavement Design
We understand that new asphaltic concrete paved passenger loading zone and or new
bikepaths may be constructed at the site In general we anticipate that the vehicle loading for
the pavements will consist primarily of passenger vehicles and light pick up trucks We have
made our preliminary pavement design assuming that the pavement subgrade will consist of
the re compacted granular materials encountered in the borings Based on the above
assumptions the following pavement section may be used for preliminary budgeting purposes
Flexible Asphaltic Concrete Pavements
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Moist Subgrade
The subgrade soils below the new pavement section should be scarified to a minimum
depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction The aggregate base course should consist of
crushed basalt aggregates compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction CBR
and field density tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils encountered during
construction to confirm the adequacy of the above section
Paved areas shouid be sloped and drainage gradients should be maintained to carry
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during or
after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or adjacent to the
pavement areas we recommend that the curbs be extended a minimum of 2 inches into the
subgrade soils below the aggregate base course to reduce the potential for migration of
landscape water into the pavement section

Utility Line Trenches
We envision that some new utility lines may be required for the proposed project In
general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials such as No 3B
Fine gravel should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the
pipes It is critical to use this free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of
voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes
Improper trench backfill could result in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to the
top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the excavated on site soils or select
granular fill material The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to
not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill
below the pavement grade should be compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction

considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between and
beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary
to re evaluate the recommendations provided in this report
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations on the logs of borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition
Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the graphic
representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed and
interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
