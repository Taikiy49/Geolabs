SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The site soils which contains recent fills
over soft compressible deposits will undergo
total settlements on the order of 3 inches
with differential settlements of about 1 inch
It is recommended that a rigid mat foundation
be used for the proposed buildings The total
loads on the mat will be on the order of 400
pounds per square foot
INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our foundation investi
gation for the proposed apartment house development located
in
The scope of this study was to investigate the subsurface
soil conditions and develop recommendations for foundation
design and site development
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is presently planned to construct an apartment complex
consisting of 3 buildings three stories in height of masonry

SITE CONDITIONS
Surface
The proposed apartment development of approximately 1 acre is
located on the southeast corner of Kahekili Highway and Hui Iwa
Street in The site is fairly level with
a slight slope towards Kahekili Highway then drops off ona
2 to 1 slope 8 to 10 feet high A concrete lined drainage
channel is located along the toe of slope
Geologically the site was a lowland sedimentation basin between
the ocean and the Koolau Range that formed swamp and marine
deposits Recent fills have been placed directly on the soft
and wet swamp deposits Based on the results of our field in
vestigation it appears the fills are a maximum of 14 feet thick
Subsurface
To explore the subsurface conditions at the site five borings
were drilled at locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 A

more detailed description of the field investigation and labora
tory testing is contained in the Appendix which also includes
the log of each boring and the results of our laboratory tests
All borings revealed that the site is immediately underlain by
about 4 to 14 feet of medium stiff clayey silt fill The in
place density of this fill varies slightly Underlying this
fill is the original marsh deposits which consist mainly of
black peat decomposed wood and vegetation that is very soft
and wet This peat deposit varies from about 2 to 8 feet in
thickness with the thicker portion at Borings 2 and 3 Under
lying this peat is a soft to medium stiff gray organic clay
that may be of marine origin In Boring 4 the peat material
was not encountered with the fill bearing on the gray clay
Beneath the organic clay is a soft to medium stiff mottled gray
and reddish brown clayey silt that appears to be a highly
decomposed residual soil This soil contains more sand or
coarser particles with an increase of depth
Groundwater was encountered from Elevations 85 to 96 feet which
is about 4 to 15 feet below the existing ground surface The
highest water level was encountered in Boring 4 at an elevation
of approximately 96
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Due to the presence of highly compressible soft organic deposits

tural design Therefore the proposed structures may be suppor

compacted material
Foundation
The proposed structures may be supported by a mat foundation
bottomed a minimum of 12 inches below final adjacent grade It
is our understanding that the mat foundation will develop
bearing pressures of 400 pounds per square foot The mat
foundation of buildings located adjacent to the existing slope
should maintain a horizontal distance of 4 feet from the outer
edge of the mat to the slope face The mat should also maintain
a minimum horizontal distance of 10 feet from the edge of the
concrete channel Due to the relatively variable condition of
the upper existing fill materials it is recommended that the
mat areas be over excavated to a depth of 2 inches and replaced
with compacted material The over excavated soils could be

Maximum total settlements for the proposed buildings will be on
the order of 3 to 3 inches Differential settlements on the
order of 1 inch can be expected for the proposed loadings It
is difficult to predict precisely the locations of variable
conditions that will cause large differential settlements
Therefore it is recommended that one inch of differential
settlement be considered acting across a span of 20 feet
It is anticipated that approximately half of the total settle
ment will take place within one month from the apptication
of the full load with the remaining settlement over a period
of 6 months All utility lines should be designed with
flexible joints capable of withstanding 2 inches differential
settlement All walkways and pavements adjacent to the build
ings should be designed with this differential settlement in
mind with provisions for repairs during the first year after

Site Preparation
Site shall be cleared of all deleterious and organic matter
In areas to receive fill the ground should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 6 inches Scarified areas shall then be
brought near optimum moisture thence compacted to a minimum
density of 90 percent of maximum Fills shall then be placed
in horizontal 6 inch maximum lifts and also compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent In cut areas the upper 12 inches
should be over excavated and compacted to at least 90 percent
of maximum densities If the high moisture site soils prove
to be very difficult to compact it is recommended that the
over excavated soils be replaced with a granular compacted
material
Water was encountered in Boring 4 at Elevation 96 This will
be close to the finished grade elevation and a subsurface
drain system may be required to prevent water seepage into
building and parking areas The exact location of subsurface
drains should be located during construction However in

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface soils at the site were explored by drilling five
test borings utilizing truck mounted rotary auger equipment
The depths of the borings ranged in 30 feet below the existing
ground surface The boring locations are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 1 The soils were classified by visual and textural exam
inations in the field by our engineer who continuously supervised
the drilling operations The classifications were later verified
by inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils were
classified in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification
System Graphical representations of the encountered soils are
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1A to A 1E Undisturbed
soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 4 I D sampler with a
140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow
counts required to drive the sampler the final 12 inches are
presented on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depth
