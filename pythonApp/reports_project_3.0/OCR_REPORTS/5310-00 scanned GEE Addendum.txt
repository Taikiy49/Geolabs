Dear Mr Beaton
The geotechnical engineering report referenced above generally covered
development of the Ke Kailani project site which is located within the existing Mauna Lani
Resort in the District of South Kohala on the Island of Hawaii The recommendations
presented in the report generally pertained to the design of mass grading retaining
structures underground utilities and pavements
As indicated in the geotechnical engineering report we recommended that the fill
areas of the project site be capped with a minimum 2 foot thick layer of structural fill
material to serve as a choking layer for the larger sized general fill materials below
Structural fill materials should consist of well graded granular materials generally less
than 6 inches in maximum dimension with sufficient fines to prevent the occurrence of
voids in the compacted mass It should be noted that the above recommendations are
generally consistent with the standard of practice for similar type projects and similar
subsurface conditions along the Kohala Coast on the Island of Hawaii
Due to budgetary considerations for the development we understand that it is
desired to reduce the thickness of the structural fill capping layer to 1 foot in the fill areas of
the project site We also understand that the finished pad elevations for the mass grading
generally is set at about 18 inches below the finished floor elevation of the new residential
structures planned at the project site We envision that the footings of the new structures
generally would be placed directly on the finished pad elevation on the 1 foot of structural
fill materials In addition we envision that an additional 13 to 14 inches of fill and cushion

In general we believe that the reduction of the structural fill capping layer in the fill

However we wish to point out that provision of only 1 foot of structural fill materials

along the Kohala Coast on the Island of Hawaii In addition the larger particle size
materials cobbles and boulders in the general and boulder fill materials would be
encountered at shallower depths during construction of future improvements such as
installation of underground utility lines swimming pools and or other below grade
construction Therefore proper representation of this information should be disclosed to
the future homeowners and lot owners if reduction of the structural fill capping layer from

owners who will be constructing structures and other improvements on the lots The
future lot owners should be required to engage the services of a qualified geotechnical
engineer to evaluate the subsurface conditions constructed for the lots and to design the
future home foundations and other improvements on the constructed lots
We also wish to emphasize that accurate surveying of the grades during
construction to maintain the 1 foot minimum thickness of structural fill should be reviewed
and diligently practiced by the Contractor during construction
In addition it is imperative that Geolabs provide continued geotechnical
engineering services during the project construction This is to confirm the subsurface
conditions assumed in our report and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may
be required in the event that the subsurface conditions at the site differ from those
assumed and anticipated The recommendations provided in the above referenced report
are contingent upon such observations
