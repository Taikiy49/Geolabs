This letter presenting our preliminary geotechnical engineering recommendations
for the design of the proposed Kona Liberty House is provided in response to the
accelerated time schedule for this project These preliminary recommendations are
based on our previous experience at the adjacent Kona K Mart store We do not
anticipate significant changes in the subsurface conditions at the Kona Liberty House
site however the preliminary recommendations presented herein are subject to
confirmation by our forthcoming field exploration A formal report summarizing our work
on the project will be submitted at a later date upon completion of the field exploration
program and office engineering work

Foundations
Based on our experience at the adjacent Kona K Mart site and visual observa
tions we anticipate the site to be underlain by a very thin surficial layer of soil over
basalt formation The basalt is anticipated to have voids and cavities in the formation
characteristic of the pahoehoe lava flows Since the entire building is anticipated to be
in cut into the basalt formation we recommend that shallow spread and or continuous
footings bearing on the basalt formation be used to support the proposed Liberty House
store and related facilities
For footings bearing on the basalt formation an allowable bearing pressure of
up to 6 000 pounds per square foot p s f may be used for preliminary design This
bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 for
transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces We recommend that
the footings be embedded at least 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the foundation and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings
For footings bearing directly on basalt rock a coefficient of friction of 0 6 may be used
Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 350 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f for footings embedded
in compacted fill Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the
upper 12 inches below finished grade should be neglected
Foundation Probing
Based on our experience at the adjacent site the basalt formation at the site
consists of lava flows prone to contain tubes voids and or cavities In order to reduce
the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of a void below a
footing we recommend that a program of cavity probing be required during
construction Probes are generally drilled at each individual spread footing location and
on 10 foot intervais along wail footing locations The probes are generally 3 inches in
diameter and should be drilled to a depth of at least 10 feet below the planned bottom

Slabs On Grade
We anticipate concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed Liberty
House store Since the slab on grade will bear on the basalt rock cut we recommend
that the rock subgrade be proof rolled with heavy equipment such as D 9 bulldozer
a minimum of 4 passes to assist in detecting and possibly collapsing near surface
cavities
In general we recommend that concrete slabs be supported on a cushion fill
layer consisting of 4 inches of No 3B Fine Gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation The
cushion fill layer would provide more uniform support of the slab and would also serve
as a capillary break A moisture membrane should be incorporated above the capillary
break to reduce potential damage to moisture sensitive floor coverings by infiltration of
moisture It is advisable to place a 2 inch layer of moist fine sand above the vapor
membrane to protect the membrane and to aid in concrete curing
Where slabs on grade will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as the loading
dock slab a 6 inch layer of compacted base course is recommended for more uniform
support in lieu of the 4 inch cushion fill and 2 inch sand The base course should
consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction For design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of 250
pounds per square inch per inch of deflection p c i may be used for the compacted
base course Provisions should be made for proper load transfer across the slab joints
which are subjected to vehicular traffic The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any
unpaved area should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade

Retaining Walls
We anticipate that a retaining wall ranging up to about 15 feet in height will be
constructed near the intersection of Makala Boulevard and Kamakaeha Avenue At this
time we do not know the top and footing elevations of the wall The proposed retaining
wall may be located in fill or may be bearing on the basalt formation near the existing
ground surface We should evaluate the foundation conditions of proposed retaining
walls for further recommendations
In general we believe that retaining wall foundations bearing on the basalt
formation may be designed with the recommendations presented in the above
Foundations section For foundations located on engineered fills we recommend that
an allowable bearing pressure of 3 500 p s f dead plus live loads be used for the
design of wail footings bearing on compacted fill The wali footings should be
embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
We anticipate that the proposed wall will retain engineered fill material consisting
of granular fills The proposed retaining wall should be designed to resist lateral earth
pressure due to adjacent soils and surcharge effects The recommended active lateral
earth pressure for design expressed in equivalent fluid pressure is 35 pounds per
cubic foot p c f for an assumed level backfill condition
The values provided above assume that the on site rock fill and or select
granular fill will be used to backfill behind the wail This lateral earth pressure does not
include hydrostatic pressures that might be caused by groundwater trapped behind the
walls Retaining walls should be well drained to reduce the build up of hydrostatic
pressures A typical drainage system would consist of a 1 to 2 foot wide zone of
permeable material No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation immediately
adjacent to the wall with a perforated pipe perforations down at the base of the wall
discharging to a discharge facility or weep holes
Surcharge effects from loads adjacent to retaining structure should also be
included in the design of the wall A rectangular distribution over the height of the wail
with a pressure equal to 30 percent of the surcharge load is normally used for a
cantilever type wall
Since the island of Hawaii is located in Seismic Zone 3 design of retaining

Site Grading
Based on the information provided we anticipate the proposed site grading will
consist of cuts of up to about 30 feet and fills up to about 15 feet Cuts are generally
planned at the rear portion of the proposed Liberty House store with fills in the parking
lot in front of the store In general we believe that the cut slopes planned at the rear
of the site may be designed with a slope inclination of one horizontal to one vertical
1H 1V or flatter In fill areas the excavated materials from the cut areas may be used
as rock fills We recommend that all fill areas be capped with a choker fill layer to
reduce the potential for migration of fines from the upper portion of the fill through the
voids of the compacted rock fill Choker fill materials should consist of well graded
granular material of 6 inches in maximum dimension with sufficient fines to prevent the
occurrence of voids in the compacted mass
