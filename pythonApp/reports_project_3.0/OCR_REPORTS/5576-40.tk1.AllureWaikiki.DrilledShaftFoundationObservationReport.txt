Project Considerations
The project site is along Kalakaua Avenue extending approximately 420 feet toward
the northwest mauka side from Ena Road in the Waikiki area of Honolulu on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project site
encompasses an area of approximately 93 700 square feet not including Makaoe Lane
and Pau Lane It should be noted that Makaoe Lane and Pau Lane traverse the project
site
The new high rise building and parking structure are supported on deep
foundations consisting of cast in place concrete drilled shafts Drilled shafts with
diameters of 30 and 42 inches and lengths ranging from about 43 5 to 123 5 feet were
used to support the new high rise residential building project As part of the construction
requirements one static load test was conducted to evaluate and confirm the foundation
design
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the
drilled shaft foundation construction for the Allure Waikiki project only The general
contractor for the project is Charles Pankow Builders Ltd and the drilled shaft
contractor is Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company Inc
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the project site are typical of the conditions found
elsewhere in the Waikiki area The project site is underlain by 3 feet of surface fills placed
over highly compressible lagoonal deposits The surface fills encountered in the borings
consisted of medium dense silty sands with gravel Below the surface sandy fills the
borings encountered highly compressible lagoonal deposits extending to depths of about
25 to 30 feet below the ground level The lagoonal deposits encountered consisted of light
gray to gray soft sandy silts and very loose to loose silty and clayey sands with coralline
gravel
We encountered tan and or white coral formation upper coral ledge below the
lagoonal deposits at about 25 to 30 feet below the ground surface The coral formation
encountered in the borings drilled ranged from soft to hard and extended down to depths
of approximately 60 to 65 feet below the ground level The subsurface conditions
encountered below the upper coral ledge consisted of interbedded layers of alluvium
coralline detritus and coral formation extending to the maximum depths explored of
approximately 137 5 feet below the ground surface
We encountered groundwater in the borings at depths of about 2 to 3 8 feet below
the existing ground surface at the time of our field explorations March and August 2006
The groundwater levels generally correspond to about Elevations 0 3 to 2 0 feet Mean

Summary of the Drilled Shaft Installation
As part of the project requirements one sacrificial load test shaft was constructed
and tested Following completion of the load test the drilled shaft lengths for the project
were revised based on the results of the instrumented load test The following subsections
provide descriptions of the load test shaft construction and discussions on the production
drilled shaft installation
Load Test Shaft
As part of the project specifications one sacrificial 42 inch diameter load test shaft
was installed at the approximate location indicated on the Foundation Plan Plate 2 The
load test shaft was excavated by the drilled shaft contractor on December 18 and 19
2007 The 42 inch diameter load test shaft was extended to a depth of about 126 feet
below the existing ground surface The drilled shaft contractor lowered the reinforcing cage
with an Osterberg Load Cell and embedment strain gage instrumentation into the drilled
hole and placed concrete in the load test shaft on
Temporary casing with a diameter of about 46 inches also was installed to about
34 3 feet below the ground surface for the load test shaft Details of the load test shaft
construction are presented on the attached Plate 4
After the load test shaft was installed and cured for at least seven days
Load Test Inc performed the load test on The 42 inch diameter load
test shaft was loaded to approximately 3 833 kips Complete details of the load test
program are presented in the report prepared by Load Test Inc

Production Drilled Shafts
The drilled shaft contractor installed 121 production shafts from January 8 through
March 13 2008 The as built diameters and the lengths of the production drilled shafts
installed for the project are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 4 of this letter report
Representatives from Geolabs observed the drilled shaft excavation placement of
reinforcing cage and concrete operations during the installation of the production drilled
shafts
In general the production drilled shafts were excavated using a Soil MEC 625 drill
rig equipped with 30 and 42 inch diameter auger core barrel and clean out bucket similar
to the load test shaft Temporary steel casings were installed to depths of about 15 to
36 feet below the ground surface in the production drilled shafts In general 34 and
46 inch diameter temporary casings were used for 30 and 42 inch diameter drilled shafts
respectively After excavation of the production shafts to their designated tip elevations
the reinforcing cages were lowered into the drilled holes to the appropriate elevations with
the use of a crane After the reinforcing cage was installed in the drilled hole concrete was
placed through a 4 inch steel tremie pipe starting from the bottom During concrete
placement we observed that at least 5 feet of concrete head was maintained above the
bottom of the tremie pipe In addition sand content measurements were performed on an
as needed basis prior to the concrete placement Where the measurements indicated that
the sand content exceeded one percent Insta Floc slurry flocculent was used to remove
the fine grained solids that were suspended in the slurry
Due to the presence of groundwater within the drilled shaft holes concrete was
placed in the drilled holes using the tremie method Concrete with slump ranging from 7 to
9 inches was used for the drilled shafts Pacific Testing Services Inc monitored the
