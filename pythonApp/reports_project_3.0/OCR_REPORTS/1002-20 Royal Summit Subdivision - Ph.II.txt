From May 20 1983 to we have inspected the
earthwork and grading operations performed at the above referenced

tests performed at the above referenced lots Grading and earthwork
inspections for other lots were covered in our previous interim reports
1 thru 7
It is our opinion that as of this date the above referenced lots
are constructed to final grade and compacted adequately The density
test results for the in place fills are indicated in the attached
Density Test Summary A site plan indicating the location and number
of the lots is also attached

RECOMMENDATIONS
House Foundations
The following general foundation guidelines are based on visual
observations and swell tests performed on the surface soils Borings or
probings might be utilized prior to or at the start of construction to
determine whether the soil conditions below the surface are consistent
with our findings
We are presenting hereunder general house foundation recommendations
for preliminary design purposes
ls Footings located on or near slopes should penetrate down a
sufficient depth in order to develop a minimum horizontal set
back distance of 5 feet measured from the outer edge of the
footing foundation out to the slope face
Individual footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum
of 12 inches below the ground surface to minimize the effect of
subsequent soil shrinkage associated with moisture loss due to
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1002 20 Page 4
drier condition under the roof area All footing backfill
should be properly compacted to obtain 90 compaction and
graded to drain water away from the footing
We recommend that a soils engineer be retained to inspect the
bottom of all footing excavations prior to concrete placement
2 An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for
foundations bearing on compacted fill or stiff natural soil
3 Slab on grade construction should be set back at least 6 feet
from the tops of slopes
Twelve inch deep perimeter footings below the lowest adjacent
grade are recommended to act as a cut off wall against the
future subgrade moisture changes due to drying and wetting
around the slab
4 The subgrade soils below the slabs should be maintained in a
moist condition from the initial foundation excavation up to
the concrete placement
Based on the swell test results and visual observations the near
surface soils on the following lots have been categorized as having

recommendations for these lots are presented below
Lots 19 thru 85 176 thru 183
Laboratory ring swell tests and visual observation indicate
that the soils near finished grade on these lots may be
moderately expansive Therefore the footng embedment should
be increased oO 18 inches below the lowest adjacent ground
surface and 12 inches of non expansive fill should be placed
below the slab on grade
Lots 42 thru 57 59 thru 64 High Swell Potential
Laboratory ring swell test indicate that the soils near
finished grade on these lots may be highly expansive There
fore the footing embedment for individual and wall footing
should be increased to 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
ground surface
Twenty four 24 inches of non expansive fill are recommended
below the slab on grade and perimeter footings

The near finish grade soils at these lots may be expansive as
indicated by the laboratory ring swell test Therefore the
non expansive fill placed below the slab on grade should be
increased to 36 inches Individual footings and wall footings
should be embeded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest

For all the houses it is critical that the subgrade soils below the
foundations be kept moist for a few days prior to concrete placement
It is imperative that all the lots be graded to drain water away
from house foundations to prevent saturation and softening of the
subgrade soils
Soil Engineering Inspection Services

concrete placement

engineer on a lot by lot basis to see if the above general recommenda
tions need to be revised for specific house and lot conditions
