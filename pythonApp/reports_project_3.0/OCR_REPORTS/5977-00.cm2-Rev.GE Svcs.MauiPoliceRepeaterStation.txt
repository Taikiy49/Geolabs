A geotechnical engineering exploration was performed for the RTR Facility Site
Access Road Maui Police Repeater Station project at Mt Haleakala on the Island of Maui
Hawaii Our findings and recommendations were presented in our report dated
This letter presents information on possible stabilization methods to
reduce erosion at the site
The subsurface conditions consist primarily of cinder deposits including sand with
silt and gravel and many cobbles and boulders The cinder deposits generally are lightly
cemented Due to the high wind velocities at the site erosion of the cinder can be severe
Alternatives to reduce the erosion of the surface soils were evaluated to determine
suitability for the project
Three general types of stabilization were considered geotextile matting grouting
and pavement Geotextile mats are often used to protect slope surfaces against erosion
particularly from water runoff Geotextile mats would be unsuitable at this site because of
the high wind forces that would lift and pull off the mats It is doubtful that sufficient
anchorage could be installed to resist the high wind forces
Grouting is often used to cement and solidify the subsurface conditions The grout
is pumped into a drilled hole under pressure to attempt to penetrate the formation within a
distance from the drilled hole A series of holes are drilled and grouted to achieve an
overlapping coverage of the formation creating a cemented mass The effectiveness of
the grouting depends on several parameters including the type of soil permeability
uniformity of formation lack of highly permeable zones and depth of stabilized zone
desired The type of grout used will depend on the grain size of the subsoils For the cinder
soils at the site micro fine cements or chemical grouts would be most effective The main

drawback to grouting at this site would be that the surface soils 5 to 10 feet deep and
slope areas would be very difficult to cement because sufficient grout pressures could not
be achieved to penetrate into the formation When the grout pressures in the drilled hole
reach the overburden pressure the grout will leak to the ground surface or slope face and
not penetrate the formation Due to these conditions grouting does not seem to be a
viable method to stabilize the surface soils
Paving the site with concrete shotcrete or asphalt would be effective in resisting
erosion provided the end conditions edges of the pavement are protected with deep
cutoff walls
