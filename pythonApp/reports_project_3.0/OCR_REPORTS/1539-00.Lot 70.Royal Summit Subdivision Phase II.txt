Soil Engineering Report
Lot 70 Royal Summit Subdivision
Phase ITI
Aiea Gahu Hawaii
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to
provide foundation recommendations for the proposed residence
The study consisted of hand augering two shallow test pits
laboratory testing of the swelling potential of the soils a
review of the subdivision site grading plans and the preliminary
house foundation plans This report summarizes our observations
and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a one story structure approxi
mately 43 by 43 feet in plan dimension Concrete on grade slab
is planned for the garage house and lanai

SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is on the northeastern corner of Kaahele Street
and Kaahele Place in the Royal Summit Phase II Subdivision
located in Aiea Oahu Hawaii Most of the lot is a relatively
flat pad which is about 2 to 3 feet higher than the adjacent
roads
A review of the site grading plans for the subdivision indicates
that this lot required cuts of up to 15 feet high during the
recent subdivision grading operation
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
The test pits encountered stiff mottled brown silty residual
soil clay to about 2 feet deep
The laboratory testing on the silty clay indicates that this
material generally has high swelling potential

DISCUSSICN AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The stiff surface soils encountered can generally provide
adequate foundation support for the proposed house provided that
some care is exercised during construction to minimize the
swelling potential of the scil
Laboratory tests performed on the stiff on site clayey soil
indicates that this material generally has high swelling
potential Therefore to minimize effects due to soil swelling
under on grade slabs we recommend that the on site soil be
capped with non expansive select fill and that the subgrade
scils underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and
protected from drying from the start of construction until the
slab concrete placement
House Foundations

finished grade should be removed and replaced with compacted
non expansive soil such as select borrow crusher waste or
coral The overexcavation and backfilling should extend a

Concrete Driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the

Site Grading
Fills should consist of select non expansive soil such as
crusher waste or coral
Fills and backfilis should be placed in maximum 68 inch lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 78

Lot Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to
avoid ponding condition Also the owners should be advised to
install a gutter system around the house and they should also be
made aware that gutter water should be diverted far away from the
perimeter footings and walkways Excessive landscape watering
near the foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded
water could cause excessive subsurface scil saturation and
subsequent loss of strength or heaving The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled tight against sleb
footing immediately after setting of the concrete to prevent
ponding condition

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
