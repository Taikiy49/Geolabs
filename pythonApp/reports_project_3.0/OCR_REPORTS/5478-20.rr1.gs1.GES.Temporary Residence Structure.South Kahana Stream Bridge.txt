Dear Mr Lock
In accordance with our fee proposal dated August 14 2015 this letter report
provides our findings and recommendations for the temporary residence structure
planned for the South Kahana Stream Bridge Replacement project on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This letter report summarizes the findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our site reconnaissance and desk top study of

recommendations presented herein are subject to limitations noted at the end of this
report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located on the makai side of South Kahana Stream and
Kamehameha Highway in the District of Kahana on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
project consists of the construction of a temporary residence structure for the relocation
of residents adjacent to the planned bypass roadway for the construction of the main
bridge structure for the South Kahana Stream Bridge Replacement project
We understand that the temporary structure will be constructed in an open area
west of the current house location adjacent to the bypass road In addition we
understand that the temporary structure will be a one story house supported on a
post and beam foundation A new gravel road will also be constructed for the access of
residence to the highway

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Temporary Building Foundations
We understand that a wooden post and beam foundation system will be utilized for
the temporary residence An allowable bearing pressure of up to 1 500 pounds per square
foot psf may be used for the design of footings bearing on the re compacted sandy
subgrade soils This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third ⅓ for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
The bottom of the footing excavations should be scarified to a depth of at least
8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and recompacted to at
least 90 percent relative compaction to provide a relatively firm and smooth bearing
surface prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D1557 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum
dry density
Soft and or loose materials encountered at the bottom of the footing excavations
should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation
may be backfilled with the on site soils compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction or the bottom of the footing may be extended down to the underlying
competent materials
In general footings should have a minimum width of 12 inches In addition the
bottom of the footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grades
If the foundations are designed and constructed in accordance with our
recommendations we estimate that total settlements of footings bearing on the
re compacted sandy soils to be less than 1 inch We estimate that differential
settlements between adjacent footings to be on the order of about 0 5 inches or less
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of foundations A
coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be used for footings bearing on the re compacted sandy
subgrade soils Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This

Slabs On Grade
We envision exterior concrete walkways will be required for the proposed project
Based on our field reconnaissance the new walkways will be underlain by loose to
medium dense beach sand The new concrete walkways may be placed on the
re compacted sandy subgrade soils
The subgrade soil under the concrete slab should be scarified to a depth of at least
8 inches moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture and re compacted to at least
90 percent relative compaction
To reduce the potential for substantial shrinkage cracks in the slabs crack control
joints should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkways or slabs with
expansion joints provided at right angle intersections
It should be emphasized that the areas adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled
tightly against the slab edges and graded to divert water away from the slabs to reduce the
potential for appreciable water ponding around the slabs
Site Grading
Based on the anticipated finished grade elevations we anticipate site grading
consisting of fills and excavations of about 1 to 4 feet thick will be required to achieve the
design finished grades at the project site
A Geolabs representative should monitor site grading operations to observe
whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation and scarification
process and to confirm whether the exposed soil conditions are similar to those assumed
in this report
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation tree roots debris deleterious materials
existing pavements and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site or in a designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the
excavated materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas
designated to receive fill and or future improvements should be over excavated to expose
firm material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill
After clearing and grubbing areas to receive fills and finished subgrades in cut
areas should be scarified to a depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the

optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight

In general the on site sandy soils may be reused as a source of general fill
material provided they are free of vegetation deleterious materials and rock fragments
greater than 3 inches in maximum dimension Imported fill materials should consist of
crushed basalt or coral The select granular fill should be well graded from coarse to fine
with particles no larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between
10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a
laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 20 or more and should have a
maximum swell of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM D1883
In general fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Compaction should be
accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or other types of acceptable
compaction equipment Water tamping jetting or ponding should not be allowed to
compact the fills
Where compaction is less than required additional compactive effort should be
applied with adjustment of moisture content as necessary to obtain the specified
compaction
Existing Cesspool
An existing cesspool was observed within the proposed gravel driveway area It
should be determined if the cesspool is being used or abandoned If the cesspool is
abandoned sludge or waste at the bottom of the cesspool should be removed The
cesspool should be backfilled with No 2 Coarse Rock ASTM C33 No 4 gradation up to
about 1 foot above the groundwater level The concrete side walls should be removed to
a minimum depth of 3 feet below finished grade The backfill from about 1 foot above the
groundwater level should consist of select granular fill material compacted to a minimum of
90 relative compaction Alternately Controlled Low Strength Material CLSM may be
used as backfill material above the groundwater level

On Moist Compacted Subgrade
The subgrade soil should be moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture and
compacted to minimum 90 percent relative compaction Drainage gradients should be
provided and maintained to carry surface water away from the roadway
It should be noted that there is a potential for raveling and rutting of the gravel
surface layer over time Therefore periodic maintenance will be required for the
gravel surfaced roads We believe the majority of the maintenance will consist of periodic
grading to remove ruts and potholes caused by the environment and traffic
Utility Trenches
We anticipate that new sewer and water lines will be installed for the project In
general good construction practices should be utilized for the installation and backfilling of
the trenches for the new utilities The contractor should determine the method and
equipment to be used for trench excavation subject to practical limits and safety
considerations
In general we recommend providing granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of
open graded gravel ASTM C33 No 67 gradation under the pipes for uniform support
Free draining granular materials such as open graded gravel ASTM C33 No 67
gradation also should be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above
the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes It is critical to use this
free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of
pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper trench backfill
could result in backfill settlement and pipe damage
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of on site soils or select granular fill
material The backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to no less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the
potential for appreciable future ground subsidence The upper 3 feet below the finished
grade in areas subjected to vehicular traffic should be compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction

It is recommended that Geolabs be retained to provide geotechnical engineering
services during construction of the proposed project The items of construction monitoring
that are critical requiring Special Inspections include observation of subgrade

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based in part upon
information obtained from our field reconnaissance and available boring information
near the vicinity of project Variations of conditions from our field reconnaissance and
available borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations presented herein
The geotechnical engineering recommendations submitted herein are based on
the assumptions that the housing unit and driveway are temporary and will be used for a
maximum period of three years
Water level data from the available boring logs was measured at the time of
drilling These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation
herein However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in tidal
fluctuation seasonal rainfall and other factors
