Dear Mr. Miyahara: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observation services performed 
during the installation and testing of the pre-production sacrificial anchor for the Kuhio 
Highway Emergency Slope Stabilization in the Vicinity of Kalihiwai Bridge project located 
on the Island of Kauai, Hawaii. 
In general, this letter report presents our observations and the results of the 
pre-production anchor installation and testing performed for the project. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The slope stabilization project is along Kuhio Highway near the Kalihiwai Bridge 
on the Island of Kauai, Hawaii.  Due to heavy rains during the months of February and 
March 2012, slope failures occurred along the adjacent roadway slopes. This project 
involves the installation of remedial measures for the failed slope areas. 
The remedial measures include slope scaling and the installation of anchored 
and draped wire mesh. 
PRE-PRODUCTION ANCHOR INSTALLATION AND TESTING 
The sacrificial anchor installation and verification test were performed by Hi-Tech 
Rockfall Construction, Inc. The approximate location of the anchor is shown on the Soil 
Nail Location Plan, Plate 2. Installation and testing of the soil nails were conducted from 
August 13 to 17, 2020.

Anchor Installation 
A Geolabs representative observed the drilling and grouting operations during 
construction of the pre-production sacrificial anchor. The anchor was drilled using a winch 
rig equipped with a 4-inch diameter downhole hammer and a 4-inch diameter button 
hammer bit. 
The sacrificial anchor was drilled to a depth of about 13 feet from the slope face 
with an inclination of about 20 degrees.  After the hole was drilled to the specified depth, 
the Grade 75 #11 rebar was inserted into the drilled hole.  Centralizers were placed along 
the rebar at 5-foot intervals. 
The sacrificial anchor was grouted using a ½-inch diameter tremie tube that was 
fully inserted into the drilled hole. Grouting of the anchor was terminated when fresh grout 
appeared at the top of the hole. The grouting tube was removed after the completion of the 
grouting operation.  
The volume of grout placed in the drilled hole was about 3 cubic feet (ft3), 
approximately 3.4 times the theoretical volume of the drilled hole. Log of the sacrificial 
anchor recording our observations and other pertinent information by our representative 
are provided in the Soil Nail Installation Record, Plates 2.1 and 2.2.  
Anchor Testing 
The pre-production anchor was tested on August 17, 2020.  The testing apparatus 
included a 60-ton hydraulic jack (Enerpac RCH603 – S/N 749511) and a 1-inch stroke dial 
gauge. The test was conducted by pre-loading the testing apparatus up to about 10 kips to 
engage the reaction timbers and ground. The verification anchor was then loaded up to 
about 200 percent of the design load (15 kips). The anchor was tested to 31 kips with a 
total displacement of about 1.038 inches.  Details of the anchor testing are presented on 
Plates 3.1 through 3.4. 
Based on the observations of our field representative, we believe that the 
pre-production anchor observed by Geolabs has been installed in general conformance 
with the project plans and specifications and within the limits of generally accepted 
construction practices. Based on our observations of the verification anchor test performed 
by the contractor, it is our professional opinion that the anchor has been installed to 
provide the sufficient load capacity with tolerable movements. Therefore, we recommend 
using the production anchor lengths provided in the contract documents.
