Soil Engineering Investigation
Free Communion Church
Kauai Pavilion Site
As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil
conditions at the above referenced site in order to provide foundation
recommendations for the proposed pavilion structure The study
included field reconnaissance drilling two shallow borings and
laboratory testing of selected samples This report summarizes our
observations and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The foundation plan for the structure was not available at the
time of our field exploration It is our understanding that the
proposed pavilion will be a one story structure with concrete on grade
slab approximately 30 by 30 feet in plan dimension Light structural
loads are anticipated

SITE CONDITIONS
The site is on the southwest end of Kaholalele Road located in
Wailua on the island of Kauai
It is bounded from the west to the south by a near vertical cliff
which extends down to the north fork of the Wailua River and
surrounded from the north to east by additional property owned by the
Free Communion Church
Ground surface elevations were not available at the time of our
investigation However the site is located at the lowest point
within the property and generally slopes toward the west at about a 10
percent grade
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Two shallow exploratory borings were drilled to depths ranging
from 6 5 to 8 0 feet to investigate the subsurface conditions and to
obtain samples for laboratory analysis
Relatively uniform soil conditions were encountered in our
investigation Stiff brown clayey silt was encountered to depths of
4 5 feet overlying a stiff mottled brown clayey silt to the depth

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by
stiff silty residual soils and is suitable for the proposed
construction
Site Grading
A preliminary grading plan was not available to us during the
course of our subsurface exploration Based on the general topography
of the site small cuts and fills less than 3 feet thick are
anticipated for the proposed development
Site Preparation
At the commencement of earthwork all deleterious materials such
as vegetation topsoil and unsuitable fill should be removed and
disposed of properly off site
Prior to filling the existing ground at the finished subgrade
level in cut areas should be scarified to a depth of 6 inches

Structural Fill Material and Placement
he
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results in
general the on site soil are suitable for use in the structural
fill The soils should be compacted at the optimum moisture
content to minimize the swelling potential
Boulders larger than 6 inches in diameter should not be used in
the fill
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 6
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90
of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
The soils at finished subgrade should be kept moist during
construction to minimize the swelling potential
Site grading should provide drainage of surface runoff away from
all foundations

Building Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundations bearing on the stiff
in situ soil is recommended for the proposed structure
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the
lowest adjacent finished grade Where the new footings are adjacent
to utility trenches they should be deepened to match the trench
bottom This is to minimize the possibility of settlement due to
loose trench backfill below the footing
For the abovementioned recommendations an allowable bearing
Capacity of 2500 p s f may be used for foundations The bearing
capacity value is for total dead plus live loads only and may be
increased by one third for short duration wind and or seismic loading

On Grade Slab
Generally the in situ soils if properly prepared have a low
swelling potential therefore on grade slab construction may be
utilized
For the light floor loads anticipated a 4 inch concrete slab
over 4 inches of well graded aggregate base course is
recommended A plastic vapor barrier over the base course layer
may be used if a moisture barrier is deemed necessary to protect
floor covering
Design Review
We recommend that plans and specifications for grading and
foundation construction be forwarded to the soils engineer for review
and written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of
our recommendations is included
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the filling footing excavation and
backfilling operations be observed by a soils engineer during
construction This is to see that adequate compaction has been
obtained and the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation Should conditions encountered during

Our services consist of professional opinions and recommen

The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are
based on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and

Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by reconnaissance and soil borings These
unforeseen conditions such as pockets of organic soils trash fill
