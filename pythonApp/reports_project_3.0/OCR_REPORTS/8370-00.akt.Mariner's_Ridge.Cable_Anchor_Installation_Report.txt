Dear Mr. Bible: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation services 
performed during the wire rope anchor installation and testing for the Mariners Ridge 
Booster II Renovation project located in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. 
In general, this letter report presents our field observations and summarizes the 
results of testing performed for the wire rope anchors from January 19, 2022, 
through September 14, 2023. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project is located at 947 Kaluanui Road in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, 
Hawaii. As part of Mariners Ridge Booster II Renovation, rockfall mitigation using a rockfall 
cabled netting was specified and constructed by Prometheus Construction to minimize the 
risk of detached rocks and/or debris falling from the slope face directly onto the service 
access road of the existing booster facility in an uncontrolled manner. 
The wire rope anchor length was specified as about 8 feet long (in rock) or 10 feet 
long (in soil) with design pull-out capacities of 25 kips for back-slope anchors and 45 kips 
for lateral termination anchors. A total of 31 wire rope anchors were installed for the project, 
including 2 lateral termination anchors (Nos. 1 and 31) and 29 back-slope anchors (Nos. 2 
through 30). The approximate locations of the wire rope anchors are presented in the Wire 
Rope Anchor Numbering Plan, Plate 1. The wire rope cable anchors were composed of 
galvanized double wire rope with a 5/8-inch diameter and 4,000 pounds per square inch 
(psi) cement grout.

The purpose of our geotechnical engineering services was to observe the installation 
and testing of the wire rope anchors for general conformance with the project plans and 
specifications. 
WIRE ROPE ANCHOR INSTALLATION AND TESTING 
The wire rope anchors were installed using a hand-operated hydraulic rotary drill with 
a 3-inch diameter drill bit and air pressure supplied by an XAVS 400 JD7 compressor. 
Each wire rope anchor was extended to a depth of approximately 8 feet (in rock 
condition) or 10 feet if there was no presence of rock material. The borehole was cleaned 
by blowing out the cuttings using the air compressor and then taped to confirm the depth. A 
galvanized double wire rope was used to insert the specified depth for the anchors. 
Grout was batched on-site using 94-pound Portland Cement in a bucket with a 
hand-operated mixer. Grout was then poured from the bucket into the borehole with the wire 
rope anchor in-place and topped off as needed. The wire rope anchor installation records 
are summarized on Plate 2 (Summary of Wire Rope Anchor Installation and Testing). Details 
of the anchor installation records are included in Appendix A. 
It should be noted that Anchor No. 25 encountered a cavity resulting in excessive 
grout overage. Anchor No. 25 was abandoned and replaced by Anchor No. 25A. 
A total of seven proof tests were performed on the selected top slope wire rope 
anchors (Nos. 7, 11, 16, 20, 24, 24a, and 27) to the maximum test load of about 33.3 kips, 
equivalent to about 133 percent of the design pull-out capacity.  It should be noted that one 
of the proof tests conducted on Anchor No. 24 was not able to reach 90 percent of the design 
pull-out capacity. Anchor No. 24 was then abandoned, and replacement Anchor No. 24A 
was installed and proof tested to meet the design requirements. 
It should be noted that the lateral termination Anchor Nos. 1 and 31 were previously 
tested to the maximum test load of about 33.3 kips. To verify the design pullout capacity of 
45 kips as specified by the manufacturer, additional proof tests were performed on lateral 
termination Anchor Nos. 1 and 31. While load testing Anchor No. 1, the double wire rope 
sheared at 133 percent of design pull-out capacity (60kips). Replacement Anchor No.1a 
was installed on September 7, 2023, at about 6 inches from the original design location, as 
shown on the Numbering Plan, Plate 1. Then, both of lateral termination Anchor Nos. 1a 
and 31 were tested to 100 percent of the design pull-out capacity (45 kips). 
The wire rope anchor head movements at each load increment up to the maximum 
test load were measured and recorded. The results of the proof testing are summarized on 
Plate 2 and, also presented in Appendix B.
