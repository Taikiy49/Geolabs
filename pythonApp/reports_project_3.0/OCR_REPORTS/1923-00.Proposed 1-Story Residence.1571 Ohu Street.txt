In accordance with your request we have performed a geotechnical
engineering exploration of the above referenced site The purpose of our
study was to examine the surface and near surface conditions at the site
in order to provide soil data for the design of retaining walls and house
foundations
This report summarizes our preliminary findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located on the slope of Kalihi Valley at the
eastern end of Ohu Street The site is bounded by Ohu Street to the north
and existing single family residences on all other sides The site has
been recently graded with the placement of fill crusher waste
It is currently proposed to construct a single family residence on
the site It is also proposed to construct retaining walls along both the
east and west property lines

It is anticipated that the house will be of post and beam
construction and that the eastern retaining wall will be built along a
slope cut in rock
FIELD EXPLORATION
The field exploration consisted of drilling five 5 borings to
depths between 5 5 and 10 0 below the existing ground surface in the area
of the proposed house and the drilling of five 5 shallow hand auger
probes to depths between 1 0 and 1 5 feet along the alignment of the
proposed eastern wall Our field work indicated that the site the house
area is underlain by stiff brown gray clay Adobe overlying basaltic
rock The previously placed fill encountered in the drilled borings
varies from 0 to 4 8 feet in thickness The probes drilled along the
eastern wall alignment indicated that basaltic rock occurs at very shallow
depths below the surface brown gray clay soil Adobe
Groundwater was neither encountered in the drilled borings nor the
probes at the time of our exploration
The locations of the borings and the hand auger probes are indicated
on the attached Site Plan The boring and probe logs are attached for
your reference
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the surface and near surface of
the house area is underlain by wet stiff clay below a variable layer of
surface fill These soils have a moderate to high expansion potential
Special foundation design or site grading may be required to resist or
reduce potential foundation movements

The field exploration also indicated that the area of the proposed
eastern retaining wall is underlain at very shallow depths by basaltic
bedrock
Foundations
Based upon the boring and laboratory test data the on site
brown gray clay soils Adobe have moderate to high expansion
potential In order to resist or reduce potential foundation movements
we recommend that there be a minimm of three 3 feet of separation
between the bottoms of the footings and the underlying adobe soil This
separation may be achieved by either excavation and replacement of the
soils below the footings or by placing additional non expansive soil on
the lot to raise the grade
For the excavation and replacement alternative we recommend that
each individual footing area be excavated to a depth of three 3 feet
below the bottom of the footing This excavation should extend a minimm
of one foot 1 beyond the exterior edges of the footing The resulting
excavation should be backfilled with non expansive select material such
as crusher waste placed in 6 inch lifts and compacted to 90 of its
maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D 1557 78 As a
sub alternative the entire lot area could be overexcavated to a depth of
three 3 feet or to rock whichever occurs first The lot could then be
backfilled as described above
For the additional fill alternative non expansive select material
should be placed in maximm 8 inch lifts moisture conditioned to slightly
above optimum moisture content and compacted to 90 maximum dry density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78 Sufficient material should be placed to
provide the recommended three foot 3 separation
For the driveway and garage slabs the same minimum separation is
recommended Due to grade considerations it may be more practical to use
either the overexcavation method or a combination of the two methods
instead of using the additional fill method alone

In all cases the remaining expansive clay soil below the
non expansive material should be maintained in a moist condition at all
times
Retaining Wallis
For the proposed eastern retaining wall the wall alignment should
first be cleared of all adobe vegetation and other deleterious
material Then the rock at the base of the wall should be cut to form a
level platform for the foundation of the wall and the first course of
stone should be set in a mortar bed on the platform
An allowable bearing capacity of 6000 p s f may be utilized for
walls bearing on basaltic rock An equivalent fluid pressure of 35 p c f
per foot of retaining above the rock may be utilized The area behind the
wall should be backfilled with free draining granular material and
weepholes should be spaced at six feet 6 on centers or less
Due to the poor soil conditions believed to exist in the subgrade of
the proposed western wall the construction of a retaining wall in this
area is not recommended If a slope facing is desired we suggest the use
of a product such as Ameron Terrace Wall or equivalent installed in
accordance with the manufacturer s instructions
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading operations

It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by
the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and

If conditions encountered during construction are different from

LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are
based on our site reconnaissance soil information from borings and
shallow hand auger probes and laboratory test data obtained from this
soil exploration
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by reconnaissance boring or probing Unforeseen
conditions such as pockets of organic soils trash fill or expansive
soils may occur in localized areas Therefore additional expenditures
may be needed during construction to attain a properly constructed
project Some contingency fund is thus recommended to accommodate
these possible costs

Field loration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
five 5 to 10 0 feet below the existing ground surface at
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Truck mounted drilling equipment was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory the soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the soils encountered
is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 5

with a 140 pound hammer free falling at a height of 30 inches The
blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are show on the
Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths

approximate alignment of the proposed eastern retaining wall by the
drilling of shallow hand auger probes at the approximate locations
