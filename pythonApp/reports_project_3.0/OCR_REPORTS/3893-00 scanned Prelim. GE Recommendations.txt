Dear Mr Rainalter
In accordance with your request we are providing the following preliminary
information and geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of
the storm water run off disposal system at the above referenced site The information
and recommendations presented herein are for preliminary design purposes only Field
percolation testing should be conducted during our geotechnical engineering exploration
to confirm and or modify the preliminary recommendations presented herein
In general we believe that drywells may be used for storm water run off disposal
at the proposed subdivision in Waimea on the Island of Hawaii Drywells on the Island of
Hawaii are typically 5 feet in diameter 6 feet if lined with precast concrete rings and
20 feet in depth Based on the subsurface conditions volcanic ash deposits overlying
saprolitic materials anticipated at the project site we recommend that the drywells be
lined with concrete rings for the full depth to reduce the potential for collapse of the
materials due to wetting of the drywell sidewalls
Based on a percolation rate of 0 005 feet min and a standard 6 foot diameter
20 foot deep lined drywell we estimate that the typical storm water disposal capacity to
be on the order of about 0 1 to 0 5 cubic feet per second cfs In general it is our opinion
that this low storm water disposal capacity is not practical nor cost effective for the design
of the storm water run off disposal system at the subject site Therefore we believe that
the proposed drywells may be designed with a higher disposal capacity on the order of
about 2 to depending on the design quantity of the future surface water run off
Due to the low storm water disposal capacity anticipated for the future drywells it is our
opinion that contingency plans for storm water disposal such as installation of additional
drywells and or drywell extensions should be incorporated into the contract documents
