Dear Mr Alcon
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations during the
cavity probing and grouting operations at the Kaupuni Village project in the Waianae area
from May 12 through 14 2009 The probing and grouting work was performed in general
accordance with our fee estimate dated May 1 2009
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Kaupuni Village residential development is located at a vacant parcel
encompassing approximately 3 35 acres off Plantation Road in the District of Waianae on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii
During the trench excavation for the 8 inch sewer line in the vicinity of Station
3 50 to Station 4 00 along Road B a coral ledge was encountered at about 7 feet
below the existing ground surface Some voids and dry powdery coralline materials were
observed below the coral ledge Since it is impractical to backfill the voids underneath the
coral ledge with compacted materials a cavity probing and grouting program was
recommended alongside the afore mentioned stationing to delineate the extent of the
cavities and to grout with sand slurry and or Controlled Low Strength Material CLSM
accordingly
PROBING AND GROUTING OPERATIONS
The purpose of the probing and grouting operation was to delineate the extent of
the cavities observed in the vicinity of the 8 inch sewer line between about Station 3 50 to
Station 4 00 along Road B during the trench excavation work It should be noted that
our scope of work did not include delineating other areas within the project limits that may
be underlain by potential cavities and or voids

Three probe lines designated as Line A through Line C and extending from
Station 3 50 to Station 4 00 were laid out by Royal Contracting Company Ltd prior to
our probing and grouting operations at the locations described below
e Line A offset 5 feet to the north from the 8 inch sewer line
e Line B located between the 8 inch sewer line and 8 inch waterline
e Line C offset 5 feet to the south of the 8 inch waterline
Each probe line consisted of six probe hole locations spaced approximately 10 feet
apart as shown on the Probe Hole Location Plan Plate 1 In general the probe holes were
drilled to a depth of about 12 to 14 feet below the existing ground surface
Geolabs drilled the probe holes about 4 inches in diameter using a truck mounted
drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers Following the probing operation a 2 inch
diameter PVC pipe was inserted into each of the probe holes to maintain the integrity of
the open hole and to reduce the potential for cave in prior to the grouting operation A total
of 18 probe holes with an aggregate length of about 220 lineal feet below the existing
ground surface were drilled for the project
The probe holes were initially backfilled with sand slurry grout supplied by Island
Ready Mix Concrete Inc injected at low pressures American Standard Concrete
Pumping Hawaii Inc performed the grouting work and a Geolabs representative observed
the grouting operations Due to the excessive amount of grout intake 8 cubic yards for
less than three probe holes CLSM was used in the remaining grouting operation The
grout quantities were estimated using the number of strokes to fill each probe hole
compared to the number of strokes to fill up a 5 gallon bucket The grouting operation
generally proceeded in the following manner
e Immediately prior to grouting of a particular hole the temporary PVC pipe
was removed A 2 inch diameter PVC grout pipe attached to the end of the
grouting hose was then inserted into the open hole and lowered to
approximately 2 feet from the bottom of the hole Grout injection then
commenced at the direction of our field representative who provided
direction as to when the injection was complete or whether additional
injection was required
e Each probe hole was grouted from the desired depth upward by low
pressure injection of grout through the PVC grout pipe which was retracted
from the probe hole as the grout filled the hole Our field representative
observed the grouting operation and recorded the approximate grout take
Approximately 8 cubic yards of sand slurry grout and 12 cubic yards of CLSM were
utilized during the grouting operation to fill up all the probe holes and cavities
encountered The probing and grouting summary is provided on Plate 2
