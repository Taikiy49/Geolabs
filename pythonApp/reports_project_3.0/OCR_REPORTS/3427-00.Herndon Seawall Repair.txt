Project Considerations
The project site is located at Highway a property owned by
Elizabeth Herndon in the Waialua area on the island of Oahu Hawaii We understand
that the existing seawall at the northern side of the property has been damaged due
to erosion Therefore it is proposed to construct a new seawall to the south of the
damaged seawall
In order to provide subsurface information for the design of the new seawall one
boring is planned at a selected location in the vicinity of the proposed seawall
alignment We understand that your firm will be providing the geotechnical engineering
and retaining wall design for the subject project Our scope of work was limited to one
day of drilling sampling and logging of the proposed boring
This letter report summarizes our work on the project and presents our findings
obtained from the field exploration

Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our work on this project was to provide drilling services in
general accordance with our fee proposal dated March 1st In order to accomplish this
we have provided a program generally consisting of the following tasks and work
efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a portable drill rig and two operators to
and from the project site
2 Drilling and sampling of one boring to a depth of approximately 27 feet
below the existing ground surface for a total of one day of field
exploration After the drilling and sampling the borehole was backfilled
with the soil cuttings generated from the drilling operation
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the boring by a
Geologist from our firm
4 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings
5 Coordination of our overall work on the project by a Project Engineer from
our firm
6 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions in the vicinity of the proposed new seawall was
explored by drilling and sampling of one boring to a depth of approximately 27 feet
below the existing ground surface The boring was drilled using a Concore portable
drilling equipment The borehole was drilled and sampled using the combination of
auger and rotary techniques to a depth of approximately 23 feet below the existing
ground surface The borehole was further advanced by continuous penetration without
sampling to a maximum depth of 27 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate location of the boring is shown on the Site Plan Plate 3
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled boring in general accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a standard split spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The
blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on
the Log of Boring at the appropriate sample depths
The materials encountered in the boring were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually in the
laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate 4 Graphic representation of the materials
encountered are presented on the Log of Boring Plate 5
Subsurface Conditions
In general our boring encountered a surficial deposit of loose to medium dense
beach sand of approximately 10 feet thick This was underlain by medium dense coral
sands and gravels coral detritus to a depth of approximately 24 to 25 feet below the
existing ground surface The boring then encountered very stiff silty clays extending
to the maximum depth explored of approximately 27 feet below the existing ground
surface
Groundwater Level
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled boring at a depth of approximately
12 feet below the existing ground surface corresponding to about 1 5 feet MSL

Limitations
The findings submitted in this report are based in part upon information
obtained from the field boring Variations of subsoil conditions at the site may occur
both laterally and vertically and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to reevaluate the findings provided in this report
