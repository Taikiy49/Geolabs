INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed New Administration Building for Waipahu High School in the
Waipahu district on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The field exploration was
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2801 00 Page 2
performed on in accordance with our fee proposal dated
This report summarizes the findings of our field exploration and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations which were derived from analyses based on data obtained
during the field exploration and laboratory testing The recommendations are intended
for design input and are not intended to form the basis for preparation of construction
cost estimates nor for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a New Administration Building located west of the
existing Administration Building within the Waipahu High School campus on the island
of Oahu Hawaii
It is our understanding that the New Administration Building will be a single story
concrete structure with CMU walls and concrete roof The New Administration Building
will be approximately 158 feet by 69 feet in plan dimension Estimated column loads of
up to 100 kips are anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface soil
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized soil data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to foundation design
of the project
The work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
The scope of work comprised the following work items

The project site is located within the Waipahu High School Campus west of the
existing Administration Building

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling four 4 borings
to depths of twenty one and one half 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface within
the proposed project boundary The approximate locations of the borings are shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered uniform soil conditions consisting of very stiff
to hard brown silty clay to clayey silt to the maximum depth drilled
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings during the field exploration

obtained The test results are presented in Appendix B
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering point of view It appears that the very stiff to hard in situ clayey soil
GEOCLABS HAWAI
W O 2801 00 Page 5
encountered at the project site should provide adequate foundation support for the
proposed structure
Laboratory swell tests indicate that the in situ clayey soils have low to moderate
expansion potential when saturated from an air dried condition Therefore a minimum
one 1 foot thick layer of non expansive capping material is recommended underneath
the four 4 inch cushion fill below the concrete slabs on grade
Detailed discussions and recommendations are presented in the following sections
Foundations
Shallow spread and continuous footing foundations may be used to support the
proposed structure
An allowable bearing value of 3500 p s f can be used for footings bearing on the
in situ stiff clayey soil A minimum footing embedment of eighteen 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent finish grade is recommended Uplift wind seismic or other loading
conditions may require that this minimum footing embedment be increased
The recommended bearing capacity is for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third when temporary loads wind and seismic are considered
Footing settlements under the design loadings are anticipated to be on the order
Resistance to lateral loads acting on the proposed building may be provided by
passive soil pressures and frictional resistance We recommend that an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth be used to calculate the passive pressure
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2801 00 Page 6
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundations A frictional coefficient of
0 4 may be used to estimate the sliding resistance between the bottom of the footings
and the in situ soils
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should extend to the bottom of the
utility trench to reduce the potential of footing settlement due to settlement of the trench
backfill
If soft loose soil pockets are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavations
they should be over excavated to stiff in situ soil and replaced with granular select borrow
compacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations This is to avoid
ponding of surface run off water that may affect the strength and swelling characteristics
of the soils around the foundation
Slab On Grade
Concrete slabs on grade are anticipated for the proposed New Administration
Building
A minimum 4 inch thick layer of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 cushion
fill should be provided below the slabs In order to protect against moisture infiltration
a plastic vapor barrier may be incorporated below the slabs A one 1 inch thick layer
of moist fine sand is recommended on top of the plastic vapor barrier to help concrete
curing
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2801 00 Page 7
Laboratory swell tests indicate that the in situ clayey soils have low to moderate
expansion potential when saturated from an air dried condition In order to reduce the
expansive effects of the soils we recommend that a minimum one 1 foot thick layer of
non expansive capping material be placed below the recommended four 4 inch thick
cushion fill layer
The non expansive capping material should consist of off site borrow materials
such as select borrow crusher waste or coral and should be placed in horizontal loose
level lifts up to a maximum of 10 inches thick and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density as determined by the ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The
underlying clayey soil remaining should be moistened by water sprinkling and then
maintained in a moist condition until such time that the capping material is placed
The thickened edge of the slabs can bear on the compacted or in situ soil The
thickened edges should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent
finish grade
Concrete Walkway
A covered pedestrian concrete walkway is planned for the New Administration
Building The 1 foot thick capping layer of non expansive materials previously
recommended underneath the concrete slab on grade should be used below the
walkway However since the walkway slab can tolerate some amount of relative
movements the non expansive capping layer could be eliminated to reduce construction
costs if some slab cracking is tolerable
A minimum 6 inch thick thickened edge is recommended for the walkways
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2801 00 Page 8
Joints are recommended at close intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow
for some relative movements of the concrete
Site Grading
Due to the generally level topography of the project site it is our understanding
that minimal site grading will be required for the proposed construction
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of vegetation rubbish and other deleterious materials The resulting grubbed and
spoil material should be disposed of properly off site
The exposed subgrade after the recommended one 1 foot over excavation for
replacement with non expansive capping material should be scarified to a depth of six
6 inches The scarified subgrade should be thoroughly moisture conditioned and
recompacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 The prepared subgrade should be kept
moist until the capping material is placed
The non expansive capping material should consist of off site borrow materials
such as select borrow crusher waste or coral Fill materials if required should be tested
and approved by the project geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the job site
The capping material and fill material should be placed in horizontal loose level lifts
up to a maximum of ten 10 inches thick and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2801 00 Page 9
Utility Trench
Construction of new utility lines is anticipated for the project A granular bedding
consisting of six 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 is recommended
under the pipes
Granular materials should be used for backfill around the pipes The backfill up
to about one 1 foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to reduce the possibility
of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one 1 foot above the pipes
to the finish grade should be placed in maximum 10 inch horizontal loose lifts
mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as determined
by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 to protect against potential future ground subsidence
Drainage
The finished grades should be shaped to shed water away from foundations to
avoid ponding conditions Also it is advised that a gutter system be installed and that
the gutter run off water be diverted as far away from the foundations as feasible
Excessive landscape watering near the foundations sh ild also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the foundation subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
recommended foundation construction since ponded water could cause excessive
subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2801 00 Page 10
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the foundations and pavements
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This
review is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and_ foundation
recommendations given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer
cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during site grading and foundation construction of the
project This is to observe compliance with the recommendations design concepts or
specifications and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from those anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in this
report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
If soil rock conditions encountered during construction are different from those
assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design should
be made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling four 4 borings to
depths of 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate boring locations
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the
borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist or engineer who continuously monitored the
drilling operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual observation and
testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are presented on the
Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch standard split
spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch D split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling
30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the
Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
