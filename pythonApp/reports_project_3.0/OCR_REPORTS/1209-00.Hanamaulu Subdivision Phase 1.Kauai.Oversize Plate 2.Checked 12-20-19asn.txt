INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to explore the
subsurface soil conditions at the site in order to provide data for
use in the design of the site grading for the proposed subdivision
development The location of the project site and its vicinity are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The scope of this investigation included the drilling of seven test
borings visual observations of the existing site conditions
laboratory testing and engineering analysis
The results of our field explorations and laboratory tests which were
used in the engineering analyses to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is adjacent to the town of Hanamaulu on the eastern
coast of the Island of Kauai approximately 1 5 miles northeast of
Lihue It is bounded to the south by the town of Hanamaulu to the
west by Kuhio Highway to the north by an unpaved cane haul road and
to the east by cane fields
The terrain is relatively level with drainage towards the east The
ground surface elevations vary from approximately 100 to 140 feet
The site is presently used for sugar cane cultivation as are the
surrounding fields to the north and the east
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Phase I increment of the proposed subdivision will involve
subdividing 27 2 acres into approximately 158 lots for single family
residences It is anticipated that the residential structures will be
of wood framed construction with post and beam foundations or
slabs on grade
The development will also involve site grading road grading and
utility main installation A grading plan was not available at the
time of our investigation however cuts and fills for site grading
are anticipated to be minimal

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The island of Kauai was built up by a single large shield volcano
During the Tertiary period basaltic lavas of the Waimea Canyon
Volcanic Series built a roughly circular island to a height of over
17 000 feet above the surrounding sea floor Near the end of the
Tertiary Period this main shield building volcanic phase came to a
halt
Following a long period of erosion and subsidence volcanism was
renewed during the Pleistocene epoch producing the Koloa Volcanic
Series These post erosional volcanics laid a veneer of fresh lavas
over the older Waimea Canyon Series across most of the eastern half of
the island
After the cessation of this renewed volcanism the geologic forces of
weathering erosion and deposition during the late Pleistocene and the
Recent epochs have shaped the island to its present form
At the site the lava rocks of the post erosional Koloa Volcanic
Series have been deeply weathered to form a mantle of stiff reddish
brown residual clayey silt soils

Site Grading
Site Preparation
ee ee
Prior to the commencement of grading the site should be thoroughly
cleared of vegetation boulders rubbish and other deleterious
materials and should be disposed of properly off site
The upper twelve inches of soil in cultivated areas should be
reserved for use as topsoil or disposed of off site Due to the
organic contamination resulting from cultivation this material
should not be used as structural fill
The existing irrigation ditches should be cleaned of soft soils
trash and other deleterious materials to firm natural ground prior
to being filled in
In areas designated to receive fill the ground should then be
scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches brought to optimum
moisture content and then compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78

After the above site preparation the fill should be moistened to
optimum moisture content placed in 9 inch loose horizontal lifts
and compacted to obtain a minimum 90 of its maximum dry density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78
Laboratory soil tests indicate that the on site soils have low
swelling potential therefore the material may be utilized for the
fill
Where it is necessary to place fill over existing slopes steeper
than 5 horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 keying and benching operation
is critical and required to properly bond the new fill to the
existing slope surface The filling operation should start at the
lowest point and continue up in level compacted layers in accordance
with the above fill requirements
Due to the limited deep fill area at this project site boulder
disposal in the new fills is not recommended If boulders are
encountered during site grading they may have to be disposed of
off site if an on site disposal area is not available
Cut areas should be proof rolled and compacted to 90 dry density as
specified above

Utility Trenches
Proper backfilling of the excavated trenches is important to minimize
ground settlement along the utility alignments On site material
generally less than 6 inches in size may be utilized as backfill All
backfill should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density as determined by ASTM D 1557 78 Most of the on site material
is fine grained and would not be suitable for compaction by water
jetting or ponding alone and therefore would require mechanical
compaction

Pavement
It is anticipated that asphalt concrete pavements will be used for the
project
Laboratory CBR tests indicated CBR values between 12 and 18 with low
swell This range indicates that the following pavement section may
be utilized
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
4 inch Base Course
6 inch Total Pavement Thickness
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils
encountered at the time of construction to confirm the above design
The recommended section also considers medium traffic and that good
drainage will be provided for adjacent to paved areas
House Foundations
For the light residential structures spread footing foundations or
slab on grade may be used To minimize the effects of Slope creep and
erosion slabs should be set back a minimum distance of 6 feet away
from the tops of cut or fill slopes Post footings located close to
the tops of slopes should be embedded a sufficient depth to obtain a
minimum horizontal set back distance of 6 feet away from the slope
face
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1209 00 Page 9
For the light loads anticipated an allowable bearing pressure of 2500
p s may be used for footings resting on very stiff in situ soil or
compacted fill
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench or the trench backfill should be
well compacted to reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to
settlement of the backfill
Slabs on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of compacted
granular cushion and capillary break such as 3 Fine rock The
thickened edge of the slabs should extend a minimum of 10 inches below
the adjacent ground surface
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material a
waterproof membrane should be incorporated beneath the slab A
one inch layer of well graded sand should be placed over the membrane
to protect it and to aid in the curing of the concrete
The slab subgrade soils should be kept moist and not allowed to dry
out prior to concrete placement
The lots should be graded to drain water away from the house
foundations
