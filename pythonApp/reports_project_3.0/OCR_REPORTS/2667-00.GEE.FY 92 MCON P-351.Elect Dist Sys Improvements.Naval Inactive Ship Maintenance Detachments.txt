INTRODUCTION
The geotechnical engineering exploration was performed for the proposed electrical
distribution system improvements at the Naval Inactive Ship Maintenance Detachment in
Middle Loch of Pearl Harbor on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the
project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the data which were
obtained during this exploration and supplemented with data from a previous exploration
for another project in Middle Loch These recommendations are intended for design input
and are not intended for construction estimation or bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to improve the electrical distribution system at the naval facility to
maintain the current level and also to meet the future demand for electrical supply to
deactivated ships moored in Middle Loch The proposed improvements include replacing
and upgrading electrical distribution lines transformer substations distribution panels
submarine cables and power station platforms dolphins
Five 5 concrete dolphins were proposed at the time of our field exploration The
then proposed dolphins included three 3 new dolphins namely D 6 W 7A and W 8A
and two 2 replacement dolphins namely D 7 and W 6A to replace the existing wooden
dolphins D 7 and D 11

the proposed construction may be deleted or delayed Therefore the new concrete
dolphin W 7A will not be included in the current scope of work and only the construction

It is also our understanding that the finished platform elevation of the four 4

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in Middle Loch of Pearl Harbor on the island of Oahu
Hawaii The loch is bounded by Pearl City Peninsula to the east and Waipio Peninsula
to the west Fleet moorings are provided in the loch for use by the Naval Inactive Ship
Maintenance Detachment NISMD Pearl Harbor
To supply electricity to the naval ships at moorings existing concrete and wooden
dolphins are provided throughout the loch Four 4 wooden dolphins namely D 7 D 8
D 10 and D 11 are located approximately between the center and the southern outlet of
the loch These wooden dolphins were built more than 40 years ago and are progres
sively deteriorating
Based on a May 1987 bathymetric survey by Pelagos Corporation the water depth
in Middle Loch generally varied from about 20 feet at the northern end to about 40 feet
towards the southern outlet
The new and replacement dolphins will primarily be located in relatively shallow
water The depths to mudline at the boring locations were measured between 22 to 25
feet below MLLW
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The geologic conditions underlying the Pearl Harbor lochs were the results of a
complex series of depositional and erosional events due to glacioeustatic sea level
changes and post erosionai voicanic activities during the Pleistocene
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2667 00 Page 6
Following the cessation of activity of the Koolau Volcano alluvial materials were
deposited in an eustarine and deltaic environment in the area of Pearl Harbor
The sea level fluctuated several times receding perhaps 300 feet below and rising
about 95 feet above the present sea level
As Oahu sank coral reef grew around the shorelines keeping pace with the
subsidence of the island
Concurrent with a rise in sea level during the late Pleistocene Era eruption of the
Salt Lake Makalapa member of the Honolulu Volcanic Series mantled the coral reefs and
alluvium with deposits of volcanic ash which consolidated to form volcanic tuff layers
Subsequent erosion had dissected and thinned the mantles of coral reefs and tuff at the
lochs
During the recent epoch as the sea ievei rose to its current stand the Middie Loch
area was covered with a thick deposit of highly compressible harbor sediments
comprising primarily cohesive soils with localized sandy soils over the underlying alluvium

explored by drilling and sampling three 3 borings to approximately 100 to 130 feet below
MLLW Five 5 previous borings presented in our geotechnical exploration report W O

The dolphin sites are generally in shallow water with a mudline at approximately
22 to 25 feet below MLLW as measured in the borings However a non uniformly thick
layer of highly compressible harbor sediments including very soft clay and silt and also
very loose silty and clayey sand was encountered below the mudline to depths of about
42 to 115 feet below MLLW
In general it appears that the thickness of the highly compressible layer increases
from both the northern end and the southern outlet of the loch towards the central area
bounded by the previous Boring Nos 1 and 3 However local variations may exist such
as in the new Boring No 3 where the stiff underlying alluvium was encountered at a
higher elevation of about 75 feet below MLLW as opposed to the deeper elevation of
about 109 feet below MLLW in the previous Boring No 3
Previous laboratory consolidation tests indicated that the upper harbor sediments
mostly of clays were very soft and generally normally consolidated Laboratory triaxial
shear tests of samples from the new borings also verify that the harbor sediments have
a very soft consistency
The underlying alluvium generally consists of stiff to hard clays and clayey silts
locally overlain by a thin layer of dense to very dense silty sand It appears that the stiff
alluvial clays and silts are heavily overconsolidated

Lateral Pile Capacity
In general the lateral capacity of a pile depends on the pile size the pile
embedment and the density or strength of the surrounding soils The bending moment
capacity and lateral resistance of a pile increase with increasing cross sectional area
while pile deflection decreases correspondingly
Based on the subsoil profile encountered we have performed lateral pile analyses
utilizing a computer program PILEDG which is based on the P Y curve approach
developed at the University of Texas at Austin
For each lateral loading condition the program calculated pile deflections by
iterations using relationships between lateral deflection and lateral subgrade modulus
values of the subsoils along the piles The deflection versus subsoil modulus relation
ships were represented by P Y curves generated by the program using input soil
parameters The pile responses including bending moments and shear forces induced
along the piles were then computed for the pile deflections corresponding to the lateral
loadings by the program

Vertical Pile Capacity
In general we believe that the pile foundations should be driven into the stiff
underlying alluvium to develop sufficient vertical load capacity from both end bearing and
adhesion between the pile shafts and the stiff to hard clay soils Due to the very
soft loose consistency of the harbor sediments above the stiff alluvium it is not
recommended to terminate the pile foundations in this upper layer in order to reduce the
amount of pile settlements
It is our understanding that the piles will be required to resist up to 43 kips of
vertical design load on each pile to support the proposed dolphins Based on our
analyses it appears that the twenty 20 inch octagonal piles should be driven to the
following minimum depths below the tops of the dolphin platforms in order to develop
sufficient capacity to support the vertical design load and the weight of the piles

Pile Driving
Ideally a test pile driving program should be implemented to verify the design soil
profile at each of the four 4 proposed dolphins However it appears that test pile
driving may not be economically justified due to the anticipated limited number of piles
to be driven and high costs to mobilize a pile driving rig to the offshore dolphin sites
Therefore it is recommended that test pile driving not be used and that the
production pile driving be observed by a qualified geotechnical engineer or a registered
professional engineer with a minimum of five 5 years experience in geotechnical
engineering and or pile driving observation to see that the piles are driven to the required
depth and sufficient penetration resistance
The piles should be driven with a hammer capable to deliver a minimum of 24 000
foot pounds of energy during final driving The piles should be driven to the required
minimum depths and also a minimum driving resistance of 2 blows per inch for the last
six 6 inches or 24 blows per foot for the last foot of pile penetration during final driving
During initial driving i e when the pile tip is within the upper soft loose materials
the hammer stroke or energy should be carefully controlled so that excessive tensile
stress is not developed in the pile

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the project should be forwarded to the project
geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This review
is needed to determine adherence to the recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation
of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that a qualified geotechnical engineer be retained to observe
the driving of the piles for the project This is to observe compliance with the design
concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event
that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such observations
if actual subsurface conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report appropriate modifications to the design
should be made
