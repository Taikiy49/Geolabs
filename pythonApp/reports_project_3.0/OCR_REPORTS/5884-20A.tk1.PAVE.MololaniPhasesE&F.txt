As requested we conducted a study to evaluate further the existing subsurface
conditions with respect to pavement design at Phases E and F of the Mololani MCBH
housing project In our geotechnical engineering report prepared for the Mololani
MCBH housing development we assumed the pavement subgrade soils will be similar
to the silty and clayey soils generally encountered during our field exploration On this
basis we recommended using the following pavement design for this project
Flexible Pavement Section
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
12 0 Inch Aggregate Subbase Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
20 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Moist Compacted Subgrade
The recommended pavement design was subsequently revised during the project
construction with the understanding that the imported aggregate subbase material from
the nearby Kapaa Quarry will satisfy the aggregate base course requirements
Revised Flexible Pavement Section
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
15 5 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
17 5 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Moist Compacted Subgrade

During the ongoing grading work it was observed that the exposed pavement
subgrade soils are highly variable with some areas exposing granular soils while other
areas encountering highly expansive clays As a result the pavement design could be
considered overly conservative in some areas granular soil subgrades and possibly
under designed in other areas highly expansive clays Therefore we understand a
detailed pavement subgrade evaluation is desired to formulate more appropriate
pavement structural sections for the various roadways based on the pavement support
characteristics of the actual exposed subgrade soil conditions
To evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the subgrade soils test pit
excavations were performed at selected locations along the planned roadways to the
pavement subgrade level and bulk samples of the pavement subgrade soils were
collected for laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR tests Fourteen bulk soil samples
designated as 21 23 through 25 and 27 through 35 were collected at
Phases E and F at the approximate locations shown on the Bulk Sample Location Plans
Plates 1 and 2 The laboratory CBR test results performed on the bulk samples collected
are shown on Plates 1 and 2 and are summarized on the attached Summary of Laboratory
CBR Test Results Plate 3
In general the following guideline was used to formulate the pavement design of
the housing roadways based on the laboratory CBR test results obtained for the pavement
subgrade soils
