INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for The Ridge at Mililani Golf Course subdivision at Waipio on the island of
Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and
engineering analyses The recommendations presented herein are intended for design
input for site slope stability site grading retaining structures foundations pavements
and infrastructure work and may not be appropriate to form the basis for preparation
of construction cost estimates nor for construction bidding purposes

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be of
the middle to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island
The younger shield Koolau is estimated to be of the late Pliocene to early Pleistocene
Ice Age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae
became extinct while Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried
below Koolau lavas banking against its eastern flank These banked or ponded lavas
formed a broad plateau referred to as the Schofield Plateau
The project site is composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of the lavas
of the Koolau Volcanic Series These rocks are generally characterized by flows of
jointed dense vesicular basalt with interbedded thin clinker layers In situ chemical
weathering of the Koolau lava flows has occurred for the last 1 to 2 million years The
weathering process has formed a mantle of residual soils which grade to saprolite with
depth In general saprolite is composed mainly of silty material and is typical of the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2569 10 Page 6
tropical weathering of volcanic rocks The saprolite grades with depth to basaltic rock
formation
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed subdivision development is located in the Waipio area adjacent
to Waikakalaua Gulch in the Ewa District on the island of Oahu Hawaii The proposed
subdivision is bounded by Kamehameha Highway to the north Mililani Golf Course to
the east and south and Waikakalaua Gulch and military reservation to the west The
general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
The project site encompasses approximately 7 acres of undeveloped land
situated in relatively rugged terrain formed by the deep weathering of in situ Koolau
Basalt The site is densely vegetated with tall trees The lower elevations of the site
contain areas of thick brush and tall grass Typical slopes at the site range in gradient
from about 1H 1V to about 24 H 1V In general the site slopes down toward the north
with ground surface elevations ranging from 550 to 680 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
During our field exploration access to the project site was from a paved roadway
leading to the existing Waipio Sewage Pump Station at the northwestern boundary of
the site Within the project area old trails traversing the site were observed These
trails were densely overgrown with trees and shrubs that required clearing for access
of the test pit excavator and drilling equipment
At the top margin of the bluff bordering the project site with the Mililani Golf
Course some soft loose soils with asphalt pavement debris were observed These
GEOLABS HAWAII

deposits extend approximately between Test Pit and 13 and occur at the top
of the slope
The near level ground surface areas near Test Pit Nos 6 and 15 were observed

A natural drainage swale exists at the northeast portion of the site parallel to
Kamehameha Highway just north of Boring No 4 and Test Pit No 3 This swale

Scattered weathered rounded boulders were observed at the lower elevations of the

previous earth grading activities

Slope Stability
The existing slopes at the site range in inclinations from about 1H 1V to about
2 H 1V In general the site slopes down toward the north with ground surface
elevations ranging from about 550 to 680 feet MSL There were no indications of
significant ground movements or slope instability based on the available information
and our observations In general major distresses were not noted at the top of the
existing slopes within the project limits
Stability analyses of the existing slope configurations and generalized subsurface
soil conditions were performed using the computer program STABRG by Geosoft
The program evaluates the factors of safety of circular slip surfaces by using both the
Ordinary Method of Slices and the Bishop s Modified Method
In general our analyses indicated that existing slopes with inclinations of 2H 1V
Based on the preliminary grading concept some areas in Lots 12 thru 14 have existing
slope inclinations steeper than 2H 1V Therefore we recommend that these existing
slopes be flattened to 2H 1V or flatter for long term stability For Lots 21 and 26
additional analyses indicates that slopes of 1 H 1V or flatter may be used

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork all areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Ajll vegetation trees rubbish construction
debris and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site to reduce potential contamination of the excavated materials
After clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrade should be proof rolled to 90
percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
Proof rolling is recommended because we anticipate the near surface soils to be
very moist to wet
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations should be
over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation should be
filled with engineered fill moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
and compacted to a minimum of 99 percent relative compaction Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2569 10 Page 12
maximum dry density The excavated soft and or organic soils should be
properly disposed of off site
It should be noted that the test pits excavated at the site were backfilled with the
excavated soils Therefore the backfill was placed with minor compactive effort
The backfill of the test pits should be over excavated during construction and
backfilled with engineered fill compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we

Mechanically Stabilized Earth Retaining Walls MSERW
Mechanically Stabilized Earth Retaining Walls MSERW are composite wall
systems which utilize welded wire mats galvanized steel strips high density
polyethylene HDPE geogrids or other reinforcing elements to reinforce the backfill
zone and to improve the shear strength of the reinforced soil zone These
reinforcements are generally attached to precast concrete panels or blocks used for
wall facing In general the MSERW are also desirable due to the flexibility of the wall
ease of construction high load carrying capacity and economy MSERW generally are
most appropriate in fill conditions such as for the retaining structures planned along the
northern and western perimeter of the site
As an alternative to conventionai retaining walls we recommend that MSERW
be used for the walls proposed at the northern and western perimeter of the site where
substantial fills are planned We believe that the MSERW would be more economical
than conventional retaining walls in fill conditions at the site considering the height and
lengths of walls proposed
Design of the MSERW will need to consider both external and internal stabilities
of the structure In evaluating external stability the MSERW structure must satisfy four
stability conditions 1 bearing failure 2 translational sliding 3 overturning stability
and 4 overall slope stability Geotechnical design parameters to evaluate these
stability conditions are presented in the following sections Some of the geotechnical
parameters necessary in evaluating the internal stability of the MSERW are presented
in the Backfill Materials subsection below The internal and external stabilities of the
MSERW will need to be evaluated by the design engineer We have evaluated the
overall slope stability for the proposed MSERW and the results are summarized in the
Overall Slope Stability subsection

Lateral Load Resistance
Resistance to lateral loads imposed on the MSERW structure may be provided
by a combination of the following 1 friction between the reinforced soil volume

and the foundation soils and 2 passive resistance acting against the near
vertical faces of the embedded portion of the MSERW
An allowable friction coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the base of the

The retained soil behind the MSERW structure will exert lateral earth pressures

retain the new fills planned to raise the grades at the site we recommend that
the MSERW be designed with lateral earth pressures due to the new fills
Based on the preliminary grading concept imported fill material will likely be
needed to raise the grades at the site In general we recommend that imported
fill materials meet the minimum requirements specified in the Fills and Backfills
subsection of this report

Slabs On Grade
We understand that concrete slabs on grade may be used for the garage floor
and interior floors We envision that the slabs on grade will be supported on the
existing near surface soils and or on compacted fills needed to raise the existing
grades where applicable Based on the laboratory test results the near surface soils
encountered at the site consist of clayey silts which exhibit low expansion potential
Based on this concrete slabs on grade may be constructed on grade with proper
subgrade preparation The subgrades for slabs on grades should be scarified to a
depth of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction If the subgrade moisture
content is at least 5 percent abave tne optimum moisture in lieu of scarification the
subgrade should be proof rolled to obtain 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry
weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
For more uniform support of the slabs we recommend that concrete slabs on
grade be supported on a minimum 4 inch cushion fill The cushion fill layer should
consist of open graded gravels such as No 3B Fine grave ASTM C33 No 67
gradation which will also serve as a capillary moisture break The slab cushion fill
layer should be placed as soon as possible after the scarification and recompaction of
the existing subgrade soils For slabs on grade with moisture sensitive surfacing we
recommend that an impermeable membrane be placed over the cushion fill to reduce
potential migration of moisture vapor through the concrete slab In order to promote
more uniform curing of the slab concrete and to provide protection of the vapor
membrane we recommend that a 2 inch layer of moist fine sand be placed above the

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on selected soil
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the logs of borings and test pits at the appropriate sample
depths
One Compaction test ASTM D 1557 91 was performed on bulk sample of the
near surface soils to evaluate the moisture content and dry density relationship The
test results are summarized on Plate B 1 Graphic presentation of the test results is
provided on Plate B 7
Two California Bearing Ratio CBR tests were performed on selected bulk
samples collected for pavement design The test results are summarized on Plate B 1
One Unconfined Compression test ASTM Test Method D 2166 85 was
performed on a selected cohesive soil sample A summary of the compression test
results is presented on Plate B 1
Three one inch Ring Swell tests were performed on relatively undisturbed
samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils in the
natural state The results of the tests are summarized on Plate B 2
Five one inch Ring Swell tests were also performed on remolded samples
Results of the remolded swell tests on the remolded samples are also summarized on
Plate B 2
Four sets of corrosion tests were performed by on selected samples obtained
during our field exploration Each set of corrosion test included the determination of pH
value and minimum resistivity The test results are presented on Plate B 3
Three Atterberg Limits tests were performed on selected soil samples to evaluate
the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The test results are
presented on Plate B 4
Two Direct Shear tests ASTM Test Method D 3080 72 were performed to
estimate the angle of internal friction and cohesion of the soil sample tested The test
results are graphically presented on Plates B 5 and B 6
