PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located on the western side of Fort Barrette Road at the
intersection with Franklin D Roosevelt Avenue Kapolei High School is located to the
east of the site across Fort Barrette Road
The project site is a deep borrow pit approximately 300 feet wide by 700 feet long
and 40 feet deep The exposed side walls are near vertical and consist of dense
coralline formation The bottom of the pit is relatively level It appears that trenches were
excavated along the bottom of the slope at the southern portion of the pit Water was
present in the trenches The bottom elevation of the pit ranges between approximately
15 to 25 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The excavated trenches are below Mean Sea
Level
The borrow pit will be filled with suitable fill materials to near the existing ground
surface about 50 feet MSL The first phase of work includes filling the pit to about
elevation 25 feet MSL The site will be developed when the pit is filled to near the
existing ground surface

BACKFILLING OF THE BORROW PIT
Geolabs Inc observed the backfilling of Kekona s Pit project up to an elevation of
approximately 25 feet MSL from August 1 2005 to Royal Contracting
Co Ltd performed the grading work for the project During the grading operations
samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as backfill materials were obtained
and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum
moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D
1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
The excavated trenches below Mean Sea Level at the bottom of the pit were
backfilled with boulder fill using boulders less than 3 feet in largest dimension The
boulders were pushed into the excavated pit with the excavator bucket and track rolled
with an excavator After compaction of the boulder fill the boulder fill was choked off with
finer materials to fill the void spaces Boulder fill was also used within the lower portions of
the pit
The general fill materials used to backfill the pit were moisture conditioned to above
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 It should be
noted that crushed concrete was also used as general fill material
During the grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the
compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 19 of this report
These tests were performed on the constructed backfills Test locations and elevations
were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual
observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement and compaction procedures during the grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the backfilling work for the above project observed
and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in conformance
with the general intent of the recommendations in the following references

LIMITATIONS

occurring simultaneously on a construction site therefore some work may have occurred
without our observation However we believe that the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project It
should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability
of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials Our
