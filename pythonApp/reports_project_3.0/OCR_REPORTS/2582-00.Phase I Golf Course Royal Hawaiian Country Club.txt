Project Considerations
The project sites are located in the Maunawili area within the Royal Hawaiian
Country Club Golf Course currently being constructed on the northeastern portion of
Oahu The general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1

7 8 and 9 are planned along the golf cart path within the subject golf

Field Exploration
One boring was drilled at each of the two 2 abutments of the proposed eight 8
cart path bridge crossings except at Bridge No 4 To expedite the foundation design
two 2 borings were drilled behind the southern abutment of Bridge No 4 due to initial
inaccessibility for our drilling equipment to the northern abutment location One 1 boring
was drilled at the approximate location of the northern abutment of Bridge No 4 after the
site was cleared and made accessible Therefore a total of seventeen 17 borings were
drilled for the proposed eight 8 bridges of the subject project The approximate boring
locations are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 to 9
In general the borings encountered alluvial deposits ranging from soft to very stiff
clayey silt with weathered gravel Medium dense to dense silty sand with weathered
gravel saprolite and weathered basalt formation were also encountered at deeper depths
of the drilled borings
Detailed descriptions and graphic representations of the materials encountered in
the borings are presented in Appendix A Laboratory tests were performed on selected
samples obtained during our field exploration The laboratory test results are presented
in Appendix B

Limitations
Our geotechnical engineering analyses and recommendations are based in part
upon information obtained from field borings and laboratory testing Variations of
conditions between the field borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then appear evident
it will be necessary to reevaluate the recommendations presented in our reports
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the sites were explored by drilling seventeen 17
borings to depths of 20 3 to 56 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 through 9 A truck
mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist or engineer who continuously monitored the
drilling operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual observation and
testing in the laboratory Ail soils were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are presented on the
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch standard split
spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch D split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling
30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the
Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
