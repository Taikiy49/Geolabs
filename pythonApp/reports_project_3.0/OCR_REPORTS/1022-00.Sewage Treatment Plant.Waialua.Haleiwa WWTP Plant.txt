of supporting the proposed structures The
surface soils are moderately expansive if

proposed wastewater treatment plant

PROPOSED CONSIDERATIONS
The current plan is to construct a wastewater treatment plant on the
site The development will include an office operations building
treatment tanks parking areas roadways and an unlined effluent
storage pond

Treated effluent will be used for irrigation with excess disposal

Site grading plans were not available at the time of our investigation
However we anticipate that site grading will be minimal
The project site is located on the north shore of the island of Oahu
in the Mokuleia area in a sugar cane field designated as Gay 11 by
the Waialua Sugar Co The site is approximately 660 by 660 feet in
plan dimensions and is bordered to the south by Farrington Highway and
to the north east and west by more cane fields The ground surface
elevations vary from about 7 feet to about 16 feet
The site slopes up very gently towards the north and is currently used
for the cultivation of sugar cane
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The project site is located on the north flank of the Waianae Shield
Volcano After the cessation of volcanic activity in the late
Pliocene early Pleistocene erosional dissection of the original
dome has created a thick alluvial apron along the seaward margins of
the Waianae Range This alluvial plain is composed of interbedded
sediments of both terrigenous and marine origin

Soil Conditions
In general our explorations encountered a surface layer of stiff
brown silty clay extending to depths between 3 and 8 feet below the
existing ground surface The upper 3 to 4 feet of this surface layer
has been disturbed and reworked by tilling operations
Below this surface layer our borings encountered two general sets of
conditions and 6 in the southern portion of the site
encountered stiff alluvial soils consisting of brown silty clays and
clayey silts with some interbeds of sand and gravel to the maximum
depths drilled
5 and 7 on the northern edge of the site encountered
Similar alluvial soils with many interbeds of marine sediments
generally consisting of dense calcareous sands and gravels

Permeability
In general permeability is a function of the grain size of the soil
Coarse grained soils such as sands and gravels readily transmit
water Fine grained soils such as silts and clays generally have
low permeability
The stiff alluvial silty clays and clayey silts encountered in the
southern portion of the site have low permeabilities that would make
their use for injection well disposal impractical
The interbeds of granular marine sediments encountered in the northern
portion of the site have relatively good permeability and are more
practical for use in underground disposal of treated effluent

General

for the wastewater treatment plant site In general the structures
can be supported on spread footing foundations bearing on the stiff
brown silty clay about 2 feet below the existing ground surface

to dry Therefore it is essential that the subgrade soils below the
concrete slabs on grade be kept moist prior to concrete placement In
addition a 12 inch thick layer of hon expansive soil should be placed
under the slabs

The on site stiff brown silty clay can provide adequate bearing
for building pump and tank foundations A design bearing value
of 2500 pounds per square foot may be utilized for footings
placed a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finish

wind or seismic loads are considered We recommend that all

relative compaction as determined by ASTM D 1557 to ensure
adequate and uniform bearing

Injection Well and Percolation Testing
Two deep NX diameter exploratory borings were drilled at the site
for the purpose of determining the feasibility of underground disposal
of treated effluent
The first deep boring Boring 6 encountered predominantly silty clay
alluvial soils A trial test performed between the depths of 155 and
190 feet yielded an injection capacity of less than 1 gallon per
minute On the basis of that test and observations of the drill water
return which indicated minimal percolation potential it was decided
to abandon the hole and to try an alternate location further toward
the north makai
This second boring Boring 7 encountered pervious strata at depths
of 5 to 20 feet 35 to 40 feet 59 to 61 feet and 70 to 75 feet in
which most or all of the drilling circulation water was lost Below
these depths monitoring of the wash return indicated that the
remainder of the stratigraphic column had rather low permeability
Trial tests during the drilling indicated an injection Capacity of up
to 25 gallons per minute on the NX size boring In view of a more
promising capacity it was decided to enlarge the boring to 6 inch
diameter and to perform a more detailed injection test
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1022 00
Page 12
The boring was subsequently reamed to a depth of 71 feet and a 4 inch
I D perforated casing along with a fine gravel pack were installed
The top of the well was sealed with a 5 foot deep cement grout pack
for details see Plates C 1 C2
Following the construction of this test well preliminary testing was
performed on the well It was first developed by pumping and surging
with a 3 inch centrifugal pump Drawdown tests using a 3 inch
centrifugal pump indicated a pumping capacity of 145 gallons per
minute with a drawdown of about 2 feet
After development and drawdown testing percolation tests were per
formed using a 3 inch centrifugal pump delivering water from a 2400
gallon water truck Flow rates were weasured using an in line paddle
wheel flow sensor These tests indicated an injection capacity of 195
gallons per minute with an injection head build up of about 6 feet
The test data for both the drawdown and percolation tests are attached
in the appendices
It is our opinion that underground disposal of treated effluent could
be accomplished along the northern edge of the site by utilizing a
series of large diameter production wells Periodic flushing and
surge pumping in the production wells is recommended to minimize the
build up of accummulated solids and to maintain effluent percolation

APPENDIX B
Moisture content unit weight and Atterberg Limit deter
minations were performed on selected samples as an aid in
the classification and evaluation of soil properties The
results of these tests are presented on the log of borings
at the appropriate sample depths
A laboratory CBR test was performed on a bulk sample of the
near surface material obtained from the proposed subgrade
elevation to evaluate its Swelling potential and subgrade
Support capacity The test result is summarized on Plate B 1
One ASTM D 1557 test modified proctor was performed on a
bulk sample of the near surface existing fill material to
evaluate its compaction characteristics The test result
is summarized on Plate B 1l
Seven sieve analyses were performed to determine the
gradation characteristics of the site materials The test
results are summarized on Plates B 1l through B 3
One direct shear test was performed on an undisturbed sample
to evaluate the strength characteristics of the natural
soil The test result is presented on Plate B 4
Seven one inch ring swell tests were performed on remolded
and undisturbed samples to evaluate the swelling potential
of the existing near surface soils The test results are
summarized on Plate B 5

APPENDIX C
PERCOLATION TEST
Injection well tests were performed in Boring No 7
to evaluate the percolation quality of the subsoils for
use in injection well disposal of treated effluent
An NX diameter exploratory boring was drilled to
a depth of 198 feet The boring indicated porous strata
within the upper 70 feet of the boring therefore the
hole was reamed to 6 inch diameter to a depth of 71 feet
A 4 inch 1 D perforated casing along with a fine gravel
pack were installed in the hole The top of the well
was sealed with a 5 foot deep cement grout pack
Following the construction of this test well
preliminary testing was performed on the well It was
first developed by pumping and surging with a 3 inch
centrifugal pump After development and drawdown testing
percolation tests were performed using a 3 inch centri
fugal pump delivering water from a 2400 gallon water
truck Flow rates were measured using an in line paddle
wheel flow sensor Sketches of the drawdown and injection
tests are shown on Plates C 1 and C 2 respectively The
test data are presented on Plates C 3 and C 4
