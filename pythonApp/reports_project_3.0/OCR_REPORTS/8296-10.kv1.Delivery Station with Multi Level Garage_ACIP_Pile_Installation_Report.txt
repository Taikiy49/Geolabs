PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The project site is located at 2101 Auiki Street in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, 
Hawaii. The project involves the development of a new delivery station facility and multi-
level garage with an overall plan dimension of about 616 feet by 436 feet. The project 
location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
Based on the geotechnical engineering report referenced above for the project, 
we recommended supporting the delivery station and multi-level parking garage project 
on a deep foundation system consisting of 18-inch diameter augered cast-in-place 
(ACIP) piles with an allowable compressive load capacity of 450 kips (design load) per 
pile. The ACIP piles would extend through the fill material, loose/soft lagoonal deposits, 
medium hard to hard coral formation, with a design pile length of about 60 feet below 
the bottom of pile cap elevation.  
BI-DIRECTIONAL STATIC PILE LOAD TEST 
In order to evaluate the adequacy of the recommended design length of the ACIP 
pile foundation, a bi-directional static pile load test was conducted on two 18-inch ACIP 
load test piles, designated as Test Pile No. LT-1 and LT-2. The load test piles were 
installed by Kelikai, Inc. on August 8, 2022, utilizing a drill rig with continuous hollow 
stem auger and a hydraulic concrete pump. The 18-inch diameter load test piles LT-1 
and LT-2 extended to depths of about 70 feet below the existing ground surface.  
To better understand the load transfer mechanism at the pile-soil interface, the 
load test piles were instrumented with vibrating wire embedment strain gages installed 
at various depths along the length of the load test piles. The load test piles were loaded 
in general accordance with ASTM D8169M “Deep Foundations Under Bi-Directional 
Static Axial Compressive Load” Procedure A Quick Test. The bi-directional static pile 
load test results are summarized in the following report. 
“AFT-Cell Bi-Directional Load Testing Report, DHI2 Delivery Station Sand 
Island” dated August 12, 2022. 
Based on the static pile load test results, the 18-inch diameter load test piles LT-
1 and LT-2 were loaded to maximum test loads of 726 and 731 kips in each direction, 
respectively, which were equivalent to total of testing loads of about 1,452 and 1,462 
kips. The load test results confirm the design length of the piles were adequate to 
support the design compressive load capacity with a factor of safety of greater than 2.0.

The General Contractor for the project was Swinerton and the ACIP pile 
installation subcontractor was Kelikai, Inc. The production pile installation program, 
consisting of installing 1,024 18-inch diameter ACIP piles, was conducted from August 
24 through November 3, 2022, under the full-time observations by our field
