LOCATION AND SITE DESCRIPTION
The site investigated covers an area of approximately 80 000
square feet located north of Kumoo Loop westerly adjacent to
Kahekili Highway and is generally Situated at the eastern portion
of the entire Proposed development
The area is naturally terraced gently sloping towards a stream
which bounds the subject site at its southern end Except for
some tall grasses adjacent to the stream the subject site is
generally cleared

SOIL CONDITIONS
The low area within the site is underlain by four distinct
soil strata The upper mantle found throughout the site consists
of man made fill soils ranging from 3 to 6 feet in depth These
111 soils consist of soft to stiff brown clayey silt with
some decomposed basalt fragments
Below the man made fill is a natural alluvial seil corsisting
of a horizontal layer of very soft organic clayey silt 6 to
12 feet thick This soft formation is found along the stream
and covers an area about 60 to 80 feet wide and 400 feet long
Below the soft deposit there is a 3 to 5 foot layer of sand
and gravel which overlies weathered basalt
The upper 10 to 15 feet of soil is soft and will not be adequate
to support structures without special treatment

General
The soils underlying the low area in the vicinity of the stream
and Kahekili Highway consist of up to 15 feet of fill and soft
organic soils This layer may be found from Elevation 14st
to Elevation 128 This soft deposit will require special
treatment prior to building construction The site is generally 7
suited for the intended usage provided the following recommenda
tions are incorporated into the planning design and construction
Stages
Various methods of treating the above soft deposits are considered
Analyses performed to arrive at our conclusions and recommendations
are outlined in the following sections
Surcharge and Settlement Analyses
One method of improving the condition and strength of the exist
ing soft deposits is the use of surcharge fill The surcharge
fill may be taken as 20 feet high and spread throughout the
areas that contain soft deposits This method can be used if
sufficient time is available for the underlying soft soils to
consolidate and gain strength to support the planned structures
Vo
Page 6
W
Based on our analyses a 20 foot fill placed over the said
layer would result in primary settlement on the order of
two feet over a period of two years Additional secondary
settlement would likely continue due to the high percentage
of organic material in said stratum
The rate of loading of fill and rate of settlement during loading
should be monitored on the site by settlement plates and also
by pore pressure gauges embedded within the consolidating
structure This will provide estimates of the increase in
strength of the underlying soft soil and provide a means of
checking the progress of consolidation in the field and to
verify predicted settlement
In view of the above elements preloading with 20 foot surcharge
is not advisable due to the time element involved _
Sand Drains
A sand drain system will accelerate the expulsion and draining
away of the pore water squeezed out of the voids of the under _
lying soft soil by the surcharge of earth fill This speeds
up the rate of settlement
Page 7
W
Sand drains can either be 8 or 10 feet on centers and penetrate
through all soft soils to the underlying weathered basalt
The depths of sand drains will range from 30 feet in Boring 13
to 50 feet in Boring 9 in a west easterly direction Use of
8 foot on center sand drain design will reduce the time required
for the settlement to take place But the cost of installation
will be too excessive to warrant this type of system
Recommended Method of Treatment of Soft Soils
In view of the cost and time element stated above we strongly
recommend the removal of the soft critical layer by mechanical
means and replaced with properly compacted fill If this is
done approximately 15 000 cubic yards of organic material and
6 000 cubic yards of clay fill will need to be removed
Majority of the organic material should be used for top soil
and landscape purposes These should be stockpiled to dry out
Some of the excavated soil may be mixed and well blended with
non organic on site material for reuse in the placement of fill
In the process of dredging the soft material particular care
must be exercised by the grading contractor to insure at all
times proper drainage along the stream to avoid any slope
