Project P 841

In accordance with our proposal dated we have performed our
field exploration at the above referenced project site Based on our current findings the
following preliminary pile foundation recommendations are presented to expedite the
project design
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
a total of five 5 off shore borings and one 1 on land boring Two 2 additional borings
had been drilled besides the originally proposed four 4 borings in order to better
delineate the different pile bearing strata and the other subsoil conditions encountered
below the site The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the attached Site
Plan Plate 2
The pier site is generally in shallow water with mudline at approximately 8 0 to 23 1
feet below Mean Lower Low Water MLLW as measured in the off shore borings drilled
The field exploration indicated that the subsurface soil conditions along the pier site
may be generalized as follows and as shown on the generalized subsoil profile Plate 3

Seaward End of the Pier Boring No 1
The boring encountered a layer of highly compressible harbor sediments
approximately 37 feet in thickness below the mudline The harbor muds consist
of very soft dark gray clay and silt underlain by a medium dense black silty sand
layer approximately ten 10 feet in thickness
A 10 foot thick layer of very dense basalt cobbles and boulders presumably
of alluvial origin was encountered below the sand layer and underlain by stiff to
hard brown sandy silt to the maximum boring depth of about 100 5 feet below
MLLW
Central Portion of the Pier Boring
The borings also encountered below the mudline a similar layer of highly
compressible harbor sediments which is generally thicker and ranges from
approximately 67 2 to 69 5 feet in thickness The soft harbor muds generally
graded medium stiff to very stiff in consistency at depths of about 87 7 to 92 0 feet
below MLLW
Below the harbor sediments alluvial deposits consisting of very stiff clay underlain
by heavily overconsolidated soft rock like hard clay and silt with gravel and
cobbles were encountered to the maximum boring depths of 109 3 to 127 0 feet
below MLLW
Near Shore Portion of the Pier Boring

On Land Boring Boring No 6
The on land boring encountered a surface fill layer approximately 20 5 feet in
thickness consisting of stiff brown silty clay and medium dense to loose coral
sand and gravel underlying an asphalt concrete pavement
Soft harbor sediments were encountered below the fill layer to about 88 feet below
the existing ground surface where it graded medium stiff to very stiff in consistency
down to the depth of approximately 101 5 feet below the ground surface
Groundwater level was measured at about 2 3 feet below the existing ground
surface in this on land boring at the time of our exploration
Preliminary Findings and Recommendations
Based on the boring data it appears that the subsoil conditions along the pier site
may be characterized by three 3 different pile bearing strata
At the seaward end portion of the pier a very dense basalt cobble and boulder
layer was encountered below the very soft harbor sediments At the central portion of the
pier rock like hard clay silt was encountered below a thicker deposits of the harbor
sediments A softer and generally penetrable stiff alluvial clay silt deposit was
encountered at even greater depth below the soft harbor sediments at the near shore
portion of the pier
Ideally additional borings should be budgeted for further exploration between the
different pile bearing stratum conditions especially between Boring Nos 1 and 2
Alternately a probe pile program during construction may be used to provide data for
more accurate determination of the production pile lengths and driving criteria If a probe
pile program is used and additional exploration between Boring Nos 1 and 2 is also
performed to better define the dense basalt cobble and boulder layer the number of pile
load tests required may probably be reduced

Lateral Pile Capacity
Lateral pile analyses were performed utilizing a computer program PILEDG which
is based on the P Y curve approach developed at the University of Texas at Austin
Our preliminary findings indicate that the piles should be able to resist the design
lateral loading without undergoing excessive bending and deflections
Alternately we believe that a coefficient of horizontal subgrade reaction of two 2
pounds per cubic inch may be used for the highly compressible harbor sediments
for lateral pile analyses utilizing other approaches such as the Equivalent Fixed
Base Method proposed by Davisson et al and a similar method proposed in the
Navy Design Manual DM 25 6
To develop sufficient pile fixity the use of a driving shoe is recommended for the
piles near the seaward end of the pier to penetrate into the dense cobble and
boulder layer

Vertical Pile Capacity
In general we believe that the pile foundations should be driven slightly into the
dense cobble boulder layer or penetrated into the soft rock like hard clay or the
stiff underlying alluvium to develop sufficient vertical load capacity Due to the very
soft consistency of the harbor sediments above the bearing strata it is not
recommended that the pile foundations be terminated in the soft upper layer since
significant pile settlements may be induced
Based on our analyses the following minimum pile lengths below the pier deck pile
caps are recommended in the three 8 different soil conditions at the site in order
to develop sufficient vertical capacity to support the different design loads and self
weights of the piles for the six 6 pile schemes being considered

Test Pile Program
Since three 8 different pile bearing strata were encountered at various depths
below the pier site we believe that a test pile program is essential in order to
provide pile driving data for accurate determination of the production pile lengths
and to verify the theorectical pile driving criteria for different areas of the pier
We recommend that approximately ten 10 percent of the total quantities of piles
be test driven as probe piles at strategically selected locations along the pier site
These probe piles may be incorporated into the actual pile foundation system
To evaluate the individual vertical support characteristics of the three 3 different
pile bearing strata a minimum of three 3 static load tests should be performed
on selected probe piles The probe piles selected for static load tests should be
loaded to two 2 times the design vertical compression capacity in accordance
with the standard loading procedure of ASTM Test Method D 1143
