SITE DESCRIPTION
The existing warehouse is situated on the western side of Iwaena
Street in Halawa Industrial Subdivision and is presently used as an
office and warehouse storage
The warehouse approximately 101 by 106 feet in plan occupies the
rear portion of the site It is constructed of wooden roof supported by
tilt up concrete panel walls pipe columns and on grade concrete slabs
Fronting the warehouse are a parking lot paved with asphaltic concrete
and a sloping driveway
Behind the rear wall of the warehouse exists a steep up to 1H 1V
or steeper slope up to 25 feet high Previous rains have washed down
some of the slope at its toe These accumlated materials prevent a door
on the rear wall from functioning properly
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling 3
borings to depths of 5 0 to 6 5 feet below the existing ground surface at
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings disclosed that the floor consists of a 4 inch concrete
slab over a 4 inch layer of brown clayey silt with gravel The pavement
was underlain by stiff to very stiff browm and reddish brown clayey silt
and further by very stiff dark gray and brown clayey silt with gravel

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the existing warehouse
concrete pavement slab and base course section is thin and marginally
suitable for the warehouse loads The subgrade soils below the pavement
and inadequate pavement jointing detail also contribute to the pavement
distress
Pavement Thickness
The pavement section consists of the concrete slab and base course
layer The thickness of these two components is determined by 1 the
vehicular traffic anticipated 2 strength of the subgrade scil soil
under the base course and 3 concrete strength
It is our understanding that a 3 kip capacity forklift is utilized
in the warehouse
The borings indicate that the subgrade scils consist of clayey silt
which can generally be classified as moderately strong
The concrete flexural strength is generally designed for 650
psi in
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 5
Utilizing the above parameters it appears that a concrete slab
thickness of about 4 5 inches would have been appropriate for the
warehouse slab instead of the 4 inch thickness Therefore it is our
opinion that the existing slab is slightly under designed for the
warehouse use
Construction Joints
Visual observations indicated significant differential movements
between the slabs on each side of the construction joints It appears
that the slab on one side of the joint may have heaved up relative to the
other Under the leaded forklift traffic the heaved slab deflects
settles creating a hazardous situation for the forklift
The differential movement between the slabs could have been
minimized by proper design and construction of doweled or keyed
construction joints which are normally recommended when vehicular
traffic is anticipated on the slab These joints are designed to
transfer load across the joints to minimize load concentrations The
existing construction joints do not have provisions for lead transfer
and therefore allow stress concentrations which cause excessive
deflections
Soil Swelling Potential
Our laboratory testing indicates that the underlying clayey silt
soil generally has low to moderate swelling potential

Based on these findings it appears that the swelling potential of
the clayey silt soil are a minor contributor to the cracking and upheaval
of the slabs

We recommend that if possible the finish lot grade outside the
panel walls of the warehouse be shaped to shed water away from the
walls to avoid ponding condition It is also advised that gutter
systems should be maintained properly around the warehouse and that
gutter water should be diverted far away from the walls Excessive
landscape watering near the walls and slabs should also be avoided
Planters next to the walls should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above reconmended remedial measures since
ponded water could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent heaving or less of strength

samplers for 12 inches are shown on the log of borings at the appropriate
sample depths
