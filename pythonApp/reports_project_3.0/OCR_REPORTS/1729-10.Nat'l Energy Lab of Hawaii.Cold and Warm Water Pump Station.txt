Results of Additional Borings
National Energy Laboratory of Hawaii NEIH
Cold and Warm Water Pump Station
Keahole Hawaii
As per our March 20 1987 revised proposal five borings were drilled
to depths of 31 5 to 45 0 feet at the above referenced project site
location
Rock coring using both triple and double tube core barrels and soil
sampling using a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler SPT were carried
out
The borings encountered primarily slightly to mederately jointed
basalt to about 30 feet below the existing ground surface Below
approximately 30 foot depth a clinker zone was encountered which consists
primarily of slightly cemented to uncemented basalt fragments gravels to
gravelly sand Locally cavities or lava tubes 1 to 5 feet thick were
encountered within this clinker zone Boring A 4
Groundwater level was encountered during our field exploration and
was found to be influenced by tidal conditions A high of 6 7 feet and a
low of 7 4 feet below the existing ground level were recorded
The five 5 additional borings drilled confirmed the presence of a
continuous basalt formation below the site and revealed that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with that indicated by the previous
boring Geolabs Hawaii August 27 1986 Report drilled in the same area
Boring 6
