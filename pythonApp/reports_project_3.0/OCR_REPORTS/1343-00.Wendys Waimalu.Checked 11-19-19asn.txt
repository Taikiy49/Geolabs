SOIL INVESTIGATION
PROPOSED WENDY S WAIMALU
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced site in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed structure The study
consisted of drilling two shallow borings laboratory testing of
selected samples obtained from the borings and reviewing of the
preliminary building foundation plans This report summarizes our
findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
According to the preliminary building plan prepared by Roy S
Oshiro Architect the proposed building will be a one story structure
approximately 30 by 78 feet in plan dimensions

Concrete slab on grade are planned for the entire building and the
outside trash enclosure
CMU bearing walls are anticipated for the entire building
Asphaltic concrete parking and driveway pavements will also be
constructed

investigation however it is anticipated that cuts and fills will
generally be less than 2 feet thick

Two borings were drilled within the proposed building location to a
depth of 20 5 and 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 1

below the existing concrete slabs

grayish brown sand and silt mixtures of varying proportions were
encountered
No groundwater was encountered in the borings at the time of our
field exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The stiff grayish brown clayey silt soil encountered can provide
suitable foundation support for the proposed building provided that some
care is exercised during construction
The field exploration and laboratory testing performed on the stiff
clayey silt soil indicates that this type of material generally has a
low swelling potential if it is kept moist However the swelling
potential may increase to a moderate range if allowed to dry excessively
and then moistened later Therefore to minimize the swelling
potential the subgrade soil should be kept moist after excavation and
protected from drying during the period of time required for preparation
prior to final concrete placement
Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
structure
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used for the
anticipated light foundation loads on the stiff in situ soil The
bearing capacity is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased
by one third for short duration wind and for seismic loading
Minimum footing embedment of 18 inches is recommended
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1343 00 Page 5
The bottom of all footings should be free of loose soils and
inspected by the soils engineer prior to concrete placement
If a footing is situated next to a utility trench it should be
extended to the bottom of the trench or the trench backfill must be
well compacted to prevent footing settlement due to settlement of the
trench backfill
Estimated total and differential settlements for Footing bearing on
the stiff in situ soil are anticipated to be on the order of less than
one fourth of an inch
To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be
used between the foundation and in situ soil
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the entire structure To
minimize the swelling potential of the in situ soils it is recommended
that the in situ soil be over excavated for a depth of 1 foot below the
Slab subgrade and replaced with select borrow crusher waste or coral
compacted in accordance with the provisions under the SITE GRADING
section of this report
Slabs should be separated from foundations to allow for some
relative movements due to the differences in structural loading

A plastic membrane should be placed below the slab if a moisture
barrier is deemed necessary to protect the flooring covering
The concreting of the slab should be coordinated in such a way that
the time period between the initial excavation and the final concrete
placement should be kept to a minimum in order to prevent excessive
drying of the subgrade
It is important that the soils engineer be notified to inspect the
above subgrade preparation prior to pouring of the slab and perimeter
footing

The preliminary grading plan was not available at the time of our
investigation However small levelling cuts and fills of generally
less than 2 feet are anticipated for the relatively level site

The finished grade outside the slab and footings should be shaped to
shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding condition In

building and that gutter water be diverted far away from foundations and

also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above referenced foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundation
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavation and backfilling
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction to sce
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given herein are contingent upon the above
inspections
