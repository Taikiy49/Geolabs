INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil engineering
investigation performed for the subject project The purpose of this
investigation was to explore the subsurface soil and groundwater
conditions along the proposed interceptor sewer alignment to provide soil
data for use in the design and construction of the proposed sewer line
The approximate location of the alignment and its vicinity is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate lL

The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
GENERAL PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed interceptor sewer system will consist of
and 18 inch diameter sewer lines approximately 5100 feet long extending
along Kailua Road from the existing Kailua Road Sewage Pump Station to
Uluoa Street The invert of the proposed sewer line will vary from about
7 to 21 feet below the existing ground surface
SITE DESCRIPTION ALONG THE SEWER ALIGNMENT
The proposed interceptor sewer system will be connected to the
existing Kailua Road Sewage Pump Station located on the western side of
Kailua Road and mauka of the Hamakua Drive and Kailua Road intersection
From the pump station the sewer alignment will cross the eastern side of
Kailua Road and generally continue in a southwesterly direction parallel
with Kailua Road for a distance of about 4800 feet and terminate at about
300 feet southwest of Uluoa Road
The ground surface elevation along the proposed alignment varies
from 6 feet above MLLW at the Kailua Pump Station up to 116 feet above
MLLW at the end of the alignment

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Generally relatively competent soil conditions were encountered
along most of the proposed interceptor sewer alignment above Sta 5450
In these upper areas stiff older fill and residual soils grading to
weathered basalt rock were encountered
Below loose coral sands and gravel were encountered at
about elevation 0 0 foot Adequate shoring and bracing are critical in
this area to minimize damage to adjacent structure due to lateral ground
movements or failure of the trench walls Water level was also
encountered above the proposed invert level in this area and therefore
dewatering will be required for the sewer construction

Soft Ground Condition
Estimated depth of excavation will be about 9 to 21 feet Easy
digging is anticipated through the upper older fill and the loose
coral sand and gravel layer
Sheeting and bracing is required for this portion to prevent
caving in of the trench walls Due to the low strengths and high
water contents of the soils and the proximity to existing
structures special care must be exercised to prevent a failure of
the trench walls or upheaval of the trench bottom due to the
excavation and dewatering operations
Sheet pile or other support systems may be required to obtain
trench stability
If practical the excavation should be made in short sections and
backfilled as soon as possible
Heavy equipment and stockpiles should be kept away from the tops of
the open trenches
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1390 00 Page 10
The ground adjacent to and near the top of the excavation should
continuously be monitored for cracks or dips and other indications
of movements and possible sloughing of the trench walls This
monitoring is particularly critical in the area where existing
structures are adjacent to the trench excavation
Dewatering
Groundwater levels were noted above the invert elevation in the
lower portion of the alignment from to 5 50
The method of dewatering should be left open to the contractor
provided adequate stability of the excavation is maintained The
deposits encountered in the borings along this portion of the alignment
consisted mostly of older fills and loose coral sand and gravel
The project dewatering operation should be done without pumping out
the soil fines i e pumping clear water only It should be coordinated
with the excavation support system so that the stability of the
excavation is not jeopardized The operations should be carried out
without creating a quick condition or softening the bottom of the
excavation
Another alternative to dewatering would be to place the pipe ina
wet trench condition

Pipe Bedding
The stress distribution against the bottom of a pipe has a great
effect upon the load supporting capacity of the pipe and therefore it
is an important design consideration
A minimum of six 6 inches of compacted granular bedding material
should be placed below the pipe The granular bedding should extend
beyond the pipe sides a minimum width of one fourth the outside pipe
diameter and extend half way up the pipe sides The bedding material
should consist of well graded granular material such as 38 Fine gravel
refer to Plate 6 for details
Where soft soil conditions are encountered in the trench bottom
to 5 50 stabilization of the soft soils is required prior to
pipe installation To provide uniform support and a working platform an
18 inch layer of granular material such as 3B Fine gravel should be
provided below the sewer pipe and the bedding layer refer to Plate 7 for
details
Backfill
In general the trench backfill should be in accordance with the
Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction May 1975
Granular material such as sand should be used for the backfill up
to about one foot above the pipe to reduce the compactive effort required
and possible damage to the pipe
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1390 00 Page 12
Most of the excavated on site soils can be re used as the upper
portion of the trench backfill from above one foot over the pipe to
within 2 feet of the finished grade Backfill should be mechanically
colipacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established by
ASTM D 1557 Below the roadway areas the backfill within 2 feet of the
finished grade should consist of select borrow and be compacted to 95 of
its maximum dry density
If practicable the trenches should be kept free of water during
backfilling Should backfill under the water level be necessary then
granular material such as base course rock should be utilized to
minimize excessive ground settlement of the trench backfill in the future
In the upper portions of the alignment where stiff soils were
encountered since the net increase in load on the subsoils caused by the
sewer construction will be small the resulting settlements should also
be relatively small if the sewer line is properly constructed
In the soft soil areas some amount of surface settlements may
occur due to disturbances of the subsoils dewatering operations and
increase in unit weight of the trench backfill The amount of settlement
experienced depends on the contractor s construction methods

Joints and Connections
Due to the various soil conditions that will be crossed by the
pipe flexible pipe joints are recommended to allow for some amount of
differential settlements These flexible connections are particularly
important in the soft ground area where the new pipe line will be
connected to the existing 36 inch sewer line supported on piles
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and construction should
be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior
to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
Field inspection and engineering observations during the sewer
construction are important since these would permit correlation between
the preliminary soil data obtained during this investigation and the
actual soil conditions encountered during construction
Backfilling operations should be inspected by a soils engineer and
density tests taken to verify that adequate compaction density has been
obtained
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
