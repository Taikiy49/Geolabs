Dear Mr Aiello
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed during
the mass grading pad grading utility installation and road construction operations for the
Kapolei Knolls Subdivision Phases 1A 1B from July 1997 through July 1999 We
observed the mass grading pad grading utility installation excluding Roads J and K and road
construction excluding Roads J K and Station to the end of Road A
The subject site is located about 1000 to 4400 feet northeast from the Farrington
Highway Makakilo Drive Intersection and between the Interstate H 1 Highway and Farrington
Highway in Kapolei on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The site is bounded by vacant lots to the
northeast and southwest Farrington Highway to the southeast and the Interstate H 1 Highway
to the northwest A Navy underground water tank is located adjacent to the northeast corner
of the site Beyond the vacant area to the northeast is the Grace Pacific Quarry Beyond
Farrington Highway to the southeast are existing Kapolei subdivisions A commercial
development is planned for the adjacent vacant lot to the southwest
The mass grading pad grading utility installation and road construction work for the
Kapolei Knolls Subdivision Phases 1A 1B was performed by Kiewit Pacific Company
The grading work involved the construction of 215 lots and 11 City County of Honolulu
dedicated asphalt roads The mass grading work generally consisted of cuts of up to 30 feet
in depth and fills of up to 20 feet in height The fill materials were generated from on site cuts
and were imported from Kiewit Pacific Company s crushing plant near the Kapolei Business
Park Some material was also imported from Waikele and the Makakilo Off Ramp Expansion
Project The available on site fill materials generally consisted of brown to reddish brown silty
clay and silty gravel material The import fill materials from Kiewit Pacific Company s crushing
plant generally consisted of grayish brown sandy clay gravel and clayey gravel The import
fill materials from Waikele and the Makakilo Off Ramp Expansion Project generally consisted
of reddish brown clayey silt with cobbles and gravel

Prior to fill placement the existing vegetation was removed the exposed subgrades
were scarified to a depth of approximately 6 inches brought to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 The fill material was
then placed in level lifts generally less than 12 inches thick moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry
density At the private park the fill material was compacted to a minimum of 85 percent of the
maximum dry density Below the asphalt pavements the subgrade select borrow subbase
and subgrade materials were moisture conditioned to near its optimum moisture content and
then compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density
For fill layers containing coarse material which could not be tested using conventional
testing procedures each layer of fill was compacted until it did not weave or creep under the
weight of the compactor The maximum particle size was 12 inches for fills below 6 feet from
finished subgrade and 6 inches for fills below 3 feet from finished grade The material was
compacted with loose lifts not exceeding 1 times the maximum particle size or to a maximum
24 inches in loose thickness with finer material within the interstices between larger particles
The fill slopes were constructed by overfilling and cutting back to compacted soil
Loose surface soil left on the fill slope was then track rolled Where fill was placed on existing
ground that is steeper than five horizontal to one vertical 5H 1V the existing ground surface
was benched into firm soil as the fill was placed Due to the encountering of weathered rock
materials many of the proposed cut slopes into rock were flattened from a 2H 1V inclination
to a 1H 1V inclination Due to the encountering of highly fractured rock at Lot
and 108 a chain link fence was installed at the slope bench and shotcrete was placed at the
lower slope
Near the drainageway along Road A in general high to very high expansive soils were
encountered Based on the grading plan this area was to be filled with low to moderate
expansive material from the adjacent areas Therefore in areas with insufficient fill thickness
the area was over excavated by 36 inches to allow placement of low to moderate expansive
material These lots with insufficient fill thickness included Lot Nos 7 through
through through 205 and 210 through 214
During the grading period various on site and imported soils were tested prior to use
in the field The maximum dry density and optimum moisture content relationships were
established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plates 2 and 4 of this report
During the fill placement operations for the finish grading phase and part of the mass
grading phase periodic field density tests were performed in general accordance with the
American Society of Testing and Materials ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge
Method The tests for the mass grading phase were performed throughout the area Results

House Foundations
During house construction we recommend that footing excavations and backfilling
operations be observed by a qualified and licensed geotechnical engineer The following are
general guidelines for house foundations
Perimeter footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade
Footings constructed near tops of slopes or on sloping ground should be
embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal set back distance of
5 feet measured from the outside edge of the footings to the slope face
Footings should be embedded below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or retaining wall heel
The bottoms of footing excavations should be lightly compacted to provide a
relatively firm and smooth bearing surface prior to placement of reinforcing steel
and concrete
If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing
excavations they should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm
materials The over excavation should be backfilled with select granular
material compacted to a minimum 95 percent relative compaction or the footing
bottom may be extended down to the underlying competent material
Prior to house construction the slab subgrades should be lightly moistened
intermittently to increase the moisture in the upper 2 to 3 feet of soil but not
over saturated where soil becomes softened
The lots with high expansive material should have 24 inch deep perimeter
footings 12 inch thick driveway thickened edges and concrete slabs reinforced
with galvanized welded wire fabric as a minimum These lots include
