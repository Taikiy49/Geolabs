SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A site reconnaissance was performed on to observe the existing
conditions at the proposed wash rack area and its vicinity Based on our visual
observation and field testing the proposed wash rack area is underlain by a surficial layer
of fill material consisting of reddish brown clayey silt with some gravel Additional hand
probing with a 3 8 inch pointed steel rod indicated the consistency of the near surface
material is generally very stiff with penetrations of less than one inch in depth

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our site reconnaissance and information obtained from previous
exploration works performed at the project site it is our opinion that the very stiff clayey
silt soils encountered at the project site appears to be adequate to provide foundation
support of the proposed wash rack facility
Vehicle Wash Slab
Concrete slab on grade is anticipated for the design of the vehicle wash slab In
consideration of the heavy vehicular loadings anticipated for the proposed facility we
recommend that the following slab on grade section may be used for preliminary design
purposes
18 Inches Portland Cement Concrete
6 Inches Base Course
12 Inches Select Granular Fill
36 Inches Total Thickness on Moist Compacted Subgrade
We recommend that the concrete slab be designed by a structural engineer for the
intended loads and should be reinforced as a structural slab to accommodate some

Subgrade Preparation

recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction If soft and or loose soils
are encountered at the subgrade level they should be over excavated to expose
