This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the grading and infrastructure installation work for the Kapolei Parkway Kualakai
Parkway Intersection Improvements project from through
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Ka Makana Ali i development approximately 67 acres is in the East Kapolei
area in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is bounded
approximately by Kapolei Parkway to the north Roosevelt Avenue to the south the
proposed Phase II North South Road Extension to the east and a drainage channel and
Kapolei Middle School to the west
As part of the regional mall development we understand that improvements will be
made to the intersection of Kapolei Parkway and Kualakai Parkway The layout of the
roadway intersection improvements are shown on the Site Plans Plates 1 1 through 1 3
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Royal Contracting Co Ltd performed the site grading operations pavement
construction and utility line installation The site grading work consisted of subgrade

preparation placement and compaction of fills and backfills pavement construction and
utility trench excavation and backfill
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the excavated subgrades or areas
designated to receive fills were scarified to a minimum depth of 12 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor The compaction requirement was
increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction for finished subgrades
subjected to vehicular traffic
During placement of the non expansive select granular fill aggregate subbase and
aggregate base course layer the fill materials were generally placed in near level lifts
brought to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D1557
Modified Proctor
New utility lines and manhole structures were installed for the intersection
improvement project In general a bedding layer consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine
gravel was provided below the piping and manholes for uniform support Free draining
granular materials consisting of No 3B Fine gravel was also used for the trench backfill up
to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to
reduce the compaction effort on the initial trench backfill The trench backfill was placed in
8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to no less than 90 percent relative
compaction Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench
backfill below the pavement grade was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 248 field density tests were performed using the
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D6938 Results of the compaction tests
performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 12 of this report These tests were
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the
site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site
survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked
until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
