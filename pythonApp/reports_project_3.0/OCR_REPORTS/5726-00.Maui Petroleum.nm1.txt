Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Maui Petroleum Self Service Station project at 385 Dairy
Road in Kahului on the Island of Maui Hawaii The project location and general vicinity
are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 This report summarizes our findings
and geotechnical engineering recommendations resulting from our field exploration
laboratory testing and engineering analyses These recommendations are intended for
foundation design and site preparation only The findings and recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report

The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration was to obtain an
overview of the surface and subsurface conditions to develop a soil data set to formulate
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the foundation design and site
preparation for the proposed project The scope of work included the following tasks and
work efforts

385 Dairy Road in the town of Kahului Island of Maui Hawaii The ground surface of the
site is approximately 12 to 13 feet above MSL and consists of asphalt pavement and a

Subsurface Conditions
The subsoil conditions of the site were explored by drilling and sampling two test
borings extending to depths of about 20 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration generally encountered about 4 5 to 5 feet of fill consisting of
medium dense to very dense fine coralline sand with traces of silt The fills were underlain
by alluvial deposits consisting of sandy silt silty sand and clayey silt with cobbles to the
extent of the boring depths Groundwater was encountered at about 8 8 and 9 3 feet below
the existing ground surface at the boring locations which corresponds to 3 7 feet MSL
Groundwater levels will vary with seasonal rainfall and other factors
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in the test borings are presented
on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2 Results of the laboratory tests performed on
selected soils samples are presented on Plates B 1 and B 2
Discussion and Recommendations
The field exploration indicated that the site is underlain by fine coral sand fill over
alluvial silt and clayey silt soils which are suitable for support of the new tanks
However it appears that the lower 4 to 5 feet of the tanks will be submerged under the
water table Therefore we recommend placing a minimum of 24 inches of No 3B Fine
ASTM C 33 No 67 Gradation crushed rock below the bottom of the tank for uniform
support A layer of geotextile such as Mirafi 140 N should be placed below the crushed
rock
Our exploration indicates that the groundwater level is relatively shallow Based
on the anticipated diameter and embedment of the new tanks it appears that the tanks
will be subject to relatively significant buoyant uplift forces Therefore a tie down or
anchoring system will be required for the tanks
Soft to stiff silt with sand material was encountered from about 4 5 to 8 feet below
the existing ground surface as well as loose to medium dense silty sand material was
encountered from 8 to 12 feet below the ground surface These materials are
considered insufficiently competent for self stabilization of sidewall excavations
Therefore temporary shoring of the tank excavation pit will be needed to provide
sidewall excavation stability during construction
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report

Excavation and Backfill of Replacement Tanks
The preliminary plan indicates that the excavation for the new tanks will extend to
an elevation of 0 to 1 feet MSL As previously mentioned the water table was
measured at an elevation of approximately during the time of our field
exploration The presence of the water table at this elevation poses two stability
concerns
1 The sidewall of the excavation for the tanks will require temporary
shoring as per OSHA standard regulations The regulations can be
found at the OSHA website gov under Safety and
Health Regulations for Construction
P Excavations
2 The saturated soil under the tank is moderately compressible
Therefore over excavation of the in place soil to at least 24 inches
below the tank and replacement with No 3B Fine ASTM C 33
No 67 Gradation rock material and geotextile is recommended
3 Our field exploration indicates that groundwater at the site occurs at a
depth of about 9 feet below the existing ground surface This
groundwater level is subject to fluctuation due to seasonal precipitation
tides and other influences
Based on the information provided it is anticipated that the new tank
will be submerged in the groundwater by about 4 to 5 feet Therefore
we recommend providing a tank anchoring system to resist the buoyant
uplift forces resulting from the submergence
The backfill around the tanks should consist of crushed rock less than
1 5 inches to at least 12 inches around and above the tanks The crushed rock may
then be capped with a select granular fill material compacted to at least 95 percent of
the relative compaction to the subgrade for concrete slab elevation Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557
A Geolabs representative should observe site grading operations to evaluate
whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation process and to
confirm whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those encountered
during our field exploration

New Kiosk and Dispenser Canopy Foundation Recommendations
The new kiosk and the replacement canopy will be at the existing grade and no
additional fills are planned for the site The near surface fine coral sand material was
dense to about 2 feet and grades to medium dense from 2 to 5 feet below the existing
ground surface The material should be able to support the foundations for both
structures with a bearing pressure of when compacted to 95 percent relative
compaction
It is anticipated that the dispenser canopy will be a lightly loaded structure
consisting of a roof with support columns and open sides For this type of structure
uplift due to wind loading is an important consideration Therefore we recommend
designing the canopy foundations to resist these uplift forces
Items of grading that are addressed in the following subsections include 1 Fills
and Backfills and 2 Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
At the start of the earthwork existing ground which is to be cut to subgrade or to
receive fill material should be scarified to a depth of 6 inches and moisture conditioned
to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 95
percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of
soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established
in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Fills and Backfills
In general the on site coral sand material less than 2 inches in largest
dimension should be suitable for general fill material The on site as well as fill
materials should also be free of vegetation and deleterious materials
Structural fill and imported fill should consist of non expansive granular material
such as crushed coral basalt or cinder sand The material should be
well graded from coarse to fine with particles no larger than 2 inches in largest
dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the
No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more
and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent when tested in
accordance with ASTM D 1883 Geolabs should test imported fill materials for
conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for
its intended use
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose

Environmental Consideration
The project site has been used for many years as a gas station Release of
petroleum products are frequently found at gas station sites Investigation of subsurface
contamination was not part of our scope for this project nor is it part of our area of
professional practice To minimize the potential of encountering subsurface
contamination during construction which could result in delays and increased costs we
recommend retaining that a qualified environmental consultant to conduct a site
assessment related to subsurface contamination and to supervise the removal and
closure of the existing underground fuel storage tanks

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
two borings designated as Boring Nos B 1 and 2 to depths of about 21 5 feet below the
existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The
borings were drilled using truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving
a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the borings drilled in general accordance
with ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow
counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive
are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples in
the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined
compressive strength of the sample Results of the pocket penetrometer tests are
summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
