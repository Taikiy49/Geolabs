LABORATORY TEST RESULTS
Atterberg Limits ASTM D 4318
The Atterberg Limits test is used to determine the plasticity of the fraction of a soil
material finer that the 40 sieve For the sample tested the material was found to have a
liquid limit of 45 a plastic limit of 20 and a plasticity of 25 The soil is classified as a CL
soil
California Bearing Ratio ASTM D 1883
The California Bearing Ratio CBR test is used to evaluate the pavement support
characteristics of a soil material A sample is compacted in the laboratory to 90 to
100 percent relative dry density and then saturated After saturation the sample is
penetrated with a ram and the corresponding stress is recorded to yield the CBR value at
0 1 and 0 2 inches of penetration The sample tested in our laboratory yielded a
swell value of 4 4 percent
Ring Swell
This test is used to estimate the swell potential of a soil under a surcharge weight
roughly equivalent to a typical floor slab Excessive swell potential can lead to structural
distresses The sample tested in our laboratory showed a swell potential of 9 9 percent
which is considered to be relatively high
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our laboratory test results it is our opinion that the material is not
acceptable for use as structural fill material nor is it acceptable for use as a blending
material for an aggregate material with insufficient fines The low support characteristics
and the relatively high swell potential could potentially result in distresses to structures
and would degrade the quality of an aggregate material rendering it unsuitable
We do feel that the material would be acceptable for use as general fill if placed at
depths greater than about 5 feet below finished grade The material may also be usable
as topsoil or as cover material in a landfill
