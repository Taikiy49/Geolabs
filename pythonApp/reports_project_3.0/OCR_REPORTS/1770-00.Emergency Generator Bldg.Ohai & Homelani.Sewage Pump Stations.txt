In accordance with your request we have performed a foundation
engineering evaluation for the subject projects The approximate
locations of the project sites are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal
of
The purpose of the investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the sites to provide soil data for use in the foundation
design of the proposed structures
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analyses to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

Site Description
The Ohai project site is located about 80 feet north of the western
end of Ohai Place and the Homelani project site is located about 75 feet
south of California Avenue in Wahiawa Oahu Hawaii
The proposed structures will be located adjacent to the northern
side of the existing Ohai Sewage Pump Station and the eastern side of the
existing Homelani Sewage Pump Station
Project Consideration
It is proposed to build an approximately 9 by 15 feet one story
structure to house a 2600 lb generator unit for the existing Ohai Sewage
Pump Station Another similar structure approximately 11 by 16 feet is
planned to house a 3300 lb generator unit for the existing Homelani
Sewage Pump Station
Subsurface Conditions
Field Exploration
Two borings were drilled one at each of the two proposed sites to
a depth of 15 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 and 3
Due to limited accessibility of the area inside the existing chain
link fence surrounding the Ohai Sewage Pump Station Boring 2 was drilled
outside the fence but as close as possible to the proposed generator
building site We believe that the encountered soil conditions are
representative of the soils under the proposed structure

Soil Conditions
The borings primarily encountered residual soil consisting of stiff
to very stiff reddish brown to brown clayey silt
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during the field
exploration
Detailed description of the soils encountered are presented on the
Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2
Discussion and Recommendations
The subsoil conditions at the project sites consist of stiff to
very stiff clayey silt and appear to be suitable for the proposed
generator building foundations
Foundation
Based on our findings we believe that shallow spread or continuous
footings may be considered for the foundation support of the proposed
buildings It is recommended that the footing bottom be embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade An allowable soil
bearing pressure of 3000 p s f may be used for the design

Site Preparation
The overall site preparation will include removal of any vegetation
debris and other organic matter After clearing and grubbing the
existing ground should be scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches
moisture conditioned and proof rolled to achieve at least 90 of its ASTM
D 1557 78 maximum density
Fill Material
If required excavated on site material free of any expansive
component may be used as a source of fill material Any imported fill
material should consist of selected non expansive silty sandy or
granular material less than 3 inches in size with less than 20 passing
the 200 sieve
All fills and backfills should be placed in 10 inch maximm loose
lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry
density
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and structure foundations
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that site grading spread footing excavations
subgrade preparations and cushion layer construction for slabs be
observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investigation

Limitations
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all

The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance and soil information from boring and
laboratory test data obtained from this soil exploration
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such as
expansive soil and soft spots may occur in localized areas and may
require additional probing or corrections in the field Therefore
additional expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a
properly constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended
to accommodate these possible costs

Field Exploraiton

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two
2 borings to a depth of 15 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 and 3 A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classificaiton System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hanmer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
