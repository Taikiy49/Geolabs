Based on a site meeting held on February 7 with Mr Tom Morioka from D A G S and
from Mitsunaga Associates Inc it was decided to conduct a
geotechnical engineering exploration to further evaluate the probable cause s and potential
remedial measures pertaining to the reported distress at the above referenced site The
following summarizes our field observations and findings during our site visit and subsequent
field exploration and presents our discussions and recommendations with respect to the
observed distress at the subject site
Project Considerations
The project site is located within the existing Kapolei High School in the Kapolei area
on the Island of Oahu Hawaii It is our understanding that the tennis courts have
experienced some distress as evident by cracking in the asphaltic concrete AC surface
along the longitudinal edges of the tennis courts Therefore it was desired to conduct a
geotechnical engineering exploration in an attempt to evaluate the probable cause s of the
distress observed and to formulate remedial recommendations pertaining to the existing
distress at the subject site
Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the surface and
subsurface conditions at the site The subsurface information was used to develop a soil and
groundwater data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations
pertaining to the existing distress observed at the subject site Our work was performed in
general accordance with the scope of services outlined in our fee proposal dated

Field Observations and Exploration
A site visit was conducted to observe the reported distress at the tennis court site of
the Kapolei High School campus Based on our field observations the distress observed at
the tennis court site generally consisted of cracking in the AC surface above the base
course layer It appears that all of the cracks observed were generally confined to the
areas along the longitudinal edges of the tennis courts Significant cracking of the AC
surface was not observed in the other areas of the tennis court The majority of the cracks
observed generally was confined to the area within about 5 feet from the curbs with a few
cracks located up to about 8 feet from the edges of the tennis courts Based on our field

Evaluation and Discussions
In general cracking in the AC may appear in different forms such as alligator
cracking block cracking edge cracking longitudinal and transverse cracking and slippage
cracking which are generally traffic related We understand that the AC surface at the tennis
court has not been subjected to traffic loads other than some construction equipment In
addition our field observations at the nearby playcourts did not reveal indications of similar
distress Because our field exploration confirmed that the underlying base course and fill
soils are generally non expansive in nature or possess low expansion potential and appear
to be well compacted it is unlikely that the edge cracking of the AC surface is related to the
base course and compacted fill materials directly below the pavement section at the tennis
courts Therefore we believe that the distress observed may be attributed to the AC surface
itself and or some surrounding factors at the edges of the tennis courts
Based on our research and experience with asphaltic concrete paving edge cracking
is generally parallel to and is usually within 1 to 2 feet of the outer edge of the pavement
The observed edge cracking of the AC surface at the tennis court is consistent with this type
of AC cracking It is possible that the edge cracking observed at the tennis court area could
have been caused by a weakened subgrade near the edge of the pavement However the
high penetration resistance of the subsurface materials at the tennis court area obtained from
our field exploration does not support this hypothesis Based on our evaluation it is possible
that the longitudinal edge cracking of the AC surface generally parallel to the length wise of
the pavement could have been caused by one or a combination of the following
1 A poorly constructed paving lane joint temporary construction joint
Zt Shrinkage of the AC surface due to hardening of the asphalt and or daily
cycling of temperature
2 A reflective crack caused by cracking beneath the surface course
Because the placement and compaction of the AC surface was not observed by our
office during construction we are unable to comment on the quality of the construction of the
AC surface However it is possible that the AC surface cracking observed could be
attributed to a poorly constructed paving lane joint temporary construction joint and or poor
compaction of the AC
Due to the generally dry weather conditions which are typical in the Kapolei area it
is highly unlikely that shrinkage of the AC due to hardening of the asphalt and or daily cycling
of the temperature would be a primary contributing factor to the distress observed In
addition it appears that the base course layer below the AC surface course did not exhibit
cracking to result in reflective cracking at the AC surface based on our field observations

Based on the available information and our engineering evaluation we believe that
the following hypothesis may explain the type of distress observed at the tennis court area
Based on our field observations it appears that the low lying drainage swales alongside the
tennis courts are subjected to substantial amounts of irrigation and surface water runoff from
irrigation This would result in softening of the near surface soils as evident by the ease of
our probing penetration outside of the tennis court area and reduction in the passive
pressure resistance
Because of the slightly sloping ground conditions at the site as compared to the
relatively flat surroundings at the basketball courts some slight movement of the ground
toward the softer near surface soils at the drainage swales in the low lying areas may have
occurred In addition wetting of the compacted fill materials due to appreciable irrigation
may have caused some adjustment of the fills even well compacted fills below the tennis
courts We believe that the slight lateral movement of the soils may have resulted in the
edge cracks observed at the AC surface along the longitudinal edges of the tennis courts
The edge cracks of the AC surface could be further enhanced by a low strength surface mix
or a poor bond between the AC surface and the underlying base course layer
Remedial Recommendations
It should be noted that it is difficult to predict whether this adjustment of the soil
masses if occurred have already reached equilibrium or not To reduce the potential for
significant future cracking of the AC surface we recommend that as a minimum a layer of
pavement reinforcing fabric such as AmoPave 4597 or 4599 or equivalent be placed
between the AC and the base course layer The pavement reinforcing fabric should be
non woven and should be heat bonded on one side to reduce the potential for the fabric to
stick to the equipment tires The fabric should extend in a single roll from the edge towards
the middle of the tennis court A tack coat should be applied to the base course surface and
the edges of the existing AC surface prior to the placement of the pavement geotextile
fabric In addition we recommend that the irrigation in the vicinity of the tennis court be
regulated and monitored to reduce the potential for excessive irrigation of the area A
windbreaker fine plastic mesh should be installed as early as practical to reduce the
potential for landscape irrigation from reaching inside the tennis court areas We believe that
the potential for distress to the tennis courts should be significantly reduced if these
measures are properly implemented and maintained
To further reduce the effects of landscape irrigation adjacent to the tennis court
consideration may be given to the installation of a perimeter cut off wall around the tennis
court curbing to confine the surface AC and underlying base course from shifting toward the
low lying areas The perimeter cut off wall should measure at least 12 inches wide placed
against the outside edge of the curbing The cut off wall should also measure at least
24 inches deep from the existing grades The excavation for the perimeter cut off wall may
be backfilled with lean concrete CLSM or similar materials If this suggestion is elected we
recommend that paving of the tennis court be delayed until the perimeter cut off wall is
