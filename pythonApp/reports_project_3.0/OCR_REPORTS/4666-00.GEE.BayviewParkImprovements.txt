for the proposed Bayview Park Improvements project located within the existing Kaneohe
Wastewater Treatment Plant in the Kaneohe area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings based on the field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical recommendations derived from our analyses for the
proposed project These recommendations are intended for the design of building
foundations slabs on grade site preparation and pavements only and are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report

SECTION 4 0 REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be of
middie to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be of later Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which time erosion incised
deep valleys into the Koolau Shield volcanic activity returned with a series of lava flows
followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These series are referred to as the Honolulu
Volcanic Series
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles
of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to fill
the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea When the
glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea levels
rose to above the present sea level
The processes of erosion and deposition were affected by these glacio eustatic sea
level fluctuations When the sea level was low the erosional base level was correspondingly
lower and valleys were carved to depths below the present sea level When the sea level was
high the erosional base level was raised such that sediments accumulated at higher
elevations
In the mountainous regions of Hawaii the erosional processes are dominated by
detachment of soil and rock masses from the valley walls and are then transported downslope
toward the axis of a valley primarily by gravity as colluvium Once these materials reach the
stream in the central portion of a valley alluvial processes become dominant and the
sediments are transported and deposited as alluvium
in general stream flows in Hawaii are intermittent and flashy such that the stream
flows transmit large volumes of water for very short durations Because of this transport of
sediments is intermittent and the bulk of the stream s hydraulic load consists of a poorly sorted

mixture of boulders cobbles gravel sands and fines When the erosional base levels
change these sediment loads are left as deposits
Depending on the local base level and rate of transport the newer sediments are
generally transient in terms of geologic time In addition their consistency and density are
generally less than those of the older partially consolidated deposits

7 1 Ground Settlements
As previously mentioned soft and or loose alluvial lagoonal deposits with varying
amounts of organic material were encountered in the borings drilled at the project site Based
on our field exploration the soft and or loose alluvial lagoonal deposits extended to depths of
about 35 to 50 feet below the existing ground surface A surface fill layer of about 4 5 to 6 feet
thick was encountered above the soft and or loose alluvial lagoonal deposits
Based on our experience at the Kaneohe Wastewater Treatment Plant and the
adjacent Bayview Golf Links project significant ground settlement has occurred and continues
to occur at the site due to the previous placement of fills over the soft and or loose
alluvial lagoonal deposits It should be noted that it is difficult to accurately predict the ground
settlements induced by the previously placed fills In addition it is difficult to accurately predict
the time for the ground to settle due to the previously placed fills
It should be noted that additional ground settlements will occur when new fills even
1 foot of new fills are placed over the existing ground to raise the existing ground surface to
the design finished grades The anticipated ground settlements are primarily the result of
consolidation of the in situ soils induced by new fill loads placed over the soft and or loose
alluvial lagoonal deposits at the site Therefore to reduce the potential for inducing significant
additional ground settlements due to new fills we recommend that filling at the project site be
kept to a minimum less than 2 feet of new fills above existing ground surface
7 2 Building Foundations

occur at the site To reduce the potential for significant structural distress due to differential
settlements between the footings and the interior slab of the new comfort station we
recommend that a structural slab on grade foundation with thickened edge footings be used
to support the new comfort station The structural slab on grade should be structurally
reinforced reinforcing bars should be spaced at 12 inch spacing in each direction and would

the foundations and slabs and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from the
foundation and slab areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs
should be avoided Planters next to foundations should also be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for significant water infiltration into the subsurface
The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab
edges immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce the potential for significant water
infiltration In addition drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should
be maintained to drain surface water runoff away from the foundations and slabs

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
six borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 6 extending to depths ranging from
approximately 6 5 to 55 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a
truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight hollow stem augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing in
the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials encountered are
presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 6 of Appendix A
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches
In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The
blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are
shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths
Pocket penetrometer and torvane shear tests were performed on selected cohesive
soil samples retrieved in the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the
unconfined compressive strength of the sample The torvane shear test provides a quick
estimate of the undrained shear strength of the soil Results of the pocket penetrometer and
torvane shear tests are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
