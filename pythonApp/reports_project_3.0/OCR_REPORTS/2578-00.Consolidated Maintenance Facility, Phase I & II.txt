SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
Johnston Atoll is located near the center of the North Pacific between the Hawaiian
Islands and the Marshall Islands Located 717 nautical miles southwest of the island of
Oahu Johnston Atoll rests on the core of an ancient volcano now buried under a coralline
limestone cap thousands of feet thick This is the result of 70 million years of reef growth
on the slowly sinking island Today Johnston Atoll is a broad shallow platform
approximately 50 square miles with a marginal reef emergent only in the northwest Four
small islands Johnston Sand North and East emerge from the lagoon
Johnston Island was originally part of the Johnston Atoll encompassing
approximately 60 acres above water Subsequent dredge and fill operations in years
1946 1963 and 1964 had enlarged the island to approximately 626 acres It appears that
the project site is situated on an area which was on the seaward edge of the original atoll
and subsequently filled in 1946
Field Exploration
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling six 6 borings to
depths of 20 0 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations
of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 3
In general the field exploration disclosed that the site is mantled by a medium
dense to very dense surface dredged fill layer consisting of coral sand and gravel
approximately 5 to 6 feet thick overlying a relatively thin layer of loose coralline silty to
gravelly sand of about 1 to 3 feet thick The loose sand layer appeared to be a loose
dredged fill near the groundwater level and was underlain by dense coral limestone reef
formation to the maximum depth of the borings drilled

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the site is mantled by dredged fill consisting of
coral sand and gravel overlying coral limestone reef formation of the original atoll The
dredged fill is generally dense to very dense near the surface the upper 5 feet but
grades to loose with depth particularly near and below the groundwater level
Shallow spread footing foundations bearing on the upper dense fill crust can be
utilized to support the proposed single story buildings in both Phase and Il of the
proposed Consolidated Maintenance Facility Site grading involving cuts which will
reduce the thickness of the competent upper dense fill crust supporting the
recommended footing foundations should be avoided insofar as possible
Site Grading
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of existing pavements vegetation debris rubbish and other deleterious materials
These materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Any loose or soft
spots encountered should be over excavated down to the firm subsoils and then replaced
with compacted non expansive select material

The fill areas should then be scarified to a depth of 6 inches moisture conditioned
and recompacted to 90 of their maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test

The on site coral sand and gravel can be used as fill or backfill materials If
required the imported fill should consist of non expansive soils such as select borrow

The fill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts moisture
conditioned and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78

Footing Foundations
Shallow spread footing foundations may be used to support the single story
metal framed buildings of the proposed Consolidated Maintenance Facility Phase and
I
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f is recommended for footings bearing on
the existing dense surface granular fill layer or compacted new fill with a minimum footing
embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
The bottoms of the footing excavation should be recompacted to a minimum of
95 of the maximum dry density according to ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 If fills are
required at footing locations fill materials should consist of select granular soil placed in
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2578 00 Page 8
maximum 10 inch loose horizontal lifts and compacted to a minimum of 95 of the
maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
If soft soil pockets are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavation they
should be over excavated to dense in situ soil and replaced with select on site sandy soil
or granular select borrow compacted to a minimum of 95 maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
If a footing is located next to a utility line it snould extend to the bottom of the
utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Alternatively the footing foundations should be structurally designed to span over the
well compacted backfill in the utility trenches to minimize possible footing settlements
Estimated footing settlement under the anticipated maximum column load of up to
40 kips is less than one half of an inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundations and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent
fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil around the foundation is well
compacted and remain tight in place A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be used in
estimating the frictional resistance
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundation to avoid ponding of
surface run off water that may weaken the strength of the soils around the foundations

Drainage
The finish grade outside the slab footings and pavement should be shaped to
shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding condition Excessive landscape
watering near foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause excessive subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent soil strength weakening The foundation excavations should
be properly backfilled tight against the wall and slab footing immediately after setting of
the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This review
is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations
given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that a qualified geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and construction of the
foundations This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
lf actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

recommendations given in this report

from structures indicated on the site plan by Aotani and Associates Inc dated October

to the degree implied by the method used
