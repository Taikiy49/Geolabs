2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age in age and
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while
Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lavas
banking against its eastern flank These banked or ponded lavas formed a broad plateau
referred to as the Schofield Plateau
Puu Makakilo is a cinder and spatter cone that was built by one or more eruptive
events that produced large quantities or pyroclastic material such as cinder spatter and
ash In some areas welding and or hypothermal action has fused or cemented the
pyroclastics into weak and strong rock e g tuff
In addtion to the pyroclastics the eruptions produced lava flows of very dense
basalt which are interbedded with the pyroclastic materials Structurally Puu Makakilo is a
heterogenous mixture of interbedded cinder tuff and dense basalt The units are
discontinuous and difficult to differentiate on an area basis in the field
In situ chemical weathering of the pyroclastics and basalts has produced a surface
layer of residual soil which mantles most of the project site This residual soil grades
rapidly to the underlying volcanic materials

3 1 Ground Settlement
Based on the preliminary grading plan provided we understand that significant fills
will be placed for the proposed development A majority of the lots on the eastern and
western sides will be constructed on the fill conditions In general there will be up to
50 feet and 17 feet of fills placed on the eastern and western sides respectively over the
existing slope in order to achieve the design grades for the proposed project Based on
the topographic survey map the existing slope ranges from approximately two horizontal
to one vertical 2H 1V to 8H 1V on the eastern side and 6H 1V on the western side
Significant ground settlements are anticipated when new fills in excess of about
20 feet high are placed over the existing ground to raise the site to the finished elevations
These settlements will affect the construction schedule and the earthwork quantity
estimates for the project Based on the results of our exploration the anticipated ground
settlements are primarily the result of the following two processes
e compression of the fill material under its own weight
consolidation of the in situ soils induced by the new fill loads
The settlement induced from compression of the fill material under its own weight is
a function of the relative percentage of compaction and time period
Our analyses indicated that the magnitude of the fill settlements can be reduced by
increasing the compaction of portions of the fills from 90 to 95 percent Additionally it is
our opinion that most of the settlements would occur within about 6 months from the
completion of the filling operations Some long term settlements may continue for the high
fill greater than 20 feet areas therefore special foundation systems are recommended
for these areas

3 4 Slabs On Grade Foundations
Based on the soil conditions at the project site we believe that slab on grade
foundations with thickened edge footings may be used to support the proposed one and
two story residential structures planned for the subject development Based on our
laboratory swell test results we categorized the on site soils into three general types as
described below
Type 1 Low Expansion Soil with 0 to 3 9 Swell
Type 2 Moderate Expansion Soil with 4 0 to 8 9 Swell
Type 3 High Expansion Soil with over 9 0 Swell
The house foundation recommendations for concrete slab on grade corresponding
to the above soil categories are as follows
Type 1 4 Inch thick concrete slab with wire mesh over 4 inches of gravel
cushion with 12 inch deep embedded perimeter footing
Type 2 5 Inch thick reinforced concrete slab over 3 to 4 inches of gravel
cushion with 24 inch deep embedded perimeter footing
Type 3 Remove 2 feet of expansive soil and replace with low expansion soil
then use Type 1 foundations
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 000 pounds per square foot psf may be
used for the design of house foundations bearing on the compacted on site clayey soils or
compacted fills needed to achieve the design finished grades This bearing value is for
those caused by wind or seismic forces The bottom of foundations should be

3 5 Driveway Slabs
We envision that concrete driveway slabs are required at the residential lots We
anticipate that the vehicle loading for the proposed driveways will consist primarily of
passenger vehicles and light pick up trucks We have made our preliminary design

at the areas which consist of Type 1 soil be supported directly on the finished grade
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction For the slabs at the areas

compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction We recommend that the
concrete slabs be designed by a structural engineer for the intended loads be 5 inches
thick and be reinforced with wire mesh to accommodate some amount of differential
movement

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation
of soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected sample
of the soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification
Results of the test are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depth Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1
One consolidation test ASTM D 2435 was performed on a selected soil sample
to evaluate the compressibility characteristics of the deeper in situ soils The test
results are plotted on Plate B 2
Seven 1 inch ring swell tests were performed on samples of the native soils to
evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils The test results are presented
on Plate B 3
Two laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR tests ASTM D 1883 were
performed on bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support
characteristics of the soils The results of the test are presented on Plates B 4 and B 5
