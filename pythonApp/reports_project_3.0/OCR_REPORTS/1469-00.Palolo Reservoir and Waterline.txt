PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to explore the
subsurface soil and groundwater conditions below the proposed reservoir
site and along the new waterline alignment in order to provide soil
engineering recommendations for use in the design of the reservoir
foundations and waterline details

Our work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated
The general project site is situated in an area which was proposed for
residential development in the early 1950 s However due to severe
drainage and soil instability problems the development was stopped by
government agencies
The general history of the development and an assessment of the site soil
and drainage conditions are presented in a report entitled Proposed
Subdivision Carlos Long Unit V Palolo Honolulu Hawaii A Site Study
prepared by Pacific Engineering Co Ltd for the Board of Water Supply
City and County of Honolulu dated August 6 1951
The report indicates that most of the general area is underlain by
potentially unstable adobe clay talus deposits except for an area
northeast of the intersection of Narcissus Place and Carlos Long Street
where the reservoir site is situated Therefore part of the purpose of
this investigation was to determine if the proposed reservoir and
waterline alignment were outside of the potentially unstable soil area

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project includes the construction of a reinforced concrete
reservoir tank access road and a new 12 inch waterline extending from
the new tank to the existing waterline on Myrtle Street
The bottom of the reservoir is planned at about elevation 590 feet and
therefore slope cuts up to about 60 feet in vertical height may be
required for the site grading Cuts up to about 30 feet in vertical
height may be required for the access road construction Filling is not
planned for the site

The waterline is planned within the road right of way and the invert
levels are anticipated to be shallow less than 5 feet deep
The layout of the proposed structures is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the westerly side of Palolo Valley along
the toe of a ridge and includes Narcissus Place Carlos Long Street
between Myrtle Street and Narcissus Place and Kawao Park Access Road
The general study area is on a sloping hillside with an average cross
slope of approximately 2 to 5 horizontal to 1 vertical 50 to 20 slope
down in a southerly direction The general project site is shown on
Photograph 1 Plate 5 The proposed reservoir will be located on the
existing steep hillside about 100 feet above Kawao Park Access Road and
the proposed waterline will run from the reservoir along the reservoir
access road and either along Kawao Park Access Road and Narcissus Place
to Carlos Long Street or will cross Kawao Park Access Road and the
drainage ditch to Carlos Long Street From there the line runs dow
Carlos Long Street to about the Myrtle Street intersection
The general elevations over the study area generally varied from about
490 to 660 feet above Mean Sea Level
The reservoir site is overgrown with a growth of brush and small trees

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The site is located on the southern flank of the elongated Koolau
Volcanic Range which forms the southeastern portion of Oahu Hawaii
The range was formed during the Tertiary Period of pahoehoe and
aa basalt lava flows from a rift zone roughly paralleling the
existing mountain trends During the Late Tertiary and Pleistocene
Ice Age deep valleys including Palolo Valley were eroded by
streams During high stands of sea level in the Pleistocene the
valleys and slope breaks at the base of the ridges were infilled with
sediment alluviated grading to the high sea level stands
Continued weathering and erosion of these ridges deposited boulders
talus and soil slope wash at their base to form alluvial
colluvial fans which form the lower portion of the site from about
Narcissus Street to beyond Myrtle Street

The ridge up slope from Narcissus Place and Kawao Park Access Road is
composed mainly of Koolau Range aa basalt Aa basalt consists of
dense blocky core rock surrounded by a layer of clinker The
alternating flows and clinker zones have weathered and eroded to form
a steep stairstep like topography in exposure above and probably
beneath the site Photograph 4 on Plate 6 shows this condition
The generalized soil conditions at the site are shown on Cross

Soil Conditions
The following three general types of soil and rock formations were
encountered
1 The surface soils are generally slope wash consisting of dark
gray silty clay These soils are usually moderately compres
sible and have moderate to high shrink swell potential
2 Underlying the slope wash is alluvium or colluvium consisting of
mottled brown silty clay with weathered gravel These soils are
usually moderately compressible and have moderate shrink swell
potential
3 The rock formation consisted of weathered Aa basalt with
alternating layers of hard rock and softer clinker zones

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the reservoir site is underlain by
basalt rock formation at shallow depth It is our opinion that the
basalt rock can provide adequate foundation support for the reservoir
tank
The borings along the waterline alignment Boring encountered
thick deposits of slope wash and alluvium To minimize the potential of
lateral ground movement we recommend that the alignment be re routed to
avoid this area

RESERVOIR
Foundation
Spread and continuous wall footings bearing on the dense basalt
formation is recommended for the reservoir An allowable bearing
value of 8 000 p s f may be used for footings bearing on the above
material A one third increase of the above bearing value may be
used for temporary wind and seismic loads
The footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
adjacent finished grade
Footings located next to utility trenches should extend down to the
bottom of the utility trench and bear on the rock formation or the
trench should be backfilled with concrete
Site drainage should be designed to divert surface water away from
the reservoir foundation
All trench backfill away from the reservoir foundation should be
properly compacted to obtain 95 of maximum density per ASTM
D 1557 78 test method
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1469 00 Page 10
To reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to possible
cavities or lava tubes in the underlying lava rock the footing
excavation should be probed at approximately 10 feet on centers
The probe holes should have a minimum diameter of 2 inches and
extend below the bottom of footing a depth of 6 feet The probe
holes should be grouted from the bottom of the hole using low
pressures If a cavity is suspected below a footing additional
probe holes should be made to delineate the horizontal extent of the
cavity The probing and grouting operations should be monitored by
the soils engineer or the project inspector to see that these
recommendations are carried out properly and to locate any potential
voids near the footing excavations

Waterline
The waterline alignment was originally planned to extend along Kawao Park
Access Road and Narcissus Place However thick deposits of slope wash
and alluvium were encountered near the intersection of the above two
roads Although this area presently appears stable we recommend that
the alignment be revised to intersect Narcissus Place between Boring Nos
3 and 5 to take advantage of the better soil conditions there This
would also result in a shorter alignment

Loads on buried conduits are influenced by the width of the trench
the size of the pipe the unit weight of backfill material and the
friction resistance between the backfill material and the trench
walls To compute the vertical loads on the pipes a unit weight of
115 p c f and a coefficient of friction of 0 25 are recommended
Earth forces upon the pipe increase rapidly with the width of the
trench and therefore the width of the trench Bd should be kept to
a minimum The attached Plate 3 indicates three alternative types of
trenches

Pipe Bedding
The stress distribution against the bottom of a pipe has a great
effect upon the load supporting capacity of the pipe and therefore
a pipe bedding is an important design consideration
A minimum of six inches of compacted granular bedding material should
be placed below the pipe The granular bedding should extend beyond
the pipe sides a minimum width of one fourth the outside pipe
diameter and should extend halfway up the pipe sides The bedding
material should consist of clean granular material such as 3B Fine
gravel
Where practicable provisions should be made to drain the bedding
layer in order to minimize water seepage into the surrounding soil

Excavation
The method and equipment used for excavation should be the choice of
the contractor and within practical limits The anticipated depth of
excavation is less than 5 feet
Relatively easy excavation through the upper alluvial soils is
anticipated however excavations into the rock formation may require
hoe ram equipment
Trench Wall Supports
Deeper trenches in the on site soils may require shoring Generally
excavations in the rock formation may not require shoring
The excavation should be provided with proper bracing in accordance
with applicable safety requirements The design and safety of the
trench supports should be the responsibility of the contractor
If sufficient space is available the excavation may be cut hack at a
temporary slope of 1 1 H V in lieu of the above wall supports
However it should be taken into consideration that greater amount of
vertical pressures will be transmitted to the pipe by widening of the
trench
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 16
Heavy equipment and stockpiles should be kept away from the tops of
the open trenches
The ground adjacent to and near the top of the excavation should be
continuously monitored for cracks or dips and other indications of
movements and possible sloughing of the trench walls Such
monitoring is particularly critical in areas where existing
structures are adjacent to the trench excavation
Granular material such as sand should be used to backfill the
utility trench up to about one foot above the pipe to reduce the
compactive effort required and possible damage to the pipe
The excavated on site soil can be reused in the upper portion of the
trench backfill from above one foot over the pipe to finished grade
Backfill should be mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78

APPENDIX 8B
Moisture content and Atterberg Limit tests were
performed on selected samples as an aid in the classifi
cation and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 1
through A 7 2
Seven sieve analysis tests were performed on samples
of the underlying soils to determine their grain size
distribution The test results are presented on Plates B 1
and B 2
Three direct shear tests were performed on undisturbed
samples of the underlying soil to evaluate its strength
characteristics The test results are presented on Plates
B 3 through B 5
Two one inch ring swell tests were performed on
samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the swelling
potential of the soils The test results are presented on
Plate B 6
