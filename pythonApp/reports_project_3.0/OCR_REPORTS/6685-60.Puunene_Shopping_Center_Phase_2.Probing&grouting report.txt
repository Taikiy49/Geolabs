The development of the shopping center will consist of some major stores retail

Probing and Grouting Operations
A foundation probing and grouting program was implemented for Buildings
and 12 at the Puunene Shopping Center project Leslie s Drilling
performed the probing and grouting operations in general conformance with the
recommendations presented in our reports dated and
prepared for the project The purpose of the probing and grouting operations was to
reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities or
compression of loose zones below these building structures
Maryl Group surveyed the footing layout and marked the probe hole locations prior
to the probing and grouting operations In general the probe holes were located at the
column footings and spaced at approximately 10 foot intervals along the wall footings The
approximate probe hole locations are shown on the Probe Hole Location Plans Plates 2 1
through 2 10 In general the probe holes at the new building structures were drilled to a
depth ranging from about 11 to 13 feet below the existing ground surface corresponding
to approximately 10 feet below the design footing bottom elevations
Leslie s Drilling performed the probing work using a truck mounted drill rig equipped
with continuous flight augers and core barrels The probe holes were about 4 inches in
diameter and extended to depths of about 11 to 13 feet below the existing ground surface
corresponding to approximately 10 feet below the design footing bottom elevations
The materials encountered during the probe drilling operations generally consisted
of surface fill materials over basalt rock formation After completion of the probe drilling
operations the probe holes were filled with sand cement grout one part sand and one
part cement The grout used to fill the probe holes was supplied by Hawaiian Cement
and the trailer pump was operated by American Standard Concrete Pumping Hawaii Inc
Our field representative observed the probing and grouting operations and recorded
the probe depth and approximate grout take A total of 464 probe holes with an
aggregate length of about 5 862 lineal feet below the existing ground surface were drilled
for the ten structures as summarized in the following table The probing and grouting
summary for each individual structure is provided on Plates 3 1 through 3 19
