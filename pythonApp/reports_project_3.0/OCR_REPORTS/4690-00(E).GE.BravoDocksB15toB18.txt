PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The existing Bravo Docks B15 to B18 is located in the center portion of the Naval
Shipyard facility between the east B20 B21 and the west quay B12 B13 walls at Pearl
Harbor on the Island of B16 and B17 B18 are on the west and east
sides of the pier respectively The existing pier extends about 1 524 feet from the back of
the head quay wall to the outboard end of the pier in an approximate south north direction
The general project location is shown on Plate 1
The dock is a reinforced concrete structure Based on the information provided we
understand that the primary pier deck 100 foot wide section is supported by cast in place
reinforced concrete columns arranged in four longitudinal rows each capped by a
longitudinal girder These columns are 48 inch and 45 inch in diameter in the two interior
and the two exterior rows respectively Each column is founded by bell shaped
cast in place concrete footings We also understand that the bell shaped concrete footing
is supported on 30 interior and 23 exterior timber piles Details of the bell shaped
concrete footing supported on the timber piles are presented on Plate 2
Based on the underwater inspection conducted by another firm we understand that
a majority of the existing columns supporting the pier are in good condition with the
exception of six columns that exhibit cracking and spalling of surface concrete due to
corrosion of the reinforcing steel We also understand that no expansion joint is present
on the majority of the pier Therefore the mooring lateral loading from Naval vessels may
be assumed to be resisted by the interaction of the whole pier

It is desired to estimate the pile foundation spring stiffness Our lateral analysis
was performed on both the interior and exterior columns foundations based on the above
assumptions The Group 4 0 computer program was used for pile foundation lateral
analysis
In general lateral load resistance of piles is a function of the stiffness of the
surrounding soil the stiffness of the pile allowable deflection at the top of the pile and
induced moment in the pile Program Group 4 0 was developed by adaptation of a finite
difference method to analyze the behavior of piles in a group subjected to axial lateral
loadings and overturning moment from the pile cap to piles The program requires a
symmetrical pile arrangement The program computes deflection shear bending moment
and soil response with respect to depth in non linear soils for each individual pile The
analysis was carried out with the use of internally generated non linear p y curves to
represent soil moduli A solution requires iteration to accommodate the nonlinear
response of each of the piles The equations of equilibrium are satisfied and compatibility
is achieved between pile movement and soil response and between the movement of the
cap and the pile head movement
The program can model various pile head boundary conditions and various pile
properties as a function of depth The group geometry pile properties soil properties and
loading conditions were input into the program based on available information loading
information provided by Sato Associates Inc and our previous experience in the
general area
Generally lateral loading from vessels through mooring lines and fenders was
transferred to the top of the pile caps based on the structural analysis The 30 timber piles
under the interior columns were modeled as a symmetrical interior group configuration
consisting of 16 piles on the outer ring with radius of 6 1 feet 10 and 4 piles on middle and
inner rings with radius of 3 9 and 1 6 feet respectively The 23 timber piles under the
exterior columns were arranged as a symmetrical exterior group configuration consisting
of 14 and 8 on the outer and inner rings with radius of 5 5 and 2 6 feet and 1 pile on the

Conclusions
Our analyses indicated that the existing pile foundation under the interior and
exterior columns have adequate lateral capacity to resist the anticipated mooring loading
It should be noted that due to lack of as built information of the existing pile foundation our
analysis was based on the assumptions mentioned previously The subsurface conditions
are based on the test borings conducted in the early 1930 s
