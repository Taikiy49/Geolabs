INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed construction yard grading development at Waiawa on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings resulting from our field exploration and
laboratory testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived
from our analyses based on the data obtained through the field exploration and laboratory
testing

appropriate to form the basis for construction cost estimates or for construction bidding
purposes

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located to the south site of Farrington Highway and
to the west of Waiawa Stream The terrain at the site slopes to the east at a gradient of
about three horizontal to one vertical H 1V for approximately 100 feet from an elevation
of approximately 47 feet above MSL down to about 14 feet above MSL The topography
becomes generally level extending to approximately 30 feet away from the Waiawa Stream
where the terrain slopes downward to the stream at a gradient of about three horizontal
to one vertical H 1V

fill crust is difficult due to the proximity to the underlying high ground water level and soft
compressible subsurface soils

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
It is our opinion that the proposed fill embankment at the project site is only
feasible from a geotechnical engineering point of view if the site is used only as open
storage yard area which could tolerate fill ground settlement and continuous maintenance
of the finish ground profile to shed water away from future ponded area due to the
uneven ground settlements on the ground surface All future utility constructions should
be delayed until the majority of initial ground settlement has occurred Subsequent utility
lines should have very flexible connections preferably to be placed on the ground surface
for easy repair and future maintenance
Soft Ground Stabilization
It is our understanding that it is proposed to fill the western portion of the site up
to elevation 25 feet above MSL The new fill area will be used as a construction storage
yard facility
Our field exploration disclosed that the project site is underlain by a soft and highly
compressible deposit of organic clayey silt material down to a depth of approximately 30
feet below the existing ground surface
Based on our analyses settlements on the order of 30 inches of the underlying soft
deposit may occur under the proposed embankment fill load It is estimated that 90
percent of the primary settlement would occur in approximately four 4 years Due to the
organic nature of the material approximately 14 inches of long term settlements may
continue to occur after this 4 year period
It is our opinion that the proposed fill embankment area may be used as a storage
yard area provided the following recommendations are followed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2961 00 Page 6
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
A working platform or drainage blanket should be installed from one side of the
site where the ground surface is firmer prior to the filling of the area in order to stabilize
the soft ground condition encountered The stabilization layer should consist of a layer
of geotextile fabric placed at the bottom of the new fill with approximately 12 inches of
gravel on top
Fill Placement
Fill material to be used should generally consist of non expansive select granular
and silty material less than three 8 inches in maximum dimension with less than 25
percent passing the No 200 sieve and a Plasticity Index less than 20 The material
should be free of organic matter debris and other deleterious material The maiterial
should be tested and approved by the project geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to
the project site
All fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of its maximum dry density as determined
in accordance with the procedures described in ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Fill
placement should commence at the lowest point and continue upward in level compacted
lifts
in areas to receive fill where the existing ground surface is steeper than five
horizontal to one vertical 5H 1V the new fill should be keyed and benched into the
existing slope to aid in the proper bonding of the new fill into the in situ soils This is
graphically illustrated on Plate 3 Sketch of Keying and Benching
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2961 00 Page 7
Slopes of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter are recommended for the
fill slope Fill slopes should be constructed by over filling past the design slope and then
cutting back
We recommend that the new fill be placed at a very slow rate overlying the existing
weak crust and high compressible soils The rate of new fill placement should not exceed
one foot per day A two week pause should occur after every five 5 feet of new fill
material has been placed
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope planting should
be utilized to reduce erosion
It is anticipated that periodic minor filling may be required due to long term fill
ground settlements to maintain the desired finish grade of the embankment fill and to
provide surface water run off disposal due to ponding condition
It is Our opinion that the new fill embankment should only be used as a storage
yard facility as proposed for the subject project Construction of permanent structures
or installation of utility lines at the new fill embankment are not recommended due to the
long term settlement effects of the underlying compressible organic deposit which may
cause structural distress to the buildings and ruptures to the utility lines
Fill Settlement Monitoring
We recommend that a number of settlement gauges be installed on the existing
ground surface to monitor the fill settlements and to observe the rate of future
embankment fill settlements due to the consolidation of the underlying soft compressible
soils

Design Review
Preliminary and final drawings and specifications for the proposed construction
should be forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for review and written
comments prior to advertisement for bidding This review is needed to determine
adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot be considered to be responsible for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and construction of the fill
embankment This is to observe compliance with design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required
in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time of
preparation of this report The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
If actual exposed soil conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by excavating three 3 test
pits to depths of 13 5 to 20 0 feet below the existing ground surface drilling and sampling
of one 1 boring to a depth of 47 0 feet below the existing ground surface Two 2
additional borings were advanced by continuous penetration without sampling to depths
of 40 0 and 65 0 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of
the test pits and borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled
using truck mounted equipment
The material encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who continuously monitored the drilling
operations These classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing
in the laboratory All soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic and written presentation of the materials encountered
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings by using a thin walled tube
sampler Shelby Tube The sampler was hydraulically pushed The gauge pressure
required to advance the sampler is shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D
2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in
the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt vs the length of the core attempt e g if 3 7 feet of material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 and would be shown on
the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any drilling
induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core
run the RQD would be 50 and would be shown on the Boring Logs as RQD 50
Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality of the rock based on the
Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals
