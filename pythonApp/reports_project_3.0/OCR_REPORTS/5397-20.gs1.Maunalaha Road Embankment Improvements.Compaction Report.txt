Dear Mr Higuchi
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing
services performed during guardrail and wall posts installation and site grading for the
Maunalaha Road Embankment Improvement project from July 8 to in
accordance with our fee proposals dated May 7 2007 and August 12 2010
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located along Maunalaha Road off of Round Top Drive on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The project includes reconstruction of the existing roadway
adjacent to a large slope area The roadway reconstruction consists of the installation
of guardrail and wall posts reinforced fill plastic lumber facing and new roadway
pavement The site grading consists of cuts and fills up to about 3 5 feet in height A
Project Location Map and Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2 respectively
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the guardrail and wall posts installation and site grading at the project site
GUARDRAIL AND WALL POSTS INSTALLATION AND SITE GRADING
We observed the guardrail and wall posts installation and site grading work for the
Maunalaha Road Embankment Improvement project from July 8 to A
total of 31 guardrail posts consisting of W6x15x8 and W8x28x18 beams and a total of
24 wall posts consisting of W6x15x8 and W6x15x16 were installed with a hydraulic
hammer or installed in 13 5 inch diameter augered holes A 12 inch diameter Sonotube
was used as temporary casing within the 13 5 inch diameter augered holes The void
between the augered hole and the post was filled with 500 to 1 000 pounds per square

The site grading work consisted of removal of the existing asphalt concrete and
excavation of about 3 5 feet below the existing grade and placement and compaction of
reinforced fills and CLSM on the order of up to about 3 5 feet thick to achieve the finished
grades for the project West Coast Construction performed the site grading work During
the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill
materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry
densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on
Plate 3
The existing subgrade materials were moisture conditioned to near the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 The compaction requirement of a
minimum 95 percent relative density in the specification was reduced to a minimum
90 percent relative density due to the depth of the subgrade material and the moist
conditions at the project site The reinforced fill materials were compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative density
During the site grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the
compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 3 of this report
These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to
be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of full time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading work for the above referenced
project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were
performed in general conformance with the project plans and specifications and
subsequent recommendations provided during construction
