Dear Mr. Fujiwara: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed 
during the foundation probing and grouting operations for Pier No. 2 for the Rehabilitation 
of Umauma Stream Bridge project between June 23, 2015 and June 25, 2015. 
Two new pier footings will be constructed for the project.  Pier No. 1 and Pier No. 2 
are located on the Hilo and Honokaa sides of the bridge, respectively.  This report is for 
the probing and grouting work for the Pier No. 2 footing. 
Because of potential subsurface cavities and/or voids within the underlying basalt 
formation, a probing and grouting program to locate and grout potential cavities beneath 
foundations was specified for the project. The probe hole locations were surveyed and 
marked in the field by the general contractor, Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company, 
Inc. (HDCC) prior to the probing operations. The probe holes were spaced at about 10-foot 
on center along the structure foundation. The total number of scheduled probe holes for 
Pier No. 2 was 14 probe holes. The approximate locations and identification of the 
scheduled probe holes for Pier No. 2 are shown on the Probe Hole Identification Plan-Pier 
No. 2, Plate 1. 
KSF, Inc. Page 2
W.O. 6820-20
July 13, 2015
The probing and grouting work for the project was performed after the excavation
for the Pier No. 2 foundation. The probing and grouting work was performed by HDCC.
The probe holes were drilled with a TE550 Hydraulic Percussion Drill using a 4.5-inch
diameter rock bit. The probe holes drilled were about 6 inches in diameter and extended
to about 10 to 13 feet below the existing ground surface, at least 10 feet below the bottom
of footing elevations. A summary of our foundation probe drilling and grouting observations
is provided on Plate 2. The field probing and grouting records are presented on Plates 3.1
through 3.14.
The materials encountered during the probe drilling operations generally consisted
of basalt rock formation. After completion of the probe drilling operations, the probe holes
were filled with 4,000 pounds per square inch cement grout mix. An Atlas Copco Unigrout
Flex D grouting platform was used for the grouting. Since the grout takes for the
scheduled probe holes were between 1.2 to 2 times the theoretical probe hole volume,
additional probe holes were not recommended.
In summary, the foundation probing and grouting operations for the project have
been conducted in general conformance with the intent of the project specifications.
Based on our observations, it is our professional opinion that the relatively small voids
encountered at the Pier No. 2 foundation location have been grouted and that the
subgrade area should provide the recommended foundation support for the Pier No. 2
foundation.
We appreciate the opportunity to be of service to you on this project. If you have
questions or need additional information, please contact our office.
