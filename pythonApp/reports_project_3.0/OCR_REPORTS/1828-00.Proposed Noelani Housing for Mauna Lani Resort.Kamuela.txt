SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site consists of a grassy strip of land about 60 feet
wide by 300 feet long located east of Opelo Road at Kamuela Hawaii
The existing parking lot for Noelani Housing Apartments is located along
the southern side of the site
The ground surface generally slopes gently down to the southwest
with ground elevations varying from about 2 635 to about 2 640 feet
above MSL

PROJECT CONSTDERATTIONS
The proposed development will consist of two 3 story wooden framed
buildings 90 by 40 feet and 110 by 40 feet in plan dimensions
The design of the sewage disposal system is not finalized at this
time but it is our opinion that a combination of a septic tank and
leaching field effluent disposal system could be utilized
A preliminary grading plan was not available at the time of our
investigation However little or no grading is contemplated at the
Site
SUBSURFACE EXPLORATION
A total of nine 9 borings were drilled at the project site
utilizing a truck mounted rotary hydraulic drill rig advancing 4 inch
diameter continuous flight augers or a NXM core barrel Soil and core
samples were obtained from each boring for visual classification and
laboratory testing
Subsurface Conditions
The borings generally encountered a surface soil layer from 4 0 to
19 0 feet thick consisting of medium dense silty gravel This material
is derived from in situ weathering of volcanic ash and clinker
Below this surface layer were volcanic formations to the maximum
depth drilled The volcanic formations appeared to consist generally of
hard gray aa basalt interbedded with medium dense coarse gravel
clinker zones
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings down to a
maximum of 45 0 feet at the time of our exploration

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATTONS
General
The field exploration disclosed basically competent foundation
bearing soils near the ground surface at the proposed project site From
a soil engineering point of view the site is suitable for the proposed
residential development
Shallow foundations may be used for the proposed structures
The near surface soils exhibit low swelling potential therefore
slab on grade construction may also be utilized
Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundations are recommended for the
structures on level or gently sloping areas
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f is recommended for
footings bearing on the stiff to hard in situ gravelly silt soils or on
compacted structural fill The above bearing value is for total dead
plus live loads and may be increased by one third when temporary wind and
seismic loads are considered
A minimum footing width of 18 inches should be utilized
The in situ soils are very silty and susceptible to erosion
therefore the bottom of all spread footings should be placed a minimm
of 24 inches below the finished grade In addition the outside face of
the footing should be set back a minimm of 6 feet from the nearest
outside slope face This set back may require deepening of the footing
embedment to maintain the above specified minimum set back

The post footings should be cast in place and the concrete should be
poured neat against the excavation to eliminate the need for backfill
around the footing blocks
Surface water should be diverted away from the building foundations
Anticipated settlements are expected to be relatively small on the
structures
Lateral Resistance
To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of 0 4 between
the foundations and the underlying soil is recommended
In addition a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f can also be
used where there is sufficient soil in front of the footing to develop
the full passive resistance
Site Grading
Little or no grading is anticipated at the site
The site should be cleared of vegetation and debris prior to
commencement of earthwork
Based on our previous report there is still a possibility that one
or more old cesspools may exist at the site These cesspools should
they be encountered should be exposed any sludge remaining should be
cleaned out and backfilled with compacted granular material at 90 of its
maximim dry density as determined by ASTM D 1557

Prior to placement of structural fill the existing ground surface
should be moisture conditioned scarified and recompacted by a heavy
vibratory roller to obtain a minimum of 95 of its maximm dry density as
determined by ASTM D 1557 This will serve to densify the loose or soft
surface silts
Fill and backfill should be placed in maximm 10 inch loose
horizontal lifts compacted to cbtain at least 95 of its maximum dry
density as determined by ASTM D 1557
Slab On Grade
Slabs should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of gravel
generally less than 3 4 inch and larger than 1 4 inch in size A
ployethylene moisture barrier should be considered between this cushion
and the slab
The laboratory soil tests indicate that the on site soils generally
have low swelling potential Slab on grade should have perimeter
footings extending down a minimum of 12 inches below the finish grade to
reduce ground movement due to drying and wetting below the slab
The slab subgrade should be kept moist and protected from drying
prior to concrete placement The subgrade should be compacted to a
minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557

PERCOLATION TESTING
The use of a septic tank and leaching field disposal system appears
feasible at the proposed multi family housing development
Five 5 4 inch diameter probings were drilled for the purpose of
percolation testing to evaluate the suitability of the underlying soil
and rock formations for a septic tank and leaching field disposal system
These probings encountered approximately 4 to 19 feet of brown silty
gravel which was underlain by a layer of hard massive basalt between 8
and 13 feet in thickness Below this ledge of massive basalt were
interbedded layers of clinker and massive basalt which forms the highly
pervious zone for effluent disposal
To further evaluate the soil rock permeabilities of the underlying
site four 4 falling head percolation tests and three 3 constant head
water injection tests were performed in the drilled probings The test
data are presented in Appendix C
Based on our falling head test results at Probing 3 and
Boring 4 it appears that the percolation rates for the upper silty
gravel and basalt layers which are up to 27 0 feet thick are low
For the thin clinker layer at about 27 0 to 30 5 foot depth our
test results indicated a marginal percolation rate of 18 G P M A water
level build up of 17 8 feet was observed in the drilled hole at this
location

However our water injection constant head test results in Probing
Nos 4 and 5 for the lower clinker layer 37 0 to 45 0 feet below the
existing ground surface indicated relatively high percolation rates
Inflow rates of about 50 to 120 G P M were utilized and the tests were
run for a minimum of one hour in each probing The injected water
dissipated without an appreciable head build up
Based on our test results data from our previous report and
capacity of the installed cesspools at the existing Noelani Apartments
it is our opinion that a cesspool system would be the most effective
method for disposal of sewage effluent However current law requires a
maximum effluent injection rate of 800 gallons per day for each
individual cesspool making this method impractible
An injection well disposal system is a second possibility although
treated effluent would be required for injection However this disposal
method is not permitted at the site
In light of the above a third alternative is a combination of a
near surface drainage leaching field with an extension into the lower
clinker layer encountered at about 37 0 to 45 0 foot depth A
preliminary conceptual design for this modified leaching field is shown
on Plate 3
It should be pointed out however that the loss of infiltrative
capacity and soil clogging can occur in the future by combined physical
chemical and biological factors The latter is the most important factor
since an organic mat of several millimeters thick may form at the
soil liquid interface Other factors such as rainfall rise in
groundwater level etc may also affect the permeability to some
extent Therefore the leaching field will probably require periodic
maintenance
