Dear Mr Moore
In accordance with your request we have been providing geotechnical engineering
services during the construction of the parking lot in support of the Kings Shops Expansion
project in Waikoloa South Kohala Hawaii This report summarizes our geotechnical
engineering observation and consultation services performed during construction of the
parking lot for the period between April 2000 through June 2000 In general our services
were coordinated and requested through your office
Project Considerations
The project site is located within the Waikoloa Beach Resort in the District of South
Kohala on the Island of Hawaii The Kings Shops Expansion consisted of the construction
of two new commercial retail buildings of approximately 18 000 square feet in the area of the
existing parking lot In addition a new parking lot across the street from the Kings Shops
was constructed to accommodate the loss in parking space due to the new construction
Construction of the new parking lot had to take place prior to the commencement of the
Kings Shops Expansion project In general the site grading for the parking lot project
consisted of minor cuts and fills on the order of about 2 feet or less The sitework contractor
for the construction of the parking lot across from the Kings Shops was Jas W Glover Ltd
Site Preparation
In general the earthwork at the site consisted of clearing subgrade preparation
placement and compaction of fills and construction of the pavement section After clearing
the fill areas and finished subgrades in cut areas were generally proof rolled with a large
vibratory drum roller In general lava tubes detected during the site preparation were
collapsed and the loose materials from within the excavation were removed The
excavations were subsequently backfilled with compacted fill materials consisting of 6 inch
minus granular materials

During construction of the project samples of imported materials utilized as fill and
backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry
densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are
summarized on Plate 2 of this report
The fill and backfill materials were generally moisture conditioned to above optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density
as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 In areas outside the parking
lot such as the sidewalks the fill and backfill materials were compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction
During the grading and utility trench backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge
Method and D 3017 Results of the compaction tests performed for the project are
summarized on Plates 3 1 and 3 2 of this report These tests were performed on the
prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations
and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These
locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were generally reworked until the
specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative
based on visual observation
Fill materials consisting of 6 inch minus structural fills were also placed in the fill
areas of the project Due to the nature of the larger sized fill materials the compaction of
the 6 inch minus materials were evaluated based on field observations during the fill
placement During the fill and backfill operations the materials were generally placed in
near level lifts brought to near the optimum moisture and compacted to obtain the
specified degree of compaction based on visual observations
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill placement
subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil
conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives it is our professional opinion
that the earthwork operations for the parking lot at the Kings Shop Expansion project
observed by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in general
conformance with the intent of the plans and specifications
Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities occurring
simultaneously on a construction site In addition because our services were on an
intermittent basis some of the earthwork was performed without our observation Our
services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical
