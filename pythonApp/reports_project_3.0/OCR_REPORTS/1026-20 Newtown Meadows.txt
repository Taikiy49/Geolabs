report summarizes our observations and compaction tests performed at the

The grading operations at the site consisted of cutting the eastern
high knoll to as much as 35 feet below the original grade and filling the
central and western areas up to 25 feet above original grade In general
the expansive Adobe clay on top of the sloping knoll area was removed
and placed in the deeper fill areas a minimm of 3 feet below finished
grade During the fill placement operations periodic field density tests
were done in accordance with the American Society for Testing and
Materials ASTM Test Designation D 1556 Sand Cone Method or Test

Water Wells and Tunnels
A total of twenty four 24 wells in the project site were sealed
with sand and cement under the supervision of the Board of Water Supply
The water tunnels which had been constructed generally along the
alignment of the wells were backfilled chiefly with imported bluish gray
crushed basalt aggregate sand or 3B Fine gravel The fill material was
placed into the water tunnels through openings drilled or dug on top of
the water tunnels
The water tunnels on the southern portion of the project site were
backfilled through drilled holes of 1 5 to 2 5 feet in diameter and spaced
about 15 feet apart The water tunnels on the northwestem portion of the
project site were backfilled through the openings dug by a clamshell
Prior to filling a portion of a water tunnel with concrete sand through an
opening on top a pump was set up for dewatering at the entrance of the
tunnel When the filling of the tunnel portion began another pump was
used to jet the fill material aggregate sand inside the tunnel The
purpose of this was to spread and densify the fill material so that the
chance of forming large cavities inside the tunnel was reduced The
operation continued until the portion of the water tunnel was filled to
the top of the opening

Pump House
The wooden superstructure of the pump house was demolished with a
backhoe and a dozer The broken wood pieces and the rubbish were hauled
out of the site The deep 30 feet concrete structure was backfilled
with granular material Prior to filling the bottom of the structure was
cleaned out of loose or soft material and a 6 inch pump was set up for
dewatering The granular fill material which included on site cobbles and
boulders mixed with imported 3B Fine gravel or select borrow was placed
in the fill area by a loader After cutting a ramp on the northern side
of the concrete structure a track mounted backhoe went down to the bottom
to spread and compact each lift 2 to 3 feet of granular fill up to about
2 feet above the water level mark about 15 feet below the ground at that
time The remainder of the structure was backfilled with compacted
on site material
Slope Behind Building No 6
Initially the slope was covered by heavy vegetation and had no signs
of instability
In the process of grading the pads for Building No 6 the toe of the
slope was grubbed and cut back exposing loose gravel cobbles and
boulders on the grubbed surface It appeared that the loose material
extended up the slope to the old railroad track bench The grading plans
indicated that the additional cuts into the hillside would be required to
provide a level area behind the units These cuts could have created a
potential instability of the loose boulders Therefore a backhoe was
used to remove most of the loose boulders on the slope and a low retaining
wall about 238 feet long was constructed behind Building No 6 to retain
the lower portion of the slope and provide a catchment area for loose
rocks

Boulder Protection
A limited boulder sweep was conducted along the hillside adjacent to
the project An engineering geologist from our office made a
reconnaissance of the high valley side slope to identify loose boulders
which could roll down the hillside and these loose boulders were removed
by the contractor In addition to the boulder sweep a 4 feet high
boulder protection fence was erected along the toe of the southern
hillside slope to restrain smaller rolling boulders and slow down larger
boulders to reduce possible damage to the units
It should be understood that whatever methods are used an inherent
risk of falling boulders could still exist for these hillside units
Therefore it should be emphasized that no guarantee against future
falling boulders can be made due to changes in soil and rock conditions
with time weathering erosion earthquake construction vibration
improvements and construction activities at and around the site
RECOMMENDATIONS
House Foundations
The following general foundation guidelines are based on visual
observations expansion and CBR tests performed on the surface soils
1 Footings located on or near general slopes should penetrate down
a sufficient depth in order to develop a minimm horizontal
set back distance of 5 feet measured from the outer edge of the
footing foundation out to the slope face
For the lots situated next to the fill slope adjacent to Waimalu
Stream foundations should be extended down to provide a minimm
horizontal set back distance of 10 feet from the slope face to
the outer edge of footing

dk Tse
Individual footings on level areas should be embedded a minimm
of 12 inches below the ground surface All footing backfill
should be properly compacted to obtain 90 compaction and graded
to drain water away from the footing
We recommend that a soils engineer be retained to observe the
bottom of all footing excavations prior to concrete placement
2 An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for
foundations bearing on compacted fill or stiff natural soil
3 Slab on grade construction should be set back at least 5 feet
from the tops of slopes
Twelve inch 12 deep perimeter footings below the lowest
adjacent grade are recommended to act as a cut off wall against
future subgrade moisture changes due to drying and wetting around
the slab
4 The subgrade soils below the slabs should be maintained in a
moist condition from the initial foundation excavation up to the
time of concrete placement
Site Regrading
Subsequent to completion of lot grading utility trenches within the
lot pad should be properly backfilled and compacted under the observation
of a soils engineer
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to
slopes or pads on the subject lots subsequent to the issuance of this
report without our knowledge and written approval
