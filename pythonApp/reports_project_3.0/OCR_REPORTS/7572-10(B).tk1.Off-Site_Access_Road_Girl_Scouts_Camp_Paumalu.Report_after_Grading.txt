Dear Mr. Doo: 
This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed 
during the site grading operations for the Camp Paumalu Access Road 1 project from 
April 23 through July 12, 2018. 
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
The project generally consists of improvements to the existing on-site access road 
to the Girl Scouts of Hawaii – Camp Paumalu in the Haleiwa area on the Island of Oahu, 
Hawaii.  Camp Paumalu is an approximate 135.5-acre site, situated roughly 2 miles to the 
southeast of Sunset Beach, and is accessed via Comsat Road off Kamehameha Highway. 
We understand it is desired to provide improvements to approximately 650 linear 
feet (Sta. 0+00  to Sta. 6+50) of the existing on-site Access Road 1 to meet the current 
Honolulu Fire Department (HFD) design requirements and to stabilize portions of the 
access road that are distressed. Roadway improvements will generally consist of widening 
the roadway and providing an engineered or Portland Cement Concrete surface. In 
addition, we understand the cut slopes will be stabilized utilizing erosion control straw 
matting with Platipus anchors.  A layout of the roadway improvements is shown on the Site 
Plan, Plate 1.

Jayar Construction Inc. performed the site grading operations during the roadway 
improvement project. The site grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing, subgrade 
preparation, placement of geotextile fabric and geogrid, placement and compaction of fill 
materials, and pavement construction. 
Following the clearing and grubbing operations, the existing ground was 
over-excavated, where necessary, to the pavement subgrade level.  The over-excavated 
subgrades were scarified to a depth of about 8 inches, moisture-conditioned to at least 
2 percent above the optimum moisture content, and recompacted to a minimum of 
90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 
(Modified Proctor). 
A layer of geogrid (Tensar BX-1200) over a layer of geotextile fabric (Mirafi 180N) 
were then placed over the prepared subgrade prior to the placement of 6 inches of 
Reclaimed Asphalt Pavement (RAP), moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture 
content, and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction.  
During the site grading operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing. 
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our 
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test 
results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report. 
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an 
as-need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures 
and soil conditions were noted. A total of 11 field density tests were performed using the 
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D6938. Results of the compaction tests 
performed are summarized on Plate 3 of this report. These tests were performed on the 
prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site. Test locations 
and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes. 
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied 
by the methods used. Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified 
minimum compaction was obtained. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading for 
the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report 
were performed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and 
specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the 
referenced report, dated February 23, 2018, and subsequent recommendations.
