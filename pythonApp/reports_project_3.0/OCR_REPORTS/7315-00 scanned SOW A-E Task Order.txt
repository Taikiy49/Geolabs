5. Inadvertent Discovery Requirement: The A-E shall take precautions to preserve
all historical, archeological and cultural resources encountered during the project,
as they existed at the time they are identified by the Contracting Officer or his
designated representative. If previously unidentified or unanticipated resources
are inadvertently discovered during construction activities such as excavation,
testing or boring, all such activities shall immediately be temporarily suspended.
These cultural resources may be prehistoric or historic; surface or subsurface; and
include but are not limited to human skeletal remains or burials; artifacts; shell,
midden, bone, charcoal, soil or other deposits; rocks or coral alignments, paving,
wall, or other constructed features; and any indication of agricultural or other uses.
Upon such discovery or find, the A-E shall isolate and provide an appropriate
buffer zone of at least 3 feet around the material and immediately notify the
Contracting Officer or the Contracting Officer’s Representative (COR). When so
notified, the Contracting Officer and/or the COR will contact the DPW
Archaeologist, Mr. Richard Davis, 655-9709, richard.d.davis154.civ@mail.mil at
Bldg 494, Schofield Barracks to determine if archaeological, historical or cultural
resources are present and what procedures will need to be implemented before
construction activities can proceed again.
