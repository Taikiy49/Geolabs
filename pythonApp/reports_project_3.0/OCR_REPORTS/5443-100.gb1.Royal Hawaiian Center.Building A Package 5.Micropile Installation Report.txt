MICROPILE INSTALLATION AND TESTING
Geotechnical recommendations for the micropile foundations were presented in
our letter report dated as mentioned above The purpose of our
geotechnical engineering observation and consultation services was to observe the
micropile foundation installation and testing for general conformance with our
recommendations
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions at the site the micropiles were
specified to extend through the loose lagoonal deposits and derive load bearing support
from the medium dense to dense coralline deposits encountered at greater depths
Kelikai Inc installed a total of four production micropiles from August 1 2017
through August 4 2017 at the approximate locations shown on the Micropile Numbering
Plan Plate 2 Details of the micropile installation are presented in the Micropile
Installation Records in Appendix A
In general the micropiles were installed using a TD 75 drill rig equipped with a
to the specified depth of the micropile to create a drilled hole of at least 5 5 inches
Advancing steel casing to the bottom of the specified depth was required in order to
facilitate the micropile installation
After the drilled holes were extended to the specified depth and cleaned out a
reinforcing bar was placed in the cased hole with centralizers Grout was batched on
site utilizing a colloidal high shear and high speed mixer equipped
with a grout pump The grout mix consisted of one sack of 94 pound Portland Cement
Type I and or II 87 pounds of masonry sand and 5 gallons of water

Micropile Testing
In order to evaluate the load carrying capacities of the installed micropiles two
proof tests pullout tests were conducted on two production micropile The proof tests
were conducted on and 3 on August 9 and August 11 2017
respectively The micropiles were loaded to a maximum test load of 80 kips
133 percent of the design load of 60 kips in approximately 15 kip load increments The
maximum test load of 80 kips was held for 10 minutes to evaluate the creep effects
Subsequently the micropile was unloaded to zero load in about three equal load
decrements
In general the production micropiles proof tested met the acceptance criteria
specified in the project requirements and our letter report dated
At the maximum test load the creep movements during the 10 minute holding
period for and 3 were about 0 003 and 0 009 inches respectively
which is less than 0 04 inches typically allowed for the ground anchor system At the
maximum test load the vertical displacements of and 3 were about
0 202 and 0 264 inches respectively The results of the proof testing are presented in
Appendix B
CONCLUSIONS
Based on our field observations of the micropile installation and micropile proof
tests performed by the subcontractor it is our professional opinion that the specified
micropiles for Project Package 5 have been installed to provide the design load with
tolerable movements
