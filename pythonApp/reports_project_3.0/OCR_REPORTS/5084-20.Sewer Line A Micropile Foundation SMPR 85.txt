MICROPILE FOUNDATION FOR SEWER LINE A
Based on the results of the foundation alternative study the use of a concrete
beam supported on micropile foundation was selected for Sewer Line A Micropile
foundation recommendations are provided below
Micropile Foundation
Based on the soft subsurface conditions encountered at the project site a deep
foundation system was recommended to provide support of the new sewer line and
manhole structure We believe that a deep foundation system consisting of micropiles
may be used In general a micropile foundation system consists of a small diameter
usually less than 12 inches drilled and grouted pile with steel reinforcing The micropile
foundation typically is constructed by drilling a borehole placing steel casing and
reinforcing steel in the hole and grouting the borehole Micropiles are desirable because
they can be installed readily in access restrictive environments and in numerous soil types
and ground conditions In addition installation of the micropiles generally causes minimal
disturbance to adjacent structures the adjacent soils and the environment
The new sewer line will be supported on a continuous reinforced concrete beam on
a micropile foundation The reinforced concrete beam will be 2 75 feet wide and about
1 75 feet in thickness We believe that a micropile system with a minimum grout bulb

for each of the new manhole structures The micropiles should be installed near each
corner of the manhole base

Micropile Foundation Settlements
Settlements of the micropile foundations will result primarily from elastic
compression of the pile member and subgrade response We estimate the total
settlement of the pile supported foundations to be less than 1 inch with differential
settlements between the micropiles not exceeding about one half of the total settlement
We believe that these settlements are essentially elastic and should occur as the loads are
applied
Construction Considerations
Installation of micropiles should be performed by a specialty subcontractor
experienced in the construction of a micropile foundation system Due to the specialized
nature of the micropile foundation construction observation of the micropile foundation
installation system and testing of the micropiles should be designated a Special
Inspection item Therefore observation of the micropile installation operations by
Geolabs Special Inspector is necessary to confirm our design assumptions and should
be designated a Special Inspection item in accordance with Section 1701 of the Uniform
Building Code 1997
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this letter report are based upon
visual observations made during our field exploration and laboratory testing of the soil
samples recovered Variations of subsurface conditions between and beyond the
sampling points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident Geolabs Inc
should be contacted to re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report
