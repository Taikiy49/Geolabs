a single point in time site visit

assist in identifying and to help in reducing sources of additional water infiltrating into the

the storm drains have the potential to scour and create voids where significant line offsets

notified of the active earth movement condition and the need to promptly detect and
correct possible continued utility damage as a result of the on going earth movements

additional infiltrating water CCTV video survey of existing drain and wastewater lines in
the Kuahea Street and Waiomao Road vicinity should be undertaken periodically in an
effort to detect possible line breaks and the undesired discharges that may contribute to
the active earth movements

Kipona Place
Based on our observations the existing roadway and upslope conditions along
Kipona Place at the toe of the Waiomao Landslide appear to be generally static
with minimal visible signs of overt ground distress and deformation
However a localized earth slump previously reported at the slope toe abutting

continue very slowly creeping toward the roadway The earth slump has developed
on a small portion of the graded slope upslope from Kipona Place The overall
graded slope appears to be generally stable and in a static condition with the
exception of the localized mass of soil comprising the creeping slump
Based on our observations the slump toe remains generally static to very slow
moving at the edge of the pavement We observed a slight encroachment of soil
debris at the edge of the pavement consisting of an 8 to 10 foot length of grass
and soil debris extending parallel along the edge of the pavement and reaching
about 4 to 6 inches onto the edge of the pavement In addition we observed
several loose surface boulders on the two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V slope
adjacent to the roadway The rocks have some potential to slide onto the road and
should be removed from the ground surface
Because the slump appears to have remained active over the years the slump
and associated creeping soil mass appear to have some potential for additional
future movement and possible encroachment onto the roadway Furthermore we
observed a broad area of topographic depression higher on the slope surface
above the embankment where the slump is located The topographic depression
may allow some ponding of water to occur Therefore some remedial slope
stabilization efforts generally consisting of slope drainage improvements and
surface grading are recommended to reduce the potential for a larger release of
earth material to affect the roadway below Future earth movements slumps
should be anticipated to occur following large magnitude rainfall events
