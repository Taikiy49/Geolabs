Jet-Grouted Column Installation Observation Report 
Ala Wai Canal Emergency Wall Repairs 
Honolulu, Oahu, Hawaii 
Dear Mr. Luke: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations 
performed during the jet-grouted column installation for the Ala Wai Canal Emergency 
Wall Repairs project in the Waikiki area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  The 
project site is located along the south bank of the Ala Wai Canal between McCully Street 
and Kalakaua Avenue. 
This 
letter 
report presents 
our 
field 
observations and 
findings 
and 
recommendations for the jet-grouted columns for the project. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
Based on the initial project drawings, the existing distress canal Concrete Rubble 
Masonry (CRM) wall will be supported on a total of 22 jet-grouted columns.  The 
jet-grouted columns had a minimum diameter of 3.5 feet and spaced at about 7 feet on 
centers.  During construction, it was uncovered that the existing CRM wall actually 
consisted of loose basalt cobbles and boulders with a plaster facing and top.  Probing 
and grouting near the wall face and patching voids at the bottom of the existing wall were 
performed for the remediation of the existing CRM wall prior to production jet-grouted 
column installation.  The probes extended about 8 feet from below the top of the existing 
wall level.  Details of the probing and grouting performed are presented in our report 
entitled “Probing and Grouting Observation Report” dated March 1, 2021.

As part of the project requirements, one sacrificial jet-grouted test column was 
installed adjacent to the Ala Wai Canal wall at about Sta. 19+75. The 3.5-foot diameter 
test column was installed by FV Coluccio Construction Company, Inc. on April 8, 2020 to

After 28 days from installation, the test column was continuously cored to a depth of

test column was accepted and approved. 
PRODUCTION JET-GROUTED COLUMN INSTALLATION 
The production jet-grouted column installation program consisted of installing 
22 jet-grouted columns.  The jet-grouted column locations and naming designation are 
presented on the Jet Grouted Column Location Plan, Plate 1. The jet-grouted column 
locations on Plate 1 were measured and provided by the contractor.  The production 
jet-grouted column installation was conducted from March 1 to 18, 2021 under the 
near-continuous (full-time) observations by a field representative from our office. FV 
Coluccio Construction Company, Inc. used a Soilmec SM-103 hydraulic mini drill to install 
the jet-grouted columns. A Soilmec high pressure pump was used to supply the grout 
mixed by a containerized GM-22 mixing plant to the drill rig and through the rods and 
injection port near the bottom of the column.   
During the installation of the initial production jet-grouted columns, the subcontractor 
indicated that he was not getting grout return and believed that the columns were not forming 
to the size and strength in the project documents.  The subcontractor then tried reducing the 
rotation speed and using a thicker mix.  Due to the installation problems the subcontractor 
was encountering, the spacing of the jet-grouted columns was increased from 7 to 10 feet 
on centers for a total of 16 columns.  In addition, six jet-grouted columns were installed 
between the columns within the wall area where the most distress was noted.  The total 
jet-grouted columns were 22.    
In general, the 22 jet-grouted columns were installed no greater than 6 inches away 
from the toe of the existing canal wall.  Two 12-foot long casings consisting of an exterior 
steel casing and an interior PVC casing were installed below the mudline of the canal prior 
to drilling to ensure no leakage of soil cuttings and grout into the canal. The larger steel 
casing was first set into the mudline using an excavator. The smaller PVC casing was set 
inside the steel casing and embedded into the mudline. Therefore, the grout return was 
observed from the smaller PVC casing. A cantilevered working platform constructed by 
Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company was used so that the jet grouting rig could 
access the jet-grouted column locations. The jet grout rig drilled inside the PVC casing to 
install the jet-grouted columns. Ten jet-grouted columns were redrilled and regrouted due 
to the limited return of clean grout observed within the PVC casing to the surface during 
the initial installation.

During the jet-grouted column installation operation, logs were maintained for each 
jet-grouted column, which included information pertaining to column number, date 
installed, depth of installation, grout flow rate, rod withdrawal rate, etc.  The records of the 
jet-grouted columns installed are presented on Plates 3.1 through 3.15. 
Continuous coring of production jet-grouted columns was conducted by the 
subcontractor at two production column locations using a drill rig equipped with a 
triple-tube core barrel on March 22 and 23, 2021.  The coring was performed from the 
drilling platform located about 6 feet above the top of the existing wall elevation. 
Therefore, the bottom of the probing and grouting zone is about 14 feet from the drilling 
platform. Core recovery ranged from 27 to 100 percent and 83 to 100 percent for Columns 
C-12 and C-20, respectively.  The core recovery just above the bottom of the existing 
stabilized wall ranged from 83 to 100 percent for the two columns. The results of the coring 
indicate that the jet-grouted columns were constructed below the existing stabilized wall and 
extended above the bottom of the existing stabilized wall. Photographs of the recovered 
core samples are presented on Plates 4.1 through 4.5.  Unconfined compression tests were 
conducted on the core samples retrieved from each column and are presented on Plate 2 
of this letter report.  The unconfined compression strengths ranged from 600 to 1,120 psi 
and 810 to 1,500 psi for Columns C-12 and C-20, respectively.  The compression tests were 
performed at 13 days and 14 days after coring for Columns C-12 and C-20, respectively. 
The probing and grouting performed for the existing wall increased the actual base 
width of the wall and therefore, reduced the bearing pressure compared to the existing 
conditions prior to construction. During the initial production jet-grouted column installation, 
grout was noted within the steel exterior casing during removal.  We understand that grout 
was noted during all the steel exterior casing removal. This may be a possible reason why 
grout was not observed coming out of the interior PVC casing during column installation and 
collecting within the exterior steel casing. 
Based on our observation of the installation operations and subsurface conditions 
encountered and the above, it is our professional opinion that the production jet-grouted 
columns have been installed to sufficient depths and sizes to provide adequate support 
for the existing canal wall as intended in the design of the Ala Wai Canal Emergency Wall 
Repairs project. 
LIMITATIONS 
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities occurring 
simultaneously on a construction site even when our representatives are on the project site 
on a full-time basis. Our observation and consultation services were performed in

repairing defects regardless of when they are found.
