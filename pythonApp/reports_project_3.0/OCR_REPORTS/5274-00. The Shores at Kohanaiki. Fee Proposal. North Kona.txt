Project Considerations
The Shores at Kohanaiki project site is located adjacent to the existing Kaloko
Honokohau National Historic Park in the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii
The project site generally is located on the makai side of Mamalahoa Trail We
understand that it is desired to develop a new resort subdivision at the project site The
project site encompasses an area of over 400 acres We understand that a total of
500 single and multi family residential units will be constructed on 351 lots at the project
site In addition an 18 hole golf course a beach club roadways lined lakes and other
infrastructure will be constructed for the future residential lots at the project site
We wish to point out that Geolabs has been extensively involved in the various
resort and residential developments along the Kohala Coast Some of the projects we
have provided and currently are providing geotechnical engineering design and
construction observation services include the following
Hualalai Resort 625 acre resort development
Kukio Bay Beach Club 675 acre resort development
Kolea at Waikoloa Beach Resort residential subdivision

Based on our extensive experience along the Kohala Coast we anticipate that the
project site may be underlain by a a basalt and basalt rock formation near the ground
surface It should be noted that lava tubes and cavities are commonly encountered in
basalt rock formation near the proposed project site
We understand that the project plans have been submitted to the County of Hawaii
without a geotechnical engineering report Based on our cursory review of the preliminary
grading plans provided we believe that preparation of a geotechnical engineering report
will be required for the proposed resort subdivision project to address the following items
e Cut Slopes We understand that cut slope inclinations of up to about one half
horizontal to one vertical is desired in many areas of the proposed project
site The project site conditions should be evaluated for the use of relatively steep
slopes In addition case by case evaluation of the cut slopes would be required
during construction of the proposed project
e Fill Slopes Fill slope inclinations ranging from 4H 1V to 2H 1V is shown on the
preliminary grading plans Recommendations pertaining to fill slopes will be
provided in our geotechnical engineering report based on the anticipated fill
materials to be used for the proposed project
e Mass Grading We envision that mass grading consisting of cuts and fills of up to
about 15 to 20 feet will be required to achieve design grades Due to the extensive
size of the project site we believe that mass grading will be a significant element of
overall project design Furthermore based on our extensive experience in the
project area presence of lava tubes and cavities should be anticipated at the
project site Our geotechnical engineering report will address items of mass
grading such as site preparation fill materials including the use of boulder fills
and compaction requirements
e Retaining Structures We understand that CRM retaining walls of up to 10 feet in
height with a 2H 1V sloping backfill is planned at the project site Because the
project site is located on the seismically active Island of Hawaii Seismic Zone 4 in
accordance with the 1997 Uniform Building Code forces due to dynamic lateral
earth pressures will need to be considered in the design of retaining structures
Our geotechnical engineering report will provide recommended parameters
required for the design of retaining structures including limitations on sloping
backfills behind the retaining structures
e Pavement Design The preliminary plans require input of a geotechnical engineer
for the pavement sections Therefore recommended pavement sections will be
provided in our geotechnical engineering report based on the anticipated subgrade
conditions

Storm Water Disposal System Drywells and seepage pits are commonly used to
dispose storm water run off on the Island of Hawaii Therefore performance of
percolation testing will be required to evaluate the subsurface permeability
characteristic of the underlying materials
Due to the various considerations discussed above we recommend that a
geotechnical engineering report be prepared for the proposed resort subdivision project
We believe that a limited field exploration program consisting of drilling and sampling
borings will be required for the proposed project Based on the existing conditions at the
project site we envision that mechanized trail clearing will be necessary to provide access
to our truck mounted drill equipment
As mentioned previously we envision that numerous drywells will be constructed at
the project site to dispose of storm water runoff The construction of drywells generally
would require procurement of an Underground Injection Control UIC permit from the
Department of Health DOH In general the initial UIC Permit to Construct drywells
should be obtained by the project Civil Engineer The subsequent UIC Permit to Operate
drywells would require preparation of a summary report for the constructed drwells The
summary report generally requires lithologic logs and injection testing of constructed
drywells Therefore this fee proposal also covers work required for the preparation of UIC
Permit to Operate drywells at the project site However work associated with obtaining
the initial UIC Permit to Construct drywells is not included in this fee proposal
Due to the preliminary nature of the project details regarding the design and

prepared Because of the uncertainty regarding the duration of the construction period a
monthly budget was estimated for this fee proposal

A Geotechnical Engineering Exploration
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration is to obtain an overview of
the surface and subsurface conditions at the site The subsurface information obtained
would be utilized to develop a soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of the proposed project To
accomplish this we propose an exploration program consisting of the following tasks and
work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a bulldozer and an operator to the project
site to conduct trail clearing
2 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig water truck and
two operators to the project site and back mo
3 Drilling and sampling of about 10 borings extending to depths of about 10 to
20 feet below the ground surface for a total of about 200 lineal feet of field
exploration
4 Performance of four percolation tests in selected borings to evaluate the
subsurface permeability characteristics for the design of storm water
disposal systems
5 Coordination of the trail clearing field exploration and logging of the borings
by an engineer geologist from our firm
6 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
as an aid in classifying the materials encountered and evaluating their
engineering properties
7 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of cut and fill slopes
retaining structures mass grading and pavements for the proposed project
8 Preparation of a formal report 8 copies summarizing our work on the
project and presenting our findings and recommendations
9 Coordination of our overall work on the project by a project engineer from
our firm
10 Quality assurance of our work and clienvdesign team consultation by a
principal engineer from our firm
11 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
