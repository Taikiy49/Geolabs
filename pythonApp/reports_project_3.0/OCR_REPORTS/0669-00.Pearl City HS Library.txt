INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to define and evaluate
the subsurface condition encountered at the project site and
to provide recommendations for the design and construction of
the proposed Pearl City High School Library
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed Pearl City High School Library is located at the
Pearl City High School complex in the Waimano Uka area of Upper
Pearl City
Pearl City High School Library 2
The general surrounding topography slopes from north to south
The proposed project is mainly situated on a previously cut
pad The existing pad is level and it is located at the bottom
of two 4 1 horizontal to vertical slopes The eastern slope
is 11 feet high and faces the Music Building The existing Food
Service Building is situated up on top of the 9 foot high north
slope
Loose fill has recently been placed on the west end of the pro
ject area along its 4 foot high slope This loose fill is appro
ximately 40 feet wide and 180 feet long varying from 3 to 7 feet
thick The uncontrolled fill material was from a project just
west of the proposed library site It was placed without proper
compaction and control The limits of the loose fill on the pro
ject area are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed library is a one story building approximately 118
feet by 126 feet in plan dimension with metal roof and CMU ex
terior wall construction Anticipated column loads will be 90
kips and wall loads will be 1 5 kips per foot The planned floor
elevation is 420 0
Site grading will require 8 to 19 feet of embankment fill to
raise the existing site grade to the elevation of 420 feet
Pearl City High School Library 3
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface exploration for the proposed building site con
sisted of drilling six test borings to a depth of 30 5 feet
each The boring locations are shown on the enclosed Site Plan
Plate 1 A detatled description of the field exploration methods
and accompanying laboratory testing is given in the appendix along
with the boring logs and laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
The subsurface soil exploration disclosed red silty clay soils
underlain with more mottled colored silty clay subsurface soil
The upper silty clay soils were very stiff and were found from
16 5 to 23 feet below the existing ground elevation All the
on site soils are residual soils which retain the rock texture
of the parent basalt formations
The in situ residual soils are generally stiff to very stiff
The upper 5 to 8 feet of the new fill layer at Boring No 3
was loose and not adequately compacted at all
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface conditions encountered during exploration indicated
that the project site is located on a cut pad The one story
library building can be supported on properly constructed embank
ment fill
Pearl City High School Library 4
Site Preparation Grading
Proposed site grading will entail construction of new Fil
embankment on the existing pad and slope areas
Prior to site filling all debris and vegetation should be
removed to expose the ground surface The exposed surface
should then be scarifted to a depth of 6 inches moisture
conditioned and compacted to 90 percent of its maximum density
established according to ASTM 1557
The on site soil and other imported materials with low expan
ston potential can be used as embankment fill material Com
pacted fill should be placed in 8 inch horizontal loose lifts
and compacted to the above specified density
Fills placed against areas having greater than 5 1 horizontal
vertical slope ratio should be keyed and benched to properly
bond the newly placed fill to the existing soil This procedure
applies for the new fill against the north and east slopes A
detail of keying and benching procedures is presented on Plate 3
Typical Fill Over Natural Slope
The recently placed loose fill should be excavated before fill
placement in this area This loose material can be re used as
part of the compacted structural fill
Pearl City High School Library 5
Slopes
As planned no major cut slopes are anticipated at this site
Fill slope should not exceed a 2 horizontal to 1 vertical 2 1
ratio During fit11 slope construction the site should always
be shaped to avoid surface run off over the slope The on site
soil will be subjected to erosion therefore slope planting
should be done immediately after completion
Foundation
Spread and wall footing foundation may be used for the proposed
building Foundation bearing on the compacted fill can be de
signed for an allowable bearing pressure of 2 500 pounds per
square foot
Footing along the edge of the fill slope should be set back a
minimum of 10 feet from the slope surface
For sliding resistance computation a friction factor of 0 4 may
be used Passive pressure equivalent to 300 p c f fluid den
sity can be developed on the face of below grade structural elements
Footing should bottom a minimum of 12 inches below the adjacent
finished grade
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade bearing on compacted structural fill of low expan
sion potential soil can be utilized without special provision for
expansive soil

Groundwater was not encountered for a depth of 30 0 feet below
existing ground surface However under the building slab a
4 inch layer of crushed rock base course ts recommended to obtain
uniform slab support and for drainage purpose
To reduce slab distress joints should be utilized between dif
ferent gections of the building
Slab pourtng should be delayed as much as possible preferably
after the completion of the butlding construction
A plastic vapor barrier should be placed below the concrete slab
to protect floor covering from moisture damage
Drainage Subdrains
Surface run off drainage from the upper and surrounding areas
should be diverted away from the project site Run off should
not be allowed to pond and to run over the fill bank Generally
the on site red soil is erosive A drainage system with swale
construction is adviseable for the areas higher than the proposed
project elevations
A system of subdrains consisting of 4 inch perforated pipe and
gravel backfill is recommended at the fill embankment Positive
drainage should be provided to relieve the build up of hydrostatic
pressure The exact subdrain locations should be determined in
the field during construction by the soils engineer
