performed for the C 17 Support Utilities Phase 2 and C 17 Parking Lot project located

geotechnical recommendations derived from our analyses for the underground utilities
and pavements planned at the project site The recommendations are intended for
design input only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this report

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated
to be middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which time erosion
incised deep valleys into the Koolau Shield volcanic activity returned with a series of
lava flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These series are referred to as
the Honolulu Volcanic Series
The geologic conditions underlying the site are the result of a complex series of
depositional and erosional events due to glacio eustatic sea level changes and
post erosional volcanic activities during the Pleistocene Epoch The Koolau Volcanic
Shield was built during the early Pleistocene Epoch by thinly bedded basaltic lava flows
Following the cessation of activity of the Koolau Volcano alluvial soil materials were
deposited in an estuarine and deltaic environment in the area of Pearl Harbor The sea
level fluctuated several times receding perhaps 90 meters below and rising about
30 meters above the present sea level Concurrent with a rise in sea level during the
late Pleistocene Epoch eruption of the Salt Lake Makalapa member of the Honolulu
Volcanic Series mantled the older alluvium with thick deposits of volcanic ash which
consolidated to form volcanic tuff locally called mudrock layers Subsequent erosion
dissected and thinned those mantles of tuff
During the Recent Epoch as the sea level rose to its current stand the area was
covered with additional sediments consisting primarily of soft and loose lagoonal marine
deposits Land development and reclamation projects in the past 40 to 60 years have
added man made fills bringing the site to its present configuration

load supporting capacity of the pipe Therefore the pipe bedding is an important
design consideration A minimum of 150 mm of compacted granular bedding
material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be

For pipes with inverts in soft and or loose soils an additional 450 mm layer of
No 3B Fine gravel wrapped in a filter fabric Mirafi 18ON or equivalent should be
provided below the bedding layer for more uniform support
The granular bedding should extend beyond the pipe sides a minimum width of
one fourth the outside pipe diameter and should extend half way up the sides of the

Backfill
The first zone of backfill for pipes extending from the bedding material to at least
300 mm above the top of the pipes or 300 mm above the groundwater level
should consist of free draining granular material such as No 3B Fine gravel It is
critical to use free draining granular materials to reduce the potential for formation
of voids below the haunches of the pipes and to provide adequate support for the
sides of the pipes Improper backfill material around the pipes and placement could
result in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The trench backfill from 300 mm above the top of the pipes or 300 mm above the
groundwater level to the finished subgrade may consist of the excavated on site
soils having particles less than 150 mm in maximum dimension or select granular
material The backfill should be placed in thin lifts normally 200 mm in loose lift
thickness with each lift mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction In areas below pavements the upper one meter from the
finished grade should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 A typical section
of the trench is presented on Plate 4

soil classification Graphic presentations of the grain size distribution of the soils are

performed on the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of
the soils Results of the test are presented on Plate B 3

APPENDIX C
Percolation Testing
APPENDIX C
Percolation Testing
The permeability of the in situ soils were evaluated by performing a total of
13 percolation tests consisting of constant and falling head tests in Boring Nos 1
through 8 and 10 The tests were performed at depths ranging between 3 to
4 9 meters below the existing ground surface
The constant head percolation tests were performed by pumping water into the
borehole until the water surface stabilized and remained at equilibrium for a sufficient
period of time The water height and the rate of pumping were then measured
The falling head percolation tests consisted of filling the test boring with water
The water in the test boring was allowed to percolate into the surrounding soils The
drop in water level with respect to time was recorded The test results of the field
percolation tests are presented on Plates C 1 through C 10
