INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to evaluate the subsurface
conditions at the site and to provide recommendations for the
design and construction of the foundations of the proposed
structures
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed Physical Education Facilities project site is located
south of the University of Hawaii Hilo campus in Hilo Hawaii
GEOLABS HAWAII
The overall dimensions of the area where the new facilities
are to be constructed are about 400 feet by 500 feet The
dimensions of the adjacent parking lot are about 280 feet
by 450 feet These two areas are diagonally next to each
other and total approximately 7 5 acres
The project site is bounded on the north makat and west
by open undeveloped land similar in surface character to
that of the project site It is also immediately bounded
on the south mauka by open land beyond which about 200
feet south of the project parking lot area are the adult
student housing buildings Kawili Street runs along the
eastern side of the site and is adjacent to the project
parking lot area Access into and through the site during
the time of the soil investigation was by way of dirt trails
off a campus roadway cleared with the use of a dozer There
were no existing structures on the project site
The relief of the site is generally of a rolling nature
consisting of small hills and shallow depressions In
general the site slopes down toward the east at 5 to 10
percent gradients On the north side where Buildings E
and D are to be located the slope averages about 20 percent
due to a localized hilly area The ground elevations vary
between 170 feet and 190 feet The lowest ground elevations
are located in the central portion of the project site
GEOLABS HAWAII
There were no visible signs of drainage paths but generally
the site appears to drain in an easterly direction along the
central portion
A cavity was exposed and noted at the surface of a dozer
cut dirt trail midway between Building A Boring 3 and
Building B Boring 18 The cavity was about 1 foot deep
and 3 to 4 feet in overall diameter with a 1 foot diameter
Opening to the surface
The vegetation consists primarily of a dense mat of California
grass about 2 to 4 feet thick covering the entire site Large
trees about 20 to 30 feet in height are also noted scattered
throughout the site
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
Five one story buildings are planned for the site The proposed
structures will have steel frames with metal roofs and masonry
walls Maximum column loads of about 50 kips are anticipated
Site grading will involve cuts up to 10 feet and fills up to
about 9 feet
A parking lot about 450 by 280 feet in plan is also planned
Cuts generally less than 5 feet are planned for the parking
lot

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The lava rock encountered at the project site belongs to the
present volcano and represents the latest
lava flows of the project area which came down the slopes in
successive flows of various thicknesses and time intervals
This relatively young rock formation generally consists of
a fractured vestcular olivine basalt with many scoriaceous
cinderlike sections From the vesicular nature of the
rock cores its round shaped vesicles and lack of clinker
it appears that the formation was probably developed from
pahoehoe lava flows
No lava tubes were encountered during the investigation
although lava tubes are a common feature for this type of
fluid pahoehoe flow A relatively small cavity was noted
between Boring Nos 3 and 18 but it appeared to be a cavity
formed tn a highly fractured section of rock rather than a
lava tube The fractured nature of this rock makes the
formation permeable in those specific areas but the entire
area is not necessarily permeable since water was ponding
on the rock surface at various low spots after heavy rains
The small hills and shallow depressions throughout the site
reflect the undulating character of the lava flow surface more
than anything else and consequent to this the surface drainage
patterns and dtrections were established
GEOLABS HAWAII
Subsurface Explorations
Twenty five borings were drilled at the site to depths of
about 5 to 28 5 feet below the existing ground surface to
explore the subsurface conditions and to obtain samples for
laboratory testing The approximate locations of the borings
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Detailed information on the field exploration and laboratory
testing ts covered in the appendtx along with the boring
logs and laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
Based on the borings the site appears to be underlain by
a surface layer 0 to 3 feet of soft brown volcanic ash
over a lava rock formation
In general groundwater was not noted in the borings during
the field explorations Some trapped or perched water was
noted in a few borings
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploratton indicated a thin surface layer of soft
volcanic ash over a lava rock formation To provide more
uniform support the soft volcanic ash material should be
removed and foundations slabs and pavements should bear
on the lava rock or compacted fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
The surface ash may be stockpiled for top soil use at the
project or for future landscape use
Foundation
For the proposed one story structures spread or continuous
footing foundations are recommended
If practicable all the footings for one building should
either bear on the in situ lava rock or on compacted fill
Allowable bearing values of 4000 p s f on fractured lava
rock and 3000 p s f on compacted select granular fill may
be used
To reduce differential settlements where a building is partly
on cut and partly on fill the footing excavation in rock
should be over excavated 12 inches and replaced with compacted
granular fill
To reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to possible
cavities or lava tubes tn the underlying lava rock every other
footing excavation should be probed The probe holes should
have a minimum diameter of 2 inches and should extend below
the bottom of footing a depth equals to 2 times the footing
width or a minimum of 5 feet The probe holes should be
grouted from the bottom of the hole using low pressures
GEOLABS HAWAII
If a cavity is suspected below a footing addittonal probe
holes should be made to delineate the horizontal extent of
the cavity The probing and grouting operations should be
monitored by the soils engineer or the State project inspector
to see that these recommendations are carried out properly and
to locate any potential voids near the footing excavations
Generally the bottoms of footings should be placed about
2 feet below the finish grade
Footings located near utility trenches should extend to the
bottom of the trench
Surface water should be diverted away from building foundations
Settlements
For the above recommendations minor settlements are anticipated
where the footings are on rock
Settlement of footings on compacted fill should not exceed inch
total and inch differential Where the building foundation is
partly on fill and partly on cut into the rock it is estimated
that the differential settlement should not exceed inch if
the rock 7s over excavated and replaced with select granular
material
Earthwork and Compaction Requirements
Cuts and fills generally less than 10 feet high are contemplated
for the site
GEOLABS HAWAII
Vegetation and debris should be stripped from the site prior
to grading The surface ash can be stockpiled for top soil
use
Most of the excavatton will probably be in fractured lava rock
It ts our optnion that most of the excavation can be accomplished
by hard ripping with heavy equipment If suffictent fine material
cannot be generated by the excavation operations some blending
with finer borrow material may be necessary to obtain a better
graded fill material In general material larger than 8 inches
should not be used tn the fill
Rocks with diameters greater than 8 inches and less than 2 feet
may be used tn the deep fill at least 3 feet below finish grade
provided that they are well embedded and the votds filled with
smaller pieces and filler matertals No rocks should be allowed
to cluster itn piles
Fill should be placed in 12 inch loose lifts and compacted to
a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density established by
ASTM Test Method D 1557 Conventional compaction testing is
generally not practicable in excavated lava rock material
Instead a testing program to determine the number of passes
of a compactor required to achieve the desired compaction
should be conducted at the start of the project The number
of passes can then be used as the criteria for compaction
GEOLABS HAWAII
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than 5 hori
zontal to 1 vertical should be keyed and benched into the
existing firm ground For construction details refer to
Plate 2
Slopes
Fill slopes should be constructed at slope ratios of 2 hori
zontal to 1 vertical 2 1 or more
For cut slopes in fractured lava rock design slope ratios
of 1 horizontal to 1 vertical 1 1 can be used
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4einch layer of base
course cushion fill compacted to 95 of its maximum dry
density
To protect against moisture damage of sensitive flooring
material a polyethylene moisture barrier should be used
To reduce differential settlement where the slab is partly
on cut and partly on fill the cut portion should be over
excavated 1 foot backfilled with broken up on site rock
material and recompacted to match the adjacent fill density
Parking Lot
In the parking lot area the soft surface ash material should
be removed and replaced with on site select granular fill
The recommended pavement section may then be as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
The subgrade should be shaped to drain
Design Review
The plans for foundations stte grading and uttlity construc
tion should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations ts tncluded in the project plans and speci
fications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It ts recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction to monitor the contractor s grading
Operations The necessary inspections include the observation
of the site grubbing removal of brown ash prior to filling
keying and benching operation to prepare the sloping site to
receive the new fill fill placement thickness gradation of
the broken up lava rock fill materials and the verification
that sufficient passes have been performed to obtain the
required density on each fill lift
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed
by a soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
GEOLABS HAWAII
Any imported material required should be non expansive
and should be tested and approved by the sotls engineer
prior to hauling to the site
