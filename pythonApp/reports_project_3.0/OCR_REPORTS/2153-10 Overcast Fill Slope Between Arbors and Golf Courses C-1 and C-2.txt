This letter is written regarding the loose overcast fill material along the slope
between the Arbors and Golf Course C 1 and C 2 for the above referenced project
A field exploration was performed on to determine the extent
of the loose overcast material along the slope The field exploration consisted of the
excavation of eight 8 test trenches using a backhoe The test trenche locations are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 The test trench cross sections are shown on Plates 2
to 9
Based on our field exploration the following observations and recommendations
are presented
Slope West of the Trench Line Between Grid Lines C D
The overcast material consisted of loose coralline sand and gravel with
thickness of about 2 to 6 feet The loose overcast material was generally
underlain by a dense coralline deposit which had been excavated into to
generate borrow fill material previously
Because of the potential for soil movements and erosion of the loose
overcast material from surface runoff we believe that the loose overcast
material along the slope face be removed down to stiff natural ground

Slope East of the Trench Line Between Grid Lines C D
For the trench lines in this area the thickness of the loose overcast
material increased to about 5 to 12 feet in thickness The loose coralline
sand and gravel material was generally underlain by a dark brown to gray
adobe clay deposit
After the removal of the loose slope material it appears that the remaining
adobe clay slope will be about 2H 1V
Based on our experience with adobe clay soil deposit on steep cut slope
ratio we believe there is a very high potential for long term downslope
creep movement of the underlying 2H 1V clay slope located below the
loose overcast materials
To reduce the potential of this long term creep movement of the
adobe clay slope we believe that the clay slope should either be flatten to a slope
of 5H 1V or a new compacted buttress fill be utilized in front of the adobe clay
formation
Since the flatter 5H 1V cut slope will extend beyond the property line it
appears that a stabilization buttress fill should be used to minimize the
downslope creep of these adobe clay soils The new buttress fill material
should consist of compacted coral material placed at the bottom of the
reconstructed fill slope with horizontal lifts and keyed and benched into the
adobe soil formation A typical detail of a buttress fill is shown on Plate 10
