This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering services performed during
the grading operations for the Kona Liberty House project in the Kailua Kona area in the
District of North Kona on the island of Hawaii Our services were performed from May
through November 1996 in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
SITE GRADING
The Kona Liberty House project site is located to the southeast of the existing
K Mart store near the intersection of Makala Boulevard and Kamakaeha Avenue on the
north side of Kailua Kona on the island of Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 The project development consists of a new Liberty House store roadways
parking areas and associated utilities A general layout of the project site is provided on
the Site Plan Plate 2

The grading operations were performed by Isemoto Construction Company In
general the site grading consisted of clearing and grubbing proof rolling excavation
placement and compaction of fills and utility trench excavation and backfilling The
building construction was done by Constructors Hawaii Inc under separate contract
and observation and testing of the building construction was not performed by
Geolabs Hawaii
The original ground surface elevations at the site ranged from about 146 to
152 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The finished grade at the site is at about 156 feet
MSL Fills placed at the site ranged from about 4 feet at the building site to about
10 feet in thickness at the southern portion of the parking lot A cut slope of
approximately 15 to 20 feet was constructed at the rear of the building
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits were cleared
and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials were
removed and disposed of off site Following the clearing and grubbing operations the
exposed rocky subgrades within the grading limits were proof rolled a minimum of four
passes with heavy construction equipment such as a bulldozer and or sheepsfoot
compactor to detect and collapse potential near surface cavities and or voids Based on
our field observations cavities and voids were not evident during the proof rolling
operations
Fills and Backfills
During the site grading operations the excavated materials were hauled to an
on site crusher where the materials were crushed and processed to generate general and
structural fill materials The fill materials used generally consisted of relatively
well graded granular materials less than 12 inches in largest dimension The structural
fill choker fill materials used consisted of well graded granular materials less than
6 inches in largest dimension General fill materials were used as fill materials up to an
elevation of about 4 feet below the finished subgrade and structural fills 6 inch minus
materials were used as fill and backfill materials within the upper 4 feet of the finished
subgrade at the site
During the grading operations the fill and backfill materials were generally placed
in level lifts of about 12 inches in loose thickness brought to above the optimum moisture
and compacted to the specified requirements Compaction was generally accomplished
by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory drum roller or other types of similar compaction

equipment Generally conventional compaction testing is not practicable in fills
composed of rock boulders and or cobbles Therefore the degree of compaction for
the general and structural fills was evaluated based on visual observations since the
over sized nature of the fill material precluded field density testing of the fill materials
For the base course and trench backfill using materials less than 1 inches in
largest dimension periodic field density tests were taken during the filling and backfilling
operations to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and
soil conditions were noted The field density tests were taken to determine the relative
compaction of the fills and backfills placed for conformance with the specified minimum
compaction Samples of the base course and backfill materials used were obtained and
transported to our office for laboratory testing The maximum dry densities and optimum
moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized
on Plate 3 of this report
A total of 52 field density tests were performed using the nuclear probe method in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designations D 2922 and D 3017 The results of the
compaction tests taken during the site grading operations are summarized on Plates 4 1
through 4 3 of this report These tests were taken on the prepared ground surfaces at the
site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from
on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory
were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Utilities
Installation of the new utility lines generally included excavation in fills and hard
basalt rock formation followed by backfilling the trench excavation after placement of
the utility line In general after placement of pipes in the trenches free draining
granular materials 1 inch minus materials were used for the trench backfill up to about
12 inches above the pipes Trenches were generally backfilled with either 3 inch minus
or 1 inch minus granular material and compacted to the specified requirements
During the sewer trench excavation a cavity was encountered at about
Station 1 35 of Road E at the site The cavity encountered traverses perpendicular to
Road E in a mauka makai direction The cavity encountered is about 10 to 12 feet high
and about 20 feet wide The roof of the cavity was collapsed to about 10 feet beyond the
edge of Road E on both sides Following the collapse of the cavity the collapsed

EVALUATION
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations observed and tested by
Geolabs Hawaii during the site grading operations at the project site were performed in
general accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the above referenced report
dated
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in
the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not
insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain
responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications and
for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
