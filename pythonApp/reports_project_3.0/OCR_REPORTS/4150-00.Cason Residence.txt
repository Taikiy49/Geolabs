This letter report presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations for the
proposed Cason Residence at Lot No 11 of the Parcel K 2 Residential Subdivision within

recommendations are based on our site reconnaissance and our previous experience in
the vicinity of the project site
Project Considerations
The proposed Cason Residence is located within the Hualalai Resort in the District
of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii It is proposed to construct a new residence at
Lot No 11 of the Parcel K 2 Residential Subdivision within the Hualalai Resort
Based on the information provided the proposed single story Cason residence will
have a building footprint of approximately 4 000 square feet In addition garage and lanai
areas consisting of about 700 square feet are planned The new residence will also have
a swimming pool and a hot tub at the northern end of the residence
In general we anticipate that site grading for this residential project will consist of
minor cuts and fills of up to about 1 foot In addition an excavation on the order of about
8 feet may also be required to construct the swimming pool

The project site is situated on the western flank of Hualalai Mountain which makes
up the west central portion of the island The lava formation encountered at the site
appears to be of pahoehoe flow which spread and ponded as it approached the ocean
Pahoehoe is characterized by a smooth rope like or billowy surface and an internal
structure of vesicular porous rock Cavities are commonly encountered in pahoehoe
lavas These cavities are formed when the lava is still in a molten state and represent both
lava tubes intra flow cavities and inter flow cavities such as blisters and pockets Lava
tubes are formed when molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like
structure which may extend for a large distance longitudinally along the flow Inter flow
cavities are generally smaller in horizontal extent
Basalt is considered to be relatively permeable rock and can transmit water quite
readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions In general water is transmitted
through the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities and
along clinker layers The permeability of the clinker and cavities is high therefore they
serve as the major water transmission features
Surface and Subsurface Conditions
The proposed Cason Residence is located at Lot No 11 of the Parcel K 2
Residential Subdivision within the Hualalai Resort The project site is currently a vacant
lot We understand that the site was mass graded by the placement of fill during the initial
development of the Parcel K 2 Residential Subdivision No significant vegetation was
observed at the site during our field exploration Based on our site reconnaissance the
project site is generally level
In general we anticipate that the subsurface conditions at the project site will likely
consist of fills placed over basalt lava flows Based on our experience in the vicinity of the
project site the fills in the area generally consist of medium dense to dense gravels with
some silt We anticipate that the thickness of the fills to be on the order of about 5 to
10 feet The surficial fills are likely underlain by medium hard to hard basalt formations
The basalt formations near the project area are typically interbedded with thin layers of
clinker seams Presence of lava tubes and cavities are also common in the basalt lava
flows
We anticipate that the groundwater level will be near the mean sea level However
groundwater levels can fluctuate depending on factors such as seasonal rainfall storm
surge conditions and other factors

Discussion and Recommendations
Based on understanding of the subsurface conditions at the project site we believe
that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be
used to support the proposed structures planned for the Cason Residence In general we
recommend that the foundations bear on structural fill materials Based on the anticipated
subsurface conditions at the site we believe that most of the excavated on site materials
near surface fills from near the existing ground surface may be re used as a source of
general and structural fill materials provided that the over sized materials are removed
Detailed discussion of our geotechnical engineering design recommendations is presented
in the following sections of this report
Building Foundations
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions at the project site we believe that
shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used to support the new residence
planned at the site In general foundations may be designed based on an allowable
bearing pressure of up to 4 000 pounds per square foot psf for footings bearing on basalt
formation and or compacted structural fills This bearing value is for dead plus live loads
and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind
or seismic forces In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below
the lowest adjacent finished grade
Where foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should
be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility tench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures
with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation settlement
If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the footing subgrades they should
be over excavated and replaced with structural fill The over excavation should be
extended until the underlying dense materials are exposed The over excavation should
be backfilled with structural fill materials moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the
bottom of footing may be extended down to the underlying competent material
lf foundations are designed and constructed in accordance with our
recommendations we estimate total settlements to be less than 1 inch with differential
settlements on the order of inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 45 may be used for footings bearing on basalt formation and or on the

Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed residence
For the interior building slabs which will not be subjected to vehicular traffic we
recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill consisting of No 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be placed below the slabs to provide more uniform
support of the slabs and to serve as a capillary break To reduce the potential for future
moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture
vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is recommended on top of the gravel cushion
layer Placement of 2 inches of moist fine sand above the plastic membrane may be
considered to provide protection of the membrane and to aid in curing of the slab concrete
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project In general
we recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer of No 3B Fine gravel be
provided below the concrete walkway slab To reduce the potential for slab cracking crack
control joints should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkways with
expansion joints at right angle intersections
Garage Slab and Concrete Driveway
For slabs on grade that will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as the garage slab
and the driveway we recommend that a 6 inch layer of select borrow subbase be provided
below the slab in lieu of the cushion fill layer for more uniform support The vapor barrier
and the 2 inch moist fine sand layer may also be omitted for these slabs The select
borrow subbase should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction
We recommend that the concrete slab be designed for the intended loads For
design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of 300 pounds per square inch
per inch of deflection pci may be used for the compacted select borrow subbase
Provisions should be made for proper load transfer across the slab joints subjected to
vehicular traffic The subgrade soils beneath the garage and driveway slabs should be
prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented in the Site Preparation
subsection of this report

Retaining Structures
We understand that retaining structures such as the swimming pool wall may be
required for the project The following general guidelines may be used for preliminary
design of retaining structures
Retaining Structure Foundations
In general we believe that retaining structure foundations may be designed in
accordance with the recommendations presented in the Building Foundations
section of this report The footings should have a minimum width of 18 inches In
addition foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded a minimum depth
of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade For sloping ground
conditions the footing should be embedded deeper to obtain a minimum 6 foot
set back distance measured horizontally from the outside edge of the footing to the
face of the slope Footings oriented parallel to the direction of the slope should be
constructed in stepped footings

Drainage
Retaining structures should typically be well drained to reduce the build up of
hydrostatic pressures behind the wall However it is generally not practical to
provide drainage for swimming pool walls Considering the relatively permeable
nature of clinker materials and basalt formations we believe that the build up of
hydrostatic pressures behind the swimming pool should be negligible without a
drainage system Therefore the typical drainage system behind retaining structures
may be omitted for the swimming pool walls

Fill and Backfill Materials
Structural fill materials should consist of well graded granular material of 6 inches
in largest dimension with sufficient fines to prevent the occurrence of voids in the
compacted mass If the excavated materials do not contain sufficient fines to
produce a well graded material off site borrow or on site rock crushing of
large sized rock fragments or boulders should be considered to provide the required
gradation and particle sizes to develop a well graded material
General fill and backfill materials may consist of on site materials with a maximum
dimension of 12 inches On site materials greater than 12 inches in maximum
dimension may be used as general fills provided that they are reduced in size to
meet the above requirements Care should be exercised to avoid placement of
boulders in the proposed utility alignment and within the depths of the proposed
utility lines to reduce the potential for encountering boulders during excavation for
the utility trenches Boulders also should not be exposed at the fill slope face
Imported materials should be observed and or tested by Geolabs Hawaii for their
suitability prior to being transported to the site

Excavation
Based on available information the project site is underlain by surficial fills and
medium hard to hard volcanic rock at shallow depths Generally the fills and
volcanic rock at the site may be excavated with normal heavy excavation
equipment such as ripping with large bulldozers where the rock is near the existing
ground surface However deeper excavations in some areas may require the use
of hoerams or chipping
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are based
on our visual observation of the existing rock formations and field data from the
probe holes and test pits performed at the site We recommend that all contractors
proposing to bid on this project be encouraged to examine the site conditions to
make their own interpretation
Cut and Fill Slopes
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions permanent cut slopes exposing
hard basalt may be designed with a slope inclination of one and one half horizontal
to one vertical or flatter Where the cut slopes expose loose clinkers and
highly weathered basalt formation the slope inclination should be flattened to 2H 1V
or flatter
Permanent fill slopes may be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter
Fills to be placed on slopes with inclinations steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed
and benched into the existing slope to provide stability of the new fill against sliding
The filling operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level
horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the above fill placement
recommendations Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back
to the design slope ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face Water should be
diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope planting should be provided as
soon as possible to reduce the potential for erosion of the finished slopes

Utility Lines
We envision that new utility lines such as water and sewer lines will be required
for development of this project It is anticipated that most of the trenches for utilities will
be excavated in the surficial fill materials placed at the site
In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM
C 33 No 67 gradation or 1 inch minus well graded granular materials is recommended
under the pipes Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel or 1 inch
minus well graded granular materials should also be used for the trench backfill up to
about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to
reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the
pipes It is critical that free draining materials be used to reduce the potential for formation
of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the
pipes to reduce the potential for settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes Where
the 1 inch minus well graded granular materials are used as bedding and initial backfill
the bedding and initial backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of granular materials generally
less than 6 inches in maximum size The backfill material should be moisture conditioned
to above the optimum moisture content placed in maximum 12 inch level loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the
potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the
upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement grade should be compacted to at
least 95 percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the buildings should be sloped to shed water away from
foundations and slabs and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the buildings and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and slab areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and
slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab
edges immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water infiltration In addition
drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to
drain surface run off away from the foundations and slabs

Construction Observation
It is recommended that Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during construction of the proposed project The items of construction
that are critical requiring Special Inspection include observation of the proof rolling
operations the foundation excavation and fill placement and compaction Other aspects of
earthwork construction should also be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii This
is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to expedite suggestions for design changes that may be required in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time this report was prepared The
recommendations provided in this report are contingent upon such observations If the
actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made
Limitations
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from our observations Variations of subsoil conditions from those
assumed may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the recommendations provided in this report
