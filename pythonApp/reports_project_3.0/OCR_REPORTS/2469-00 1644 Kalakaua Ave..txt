INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical
engineering exploration performed for proposed pre purchase evaluation of
the subject property at 1644 Kalakaua Avenue The general location of the
project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our field exploration and
presents our preliminary geotechnical engineering recommendations derived
from analyses of the data obtained from one drilled and sampled boring
These recommendations are intended to provide input for the evaluation of
the proposed purchase of the subject property and as such are not
intended for development or design purposes

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the available boring data it appears that the subsoil
conditions at the project site are generally competent and could provide
support for either shallow and or deep foundations depending on the types
of structures to be built and the anticipated structural loadings The
bearing value required for foundation design will depend upon the final
column loads and uniformity of the underlying subsoils within the project
site
Shallow Foundations
Based on the preliminary boring data it is our opinion that shallow
spread or continuous wall footing foundations may be used to support one
1 to two 2 story light weight structures of timber post and beam
construction For heavier masonry structures and or tall buildings higher
than 2 story pile foundations should be considered if footing settlements
exceeding one 1 inch or more are not tolerable
An allowable bearing capacity of up to 2 000 p s f may be used for
spread continuous footings bearing on the upper fill crust with a minimm
footing embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
The thickness and uniformity of the upper fill crust material supporting
the shallow footing foundations should be verified by additional
exploration
Footing settlements is estimated to be less than one inch total
provided that the above recommendations are followed and that the colum
load is limited to about 45 kips or less

Pile Foundation
Based on our preliminary field exploration a short pile foundation
system appears to be the most feasible means to transmit heavy multi level
structural loads to the underlying coral formation The uniformity of and
the depth to the underlying coral formation should be verified by
additional exploration and analyses to provide detailed design
recommendations
End bearing piles extending through the upper fill and loose lagoonal
deposits layers and bearing on the in situ dense coral formation are
recommended

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling one
1 boring to a depth of 38 5 feet below the existing ground surface at
the approximate location shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Truck mounted
equipment was used to drill the boring
The materials encountered in the boring were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our geologist who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a standard
penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with a
140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches or by hydraulically pushing a
2 5 inch diameter thin wall tube The blow counts to drive the sampler
the last 12 inches or the hydraulic pressure to push the sampler the
first 24 inches when available are shown on the Boring Logs at the
appropriate sample depths
Core samples of the coral were obtained by an NX core barrel The
core recovery REC and Rock Quality Designation RQD values are shown on
the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
