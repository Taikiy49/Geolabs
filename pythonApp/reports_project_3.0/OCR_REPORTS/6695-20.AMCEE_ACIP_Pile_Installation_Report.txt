PROJECT DESCRIPTION
In general the Ala Moana Center Ewa Expansion project involves expansion of the
western Ewa end of the Ala Moana Center in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The project area was formerly occupied by Sears department store and mall parking and
will be converted to four levels of retail development
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions and structural loads a deep
foundation system consisting of augered cast in place ACIP piles was recommended to
reduce the potential for appreciable total and differential settlements from the structural
loads Due to accessibility and clearance considerations some of the renovation work
required the use of micropiles instead of ACIP piles
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the
ACIP pile foundation construction for the Ala Moana Center Ewa Expansion project only
Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company Inc HDCC is the general contractor for the
project and the ACIP pile installation subcontractor is Hawaiian Dredging Construction
Company Inc HDCC Waterfront Division
STATIC PILE LOAD TEST
In order to evaluate the adequacy of the recommended design lengths of the
ACIP pile foundation a static pile load test was conducted on a dedicated ACIP pile on
October 4 and 5 2013 The pile load test was conducted on an 18 inch diameter ACIP pile
with a length of approximately 86 feet relative to the existing ground surface To better
understand the load transfer mechanism at the pile soil interface the load test pile was
instrumented with seven vibrating wire embedment strain gages installed at various depths
along the length of the load test pile
A Manitowoc 12000 series crane with crane mounted hollow stem auger powered
by an APE 75C Hydraulic pump was used to install the load test pile A hydraulic concrete
pump was used to pump the grout into the pile system The test pile was loaded in general
accordance with ASTM D1143 Quick Load Test Method The maximum test load on the
pile was planned at 930 kips The static pile load test results are summarized in the report
entitled ACIP Pile Load Test Report Ewa Mall Expansion Ala Moana Center Honolulu
Oahu Hawaii dated
Based on the static pile load test results it appears the load test pile behaved like a
friction pile with nearly 100 percent of the maximum test load being resisted by adhesion
between the ACIP pile surface and the surrounding soils Based on our field observations
and the results of the static pile load test conducted we recommended revising the drilled
shaft lengths for the project as shown in the following table to support the allowable
compressive loads Design Capacity Changes to the ACIP Pile Length Schedule are

PRODUCTION PILE INSTALLATION
The production ACIP pile installation program consisting of installing 940 18 inch
diameter ACIP piles including 21 ACIP piles for the tower crane foundations was
conducted from through under the near continuous
full time observations by a field engineer from our office HDCC used the same
drilling grouting equipment utilized for the test pile program to install the production piles
Automatic monitoring equipment was mounted on the drilling pumping equipment with the
intent of providing auger depth grout flow rate grout pressure etc on a near continuous
basis However the equipment on occasion did not function reliably and manual
monitoring was conducted by our field engineer for the piles installed During the pile
installation operation logs were maintained for each ACIP pile which included information
pertaining to pile number date installed depth of pile grout volume used grout return
depth etc The locations of the production ACIP piles installed are shown on the
Foundation Location Plans Plates 2 1 through 2 5 The production ACIP pile installation
summary is presented by pile number on Plates 3 1 through 3 38 of this letter report
As with most foundation installation projects some problems did arise during
construction The various problems encountered and their remedial action where
applicable are shown in the Remarks column of the ACIP Pile Installation Summary
Based on our observation of the equipment performance and subsurface conditions
encountered it is our professional opinion that the production ACIP piles have been
installed to sufficient depths and or resistance to provide the design load capacities of 300
and 430 kips per pile with a safety factor of 2 0
