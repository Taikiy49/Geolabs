SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the Ewa Plain which is the ren
nants of a vast coral algal reef formation This reef formation
was built during the Pleistocene as the result of a glacio
eustatic high stand of the sea
Following the formation of these reef deposits sea level
adjusted to its current stand During this period the terrain
has been modified by weathering and the efforts of man to its
present form

Site Grading
Site Preparation
Se a ee
Prior to grading all deleterious materials such as vegeta
tion debris unsuitable fill and organically contaminated
soil should be removed and disposed properly off site
Following the clearing the ground surface should be
moisture conditioned to optimum moisture and proof rolled
with a heavy roller to obtain 95 of its maximum dry density
as established by ASTM D 1557 78
Any soft weak or pumping areas disclosed during the proof
rolling operations should be excavated to firm ground and
backfilled with select on site coral sand or other non
expansive import fill material compacted to the above
specified density

Kimura Ybl Associates Ltd
Structural Fill Material and Placement
b neat a
Based on laboratory test results and field exploration the
on site coral algal reef materials when broken down to
well graded sandy gravel less than 6 inches in size is
generally suitable for use as structural fill
If required imported fill material should be non expansive
free of organics and other deleterious materials and
generally less than 6 inches in size
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding
8 inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum
of 95 of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78
Site grading should provide drainage of surface runoff away
from all foundations
Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundations may be used to support
the proposed structure
Footings for the post office structure should be placed a
minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade and should
bear on the dense coral algal deposits
GEOLABS HAWAII
Kimura Ybl Associates Ltd

An allowable bearing value of 4000 p s f may be used for the
anticipated light foundation loads on the dense coral The
bearing capacity is for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third for short duration wind and for seismic
loading
Minimum footing widths of 18 inches for spread footings and 12
inches for continuous footings are recommended
The bottom of all footings should be free of loose soils and
inspected by the soils engineer prior to concrete placement
If a footing is situated next to a utility trench it should be
extended to the bottom of the trench or the trench backfill
must be well compacted to prevent footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
Estimated total and differential settlements for footing
bearing on the coral formation are anticipated to be on the
order of less than one fourth of an inch
To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of 0 45 may
be used between the foundation and in situ coral

For the flag pole design a passive soil resistance of 8 000
p s f may be used The first 1 5 feet below the ground
surface should be ignored in the passive pressure calculation
To obtain the above value the footing should be poured
directly against the coral formation
Lateral Pressure
For the loading dock design a lateral earth pressure of 35
p c f equivalent fluid pressure is recommended Additional
traffic and surcharge loading should also be included where
applicable
Cavity Probing
To detect potential cavities in the coral algal reef deposits
below the footings it is recommended that small diameter probe
holes be drilled at each column location or on 8 foot centers
along continuous wall footings The probe holes should be
drilled to a minimum of 6 feet below the bottom of the
footing As a result the probe holes should be backfilled
with neat cement grout mix injected at low pressures This
grouting of probe holes will aid in detecting other potential
voids below the footings
We recommend that the probing and grouting operations be
monitored by a soils engineer

Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of
gravel size crushed rock material such as No 3 Fine rock to
act as a capillary break and to provide uniform support
Should an impervious membrane be placed under the slab to act
aS a vapor barrier one inch of moist sands shuld be placed on
top of the membrane to aid in concrete curing
The subgrade soils after proof rolling should be kept moist
and undisturbed If deemed necessary it should be recompacted
prior to placing the gravel cushion
Pavement
It is our understanding tht asphaltic concrete pavements may be
used for the driveway and parking We recommend that the A C

Over a Prepared Subgrade
