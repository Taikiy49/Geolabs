PRELIMINARY GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING EVALUATION
RED HILL ELEMENTARY SCHOOL BUILDING B
Dear Mr Wong
As requested we have performed a preliminary geotechnical engineering
evaluation for Building B at the Red Hill Elementary School in the Moanalua area on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii This letter report summarizes the findings from our visual
observations at the project site and presents our opinions regarding the observed
distresses in the building These findings and opinions are preliminary only and are
intended for use in the planning of future design studies to correct the building distresses
observed and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Red Hill Elementary School is located along the eastern side of Ala Kula Place
in the Moanalua area of Oahu Hawaii Building B is situated within the southwestern
portion of the school grounds and consists of a two story concrete structure Cracks were
observed in the southern end wall of the building at the stairway and in portions of the
second floor hallway near the stairway It is desired to evaluate whether the cracks are
related to structural or geotechnical considerations or both At this time only a preliminary
assessment is desired in order to determine suitable studies for future design of the
repairs to the building Borings were not drilled for this study but are anticipated for future
studies
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our preliminary geotechnical engineering evaluation was to attempt
to evaluate whether the observed building distress could be related to geotechnical or
foundation considerations This information would be used to plan future studies for the
design of the building repairs Our scope of work consisted of the following tasks and work
efforts

FINDINGS
Subsurface Conditions
The grading plans for the school disclosed that most of the Building B site was
primarily cut from about 1 to 4 feet while the southwestern corner of the building site was
filled up to about 2 feet The boring information presented on the plans indicates that the
subsurface conditions prior to construction consisted of medium stiff to stiff reddish brown
silt Our site observations confirmed the presence of stiff reddish brown residual silty clay
soils exposed in cuts around the site Therefore available information indicates that most
of the building site is underlain by competent stiff residual soil at shallow depths
Probing
Hand probing with a one half inch diameter steel rod was performed around the
building perimeter Except for the southwestern corner of the building the hand probe
could not be pushed more than about 8 to 12 inches deep At the southwestern building
corner the probe was pushed by as much as 30 inches below the ground surface
The plans indicate that the building footings should be buried about 24 inches
below grade Therefore for most of the building the hand probing indicates that the
footings may bear on stiff soil At the southwestern corner the hand probing indicates that
there may be some soft material near the footing bottom

Observed Cracking
Cracking and signs of building distress were primarily confined to the southern end
wall of the building adjacent to the stairway Horizontal cracks were observed between the
Concrete Masonry Unit CMU blocks in this southern end wall The CMU blocks of the
southern end wall appeared relatively horizontal indicating that significant differential
settlement had not occurred
CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The site observations and limited subsurface information indicate that the building
distresses may be more attributable to structural considerations rather than foundation
issues The observed cracks did not appear to be due to foundation settlement or heave
Nevertheless soft soils were encountered at the southwestern corner of the building This
condition should be further explored by the drilling of a shallow boring or excavation of a
trench to expose the existing footing
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based upon
information obtained from the construction drawings and field observations Variations of
subsoil conditions may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the findings and discussions provided in this letter report
