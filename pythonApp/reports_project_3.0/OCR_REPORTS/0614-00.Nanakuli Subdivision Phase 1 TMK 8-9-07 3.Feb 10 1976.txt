INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this expansive soil investigation was to determine
the nature of soils underlying the site to ascertain their ex
pansion potential and to provide economical alternative for the
design and construction of the residential foundations

DISCUSSIONS RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The on site problem appears to be the underlying soils which
are highly expansive exhibit large volume change with moisture
variation With alternate drying and wetting conditions the
expansive soil will move lightly loaded foundation vertically
The simplest solution is to remove all of the underlying soils
under the building garage pad and walkway areas and replaced with
a non expansive select borrow capping However the cost of
removal and replacement of a 2 foot capping layer could be
economically prohibitive for the subject low income housing
project Therefore our study focuses on these areas

construction This will raise the slab finish grade slightly above

Lime Stabilization
Addition of lime about 5 to 6 by weight to the on site
expansive clay soil samples resulted in decreased density
lower plasticity properties of the soil and reduced swelling
A check was made with the local lime manufacturer in Waianae
and off grade lime can be purchased at substantially reduced
prices Based on the average size building and driveway areas
approximately 2 tons of lime would be required for each lot
The mixing of the lime with the underlying 6 inch subgrade
soil can be done by the use of simple farm equipment including
harrows plows and blade graders Regardless of the type of
construction equipment used several basic steps must always
be followed First the soil that is to be stabilized is
scarified and pulverized During the pulverizing process it
is essential that the soil chunks be broken down to as fine a
size as is economically possible Minimum of 70 of the soil
should pass a inch size sieve Second the lime is spread
either by hand to avoid dusting or by means of a spreader box
and that the lime and soil is thoroughly mixed
W O 614 00
Page 9
After the soil and lime are mixed ample water is added to
saturate the mix The continuous wetting of the mix is
critical to obtain the proper lime reaction with the alumina
and silica components in the expansive soils This reaction
is a long term one and one that results in greater strengths
The wetting and curing should last a minimum period of 48 hours
This reaction is important in order to change the high swelling
and shrinkage characteristics of the expansive soils
After the mixture is properly water cured it should be let
dry and lower its moisture content down to about 2 above
optimum before compaction Compaction should be no less than
80 of its maximum density and no more than 85 compaction
Prior to the placement of base course and plastic vapor barrier
and before final concrete pouring the lime stabilized subgrade
soil should not be allowed to dry and crack Occasional light
watering should be applied to keep all subgrade areas moist
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
To a degree the performance of the above recommendations is
dependent upon construction procedures and quality Hence
observation of site subgrade preparation proper lime mixing
Set curing and compaction of the stabilized subgrade should
be carried out by a soil engineer to permit any necessary field
adjustments and correlation between the preliminary soil data

obtained during this investigation and the actual soil
conditions encountered during each slab construction
The field monitoring by a soil engineer should include
the necessary testing to assure that the proper degree
of moisture content and compactionare consistently achieved
Excessive compaction and inadequate water curing of the lime
stabilized subgrade can increase future heaving on the slab
on grade and driveways The recommendations given in this
report are contingent upon the above inspections
