Pile Foundation

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Pile Foundations
Based on the exploration results and the anticipated heavy loading requirements
we believe that the upper subsoils encountered cannot provide adequate foundation
support for the proposed new deck areas Therefore deep pile foundations penetrating
the upper soils and bearing in the underlying competent layer are recommended to
support the proposed new deck areas
Pile Capacities
Based on our pile foundation analyses we believe that 1612 inch octagonal precast
prestressed concrete piles may be used The piles should penetrate through the
upper soils to develop shaft friction and or adhesion and bear directly on the
underlying competent bearing layer to develop the required additional end bearing
Capacity
An allowable vertical capacity of up to 100 tons per pile may be used for the pile
foundation design A minimum pile spacing of five 5 feet on centers is
recommended
It is not recommended that the piles be terminated in the softer or looser upper soil
deposits unless a much lower pile capacity such as 50 tons per pile can be used

be determined from the lateral pile analysis results presented on Plate 3 Lateral
capacities of larger 20 inch octagonal piles in the two 2 different soil conditions
are also presented for consideration should higher lateral pile capacity be desired
Pile Lengths
To obtain a vertical pile capacity of 100 tons per pile we estimate that the piles
would have to be driven a minimum of 115 feet wnen measured from the surface
of the existing deck at Pier 35 in the area of Boring No 1 In the area of Boring
No 2 the piles would have to be driven deeper to a minimum of 125 feet when
measured from the surface of the existing deck at Pier 33
If the larger 20 inch octagonal piles are used we recommend that the same
minimum pile lengths of 115 feet in the area of Boring No 1 and 125 feet in the
area of Boring No 2 also be used in order to obtain the design vertical pile
capacity of 100 tons per pile As indicated previously with regard to the 16 2 inch
piles we recommend that the larger piles should not be terminated in the softer
or looser upper soil deposits unless a much lower pile capacity can be used for
design
To facilitate pile delivery and driving we believe that the long piles should be cast
in two sections to be connected by adequately designed splice cans We believe
that the upper sections should be a minimum of 65 feet long in order to develop
adequate lateral pile capacity if a simple non mechanical type of splice can is used
Otherwise we recommend that special mechanical splice connections capable of
transmitting bending moment forces be used to connect the pile sections

Pile Driving Criteria
The piles should be driven with a hammer capable of delivering a minimum rated
energy of about 30 000 foot pounds The hammer should be equipped with an
energy level control so that driving within the loose deposits may be performed
at a reduced energy level to minimize the development of tensile stresses within
the piles which could cause damage cracking to the piles
Additional pile driving criteria are as follows
1 The driving process should be continuous without interruption for each pile
A pile may be rejected or its design capacity modified when the driving
process is interrupted for more than four 4 hours
2 During easy driving when the pile tip is still within the loose sandy soil the
hammer stroke should be carefully controlled with reduced energy levels so
that excessive tensile stresses are not induced in the pile
3 The pile driving may be terminated when the blow counts steadily increase
with depth and reach six 6 blows per inch for the last three 3 inches or
when aggregated blow counts reach 72 blows for the last foot

Test Pile Program
The pile capacity and driving criteria recommended herein were developed from
our analyses of the data obtained from an exploration consisting of two 2 borings A
test pile program is recommended to provide field data to verify some of the design
assumptions and to develop an economical pile foundation system for the new deck
areas
The test pile program should be undertaken to satisfy the following objectives
1 To examine the driveability of piles with the use of the selected hammer
2 To more accurately determine production pile order lengths and to further
verify delineate potential areas requiring different production pile lengths
3 To help evaluate and modify if necessary the driving criteria
To achieve these objectives we recommend that the test pile program should
consist of a minimum of ten 10 percent of the total number of piles as indicator piles for
the project Test piles that meet the specified driving criteria may be incorporated into the
actual foundation system on approval by the engineer
When the final foundation plan is available the project geotechnical engineer
should be consulted for selection of the test pile locations The initial test piles should be
cast ten 10 feet longer than the estimated production pile lengths of 115 feet in the area
of Boring No 1 and 125 feet in the area of Boring No 2 Also the test pile program
should provide flexibility by allowing for addition or relocation of the test piles as may be
deemed necessary by the project geotechnical engineer to suit the conditions
encountered during the test pile driving

A number of splice cans should be made available at the site during the test pile
driving program Splice cans may be fabricated with 4 inch thick high grade steel and
should be at least six 6 feet long
A minimum of one 1 pile load test should be performed as a part of the test pile
program The selected test pile should be load tested to two 2 times the design pile
capacity or 200 tons in accordance with the standard loading procedure described in
ASTM Test Method D 1143
The test piles should be driven with the same hammer which will be used for
production pile driving The test pile program should be conducted under close and
continuous field observation by the project geotechnical engineer who will determine when
pile driving may be stopped and provide guidance should revised or additional measures
be required due to unforeseen driving conditions
During the test pile driving period the geotechnical engineer may select to redrive
retap certain piles to evaluate the soil set up freeze effect on pile capacity
The geotechnical engineer should be consulted to evaluate the final pile bearing
capacity and production pile lengths based on the results of the test pile driving and the
pile load test
