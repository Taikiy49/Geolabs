In accordance with your request we have performed a soil
engineering exploration for the subject project The approximate
location of the proposed site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general accordance with
our proposal of
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this exploration was to study the subsurface soil and
water conditions at the site to obtain pertinent soil data for use in the
foundation design of the proposed structure
The main objectives were to explore the thickness and competency of
the upper crust of fill and the underlying soil deposit for spread
footing design

The scope of our exploration included drilling and sampling four 4
borings 22 to 32 feet and probing one of the borings from about 22 to 37
feet below the existing ground surface Also included were laboratory
testing of the selected samples obtained engineering evaluation of the
field and laboratory data development of design recommendations and
report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project originally included the construction of a two story
building with a small mezzanine and an open parking deck on the second
floor However after completion of the drilled borings which indicates
the presence of highly compressible subsoils under a thin crust it was
decided to delete the second floor including the parking deck to reduce
building loads
Therefore only a one story building with a small mezzanine is
presently planned
It is anticipated that the proposed one story building will include
three main portions as originally planned including a showroom a
warehouse and a service area with a total floor area of approximately
6500 square feet It is anticipated that some light vehicular traffic
may utilize the building floor
A parking area is planned in front of the building along Dillingham
Boulevard A loading zone is planned adjacent to the building along
Kaaahi Street

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the corner of Dillingham Boulevard
and Kaaahi Street in Iwilei Honolulu Hawaii
The lot is presently unoccupied and is rectangular in shape with a
frontage of approximately 150 feet on Dillingham Boulevard and
approximately 100 feet on Kaaahi Street
Ground surface at the site is fairly level but the ground
elevations were not available at this stage of the report preparation
The site is covered with compacted coral gravel fill
SUBSOTL CONDITIONS
The subsoil conditions at the project site were explored by drilling
and sampling four 4 borings to depths of about 22 to 32 feet below the
existing ground surface Boring B 1 was probed to a deeper depth of
about 37 feet in an initial attempt to explore the depth of a stiff soil
layer underlying the soft loose saturated lagoonal deposits The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings
consist of the following units
1 A 5 0 to 6 5 feet thick surface fill layer of medium dense to
very dense coral sand and gravel
2 a 20 0 to 23 5 feet thick layer of soft loose lagoonal deposits
composed of soil varying from clayey silt to silty gravel and
3 a stiff older lagoonal clay layer underlying the soft loose
lagoonal deposits

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The project site is underlain by a thin crust of fill over highly
compressible lagoonal deposits Settlements may occur due to building
loads and site filling However spread or continuous footing
foundations may be used for the building support provided a lower
allowable bearing capacity is used and the footings are restricted both
in size and embedment to reduce settlements
Major site filling should be avoided to prevent significant ground
settlements
Our recommendations are discussed in detail and presented in the
following sections
Foundation
Our subsurface exploration encountered a variable but generally
competent crust of fill at the anticipated foundation elevation for the
proposed building Shallow spread or continuous footings may be used to
support light column loads on the fill crust
We recommend that the size of spread footings be limited to less
than 5 by 5 feet Continuous footings should have a maximm width of 3
feet An allowable bearing capacity up to 1500 p s f may then be used
in the footing design The bearing capacity value is for total dead plus
live load only and may be increased by one third for short duration wind
and seismic loading
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1930 00 Page 5
Dk No 42
In general the footings should be placed as shallow as possible
below the existing ground surface to reduce the footing bearing pressure
on the underlying compressible deposits It is recommended however
that the footing bottom be embedded at about 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade
Estimated footing settlements under the design loading are
anticipated to be on the order of less than 1 inch total and 0 5 inch
differential
Resistance to lateral loads on the building can be obtained by
frictional resistance and passive earth pressure A frictional
coefficient of 0 4 may be used to calculate the frictional resistance
between the bottom of the footings and the recompacted in situ soil In
addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth
may be used to determine the passive earth resistance against the buried
vertical faces of the footings
Site Preparation
Major site filling is not recommended since the additional weight of
fill may initiate significant settlements due to compression of the loose
and soft subsoils Therefore only minimal cut and or fill should be
used for the site preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All debris rubbish and
other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly
off site in order to prevent contamination of the excavated material
The areas to receive footing pavement and slab on grade should be
scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches below the subgrade elevations
moisture conditioned to optimum moisture content and recompacted to at
least 95 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 test method

loose soft or yielding spots encountered at the footing or pavement
subgrade level should be removed and replaced with select on site

The on site granular fills may be used as a source of backfill and
fill material If required new imported fill material should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum
size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is anticipated for the proposed structure and
loading zone
It is recommended that the slabs be separated from structural
columns and the slab placement delayed as much as possible preferably
near the end of construction to reduce differential settlement Concrete
on grade slabs should have sufficient mmber of joints to accommodate
future slab differential settlements Any slab on grade edge exposed to
unpaved area should be embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer can assume no
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundation phase of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design

If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
and probes Variations of conditions between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping from references on the preliminary site plan from Pacific
Architects for the originally proposed two story building Elevations of
the data points were not available at this stage of report preparation
The physical locations and elevations of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
