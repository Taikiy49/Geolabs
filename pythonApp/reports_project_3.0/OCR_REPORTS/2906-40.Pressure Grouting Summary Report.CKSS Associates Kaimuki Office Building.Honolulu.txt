We have recommended allowable bearing pressures of up to 15 000 pounds per

Drilling and Pressure Grouting Operations
Our additional field exploration consisted of drilling and rock core sampling of 6
borings In generally our additional exploration encountered a surface layer of hard
basalt to depths of about 2 to 6 feet below the existing excavation surface which varies
from about 122 to 125 feet Mean Sea Level MSL Below this hard rock layer highly
weathered rock formation clinker was encountered to a depth of about 10 feet where
another dense basalt formation was encountered After evaluating the rock core samples
we further confirmed our previous opinion that pressure grout would be an economical
and effective method to improve the underlying clinker materials below the mauka portion
of the project site
The pressure grout operation was performed from to November
16 1993 in general accordance with our fee proposal using cement pressure grouting
This was an attempt to inject the cement slurry into rock fractures and fine voids using
sleeve port grouting method in order to strengthen the underlying clinker rock formation
After each probing hole was drilled using air track equipment by California Blasting
2 inch diameter PVC pipes with grout holes covered by rubber tubings at different depths

that the above cement pressure grouting operation under the footing areas has improved
the strength and bearing value of the loose clinker formation at the project site We
believe that with the grouting improvement the original bearing pressure of up to 15 000
pounds per square foot may be used for footings located on the mauka half of the
building site
