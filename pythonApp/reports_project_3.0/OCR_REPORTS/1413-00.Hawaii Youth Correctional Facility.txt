PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the study is to develop an ultimate site and
facilities plan for the Hawaii Youth Correctional Facility The overall
project site covers an area of approximately 500 acres The general
location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
The purpose of this soil engineering and geologic reconnaissance
was to obtain general information of the surface and subsurface soil
rock and groundwater conditions at the site in order to provide
preliminary data for use in the feasibility study of the site grading
design and construction for a facility development Major
considerations in the construction of this facility includes slope
stability excavations drainage problems and presence of expansive
soils
Our study consisted of a review of available soils and geologic
maps and literature concerning the area partial geologic field
reconnaissance and engineering analysis
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed plan is to construct new Youth Correctional Facilities
over portions of the existing area The locations and sizes of the
various facilities have not been determined

areas
The general locations of the existing facilities and ground

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located between Olomana Mountain and Kawainui
Swamp on the Island of Oahu Hawaii About one third of the site is
located on the north side of Kalanianole Highway and about two thirds
located on the south side of the highway The approximate location of
the site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The topography is usually characterized by rolling hills with
well defined gullies or drainageways The southern portion of the site
along the north slope of Olomana Mountain and along the northern and
eastern boundaries is much steeper Ground elevations may vary from
about 1500 to 105 feet
Access through the central and northern portions of the site is by
paved and dirt roads

The southern portion of the site along the slopes of Olomana is
inaccessible except by foot trails
The northern side of the mountain slopes down towards Kalanianole
the site is generally flatter with Slopes of 3 1 to V with
steeper side slopes along gullies or drainageways
Approximately 70 of the 500 acre site has Slopes steeper than 5 1
H V and about 20 of the area is flatter than 10 1 H V
The steeper slopes and drainageways are densely covered with brush
and trees The remainder of the area is grassed and used as pasture
land and yard areas for the existing facilities

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The geologic conditions of the project site are fairly complex
The area is generally located on the easterly side of the Koolau caldera
complex During the active phases of the volcano rising heated fluids
and gases altered the original basalts into softer more easily erodable
materials Following cessation of the volcanic activity the area was
deeply eroded by stream action
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1413 00 Page 5
During the late Pleistocene Geologic time a period of renewed
vulcanism resulted in a series of vents one of which is called the
Training School cinder cone that is located about one half mile north of
the summit of Olomana These vents deposited both pyroclastic materials
and lava flows over portions of the project site In situ weathering
has reduced the upper portions of these deposits to residual soils and
Saprolites Continued erosion and deposition by the intermittent
streams has resulted in the burial of these Pleistocene Volcanics by
alluvial materials

ENGINEERING GEOLOGIC CONSIDERATIONS CONCLUSIONS
EE EBL LONG G CONCLUSIONS
The field observations and studies of geologic and soil data of the
area disclosed that the site is generally underlain by weathered and hard
lava rock formations at fairly shallow depths
It is estimated that the thickness of the alluvial and residual
soils may range from 2 to possibly 10 feet with the thickness of soil
cover increasing towards the lower northern portion of the site near
Kailua High School
Based on the limited data it appears that the majority of the
soils and rocky formations could provide adequate support for the
proposed structures except where expansive clay soils are encountered
In these areas site preparation particularly the stripping of surface
clayey soils should be considered
The excavated soil and rock materials should provide adequate fill
material for the embankments Expansive soils should be placed a minimum
of 3 feet below finished grade and away from sloping areas
The smaller sized 12 inch minus excavated rocky material may be
used in the embankments The larger boulders up to 3 feet size may be
used in the construction of deep fills if the voids between the boulders
are properly filled General recommendations for site grading are
presented below as a guide

Prior to construction surface vegetation and organic material
should be removed and wasted off site In areas to receive fill or
at grade construction tree roots should be removed to a minimum
depth of 3 feet and the resulting excavation backfilled and
compacted to 90 of its maximum density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78
Prior to receiving fill the existing ground should be scarified to
a depth of 6 inches moistened and compacted to 90 maximum density
as per ASTM D 1557 78

In general the excavated rock material less than 12 inches in
size may be used as general fill material If the excavated rock
material does not contain sufficient fines to produce a well graded
material the surface soils may be mixed into the excavated rocky
material to increase the percentage of fines to provide a better
graded material for compaction and increase the stability of the
fills

Boulders encountered in excavations may be used in deep fills
provided that the following recommendations are followed
1 Boulders should be spread out and must not be nested together
They should be placed so that compaction equipment would be
able to compact around them
2 No part of the boulder should encroach any Closer to finished
subgrade than the boulder s largest dimension and in no case
be closer than 3 feet to finished subgrade
3 No boulder larger than 3 feet in its largest dimension should
be placed in the fill It may be necessary to reduce the size
of the larger boulders or dispose of them off site
4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in the
proposed utility alignments to prevent difficulty in the later
excavation of the utility trenches
Fills should be placed in horizontal loose layers not to exceed 10
inches in thickness Excavated rock fill may be placed in
horizontal loose layers not to exceed 18 inches in thickness
