corrosion tests are presented in the following sections of this letter report

FIELD SURVEY
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the project site
on for the field survey Our field work was performed under clear and sunny
conditions with generally dry to moist surface conditions
Sixty soil resistivity surveys were conducted at a maximum of 300 foot intervals at
15 locations designated as R 1 through R 15 along the sidewalk and shoulder of the streets
using the in Pin Method and a Nilsson Model 400 resistance meter
manufactured by Nilsson Electrical Laboratory Inc The method utilizes four surface probes
or pins electrodes to evaluate the average resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the
underlying soil mass The average resistivity values of the soil mass extending from the
existing ground surface to the test depth was assessed by using this method at the
approximate survey locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Results of the resistivity
surveys are presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 3
The soil resistivity surveys were performed at the existing ground surface along the
sidewalk and shoulder of the existing streets and highway of the proposed water mains The
average soil resistance along the proposed alignment was evaluated at each survey location

with the electrodes aligned at equal spacings of generally 3 to 9 feet at the existing ground
surface The depth of penetration of a resistivity sounding is a function of the electrode
spacing Since the depth of the water main invert was not available at the time of our field
exploration spacings of 3 to 9 feet were used for the electrode spacing for each soil resistivity
survey location
The resistance meter Nilsson Model 400 impresses a voltage between the outer
electrodes causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between the
inner electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the soil
resistance in ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity in ohm cm which
is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The evaluated soil
resistances the computed resistivity values and the corresponding corrosion ratings are
presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 3 of this report Field Resistivity Testing Summaries are
presented on Plates 4 1 through 4 4 It should be noted that the soil resistivity may be
influenced by temperature weather conditions existing underground facilities and other
factors
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics with
regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In general the lower the
resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic structure Conversely
the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion of metallic objects
On the basis of the field resistivity test results a majority of the tests indicate the
subsurface soils along the proposed water mains of Wailehua Road within the project site
have corrosion ratings of 1 to 2 This indicates the subsurface soils have extremely corrosive
to very corrosive potential Isolated resistivity measurements indicating corrosion ratings of
3 and 4 were noted A majority of the test locations indicate the subsurface soils along the
proposed water main of Wailehua Place Waiohia Street Waiohia Place and Pulu Place within
the project site have corrosion ratings of 2 and 3 Isolated resistivity measurements indicating
corrosion ratings of 1 and 4 were noted

data points by means of geophysical surveys Variation of soil conditions between and beyond
the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations may not become
evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the ratings provided in this report
