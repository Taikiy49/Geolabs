INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed parking structure addition at the
Outrigger Canoe Club at Waikiki Oahu Hawaii
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our evaluation was to explore the general soil
conditions at the site outside the existing parking structures to obtain
soil data for the evaluation of the existing parking structure foundations
and to render a professional opinion as to whether the underlying bearing
soil crust would be adequate to support the proposed additional loads from
the construction of a new parking deck on the third level

The main objectives of the evaluation were
1 to evaluate the thickness uniformity and competency of the
upper crust soils and underlying compressible lagoonal deposit
and
2 to determine the bearing properties of the various soil layers
below the site in order to evaluate the allowable bearing
capacity and potential settlements for the existing 8 foot wide
continuous strip footings under the proposed additional loads
Because of the low height clearance of the existing structure and
in situ footings four 4 borings were proposed to be drilled on the
outside The two 2 borings at the Kalakaua Avenue mauka sides were
planned to be drilled with a truck mounted drill rig while portable hand
carried drilling equipments were planned for the makai sides of the
existing parking structures However because of the presence of
underground utilities septic tank and to avoid obstruction to the
entrance of the Canoe Club the two borings on the mauka side could not be
drilled with the truck mounted drill rig Therefore one 1 boring was
drilled on the mauka side of the parking structure using portable drilling
equipment instead
Therefore the scope of our exploration included drilling probing
and sampling a total of three 3 borings to depths between 35 0 to 41 0
feet below the existing ground surface outside the existing parking
structure utilizing portable drilling equipments Also included were
laboratory testing of selected samples obtained engineering analysis and
evaluation of the field and laboratory data development of the foundation
design recommendations and report preparation

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to add an additional parking deck level to the
existing two story parking structure The loads from the new deck will be
applied to the existing parking structure foundations It is our
understanding that the existing continuous footings were originally
designed for the 3 levels of parking although only two levels were
constructed
The design loading under the existing parking decks is about 13 to 15
kips per linear foot The additional deck will increase the loads by
about 8 to 10 kips per linear foot for a total of about 24 kips per linear
foot
The layout of the building foundation is shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is situated on the makai side of Kalakaua Avenue adjacent to
the Honolulu Elks Club parking lot It is relatively level and occupied
by an existing two story reinforced concrete parking structure with
split level basements
The two existing split basement levels are at elevations about 7 83
feet and 3 66 feet above MSL
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface soil conditions for our analysis were developed by
drilling probing and sampling three 3 borings to about 35 0 to 41 0
feet below the existing ground surface outside the parking structure at
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

Based on the drilled borings outside of the parking structure it
appears that an approximately 7 to 11 feet thick upper crust consisting
primarily of dense coralline sands and gravels may exist beneath the
existing park structure Below this upper crust layer is 15 to 25 foot
thick layer of very loose to medium dense coralline sand and silty sand
deposits
Groundwater level was encountered during our field exploration and
it was found to be influenced by tidal conditions A high of 8 feet anda
low of approximately 14 feet below the existing ground level were
recorded
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The drilled borings disclosed that the site is underlain by coral
sand and gravel of varying consistency The upper 7 to 11 feet of sand
below the foundation level was found to be in a dense condition and
appeared suitable to provide adequate bearing support for the design
loads However the sand below this upper dense crust level was found to
be generally loose to medium dense consistency
Based on the drilled borings and our settlement analysis it appears
that the upper dense fill and coralline crust may provide adequate
foundation bearing capacity support for the existing and the proposed
additional parking deck loading
Since the above findings were based on the borings drilled outside of
the existing structure and that there is always a possibility that soil
conditions may actually vary under the footings we strongly suggest that
additional probings should be carried out near the existing footing
locations during construction The drilling probings should be used to
verify the uniformity and consistency of the surficial fill and coralline
crust immediately beneath the existing parking structure

Shallow Continuous Footing Foundations
The plans indicate that the existing structure is supported primarily
by continuous footing foundation extending across the length of the
structure The footings are believed to be 8 feet wide and embedded a
minimm of 2 feet below the floor elevation Based on this information
and the subsurface data from our recent borings it is our opinion that an
allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used for the existing
continuous footing foundations
Estimated foundation settlements due to the additional 10 kip per
linear foot loading are on the order of 1 inch total and one half inch
differential Due to the granular nature of the subsoil we believe the
settlements should occur rapidly
Additional Probings
Due to the limited access to the basement area and the need to
maintain the current parking operations at the site borings could not be
drilled within the interior portions of the existing structure
Therefore the borings were drilled only outside the structure and we
have assumed that the conditions in our three 3 outside borings are
representative of the entire area In order to verify this assumption we
recommend that additional probe drillings be drilled within the interior
of the basement level during construction We would be able to discuss
with you and your consultants the details and scope of this additional
work
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the foundations should be forwarded to
the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to construction
usage to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the new stairwell footing excavations be
observed and the settlements of the existing footings be monitored by
optical survey on referenced bench marks on the columns during
construction to see that the soil conditions at the footing excavations
and the settlements are consistent with the findings of our investigation

LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering
principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other
warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance and soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this soil exploration
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such as
cavities expansive soil and soft spots may occur in localized areas and
may require additional probing or corrections in the field Therefore
additional expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a
properly constructed project Some contingency fund in thus recommended
to accommodate these possible costs
