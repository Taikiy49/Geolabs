EARTHWORK OPERATION
The Nanakuli Makai Improvements project will involve the construction of new
roadway and stream crossing along Pohakunui Avenue extending from Nanakuli Avenue
to Laumania Avenue for a total length of approximately 1 700 lineal feet This report only
addresses the Phase 1 portion of the project extending from Nanakuli Avenue towards
Laumania Avenue for approximately 1 000 lineal feet The new roadway extension
generally consists of two 12 foot wide travelways with a 6 to 8 foot wide grassed area on
both sides
In general the roadway improvement project involved the subgrade preparation
and placement of the pavement structural section about 1 000 lineal feet for pavement
widening along a portion of the roadway

The earthwork operation was performed by Highway Construction Company Ltd
The earthwork operation consisted of clearing of the existing pavements over excavation
of the surface expansive clays where encountered preparation of pavement subgrades
placement and compaction of select borrow and base course
Following the clearing operation the areas within the new pavement limits were
generally over excavated to the bottom of the base course layer Where expansive
clays were encountered at the over excavated subgrade level the over excavation was
extended an additional 18 inches below the base course cushion to provide the
recommended 18 inches of select borrow subbase material The over excavated
subgrades were moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction The select borrow
subbase and base course were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction The maximum dry density of the materials referred to in this report were
determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
During the earthwork operation samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 1 of this report
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 11 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 2922 The results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on
Plate 2 of this report These tests were taken on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed engineered fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes and construction plans
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the
specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative
based on visual observation and hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations for the above project
observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in
general accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the referenced report dated
and subsequent recommendations

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in
the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
