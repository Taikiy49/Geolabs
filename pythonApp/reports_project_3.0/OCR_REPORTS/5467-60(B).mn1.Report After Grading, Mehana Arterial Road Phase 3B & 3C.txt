This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing
services performed during construction for the Mehana Arterial Roadway System Phases
3B and 3C at Kapolei on the Island of Oahu Hawaii from to
in accordance with our fee proposal dated August 14 2012
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Mehana at Kapolei development site is bordered by a drainage easement and
Roosevelt Road to the south Fort Barrette Road to the east and the future James
Campbell Estate residential development to the north and west The Arterial Roadway
System Phases 3B and 3C is on the central portion of the Mehana at Kapolei
development and includes segments of Road A Road B and Road W The general
layout of the roads is shown on the Mehana at Kapolei Alternate Phasing Plan Plate 1
and shown with more detail on Plates 2 1 through 2 5 Grading and Erosion Control Plan
for Phase 3B and 3C respectively
The grading and construction of this portion of the arterial road system was
implemented from to and generally consisted of
trench excavations for utility installation including backfills subgrade fills and pavement
base course preparations
This report summarizes our field observations and compaction testing services for
the site grading operations and infrastructure installation for the project

SITE GRADING
The site grading consisted primarily of cuts and fills up to about 1 foot At the onset
of grading the area was cleared and grubbed prior to filling Delta Construction performed
the site grading During the site grading operations samples of the imported materials
utilized as fill materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory
in general accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are
summarized on Plate 3 The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 In the roadway areas the fill
and backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938 Nuclear Gauge Method
Compaction test results are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 38 These tests were
performed on compacted surfaces of constructed fills and backfills Test locations and
elevations were estimated using on site survey stakes for reference These locations and
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction requirement were obtained and or approved by our field representative based
on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of full time and part time field observation of the contractor s
fill placement subgrade preparation compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade
soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on our field representative s observations and the referenced test results it
is our opinion the site grading and utility installation work for the Mehana at Kapolei Arterial
Roadway System Phases 3B and 3C project observed and tested by Geolabs in the
period covered by this report were performed in conformance with the general intent of
the recommendations in the following references

Chapter 14 of the Revised Ordinances of Honolulu 1990 as amended
2
