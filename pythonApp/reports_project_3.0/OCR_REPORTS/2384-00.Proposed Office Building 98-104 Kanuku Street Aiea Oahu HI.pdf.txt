INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical evaluation
performed for the proposed office building located at
Street in Aiea Hawaii The general location of the site and its
vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this geotechnical evaluation was to explore the
subsurface soil and water conditions underlying the proposed site to
provide pertinent soil parameters for use in the design of the foundation
of the proposed structure
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report

W O 2384 00 Page 2
The scope of the work performed in this evaluation was in general
accordance with our proposal dated August 25 1989 Our scope of work
included drilling probing and sampling a total of four 4 borings to
depths from 53 to 75 6 feet below the existing ground surface laboratory
testing engineering evaluation of the field and laboratory findings and
report preparation
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
It is our understanding that a two 2 story reinforced concrete
office building structure about 36 by 103 feet in plan dimensions is
planned for the project site
Parking areas around the proposed building are also planned for the
development
GENERAL SITE CONDITION
The project site is located at the corner of Kamehameha Highway and
Kanuku Street in the Waimalu Shopping Center
The area is generally flat and unoccupied
A Chevron Service Station previously occupied the site The service
station has been demolished and the underground gasoline tanks have been
removed
SUBSURFACE CONDITION
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling four
4 borings to depths of 22 0 to 75 6 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
feet respectively in an attempt to identify the extent of the soft
deposit

The borings encountered a 3 5 to 4 5 foot thick surface fill layer
consisting of stiff brown clayey silt and stiff clay with gravel underlain
by a 42 0 foot thick lagoonal deposit consisting of very soft dark gray
clayey silt and very loose dark gray silty sand Below the lagoonal
deposit an alluvium deposit was encountered consisting of stiff dark gray
to brown clay and very dense brown silty gravel with sand to 75 6 feet

Groundwater was encountered in the borings at a depth of

Artesian water was encountered in Borings B 2 and B 3 at a depth of
approximately 65 0 and 55 0 feet respectively The artesian head was
approximately 2 feet above the existing ground surface at the time of our
exploration These two 2 borings were grouted to prevent leakage of the
artesian water
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The project site is underlain by a 42 foot thick layer of very soft
and loose lagoonal deposits
Estimated settlements on the order of 2 inches are anticipated due
compression of the soft layer from the weight of the proposed reinforced
concrete structure If practicable a lighter type of structure should be
considered to reduce the building loads and the resulting settlements
If the settlements for the spread footing design are not tolerable
then pile foundations may be considered
Spread Footing Foundation
Spread or continuous footing foundation bearing on the surface fill
layer were evaluated for the two 2 story reinforced concrete office
building planned for the site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2384 00 Page 4
A maximum allowable soil bearing pressure of 1250 p s f may be used
for the design
a
For the estimated column loads of 77 kips and wall loads of 2 kips
per linear foot estimated settlements are on the order of 2 inches
If practicable a lighter steel frame and or wood structure should
be considered to reduce the building loads and the resulting settlements
It would be desirable to limit the maximm column load to about 10 kips to
limit the settlements to tolerable levels
The footings should be placed at a level as high as possible to
reduce the footing load applied over the underlying compressible deposits
Pile Foundation
If the estimated settlement presented above for spread footing design
are not tolerable then pile foundations may be considered
The piles would develop their load capacity from end bearing in the
very dense silty gravel layer encountered at depths of about 50 0 to 75 0
feet below the existing ground surface Our analysis indicates that with
the use of a 12 inch square prestressed concrete pile an allowable design
capacity of 50 tons may be used
anticipated
Pile Driving
We recommend that a pile driving hammer which can deliver a minimm
of 32 000 foot pounds of energy be used for the pile driving The
piles should be driven to a resistance of about 40 blows per foot for
the last foot of penetration or 10 blows per inch

Page 5
The driving process should be continuous without interruption for
each pile A pile may be rejected or its design capacity may be
modified if the driving progress is interrupted for more than four
4 hours
During easy driving when the pile tip is within soft loose soils
the hammer stroke or energy should be carefully controlled so that
excessive tensile stresses are not developed in the pile
The pile driving should be observed by a qualified geotechnical
engineer to see that the piles are driven to the sufficient depth or
Test Pile Program
A test pile program is recommended to aid in the development of a
safe and economical pile foundation design for the proposed
buildings
The test program should be undertaken to fulfill the following
objectives
1 To more accurately determine the production pile length s
2 To help evaluate and modify if necessary the pile driving
criteria
We recommend that about ten 10 percent of the total number of
piles be test driven These test piles may later be incorporated
into the actual foundations of the proposed office building

Splice cans should be provided at the site during test pile
driving to expedite splicing if required Splice cans may be
fabricated of high grade steel one fourth 1 4 of an inch thick
and should be at least 6 feet long

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the recommendations given herein If this review is not made the
geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation
of the recommendations
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations

Laboratory Testing
The moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of the test are presented on the Boring Logs at
the appropriate sample depths
Four 4 one inch ring swell tests were performed on natural and
remolded samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils The
test results are summarized on Plate B 1
Two 2 direct shear tests were performed on undisturbed samples of
the subsoils to evaluate the shear strength parameters The test results
are presented on Plates B 2 and B 3
Two 2 Atterberg Limits were performed on selected samples of the
soils to aid in the classification of the materials The test results are
presented on Plate B 4
Two 2 consolidation tests were performed on selected soil samples
to evaluate their compressibility The test results are plotted on Plates
B 5 and B 6
One 1 unconsolidated undrained triaxial compression test was
performed on a selected soil sample to evaluate the shear strength
parameters The test result is presented on Plate B 7

Field Exploration

probing four 4 borings to depths of 22 0 to 75 6 feet below the existing

and textural examination in the field by our geologist who continuously
monitored the drilling and excavating operations The classifications
were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory
The soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving a standard
penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with a
140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
