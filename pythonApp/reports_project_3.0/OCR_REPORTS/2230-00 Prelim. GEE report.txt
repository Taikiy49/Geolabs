INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed expansion of Hookipa Cottage
facility at the Women s Community Correctional Center WCCC within the
Koolau Correctional Complex in Kailua Oahu Hawaii
of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate
The exploration was performed in general accordance with our proposal

This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and presents
our preliminary geotechnical engineering recommendations based on the data
obtained during the exploration These recommendations are intended to
provide preliminary design input for the site grading general siting of
the proposed buildings and evaluation of foundation types and may not be
adequate for detailed design construction estimation or bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed site improvements consist of several irregular shaped
buildings to the south of the existing Hookipa Cottage in a roughly
rectangular shaped area about 900 feet in length and about 500 feet wide
Preliminary sketches of the housing units indicate that 1 to 3 story
reinforced concrete buildings are planned and may be constructed on fill
embankment across drainageways or on relatively level ground
Paved access roads and a parking area are also proposed in the
western portion of the site
Details of the proposed site improvements grading plans and final
building location plans were not available at the time this report was
prepared

The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
general subsurface soil conditions at the site to provide preliminary

and evaluation of foundation type to be utilized for this project

SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located about 3 000 feet southeast of the intersection of
Kailua Road and Kalanianaole Highway in Kailua Oahu Hawaii Mount
Olomana lies about one mile south of this 9 5 acre site
The site generally slopes down in a northerly direction The western
and eastern portions are parallel and dip approximately from elevation 210
to 190 feet with an approximate gradient of 4 The middle portion is
characterized by a drainageway with a maximm elevation difference about
14 feet The side slopes of this drainageway slope has a slope ratio
varying from ten horizontal to one vertical 10H 1V to three horizontal
to one vertical 3H 1V This drainageway runs down longitudinally
towards the north with an average gradient of 4 towards the north Its
bottom elevation varies from elevation 210 feet on the upper southern end
to elevations 174 feet at the lower northern section
It appears that the proposed buildings will be sited in the southerm
two thirds of the site which is currently open area covered with grass
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The soil conditions encountered by the seven 7 borings generally
indicate that the site is underlain by residual soils developed from the
in situ weathering of Koolau Basalts Boring 3 6 and 7 which
were drilled in the high ground of the western and the eastern portions of
the site encountered stiff to very stiff reddish brown clayey silt soils
to the maximum depths explored of 26 5 to 31 5 feet below the existing
ground surface These clayey silt soils have Standard Penetration N
values ranging from 10 to 60 blows per foot with soil water contents
ranging from 30 to 70 percent Similar clayey silt strata were
encountered in the remaining two 4 and 5 in the
drainageway However our exploration and laboratory testing indicate

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a
geotechnical point of view provided that the recommendations presented in
this report are closely followed in design and during construction
Our exploration encountered moderately compressible medium stiff to
stiff deposits of residual clayey silt with moderate swelling potential in
all seven 7 borings drilled It appears that these soils are generally
competent and will provide adequate bearing to support the proposed
3 story buildings on spread or continuous footing foundations provided
that our recommendations are followed
Ground Settlement
Significant fill ground settlements are anticipated in the
drainageway area due to the loads which will be imposed by new fill placed
over relatively thick moderately compressible soils The estimated
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2230 00 Page 6
settlements due to the placement of 14 feet of fill are om the order of
2 to 4 inches The estimated time required to achieve 95 of the
estimated settlement is roughly two 2 to four 4 months
It is therefore recommended that site grading within the drainageway
area be completed at least four 4 months prior to construction of the
structures and utilities Otherwise a preloading or surcharge program
will be required to shorten the waiting period These recommendations are
intended to minimize the amount of settlement which the proposed
structures and utilities located within this area may experience
Settlement Gauges
It is recommended that an adequate number of settlement gauges be
installed prior to and after completion of site filling within the
drainageway area to monitor the rate and magnitude of the fill ground
settlements Construction of buildings utilities retaining walls and
pavements should not commence until readings obtained from the installed
settlement gauges indicate that the remaining settlements will be
tolerable to the proposed facilities Based on the limited soil data
obtained from the exploration it is preliminary estimated that the
waiting period of fill ground settlement would be roughly about four 4
to six 6 months
Settlement gauges consisting of conventional settlement platforms
see Plate 5 may be utilized for this project They should be monitored
by surveyor s level readings once every two weeks for the first month and
once a month thereafter until settlement has slowed to a tolerable
rate All readings obtained from these settlement gauges should be
submitted immediately to the project geotechnical engineer for review and
analyses

Site Preparation
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared of vegetation and deleterious
materials The resulting grub and spoil material should be properly
disposed off site or stockpiled for use in landscaping Any soft
weak or yielding areas exposed during the clearing operations should
be over excavated to firm ground and then backfilled with
well compacted engineered fill
Generally the on site soils appear suitable for use as engineered
fill Since most of the on site soils are fine grained they should
be compacted by mechanical compaction Compaction by water jetting
or ponding is not suitable for the on site soils

Fill Placement
_Our exploration and laboratory testing indicate that the on site
soils have in situ moisture contents on the order of 20 to 30 percent
higher than optimm moisture content for compaction These high
in situ moisture contents reduce the workability of soils and
frequently necessitate double or triple handling of the soils to
reduce the moisture to a level where the standard 90 percent
compaction may be achieved Previous experience in similar areas
with relatively high rainfall and high in situ soil moisture contents
indicates that a reduction of the level of compaction to 85 percent
will still provide adequate bearing strength to support relatively
light structures Therefore to facilitate earthwork construction
for this project we are recommending such a reduction

All fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight 8

Foundations
It is our opinion that spread or continuous wall footings may be used
for the proposed buildings A preliminary allowable bearing value of
about 3000 p s f may be used for foundations bearing on the residual soil
and on fill placed as recommended above These bearing values are for
total dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third when
temporary wind and seismic loads are considered Estimated footing
settlements are on the order of one inch total and one half of an inch
differential assuming the initial site filling is completed first and
that the building construction is delayed until the fill settlements have
reached tolerable limits For more details refer to the section under
Ground Settlement
Individual spread footings and continuous wall footings should be
embedded to a minimum depth of two 2 feet below the lowest adjacent
finished grade It is recommended that the building foundations should be
located away from the top of fill cut slopes with a minimm set back of
about ten 10 feet from the outer edge of the footing to the outside face
of the slope Additional footing embedment would be required for the
footings located closer than ten 10 feet from the slope

placed and compacted conforming to the requirements specified in Site

subgrade soil Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive lateral
earth resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected

Slabs On Grade
Concrete slabs on grade are feasible for the floors of the proposed
buildings A minimum of six 6 inches of imported non expansive select
borrow material compacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximum dry density
per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 should be placed below the slab subgrade
The select material should be less than three 3 inches in maximm
dimension with less than 15 passing the 200 sieve and a Plasticity
Index less than 20 per ASIM Test Method D 4318 84 The material should be
free of organic matter debris and other deleterious material A 4 inch
cushion layer consisting of 3 Fine gravel should be provided below the
slabs The slab subgrades should be kept moist prior to concrete
placement
Where slabs are situated partly on cut and partly on fill there isa
potential for some slab cracking due to the difference in stiffness
between the two materials
In order to reduce the differential settlement between portions of
the slab on cut and fill we recommend that the cut portion be
over excavated for a minimm depth of 2 feet below the bottom slab and
recompacted to 85 of its maximm dry density per ASIM Test Method
D 1557 78
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration and
subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture barrier is
recommended to be installed below the slabs
The slabs on grade should have thickened edges which extend dowm a
12 inch minimm below the outside finish ground surface elevations

Drainage
The finished grade outside the building should be shaped to shed
water away from foundations and to avoid ponding conditions Also it is
recommended that gutter systems be installed on the proposed buildings and
that the discharge be diverted away from the foundation and pavement
areas Excessive landscape watering near foundations and slab should also
be avoided
Planters or landscaping next to foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are generally essential for the proper
performance of the above foundation recommendations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or
loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled tight against the wall or slab footing immediately after
setting of the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

Laboratory Testing
Moisture content determinations were performed on selected samples as
an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The
results are presented on the Boring Logs at the corresponding sample
depths
One 1 Atterberg Limits test was performed on a selected sample to
evaluate the classification of the sampled materials The results are
presented on the Boring Logs at the corresponding sample depths
Two 2 swell tests were performed on selected samples to aid in
evaluating the shrink swell potential of the materials sampled The
results are shown on Plate B 1
Two 2 consolidation tests were performed on selected soil samples
to evaluate their compressibility The results are plotted on Plates B 2
and B 3
Three 3 direct shear tests were performed on a selected sample to
aid in evaluating the in situ strength of the material sampled The
results are shown on Plates B 4 thru B 6
