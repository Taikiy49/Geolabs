This report summarizes the findings of our geotechnical engineering review of
available information for the existing subsurface conditions at the project site
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves a study to select several potential sites for the location of new
2 to 6 Million Gallon MG reservoirs to supplement the existing Honolulu 180 System
The purpose of our work on the project is to provide preliminary geotechnical engineering
information for the sites to aid in evaluating their suitability for the intended use
This report addresses our review of available information including published
geologic maps soil surveys and aerial photography for the project site
SITE
The Kalauao Site BWS 1 is in Kalauao Valley above Pearl Country Club and
adjacent to the existing Kalauao Wells site Ground surface elevations range from about
160 to 210 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL
Based on a geologic map published by the US Geological Survey the site is
situated in a location which appears to be a contact between Koolau Basalt QTkl and
Older Alluvium QTao On this basis it is anticipated that the site is underlain by shallow
residual soils saprolite and weathered basaltic rock formation derived from the in place
weathering of Koolau Basalt lavas and the alluvial soils are anticipated to be very stiff to
hard clayey silts with gravel and cobbles
Using Google Earth and the Preliminary Grading Plan the geomorphology of the
site was reviewed The general morphology confirms that the site straddles a contact

Based on our review of the site geology and geomorphology it appears that the site

support a new reservoir However special design considerations might be required if the
structure does straddle between the volcanic rock formation and alluvial deposits Hard
excavation may also be required for grading work in the volcanic rock formation
