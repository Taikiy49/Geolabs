INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this soil investigation was to evaluate the
subsurface soil conditions at the site to provide data for
the design and construction of the proposed subdivision
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed development will consist of clearing and
grading the site for 14 single family residential lots with
a 44 foot and 32 foot wide roadway into the site Cuts and
fills generally less than 5 to 10 feet are planned mostly
along the proposed access road A retaining wall about 10
feet high is planned at the western end of the proposed
44 foot roadway The wall will be situated on a small
terrace in the relatively steep slope between the upper
flat area and the lower stream
Light weight single story structures would be constructed
on the individual residential lots
SOIL CONDITIONS
Geology
The site of the development is located roughly midway be
tween the Likelike and Pali Highways on the windward slope
of the Koolau Range This area is part of a deeply eroded
and weathered section typified by thick alluvial deposits

Surface Conditions
The project site is located at the ends of Apuakea Street
and Apiki Street in the Kaneohe area The topography over
most of the central portion of the site is gently sloping
down toward the northwest The terrain near the western
and southwestern boundaries of the site Slopes down steeply
toward the existing Kamooalii Stream which is about 20
feet lower than the project site
The project site is presently vacant but heavily covered
with grass and Hale Koa trees

testing is covered in the Appendix along with the boring
logs and laboratory test results

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed competent bearing soils at
the site Shallow foundation bearing on the undisturbed
in situ clayey silt will provide adequate support for the
proposed light single story structures and retaining wall
for this subdivision
Laboratory swell tests on the on site soils indicate low to
moderate expansion potential Therefore slab on grade can
be designed without special provisions for expansive soil
conditions
Site Grading
About 5 feet of fill was encountered at Boring No 1 This

general area should be proofrolled with the compaction equip
ment to detect near surface soft pockets If encountered
the soft pockets should be overexcavated and replaced with
compacted fill
Cut and fills generally less than 5 to 10 feet are planned
along the proposed access road Most of the proposed lots
will not be graded
The reddish brown clayey silt soils at the site may be used
for fill and footing backfill Fill and backfill should be
placed in horizontal loose lifts of 10 inches maximum and
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density established by
ASTM Test Method D 1557
Slope ratios of 2 1 H V are recommended for cut and fill
slopes
The backfill behind the proposed retaining wall should be
properly keyed and benched into the existing ground to bond
the new fill to the existing ground and prevent excess
lateral pressures on the new retaining wall
Foundations
For the proposed light structures spread or continuous
footings are recommended An allowable bearing value of
2 500 p s f can be used for footing resting on the stiff
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 997 00 P 6
in situ soil The bearing value can be increased by one
third when considering seismic and wind loads
The footings should have aminimum width of 12 inches and
be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade and on the above recommended clayey silt material
Footings located near utility trenches should extend to the
bottom of the trench
Surface water should be diverted away from the building
foundations
Some of the proposed residential structures may be located
near or over the tops of the existing Slopes about 20 feet
high
To resist the downhill creep movement of the near surface
soil the footings and slab perimeter footings close to the
top of slope should extend down a sufficient depth to obtain
a minimum horizontal set back distance of about 6 feet from
the slope face
Retaining Wall
A retaining wall up to about 10 feet high is planned at the
northwestern slope at the western end of the 44 foot wide
proposed roadway

The recommendations given under foundations should be fol
lowed for the design of the retaining wall foundation
The retaining wall should be designed for an equivalent
fluid pressure of 40 p c f A friction factor of 0 40 can
be utilized to determine the sliding resistance of the
retaining wall foundation
The site is located in a high rainfall area of Kaneohe
Therefore it is recommended that the retaining wall construc
tion include a subsurface drainage Well graded granular
material such as 3B Fine rock should be used as backfill
behind the retaining wall to prevent the build up of hydro
static pressure behind the wall during periods of high
precipitation
Slab on Grade
It is recommended that slab on grade should be placed over
a 4 inch layer of base course compacted to 95 of its maxi
mum dry density
The slab perimeter footing should be embedded a minimum of
12 inches below the outside finished grade
The subgrade should be protected from drying and kept moist
during construction It should be compacted to a minimum
of 90 of maximum dry density

To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
a polyethylene moisture barrier should be used
Roadway Pavement
Based upon the on site materials and City County of Hono
lulu design standards for flexible pavement and light traffic
the following pavement section could be considered for
preliminary planning
2 inch Asphalt Concrete Pavement
6 inch Base Course
6 inch Select Borrow
14 inch Total Pavement Thickness
Final pavement sections should be determined during the
grading operations by testing the actual subgrade materials
and establishing expansion and CBR values
The base course should be crushed rock or other granular
material with a minimum CBR of 85 and conforming with Section
31 2 Standard Specifications for Public Work Construction
The select borrow should be crusher run quarry waste coral
sand or cinder with a minimum CBR of 25 and conforming to
Section 30 2 Standard Specifications for Public Works Con
struction The select bottow should be compacted to 95
of its maximum dry density

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations filling and
backfill operations be observed by a soils engineer during
construction to see that adequate bearing has been obtained
and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site

split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
