Dear
This report presents the results of our Soil and Foundation
Investigation for the proposed reconstruction of the residence
The site is located on the Ewa side of Kalihi Street adjacent
to Kalihi Stream We performed a limited site inspection of
the residence in July 1972
The purposes of our investigation were to evaluate subsurface
soil conditions and to develope recommendations for foundation
design of the proposed reconstruction
During the course of our investigation the conditions seemed
critical enough for us to inform you by letter concerning the
potential collapse of the residence

Site Conditions
It is our understanding that 111 was placed on the site approx

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our study has revealed that the site is undergoing a slump
slide where the fill is settling and at the same time sliding
downhill The rate of the movement appears to be fairly
rapid judging from the observations made by this firm over a
period of a year
In a letter to the residence owners dated 28 August 1973 it
was suggested that the house may be in a state of immenent
collapse and that a structural engineer should assess the
residence
The residence may be reconstructed on the site if all building
loads are transferred to the basalt rock in accordance with
the recommendations present in this report
Foundations
The present existing loose fill will not be able to provide
adequate support for the reconstructed residence as it is still
undergoing settlement and downslope movement
It is not recommended that this fill be removed and recompacted
because the exact extent of the fill is unknown and is most
Page 5
W O 476 00
likely part of a larger fill Any disturbance to this fill
may accelerate movements of adjacent residences
It is therefore recommended that the reconstructed residence
be entirely supported on pre drilled cast in place piles
or caissons bearing directly on the basalt rock found from
10 to 12 feet below the existing grade The pre drilled
pile or caisson foundations should penetrate a minimum of
two feet into competent rock
A bearing value of 15 kips per square foot may be used for
foundations resting on the basalt rock
The piles or caissons should be designed to resist the lateral
forces of the downslope movement of the slide mass We
recommend using a value of 1 000 pounds per lineal foot of
penetration through all fill soil and badly weathered rock
For lateral design an allowable load of 1 000 pounds per
square foot of depth in firm bedrock and a coefficient of
friction of 0 8 may be used
It is also recommended that the ground floors not be allowed
to rest directly on grade but be supported by the foundations
because of the active nature of the slide
All utility lines should also be tied to the foundation and
not buried underground The existing sewer line should be
inspected for leaks and if any found repaired
If the above recommendations are followed the reconstructed

residence should be protected against future distress
However the fill will still be subjected to settling and
slidings The downward movement could be restricted if a

Because of the critical nature of reconstructing a residence
on this site it is recommended that our firm be consulted
