Test Pile Driving Report
Kekaulike Revitalization Project
Ewa Block
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
In general accordance with the scope of services outlined in our revised proposal dated
we have observed the test pile driving at the subject project
A diesel hammer was used to drive the 16 inch octagonal precast and
prestressed concrete piles It is our understanding that the pile hammer is capable of delivering
a maximum driving energy of approximately 69 898 foot pounds
A total of 36 test piles consisting of Pile 82
268
295 and 310 were driven to depths of about 62 to 93 feet below the existing
ground surface at the site Due to the limited space at the original test pile location Pile No 134
was driven in place of Pile No 131 Pile No 246 was driven in place of Pile No 250 due to the
presence of a telephone pole at the original test pile location The test pile location plan driving
records and summary are attached for your reference
Pile No 175 was selected for a static load test and was load tested up to two 2 times
the design capacity of 55 tons per pile The load test data indicated net settlements of less than
one tenth of an inch The pile load test report will be transmitted in a separate report
Based on our pile driving observation and the static load test results we believe that the
test piles have been driven to sufficient depths and resistance to provide the design capacity
As a result we estimate that the remaining project production piles may consist of ninety three
shown on the attached pile location pian The project production piles should be driven with the
same hammer with the following driving criteria

The pile driving may be terminated when the blow counts reach 10 blows per inch for the
last 3 inches 3 or when the blow counts steadily increase with depth and reach an
aggregated blow count of 36 blows for the last foot The pile driving may also be
terminated when an embedment length of 85 feet is achieved or as requested by the field
representative from Geolabs Hawaii
Based on our vibration monitoring results for the test pile driving operation we
recommend that predrilling be performed for the remaining production piles to reduce any
potential adverse effects on the existing buildings adjacent to the project site Predrilling depths
of 15 and 30 feet below the existing ground surface may be used as shown on the attached pile
location plan Deeper predrilling depths may be required to penetrate through any hard coral
formation encountered at the bottom of the proposed predrilling depths
