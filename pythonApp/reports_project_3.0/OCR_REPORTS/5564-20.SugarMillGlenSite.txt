EARTHWORK OPERATION
Highway Construction Company Ltd performed the site grading operations utility
installation and pavement construction for the Roads X and Z of the Sugar Mill Glen
Site project The site grading consisted of clearing and grubbing subgrade preparation
placement and compaction of fills and backfills pavement construction and utility trench
excavation and backfill
The excavated subgrades underneath pavement areas were scarified to a depth of
about 6 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor
Fill and backfill materials required for the project were generally placed in
near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor
New sewer water and drain lines were installed to serve the new development
The trench backfill was placed in maximum 10 inch level loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to no less than 90 percent relative compaction Where trenches are below
pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement grade was
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for
testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in
our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on the attached Plate 2
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as needed basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction
procedures and soil conditions were noted A total of 318 field density tests including
retests of failed areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance
with ASTM D 2922 and D 3017 Compaction test results are summarized on Plates 3 1
through 3 23 These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing
and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed
to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained

occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
