Project Description
The project site is located to the northeast of the intersection of Alakawa Street and
Nimitz Highway in the lwilei area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The property
is approximately triangular in shape and encompasses approximately 3 67 acres in land
area The project site is located within the proximity of the existing Nimitz Center to the
west and the Home Depot and Costco stores across Alakawa Street on the east
Based on the conceptual plan provided we understand that a Best Buy retail store
is planned at the project site The new retail store is anticipated to be a one story structure
with rooftop parking covering approximately 50 000 square feet in floor area In addition a
new parking lot and loading zones subjected to container truck traffic are planned to serve
the new retail development The layout of the new retail development is shown on the

Test Pile Program
A test pile program which consisted of driving 14 concrete piles at selected
locations across the building footprint and driveway ramp was conducted on October 25
and 26 2004 The locations of the test piles are shown on the Pile Location Plan Plate 2
The purpose of the test pile program was to establish the driveability of the concrete piles
given the subsurface conditions at the project site the selected driving equipment and
methods of concrete pile installation In addition the test pile program also provided
information on the driving resistance and contours of the load bearing strata across the
project site which was used to estimate the length of the production concrete piles
Prior to the pile driving operations the pile locations were predrilled up to a depth of
about 10 feet below the existing ground surface to facilitate the pile driving operations
through the surface fill crust A hydraulic impact hammer was used to
drive the 16 5 inch octagonal precast pre stressed concrete piles test piles Our field
observations indicated that a stroke height of about 3 feet used for the
pile hammer had a maximum rated energy of approximately 33 000 foot pounds per blow
of the hammer
Each test pile consisted of an upper 50 foot and a lower 40 foot pile segment
connected by a splice can A total of 14 test piles were driven to tip elevations ranging
from about 64 to 74 feet Mean Sea Level MSL A summary of the test pile driving
conditions and observations is provided on Plate 3 of this report for reference

Static Pile Load Test
In accordance with the project specifications a compressive pile load test was
conducted on a selected test pile designated as TP 7 on The test
pile was loaded to a test load of 315 kips 225 percent of the design load of 140 kips in
17 5 kip load increments in general accordance with the Quick Loading Procedure of
ASTM Test Designation D 1143 Detailed discussions and results of the static pile load
test are provided in the report entitled Pile Load Test Result Test Pile No TP 7 Best Buy
Honolulu Honolulu Oahu Hawaii dated transmitted under separate
cover
Based on our field observations and the results of the test pile program we believe
that the driving equipment and methods of installation are acceptable for installing the
specified production piles in general accordance with the project specifications In
addition it is our professional opinion that the test piles have been driven to sufficient
depths and or resistance to provide the design load capacity of 140 kips 70 tons with a
factor of safety of 2 25
Based on the results of the test pile program we believe that the following
production pile driving criteria should be used for the project The production piles for the
project should be driven with the same hammer delivering a rated energy of approximately
33 000 foot pounds at the end of the pile driving operations The pile driving operation
may be terminated when one of the following driving criteria is satisfied
1 When the aggregate blow counts are at least 50 blows per foot for the last
foot or 5 blows per inch for the last 3 inches
2 When driven to about the full depth of the production pile ordered 76 feet
3 When requested by the field engineer from Geolabs
Based on our analyses of the test pile driving behavior the penetration resistance
the static load test and the required design load capacity we recommend that a
production pile length of 76 feet cast in one pile segment be considered for the above
project Splice cans should be made available during the course of the production pile
driving operations to allow splicing of the piles if needed It should be noted that some of
the production piles have already been cast by the general contractor prior to the
completion of the static load test
