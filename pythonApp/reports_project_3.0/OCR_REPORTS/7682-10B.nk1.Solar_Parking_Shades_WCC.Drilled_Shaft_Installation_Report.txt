PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The project site is located at the WCC campus in the Kaneohe area on the Island of 
Oahu, Hawaii. The college campus is approximately bounded by Windward Oahu Mental 
Health facility to the north, Pookea Street to the east, Hawaii State Hospital to the south, 
and a residential subdivision to the west. 
The new solar parking shade structures were constructed in four existing parking lot 
areas: two near the campus main entrance, one adjacent to Paliku Theatre, and one to the 
east of the college campus center. Based on the provided layout plans, the project 
included constructing eight solar parking shade structures at the above four locations. In 
general, the project sites slope downward from the southwest (mauka) to the northeast 
(makai) with ground surface elevations ranging from about +260 to +197 feet Mean Sea 
Level (MSL). 
This report summarizes our observation during the drilled shaft foundation 
construction for the Solar Parking Shade Structures project at WCC only. Drilco Inc. was 
the drilled shaft contractor. 
DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION 
A total of 57 drilled shafts were installed to depths between about 10 and 16 feet 
below the ground surface. The production shafts were installed between April 5 and 
June 13, 2019 for the project. The approximate drilled shaft locations are shown on the 
Overall Drilled Shaft Location Plan, Plate 2.1, and the individual Drilled Shaft Location 
Plans, Plates 2.2 through 2.4. 
The drilling and concrete placement operations were conducted under the 
near-continuous observation of our representative.  During the drilled shaft construction, 
logs were maintained for each drilled shaft, which included information pertaining to shaft 
number, size and length of the shaft, soil and/or rock conditions encountered during 
excavation, and concrete placement.  A summary of the drilled shaft installation is 
presented on the Drilled Shaft Installation Summary, Plates 3.1 and 3.2.  Individual Drilled 
Shaft Records are provided on Plates 1.1 through 57.4 of the Appendix. 
The drilled shafts were drilled with a Low Drill rig mounted to a Komatsu excavator. 
The drill rig was equipped with a 36-inch auger, core barrel and clean-out bucket. The 
drilled shafts were excavated down to elevations ranging from about +180.5 to +244.9 feet 
MSL. After drilling to tip elevation, loose/soft spoils were removed from the excavation with 
the 36-inch diameter clean-out bucket. 
After each shaft was excavated to the tip elevation and cleaned out, the rebar cage 
was suspended in the borehole to the design elevation. Concrete was placed into the 
excavated shafts by using a top pour method. HC&D supplied the concrete with a 
compressive strength of 4,500 pounds per square inch (psi). Each shaft was inspected by 
our engineer prior to rebar and concrete placement.
