PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site for the new Big K Mart Store 9430 in Kapolei is an irregularly shaped
vacant parcel encompassing approximately 10 acres in area The project site is located
immediately southwest of the existing parking lot for the Kapolei Theater Complex in Kapolei
on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The property is approximately bounded on the southeast by
Kamokila Boulevard on the southwest by vacant land and on the northwest by a concrete
drainage ditch running parallel to the off ramp from the Interstate Route H 1 Highway as
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The development consists of a new Big K Mart Store of approximately 122 811 square
feet in floor space located within the northwestern half of the property The new Big K Mart
Store will be constructed of precast concrete tilt up wall sections and reinforced concrete
slabs on grade The design finished floor elevation for the new building is set at 71 feet
Mean Sea Level MSL Shallow foundations consisting of isolated spread and continuous
footings were recommended to support the interior column and Perimeter wall loads of the
building structure

SITE PREPARATION
As previously mentioned the site grading work consisted of placement and compaction
of fills on the order of about 2 to 8 feet thick to achieve the design finished grades for the
project The earthwork operations and foundation excavations were performed by RHS Lee
Inc
Observations during our geotechnical engineering exploration indicated that abundant
shrinkage cracks and remnants of previously excavated and backfilled test pits existed across
the project site Shrinkage cracks located adjacent to the building foundations are potentially
detrimental since water can readily infiltrate through the crack opening and soften the
subsurface soils around the building foundations thus affecting their performance In addition
it was not clear whether the previously excavated test pits have been properly backfilled
Therefore special subgrade preparation procedures were followed during the site grading
operations for the project development
After clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrades were proof rolled with a 10 ton
vibratory drum roller a minimum of six passes to assist in detecting and collapsing
near surface voids and loose zones created by the shrinkage cracks and remnants of the old
test pits The exposed subgrades were proof rolled without moisture conditioning of the
subgrade soils at the pre existing moisture contents Loose areas disclosed during the proof
rolling operation were backfilled with general fill material compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction The proof rolling operations were conducted under the
observation of a representative from Geolabs

Subsequent to the proof rolling operations the subgrades were scarified to a depth of
about 12 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance
with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified
Proctor
Following the subgrade preparation the excavated coralline materials from the borrow
site located in the vicinity were placed as general fill at the project site The material was also
used for the 24 inch thick non expansive select granular fill layer required below the building
slabs sidewalk slabs and pavement areas
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
During placement of the general fill the fill materials were generally placed in near level
lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor The 24 inch thick non expansive select granular fill
under the building slabs sidewalk slabs and pavement areas were compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative compaction During the foundation excavation and preparation the
footing subgrades were compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction prior to
the placement of reinforcing steel and concrete
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions were
noted A total of 830 field density tests including retests of failed areas were performed using
the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test Designations D 2922 and D 3017
The results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 37 of
this report
The tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating
from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field
representative based on visual observation and hand probing
