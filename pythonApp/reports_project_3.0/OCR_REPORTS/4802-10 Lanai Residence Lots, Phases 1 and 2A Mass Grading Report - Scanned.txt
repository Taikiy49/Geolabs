This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed
during the mass grading pad grading utility installation and road construction operations
from to December 2005 for the above project in accordance with our
fee proposals dated May 21 2002 and subsequent addendums
Project Considerations
The project site is located at the west end of Fifth Street in Lanai City on the
Island of Lanai Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project site
covers an area approximately 10 acres and is bounded by existing single family houses
to the east and undeveloped pastureland on the north south and west sides as shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2 The project involves site grading for the development of
46 single family residential lots
The mass grading work consisted of cuts and fills of 2 to 4 feet in the majority of the
site
Site Grading
The earthwork operations for the project were performed by Goodfellow
Brothers Inc At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits were
generally cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials Materials
generated from the clearing operations were disposed of off site Following the clearing
and grubbing operations the areas to be cut were excavated Finish grades were
compacted using a sheepsfoot and or smooth drum vibratory roller
The rubbish fill encountered at TP 1 during the geotechnical exploration on the
southeastern portion of the site for future Lots 1170 1171 was removed and disposed of
Also loose material encountered in the drainage ditch at the southeastern corner of
project site was removed

In general on site soil materials generated from the excavation were used for the
general fill area The on site fill materials generally consisted of clayey silts and silty clay
soils The expansion potential of the on site fill material ranged from moderate to high
Samples of the fill and backfill materials utilized during construction were obtained and
transported to our office for laboratory testing The maximum dry densities and optimum
moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with American
Society of Testing and Material ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor The test results are
summarized on Plate 3 of this report
In general fill materials consisting of on site material less than 6 inches in size were
placed for the fills and utility trench backfills Field density tests were performed using the
nuclear density gauge ASTM D 2922 and ASTM D 3017 During the fill and backfill
operations the materials were generally placed in near level lifts brought to near the
optimum moisture and compacted to obtain the specified degree of compaction based on
visual observations and or field density tests Periodic field density tests were performed
during the fill and backfill operations to confirm our field observations
A total of 430 field density tests including re tests of failed areas were performed
The results of the compaction tests performed during construction of the above project are
summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 35 of this report Test locations and elevations were
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes where available These
locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the

House Foundations
The on site soils have moderate to high shrink swell potential House
foundations including footings and slabs should be designed for these expansive soil
condition The services of a qualified geotechnical engineer should be retained for
house foundation design and subsequent additions or improvements
Limitations
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or finished
grades on the project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
knowledge and written acknowledgment Lot owners should be advised not to block the
surface drainage and to obtain professional consultation in the planning design and
construction of any future improvements Lot owners should also be advised not to water
excessively adjacent to the house structure
