PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the vicinity of Duke Kahanamoku Pool at the
University of Hawaii campus in Manoa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the
information provided we understand that it is planned to replace six 75 foot floodlight
poles Four light poles will be relocated and two light poles will be constructed at the
existing locations Based on the information provided by the project structural engineer
we understand that an overturning moment of up to 794 foot kips will be acting at the top
of the light pole foundation

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
We drilled a total of three borings at the project site Boring Nos 1 and 2 were
drilled on the north side of the site and Boring No 3 was drilled on the south side Our
field exploration disclosed different subsurface conditions on two sides of the project site
Boring Nos 1 and 2 generally encountered about 11 feet of fill materials consisting
of very stiff clayey silt and medium dense to dense silty sand with gravel and cobbles The
fill layer was underlain by hard basalt rock formation extending to the maximum depth
drilled of approximately 35 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Boring No 3 encountered about 9 feet of fill consisting of stiff clayey silt with sand
The fill layer was underlain by medium hard to hard coral formation extending to about
21 5 feet below the existing ground surface Alluvial deposits consisting of dense silty
sand were encountered below the coral formation extending to the maximum depth drilled
of approximately 33 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater levels were encountered at depths of about 12 2 to 13 5 feet below
the existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration It should be noted that
water levels at the site are subject to fluctuate due to rainfall time of year seasonal
precipitation runoff storm surge conditions and other factors

Construction Considerations
Drilled shafts are desirable for the light pole foundations because of the significant
increase in lateral and uplift load capacities when compared to shallow foundations
However the performance of the drilled shafts will depend significantly upon the
contractor s method of construction and construction procedures The construction
workmanship has a substantial effect on the effectiveness and cost of the drilled shaft
foundations
The load bearing capacities of drilled shafts depend to a large extent on the
contact between the drilled shafts and the surrounding soils Therefore proper
construction techniques are important The contractor should exercise care in drilling the
shaft holes and placing concrete into the holes Drilling by methods utilizing drilling fluids
is not recommended
Based on our field exploration groundwater is expected in the drilled holes during
the shaft installation Since groundwater will likely be encountered in the drilled holes
concrete placement by tremie methods is recommended During concrete placement a
minimum of 5 feet of concrete head should be maintained above the bottom of the tremie
pipe A low shrink high slump 7 to 9 inch slump range concrete mix should be used to
provide close contact between the drilled shafts and the surrounding soils The concrete
should be placed promptly after drilling to reduce the potential for softening and caving in
of the sides of the drilled holes A Geolabs representative should be present to observe
the drilled shaft installation during construction to confirm the subsoil conditions and
embedment depths
Utility Trench
We envision that new electrical duct lines will be required to service the proposed
new lighting system In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of clean sand or
S4C is recommended under the duct lines Free draining granular materials such as
clean sand or S4C should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 6 inches
above the duct lines to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the
compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is
critical to use this free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below
the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes
Improper backfill material and placement procedures could result in settlement of the
backfill and damage to the pipes

The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 6 inches above the duct lines
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of on site granular soils or
imported select granular fill material The backfill should be placed in maximum 6 inch
level loose lifts moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the
potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the
upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement grade should be compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Optimum moisture is the
water content corresponding to the maximum dry density
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this letter report are based in
part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of subsurface conditions
between and beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations
may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident
it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations provided in this letter report
The field boring locations indicated in this report are approximate having
been taped from features shown on the Electrical Plan provided by Ronald N S Ho
Associates Inc on The physical locations and elevations of the borings
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil rock types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or presented in the text of this report These data have been
reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be
noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in tides rainfall temperature and other
factors
