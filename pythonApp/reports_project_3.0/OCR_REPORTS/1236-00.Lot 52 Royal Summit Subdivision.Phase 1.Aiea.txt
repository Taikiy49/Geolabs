Dear Mr Mrs Yap
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the
near surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in
order to provide foundation recommendations for the proposed
residence The study consisted of drilling two shallow borings
laboratory testing of the swelling potential of the soils a
review of the subdivision site grading plans and the preliminary
house foundation plans This report summarizes our observations
and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a one story structure approxi
mately 46 by 48 feet in plan dimension with a 2 car garage and a
covered lanai Concrete slab on grade is planned for the entire
structure

SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is on the northwestern side of Naalii Street in
the Royal Summit Phase 1 Subdivision located in Aiea Oahu
Hawaii The lot is generally level with a slight slope down
toward the northwest
A review of the site grading plans for the subdivision indicates
that Lot 52 required cuts up to 23 feet deep
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
The borings encountered 2 0 to 5 0 feet of mottled gray and red
silty clay residual soil which was underlain by weathered rock
and saprolite
Laboratory swell tests performed on the near surface material
indicated that the underlying residual soil is generally very
moist 32 to 49 moisture contents and exhibited low expansion
potential in its natural state However previous experience
indicates that this type of material could have high swelling
potential if allowed to dry out Therefore measures should be
taken to prevent excessive drying of the soil

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the existing near surface
soils may have moderate swelling potential if they are allowed
to dry out Therefore in order to minimize the swelling
potential the soils should be kept moist after excavation and
protected from drying during the several days required for
preparation prior to final concrete placement
Concrete slab on grade is planned for this house In order to
provide a barrier against future water infiltration under the
slab we recommend that the slab perimeter footing be deepened
another 6 inches from 12 to 18 inches below the outside finished
ground surface
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f could be used on the
residual soil
The bottom of all footing excavations should be observed by a
soils engineer to see that adequate bearing soil has been
reached
The ground surface should be shaped to drain water away from the
foundations

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the house garage and
driveway To reduce the swelling effects of the residual soils
special lime saturation is recommended The general procedure
is listed below
1 The slab subgrade soils should be saturated with hoses with
holes by constant sprinkling at least 2 days prior to
concrete placement to reduce the swelling potential of the
underlying subsoil
2 Hydrated lime should be placed at the bottom of the slab
perimeter footing excavation and thickened edge of the
driveway prior to water saturating of the subgrade soil
We believe that about ten to fifteen 50 1b bags of hydrated
lime would be adequate to cover the slab subgrade It is
important that the soils engineer should inspect the above
subgrade preparation and check the moisture contents of the
underlying soils prior to pouring of the slab and perimeter
footing
3 The bottom of the footing excavation should be neat and free
of loose soils It is anticipated that the very soft silt
and lime mud deposited at the footing after saturation would
be removed just prior to concrete placement

4 The concreting of footing and slab should be coordinated
in such a way that the time period between the initial
excavation and final concrete placement at each lot should
be kept to a minimum in order to prevent excessive subgrade
drying
5 The above recommendations for saturating the subgrade should
also be followed for the sidewalk driveway and patio slabs
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a 4 inch
layer of base course and should be separated from foundations
A plastic vapor barrier placed over the base course layer may be
used should a capillary break be deemed necessary to protect
floor covering
Lot Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to
avoid ponding condition Also the owners should be advised to
install gutter around the house and they should also be made
aware that gutter water should be diverted far away from the
Slabs and footings
Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slab
should also be avoided

Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper perfor
mance of the above recommended foundations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
loss of strength or heaving The foundation excavations should
be properly backfilled tight against the slab footing immediately
after setting of the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and main
tained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and
footing foundation
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
