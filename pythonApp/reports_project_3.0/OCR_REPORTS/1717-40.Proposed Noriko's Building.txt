ADDITIONAL FOUNDATION EVALUATION
PROPOSED NORIKO S BUILDING
In accordance with your request an additional foundation evaluation
was performed for the above referenced project
A previous exploration was conducted at the site by our firm to
provide subsurface information for the site grading and preliminary
foundation design of potential lightly loaded warehouse type structures
Following design of the proposed Noriko building it was determined
that portions of the building would be heavily loaded and could result in
differential settlement of the structure if footing foundations were
utilized Therefore pile foundations are now being considered for the
structures
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this exploration was to determine the depth of a
suitable bearing stratum for the pile foundations Previous information
indicated that a basalt rock formation may be encountered at deeper depths
below the site We recommended that a minimum of one boring be drilled to
a depth of about 100 feet to evaluate the competency of the bearing layer

PILE FOUNDATIONS
Discussion with the project structural engineer indicates that a pile
capacity of up to 40 tons per pile is required for the present design
Based on the boring data we recommended that the piles extend to a
minimum depth of 100 feet below the finished pad grade to develop
sufficient frictional resistance in the stiff clay layer to obtain the
40 ton design capacity
Twelve inch square prestressed precast concrete piles may be used
The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering a rated energy of
at least 24 000 foot pounds The hammer should be equipped with an energy
level control so that driving within the soft deposits may be effected at
a reduced energy level to minimize the tensile stresses within the piles
which could cause damage to the piles

Additional pile driving criteria are as follows
1
The piles should be cast in one continuous section
The driving process should be continuous without interruption for
each pile A pile may be rejected or its design capacity
modified when the driving process is interrupted for more than
four 4 hours
During easy driving when the pile tip is still within loose
soil the hammer stroke should be carefully controlled reduced
energy so than excessive tensile stresses are not induced in
the pile
The pile driving may be terminated when the blow counts steadily
increase with depth and reach 10 blows per inch for the last
inch or when aggregated blow counts are 50 blows for the last
foot or the piles are driven to their full lengths
The pile driving should be conducted under close and continuous
observation of the project geotechnical engineer who will
determine when piles may be stopped and to provide guidance
should additional measures be required due to unforeseen
conditions

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during pile driving of the
project This is to observe compliance with the recommendations design
concepts or specifications and to allow design changes in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of
construction The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon such observations
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field borings Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping from lot boundaries indicated on the Grading Plan by Hida
Okamoto Associates The physical location of the field data points
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method
used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
boring points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and
as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings
and may not represent actual subsurface conditions

This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the

purposes
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions
are commonly encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as perched
groundwater soft deposits hard layers or cavities may occur in
localized areas and may require additional probing or corrections in the
field which may result in construction delays to attain a properly
constructed project Therefore sufficient contingency fund is thus
recommended to accommodate these possible extra costs
