In accordance with our proposal of May 14 1979 we have
performed field and laboratory soil tests at the above
referenced site
The purpose of the soil testing was to determine the charac
teristics of the pavement soil below Taxiways A B C G
at the Marine Corps Air Station Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii
The scope of our work was limited to performance of field
and laboratory soil testing and did not include pavement
analysis
This report summarizes our field and laboratory testing
results

P 2
PROJECT SITE
The Kaneohe Marine Corps Air Station is located on the
windward side of Oahu at Kaneohe Bay see Location Map
Plate 1
The base airfield is located in the western end of the
station The runways and taxiways generally extend in a
northeast southwest direction The field exploration was
concentrated in the southwestern portion of the taxiways
where most of the pavement damage was noted This south
western portion of the airfield is relatively flat and
lower than the northwestern portion Some rutting and
cracking were noted in these pavements
FIELD EXPLORATION

taxiways The locations of the test pits were determined
by the Naval Facilities Engineering Command personnel and
the approximate locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The test pit locations were selected to represent insofar
as possible and based on visual inspection pavement conditions
indicated in the following tabulation

In each of the ten test pits the following field tests
were performed on each of the major soil layers encountered
1 In place moisture and density The in place density
of the soil was determined by the sand cone method
ASTM D 1556 The moisture content was determined
by oven drying ASTM D 2216 The results of the
in place moisture and density tests are presented
in the test pit logs
2 Field CBR California Bearing Ratio The field
CBR tests were performed with standard manually
operated field CBR equipment manufactured by Soiltest
Inc The reaction load was provided by a
flatbed truck carrying a 4 ton weight Three CBR
penetrations were performed for each major soil layer
encountered The CBR curves obtained from these tests
are presented in Appendix B The corrected CBR values
at 0 1 inch penetration are shown on the test pit logs
GENERAL SOIL CONDITIONS
The subsoils encountered in the test pits consisted basically
of carbonate sand and coral gravel with varying amounts of
fines
The laboratory gradation tests indicated the percentage
of fines 200 size material varies from 1 to 34 percent

APPENDIX A
TEST PIT LOGS
Ten test pits were excavated with a backhoe to 4 to
6 8 foot depths approximately where the water level was
encountered
The soils encountered in the test pits were logged
by an engineer or geologist from our office Representative
samples of the major soil layers encountered were obtained
and tested in our laboratory Based on visual observations
and identification tests performed in our office the soils
encountered were described and classified in conformance
with the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic
presentation of the soils encountered is presented on the
Test Pit Logs Plates A 1 thru A 10
The surface elevations of the test pits were provided
by a survey performed by the Marine Corps Air Station
Kaneohe in November 1979
During the field exploration in place moisture and
density and field CBR determinations were made on the major
soil layers encountered The results of these tests are
presented on the Test Pit Logs at the appropriate depths

APPENDIX B
FIELD CBR TEST RESULTS
ld
Field CBR tests were performed on the major soil
layers encountered in the test pits The tests were
performed using standard manually operated CBR equipment
The reaction vehicle consisted of a flatbed
trucking carrying an additional 4 ton weight
A minimum of 3 determinations were made per test
Due to the natural variability in the composition and
compactness of the soil some amount of variability of
the test results were noted
The load penetration curves developed from the tests
are presented in Plates B 1 through B 24
Due to the gravelly nature of the coral material
tested some difficulty was experienced in keeping the load
piston vertical particularly at higher loads Whenever a
large piece of coral was encountered below one portion of
the load piston it tended to unbalance the load and cause
the piston to tilt out of plumb When this occurred early in
the test penetration the test was rerun When piston tilting
occurred after sufficient penetration readings were obtained
to develop the CBR curve the test was stopped and the results
recorded therefore some of the tests did not reach the full
0 5 inch piston penetration

content These test results are presented on Plate C 31 It was
noted that due to the gravelly nature of some of the soil some
what variable CBR test results were noted particularly at the
low and high moisture ranges

separate and composite samples to determine the compaction
characteristics of the subsoils These test results are
presented on Plates C 23 to C 30
