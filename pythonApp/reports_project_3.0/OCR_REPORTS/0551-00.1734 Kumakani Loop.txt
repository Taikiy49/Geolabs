Dear Mr Ikeno
On we examined the existing cut slope near
the up hill property line at 1734 Kumakani Loop This letter
summarizes our observations and comments
At the present time an approximately 4 to 10 foot cut exists
at the up hill property line which separates the subject dwell
ing and the adjoining dwelling presently under construction
It appears that the slope excavation was made approximately 1
to 2 feet inside the property line The front section is a
vertical cut slope within hard lava rock see Photograph 1
The rock contains some weathered zones that are filled with
hard clayey silt and numerous rock fragments Photograph 2
Between the end of the rock cut and the end of the swimming
pool retaining wall a distance of approximately 25 feet
the center section of the cut varies from 1 to 3 feet in
height Photograph 3 It was also made through rocky mate
rials The ground surface in this area slopes upward from
the top of the cut at approximately a 2 to 1 slope It
appears that 2 to 3 feet of loosely placed fill has been
placed on the upper slope as part of the construction of
the adjoining dwelling
The remaining rear section of the slope which includes the
rear 1 3 of the property line is gently sloping soil with
no apparent stability problems
It is our opinion that the present rock slopes are stable as
constructed and do not require a retaining wall for support Some
