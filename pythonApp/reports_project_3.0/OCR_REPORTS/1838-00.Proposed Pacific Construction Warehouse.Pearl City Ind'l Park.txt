Based on the soil conditions we recommend that
shallow spread or continuous wall footings bearing
directly on the existing fill be used to support

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the foundation design of the proposed Pacific
Construction Warehouse at Pearl City Industrial Park Pearl City Oahu
Hawaii
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this study was to explore the subsurface soil and
water conditions at the site to obtain pertinent soil data for the
foundation design of the proposed structure

The scope of our study included drilling and sampling three 3
borings to depths of 20 0 to 20 5 feet below the existing ground
surface Also included were laboratory testing of selected samples
obtained engineering evaluation of the field and laboratory data
development of design recommendations and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a one story warehouse structure
approximately 90 by 250 feet in plan with a partial second floor
level The building will be a steel framed structure with concrete
masonry walls and concrete on grade slabs Generally the estimated
design column loads are about 28 kips per column for the one story
portion and higher loads in the second floor areas
The eastern one fourth of the ground floor of the building will be
used as a shop for the maintenance of heavy construction equipment It
is our understanding that these construction equipment may include
heavy track vehicles with a maximum weight of about 120 kips
It is also our understanding that a loading dock area not shown on
the Site Plan is planned adjacent to the western portion of the
building site The proposed docking area will be formed by up to about
3 feet of cut into the existing ground surface

SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is situated at the northeastern end of Pearl City
Industrial Park near the base of the hillside adjacent to Waimano
Stream
The proposed building area is generally flat and covered with
compacted coral gravel fill Portions of the area are presently
occupied by heavy construction equipment stockpiles and machinery The
eastern and northern portions of the site are adjacent to the banks of
Waimano Stream To the south of the building area the hillside slopes
upward at a ratio of 2 1 H V or steeper Rock outcrops are visible in
portions of the hillside Some large boulders are also visible on the
hillside
SUBSURFACE SOTL CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling
three 3 borings to depths of 20 0 to 20 5 feet below the existing
ground surface
The borings disclosed that the site was mantled by an approximately
10 inch thick surface layer of compacted coral gravel underlain by about
17 feet of stiff clayey silt fill Below the fill layer was very stiff
to hard alluvium consisting of clayey silt with occasional gravel
cobbles and boulders to the drilled depths of the borings
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during our field
exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our subsurface exploration encountered a generally competent upper
crust of clayey silt fill at the project site Therefore shallow
spread or continuous wall footings bearing directly on this existing
stiff clayey silt fill may be used for foundation support of the
proposed structure
Foundation Support
Based on the anticipated soil conditions and design loadings we
believe that an allowable bearing capacity of 3500 p s f may be used
for spread or continuous footing foundations bearing directly on the
existing stiff clayey silt fill materials
Footing should be placed a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Uplift wind or other lateral loading
conditions may further increase the embedment of the footing foundation
The bearing capacity value is for total dead plus live loads only
and may be increased by one third for short duration wind loading
Estimated settlements under the design loading are on the order of less
than one half of an inch total and one fourth of an inch differential
Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed structure can be
obtained by frictional resistance and passive earth pressure A
frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the bottom of the
footing and the stiff in situ fill In addition an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be used to determine the
passive earth resistance against the buried face of the footing
foundation

Site Grading
No major site filling is anticipated for the construction of the
proposed building while up to 3 feet of cut is anticipated for the
proposed docking area adjacent to the building
At the on set of earthwork the areas within the project grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared Any unsuitable materials such as
debris and rubbish should be removed and disposed of properly off site
After clearing the areas to receive footing and
slab on grade pavement should be scarified to a depth of at least 6
inches below the bottom of footing or subgrade elevation moisture
conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximm density as established
by ASTM D 1557 78 test method Any loose or wet spots encountered at
the footing bottom or subgrade level should be removed and replaced with
compacted select on site granular soil or with suitable imported
material compacted to the above mentioned standard
The on site gravelly top soil may be reused as backfill material
If required new imported fill material should consist of non expansive
select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum size with less
than 20 passing the 200 sieve

Boulder Protection
The proposed building area is situated adjacent to a relatively
steep hillside slope and may be subject to boulders rolling down the
hillside
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1838 00 Page 8
Dk No 30
Falling boulders are a general phenomena occurring at almost all
ridge slopes on Oahu Various types of boulder barriers or catchments
may be used to reduce the hazard of falling boulder However the
degree of safety of various methods is a function of economics For
this site the following methods may be used
1 A boulder catchment area about 10 to 15 feet wide with a
fence or wall may be located near the property boundary to
catch falling boulders
2 Boulder sweeps along the upper slope of the hillside may be
performed to remove the loosely seating boulders that appear
likely to roll down
3 If practicable the proposed warehouse should be set back away
from the toe of the hillside slope to create an additional
boulder catchment area
It should be noted that whatever methods are used an inherent risk
of falling boulders could still exist for this hillside building The
state of the art of soil engineering practice has not advanced to a
point that a solution is available to eliminate the risk of falling
boulders Whatever measure is chosen it would only reduce the risk
Therefore it should be emphasized that no guarantee against future
falling boulders can be made due to changes in soil and rock conditions
with time weathering erosion earthquake construction vibration
improvements and construction activities at and around the site

Plans and specifications for site grading foundation and pavement
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the grubbing site grading foundation
excavation backfilling and subgrade preparation be observed by the
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate compaction has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site for use
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this study
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such
as pockets of organic soils trash fill or expansive soils may occur in
localized areas Therefore additional expenditures may be needed
during construction to attain a properly constructed project Some
contingency fund is thus recommended to accommodate these possible
costs
