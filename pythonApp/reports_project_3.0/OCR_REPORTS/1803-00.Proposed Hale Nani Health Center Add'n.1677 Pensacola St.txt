PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this study was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions at the site to obtain pertinent soil data for the foundation
design of a four story reinforced concrete structure
The main objective was to evaluate the competency of the subsoils
for spread and continuous footing design
The scope of our investigation included drilling and sampling
three 3 borings to depths of about 30 feet below the existing ground
surface The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 Also included were laboratory testing of selected samples
obtained engineering evaluation of the field and laboratory data
development of design recommendations and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
It is our understanding that the proposed building addition will
consist of a four story reinforced concrete structure with a
semi basement and a sunken garden It will be approximately 50 by 140
feet in plan and will be an addition to the existing Hale Nani Health
Center

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located to the west of Lewalani Drive and
north of Piikoi Street in the Makiki district of Honolulu The site is
accessible from both Lewalani Drive and the existing Hale Nani Health
Center
Kanaha Stream is located about 50 feet northeast of the site At
this general location the stream is confined in an 8 by 5 foot box
drain that is covered over by fill
The site is generally flat with a slight gradient down towards the
southwest
Portions of the northern and southern property lines are
surrounded by existing concrete and CMU walls Several abandoned
wooden buildings also occupied the site
Entry driveways from Lewalani Drive to the residential structures
on site are paved with broken asphaltic concrete while the lawn areas
are grown with dense vegetations

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling
three 3 borings to depths of 29 5 to 33 2 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
The drilled borings generally encountered a 12 to 27 foot thick
soil unit of stiff to hard clayey silt weathered tuff and medium
dense to dense silty sand cinder overlying hard basalt formation at
the project site The weathered tuff and cinder deposits appear to
gently dip downhill in a southwesterly direction See Plate 3
In Boring 1 medium stiff clayey silt fill material and loose
silty sand were encountered above the stiff weathered formation We
believe this may represent a localized soil condition occurring behind
the existing retaining walls and near the loading dock

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our borings we believe that the proposed building
addition may be supported by spread or continuous footing bearing on
the competent weathered tuff cinder soil unit or recompacted backfill
Special design considerations should be used for the footings in
the area behind the western retaining walls near the existing loading
dock where loose subsoils were encountered
It is our opinion that the existing on site 12 inch diameter sewer
should either be relocated or the new footings adjacent to the existing
sewer be set back away from the left in place sewer pipe and embedded
down to match the elevation as the elevation of the sewer invert
Foundation
Based on the anticipated soil conditions we believe that shallow
spread or continuous footing foundation bearing on the weathered tuff
and cinder or on re compacted backfill may be used for the proposed
building addition
Allowable Bearing Pressures
An allowable bearing capacity of 3500 p s f may be used for the
footing foundation design except for the general area behind the
existing retaining walls near the loading dock where inadequate
old backfill material and loose sand were encountered

At the loading dock retaining wall location we recommend that the
footings be overexcavated by at least 2 feet below the footing
bottoms and backfilled with recompacted non expansive select
on site soils in 8 inch loose lifts to obtain a minimm 95
relative density per ASTM D 1557 78 Test Method A lower
allowable bearing capacity of 3000 p s f may be used for footings
bearing on the above mentioned recompacted backfill
The above allowable bearing capacity values are for total dead
plus live loads only and may be increased by one third for short
duration wind seismic loading
Footing Embedment
We recommend that all footing foundation be placed a minimum of 24
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
However new footings to be located at the existing retaining
wall loading dock locations should be set back a minimum
horizontal distance of 3 feet away from the existing retaining
wall and building footings The new footings should be embedded
to match the existing adjacent footing bottom elevations
Uplift wind or other loading conditions may further increase the
embedment of the footing foundation
Settlements
Based on the above recommended allowable bearing values estimated
settlements are on the order of less than one inch total and
one half of an inch differential

Lateral Resistance
Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed building addition can
be obtained by passive soil pressure and frictional resistance A
frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the bottom of
the footing and the recompacted subgrade In addition an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be
used against the buried footing of the proposed structure to
develop the passive soil resistance
Existing Sewer
Relocation of Existing Sewer
The existing 12 inch diameter sewer across the new building site
could be relocated outside the proposed building area prior to
footing construction if economically feasible
Relocation of the existing sewer could avoid potential damage to
the existing sewer pipe and minimize potential future building
settlements due to the new footing stress on the in place sewer
Another advantage of re routing the sewer outside the proposed
building is to minimize the accessibility problem of the sewer for
If relocation of the existing sewer is selected the abandoned
sewer and trench backfill within the sewer easement should be
removed and backfilled with compacted select on site soils to
avoid potential settlements of footings located over and near the
sewer easement

Footings Adjacent to Left In Place Sewer
Should it be decided to leave the existing sewer in place new
footings located adjacent to the existing sewer easement should be
designed and constructed to avoid imposing additional loading on
to the sewer
We recommend that the new footings be set back and placed outside
the existing sewer easement The footing bottom should be
embedded sufficiently to match the elevation of the sewer invert
For the above special spread footings design same bearing values
lateral resistance and settlements per recommendations presented
in the Foundation section may be utilized
Site Grading
Anticipated site grading consists of about 3 feet of cut within
the building area and up to 6 feet of excavations would be required for
the sunken garden area About 4 feet of new fill may be required for
the lower eastern area in front of the retaining walls adjacent to the
health center s loading dock
At the on set of earthwork the areas within the project grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable
surface materials such as vegetation rubbish and abandoned building
foundations should be removed and disposed of properly off site to
prevent contamination of the excavated material
After clearing and grubbing the building areas should be
scarified to a depth of six 6 inches below the subgrade elevation
moisture conditioned and recompacted to 95 of its maximum dry density
per ASTM D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered during
proof rolling should be removed and replaced with compacted select
on site granular soil or other suitable imported material and compacted
to the above mentioned density

The on site silty and sandy soils may be reused as fill and
backfill materials If required new imported fill material should
consist of non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches
in maximum size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
We recommend the new fills utility trench and footing backfill be
placed in 8 inch compacted lifts and compacted to 90 of its maxim
dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Slab On Grade
Tt is our understanding that 4 inch concrete slab on grade is
planned for the entire building area The on grade floor slab should
be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of gravel size crushed rock
material such as 3 Fine rock to provide uniform support A moisture
barrier membrane should be placed under the slabs for moisture
sensitive flooring
The exterior slab thickened edges should be embedded at least 12
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
Retaining Walls
Two new retaining walls are planned to provide grade separation
for the proposed sunken garden at the eastern side of the project
site The guidelines for the retaining wall designs are listed below
1 The following active earth pressure can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
level backfill fluid pressure
per foot of depth
2 A passive earth pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth may
be used for the wall footing on level areas The passive
pressure must be reduced for wall on slope For a 2 1 sloping
condition a passive earth pressure of 200 p s f per foot of
depth may be used

3 A base friction factor of 0 40 may also be used to determine
the resistance against sliding of the wall
4 An allowable bearing value of up to 3500 p s f is recommended

5 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be
embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent

6 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading foundation pavements
and retaining walls should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and written comments prior to construction to see that the
intent of our reconmendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the grubbing site grading foundation and
sewer easement utility trench excavation backfilling and subgrade
preparation be observed by the soils engineer during construction to
see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our study
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site for use
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

Field Exploration

three 3 borings to depths of 29 5 to 33 2 feet below the existing

Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings

textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to
drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Logs of Borings
