Dear Mr Ishii
In accordance with our fee proposal dated March 13 2000 we have performed a site
reconnaissance of the project site and reviewed the geotechnical aspects of the grading
plans for the proposed Makalapua Business Center Mass Grading project in the Kailua
Kona area in the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii This letter report presents
our geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of mass grading and
infrastructure installation for the proposed project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located on the makai side of Queen Kaahumanu Highway in the
Kailua Kona area on the Island of Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The project site is located at the intersection of Queen Kaahumanu Highway and Makala
Boulevard The proposed project site is on both sides of Makala Boulevard Based on the
information provided we understand that the project site encompasses approximately
16 acres in area The site presently exists as vacant land and is bounded by Queen
Kaahumanu Highway to the north and by Luhia Street to the south
Based on our review of the proposed mass grading plans for the project we
understand that it is desired to grade the area to provide for future development of the
Makalapua Business Center The existing Makala Boulevard and Luhia Street within the
project limits will be widened and graded to match the proposed finished grades The
grading would include continuing the existing cut slope along Queen Kaahumanu Highway
constructing fill slopes on the western portion of the project site and grading on both sides
of the existing Makala Boulevard as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the grading plan provided we understand that the grading would include
approximately 10 to 16 feet of cuts and about 9 to 13 feet of fills The embankment fills for
Makala Boulevard within the project limits are up to about 21 feet thick Based on our site
reconnaissance boulders on the order of about 2 to 5 feet in diameter are present at the
project site

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering reconnaissance of the site was to obtain
an overview of the surface conditions at the project site The information obtained was
utilized to develop a soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertaining to the design of mass grading and infrastructure installation at
the site In order to accomplish this we performed the following tasks and work efforts
1 Review of available in house geotechnical information pertaining to the site and
vicinity
2 Reconnaissance of the site by an engineer from our firm to observe the existing
ground conditions for earthwork considerations
3 Analyses of the field data for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertaining to the proposed mass grading and infrastructure
installation project
4 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
5 Coordination of our overall work on the project by an engineer from our firm
6 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
principal engineer from our firm
7 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

Based on our reconnaissance of the project site and review of the mass grading plans
site grading will generally consist of cuts of up to about 16 feet deep In addition fills on the
order of about 9 to 13 feet thick are planned in order to provide a relatively level pad for future
development of the site A cut slope on the order of about 10 feet high will be required along
Queen Kaahumanu Highway on the north of the project site to connect the existing cut slope
A fill slope of up to about 21 feet will be constructed on both sides of the existing Makala
Boulevard within the project limits for the roadway widening In addition a fill slope on the
order of about 10 feet will be required on the western portion of the project site In general

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site to reduce the potential
for contamination of the excavated materials to be used as embankment fill materials
We wish to point out that caution should be exercised by the contractor while clearing
and grading near the collapsed features which may represent potential lava tubes or
cavities that may further collapse under the load of heavy equipment
Based on our site reconnaissance loose a a materials and collapsible pahoehoe lavas
are prevalent at the ground surface throughout the site These near surface materials
are relatively porous and will compress when subjected to structural and vehicular
loads Therefore at the commencement of grading we recommend that the fill areas
of the project site be leveled with the use of a Caterpillar D 9 bulldozer or similar size
bulldozer a minimum of six passes to provide a relative level surface
After leveling of the rugged surfaces with a bulldozer we recommend that the fill
subgrades be proof rolled with a large vibratory drum roller minimum 10 tons for a
minimum of eight passes to help detect and collapse near surface cavities and or

Fill and Backfill Materials
In general we recommend that the fill areas be capped with a minimum 2 foot thick
layer of structural fill material to serve as a choking layer for the larger sized general
fill materials below Structural fill materials should consist of well graded granular
materials less than 6 inches in maximum dimension with sufficient fines to prevent
the occurrence of voids in the compacted mass in general the excavated materials
a a lava and pahoehoe basalt may be re used as a source of structural fill
materials provided that the materials are processed to meet the maximum size and
gradation requirements presented above Where appropriate such as within utility
corridors the thickness of the structural fill layer may be increased to a minimum of
1 foot below the invert elevation of the utilities to facilitate future construction of the
utilities
Fill and backfill materials below the minimum 2 foot thick structural fill layer may
consist of general fill materials General fill and backfill materials should consist of
well graded granular materials the majority of which are less than 12 inches in size
with an absolute maximum dimension of 18 inches The excavated on site materials
if less than 18 inches in maximum dimension may be used as a source of general fill
or backfill materials other than for trench backfill provided that they are processed
to meet the above gradation requirements If the excavated materials do not contain
sufficient fines to produce a well graded material off site borrow or on site rock
crushing of larger rock fragments or boulders should be considered to provide the
required gradation and particle sizes to develop a well graded material

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Structural fill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose
thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to
at least 95 percent relative compaction General fills and backfills should be placed
in level lifts not exceeding 18 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above

the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed
as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or other
types of acceptable compaction equipment
Rock fills should be placed in lifts not exceeding the maximum dimension of the
majority of the rocks or 5 feet whichever is less Placement should be by haul trucks
traversing previously placed lifts and dumping at the edge of the currently placed lift
Spreading of the rock fill should be by a large bulldozer to facilitate seating of the
rocks The rock fill should be watered heavily during placement by water trucks
traversing in front of the current rock lift face and spraying water continuously during
placement Compaction equipment such as a Caterpillar D 9 bulldozer or similar
heavy compaction equipment providing suitable energy to achieve the required
compaction should be utilized The number of passes usually six to eight passes
to be made to achieve proper compaction should be determined at the on set of site
grading by the performance of a trial compaction test or by plate bearing tests
Subsequent layers of rock fill above the previously placed rock fill should provide a
choking layer of smaller sized boulder materials to fill in the voids between the
boulders of the preceding rock fill layer
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in fills composed of
rocks boulders and or cobbles Instead a testing program to evaluate the number
of passes by a compactor needed to achieve the desired level of compaction should
be conducted at the start of the grading phase of the project under the observation
of a representative from Geolabs Based on this testing program the number of
passes may then be used as the field criterion for adequate compaction For
preliminary estimating purposes compaction may be accomplished by using a large
vibratory drum roller having a static weight of at least 15 tons traveling at a speed of
about 150 feet per minute The roller should pass over the fill surface at least eight
times for structural fill and six times for general fill For general fill a Caterpillar D 9
bulldozer or similar size equipment may be used in lieu of the vibratory drum roller
Excavation
Based on our reconnaissance we anticipate that the site is underlain by slightly to
moderately fractured volcanic rock materials ranging from medium hard to very hard
It is anticipated that the volcanic rock may be excavated with normal heavy excavation
equipment such as ripping with large bulldozers where the rock is near the existing
ground surface However excavations into the deeper formations may require the
use of hoerams chipping or blasting

Cut and Fill Slopes
We understand that some cut slopes are planned for the proposed mass grading
project Based on our experience in the area we anticipate that the cut slope would
expose relatively dense basalt rock with some fractures In general we believe that
the cut slopes planned may be designed with a slope inclination of one horizontal to
one vertical 1H 1V or flatter Since the cut slopes will likely expose basalt rock
formation we believe that slope benches may be omitted for cut slopes up to 30 feet
in vertical height
Based on the subsurface conditions anticipated at the site cavities and or clinker
zones may be encountered at the cut slope face In the event that clinker zones are
exposed at the cut slope face the cut slope may need to be flattened to a slope
inclination of 14H 1V Large cavities that may be exposed on the cut slope face
should be backfilled and or grouted if necessary A representative from Geolabs
should observe and map the cut slope during construction to evaluate if the cut slope
needs to be flattened and if the exposed materials are consistent with our
assumptions
In the event that over cutting of a cut slope occurs consideration should be given to
leaving the slope as is considering the relatively steep cut slopes recommended
Otherwise keying and benching the slope with cement treated granular fills should
be implemented instead of backfilling the slope to the design grade with sliver fills
Permanent fill slopes constructed of the granular general fill and structural fill materials
may be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter For fill slopes with
vertical heights greater than 30 feet we recommend that a minimum 8 foot wide
bench be provided at the mid point of the slope to reduce the potential for erosion due
to surface water runoff
Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the
existing slope to provide stability of the new fill against sliding The filling operations
should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers
in accordance with the above fill placement recommendations Fill slopes should be
constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the design slope ratio to obtain a
well compacted slope face
We recommend that the top of cut and fill slopes be rounded and that loose materials
be removed from the finished slopes The fill slope face should be finished to a
relatively smooth and well compacted surface The fill slope face should be free of

Underground Utility Lines
We envision that new underground utility lines such as water and sewer lines will be
required for development of this project It is anticipated that most of the trenches for
underground utility lines will be excavated in the fill materials clinker materials and or hard
basalt formation encountered at the site As previously mentioned we recommend that
consideration be given to avoid the placement of rock fills boulder fills within the major utility
line corridors such as the roadways and within the depths of the proposed utility lines This
recommendation is provided to reduce the potential for encountering the large boulders
during excavation for the utility trenches
In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation or 1 inch minus well graded granular material is recommended under the
pipes As an alternative the granular bedding layer may consist of 12 inches of structural
fill material Free draining granular materials No 3B Fine gravel or 1 inch minus well
graded granular materials should be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches
above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes It is critical that free draining
materials be used to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes
and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Where the 1 inch minus well
graded granular materials are used as bedding and initial trench backfill the bedding and
initial trench backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Improper trench backfill could
result in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to the
top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of granular materials generally less than
6 inches in maximum size structural fill The backfill material should be moisture
conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in maximum 12 inch level loose
lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce
the potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the
upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement grade should be compacted to at least
95 percent relative compaction
