SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS

formations underlying approximately nine 9 to 13 5 feet of

Groundwater was encountered at approximately 26 5 feet

The proposed pump station structures may bear directly on
the underlying competent basaltic formations with an

Due to the presence of overhead and underground utilities

INTRODUCTION
The report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Pump Stations for Well Nos G 11 and G 12 at the HECO
Power Plant at The general location of the site is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the data obtained
during our field exploration These recommendations are intended for the use of the
designer and may not be appropriate to form the basis for preparation of construction
cost estimates nor for construction bidding purposes

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The overall project of which the two Pump Stations are a part includes a one story
Control Building approximately 1430 square feet in overall plan dimensions and
approximately 371 linear feet of twelve 12 sixteen 16 and twenty four 24 inch water
lines with appurtenant valves and controls
The two Pump Stations are essentially identical each consisting of a ten foot by
fourteen foot valve chamber adjoining a twelve foot by thirteen foot wet well The bottom
of the wet wells will be approximately forty 40 feet below the finish grade
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the site in order to develop a generalized soil and rock data set to provide
evaluation of the basis of current design of the two proposed Pump Stations for the site
The preliminary designs were based on previous explorations performed in the same
general area
The work was performed in accordance with our proposal dated March 20 1992
to Park Engineering Inc The scope of work included the following tasks
and work efforts
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
2 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and our drill crew
to and from the project site
3 Drilling and sampling of two 2 borings to depths of 50 and 48 feet below
the existing ground surface At the request of the Board of Water Supply
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2881 00 Page 3
we have surrendered selected samples to the project environmental
consultant monitoring the drilling and sampling operations on site
4 Coordination and logging of the borings by a Field Geologist of our firm
5 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
to determine the moisture content of the materials encountered
6 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data obtained to formulate
geotechnical engineering recommendations
7 Preparation of this report summarizing our findings and presenting our
recommendations
8 Quality assurance and report review by a Principal Engineer of our firm
9 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Detailed
descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing are presented in the appendix
of this report
GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Hawaiian Electric Company Power Plant
compound which is situated on the makai side of Kamehameha Highway at its inter
section with Kuleana Road in Waiau Oahu The general project location is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1

The proposed Control Building is located near the entrance roadway into the plant
The proposed Pump Stations are next to and approximately one hundred 100 feet east
of the entrance road respectively in a vehicular parking area
There are many existing structures in the area and numerous underground and
overhead utilities at the Pump Station locations and along the proposed waterlines

Generally the materials encountered at the site are composed of competent basalt
formations with varying degrees of jointing weathering and hardness overlain by nine 9
to fourteen 14 feet of overburden composed mainly of stiff to very stiff silty clay with
some dense to very dense silty sand and saprolite The parking area in which the
proposed Pump Stations are located is surfaced with asphaltic concrete pavement

during the field explorations indicating a possibility of artesian conditions

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field explorations encountered stiff overburden materials overlying moderately
hard basalt rock formations Relatively hard excavation into the hard rock formations is
anticipated for the proposed underground structures
Groundwater will also be encountered in the excavations
Site Grading Control Building Foundations and Paving
Geotechnical recommendations regarding site grading the foundations for the
proposed Control Building and the proposed paving at the Control Building are discussed
in our report dated and will not be repeated here
Pump Stations
The geotechnical recommendations regarding the Pump Stations are similar to
those presented in the previous report under the paragraphs entitled Valve Vault Pump
Station and Diversion Manhole with minor revisions The recommendations are
presented in the paragraphs that follow
The excavations for the proposed Pump Stations will be approximately thirty eight
38 feet deep below the existing ground surface Relatively hard excavation requiring the
use of hoe rams or similar equipment is anticipated to be required in the rock formation
encountered at approximately nine 9 to thirteen and one half 13 5 feet below the
existing ground surface
It was learned during our field exploration that overhead electrical lines as well as
underground utilities exist at the Pump Station sites Extreme care should be exercised
during excavation Due to the existence of these utilities and of other nearby structures
blasting should not be permitted for excavation in the hard rock formations anticipated
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2881 00 Page 6
As previously noted groundwater possibly under artesian conditions may be
anticipated within the excavations Dewatering is thus anticipated to be required Due
to the presence of clinker formations and the degree of jointing of the basalt formations
large amount of dewatering should be anticipated during excavation
All shoring bracing and dewatering required should be made the sole
responsibility of the contractor and should be in strict accordance with applicable health
and safety regulations It is recommended that the contractor retain qualified and
dewatering consultants to design and implement the shoring bracing and dewatering
systems
For the anticipated foundation design of the structures an allowable bearing value
up to 10 000 p s f may be used on the dense basalt materials A site coefficient S of
1 0 may also be used for seismic design of the structures based on Uniform Building
Code Table No 23 J 1988 Edition
Assuming at rest condition the following lateral soil pressures may be used for
the design of the structure walls
1 Overburden Material 50 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
2 Basalt Formation Above Water 20 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
3 Basalt Formation Below Water 10 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
plus water pressure
As discussed above the water level may be affected by artesian head If
practicable the foundation walls should be poured directly against the rock formation to
attempt to seal off the artesian water

Installation of Water Lines
Trench excavations of approximately six 6 to approximately eleven 11 feet are
anticipated to be required for installation of the water lines Dense basaltic material may
be encountered in the lower portions of the trenches which may require hard excavation
As noted above blasting should not be permitted
Shoring or bracing may be required for the trenches particularly if areas of loose
material are encountered Shoring and bracing design and installation conforming to the
appropriate health and safety requirements should be made the sole responsibility of the
contractor
A minimum six 6 inch thick bedding layer consisting of 3B Fine gravel or ASTM
C 33 No 67 is recommended below the bottom of utility pipes To minimize the
compaction efforts for trench backfill we recommend that the first lift from the bottom of
the pipe to twelve 12 inches above the pipe barrel should also consist of 3B Fine
gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot above the pipes to
the subgrade or finished grade should be placed in maximum ten 10 inch loose level
lifts mechanically compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of its maximum dry density in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
It is considered to be the contractor s sole responsibility to protect all existing utility
lines during construction
Where trenches cross paved areas the pavement section should be equivalent to
that recommended for the pavement at the proposed Control Building

Design Review
Final drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to
advertisement for bid This review is needed to determine adherence to the
recommendations given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the project geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction of the work This is to identify
possible deviations from the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to
allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those described
in this report The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the project geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered during preparation of this report then appropriate
modifications to the design should be made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration and Laboratory Testing
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling two
2 borings to depths of 50 and 48 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled by a
truck mounted rotary drill rig advancing continuous flight augers or rotary core drilling
tools as appropriate
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Spilt Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a standard spilt spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The
blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches or for fraction of a foot where a full
foot of penetration was not practicable are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
Moisture content determinations were performed on selected samples The results
appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice
D 2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in
the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt vs the length of the core attempt e g if 3 7 feet of material is
recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 and would be shown on
the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any drilling
induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core
run the RQD would be 50 and would be shown on the Boring Logs as RQD 50
Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality of the rock based on the
Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals
