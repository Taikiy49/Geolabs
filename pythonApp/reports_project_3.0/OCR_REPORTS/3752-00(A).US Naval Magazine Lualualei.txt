INTRODUCTION

performed for the proposed Whole House Revitalization Phase 2 project within the
U S Naval Magazine Lualualei NAVMAG facility in the District of Waianae on the island
of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for the evaluation and design of the
drainage only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of
the western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of the
eastern two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while Koolau
was still active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lava in central Oahu
The project site is situated in Lualualei Valley at the base of Puu Heleakala a high
ridge extending along the southern limits of the project site The climate along the north
and west sides of the ridge which makes up a portion of the Waianae Range is typified
by low annual rainfall
Lualualei Valley was formed by the lower and middle members of the Waianae
Volcanic Series during the Tertiary Period Erosion has since removed most of the
western slope of the Waianae shield and exposed the internal structure of the volcano
This erosion formed the large broad Lualualei Valley which is filled with enormous
accumulations of alluvium and colluvium The older consolidated alluvium of the western
portion of the Waianae Range appears to be graded to various elevations reflecting
higher stands of the sea during the Pleistocene Epoch These alluvial deposits are
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3752 00 A Page 6
largely a result of stream deposits and to a lesser extent mud flows Toward the mouth
of the valley the non marine alluvium grades to marine deposits of stratified clay with
lenticular beds of sand and gravel
Eustatic fluctuations of sea level in the Hawaiian Islands have caused changes in
the shoreline in the past The alluvial deposits in the valley and abutting the Waianae
Range grade in part to former high sea levels and are being slowly removed by present
day stream erosion Weathering of the upper portions of the ridges has led to the
accumulation of colluvium found at their bases
The soils underlying the proposed housing improvements project range from
Mokuleia clay consisting of recent alluvium of high plasticity overlying coral deposits and
consolidated terrestrial sediments In addition Lualualei stony clay occurs in the alluvial
fans adjacent to drainageways and is very plastic and often contains gypsum crystals

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and
sampling ten borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 10 extending to depths of
about 3 7 to 6 6 meters below the existing ground slab surface Boring No 6 was drilled
within Building No 510B one of the distressed buildings that was available for our field
exploration work The approximate locations of the borings drilled are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
Continuous sampling was performed in the upper 3 meters during our field
exploration to obtain a detailed subsoil profile for evaluation purposes In general our
borings at the project site encountered very stiff to hard silty clay soils to a depth of
approximately 1 5 meters below the existing ground surface We believe the surficial
clayey soils encountered were primarily fill materials placed during the initial construction
Based on our laboratory testing the surficial clayey soils may be classified as moderately
to highly expansive Therefore the on site soils are susceptible to significant volume
changes when subjected to fluctuations in the moisture content
Below the surficial fill layer colluvial deposits consisting of very stiff to hard clayey
silt soils intermixed with basalt cobbles and boulders were encountered extending to the
maximum depth explored of approximately 6 6 meters below the existing ground surface

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration at the proposed housing improvements site the
area is generally underlain by moderately to highly expansive clayey soils at shallow
depths Because of the negative drainage patterns observed at the project site we
believe that water infiltration into the underlying expansive soils directly below the house
structures caused the expansive soils to swell resulting in heaving of the lightly loaded
floor slabs The existing house slabs were generally not designed for the expansive
subgrade soil conditions
For those floor slabs exhibiting severe distress we recommend that the existing
floor slabs be completely removed and replaced with new slabs The near surface
expansive soils should then be over excavated and be replaced with a minimum of
300 millimeters of compacted non expansive select granular fill materials Alternately the
existing floor slab may be replaced by a structural floor slab supported on grade In
addition a concrete sidewalk with a minimum 900 mm deep cut off wall should be
provided around the perimeter of the house to reduce the potential for water infiltration
into the underlying foundation soils For those house structures that exhibit only minor
slab distress we believe that the removal of the existing floor slab would not be
necessary considering the budget constraints for this project However the concrete
cut off wall recommended above should be implemented for these house structures to
reduce the potential for moisture fluctuations of the expansive clayey soils underneath the
house slabs

used to support the proposed new additions anticipated for the subject project or new

Evaluation of Existing Distresses
In general positive drainage away from the house structures should be
established across the project site The finished grades at the project site should be
designed to carry surface run off water away from the house structures Ponding of water
against the building foundations and slabs should be avoided Based on our field
observations it appears that the existing house structures may be subjected to the
infiltration of water below the foundations and slabs as a result of the following negative
drainage features observed at the project site
e As a result of the existing undulating topography across the project site
sheet flow from surface water run off will flow toward the house structures in
the downslope areas This negative drainage pattern is especially evident
at Building 509 and 510 which are
subjected to sheet flow run off from the upslope area
e Due to the lack of a gutter system rain water will discharge from the roof
down to the area around the house structure The area underneath the roof
discharge is not paved or covered by a sidewalk allowing the roof discharge
and other water sources to infiltrate into the underlying subsoils alongside
the house foundations
e The existing grades around the house structure especially for the area
directly below the edge of the roof appear to be higher relative to the
grades directly adjacent to the house structure The negative drainage
pattern established by the lower grades adjacent to the house structure will
cause water to pond around the house and infiltrate into the foundation
soils This is especially critical when irrigation water is applied on a regular
basis to the heavy landscaping alongside the perimeter of the house
Based on our evaluation water infiltration into the underlying expansive soils
directly below the house structure causes the expansive soils to swell resulting in
heaving of the lightly loaded floor slabs In addition the existing house structures were

effects of the expansive clay soils underlying the project site the slabs on grade should

Remedial Measures for Existing Slab On Grade Distress
For the existing house slabs that have exhibited severe distress from heaving
settlement and or separation such as Building No 510 we recommend that the existing
house slab be completely removed and be replaced The underlying expansive clayey
soils should be over excavated and be replaced with at least 300 millimeters of
non expansive select granular fill The over excavated subgrade should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 200 millimeters moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage
by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
In addition a 1 meter wide minimum concrete sidewalk with a 900 mm deep
cut off wall should be provided around the perimeter of the house to reduce the potential
for water infiltration into the underlying foundation soils The sidewalk should be
underlain by at least 300 millimeters of non expansive select granular fill or select borrow
subbase and should be sloped to shed water away from the house foundations Graphic
illustration of the recommended remedial measure is presented on Plate 3 Concrete
Cut Off Wall with Over Excavation and Replacement of Expansive Soils of this report
Alternately the existing interior slab may be replaced with a structural slab
supported on grade with grade beams as needed In addition the bottom of the interior
grade beams should be embedded a minimum of 600 millimeters below the adjacent
finish grade The slab subgrade should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3752 00 A Page 12
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The concrete sidewalk with cut off
wall as recommended in the first option should also be implemented for this remedial
measure It should be noted that this option may experience some slab distress when the
clayey subgrade soils are subjected to moisture fluctuations
We understand that removal of the existing house slab may not be economically
viable where only minor slab distresses are evident For these house structures it is our
opinion that removal of the expansive clay soils and replacement with non expansive
select granular fill under the house slab may be omitted However the 1 meter wide
minimum concrete sidewalk with the 900 mm deep cut off wall as recommended above
should be implemented to reduce the potential for moisture fluctuations of the slab
subgrade soils
In order to reduce the potential for the observed distress from recurring it is our

footings may be reduced to a minimum depth of 400 millimeters below the adjacent

An allowable bearing pressure of up to may be used for the
design of shallow foundations bearing on the recompacted in situ silty clay soils or
engineered fill material This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive
earth pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A
coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted in situ
clay soils or engineered fill materials Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be
estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 50 kilonewtons per square meter per
meter of depth kKN cu m This assumes that the soil around footings is well compacted
or undisturbed Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the
upper 300 millimeters of soil should be neglected
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement If foundations are designed in accordance with our recommendations we
estimate total settlements to be less than 25 millimeters with differential settlements on
the order of 15 millimeters

Site Preparation
Based on our evaluation and engineering analyses we anticipate that the majority
of the site grading work will consist of re establishing a positive drainage pattern across
the project site Minimal site grading work will be required for the construction of new
improvements for the subject project At the on set of earthwork the area within the
contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris
rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of off site
Slabs and foundations of the existing structures that are to be demolished should
be completely removed Any over excavation resulting from the demolition should be
backfilled with compacted granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned should
be removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with select
granular fill material moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in
200 mm loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Utilities that are to be abandoned in place under the proposed structures should be
backfilled by pumping lean concrete under low pressure
After demolition clearing and grubbing all areas beneath future new floor slabs
should be over excavated to provide at least 600 millimeters of compacted
non expansive select granular fill material below the bottom of the slab cushion The
select granular fill may be reduced to 300 millimeters under the new sidewalks around the
perimeter of house structures If zones of soft loose or saturated soils are encountered
at the over excavated subgrade level deeper over excavation may be required to expose
the underlying firm material The resulting over excavation should be backfilled with
compacted non expansive select granular material
The over excavated subgrade surface should be scarified to a minimum depth of
200 millimeters moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
GEOLABS HAWAIlI

maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91
test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density Slab subgrades should be kept moist and

cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the soil be

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling ten
borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 10 to depths ranging from about 3 7 to
6 6 meters below the existing ground surface using truck mounted and trailer mounted
drill rigs equipped with continuous flight augers and coring tools The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 10
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
California sampler with a 63 5 kilogram hammer falling 762 millimeters The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the last 300 millimeters of a 460 mm drive are shown on the
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice
D 2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in the
hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt If 1 2 meters of material is
recovered from a 1 5 meter core run the recovery would be 80 percent and would be
shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 80
