INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this exploration was to obtain an overview of soil
conditions at the site in order to provide data for the formulation of
engineering recommendations for the design of the site grading structural
foundations and pavement design for the proposed development The general
location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1

The scope of this exploration included the excavation of seventeen
17 test pits four 4 seismic survey lines visual observations of the
existing site conditions and engineering analysis
The results of our field exploration which we used in the engineering
analysis to formulate our conclusions and recommendations are presented
in the appendices of this report The approximate test pit and seismic
survey locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located one half mile southwest of the Mauna Lani
Hotel between Golf Course Fairway 9 Kaniku Drive and the Resort fish
ponds
The topography of the site located over the original surface of an
uneven lava flow generally varies from an elevation of about 25 to 30 feet
at the northerly end to about 50 to 55 feet at the southerly end
The easterly and southerly portions of the site isa very rough rock
area except for a dozed access road down the center Much of the
northerly portion of the development has been graded to a fairly smooth
surface that is covered with gravel to cobble size clinkers
An existing 15 inch underground sewer line runs along the northern
boundary of the site The sewer line inverts vary from about 12 to 26
feet below the existing ground surface

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
Hawaii the largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area
of approximately 4000 square miles The island was formed by the activity
of five 5 shield volcanoes These are Kohala which is long extinct
Mauna Kea which has had activity during recent geologic times Hualalai
which last erupted in 1807 and Mauna Loa and Kilauea both still active
The site is located on the northwestern flank of Mauna Ioa mountain
which makes up the central and southern part of the island consists
largely of the prehistoric member of the Kau Volcanic Series These
volcanics are the assemblage of Recent and Latest Pleistocene Aa and
Pahoehoe basalts laid down by the volcano The Kau lavas form a thin
veneer on the lower slopes of Mauna Loa
The lave flow at the site is an Aa flow consisting of thick beds of
rough broken sharp clinker rock locally the Aa basalt is called The
Kaniku Flow The clinker is extremely permeable

Soil Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by excavating
seventeen 17 test pits to depths between 8 to 18 feet below the existing
ground surface Also four 4 seismic survey lines were done at selected
areas within the site to evaluate the depth and rippability of the lava
formation
The field exploration disclosed that the site is covered with a fairly
thick unconsolidated clinker deposit that may vary from 11 to 20 feet or
more thick The clinker consists of cobble and gravel sized scoraceous
basaltic fragments Occasionally boulders are scattered through the
clinker layers
The subsurface conditions throughout the site appear to be relatively
uniform
No water was encountered in the test pits during the field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
From a geotechnical point of view the site is suitable for the
proposed site development
The field explorations disclosed that the site is underlain by
unconsolidated clinker rock formation Provided a well graded choking
layer is developed first the rugged volcanic materials after
scarification and recompaction should provide adequate bearing for the
proposed embankment fill structures related roads and parking areas
The proposed grading plan indicates that fills will enroach onto the
existing sewer line easement in several locations The sewer invert
appears to vary from about 12 to 26 feet below the existing ground
surface
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2166 00 Page 5
Three 3 test pits excavated to depths of 8 to 9 feet in these areas
encountered heavy concentrations of large boulder sized rock nested
together with void spaces and loose pockets of gravel
From visual observations of the upper 8 to 9 feet of the trench
backfill and unconsolidated clinker deposit encountered in the dug test
pits it appears that any future excavation work to repair the sewer would
be very difficult because of instability of the trench sidewalls and
detrimental effects to the proposed house foundations
Therefore we recommend the following three alternatives
Alternative No 1 Foundations Bearing on Sewer Backfill
Where the proposed fill embankment is placed over the existing sewer
easement over excavation and recompaction of the loose backfill would
be required
Because it is difficult to properly compact with compaction equipment
around large boulders we recommend that the upper portion of the
previous sewer trench backfill estimated to be an area about 35 to 45
feet in width be over excavated five 5 feet deep as indicated on
the Site Plan This excavated zone should stay beyond a zone that
will not damage the existing manhole structures
The large boulders from the over excavation should be removed
off site
After the trench is excavated it should be wetted heavily to wash the
fines into openings of the underlying rocky fills which are to remain
Additional well graded gravel cobble sized material should be placed
to obtain a dense choking layer prior to backfilling the trench with
broken up rock fill materials in 12 to 18 inch maximm loose lifts
compacted to 90 relative density
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2166 00 Page 6
However with this method future work on the existing sewer line
would be limited due to the new fill placement and building
construction
Alternative No 2 Move Structure and Fills Away from Sewer Easement
Move the proposed fill embankments and house pads away from the
existing deep sewer trench pipe This method would provide the most
stable foundation condition for the structures and would allow
future work on the sewer line if necessary
Alternative No 3 Provide High Retaining Structures or Caisson
Support
Construct high retaining walls or provide caisson deep foundation
support for Buildings A B and C along the top of the proposed
embankment
The wall or caisson foundations should be embedded down below the
sewer trench excavation slope assumed to be about 1H 1V a sufficient
depth to obtain adqueate setback distance away from the assumed
trench excavation slope face Alternately the footings may extend
down to the sewer invert depending on the subsurface conditions
This alternative would require that additional exploration and
engineering analysis be performed using core drilling methods to
determine the final foundation embedment depths to a competent dense
rock formation or other suitable footing bearing level

Site Preparation
Generally the existing site surface consists of unconsolidated
clinker gravel and cobble sized basaltic fragments with some
boulders
Prior to filling the area to receive fill should either be scarified
to a depth of 12 inches and recompacted with a heavy dozer or roller
to obtain a dense choking surface layer or a choking gravel fill be
placed over the clinker surface We believe several passes would be
required to provide a dense and compacted surface layer Any loose
areas disclosed during site preparation should be repaired by removing
the loose materials and backfilling with compacted suitable on site
materials
Fill Material
Generally the on site small clinker material less than 12 inches in
size will be suitable for fill Clinkers or large rock fragments in
excess of 12 inches in size should be crushed down to smaller sizes or
disposed of in deep fill section in accordance with the procedures
recommended under the subsequent section BOULDER DISPOSAL

If required imported fill material required for the embankment fill
should be similar to clinker or other well graded granular rocky
materials The maximmm rock size should generally be less than 12
inches The fill materials should be tested and approved by the soils
engineer prior to being transported to the project site
If the excavated rock material does not contain sufficient fines to
produce a well graded material off site cinders or soil may be mixed
into the excavated rock material to increase the percentage of fines
to provide a better graded material for compaction and increase the
stability of the fills
The larger boulders used in the construction of fills should be placed
along the toe sections or waste off site
Boulders encountered in excavations may be used in the deeper fills
provided that the following recommendations are followed
1 Boulders should be spread out and must not be nested together
They should be placed so that compaction equipment will be able
to compact around them
2 No part of the boulder should enroach any closer to finished
subgrade than the boulder s largest dimension and in no case be
closer than 3 feet to finished subgrade
3 No boulder larger than 3 feet in its largest dimension should be
placed in the fill It may be necessary to reduce the size of
larger boulders or dispose of them off site

Fill Placement
Embankment fills should be placed in horizontal loose lifts up to 18
inches thick and compacted to its maximum density
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in
excavated volcanic rock material due to the large particle size
Instead a testing program consisting of visual observations by the
geotechnical engineer and test strips to determine the lift thickness
and number of passes of a compactor required to achieve the desired
compaction should be conducted at the start of the project The
number of passes can then be used as the criteria for compaction
All fills to be placed on slopes having ratios steeper than 5 1
horizontal to vertical should be keyed and benched into the
underlying clinker formation to lessen potential instability of the
new fill see Plate 4 The keys and benches should not be based in
the upper loose clinker Proper scarifications and recompaction of
the loose clinker surface would be required to develop a dense choking
surface layer to receive the subsequent fill lifts

Foundations
We believe the in situ clinker and compacted granular fill resulting
from the above site grading will provide adequate foundation support for
the proposed buildings
Spread and wall footing foundations may be used for the proposed
structures
All exterior footing bottoms should be embedded a minimum of
twenty four 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade The
minimum embedment for interior footings may be reduced to eighteen 18
inches below the finished grade
For the footing bearing on the compacted clinker rock fill an
allowable net soil bearing pressure of 3000 p s f may be used The above
recommended soil bearing could be increased for footing bearing on natural
clinker densely backfilled with a choking fill material We believe if
required an allowable bearing value up to 6000 p s f could be used
These values may be increased by one third when temporary wind or seismic
loads are considered
It is an opinion that cavity probe drilling and grouting of the
drilled probe holes are not appropriate for the clinker formation and
compacted clinker fill
Slabs and spread foundations located close to the tops of the fill
Slopes should have extra sufficient footing embedment to obtain a minimm
horizontal set back distance of eight 8 feet measured from the outer
edge of the footing to the slope face The bottom of footing excavations
should have a choking fill layer to support the poured concrete footing
and the footing excavation should be properly backfilled with well graded
gravel size rock material in 10 inch lifts compacted to obtain a minimum
of 90 relative density

The ground surface should be shaped to divert surface runoff away from
building foundations and slabs
It is anticipated that the footings if constructed in accordance with
the above recommendations will have negligible less than 1 2 inch
settlements
Slab On Grade
Based on a non expansive slab subgrade condition anticipated at this
site on grade slab should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base course
materials compacted to 95 of its maximm density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78 On level areas the thickened edge around the on grade slab
should extend a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished
ground surface
To reduce differential settlement where the slab is partly on cut in
rock and partly on fill the cut portions should be over excavated a
minimum of about one foot a choking layer developed in the rugged clinker
rock surface and then recompacted to match the adjacent subgrade density
Utility Trenches
It is anticipated that most of the trenching will be done in
unconsolidated clinker material However some large boulders may be
encountered especially in the deeper trenches
Trench backfill should be granular material generally less than 6
inches in size and compacted by water and mechanical tamping to 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 except for the top 2 feet below
finished road subgrade which should be compacted to 95 of its maximm dry
density

Pavement
Asphalt concrete pavements may be used for the roadways and parking
areas In general the on site smaller clinker materials will provide an
excellent subgrade support when evenly mixed and compacted Assuming that
either the on site clinker or imported select granular fills with CBR
greater than 25 are used in the pavement subgrade we recommend that the
following preliminary pavement design be used for cost estimate purposes
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
4 Inch Base Course
6 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on
Compacted Clinker
The subgrade soil under the pavement area should be compacted to at
least 95 of the ASIM D 1557 78 maximum density to a depth of 6 inches
below the bottom of the base course
CBR tests and or field observations should be performed on the actual
subgrade soil samples during construction to verify the above design
sections The recommended sections consider medium traffic and that good
drainage will be provided for adjacent to paved areas

part upon information obtained from field data points such as test pits
and seismic survey lines Variations of conditions between the field data

indicated on the topographic map dated July 1988 provided by Belt
Collins Associates

estimation from the same map The physical location and elevation of the
field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field
data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as
such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on profiles
or cross section are based upon interpolation between field data points
and may not represent actual subsurface conditions

The work under this section includes preparation of fill areas placement
and compaction of fill These specifications are of a general nature and
may be superseded by specific requirements or recommendations within the
soils report
I
IT
Preparation of Fill Areas
A Any loose areas disclosed during site preparation should be
repaired by removing the loose materials and backfilling with
compacted suitable on site materials
B After approval the area to receive fill should be scarified to
a depth of 12 inches and recompacted by a heavy dozer or sheeps foot
roller to obtain a dense choking surface layer or a choking gravel
fill be placed over the clinker surface
Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site clinker material or
approved imported material Imported material shall be tested and
approved a minimum of seven 7 working days prior to transportation
to the site Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent
organic matter and other deleterious material and shall not contain
rocks or lumps in excess of twelve 12 inches in diameter

TIT
If the excavated rock material does not contain sufficient fines to
produce a well graded material imported cinders or soil may be
mixed into the excavated rocky material to increase the percentage
of fines to provide a better graded material for compaction and
increase the stability of the fills
Placement and Compaction of Fill
A The fill shall be placed in horizontal loose layers not to
exceed eighteen 18 inches in thickness Each layer shall be
spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain uniformity of material
and water content within each layer
B Rock fragments or boulders less than twelve 12 inches in
diameter may be utilized provided they are not concentrated in any
one area and that there is sufficient fine grained material to fill
the voids between the rocks
C Boulders encountered in excavations may be used in the deeper
fills provided that the following are followed
1 Boulders shall be spread out and must not be nested
together They should be placed so that compaction
equipment will be able to compact around them
2 No part of the boulder should enroach any closer to finished
subgrade than the boulder s largest dimension and in no case
be closer than 3 feet to finished subgrade

Appendix B
3 No boulder larger than 3 feet in its largest dimension
should be placed in the fill It may be necessary to reduce
the size of larger boulders or dispose of them off site
4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in the
proposed utility alignments to prevent difficulty in the
later excavation of the utility trenches
D Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in
excavated volcanic rock material due to the large particle size
Instead a testing program consisting of visual observations and
test strips to determine the lift thickness and mumber of passes of
a compactor required to achieve the desired compaction should be
conducted at the start of the project The mumber of passes can
then be used as the criteria for compaction
E The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot or vibratory rollers
suited to the rock type and capable of producing the specified
results in an expedient and efficient manner The equipment shall
be in good working condition fully ballasted and self cleaning
F The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic and uniform
manner over the entire fill area to produce a uniformly compacted
fill
G Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition and not in
accordance with these specifications shall not be approved and shall
be removed or reworked until the required density is obtained
H The soils engineer shall be provided unrestricted access to the
fill to perform the necessary inspections and testing

Appendix B
IV Seasonal Requirements
A No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather conditions
When interruption of work is due to heavy rain the previous fill
surface shall be approved before resumption of earthmoving
operations
B The contractor shall maintain the surface of the fill so that it
will be free draining and will not pond surface water
