Project Considerations
The Ka Makana Ali i development approximately 67 acres is in the East Kapolei
area in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is bounded
approximately by Kapolei Parkway to the north Roosevelt Avenue to the south the
proposed Phase II North South Road Extension to the east and a drainage channel and
Kapolei Middle School to the west
Based on the information provided the project will involve the development of the
property into a commercial retail center providing approximately square feet of
retail and or commercial space We understand the project will be constructed in two
separate phases The Phase 1 portion of the development will encompass about 50 acres
over the northern and central portions of the property The Phase 2 portion of the
development will encompass the remaining 17 acres of the development situated over the

Probing and Grouting Operations
A foundation probing and grouting program was implemented for Buildings B C D
E G H and J for the project The locations of these buildings are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 1 Pacific Ground Systems Inc performed the probing and grouting operations in
general conformance with the recommendations presented in our report dated
for the project The purpose of the probing and grouting operations was to
reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities or
compression of loose zones below these building structures
Nordic PCL Construction Inc surveyed the footing layout and marked the probe
hole locations prior to the probing and grouting operations In general the probe holes
were located at the column footings one probe hole for every 50 square feet of footing
area The approximate probe hole locations are shown on the Probe Hole Location Plans
Plates 2 1 through 2 16 In general the probe holes at the new building structures were
drilled to depths ranging from about 11 5 to 15 5 feet below the existing ground surface
corresponding to approximately 10 feet below the design footing bottom elevations
The probing and grouting operations were accomplished using hollow drill rods with
3 inch diameter drill bits with side discharge holes for water and grout discharge Each
probe hole was advanced by rotary percussion using a TEI 300HT drill mounted on a
Komatsu PC75 excavator Water was injected continuously through the hollow rods while
advancing to the target probe depth Evidence of cavities voids was observed by the loss
of water circulation with rapid receding of water into the drill hole For the probe hole
where cavities voids were not encountered the probe hole was tremie grouted through the
drill rod while the rod is being retracted The grout used for these probe holes generally
consisted of one sack of cement mixed with 7 5 gallons of water with a water cement ratio
of about 0 70
For the probe hole where cavities voids were encountered or suspected a 1 5 inch
diameter PVC pipe was inserted into the probe hole to maintain the integrity of the
open hole and to reduce the potential for cave in prior to the grouting operation The
probe hole was subsequently backfilled with sand cement grout batched by
Pacific Ground Systems Inc on site and injected at low pressures Additional probe holes
were also drilled in the proximity to better delineate the lateral extent of these potential
voids and or cavities
