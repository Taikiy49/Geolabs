SECTION 1 PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The beach fronting the Kaanapali Alii Resort experiences an annual beach
erosion of approximately 18 feet in depth during the summer months Then the beach
is generally fully replenished by November and lasts through the winter months During
the erosion period portions of the vegetation line and the adjacent beach walkway have
been compromised by the eroding sand process The project location and general
vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
In order to provide geotechnical data for the project we conducted a field
exploration at the site consisting of the drilling and sampling of six borings to depths of
26 to 28 5 feet below the existing ground surface Soil samples were collected from the
drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split
Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the
same hammer and drop
Core samples of the rock materials encountered at the project site were obtained
by using diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM D 2113
Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation

3 1
Regional Geology
The Island of Maui is comprised of two major volcanoes the older West Maui
Tertiary Epoch and the more recent East Maui Pleistocene Epoch also known as
Haleakala The Isthmus of Maui is a narrow gently sloping plain between these
two volcanoes The Isthmus of Maui was created by lava flows from Haleakala Volcano
ponding against West Maui Volcano The gently sloping plains were subsequently filled
with alluvium washed down from the slopes of West Maui and East Maui Haleakala
Volcanoes The erosional processes are dominated by the detachment of soil and rock
masses from the mountain walls and the soil materials are transported downslope
toward the isthmus primarily by gravity as colluvium Once these materials reach the
stream in the central portion of a valley alluvial processes become dominant and the
sediments are transported and deposited as alluvium
The project site is on the western flank of the West Maui Volcano which forms
the west side of the Island of Maui The West Maui Mountains have been deeply
incised by erosion depositing large quantities of unconsolidated recent sediments along
the coastal regions The site is underlain by recent littoral deposits of beach sands
overlying poorly consolidated beach and coral materials
3 2
Subsurface Characteristics
In general our borings encountered loose to medium dense calcareous beach
sands which extended from the surface to depths of about 15 to 18 feet below the
existing ground surface Underlying the surface sands our borings encountered poorly
consolidated sands and gravels which appear to represent discontinuous coral reef and
detritus deposits
We encountered groundwater in the drilled borings at depths of about 6 0 to
6 8 feet below the ground surface at the time of our exploration It should be noted that
groundwater levels are subject to change due to tidal fluctuation seasonal precipitation
storm surge conditions groundwater withdrawal and other factors

SECTION 4 GEOTECHNICAL EXPLORATION PROGRAM
4 1
Soil Borings Rock Coring and Associated Field Testing
Soil and rock sampling methods and field testing are described in more detail in
Appendix A The following discussion provides an overview of the methods and
procedures
Typically the soil borings were advanced with a combination of 4 inch diameter
continuous flight solid stem augers and continued with PQ size wireline coring methods
to advance the PQ size casing Sampling of the soils was performed using the Standard
Penetration Test SPT split spoon sampler ASTM D 1586
Where the borehole was advanced utilizing the PQ core barrel in the soil
formations samples of the materials recovered from the PQ core barrel were retained in
core boxes for visual observation
In situ testing of the subsurface materials in the borings using the SPT which
was performed in accordance with ASTM D 1586 provides a measure of the resistance
or consistency of a soil and is reported as the uncorrected N value which is defined as
the number of blows required to drive a standard split spoon sampler 12 inches with a
140 lb hammer after 6 inches of initial seating
Rock core recovery REC and rock quality designation RQD were measured
as described in Appendix A ASTM D 6032 Standard Test Method for Determining
Rock Quality Designation RQD of Rock Core states that Core sizes from BQ to PQ
with core diameters of 1 44 in to 35 in respectively are normally
acceptable for measuring RQD as long as proper drilling techniques are used that do
not cause excessive core breakage or poor recovery or both The NX size
RQD The RQD is also useful for large core diameters provided the core diameter is
clearly stated

Test Boring Exploration
We explored the subsurface conditions at the site by drilling and sampling
six borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 6 The borings extended to depths
ranging from about 26 0 to 28 5 feet below the existing pavement surface using a
track mounted drilling rig equipped with continuous flight augers and rotary coring tools
The field exploration was conducted on April 26 and The boring locations
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Soil borings were advanced with a combination of 4 inch ID continuous flight
solid stem augers and continued with PQ size wireline coring method to advance PQ size
casing
Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual and
textural examination in the field in general accordance with ASTM D 2488
Standard Practice for Description and Identification of Soils and monitored the drilling
operations on a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed
visually and by testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general accordance with
ASTM D 2487 Standard Practice for Classification of Soils for Engineering Purposes
Unified Soil Classification System as shown on the Soil Log Legend Plate A 0 1
Graphic representations of the materials encountered and other observations such as
groundwater levels etc are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 6
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM D 3550 Standard Practice for Thick Wall Ring Lined
Spilt Barrel Drive Sampling of Soils in general accordance with ASTM D 1586 Standard
Test Method for Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch
OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown
as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of the rock materials encountered at the project site were obtained
by using diamond core drilling techniques consisting of a PQ wireline coring system with
double tube core barrel core size of 3 35 inches in general accordance with
ASTM D 2113 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method that uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in the
hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description The
coring rates indicated on the Logs of Borings refer to the time required to complete that
particular core run at that depth Rock cores were described in general accordance with
the Rock Description System as shown on the Rock Log Legend Plate A 0 2 The Rock
Description System is based on the publication Suggested Methods for the Quantitative
Description of Discontinuities in Rock Masses by International Society for Rock
Mechanics March 1977

Recovery REC may be used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the
relative quality of rock masses where appropriate Recovery is defined as the actual
length of material recovered from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt
For example if 3 7 feet of material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery
would be 74 percent and would be shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run in rock that is
sound rock material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting
any drilling mechanical and handling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound
material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run in rock the RQD would be 50 percent and
would be shown on the Logs of Borings as RQD 50 Generally the following is used to
describe the relative quality of the rock based on the Practical Handbook of Physical
Properties of Rocks and Minerals by Robert S Carmichael 1989
