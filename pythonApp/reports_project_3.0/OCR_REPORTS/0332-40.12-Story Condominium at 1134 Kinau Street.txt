I INTRODUCTION
A GENERAL
This report presents the results of our investigation to
determine the subsurface soil and foundation conditions for
the project The purpose of the investigation was to provide
subsurface information necessary for the design of a founda
tion support system for the proposed structure The study
included subsurface borings laboratory testing and engineer
ing analyses The report summarizes our work on the project
and presents recommendations pertinent to foundation and
site development requirements
All work was performed in general agreement with our proposal
dated March 17 1972
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The proposed building site is located on the mauka side of
Kinau Street between Pensacola and Piikoi Streets and is
approximately 75 feet wide along Kinau and extends 250 feet
deep The site is presently occupied by 1 and 2 story
residences
The 12 story condominium tower will be approximately 85 feet
by 45 feet and is presently planned for the mauka north
portion of the property The makai portion of the site will
have a 2 level parking structure approximately 130 feet
by 55 feet
The 12 story tower will be bearing wall construction with
relatively heavy wall loading on the order of 30 kips per
lineal foot The 2 level parking structure will have
relatively light column and or wall design loads
II FIELD EXPLORATION
Four subsurface borings ranging in depth from 13 to 32 feet were
completed on March 23 and 24 1972 under the supervision of an
engineer from our firm The borings were accomplished using
truck mounted rotary drilling equipment with continuous flight
hollow stem augers and NX core barrel
The boring locations are shown on the site sketch Plate 1 and
the boring logs are presented in Appendix A
In all borings either Standard Penetration Test or 2 5 inch I D
ring samples were taken at selected depth intervals not exceed
ing 5 feet The Standard Penetration Test consists of driving
a 2 inch O D split spoon sampler 18 inches with a 140 pound
hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The number of blows
required to drive the sampler the last 12 inches is termed the
Standard Penetration Resistance N and is an approximate
measure of the relative density or consistency of a soil These

III SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
GEOLOGY
The City of Honolulu is located on the southwest side of the
elongated Koolau Volcanic dome which forms the northeast
portion of the island of Oahu Honolulu is underlain by
Koolau basalts composed primarily of basaltic lava flows of
early pleistocene or Tertiary age The basalt is overlain
by a complex interlayered system of non marine detritus
eroded from the Koolau basalts and of calcareous marine
sediments derived from reefs
Volcanic material of the Honolulu Volcanic Series ejected
from vents such as Diamond Head and Punchbowl are super
imposed on all of the previous deposits The most recent
deposits in the Honolulu area are non marine sedimentary
detritus consisting of sand gravel silt and clay eroded
from the older rock and calcareous marine deposits of coral
sand and coral rock
Borings made on the site generally show soils consisting of
three major classifications
Samples obtained
a an upper layer of brown sandy silt with variable
clay gravel and cinder content
b a lower layer of predominantly bedded cinder with
occastional layers of sandy silt
c a lower layer of white honeycombed coralline lime
stone coral rock
The coralline limestone encountered in the borings are
likely remnants of recent coral fringing reefs Coral reefs
in Hawaii have diverse origin and composition and are domi
nantly algal skeleton
The deposition of the black Sand layer probably corresponds
to the Shugar Loaf Round Top Tantalus eruption This
eruption took place about 5000 years ago and deposited a
vast quantity of black sand really fire fountain debris
over the area 10 to 50 feet thick
The upper layer of brown silt and or silty clay represents
further alluvial deposition of material produced by the
eruption but which was transported largely after volcanic
activity had ceased
