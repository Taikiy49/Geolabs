INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Department of Health Laboratory
Facility at Pearl City on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in general accordance with
our proposal dated
This report summarizes the findings of the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2209 00 Page 2
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a new central laboratory facility for the
Hawaii State Department of Health at the site Details of the proposed
facility were not available at the time this report was prepared however
it is anticipated that the building will be a one to two story concrete
or masonry structure with a _ semi basement It is anticipated that
concrete slabs on grade will be utilized for the building floors
Construction of new utility lines and flexible asphalt pavements for
parking areas are anticipated
Details of the proposed site improvements and final building location
were not available at the time this report was prepared
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading pavement and
foundation design of the project The subsurface information from this
exploration will be used to help determine the siting of the new facility
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated
The scope of work consisted of the following
1 Coordination and supervision of the drilling and sampling of six
6 borings by a drilling company under separate contract to the
Department of Accounting and General Services to depths between
24 5 and 25 0 feet below the existing ground surface
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual
observations of the surficial soil conditions

W O 2209 00 Page 3
3 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the
purpose of developing site grading pavement and foundation
design recommendations
6 Preparation of this report presenting our findings and design
recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing
are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located at the existing Department of
Health Waimano Training Facility in the upper ridges of Pearl City on the
island of Oahu The site has a gentle topographic relief and slopes down
in a southerly direction at about 12 to 17 percent
Currently about one third of the site is covered with grass and is
used for the grazing of livestock e g cattle and horses The remainder
of the site is covered with guava thickets

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a
geotechnical engineering point of view
The field exploration generally encountered stiff soils below the
site Our laboratory testing indicates that these soils generally have
low to moderate shrink swell potential It is our opinion that the stiff
on site soils may provide adequate foundation support for the proposed
structure and pavement
Site Grading
The details of the proposed site grading were not available at the
time this report was prepared The following recommendations pertaining
to site grading are of a general nature
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious
materials The resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of
properly off site or stockpiled for use in landscaping Any soft weak
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2209 00 Page 5
or yielding areas disclosed during the clearing operations should be
over excavated to firm ground and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with well compacted engineered fill
Generally the on site soils should be suitable for use as engineered
fill If additional imported material is required the material should be
non expansive select material less than three 3 inches in maximm
dimension less than 25 passing the 200 sieve and a Plasticity Index
less than 20 as determined by ASTM Test Method D 4318 84 The material
should be free of organic matter debris and other deleterious material
Areas designated to receive fill should then be scarified to a depth
of six 6 inches moisture conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or soft
spots encountered should be over excavated down to firm subsoils and then
replaced with compacted non expansive select material
All fills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding eight 8
inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Any fill to be placed on existing slopes steeper than 5 horizontal to
1 vertical should be keyed and benched to bond the new fill to the
existing subsoils This is graphically illustrated on Plate 3 Sketch of
Keying and Benching
Cut areas designated as structural or pavement areas should be
excavated to subgrade level then scarified and recompacted as recommended
above
The site should be graded to drain water away from building
foundations and pavement areas

Slope ratios of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical or flatter may be used for
the design of both cut and fill slopes
There may be buried abandoned cesspools on the project site The
available county and state records should be researched to
determine their locations Any cesspool found on the site should
be open cleaned of loose soft materials and backfilled with
granular material such as 3 Fine to two 2 feet below finished
subgrade Above this level the cesspool excavation should be
filled and compacted as specified above
Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
structure Allowable bearing values of 3000 p s f may be used for the
design of foundations bearing on stiff residual soil and 2500 p s f
for the design of foundations bearing on compacted fill These bearing
values are for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered
Estimated settlements are on the order of less than one half of an inch
total and one fourth of an inch differential
Individual footings and continuous wall footings should be
embedded a minimum of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade
If foundations are located next to utility easements they should
be embedded below a 1 1 H V imaginary plane extending upward from the
bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the
utility lines
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2209 00 Page 7
The bottom of all footing excavations should be observed by the
geotechnical engineer prior to placement of the reinforcement to
determine if adequate bearing has been obtained and that loose soils
are removed prior to concrete placement
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using
an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p c f per foot of depth This
assumes that the soil around the foundation is well compacted A
coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be used Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 18 inches of
soil should be neglected

Allowable bearing capacities of 3000 p s f for retaining wall
foundations bearing on undisturbed natural soils and 2500
p s f for retaining wall foundations bearing on compacted
fill are recommended
A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth
may be used for wall footings on level areas The passive
pressure must be reduced for walls located on slopes
A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine
the resistance of the wall against sliding
The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be
embedded a minimm of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade For sloping ground the footing should extend deeper
to obtain a minimm 8 foot set back distance from the outside
edge of the footing measured horizontally to the slope face
A subsurface drainage system subdrains and or weepholes
should be utilized behind the walls to minimize the build up
of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls Subdrains should be
constructed properly to drain water away from the walls
Select granular material e g select borrow or base course
is recommended as backfill behind the retaining walls to
reduce the amount of compactive effort required The upper
two 2 feet of backfill should be an impervious non expansive
material to reduce the infiltration of surface water behind
the walls
Care should be taken in the siting of proposed walls and
proposed foundations for structural elements above behind the
walls so that the foundation loads do not impose an excessive
surcharge on the walls

Slabs On Grade
Concrete slab on grade construction is anticipated for the floors
of the proposed laboratory facility A minimm of six 6 inches of
imported non expansive select material compacted to a minimm of 90 of
its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 should be placed
below the slab subgrade to reduce the potential effect of the on site
moderately expansive soils The select material should be less than
three 3 inches in maximum dimension with less than 15 passing the
200 sieve and a Plasticity Index less than 20 per ASTM Test Method
D 4318 84 The material should be free of organic matter debris and
other deleterious material A 4 inch layer of 3 Fine gravel should be
provided below the slabs as cushion fill The slab subgrade should be
kept moist prior to concrete placement
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration and
subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture barrier is
recommended below the slabs
Drainage
The finished grade outside the building should be shaped to shed
water away from foundations and to avoid ponding condition Also it
is advised that gutter systems be installed around the building and
that the discharge be diverted away from the foundation and pavement
areas Excessive landscape watering near foundations and slab should
also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above foundation recommendations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2209 00 Page 10
or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled tight against the wall or slab footing immediately after
setting of the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing
foundations
Utility Trenches
New utility lines are anticipated for the project A gramular
bedding consisting of six 6 inches of ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel 3B
Fine is recommended under pipes
On site soils or select material may be used for the trench
backfill The backfill from the bedding to about one foot above the
pipes should be granular material and should be lightly tamped to
reduce the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot
above the pipes to finished grade in non structural or unpaved areas
should be placed in maximm 10 inch horizontal lifts mechanically
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximm dry density per ASIM Test
Method D 1557 78 to reduce future ground subsidence

Additional Exploration
The findings and recommendations presented in this report are based
on a geotechnical engineering exploration performed without definite
design concepts and plans for the proposed development As such the
borings may not have been located in the proper locations and the borings
may not have penetrated to sufficient depths to be relevant to the
proposed structures and or site grading
Therefore it is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be
retained to perform additional exploration if necessary to verify
subsurface conditions to a greater extent when better plans for the
proposed site improvements are developed The recommendations presented
in this report are contingent upon this additional verification
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during site grading and
construction of the foundation This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2209 00 Page 12
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings Variations of conditions between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping from objects indicated on the Topographic Survey Map prepared
by Park Engineering dated March 22 1988 Elevations of
the data points were estimated from the same plan The physical location
and elevations of the field data points should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
