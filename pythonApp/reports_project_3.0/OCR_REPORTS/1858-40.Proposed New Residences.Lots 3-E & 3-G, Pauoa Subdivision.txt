INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering evaluation
performed for the proposed residences at Lots 3 E and 3 G Pauoa Subdivision Pauoa
Valley on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in general
accordance with our proposal dated
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1858 40 Page 2
This report summarizes the findings from the exploration and geotechnical
engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based on the data obtained
These recommendations are intended for design input and are not intended for
construction estimation or bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the plans provided to us the proposed new residences will be two story
structures with concrete on grade slabs for the entire first floor levels
Site grading involving minor cuts and fills up to about two 2 feet are anticipated
Short retaining walls are planned for grade separation of portion of the houses
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project sites in order to develop a generalized soil rock data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading and
foundation design of the project
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated January 16
1991 The scope of work was limited to
1 drilling and sampling of two 2 borings to depths at each lot of 10 feet
below the existing ground surface
2 reconnaissance of the project site and its vicinity to make visual
observations of the surficial soil conditions

W O 1858 40 Page 3
3 review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project site
and its vicinity
4 laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
to determine the engineering properties and classification of the materials
encountered
5 engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the purpose of
developing pavement and foundation design recommendations and
6 preparation of this report presenting the design recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing are presented
in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The subject lots are located at the northern portion of the Pauoa Subdivision which
is at the eastern end of Pauoa Road
Lot 3 E is a flag lot situated adjacent to Pauoa Stream The topography of the lot
slopes upward toward the west with an average gradient of approximately 15 percent
The current ground elevations range from about 274 feet to 286 feet There is an existing
sewer easement along the northern and eastern boundaries of the lot
Lot 3 G is located at the western end of the subdivision The ground surface
slopes upward toward the north on an average gradient of about 10 percent with ground
elevations ranging from 266 to 274 feet

The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two 2 borings at each
lot to depths of 10 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The stiff on site soil encountered can generally provide adequate foundation
support for the proposed residences provided that some care is exercised during
construction to reduce the swelling potential of the soil as recommended herein
The field exploration and laboratory tests performed on the near surface stiff on site
clayey silt and silty sand indicate that this material generally has low swelling potential
provided the soils are not allowed to dry out Therefore to reduce the effects due to soil
swelling under on grade slabs we recommend that the subgrade soils underneath the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1858 40 Page 5
cushion and slab be moistened and protected from drying from the start of construction
until the slab concrete placement Additionally a thicker slab cushion of 8 inches is
recommended
Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimum below the on grade slabs
should consist of non expansive soils such as select borrow crusher waste or coral
All imported fill material should be tested and approved by the geotechnical
engineer prior to transportation to the site
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other deleterious materials These materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site Any loose or soft spots
encountered at fill areas should be over excavated down to the firm subsoils and then
replaced with compacted non expansive select material
The fill areas should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture
conditioned and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM
D 1557 78
The fill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts and compacted
to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed residences An
allowable bearing value of 2000 p s f may be used for footings bearing on the compacted
fill soil or stiff natural ground
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1858 40 Page 6
Individual footings and continuous wall footings should be embedded a minimum
of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
Footings next to utility lines should be deepened to extend below a one and one
half horizontal to one vertical plane extending from the bottom of the utility pipe
Slab On Grade
It should be noted that the expansion potential of the on site soils will increase if
allowed to dry then are saturated Therefore the on site soil should be kept thoroughly
moistened The thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade
An 8 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided below the slab
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration an impervious moisture barrier may
be incorporated below the slab
Concrete Driveway
The subgrade soils should be maintained in a moist condition and prevented from
drying out prior to concrete placement

Retaining walls will be used to provide grade separation for the front portion of the
house The guidelines for design of retaining walls are as follows

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should be shaped to
shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding condition Also it is advised that
gutter systems be installed around the houses and that gutter water should be diverted
far away from the foundations and driveways Excessive landscape watering near
foundations and slabs should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
uirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause excessive subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled tight against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting
of the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This review
is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations
given herein if this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and construction of the
foundations This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should b
