PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed building will be located at the rear northeastern
portion of the new Mountain View Center in Kahaluu The building will be
a one story steel framed structure with a mezzanine level and CW
perimeter walls approximately 80 by 125 feet in plan
W O 1717 10 Page 2
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to explore the subsurface soil and
water conditions at the current retail store building site to provide
up dated recommendations for use in the design of the site grading and
foundations of the proposed structure particularly to evaluate if there
is an adequate surface crust to support a spread and continuous footing
type of foundation system for the proposed one story building
The scope of our exploration included drilling and sampling of three
3 borings to depths ranging between 27 and 62 feet below the existing
ground surface Also included were laboratory testing of selected samples
obtained engineering analysis and evaluation of the field and laboratory
data and development of the foundation design recommendations
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in Kahaluu near the mouth of the Kahaluu
Stream about 300 feet northwest of the junction of Kamehameha and
Kehekili Highways The site is flanked by the Kahaluu Fish Pond on the
east First Hawaiian Bank Kahaluu Drive Inn and a Chevron Gas Station on
the south and the Kahaluu stream on the west
Presently the western half of the site is occupied by a soil
stockpile about eight 8 feet high The remaining area is generally
flat The site is covered with trees and shrubs

DISCUSSTON AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our three 3 drilled borings indicate that a minimm of 47 feet of a
highly compressible layer of lagoonal clayey silt with fine sand deposits
exist below the upper 6 to 7 feet of surface fill crust at the proposed
development site Based on our laboratory soil testing and study of old
1969 aerial photograph most of the existing surface fill crust was
probably placed at least 15 to 20 years ago and it appears that the
underlying soft clayey silt has compressed due to the weight of this fill
and is normally consolidated Therefore it is our opinion that provided
no new fill is placed over the existing grade future settlement of the
existing ground is expected to be minimal However about 1 to 2 feet of
new fill is required within the site for drainage considerations
Therefore some significant ground settlement would occur due to
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 1717 10 Page 4
compression of the soft deposits caused by the new fill load Because of
the low permeability of the soft clayey silt layers the rate of ground
settlement due to the new fill loads will be slow The laboratory data
indicates that it would take about four 4 years for 90 of the total
primary settlement to take place
An 8 foot high fill stockpile is located over the western half of the
building site Upon removal of the fill stockpile the soil beneath
approximately half of the building site area will be over consolidated due
to the weight of the stockpile Consequently when the subsurface is
subjected to the new fill and building loads differential settlement may
occur
In order to reduce potential short and long term differential
settlements that may be detrimental to the building structures we believe
that a surcharge program should be considered for the portions of the
building situated outside of the existing stockpile surcharge prior to the
construction of the proposed building structure
Surcharge of Building Site
To reduce excessive differential ground settlements we recommend the
eastern half of the proposed building site without the fill stockpile
should be preloaded with surcharge fill to induce ground settlements prior
to building construction
The total height of the surcharge should be a minimm of about five
5 feet
For a five foot high surcharge our estimate of the surcharge time
would be on the order of three 3 to four 4 months Estimated ground
settlement due to the five foot surcharge is on the order of 6 to 12
inches depending of the actual thickness of the very soft compressible
soil stratum

Settlement gauges should be installed in the area prior to surcharge
fill placement and periodic survey readings of the gauges should be used
to monitor the actual settlement The surveyed gauges will be utilized to
verify the rate of settlements and to help determine when the surcharge
fill can be removed
After completion of the surcharge program construction of the
proposed building may commence
Foundation Recommendation
Since the design of the proposed building have not yet been
finalized details of the structure and its loading are not available
However it is our understanding that the individual column load for the
proposed building will be small probably less than eight 8 kips
Our subsurface exploration encountered a variable but generally
competent upper crust of fill which could provide adequate bearing support
for the shallow spread or continuous footings which may be used to provide
foundation support of the structure
In general the recommended spread footings should be placed at a
level as high as possible to reduce the footing load applied to the
underlying compressible deposits The footing bottom should be embedded
at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade Foundations
should not be placed below about elevation 3 feet or special design may
be required due to the close proximity of the soft subsoils and water
level
An allowable net bearing capacity up to 1500 p s f may be used in
the footing design The bottoms of all footing excavations should be
recompacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established by
ASTM D 1557 78 Test Method

It is our current understanding that a one story steel framed
structure with CMU perimeter walls is planned for the site To reduce the
effects of differential settlements the CMU walls should be designed with
expansion joints at horizontal intervals of about 10 to 15 feet
Settlements
Provided the recommended surcharge program have been followed the
estimated footing settlements under the design loading are anticipated to
be on the order of less than about 1 inch total and 0 5 inch
differential It is probable that some footings would be underlain by
organic silt or clay lens beneath the fill For these footings we
believe that settlements would be slightly higher and occur slowly over a
period of years
Site Preparation
Major site fill is not recommended since the additional weight of
fill will initiate another cycle of ground settlements within the
underlying very soft subsurface soils encountered at the proposed project
site
We recommend that the upper six 6 inches of the existing subgrade
soil beneath the future new foundation concrete slabs and pavement areas
should be first scarified moisture conditioned and recompacted to 95 of
its maximm density as established by ASIM D 1557 78 Test Method Any
loose or soft clayey soils encountered at subgrade level should be removed
and replaced with compacted select on site granular soils or with other
suitable imported select borrow material compacted to the above mentioned
standard
Minimum required fill should consist of select on site granular
materials and or imported fill material consisting of non expansive select
granular materials less than three 3 inches in maximum size with less
than 30 passing the 200 sieve

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading foundation and pavement
design should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the borings were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the topographic survey Elevations of the borings
were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation points on
the topographic survey map The physical location and elevation of the
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
boring points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and
as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings
and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the boring points were collected at the times
given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this report
This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of
this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to
variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
