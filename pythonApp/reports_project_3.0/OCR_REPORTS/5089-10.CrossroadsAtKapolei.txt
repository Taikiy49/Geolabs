PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Crossroads at Kapolei project site is located at Lot 7248 Map 595 of Land
Court Application 1069 within the Kapolei area in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The project site is about 2 794 acres and is between Kamokila Boulevard and
Haumea Street and between Wakea Street and Alohikea Street
We understand that two single story commercial retail structures are planned at the
western and southern corners of the site The area remaining will be dedicated to
on grade parking Based on the existing site topography we envision that minimal
grading work will be required to achieve the finished grades for the proposed
development The layout of the commercial retail development is shown on the Site Plan

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Allied Builders System is the general contractor for the Crossroads at Kapolei
project Royal Contracting Co Ltd performed the site grading operations foundation
excavation pavement construction and utility installation The site grading consisted of
clearing and grubbing footing excavation subgrade preparation placement and
compaction of fills and backfills pavement construction and utility trench excavation and
backfill
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the existing ground was generally
over excavated where necessary to approximately 18 inches below the slab cushion
within the building limits to provide the recommended 18 inch thick non expansive select
granular fill layer The pavement areas were also over excavated where necessary to
approximately 12 inches below the base course layer to provide the recommended
12 inch thick non expansive select granular fill in lieu of aggregate subbase course The
over excavated subgrades were moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor During
placement of the non expansive select granular fill layer the fill materials were generally
placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content and compacted
to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
New sewer water and drain lines were installed to serve the new development In
general a bedding layer consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel was provided below
the piping and manholes for uniform support Free draining granular materials consisting
of No 3B Fine gravel was also used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the
pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort
on the initial trench backfill The trench backfill was placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to no less than 90 percent relative compaction Where trenches
are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement
grade was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for
testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in
our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on the attached Plate 2
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 209 field density tests including retests of failed
areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 2922
and D 3017 Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1
