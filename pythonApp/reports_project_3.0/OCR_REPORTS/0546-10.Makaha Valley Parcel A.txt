In accordance with our recent conversation enclosed are the boring
logs for Makaha Valley Parcel A The subsurface soils at this site
are Similar to Pokai Bay They consist of a thick blanket of
colluvial soils with boulders which is subject to creep movements
The thickness of the soil mantle is greater at Makaha Valley and
would require deep stabilization measures It does not appear that
the cost for the stabilization would be justified It is therefore
our opinion that the site is not suitable for the proposed develop
ment
Two test borings were drilled at the locations shown on the enclosed
Boring Location Plan Plate 1 At each boring location several
attempts were made to penetrate the upper boulder layers With the
exception of Boring 2D the remaining borings encountered refusal
from 2 0 to 5 5 feet below the existing ground surface within a
hard boulder Boring 2D was extended through the boulders to a
depth of 28 5 feet As can be seen from this boring log the sub
surface condition consist of numerous basalt boulders with a matrix
of brown clayey silt with some sand and gravel No groundwater was
encountered during the subsurface explorations
The boring locations are approximate and are based upon visual ob
servations in the field The ground elevations are based upon the
ground surface contours at the approximate boring location
Should you have any questfons concerning the findings or conclusions
please do not hesitate to contact us
