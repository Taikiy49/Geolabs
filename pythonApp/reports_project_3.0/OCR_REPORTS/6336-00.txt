SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
A Geolabs representative should monitor the earthwork operations to observe
whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation and filling
process and to confirm whether the exposed soil and or rock conditions are similar to
those encountered and assumed in the design
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious materials and
other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly off site to
reduce the potential for contaminating the excavated materials to be used as
embankment fill materials Soft loose weak or yielding areas disclosed during site
preparation operations should be over excavated to expose firm ground and the
resulting excavation should be backfilled with structural fill material well graded
75 mm minus granular materials compacted to a minimum 90 percent relative
compaction The material resulting from the over excavation may be re used as a
source of general fill material under the landscape areas
We understand that the two rows of trees lining the project site will be removed or
relocated to accommodate the planned construction The excavation resulting from
the tree removal should be backfilled with compacted granular fill material The
select granular fill material should be moisture conditioned to above optimum
moisture content placed in 150 to 200 mm level loose lifts and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
After clearing and grubbing areas at grade or designated to receive fills should be
scarified to a minimum depth of about 200 millimeters moisture conditioned to
above the optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction The compaction requirement of the soil subgrades subjected
to vehicular traffic such as pavement subgrades should be increased to at least
95 percent relative compaction Where shrinkage cracks are observed after
compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened
to close the cracks Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade
