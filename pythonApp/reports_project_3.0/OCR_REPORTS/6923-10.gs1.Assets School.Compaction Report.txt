Dear Ms. Ka’anoi: 
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing 
services performed during site grading, utility installation, and foundation preparation for the 
Assets School Lower Campus K-4 Village project from November 6, 2017 to May 16, 2018 in 
accordance with our fee proposal dated November 6, 2017. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located at the existing Assets School campus between Main Street 
and North Nimitz Highway in the Honolulu area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project 
generally involves the construction of three new classroom building structures at the 
northwest corner of the existing campus. We understand that this area will be designated as 
the K-4 Village. In addition to the new building structures, concrete walkways, an asphaltic 
concrete surfaced fire lane, and other related site improvements are planned for the project. 
The location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1 and the new 
building structures are shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services 
pertaining to the site grading, utility installation, and foundation preparation for the project 
site. 
SITE GRADING, UTILITY INSTALLATION & FOUNDATION PREPARATION 
The site grading, utility installation, and foundation preparation work for the Assets 
School Lower Campus K-4 Village project was observed by Geolabs, Inc. from 
November 6, 2017 to May 16, 2018.  The site grading, utility installation and foundation 
preparation consisted of clearing and grubbing, and placement and compaction of fills up to 
about 2 feet to achieve the design finished grades for the project. The site grading, utility

installation and foundation preparation work was performed by BC Construction. During the 
site grading and backfilling operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials utilized 
as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing. The 
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in 
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test 
results are summarized on Plate 3. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above optimum moisture 
content, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as 
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D1557. 
During the site grading and backfilling operations, periodic field density tests were 
performed in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D2922 (Nuclear Gauge 
Method).  Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 
4.9 of this report. These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on 
constructed fills and backfills at the site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by 
pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These locations and elevations should 
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed to 
be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained 
and/or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill and backfill 
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures during the above grading 
operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the site grading, backfill installation, and foundation 
preparation work for the Assets School Lower Campus K-4 Village project, observed and 
tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report, were performed in conformance with 
the general intent of the following references. 
1. 
Chapter 14 of the Revised Ordinances of Honolulu 1990, as amended. 
2. 
Report by Geolabs, Inc. entitled, “Geotechnical Engineering Exploration, 
Assets School, Honolulu, Oahu, Hawaii”, dated December 17, 2013.
