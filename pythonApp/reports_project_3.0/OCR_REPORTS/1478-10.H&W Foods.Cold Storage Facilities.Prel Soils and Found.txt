Project Consideration
It is proposed to construct a cold storage facility with
approximately 25 000 square feet of freezer and storage area on the
vacant lot The facility will include freezers a chiller a dry
storage area a loading zone and an equipment room An employee parking
zone is also planned
The building construction is anticipated to consist of CMU walls
with metal siding and roof

Fill probably up to 3 to 4 feet may be required to raise the
finished grade for drainage and cold storage design purpose Estimated
design wall load of 3 0 kips per foot are anticipated Also live load
of up to 300 p s f is expected
Purpose and Scope
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the subsurface
soil conditions at the site in order to develop preliminary soil data
for the design of the site grading foundation and pavement sections for
the proposed structure
The scope of investigation included drilling and sampling three 3
borings to 22 to 80 5 foot depths Also included in the scope were
laboratory testing of samples obtained engineering evaluation of the
field and laboratory data development of design recommendations and
report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
Site Description
The site is located on the western makai side of Nimitz Highway
in the Iwilei district of Honolulu
The area is generally flat and presently paved with asphaltic
concrete The surrounding properties are occupied by warehouse type
structures and storage yards

Discussion and Recommendations
The project site is underlain by a layer of compressible
lagoonal harbor deposit approximately 20 feet thick This layer varies
from a loose gravel in the mauka or western portion of the site to a
soft clay in the makai or eastern portion
settlements are anticipated due to the weight of the new fill and
building loads
To induce ground settlements prior to foundation construction we
recommend that the required new fill and additional surcharge fill be
placed as soon as possible

Ideally the building construction should be delayed until most of
the settlement due to site filling have occurred Then spread footing
foundations may be used for the building support with allowance for some
future settlements due to the structural and live loads
Our recommendations are discussed in detail and presented in the
following sections
Settlements
Based on the findings we believe that ground settlements will
occur due to 1 the weight of the new Fill 2 the building load and
3 additional settlements that may develop if a heavy floor loading is
applied
Due to the variation in soil characteristics in the mauka makai
direction we anticipate that the settlement experienced will also
vary Generally less settlement and shorter waiting time are
anticipated for the mauka areas which are underlain by gravelly soils
The anticipated areal settlement due to 41 inches of new fill is
estimated to be on the order of 3 to 6 inches Based on the laboratory
test results we estimate that the time required for 90 of the
settlements to occur would be about 2 to 6 months In order to
accelerate the settlements and reduce the waiting time a temporary
surcharge fill may be utilized over the permanent fill as described in
the subsequent section
For the anticipated wall loads of about 3 kips per linear foot
estimated additional settlements are on the order of 1 to 1 5 inches
Substantial live loads due to food storage may occur Calculated
settlement under a floor loading of 300 p s f is on the order of about
Z to 4 inches

Surcharge Fill
We believe a surcharge program should be considered to minimize
ground settlements due to the new fill and floor loads
The length of the surcharge period will depend on the amount of
surcharge used If a 10 foot high surcharge is used our estimated
surcharge time would be on the order of 2 to 3 months To minimize the
amount of material required the surcharge fill height may be reduced to
5 feet over tne mauka portion of the site due to the faster settling
time anticipated there
Periodic optical survey readings of the installed settlement
markers would be used to monitor the actual settlement The readings
should be taken by the project surveyor and transmitted to the soils
engineer to determine the actual surcharge time required
It shouid be understood that although surcharge fill might help to
minimize ground settlement due to the new fill and floor loads footing
settlements due to building loads may still occur

After completion of the filling and surcharging program shallow
spread or continuous footing foundation may be used
In general spread footings should be placed at a level as high as

compressible deposits We believe that a maximum net allowable

Foundations should not be placed below about elevation 3 feet
otherwise special design may be required due to the close proximity
of the soft subsoils and water level
The anticipated footing settlement under 3 kips per foot of wall
loading applied after the new fill and surcharge have been placed
and allowed to settle is estimated to range between 1 to 1 5
inches Approximately 70 of these settlement will occur slowly
probably over a number of years after completion of the project
We recommend that all footing bottoms be embedded at least 12
inches below the lowest adjacent grade Lateral loads acting on
the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure developed
against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by
friction resistance developed between the bottom of the foundations
and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil
around the foundation is well compacted The frictional resistance
may be calculated by using a coefficient of friction of 0 4 The
above resistance values are maximum values and do not contain a
factor of safety
Pile Foundation
If the amount of settlement predicted are not tolerable then pile
foundations may be utilized The piles should be used to support
the building loads in conjunction with a structural slab to
eliminate the fill required Otherwise a surcharge fill program
would be required to minimize downdrag forces on the piles

Freezer Unit
Due to the proximity of the water level provisions for a capillary
water break are an important consideration to minimize the possibility
of frost heave under the freezer units
Frost action occurs when there is a combination of freezing
temperature high water level and subgrade soils which have high
capillary rise such as very fine sands and silts When the surface of
the soil is below freezing temperature upward moisture migration from
the high water level occurs due to capillary action and condensation
Tais water collects and freezes to form lenses of ice in the upper part
o the ground which displaces the soil and causes heaving The
formation of the ice lenses can continue indefinitely as long as
suitable conditions exist
The existing upper fill soils consist of silty sands which is
highly susceptible to frost action In order to reduce the potential
for frost action under the freezer a capillary break layer consisting
of a minimum of 2 feet of clean gravel such as 3B Fine gravel over 12
inches of impervious soil is recommended under the freezer floor slab in
order to prevent capillary rise

Site Grading
Site Preparation
The overall site preparation will include removal of any
vegetation old pavements debris and other structures The top 10
inches of the site should then be scarified and recompacted to at
least 90 of the ASIM D 1557 78 maximum density before new fill is
placed
Fill Material
The material below the slab or pavement base course and within 24
inches of the finished grade should be granular material with a CBR
value greater than 25 and the maximum size of the particle less
than 6 inches
The material below the top 24 inches may consist of the general
borrow type material as specified in Section 16 of the Standard
Specification for Public Works Construction City County of
Honolulu May 1975
Fill Placement
All fills and backfills should be placed in horizontal lifts not
exceeding 9 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a dry
density of at least 95 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
Cohesive materials if used below the top 24 inches may be
compacted to 90 minimum
Pavements
Because of the projected settlement flexible A C pavement should
be considered whenever appropriate For pavements outside the cold
storage facility the following pavement section may be considered

Utility Lines
The underground utility lines should be placed as late as possible
after the completion of site grading to minimize post installation
settlements In addition flexible joints should be considered
particularly the connections at building should have a higher degree of
flexibility to allow for differential settlements
Trench excavations would require proper shoring particularly if
patches of soft organic clays or silts are encountered Since
dewatering may affect adjacent structures a soils engineer should be
consulted if the water level is to be lowered
The materials and compaction requirements for the backfilling of
the trench should be consistent with those recommended under the Fill
Placement section

Additional Borings
Additional borings are recommended after more details regarding the
site development are available to evaluate the variability of the
subsurface conditions and to refine the limits of the surcharge fill
program
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation and pavement should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
The earthwork operation including excavation fill placement and
compaction and surcharge should be observed by the soils engineer
during construction to see that adequate compaction has been obtained
and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of
our investigation
We also recommend that the footing excavations be inspected by the
soils engineer prior to placement of concrete or reinforcing steel to
check that adequate bearing has been obtained
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
