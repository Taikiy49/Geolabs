MASS GRADING
The mass grading for Mehana at Kapolei Phase 5A generally consisted of clearing
and grubbing removal of existing stockpiles and site grading for cuts and fills less than
5 feet in thickness Royal Contracting performed the earthwork operations for the
project
At the onset of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits were generally
cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials Materials generated from
the clearing operations were disposed of off site Following the clearing and grubbing
operations the areas to be cut were excavated Finished grades were compacted using
a sheepsfoot and or smooth vibratory drum roller In general the site was capped with
3 feet of coralline materials
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 In the roadway areas the fill
and backfill materials within 3 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative density
Samples of the materials utilized as fill were obtained and transported to our
laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were
established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor The test
results are summarized on Plate 3
Periodic field density tests were performed on compacted subgrade on fills and
utility backfills in general accordance with ASTM D6938 Nuclear Gauge Method The
test results are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 11 The test locations and
elevations were estimated using on site survey stakes for reference These locations
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used Compacted areas that did not conform to the required moisture and
density were reworked until the measured relative compaction met or exceeded the
minimum specified for the project
Our services consisted of part time field observation and testing for subgrade
preparation fill and backfill placement compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above site grading operations
Based on our field representative s observations and the referenced test results
it is our opinion that the mass grading for Mehana at Kapolei Phase 5A Kawena of the
Mehana at Kapolei Development project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period
covered by this report were performed in accordance with the general intent of the
