INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Kokea Business Center at 1040 and 1070 North King
Street in Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of
the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design
input only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at 1040 and 1070 North King Street in Honolulu on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The site is bounded by Kokea Street to the west and King
Street to the south Based on the tax map key plan the subject land parcel is
approximately rectangular in shape covering approximately 25 300 square feet in area
Based on the demolition plan provided a portion of the site is currently occupied
by an old gas station building metal parking structure and walls The site had been

site into a commercial business center The business complex will be one story high
on the Diamond Head side two stories high in the middle and up to three stories high
on the Ewa side Ground floor level parking consisting of asphalt concrete pavement
is also planned at the mauka side of the proposed business center
Based on the information provided by the project architect we understand that
the proposed building will generally be of CMU wall construction at the ground floor

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The project site is located on the southern
flank of the Koolau shield Its geomorphology and subsurface conditions are directly
related to glacio eustatic fluctuations of the sea level during the Pleistocene Epoch and
the genesis of the Honolulu Coastal Plain The subsurface conditions in the area are
dominated by fossil Pleistocene Age coral algal reef deposits and related deposits
which are intercalated with terrigenous sediments laid during high stands of the sea
The base of the stratigraphic section in this area is Koolau Basalt
In the last 10 000 years or so the sea level has adjusted to its present stand
Terrigenous and marine sediments have continued to accumulate in low energy
estuarine or lagoonal environments resulting in thick deposits of soft loose sediments
along the coast in the areas which were formerly valleys and drainageways Land
development and reclamation projects within the last 100 years have brought the
project area to its present form

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by a
relatively thick and competent deposit of fill materials and alluvial soils extending to a
depth of about 10 feet below the existing ground surface This was underlain by loose
sands and localized soft silts extending to a depth of approximately 25 feet below the
existing ground surface
Shallow foundations consisting of spread and or continuous footings bearing on
the recompacted fill soils encountered at shallow depths may be used to support the
proposed building provided that some settlement is tolerable Based on the subsoil
conditions encountered and the moderately high structural loads anticipated for the
proposed development footing settlements on the order of about 1 and 1 inches may
be anticipated for the column and wall footings respectively Efforts should be made
to reduce the building loads if these settlements are not tolerable
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot p s f may
be used for the design of footing foundations bearing on the recompacted near surface
fill soils encountered at shallow depths We recommend that the footing foundations
be structurally connected together with tie beams to reduce the potential for total and
differential settlements In general the footings should be placed at a level as high as
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3551 00 Page 8
possible to take advantage of the fill crust thereby reducing the footing load applied
over the underlying compressible deposits In order to reduce the potential for distress
due to differential movements between the slab and foundations we recommend that
free floating slabs on grade with no structural connections to the wall and column
foundations be used
Based on the existing topography and the proposed finished floor elevation fills
on the order of about 2 feet will be required to raise the existing ground surface to the
finished subgrade level Because of the soft loose soils underlying the site we
recommend that site filling should be initiated as early as possible to allow the existing
ground to adjust to the new fill loads In addition pouring of the slabs on grade should
be delayed as long as practicable to take advantage of a longer time for fill ground
settlement We strongly recommend that a settlement monitoring program be

settlements of the planned fills and proposed footing foundations Detailed discussion

Foundations
It is our opinion that shallow foundations consisting of spread and or continuous
footings bearing on the recompacted fill soils encountered at shallow depths may be
used to support the proposed building provided that the anticipated settlement is
tolerable Based on the subsoil conditions encountered and the moderately high
structural loads anticipated for the proposed development footing settlements on the
order of about 1 and 1 inches may be anticipated for the column and wall footings
respectively with differential settlement of approximately one half the amount Efforts
should be made to reduce the building loads if these settlements are not tolerable
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot p s f may
be used for design of footing foundations bearing on the recompacted fill soils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3551 00 Page 9
encountered at shallow depths This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may
be increased by one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces We recommend that the footing foundations be structurally connected together
with tie beams to improve the rigidity of the foundation thus reducing the anticipated
differential settlement
In general the footings should be placed at a level as high as possible to take
advantage of the fill crust thereby reducing the footing load applied over the underlying
compressible deposits To utilize the surficial fill crust we recommend that footings
be embedded to a maximum depth of about 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they
should be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane
extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts
of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below
grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for
foundation settlement
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that
the soil around footings is well compacted or undisturbed Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be
neglected A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the
recompacted near surface soils
It is recommended that the bottom of all footings bear on the recompacted near
surface soils If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3551 00 Page 10
excavations they should be over excavated to expose firm materials The over
excavation should either be backfilled with granular material compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom should be extended down to the
underlying competent material Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density
of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Designation
D 1557 91
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii prior to the placement of reinforcing steel and concrete to confirm the
competency of the foundation bearing materials and the required embedment depths
Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed building
structures Based on the existing topography and the proposed finish floor elevation
we anticipate that fills up to about 2 feet high will be required to raise the existing
ground surface to the proposed finished subgrade Because of the soft loose deposits
underlying the site some amount of ground settlement may be anticipated under the
new fill loads Therefore in order to reduce the potential for distress due to differential
movements between the slab and foundations we recommend that free floating slabs
on grade with no structural connections to the wall and column foundations be used
Joint filler and sealant may be used to fill the opening between the edge of slab and
other structural elements In addition construction of the slabs on grade should be
delayed as long as practicable in order to permit maximum ground settlement to occur
Slab subgrades should be prepared in accordance with the recommendations
presented in the Site Preparation section of this report For slabs on grade that are
not subjected to traffic loading we recommend that a 4 inch cushion layer of No 3B
Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used below the slabs A vapor
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3551 00 Page 11
membrane should be incorporated on top of the gravel cushion layer to reduce potential
damage to moisture sensitive floor coverings by infiltration of moisture It is
recommended that a 2 inch layer of moist fine sand be placed above the vapor
membrane to protect the membrane and to aid in concrete curing
For slabs on grade that will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as the concrete
driveway a 6 inch layer of base course compacted to 95 percent relative compaction
is recommended for more uniform support The base course should consist of crushed
basalt aggregate We recommend that the concrete slab be designed by a structural
engineer for the intended loads and should be reinforced as a structural slab to
accommodate some amount of differential settlement For design of structural slabs
a modulus of subgrade reaction of 250 pounds per cubic inch p c i may be used for
the compacted base course Provisions should be made for proper load transfer across
the slab joints which are subjected to vehicular traffic
Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography and the proposed finished floor elevation new
fills on the order of about 2 feet are anticipated for the project construction The
weight of new fiils will initiate ground settlement due to compression of the loose soft
subsoils Therefore the site filling required should be initiated as soon as possible to
allow the existing ground to adjust to the new fill loads
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site
Slabs and foundations of the existing structures that are to be demolished should
be completely removed Any over excavation resulting from the demolition should be
backfilled with compacted granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3551 00 Page 12
should be removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with
select granular fill material placed in 6 to 8 inch loose level lifts and compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Utilities that are to be abandoned in place
under the proposed structure should be backfilled by pumping lean concrete under low
pressure
After demolition clearing and grubbing all areas at grade or to receive fills
should be scarified to a minimum depth of 10 inches moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed
as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Fill material required for the project construction should be placed in level lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
Where needed imported materials should consist of select borrow crushed coral or
basalt gravel The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles
greater than 3 inches in largest dimension The material should have a laboratory
California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell potential of not more
than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 It
should also contain between 10 and 30 percent passing the No 200 sieve Any
imported fill material should be tested and approved by Geolabs Hawaii prior to delivery
to the project site for its intended use
Settlement Monitoring
It should be recognized that it is very difficult to accurately predict the exact time
required for the filled ground and footings to settle because of variable localized soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3551 00 Page 13
conditions Therefore the actual settlement rate should be monitored during
construction
To monitor the actual settlement rate we recommend that settlement markers
or referenced survey points be placed as appropriate on the existing ground surface
structural fill footings and columns as reference points The settlement markers or
survey points should be surveyed by optical instruments and the readings should be
transmitted to Geolabs Hawaii for review The readings should be taken on a weekly
basis for the duration of the project construction and or until the settlement readings
indicate that the settlement has stabilized
Pavement Design
We understand that asphalt concrete pavement is planned for the ground floor
level parking at the mauka side of the proposed business center We anticipate the
vehicle loading for this project will consist primarily of passenger vehicles and light
trucks We have made our preliminary pavement design assuming the pavement
subgrade soil will consist of the surficial sandy soils or imported fill materials prepared
in accordance with the recommendations presented in the Site Preparation section
of this report Based on the above assumptions we recommend the following
pavement section be used for preliminary design purposes
Asphaltic Concrete Pavement
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course 95 percent relative compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Moist Subgrade
The subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of about 10 inches moisture
conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to not less than 95 percent
relative compaction The base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction CBR and density tests
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3551 00 Page 14
should be performed on the actual subgrade soils encountered during construction to
confirm the adequacy of the above section
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site
during or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in
or adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs be extended a
minimum of 2 inches into the subgrade soils below the aggregate base course to
reduce migration of landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively a

For improved long term performance we believe that a subdrain system should be
considered adjacent to paved landscaped areas
Utility Trench
We envision that new utility connections may be required for the proposed
project A granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials
such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for
the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support
around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the
possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material
or the on site granular soils The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content placed in about 8 inch loose level lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet
of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to not less
than 95 percent relative compaction

Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting
of the concrete to reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as
soon as possible and should be maintained to drain all surface run off away from the
slab and footing foundations
