Preliminary Geotechnical Findings and Recommendations
Proposed Waimanalo Kupuna Housing Project
Waimanalo Oahu Hawaii
This letter serves to present our preliminary geotechnical findings and recommen
dations for the proposed Waimanalo Kupuna Housing Project to be located in the
Waimanalo area on the island of Oahu Hawaii Detailed discussions and geotechnical
engineering recommendations will be presented in our forthcoming report
Subsurface Conditions
Our field exploration at the subject site consisted of hand augering and sampling
four borings to depths ranging from about 0 5 to 3 feet below the existing ground surface
and excavating and sampling ten test pits to depths ranging from about 1 to 10 feet below
the existing ground surface Our field exploration generally encountered surficial fills of
about 1 to 2 feet thick overlying moderately stiff to very stiff dark gray silty clay to depths
ranging from about 2 5 to 7 5 feet below the existing ground surface The silty clay layer
was underlain by hard coral formation or hard breccia material Toward the northeastern
end parallel to Kalanianaole Highway a sandy soil layer of about 2 to 3 feet thick was
encountered above the coral rock or breccia material The silty clay layer was not
encountered in some of the test pits excavated A relatively deep loose sandy soil layer
was encountered in Test Pit No 6 Hand augered borings were drilled adjacent to Test
Pit No 6 to approximate its lateral limits
Groundwater was not encountered at the time of the field investigation however
due to the low elevation and close proximity to the beach it is possible that water may be
encountered in deep excavations during construction as water levels are affected by tidal

Preliminary Recommendations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered the principal geotechnical
considerations for this project include the following
e Presence of the highly expansive silty clay layer near the existing ground
surface and its effects on the future surface structures and
e Presence of loose and or uncontrolled fills at the site and their effects on
future improvements constructed above these areas
In general due to the presence of the highly expansive silty clay layer encountered
near the surface of the site we recommend that slabs on grades for the proposed
structures at the site be supported on at least 2 feet of non expansive granular fill
material Depending on the proposed grading at the site consideration may be given to
raising the grades at the site in order to accommodate the minimum 2 feet of non
expansive granular fill recommended beneath future structures especially at the
northwestern portion of the site where the expansive silty clay layer is exposed at the
surface The non expansive granular fill layer recommended below the structures may be
reduced to a minimum of 12 inches below exterior flatwork such as concrete walkways
In general the proposed structures at the site may be supported on a shallow
foundation system bearing on the compacted fill or on the stiff natural ground An
allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot p s f may be utilized
for the design of shallow foundations bearing on compacted fill material or on the stiff
natural ground This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces Lateral
loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure acting against
the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance developed
between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due to passive
earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per
square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that the soil around footings is well
compacted or undisturbed A coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be used for footings
bearing on the recompacted in situ clay soils or engineered fill materials Unless covered
by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be
neglected
