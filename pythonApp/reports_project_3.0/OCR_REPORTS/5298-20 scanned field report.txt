As requested a site visit was made today to observe the top of the subgrade at the
bottom of the footings along Column Lines A and 12 The column and wall footings were
excavated to the required depth Materials encountered were dense coral with pockets of
clayey silt Loose coral materials were removed and soft silt pockets were replaced with
base course materials The top of the subgrade was compacted using a plate compactor
until it was observed to be dense and unyielding
The consistency of the top of subgrade below the bottom of the footing was
observed using a 3 8 inch diameter probe rod The rod was inserted by pushing and
observing the depth of penetration at several locations along the column wall footing
Based on the probing performed we observed that the top of the subgrade below the
bottom of footing is dense and unyielding and it would be ready for placement of
concrete Rebars for the column and wall footings have been installed at the time of our
observation
