Off Road Movement of Drill Rigs Before moving the rig the operator must do the

To the extent practical walk the planned route and inspect it for depressions gullies
ruts and other obstacles
Ensure that reverse signal alarms are functioning

Engage the front axle on vehicles before travelling rough or steep
terrain
Driving drill rigs along the sides of hills should be avoided however if side hill travel
becomes necessary the operator must conservatively evaluate the ability of the rig to
remain upright while on the hill Rigs will be equipped with seatbelts The possibility
that the presence of drilling tools on the rig may reduce the ability of the rigs to remain
upright by raising the center of gravity of the rig must be considered
Logs ditches road curbs and other long horizontal obstacles should normally be
approached and driven over squarely not at an angle
When close lateral or overhead clearance is encountered the driver of the rig should
be guided by another person on the ground
Loads on the drill rig and truck must be tied down while the truck is moving and the
mast must be fully in the lowered position

PERSONAL PROTECTION
Federal safety regulations call for four 4 different levels of personal protection
depending on the site conditions and relative risk or hazard to workers These levels
are
1 Level D This is the lowest level of risk The personal protective
equipment required is a work uniform with safety equipment such as
hardhats and safety goggles as necessary
A Level C This is the next level of risk where workers are potentially
exposed to toxic hazardous substances at levels which may exceed safe
occupational doses The personal protective equipment includes
protective clothing such as Tyvek coveralls and or splash suits and
respiratory protection
3 Levels B and A These higher levels of protection and are not to be
practiced by employees unless specifically instructed and prepared for
these situations In general these levels require full protective clothing
and supplied breathing air
Daily operations will be conducted at Level D i e work clothes sturdy work
shoes and hardhats It is strictly prohibited to wear loose ragged clothing and athletic
shoes for field work
Employees will wear proper eye protection when engaged in activities where eye
hazards are presented including but not limited to chipping grinding welding chainsaw
operation mixing of chemicals including cement and drilling foam grouting and using
pressure washers steam cleaners
Employees will wear proper hearing protection in environments where noise
hazards are present including but not limited to pile driving operation of pneumatic
equipment and during transportation via military aircraft In general hearing protection
should be worn any time the noise levels are louder than normal conversation

Hazardous Waste Drilling
Some of our drilling projects will include work at sites which are potentially
contaminated with low levels of hazardous or toxic materials Generally these
substances are gasoline diesel fuel or similar hydrocarbon products For work
at such sites there are specific additional procedures to follow
Modified Level D At site where there is only a slight potential of encountering
hazards or toxics work shall be conducted in Modified Level D protection This
shall consist of work clothes worn under a Tyvek suit Hand skin protection
shall consist of inner surgical gloves and outer nitrile or butyl gloves Feet are
to be protected from potential contamination by wearing rubber boots or disposal
booties Respirators shall be at the site and ready for use
Dress out procedures are as follows
1 Put on the Tyvek over work clothes
2 Put on rubber boots and securely tape the boots to the Tyvek suit
3 Put on the inner gloves so that the opening is inside the sleeve of
the Tyvek and tape the seam
4 Put on the outer gloves so that the opening is on the outside of the
sleeve and tape
Level C At any time the concentration of fumes vapors dusts or mists in the
breathing zone of the work area exceeds the time weighted average
employees shall use proper respiratory protection This shall be considered an
upgrade to Level C protection
The level of protection may also be upgraded as deemed necessary by the SSO
Work Zones
Work zones including designation of a hot zone contamination reduction zone
and aclean zone shall be established for any field activity which require Modified
Level D and or Level C protection All work zones will be marked clearly Work
zones may vary depending on the proposed field activity Work zones will be
established in the field activity specific health and safety plan

Air Monitoring
Owner Client s field personnel will perform air monitoring and record readings
with a direct reading organic vapor analyzer OVA or HNU in the breathing zone
periodically at each work location Readings should be recorded in the field All
direct reading instruments should be calibrated daily with commercial standards
lf the reading consistently exceeds 5 ppm above background then work will
cease and personnel will withdraw from the site If concentrations persist above
5 ppm then respirator use will be required and work is to be continued If OVA
readings exceed 50 ppm in the breathing zone while in level C protection then
work will cease and the source of the emissions will be identified and controlled
before work continues
Decontamination
For activities using Modified Level D protection it is unlikely that major
decontamination will be necessary Each time an employee leaves the work site
and at lunch break the following procedure shall be used
1 Remove and clean rubber boots Disposable booties shall be disposed
of in an open head 55 gallon drum
2 Remove and dispose of outer gloves
3 Remove and dispose of Tyvek suits
4 Remove and dispose of inner gloves
5 Thoroughly wash hands and face
Any time the outer gloves become torn they should be removed and replaced
In addition work boots will be removed and cleaned if necessary prior to
leaving the site

Potential Physical Hazards
Field personnel should be cognizant or potential physical hazards associated
with use of heavy equipment and electrical equipment during field operations
Field personnel will wear appropriate foot head eye and hearing protection to
minimize physical hazards Field personnel should keep clear of heavy
equipment which may be operating on site
Boring and monitoring well locations should be cleared toned for underground
utilities prior to the start of drilling work
Physical exertion during hot weather conditions may result in heat exhaustion
and sunstroke Field personnel should drink fluids often
Potential Chemical Hazards
Field personnel working on Hazardous Waste Drilling could potentially be
exposed to metals volatile organic compounds VOCs and petroleum
hydrocarbons at any given site by direct contact with soil and groundwater
through inhalation of dust containing metals or through inhalation of petroleum
hydrocarbons and VOCs Field personnel will minimize potential chemical
hazard by 1 avoiding direct contact with soil and groundwater 2 performing air
monitoring to determine if vapors are present and 3 avoiding generation of
dusts

aid and CPR training Personnel working at Modified Level D and Level C will
have completed a 40 hour health and safety training course and participate in
a medical surveillance program A safety meeting will be held by the designated
SSO at the site before the initiation of each field activity
