SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is generally underlain by a calcareous
soil formation several feet below the upper surface fill
material consisting of coral fragments coral sand silt
and some seashell fragments This type of calcareous for
mation is common throughout the relatively lower level areas
of Honolulu and constitutes a major part of the cap rock for
the island s groundwater system
These calcareous formations were developed during a higher
stand of the sea which drowned the lower coastal land areas
and created upon them these shallow water coral reefs and
sand silt deposits The subsequent lowering of sea level
then exposed these shallow water reefs and the coastal land
areas became comprised of emerged coral reef material
Swampy surface conditions can be associated with these
emerged coastal lands when there is drainage from higher
elevations running into and collecting upon them because
of its flatness and closeness to base level The swampy
surface material would generally consist of organic silts
and clays and peat deposits overlying the calcareous emerged

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the result of our field investigation the proposed
parking and apartment structures may be supported on spread
footing foundation bearing upon the underlying coral and
coral sand formation The dense coral layer is encountered
near water level at Boring No 1 location Boring Nos 2
and 3 located in the center and makai portion of the structure

4 feet below the water level Dewatering could be required
for the foundation excavation and footing construction
Since there is no basement level parking in this project
and due to the high water level above the foundation sup
porting coral formation pile foundation could be used as
an alternate foundation system especially if the added cost
of dewatering makes the spread footing system non competitive
Spread Footing Foundation
The proposed parking and apartment structures should be
supported on spread or continuous footings placed on a
minimum of 1 foot in the coral material
Footings may be designed to impose a dead plus real live
load pressure of 5 000 pounds per square foot A one third
increase may be used when considering wind or seismic loads
The maximum ultimate settlement of the proposed building
supported on spread footings as recommended will be on the
order of three quarter of an inch Differential settlements
Will be less than one half of an inch
Parking ramp slab to the upper parking level should be
structurally supported to minimize differential settlement
Due to the high variability of coral formations it is our
opinion that all footing excavations be inspected by the

Footing excavation may be costly due to the requirement
of dewatering and trench shoring As an alternate pile
foundation can be used
It is estimated that a 16 inch diameter octagonal concrete
pile extending into the dense to hard coral stratum can
develop an allowable hearing capacity of 100 tons per pile
For a 12 inch square pile 70 ton bearing capacity may be
used This pile capacity would be developed mainly from
end bearing within the coral formation
Based on the present subsurface information the pile lengths
may vary between 10 to 25 feet Predrilling will be required
in order to develop a 10 foot minimum length at Boring Nos
and 2 areas The actual driving characteristics should be
evaluated by a series of 10 pile driving resistance tests
and a pile load test
For estimate purpose the piles should be driven to a final
driving resistance of not more than 25 blows per inch for
the last 2 inches with a hammer delivering a rated energy
of 24 000 foot pounds
Most of the end bearing piles for this project will be of
short lengths In general the short and end bearing piles
penetrated through the soft organic clay layer are not
designed to resist lateral loads But in this case the
required 10 foot minimum length will have the pile tip at
least 3 feet into the coral layer A lateral capacity

Site Preparation
After all debris structures and utility lines have been
removed from the site the ground surface should be stripped
and cleared of all deleterious material The upper 12 inches
of existing material at subgrade elevation should be compacted
to a minimum of 90 of maximum density within the slab and
pavement areas
All voids created by the removal of structures and utility
lines should be scarified brought to optimum moisture and
compacted to at least 90 of maximum density Fill shall
then be placed in 9 inch horizontal lifts and also compacted
to the above specified density
Subgrade soil should be inspected and its density tested
by the soils engineer prior to placing base course materials
Old cesspools are known to exist in this area and if any is
located during the site grading and clearing period the
sludge in the cesspools should be completely removed Where
the old cesspool is located below the footing area the side
walls should be removed to a depth of three feet below the
footing bottom The cesspool should then be filled with a
compacted granular material Beneath slab or pavement areas
