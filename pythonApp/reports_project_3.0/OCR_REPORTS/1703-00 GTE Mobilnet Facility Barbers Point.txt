In accordance with your request we have performed a soil and
foundation investigation at the above referenced site Our work was done
in general accordance with our proposal of May 20th
The purpose of the investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil data for use in the foundation
design of the proposed antenna and building
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusion and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The GTE Mobilnet network of the island of Oahu includes several cell
sites which together will create a mobile communication network
The Barbers Point Facility includes a new 12 by 20 feet by 10 feet
high prefabricated building placed on a permanent foundation and an
_80 foot high guyed tower
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the end of Olai Street in the Barbers
Point area of Oahu The site is in a Coast Guard Reservation and is
presently occupied by a small building and an 80 foot steel tower
formerly used by the Hawaiian Telephone Company for ship to shore
communications
The topography at the site is flat and the ground elevations are
relatively low
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A total of four 4 borings were drilled at the site at the
approximate locations indicated on the Site Plan Plate 2 The three 3
borings drilled at the anchor locations extended to about 16 feet below
the existing ground surface while the boring at the tower was drilled to
a depth of 51 5 feet

In general the borings encountered a surface layer approximately 5

Groundwater was encountered in the borings at depths of about 5 to 6

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed competent soil conditions for
support of the proposed structures
Building Foundation
Shallow spread footing foundation may be used for the building
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on the stiff
in situ soil Minimal settlements are anticipated for the structure
loads
Design lateral earth pressures and unit weights are as follows
ae Active soil pressure 35 p c f
b Passive soil pressure 330 p c f
c Soil moist unit weight 110 p c f
Tower Foundation
For the tower foundation an allowable bearing capacity up to 3000
used The bottom of the footing should be embedded at least three 3
feet below the lowest adjacent grade

Settlement of the base pier footing under its dead weight and the
anticipated light loading is estimated to be minimal and probably less
than 1 4 inch
Guy_Anchor
The guy anchors will be used to resist latteral and uplift forces
To resist the up lift forces the dead weight of the anchor as well
as the weight of the soil above the anchor and frictional resistance from
the soil may be considered A total soil unit weight of 110 pounds per
cubic foot can be assumed For soil below the water table a submerged
unit weight of 47 pounds per cubic foot should be used
A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used between the soil and
concrete
To determine the allowable up lift capacity a factor of safety of
at least two 2 should be used
Lateral loads acting on the guy anchor may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the anchor
and by frictional resistance developed between the bottom of the
foundations and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 330 p s f This assumes that the soil
around the anchor is well compacted The base frictional resistance may
be calculated by using a coefficient of friction of 0 35 The above
resistance values are maximum values and do not contain a fractor of
safety

Excavation and Backfilling
Excavation and backfilling would be required for the construction of
the tower base and guy anchors The bottom of the excavation should be
thoroughly tamped prior to the placement of the tower base and the guy
anchors For excavation below the water table dewatering may be
required
Excavated on site granular sand material free of any organic and
other expansive component may be used as a source of fill material All
imported fill material should consist of selected non expansive silty
sandy or granular materials less than 3 inches in size with less than 20
passing the 200 sieve All backfill materials should be placed in
maximum of 8 inch horizontal loose lifts and compacted to 95 of the
maximum density as established by the ASTM D 1557 78 test method
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundation support of the antenna
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included
Construction Monitoring
We recommend that the excavations for the tower foundation and the
guy anchors be inspected by the soils engineer prior to placement of
concrete or reinforcing steel to check that adequate bearing has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our subsurface investigation
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration and Laboratory Testing
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling four
4 borings to depths of 15 to 51 5 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted rotary drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soil and rock encountered in the borings were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our geologist who
continuously monitored the drilling operations The classifications were
further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The
soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
sample depths
Moisture content determinations were performed on selected samples
as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The
results of these tests are presented on the Log of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
