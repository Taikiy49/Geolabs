This report contains our recommendations based on a foundation
investigation for the proposed tower on Mt Maunakapu Point
Oahu Hawaii
The scope of our work at the site was to investigate the sub
surface soil and rock conditions by drilling borings to evaluate
foundation requirements for the proposed tower structure
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
It is presently planned to construct a tower of approximately
60 feet in height The tower will be supported on four legs
and will not be guyed The base of the tower will be approxi
mately 8 feet square and will be located approximately 6 feet
off from the edge of the existing dirt road

The tower is designed that all lateral loads will be transferred
axially no lateral loads will be transferred to the base
Anticipated axial downward loads on the order of 45 kips per
foot are anticipated for the tower Three foot square pier
caps will be constructed at each leg
FIELD INVESTIGATION
To explore the subsurface conditions at the site 3 test borings
were drilled with truck mounted rotary auger equipment The
locations of the borings with respect to the proposed tower
base footings and existing road are shown on the Site Plan
and Boring Location Plate l
An engineer from our office was present throughout the field
investigation to supervise drilling operations obtain samples
of the sub soils and to observe general site conditions The
Logs of Borings are presented on Plates 2A through 2C
Soil samples were obtained by driving a sampler with 140 pound
hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The blows required
to drive the sampler the last 12 inches were recorded The
blow counts are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
depths All soils were classified in accordance with the
Unified Soil Classification System and rock with local geologic

The cable tower site is located on the side shoulder of a
dirt road in the Waianae Mountain range near Maunakapu Point
The site is fairly level and is approximately 16 feet wide
20 feet long and drops off very steeply The site is
covered with a moss like vegetation cover Five tree stumps
are located on the site
The site is immediately underlain from about 6 inches to 4h
feet with a dark brown clayey silt with basalt fragments The
root structure of trees appears to extend down about 5 to 6
feet The root system of the tree most likely follows the
joints and fractures of the decomposed rock Beneath the
upper clayey silt and to the depths explored a decomposed
basalt was encountered which consisted of unweathered basalt
rock fragments with brown clayey silt This decomposed
material is residual with the silt materials formed in the
joints and fractures inherited from the parentyrock No
groundwater was encountered in any of our borings

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The site is suitable for the proposed tower supported on
drilled cast in place piers
Foundations
It is recommended that each tower leg derive its support
from a drilled pier The pier should be bottomed in the
decomposed basalt located below the upper clayey silt

Structure and Foundation Conditions
The existing tower structure is square in shape with each of
the four legs 8 feet apart The tower is approximately 60
feet high and situated about 2 feet away from a 1
H V natural slope
Presently there are several antenna dishes on the tower
Additional dishes are planned
It is our understanding that the existing tower foundations
consist of 9 foot deep individual concrete piers 2 feet in
diameter with the bottom belled to 3 feet diameter The
construction of these piers were not monitored by our firm

1974

sented in the report
