INTRODUCTION
This report contains the results of a foundation investigation
for the proposed bank office building un Hickam Air Force Base
Hawaii
The purposes of our work were to investigate the subsurface
soil and rock conditions to evaluate foundation depths
bearing pressures and subgrade preparations for slabs on grade
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed bank building will be a one story block wall
building with concrete slabs on grade and a mezzanine floor

The plan dimensions will be approximately 112 by 60 feet
Maximum dead plus live column loads for the structure will
be on the order of 100 kips Wall loads will be about 3
kips per lineal foot The existing topography indicates that
minimal site grading will be required to prepare the site
for construction of the bank building
FIELD INVESTIGATION
To explore the subsurface conditions at the Site 5 test
borings were drilled utilizing truck mounted rotary auger and
rock coring equipment The locations of the borings are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 1 An engineer from our office contin
uously supervised the drilling operations maintained a boring
log obtained undisturbed samples of the subsoils and rocks
and made detailed observations of site conditions The Log
of Borings are presented on Plates 2A through 2E All materials
have been described in accordance with the Unified Soil Clas
sification System and local geologic terminology
SITE AND SOIL CONDITIONS
The proposed bank site is located in a parking lot north of
the intersection of Hanger and H Streets in Hickam Air
Force Base The proposed structure will be located Diamond
Head end of the future Credit Union Building
First Hawaiian Bank 1973
The existing ground surface is level and paved with 2
A C and approximately 6 of coral base course Below
this is a light brown clay layer varying between 1 5 to
3 feet thick which is underlain by a dense volcanic tuff or
mudrock formation The tuff formation was found to the
depths explored Based on our experience in the local area
the tuff will extend for considerable depths Graphical
representations of the soils and mudrock encountered appear
on the Log of Borings Plates 2A through 2E
All samples were classified in accordance with the Unified
Soil Classification System The description of rock is
based on local geologic terminology
LABORATORY TESTING
In place densities and moisture determinations were made on
selected samples and the results are presented on the Log
of Borings at the appropriate location An Atterberg limit
test was performed on a sample of the silty clay and is
presented in the Log of Borings A consolidation test was
performed to check the compressibility and expansiveness of
the silty clay The results of this test are presented on
Plate 3

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The site is suitable for the proposed bank office building
supported on conventional shallow footings Slab on grade
may be used
Foundations
It is our recommendation that the building be supported on
foundations bearing directly on the cemented volcanic tuff
mudrock located 2 5 to 3 feet below the existing ground
surface A bearing value of 7 000 pounds per square foot
may be used for dead plus live loads Footings should be
placed a minimum of two feet below final adjacent grade
Continuous footings should have a minimum width of 12 inches
Bearing values may be increased by one third when considering
wind or seismic loads
The footing excavations should be inspected by a qualified
soils engineer to verify the design bearing values
We anticipate that total and differential settlements from
the imposed loads will be negligible
Lateral Loads
Lateral loads may be resisted by passive pressures or friction
First Hawaiian Bank 1973
between footings and the site sails Passive resistance
may be assumed to be equal to the pressure developed by
a fluid having a weight of 350 pounds per cubic foot A
coefficient of friction of 0 45 may be assumed
Slabs on Grade
It is our belief that the upper clayey soils on the site
will tend to undergo slight volume change with the addition
of water We therefore recommend that a minimum of four
inches of granular material be used beneath slabs This
granular layer will act as a capillary break beneath the
slab In areas where moisture sensitive flooring is to be
used we recommend that a moisture barrier consisting of
plastic membrane be placed above the granular layer All
slabs on grade should be structurally separated from
column and wall footings
