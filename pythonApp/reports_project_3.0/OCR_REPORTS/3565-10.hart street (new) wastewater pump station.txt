INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration performed
for the proposed Hart Street New Wastewater Pump Station WWPS project in Honolulu
on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
A preliminary geotechnical engineering evaluation for the project was performed by
our Office and the findings and preliminary recommendations are summarized in the report
entitled Preliminary Geotechnical Engineering Evaluation Hart Street New Wastewater
Pump Station Honolulu Oahu Hawaii dated August 6 1996 This report summarizes our
findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations resulting from our current field
exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses The recommendations presented
herein are intended for the design of foundations retaining structures pavements shoring
and dewatering and site preparation only The findings and recommendations presented
herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The existing Hart Street WWPS is located off Nimitz Highway in the Kapalama area
of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The existing Hart Street WWPS serves the
central Honolulu area which extends from Salt Lake to Downtown Honolulu The WWPS
pumps wastewater generated in the Hart Street subdistrict to the Sand Island Wastewater
Treatment Plant WWTP through a force main which crosses under Honolulu Harbor
The existing Hart Street WWPS was built in 1950 and has been in continued service
since its construction Due to age probable corrosion and out dated equipment and design
we understand that the facility has exhibited building and equipment problems Therefore
it is desired to rehabilitate the existing WWPS with the following new construction
e A one story electrical building approximately 24 feet by 54 feet in plan
dimensions located immediately to the east of the existing Hart Street WWPS
e A replacement office about 8 feet by 10 feet toilet and shower about 8 feet
by 10 feet and a meeting break room about 8 feet by 8 feet located to the
east of the existing Hart Street WWPS
e A one story diesel generator building approximately 22 feet by 35 feet in plan
dimensions located on the south side of the existing Hart Street WWPS
e A 15 000 gallon underground fuel storage tank
A diversion pumping station and large diameter underground sewer lines
e New asphaltic concrete pavements
The rehabilitation project will also involve installing new electrical switch gear and
motor controls installing new motors and drives and dividing the wet well into
two compartments in order for the operations to continue during maintenance activities
Due to the presence of soft loose soil deposits anticipated at shallow depths within the
pump station premises shoring of the excavations would be of critical consideration during
the project construction Because of the relatively shallow groundwater table anticipated at
the site dewatering would be required during construction of the below grade structures

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of the
western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of the eastern
two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while Koolau was still
active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lava in Central Oahu When the
building of the Waianae and Koolau Shield Volcanoes came to an end only a slight
embayment existed on the southern coast of Oahu
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available
to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea When
the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea
levels rose to above the present sea level
The processes of erosion and deposition caused the top of the basaltic lava flows to
be highly weathered and mantled by residual soils During the Pleistocene Epoch the Kaena
stand of the sea at approximately 90 feet Mean Sea Level a delta of silt sand and gravel

reclamation projects within the last century have brought the project site to its present form
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located within the existing Hart Street WWPS in the Kapalama
area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The Hart Street WWPS site is an
odd shaped parcel encompassing an area of approximately 0 57 acres It is bounded
approximately by the future Domestic Commercial Fishing Village parking lot to the north
and west Nimitz Highway to the east and existing asphaltic concrete pavements to the
south as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The existing Hart Street WWPS is generally occupied by a main pump station
building in the center and by two smaller buildings toward the northwest corner Other
minor structures vaults and piping were observed across the project site at the time of our
field exploration The remaining areas are either landscaped with a thin grass cover or
paved with asphaltic concrete for vehicular traffic and parking Based on our field
observations and the grading plan provided the topography of the project site is relatively
level with existing ground elevations of approximately 6 feet Mean Sea Level MSL

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field explorations disclosed that the project site is generally underlain by a surface
fill layer extending to a depth of about 5 feet below the existing ground surface Below the
near surface fill layer loose to very loose lagoonal deposits comprised of coralline detritus
interbedded with occasional layers of very soft clayey silts were encountered to a depth of
about 25 feet below the existing ground surface The lagoonal deposits were underlain by
stiff alluvial soil deposits overlying medium dense coralline detritus extending to the
maximum depth explored of approximately 51 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered we believe that shallow foundations
consisting of spread and or continuous footings bearing on the re compacted near surface
fill soils may be used to support the lightly loaded diesel generator building provided that
some minor settlements are tolerable Based on the subsurface conditions and structural
loads anticipated for the proposed diesel generator building footing settlements on the order
of about 1 to 1 inches may be anticipated for the foundations of the lightly loaded structure
GEOLABS INC
W O 3565 10 Page 8
We recommend that the foundations be structurally connected with tie beams to reduce the
effects of potential total and differential settlements In addition the footings should be
placed at a level as high as possible to take advantage of the fill crust thereby reducing the
footing load applied onto the underlying compressible soil deposits Efforts should be made
to reduce the building loads in order to utilize a shallow foundation system for support
otherwise a driven pile foundation should be considered if the anticipated settlements are
of concern
In order to reduce the potential for differential settlements between the existing pump
station and the new electrical building we recommend that driven pile foundations consisting
of 12 inch square precast prestressed concrete piles be used for support of the proposed
electrical building In addition we believe that the use of driven pile foundations would also
avoid surcharging the existing basement walls with additional structural loads In general
we believe that an allowable compressive load capacity on the order of up to 30 tons per pile
with a preliminary pile length of about 50 feet below the existing ground surface may be used
for the design of the pile foundations
Due to the potential for total and differential settlements as a result of the soft soil
deposits and potential future dewatering operations we recommend that the diversion
pumping station phoenix building junction box large diameter force main and outflow lines
and above ground tank pads be supported on driven pile foundations In order to facilitate
the construction of the pile supported concrete cradle over the soft loose soil deposits we
believe that a 12 inch thick layer of No 2 Rock ASTM C 33 No 4 gradation wrapped in a
layer of non woven filter fabric should be placed on top of the soft loose soil deposits for
more uniform support
For the underground fuel storage tank we recommend that a concrete mat be used
to provide the necessary bearing support and uplift resistance for the tank structure

Shallow Foundations
As previously mentioned we believe that shallow foundations consisting of spread
and or continuous footings bearing on the re compacted near surface fills anticipated at
shallow depths may be used to support the proposed diesel generator building provided that
some ground settlements are tolerable Based on the subsurface conditions and structural
loads anticipated for the proposed building settlements on the order of about 1 to 1 inches
may be anticipated for the shallow foundations with differential settlements of approximately
one half the amount Efforts should be made to keep the building loads light in order to
utilize a shallow foundation system otherwise driven pile foundations should be considered
if these anticipated settlements are of concern
An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 000 pounds per square foot psf may be
used for design of footings bearing on the re compacted fills anticipated at shallow depths
This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for transient
loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces We recommend that the footings be
structurally connected with tie beams to improve the rigidity of the foundation thus reducing
the effects of the anticipated differential settlements
GEOLABS INC
W O 3565 10 Page 10
In general the footings should be placed at a level as high as possible to take
advantage of the fill crust thereby reducing the footing load applied onto the underlying
compressible soil deposits To utilize the near surface fill crust we recommend that the
bottom of footings be embedded a minimum depth of 12 inches and a maximum depth of
about 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade If foundations are located next
to utility trenches or easements they should be embedded below a 45 degree imaginary
plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts
of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for appreciable
foundation settlement
The foundation subgrade soils should be re compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction prior to placement of reinforcing steel and concrete Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing
excavations the materials should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials
The over excavation should be backfilled with select granular fill materials compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for footings
bearing on the re compacted near surface fills as recommended herein to be on the order
of about 1 to 1 inches with differential settlements of about one half 2 the amount
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance

Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid

Pile Foundations
We understand that the proposed electrical building will be located immediately
adjacent to the existing pump station with basement walls Due to the location of the new
electrical building a shallow building foundation will impose a surcharge load on the adjacent
basement walls which may be structurally inadequate to support the additional surcharge
loads from the new foundations In addition there exists the potential for differential
settlements between the existing pump station and the new electrical building resulting from
the consolidation of the underlying compressible soil deposits at shallow depths Therefore
we recommend that driven pile foundations be used for support of the proposed new
electrical building
Based on our field explorations and engineering analyses we recommend that
12 inch square precast prestressed concrete piles be used for the pile foundation design
The piles would derive support from both friction and end bearing in the stiff alluvial soils and
medium dense coralline detritus anticipated at depths below about 25 feet from the existing
ground surface
Based on our analyses we believe that an allowable compressive load capacity of up
to 30 tons per pile may be used for the preliminary design of the pile foundations The
allowable compressive load capacity of the piles is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third for transient loads such as wind or seismic forces In general piles
should be spaced a minimum of 3 feet center to center to avoid reduction in vertical capacity
due to group action

Predrilling
The upper 5 feet below the existing ground surface should be predrilled to facilitate
the pile driving operations through the fill crust This also serves to reduce the
potential for distress to the existing pump station resulting from vibrations generated
during the pile driving operations In addition we recommend that the driven pile
foundations for the new electrical building be setback a minimum distance of 5 feet
from the existing pump station
The predrilled holes should have a diameter of about 16 inches for the 12 inch square
piles The annular space between the piles and predrilled holes should be backfilled
with pea gravel S4C sand or grouted with tremie concrete to fill in the voids The
required predrilling size and depth should be verified and or modified by Geolabs
during the test pile driving operation depending on the conditions encountered and
pile driving equipment used

Uplift Load Resistance
Uplift loads imposed on the pile foundation may be resisted by a combination of the
dead weight of the driven pile and by shear resistance acting along the pile surface
and the adjacent soils Based on our estimate an uplift load capacity of 30 kips per
pile may be used in the design to help resist the buoyant uplift forces on the
structures The uplift load capacity already includes the weight of the pile The
recommended uplift load capacity also assumes that the pile will be cast in one piece
and will be installed without the use of a splice can The tensile capacity of the pile
member should be checked by the project structural engineer
Pile Foundation Settlements
Settlement of pile foundations will result primarily from elastic compression of the pile
member and the subgrade response We estimate that the total settlement of the
pile supported foundation to be less than 0 5 inches Differential settlements between
adjacent piles should not exceed about one half of the total foundation settlement
We believe that these settlements are essentially elastic and should occur as the
loads are applied
Pile Hammer Selection
The piles should be driven with a hammer capable of delivering a rated energy of
about 30 000 foot pounds of energy per blow The hammer should be equipped with
an energy control level such that driving within the soft loose soils may be controlled
at a lower hammer energy to reduce the potential for tensile stress development
within the piles which could damage or cause cracking of the piles
Determination of driving equipment to be used for this project should take into
consideration the matching of the pile hammer with the weight of the piles to be
driven This will result in the pile hammer efficiently delivering the required energy to
the top of the pile and also reducing potential damage to the piles Geolabs should
review the pile driving equipment proposed to evaluate whether the equipment is
capable of installing the piles to the recommended capacity

review prior to construction Geolabs should review the submittal and assess the
driveability of the piles with the use of the selected hammer A wave equation
analysis should be performed after selection of the pile driving equipment to assess
the driveability of the piles and to establish the preliminary pile driving criteria for the
test pile program
Pile Driving Criteria
For preliminary design of the 12 inch square prestressed precast concrete piles pile
driving may be terminated when aggregate blow counts are at least 40 blows per foot
for the last foot or 4 blows per inch for the last 6 inches The preliminary driving
criteria provided should be confirmed and or modified after selection of the pile driving
equipment and performance of a wave equation analysis
Each pile should be driven continuously without interruption A pile may be rejected
or its design capacity may be reduced when the driving resistance is interrupted for
more than 4 hours and the pile cannot be driven to the required depth During soft
driving such as when the pile tip is within soft loose soils the hammer stroke or
energy should be carefully controlled such that significant tensile stresses do not
develop within the pile
Because variations in soil strength characteristics are likely to exist in the bearing
soils the supporting capacity of each pile must be confirmed in the field by a
representative from our office during driving It should be noted that the pile design
was developed from our pile analysis using the field exploration data Therefore a
test pile program should be carried out to confirm our preliminary pile design and to
establish the pile driving criteria The production pile driving criteria may be revised
based on the test pile program

Test Pile Program
We recommend that a test pile program which includes at least one load test be
undertaken to fulfill the following objectives
1 To examine the driveability of the piles with the use of the selected
hammer
2 To estimate the production pile lengths
3 To assess the test pile driving criteria which may be modified for use
as the production pile driving criteria
To achieve these objectives we recommend that the test pile program consist of
approximately 10 percent of the total number of piles for the project The test piles
may be incorporated into the actual foundation system upon evaluation of the load
test results When the final foundation plan is available Geolabs should be consulted
for selection of the test pile locations Test piles should be cast at least 10 feet longer
than the estimated pile lengths to account for potential variations In addition the test
pile program should provide flexibility to allow for addition or relocation of the test
piles as may be deemed necessary by our office to suit the conditions encountered
during the test pile driving Re striking of all the test piles may be required as part of
the test pile program The pile load test should be performed in general accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1143 loading the test pile to 200 percent of the design
load
We recommend that a representative from Geolabs be present during pile driving
operations to observe the actual driving behavior and to further evaluate the field
performance It should be noted that the pile design and driving criteria
recommended were developed from our pile analysis using the field exploration data
Therefore observation of the pile driving operations by Geolabs is necessary to
confirm our design assumptions and should be designated a Special Inspection
item

Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be required for the various buildings
and equipment pads planned for the project Based on the existing topography and the
anticipated design finished floor elevations for the new improvements we believe that the
slabs on grade for the proposed structures will be supported on the re compacted near

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were previously explored by drilling and
sampling three borings Boring Nos 1 through 3 extending to depths of about 26 5 feet
below the existing ground surface Two deeper borings designated as Boring Nos 4 and
5 were drilled to depths of about 41 5 and 51 5 feet below the existing ground surface during
our current field exploration The approximate locations of the borings drilled are shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with
hollow stem augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who observed the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual observation and
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 5
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch
OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition
some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as
the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples in the
field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined compressive
strength of the sample Results of the pocket penetrometer tests are summarized on the
Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
