SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Alapa Street Subdivision project located in Lanai City on
the Island of Lanai Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our ffindings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and
engineering analyses These recommendations are intended to assist in the design of
the mass grading construction structure foundations road pavements utilities
excavation and fill placement only The findings and recommendations presented
herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project involves the development of three lots on Alapa Street on the Island
of Lanai Hawaii Details for the proposed site development and grading were not
available at this time We anticipate that the houses will be one story wood structures
supported on individual post footings
The project will involve minimal site grading and infrastructure installation
including driveways and utilities
1 3 Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the surface and
subsurface conditions to develop a soil rock data set to formulate geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertaining to the proposed residential development Our
work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
The scope of our work included the following tasks and work
efforts

SECTION 2 0 SITE CHARACTERIZATION
2 1 Site Description
The project site is located on the north side of Alapa Street in Lanai City on the
Island of Lanai Hawaii Alapa Street is bounded by 5 Street to the west and 6 Street
to the east The approximate area of the three proposed single family lots are 9 788
square feet 9 788 square feet and 11 533 square feet from north to south respectively
At present the project site is an undeveloped hilly area with large Norfolk pine trees and
tall patches of grass and shrubs
The project site slopes steeply down towards the southwest to Alapa Street
Based on the topographic plan provided the existing ground surface elevation at the
site ranges from approximately 1 660 to 1 710 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
2 2 Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling three borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 using a truck mounted drill rig The borings
were drilled in a clearing covered by an erosion control net and mulch at a ground
surface elevation of approximately 1 685 feet MSL The approximate boring locations
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration generally encountered stiff to hard residual soil underlain by
weathered basalt rock to the maximum depth explored of about 15 feet below the
existing ground surface The residual soil generally consisted of clayey silt with
increasing basalt material at a deeper depth The highly weathered basalt was
encountered at about 5 feet below the existing ground surface at Boring No 1 about
10 feet at Boring No 2 and about 15 feet at Boring No 3
Hawaii California
SECTION 2 SITE CHARACTERIZATION
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field
exploration However it should be noted that groundwater levels may fluctuate
depending on seasonal precipitation and other factors Detailed descriptions of the
materials encountered in the borings are presented in Appendix A Results of the
laboratory tests performed on selected soil samples are presented in Appendix B

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our laboratory testing on the on site soils indicated low expansion potential when
subjected to changes in the moisture content Based on the results of our exploration
we believe that the proposed residential structures may be supported on shallow
foundations The shallow foundations should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches
below the finish grade and bear on properly compacted fill or stiff in situ material
To reduce the potential for structural distress to the future slabs on grade
resulting from the potential swelling of the clayey soils we recommend supporting
concrete slabs on grade on at least 12 inches of structural fill materials Special
attention should be given to the subgrade preparation requirements presented in the
Site Preparation section of this report to reduce the potential for shrink swell effects on
concrete slabs
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report
3 1 Site Grading
We anticipate that the mass grading work will generally consist of cuts and fills
on the order of about 5 feet or less in the majority of the site Items of grading that are
addressed in the following subsections include 1 Site Preparation 2 Fills and
Backfills 3 Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements 4 Excavation and 5 Cut
and Fill Slopes
A Geolabs representative should observe the site grading operations to evaluate
whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation process and to
confirm whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those encountered in our
field exploration
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious materials and

other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly off site or
stockpiled in a designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the
excavated materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should
be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils may be
used as fill provided that it meets the requirements for fill material
Prior to filling the existing ground should be scarified to a depth of 10 inches and
moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction For pavement
subgrades the minimum relative compaction should be 95 percent Where
shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that
the soil be thoroughly moistened to close cracks or prepared again as
recommended above
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement
weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and
replacement with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils may be
used as fill provided that it meets the requirements for fill material Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557
Fills and Backfills
Generally fill material should consist of materials less than 3 inches in largest
dimension with a CBR swell value less than 1 percent The material should also be
free of vegetation Our laboratory test results indicated a low swell potential for the
on site clayey silt material Therefore most of the on site clayey material may be
suitable for fill material provided it has been cleared and grubbed thoroughly as
stated in the section above
Hawaii California
SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Base course and select borrow subbase material should consist of crushed basalt
aggregate and should conform to the City and County of Honolulu Department of
Public Works Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction dated
September 1986
Structural fill should consist of non expansive granular material such as crushed
coral basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to fine
with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain
between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material
should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum
swell of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883 If
imported fill materials is used it should be tested by Geolabs for conformance with
these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in loose thickness
and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction
Base course and select borrow subbase materials should be moisture conditioned
to above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance
with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers
multiple wheel pneumatic tired rollers or other types of acceptable compaction
equipment
It should be noted that proper moisture conditioning and degree of compaction of
the clayey subgrade soils are critical elements of earthwork Geolabs should
Hawaii California
SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
perform observations and soil density tests during site grading to assist the
contractor in obtaining appropriate compaction results Where compaction is less
than required additional compaction effort should be applied to obtain the specified
compaction along with necessary adjustment of moisture content The requirement
of moisture content for the fills and subgrades about 2 percent above the optimum
moisture is also an important requirement for the use of on site clayey soils in
order to decrease the potential swell due to fluctuation of moisture condition
Excavation
Based on our field exploration we envision utility installation excavation may
involve cuts into the underlying residual soil deposit consisting of clayey silts It is
anticipated the residual soils may be excavated with normal heavy excavation
equipment However isolated remnant boulders may require over excavation for
their removal or breaking to extend the trench
Our discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are based on field
data obtained from the borings Contractors bidding on this project should be
encouraged to examine the site conditions and soil data to make their own
interpretation
Cutand Fill Slopes
Based on the plan provided we envision the project will involve up to 5 feet of cut
and fill along the soil slope Based on the subsurface conditions encountered it
appears that cut slopes would expose soils consisting of stiff to hard clayey silts In
general we believe that cut slopes into the soil material may be cut at an inclination
of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter
Fill slopes should be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter Fills
placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the
existing slope to provide stability of the new fill against sliding The filling operations
should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers
in accordance with the above fill placement recommendations
Hawaii California
SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
We recommend that fill slopes in excess of 5 feet high should be constructed by
overfilling and cutting back to the design slope ratio to obtain a well compacted
slope face Track rolling of slopes in excess of 5 feet high should not be accepted
In the event that over cutting of a slope occurs keying and benching requirements
should be implemented instead of backfilling the slope to the design grade with
sliver fills Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope
planting should be provided as soon as possible to reduce the potential for erosion
of the finished slopes
3 2 House Foundations
Shallow spread and or shallow drilled pier footings may be used for support of
the proposed residential structures provided they are embedded a minimum of
12 inches below the finished grade to reduce the potential effects of the clayey soils
We recommend utilizing an allowable bearing pressure of 3 000 pounds per square
foot psf for the design of residential building foundations bearing on properly compacted
fill or stiff on site clayey silts This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces
The proposed shallow footings should have a minimum width of 12 inches and
should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the existing grade Footings
constructed near tops of slopes or on sloping ground should be embedded deep enough
to provide a minimum horizontal setback distance of 5 feet measured from the outside
edge of the footings to the slope face
We estimate that settlements for footings bearing on properly compacted fill or
on site stiff to hard clayey silts as recommended herein to be less than 1 inch total
Differential settlement is estimated to be less than inch The foundation system shall be
design to resist such total and or differential settlement
Soft and or loose materials encountered at the bottom of footing excavations should
be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation should be
Hawaii California
SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
backfilled with select granular material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction or the footing bottom may be deepened to the underlying firm materials
Foundations located next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded
below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid
surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce
the potential for foundation settlement
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 psf
This assumes the soil around the footings is relatively undisturbed or well compacted
Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 6 inches of soil
should be neglected For footings bearing on properly compacted fill or on site stiff silty
clay a coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used
We recommend that a Geolabs representative observe footing excavations prior to
placement of reinforcement steel and concrete to confirm the foundation bearing
conditions and the required embedment depths
3 3 Slabs On Grade
Based on our field exploration the project site is underlain by near surface
clayey soils with low expansion potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations To
reduce the potential for future distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from
shrinking and swelling of the clayey soils due to changes in the moisture content we
recommend properly preparing and capping the subgrade soils below the single family
houses with at least 12 inches of structural fill material capping fill below the slab
cushion The capping fill should also extend beyond the new slab on grade footprint a
minimum of 12 inches
Hawaii California
SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The non expansive select granular capping fill should be placed as soon as
possible after the subgrade soils are prepared in accordance with the recommendations
presented in the Site Preparation section of this report The completed capping fill and
the underlying subgrade soils should be maintained at above optimum moisture content
prior to placement of concrete Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of
the subgrade we recommend thoroughly moistening the soils to close the cracks
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather
and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted engineered fill
In general we recommend placing a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill
consisting of No 3 Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation between the slab and the
capping fill to serve as a capillary break and to provide more uniform support of the
slab To reduce the potential for moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor
coverings an impervious moisture barrier is recommended on top of the No 3 Fine
gravel
The thickened edges of slab adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slab
should be backfilled with on site clayey soil tightly against the slab and graded to divert
water away from the slabs and to reduce the potential for water ponding around the
slabs and foundations
Retaining Structure Foundations

should have a minimum width of 18 inches In addition wall foundations located
on relatively flat areas should be embedded a minimum depth of 24 inches below
the lowest adjacent finished grade
For sloping ground conditions the footing should extend deeper to obtain a
minimum 6 foot setback distance measured horizontally from the outside edge of

Drainage
In general retaining structures should be well drained to reduce the potential for
build up of hydrostatic pressures A typical drainage system would consist of a
12 inch wide zone of permeable material such as open graded gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation placed immediately around a perforated pipe
perforations down at the base of the retaining wall The perforated pipe should
discharge to an appropriate outlet or weepholes
As an alternative a prefabricated drainage product such as MiraDrain or
EnkaDrain may be used instead of the permeable drainage material The
prefabricated drainage product also should be hydraulically connected to a
perforated pipe at the base of the retaining wall Unless covered by concrete or
asphaltic concrete the upper 12 inches of backfill should consist of relatively
impervious materials such as the on site clayey soils to reduce the potential for
significant water infiltration behind the walls

3 5 Drainage
The finished grades outside the structures should be sloped to shed water away
from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is highly recommended
that gutter systems be installed around the structures to divert discharge away from the
foundation and pavement areas Blockage or clogging of drainage system could cause
ponding of water at the house structure and foundations resulting foundation and house
distress
Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should also be
avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils Gravel strips adjacent to slabs and
foundations should be avoided
Because ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
loss of strength of the near surface soils we believe these drainage requirements are
very important for the proper performance of proposed structures Immediate backfill of
the foundation excavations after setting of concrete is strongly recommended to reduce

be maintained to drain surface run off away from foundations

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by the drilling and sampling
of three borings to depths of approximately 15 feet below the existing ground surface
The borings were drilled with a truck mounted drill rig equipped with auger equipment at
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who observed the field exploration operations
on a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual
observation and testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance
with the Unified Soil Classification System Logs of the materials encountered are
presented on Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM D 3550 and D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving
with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler
the second and third 6 inches of an 18 to 24 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings
at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight determinations ASTM D 2937
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Three one inch Ring Swell tests were performed on remolded samples to evaluate
the swelling potential of the soils under surcharge pressures These tests were run on
air dried samples Swell tests results are presented on Plate B 1
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on selected samples of
the soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Graphic
representations of the test results are provided on Plate B 2
