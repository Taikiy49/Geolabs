SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
At the proposed invert elevation the force main
will be supported upon loose to medium dense coral
debris which was encountered immediately below the
existing fill
Stabilized bedding or piles will be required to
provide adequate support and to minimize the

The excavations will extend below water level and

INTRODUCTION
This investigation was undertaken to define subsurface
conditions along the proposed sewer alignment and to pro
vide recommendations for the design and construction of the
force main including the proposed shoring and trenching
W O May 29 1975
The subsurface investigations for the adjoining Pump Station
Honolulu Harbor Channel Crossing and Sand Island Sections are
covered by separate reports
Proposed Construction
In this section the proposed sewage force main will consist
of a 78 inch diameter reinforced concrete pipe from the new
pump station to Honolulu Harbor entrance The proposed
force main invert will be at approximately elevation 4 feet
to provide a 3 foot minimum cover over the pipe
Site Description
As shown on the enclosed general location map this section
of the proposed force main lies almost entirely within the
Matson Container Yard It extends makai from the proposed
pumping station approximately 1 500 feet along Keawe Street
and the drainage ditch to approximately the water s edge
It then turns toward the west and essentially parallels the
shoreline for a distance of approximately 1 000 feet to the
Honolulu Harbor entrance at the makai end of Pier l
The asphalt covered ground surface is essentially level at
approximately elevation 7 and was created by fill placed
upon a shallow reef The lower portion of the fill is gen
erally large basalt boulders similar to the boulders visible
W O May 29 1975
on the present shoreline The boulders were covered by a
thin blanket of soil which was subsequently paved with
asphalt
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
This container yard section is adjacent to Honolulu Harbor
within the coastal plain This coastal plain consists mainly
of coral reefs marine sediments and alluvial sediments from
the Koolau Volcanic Range
Coral algal reefs apparently developed during the late Pleis
tocene Epoch and grew in the shallow slowly submerging embay
ment Influxes of non marine alluvial deposits clays and
silts apparently periodically smothered the reef development
Fluctuations of the sea level gave the harbor its latest
natural configuration while the recent filling operations
particularly in the vicinity of the container facility has
given Honolulu Harbor its present outline
The force main section appears to have been in an area mauka
of the main coral reef where thick layers of loose reef
debris developed in the protected waters
W O May 29 1975
The reef debris was subsequently blanketed by fill placed
during the recent site development
Subsurface Explorations
Subsurface conditions along the proposed alignment were
explored utilizing five test borings drilled at the locations
shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 1 Test borings from
the Pier 1 Extension and from the subsurface investigation
for Kakaako Area Major Drain along Keawe Street were also
evaluated as part of this investigation
A detailed description of the field exploration methods and
accompanying laboratory testing is given in the appendix
along with the boring logs and laboratory test results
Boring Logs B 12 B 13 and B 14 from the subsurface inves
tigation along Keawe Street are also included for general
reference
Soil Conditions
The subsurface explorations and laboratory testing disclosed
that the site is underlain at the boring locations by three
general soil layers The upper layer varying from 3 to 14
feet in thickness generally consists of 3 feet of silty sand
and gravel fill underlain by dense to very dense basalt
boulders The fill extends to between elevations 3 and 6 8
feet
W O May 29 1975
The fill is underlain by 5 to 24 feet of loose to medium
dense silty sand with shells and coral fragments reef
debris The silty sand extends to between elevation 9 6
and 27 5 where dense coral was encountered
Groundwater was encountered at approximately mean sea level
but changed with tidal fluctuations
The findings of the subsurface explorations are summarized
on page 6 Detailed descriptions of the soils encountered
are given in the appended boring logs along with the labora
tory test results
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The proposed force main invert will be 10 to 11 feet below
the existing ground surface if a minimum three foot soil
cover is provided With normal bedding the trench excava
tion will completely penetrate the surface fill and the pipe
will be supported upon the loose to medium dense reef debris
Special provisions will therefore be required to provide uni
form support and to minimize differential settlements

Pipe Support
The reef debris on which the pipe will be bearing is an
erratic deposit varying in both composition and density
Even though the net loadings resulting from the sewer con
struction will be equal to or slightly above the previous
loadings large settlements can occur in the underlying
loose materials
Deflections due to localized stress concentrations or irreg
ularities in the supporting soils can also increase the
differential settlements
It is therefore recommended that the pipe be supported upon
a crushed rock cradle having a minimum thickness of three
feet This rock cradle will provide more uniform support
equalize differential movements and permit some spanning of
localized soft spots The crushed rock cradle should reduce
the settlements to less than 2 inches total and 1 inch
differential
The cradle should be constructed of compacted crushed rock
meeting the requirements for No 3 Coarse Bedding of Section
15 of the Standard Specifications for Public Works Construc
tion of the City and County of Honolulu
W O May 29 1975
The differential settlements can be eliminated by supporting
the proposed force main upon reinforced concrete piles
extending to the coral layer encountered between elevations
9 6 and 27 5 An estimated 40 ton capacity could be
developed by a 10 inch square pile driven to the coral layer
It is anticipated that the pile support will not be required
and that the stabilized bedding will be sufficient to sup
port the proposed force main
Additional strengthening of the pipe or a reinforced concrete
bedding may be necessary for the transition between the pipe
supported by the crushed rock cradle and any rigid non
yielding structures such as the pump station
Earth Pressures
Loads on buried conduits are influenced by the width of the
excavation the unit weight of the backfill materials and
the trench walls
To minimize the loads on the underground conduits the trench
width should be maintained at the minimum necessary for con
struction operations pipe placement and backfill compaction
With a trench excavation width of less than forty feet and
utilizing granular backfill it is estimated that earth
W 9 May 29 1975
pressure on the conduit will be approximately 16 kips
per lineal foot Surface loadings due to truck traffic or
material storage must be added to this value
Construction Excavations
The trench excavations will generally penetrate the upper
boulder fill Due to the large boulders it is not antici
pated that sheeting can be driven prior to their removal
It may therefore be necessary to slope the sidewalls of the
excavations at a stable slope or utilize prefabricated
shields or shoring
Each contractor should determine the construction methods
and equipment he will utilize for the force main including
the optional use of sheeting shoring temporary backfill
etc The proposed method must meet the applicable safety
requirements and not endanger adjoining structures The
proposed construction methods should be reviewed and approved
prior to construction
The excavation operations should not disturb the underlying
soils on which the pipe will be supported
The following soil parameters should be utilized for the
design of the trench supports through the existing fill

At the proposed invert level of approximately elevation 4
feet the trench excavation would extend approximately seven
feet below the groundwater level Dewatering will be diffi
cult due to the boulder fill and the close proximity of the
ocean
The contractor should be given the option of dewatering the
excavation or placing the crushed rock cradle and pipe in
water
Proposed dewatering methods should be reviewed and approved
prior to construction to assure that adequate excavation
stability is maintained and that the proposed dewatering
methods will not create significant settlements of the nearby
structures
W O May 29 1975
Backfill
The backfill material around the conduits should consist of
select free draining well graded materials compacted to
between 90 and 95 percent of the maximum density It is
anticipated that jetting would be the preferred method of
compaction however mechanical equipment can be utilized
The excavated on site materials will contain numerous
boulders and will not be suitable for trench backfill An
off site material must therefore be provided
Inspections During Construction
It is recommended that the trench excavating rock cradle
construction pipe placement and backfill compaction be
inspected on a full time basis to assure that the recommen
dations are correctly implemented
The area through which the force main will be constructed is
man made land which is generally not uniform Conditions
quite different from those encountered in the subsurface
explorations may therefore be disclosed during the construc
tion operations
The construction inspections should also verify that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of
W O May 29 1975
our investigation If unforeseen or undetected soil condi
tions such as concrete rubble pockets of soft cohesive
soils or unusual water conditions are encountered correc
tive measures should be made in the field as they are
detected

FIELD EXPLORATIONS
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drill
ing five borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The
depths of the borings varied from 50 to 60 feet below the
existing ground surface The locations of the borings are
shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination
in the field by our engineer who continuously supervised the
drilling operations The classifications were verified by
visual inspection and testing in the laboratory A111 soils
were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil Classi
fication System A graphic presentation of the soils encoun
tered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through
A 5 and B 1 through B 3
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D
or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer
free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to
drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring
logs at the appropriate sample depth
