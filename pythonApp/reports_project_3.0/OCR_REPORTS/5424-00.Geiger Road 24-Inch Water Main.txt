PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located near the Honouliuli Recycled Water Facility in the Ewa
District on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project consists of the installation of
approximately 6 400 linear feet of 20 and 24 inch recycled water main and appurtenances
from Honouliuli Treatment Facility to Geiger Road Geiger Road to Kapolei Parkway and
Kapolei Parkway to the future Keaunui Drive
Based on preliminary drawings the invert elevations for the water main ranges from
about 5 to 10 feet below the existing ground surface We understand that gate and air
relief valve manhole structures and thrust blocks and beams for the new recycled water
main will be constructed for the project There are approximately 21 manhole structures
planned for the project The invert elevation for the manhole structures range from about
26 5 to 33 1 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The anticipated depth of the thrust blocks
range from about 19 to 32 feet below the existing ground surface Thrust beams are
planned at the reducers at Station and 10 52

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age in age and

forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct while
Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau lavas
banking against its eastern flank These banked or ponded lavas formed a broad plateau
referred to as the Schofield Plateau
The forces of weathering and erosion attacked this plateau generating sediments
that were transported to the coast In the vicinity of the project site and further south
these alluvial sediments were interbedded with marine sediments and coral algal reef
formations to form a sedimentary wedge ranging in thickness from zero feet in the area of
H 1 freeway to over 1 000 feet at Ewa Beach This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and
serves as the confining formation or caprock over the artesian basal aquifers of
southern Oahu Deposition of these sediments has continued from earlier geologic time
through the present
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the Ewa District on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
project site is located at the Honouliuli Wastewater Treatment Plant and along Geiger
Road and Kapolei Parkway as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The new recycled water main alignment starts at the Honouliuli Wastewater
Treatment Plant turns east and travels along Geiger Road and turns south along Kapolei
Parkway The water main alignment crosses several existing utility lines consisting of
drain water sewer and fuel lines In addition the alignment crosses two drainageways
near the Coral Creek Golf Course area
The project site is relatively flat and is mainly covered with asphaltic concrete and
grass Based on the topographic survey map received from Belt Collins Hawaii Ltd on
March 29 2005 the existing ground surface generally slopes up to the north with
elevations ranging from 31 to 37 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS

approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

explored depth of 17 feet The coralline deposits consisted of loose to dense silty coralline
sand and gravel medium dense clayey sand medium hard to dense coral formation and

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration generally encountered a surface fill layer consisting of medium
dense to very dense silty sand and gravel medium stiff to hard sandy silt sandy clay and
silty clay The fill layer was underlain by coralline deposits consisting of loose to dense
silty coralline sand and gravel medium dense clayey sand medium dense to hard coral
formation and hard sandstone formation at the project site Groundwater was not
encountered in the drilled borings at the time of the field exploration
A granular bedding layer should be provided below the new water main pipes and
the bottom of the valve manhole structures to provide more uniform support for the pipes
and the manhole structures The granular bedding should consist of 6 inches of
open graded gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
Thrust blocks are anticipated at the vertical and horizontal bends along the pipe
alignment In addition thrust beams are planned at the pipe reducer locations A lateral
bearing pressure of may be used for the design of thrust blocks and beams
bearing on the medium dense to dense and medium stiff to hard fill soils and the medium
dense to dense sands and gravels For thrust blocks bearing on the medium dense to
hard coral and sandstone formations a lateral bearing pressure of may be used

Open Trench Installation
Based on the information provided we understand that excavations on the order of
about 5 to 10 feet below the existing ground surface will be required for the water line
installation The pipe invert along the water line ranges from about Elevation 48 9 and
42 6 feet MSL respectively Based on the field exploration the trench excavation may
generally encounter medium dense to dense and medium stiff to hard fill soils and
coralline deposits consisting of loose to medium dense silty coralline sand and gravel
medium dense clayey sand dense to hard coral and sandstone formations

In general the method and equipment to be used for trench excavation should be
determined by the contractor subject to practical limits and safety considerations The
excavated soils from the trench excavation should be stockpiled no closer than a
horizontal distance equal to the depth of the excavation from the edge of the excavation to
reduce the potential for appreciable ground movement In addition the trench excavation
should comply with all applicable local state and federal safety requirements
Pipe Bedding
The stress distribution against the bottom of a pipe has a significant effect on the
load supporting capacity of the pipe Therefore the pipe bedding is an important
design consideration In general we recommend that granular bedding consisting
of 6 inches of open graded gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be provided
below the pipes The granular bedding should extend beyond the sides of the pipe
by a minimum width of one fourth the outside diameter of the pipe The contractor
should confirm that the excavated trench bottom exposes firm and or dense
materials prior to the placement of the bedding material
Trench Backfill
The first zone of backfill initial trench backfill extending from the bedding material
to at least 12 inches above the top of the pipes should consist of open graded
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation Itis critical that a free draining granular
material be used to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches
of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper
trench backfill around the pipes could result in settlement of the backfill and
damage to the pipes The use of on site soils as backfill immediately around the
pipes is not recommended
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the finished subgrade may consist of the excavated on site soils provided that the
excavated on site soils are generally less than 6 inches in maximum particle size or
select granular material Select granular fill and imported fill should consist of
non expansive granular material such as crushed coral basalt or cinder sand The
material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than
3 inches in largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent
particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR
value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent when
tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 Imported fill materials
should be tested by Geolabs Inc for conformance with these recommendations
prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use
The backfill material should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in relatively thin lifts normally 8 inches in level loose lifts
and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to

The backfill material should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in relatively thin lifts normally 8 inches in level loose lifts
and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to
reduce the potential for significant future ground subsidence Where trenches are
located below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the
pavement grade should be compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Excavation
Based on the topographic plan and our field exploration excavation may involve
cuts into the underlying coral and sandstone formation It is anticipated that the
coral rock and sandstone may be excavated with normal heavy excavation
equipment such as ripping with large bulldozers where the material is near the
existing ground surface However deep excavations into coral and sandstone
formations may require the use of hoerams
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are based
on field data obtained from the borings performed at the project site We
recommend that contractors bidding on this project be encouraged to examine the
site conditions and soil data to make their own interpretation
Valve Manhole Structures
Gate and air relief valve manhole structures are planned along the water main
alignment Based on preliminary drawings the manhole structures will have invert
elevations ranging from about 26 5 to 33 1 feet MSL Based on our field exploration we
anticipate that the manhole structures will be constructed in medium dense to very dense
and medium stiff to hard fill materials loose to dense coralline deposits and dense to hard
coral and sandstone formations
In general we recommend that the bottom of the manhole structure bear on
6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation Based on our engineering
analyses we believe that the net increase in bearing pressure on the underlying soil
deposits resulting from the manhole construction would be minimal Settlements on the
order of less than 1 inch are anticipated for the manhole structures
The lateral earth pressures acting on the proposed underground manhole
structures will depend on the type of adjacent soils type of backfill used and the
compactive effort on the backfill material around the structure We recommend that the
new manhole structures be designed to resist the following lateral earth pressures at rest
conditions from the adjacent soils

pressurized pipes The thrust force acting on the pipes may be resisted by passive earth
pressure acting against the face of the thrust block In addition thrust beams are planned
at the reducer locations for the 24 inch and 20 inch pipes
