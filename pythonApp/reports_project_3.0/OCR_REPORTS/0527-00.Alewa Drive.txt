This report presents the results of our preliminary soil
investigation for the proposed residence on Alewa Drive
Honolulu Hawaii The purpose of our study was to investigate
the surficial conditions at the site determine foundation
requirements for the residence and to provide recommendations
for the retaining wall construction
FIELD EXPLORATION
In order to evaluate the near surface conditions at the site
two shallow borings were hand drilled utilizing light portable

SITE CONDITIONS
The lot has been utilized as a landscaped garden The lot
slopes down southerly from Alewa Drive from a grade of 570
feet at street level to 558 feet at its southerly boundary
The slope is presently covered with gardening and low rubble
stone retaining walls and did not show signs of instability
at the time of our investigation
On the site is an existing one story open sided pavilion at
grade concrete slab and numerous trees

It was noted that on the adjoining easterly lot the existing
two car garage and the two story concrete residence exhibited
excessive wall distress It is believed that this condition
may be a result of nearby tree roots and or the expansiveness
of the soil
The site is immediately underlain by a medium stiff to stiff
reddish brown clayey silt that is slightly expansive Generally
below the upper two feet the silt becomes stiffer Heterogeneous
loose fill was encountered in boring 2
No groundwater was encountered or signs of water seepage
observed
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Except for pockets of loose soils on the slope surface and
shallow loose fill the site appeared to be firm original ground
No evidence of deep fill is believed to exist along the existing
slope therefore it is our opinion that the site is suitable for
the proposed residence supported on shallow conventional foot
ings The proposed foundations should bear into stiff clayey

silt soils and bottomed a minimum of two feet below adjacent
ground Should loose fill be encountered in proposed footings
areas this should be thoroughly recompacted or footing
carried through into competent material A bearing value of
2 500 pounds per square foot may be used for dead plus live
loads Continuous footings should have a minimum width of 12
inches Pressures may be increased by one third when consi
dering wind or seismic loads Individual post footings for the
residence should extend below the slope surface such that the
outer edge of footing should have a minimum horizontal set back
distance of 4 feet to the slope surface We recommend for
erosion protection the footing excavation should be backfilled
with concrete to match the upper slope surface Continuous
footings should be provided with two No 4 reinforcing bars
one top and one bottom
Due to the slightly expansive site soils slabs on grade should
be underlain with a minimum of four inches of granular material
In areas where dampness in the floor would be undesirable a
plastic membrane should be placed over the granular material
A minimum of 6 x 6 10 welded wire mesh should be provided in
slabs on grade

Retaining Wall
It is anticipated that a maximum height of retaining wall will
be on the order of 7 vertical feet Based on surficial infor
mation it appears that full depth of the retaining wall cut will
be through stiff residual clayey silt and some occasional
decomposed rock It is recommended the cut slope be inspected
by a geologist or soils engineer from our firm to verify that
no geologic planes of weakness or groundwater seepage exist
below the present slope and subsurface conditions are as anti
cipated For design of the retaining wall it may be assumed
that the soils will impose a lateral pressure equal to that
developed by a fluid with a density of 40 pounds per cubic foot
Resistance to horizontal sliding may be provided by a combina
tion of passive pressure and friction along the bottom of
footings Passive pressures may be assumed equal to the equi
valent fluid density of 250 pounds per cubic foot The
coefficient of friction between concrete and site soil may be
taken at 0 4 The backfill immediately against the wall should
be an open grade compacted granular material and adequate
drainage system should be designed to prevent the build up of
excessive pore pressure behind retaining wall

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
The materials encountered on the project site and utilized in
our evaluation are believed representative of the total area
however soil materials may vary in characteristics between
boring locations Should other soils conditions be encountered
during construction a foundation engineer should be consulted
and appropriate construction modification implemented if
necessary Due to the possible variations that may be encoun
tered in the existing fill and onsite soils we recommend that
all foundation and retaining wall excavations be inspected and
approved by a soils engineer prior to placement of steel or
concrete to assure that the subsurface conditions encountered
during construction are of comparable competency as those
encountered during this investigation and upon which the
recommendations presented in this report are based If loose
or debris materials are encountered they may have to be
replaced and compacted with competent material
Erosion control measures should be provided to minimize surface
water running over exposed slopes
Since our investigation is based upon the site materials
observed selective laboratory testing and engineering analyses

Following a discussion and a site visit with Messrs Rodney
Yamamoto and Sam Sweitzer we are pleased to submit this proposal
to perform a preliminary soil investigation for the proposed
residence on the subject site It is presently planned to con
struct a retaining wall approximately 6 7 feet high mauka of
the Alewa Drive property line
It is our understanding the purpose of the study will be to
provide design parameters for the proposed retaining walls and
bearing pressures for foundations of the proposed residence
Based on previous investigation in the area and a review of the
general geology we believe the soil condition on the site con
sist of uniform residual soil underlain by decomposed basalt
To perform our investigation a geologist or engineer from our
firm will perform a geological inspection of the site with
particular emphasis on slope and retaining wall stability in the
area Based on the result of our investigation we would
evaluate the stability of the proposed retaining wall and footing
constructions The results of our investigation would be
summarized in a written report We will provide evaluations on
possible problems during construction and will provide recom
mendations for retaining wall and footings design
