PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves construction of a new Makai comfort station at Pier 1 at
Kahului Harbor on the Island of Maui Hawaii There is an existing large warehouse
structure on the pier A comfort station was recently constructed outside the
southeastern end of the warehouse The planned improvements involve the demolition
of the existing unisex comfort station outside the north makai end of the warehouse
and the replacement with a facility that is up to modern standards with sufficient
capacity to accommodate stevedore needs

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is at Kahului Harbor in Kahului on the Island of Maui Hawaii The
site begins at the northern end of the existing Pier 1 building which also consists of an
existing comfort station at the southern end of Pier 1 and extends to the new sewer line
alignment due south near the old Kahului Store The entire project site surface consists of
an asphalt concrete pavement along the shoreline In the Pier 1 vicinity the area is an
existing parking lot for the shipyard visitors To the north and east of the parking lot is the
existing Matson storage container yard The proposed alignment traverses the container
yard until it meets Ala Luina Road at which point the alignment turns south and traverses
parallel with the roadway to connect with the existing sewer manhole near the old
Kahului Store

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally underlain by medium
dense to dense silty gravel and sands throughout the depths explored Based on the
generally competent subsoil conditions encountered we envision that slabs on grade with
thickened edge footings may be used to support the proposed new comfort station
anticipated for the above project Based on the sandy soil conditions encountered in the
borings special attention should be given to the site preparation recommendations to
compact the subgrade prior to the construction of new improvements Detailed discussion
and our geotechnical engineering design recommendations are presented in the following
sections of this report
Slab On Grade Foundations
We envision that slab on grade foundations with thickened edge footings will likely
be used to support the proposed new comfort station anticipated for the above project
Based on our field exploration an allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per
square foot psf may be used for the design of shallow foundations bearing on the
recompacted in situ soils or select granular fill material This bearing value is for
those caused by wind or seismic forces In general thickened edge footings should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade The thickened edges

of concrete walkway slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at least
6 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
We recommend that the bottom of footings bear on the firm and unyielding
near surface soils The bottom of the footing excavations should be recomipacted to at
least 90 percent relative compaction to provide a relatively firm and unyielding bearing
surface prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete If soft and or loose materials are
encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they should be over excavated to
expose firm materials The over excavation should be backfilled with granular material
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the bottom of footing
should extend down to the underlying competent material Relative compaction refers to
the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of
the same soil established in accordance with procedures described in ASTM D 1557
Foundations next to utility trenches or easement should be embedded below a
45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or
as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid
surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to reduce
the potential for appreciable foundation settlement If foundations are designed and
constructed in accordance with our recommendations we estimate total settlements to be
less than 1 inch with differential settlements on the order of about 0 5 inch
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by friction developed
between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of friction of
0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted in situ soil or select granular fill
materials Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent
fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the
soil around the footings is well compacted or undisturbed Unless covered by pavements
or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
In general we recommend supporting concrete slabs on grade on a minimum
4 inch layer of cushion fill The cushion fill should consist of open graded gravels such
as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation for more uniform support of the
slab and to serve as a capillary break To reduce the potential for moisture infiltration
through the slab concrete an impervious moisture barrier membrane should be
provided below the slab
The subgrade soils supporting the slabs on grade should be scarified to a depth of
about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The slab subgrades should be firm
and unyielding prior to the placement of the cushion fill A Geolabs representative should
observe subgrade preparation and footing excavations prior to placement of reinforcing
steel or concrete

Site Preparation
Based on our field observation we anticipate that minimal site grading work on the
order of less than 1 foot will be required for construction of the new comfort station In
general the area within the contract grading limits should be cleared thoroughly at the
on set of earthwork Old foundations pavements debris deleterious materials and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of off site
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas designated to
receive fill should be over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting
excavation should be backfilled with compacted granular fill material The excavated soft
and or organic soils should be properly disposed off site or used in landscaping areas if
appropriate
After clearing and grubbing areas designated to receive fills should be scarified to
a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density
Slab subgrades should be kept moist and free of shrinkage cracks until covered by
slab cushion fill or concrete Where shrinkage cracks are noted after preparation of the
subgrade we recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather
and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
compacted granular fill material
In general the on site sandy soils generated from the excavations may be used as
a source of fill materials At this time we do not anticipate that imported fill materials for
grading purposes will be required for this project However if imported fill materials will be
necessary the imported fill materials should consist of non expansive granular material
such as crushed coral basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from
coarse to fine with particles no larger than 3 inches in maximum dimension and should
contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material
should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of
less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883
Imported fill materials should be tested by Geolabs for conformance with these
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use Imported fill
materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in level
lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction

Utility Trench
New utility lines will be required for the proposed project In general granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is
recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine
gravel should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the
pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of
the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is critical to use this
free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of
pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper trench backfill
could result in backfill settlement and pipe damage
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the excavated on site soils or
select granular fill material The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to no less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future
ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the
trench backfill below the pavement grade should be compacted to at least 95 percent
relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the new comfort station should be sloped to shed water
away from foundations and slabs to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised to
install gutter systems around the building and divert the discharge away from the
foundation and slab areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs
should be avoided Planters next to foundations should also be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoil
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be
properly backfilled against the walls or slab edges immediately after setting of the concrete
to reduce the potential for water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon
as possible and should be maintained to drain surface water runoff away from the
foundations and slabs

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs Inc be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during construction of the proposed comfort station project The
critical items of construction monitoring that require Special Inspection include
observation of footing excavation and subgrade preparation A Geolabs representative
should monitor other aspects of earthwork construction to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for
design changes that may be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from
those anticipated at the time this report was prepared The recommendations provided
herein are contingent upon such observations If the actual exposed subsurface conditions
encountered during construction are different from those assumed or considered herein
then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between and
beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations provided herein
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations on the logs of borings
depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made to formulate this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
