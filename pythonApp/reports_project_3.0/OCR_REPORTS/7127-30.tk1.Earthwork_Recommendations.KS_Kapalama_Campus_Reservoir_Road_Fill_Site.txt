As requested, we have prepared this letter report to provide earthwork 
recommendations pertaining to the proposed site grading work at the Reservoir Road 
Fill Site (Areas 1, 2 and 3) within the Kamehameha Schools Kapalama Campus in

and presents our earthwork recommendations for the proposed site grading work. 
EARTHWORK RECOMMENDATIONS 
The project site is located on the down slope (south) side of Reservoir Service Road 
in the upper portion of the Kamehameha Schools Kapalama Campus, on the Island of Oahu, 
Hawaii.  Based on the information provided, there are three undocumented fill site areas 
within the project limits. The three fill areas are designated as Area Nos. 1, 2, and 3. The 
Reservoir Service Road is an asphalt paved road accessed by Waonahele Road, which 
provides vehicle access to two existing reservoir tank sites.  
Fill Area Nos. 1 and 2 adjoin each other and are situated between an active water 
reservoir tank and an inactive tank. Fill Area No. 3 is located near the end of Reservoir 
Service Road with a much smaller footprint area than Fill Area Nos. 1 and 2. 
We understand that the fill areas contain undocumented soil, rock materials, and 
limited volume of construction debris, other solid waste and organic green waste. The fills 
were not placed under controlled and engineered conditions.

Site Grading 
The following grading items are addressed in the succeeding subsections:  
(1) Site Preparation, (2) Fill and Backfill Materials, (3) Undocumented Fills, (4) Fill 
Placement and Compaction Requirements, (5) Excavation, and (6) Cut and Fill Slopes. 
A Geolabs representative should monitor site grading operations to observe 
whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation process and to 
confirm whether the exposed soil/rock conditions are similar to those assumed in this 
letter report and/or encountered during our field exploration. 
Site Preparation 
At the on-set of earthwork operations, areas within the contract grading limits 
should be cleared and grubbed thoroughly. Vegetation, debris, abandoned 
equipment, deleterious and other unsuitable materials should be removed and 
disposed of properly off-site. Soft and/or yielding areas encountered during 
clearing and grubbing below areas designated to receive fill and/or future 
improvements should be over-excavated to expose firm natural material, and the 
resulting excavation should be backfilled with well-compacted fills. The 
excavated soft and/or organic soils should be properly disposed of off-site and/or 
used in landscape areas, where appropriate. Contract documents should include 
additive and deductive unit prices for over-excavation and compacted fill 
placement to account for variations in the over-excavation quantities. 
After clearing and grubbing, the exposed subgrades should be scarified to a 
minimum depth of about 6 inches, moisture-conditioned to about 2 percent above 
the optimum moisture, and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative 
compaction. Relative compaction refers to the in-place dry density of soil 
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil 
established in accordance with ASTM D1557. Optimum moisture is the water 
content (percentage by dry weight) corresponding to the maximum dry density. 
Where shrinkage cracks are observed at the ground surface after compaction of 
the subgrade, we recommend preparing the subgrade soil again as 
recommended above. Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed 
subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require 
over-excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well-compacted fill at no

Fills and Backfills 
We envision the excavated on-site soils may be reused as a source of general fill 
materials provided that the materials are free of vegetation, deleterious materials, 
and clay lumps and rock fragments greater than 6 inches in maximum dimension. 
Imported general fill materials needed to fill the site may consist of materials with 
a low to moderate expansion potential. Imported general fill materials should 
consist of soil materials with a maximum particle size of 6 inches or less with 
sufficient fines (between 10 and 60 percent particles passing the No. 200 sieve) 
to prevent the occurrence of voids in the compacted mass. In addition, general fill 
materials should have a California Bearing Ratio (CBR) value of 8 or greater and 
a swell of 2 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM D1883. It 
should be noted that the general fill requirements presented herein are intended 
as guidelines only and may be modified based on additional laboratory testing 
and field observations on the available fill materials conducted by Geolabs.  
Select granular fill required for the project should consist of non-expansive select 
granular materials, such as crushed coral or basalt. The material should be 
well-graded from coarse to fine with particles no larger than 3 inches in largest 
dimension. The material also should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles 
passing the No. 200 sieve. In addition, the material should have a laboratory 
CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less 
when tested in accordance with ASTM D1883. 
Imported fill materials should be free of organics and deleterious materials and 
should be suitable for the intended use based on the recommendations provided 
herein. Geolabs should test imported fill materials for conformance with these 
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use. 
Undocumented Fills 
As mentioned above, some of the fill areas contain undocumented soil, rock 
materials, and limited volume of construction debris, other solid waste and organic 
green waste. The fills were not placed under controlled and engineered conditions.  
Undocumented fills, where encountered within the grading limits for the proposed 
construction, should be removed completely to expose firm materials and 
recompacted to general fill requirements. 
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements 
General fill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose 
thickness, moisture-conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture 
content, and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction.

Non-expansive select granular fill materials, if used, should be placed in level lifts of 
about 8 inches in loose thickness, moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture, and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction. 
Relative compaction refers to the in-place dry density of soil expressed as a 
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance 
with ASTM D1557. Optimum moisture is the water content (percentage by weight) 
corresponding to the maximum dry density. Compaction should be accomplished 
by using sheepsfoot rollers, vibratory rollers, or other types of acceptable 
compaction equipment. 
Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the clayey subgrade soils and 
general fill materials are critical elements of the earthwork, Geolabs should perform 
observations and soil density tests during the site grading operations to assist the 
contractor in obtaining the required degree of compaction and the proper moisture 
content on each fill lift. Where compaction is less than required, additional 
compactive effort should be applied with adjustment in the moisture content as 
necessary, to obtain the specified compaction. It should be noted that the moisture 
requirement of the on-site fills and subgrades (at least 2 percent above the 
optimum moisture) is an important requirement considering the moderately to highly 
expansive nature of the on-site clayey soils. 
Excavation 
It is anticipated that most of the excavation work, if required, may be accomplished 
with normal heavy excavation equipment, such as bulldozers and scrapers.  It is 
anticipated that some of the excavations could encounter boulders and/or other 
obstructions, especially within the undocumented fills.  Therefore, we anticipate 
some difficult excavation conditions may arise in localized areas during 
construction. It is possible that the excavation may require the use of hoerams or 
chipping. 
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the materials are based on our 
site reconnaissance and available field and laboratory data from the borings drilled. 
Contractors should be encouraged to examine the site conditions and the 
subsurface data to make their own reasonable and prudent interpretation. 
Cut and Fill Slopes 
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions, we believe that cut slopes for 
the project may be made at an inclination of two horizontal to one vertical 
(2H:1V) or flatter provided that erosion control and slope protection is provided 
for the cut slopes in the final configuration. 
Permanent fill slopes constructed using the excavated on-site soils or imported 
fill materials may be designed with a slope inclination of 2H:1V or flatter. Fills 
placed on slopes steeper than 5H:1V should be keyed and benched into the

existing slope to provide stability of the new fill against sliding. The filling
operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal
compacted layers in accordance with the fill placement recommendations
presented in the “Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements” subsection
herein. Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the

implemented instead of backfilling the slope to the design grade with sliver fills.
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope planting should
be provided as soon as possible to reduce the potential for erosion of the finished
slopes.
Construction of earth berms, interceptor ditches, and the use of geotextile fabrics
over the fill slope face should be considered to reduce the potential for significant
erosion, thus enhancing the long-term stability of the fill slopes. Appropriate slope
planting or other permanent erosion control measures to reduce the potential for
significant erosion of the exposed slopes (including a permanent irrigation system)
should be implemented as soon as possible after the finished slope faces are
completed.
