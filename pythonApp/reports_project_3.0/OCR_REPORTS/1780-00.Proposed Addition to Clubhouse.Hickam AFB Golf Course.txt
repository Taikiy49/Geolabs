SUBSURFACE SOTL CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling
three 3 borings to depths of 15 to 20 feet below the existing ground
surface
The borings disclosed that the site was mantled by a medium dense
layer of sand and gravel fill about 2 to 3 5 feet thick Underlying the
upper fill was another generally medium dense fill layer of silty sand
with gravel and occasional loose pockets including broken glass and scrap
metal pieces
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during our field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
General
Our subsurface exploration encountered generally medium dense layers
of sand and gravel fill with occasional loose pockets at the proposed
building site Despite of the localized loose soil condition shallow
spread or continuous wall footings bearing directly on the recompacted
existing fill may be used for foundation support of the light structure
Minimal site grading is anticipated for the construction of the
proposed building

Foundation Support
Based on the anticipated soil conditions and light design loading
we believe that an allowable bearing capacity of 2000 p s f may be used
for spread or continuous footing bearing on the recompacted existing fill
materials
The bearing capacity value is for total dead plus live loads only
and may be increased by one third for short duration wind loading
Estimated settlements under the design loading are anticipated to be on
the order of less than one inch total and one half of an inch
differential
Footings should be placed a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Uplift wind or other lateral loading condition
may further increase the embedment of the footing foundation
Footings adjacent to the existing clubhouse should be embedded to
the same elevations as the adjacent footings of the clubhouse the
footings at the eastern corner area of the proposed building near the top
of the existing slope should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum
5 foot horizontal set back distance from the outer edge of footing to the
slope face
Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed structure can be
obtained by frictional resistance and passive pressure of the in situ
soils A frictional coefficient of 0 35 may be used between the bottom
of the footing and the recompacted existing fill In addition an
equivalent fluid pressure of 240 p s f per foot of depth may be used to
determine the passive resistance of the in situ soils for a horizontal
ground condition For a 2 1 sloping condition the passive resistance of
the in situ soils should be reduced to an equivalent fluid pressure of
160 p s f

Site Grading
The finished grade of the proposed structure will match with the
finished grade of the existing clubhouse Therefore no major site
grading is anticipated
At the on set of earthwork the areas within the building grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable
materials such as vegetation and rubbish should be removed and disposed
of properly off site in order to prevent contamination of the excavated
material
After clearing and grubbing the areas to receive footing
Slab on grade and sidewalk should be scarified to a depth of at least 6
inches below the bottom of footing or subgrade elevation moisture
conditioned and recompacted to 95 of its maximum density as established
by ASTM D 1557 78 test method Any loose or wet spots encountered at the
footing or subgrade level should be removed and replaced with compacted
select on site granular soil or with suitable imported material compacted
to the above mentioned standard
The on site sandy and gravelly soils may be reused as backfill
material If required new imported fill material should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum
size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
Slab On Grade
The on grade floor slab should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer
of gravel size crushed rock material such as 3 Fine rock to provide
uniform support A moisture barrier membrane should be placed under the
slabs
The slab thickened edges exposed to any unpaved area should be
embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade

Retaining Walls
Two short retaining walls are planned at about 6 feet east of the
proposed building adjacent to the existing golf cart path The
guidelines for the short retaining wall designs are listed below
An allowable bearing value of up to 2000 p s f is recommended
for foundations bearing on imported select borrow or on
recompacted on site soil
For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
A passive earth resistance of 240 p s f per foot of depth may
be used for the wall footing on level areas The passive
pressure must be reduced for walls on slopes For a 2 1 sloping
condition a passive earth pressure of 160 p s f per foot of
depth may be used
A base friction factor of 0 35 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the wall
The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded
a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading foundation pavements and
retaining walls should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the grubbing site grading foundation
excavation backfilling and subgrade preparation be observed by the soils
engineer during construction to see that adequate compaction has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site for use
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance and soil information from boring and
laboratory test data obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such as
pockets of organic soils trash fill or expansive soils may occur in
localized areas Therefore additional expenditures may be needed during
construction to attain a properly constructed project Some contingency
fund is thus recommended to accommodate these possible costs

three 3 borings to depths of 15 to 20 feet below the existing ground

were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 3

a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the
