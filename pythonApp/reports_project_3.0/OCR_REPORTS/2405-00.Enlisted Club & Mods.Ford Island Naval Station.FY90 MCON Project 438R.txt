This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed addition to the Enlisted Club
Facility at Ford Island on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate
1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project consists of an addition to existing Building 88 on Ford
Island The proposed addition is a single story structure covering about
5 500 square feet The building will be of reinforced concrete and
masonry construction with concrete slabs on grade Minimal site grading
is anticipated for the construction of the building addition
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface soil and groundwater conditions at the proposed site to develop
soil data for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations
pertinent to the foundation design of the proposed structure

GENERAL SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed addition is located on the west side of the existing
Enlisted Club Facility Building 88 at Ford Island on the island of Oahu
Hawaii The general project area is bounded by Enterprise Street on the
north St Lo Avenue on the east Block Island Street on the south and
Lexington Blvd on the west
The existing Enlisted Club Facility is a single story structure of
reinforced concrete and masonry construction with concrete
slabs on grade The proposed one story addition will consist of a new
enlarged lanai dining area and a new kitchen area at the west end of
Building 88
The topography of the site is generally flat

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
General
From a geotechnical engineering point of view it is our opinion that
the proposed building addition is feasible Based on the soil conditions
encountered and the anticipated light to moderate building loads we
believe that shallow spread or continuous wall footings bearing on the
upper hard clayey silt and medium dense silty sand may be used
Site Grading
Minor site grading is anticipated for the proposed building addition
project
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris
rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed
properly off site in order to prevent contamination of the excavated
material or stockpiles of imported material
The areas to receive fill or which will support slabs on grade and
footings should be scarified to a depth of at least six 6 inches below
existing grade or the bottom of the footing or subgrade elevation

moisture conditioned and recompacted to a minimm of 90 of maximm
density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose or wet spots
encountered at the footing or subgrade level should be removed and
replaced with select on site material or suitable imported material
compacted to 90 maximum dry density
The on site sandy soils may be used as a source of backfill and fill
material If required imported fill material should consist of select
non expansive granular material less than 3 inches in maximum size with
less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
The fill should be placed in maximm nine 9 inch thick loose level

ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The site should be graded to drain surface run off away from all
foundations
Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
building addition An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used
for footings bearing on the upper hard clayey silts and medium dense silty
sands which have been recompacted to 90 maximum dry density as
recommended in Site Grading The recommended bearing value is for total
dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third when temporary wind
and or seismic loads are considered Estimated spread footing settlements
are on the order of less than one half of an inch total and one fourth of
an inch differential
New footings should be placed at about the same level as the adjacent
existing footings of Building 88 In general a minimum footing embedment
of eighteen 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade is
recommended

Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil around
the foundation is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may
be used Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in
the upper 18 inches of soil should be neglected
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade construction is anticipated for the floor of
the structure A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel should be provided below
the slabs as cushion fill The slab subgrade should be kept moist prior
to concrete placement
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration an
impervious moisture barrier is recommended below the slabs provided that
an inch of moist fine sand is placed above the plastic membrane to help
concrete curing

Utility Trench
New utility lines may be planned for the project A granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of ASTM C 33 No 67B gravel 3B Fine is
recommended under the pipes
Granular materials should be used for the trench backfill to reduce
the level of effort needed to achieve compaction The backfill up to
about one foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to reduce the
possibility of damaging the pipes
The trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to the
subgrade or finished grade should be placed in maximum 9 inch horizontal
lifts and mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 of maximm ary
density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78 to reduce potential future ground
subsidence
Existing utilities should be located and verified It may be
necessary to move an existing line or adjust structural details to
accommodate the utility Should an unanticipated condition or buried
structure be encountered during construction each condition should be
evaluated by the soils engineer and the design modified if necessary
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations qiven herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer can assume no responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundation phase of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
and probes Variations of conditions between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extend of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping from references on the preliminary site plan Elevations of the
data points were not available at the time this report was prepared The
physical locations and elevations of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
a UN ei i
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two
2 borings to depths of 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface at
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
