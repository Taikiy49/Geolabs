INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed realignment of Ka Haku Road in
Princeville on the island of Kauai Hawaii The general location of the
project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes

PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
The existing Ka Haku Road is the main entrance into the Princeville
development area As part of the site improvements in conjunction with
the construction of the Phase II golf course and development of the Phase
II residential subdivisions it is proposed to realign Ka Haku Road to
provide a new entrance to Princeville that is more efficient and has a
greater aesthetic impact than the existing entrance
No site grading plans are currently available however we anticipate
that the project site grading will be require fills on the order of ten
10 to fifteen 15 feet in thickness It is our understanding that the
Princeville Airport runway extension project will be the borrow site
In addition to the construction of the new road alignment it is
proposed to construct water features with sculptures It is also proposed
to install tunnels under the new road sections for golf cart access to the
adjacent golf course
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of site grading and
pavement design of the proposed road realignment and foundation design of
the related structures

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated in the Princeville area on the northern
coast of the island of Kauai Hawaii The project site is located to the
east of the existing Princeville development area in the Princeville Phase
II Master Plan area to the north of Kuhio Highway The general location
of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate
1
Formerly the site was used for the cultivation of pineapple and then
as a pasture for the grazing of cattle and horses Currently the site is
being maintained as a well trimmed open field and or as part of the
interim Phase II golf course

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is situated on a broad gently sloping plateau along
the north shore of the island of Kauai This plateau was built during the
late Pliocene and Pleistocene by the extrusion of basaltic lavas and
related pyroclastics from the Pooku vent of the Koloa Volcanic Series
Most of the soils in the project area are residual soils derived
from the in situ weathering of these basic igneous rocks The residual
soils grade with depth to saprolite a form of residual soil which retains
the relict structures vesicles and joints of the parent rock
Soil Conditions
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling
three 3 borings to depths of 11 5 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the soil conditions encountered throughout the project
site appear relatively uniform The borings generally encountered a
surface layer of stiff to very stiff reddish brown to brown clayey silts
varying in thickness between 3 5 and 8 5 feet These are residual soils
that derived from the in situ weathering of Koloa Basalts and are
generally structureless
Underlying the residual soils our borings encountered generally
medium stiff to stiff saprolite soils consisting of clayey silts and
silts having predominantly reddish brown color mottled with black spots
or streaks to the maximum drilled depth of the borings
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2306 00 Page 5
During our field exploration groundwater was not encountered in the
drilled borings However groundwater has been encountered in deep
borings in other areas of Princeville This groundwater is in a perched
condition and is generally the result of low permeability ash beds or
lenses within the Koloa Volcanic Series Therefore it may be possible
that isolated lenses of groundwater may be encountered if deep excavations
are made within the project site
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered generally competent subsoils within
the project site However modified grading techniques are required for
the earthwork portion of the proposed site improvements due to the
relatively high in situ moisture content of the subsoils and the
relatively high rainfall occurring in the area
We recommend that spread footing foundations be used to support the
proposed structures
Site Grading
The proposed development is in the conceptual stage and detailed
grading plans are not available However fills of up to about 10 to 15
feet in thickness are anticipated
Fill Compaction
Since the project site is located in a high rainfall area most of
the on site subsoils within the top 15 feet had in situ moisture
contents of about 33 to 50 per cent at the time of our
exploration Based on the available information presented in a
report prepared by others concerning the proposed borrow site the
in situ moisture contents at the runway extension project site
range from about 35 to in excess of 60 per cent These moisture
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O
2306 00 Page 6
contents are higher than the anticipated optimum moisture contents
which range from about 32 to 36 per cent for similar soils in the
Princeville area Therefore considerable drying of the excavated
materials would normally be required prior to their use as fill
materials
However in view of the relatively frequent rainfall that generally
occurs in the area throughout the year drying of large quantities
of soils might not be readily achieved during mass grading
operations To facilitate construction it is our opinion that the
normal 90 ASTM D 1557 78 compaction standard for general site
fills as used in drier areas may be reduced to 85 for this
project Our previous experience in Princeville and other similar
areas indicates that 85 compaction is generally adequate for site
fills under roadway and light building loads
Fills within the upper 12 inches below roadway or footing subgrades
should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of its ASTM D 1557 78
maximum dry density This may require additional handling drying
of high moisture content soils from borrow sites in the Princeville
area or import of drier select material from other areas
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Topsoil should be
stripped and stockpiled for later use in landscaping Any
unsuitable materials such as debris rubbish vegetation and other
deleterious materials should be removed and disposed of properly
off site
The area to receive fill should be scarified to a minimum depth of
6 inches below the cleared grade level air dried if necessary
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2306 00 Page 7
and recompacted to the above recommended minimum of 85 of its
maximum Gry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The ground surface below pavements or footings to be constructed at
grade or in cut should be scarified to a minimm depth of 6 inches
below subgrade level air dried if necessary and recompacted to a
minimm of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
Any loose soft or yielding areas spots encountered at the base of
the new fill or at the pavement or footing subgrade level should be
over excavated down to in situ firm ground and replaced with
air dried select on site soil or imported backfill compacted to a
minimm of 85 of its ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density Backfill
within 12 inches below roadway or footing subgrade should be
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its ASTM D dry
density
Fill Placement
a a Bane
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results it is
our opinion that the on site soils have low expansion potential and
may be used as a source of fill and backfill materials Off site
borrow material should be tested for its suitability by the
geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site The material
should generally be non expansive and should not contain large
percentages of boulders organic or deleterious matters
It is noted that most of the soils in the Princeville area within
the top 15 feet have dry densities in the range of about 65 to 88
p c f When they are compacted to higher densities corresponding
volume reduction will occur For earthwork quantity estimation we
believe a relatively high shrinkage factor should be used A

Fills should be placed in level horizontal lifts not exceeding ten

Foundations
Based on the soil conditions we believe that shallow spread and
continuous footing foundations bearing on the recompacted in situ
subsoils or on fill placed and compacted in accordance with the
recommendations given herein may be used to support the proposed
structures
An allowable bearing value of up to 2 000 p s f with a minimm
footing embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade is
recommended The bearing value is for total dead plus live load only
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2306 00 Page 9
and may be increased by one third for short duration wind or seismic
loading
For the large sculptures in the proposed water features a mat
foundation is recommended to facilitate construction
For structures to be constructed on or adjacent to slopes the
structure should be set back or the depth of the footing embedment
extended so that there is a minimum of ten 10 feet between the toe of
the footing and the outside slope face
If a footing foundation is located next to a utility trench the
footing should be extended to the bottom of the utility trench or be
designed to span over the trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
Based on the boring data estimated settlements for foundations
bearing on compacted fill are anticipated to be on the order of less
than one 1 inch total and one half 1 2 of an inch differential
Resistance to lateral loads on the proposed structure may be
provided by passive soil pressure and frictional resistance A
frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used to compute the frictional
resistance between the bottoms of the footings and the new compacted
fill or recompacted in situ soils In addition an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be used against the buried
vertical faces of the footings to determine the passive soil pressure
Slabs On Grade
Slab on grade construction with 4 inch concrete slabs placed over a
4 inch layer of compacted base course may be used for proposed
structures which require floor slabs The thickened edges of the
on grade slabs should extend a minimum of twelve 12 inches below the
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2306 00 Page 10
lowest adjacent ground surface For slabs located adjacent to slopes
the building should be set back or the depth of embedment extended so
that there is a minimm of ten 10 feet between the toe of the
thickened edge and the outside slope face
To protect against moisture damage of the floor covering material
a moisture barrier should be considered for use below the slabs
Our laboratory test results indicate that the on site soils in
general have low swelling potential at their natural moisture contents
and exhibit a tendency to shrink when allowed to dry Therefore the
slab subgrade should be kept moist and not allowed to dry out prior to
concrete placement
Utilities
Underground utilities are anticipated for this development It is
anticipated that most trenching will be in new fill or the in situ
medium stiff residual or saprolite soils
On site soils are generally suitable for trench backfill and should
be compacted as recommended in the FILL PLACEMENT section Mechanical
compaction is recommended as the on site fine grained soils are not
conducive to water tamping or jetting
Retaining Walls
If structural cart tunnels or other buried structures are to be
installed as part of the site improvements the walls should be designed
as retaining walls under a restrained condition
An allowable bearing value of 2 000 p s f is recommended for the
design of wall foundations bearing on fill or recompacted natural
ground
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2306 00 Page 11
Granular materials such as base course rock 3B Fine gravel sand
or similar should be used as backfill material directly behind the
walls This would reduce the compactive effort required for the
backfill and reduce the build up of additional lateral pressures on the
walls Impervious on site silty material should be used for the top 2
feet of the backfill
We recommend that the walls be designed to resist a lateral earth
pressure of 55 p c f equivalent fluid pressure under restrained
condition
A subdrainage system and waterproofing should be used to prevent
the build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls and to reduce the
potential of moisture migration through the walls to the interior of the
structure if necessary
A friction factor of 0 4 between the concrete footing bottom and
soil may be utilized to determine the sliding resistance of the walls
Additionally a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f equivalent fluid
pressure may be used

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment

If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during the earthwork and
foundation construction phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations

If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during

report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings Variations of conditions between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field data points were determined by
measurement from reference points shown on The Bluffs Preliminary
Plan by Belt Collins Associates dated Elevations
of the data points were determined by interpolation from contours and
elevation points on the same plan The physical location and elevation
of the field data points should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown
on profiles or cross section are based upon interpolation between field
data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions

Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which when
compacted will not exceed ten inches 10 in loose thickness
Each layer shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain
uniformity of material and water content within each layer
Additional fill material shall not be placed on any fill layer
which has not been properly compacted Subsequent removal of
any unacceptable layer shall be done at the Contractor s
expense
Rock fragments or boulders less than twelve inches 12 in
diameter may be utilized provided they are not concentrated in
any one area and that there is sufficient fine grained material
to fill the voids between the rocks
Rocks greater than twelve inches 12 in diameter shall be
removed from the fill unless specifically approved for placement
in the fill by the Geotechnical Engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 85 percent of the
maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
unless otherwise specified in the Geotechnical Engineer s
Report
Boulders encountered in excavations may be used in the deeper
fills provided that the following are followed
1 Boulders shall be spread out and must not be nested
together They should be placed so that compaction
equipment will be able to compact around them
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2306 00 Plate C 8
Appendix C Continued
2 No part of the boulder should enroach any closer to finished
subgrade than the boulder s largest dimension and in no
case be closer than 3 feet to finished subgrade
3 No boulder larger than 5 feet in its largest dimension
should be placed in the fill It may be necessary to reduce
the size of larger boulders or dispose of them off site
4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in the
proposed utility alignments to prevent difficulty in the
later excavation of the utility trenches
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibratory or
pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil type and capable of
producing the specified results in an expedient and efficient
manner The equipment shall be in good working condition fully
ballasted and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic and uniform
manner over the entire fill area to produce a uniformly
compacted fill
The Contractor shall be required to obtain the above specified
levels of compaction out to the finish slope of fill slopes
This may be achieved by either overbuilding the slope and
cutting back to the compacted core or by direct compaction of
the slope face with suitable equipment
Fill placed against previously graded cut or fill slopes shall
be properly keyed through topsoil or loose slope material into
firm material and the transition stripped of loose material
prior to fill placement Fill shall be placed and compacted
from the low side up
