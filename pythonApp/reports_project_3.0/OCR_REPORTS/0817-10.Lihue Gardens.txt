SUMMARY
The subsurface exploration disclosed
competent bearing soils at or near
the existing ground surface
Shallow foundations are recommended
for relatively light residential
structures An allowable bearing
value of 3500 p s f is recommended
The surface soils appear to have
low expansion potential and slab
On grade construction can be used
The text of this report should be
referred to for detailed recommen
dations and special design considera
tions
INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the
subsurface conditions at the site to provide recommenda
tions for the site grading and foundation design for the
proposed development
A grading plan was not available during our investigation
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located west of Kuhio Highway in Lihue
Kauai covering an area of approximately 15 acres
The site is bordered by undeveloped land similar in character
to the project site on the west and south commercial develop
ments on the east and Poinciana Street on the north Except
for a 20 to 30 foot deep gully with a 2 1 horizontal verti
cal slope which is located in the southern portion of the
site the area is fairly flat Surveyed elevations vary
from 172 to 212 feet During the field exploration the
site was covered with dense trees shrubs and grass except
for several dirt roads and trails throughout the site Also
scattered throughout the site was miscellaneous rubbish
consisting of abandoned cars broken concrete and metal
debris
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed development consists of the following
1 Multi family housing consisting of 211 one and two
bedroom units One and two story wood framed town
house buildings are anticipated
2 Commercial area consisting of 4 three and four story
wood framed structures a one story restaurant and
a 2 story parking structure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 3
3 Recreation area consisting of a one story recreation
center and an open park area in the existing drainage
way in the southern portion of the site
The final grading and foundation plans were not available
during our study
GEOLOGY
The project site is located in the Lihue Basin on the south
eastern corner of the single shield volcano that forms the
island of Kauai The Lihue Basin consists of an ancient
collapsed caldera which has been filled with post erosional
volcanics of the Koloa Series The Koloa Series volcanics
consisted of both pahoehoe and aa lava flows which cover
about half the surface of the eastern part of the island
and the entire Lihue Basin These flows were originally
flat and have been moderately eroded and extensive residual
soils have developed in the upper sections grading down to
basaltic rock at an unknown depth at this site

Soil Conditions
Based on the test borings the site is underlain by stiff
to hard residual soils consisting of reddish brown clayey
silt to depths of 3 to 13 feet yellowish brown clayey silt
to depths of 13 to 15 feet and red to gray and black mottled
clayey silt to the extent of the borings
Some fill may have been encountered in Boring Nos 1 and 2
near the southeastern portion of the site
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at 22 to 23 foot
depths during the field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface exploration disclosed competent bearing
soils at the site Shallow foundations bearing on the
stiff in situ soil will provide adequate support for the
proposed one to four story structures
Some surface fill is suspected near the top of the existing
drainageway Proofrolling with a heavy roller is recommended
in this area to attempt to locate possible soft pockets
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 5
Laboratory swell tests on the on site soils indicate low
expansion potential therefore slab on grade can be
designed without special provisions for expansive soil
conditions
To minimize deep footing design the buildings should be
set back about 5 feet away from the top of existing slopes
Foundations
For the proposed relatively light wood structures spread
or continuous footing foundation are recommended
An allowable bearing value of 3500 p s f is recommended
for footings bearing on the stiff in situ soil For
footings on compacted fill an allowable bearing value of
2500 p s f is recommended
On level areas the bottom of footings should be placed
about 18 inches below the finish grade Where the footings
are located near the top of slope they should be embedded
an additional amount such that there is a minimum 5 foot
horizontal set back distance between the outer edge of
footing and the slope face
Footings located near utility trenches should extend to
the bottom of the trench
Surface water should be diverted away from the building
foundations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 6
Site Grading
Minor grading is planned for most of the site Site filling
is anticipated near the existing drainageway
Prior to filling the site should be cleared of vegetation
and debris then scarified and recompacted to 90 of its
maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557
Fills should be placed in 9 inch loose horizontal lifts
compacted to the above specified density
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than 5 hori
zontal to 1 vertical should be keyed and benched into the
existing firm ground For details see Plate 2
Fill slopes should not be constructed at slopes steeper
than 2 horizontal to 1 vertical
Drainage benches should be provided for slopes having
a vertical height greater than 20 feet
Slab On Grade
To resist the downhill creep movement of the near surface
soil and to develop the full bearing capacity of the sub
soil the slab perimeter footing close to the top of slopes
should extend down a sufficient depth to obtain a minimum
horizontal set back of 5 feet from the slope face
GEOLABS HAWAII
W
Conventional slab on grade can be used on level areas
located 5 feet or more away from the tops of slopes
Slabs should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
course generally less than 3 4 inch and larger than
1 4 inch in size
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring
material a polyethylene moisture barrier should be
considered
Retaining Wall
A retaining wall may be used to raise the southern
portion of the proposed restaurant site
Guidelines for retaining wall design are as follows
1 The bottom of wall footing should be 2 feet below
the outside finish grades Also if the wall is
located on or near the top of a slope the footing
should extend down a sufficient depth such that
there is a minimum 10 foot horizontal setback
distance from the edge of footing to the slope
face
2 An allowable bearing value of 3500 p s f is
recommended for footings resting on the stiff
natural soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P
3 Well graded granular material such as base course
rock should be used as backfill material behind
the walls
4 The wall should be designed to resist a lateral
equivalent fluid pressure of 45 p c f Additional
load due to traffic or sloping surcharge should be
included where applicable
5 A friction factor of 0 4 can be utilized to determine
the sliding resistance of the wall foundation
6 A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent
the build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
Roadway Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the project
roads
Based on the laboratory CBR test on the on site soil the
preliminary pavement design is as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 9
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual
subgrade soil samples at the time of the roadway construc
tion and the above design section may be revised accordingly
if other subgrade soil conditions are encountered
Existing Cesspools
Existing cesspools may be located throughout the site If
encountered the cesspool locations should be flagged in
the field Where existing cesspool are located below or
adjacent to footings and slabs the sludge should be
removed and the cesspool filled with compacted granular
backfill The cesspool walls within 5 feet of the finish
grade should be removed
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility con
struction should be forwarded to the soil engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that
the intent of our recommendations is included in the
project plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soil engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement
to monitor the contractor s grading operations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 10
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soil engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soil engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections

FIELD EXPLORATION

by drilling 12 borings to depths of 16 5 to 31 5 feet below
the existing ground surface at the locations shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1

testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 11
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
depths
