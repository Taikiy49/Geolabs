PROJECT DESCRIPTION
In general the Maui Hyatt Vacation Club MHVC project site is off Nohea Kai Drive
in the Kaanapali area on the Island of Maui Hawaii Based on the drawings provided the
resort facility is approximately rectangular in shape with the project site encompassing
approximately 4 5 acres We understand the project consists of a 12 story building with a
finished floor elevation of about 5 0 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The layout of the new
timeshare building is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Based on Berkel Company s referenced report for the project the new hotel
tower structure will be supported on a deep foundation system consisting of 18 inch
diameter augered cast in place piles with an allowable compressive load capacity of up to
360 kips 180 tons per pile The project consisted of 5 indicator piles to be tested by
high strain dynamic testing and 319 production piles for a total of 324 ACIP piles for the
foundation support of the hotel tower The ACIP piles would extend through the soft
and or loose lagoonal deposits near the surface and derive support primarily from skin
friction within the loose to medium dense coralline sands encountered and end bearing on
the dense boulder deposits and or basalt rock formation at greater depths The termination
depth of each pile would be determined upon practical refusal at the bearing layer of the
dense boulder deposits and or basalt rock formation Typical pile lengths would range
from about 70 feet at the southern portion to about 95 feet towards the northern portion of
the site
The general contractor for the MHVC project was Nordic PCL Contractors Inc and
the ACIP pile installation subcontractor was Berkel Company Contractors Inc Berkel

STATIC PILE LOAD TEST
In order to evaluate the adequacy of the recommended design of the ACIP pile
foundation a static pile load test was conducted on a dedicated ACIP pile on April 23
2013 The pile load test was conducted on an 18 inch diameter ACIP pile with a length of
approximately 79 feet relative to the existing ground surface The location of the load test
pile is shown on the ACIP Test Pile Location Plan Plate 2
A series crane with a crane mounted hollow stem auger was
used to install the load test pile A Berkel manufactured grout pump with an average stroke
volume of 0 031 cubic yards per stroke was used to pump the grout into the system The
test pile was loaded in general accordance with ASTM D1143 Quick Load Test Method
The maximum test load on the pile was planned at 800 kips Results of the static pile load
test Maui Hyatt Static Load Test Data were transmitted to our office on
Based on the static pile load test results it appears the load test pile behaved like a
friction pile with nearly 100 percent of the maximum test load being resisted by adhesion
between the ACIP pile and the surrounding soils Based on our field observations and the
results of the static pile load test we confirmed that the recommended installation method
would be sufficient to achieve the design compressive load capacity of 180 tons per ACIP
pile with a factor of safety of at least 2 0

on a near continuous basis During the pile installation operation logs were maintained for
