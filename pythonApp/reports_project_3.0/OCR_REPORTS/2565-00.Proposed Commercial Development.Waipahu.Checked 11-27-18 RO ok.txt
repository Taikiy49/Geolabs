INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for one 1 of the proposed commercial buildings in the new Sam Sung
Commercial Development in Waipahu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
exploration was performed in accordance with our proposal dated August 21 1990
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2565 00 Page 2
This report summarizes the findings resulting from the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based on the
data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are intended for
design input and are not intended for construction estimation or bidding purposes
This report was prepared for Building E of the proposed commercial development
only The findings and recommendations presented herein should not be considered
applicable to any other building in the same development
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a new three 3 story steel framed structure for
various commercial uses Building E within the proposed commercial development
site in Waipahu Oahu Hawaii The proposed building is rectangular in shape
approximately 40 by 225 feet in plan dimension Exterior CMU wall and interior
concrete column construction for the proposed structure is currently contemplated
Concrete slab on grade will be utilized for the building floor Construction of new
utility lines and flexible asphalt pavements for the access roadway and parking areas
are anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the Building E project site in order to develop a generalized soil rock
data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent
to site grading pavement and foundation design of the project
The work was done in accordance with our proposal dated August 21 1990
The scope of work included the following items
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2565 00 Page 3
1 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the
project site and its vicinity
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual
observations of surficial soil conditions and select sites for borings
3 Drilling and sampling five 5 borings to depths ranging between 11 5
and 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the classification and engineering properties of
the materials encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the purpose
of developing site grading pavement and foundation design
recommendations
6 Preparation of this report presenting the findings and design
recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing are
presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is situated in the westerly end of the town of
Waipahu on the island of Oahu Hawaii Formerly the site was occupied by a
bowling alley and parking area
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2565 00 Page 4
Currently a used car lot occupies the building site The topography of the site
is generally level An existing 50 foot wide drainage channel is located along the
southwestern property boundary
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling five 5 borings to
depths ranging between 11 5 and 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered a three 3 to seven 7 foot thick surface
layer of fill material consisting of stiff brown clayey silt and silty clay with coral sand
and gravel Below the surface fill layer alluvium consisting of soft to stiff reddish
brown to dark brown clayey silt with pockets of gray clay were encountered to 21 5
foot depths the maximum depth drilled
The site is paved with about 1 5 inches of asphaltic concrete overlying 6 inches
of crushed coral and basalt base material
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field
exploration
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered are presented on the Boring
Logs Plates A 1 through A 5 Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples
obtained The test results are presented in Appendix B

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The project site is underlain by soft to stiff alluvial deposits below a three 3
to seven 7 foot thick layer of fill The current estimate of maximum column loads
is in the range of 270 kips Based on this value settlements of up to one and one
anticipated This amount of settlement generally is tolerable for steel framed
structures however it may result in some cracking in CMU construction Provisions
should be made in the structural detailing to accommodate the settlements or
substantial reduction of column loads should be considered
Foundation
Spread or continuous footing foundation bearing on the surface fil layer were
evaluated for the three 3 story steel framed building planned for the site
An allowable bearing value of 4000 p s f may be used for footings bearing on
the stiff surface fill or compacted fill The bearing value is for total dead plus live
loads and may be increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are
considered
For the estimated column loads of 270 kips estimated settlements are on the
order of inches
If the inch settlements are not tolerable a lighter type of structure or
shorter spans should be considered to reduce the building loads and the resulting
settlements For a reduced column loads of 100 kips the footing settlements are
estimated to be about 3 4 inch total
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 2565 00 Page 6
A minimum footing embedment of 24 inches below the finished grade is
recommended
The subgrade of all footings should be scarified and recompacted to a
minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method
D1557 78
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the
utility lines
The bottom of all footing excavations should be observed by the geotechnical
engineer prior to placement of the reinforcement to determine if adequate bearing
has been obtained and that loose soils are removed prior to concrete placement
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p c f per foot of depth This assumes that the soils around the
foundation is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be used Unless
covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 18 inches of soil
should be neglected
Site Grading
Minor site grading work is anticipated
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2565 00 Page 7
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should
be cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other deleterious materials These
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
The areas should then be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture
conditioned and recompacted to 90 of the maximum dry density as determined by
ASTM Test Method D1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered should be over
excavated down to firm subsoil and then replaced with compacted non expansive
select material
Based on the field exploration results most of the on site near surface soils
may be used as fill material
If required imported fill material should consist of non expansive select
granular material less than three 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20
passing the 200 sieve All imported fill material should be tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to transportation to the site
The fills should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined by
ASTM Test Method D1557 78
The site should be graded to drain water away from the foundations
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade construction is anticipated for the floor of the structure
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel should be provided below the slabs as cushion fill
The slab subgrade should be kept moist prior to concrete placement
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2565 00 Page 8
The thickened edge slab footings may bear on the very stiff natural soils or
compacted fill The thickened edges should be embedded a minimum of twelve 12
inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration an impervious moisture
barrier is recommended below the slabs One 1 inch of moist fine sand may be
placed above the plastic membrane to help concrete curing
Pavement Section
Asphaltic concrete pavements are anticipated for the parking area The
following preliminary pavement section may be used
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Moist Subgrade
CBR and density tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils
encountered during construction to verify the adequacy of the pavement section The
recommended section considers medium traffic and assumes that adequate drainage
will be provided in the paved areas
Utility Trenches
New utility lines are anticipated for the project A granular bedding consisting
of six 6 inches of ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel or 3B Fine is recommended under the
pipes
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2565 00 Page 9
Granular materials should be used for the trench backfill The backfill up to
about one foot above the pipes should be lightly tamped to reduce the possibility of
damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level one foot above the pipes
to the subgrade or finished grade should be placed in maximum 10 inch horizontal
lifts mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as
determined by ASTM Test Method D1557 78 to reduce future ground subsidence
All existing utility lines should be located and protected by the contractor
during construction
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction
This review is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation
recommendations given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer
cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during site grading and construction of the
foundations and grading This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that
subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such observations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2565 00 Page 10
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved
by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate
modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the
field boring may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re
evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field borings are approximate having been determined by
taping from the features shown on the Site Plan by Merritt T Sakata A LA
Elevations of the data collection points were not available during the preparation of
this report The physical location of the field data collection points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field data
collection points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such
may denote a gradual transition
