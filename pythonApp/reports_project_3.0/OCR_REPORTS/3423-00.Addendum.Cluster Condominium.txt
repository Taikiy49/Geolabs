Foundations
Based on the subsurface soil conditions at the project site we recommend that
shallow foundations consisting of isolated spread and or continuous footings bearing on a
minimum 2 foot thick compacted fill be used to support the new residential structures
planned at the project site An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per
square foot psf may be used for the design of foundations of the new structures bearing
on the minimum 2 foot thick compacted fill The compacted fill should extend beyond the
edges of the footing by a minimum of 2 feet The bearing value provided is for
those caused by wind or seismic forces
The 2 foot thick compacted fill generally would serve to improve the foundation
bearing conditions and to provide uniform support for the footings The existing fill
materials at the site will need to be over excavated in order to place the minimum 2 feet of
compacted fill below the footings Over excavation for the minimum 2 foot thick
compacted fill should extend beyond the edges of the footings a minimum of 2 feet Prior
to placement of the compacted fill the bottom of the footing over excavations should be
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction Soft and or loose materials
encountered at the bottom of the over excavated subgrade should be further
over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation should be
backfilled with compacted fill materials compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction
The compacted fill below the footings may consist of the re compacted on site soils
or imported select granular fill material such as crushed coral or basalt If the select
granular fill material is to be imported the material should be well graded from coarse to
fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension In addition the material
should also contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The
material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum
swell of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883
The minimum 2 foot thick compacted fill below the footings should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry
weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grades Footings constructed near tops of slopes or on sloping ground
conditions should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal setback
distance of 6 feet measured from the outside edge of the footings to the face of the slope
Footings located adjacent to planned or existing retaining walls should be embedded deep

Foundations located next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded
below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trench As an alternative the footings should extend to a depth as deep as the inverts of
the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for appreciable
foundation settlement
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 30 may be used for footings bearing on the compacted fills Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds
per square foot per foot of depth pcf The passive pressure resistance assumes that the
soils around the footings are well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the
passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
If the foundations are designed and constructed as recommended herein we
estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for footings bearing on
the compacted fills to be on the order of about 1 inch Differential settlements between
adjacent footings supported on the compacted fills should be on the order of about
0 5 inches or less
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative from
Geolabs prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the foundation
bearing conditions and the required embedment depths In addition the rock anchor
installation should also be observed by a representative from Geolabs
Slab On Grade Construction
We anticipate that slabs on grade will be required for the garage slabs for the
residential structure To reduce the potential for appreciable differential movement and
cracking of the slabs on grade resulting from soft spots in the near surface fill materials
we recommend that the subgrade soils below concrete slabs on grade be over excavated
and replaced with 2 feet of compacted fill The compacted fill below slabs on grade should
extend a minimum of 2 feet beyond the edges of the slab In addition we recommend that
the building slabs on grades be reinforced with reinforcing bars spaced at least 18 inches
in each direction in lieu of the standard welded wire fabric This recommendation is
provided to reduce the potential for structural distress due to differential movements of the
slabs due to the nature of the on site soils

The subgrade below the compacted fill should be scarified to a depth of about
8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to at least
90 percent relative compaction The compacted fill below the slabs may consist of the
re compacted on site soils or imported select granular fill material such as crushed coral
or basalt The minimum 2 foot thick compacted fill below the slabs should be
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction
Where the slabs will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as the garage slabs a
6 inch layer of aggregate subbase compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction is recommended in lieu of the typical gravel cushion fill layer The thickened
edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at least 18 inches below
the lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slab should be backfilled tightly
against the slab edges with on site soil and graded to divert water away from the slab to
reduce the potential for water ponding around the foundations and slabs
Design Review
Final drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to Geolabs for review and written comments prior to advertisement for bidding
This review is needed to evaluate conformance of the plans and specifications with the
intent of the foundation and earthwork recommendations provided herein If this review is
not made Geolabs cannot be responsible for misinterpretation of our recommendations
Consiruction Monitoring
Considering the uncertainty in the nature and distribution of the undocumented fill
materials at the site it is strongly recommended that Geolabs be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction of the project Items of
construction monitoring that are critical requiring Special Inspection include
over excavation and replacement of the undocumented fill below foundations and slabs
observation of subgrade preparation fill and backfill placement and foundation
construction
Other aspects of earthwork construction should also be observed by a
representative from Geolabs This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design
concepts specifications or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design
changes that may be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those
anticipated at the time this addendum letter was prepared The recommendations
provided in this addendum letter are contingent upon such observations If the actual
exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are different from those
assumed or considered in this addendum letter then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

Limitations
The findings and discussions submitted in this addendum letter are based in part
upon information obtained from the test pits and field borings from our field exploration in
1995 Variations of subsurface conditions between and beyond the test pits and borings
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the findings and discussions provided in this addendum letter
The locations of the field borings and test pits indicated in this report are
approximate having been estimated by taping and pacing from the features shown on the
site plan provided by Sato Associates Inc transmitted to our office in 1995 The
approximate elevations of test pits were estimated from the contour lines shown on the
topographic survey map prepared by M E Pacific Inc dated The physical
locations and elevations of the borings and test pits should be considered accurate only to
the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings and test
pits depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a
gradual transition As mentioned previously groundwater was not encountered in our
borings at the time of our exploration However it must be noted that groundwater level
changes may occur due to seasonal precipitation surface runoff and other factors
This addendum letter has been prepared for the exclusive use of Stanford S Carr
Development Corporation for specific application to the proposed Cluster Condominium
project at Drive in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical
engineering principles and practices No warranty is expressed or implied
This addendum letter has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the
architect and engineer in the design of the proposed project Therefore this addendum
letter may not contain sufficient data or the proper information to serve as the basis for
preparation of construction cost estimates A contractor wishing to bid on this project is
urged to retain a competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation of this
addendum letter and or in the performance of additional site specific exploration for bid
estimating purposes
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are commonly
encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as perched groundwater soft deposits
hard layers or loose fills may occur in localized areas and may require additional probing
or corrections in the field which may result in construction delays to attain a properly
constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to
accommodate these possible extra costs
