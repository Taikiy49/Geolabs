INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soils investigation performed
for the proposed Hale Lumi Subdivision at Waipio Oahu Hawaii The
general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project will include development of 9 house lots and an
access road Lumipolu Street Extension Substantial amount of cuts are
required to grade the site for the subdivision lots A general layout of
the project is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1523 00 Page 2
Based on the preliminary grading plan cuts of up to 15 to 30 feet
may be required at the lower portion of the slope in order to provide the
desired buildable area for the future houses Thus consideration is
required for steepening the existing slope and providing a retaining wall
along the northwestern portion of the site
House foundation loadings are anticipated to be light and typical
for single family structures
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation was to provide recommendations for
site grading and house foundation designs based on the soil information
obtained from borings drilled The borings were generally drilled and
sampled to depths of about 15 to 25 feet below the existing ground at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Soil sampling and borings were performed under the observation of
one of our engineers The results of our field exploration and
laboratory tests which we used in the engineering analysis to formulate
our findings and recommendations are presented in the appendices of this
report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed development site is located on the western slope of
Panakauahi Gulch just below and to the east of the existing Seaview and
Crestview Subdivisions and north of the end of Lumipolu Street in Waipio
Oahu Hawaii A dirt road along the eastern site boundary provides
access into the area The eastern side below the road slopes steeply
down to Panakauahi Gulch below At the time of our exploration the site
was overgrown with Haole Koa bushes and other trees A trail was cleared
by a bulidozer through the bushes to the proposed boring locations
At the central portion of the site numerous boulders were observed
on the ground surface in the dozer excavations for the trail on the slope
that rose up towards Seaview Subdivision There is an outcrop of basalt
rock ledge along the toe of the slope near Boring 5
The northwest portion of the site was occupied by temporary wooden
sheds and fences for chickens Also some small rubbish piles and
discarded appliances etc were noted along the upper westerly sides of
the site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The site is located in a deeply weathered and eroded lower gentle
slope of the Koolau Volcano The weathering has altered the basalt rocks
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1523 00 Page 4
to an iron rich mass of reddish brown colored soils with spheroidally
weathered boulders Streams have cut deep gullies with steep side slopes
into the residual soils The site is located on one such stream cut
gully The site soils consist of stiff silts with varying amount of
clays and sands
Soil Conditions
Soil and boulders up to 5 feet in diameter cover much of the central
part of the project site Also some concrete slabs and blocks were
noted along the toe of the slope near Boring 3
Underlying the overgrowth and buried materials the natural soils
consisted mainly of stiff to very stiff low to moderately expansive silty
clays with boulders Rock formation was encountered within about 10 foot
depth in the lower portions of the site
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings which were drilled to
depths of about 25 feet below the ground surface
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our findings disclosed that the on site soils generally consist of
stiff residual soils with boulders grading to weathered rock formation

Based on the field exploration we believe that the proposed slope
cuts are suitable for the site It appears that rock formation may be
encountered at or near the finished grade along the proposed 4 1 H V
slope area where the house construction is planned at Lots 3 through 9
Residual soil is anticipated at the finished grades of Lots 1 and 2
It should be noted that if boulders are exposed on the slopes above
the western side of the development area they may impose a potential for
rockfall To minimize the rockfall hazard exposed boulders in the slope
should be removed
Site Grading
Clearing Grubding and Stripping

Existing vegetation should be cleared and stripped Tree roots in

Any localized sott or loose pocket encountered during clearing and

The stripped surface soil containing organic matter may be reused as

In addition the large boulders concrete debris and other

original ground surface cleaned of decayed vegetation to firm soils
The stripping operation should be continuously monitored by the
soils engineer to verify removal of unsuitable materials
Surface Preparation
a
After clearing and grubbing the area to receive fill should be
scarified at least 6 inches below the stripped grade moisture
conditioned and recompacted to 90 of the maximum density as
determined by the ASIM D 1557 78 test Any soft or weak areas
disclosed during this recompaction should be over excavated removed
and then backfilled with engineered fill
Monitoring of the surface preparation by a soils engineer is
particularly important especially to identify soft areas or
unsuitable materials
Slope Construction
Based on our findings it appears that the required cut slope for
the development would be partly cut in rock and partly in soil A
cut slope ratio of 2 1 H V is recommended for slope cuts in soil
Where firm weathered rock is encountered slope cut ratios up to 1 1
H V may be used

Fill slope should not be steeper than 2 1 H V
The on site soil is clay and silt with some sand and easily
erodable Therefore the slopes should be protected from run off
water to reduce soil erosion Where practicable interceptor
ditches or swales should be provided at the top of slopes to direct
water away from the slope face
Slope benches are recommended where the vertical height of slope
exceeds 20 feet This height may be increased to 30 feet where
weathered rock is encountered in the slope face
To provide adequate compaction of fill slopes the slopes should
first be over filled with compacted material and then trimmed back
to the design grade

Spread Footings

We believe if the site grading procedures are performed in
accordance with the recommendations presented herein spread footing
foundations bearing on the natural soil or compacted fill may be
used to support the proposed structures
For footings on level area and a minimum of 5 feet away from the top
of slope they should be placed at a minimum depth of 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent grade or embedded 6 inches into rock
formation The spread footing may be designed using a net allowable
for total of dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third
for wind or seismic loads
Anticipated settlements are small and probably less than one half of
an inch if the footings are properly constructed in accordance with
the above details

Fill Materials

have CBR values of 8 or greater wnen compacted to 95 of the
