In accordance with our proposal of we have made a
preliminary study of the subsurface soil conditions at the above
referenced site in order to evaluate the rippability or excavation
difficulty anticipated for the proposed gymnasium site grading The
study consisted of drilling two borings within the proposed cut area
site reconnaissance and a review of available geologic data This
report summarizes our observations and recommendations
Proposed Construction
Based on the preliminary site plans made available to us the
proposed gyumasium will be approximately 95 by 140 feet in plan
dimensions

Cuts up to 22 feet below the existing ground surface are planned to

Site Conditions
The subject site is located within the Hawaii School For Girls
campus at La Pietra Circle Waikiki Oahu Hawaii The general location
of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate
1
The site is bounded to the east and south by the foothills of Leahi
Crater Diamond Head and to the west and north by the existing school
campus
A relatively level pad about 60 feet wide is situated along the
back portion of the site and parallel to the southeastern boundary The
ground surface then slopes downward toward the front northwest of the
site at ratios of 2 1 to 8 1 H V and continues along a level playing
field and basketball court The existing ground surface elevation
ranges from 89 feet at the front of the site to 107 feet toward the rear
Ground cover consisted of well maintained lawn grass and trees
along the slope During our field exploration an abandoned steel
ferris wheel structure with slab on grade foundation was noted in the
eastern corner of the site

Geology
After the Koolau Volcanic Series that built up the northeastern
Side of Oahu ceased about 2 2 to 2 5 million years ago MacDonald
Abbott 1970 the coastal plain developed along the southern shores of
Oahu during the Pleistocene Epoch from a complex series of erosional and
depositional processes
Numerous glacial eustatic sea level variations caused the emergence
and submergence of the land mass which produced coral reef deposits at
various levels both above and below the present sea level
Post erosional volcanic activity renewed about 0 1 to 0 5 million
years ago with more than 30 separate eruptions which poured lava flows
over the eroded topography and out onto the fringing reef These
constitute the Honolulu Volcanic Series which created some of the best
known landmarks such as Leahi Crater Diamond Head
Leahi Crater is a tuff cone formed by hydromagmatic explosions It
consists largely of consolidated ash tuff but can contain occasional
blocks of Koolau basalt and numerous fragments of coral limestone torn
from the underlying reef
In more recent years erosion has cut shallow valleys into the
crater face and deposited thin surface soil layers at the base

Subsurface Conditions
Two borings were drilled with 4 inch diameter continuous flight
augers to depths of 25 4 to 26 0 feet below the existing grade at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered weathered tuff consisting of
dense moderately cemented sandy silt which grades to silty sand with
increasing depth
Detailed description of the soils encountered in the borings are
presented on the attached Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2
No groundwater was encountered in the borings
Discussion and Recommendations
The proposed site grading will involve cutting about 22 feet below
the existing ground surface to match the adjacent athletic fields and
facilities
In general weathered tuff consisting of moderately cemented dense
silty sand was encountered at the proposed finished elevation Based on
a review of available geologic data the method of drilling and sampling
techniques used it is our opinion that the underlying weathered tuff
may be excavated with conventional site grading equipment such as a D 9
bulldozer or equivalent Some localized hard zones or large boulders
may require special techniques such as the use of a hoeram

In general the borings and laboratory testing indicate that the
underlying weathered tuff can provide suitable bearing for the proposed
structure provided the foundations are properly designed
Laboratory swell tests performed on selected samples indicate that
this material generally has a low swelling potential
