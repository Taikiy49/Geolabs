foundation investigation for the above subject project The approximate
location of the proposed site and its vicinity is shown on the Project

PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed center consists of four one story commercial
buildings namely A B C BD constructed along a long narrow site
W Page 2
Building A is L shaped and approximately 125 by 77 feet in plan
Buildings B and C are approximately 35 by 75 feet in plan Building D is
connected with Building C and is approximately 64 by 96 feet in plan
The buildings will be of steel frame supported by pipe columns and
pilasters construction CMU walls and 4 inch on grade concrete slabs are
also planned
A parking lot paved with asphaltic concrete and two loading zones
are planned for the area fronting the buildings
A general layout of the center is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the subsurface
soil and water conditions at the site to provide soil data for the
design of the structural foundations and on grade pavements Particular
attention would be paid to locate the underlying coral reef formation and
coral cavities cr soft pockets within the dense layer near the ground
surface as indicated by previous experience

were laboratory testing of the samples obtained engineering evaluation
of the field and laboratory data development of design recommendations
and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is a 40 000 square foot parcel located on the
mauka side of South King Street in the University Moiliili area adjacent
to the existing University Villa Condominium building at its northern
side and two huge spherical gas storage tanks at its rear The long
narrow site is presently occupied by numerous one story structures
including a gas station and auto mechanic shop Many of the one story
structures were already deserted
Along the property limit at the condominium side and the rear the
site is partially lined by two tile walls of various heights The wall
at the condominium side about 4 to 7 feet high connects with the
existing adjacent buildng at the front extends all along the property
line and runs parallel to a nearby series of tall trees in the adjacent
GEOLABS HAWAII

the property line and separates the site from the storage tank areas
The southern property line runs along existing adjacent buildings and a

The site is bisected by a 10 foot wide sewer easement running in an
east west direction Another 10 foot wide sewer easement runs adjacent
and parallel to the Ewa property line next to the University Villa
Condominium and it intersects with the first one Generally the site is
relatively flat and the ground slopes down slightly toward the front

used as dumping areas where rubbish and miscellaneous fill with abandoned
vehicles broken household appliances and furnitures concrete debris and
wooden planks etc were encountered during cur exploration
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling 3
borings to depths of 5 5 to 15 feet below the existing ground surface at
the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings disclosed that the site was mantled by a thin layer of
fill overlying a coral reef formation The fill materials varied from
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 5
medium stiff to stiff brown and reddish brown clayey silt with gravel
yellowish brown and tannish brown sandy silt and loose to dense brown
tannish brown and dark brown silty sand and gravel The coral reef
formation was generally very dense cemented tan coral sand and gravel
near its surface but Boring and 4 indicate that it may also
contain many soft and open areas near its water level as indicated by
its lower blow counts denoting variability in consistency Generally
these cavities might be filled with loose coral debris and their
locations would be quite variable and therefore difficult to delineate
without sufficient number of borings or probings
The depth to the top of the coral layer varied from approximately
1 0 feet in the western portion of the site to about 6 0 feet in the
eastern portion
Groundwater level was monitored during our exploration and was
found to be quite stable The cbhserved water levels varied from 8 5 to
10 feet below the ground surface
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
General
Generally our findings indicate that the site is predominantly
mantled with a thin layer of clayey silt to silty sand fill materials of
GEOLABS HAWAI
W Page 6
variable thickness and consistency underlain by dense coral formation
Some coral cavities or soft pockets may be present within and below the
upper dense coral layer near the ground surface
We believe that the proposed buildings may be supported by spread
footings over the dense coral formation or recompacted on site fill
materials Whether the presence of coral cavities or soft pockets are
located beneath individual foundations should be checked by probe
Grilling at intervals below the footing bottom to a minimum depth of four
4 feet
Our laboratory tests indicate that the near surface brown clayey
silt at Boring 5 is moderately expansive while the reddish brown clayey
silt at Boring 6 is only low to moderately expansive It is difficult to
predict the extent of the moderately expansive silty soil at this time
with the limited number of borings and sampling The expansive soil may
only be localized around the Boring 5 area at Building B We recommend
that the extent of this expansive clay should be verified during
construction by the soils engineer Therefore some contingent funding
should be provided in the construction budget for the removal of this
expansive soil if they are encountered under the slab on grade areas

Spread Footing Foundation
Building A Borings 6 and 7 indicate the existence of an about
6 0 foot thick fill layer at the area of the proposed building
The fill materials vary from low to moderately expansive medium
stiff clayey silt to poorly compacted loose silty sand
Due to the relatively large thickness of the fill we recommend
that the footings be placed directly on recompacted on site fill
materials with a minimum footing embedment depth of 18 inches We
recommend the spread or continuous footings be designed for an
allowable soil bearing pressure of 2500 p s f The bearing may be
increased by one third when temporary wind or seismic loads are
considered
Prior to placement of the footings the subgrade soils below the
footing bottom should be overexcavated a minimum of 12 inches
it should then be scarified another 6 inches below this level
moisture conditioned to a moisture content of 3 to 5 percentage
point wet of the optimum moisture content and recompacted to 95 of
the maximum density as established by the ASTM D 1557 78 test
method Any soft spots encountered should be removed to firm
material and replaced with select on site or imported granular soil
and compacted to the standard mentioned above The 12 inch
GEOLABS HAWAII

backfill below the footing bottom should be in 6 inch compacted
lifts to 95 compaction up to the footing bottom elevation The
backfill under the footing should consist of select on site
materials which are free of organic matter generally less than 3
inches in size and form a dense stable soil mass when compacted
For the footings bearing on the above specified recompacted
backfill an allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for
footing embedded a minimum depth of 18 inches
Building B Boring 5 indicates that the area of the proposed
building may also be underlain by a 5 foot thick fill consisting of

We recommend that the footings be placed directly on a 12 inch

The overexcavation and recompaction procedures indicated above for
Building A also apply for the Building B site
An allowable bearing value up to 2500 p s f is recommended for the
footings embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the outside ground
surface elevation and on the recompacted backfill

Site Preparation
Prior tc placing the on grade concrete slabs for the buildings
and the asphaltic concrete pavement for the parking lot the existing
paved area unpaved natural ground should be removed cleared of
vegetation rubbish debris and other unsuitable materials and the
resulting excavation be backfilled with compacted select borrow or
on site material Particular attention should be paid towards the
planting and dumping areas
The existing on site fill materials except for the brown and
reddish brown clayey silt may be used as a source of backfill Our
laboratory test results indicate that these silty to sandy fill materials
generally have low expansion potential
Any imported fill material should consist of selected non
expansive silty sandy or granular materials less than 3 inches in size
with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
GEOLABS HAWAI
W Page 11
Special provisions of site grading should be made for Buildings A
B if the moderately expansive brown clayey silt as indicated in Boring 5
is verified to exist all across the building areas The backfill within
the top 12 inches minimum should then consist of either compacted
select existing fill materials excluding the clayey silt soil or
non expansive import fill materials such as select borrow crusher waste
or coral as described above If the expansive soil is encountered only
as soft spots or pockets they should be removed to firm and
non expansive material and replaced with compacted select on site or
imported granular soils as described above under the direction of the
soils engineer
We recommend that the subgrade soils be scarified first and then
moisture conditioned to within 3 percentage points on the wet side of the
optimum and recompacted to at least 90 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum
density

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems should be installed
around the buildings and that gutter water should be diverted far away
from the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving
or less of strength

design should be made
