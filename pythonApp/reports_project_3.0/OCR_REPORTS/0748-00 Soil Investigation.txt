INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to evaluate the
subsurface conditions at the project drainageway areas
and to provide soil data for the design and construction
of the proposed fill along these areas in the proposed
Kaunakakai Unit IV Subdivision at Kaunakakai Molokai
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in Kaunakakai on the south
side of Molokai above the town of Kaunakakat The entire
project area has a rough triangular configuration in plan
view and covers approximately 25 acres
The northeast mauka and southeast sides of the project
site are bordered by undeveloped land covered with grass
brush and Kiawe trees The northwest property line runs
along a drainageway beyond which lies residential lots
along Kaiwi Street The southwest makai side is
bordered by restdential lots along Ala Malama Drive
Access into the site is by way of Ala Malama Drive or
Kikipua Street There were no existing structures on
the project site during the time of the soil investi
gation but utility trench digging construction was being
done at the roadway areas
The site generally slopes down in a southwest direction
at about a 5 to 10 percent gradient and ground elevations
ranged from approximately 50 to 130 feet
The most prominent surface features of the project site
are the three drainageways that havea northeast to
southwest alignment and comprises the primary areas of
interest for this soil investigation For convenience
and clarity the drainageways have been separated into

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Concentrated boulders were noted in the major drainageway
The loose surface boulders should be removed prior to fill
placement over the drainageway areas
The existing loose boulderly fill already placed at Area
No 2 drainageways should be removed and backfilled with
compacted fill layers
Laboratory swell test indicated that the on site surface
silty soil at its present dry moisture condition will
exibit low to moderate expansion potential Slab on grade
bearing on the above silty material should be designed for
moderate expansion potential subgrade soil condition
Earthwork and Grading
In general no cut is anticipated for the proposed subdivision
The fill will mainly be placed over the drainageway areas
Prior to filling the lowlying areas the site should be
Cleared of surface vegetation debris and loose boulders
The ground should then be scarified moisture conditioned
and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density established
by ASTM D 1557
Additional fill should be placed in 10 inch loose horizontal
lifts and compacted to the above specified density

Foundation
During the investigation it was noted that the surface
soils at the project site were generally dry and loose
Medium stiff soil was encountered few inches to about
a foot in depth below the existing ground
Spread and wall footing foundation may be utilized if
they bear on the stiff on site residual soil or on
properly compacted fill
For the light structural load anticipated from this
project an allowable bearing pressure of 3 000 pounds
per square foot may be used for the footing on stiff
on site soil or upon the compacted fill For footing
in limestone or weathered basalt the bearing value may
be increased to 6 000 pounds per square foot

Slab On Grade
The laboratory swell test on the near surface clayey
soil sample indicated a 4 2 swell from a dry to saturated
moisture condition The clayey soil is generally considered
to be of low to medium expansion potential
For slab on grade on the medium expansion potential soil
deeper perimeter footings are recommended For construction
details refer to Plates 2 and 3
For slab on grade on limestone or basalt formation non
expansion potential soil conditions can be considered
To provide uniform support a 4 inch base course layer
is recommended below the concrete siab and compacted to
95 of its maximum dry density Prior to placing the
base course layer the subgrade clayey soil should be
wetted to above its optimum moisture content and compacted
to a minimum of 90 compaction
