PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to stabilize the landslide in the existing open park between Mona
Street and Hind luka Drive in the Aina Haina area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 This area is an old slide zone and exhibits signs of on going movements Cracks
and depressions are visible in the pavements along the lower side of Mona Street The
locations of the cracks are shown on the attached Crack Location Plan Plate 3
We understand that it is desired to stabilize the on going landslide prior to
construction of improvements to the existing park between the two streets Therefore it is
critical to evaluate the characteristics of the slide particularly the depth of movement and
lateral limits Once the depth and limits of the slide are determined the stabilization
structures can be designed
Our subsurface investigation program consisted of drilling and sampling three deep
borings to depths of about 100 feet below the existing ground surface and installing slope
inclinometers in each boring to monitor soil lateral movement along the depth We believe
that the top 25 feet is a critical zone for ground water fluctuation Based on our previous
experiences of land sliding stabilization one of alternative of stabilization options is the
installation of a 25 foot deep drainage trench to reduce the sliding wedge driving force
from the excess pore pressure To monitor the groundwater fluctuation within the 25 foot

FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program at the project site consisted of drilling and sampling
three borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 extending to depths of
approximately 100 feet below the existing ground surface These three borings were used
for the purpose of installing slope inclinometers Two additional borings were also drilled
for the purpose of installing piezometers These two borings designated as P 1 and P 2
were drilled to depths of about 25 feet below the ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings drilled are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous
flight augers and rotary coring tools The materials encountered in the borings were
classified by visual and textural examination in the field by our engineer who monitored
the drilling operations on a near continuous basis Soils were classified in general
conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same
hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third
6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of consolidated materials encountered at the site were obtained by
using diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard
Practice D 2113 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary
drilling method that uses a hollow bit to cut into the consolidated materials The
consolidated material left in the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for
examination and description

LABORATORY TESTS
Selected soil samples from the field exploration were tested in the laboratory with
an emphasis on evaluating the moisture density the characteristics of shear strength and
aiding in soil classification The following laboratory tests were performed on selected soil
samples obtained from the field exploration
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected samples
of the soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The
results of the tests are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1
One direct shear test ASTM D 3080 was performed on a selected sample of the
soils to evaluate the shear strength characteristics Results of the direct shear test are
presented on Plate B 2
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
In general our field exploration disclosed that the project site is underlain by stiff to
very stiff surface fill material consisting of clayey soil extending to depths of 3 to 7 feet
below the ground surface The surface fill material was underlain by colluvium deposit
consisting of a clay matrix with boulder cobble and gravel The very stiff and dense
colluvium material extended to the maximum depth drilled of approximately 100 feet below
the ground surface Groundwater was encountered at depths varying from 4 to 27 feet
below the ground surface during our field exploration It should be noted that groundwater
levels are expected to vary with seasonal rainfall storm surge conditions time of the year
and other factors

INSTALLATION OF GEOTECHNICAL INSTRUMENTATIONS
Instrumentation was installed in the drilled borings to allow the continued monitoring
on a real time basis Two types of instrumentation were installed in the borings
Slope inclinometers manufactured by Slope Indicator Company were used in this
project The inclinometer casings made of ABS semi rigid plastic with an outside
diameter of 2 75 inches were installed in Boring Nos 1 through 3 to depths of 100 feet
The purpose of the inclinometer casing is to allow the lowering of the slope inclinometer
device into the hole to measure the lateral deflection of the soil at various depths in order
to determine where the earth movements are taking place as well as how much
movements have occurred These inclinometers will be read at approximately one month
intervals to measure the rate of ground movement and to monitor the effects of heavy rain
in relations to movement
Electrical vibrating wire piezometers manufactured by Geokon Inc were installed
in the borings P 1 and P 2 to measure the fluctuation of groundwater levels in response
to weather conditions Generally the piezometers were installed at two different depths
within the boreholes in order to intercept perched water levels The piezometers will
automatically record the water levels at set intervals of time by a datalogger device also
manufactured by Geokon Inc This continuous record allows us to observe the
fluctuation of water levels with respect to time and weather conditions
