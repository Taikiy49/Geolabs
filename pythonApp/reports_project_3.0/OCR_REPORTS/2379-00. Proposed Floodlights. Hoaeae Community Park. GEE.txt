INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
evaluation for the foundation design of the proposed new floodlights at
the Hoaeae Community Park The general location of the site is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that the project involves the installation of
six 6 floodlights at the baseball field at the park The floodlights
will be on about 60 foot high poles The design lateral loads to which
the poles will be subjected was not available at the time of this report
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our evaluation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil data for the foundation design of
the proposed floodlight poles
The scope of our investigation included drilling and sampling four
4 borings to depths of 15 1 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The results of our field exploration laboratory testing data
and engineering analyses are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at Hoaeae Community Park in the Village
Park Development at The park is located along the
east side of Ka aholo Street
At present four 4 tennis courts two 2 volleyball courts two
2 basketball courts and a softball field are part of the park complex
The floodlight pole sites are graded level and grassed
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Four 4 borings were drilled at the site to depths of 15 1 to 16 5
feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2

In general the borings encountered a surface layer of residual soil
about 5 0 to 9 5 feet thick consisting of hard to very stiff clayey silt

The residual surface soil layer was not encountered in Boring B 3

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATTONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered competent soil conditions
consisting primarily of hard to very stiff clayey silt deposits and very
dense weathered basalt deposits
For the support of the floodlights drilled pier foundations are
planned
Pier Foundation
Based on the soil data from the borings and the anticipated lateral
loads drilled pier foundation may be used as foundation support for the
new floodlights
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2379 00 Page 4
For the passive earth pressure resistance against the side of the
pier an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot of footing depth
may be used
The resistance in the upper 18 inches below the ground surface should
be ignored in the foundation design The coefficient of friction may be
taken as 0 4 at the base and the sides of the piers
An allowable bearing capacity up to 3 000 p s f may be used and may
be increased by one third 1 3 for wind and seismic loading
The vertical compressive load on the pole is anticipated to be
relatively light and may be supported by the frictional resistance between
the shaft of the pier and the subsoils
Since cobbles and boulders were encountered near the surface in some
of the borings hard drilling should be anticipated during caisson
drilling
Since the pier would be subjected to repeated wind loads a critical
consideration would be to limit the permanent lateral deflections of the
pier This may be achieved by sufficient embedment of the pier
The bottom of the caisson should be cleaned of loose materials The
caisson excavations should be inspected by a soils engineer prior to
placing concrete or reinforcing steel
The concrete mix should be designed to minimize shrinkage in order to
maintain high frictional contact between the soil and pier concrete The
concrete should also be placed in a suitable manner to avoid segregation
of the aggregates

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review is
not made the geotechnical engineer can not be responsible for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during the excavation and
foundation construction phases of the work This is to observe compliance
with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow
design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from the field data points such as
borings probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report

The locations of the field data points were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the topographic survey Elevations of the data
points were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation
points on the topographic survey map The physical location and elevation
of the field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between field data
points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuations
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling four
4 borings to depths of 15 1 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the boring were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hanmer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
