PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to
explore the subsurface goil conditions at the site in order
to provide soil data for use in the design of the site
grading for the proposed subdivision development
GEOLABS HAWAII
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analyses that formulated
our conelusions and recommendations are presented in the
appendices of this report The approximate boring locations
are shown on the Boring Location Plan Plate 1 and the logs
of borings are presented in Appendix A
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated at the eastern mauka end of
Halawa Heights Road and extends along the northern side of
the road
The general area is situated on a mountain ridge The
ground surface generally slopes down toward the north at
ratios of about 7 1 H V near Halawa Heights Road and
increasing to about 2 1 H V near the edge of the ridge
There is an existing residence adjacent to the terminus of
Halawa Heights Road The road from the terminus of Halawa
Heights Road into the proposed site is presently a private
road
GEOLABS HAWAII
The area surrounding the house is covered with a lawn trees
and shrubbery The remaining portions are covered with high
Srass bushes and tall trees There are some coconut guava
and passion fruit trees on the site
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
nner er TR BA ON
The proposed plan is to clear and grade the site for 2
single family and duplex residential lots and a 32 foot wide
roadway into the site
The preliminary grading plan indicates that most of the
grading will be concentrated in the southeastern portion of
the site and along the access road Most of the lots will
not be graded
Cuts up to about 10 feet and fill generally less than 5
feet are planned
A short retaining wall less than 10 feet high is planned
along the southern side of the extension of Halawa Heights
Road
A second retaining wall about 10 feet high is planned
along a portion of the eastern boundary

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration encountered very stiff to hard resi
dual material consisting of reddish brown and brown silty
clays Laboratory tests indicate that these soils generally
have low swelling potential
In general slab on grade construction may be utilized on
level areas away from the tops of slopes Post and beam
construction is recommended for sloping lots

Site Preparation
Prior to filling the site should he cleared of
boulders vegetation rubbish and other dele
terious material

The ground should then be scarified to a minimum
depth of 6 inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and then compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 70
test method
Filling and Compaction Requirements
After the above site preparation the fill should be
moistened to above optimum moisture content placed
in 10 inch maximum loose lifts and compacted to obtain
a minimum 904 of its maximum dry density established by
the above ASTM test designation
Laboratory soil tests indicate that the on site soils
have low swelling potential therefore this material
may be used for the fill
Slopes
Cut and fill slopes generally less than 10 feet in
vertical height are anticipated
GEOLABS HAWAII

Fill slope ratio of 2 1 H V are recommended
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling
then cutting back to the design slope to obtain a
well compacted slope face
into the stiff residual soil
Surface water should be diverted away from the top of
the slope and slope planting utilized as soon as
practicable to reduce erosion
Utility Trenches
Proper backfilling of all the trenches is important to mini
mize surface ground settlement along the utility align
ments On site material generally less than 6 inches in
size may be utilized as backfill All backfill should be
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density as determined by
ASTM D 1557 Most of the on site material is fine grained
and would not be suitable for compaction by water jetting or
ponding and therefore this would require mechanical
compaction

House Foundations
The lots along the northern and eastern portions of the site
have relatively steep slopes Therefore post and beam con
struction utilizing deeper spread footing or pier type foun
dations are recommended for the houses on these lots
On relatively level areas slab on grade construction can be
utilized
To minimize the effects of slope creep and erosion slabs
should be set back a minimum distance of 5 feet away from
the tops of slopes Also post footings located on sloping
ground or close to the tops of slopes should be embedded a
sufficient depth to obtain a minimum horizontal set back
distance of 5 feet measured from the outer edge of the
footing to the Slope face
All footings should bear on the stiff natural soil
For the light loads anticipated an allowable bearing pres
sure of 2500 p s f can be used for footings resting on very
stiff in situ soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
Page 8
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should
extend to the bottom of the utility trench or the trench
backfill should be well compacted to reduce the footing
settlement due to settlement of the backfill
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of com
pacted base course The thickened edge around the slab
on grade should extend a minimum of 12 inches below the
adjacent ground surface
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
a polyethylene moisture barrier should be considered
The slab subgrade soils should be kept moist and not be
allowed to dry out prior to concrete placement
The lots should be graded to drain water away from the house
foundations
Retaining Wall

Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the project

STANDARD GRADING SPECIFICATIONS
PROPOSED NORTHRIDGE SUBDIVISION
The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing
preparation of fill areas placement and compaction of fill
and removal and backfilling of underground structures These
specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded
by specific requirements or recommendations contained within
the soils report
I Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of
trash debris organic matter and all other deleterious
materials These materials shall be removed and disposed
offsite
II Preparation of Fill Areas
A Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose compe
tent natural soils Localized soft pockets shall be
undercut and backfilled with compacted select mate
rials
B All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the
soils engineer prior to the placement of any fill
C After approval the surface shall be scarified to a
depth of six inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted as herein specified
IIl
IV
2
D Where the existing surface exceeds a slope of 5
horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 the ground surface
shall be keyed and benched into competent soil to
bond the fill to the existing ground surface
E Subsurface drains may be required as directed by the
soils engineer where springs or zones of seepage are
encountered
Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or
approved import material Import material shall be
tested and approved prior to transportation to the site
Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent organic
matter and other deleterious material and shall not
contain rocks or lumps in excess of eight inches 8
in diameter
Placement and Compaction of Fill
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which
when compacted will not exceed ten inches 10 in
thickness Each layer shall be spread uniformly and
blade mixed to attain uniformity of material and
water content within each layer
GEOLABS HAWAII
3
Rock fragments or boulders less than eight inches
8 in diameter may be utilized provided they are
not concentrated in any one area and that there is
sufficient fine grained material to fill the voids
between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter
shall be removed from the fill unless specifically
approved for placement in the fill by the soils
engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90
percent of the maximum dry density established by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 unless otherwise
specified in the soils report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibra
tory or pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil
type and capable of producing the specified results
in an expedient and efficient manner The equipment
shall be in good working condition fully ballasted
and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic
and uniform manner over the entire fill area to pro
duce a uniformly compacted fill
