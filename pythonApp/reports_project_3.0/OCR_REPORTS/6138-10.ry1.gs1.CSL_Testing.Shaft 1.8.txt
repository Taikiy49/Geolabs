No. 1.8 installed for the above project. Based on our field observations and the CSL test
results, the following recommendations are presented for your review and consideration.

CSL testing on Shaft No. 1.8 installed for the project. We performed CSL testing on
December 27, 2023 for Shaft No. 1.8 installed for the above project.

on December 21, 2023. The approximate location of the shaft is presented on the Site
Plan, Plate 1. Four 2-inch diameter steel CSL access tubes were installed along with the
reinforcing cage. Details of the drilled shaft as-built conditions are summarized in the

The average apparent sonic wave velocity can be determined from the CSL test 
results by dividing the average tube distances by the average first arrival times for each 
tube combination in a drilled shaft. It should be noted that the average apparent sonic 
wave velocity computed in this manner is not an absolute number and should be used with 
caution because the actual tube distances below the top of the drilled shaft are not known 
accurately. 
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
A summary of the CSL test results, including the tested concrete length, computed 
apparent sonic wave velocity, and comments on the concrete condition, are presented on 
Plate 2. The CSL test results for the drilled shafts tested are summarized on Plate 3. The 
individual CSL test plots for each tested tube combination (profile) and a plan showing the 
tube locations are presented on Plates 4.1 and 4.2. 
Based on the CSL test results, the increased first arrival times (FATs) were 
generally less than 10%, corresponding to a calculated wave velocity reduction of less 
than 10%, and the wave energy was relatively uniform in all the profiles for Shaft No. 1.8 
except for the areas within the profile as described below. It should be noted that changes 
in the relative energy can sometimes indicate defects depending on the degree of the 
reduction and the associated increase in FAT. 
Profile 1-2: FAT increases up to 17.9 percent from approximately 12.8 to 
13.0 feet below the top of concrete. 
Profile 1-3: FAT increases up to 16.1 percent from approximately 10.3 to 
11.4 feet below the top of concrete. 
Profile 1-3: FAT increases up to 37.4 percent from approximately 12.6 to 
13.1 feet below the top of concrete. 
Profile 1-3: FAT increases up to 24.4 percent from approximately 14.1 to 
14.8 feet below the top of concrete. 
Profile 2-3: FAT increases up to 20.0 percent from approximately 11.6 to 
12.1 feet and from approximately 15.8 to 16.9 feet below the top of concrete. 
Profile 2-4: FAT increases up to 27.5 percent from approximately 19.1 to 
19.5 feet below the top of concrete. 
The average apparent sonic wave velocities for the in-placed concrete for Shaft 1.8 
ranged from approximately 10,936 to 13,368 feet per second. We understand that during 
the concrete operations, the drilled shaft consisted of an overpour extending to the road 
surface and concrete chipping will need to be performed down to the shaft cutoff elevation
