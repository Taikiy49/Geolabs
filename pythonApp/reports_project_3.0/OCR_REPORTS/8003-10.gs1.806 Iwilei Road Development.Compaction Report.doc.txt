PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located at 806 Iwilei Road in the Honolulu District on the Island 
of Oahu, Hawaii.  The property is located at the corner of Iwilei Road and Sumner Street.  
The Project Location Map and Site Plans are provided on Plate 1, and Plates 2.1 and 2.2, 
respectively.   
The project involves the construction of a new four-story structure in place of the 
existing one-story building adjacent to Sumner Street. We understand the existing steel 
frame structure will be reused with exterior and interior walls to be replaced. In addition, 
the project consists of replacing the existing carport with a pre-manufactured building 
using a light metal framed canopy.  
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for 
the site grading construction at the project site.

SITE GRADING 
We observed the site grading work for the 806 Iwilei Road Development project 
from March 10, 2020 to June 22, 2021.  The site grading work consisted of placement and 
compaction of fills and backfills on the order of up to about 3- foot thick to achieve the 
finished grades for the project. Koga Engineering and Construction Inc. was the grading 
subcontractor and performed the site grading work. During the site grading operations, we 
obtained samples of the on-site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials 
and transported them to our laboratory for testing. The maximum dry densities and 
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with 
ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 3. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density 
as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. In the pavement areas, the fill and 
backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 
95 percent relative compaction. 
During the site grading, periodic field density tests were performed in general 
accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). Results of the compaction tests 
performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.9 of this report. These tests were 
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the 
site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site 
survey stakes. These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the 
degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked 
until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and/or approved by our field 
representative based on visual observation and hand probing. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill 
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures and confirmation of 
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the site grading work for the above project observed 
and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in conformance 
with the general intent of the recommendations in the following references.
