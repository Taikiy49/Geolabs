Based on our field observations and the above discussions it appears that part of the
observed distresses may be attributed to moisture infiltration into the subsurface soils The
water infiltration or excess moisture once trapped in the subsoils underneath the sidewalk
and the perimeter of the house structure are not able to evaporate as a result of the
protective cover provided by the concrete slab Due to the clayey nature of the subsoils in
the Ewa area the clayey soils exhibit swelling properties and are susceptible to softening
upon wetting The swelling of the underlying clayey soils likely resulted in lifting of the edges
of the perimeter building walls causing some of the observed distresses described above
Remedial Recommendations
Because the distresses observed at the subject subdivision are attributed to the
shrinkage and or swelling behavior of the expansive clayey soils it is important that the
remedial measures are directed at stabilizing the moisture content of the expansive clayey
soils Stabilizing the moisture content of the expansive clayey soils should reduce the
potential for further shrinkage and or swelling of the underlying clayey soils
In order to reduce the potential for moisture fluctuations undemeath the sidewalks and
house structures we recommend that the following remedial measures be considered for
implementation These may include the following 1 chemical stabilization of the in situ
expansive clayey soils 2 installation of a moisture cut off barrier along the perimeter
sidewalk and 3 construction of a new sidewalk with a concrete cut off wall Detailed
discussions of these alternatives are presented in the following sections of this letter
Chemical Stabilization of Expansive Soils
We believe that strong consideration be given to chemical stabilization of the
expansive soils at the site Based on the available information we believe that chemical
stabilization using the Electrochemical Soil Stabilizer EcSS 3000 may be an effective
remedial measure at the subject subdivision site In general the EcSS 3000 chemical
stabilization involves the injection of a diluted chemical diluted in water into the ground to
chemically stabilize the expansive nature of the soils The chemical injection reacts with the
clay particles in the soil to form a soil mass that is less susceptible to shrink swell effects
when subjected to moisture fluctuations
We believe that chemical stabilization of the on site expansive clayey soils would
be an effective way of coping with the moderately to highly expansive nature of the on site
soils Based on our evaluations we believe that the locally available EcSS 3000 for
chemical stabilization may be a technically effective alternative to reduce the shrink swell
effects on the slabs on grade at the site
For preliminary estimating purposes we believe that the chemical stabilization would
need to be injected to a minimum depth of about 7 feet below the existing sidewalk slabs

In addition the spacing of the injection holes should not be further than 3 feet on center

72 hours should be allowed prior to post treatment sampling and testing for evaluation of
the treated product if desired Based on our assessment the chemical stabilization

supplemental recommendations for implementation during the design and construction
Installation of Perimeter Moisture Cut Off Barrier
In lieu of stabilizing the on site expansive clayey soils with EcSS 3000
consideration may be given to the installation of a perimeter moisture cut off barrier along
the existing sidewalk This will serve to reduce the potential for water infiltration into and out
of the non expansive select granular fill and underlying expansive clayey soils below the
sidewalk and house structure A schematic detail of the perimeter moisture cut off barrier
is shown on Plate 1 Schematic Section Perimeter Moisture Cut Off Barrier The moisture
cut off barrier should consist of a minimum 20 mil thick polyethylene membrane extending
a minimum of 4 inches below the non expansive select granular fill material Subsequent
to the installation of the moisture barrier the excavated trench should be backfilled with
relatively impervious compacted materials such as the on site clayey soils For this remedial
approach the existing sidewalk may need to be repaired to provide positive drainage away
from the house structure
Construction of New Sidewalk with Perimeter Concrete Cut Off Wall
To combine the repair of the existing sidewalk with the installation of a moisture cut
off barrier consideration may be given to the construction of a new sidewalk with a
perimeter concrete cut off wall Graphic illustration of this remedial measure is presented
on Plate 2 Schematic Section New Sidewalk with Perimeter Cut Off Wall The concrete
cut off wall should also extend a minimum of 4 inches below the non expansive select
granular fill material The excavation outside the cut off wall should be backfilled with
compacted on site clayey soils as discussed above
In addition to the implementation of these remedial measures positive drainage away
from the house structure should also be re established at all of the residences This would
help reduce the potential for swelling and loss of bearing strength of the underlying clayey
soils due to saturation Therefore we recommend that the following measures be
implemented at the subject subdivision site as soon as possible
1 The finished grades of the yard areas outside the house structure should be
regraded and sloped to shed water away from the house foundations It is
critical to re establish positive drainage away from the house structure in order
to reduce the potential for ponding around the house structure and
foundations

Well defined drainage swales should be re established and maintained to drain
surface water runoff away from the house slab and foundations Blockage or
clogging of these drainage features could cause ponding of water at the house
structure and foundations resulting in further potential foundation and house
distress
Gutter systems should be installed around the building and the discharge of
water should be diverted away from the foundation discharging to an
appropriate outlet
Excessive landscape watering near the foundations should be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to reduce the potential for water infiltration into the subsoils In addition
care should be exercised by the homeowner with the use of automatic
sprinkler systems since there is a potential for over watering with the system
left unattended
These drainage requirements and recommendations are essential for the proper
performance of the foundation support system since ponded water could cause subsurface
soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The clayey soils in the Ewa Plain
area are susceptible to heaving or loss of strength when saturated These conditions are
further accentuated by the prolonged dry weather conditions typical of the Ewa Plain area
We believe that the potential for structural distress to the house structure should be
significantly reduced if these measures are implemented and maintained
