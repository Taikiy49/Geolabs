EARTHWORK OBSERVATION AND COMPACTION TESTING REPORT
DOLE PLANTATION STORM DRAINAGE IMPROVEMENTS
Dear Mr Gaston
This report summarizes the geotechnical engineering observations for the Dole
Plantation Storm Drainage Improvements project in Wahiawa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The project consisted of the installation and backfilling of 292 linear feet of 12 inch
drain line manholes inlet and outlet structures The backfilling was observed by Geolabs
Inc at the Dole Plantation project site from May 14 2000 through May 30 2000 The backfill
compaction was tested by Geolabs Inc from May 16 2000 through May 30 2000
The subject site is located on Kamehameha Highway near Plantation Road in
Wahiawa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii A copy of the site plan is attached for your
reference
The drain line installation and backfilling work were performed by Koga Engineering
Construction Inc The invert of the drain line is approximately 3 to 5 feet below the existing
ground surface The fill materials were generally imported from Makakilo Quarry and
generated from the trench excavations on site
The backfill materials were brought to near optimum moisture content and compacted
to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1557 A minimum of 90 percent relative compaction was used in the
lawn area
Modified Proctor tests were performed by Geolabs Inc on the base course material
from Makakilo Quarry on site reddish brown silty clay material and a mixture of cold plane
material existing base course and reddish brown silty clay material The maximum dry
density and optimum moisture content relationship was determined by Geolabs Inc in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Pipetet The test results
are summarized on Plate 2 of this report

During the backfilling operations and testing of the drain line backfill periodic field
density tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922
Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests are summarized on Plates 3 1
through 3 3 of this report
Wet subgrade soils were encountered during the excavation for Drain Inlet 6 and the
pavement repair area at the North End Entrance The wet soils encountered at Drain Inlet
6 and the North End Entrance were over excavated about 12 and 24 inches below the finish
subgrade respectively and backfilled with compacted fill A filter fabric was used at the
bottom of the excavation to reduce the potential for migration of the fines into the granular
backfill material Since the contractor ran out of base course material 6 inches of base
course was used instead of 8 inches at the North End Entrance
Our services consisted of full time field observation of the contractor s fill placement
and compaction procedures during the above grading operations
Our test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from
on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only
to the degree implied by the methods used Where low test results were noted the areas
were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representative and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the backfilling work and testing of the backfill for the
drain line for the project observed and tested by Geolabs Inc in the period covered by
this report were performed in accordance with the general intent of the recommendations
in the project documents and Chapter 14 of the Revised Ordinances of Honolulu 1990 as
amended
