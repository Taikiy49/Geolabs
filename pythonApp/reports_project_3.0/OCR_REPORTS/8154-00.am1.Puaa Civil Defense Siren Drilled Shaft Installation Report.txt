Dear Ms. Carman-Vergara: 
In accordance with the scope of services outlined in Purchase Order 2777 by 
Commercial Electric dated July 13, 2020, Geolabs observed the drilled shaft foundation 
installation for the New Civil Defense Sirens, Puaa and Kualono Subdivisions project located 
on the Island of Maui, Hawaii. The project locations and general vicinity are shown on the 
Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
The purpose of our geotechnical observations was to monitor the geotechnical 
engineering aspects of the drilled shaft foundation installation for conformance with the 
project plans and specifications, and to provide subsequent recommendations, as 
appropriate. 
PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The Puaa Siren project site is located near the intersection of Nopu Street and South 
Firebreak Road, and the Kualono Siren project site is located on Kula Highway adjacent to 
the Pukulani 251 Water Tank. The project includes the construction of new civil defense 
sirens (omnidirectional speaker pole) for general public emergency purposes. 
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report consists of the observation of 
the drilled shaft foundation construction for the New Civil Defense Sirens at the Puaa and 
Kualono Subdivisions project only. Aina Excavation was the drilled shaft contractor for the 
project and was responsible for drilling the shafts, installing the reinforcing cages, and 
concrete placement.

DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION 
A deep foundation system consisting of cast-in-place concrete drilled shafts 
(designed by others) embedded into the underlying alluvial deposits was utilized for 
foundation support of the new Civil Defense sirens. 
The drilled shaft contractor installed one drilled shaft at the Kualono Subdivision from 
August 31, 2020 through September 4, 2020 at the location shown on the Site Plan, 
Plate 2.1. One drilled shaft was also installed at the Puaa Subdivision from August 31, 2020 
through September 2, 2020 at the location shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2.2. A 
representative from Geolabs, Inc. observed the drilled shaft excavation, placement of 
reinforcing cage, and concrete placement on a near-continuous (full-time) basis during the 
installation of the drilled shafts. The drilled shafts were excavated by the contractor utilizing 
a Bay Shore TR70 LoDril drill rig equipped with a 42-inch diameter auger and a 42-inch core 
barrel. Groundwater was not encountered during the excavation of the drilled shafts. 
After excavation of the drilled shafts to their designated tip elevations, the reinforcing 
cages were lowered into the drilled holes to the appropriate elevations with the use of an 
International Altec Boom Crane. After the reinforcing cage was installed in the drilled hole, 
concrete was placed through the end dump method, which consists of using the concrete 
truck’s chute for placement. Concrete with a slump of 3.5 inches supplied by Hawaiian 
Cement was used for the drilled shafts. The drilled shaft installation records for the Kualono 
and Puaa sirens are presented on Plates 3.1 through 3.4 and 4.1 through 4.4, respectively. 
Based on our field observations, we believe that the drilled shafts for the Puaa and 
Kualono Subdivision Civil Defense Sirens have been installed in general conformance with 
the project plans and specifications and within the limits of generally accepted construction 
practices. The location of the top of the shaft should be measured using optical survey 
equipment to verify that the shaft position is within tolerances specified in the project 
specifications.  
LIMITATIONS 
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities 
occurring simultaneously on a construction site, even when our representatives are on 
the project site on a full-time basis. Our observation and consultation services were 
performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering 
practice; no warranty is expressed or implied. Provision of construction monitoring by an 
engineer is not insurance, nor does it guarantee construction of any type. The contractor 
shall retain primary responsibility for the quality of the work, for adhering to plans and 
specifications, and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found.
