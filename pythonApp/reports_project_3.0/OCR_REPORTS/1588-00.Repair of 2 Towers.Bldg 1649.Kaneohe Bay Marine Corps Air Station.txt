INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical investigation
performed for the above referenced project The general location of the
site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The purpose of our study was to explore the subsurface conditions
at the sites for the two distressed towers in order to provide data for
use in the design and construction of the proposed new towers
was performed in general accordance with the scope of services outlined
in our proposal of

PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The project involves the reconstruction of 2 existing 2 story CMU
tower structures each approximately 8 feet square in plan These
existing structures currently exhibit signs of distress which appear to
be caused by settlement
The total building loads from each individual new tower is
anticipated to be approximately 51 kips and wall load is on the order of
1 5 kips per foot
SITE DESCRIPTION
The subject towers are situated at the Skeet Field located at the
eastern end of the Kaneohe Marine Corps Air Station on the island of
Oahu Hawaii
The Skeet Field was constructed on the level portion of a terraced
area with the two towers standing at a horizontal distance of
approximately 20 feet from the edge of the terrace Ground cover
consists of some dry vegetations on sandy to gravelly fill material
Drainage is generally in a direction towards the edge of the terrace

SUBSURFACE CONDITION
Field Exploration
Four 4 borings were drilled to a depth of 16 5 feet below the
existing ground surface at the sites The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 In general the borings are
located at a horizontal distance varying between 3 to 5 feet from the
damaged towers Detailed description of our field exploration and boring
logs are presented in the appendices of this report
Subsurface Condition
The subsoil condition at the site consisted of 2 to 4 feet of stiff
fill consisting mainly of clayey silt with some sand and gravel Under
neath the fill material very stiff clayey silt was encountered to the
full depth of all the borings
Groundwater was not encountered during our soil exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
Causes of the Building Distresses
Since it is not possible to obtain the subsoil condition directly
underneath the two towers interpretation frem the borings drilled around
the towers had to be made Despite the overall stiff subsoil condition
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 4
encountered in our borings the interpretation from the borings and the
laboratory test results lead us to believe that the building distresses
were most likely caused by the densification of a layer of loosely
compacted fill underneath the buildings
The densification is likely to be caused by the water tamping
effect resulted from water infiltration into the underlying fill material
which consisted of clayey silt with sand and gravels
We also believe that the shallow embedment of the building
foundation which provides only limited amount of resistance towards
lateral loading e g from wind may possibly constitute another cause
for the building distresses
Foundation Design for Proposed New Towers
For the foundation support of the proposed new towers spread or
continuous wall footing bearing on the recompacted fill can be used
Maximum allowable bearing pressure of 2 000 p s f is recommended
Provided that the fill material underneath the building foundation
is thoroughly compacted settlement under the relatively light load is

The footing should be buried at least 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent grade Wind or other lateral load consideration may further
increase this footing embedment requirement
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by friction resistance developed between the bottom
of the foundations and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil
around the foundation is well compacted The frictional resistance may
be calculated by using a coefficient of friction of 0 4 The above
resistance values are maximum values and do not contain a factor of
safety
Drainage Considerations around Footing Foundation
To prevent possible future distresses to the towers due to the
water tamping effect we believe the drainage condition surrounding the
footing foundation should be considered
We recommend that a subdrain be installed on all but the oceanside
of the foundation footing The purpose of the subdrain is to intercept

and redirect the flow of underground water which may otherwise flow
through the foundation soil The subdrain should daylight out on the
edge of the terrace slope Detailed plan and section of the subdrain
installation are shown on Plate 3
Site Preparation for Footing Foundation
Before the construction of the footing foundation we recommend
that the fill material underlying the damaged towers be excavated and
replaced by controlled backfill material The overexcavation should
extend to a minimum depth of 12 inches below the bottom of the new
footing foundation Any soft spots encountered should also be removed to
firm material
It is our opinion that the on site fill material with low to
moderate swell potential can be reused as backfill material However
this material should be moisture conditioned to 2 wet of optimum prior
to placement If required imported fill material should be
non expansive free of organic matter generally less than 3 inches in
size and form a dense stable soil mass when compacted
Prior to backfilling the bottom of the excavation should be
thoroughly tamped to obtain 90 compaction The subsequent overlying
backfill should then be placed in no more than 8 inches loose lift and be
compacted to at least 90 of its maximum density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78 test method

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the foundations should be forwarded to
the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to construction
to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and preparations be
observed by the soils engineer during construction tc see that adequate
compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
Gesign should be made

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
a ON I OR A
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected samples to aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Boring Logs
at the appropriate sample depths
One Atterbeg Limits test was performed on the underlying
fine grained material to further identify their physical characteristics
towards change of water content The test result is summarized on Plate
B l
Three sieve analyses were performed on the upper fill soil to
determine the grain size distribution of the soil The test results are
summarized on Plate B l
Two one inch ring swell tests were performed on natural
air dried samples obtained from near surface to determine its swelling
potential The test results are summarized on Plate B 2
One direct shear test was performed on an undisturbed sample
to evaluate the strength characteristics of the fill soil The test
result is summarized on Plate B 3
One consolidation test was performed on a sample of the
underlying natural soil to evaluate its settlement characteristics The
consclidation curve is presented on Plate B 4
