2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The younger
Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age in age
and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island The Koolau shield
reached the end of its main shield building phase about 2 million years ago
The geologic conditions underlying the site are the result of a complex series of
depositional and erosional events brought on by glacio eustatic sea level changes and
post erosional volcanic activities during the Pleistocene Epoch Following the cessation
of activity of the Koolau Volcano alluvial materials were deposited in a deltaic or
estuarine environment in the area of Pearl Harbor The sea level fluctuated many times
receding perhaps as much as 300 feet below and rising up to 95 feet above the present
sea level
Concurrent with a rise in sea level during late Pleistocene Era eruption of the
Salt Lake Makalapa member of the Honolulu Volcanic Series mantled the older
alluvium with thick deposits of volcanic ash which consolidated to form volcanic tuff
locally known as mudrock layers Subsequent erosion dissected and thinned those
mantles of tuff During the Recent epoch as sea level rose to its current stand the area
was covered with lagoonal marine deposits During the construction of the existing
roadways man made fills were placed in localized areas to raise the site to the existing
grades

3 7 New Ultra Thin Whitetopping Pavements
In addition to the conventional asphalt concrete pavement design as presented
above we understand that the SDOT would like to evaluate the option of using
Ultra Thin Whitetopping UTW with Cement Treated Base CTB in place cement
treatment of existing base subbase for the pavement reconstruction work
In general UTW is a relatively new pavement rehabilitation process where a thin
layer of concrete 2 to 4 inches usually high strength with closely spaced joints is
bonded to an existing prepared surface distressed asphalt The bonding allows the
Hawaii California

concrete overlay and the underlying asphalt to act as a composite section reducing
critical stresses Short joint spacing further reduces the stresses caused by wheel loads
and temperature changes The new concrete provides an economical smooth riding
surface that is much stronger and more durable than the old asphalt pavement Most of
the concrete used for UTW has contained fibers such as polyproplylene steel and
nylon to improve crack resistance and enhance the toughness or ductility of the
concrete To be successful the UTW must be placed over asphalt that does not show
signs of deep pavement distress If potholes alligator cracking or deep fissures exist in
the asphalt the concrete will not form an adequate bond resulting in pavement that
lacks adequate support Based on field tests conducted by the Federal Highway
Association FHWA asphalt rutting potential is a significant factor in the UTW
performance The UTW sections built on stiffer asphalts showed less distress than
those on softer asphalts
In this application it is assumed that the combination of UTW and CTB will
behave as one composite slab and function as a rigid pavement section The pavement
design generally follows the procedures described in the Portland Cement Association
article entitled Thickness Design for Concrete Highway and Street Pavements with the
assumptions and modifications as noted herein

3 10 Surface Drainage
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due
to saturation of the subbase and base course and or subgrade soils Therefore the
pavement surface should be sloped to drain and drainage gradients should be
maintained to carry surface water off the pavement to appropriate drainage structures
Surface water ponding should not be allowed on site during or after construction We
strongly recommend the development of good shoulder drainage along with a program
to prevent obstructions in the drainage structures be implemented to reduce the
potential for significant pavement deterioration or premature failure of the pavements
