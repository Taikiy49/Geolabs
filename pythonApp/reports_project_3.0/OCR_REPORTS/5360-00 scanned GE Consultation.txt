As requested we have reviewed the limited information provided to us pertaining to
the driven pile foundations installed at the former Queen Emmalani Tower project located
at 600 Queen Street in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii In addition we also
reviewed some additional information made available from other sources in our evaluation
of the pile foundations that were installed at the project site previously It should be noted
that the preliminary findings provided herein are based on the limited information and data
available to us at the time this consultation letter was prepared
The property located at 600 Queen Street at the northeast corner of Queen Street
and South Street was the site of the former Queen Emmalani Tower project The project
consisted of a high rise tower structure flanked by a low rise parking structure and
commercial retail development As part of the project the driven pile foundations for the
structures were installed in about 1992 and 1993 We understand that over 1 300 piles
were installed at the project site for support of the structures The driven piles consisted of
16 5 inch octagonal precast prestressed concrete piles designed based on an allowable
compressive load capacity of 70 and 100 tons per pile for the parking structure and tower
structure respectively The pilings were installed by Hawaiian Dredging and Construction
Company HDCC under the field observations by representatives from
Pacific Geotechnical Engineers Inc PGE In general the pile foundation installation was
completed for the project in 1993
It should be noted that not all the pile driving records and or pile driving summaries
were available for our review and evaluation at this time Based on our review of the
available information we believe that a large portion of the existing pilings likely has been
driven to sufficient depths and or resistances to achieve the design load capacity of 70 or
100 tons per pile However it appears that a substantial number of problem piles
over 150 problem piles in the tower structure area alone were identified during the pile
installation program The general nature of the problem piles likely was caused by erratic
subsurface conditions subsurface obstructions and or other issues Based on the
