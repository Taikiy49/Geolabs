INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed renovation project of Linekona
School in Honolulu Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and
its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
exploration was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated

This report summarizes the findings from the field exploration and
our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses
based on data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction bidding
estimation purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to demolish the interior portions of the existing
Linekona School and construct a new 2 story structure with a basement in
the remaining shell The exterior brick walls are to be retained The
proposed renovation generally follows the existing configuration of the
building No major additional excavation and filling are anticipated
Based on the preliminary plans supplied to us the existing basement
construction extends about six 6 feet below the existing ground
surface The basement walls serve at least in part as retaining
structures New footings are anticipated New concrete on grade slabs
are also planned for the basement area
Minimal site grading is anticipated

The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface soil conditions at the site in order to develop a generalized
soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertinent to the foundation design of the proposed
renovated structure

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered a surface fill layer of
about five 5 feet thick overlying medium dense cinder sand Cemented
beach sand formation was encountered at about 15 feet in all the borings
drilled It appeared that the upper fill layer was not properly
compacted
It is recommended that spread and continuous wall footings bypass the
upper fill layer and bear on the underlying in situ cinder sand to support
the proposed renovation
It is the contractor s responsibility to maintain the integrity of
the exterior brick walls during demolition and construction of the
project Design of necessary shoring systems and or undertaking of all
appropriate protection measures should be the responsibility of the
contractor
Foundation Support
Based on our borings it is our opinion that shallow spread or
continuous wall footings bearing on the in situ cinder sand which is
generally located about 5 feet below the existing ground surface may be
used to support the proposed structure Foundations should not bear on
the upper existing loose fill
GEOLABS HAWAII

An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be utilized for
foundations bearing on the undisturbed cinder sand
If foundations are located next to the basement excavations they
should be embedded below a 1 1 H V imaginary plane extending upward from
the bottom edge heel of the basement wall footings
For footings in the basement area a minimum footing embedment of 24
inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade is recommended
Settlements for foundations bearing on the in situ cinder sand are
anticipated to be on the order of less than one half of an inch total and
one quarter of an inch differential
Slab On Grade
New concrete on grade slabs are planned for the basement The
on grade floor slabs should be placed over a 4 inch thick cushion layer of
gravel size material such as 3B Fine crushed rock to provide uniform
support A moisture barrier membrane is recommended between the slab and
the cushion layer to reduce the possibility of moisture damage to the
floor coverings provided that one inch of moist sand is placed above the
membrane as an aid to curing of the concrete
Cinder sands may be anticipated at slab subgrade If fill materials
are encountered instead of cinder the slab subgrade should be wetted and
then compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry density as established by
ASTM D 1557 78

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction phases of
the work This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the
event that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to
start of construction The recommendations given in this report are
contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping from existing features shown on the Preliminary Site Plan
undated by CJS Group Architects Ltd Elevations of the data points
were not available at the time of our report preparation

three 3 borings to depths of 15 3 and 15 8 feet below the existing

the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of
the materials encountered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1
through A 3
