FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two borings designated
as Boring Nos 1 and 2 extending to depths of about 20 3 and 44 5 feet below the existing
ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with
solid stem continuous flight augers and rotary coring tools The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings and test pits were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 4 Graphic representations
of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates 5 and 6
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch
OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition
some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM
Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving
a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow
counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are
shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration generally encountered 3 to 8 inches of asphaltic concrete
underlain by fills consisting of dense silty basaltic gravel and sand medium dense clayey
gravel and very stiff silty clays extending to depths of about 5 to 7 feet below the existing
ground surface The fills were underlain by alluvial deposits consisting of hard basalt boulders
and cobbles in a silty clay and sand matrix extending to the maximum depths explored of
about 20 3 to 44 5 feet below the existing ground surface Alluvial deposits consisting of very
stiff clayey silt with some coralline gravel and sand were encountered at a depth of about
18 feet and extending to a depth of about 23 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings or the test pits during our field
exploration However it should be noted that groundwater levels are subjected to change due
to seasonal precipitation run off construction activities and other factors

FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Horizontal Directional Drilling HDD
We believe that the use of horizontal directional drilling may be used for the installation
of the fiber optic cable across Farrington Highway however difficult drilling is anticipated Due
to the alluvial colluvial deposits consisting of boulders cobbles and gravel we believe
difficulties may arise during the installation of the fiber optic cable by horizontal directional
drilling
Horizontal directional drilling is a trenchless steerable installation method using a
multi axis drilling machine to bore a small diameter pilot hole in the subsurface The pilot hole
is bored using either controlled fluid jetting or fluid assisted mechanical cutting or a
combination of the two methods The pilot hole is reamed as necessary to accommodate the
pipe to be installed The pipe is pulled back into the reamed hole by the drilling machine
Most HDD systems use a slurry to stabilize the walls of the borehole and to reduce the
frictional drag on the pipeline being installed Steering corrections are made in the soils by
rotating the slanted cutter shoe to the desired orientation and then advancing the shoe and
string without rotation

in general the HDD pilot hole would be drilled nearly horizontal with entry and exit
angles limited to a maximum of about 14 degrees measured from horizontal The drill unit
should be positioned at an appropriate setback distance such that the required depth starting
from the entry point can be achieved In general the product pipe or utility line should be
installed in one continuous operation preferably without pause and should protrude a
sufficient distance beyond the entry and exit points to allow proper termination or connection
at a later time Shallow working pits may be required at each end of the bore to allow
placement of the drill pipe and to allow transition to the conventional pipe trench installation
The vertical and horizontal alignments of the drill hole are monitored from the surface
by a locator which tracks signals from the transmitter contained inside the drill head housing
Other methods of tracking the cutting tool such as in hole steering are generally
recommended to provide more precise line and grade information The contractor should
carefully monitor the drilling operation when approaching property boundaries and the
existing underground utilities or structures to assure proper clearance Soils containing gravel
cobbles and boulders may present difficulties in steering control due to the effect of possible
deflection or skidding around the potential hard obstructions
The feasibility of the HDD technique should be established based on ground
conditions diameter and length of the pipe or duct line and the required achievable degree
of accuracy in both alignment and grade HDD is less attractive where precise line and grade
tolerances are required in soils containing cobbles and boulders in a soil matrix especially with
larger diameter pipelines The HDD method is best suited for soils with some cohesion i e
clays However the method has been successfully used in granular soils by adding bentonite
to the drilling fluid to aid in the borehole stability The HDD method is not particularly well
suited for gravelly soils greater than 50 percent gravel size particles due to difficulties in
drilling the pilot hole and maintaining the stability of the hole It should be noted that widely
heterogeneous formations such as alluvial soils with sands gravel cobbles and boulders
such as those encountered at this project site present some challenges to penetration and
steering control Therefore use of the HDD method in these heterogeneous ground
conditions may be difficult and additional care is required in the selection of suitable HDD
equipment to maintain the precise alignment and grade of the bore
The advantage of HDD installation on this project is that a buried installation across
Farrington Highway can be achieved without the need for disruption of the traffic flow In
addition the need for precise alignment and grade tolerances for a communication line is not
as significant as for gravity sewer lines
We understand that a 6 inch diameter HDPE pipe will serve as the duct line for the
communication cable installation In general the HDPE pipe is suitable for the relatively small
diameter and short length HDD installations for this project However because of the low
tensile strength the pipe may be susceptible to failure during the HDD pullback installation

Geotechnical Conditions
The technical feasibility of the HDD method depends on the characteristics of the
materials at the depth of the pipeline profile as well as the length of the drill path and
diameter of the pipe to be installed American Gas Association 1995 In general
gravel sized material cannot be readily fluidized by the drilling fluid and may not be
stable enough to be cut and removed by the drilling fluid without caving in Therefore
subsoils containing a high percentage of gravel may present difficulties with the
utilization of the HDD method In addition it should be noted that obstructions such
as cobbles and boulders within the drill path might halt the advance of the drill bit
reamer or pipeline during pullback
Based on our field exploration the subsurface conditions along the fiber optic cable
alignment consisted of boulders and cobbles in a silty clay and sand matrix at depths
of about 5 to 7 feet below the existing ground surface The alluvial deposits extended
down to the maximum depths explored of about 20 3 to 44 5 feet below the existing
ground surface
The alluvial deposits containing high gravel content about 50 percent or more and
cobble sized fragments may be problematic and are expected to cause difficulty in
drilling and steering the pilot hole as well as hole stability problems such as caving in
of the pilot and reamed holes The caved in materials will obstruct or divert reaming
tools or the pipe string during pipe pullback This will substantially increase the pulling
stresses on the pipe string and may impede pipe pulling or damage the pipeline
Therefore consideration should be given to reaming the pilot hole to a minimum of
1 5 times the product pipe diameter In addition consideration should be given to
product pipes with small Standard Diameter Ratio SDR to offset the increased soil
loading if the hole collapses
Based on our subsurface exploration we have assumed that the interface between the
fill layer and the alluvial deposits is parallel to the existing ground surface Based on
the available information the alignment of the proposed cable line near Station 2 08
may run parallel to the interface between the fill layer and the alluvial deposits If the
drill is launched at Station 2 08 we believe that deflection of the pilot drill bit may occur
at the interface between the fill layer and the alluvial deposits and the pilot drill bit may

For the HDD method the most important design consideration for the pipe is the tensile
stresses imposed on the pipe when it is pulled into place through the drill hole The
magnitude of these tensile stresses is a function of the length of the installation as well
as the subsurface conditions Other important pipe design considerations are the
internal and external pressures acting on the pipe during and after installation In
addition the selected pipe material must have adequate corrosion resistance or be
protected to resist internal and external corrosion
HDPE pipes generally have low tensile strength and may be susceptible to failure
during installation This limits the length of installation that can be completed with an
HDPE pipe Therefore it is critical that the pipe be selected with an adequate
allowable pulling tensile capacity Pipe stress analyses should be performed to
evaluate if an HDPE pipe can withstand the pulling stresses during pipe installation
and the external pressures hydrostatic and soil on the installed pipeline
The HDPE pipe should be selected to provide the maximum pipe thickness and
strength for the proposed installation based on the results of the pipe stress analyses
considerations regarding HDD installation procedures probable drill hole sizes and
materials in filling the annulus between the HDD installed HDPE pipeline and the drill
hole Proper ballasting of the pipelines during installation into the HDD drill hole should
be performed by the contractor to reduce pipe weight the effect of buoyancy and the
corresponding reduction of the tensile loads on the pipe string during pullback Finally
HDPE pipes should conform to the requirements of AWWA C 906 Pressure Class 125
as a minimum

Construction Staging Area
Staging areas will be required at the entry and exit locations for the HDD installed
pipelines Additional staging area is also needed for pipe storage spoil dewatering
and for laying out the pipe when the pipe string is assembled for pullback The pipe
string should be hoisted at least 20 feet above the ground surface when crossing active
traffic lanes to avoid disturbance to vehicular traffic Other potential obstacles include
overhead power lines and roadway entrances along the pipe layout corridor
Construction Considerations
Buried underground hazards including electrical cables water lines and
telecommunication cables may be present within the limits of the drill path during the
HDD operation Therefore the directional control of the pilot drill bit is critical to the
success of the proposed pipeline installation by HDD method These underground
utilities and structures should be identified located and avoided during the project
construction It is important that the HDD contractor utilize an in hole steering tool in
conjunction with a surface tracking system to provide reliable horizontal and vertical
directional control during the pipeline installation
The primary selection criteria for the HDD machine are thrust pullback and torque
capabilities However project related requirements such as length of installation and
diameter of the product pipe play an important role in the selection process Depending
on the length and size of the drill hole required for the HDD installation the size of the
drilling equipment and its capacity in rotational power as well as pulling power should
be checked by the contractor for the specific project
Type of Drilling Fluid
Depending on the soil conditions water only or water mixed with bentonite and or
polymer can be used as a drilling fluid in a directional drilling application Water alone
is normally suitable for drilling in silty or clayey soils If a highly plastic or expansive
clay is encountered polymer mixed with water will improve the hole lubrication and
reduce the swelling tendency of clay swelling tends to close off the hole which
increases torque and pullback requirements For sand or other poorly consolidated
formations such as the boulders and cobbles in a silty clay and sand matrix
encountered at the project site a bentonite mixture should be used to support the
borehole The amount of drilling additives bentonite or polymer depends on the soil
conditions and experience of the contractor The water used in the drilling fluid should
not be hard or salty because this will cause poor mixing or yield with the bentonite or
polymer and the flocculated bentonite may tend to settle to the bottom of the tank
Hard water should be treated with soda ash and salt water should be avoided

Muck and Slurry Disposal
Special muck and slurry handling procedures may be required for the HDD installation
method for the proposed cable crossing The properties of the various geologic units
anticipated along the drill path as indicated by the grain size distribution tests should
be taken into account in selecting an appropriate slurry separation plant
In order to aid in a clean site and to more readily separate the materials a solids
control or slurry separation plant with shakers screens de sanders de silter cones
and centrifuge may need to be considered This will facilitate a more rapid separation
and disposal process Additives may need to be included to get the fine grained
materials to flocculate so that a clean slurry can be returned to the drill head
Muck resulting from the HDD operations should be removed from the site and should
be disposed of properly at an acceptable facility or designated area Slurry from the
HDD operations should be pumped into trucks for disposal in accordance with the
applicable governmental regulations and agencies Contaminated soils and or
groundwater if applicable should be handled tested and disposed of in accordance
with the project specifications guidelines and government requirements
