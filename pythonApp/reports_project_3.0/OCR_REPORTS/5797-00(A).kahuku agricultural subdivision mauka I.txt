exploration performed for the Kahuku Agricultural Subdivision Mauka project in the

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lava from the Waianae and
Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to be middle to late
Pliocene in age 4 million years ago and Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late
Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age in age 5 million years ago After a long
period of volcanic inactivity during which time erosion incised deep valleys into the
Waianae and Koolau shields volcanic activity returned with a series of lava flows followed
by cinder and tuff cone formations located mainly at the southeastern portion of the Island
of Oahu
The project site straddles the north facing foothills of the Koolau Range and the
Kahuku coastal plain as indicated on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The Hoolapa
Gulch drainage basin encompasses the eastern portion of the project site The drainage
basin contains an intermittent stream channel which passes through the project site The
project site contains some prominent hills composed of weathered basaltic rock outcrops
mixed with residual soils within and along the southern perimeter
Based on our review of available geological mapping information the flattened
terrain at the northern portion of the project site is underlain by alluvial clay sediments
likely overlying deeper hard coral limestone reef mixed with calcareous sediments The
hilly terrain which encompasses the southern portion of the project site is mainly
underlain by weathered volcanic rock and derived residual soils of the Koolau Volcanic
Series The basaltic rock materials generally consist of layered lava rock with frequent
massive seams of very dense rock and clinker
In addition a localized area of emerged coral limestone reef and lithified calcareous
dune sands is mainly present between about Elevation 80 and 120 feet Mean Sea
Level MSL at the east central portion of the project site The exposed reef and
sandstone formation was encountered as a sub vertically inclined north facing linear
outcrop which forms a continuous elongated wall of about 20 to 35 feet in height

Kaena Series KaB KanE and KaeC These clay and stony clay soils are
deep and poorly drained which develop on alluvial fans and talus slopes Slopes
range between about 2 percent to as steep as 35 percent These soils are poorly
suited for engineering purposes and are plastic generally sticky and have a high
shrink swell potential
Lahaina Series LaB and LaC These silty clay soils are well drained and
develop on generally smooth uplands Slopes range from about 3 percent to as
much as about 15 percent They generally have fair to good engineering
properties and a moderate shrink swell potential
Paumalu Series PeC PeD and PeF These silty clay soils are well drained
and develop on upland surfaces with slopes ranging between about 8 and
40 percent These soils generally have fair to good engineering properties with a
low to moderate shrink swell potential
Waialua Series WkA and WkB These silty clay soils are moderately well
drained and develop on coastal alluvial plains with slopes of 0 to about 8 percent
These soils have generally fair to poor engineering properties and a moderate to
high shrink swell potential
Rock Land rRK Rock land is comprised of areas of exposed rock covering
between about 25 and 90 percent of the surface Basaltic rock outcrops and
shallow soils are the primary characteristic

2 2 Site Description
The project site straddles the lower elevation foothill pediment of the Koolau
Range and the Kahuku Coastal Plain in the Kahuku area of the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The project site is bounded by Kamehameha Highway and Marconi Road towards the
north the existing Turtle Bay Wastewater Treatment Facility towards the west and
other military and private lands towards the east and south of the project site The
existing north south trending Charlie Road bounds the project site along the eastern
perimeter The project location and general vicinity is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
The project site generally encompasses both an open range vegetated with
scattered trees brush and grasses and existing agricultural farms An existing jeep
trail and utility pole alignment traverses generally east to west along the base of the
foothills The existing farms are generally located on the coastal plain adjacent to
Kamehameha Highway An intermittent stream with confining banks on the order of
about 5 to 10 feet high occupies Hoolapa Gulch which passes through the east central
portion of the site as depicted by the Site Plan Plate 2
The project site encompasses very gently sloping to nearly flat terrain adjacent to
Kamehameha Highway Slopes generally increase towards the south and several
relatively small to medium hills comprise the interior of the project site A single
oval shaped hill with steep side slopes radiating towards the north east and south
resides at the central portion of the project site An existing communication facility
occupies the relatively flat hill top This hill is the primary source for potential rockfall
hazards which could affect the surrounding down slope lots Another hill with several
existing hill top communication towers bounds the site along a portion of the southern
perimeter
Ground surface elevations generally range between about 20 feet Mean Sea
Level MSL at the northern portion of the proposed development adjacent to
Kamehameha Highway to a maximum elevation of about 350 feet MSL at the hill top

the contractor should anticipate significant over break of the sides of the trench

In general a granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended below the pipes Free draining
granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should
be used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide
adequate support around the pipes and to reduce compaction of the backfill thus
reducing the possibility of pipe damage
The upper portion of the trench backfill from a level 1 foot above the pipes to the
finished subgrade should consist of the approved on site soils or general fill material
The backfill material should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence The upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement grade should be
compacted to no less than 95 percent relative compaction Mechanical compaction
equipment should be used to compact the backfill materials at the project site
Compaction efforts by water tamping jetting or ponding should not be allowed
considering the expansive soil conditions at the site
In general we recommend providing a minimum cover of 3 feet between the top
of the utility pipes and the finished grades to facilitate construction of the pavement
structure and compaction of the trench backfill If the select granular fill is placed before
the installation of underground utilities the contractor should exercise care during
backfilling operations to replace the required thickness of select granular fill in the
trench backfill
