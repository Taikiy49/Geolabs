PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a single family residence at 2130 University Avenue
in the Manoa area on the island of Oahu Hawaii Based on information provided to us
the proposed residence will consist of a two story structure with slab on grade
construction Construction of new driveway and utility lines are also planned for the
proposed project
Detailed grading plans for the project were not available at the time this report
was prepared Based on available information we anticipate that minor cuts and fills
of up to approximately 3 feet will be required to provide a level pad for the proposed
residence
Our previous experience in the Manoa area suggests that the project site may
be underlain by alluvial deposits over decomposed basalt rock For this project special
attention was given to the swelling characteristics of the near surface soils and their
potential impact on the future floor slabs supported on grade

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration and laboratory testing we believe that a shallow
foundation system consisting of spread and continuous footings bearing on the stiff in
situ soils and or compacted fills may be used to support the proposed residence
Laboratory tests performed on the near surface clayey soils indicate that the materials
generally have a high shrink swell potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations
Therefore we recommend that concrete slabs on grade be supported on a minimum
24 inch thick layer of non expansive capping fill to reduce the effects of shrink swell of
the near surface expansive soils
Detailed discussions and recommendations for design of foundations site
grading pavements and other geotechnical aspects of the project are presented in the
following sections of this report

Foundations
Based on our field exploration and laboratory tests we believe that a shallow
foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be used to
support the proposed residence An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 p s f
may be used for the design of footing foundations bearing on the stiff in situ soils
and or compacted engineered fill materials This allowable bearing pressure is for dead
plus live loads and may be increased by one third for transient loadings such as
temporary wind and or seismic forces Footings should be embedded a minimum of
24 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade We estimate that the total footing
settlements under the anticipated design loads to be on the order of 1 inch with
differential settlements of approximately inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the footings and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings
A coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be used between the base of the footings and the
supporting materials Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f for
footings embedded in the in situ soils and or compacted engineered fill materials
These values assume that the soils around footings are well compacted or undisturbed
Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches
below finished grade should be neglected
It is recommended that the bottom of all footings bear on the in situ stiff
clayey silty soils or compacted engineered fill materials If soft and or loose materials
are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they should be over excavated
to expose firm materials The over excavation should either be backfilled with granular
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3360 00 Page 7
material compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the footing
bottom be extended to the undisturbed firm in situ soil
Footing excavations should not be allowed to dry before placing concrete If
shrinkage cracks appear in the footing excavations the soil should be thoroughly
moistened to close all cracks and recompacted prior to concrete placement
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the competency of the
bearing materials and the embedment depths
Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed
residence The near surface soils encountered at the proposed project site consist of
clayey soils which exhibit high expansion potential when subjected to fluctuations in
the moisture content Unless floor slabs supported by these soils are properly
designed there is a potential for future distress to lightly loaded slab on grade
construction resulting from the shrinking and swelling of these soils due to changes in
the moisture content
To reduce the potential of structural distress resulting from the swelling of the
on site expansive soils we recommend that a minimum of 24 inches of non expansive
capping fill material be provided below slabs on grade The non expansive capping fill
should extend a minimum of 2 feet laterally beyond the limits of the proposed building
structure
The non expansive capping fill should provide a capping layer of moist soil above
the underlying potentially expansive subgrade soil thereby reducing the loss of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3360 00 Page 8
moisture from the clayey subgrade soils In our opinion the reduction in potential
drying and wetting of the subgrade soils should reduce the distress on the slab due to
potential heaving of the underlying expansive clayey soils when wetted
The non expansive capping fill material should consist of imported granular
material such as crushed coral basalt gravel or cinder sand The material should be
well graded with no particles greater than 3 inches in size and should have a
laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR swell value not exceeding 1 percent when
prepared above the optimum moisture
In general we recommend that the capping fill material be placed in level lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage
of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
The non expansive capping fill should be placed as soon as possible after the
scarification and compaction of the underlying subgrade soils The subgrade should
be scarified to a depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction
Soil subgrades should be kept moist prior to placement of capping fill and or concrete
If shrinkage cracks appear in the subgrades the soil should be thoroughly moistened
to close all cracks and recompacted prior to capping fill or concrete placement
For more uniform support we recommend that slabs on grade be supported on
a 4 inch cushion layer consisting of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3360 00 Page 9
The gravel cushion layer should be placed above the non expansive capping fill and
would also serve as a capillary break The slab subgrade should be moistened prior
to concrete placement to allow the capping fill layer to remain in a moist condition To
reduce moisture infiltration through the slab a plastic vapor membrane may be
considered above the cushion layer A 2 inch thick moist fine sand layer should be
placed on the plastic vapor membrane to protect the membrane and to aid in the curing
of concrete
For concrete slabs that will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as driveways
and garage slabs we recommend that the slabs be supported on a 6 inch layer of base
reduced from 24 inches to 12 inches for concrete driveways and garage siabs The
thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved area should be embedded at least
12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade The base course should consist of crushed
basalt aggregate compacted to 95 percent relative compaction
Site Grading
We anticipate minor cuts and fills on the order of about 3 feet relative to the
existing ground surface may be required to achieve the design finish elevations for the
proposed project construction
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris and other unsuitable materials
should be removed and disposed of off site After clearing and grubbing all areas
beneath future slabs should be over excavated to a depth of at least of 24 inches below
the future slab subgrade level such as the bottom of gravel cushion layer If zones of
soft loose or saturated soils are encountered at the over excavated subgrade level
additional over excavation may be required to expose firm material The resulting
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3360 00 Page 10
excavation should be backfilled with imported non expansive capping fill material The
clayey material resulting from the over excavation should be removed and properly
disposed of off site
Areas to receive fills should be scarified to a depth of 8 inches moisture
conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted
to at least 90 percent relative compaction Fill material should be placed in level lifts
not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above optimum
moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction Any
imported fill material should be tested and approved for its intended use by Geolabs
Hawaii prior to delivery to the project site
Drainage
The finished grades outside the house should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the house and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving because of the swelling potential of the near
surface soils Saturation of the near surface soils can also cause settlement due to
softening of the soils The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled against
slab footings immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water infiltration
GEOLABS HAWAII

Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations
Pavements
Based on the current design concept rigid concrete pavement is anticipated for
the access driveway We anticipate the vehicle loading for this project will consist
primarily of passenger vehicles and light pick up trucks We have made our preliminary
pavement designs assuming the pavement subgrade soil will be similar to the clayey
soils encountered in the borings with a minimum CBR of 5 and a swell value of less
than 4 percent Based on the above assumptions we recommend the following
pavement section be used for preliminary design purposes
Rigid Portland Cement Concrete Pavement
6 0 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course 95 percent relative compaction
12 0 Inch Subbase Course 95 percent relative compaction
24 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Moist Compacted Subgrade
The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction
The subbase and base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction In general select borrow
material may be used for the subbase course provided it meets the requirements
specified in Section 30 of the Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction
City and County of Honolulu CBR and density tests should be performed on the actual
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3360 00 Page 12
subgrade soils encountered during construction to confirm the adequacy of the above
section
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed anywhere on
the site during or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate
landscaping in or adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend the curbs extend a
minimum of 2 inches into the subgrade below the aggregate base course or subbase
to reduce migration of landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively a
subdrain system could be constructed to collect excessive water from landscaping
irrigation For long term performance we recommend a subdrain system be
constructed adjacent to paved landscape areas
Utility Trench
We envision that new utility connections may be required for the project A
granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C _ 33
No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials
such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also be used for
the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support
around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the
possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material
The backfill should be placed in about 8 inch loose level lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence The on site materials may be used as backfill materials
however it may be difficult to attain the required compaction due to the moderately high
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 3360 00 Page 13
in situ moisture contents Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet
of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to not less
than 95 percent relative compaction

Field Exploration

three borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The depths of our borings ranged from about 15 to

testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the
materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate sample depths
