INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed sewer reconstruction project along
River Street and Nimitz Highway in downtown Honolulu on the island of
Oahu Hawaii The general location of the proposed sewer line alignments
is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses of the
data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are intended
for design input and not intended for construction estimate or bidding
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct two 2 new major sewer lines Sewer
Lines 1 and 2 to replace the existing smaller diameter sewers along River
Street between Nimitz Highway and Kukui Street and also along Nimitz
Highway
The proposed alignment of Sewer Line 1 extends along River Street
between Hotel Street and Nimitz Highway then south along Nimitz Highway
to Smith Street The proposed invert elevations will be approximately
below the existing ground The new sewer pipes will have diameters
ranging between 18 and 30 inches
The proposed alignment of Sewer Line 2 extends along the Chinese
Cultural Plaza Mall next to Nuuanu Stream and along River Street between
Beretania Street and Kukui Street The proposed invert elevations will be
approximately between 1 5 and 2 5 feet MSL or at depths of about 8 5 to
9 5 feet below the existing ground The new sewer pipes will have
diameters ranging between 18 and 24 inches

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
General
It is our opinion that the proposed replacement sewers may be
constructed along the explored alignments provided our geotechnical
engineering recommendations presented in this report are closely followed
Since the proposed sewer lines will be constructed amid existing
high density urban development the selection of appropriate construction
procedures good workmanship and a proper construction monitoring program
are essential for the successful execution of this project The
above mentioned are vital to reduce the risk of distress or damage to the
existing adjacent structures and provide satisfactory future perfomnance
of the proposed sewer lines
A major portion of the proposed sewer lines and the affiliated
manhole structures will be supported by the underlying soft loose
subsoils Based on our exploration data the calculated settlements of

It is also anticipated that there will be some differential

conditions i e hard coral and soft loose deposits Therefore we

proper dewatering are required for the installation of the new sewer

Earth Pressure Loads on Pipe
Loads on buried conduits are influenced by the width of trench the
size of pipes the unit weight of backfill material and the frictional
resistance between backfill material and trench wall To calculate the
vertical loads on the buried sewer pipes an average unit weight of 120
p c f for backfill material and a coefficient of friction of 0 25 are
recommended Earth forces upon a pipe increase rapidly with the width of
trench and therefore the width of the sewer trenches should be kept toa
In addition traffic loads on the buried pipes should also be
considered since the proposed sewer alignments are located within road
rights of way

Settlements
Primary settlement of the sewer structures is normally caused by the
difference in unit weight between the lighter excavated original earth and
the heavier new backfill over the structures The net increase in loading
will cause primary consolidation or settlement of the underlying subsoils
below trench invert Based on our estimate primary settlement up to two
2 inches is anticipated along the portions of the proposed sewer
alignments underlain by soft loose subsoils below the natural groundwater
level
Due to the organic content of some underlying deposits additional
long term settlement on the order of up to half an inch may also result
from secondary consolidation of the subsoils along portions of the
proposed alignments over the expected life of the new sewer structures
Differential settlement between the portions of the proposed
alignments spanning between subsoils of different competency or density
i e hard coral and soft clay should also be an important design
consideration In order to reduce abrupt differential settlements in the
pipes at these transition areas we recommend that an additional 12 inches
of cushion should be placed below the pipe bearing on hard coral within
15 feet of the transition between hard coral and soft clay
The above estimates assume proper construction procedures and good
workmanship Additional settlement could occur as a result of improper
trench support or dewatering procedures
Therefore we believe that the proposed sewer lines should be
designed with sufficient flexibility to accommodate all these possible
settlement conditions

Alternately light weight backfill material such as cinder sand may
be used to reduce the difference in unit weights between the backfill and
the in situ subsoils
Stockpiling of excavated material adjacent to trench excavations
should be strictly prohibited in order to reduce the potential of adjacent
ground movement or settlements
Pipe Bedding
Distribution of bearing stresses against the bottom of a sewer pipe
has a great impact upon the load carrying capacity of the pipe itself
Pipe bedding is therefore an important design consideration
Due to the prevailing groundwater levels above the proposed sewer
inverts and the soft loose underlying subsoil conditions encountered in
most areas we recommend that a two foot thick crushed rock cradle be used
to stabilize the bottom of the sewer trenches and provide a working
platform for the sewer pipe installation The rock cradle material should
consist of clean granular material such as 3B Fine gravel and be
brought up to within six 6 inches of the invert grade or to the bottom
of any concrete jacket or cradle
We also recommend that clean granular material such as 3B Fine
gravel be used as the bedding material for the proposed sewers A
minimum of six 6 inches of compacted granular bedding material should be
placed below the new sewer pipes and the granular bedding should extend
beyond the pipes a minimum width of one fourth the outside pipe diameter
and also half way up both sides of the pipes The recommended bedding
detail is illustrated on Plate 3
Where hard coral ledge or dense reef detritus is encountered at the
bedding subgrade level the two foot rock cradle cushion may be deleted
below the pipe bedding

Trench Excavation
Based on the current design concept the anticipated trench
excavation depths vary from about 12 to 15 feet below the existing ground
surface along the proposed sewer alignments
Relatively easy trench excavation is anticipated for the replacement
sewer installation except along the southerly portion of Sewer Line 1 on
Nimitz Highway where hard ripping or special excavating methods may be
required to remove coral ledges and or reef detritus
The method and equipment used for excavation should be the choice of
the contractor within practical limits and safety considerations
Due to load considerations discussed in the preceding sections the
width of the sewer trench excavations should be kept to a minimm to
reduce the potential of significant ground settlements
In addition excavated material should not be stockpiled within 15
feet of the excavated trenches in order to reduce the potential of
adjacent ground movement or subsidence which may damage existing adjacent
structures
Trench Support
Since soft loose subsoils are anticipated along most of the proposed
sewer alignments we recommend that continuous steel sheetpile walls with
cross bracing be used to support the trench excavations
It is essential that properly interlocked steel sheeting be installed
prior to trench excavation and be maintained tight against the trench
walls with proper bracing to reduce the potential of adjacent ground loss
or movement which may jeopardize adjacent existing structures This
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2044 00 Page 12
consideration is particularly critical since most if not all of the
existing adjacent structures are older low rise buildings which may be
supported on spread footings bearing on the upper subsoils
The steel sheeting should be driven properly with a suitable
sheetpile hammer to sufficient depth to improve the stability of trench
bottom and to reduce the possibility of bottom heaving or quick
conditions which may be created by upward seepage pressure during
dewatering Based on our estimate the sheeting should be driven to a
minimum penetration depth of 11 feet below the crushed rock cradle cushion
or the trench bottom along the proposed alignment of Sewer Line 1 and a
minimum penetration depth of 7 feet along the proposed alignment of Sewer
Line 1 for this purpose A factor of safety of 1 5 is assumed for
determining the design sheetpile penetrations
The required depths of sheetpile penetration will also be affected by
the method of dewatering The above recommended sheetpile penetration
depths are based on the assumption that there is no groundwater drawdown
outside of the sheeting If a deep well system is used to create
groundwater drawdown outside the sheeting the depths of sheetpile
penetration may be reduced However care must be taken to prevent bottom
heaving or piping when pumping from the deep well system is stopped
The sheeting and bracing system used must comply with all applicable
safety requirements The adequacy and safety of the sheeting and bracing
installation operations should be made the responsibility of the
contractor since his representative will be continually present on site
and has the opportunity for observing changing conditions during
construction which might affect the trench support stability
Where hard coral ledge and or dense reef detritus are encountered
during trench excavation and or sheetpile driving the steel sheeting may
be eliminated or the required penetration depths may be reduced to refusal

into the hard coral ledge or dense reef detritus provided a suitable
sheetpile driving hammer such as a vibratory hammer is used to drive the
steel sheetpiles The adequacy and safety of the sheeting and bracing
installation operations under these circumstances should also be made the
responsibility of the contractor
For calculating earth loads in the struts of the trench bracing the

Appropriate loads due to surcharge traffic or sloping ground

Even with good construction procedure some movement of the trench
support bracing system might still occur because of changes in earth
stresses during excavation Movements depend greatly on workmanship for
example how quickly and tightly the support is installed It also
depends on the types of subsoils the depth and width of excavation and
the type of support In view of the complexity of the problem it is
difficult to accurately predict movement of a bracing system and its
adjacent ground Therefore it is important to realize that the trench
support should be installed as early as practicable and that measurements
of vertical and horizontal movements are useful especially in areas where
ground movements may result in damage to the existing adjacent structures
The ground adjacent to the trench excavations should be continuously
monitored for cracks or dips and other indications of movement and
possible sloughing of the trench walls Such monitoring is particularly
critical in areas adjacent to existing structures and utilities

Dewatering
Dewatering will be necessary since the groundwater levels are
generally above the proposed sewer pipe inverts throughout the alignments
and the subsoils immediately below trench bottom may consist mostly of
loose sand and gravel The granular subsoils are relatively permeable and
may be susceptible to piping and quick conditions Therefore
dewatering operations should be done without pumping out soil fines
pumping clear water only and should be coordinated with the trench
shoring support so that the excavation stability are not jeopardized The
operations should be carried out without creating a quick condition or
softening the bottom of the excavations
The method and equipment to be used for the dewatering operations
should be the choice of the contractor within practical limits and safety
considerations It should be realized that the dewatering system chosen
will have an effect on the penetration depth of sheeting used
We suggest that the following three 3 basic selection criteria be
considered in determining a suitable method of dewatering
a The method should impose the least disturbance or damage to the
adjacent existing development
b The method should maintain adequate excavation stability and
provide safe working conditions
c The method should be sufficiently flexible to allow modification
to accommodate various ground conditions
Dewatering by means of a well point system along the outside of the
trench excavations could be used This method could reduce the required
sheetpile penetration However the resultant areal depression of the
water table could induce consolidation of subsoils or settlements below
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2044 00 Page 15
the adjacent existing structures Therefore recharging inj ecting water
back into the subsoils to balance the natural groundwater levels and
minimize areal drawdown in areas near the adjacent existing structures
may be required to reduce the risk of settlement to these buildings
Alternately it appears that sump pumping inside the trenches may be
used if the sheeting is driven sufficiently deep to prevent piping and
heaving of the bottom of the trenches
Backfilling
The trench excavations should be backfilled as soon as the proposed
sewer pipes are properly bedded and installed In general the trench
backfill requirements should follow the Standard Specifications for Public
Works Construction City and County of Honolulu September 1986
The first zone of backfill extends from the bedding material to at
least 12 inches above the top of the sewer pipes Select free draining
well graded granular material is recommended for the first zone of
backfill to minimize the compaction effort required and resulting stresses
to the pipe The granular material should be well graded less than three
3 inches in size and have no more than 10 passing No 200 sieve
Compaction by water ponding or jetting may be allowed provided the
granular material is self draining
The trench backfill from 12 inches above the top of the pipes to
within two 2 feet of the finished road grade may consist of the
excavated on site soils except organic clays and silts The backfill
should be mechanically compacted to a minimm 90 of its maximm dry
density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The trench backfill from within two 2 feet of the finished road
grade to subgrade level should consist of select borrow compacted to a
minimum 95 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78

Joints and Connections
Due to the variable subsoil conditions along the proposed sewer
alignments we recommend that flexible pipe joints be used to connect the
sewer pipes to allow for some amount of differential settlement These
flexible connections are particularly important in the transition areas
between the soft loose subsoil areas and the more competent coral areas
Manhole Structures
It is anticipated that new manholes will be constructed on soft loose
subsoils The proposed manhole structures will not materially increase
the loading on the underlying subsoils due to the earth removal required
to construct the embedded structures Therefore minimal settlements are
anticipated for these structures if proper shoring and dewatering
operations are used during construction
However it is our opinion that an 18 inch working platform with 3B
Fine gravel should be provided below the proposed manhole structures for
uniform ground support An allowable bearing capacity of 1500 p s f may
be used to design the proposed manhole structures bearing on the 18 inch
gravel platform

W O 2044 00 Page 17
Where hard coral ledge and or dense reef detritus are encountered at
the manhole invert level the 18 inch gravel platform may be eliminated or
reduced in thickness to 6 inches depending on the hardness or density of
the coral material
The lateral earth pressure acting on the proposed underground
structures will depend on the type of backfill to be used the extent of
backfill and the compactive effort on the backfill material around the
structures Therefore granular material such as select on site sand and
gravel is recommended for use as backfill around the structures to reduce

earth pressures We recommend that the new manholes be designed to resist
the following lateral earth pressure provided granular backfill is used
1 Above water level 50 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
2 Below water level 25 p c f equivalent fluid pressure plus
