PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves site grading and utility installation for the Kapolei Parkway
Extension from the Sun Terra Subdivision to the wastewater pump station with a length of
about 2 300 linear feet The site is west of Fort Weaver Road south of Geiger Road and
north of the Haseko Development area A Project Location Map and a Site Plan are shown
on Plates 1 and 2 respectively The site grading work will involve cuts and fills generally
less than 6 feet thick
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the site grading operations
SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
Geolabs Inc observed the site grading and utility installation work for the Kapolei
Parkway Extension project from to The site
grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills on
the order of up to about 6 feet thick to achieve the design finished grades for the project
Royal Contracting Ltd performed the site grading and utility installation work During the
site grading and utility installation operations samples of the on site and imported
materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office
for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established

The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill
and backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative density
During the site grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method Compaction test results
are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 45 of this report These tests were performed on
the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills Test locations and
elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These
locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified
minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on
visual observation and hand probing
During the site grading work three subsurface cavities were encountered at the
project site The first cavity was encountered along the drain line from about
Station No 59 75 to 59 90 at about Elevation 15 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The cavity
was about 15 feet by 8 feet in plan dimension and about 18 feet deep The second cavity
was about 12 feet by 12 feet in plan dimension and about 18 feet deep and was
encountered at a depth of about 5 feet from the existing ground surface along Ditch 1 at
about Station No 3 00 The third cavity was encountered along the box culvert at about
210 feet right of Kapolei Parkway at about Station No 70 75 The cavity was about 6 feet
by 6 feet in plan dimension and about 10 feet deep The top of the cavity was encountered
at about Elevation 12 feet MSL The cavities were backfilled with surge rock and
compacted fill material
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
