This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing
services performed during site work construction for the Waikiki Beach Walk Development
project in general accordance with our fee proposal dated August 31 2004
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Waikiki Beach Walk project site is in the Waikiki area of Honolulu on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is bounded by Kalakaua Avenue to the east
Lewers Street to the south Kalia Road to the west and Beach Walk to the north We
understand that it is planned to develop the project site into a mixed use development
with the majority of the new development to be used as a retail facility
The retail facility will consist of a two story retail complex area in the central
portion of the facility and a three level parking structure and storage area at the
southwest portion of the project site A fourth level consisting of a pool area meeting
rooms and a pavilion are planned above the parking structure area A driveway and
loading area is planned at the northwest portion of the project site
The finished floor elevations of the new structures will be set at 5 25 and
7 00 feet Mean Sea Level MSL Therefore we envision that some fills on the order of
about 1 to 3 feet relative to the existing road grades may be required Because some of
the existing buildings to be demolished consist of partial basements the partial
basements will need to be backfilled to attain the finished floor elevations and design
finished grades outside of the building structure footprint This report addresses our field
observations and compaction testing services pertaining to the earthwork construction for
the Waikiki Beach Walk Development project only

EARTHWORK CONSTRUCTION
Geolabs Inc observed the earthwork construction for the Waikiki Beach Walk
Development project from to The earthwork construction
generally consisted of clearing and grubbing placement and compaction of fills to achieve
the design finished grades for the project excavation and backfilling of pile caps and grade
beams subgrade preparation of slabs on grade and installation of underground utility
lines Nuprecon Demolition Specialists demolition contractor and JJS Construction
grading contractor performed the earthwork operations
Samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials
were obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were generally moisture conditioned to above
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway and parking
areas the last lift of fill and backfill materials were generally compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the earthwork operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method The compaction test
results performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 25 of this report These tests
were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills
Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site
survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were generally
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our
field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
The imported fill material select borrow from Grace Pacific Corporations s Makakilo
Quarry placed between the area bounded by Grids 14 21 B and E at an elevation of
about 4 5 feet MSL did not meet the compaction requirement The grading contractor
was not able to recompact the fill material due to the Auger Cast In Place Pile construction
work occurring in the area The imported fill material was contaminated from the drilling
and grouting spoils generated during the ACIP pile construction work Therefore the
grading contractor removed and replaced the contaminated fill material at later dates The
new fill material was compacted to meet the compaction requirement
