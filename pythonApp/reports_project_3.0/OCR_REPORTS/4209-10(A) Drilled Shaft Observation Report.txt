In accordance with our revised fee proposal dated March has
provided geotechnical engineering observation and consultation services during the drilled
shaft foundation excavation for the signage and graphics improvement project located within
the Kona International Airport on the Island of Hawaii This report summarizes our
geotechnical engineering observations performed during the drilled shaft excavations for the
above referenced project
A geotechnical engineering exploration was performed by Geolabs Inc and our
recommendations were presented in the report entitled Geotechnical Engineering
Exploration Statewide Signage and Graphics Kona International Airport Keahole North
Kona Island of Hawaii dated The purpose of our geotechnical engineering
services was to observe the drilled shaft foundation excavation for general conformance with
the project plans and specifications
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the existing Kona International Airport at Keahole in
the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Based on the information
provided a total of five 5 new overhead cantilever signs will be installed at various locations
along the terminal building to improve the signage and graphics at the airport premises
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the site and the structural loads
provided we have recommended that either shallow spread footings or drilled shaft
foundations 5 foot diameter with an embedment length of 6 feet be used to support the
proposed new signage planned for the project In order to facilitate the project construction

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on the field exploration performed in 1999 the project site is underlain by a
surface layer of silty sands and gravel of approximately 1 5 to 2 5 feet thick The surface layer
was underlain by hard basalt rock formation interbedded with some thin clinker layers
extending to the maximum depth drilled of approximately 25 feet below the existing ground
surface Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field
exploration However groundwater levels can fluctuate depending on factors such as
seasonal rainfall groundwater withdrawal and or injection and other factors
PRODUCTION DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION
The installation of the production shafts was performed by DeLima s Drilling A total
installed from March 27 to 30 and on May 1 and 2 2001 The approximate locations of the
five drilled shafts are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A representative from Geolabs
observed the excavation process during the installation of these production shafts
Observation of the steel reinforcement placement and drilled shaft concreting were specifically
excluded from our scope of work for this project Due to the limited number of drilled shafts
for this project a test shaft and or a load test was not specified in the contract documents In
addition the foundation excavation work was conducted between the hours of 10 30 P M and
4 00 A M due to the restrictions imposed by the airport management
The production shafts were excavated using a Watson 3000 track mounted drill rig
equipped with a 36 inch diameter core barrel The production shafts were excavated to depths
ranging between about 10 and 10 5 feet below the design finish grades Based on our field
observations unraveled clinker materials at Sign 4 C 10 and standing water from drilling at
Sign 6 C 10 were noted at the bottom of these shafts which required further clean up of the
bottom prior to the placement of the steel reinforcement Logs of the production shafts and
the materials encountered during the shaft excavation are provided on Plates 3 1 through 7 2
Based on our field observations we believe that the five drilled shafts for the new
signage support have been installed in general conformance with the project plans and
specifications and within the limits of generally accepted construction practices Based on our
observations of the drilled shaft installation it is our opinion that the drilled shafts have been
installed to the required diameter and depth to provide the design load capacity
