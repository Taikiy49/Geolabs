Brief Project History
The project site is located at the eastern corner of the intersection between
Wakea and Haumea Streets in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
Generally the parcel is situated to the south of Campbell Square Bank of Hawaii and
Kapolei Public Library
Geolabs conducted a geotechnical engineering exploration at the site and issued
a report entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Academex School at Kapolei
Kapolei Oahu Hawaii dated Due to the expansive clay soils
encountered in our exploration we recommended special foundation site preparation
including moisture conditioning of the clay soils and capping with a minimum of
18 inches of non expansive select granular fill
The first phase of the school construction involved the two story concrete
classroom building along Haumea Street We were not involved in the construction of
this portion of the project Therefore we cannot confirm the details of the compaction
testing subgrade soil preparation or foundation construction

The second phase of the school construction involved the three story
administration building at the northeastern portion of the site Geolabs Inc observed
the site grading and performed compaction testing for this phase of the work
We understand that sometime after completion signs of foundation slab
movement were noticed in the Phase 1 building The signs of the movement consisted
of lifting portions of the concrete slab cracks in the drywalls and CMU walls sticking
doors and cracking of some large glass windows on the first floor level adjacent to the
driveway
Geolabs was asked to confirm evaluate the foundation s movements It was
decided to conduct an initial level survey on the first and second level floors to confirm
the magnitude and trends of the movements A hydrostatic altimeter level was used to
measure relative elevation differences from a reference point on the floor
In our letter report entitled Floor Level Survey Island Pacific Academy Phase 1
Kapolei Oahu Hawaii dated we summarized the results of our
initial floor level survey and recommended three alternatives for remedial work
Stabilization of the underlying expansive clay soils using a chemical stabilizer to modify
the clay soils and reduce their swell potential was selected Kelikai Inc was contracted
to perform the chemical stabilization We understand that the chemical injection of the
clay soils beneath Classroom Nos 109 and 110 was completed in June 2015 Chemical
injection of the clay soils around the outside perimeter of the classroom building and
beneath Classroom 108 and 111 was completed in August 2016
Geolabs was not contacted during the chemical injection work and therefore cannot
confirm the extent or details of this work
After our site visit on we recommended that further injection of
the chemical stabilizer be delayed so the effectiveness of the chemical treatment on the
underlying clay soils could be evaluated Geolabs recommended conducting
two additional sets of floor level surveys on the first and second floors to evaluate if the
injection remedial measure is effective in reducing ground movements
In our letter report entitled Additional Floor Level Surveys Island Pacific
Academy Phase 1 Kapolei Oahu Hawaii dated we summarized the
results of the two additional floor level surveys used to evaluate the effectiveness of the
chemical injection remediation Based on the first floor survey readings Geolabs
recommended that additional chemical stabilization work not be done unless future
differential movement of the first floor level is observed and confirmed The second floor
readings generally indicated settlement primarily along through 4 and
Gridlines A through E The maximum measured settlement reading on the first floor
level was about 1 inch while the maximum measured settlement reading on the
second floor was approximately 4 inches The discrepancy may be due to structural
and or construction issues Therefore Geolabs recommended that the project structural
engineer inspect and evaluate the condition of the existing building

A walkthrough inspection of the existing structure was conducted by the project
structural engineer of Island Pacific Academy and Geolabs on The
project structural engineer concluded that the condition of the second floor structure
appeared satisfactory and suggested that the measured settlements are primarily due to
the movement of the column footings
After the walkthrough inspection Island Pacific Academy personnel observed
signs of additional building distress in the form of cracked windows in the eastern
portion of the elementary school Geolabs conducted a floor level survey on
to evaluate the extent and magnitude of the differential movement
compared to the previous floor level readings In our letter report entitled Floor Level
Survey Conducted on Island Pacific Academy Phase 1 Kapolei
Oahu Hawaii dated we summarized the results of the floor level
survey and presented recommendations for remediation In general the floor level
readings indicated that either the footings settled or the concrete slabs heaved it is
also possible that both occurred simultaneously recommended
installing exterior grade beams and sidewalks around the perimeter of the building to
stiffen the exterior foundations and protect the subgrade from water infiltration
In November 2019 we understand an additional floor level survey was requested
due to observed signs of distress in Room Nos 108 and 109 It should be noted that the
installation of the exterior grade beams and sidewalks was not performed yet This floor
level survey focused on these two classrooms on the first floor of the elementary school
building The readings were used to evaluate if significant additional ground movements
occurred since our last set of measurements in December 2017 and examine the
differential movement between the first floor slab and the column footings In our letter
report entitled Floor Lever Survey conducted on Island Pacific
Academy Phase 1 Kapolei Oahu Hawaii dated we summarized
the results of the floor level survey in Room Nos 108 and 109 and presented
recommendations for remediation In general the floor level readings indicated that the
ground surface has dropped by 0 3 to 0 4 inches near the exterior column footing In our
report we recommended providing positive drainage away from the footing and
installation of grade beams and sidewalks to stiffen the exterior building foundation and
reduce water infiltration
We understand that the exterior grade beams and sidewalks around the
perimeter of the building were constructed sometime between December 2019 and
October 2021 Additional floor level surveys were requested to evaluate the
effectiveness of the remediation measure with respect to mitigating soil and foundation
movement Because the exterior grade beams and sidewalks tie into the building slabs
and foundations we felt that the conditions warranted taking new baseline readings of
the first and second floors Therefore the floor level survey conducted in October 2021
was used as the new baseline measurement A follow up floor level survey was
conducted in March 2022

Results of the Ground Floor Level Survey and Observations
The floor level survey was performed using a hydrostatic altimeter that utilizes a
pressurized system that measures changes in elevation between a determined datum
zero location and a point of interest A datum location was established at the building
entrance for the first floor level and in the faculty breakroom area for the second floor
level readings The same datums were used for both sets of reading
Contour maps of the first and second floors are presented on Plates 1 1 through
1 4 Cross sections comparing the relative change in profile elevation of both surveys
performed for the first and second floors are presented on Plates 2 1 through 2 6 and
3 1 through 3 6 respectively
The results of the floor level survey measured a maximum elevation difference of
about 1 inch on the first and second floors between October 2021 and March 2022
readings Note that some of the elevation differences may be attributed to construction
tolerances
Findings and Recommendations
Based on the results of the level surveys and our observations our findings and
recommendations are provided below
In general the difference between the level survey readings for the first and
second floors conducted in October 2021 and March 2022 were generally on the order
of 0 2 inches or less Elevation differences up to about 1 inch were observed on the
second floor in Room No 205 the March 2022 readings were about 1 inch higher than
the October 2021 readings However the movement across the floor of Room No 205
was relatively uniform with relatively low differential movement
Based on our observations it appears that the exterior grade beam and sidewalk
improvements have helped mitigate the total and differential movement of the building
foundations and slabs Island Pacific Academy should notify Geolabs if additional signs
of movement such as sticking doors or cracked windows are observed
Limitations
Our findings and recommendations are based on visual observations and level
survey data and previous subsurface explorations only The actual conditions below the
building may vary
Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of
geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied
