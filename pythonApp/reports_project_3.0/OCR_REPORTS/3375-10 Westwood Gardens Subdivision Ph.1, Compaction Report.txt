EARTHWORK OBSERVATIONS
The earthwork operations were performed by Kiewit Pacific Company Prior to
filling operations the exposed subgrades to receive fill were scarified brought to near
the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with American Society of Testing
and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
The fill materials needed for the Phase 1 earthwork were imported from the
Mililani Mauka stockpile site Samples of the imported materials utilized as fill materials
were obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are
summarized on Plate 2 of this report
During the fill operation the fill materials were generally placed in near level lifts
brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 1557 Modified Proctor Periodic field density tests were taken during the fill
operations to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted
A total of 14 field density tests were performed using the nuclear probe method
in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 The results of the compaction
tests taken during the house pad grading operations are summarized on Plate 3 of this
report These tests were taken on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed
engineered fills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed
to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained

POST EARTHWORK CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
Based on a site visit by our field personnel on some minor
cracks were noted on the compacted finish subgrades Based on the laboratory test
results the imported silty soils from the Mililani Mauka stockpile site generally have a
slight shrink swell potential when subjected to fluctuations in the moisture content
Because of the relatively dry weather conditions at the project site we believe that the
cracks observed at the ground surface are shrinkage cracks due to drying of the near
surface soils To reduce the potential for development of shrinkage cracks of the
finished subgrade the moisture content of the subgrades should be maintained such
as by sprinkling water on the surface
To provide a firm base for house construction we recommend that the finish
subgrade be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks and recompacted prior to house
construction In addition based on our field observations compaction was not
performed for the backfill in a strip area next to the retaining wall as indicated in our
field report dated We recommend that compaction be performed
on the backfill materials in this area in accordance with the specified criteria To reduce
potential for over stressing the retaining wall due to compaction light equipment should
be used for compaction
We also recommend that proper surface drainage be maintained at the site for
good performance of the house foundations Good drainage requirements are essential
for the proper performance of the foundation support system since ponded water could
cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength
