Dear Mr. Goto: 
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing 
services performed during the installation of the synthetic lawn for the Aulani Event 
Lawn Renovation project on May 19, 2017 in accordance with our fee proposal dated 
May 25, 2017. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located makai of Aliinui Drive in the Ko Olina area on the 
Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  The site is adjacent to the Aulani Resort Hotel.   
The site grading work consisted of the compaction of fill materials below the base 
panels for the synthetic lawn, and concrete walkway and pavement slabs.  Our office 
was not provided an opportunity to observe and conduct compaction testing on the fill 
materials placed below the concrete walkway and pavement slabs. Therefore, this 
report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for only the fill 
material below the base panels for the synthetic lawn. 
EARTHWORK OBSERVATION & COMPACTION TESTING 
We observed the site grading work for the fill materials below the synthetic lawn 
for the Aulani Event Lawn Renovation on May 19, 2017.  Fills consisting of S4C material 
from the Makaklio Quarry were placed and compacted to achieve the finished grades.  
Total Landscape Management performed the site grading work. During the site grading 
operations, a sample of the imported S4C material utilized as fill material was obtained 
and transported to our office for testing. The maximum dry density and optimum

The fill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture

During the site grading, field density tests were performed in general accordance 
with ASTM D 6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). Results of the compaction tests performed 
are summarized on Plate 4. These tests were performed on the prepared ground 
surfaces and on constructed fills at the site. Test locations and elevations were 
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These locations and 
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods 
used. Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum 
compaction was obtained and/or approved by our field representative based on visual 
observation and hand probing. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill 
placement and compaction procedures during the above grading operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representative and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the site grading work for the fill materials below 
the synthetic lawn base panels for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in 
the period covered by this report were performed in conformance with the general intent 
of the recommendations in the following references. 
1. 
Chapter 14 of the Revised Ordinances of Honolulu 1990, as amended. 
2. 
Aulani Event Lawn Renovation project drawings dated May 3, 2017. 
LIMITATIONS 
Geolabs assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or finished 
grades on the above project subsequent to our observation and testing period without 
our knowledge and written acknowledgment. 
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities 
occurring simultaneously on a construction site; therefore, some work may have 
occurred without our observation. However, we believe that the level of earthwork 
observation and testing services provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical 
risks to the project. It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and 
statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork 
construction and materials. Our services were performed in accordance with generally
