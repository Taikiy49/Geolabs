GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING EVALUATION
EWA WATER SYSTEM
HONOULIULI WELL AND 4
In accordance with your request we have performed a geotechnical
engineering evaluation for the above referenced project The
approximate location of the project site is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general accordance
with our proposal dated August 8 1988
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to evaluate the subsurface
conditions below the site to provide pertinent soil data to aid in the
site grading and foundation design of the project
The scope of our evaluation include field reconnaissance drilling
and sampling of six 6 borings laboratory testing of selected soil
samples engineering analysis and report preparation
W O 2120 00 Page 2
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed improvements include the following
1 A new 30 inch water line
2 A new control building approximately 30 by 75 feet in plan
near Well No 3 About 4 feet of fill is planned to raise the
building site
3 An access road from the control building for the deep Well Pump
No 1 to the new control building Most of the new access road
will follow an existing cane haul road and require minimal
grading However a portion of the new road will cross the
gulch and require a culvert and fill
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located near Honouliuli Gulch about 5000 feet
north of the H 1 Freeway
Most of the water line alignment follows existing dirt trails or
haul roads except where the new road crosses Honouliuli Gulch The
side walls of the gulch slope down at a ratio of about H V to
and is heavily vegetated The remainder of the site is generally
clear

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling six
borings to depths ranging from 7 0 to 20 0 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
Based on our borings the subsoil conditions at the site generally
consist of stiff clayey silt residual soil with boulders grading to
weathered rock formation at depths
Groundwater was not encountered in any of the borings during our
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In general the results of our exploration indicate that the
near surface soil at the site can provide sufficient support for the
proposed water line and related improvements
Water Main Trench Section
The water line trench section presented on Plate 3 is recommended
The subgrade soil should be thoroughly tamped prior to the placement of
the pipe and bedding material
To minimize the required compactive effort and possible damage to
the pipe the backfill around and up to 1 foot above the pipe should
consist of clean granular material such as sand and should be lightly
tamped or jetted as appropriate The remaining portion of the backfill
can consist of on site soil compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum
density as established by the ASTM D 1557 78 test method
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2120 00 Page 4
Settlements under the recommended bearing pressure are anticipated
to be minimal
Water Main Trench Construction
Trench Excavation
The method and equipment used for excavation should be the choice
of the contractor within practical limits The anticipated depth
of excavation is 5 to 10 feet
Relatively hard excavation is anticipated in the lower portions of
the trench where boulders and weathered rock formation may be
encountered
Trench Wall Supports
The excavation should be provided with proper bracing in accordance
with applicable safety requirements The design and safety of the
trench supports should be the responsibility of the contractor
If sufficient space is available the excavation may be cut back at
a temporary slope of 1 1 H V in lieu of the above wall supports
However it should be taken into consideration that greater amounts
of vertical pressure will be transmitted to the pipe by widening of
the trench
Heavy equipment and stockpiles should be kept away from the tops of
the open trenches
The ground adjacent to and near the top of the excavation should be
continuously monitored for cracks or dips and other indications of
movements and possible sloughing of the trench walls Such
monitoring is particularly critical in areas where existing
structures are adjacent to the trench excavation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2120 00 Page 5
Backfill
Granular material such as sand should be used for the backfill up
to about one foot above the pipe to reduce the compactive effort
required and possible damage to the pipe
Most of the excavated on site clayey silt soils excluding boulders
can be re used as the upper portion of the trench backfill from
above one foot over the pipe to the finished grade Backfill
should be mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 Due to the fine grained
characteristics of the on site soil water ponding or jetting of the
backfill would not be effective and should not be used
A typical section of the pipe trench is presented on Plate 3
Reaction Blocks
It is our understanding that concrete reaction blocks will be
required at the pipe bends The reaction blocks should be constructed
against the stiff undisturbed clayey silt However if the block is
located in loose backfill the loose material should be over excavated
laterally at least 24 inches around the block except next to the pipe
and then backfilled with select borrow or suitable granular material
approved by the soils engineer The subsequent backfill should be
placed in horizontal lifts of 8 inches maximum and compacted to a
minimum density of 90 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
For the design of the reaction blocks an ultimate uniform lateral
bearing of 4000 p s f may be used for the stiff in situ clayey silt
soil A suitable factor of safety should be used in the reaction block
design

Site Grading
Significant filling is limited to the drainageway crossing between
Stations 4 00 and 5 00 and the new control building site
Site Preparation
The site to be graded should be cleared of all vegetation organic
matter debris and soft material
Material and Compaction
Excavated on site material smaller than 6 inches may be used for
fill If required import fill should consist of select granular
material such as base course or select borrow The backfill
should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 78
For the proposed hillside cuts near Stations 0 00 to 4 00 stiff
residual is anticipated
The recommended cut slope ratios are as follows
Cut slopes up to 10 feet high 1 1 H v
Cut slopes in excess of 10 feet high H V
For fill slopes a slope ratio of 2 1 H V is recommended
A steeper fill slope of up to H V may be used if a grouted
riprap lining is placed on the slope face
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2120 00 Page 7
Slope benches are recommended for slopes in excess of 20 feet in
vertical height
Drainage Culvert
A 95 by 67 inch CMP arch culvert is planned near Station 4 50 and
about 8 feet of fill is planned over the culvert structure
In order to provide sufficient compaction around the culvert and
minimize possible damage to the culvert due to compaction operations
the general embankment should first be placed up to the top of culvert
prior to construction of the culvert General guidelines for the
culvert construction are presented below
Excavation
The general embankment fill should first be placed in 10 inch
maximum loose level lifts to the top of culvert Upon completion
of the filling a trench should then be excavated through the
compacted fill to the culvert invert and the pipe placed in the
trench
Cushion
A minimum 12 inch cushion layer of gravel such as 3B Fine ASTM
C 33 No 67 gravel is recommended below the culvert structure
to provide drainage and uniform support
Trench Backfill
Select well draining granular material such as 3B Fine gravel or
sand which could be compacted by water tamping may be used as
backfill to the top of culvert Above this level general select
fill may be used

Headwall Structures
Headwall structures are required at the inlet and outlet of the
culvert An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f is recommended
for walls resting on stiff natural ground or compacted fill
Well graded granular material such as base course 3B Fine
gravel sand or equivalent should be used as backfill material
directly behind the walls This would reduce the compaction
effort required for the backfill and reduce the build up of
additional lateral pressures on the walls
The walls should be designed to resist the following lateral earth

Control Building Foundations
For the proposed control building spread or continuous footing
foundation are recommended
An allowable bearing value of up to 2500 p s f is recommended for
footings bearing on the previously specified compacted fill This
value of bearing capacity is for a total of dead plus live loads and
may be increased by 33 when temporary lateral loads wind and seismic
are considered
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2120 00 Page 9
A coefficient of friction of 0 4 and a passive soil resistance of
300 p s f per foot of depth may be used to determine the lateral
resistance of the structure
The bottom of all footings should be placed a minimum of 18 inches
below the finished grade
Surface water should be diverted away from the building
foundations
Anticipated settlements are expected to be relatively small
probably on the order of less than one half of an inch
Slab On Grade
Slabs should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of gravel such
as 3B Fine gravel
The thickened edge around the slab on grade should extend a
minimum of eight 8 inches below the adjacent ground surface
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material a
polyethylene moisture barrier should be considered

surface course of the parking and access road areas The following
preliminary pavement section may be used

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations
and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings Variations of conditions between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the plan and profile undated provided by Belt
Collins Associates The physical location of the field data points
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method
used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of the project
designers for specific application to the site grading and design of
foundations of the project in accordance with generally accepted
geotechnical engineering principles and practices No other warranty
expressed or implied is made
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the
architect engineer in the preliminary design evaluation of the proposed
project Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the
proper information for use in contract bid estimation A contractor
wishing to bid on this project is urged to retain a competent geotechnical
engineer to assist in the interpretation of this report and or in the
performance of additional site specific exploration for bid estimating
purposes

Field Exploration

6 borings to depths of 7 0 to 20 0 feet below the existing ground

reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 6
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
