INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed new building for Quality Graphics at 1024 Queen Street in
the Kakaako area of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design
input only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located at 1024 Queen Street in the Kakaako area
of Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The subject lot is approximately rectangular
in shape with an area of approximately 5 000 square feet The site is currently
occupied by a 2 story apartment building of hollow tile and wood construction The
existing building is generally surrounded by landscape areas with asphalt concrete
pavement at the front
It is proposed to demolish the existing structure and develop the subject site with
the construction of a new 2 story building for Quality Graphics We understand that the
new building will consist of ground floor level parking with asphalt concrete pavement
at the front A printing press with concrete slabs on grade subjected to forklift traffic
is planned at the rear of the proposed building The second floor will generally consist
of office space Based on the information provided by the project architect we
understand that the proposed building will generally be of CMU wall construction at the
ground floor level with wood construction for the second floor level and roof framing
According to the project civil engineer the proposed finish floor elevation for the new
building will be set at approximately the same finish floor elevation of the existing

Quality Graphics building located to the southeast of the subject lot or at about
5 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL Based on the topographic survey map provided and
the proposed finish floor elevation minor cuts and fills on the order of 1 foot are
anticipated for the site grading
Based on the information provided by the project structural engineer maximum

loads of approximately 3 kips per lineal foot

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The project site is located on the southern
flank of the Koolau shield Its geomorphology and subsurface conditions are directly
related to glacio eustatic fluctuations of the sea level during the Pleistocene Epoch and
the genesis of the Honolulu Coastal Plain The subsurface conditions in the Ala Moana
area are dominated by fossil Pleistocene Age coral algal reef deposits and related
deposits which are intercalated with terrigenous sediments laid during high stands of
the sea The base of the stratigraphic section in this area is Koolau Basalt
In the last 10 000 years or so the sea level has adjusted to its present stand
Terrigenous and marine sediments have continued to accumulate in low energy
estuarine or lagoonal environments resulting in thick deposits of soft loose sediments
along the coast in the areas which were formerly valleys and drainageways Land
development and reclamation projects within the last 100 years have brought the
project area to its present form

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two borings to depths
of about 20 and 26 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations
of these borings designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2

correspond to elevations of approximately 0 7 to 1 0 foot MSL Groundwater levels
are expected to vary with tidal fluctuations seasonal rainfall and time of year

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is underlain by a relatively thick
deposit of loose to medium dense coralline sands and gravels extending to a depth of
about 17 feet below the existing ground surface Based on our exploration and the
anticipated maximum column loads of up to 56 kips we believe that shallow
foundations consisting of spread and or continuous footings bearing on the
recompacted in situ sandy soils encountered at shallow depths may be used to support
the proposed 2 story building New footings to be located adjacent to the existing
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3532 00 Page 7
structure on the southeast of the subject lot should be lowered to the same depth as
the existing building footings to avoid surcharging the existing foundations
Because of the relatively shallow groundwater table and loose consistency of the
near surface soils special attention should be given to the subgrade preparation for this
project to reduce the potential for pumping subgrade conditions In addition we
recommend that contract documents include additive and deductive unit prices for over
excavation engineered fill placement and geotextile fabric in the event that loose and
wet pumping subgrade soils are encountered during the subgrade preparation work
Detailed discussions and recommendations pertaining to the design of foundation slab
on grade pavement site preparation and other geotechnical aspects of the project are
presented in the following sections of this report
Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the site we believe that
shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used to support the proposed new
building construction An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 000 pounds per square
foot p s f may be used for design of footing foundations bearing on the recompacted
in situ sandy soils encountered at shallow depths The foundation soils should be
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction and should be unyielding
This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for
transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
In order to utilize the thin surficial fill crust we recommend that footings be
embedded to a depth of about 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade New
footings to be located adjacent to the existing structure on the southeast side of the
subject lot should be lowered to the same depth as the existing building footings to
avoid surcharging the existing foundations The actual depths of the existing footings
should be confirmed in the field during construction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3532 00 Page 8
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on the recompacted in situ soils as recommended herein to be on the
order of 1 inch total and inch differential
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that
the soil around footings is well compacted or undisturbed Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be
neglected A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the
recompacted near surface soils
It is recommended that the bottom of all footings bear on the recompacted near
surface soils If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing
excavations they should be over excavated to expose firm materials The over
excavation should either be backfilled with granular material compacted to a minimum
of 90 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom be extended down to the
underlying competent material Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density
of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Designation

D 1557 91 In the event that firm materials are not encountered after excavating toa

No 3B Fine gravel wrapped in a filter fabric

Slab On Grade
We understand that concrete slab on grade are planned for the printing press
located at the rear of the proposed project Based on the information provided to us
by the project civil engineer the concrete slab on grade at the printing press will be
subjected to forklift traffic with maximum axle loads on the order of 5 to 10 kips
Generally we recommend that the concrete slab be designed by a structural
engineer for the intended loads and be reinforced as a structural slab A modulus of
subgrade reaction of 150 pounds per cubic inch p c i may be used for structural
design of the slab The following slab on grade section may be used for preliminary
design and budget estimating purposes
Concrete Slab On Grade
6 5 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course 95 percent relative compaction
The subgrade soils for the slab on grade section should be scarified to a depth
of at least 10 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3532 00 Page 10
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction The base course should
consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to not less than 95 percent relative
compaction The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved area should be
embedded at least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Due to the relatively shallow groundwater table anticipated at the project site a
layer of reinforcing fabric such as Mirafi 500X or equivalent may need to be
incorporated between the base course layer and the subgrade for stabilization purposes
in the event that pumping subgrade conditions are encountered in the field
The area adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab with
non expansive impervious soil and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce
the potential for water ponding around the foundations
Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography and the proposed finish grades minimal site
grading work is anticipated for the project construction The weight of new fills will
initiate ground settlement due to compression of the loose subsoils Therefore any site
filling that may be required should be initiated as soon as possible and should be
limited to a maximum height of about 1 to 2 feet above the existing ground surface
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site
Slabs and foundations of the existing structures that are to be demolished should
be completely removed Any over excavation resulting from the demolition should be
backfilled with compacted granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned
should be removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3532 00 Page 11
select granular fill material placed in 6 to 8 inch loose lifts and compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative compaction Utilities that are to be abandoned in place under the
proposed structure should be backfilled by pumping lean concrete under low pressure
After demolition clearing and grubbing all areas at grade or to receive fills
should be scarified to a minimum depth of 10 inches moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed
as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Due to the relatively shallow groundwater table anticipated at the project site a
layer of reinforcing fabric such as Mirafi 500X or equivalent may need to be
incorporated between the base course layer and the slab pavement subgrades for
stabilization purposes in the event that pumping subgrade conditions are encountered
in the field Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for
over excavation and engineered fill placement and reinforcing fabric to account for
variations in the over excavation quantities
The on site granular materials may be used as a source of fill materials provided
that they are free of deleterious materials and that they may achieve the specified
compaction Fill material required for the project construction should be placed in level
lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative
compaction Where needed imported materials should consist of select borrow

a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 20 or higher and a swell potential

Pavement Design
We understand that asphalt concrete pavement will be required for the ground

in situ silty sands prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented in the

stabilization purposes in the event that pumping subgrade conditions are encountered
in the field

Utility Trench
We envision that new utility connections may be required for the proposed
project A granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes It should be noted that
groundwater was encountered at a depth of about 4 feet below the ground surface
Therefore utility trenches extending near this depth will likely encounter groundwater
For pipes with inverts near the underlying loose sand and gravel deposits an additional
18 inches of No 3B Fine gravel wrapped in filter fabric Mirafi 140N or equivalent
should be provided below the bedding layer to provide more uniform support of the
pipe Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No
67 gradation should also be used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above
the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction
effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material
or the on site granular soils The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content placed in about 8 inch loose lifts and mechanically
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3532 00 Page 14
of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to not less
than 95 percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting
of the concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

Field Exploration

borings to depths of about 20 and 26 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using

testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the
materials encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2

ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
