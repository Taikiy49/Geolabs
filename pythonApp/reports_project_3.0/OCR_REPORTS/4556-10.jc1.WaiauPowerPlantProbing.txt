This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering consultation
performed for the proposed Sheet Pile Wall Replacement at Waiau Power Plant project in
the Pearl City area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and general vicinity
are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical recommendations derived
from our field measurements and engineering analyses These recommendations are
intended for the design of retaining structures only The findings and recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The existing Waiau Power Plant is along Kamehameha Highway in the Pearl City
area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Two existing sheet pile barriers located on the
south of the power plant separate the intake and discharge water from the power plant
Due to the deteriorated condition of the sheet piles construction of new sheet pile
separators is desired
A geotechnical exploration at the site was previously conducted by Geolabs Inc
presented in the report entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Waiau Power
Plant Improvements Pearl City Oahu Hawaii dated March 23 2001
Based on the available information the existing sheet pile separators extend
approximately 300 feet along the western intake and 1 200 feet along the eastern intake
from the landside into Pearl Harbor We understand that the current phase of the design
only includes about 230 linear feet of the western sheet pile wall in the land portion as
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering consultation is to re evaluate the
previous subsurface data to assist in the design of the new sheet pile wall The work
was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated March 1 2010 Our
scope of work included the following tasks and work efforts
1
Mobilization and demobilization of a small boat and operators to and
from the project site
2
Sounding of the mudline in front of the sheet pile walls
3
Coordination of the field exploration by our engineer
4
Analyses of the previous field and laboratory data and new sounding
data to formulate geotechnical recommendations for the design of the
new sheet pile walls
5
Preparation of a letter report summarizing our work and presenting our
findings and recommendations
6
Coordination of our overall work on the project by our senior project
engineer
7
Quality assurance and client design team consultation by our principal
engineer
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS

the current study was divided into two segments of about 190 and 40 feet long by a
concrete sea wall as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The mudline depths along the
sheet pile walls were measured at stations established at about 25 foot intervals
Five measurements were conducted at each station at approximately 15 and
20 feet perpendicular from the sheet pile walls

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from the field measurements Variations of subsoil conditions
between and beyond the measurement points may occur and the nature and extent of
these variations may not become evident until construction is underway If variations
then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations provided
in this report
The locations of the field measurement points indicated in this report are
approximate having been estimated by taping from the features shown on the Site Plan
transmitted by Wilson Okamoto Associates Inc on March 16 2001 Elevations of the
measurement points were only based on the water level at the time of the field
measurement The physical locations and elevations of the measurement points should
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the measurement
plots depict the approximate mudline depth and as such may denote a gradual
transition These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation
of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in
rainfall tides temperature and other factors
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of Hawaiian Electric
Company and their consultants for specific application to the proposed Sheet Pile Wall
Replacement at Waiau Power Plant in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical
engineering principles and practices No warranty is expressed or implied
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the architect
and engineer in the design of the proposed project Therefore this report may not
contain sufficient data or the proper information to serve as the basis for preparation of
construction cost estimates A contractor wishing to bid on this project is urged to retain
a competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation of this report and or in
the performance of additional site specific exploration for bid estimating purposes
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are
commonly encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as perched groundwater soft
deposits hard layers or loose fills may occur in localized areas and may require
additional probing or corrections in the field which may result in construction delays to
attain a properly constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is
recommended to accommodate these possible extra costs
