Dear Mr Leong
In accordance with our fee proposal dated August 18 1998 we have conducted a
limited geotechnical engineering exploration as required in the contract documents for the
East Kapolei Mass Grading Increment 1 Portion of Lot 8861 C project to evaluate the
subsurface conditions prior to the mass grading operations This letter report summarizes
our work and presents our findings for the project
Project Considerations
The proposed project site is located in the East Kapolei area in the District of Ewa on
the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1 We understand that it is proposed to construct a
baseball sports complex consisting of a 4 000 seat baseball stadium and multiple full size
practice fields The proposed development will also include one and two story structures
such as the team clubhouse maintenance facility field observation towers and other
buildings Various parking lots and driveways are also planned within the project site to
service the sports complex
Scope of Services
As required in the contract documents for the mass grading project it was desired to
drill three borings extending to depths of about 20 feet below the existing ground surface
The purpose of the three borings was to establish the existing subsurface conditions prior to
the mass grading operations In general the scope of our services included the following
tasks and work efforts

Field Exploration
The field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling three borings designated as
Boring Nos 1 through 3 at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The
depths of our borings ranged from about 21 5 to 24 5 feet below the existing ground surface
The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight
augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic representations
of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates 4 through 6
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and SplitBarrel Sampling of Soils by
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths

Laboratory Testing
Selected soil samples from the field exploration were tested in the laboratory with an
emphasis on evaluating the moisture density plasticity and gradation The following
laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples obtained from the field exploration
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The
results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected samples of
the soils to evaluate their liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Results of
these tests are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths and
presented graphically on Plate 7
Two sieve analysis tests ASTM C 136 were performed on selected materials
encountered in the borings to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid
in soil classification Graphic representations of the test results are provided on Plate 8
Surface and Subsurface Conditions
The proposed project site is located in the East Kapolei area in the District of Ewa on
the island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project site
is bounded by the future Kapolei Parkway to the North the existing Kapolei Lower Channel
to the West and Renton Road to the South
The project site is currently undeveloped land that was previously used for agricultural
purposes The site is relatively level with ground elevations on the order of 50 feet Mean
Sea Level MSL The site is vegetated with weeds and brush as high as 5 feet Some soil
stockpiles were observed near the center of the project site
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three exploratory
borings extending to depths ranging from about 21 5 to 24 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration encountered an alluvial soil layer consisting of stiff to
hard silty clays extending to depths of about 1 5 to 3 5 feet below the existing ground
surface Below the alluvial soil layer medium dense to dense silty coral sands and gravels
coral detritus were encountered to the maximum depth explored of about 24 5 feet below
the existing ground surface A void was encountered in Boring No 3 between depths of
about 16 and 23 feet below the existing ground surface
