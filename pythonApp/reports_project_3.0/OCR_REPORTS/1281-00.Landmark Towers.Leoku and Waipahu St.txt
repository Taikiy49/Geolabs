SOILS INVESTIGATION
PROPOSED LANDMARK TOWERS
CORNER OF LEOKU AND WAIPAHU STREETS
This report presents the results of a foundation investigation for
the proposed Landmark Towers to be situated at the southeastern corner
of Leoku and Waipahu Streets in Waipahu Oahu Hawaii The general
location of the site and its vicinity are shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that the project will consist of two
24 story towers each with a 4 level attached parking structure No
basement is planned at this time The proposed layout of these
structures is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
W O 1281 00 Page 2
According to the plan a wedge of fill of up to about 8 feet will
be needed under the eastern ends of Building A and its parking area
Similarly fill of varying depths up to 5 feet will be required under
Building B and its parking area Other amenities such as a swimming
pool and tennis courts are also planned
It is further understood that colum loads will be 800 to 1200 kips
each and the maximum dead and live wall loadings will be about 60
kips linear foot for the highrise building The parking structures will
be supported by columns with the maximum loading of about 300 kips per
column
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this investigation is to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site and to provide design recommendations for the
foundations of the proposed twin towers and parking structures
For this investigation eleven 11 borings were drilled at the
approximate location shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The depth of the
borings varied from 40 to 80 feet totalling 450 linear feet Subsoil
samples obtained were examined in our laboratory and selected samples
were subject to a series of laboratory testing Engineering analyses
were made and our recommendations and conclusions were developed based
on these field and laboratory findings
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1281 00 Page 3
Detailed descriptions of the field exploration laboratory testing
together with logs of the borings are presented in the appendices
The scope of our work was performed in accordance with our proposal
dated This report summarizes our findings and
recommendations
SITE CONDITIONS
General
The project site consists of a large open trapezoidal shaped
field It is bounded by Waipahu Street to the north Leoku Street to
the west by Kaiser Permanente s Punawai Health Center to the southeast
and a concrete lined drainage channel to the northeast
The site slopes gently to the northeast towards the drainage
channel
At present the site is covered by sparse vegetation with
moderately sized areas of bare soil Numerous small shallow erosion
gullies cross the site enlarging to a few feet deep near the top of the
lined drainage channel
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1281 00 Page 4
Geology
The site is located near the boundary of the coastal alluvial plain
and the underlying Koolau basalt Our field exploration of the site
disclosed a relatively uniform geological cross section of Quaternary
alluvium overlying residual soil atop the Koolau Basalt A sequence of
alluvium and basalt rock of the older Waianae series volcanics may be
expected at great depths below the Koolau basalt rocks
Subsurface Conditions
Mantling the entire site under occasional patches of thin fill is a
relatively thick layer of geologically older alluvium of the Quaternary
period This older alluvium is typically about 20 feet thick and
consists mainly of interbedded brown to dark brown clays or clayey silts
of very stiff to hard consistency Our laboratory tests indicate that
these soils have low to moderate expansion potential Selected samples
of this material produced a laboratory CBR value of about 8 when
compacted to 90 to 100 of the maximum ASTM D 1557 78 density One
exception was encountered in Boring 10 where the fill was about 9 feet
thick and included a relatively soft silt pocket at the bottom
Under the older alluvium is a 10 to 15 feet thick layer of reddish
brown very stiff clayey silt residual soil This residual soil had been
derived from in place weathering of the parent basalt The soil
contains some boulders that become more frequent with depth
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1281 00 Page 5
The residual soil grades into highly weathered decomposed rock
saprolite that has the strength of soil but retains the relict texture
of the parent rock At depths ranging from 30 to 40 feet the material
grades to moderately weathered vesicular basalt except at Boring 4
where less weathered basalt exists at a depth of about 55 feet
A generalized cross section of soil conditions interpreted from the
borings is presented on Plate 3
Groundwater
Groundwater levels were noted in the borings Occasional caving of
holes prevented the recording of water level readings in every boring
Generally we believe that the water level exists at a depth of about 30
feet or ranges between Elevations 15 to 19 feet Mean Sea Level
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our findings from this investigation we believe that the
site is suitable for the foundation support of the proposed project
Shallow foundations spread footings for the parking structures and mat
foundations for the towers may be used provided that small settlements
of 2 to 3 inches are acceptable Because of the expansion potential of
the soil the entire mat foundation should be placed on an 18 inch
cushion of compacted select borrow fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1281 00 Page 6
Spread Footing for Parking Structures
Based on our findings from this investigation we believe that
spread footing may be used to support the parking structures provided
that some settlements can be tolerated
The footings should be placed in very stiff to hard brown
undisturbed natural clays and silts or in compacted engineered fill
This is very important since soft soil may still exist below an existing
fill as evidenced in Boring 10 A special probing program should be
implemented for footings in the vicinity of Boring 10 Starting from
the southwestern corner footing of the Building B parking structure all
adjacent footings should be probed for any soft soils to at least
Elevation 40 feet Any soft soil under the footing should be removed
and replaced with compacted select borrow fill
The bottom of major column footings should be placed at least 4
feet below the lowest adjacent grade and designed to a net allowable
soil bearing pressure of 5000 p s f with a one third increase allowed
for wind or seismic loads For lightly loaded column footings where
loads do not exceed 30 kips each the embedment may be reduced to 2 feet
minimum with the allowable soil bearing pressure reduced to 3000 p s f
If a footing is located in new fill the top 6 inches of ill below
the footing bottoms should be recompacted to 95 of the maximum density
per ASTM D 1557 78
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1281 00 Page 7
The bottom of the footing excavations should be free of debris or
loose soils If any weak or soft materials are encountered at the
bottom of any footing excavation they should be removed and the
resulting over excavations backfilled either with concrete or compacted
fill as deemed appropriate All footing excavations should be
inspected and approved by a qualified soils engineer
Footing excavations should not be exposed for a prolong period of
time to avoid excessive drying of the exposed materials
Set Back from Slope

Any footing on or adjacent to a slope should be embedded
sufficiently so that the bottom outside edge of the footing will be
set back at least 10 feet horizontally from the face of the slope
Settlements
ms em sm ms ra
Based on soil conditions disclosed we estimate that settlements
for the heavier parking structure interior column footings will be
on the order of one inch and the lighter exterior footings about
one half of an inch when designed to the criteria recommended
above The differential settlements should be less than one half
of an inch in 20 feet
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1281 00 Page 8
Mat Foundation for Highrise Towers
A mat foundation may be considered for the Support of the highrise
towers It is anticipated that the average mat foundation will impose a
net soil bearing pressure ranging from 3000 to 4000 p s f
Because of the low to moderately expansive nature of the under lying
soils we recommend that the natural clayey silt soils 18 inches below
the bottom of the mat be replaced with select borrow material compacted
to 95 of the maximum density
To minimize potential swelling after casting the concrete the
subgrade soil below the select borrow cushion should be kept moist and
not be allowed to dry out
For the mat foundation design calculations the modulus of subgrade
reaction may be assumed to be 200 kips per cubic foot We estimate that
mat foundation settlements may range from 2 to 3 inches total with
smaller settlements occurring at the edges
The settlement distribution will be influenced considerably by the
thickness of the mat as well as overall rigidity of the structure above
that mat In general we estimate that the differential settlement will
be on the order of one half to three fourths of an inch between the edge
and the middle of the mat
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 9
Spread Footing for Highrise Towers
We believe that larger settlements will result if spread footing
foundations are used for the towers It is estimated that the
accompanying differential settlements would increase to about 1 5 inches
between the central core footing clusters and the perimeter footings
If these magnitudes of settlement are acceptable then spread footing
may be used The net allowable soil bearing pressure should be limited
to 6000 p s f with the footing bottom embedded a minimum of 6 feet
below the lowest adjacent grade A one third increase may be allowed
for the soil bearing pressure under wind or seismic load All tower
footings should have a minimum footing dimension of 10 feet
Similar to mat foundation preparation we recommend that an 18 inch
thick layer of compacted select borrow materials be placed under the
spread footings and compacted to a minimum of 95 of the ASTM D 1557 78
maximum density
Pile Foundations
If the anticipated settlements for the mat or spread footing
foundation are not tolerable pile foundation should be considered
Predrilling may be required to install piles through the very stiff
to hard soils and basalt boulders
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1281 00 Page 10
Generally because the basalt rock is highly weathered we believe
that the pile capacity should be limited to 100 tons maximum for piles
driven into the basalt which ranged in depth from 30 to 55 feet in our
borings
More detailed design recommendations for pile foundation could be
developed should you decide to use it
Differential Settlements Between Tower and Parking Structure
Provisions should be made to take into account the differential
settlements between the tower and its respective parking structure We
anticipate that the majority of settlements will be completed within one
month after the completion of the structures We suggest that the tower
and the parking structure be structurally separated to allow for
differential settlements Special details may be required for
construction of water proofing members between the tower and the parking
structure

Site Preparation
Ce ee
Prior to receiving any fill the existing ground surface in the
fill area should be cleared of any vegetation debris and
deleterious materials The existing erosion gullies particularly
the larger ones in excess of 12 inches in depth should be
carefully cleaned of all loose materials This is especially
important in the area along the existing lined drainage channel
GEOLABS HAWAIi

Several of the erosion gullies have developed notches in the
steeper areas below the top of the bank where the more erosion
resistant layers have been undercut and formed small overhangs
These overhangs should be cut back to avoid entrapping cavities
during subsequent filling This may be done by leveling out a
keyway about 8 to 12 feet wide at the top of the lined channel
then filling the keyway with compacted fill material in 6 inch
thick layers to form the base of a new slope along the channel
Merely filling the gullies from the top of the existing uneven bank
slope should be avoided since this would create a poorly compacted
i11 which would be susceptible to erosion and might slough down
into the channel during heavy rains
The existing fill if any under the highrise towers should be
removed completely and replaced with a compacted select borrow
material In the paved areas or under the slab areas any existing
fill should be removed to at least 3 feet below the inished
grade If soft condition exists at the bottom of such removal the
soft material should be further removed as deemed necessary by the
soils engineer The excavation should then be backfilled us ing
Suitable on site material tested and approved by the soils
engineer or select borrow material

Proofrolling
After the erosion gullies and weak previously filled areas have
been cleaned and refilled the entire area should be proofrolled by
a pneumatic tired roller weighing at least 30 tons making at least
4 passes over any given point This proofrolling in the cut area
in the back half of the parking structure for Building B should be
made when the area is excavated roughly to the design subgrade
Any weak or soft areas further disclosed by the proofrolling should
be over excavated and backfilled with a suitable backfill material
compacted to the densities specified in the aforementioned sections

Fill Material
ee ee
The proposed project will require a substantial amount of imported
fill Although some material may be generated from the mat
foundation excavation swimming pool tennis court and regrading
work near Leoku Street it is anticipated that the quantity of
these cuts will be relatively small
In general our laboratory tests indicate the on site material of
brown sandy silt and clay are low to moderately expansive
However we believe that these soils would be suitable for fill
provided that they are kept a minimum of 2 feet below the finished
floor grade or roadway and at least 18 inches under the mat or
spread footings
The imported fill materials should be non expansive soil free of
organic matter and should form a dense stable mass when compacted
to the densities required In general the imported fill materials
should meet the following requirements specified in the Standard
Specifications for Public Works Construction City and County of
Honolulu May 1975

Fill Placement
The engineered fill materials within the top 3 feet of the finished
grade should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 6 inches
in compacted thickness and compacted to dry densities not less than

the lift may be increased to 8 inches
If the material is too wet or too dry it should be dried or
moistened so that the moisture content at the time of compaction
will be within 3 percentage points of the optimum moisture content

The construction of fill on slopes steeper than 5 horizontal to 1
vertical should be keyed and benched properly Keying and benching

Retaining Walls
It is anticipted that retaining walls may be required along the
northeastern perimeter of the parking structures
GEOLABS HAWAII

The general guidelines for retaining wall design are listed below
1 For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Unrestrained Wall 35 p s f equivalent fluid
active condition pressure per foot of depth
level backfill
Restrained Wall 50 p s f equivalent fluid
at rest condition pressure per foot of depth
level backfill
Backfill material behind retaining walls should consist of
granular material such as select borrow Such granular
backfill should extend at least 4 feet behind the back of the
retaining wall Otherwise a higher active earth pressure
would result
It is recommended that the retaining walls of the parking
structures be considered as a restrained wall to limit their
lateral deflection at the top
Surcharge load selected may be converted to equivalent wall
height of 1 foot high for every 100 p s f of surcharge
2 An allowable bearing value of up to 5000 p s f is recommended
for retaining wall foundations bearing on the compacted fill or
on undisturbed very stiff natural soils
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 1281 00 Page 17
3 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may
be used for wall footings on level areas
4 A base friction factor of 0 4 can also be used against sliding
of the wall foundations
5 The retaining wall foundations on relatively flat areas should
be embedded a minimum of 4 feet below the lowest adjacent grade
6 A Subdrain system behind retaining walls should be provided to
prevent build up of hydrostatic pressure Subdrains should be
properly constructed and drain freely by gravity into open
space or drain manhole The subdrain pipe should not be tied
to the down spout of roof drains or other type of small
diameter drains This is to avoid drainage water from flowing
back into the subdrain system and into the back of the
retaining walls
Slab On Grade
Slabs may be supported directly on non expansive select borrow fill
of at least 8 inches thick Therefore the fill between the bottom of
on grade slab and the top of the mat foundation under the tower should
consist of the select borrow material
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1281 00 Page 18
We recommend that for the tennis courts and the swimming pool
areas where natural soil exists select borrow cushion layer of at
least 12 inches thick should be used to reduce the effect of expansion
of the on site soil The subgrade under tennis court or swimming pool
should be scarified to a depth of 8 inches and recompacted to a dry
density of 90 of the maximum The moisture content during compaction
should be kept at the optimum
The expansion potential should be further reduced by maintaining
the on site subgrade soil in a moist condition and pre saturating the
subgrade area prior to placing concrete slab
The depth of the thickened slab edges where underlain by a select
borrow material cushion should be a minimum of 8 inches below the
lowest adjacent finished grade

Field Exploration

by drilling 11 borings at the approximate locations shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drilling rig
was used to drill the borings

were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of the
materials encountered is presented on the Boring Logs
In the borings soil samples were obtained by
driving a 2 inch standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch
I D split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition a inch 0 D Denison sampler
was used The blow counts used to drive the samplers the
last 12 inches are shown on the log of borings at the
appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX _B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content determinations were performed on selected
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the boring
logs at the appropriate sample depths
To further identify soil characteristics additional tests were
performed These include 8 Atterberg limit tests and the results are
presented on Plates B 1 and B 2
Two laboratory CBR tests and one compaction test were performed
on bulk samples of the on site surface soils to evaluate their
pavement subgrade support and compaction characteristics The test
results are summarized on Plate B 2
Four sets of direct shear tests were performed on undisturbed
samples to evaluate the strength characteristics of near surface
soils One of these tests contrasts the soil strength under both
natural and saturated conditions The test results are presented on
Plates B 3 through B 5
Four consolidated drained triaxial compression tests were
performed on select undisturbed samples to also evaluate the soil
strength characteristics of the soil The test results are summarized
on Plate B 6
Thirteen unconfined compression tests were performed on
Slightly disturbed and undisturbed samples to further evaluate the
soil strength characteristics The peak compressive strength values
for these tests are summarized on Plate B 7
Two one inch ring swell tests were performed on natural and
air dried undisturbed samples to evaluate the swelling potential of
the soils in both normal and worse case conditions The test results
are summarized on Plate B 8
Five consolidation tests were performed on selected undisturbed
samples to evaluate the settlement characteristics of the site The
test results are presented on Plates B 9 through B 13
