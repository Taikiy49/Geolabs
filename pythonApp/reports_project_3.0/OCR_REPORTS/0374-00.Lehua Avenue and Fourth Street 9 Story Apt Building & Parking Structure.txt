I INTRODUCTION
General
This report presents the results of our investigation to
determine the subsurface soil and foundation conditions
for the project The purpose of the investigation was to
provide subsurface information necessary to determine the
feasibility of constructing a 9 story condominium and park
ing structure on the subject site The study included
subsurface borings laboratory testing and engineering
analyses The report summarizes our work on the project
and presents recommendations pertinent to foundation and
Site development requirements
Project Descriptions
The proposed project is located on the corner of Lehua Avenue
and Fourth Street Pearl City Oahu Hawaii The site is
approximately 140 feet wide along Lehua Avenue and 150 feet
long along Fourth Street
The 9 story condominium plus a parking structure has a total
weight of 8000 kips The structures are planned to be
of precast concrete panel construction Maximum wall loads
will be 18 to 20 kips per linear foot No basements are
presently planned

II FIELD EXPLORATION
Three subsurface borings averaging 21 5 feet deep were completed
on under the supervision of an engineer or geologist
from our firm The borings were excavated with rotary drilling
equipment having continuous flight solid stem augers
The boring locations are shown on the site sketch Plate 1 and
the boring logs are presented in Appendix A
In all borings Standard Penetration Tests were taken at selected
depth intervals not exceeding 5 feet The Standard Penetration
Test consists of driving a 2 inch 0 D split spoon sampler 18
inches with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30
inches The number of blows required to drive the sampler the
last 12 inches is termed the Standard Penetration Resistance
N and is an approximate measure of the relative density or
consistency of a soil These resistance values are plotted on
the boring logs Samples obtained were visually classified in
the field and representative material was placed in air tight
containers and returned to our laboratory for inspection and
testing

A Geology
The Pearl Harbor embayment was formed by the banking of the

The Pearl City peninsula was probably initially formed as
a divide between two ancient branches of the present Waikele
and Walana Streams The region has undergone a number of
periods of emergence and submergence which have intermixed
marine bay muds with non marine alluvial clastic debris
Late Pleistocene Age volcanism also blanketed the area with
volcanic ash During periods of emergence subaerial weather
ing produced the characteristic red to brown soil profiles
present in the subsurface

is underlain by at least 21 5 feet of stiff to hard silty

Construction of the proposed buildings on the site is
considered to be feasible from a geology and soils engineer
ing standpoint
Due to settlement characteristics under applied loads the
near surface red to brown silty clay soils are not considered
Satisfactory for support of the proposed structures It is
therefore recommended that the structure be founded on
deepened spread footings in the hard silty clay or sand
silt clay mixture soils at a depth below existing grade of
approximately 6 to 7 feet It may be desirable to plan a
basement or subterranean parking structure beneath the
condominium building due to the increased depth of footings
required and may reduce the overall cost of the proposed
two level parking structure
Near vertical footing excavations necessary to construct the
foundation system should have short term stability However
heavy equipment or stockpiles should not be allowed to sur
charge the excavations and should be kept at least 10 feet
away from the top of slope
We have based the conclusions and recommendations of this
feasibility level soils and geology report on selective
accessible shallow holes It will be necessary to excavate
deeper borings in the phase II design level subsurface
investigation to verify that subsurface conditions

LABORATORY TESTING
The laboratory testing program was developed to establish the
engineering properties of the soils and rocks encountered at
the project site
1 Classification Tests
a Visual Classification Ali soil samples obtained from
the borings were brought to our laboratory where they were
visually reclassified to confirm or modify the field classi
fication prior to finalizing the Logs of Borings
b Water Content Determinations In addition to visual
classification typical samples were tested for natural
water content as an aid in soil classification and in
evaluating soil properties The water content values are
based upon the dry weight of the soil
Z2 Shear Strength Tests
a Direct Shear Test In general soil samples were tested
for shear strength using a 2 41 I D direct shear box Each
set consisted of at least two tests each test run under
different normal loads The results are plotted as Normal
Stress vs Shear Strength with a failure envelope drawn to
determine values of cohesion c and angle of frictional
resistance
LJ
b Consolidation Tests Consolidation tests were performed
on selected undisturbed ring samples of the compressible
soils to provide basic data for making settlement calcula
tions Porous stones were placed on the top and bottom of
the samples to allow drainage Vertical loads were applied
in increments with each load increment being allowed to
consolidate prior to adding the next increment Measurements
of the time and consolidation were obtained during each load
increment and rebound was measured during the unloading
portion Consolidation test results are plotted in terms
of percent settlement versus applied loads
