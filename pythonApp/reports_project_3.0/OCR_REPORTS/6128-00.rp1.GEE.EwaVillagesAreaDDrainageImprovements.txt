PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves the installation of about 600 feet of concrete box drain from
the proposed Franciscan Vistas development to the intersection of Kapolei Parkway and
the future extension of Park Row The box drain will be about 6 feet by 8 feet in section
and the invert will be about 11 feet below the existing ground The overall project
location is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
It is anticipated that trench excavation and shoring will be required for the
construction The culvert location is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is in the Ewa area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The area is presently undeveloped and covered with a growth of low grasses and
shrubs The project site is northwest of Puanaeole Street and the abandoned railroad
tracks and north of the future Kapolei Parkway The existing terrain is gently sloping to
relatively flat with ground elevations varying from 36 to 41 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
The alignment of the new box culvert extends from an existing inlet box at the proposed
Kapolei Parkway approximately 600 feet northeasterly to a proposed new residential
development The alignment also follows an adjacent fuel line easement The project
location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
the Waianae and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to
be middle to late Pliocene in age and the younger Koolau Volcano is estimated to be
late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age

The project site is situated on the Ewa Coastal Plain which extends seaward off
the southeastern flank of the Waianae Volcano and the southwestern flank of the
Koolau Volcano This plain was formed by the accumulation of a thick wedge of
sediments and coral reef formations on the flanks of the two volcanoes
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to
fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea
When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that
the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of reef deposits at
correspondingly higher elevations Subaerial exposure of the sediments and calcareous
materials caused consolidation of the lagoonal deposits and induration of the
calcareous reef materials
The soils and rocks underlying the project site were described by H T Stearns
and K N Vaksik as follows
Pleistocene Non Calcareous Deposits Qa This deposit consists chiefly of
alluvium
The surface soils underlying the project site are described by the
U S Soil Conservation Service in their publication Soil Survey of Islands of Kauai
and Lanai State of Hawaii 1972 as follows
Honouliuli Clay 0 to 2 slopes HxA These soils consist of well drained
soils developed in alluvium derived from basic igneous rocks The soils have generally
high shrink swell potential and low shear strength

designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 each extending to a depth of 21 5 feet below the

LABORATORY TESTS
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected samples of
the clayey soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits for soil classification The test
results are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths Graphic
presentation of the test result is provided on Plate 6
One Direct Shear test ASTM D 3080 was performed on a selected sample to
evaluate the shear strength characteristics of the material tested Direct shear test results
are presented on Plate 7
Four one inch ring swell tests were performed on natural remolded samples to
evaluate the swelling potential of the on site clayey soils The test results are summarized
on Plate 8
One set of resistivity and pH tests was performed to evaluate the corrosivity
characteristics of the subsurface soils encountered at the project site The pH value was
determined in general accordance with ASTM G 51 The resistivity was determined in
general accordance with ASTM G 57 Results of the resistivity and pH tests conducted on
selected soil samples are summarized on Plate 9

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious material and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site or
stockpiled in a designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the
excavated materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should
be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils should
be properly disposed off site and or used on landscaping areas where appropriate
Prior to filling and backfilling the existing ground should be scarified to a depth of
12 inches and moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum
moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Where
shrinkage cracks are observed after compaction of the subgrade we recommend
preparing the subgrade soil again as recommended above Saturation and
subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor
drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted fill Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density

The concrete box culvert structure should be designed to resist the lateral earth
pressures due to the adjacent soils and surcharge effects Since the tops of the
structures are restrained the retaining structures should be designed for the at rest
condition These lateral earth pressures do not include hydrostatic pressures that
might be caused by groundwater trapped behind the structures

resist the following lateral earth pressures expressed in equivalent fluid pressures

Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by friction developed

Backfill
For support of the new concrete culvert box we recommend using granular
bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation below the box culvert The 6 inch gravel will provide a level working
platform and also serve as a stabilization layer which will provide uniform
support to the box culvert
As mentioned the backfill around the culvert structures may consist of on site
soil or imported fill Imported fill should consist of low expansive material with
particles no larger than 3 inches in largest dimension The material should have a
laboratory CBR value of 8 or more and should have a maximum swell of less
than 1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883 The backfill
should be compacted to 90 to 95 percent relative compaction
Backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Compaction should be accomplished by suitable compaction
equipment i e sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers multiple wheel
pneumatic tired rollers or other types of acceptable compaction equipment
Water tamping jetting or ponding should not be allowed when compacting the
backfill
Because compaction of fills are critical elements of earthwork Geolabs should
conduct observations and soil density tests during backfill to assist the contractor
in obtaining the required degree of compaction Where compaction is less than
required additional compactive effort should be applied with adjustment of
moisture content as necessary to obtain the specified compaction

Corrosion Protection for Utility Lines
We expect that some utility lines will be installed along with the culvert One set
of pH and minimum resistivity tests was performed to evaluate the corrosivity of the
on site silty clay soils generally encountered at the project site The test results are
presented on Plate 9 The minimum resistivity value of the near surface soil sample
tested is about 6 800 ohm cm which corresponds to Corrosion Rating of 4 moderately
corrosive based on the guidelines provided by the City County of Honolulu Board of
Water Supply Therefore we recommend properly designing all metallic substructures
in contact with the soils to be protected against potential corrosion if needed
