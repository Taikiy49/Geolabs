INTRODUCTION
This report presents our recommendations for the foundation
design and construction of the proposed 3 story structure
Subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
a total of three 3 borings each to 21 5 feet deep below
the existing ground surface The approximate boring loca
tions are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 and the descrip
tion of the materials encountered in the borings is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 3
Various laboratory tests were conducted on selected samples
Laboratory test results are presented on Plates A 4 through
A 7

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed 3 story structure would consist of light weight
concrete construction The ground floor would be used as
tenant parking The site is relatively flat with elevations
ranging from 53 4 on south Side to about 57 0 feet on
north side Fill and cut are anticipated to be minimal
for the ground floor parking
GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The site of the proposed development is located in Makiki
on Davenport Place and is bordered by a high rise structure
on the northeast side and Single story dwellings on the
remaining three 3 sides The area has been cleared of
old dwellings prior to our field exploration work
SOIL CONDITIONS
Based on the information obtained from borings the site is
generally underlain by a layer of fill material about
4 0 feet thick consisting of silty clay with some sand
Below this a layer of stiff to very stiff clayey silt
highly decomposed basalt was encountered down to about
17 5 feet below the existing ground surface

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of our field exploration laboratory
testing and the results of our engineering analysis the
Site could be developed as planned
The surface fill consists of expansive clayey materials and
should not be used for foundation Support Fairly competent
materials were encountered underlying the surface fill at a
depth of about 4 0 feet below the existing ground surface
which could provide the desired foundation Support
Due to the presence of expansive surface fill material
asphaltic concrete pavement should be used for the first
floor parking
Additional recommendations for the design of foundations and
construction are presented in the following paragraphs
Foundation
For the proposed structure spread or strip footing foundations
are recommended All footings should be founded on undisturbed

An allowable bearing pressure of 3 000 pounds per square

The footings should have a minimum width of 12 inches and be

For the above recommendations it is estimated that the total
and differential settlements will not exceed one quarter of
an inch

our recommendations are based Any loose areas encountered
during excavation should be removed and replaced with either
concrete or granular material compacted to a minimum of

the material encountered in Boring No 2

Drainage should be provided around the parking to keep water

Earthwork
Earthwork operations at the site will include footing
excavation backfilling and foundation inspection during
construction
The required foundation excavation would extend to depths
of approximately 4 0 feet below the existing ground surface
It is anticipated that conventional earthmoving equipments
could be used to conduct all the excavations for the founda
tions
The brown silty clay surface fill being moderately to
highly expansive is not recommended as fill under the
parking area However this material could be stockpiled
for landscaping purposes if required Excavations should
be protected from surface runoff which may enter the
excavations All loose soils or mud should be removed from
the bottom of all footing excavations prior to concrete
placement
Backfilling
Some minor backfilling operations would be required around
the footings and under the ground floor parking Granular
non expansive imported material could be used for backfilling

Foundation Inspection
It is recommended that all the footing excavations and
