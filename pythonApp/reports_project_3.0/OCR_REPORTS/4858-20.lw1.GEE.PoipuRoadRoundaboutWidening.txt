In accordance with our proposal dated we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration for the proposed Poipu Road Roundabout and
Poipu Road Widening project for the Kukui Ula Resort Community in Koloa on the Island
of Kauai Hawaii This letter report summarizes the findings from our field exploration at
the project site and presents our findings and recommendations for the proposed project
These findings and recommendations are intended for design input only and are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the vicinity of Poipu Road and Lawai Road in Koloa on
the Island of Kauai Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The planned Kukui Ula Resort Community development encompasses about
1 045 acres in Koloa on the south shore of Kauai and includes hotel vacation ownership
commercial park golf course and residential uses The proposed project is part of the
planned infrastructure improvements for the Kukui Ula Resort Community
This phase of the project involves an approximately 160 foot outside diameter of
roundabout and approximately 1 000 feet of widening at the southern side of the
roundabout Based on the available grading plan the proposed road widening involves
placement of minimal fill materials to attain the design grade Details for the proposed
structures were not available at the time this letter report was prepared

FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling two borings designated as
Boring Nos 1 and 2 extending to depths of about 14 and 15 5 feet below the existing
ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with
continuous flight solid stem augers The approximate locations of the borings drilled at
the site are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings
Plates 4 and 5
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel
Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same
hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third
6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
LABORATORY TESTING
Selected soil samples from the field exploration were tested in the laboratory with
an emphasis on evaluating the moisture and density characteristics The following
laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples obtained from the field
exploration
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio Test ASTM D 1883 was performed on a
bulk sample of in situ soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the soils

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration at the project site consisted of drilling and sampling two
borings Different subsurface soil conditions were discovered at these two locations
Boring No 1 encountered approximately 8 feet of very stiff to hard silty clay overlying
2 feet of saprolite materials consisting of dense gravel with clay silt and sand The
saprolite materials were underlain by basalt formation extending to the maximum depth
drilled of 14 feet below the existing ground surface Boring No 2 encountered
approximately 1 foot of dense silty sand overlying basalt formation extending to the
maximum depth drilled of 15 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our field exploration
However groundwater levels are subject to change due to rainfall seasonal precipitation
surface water run off and other factors
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the existing topography and the anticipated design finish grade of the
new road structures we anticipate that site grading work for the proposed construction will
consist generally of minor fill and cuts less than 2 feet thick We anticipate that pavement
sections will be constructed on top of the finish grade Items of grading that are addressed
in the subsequent sections include the following
1 Site Preparation
2 Fill Materials
3 Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
4 Cut and Fill Slopes
Moisture conditioning and compaction of the clayey subgrade soils and new fill
materials are critical elements of earthwork therefore site grading operations should be
observed by a representative from Geolabs It is important that a representative from our
office observe the site grading operations to evaluate whether undesirable materials are
encountered during the excavation and scarification process and whether the exposed
soil rock conditions are similar to those encountered in our field exploration
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris deleterious material and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and or
yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas designated to
receive fill should be over excavated to expose firm material and the resulting excavation
should be backfilled with well compacted fills The excavated soft and or organic soils
should be properly disposed of off site and or used in landscape areas where appropriate
Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation
and compacted fill placement to account for variations in the over excavation quantities

The over excavated subgrades finished subgrades and areas designated to
receive fills should then be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned
to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction However areas under the future pavement sections
should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test
procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the subgrade soil be prepared again as recommended above Saturation
and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor
drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted fill The need for over excavation due to soft subgrade soil conditions
should be evaluated by a representative from Geolabs in the field
Fill Materials
General fill materials required to raise the existing ground surface to the proposed
finished subgrades may consist of the excavated on site soils provided that they are free
of vegetation deleterious materials and rock fragments greater than 3 inches in maximum
dimension Imported general fill materials needed for the site filling should consist of
materials with a low expansion potential Imported general fill materials should consist of
materials with particle sizes of 3 inches or less in maximum dimension and should be free
of deleterious materials The material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio
CBR value of 8 or higher and a swell potential of not more than 1 percent when tested in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883
The imported select granular fill if required should consist of non expansive select
granular material such as crushed coral or basalt The material should be well graded
from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension The
material should also contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200
sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a
maximum swell of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1883
Aggregate base course and aggregate subbase materials required for the
pavement sections should consist of crushed basalt aggregate and should conform to
Sections 703 06 and 703 17 of the State of Hawaii Standard Specifications for Road
Bridge and Public Works Construction 1994 HSS Imported fill materials should be
tested by Geolabs for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the
project site for the intended use

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
General fill materials should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The fill materials within 2 feet
of the subgrade should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum
moisture placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to
a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction in the areas where future pavement sections
will be constructed Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
The non expansive select granular fill materials if required should be placed in
level lifts of about 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture and compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction Aggregate base
course and subbase materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optinum
moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Based on the grading plan we understand that minimal fill and cut is planned at the
area where the existing double barrel culverts are located under the roadway In general
we believe that the grading work will not significantly affect the existing culverts However
we recommend that the culverts be closely monitored during the construction period within
the area If structural distresses are observed the engineers should be immediately
notified and adjustments may be made to avoid structural damages to the existing
culverts
Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the clayey subgrade soils and
general fill soils are critical elements of the earthwork observations and soil density tests
should be performed by Geolabs during the site grading operations to assist the contractor
in obtaining the required degree of compaction and the proper moisture content on each fill
lift Where compaction is less than required additional compactive effort should be
applied with adjustment of moisture content as necessary to obtain the specified
compaction It should be noted that the moisture requirement of the general fills and
subgrades at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture is an important requirement
considering the moderately expansive nature of the Clayey soils
Cut and Fill Slopes
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our field exploration we believe
that the planned cut slopes will expose the stiff clayey materials or basalt formation In
general the cut slopes planned at the project site may be designed with a slope inclination
of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter

Permanent fill slopes constructed using the on site materials or imported fill
materials may also be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter Fills placed on
slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the existing slope to provide
stability of the new fill against sliding The filling operations should start at the lowest point
and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the above fill
placement recommendations Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting
back to the design slope ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face In addition slope
planting should be provided as soon as possible to reduce the potential for erosion of the
finished slopes
Pavement Design
Asphaltic concrete pavements are anticipated for the proposed roads Based on
the average CBR value of the on site natural clayey subgrade material and an assumed
Traffic Index of 9 the following pavement section may be used for preliminary budgeting
purposes until the finish subgrade material is verified and laboratory testing of this material
is conducted
Flexible Pavement
4 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
12 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
16 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Subgrade
The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
The aggregate base course materials should meet the requirements of Subsection
703 06 of the State of Hawaii Standard Specifications for Road Bridge and Public Works
Construction 1994 HSS The aggregate base course should be compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
The paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients should be maintained
to carry surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the
site during or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in
or adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs extend a minimum of
2 inches into the subgrade below the aggregate base course to reduce the potential for
migration of landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively a subdrain system
could be constructed to collect excess water from landscaping irrigation For long term
performance we recommend that a subdrain system be constructed adjacent to
paved landscape areas

report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The findings and discussions submitted in this letter report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between and
beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the findings and discussions provided in this letter report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping and pacing from the features shown on the Grading Plan
transmitted by Austin Tsutsumi Associates Inc on The approximate
elevations of field borings were estimated from the contour lines shown on the same plan
The physical locations and elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only to
the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition
As previously mentioned groundwater was encountered in our borings at the time of our
exploration However it must be noted that groundwater level changes may occur due to
tidal fluctuations seasonal precipitation surface runoff and other factors
