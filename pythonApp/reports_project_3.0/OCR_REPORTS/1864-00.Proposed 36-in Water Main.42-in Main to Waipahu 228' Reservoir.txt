In accordance with your request we have performed a soil
engineering evaluation for the above referenced project The
approximate location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general
accordance with our proposal dated March 30 1987
Purpose and Scope
A new 36 inch water main is planned to join the existing 42 inch
water main and the Waipahu 228 Foot Reservoir The total length of the
proposed water line is approximately 900 feet
The purpose of our study was to evaluate the anticipated excavation
conditions of the soils at the pipe bends in order to provide soil data
to aid in the design of the reaction blocks

The scope of our evaluation included field reconnaissance drilling
and sampling of three 3 borings laboratory testing of soil samples
obtained engineering analysis and report preparation
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
Proposed Construction
The proposed water line will be 36 inches in diameter The line
will be located between the existing 42 inch water main and the Waipahu
228 Foot Reservoir The horizontal distance of the line is
approximately 900 feet
It is anticipated that the invert of the proposed line would be at
a depth of about 5 to 10 feet below the existing grade
Site Description
The site is located in the Waikele District of Oahu approximately
one mile north of the Waipahu Sugar Mill The reservoir site is
situated in a cleared portion of cultivated sugar cane land east of
Waikele Stream Access to the site is by cane haul roads The site
slopes uniformly to the southeast at a 5 percent gradient Ground
surface elevations range from 180 feet to 206 feet above sea level

Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
three 3 borings to depths ranging from 15 9 to 19 5 feet below the
existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
Based on our borings the subsoil conditions at the reaction block
locations consist of dense clayey silt residual soil
Groundwater was not encountered in any of the borings during our
exploration
Discussion and Recommendations
General
In general the results of our exploration indicate that the
near surface soil at the reaction block locations can provide sufficient
bearing capacity for the proposed water line
Water Main Trench Section
The water line trench section presented on Plate 3 is recommended
The subgrade soil should be thoroughly tamped prior to the placement of
the pipe and bedding material
To minimize the required compactive effort and possible damage to
the pipe the backfill around and up to 1 foot above the pipe should
consist of clean granular material such as sand and lightly tamped or
jetted as appropriate The remaining portion of the backfill can
consist of on site soil compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum
density as established by the ASTM D 1557 78 test method
Settlements under the recommended bearing pressure are anticipated
to be minimal

The method and equipment used for excavation should be the choice
of the contractor within practical limits The anticipated depth
of excavation is 5 to 10 feet
Relatively easy excavation is anticipated in the clayey silt soils
Trench Wall Supports
The excavation should be provided with proper bracing in accordance
with applicable safety requirements The design and safety of the
trench supports should be the responsibility of the contractor
If sufficient space is available the excavation may be cut back at
a temporary slope of 1 1 H V in lieu of the above wall supports
However it should be taken into consideration that greater amounts
of vertical pressure will be transmitted to the pipe by widening of
the trench
Heavy equipment and stockpiles should be kept away from the tops of
the open trenches
The ground adjacent to and near the top of the excavation should be
continuously monitored for cracks or dips and other indications of
movements and possible sloughing of the trench walls Such
monitoring is particularly critical in areas where existing
structures such as other water lines are adjacent to the trench
excavation

Backfill
Granular material such as sand should be used for the backfill up
to about one foot above the pipe to reduce the compactive effort
required and possible damage to the pipe
Most of the excavated on site clayey silt soils can be re used as
the upper portion of the trench backfill from above one foot over the
pipe to the finished grade Backfill should be mechanically compacted
to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM
D 1557 78
A typical section of the pipe trench is presented on Plate 3
Reaction Blocks
It is our understanding that concrete reaction blocks will be
required at the pipe bends The reaction blocks should be constructed
against the stiff undisturbed clayey silt However if the block is
located in loose backfill the loose material should be over excavated
laterally at least 24 inches around the block except next to the pipe
and then backfilled with select borrow or suitable granular material
approved by the soils engineer The subsequent backfill should be
placed in horizontal lifts of 8 inches maximm and compacted to a
minimum density of 90 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
For the design of the reaction blocks a uniform lateral bearing of
3000 p s f may be used for the stiff in situ clayey silt soil
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the construction of the new water line
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and comments prior
to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the excavation and backfilling operations be
observed by the soils engineer during construction This is to see that
an adequate bearing has been obtained and the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our study The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon the above observations
Any imported backfill material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
Limitations
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed of implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this soil evaluation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by reconnaissance and soil borings These
unforeseen conditions such as pockets of organic soils trash fill or
hard layers may occur in localized areas Therefore additional
expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a properly
constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended to
accommodate these possible costs

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the proposed water main reaction blocks
were explored by drilling 3 borings at the approximate locations shown
on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drilling rig was used to
arill the borings which ranged in depth from 15 9 to 19 5 feet below the
existing ground surface
The soil encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by one of our engineers who
continuously monitored the drilling operations The classifications
were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the
laboratory The soils were classified in general conformance with the
Unified Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of the
materials encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1
through A 3
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler
with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts used to
drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Logs of Borings at
the appropriate sample depths
