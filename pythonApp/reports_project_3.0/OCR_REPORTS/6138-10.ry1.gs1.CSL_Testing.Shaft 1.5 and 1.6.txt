As requested, we performed Crosshole Sonic Logging (CSL) testing on Shaft
Nos. 1.5 and 1.6 installed for the above project. Based on our field observations and the
CSL test results, the following recommendations are for your review and consideration.
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project specification, Section 511 — Drilled Shafts, requires performing
CSL testing on Shaft Nos. 1.5 and 1.6 installed for the project. We performed the
CSL testing on May 17, 2022, for Shaft Nos. 1.5 and 1.6 installed for the above project.
Shaft No. 1.5 was drilled on May 4 and 5, 2022, followed by concrete placement on
May 6, 2022. Shaft No. 1.6 was drilled from May 9 to 11, 2022, followed by concrete
placement on May 11, 2022. The approximate locations of the shafts are presented on the
Site Plan, Plate 1. Four 2-inch diameter steel CSL access tubes were installed along with
the reinforcing cage. Details of the drilled shaft as-built conditions are summarized in the
following table:

CROSSHOLE SONIC LOGGING TESTING 
In general, the CSL testing was conducted in accordance with ASTM D6760 using 
the CHUM CSL Testing equipment manufactured by Piletest. Crosshole Sonic Logging 
tests were performed between all the possible access tube combinations in the drilled 
shaft (4 tubes resulting in 6 profiles). The northernmost access tube was designated as 
Access Tube No. 1 and subsequent tubes were numbered in a clockwise direction. 
The steel access tubes were extended an average of approximately 3.4 and 
5.2 feet above the top of the drilled shaft concrete for access considerations for Shaft 
Nos. 1.5 and 1.6, respectively. We understand that potable water was used to fill the 
access tubes after concrete placement. Based on our observation in the field, the water 
levels within the access tubes were above the top of shaft concrete in the access tubes at 
the time of testing. 
The CSL test results are based on the percentage decrease in velocity as 
correlated to the following Concrete Condition Rating Criteria (CCRC). Deviations from the 
following values are generally used for determining the Concrete Condition Rating. The 
CCRC is summarized in the table below.

The average apparent sonic wave velocity can be determined from the CSL test 
results by dividing the average tube distances by the average first arrival times for each 
tube combination in a drilled shaft. It should be noted that the average apparent sonic 
wave velocity computed in this manner is not an absolute number and should be used with 
caution because the actual tube distances below the top of the drilled shaft are not known 
accurately. 
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
A summary of the CSL test results, including the tested concrete length, computed 
apparent sonic wave velocity, and comments on the concrete condition, are presented on 
Plates 2.1 and 2.2. The CSL test results for the drilled shafts tested are summarized on 
Plates 3.1 and 3.2. The individual CSL test plots for each tested tube combination (profile) 
and a plan showing the tube locations are presented on Plates 4.1 through 4.4. 
Based on the CSL test results, the increased FATs were generally less than 10%, 
corresponding to a calculated wave velocity reduction of less than 10%, and the wave 
energy was relatively uniform in all the profiles for Shaft Nos. 1.5 and 1.6 except for the 
areas within the profile as described below. It should be noted that changes in the relative 
energy can sometimes indicate defects depending on the degree of the reduction and the 
associated increase in FAT. 
Shaft No. 1.5 (Profiles 1-2, 1-3, 2-3, and 2-4): FAT increases up to 22.3 
percent from approximately 9.2 to 11.3 feet below the top of concrete. 
Shaft No. 1.5 (Profile 1-3): FAT increases up to 13.5 percent from 
approximately 5.0 to 5.9 feet below the top of concrete. 
Shaft No. 1.5 (Profile 1-3): FAT increases up to 12.2 percent from 
approximately 7.2 to 8.1 feet below the top of concrete. 
Shaft No. 1.6 (Profile 1-4): FAT increases up to 18.2 percent from 
approximately 2.4 to 3.8 feet below the top of concrete. 
Shaft No. 1.6 (Profile 1-4): FAT increases up to 12.6 percent from 
approximately 5.0 to 5.7 feet below the top of concrete. 
Shaft No. 1.6 (Profile 1-4): FAT increases up to 21.7 percent from 
approximately 12.1 to 12.8 feet below the top of concrete. 
Shaft No. 1.6 (Profile 3-4): FAT increases up to 18.0 percent from 
approximately 7.4 to 8.8 feet below the top of concrete. 
The average apparent sonic wave velocities for the in-placed concrete for Shaft 
Nos. 1.5 and 1.6 ranged from approximately 11,507 to 12,813 and 11,126 to 14,289 feet
