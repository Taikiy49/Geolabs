Site Grading
Based on the information provided we anticipate the proposed site grading will
consist of cuts up to about 20 feet and fills up to about 25 feet In general we
recommend that fill areas be capped with 3 feet of structural fill to reduce the potential for
migration of fines from the upper portion of the fill through the voids of the compacted
rock fill Items of grading that are addressed in the following subsections include the
following 1 Site Preparation 2 Fill and Backfill Materials 3 Boulder Disposal 4 Fill
Placement and Compaction Requirements 5 Excavation and 6 Cut and Fill Slopes
Site grading operations should be observed by a _ representative of
Geolabs Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office observe the site
grading to evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation
and scarification process and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to
those encountered in our exploration
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork all areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site to reduce
potential contamination of the excavated materials Any scattered loose boulders
lying on the existing ground surface should be removed to expose firm ground
The boulders may be used as fill material provided that they are placed in
accordance with the Boulder Disposal subsection of this report
In areas to receive fill the subgrade should be proof rolled with a Caterpillar D 9
bulldozer or similar heavy equipment minimum 6 passes to help detect and
collapse near surface cavities Loose areas or cavities disclosed during clearing
operations should be backfilled with compacted fill

Fill and Backfill Materials
The fill and backfill materials to be used as general fill up to 3 feet below the
pavement subgrade level should consist of relatively well graded granular
materials less than 12 inches in largest dimension The on site cut material if less
than 12 inches in maximum dimension may be used as general fill or backfill
materials Fill materials with boulders greater than 12 inches in diameter may also
be used as fill material provided that they are placed at least 3 feet below the finish
subgrades In general placement of boulders in fills should be in accordance with
the procedures recommended in the Boulder Disposal subsection of this letter
report
The upper 3 feet of fill below the pavement subgrade level should consist of
well graded granular material 6 inches in maximum dimension with sufficient fines
to prevent the occurrence of voids in the compacted mass This is to facilitate
construction of the pavement structural section wall footings and sidewalks
If the excavated rock material does not contain sufficient fines to produce a
well graded material off site cinders or non expansive soil may be mixed into the
excavated material to increase the percentage of fines to provide a well graded
material for compaction and increase the stability of the fills Since imported fills
will be required for this project imported fills should conform to the requirements
above and should be observed and or tested by Geolabs Hawaii for its suitability
prior to being transported to the site for its intended use
Boulder Disposal
Boulders i e rock fragments larger than 12 inches in maximum size encountered
during site grading or imported may be used in the rock fills except within the
3 foot structural fill layer recommended as a cap above the general fill Boulders
may be used in rock fill provided that the following recommendations are followed
1 Boulders should be spread out and should not be nested together They
should be placed such that compaction equipment will be able to compact
between and around them
2 Boulders or portions of boulders should not encroach within 3 feet of the
finish subgrade elevation

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
In general fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches
in loose thickness moisture conditioned and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 The
finished subgrade of paved areas should be compacted to not less than
95 percent relative compaction The finished subgrade of fills should be well
graded free of voids not bony and unyielding
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in fills composed of
rocks boulders and or cobbles Instead a testing program to evaluate the number
of passes of a compactor needed to achieve the desired level of compaction
should be conducted at the start of the grading phase of the project under the
observation of a representative from Geolabs Hawaii On the basis of this testing
program the number of passes may then be used as the field criterion for
adequate compaction
Excavation
Based on experience in the area we anticipate that the site is underlain by slightly
to severely fractured volcanic rock ranging from medium hard to very hard It is
anticipated that the volcanic rock may be excavated with normal heavy excavation
equipment such as ripping with large bulldozers where the rock is near the
existing ground surface However excavations into the deeper formations may
require the use of hoerams chipping or blasting The above discussions
regarding the rippability of the surface materials are based on our visual

Cut and Fill Slopes
We understand that cut slopes up to about 20 feet high are planned along a short
segment of Kamehameha Ill Road Based on our experience in the area we
anticipate that the cut slope would expose relatively dense basalt rock with
frequent fractures In general we believe that the cut slopes planned may be
designed with a slope inclination of 1H 1V or flatter For cut slopes exposing
basalt rock formation we believe that intermediate slope benches may be omitted
for cut slopes up to 40 feet in vertical height
Based on the subsurface conditions anticipated at the site cavities and or clinker
zones may be encountered at the cut slope face In the event that clinker zones
are exposed at the cut slope face the cut slope may need to be flattened Large
cavities that may be exposed on the cut slope face should be backfilled and or
grouted if necessary A representative from Geolabs Hawaii should observe and
map the cut slope during construction to evaluate if the cut slope needs to be
flattened and if the exposed materials are consistent with our assumptions
In the event that over cutting of a cut slope occurs consideration should be given
to leaving the slope as is Otherwise keying and benching the fills should be
implemented instead of backfilling the slope to the design grade with sliver fills
Alternatively cut slopes may be buttressed with fill slopes constructed of
non expansive fills Water should be diverted away from the top of slopes and
slope planting should be provided as soon as possible to reduce potential erosion
of the finished slopes
Permanent fill slopes may be designed with a slope inclination of or flatter
For fill slopes steeper than 4H 1V with vertical heights greater than 30 feet we
recommend that a minimum 8 foot wide bench be provided at the mid point of the
slope to reduce the potential for erosion due to surface run off water
Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed and benched into the
existing slope to provide stability of the new fill against sliding The filling
operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level horizontal
compacted layers in accordance with the above fill placement recommendations
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the design slope
ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face

Construction Observation
Construction observation of the site grading work during construction is an integral
part of the geotechnical engineering recommendations provided herein The items of
construction monitoring that are critical which require Special Inspection include
observation of the site preparation fill placement and compaction and pavement
construction Other aspects of earthwork construction should also be observed by a
representative from Geolabs Hawaii This is to observe compliance with the intent of the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for
design changes that may be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from
those considered in this report The recommendations provided in this report are
contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications
to the design should be made
