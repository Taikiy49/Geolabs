In accordance with our revised fee proposal dated we have recently
conducted a geotechnical engineering study of foundation alternatives for the SMPR 85
Kahuanani Street Sewer Replacement project This letter report summarizes the
foundation alternatives studied provides estimated construction cost and presents
advantages and disadvantages of the foundation alternatives for the sewer replacement
for the above project
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed Street Sewer Replacement project is located in
the Waipahu area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 We understand that the
project consists of rehabilitation reconstruction of approximately 2 075 linear feet of 8 inch
diameter sewer lines within the project limits Sewer Line A is located along Kahuanani
Street and extends from Kahuanui Street to Kaha Street The project limits for Sewer Line
A are defined by Manhole 29322636 HNO5CR0281 located at the intersection of
Kahuanui Street and Kahuanani Street and extends to Manhole 29321579 HNO5CR0302
located at the intersection of Kahuanani Street and Kahu Street Sewer Line B is located
near Kaniau Place and is within a 10 foot easement between two privately owned parcels
Sewer Line B project limits are defined by Manhole 29322149 HNO5CR0632 at an
existing drainage canal and extends to Manhole 29321682 HNO5CR0728 located near
the end of Kaniau Place
The rehabilitation reconstruction of the existing sewer lines consist of a combination
of open trench and Cured In Place Pipe CIPP Liner methods of construction For
Sewer Line A two segments with a total length of 450 linear feet have been designated
as open trench construction Due to the soft compressible soils encountered below the
Sewer Line A site a gravel stabilization layer supported on 4 foot diameter jet grouted

CURRENT SEWER LINE FOUNDATION
The current foundation system design for Sewer Line A consists of a continuous
12 inch thick gravel stabilization layer supported on 4 foot diameter jet grouted columns
The gravel for the stabilization layer is wrapped with a filter fabric The jet grouted
columns are spaced in a staggered pattern at 5 feet on centers The columns extend
through the very soft swamp deposit and into underlying medium stiff to stiff alluvial
deposits with column lengths of about 28 feet
Construction of the current foundation system specifies that the trench shoring be
installed prior to jet grouting and the jet grouted columns be installed prior to trench
excavation for the replacement of the existing sewer line
For Sewer Line A one sewer manhole structure will be replaced The new
manhole structure will be supported on four 4 foot diameter jet grouted columns
The current foundation system design for Sewer Line B consists of a reinforced
concrete beam 2 feet wide by 1 75 feet in thickness supported on 12 inch square precast
prestressed concrete piles The concrete piles are spaced at 15 feet center to center
The piles extend through the very soft swamp deposit and bear on the underlying basalt
rock formation with pile lengths of about 10 feet

SEWER LINE A FOUNDATION ALTERNATIVES
Foundation alternatives that were studied for Sewer Line A consist of the use of a
concrete beam supported by different foundation elements consisting of driven concrete
piles micropiles and jet grouted columns The different pipe foundation systems are
described below and are shown on Plates 3 through 6
Concrete Beam Supported On Driven Pile Foundation
This foundation alternative consists of supporting the replacement sewer line on a
continuous reinforced concrete beam and driven pile foundation The reinforced concrete
beam is 2 feet wide and 1 75 feet in thickness The concrete beam will be supported on
12 inch square precast prestressed concrete piles driven through the very soft swamp
deposit and into the underlying medium stiff to stiff alluvial deposit The driven piles would
derive support primarily from side resistance developed between the pile perimeter and
the subsoil A sketch of this alternative is shown on the Concrete Beam on
Driven Pile Foundation Plate 3
The 12 inch square precast prestressed concrete piles will be spaced at 15 feet
from center to center Based on the above pile spacing we understand that the vertical
load on each pile will be about 52 kips
The new sewer line inverts range from 7 to 9 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
Based on our analysis a pile length of 52 feet is anticipated and was used in the study
For this foundation alternative we envision that the piles will be driven from the
existing ground surface Predrilling through the surface fill layer and existing sewer pipe
will be performed prior to pile driving to reduce the potential for significant noise and
vibration during pile driving Therefore a sewer line bypass system between existing
manholes will be required prior to predrilling to provide continuous sewer service in the
residential area In addition numerous temporary relocation of overhead utility lines is
anticipated to provide clearance for the pile driving equipment
There is one sewer manhole replacement for Sewer Line A The new manhole
structure will be supported on two 12 inch square concrete piles
Concrete Beam Supported On Micropile Foundation
The concrete beam supported on a micropile foundation alternative consists of a
continuous reinforced concrete beam and small diameter pile foundation The reinforced
concrete beam will be 2 75 feet wide and about 1 75 feet in thickness The concrete beam
will be supported on 7 inch diameter micropiles installed into the underlying medium stiff to
stiff alluvial deposits The upper portion of the micropile within the very soft swamp
deposit will be cased with steel tubing The steel casing will facilitate pile construction

Concrete Beam Supported on Jet Grouted Columns
This foundation alternative consists of a continuous reinforced concrete beam
structure supported on 4 foot diameter jet grouted columns The concrete beam will be

15 feet center to center The columns are spaced further apart compared to the current
stabilization layer design A sketch of this alternative is presented on the Concrete Beam
on Jet Grouted Column Foundation Plate 5
We understand that the jet grouted column loads will be about 73 kips per column
To resist the vertical loads a column length of about 51 feet is anticipated
For this foundation alternative we envision that the trench shoring and jet grouted
column installation will be performed prior to trench excavation and concrete beam
construction In addition some temporary relocation of existing overhead lines is
anticipated to accommodate the jet grouting equipment
The new sewer manhole structure will be supported on four 4 foot diameter
jet grouted columns similar to the current design

The foundation alternatives studied for Sewer Line B consist of a continuous
reinforced concrete beam supported on micropiles and jet grouted columns Based on the
field exploration performed at the site high artesian water pressure was encountered

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Foundation alternatives for the Street Sewer Replacement
project were studied for Sewer Line A These alternatives consist of a concrete beam
structure supported on driven piles micropiles and jet grouted columns In addition using
a steeper sewer line grade between SMH B 3 and SMH B 4 was studied
Concrete Beam Supported On Driven Pile Foundation
This foundation alternative was the least costly compared to the other foundation
alternatives and the current design utilizing a gravel stabilization layer supported on
jet grouted columns The estimated construction cost was about 1 14 MIL Because of
the low capacity of the piles we anticipated that load testing of the piles is not required
There are several disadvantages that need to be considered for this foundation
alternative system One of the major disadvantages is the longer construction duration
compared to the other alternatives We envision that the concrete piles will be driven from
the existing ground surface Therefore driving piles will occur early in the construction
schedule In addition a sewer bypass system would need to be in place prior to pile
driving Noise and vibration from pile driving would need to be considered Predrilling the
surface fill layer prior to pile driving would assist in reducing the amount of noise and
vibration to the surrounding area Another disadvantage is the numerous existing
overhead lines that need to be temporarily relocated to accommodate the pile installation
equipment
This alternative also presents a safety issue during pile installation to the existing
homes and homeowners Handling and installation of the long piles within the limited work
area would need to be carefully considered and planned to prevent accidents The
advantages and disadvantages of this foundation alternative are presented in the
Summary of Foundation Alternatives Plate 7
Although this foundation alternative is the least costly it would create major
disruption to the residential neighborhood Due to the above disadvantages we
recommend that this foundation alternative not be considered for Sewer Line A
Concrete Beam Supported On Micropile Foundation
Based on the cost estimate analysis this foundation alternative was the second
least costly with an estimated construction cost of about 1 16 MIL We envision that the
micropiles will be installed adjacent to the existing sewer line prior to excavation
Therefore the in place duration of the sewer bypass system will be less compared to the
driven pile alternative Since the micropile installation equipment has a lower overhead
clearance requirement we anticipate that temporary relocation of the existing overhead
lines may not be required In addition the micropile installation equipment is smaller in

This foundation alternative would require a pile load test program A disadvantage

Concrete Beam Supported on Jet Grouted Columns
This foundation alternative consists of a reinforced concrete beam supported on
widely spaced jet grouted columns This foundation alternative is less costly compared to
the current design with an estimated construction cost of 1 33 MIL but more costly
compared to the micropile alternative The jet grouted columns will be installed adjacent
to the existing sewer line prior to trench excavation Similar to the micropile alternative
the in place duration of the sewer bypass system will be shorter compared to the driven
pile alternative
The construction duration is longer for this foundation alternative with the
construction of the cast in place concrete beam compared to the current design with the
installation of a gravel stabilization layer In addition a jet grouted column test program
would be required The advantages and disadvantages of this foundation alternative are
presented in the Summary of Foundation Alternatives Plate 7
Based on the above we believe that the concrete beam supported on jet grouted
columns is a viable foundation alternative for Sewer Line A
Sewer Line B

SMH B 3 is adjacent to an existing house structure Condemnation of the house property
or acquiring additional easement may be required for sewer line installation
Based on the available subsurface information for the project it appears that the
thickness of the very soft swamp deposit may increase significantly between SMH B 2 and
SMH B 3 Therefore we recommend that additional field exploration be performed to
