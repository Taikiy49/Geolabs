PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The project is situated at the former CompUSA site in the Kaka`ako area of 
Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The site is bounded by Auahi Street to the north, 
Keawe Street to the east, Ala Moana Boulevard to the south, and South Street to the west. 
In general, the project involves the construction of a 43-story high-rise building, three and 
four-story townhomes and mid-rise residential buildings, and a seven-level parking 
structure. 
The new condominium tower is supported on deep foundations consisting of 
cast-in-place concrete drilled shafts. Drilled shafts with diameters of 48 and 60 inches 
and lengths ranging from about 92.2 to 110 feet were used to support the new high-rise 
residential building. As part of the construction requirements, one static load test was 
conducted to evaluate and confirm the drilled shaft foundation design. 
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the 
drilled shaft foundation construction for The Collection project only.  Hawaiian Dredging 
Construction Company, Inc. (HDCC) was the general contractor for the project and also 
installed the drilled shaft foundation support system. 
DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION 
As part of the project requirements, one sacrificial load test shaft was constructed 
and tested. Following completion of the load test, the drilled shaft lengths for the project 
were revised based on the results of the instrumented load test. The following subsections 
provide descriptions of the load test shaft construction and discussions on the production 
drilled shaft installation. 
Load Test Shaft 
As part of the project specifications, one sacrificial 48-inch diameter load test shaft 
was installed at the approximate location indicated on the Drilled Shaft Location Plan, 
Plate 2.1. The load test shaft was excavated by the drilled shaft contractor on 
November 17, 18, and 19, 2014. The 48-inch diameter load test shaft was extended to a 
depth of about 99.8 feet below the existing ground surface. The drilled shaft contractor 
lowered the reinforcing cage with an Osterberg Load Cell and embedment strain gage 
instrumentation into the drilled hole and placed concrete in the load test shaft on 
November 19, 2014. 
Temporary casing with a diameter of about 52 inches also was installed to about 
27.1 feet below the ground surface for the load test shaft. Details of the load test shaft 
construction are presented on the attached Plates 4.1 through 4.9.

Production Drilled Shafts 
The drilled shaft contractor installed 96 production shafts from December 17, 2014 
through February 13, 2015 at the locations shown on the Drilled Shaft Location Plans, 
Plates 2.1 and 2.2. The as-built diameters and the lengths of the production drilled shafts 
installed for the project are summarized on Plates 3.1 through 3.3 of this report. 
Representatives from Geolabs observed the drilled shaft excavation, placement of 
reinforcing cage, and concrete operations on a near-continuous (full-time) basis during the 
installation of the production drilled shafts. 
In general, the production drilled shafts were excavated using a combination of a 
Soilmec R-625, Bauer BG-40, and Bauer BG-28 rotary drill rigs. Tooling used for the 
60-inch diameter shafts included 63-inch diameter auger and 60-inch diameter auger, 
digging bucket, and clean-out bucket. A temporary steel casing with a diameter of 
62 inches was installed to depths of about 27 feet below the ground surface in the four 
60-inch diameter production drilled shafts. For the 48-inch diameter shafts, the tooling 
used was a 54-inch diameter auger, 48-inch diameter auger, 45-inch diameter digging 
bucket and clean-out bucket, and 42-inch diameter digging bucket. Temporary steel 
casings with a diameter of 52 inches were installed to depths of about 27 feet below the 
ground surface in the 48-inch diameter production drilled shafts. 
Basalt formation was encountered in several of the drilled shaft excavations at 
elevations higher than originally anticipated. Due to the very hard and slow drilling 
conditions in the basalt formation, the drilled shafts lengths were re-analyzed and reduced

After excavation of the production shafts to their designated tip elevations, the 
reinforcing cages were lowered into the drilled holes to the appropriate elevations with the 
use of a crane. After the reinforcing cage was installed in the drilled hole, concrete was 
placed through a 4-inch steel tremmie pipe starting from the bottom. During concrete 
placement, we observed that at least 5 feet of concrete head was maintained above the 
bottom of the tremmie pipe. 
Due to the presence of groundwater within the drilled shaft holes, concrete was 
placed in the drilled holes using the tremmie method. Concrete with slump ranging from 
7 to 9 inches was used for the drilled shafts. Consolidated Engineering Laboratories 
monitored the concrete used for the drilled shafts, including the performance of slump 
tests and compression tests. 
As with most foundation installation projects, some problems did arise during 
construction.  The various problems encountered and their remedial action, where 
applicable, are shown in the “Remarks” column of the Drilled Shaft Installation Summary. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives, it is our professional opinion 
that the production drilled shafts supporting The Collection project were installed in general 
accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and the geotechnical 
recommendations presented in our geotechnical engineering report (September 8, 2014) 
for the project and subsequent recommendations. In addition, it is our professional opinion 
that the drilled shafts have been installed to sufficient depths to provide the allowable 
design load capacities with a safety factor of 2.0.
