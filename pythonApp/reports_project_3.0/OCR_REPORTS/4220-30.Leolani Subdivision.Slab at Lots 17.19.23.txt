In accordance with our fee proposal dated we have
conducted a limited geotechnical engineering exploration for the reported moisture
infiltration experienced at some of the house slabs at the Leo lani Subdivision
Project Considerations
The project site is located at the existing Leo lani Subdivision in the Hawaii Kai
area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the information provided we understand
that some of the house lots may have experienced moisture infiltration through the
ground floor slab resulting in minor delamination of the flooring material
Therefore it was desired to conduct a limited geotechnical engineering
exploration to evaluate the moisture content of the in situ soils around the house slabs
at Lot and 23 The information obtained was used to evaluate whether the
existing house foundations have been compromised
Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the moisture content
of the in situ soils around the house slabs at Lot and 23 The information
obtained was used to evaluate whether the existing house foundations have been
compromised To accomplish this we conducted an exploration program consisting of
the following tasks and work efforts
1
Hand augering and sampling a total of seven boreholes to depths of about
1 to 2 feet below the existing ground surface at selected locations around
the house slab at Lot and 23
2
Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a
geologist from our firm

Findings and Discussions
As previously mentioned a limited geotechnical engineering exploration was
conducted to evaluate the moisture content of the in situ soils around the house slabs
at Lot and 23 The information obtained was used to evaluate whether the
existing house foundations have been compromised
The field exploration was conducted on by using a 1 5 inch
diameter hand auger with a maximum auger depth of approximately 5 feet A total of
nine soil samples were obtained at selected boring locations around the house slabs at
Lot and 23 as shown on the attached Site Plan Plate 1 The boring
locations were sited as close as possible to the exterior building walls of the house
structure The soil samples were collected at a depth corresponding to approximately
the slab subgrade level or at depths of about 1 to 2 feet below the existing ground
surface The soil samples collected were placed in a sealed container and transported
to our laboratory for testing immediately upon completion of our field exploration
program
The soil samples consisted of brown and gray clay with varying amounts of
gravel Based on the laboratory test results it appears that the moisture contents of the
in situ soils at the slab subgrade level generally range between about 12 to 41 percent
The major difference in the moisture contents reflects the varying amounts of gravel
present Our review of the corresponding proctor values for the materials encountered
suggested that the in situ soils at the slab subgrade level generally exhibit moisture
contents at or slightly above the optimum values This is also confirmed by our visual
observation of the soil samples collected at the time of the field exploration which were
generally damp to slightly moist in condition Results of the moisture content tests
conducted on the soil samples collected during our field exploration are summarized in
the following table

Based on our visual observations and laboratory test results we believe that the
in situ soils at the slab subgrade level generally exist in an optimal state with moisture
contents at or slightly above their optimum values
It should be noted that special house foundations were designed for the
subdivision utilizing perimeter footings deepened to 24 inches to reduce the potential
for soil moisture fluctuations underneath the house slab Furthermore significant
additional steel reinforcement was incorporated into the house slab to reduce the
potential for slab cracking resulting from the swelling of the in situ clayey soils The
concrete house slabs were placed on 3 feet of non expansive structural fill Based on
the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration and our evaluation it is our
opinion that the existing house foundations at Lot and 23 are functioning
as designed and have not been compromised
