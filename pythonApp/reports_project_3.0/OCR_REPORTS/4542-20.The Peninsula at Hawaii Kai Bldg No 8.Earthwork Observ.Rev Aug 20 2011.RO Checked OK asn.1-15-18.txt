Dear Mr Riegels
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing
services performed during site work construction building foundation construction and
utility line installation for the Building No 8 formerly known as Building Three portion of
The Peninsula at Hawaii Kai project in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
May 7 2001 and additional budget request dated
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located along both sides of Wailua Street and west of the
intersection with Lunalilo Home Road in the Hawaii Kai area on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The general location and vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 Most of the site is a peninsula constructed of man made materials extending
out into Kuapa Pond The total project area is about 42 65 acres Building No 8 is
located at Parcel C at the southeastern portion as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the site work construction building foundation construction and utility line
installation for the Building No 8 portion of The Peninsula at Hawaii Kai project only
SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
We observed the site work construction building foundation construction and utility
line installation for the Building No 8 portion of The Peninsula at Hawaii Kai project from
August 2004 to November 2005 The mass grading construction consisted of clearing and

grubbing and placement and compaction of fills and backfills on the order of up to about
5 feet thick to achieve the design finished grades for the project The mass grading work
was performed by Goodfellow Brothers Inc The building foundation construction

foundations slabs on grade and utility installation for the project The building foundation
construction was generally performed by Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company Inc
Samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials
were obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were generally moisture conditioned to above
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway and parking
areas the last lift of fill and backfill materials were generally compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the earthwork operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the
compaction tests performed are summarized on the attached Plates 4 1 through 4 13
These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed as
unsatisfactory were generally reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the

period covered by this report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the
