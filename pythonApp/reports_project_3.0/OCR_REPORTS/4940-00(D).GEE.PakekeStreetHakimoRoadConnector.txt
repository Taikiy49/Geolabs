PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves the construction of emergency access roads along the
Waianae Coast on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The proposed emergency access roads
will involve improvements of existing roads and construction of new roads to
interconnect existing roads for community use during emergency situations The
general location and vicinity of the various sites are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 The proposed new roadway alignments include the following
Helelua Place Extension
Paakea Road Hakimo Road Lualualei Naval Road Connector
Nanakuli Makai Improvements Nanakuli Avenue to Laumania Avenue
Pakeke Street Hakimo Road Connector
Kaulawaha Road Ala Hema Street Plantation Road Connector
Kaulawaha Road Makaha Valley Road to Mahinaau Road
This report only addresses the portion of emergency access roads connecting
Pakeke Street to Hakimo Road for a total length of approximately 5 280 lineal feet The
new roadway will consist of two 12 foot wide travelways with a 10 foot wide grassed area
on both sides In general we envision that the proposed roadway improvements will only
involve subgrade preparation and placement of the pavement structural section with
minimal site grading work anticipated for the project construction

The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration was to obtain an overview
of the surface and subsurface conditions at the site The subsurface information obtained
was utilized to develop a soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertaining to the design of pavements and site preparation only To
accomplish this we conducted an exploration program consisting of the following tasks
and work efforts

operators to and from the project site

_ 5 to 6 5 feet below the existing ground surface

exploration as an aid in classifying the materials encountered and
evaluating their engineering properties
5 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of
pavements and site preparation for the proposed project
6 Preparation of this report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
7 Coordination of our overall work on the project by a senior engineer from

9 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the Lualualei area in the District of Waianae on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The proposed roadway extension traverses over vacant land
extending from Pakeke Street on the west to Hakimo Road on the east as shown on the
Site Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2 The project area is generally vacant lands slightly
vegetated with small trees and short grasses Based on the plan and profile drawing
provided the existing ground elevations within the new roadway extension alignment
generally range between about 66 and 92 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling five borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 5 extending to depths of about 5 to 6 5 feet below
the existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig
equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers Two bulk samples designated as
Bulk 1 and Bulk 2 were obtained from the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement
support characteristics The approximate locations of the borings drilled and bulk
samples collected at the site are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings
Plates 4 through 8
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 3 inch outside diameter OD Modified California sampler with a
140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the
drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration
Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration
sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler
the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration
Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling five borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 5 extending to depths of about 5 to 6 5 feet below
the existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig
equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers The approximate locations of the
borings drilled at the site are shown on Site Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2
In general our field exploration disclosed that the project site may be underlain by
highly expansive clays to surface fills over coral reef formation at shallow depths Based
on a review of the geology and our experience in the area we anticipate that the subsoils
underlying the proposed roadway alignment could range from Mokuleia clay consisting of
recent alluvium of high plasticity to coral deposits and consolidated terrestrial sediments in
a very dense state with cementation Laboratory CBR tests conducted on the Mokuleia
clays indicate the soils exhibit high swelling potential with relatively low CBR values when
subjected to moisture fluctuations
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our field exploration
However groundwater levels are subject to change due to rainfall seasonal precipitation
surface water runoff and other factors Descriptions and graphic representation of the
materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates 3 through 8 of this
report
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Detailed discussion of our geotechnical engineering recommendations for design of
pavements and site preparation are presented in the following sections of this report
Pavement Design
Based on our field exploration and laboratory testing performed for the project
we believe that the majority of the proposed roadway alignment connecting Pakeke
Street and Hakimo Road is underlain by highly expansive clayey soils with localized
areas exposing coralline materials Laboratory testing performed on the clayey soils
generally indicated a relatively low CBR value of less than 5 and a swell of over
10 percent Based on the field exploration and laboratory tests the following pavement
sections are recommended

Where expansive clayey soils are encountered at or near the bottom of the
base course layer the pavement subgrade should be over excavated to the coral layer or
to provide the 18 inch aggregate subbase layer as recommended above The
over excavated subgrade should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to
a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction If coralline deposits are exposed at or near
the bottom of the base course layer the subgrade should be proof rolled by a minimum
10 ton vibratory drum roller a minimum of eight passes to help detect near surface cavities
and or loose zones prior to the pavement construction The aggregate base course and
aggregate subbase should consist of crushed basalt aggregate compacted to not less than
95 percent relative compaction CBR and field density tests should be performed on the
actual subgrade soils encountered during construction to confirm the adequacy of the
above sections The recommended sections assume that good drainage will be provided
for the paved areas
Site Preparation
Based on the existing topography and the anticipated design finish grades for the
proposed roadway improvements we anticipate that minimal site grading work will be
required for the project construction In general the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed at the on set of earthwork All
vegetation debris deleterious material and other unsuitable materials should be
removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and yielding areas encountered during
clearing below any areas designated to receive fill or future improvements should be
over excavated to expose firm and or stiff material and the resulting excavation should
be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft and or organic
soils should be properly disposed of off site

Existing structures and pavements that are to be demolished should be
completely removed Any over excavation resulting from the demolition should be
backfilled with compacted granular fill material Existing utilities to be abandoned
should be removed and the resulting excavation should be properly backfilled with
granular fill material moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Utilities that are to be abandoned in place under the proposed structures
should be backfilled by pumping lean concrete under low pressure
After clearing grubbing and demolition future pavement areas should be
excavated where necessary to the pavement subgrade level i e bottom of the base
course or subbase layer In areas where expansive clay soils are encountered at the
pavement subgrade level the pavement subgrade should be scarified to a depth of
about 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density If coralline deposits are exposed at the pavement subgrade
level the subgrade should be proof rolled by a minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller a
minimum of eight passes to help detect near surface cavities and or loose zones prior to
pavement construction
If the subgrade soils are pumping during subgrade preparation the pumping
subgrade may be stabilized by cement treatment to reduce the potential for pumping
subgrade conditions As a guide one sack of cement may be used for approximately
25 square feet of subgrade area
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soils be scarified and re prepared as recommended above
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather
and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted engineered fill Contract documents should include additive and
deductive unit prices for over excavation and engineered fill placement to account for
variations in the over excavation quantities
The aggregate base course and aggregate subbase materials should meet the
requirements of Sections 30 and 31 of the City and County Standard Specifications
The materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction

Imported fill materials below the subbase and base course layers if required
should consist of non expansive select granular material such as crushed coral or
basalt The material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger
than 3 inches in largest dimension In addition the material should contain between
10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have a
laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or
less when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 The materials
should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in
8 inch level loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Imported fill materials should be tested by Geolabs for conformance with these
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative from Geolabs It
is important that a representative from our office observe the site preparation to
evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation and
scarification process and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those
encountered in our field exploration
Pavement Drainage
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due
to saturation of the base course subbase and or subgrade soils Therefore the
pavement surface should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the pavement to appropriate drainage structures Surface water
ponding should not be allowed anywhere on the site during or after construction Where
concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or adjacent to the pavement areas we
recommend that the curbs extend a minimum of 2 inches into the subgrade soils below
the base and subbase course layers to reduce migration of landscape water into the
pavement section Alternatively a subdrain system could be constructed to collect
excessive water from landscaping irrigation For long term performance we
recommend that a subdrain system be considered along the sides of the roadway
