PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The 2100 Kalakaua Avenue development is located in the middle of Waikiki in the
District of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is located at the
northeast corner of the intersection between Kalakaua Avenue and Kalaimoku Street It is
proposed to develop the site into a 110 000 square foot retail complex We understand
that the proposed commercial development will consist of a three story steel framed
structure with some associated infrastructure The commercial development will comprise
of eight units ranging in sizes from about 5 000 to 15 000 square feet
Based on the development plans provided the finished floor elevation at the ground
surface level for the proposed retail stores is set at Elevation 7 5 feet Mean Sea Level
MSL In order to provide a level building pad for the proposed construction we envision
that fills of up to 3 feet thick will be required to raise the existing ground surface to the
design finished grades Due to the presence of loose subsoils a settlement monitoring
program was recommended
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the mass grading operations for the project site In addition the results of the
settlement monitoring program are presented The location sof the settlement gauges are
presented on the Site Plan Plate 2

MASS GRADING
The mass grading operation for the 2100 Kalakaua Avenue project was observed
by Geolabs Inc from to The mass grading
operation consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills of up
to about 3 feet in thickness to achieve the design finished grades for the project The
mass grading operation was performed by Delta Construction Corporation During the
mass grading operations samples of imported materials utilized as fill materials were
obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized
on Plate 3
In general the fill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 In the parking lot area the fill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction
During the mass grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 and D3017 Nuclear Gauge
Method Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 and
4 2 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes where available These locations and elevations
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas
assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of the
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
