This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing
services performed during mass and pad grading and utility and roadway construction for
the Ewa by Gentry Area 40 Phase 3 project from August 6 2012 to March 6 2013 in
accordance with our fee proposal dated August 2 2012 and additional budget request
dated March 7 2013
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Ewa by Gentry Area 40 site is on the western side of Fort Weaver Road
south of Geiger Road in the Ewa District on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site
is bounded by Area 12 to the north Fort Weaver Road to the east Keaunui Drive to the
south and Kapolei Parkway to the west The Area 40 site is divided into three
increments with Phase 3 at the western portion of the site adjacent to Kapolei Parkway
between the Phase 2 increment and the future park site A Project Location Map and
Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2 respectively
The project involves mass and site grading 75 house pads for a residential
development consisting of house lots and related access roads The site grading work
involves fills up to about 7 feet thick In addition the project includes utility and roadway
construction
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the mass and pad grading and utility and roadway construction at the project site

MASS AND PAD GRADING AND UTILITY AND ROADWAY CONSTRUCTION
We observed the mass and pad grading and utility and roadway construction work
for the Area 40 Phase 3 project from August 6 2012 to March 6 2013 The mass and site
grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills on
the order of up to about 7 feet thick to achieve the finished grades Paradigm Construction
LLC performed the mass and site grading and utility and roadway construction work
During the mass and site grading operations samples of the on site and imported
materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office
for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established
in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to near the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the mass and site grading and utility and roadway construction operations
periodic field density tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 6938
Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on
Plates 4 1 through 4 50 These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and
on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated
by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas
assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was
obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and
hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the mass and site grading and utility and roadway
construction work for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period
covered by this report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the
recommendations in the following references

LIMITATIONS

grades on the above project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
knowledge and written acknowledgment
