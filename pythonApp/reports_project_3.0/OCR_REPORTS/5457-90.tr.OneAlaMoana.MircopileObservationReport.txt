PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The One Ala Moana condominium project consists of a 16 story high rise
residential tower and two levels of additional parking to be built on top of the existing
parking garage structure on the mauka side of the Ala Moana Center in Honolulu
Hawaii The existing parking garage is abutted by the Nordstrom Building to the west
the existing Ala Moana Pacific Building to the east Kapiolani Boulevard to the north
and Kona Street to the south
Based on the updated design of the tower structure the loading for one of the
columns will increase from 2 000 kips to 5 000 kips Therefore 21 micropiles were
designed and installed to augment the existing drilled shaft foundation at the column
location to provide the necessary load bearing support for the new residential tower
The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the
micropile foundation construction and testing for the One Ala Moana project only The
micropile contractor for the project is Kelikai Inc

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on previous studies performed by others and the information obtained
from Boring No 7 conducted during our 2005 field exploration subsurface conditions at
the column location generally consist of fill materials comprised of medium stiff silty
clays at the surface extending to a depth of about 3 feet below the existing grades The
surface fill materials generally were underlain by lagoonal deposits consisting of loose
sandy gravel and soft sandy clays extending to a depth of about 26 5 feet below the
existing ground surface It should be noted that the lagoonal deposits encountered at
the project site are highly compressible when subjected to structural loads
Below the compressible lagoonal deposits our boring generally encountered a
coral ledge on the order of about 14 feet in thickness The lagoonal deposits and coral
ledge generally were underlain by coralline detritus materials consisting of medium
dense silty coralline sands and gravel with localized loose and dense zones extending
to the maximum depth explored of about 128 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled boring at a depth of about 5 feet below the
existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration The groundwater level
encountered generally corresponds to about Elevation 1 9 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
MICROPILE INSTALLATION
Geotechnical recommendations for the micropile foundations were presented in
the Micropile Foundations section of our geotechnical consultation letter report dated
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering observation and
consultation services was to observe the micropile foundation installation for general
conformance with our recommendations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the project vicinity the
micropiles were specified to extend to a minimum depth of about 60 feet below the
ground level with a 35 foot load bearing grout bulb developed within the coralline
materials and a 25 foot grout zone neglected within the lagoonal deposits To reduce
risk of micropile necking within the loose soft lagoonal material 25 feet of permanent
steel casing was specified to be left in place at the top of each micropile The design
micropile allowable compressive load capacity was specified to be 150 kips
In general the micropiles were drilled using a TE HEM550R rig equipped with
steel casing to the specified depth Advancing steel casing to the bottom of the specified
depth was required in order to facilitate the micropile drilling and maintain grouting
quality Air pressure up to 100 pounds per square inch psi was utilized during drilling
operation to create a bulb around the drilling bit for advancing temporary steel casing
After the drilled holes were extended to the specified depth and cleaned with air
pressure a reinforcing bar consisting of Grade 75 all threaded 18 rebar was placed in
the cased hole with centralizers Grout was batched on site utilizing a colloidal

Micropile Testing
In order to evaluate the load carrying capacities of the installed micropiles
pullout tests proof tests were conducted on production and MP 12
on September 25 and 26 2013 respectively In general the micropiles were loaded to
a maximum test load of 225 kips 150 percent of the design load of 150 kips in
approximately 18 75 kip load increments The micropiles were held at the maximum test
load of 225 kips for 10 minutes to evaluate the creep effects Subsequently the
micropiles were unloaded to zero load in about 56 25 kip decrements
Based on our review and engineering evaluation the and MP 19
did not fail at the maximum test load of 225 kips At the maximum test load the creep
movement during the 10 minute holding period was about 0 002 and 0 001 inches for
and MP 19 respectively which is less than 0 04 inches typically allowed
for the ground anchor system At maximum test load the vertical displacements of
testing are presented in Proof Test Data Plates 4 1 and 4 2 The testing load versus
micropile displacements are presented on Plates 5 1 and 5 2 while the creep movements
are presented on Plates 6 1 and 6 2
CONCLUSIONS
Based on our field observations and the proof tests conducted it is our
professional opinion that the specified micropiles for the project have been installed to
the required diameter and sufficient depth to increase the design load capacity of the
pile cap from 2 000 to 5 000 kips with a factor of safety of 2 0
