As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil conditions
below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study was to
evaluate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation
support for the proposed house The study consisted of drilling three
3 borings within the proposed building site performing laboratory
soil tests and reviewing of the grading and compaction test records
on the building site This report summarizes our observations and
recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed single family residence consists of a two story wood
structure approximately 44 by 65 feet in plan dimension with a
basement level in the rear portion of the house
Based on the preliminary plans the house will be set back about 33
feet from Kahena Street Concrete on grade slab is planned for the
lower level garage floor driveway and basement The basement
finished floor elevation is anticipated to be about 227 feet
Retaining walls are planned around the northern eastern and southern
sides of the house Up to 9 feet of cut is planned for the basement
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lot 22 is located in the northern portion of the subdivision
development on the northeastern side of Kahena Street It is bounded
by Kahena Street on the west subdivision lots on the northeast and
south An existing concrete drainage ditch is located along the lower
eastern boundary of the lot The lot generally slopes downward from
Kahena Street towards the east at about 10 percent gradient The
existing elevation of the lot generally varies from about 213 to 242
feet
SUBDIVISION LOT GRADING
Prior to the recent subdivision site grading operation most of Lot 22
was covered with a large stockpile of soil and boulders left over from
previous grading operations in the adjacent development area

During the project site grading operation the stockpile was removed

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A review of the grading compaction test record and the site
reconnaissance indicate that Lot 22 is underlain by a layer of gray
brown expansive adobe clay of at least 8 5 feet thick In the rear
portion of the lot where the basement area is planned the gray brown
clay is underlain at 8 5 feet by generally non expansive mottled
orange brown silty sand layer containing highly weathered gravel and
cobble
Detailed description of the soils encountered in borings are presented
on the attached Log of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3 The approxi
mate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Laboratory soil tests on the near surface material indicate that the
soil can be classified as clay CH according to the Unified Soil
Classification System Laboratory one inch ring swell tests indicate
that the soil has a moderate to high shrink swell potential The
laboratory test results are summarized on Plate B 1l

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by grayish
brown clay fill The laboratory test results on this material
generally indicated that they have moderate to high expansion
potential Therefore for Lot 22 the on grade slab and foundation
construction would require special design procedures in order to
reduce the shrink swell effect of the subsoils
House Foundation
On grade slab is planned for the garage and basement floor level The
on site clay soils are expansive and unsuitable as slab subgrade
soils In order to reduce the expansive effects of the soils the
expansive clays within a depth of 3 feet below the slab subgrade level
should be over excavated and replaced with non expansive borrow soils
such as select borrow crusher waste or coral compacted to a minimum
of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The
over excavation should extend for a minimum distance of 3 feet beyond
the slabs
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab and
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to protect
against moisture infiltration a polyethylene moisture barrier may be
incorporated below the slab The membrane should be protected by a
thin layer of sand to aid in the concrete curing

The thickened edge slab footings can bear on the compacted
non expansive fill The thickened edges should be embedded a minimum
of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on the compacted
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations
Alternate Foundation System
We recommend that the proposed house foundation system be re evaluated
in an effort to reduce the foundation construction costs
For this lot post and beam construction is desirable and recommended
due to the sloping terrain and expansive soil conditions The
post and beam construction generally results in lower foundation costs
and the individual footings can be readily adjusted should the need
occur
For post and beam construction we recommend that the expansive clays
be over excavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the bottom of the
individual post footings and then replaced with non expansive borrow
soils such as crusher waste select borrow or coral compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The
spread footings can then be placed on the compacted non expansive soil

Alternatively the individual post footing can be embedded a minimum
of 36 inches below the finished ground surface
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the compacted select material or stiff existing ground
The use of post and beam construction could also eliminate the
basement retaining wall presently planned
Foundations next to Utility Lines
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
The rear wall of the house is situated adjacent to the sewer
easement The as built drawings indicate that the sewer line is about
3 feet deep in this area We recommend that the foundations along the
rear wall be extended down to about 4 feet deep in order to minimize
the possibility of footing disturbance due to future sewer excavation
and to prevent footing load on the sewer pipe

Basement and Retaining Walls Foundation
Basement and retaining walls up to 9 feet high are planned
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the existing clay soils on
the wall we recommend that the expansive clays be over excavated down
for a depth of 24 inches below the bottom of the footing and then
replaced with non expansive borrow soils such as crusher waste
select borrow or coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The overexcavation and replacement
should extend a minimum distance of 12 inches beyond the footing width
The wall footing should be embedded a minimum depth of 12 inches below
the outside finished grade Therefore the total depth of the above
over excavation and replacement would be 3 feet below the outside
finished grade
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used
In order to reduce the build up of high lateral earth pressures on the
wall due to the existing clay soils off site non expansive soil
should be used as backfill for a minimum distance of 4 feet behind the
wall

Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
Expansive clay soils were encountered along the proposed driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the clay
over excavation and replacement can be reduced to 2 feet below the
driveway subgrade level Where 2 or more feet of fill is planned
select non expansive borrow material should be used to cap over the
expansive clay Where less than 2 feet of fill is planned
over excavation and replacement of the expansive clay with select
non expansive material is required The above requirements should be
followed for a minimum distance of 3 feet beyond the driveway

For future sidewalks capping of the clay or over excavation and
replacement similar to the driveway is recommended to reduce heaving
caused by the expansive clay soil
Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down at about 10
gradient
Up to 9 feet of cut in the basement and 4 feet of fill in the garage
are anticipated
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and debris prior
to filling
Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established
by ASTM D 1557 78
Two horizontal to 1 vertical cut and fill slope ratios are recommended
The finished grade outside the slabs pavements and foundations
should be shaped to avoid ponding conditions and to shed water away
from the structures

Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during earthwork
construction particularly the fill placement slope cuts and to
monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be examined by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the finding of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections

principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other
