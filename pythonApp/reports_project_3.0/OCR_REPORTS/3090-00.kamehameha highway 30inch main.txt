INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Kamehameha Highway 30 inch water main in Waikane and
Kualoa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site
are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses The
recommendations presented herein are intended for design input and may not be
appropriate to form the basis for preparation of final construction cost estimates nor for
construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a 30 inch diameter water main from Kamaka Place in
Waikane to about Kualoa Regional Park in Kualoa for a distance of approximately 6 600
lineal feet The project will encompass a bridge crossing at Hakipuu Stream and
connections to the existing water mains
Based on the plan and profile provided the invert of the proposed water main will
be about 6 to 8 feet below the existing ground surface elevation of about 25 to 82 feet
Mean Sea Level MSL We understand that approximately 6 600 lineal feet of trench
excavation water pipe installation and trench backfill will be required for this project The
project will also include construction of a water pipe bridge crossing Hakipuu Stream

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration indicates that in general very stiff clayey subsoils encountered
within the planned excavation depths along most of the proposed water line alignment
should provide adequate bearing support for the new water main Soft soils were
encountered at about 8 feet below the ground surface in Boring No 4 Therefore it
should be anticipated that trench excavation for waterline installation may encountered
soft soils The very stiff soils encountered along water main alignment should provide
adequate bearing for the proposed water main

Because of the medium stiff and medium dense soils encountered at bearing
stratum in Boring Nos 6 and 7 the shallow foundation system is not adequate to support
water main pipe at stream crossing Therefore a deep foundation system consisting of
drilled piers of 15 to 20 feet is recommended to support the proposed water main at
stream crossing
Detailed discussions and recommendations of the geotechnical aspects of the
project are presented in the following sections of this report
Pipeline at Stream Crossing
Because of the proximity of the abutments to the edge of the steep stream bank
medium stiff and medium dense soils encountered at bearing stratum and the moderate
structural loads a shallow foundation system for support of the water main is not suitable
In lieu of a costly driven pile foundation we recommend a foundation system consisting
of 24 inch diameter cast in place drilled piers for support of the proposed water main at
stream crossing Drilled piers should be limited to about 20 feet below surface due to the
presence of granular soils about 20 feet below surface which may pose difficulties during
construction
The cast in place drilled piers would derive vertical support from skin friction
between the pier shaft and the surrounding soils The drilled piers may be designed with
an allowable skin friction of 600 pounds per square foot p s f The allowable skin
friction value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 4 for
transient loads such as wind or seismic forces The end bearing capacity of the piers
should be discounted due to the presence of groundwater and potential caving of the silty
sand layer near the anticipated tip of the drilled piers that would preclude obtaining a
clean bottom during drilled pier installation Drilled piers should have a minimum center
to center spacing of 21 times the diameter to avoid capacity reduction due to group action

the stiffness of the pier shaft allowable deflection at the top of pier and included moment

under service loading inch deflection at top of pier and ultimate loading inch

The load bearing capacities of drilled piers depend to a large extent on the
contact between the pier shaft and the surrounding soils Therefore proper construction
techniques are important The contractor should exercise care in drilling the pier holes
and in placing concrete into the holes Drilling by methods utilizing drilling fluids is not

the groundwater level A representative of Geolabs Hawaii should be present to observe
the installation of drilled pier during construction
A low shrink concrete mix should be used to provide close contact between the
pier shafts and the surrounding soil The concrete should be placed promptly after drilling
to reduce the potential of caving in The concrete should also be placed in a suitable
manner to reduce segregation of the aggregates from the concrete mix In lieu of
dewatering the pier holes placement of concrete by tremie method is recommended
During concrete placement the tremie pipe should be kept in the concrete at all times
maintaining a minimum of 5 feet of concrete above the bottom of the tremie pipe

Settlements
The new water main construction in general should not materially increase the
vertical loading on the underlying soils if no additional fills are placed above the
existing ground surface The net difference in loading between the weights of the
excavated material and backfill should be minimal
Based on the boring data we believe that the stiff soils encountered in Borings
9 and 10 at the planned water invert elevations generally have
good bearing characteristics Therefore significant settlements are not anticipated
along the proposed water alignment if proper construction techniques are utilized
to backfill the trench Generally ground settlements on the order of inch are
anticipated for these trench areas
However soft clayey soil may be encountered at the anticipated invert elevations
of the proposed water main close to the vicinity of Boring No 4 and possibly at
other areas The new water pipes embedded in soft ground areas should be
provided with a minimum 2 foot thick bedding and cushion layer as discussed in
the following section entitled Pipe Bedding It is estimated that total long term
settlements on the order of inch may occur
Pipe Bedding
Pipe bedding is an important design consideration since distribution of bearing
stresses against the pipe bottom has a great impact upon the load support
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3090 00 Page 11
capacity of the pipe We recommend that a minimum of 6 inches of clean granular
bedding material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel be
placed below the proposed water pipes bearing on the stiff subsoil conditions
generally between Boring Nos 1 through 3 and Boring Nos 5 through 10
The granular 3B Fine gravel bedding material should extend beyond the pipe
sides a minimum width of one fourth the outside pipe diameter and it also should
extend half way up the pipe sides
For pipes bearing on the soft soil deposits an additional 18 inch layer of clean
granular material such as 3B Fine gravel wrapped in filter fabric should be
provided below the pipe and the 6 inch bedding layer to provide more uniform

Trench Support
Since the trench excavations are anticipated to be 10 feet below the surface and
groundwater may encountered during excavations appropriate type of temporary
shoring systems should be used to reduce the potential for caving in of the trench
walls
The shoring and bracing system used to support the trenches must comply with
all applicable safety requirements The adequacy and safety of the shoring and
bracing installation operations should be made the responsibility of the contractor
His representative who should be required to continually monitor the excavation
and dewatering work at the site would therefore have the opportunity for
observing changing conditions which might affect the excavation stability during
construction
For estimating earth loads in the struts of the trench bracing the apparent
pressure diagram presented on Plate 6 may be used as a guide An appropriate
safety factor should be included by the contractor s shoring structural designer
Appropriate loads due to surcharge equipment traffic or sloping ground conditions
adjacent to the excavations should be added to the recommended earth pressures
for design purposes
Even if good construction procedures are followed some movement of the
excavation support bracing system may be anticipated because of changes in
earth stresses during excavation Movements depend greatly on workmanship
e g how quickly and tightly the support is installed It also depends on the types
of subsoils the depth and width of excavation and the type of support In view of
the complexity of the problem it is difficult to accurately estimate the amount of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3090 00 Page 13
movement to be anticipated of a bracing system and the adjacent ground
Therefore it is important to realize that the excavation support should be installed
as early as practicable and that measurements of vertical and horizontal
movements are required especially in areas where ground movements may result
in damage to existing adjacent structures
The ground adjacent to excavations should be continuously monitored for cracks
or dips and other indications of movements and possible sloughing of the
excavation walls Such monitoring is particularly critical in areas adjacent to

Backfill
In general the requirements for trench backfill should be in accordance with the
Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction September 1986
The first zone of backfill extends from the bedding material to at least 12 inches
above the top of the water main pipes Select free draining well graded granular
material is recommended for the first zone of backfill to reduce the compaction
effort required and resulting stresses to the pipe The granular material in this
zone should be of basaltic rock aggregates less than 1 inch in size and have no
more than 10 percent passing No 200 sieve Compaction by water ponding or
jetting may be allowed provided the granular material is self draining The use of
coral material to backfill around the waterline should be evaluated in conjunction
with the water pipe s corrosion protection requirement
The trench backfill from 12 inches above the top of the pipes to within 2 feet of the
finish subgrade may consist of the excavated on site soils with the exception of the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3090 00 Page 14
soft to medium stiff clayey soil deposits The backfill should be placed in thin lifts
with each lift mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
The trench backfill within 2 feet of the pavement surface elevation should consist
of select borrow compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 A
typical section of the pipe trench is presented on Plate 5
Precautions should be taken against the occurrence of pipeline floatation before
and during trench backfilling due to possible influx of large quantities of groundwa
ter into the trenches The uplift buoyant forces due to groundwater influx may lift
empty or even partially filled pipes
Trenches should be kept free of water during backfilling Should backfilling under
water be necessary granular material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 should be utilized to reduce the potential of future settlement of the trench
backfill

Manhole Structures
Structures supported on the stiff to very stiff subsoil may be designed using an
allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 p s f Settlements on the order of less than
one half inch 4 are anticipated for these structures
If it is necessary to place the manhole structures on the soft soil deposits an 18
inch layer of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 wrapped in a filter fabric Mirafi
140N or equivalent should be provided below the structure to provide more uniform
gravel layer The increase in load on the subsoils due to the manhole structure should
be small Therefore significant settlements are not anticipated if proper shoring
backfilling and dewatering techniques are utilized
The lateral earth pressure acting on the proposed underground manhole structures
will depend on the type of backfill used the extent of backfill and the compactive effort
on the backfill material around the structures Therefore granular material with no more
than 10 percent passing the No 200 sieve is recommended for use as backfill around
the structures to reduce the compactive effort required and thus the build up of additional
lateral earth pressures We recommend that the new manholes be designed to resist the
following lateral earth pressures provided that granular backfill materials are used
1 Above water level 50 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
2 Below water level 80 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
The above values assume that the backfill around the manhole structures is
properly placed and compacted Surcharge loading due to traffic and other applicable
sources should be added to the above values for design

Dewatering
Dewatering of trench excavations for water main may be necessary since the
groundwater was encountered in Boring No 4 at about 7 feet below the ground surface
at the time of our exploration Dewatering for construction is the sole responsibility of the
contractor The dewatering operations should be coordinated with the trench shoring
support so that the stability of the excavations is not jeopardized The operations should
be carried out without softening the bottom of the excavations
It is Our opinion that the definition of Dewatering in the contract documents should
be written to include all works or systems required to lower the natural groundwater table
and to exclude the water from the excavations to permit construction of underground
utilities under safe and dry conditions Therefore these works or systems should include
but not be limited to pumping
Dewatering Method
The method and equipment to be used for the excavation dewatering should be
determined by the contractor subject to practical limits and safety considerations
We suggest that the following three basic criteria be considered in selecting a
suitable method of dewatering
1 The method should result in the least disturbance or damage to the
adjacent existing structures
2 The method should maintain stability of and also provide safe and
dry working conditions in the excavations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3090 00 Page 17
3 The method should be sufficiently flexible to allow modifications to
accommodate various ground conditions
Dewatering by means of a well point system along the outside of the excavations
is generally not recommended The resultant areal depression of the natural
groundwater table could induce consolidation of subsoils resulting in potential
ground settlements
Construction Precautions
The potential impact of the dewatering system selected on depressing the natural
groundwater table must be carefully evaluated by the contractor prior to
dewatering It is also recommended that a qualified geotechnical engineer be
retained by the contractor to design and evaluate the dewatering system used
The impact and safety of the dewatering operations should be made the sole
responsibility of the contractor His qualified representative who should be
required to be continuously present at the site will have the best opportunity to
promptly observe the effects of dewatering during construction and to implement
as soon as possible necessary precautionary or remedial measures including but
not limited to stopping or slowing down the dewatering operations
Where encountered at the excavation bottoms permeable granular subsoils may
be susceptible to piping and quick conditions Therefore the project dewatering
operations should be done without pumping out soil fines bumping clear water
only and should be coordinated with shoring installation so that the excavation
stability is not jeopardized The dewatering operations should be carried out
without creating a quick condition or softening at the excavation bottoms

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling 10 borings to depths
of 10 to 51 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown
on the Site Plans Plates 2 3 and 4 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the
borings
The materials encountered in the borings were examined and classified in general
accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84 Description and Identification of
Soils Visual Manual Procedure by an engineer from our firm The logs of the borings
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in accordance with ASTM
Standard Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to advance the
sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at the
approximate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to schedule appropriate tests
to aid in evaluating pertinent engineering properties of the soil materials sampled
Moisture content unit weight and Atterberg Limits tests were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The
at the appropriate sample depths
Three one inch ring swell tests were performed on selected natural and remolded
samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils The test results are
presented on Plate B 1
One sieve analysis test was performed on a selected sample to evaluate the grain
size distribution of the soil encountered The results of test are summarized on Plate B 2
Two Atterberg Limits tests were performed on selected samples of the soils to
evaluate their liquid and plastic limits The test results are summarized on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths Graphic presentation of the test results is
shown on Plate B 3
Two unconfined compression tests were performed on a selected sample to
evaluate its strength characteristics Results of the unconfined compression tests are
summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate samples depths and on Plate B 2
