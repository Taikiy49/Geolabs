PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site involves site grading and roadway construction for the Hawaii Prince
Golf Course Access Road Realignment project The site is east of Fort Weaver Road south
of Area 39 and north of the Hawaii Price Golf Course in the District of Ewa on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii The roadway realignment covers a distance of about 493 linear feet A
Project Location Map and a Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2 respectively
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for the
site grading operations and infrastructure installation for the project site
SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
We observed the site grading and roadway work from to
The site grading work consisted of clearing and grubbing and
placement and compaction of fills on the order of up to about 2 feet thick to achieve the
design finished grades Albert C Kobayashi Inc performed the site grading and roadway
construction work During the site grading operations we obtained samples of the on site and
imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials and transported them to our laboratory
for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in

The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations we performed periodic field
density tests in general accordance with ASTM D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of
the compaction tests performed are summarized on the attached Plates 4 1 through 4 3
These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating
from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field
representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill placement
subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil
conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading and utility installation work for the
above referenced project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this

Geolabs assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or finished
grades on the project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
knowledge and written acknowledgment

observation However we believe that the level of earthwork observation and testing
