As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil
conditions below the above referenced lot The purpose of the
study was to evaluate the suitability of the near surface soils
as foundation support for the proposed house The study
consisted of three 3 borings drilled within the proposed
building site laboratory soil tests and a review of the
grading and compaction test record on the building site This
report summarizes our observations and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed single family residence consists of a one story
wood structure approximately 46 by 40 feet in plan dimension
Based on the preliminary plans provided to us the garage will
be set back about 19 feet from Kahena Street

Lot 25 is located on Kahena Street in the eastern portion of
the subdivision development about 1600 feet north of Ainapo

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by
grayish brown clay and gravel fill The laboratory test
results on this material generally indicated that they have
moderate to high expansion potential Therefore for Lot 25
the slab on grade and foundation construction would require
special design procedures in order to reduce the shrink swell
effect of the subsoils
House Foundation
For this lot post and beam construction is highly desirable
and recommended due to the sloping terrain and expansive soil
conditions The post and beam construction generally results
in lower foundation costs and the individual footings can be
readily adjusted should the need occur Where utilized
concrete slab on grade should also be properly designed for
expansive soil conditions
For post and beam construction we recommend that the expansive
clays be overexcavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the
bottom of the individual post footings and then replaced with
non expansive borrow soils such as crusher waste select
borrow or coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The spread footings can then
be placed on the compacted non expansive soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
Alternatively the individual post footing can be extended a
minimum depth of 36 inches below the finished grade and
backfilled with on site soil compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density The footings should be poured
in place and the excavation backfilled and compacted to
provide an impervious layer over the footing
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for
footings bearing on the stiff natural ground or compacted fill
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should
extend to the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing
settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations
Retaining Wall Foundation
Retaining walls up to 4 feet high are planned in the rear
portion of the garage
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the existing clay
soils on the wall we recommend that the expansive clays be
overexcavated down for a depth of 24 inches below the bottom of
the footing and then replaced with non expansive borrow soils
such as crusher waste or coral compacted to a minimum of 90
of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage It appears
that the garage area would require fill up to two feet in order
to obtain sufficient level area for the garage Select
non expansive borrow soils should be used for fills The
on site clay soils are expansive and are unsuitable as slab
subgrade soils
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the clay the
expansive clays should be overexcavated down for a depth of 3
feet below the slab subgrade and then replaced with
non expansive borrow soils such as select borrow crusher
waste or coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 This overexcavation and
replacement should be extended for a distance of 3 feet beyond
the perimeter of the slab
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab
and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to
protect against moisture infiltration a polyethylene moisture
barrier may be incorporated below the slab The membrane
should be protected by a thin layer of sand to aid in the
concrete curing

Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
Expansive clay soils were encountered along the proposed
driveway Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of
heaving the clay overexcavation and replacement can be reduced
to 2 feet below the driveway subgrade level Where 2 or more
feet of fill is planned select non expansive borrow material
should be used to cap over the expansive clay Where less than
2 feet of fill is planned overexcavation and replacement of
the expansive clay with select non expansive material is
required The above requirements should be followed for a
minimum distance of 3 feet beyond the driveway
Since a relatively long driveway is planned joints are
recommended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals in order to allow
for some relative movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or overexcavation and
replacement similar to the driveway are recommended to reduce
heaving caused by the expansive clay soil

The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down at about
8 5 gradient
Minor grading is anticipated for most of the area below the
house

The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and

Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and

The finished grade outside the slabs pavements and

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during

cuts and to monitor the contractor s grading operations
