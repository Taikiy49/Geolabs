Based on our records it appears that a two foot coral capping layer was provided
under the residential structures at the Arbors Therefore we recommend that the
top of the new buttress fill be placed at a maximum depth of two feet 2 below
the finish grade for the Arbor structures See Plate 1 O for details
Test trenches were performed along the slopes of the relatively flat area that
extends approximately 150 feet beyond the Arbors property line between Gridline
A and B Loose fill consisting of reddish brown clayey silt was encountered in
the test trenches and was underlain by a coral formation
During the re construction of the slope in this area loose overcast fill materials
encountered on the slope surface should be removed down to stiff natural ground
prior to the construction of the buttress fill For detailed of buttress fill refer to
sketch on Plate 10
Slope Along Arbors Between Gridline A and Fort Weaver Road
Three 3 trench lines were performed in this area
For Trench Line Nos 13 and 14 stiff reddish brown clayey silt underlain by a coral
formation was encountered A minimum slope ratio of two horizontal to one
vertical 2H 1 V is recommended for cut slope in the stiff clayey silt soils
In Trench Line No 12 loose coralline sand and gravel about eight feet 8 in
thickness underlain by adobe clay soils was encountered The adobe clay soils
encountered was relatively deep with a slope ratio of about five horizontal to one
vertical 5H 1V After the removal of the loose slope material it appears that the
remaining slope consisting of dense coralline sand and gravel will be about one
horizontal to one vertical 1 H 1V
Because of the potential for soil movements and erosion of the loose material from
surface runoff it is recommended that the loose slope material be removed down
to stiff natural ground and the new slope be reconstructed by keying and benching
with a minimum finish slope ratio of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V
