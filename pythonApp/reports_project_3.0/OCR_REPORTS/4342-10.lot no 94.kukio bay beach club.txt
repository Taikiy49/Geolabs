Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii is the largest in the Hawaiian Archipelago and covers an area
of approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of the
following five shield volcanoes Kohala long extinct Mauna Kea activity during recent
geologic time Hualalai last erupted in 1801 1803 and Mauna Loa and Kilauea both still
active
The project site is situated on the northwestern flank of Hualalai Mountain which
makes up the west central portion of the island The lava formation observed at the
project site prior to site grading activities appears to consist of both a a and pahoehoe
type lava flows which spread and ponded as they approached the ocean A a lava
typically is characterized by a porous rough and irregular flow surface resembling a
jagged accumulation of rock fragments including cobbles and boulders Pahoehoe lava is
characterized by a smooth rope like or billowy surface and an internal structure of
vesicular porous rock
Cavities are commonly encountered in basaltic lavas especially the pahoehoe type
lava Cavities form when the lavas are still in a molten state and they represent both lava
tubes intra flow cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow cavities Lava tubes form
when molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like structure which
may extend for a large longitudinal distance along the flow Blisters and pockets are
generally smaller in horizontal extent
Basalt is considered to be a relatively permeable rock and can transmit water quite
readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions In general water is transmitted
through the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities and
along clinker layers Clinkers and cavities serve as primary water transmission features
because of their high permeability
Subsurface Conditions
The Kukio Bay Beach Club project site is in the Kaupulehu area in the District of
North Kona on the Island of Hawaii In general the original topography of the site prior
to site grading activities consisted of irregular or hummocky lava flow terrain with
frequent topographic depressions separated by adjacent higher relief mounds and low
ridge features
Based on observations during the mass grading operations the project site is
generally underlain by medium hard to hard basalt formation with interbedded layers of
clinker materials It should be noted that lava tubes cavities and blisters are commonly
encountered in basaltic lava flows in the project vicinity In addition numerous lava tube
features were observed at the project site during the mass grading operations It should
be noted that near surface lava tubes detected within the limits of grading for the mass
grading operations were generally collapsed and the loose materials from within the

Discussion and Recommendations
Based on the grading plan for the project the original ground surface elevations at
the building pad areas of Lot No 94 ranged from about 12 to 24 feet Mean Sea Level
MSL Lot No 94 was generally mass graded to a finished pad elevation of 22 feet
MSL Based on a pad elevation of 22 feet MSL mass grading at Lot No 94 generally
consisted of placing up to about 10 feet of new fills and excavating up to about 2 feet to
achieve the design grades
During the mass grading operations extensive subgrade preparation procedures
were employed within the limits of grading to reduce the potential for collapse of
near surface cavities and or voids within the lots The intent of the subgrade preparation
procedures is to assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or voids
contained in the porous near surface materials It should be noted that the subgrade
preparation procedures employed for this project are consistent with the standard of
practice for similar type projects and similar subsurface conditions along the Kohala Coast
on the Island of Hawaii
In general the subgrade preparation in fill areas consisted of ripping the original
ground to a depth of about 3 feet or until refusal using a ripper tooth of a large bulldozer to
assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or voids contained in the
porous near surface materials After the fill surfaces have been ripped the ground
generally was leveled with the use of a Komatsu 375 bulldozer working the surface for
about four passes to provide a relatively level surface After leveling of the rugged
surfaces with the bulldozer the fill subgrades were generally proof rolled with a
Caterpillar 825 sheepsfoot roller compactor minimum 20 tons for about eight passes to
help detect and collapse near surface cavities and or voids
Fill materials consisting of some cobbles and structural fills were placed in the fill
areas of the project site Structural fills consisted of well graded granular materials
generally smaller than 6 inches in dimension In general the fill materials within the top
2 feet of the lots within the pad areas consisted of structural fill materials It should be
noted that general fills consisting of cobbles and boulders of up to about 2 feet in
dimension were generally placed at least about 2 feet below the finished grades within the
pad areas in fill areas and choked off with finer sized materials In addition boulders of
up to about 3 to 4 feet in dimension were generally placed at least about 4 feet below the
finished grades within the pad areas and choked off with finer sized materials Boulders
larger than 18 inches in dimension were generally placed about 6 feet or more
horizontally from the face of the fill slopes
