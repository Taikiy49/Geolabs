It is proposed to construct three 3 pre engineered metal framed
buildings Two of the buildings will be approximately 50 by 160 feet in
plan dimension The third building will include a maintenance bay and
office storage areas which will increase the building size to 50 by 111

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated approximately 800 feet east of the
intersection of Harris Avenue and Seldon Street in the Kaneohe Marine
Corps Air Station on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The facility will cover an area of approximately 400 by 740 feet with
access from Mokapu Road
The topography is generally level with elevations ranging from 100
to lll feet based on U S Navy Datum MLIW 100 feet The area is
presently covered with a thick growth of haole koa and grass Water was
noted at the northwestern boundary of the site which borders a lowlying
drainageway
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the Mokapu Peninsula of the island of
Oahu The foundation of this peninsula was deposited during the Kahipa
low stand of the sea 300 feet by several vents of the Honolulu
Volcanic Series These vents laid down extensive deposits of pyroclastic
materials in the form of ash and cinder with some associated lava flows
Since the deposition of these materials during Kahipa time the sea
level has risen and fallen several times as a result of glacio eustatic
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 5
fluctuations These changes in sea level have caused the deposition of
coral algal reef materials and littoral shoreline deposits over
portions of the peninsula
During the past century the efforts of man have shaped the land to
its present form by placement of fill in low areas
Soil Conditions
The soil conditions were explored by drilling twelve 12 borings at
the heavy gun shop site and two 2 borings along the sewer line
alignment to depths of 11 5 to 31 3 feet below the existing ground
surface
The soil conditions encountered were relatively uniform and consisted
of a thin surface layer of stiff brown silt 0 to 3 feet thick over
dense silty sand and gravel weathered coral formation to the full depth
of the borings
Hand probing in the lowlying area along the northwestern boundary
indicated that the water may be about 1 to 2 feet deep and underlain by
coral formation
Groundwater was encountered at depths of 7 to 9 feet in the borings
at the time of our field exploration

Engineered Fill
The term engineered fill used in this text covers any fill of
acceptable quality which is placed and compacted in accordance with
our recommendations for general filling Special materials such as
base course subbase cushion and pipe bedding are specified
elsewhere and not included as engineered fill consideration
Engineered fill material should be non expansive soil free of
organic matter and form a dense stable soil mass when compacted to
the required density Generally the particle size should be less
than 6 inches in size The pavement designs presented in this report
were based on a subgrade CBR value of 12 and therefore the material
placed within the top 3 feet of the fill under the pavements should
have a soaked CBR of at least 12 per ASTM D 1883 73 when compacted
to 95 of the maximum densities determined according to ASTM
D 1557 78 The total expansion of sample during the soaking for CBR
test should be less than 1

Building Foundation
Spread or continuous wall footings bearing on the compacted fill is
recommended for the structure An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f
may be used for footings bearing on the above material A one third
increase of the above bearing value may be used for temporary wind and
seismic loads
The footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
adjacent finished grade
The bottom of the footing excavation should be compacted to 95 of
its maximum dry density prior to concrete placement
Footings located next to utility trenches should extend down to the
bottom of the utility trench or the trench backfill should be well
compacted to minimize building settlement due to settlement of the trench
backfill
Site drainage should be designed to divert surface water away from
the building foundation

Bearing Capacity and Settlement
The dense sandy soils which were encountered along the alignment have
good bearing capacity
The new sewer line construction in general will not materially
increase the vertical loading upon these underlying soils if no
additional fills are placed upon the existing ground surface The
resulting net loading between the weight of the excavated material
and the backfill will be essentially small and therefore
significant settlements are not anticipated if proper construction
techniques are utilized

Pipe Bedding
The stress distribution against the bottom of a pipe has a great
effect upon the load supporting capacity of the pipe and therefore
the pipe bedding is an important design consideration
A minimum of six inches of compacted granular bedding material should
be placed below the pipe The granular bedding should extend beyond
the pipe sides a minimum width of one fourth the outside pipe
diameter and extend halfway up the pipe sides The bedding material
should consist of clean granular material such as 3B Fine gravel

Backfill
Granular material such as sand should be used to backfill the
utility trench up to about one foot above the pipe to reduce the
compactive effort required and possible damage to the pipe
The excavated on site soil can be reused in the upper portion of the
trench backfill from above one foot over the pipe to finished grade
Backfill should be mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Boring
Logs at the appropriate sample depths
To further identify soil characteristics 3 sieve analysis tests
were performed The test results are presented on Plate B l
Five laboratory CBR tests were performed on the near surface
soils to evaluate their suitability as pavement subgrade support The
test results are summarized on Plates B l and B 2
Three direct shear tests were performed on undisturbed samples
to evaluate the strength characteristics of the subsurface granular
materials The test results are presented on Plates B 3 through B 5
Two unconsolidated undrained UU triaxial compression tests
were performed The results of the triaxial compression tests are
summarized on Plates B 6 and B 7
