Geolabs, Inc. is pleased to submit the results of our percolation testing performed 
at the proposed project. This letter report summarizes our findings and geotechnical 
recommendations derived from our field percolation testing only. These geotechnical 
recommendations are intended to assist the client in the design of individual wastewater 
systems (IWS) at the project site. The findings and recommendations presented herein 
are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report. 
Project Considerations 
The project site is at the Enterprise Rent-A-Car facility (TMK:  2-1-12: 096 and 098) 
near Hilo International Airport on the Island of Hawaii. The project location and general 
vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
The existing cesspools will be converted to new individual wastewater systems 
(IWS) with a septic tank and leach field. To aid in the design of the IWS, percolation tests 
were performed at the proposed leach field locations in accordance with the State of 
Hawaii, Department of Health's Administrative Rules, Chapter 11-62-31.2, "Wastewater 
Systems" and Chapter 10 of the Ten State’s Standards to evaluate the infiltration 
characteristics of the subsurface soils for disposal of wastewater effluent.  
Percolation Testing 
Two percolation tests, designated as P-1 and P-2, were performed near the 
proposed leach field locations in general accordance with the procedures outlined by the 
Department of Health for the design of IWS. The locations of the percolation tests are 
shown on the Site Plans, Plates 2.1 and 2.2.

The percolation tests were performed in a boring drilled down to about 5 feet below 
the existing ground surface. Approximately 1 inch of gravel was placed at the bottom of 
the test pit to protect the bottom from scouring and sediments. 
During testing, the test holes were filled with water to a depth of about 6 inches 
above the gravel layer and refilled as necessary. The time intervals and water drops were 
then recorded to provide data upon which the calculation of the percolation rates were 
based. 
Based on the percolation tests conducted, the percolation rates are about 1.7 and 
10.7 minutes per inch at the test elevation for P-1 and P-2, respectively. In accordance 
with the "Manual of Septic-Tank Practice" prepared by the U.S. Department of Health, 
Education, and Welfare, the leach field location with the percolation rate obtained at the 
site would be suitable for siting of the absorption system at these locations. The 
percolation test results are presented on Plates 3.1 and 3.2. 
Limitations 
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based, in part, upon 
information obtained from our in-situ testing. Variations of subsoil conditions beyond the 
in-situ testing locations may occur, and the nature and extent of these variations may not 
become evident until construction is underway. If variations then appear evident, it will be 
necessary to re-evaluate the recommendations provided herein. 
The percolation test locations indicated herein are approximate, having been 
estimated by taping from visible features shown on the topographic survey map transmitted 
by Okahara & Associates, Inc. on August 22, 2022. The physical locations of the percolation 
test locations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods 
used.

Inc. for specific application for the design of new IWS at the Enterprise Rent-A-Car Hilo

The owner/client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are commonly 
encountered. Unforeseen soil conditions, such as perched groundwater, soft deposits, 
hard layers or cavities, may occur in localized areas and may require additional probing 
or corrections in the field (which may result in construction delays) to attain a properly 
constructed project. Therefore, a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to 
accommodate these possible extra costs.
