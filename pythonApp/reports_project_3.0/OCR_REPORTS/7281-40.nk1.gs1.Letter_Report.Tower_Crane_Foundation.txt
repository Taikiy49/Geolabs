Geolabs, Inc. is pleased to submit our letter report entitled "Tower Crane 
Foundation, Moanalua High School Performing Arts Center Phase 2B, Honolulu, Oahu,

This letter report summarizes our findings and geotechnical recommendations

observations only. The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to 
the limitations noted at the end of this letter report. 
Project Considerations 
Moanalua High School is located in the Salt Lake area of Honolulu on the Island of 
Oahu, Hawaii. The high school is situated west of Puuloa Road, north of Salt Lake 
Boulevard, east of Ala Napunani Street, and south of a residential development. The 
Moanalua High School Performing Arts Center Phase 2B project will be located in the 
southern portion of the campus. The new auditorium and back of house portion will be 
constructed adjacent to the west side of the existing rehearsal building. The project will 
also encroach into the north side of the existing parking area. 
Based on the information provided, the tower crane will be situated in the existing 
parking lot on the southeast side of the new auditorium and will be supported on 
four crane pads. We understand that the structural engineer for the crane foundation is 
using the foundation parameters for the building foundations in our February 12, 2018 
report.  Based on the drawings provided, the closest crane foundation will be set back 
behind a 45 degree imaginary line from the bottom edge of the new building foundation.

Anticipated Subsurface Conditions 
Boring information from our report entitled “Geotechnical Engineering Exploration, 
Moanalua High School, Performing Arts Center Phase 2B, Honolulu, Oahu, Hawaii” dated 
February 12, 2018 was used to determine the anticipated subsurface conditions at the 
tower crane site. 
The closest boring (Boring No. 4) near the proposed crane pad site was used to 
determine the anticipated subsurface conditions. The approximate boring locations from 
our previous field exploration program are shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
In general, the anticipated subsurface conditions at the crane pad site consists of 
a thin layer of surface fill about 1 foot thick underlain by weathered volcanic tuff and 
volcanic tuff extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately 14 feet below 
the existing ground surface. The surface fill material generally consisted of 2 inches of 
asphaltic concrete and dense sandy gravel. The weathered volcanic tuff consisted of 
dense silty sand.  The volcanic tuff was hard to very hard.  
Groundwater was not encountered in the boring at the time of our field exploration.

Site Reconnaissance 
Site reconnaissance of the project site was conducted by our engineer on 
April 26, 2019 to evaluate the existing conditions of the four (4) tower crane footing 
excavations. Based on our field observations, the tower crane foundations are located 
southeast of the proposed Performing Arts Center Phase 2B structure in the existing 
parking lot. The approximate location of the tower crane foundation excavations is shown 
on the Site Plan, Plate 2.  
The presence of competent volcanic tuff formation was observed at the bottom of 
the crane pad footings. Where volcanic tuff formation was not encountered at the bottom 
of footing excavation, the excavation was extended deeper to competent volcanic tuff 
formation. Photographs depicting the final conditions of the four tower crane footing 
excavations are presented on Plates 3.1 and 3.2. The as-built measurements of each of 
the tower crane footing excavations are provided in the table below.

Recommendations 
Based on our field observations and the information provided, we believe that our 
recommendations for the building foundations in our February 12, 2018 report may be 
used for the tower crane foundation.  We understand that the tower crane foundations 
are not located near existing underground utility lines.  In addition, based on the drawings 
provided, the crane foundations appear to be adequately set-back from the building 
foundations.   
Limitations 
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based, in part, upon 
information obtained from our previous field borings and visual field reconnaissance 
observations. Variations of subsoil conditions between and beyond the field borings may 
occur, and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until 
construction is underway. If variations then appear evident, it will be necessary to  
re-evaluate the recommendations provided herein.
