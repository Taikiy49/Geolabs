PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is at the southern end of Pakeke Street in the Maili area on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is along the northern pediment of the ridge
crested by Puu o Hulu Kai and Puu o Hulu Uka and is bordered by existing residential
developments as shown on the Site Plans Plates 1 1 and 1 2
The project site includes a total of 127 residential lots and three roadways The site
mass grading was constructed previously and observed tested by others Generally the
site mass grading consisted of cuts and fills and capping the expansive clay soils with a
minimum of 3 feet of coral fill The thickness of the coral capping was found to be
inconsistent
The current grading consisted of minor fine grading at the lots and roads and
installation of a minimum of 5 feet of coral fill capping within the house and patio footprint
The grading plan for the site is shown on the Site Plans Plates 1 1 and 1 2
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the site grading operations and infrastructure installation for the project site
SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
Only minor fine grading was done for the site The majority of the grading consisted
of over excavating the materials within 1 foot around the house and patio footprints to a
depth of about 5 feet below the slab slubgrade level and replacing with structural fill
primarily coral Prior to backfilling the underlying clay soils were moistened to above the

HOUSE CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
The finished grades adjacent to the house structures should be sloped to shed to
install water away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also
advised to install gutter systems around the house structures and to divert discharge away

from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering within 3 feet of
the foundations and slabs should be avoided Gravel strips adjacent to slabs and
foundations should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the house
foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water
infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain surface run off away from foundations Landscaping of the house lots
by the homeowners is recommended to reduce the potential for erosion and for altering
the drainage patterns
Consideration should be given to constructing a 3 foot wide sidewalk adjacent to
and surrounding the entire house structure A sidewalk with an 8 inch deep
thickened edge should help stabilize the subgrade soil moisture below the house
structure and therefore reduce the potential for swelling shrinking and softening of the
subgrade soils
