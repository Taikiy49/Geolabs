Dear Mr. Fujiwara: 
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical observation services performed 
during the installation and testing of the production soil nails for the Leeward YMCA 
Rock Wall project located in the Waipahu area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The 
project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
This letter report presents our field observations and the results of the installation 
and testing performed on the soil nails from July 11, 2019 through July 22, 2019 for the 
project. Ron’s Construction was the general contractor and Prometheus Construction 
was the specialty subcontractor responsible for installation and testing of the soil nails 
for the project. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is at the Leeward YMCA in the Waipahu area of the Island of 
Oahu, Hawaii. The project site is located north of Waipahu Street. The new wall system 
consisted of a soil-nailed wall and a conventional retaining wall at the site. The new wall 
will be set further away from Waipahu Street than the existing wall location. 
A total of 25 soil nails were specified for the new soil-nailed wall.  The specified 
5.5-inch diameter soil nail length was 20 feet with an inclination of 15 degrees from the 
horizontal.  The soil nail was composed of No. 10, Grade 60 epoxy-coated threaded 
steel bar and 4,000 psi cement grout.  Proof testing was specified for selected soil nails.

SOIL NAIL INSTALLATION AND TESTING 
Soil Nail Installation 
The soil nail installation was performed by Prometheus Construction. A total of 
25 production soil nails were installed for the project. The approximate locations and 
identification of the soil nails are shown on the Soil Nail Identification Plan, Plate 2. The 
25 production soil nails (1-1 through 1-13 and 2-1 through 2-12) were installed between 
July 11 and 19, 2019. 
The production soil nails were drilled using a TEI drill rig mounted on a CAT 315C 
excavator. The TEI drill rig was equipped with a 6-inch down-the-hole hammer and a 
6-inch drag bit. The production soil nails were drilled to a minimum length of 20 feet at an 
inclination about 15 degrees from the horizontal.  After the hole was drilled to the specified 
length, the hole was cleaned by blowing out the cuttings and loose material in the drill 
hole. A No. 10, Grade 60 epoxy-coated threaded bar with PVC sheathing was inserted into 
the drilled hole. Centralizers were placed along the bar at 10-foot intervals with a minimum 
of 2 feet from each end of the bar.  
A ½-inch diameter high-density plastic grouting tube was inserted into the drilled 
hole along the reinforcing bar for grouting purposes. Grouting began progressively from 
the bottom to top through the grouting tube until fresh grout appeared at the top of the 
hole. The grouting tube was removed after completion of the grouting operation. The grout 
was designed with a minimum 28-day compressive strength of 4,000 psi. A summary of 
the soil nail installation is provided in the Summary of Soil Nail Installation and Testing, 
Plate 3. 
Soil Nail Testing 
In accordance with the project specifications, 10 percent of the production nails 
(three nails) were selected for proof testing. Production soil nails (1-4, 2-8 and 2-12) were 
proof tested. Proof test nails were load tested up to 150 percent of the design load 
(30 kips). At this point, the test load was maintained for 10 minutes to measure the creep 
movements. The criteria for acceptance of the soil nails consist of the following: 
A total creep movement of less than 0.04 inches is measured between 
1 and 10-minute readings or a total creep movement of less than 
0.08 inches is measured between 6 and 60-minute readings and the creep 
rate is linear or decreasing throughout the creep test load hold period. 
Total measured movement at the maximum test load exceeds 80 percent 
of the theoretical elastic elongation of the test nail unbonded length. 
Pullout failure does not occur at the maximum test load. 
Maximum total head movement was about 0.135 inches for Soil Nail 2-12. Results 
of the soil nail testing are provided on Plates 4.1.1 through 4.3.3.
