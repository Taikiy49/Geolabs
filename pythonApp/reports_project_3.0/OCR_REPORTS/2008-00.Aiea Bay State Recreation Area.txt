INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration for the proposed improvement to Aiea Bay State Recreation
Area in Aiea on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of
the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate
1
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed improvement includes the construction of the
following structures in the existing Aiea Bay State Recreation Area
1 An approximately 700 foot long access driveway partially
retained by a short wall along its northern boundary near
McGrew Loop
2 An approximately 8500 square foot parking area
3 An approximately 560 square foot comfort station
Based on the available preliminary plans we anticipate that site
grading will involve minimal cuts and fills
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our evaluation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide pertinent soil data for the pavement
retaining wall and comfort station foundation design
Our scope of work included drilling and sampling five 5 borings
to depths ranging from about 11 to 26 feet below the existing ground
surface The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests
which were utilized in the engineering analyses to formulate our
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2008 00 Page 3
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is situated between Aiea Bay and Kamehameha
Highway in the Aiea Bay State Recreation Area McCrew Loop is to the
west of the site while the mouth of Aiea Stream forms the eastern
boundary of the project site
Currently the site is a gently sloping grassy picnic area
surrounded by heavy brush
An approximately 80 foot wide gully extends adjacent and parallel
to the north side of the site It is our understanding that there are
several existing major utility lines routed along the center of the
gully and that an existing major sewer line 36 inch diameter
traverses across and alongside portions of the project site
Ground elevations at the project site vary from approximately 36
feet above Mean Sea Level MSI in the higher western area near McGrew
Loop to approximately 4 to 8 feet MSL in the eastern lowland near the
bay
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling
five 5 borings to depths ranging from 11 to 26 feet below the
existing ground surface The locations of the borings are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
Three 3 of the borings were drilled at the western half of the
proposed access driveway along the alignment of the proposed retaining
wall The other two 2 borings were drilled in the vicinity of the
proposed parking area and comfort station in the lower area

Based on the borings the subsoil conditions within the project
site generally consist of stiff to hard alluvial clayey silt subsoils
mostly covered by thin surface fills It was noted that Borings 1
through 4 encountered a thin layer of soft surface fill materials
Groundwater was not encountered to the depths drilled in the
and 3 along the access driveway in the higher area In
the lower area groundwater was observed at depths from about 3 6 to
9 4 feet below the existing ground surface during our exploration
Due to the close proximity of the project site to Aiea Bay and
Stream it is anticipated that groundwater levels will fluctuate in
response to tidal influences and streamflows
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered generally competent subsurface
conditions at the project site However laboratory testing indicates
that some of the subgrade material is moderately expansive Therefore
a minimum 6 inch thick select borrow subbase is recommended below the
flexible pavement section for the proposed access driveway and parking
area
We believe that shallow footing foundations may be used for the
proposed light comfort station structure
Site Grading
Minimal site grading is anticipated
At the onset of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable
materials such as debris rubbish vegetation and other deleterious
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2008 00 Page 5
The ground surface below the pavements or footings should be
scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches below subgrade level moisture
conditioned to near the optimum moisture content and recompacted to a
minimum of 95 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
Any loose or soft areas spots encountered at the pavement or
footing subgrade level should be over excavated down to in situ firm
ground and replaced with imported backfill compacted in 8 inch loose
lifts to a minimum of 90 of its ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density
The in situ alluvial subsoils are moderately expansive and should
not be used as a source of backfill material directly below
structures New imported backfill materials should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum
size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
Site grading should provide drainage of surface run off away from
all pavements and foundations
Access Driveway and Parking Area
Pavement Section
Based on the anticipated low to moderately expansive subgrade soil
conditions a 12 inch select borrow subbase would be required for
the pavement section in accordance with City County of Honolulu
standards for pavement design in residential subdivisions
However in light of the anticipated light traffic conditions for
the recreation area we believe that the following thinner
flexible pavement section may be used as a means of cost savings

Retaining Wall
For the low retaining wall planned along the portion of the new
access driveway parallel to the gully the guidelines for design
are as follows
1 Retaining wall backfill should consist of granular materials
4
such as select borrow or crusher waste This type of backfill
would improve drainage reduce the compactive effort required
for the wall backfill and reduce lateral pressure on the
wall Impervious non expansive soil should be used for the
top 6 inches of backfill to reduce seepage of surface run off
behind the wall
A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should
be utilized behind the wall Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall
The following lateral earth pressure from the granular
backfill may be used for the wall design
Wall unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level backfill fluid pressure per
foot of depth
An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f may be used
for the design of wall foundation bearing on the in situ
alluvial soils

5 The retaining wall footing should be embedded a minimum depth
of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Where
the wall is situated close to the steep gully slope the
footing embedment should be increased to provide a minimum
6 foot horizontal set back distance between the outer edge of
footing to the slope face
6 A base friction factor of 0 4 may be utilized to determine the
sliding resistance of the wall on the recompacted alluvial
subgrade Due to the steeply sloping bank of the gully the
passive soil resistance should be ignored
Comfort Station
Foundation
Based on the anticipated soil conditions and light foundation
loads from the proposed structure we believe that shallow spread
footing foundations bearing on the in situ alluvial subsoils may
be used
An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f with a minimum
footing embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade is
recommended The bearing value is for total dead plus live load
only and may be increased by one third for short duration wind or
seismic loading
Estimated footing settlements under the design load are
anticipated to be on the order of less than one half V 2 of an
inch total and one fourth 1 4 of an inch differential
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend
to the bottom of the utility trench or the trench backfill must be
well compacted to reduce footing settlement due to settlement of
the trench backfill
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2008 00 Page 8
Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed building can be
obtained by passive soil pressure and frictional resistance A
frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used to compute the
frictional resistance between the bottoms of the footings and the
in situ soil In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300
p s f per foot of depth may be used against the buried vertical
faces of the footings to determine the passive soil pressure
Slabs On Grade
On grade concrete slabs are anticipated for the proposed comfort
station
The floor slabs should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of
gravel size material such as base course rock to provide a
uniform support A 6 inch select borrow subbase should also be
provided below the cushion layer to reduce the swelling potential
of the in situ alluvial subgrade materials A moisture barrier
membrane should be placed under the slabs to prevent future
moisture infiltration if potential damage to the floor coverings
is a consideration
The subgrade of the slab areas including the thickened edges
should be kept moist until the concrete is poured The backfill
against the concrete slabs should be adequately compacted and
graded to shed water away from the slab perimeters
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer can assume no
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations

Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction and
foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given
in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are
based in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings soil samples and or visual observation Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction
If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate
the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field data points were determined by taping
from structures indicated on the site plans undated provided by
Wilson Okamoto Associates Inc Elevations of the data points were
determined by interpolation from elevation points on the site plans
The physical location and elevation of the field data points should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2008 00 Page 10
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of the project
designers for specific application to the site grading pavement
retaining wall and foundation design of the project in accordance with
generally accepted geotechnical engineering principles and practices
No other warranty expressed or implied is made
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting
the architect engineer in the preliminary design evaluation of the
proposed project Therefore this report may not contain sufficient
data or the proper information for use in contract bid estimation A
contractor wishing to bid on this project is urged to retain a
competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation of this
report and or in the performance of additional site specific
exploration for bid estimating purposes
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil
conditions are commonly encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such
as perched groundwater soft deposits hard layers or cavities may
occur in localized areas and may require additional probing or
corrections in the field which may result in construction delays to
attain a properly constructed project Therefore sufficient
contingency fund is recommended to accommodate these possible extra
costs
