Dear Ms Coila
This letter serves as an update to the above referenced geotechnical engineering
report The report dated was prepared by Geolabs for the Kulana
Hale Il project It included detailed discussion and geotechnical engineering
recommendations based upon our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses The recommendations were intended for the design of the Kulana Hale II
foundations slabs on grade and site grading which are subject to certain conditions
and limitations noted within that report
The geotechnical engineering exploration for the project was directed toward
obtaining a broad overview of the surface and subsurface conditions at the building site
The subsurface information obtained was utilized to develop an idealized subsurface
data set for the design of foundations slabs on grade and site grading for the project
In general our field exploration encountered near surface stiff to very stiff sandy silts
and medium dense to dense silty sands These soil conditions are typical of the
regional geology of the site and should be representative of the normally dense
subsurface conditions in the immediate area At this time we do not believe that
additional borings would yield new information that would substantially alter our
recommendations for design of the project especially since various commercial and
office buildings presently occupy the site We do not believe that the subsurface soil
conditions have substantially changed since the time of our field exploration

Proposed Drilling Operations
Seven Eleven Gas Station
Kulana Hale Il Assisted Living Makai Tower
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with our site meeting held August 20th we are providing herewith
additional information pertaining to our proposed drilling operations within the premises of
the Seven Eleven gas station at the corner of Kalakaua Avenue and Young Street The
purpose of our drilling operations is to obtain the necessary subsoil information for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations in order to allow the project
structural engineer to proceed with their foundation design work for the above referenced
project
Our proposed drilling operations will consist of drilling one boring within the
triangular landscaped area between the sidewalk and the parking stalls as shown on the
attached Boring Location Map Plate 1 The proposed boring will consist of drilling an
8 inch diameter hole extending to a depth of approximately 60 to 75 feet below the existing
ground surface Our equipment used for the drilling operations will consist of a drill rig
about 8 feet wide and 26 feet long and a pick up truck The equipment will be parked
parallel to the existing wall and will probably occupy the first four parking stalls as shown on
the attached map Prior to our drilling operations the proposed boring location will be
toned by our engineer for clearance of underground utilities Upon completion of our
drilling operations the boring will be backfilled with the drilled cuttings and additional
imported material if necessary back to the existing ground surface The surface will be
leveled the area will be cleaned and restored to near its original condition
