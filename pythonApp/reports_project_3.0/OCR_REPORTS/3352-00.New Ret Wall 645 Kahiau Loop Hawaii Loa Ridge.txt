INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed new retaining wall located at the rear of the residence at
645 Kahiau Loop within the Hawaii Loa Ridge Subdivision in Honolulu District on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recom
mendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses The recommendations presented herein are intended for design input of the
proposed new retaining wall and may not be appropriate to form the basis for the
preparation of construction cost estimates nor for construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at 645 Kahiau Loop in the Hawaii Loa Ridge
Subdivision on the island of Oahu Hawaii Based on observation from our initial site
visit settlements on the order of 1 foot were observed around the rear portion of the
existing retaining wall below the pool deck In addition there was some distress noted
in the perimeter walls on the north and south sides of the lot Therefore there was
some concern regarding possible movements at the rear of the lot Based on
discussions with the structural engineer a new retaining wall is planned at the rear of
the lot to stabilize the existing retaining wall which will be left in place The existing
wall does not appear to be distressed The existing and new retaining walls are shown
approximately on the Site Plan Plate 2
Detailed plans for the new retaining wall were not available at the time this report
was prepared Based on information provided to us we anticipate the new retaining

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration indicated the site is underlain by stiff to very stiff silty clays
over basalt gravel cobbles boulders and basalt formation to the maximum depth drilled
of approximately 14 feet below the existing ground surface Based on the results of our
field exploration we believe that a shallow foundation system bearing on the stiff to
very stiff to clayey soils may be used to support the proposed new retaining wall
Settlements of the surface soils on the order of 1 foot were noted around the rear
of the existing retaining wall below the pool deck However significant distress of the
existing retaining wall was not noted during our site visit Based on the results of our
field exploration and engineering analyses we believe that settlements of surface soils
observed may be due to loosely placed soil near the ground surface Our borings in
the area did not encounter deep deposits of loosely placed material at the locations
drilled
For the proposed new retaining wall we recommend that the footings be
embedded at least 30 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade or the bottom level
of the existing retaining wall footings This requirement is necessary to avoid
surcharging the existing wall footings with additional structural loads and to reduce the
potential for foundation settlement

Wall Foundations
Based on our field exploration we believe that the proposed new retaining wall
may be supported on a shallow foundation system bearing on the stiff to very stiff
clayey soils and or the dense basalt gravel and fragments encountered in the borings
An allowable bearing pressure of 3 000 p s f may be used for design of footings
bearing on the stiff materials The bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may
be increased by one third when considering transient loads such as wind or seismic
forces
Because of the proximity of the existing wall to the new wall footings for the new
wail should be embedded to the same level or deeper than the footing for the existing
retaining wall As a minimum the new footings should be embedded at least 30 inches
below the lowest adjacent finish grade In addition due to the proximity of the new
retaining wall to the existing slope footings for the new retaining wall should be
embedded deep enough to maintain a horizontal setback distance of at least 6 feet
measured from the edge of the footing to the face of the slope
Footing excavations should not be allowed to dry before placing concrete If
shrinkage cracks appear in the footing excavations the soil should be thoroughly
moistened to close all cracks and recompacted prior to concrete placement
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the competency of the
bearing materials and the embedment depths If unsuitable materials are encountered
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3352 00 Page 8
in the footing excavations these materials should be over excavated to firm soils and
replaced with compacted engineered fill or lean concrete
Lateral loads acting on the retaining structure may be resisted by frictional
resistance between the base of the foundation and the bearing materials and by
passive earth pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded
portion of footings A coefficient of friction of 0 30 may be used between the base of
the footings and the supporting subgrade soil Resistance due to passive pressure may
be calculated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot
of depth p c f for level ground For sloping ground conditions the passive pressure
should be neglected These values assume that the concrete for footings is placed
directly against undisturbed soils Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive
resistance in the upper 12 inches below finish grade should be neglected
Lateral Earth Pressures
The proposed new retaining wall should be designed to resist the lateral earth
pressures due to adjacent soils and surcharge effects The recommended lateral earth
pressure for design of cantilever walls expressed in equivalent fluid pressure is 45
pounds per cubic foot p c f This assumes that on site or granular materials are used
for backfill behind the retaining walls This lateral earth pressure does not include
hydrostatic pressures that might be caused by groundwater trapped behind the walls
Retaining walls should be well drained to reduce the build up of hydrostatic pressures
A typical drainage system would consist of a 1 to 2 foot wide zone of permeable
material No 3B Fine gravel or ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation immediately adjacent
to the wall with a perforated pipe perforations down at the base of the wall discharging
toa discharge facility or weep holes

Surcharge stresses due to traffic areal surcharges line loads and point loads
within a horizontal distance equal to the height of the wall should be considered in the
design For uniform surcharge stresses imposed on the loaded side of the wall a
rectangular distribution with uniform pressure equal to 45 percent of the surcharge
pressure acting on the entire height of the wall which is free to deflect cantilever may
be used in the design
Earthwork
Grading plans were not available to us at the time this report was prepared
However we anticipate excavations and backfills of up to 4 feet relative to the existing
ground surface would be required for the proposed project construction Earthwork
operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii It is important
that a representative from our office observe whether any undesirable materials are
encountered during the excavation process and whether the exposed soil conditions
are similar to those encountered in our exploration
At the on set of earthwork the area within the project limits should be thoroughly
cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials
should be removed and disposed of off site In addition all the loose and soft materials
in front of the existing wall should be removed to expose firm materials Care should
be exercised not to remove materials below the bottom of the footing for the existing
retaining wall
After clearing and grubbing areas within the proposed new retaining wall should
be excavated to the finish subgrade The exposed subgrade should be compacted to
obtain a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction If soft or loose materials are
exposed at the finish subgrade the soft or loose materials should be over excavated
to expose firm material and the over excavation should be backfilled with engineered
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3352 00 Page 10
fill or lean concrete compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91
The on site clayey fill soils generated from the excavations may be reused as a
source of backfill material If needed imported fill and or backfill material should
consist of granular materials with less than 3 inches in maximum dimension The
material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 25 or more
and should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 In addition it should contain less than 15 percent
passing the No 200 sieve
In general imported fill material should be used at a level above the drainage
system of the new retaining wall Below the drainage system impervious soil such as
the on site clayey soil should be used This is necessary to reduce the potential of
water seepage into the footing foundations Imported fill material should be tested by
Geolabs Hawaii for conformance with our recommendations prior to delivery to the
project site for its intended use
All fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in
loose thickness moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Fill
placement should commence at the lowest point and continue upward in level
compacted lifts

Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to Geolabs Hawaii for review and written comments prior to advertisement for bids
This review is necessary to evaluate adherence of the plans and specifications to the
intent of the foundation and earthwork recommendations provided herein If this review
is not made Geolabs Hawaii cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the
recommendations presented
Construction Observation
It is recommended that Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during the project construction to observe the footing excavations
wall construction and backfill operation This is to observe compliance with the intent
of the design concepts specifications and or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated during
design The recommendations presented in this report are contingent upon the above
observations
If the actual soil conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made
Any imported material required should be non expansive and should be tested
and approved by our office prior to delivery to the project site
