PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is within the old Happy Valley town of Wailuku on the Island of
Maui Hawaii This second phase of the Market Street Improvements project involves the
installation of various utilities including drain sewer and water roadway rehabilitation new
sidewalks and concrete pavement crosswalks The earthwork consisted of cut and fills up
to 14 feet deep for 54 and 48 inch drain lines and minimal cuts and fills of 1 to 2 feet for
the roadway rehabilitation work Also some additional improvements were made to the
properties adjacent to the utility easements
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the site grading operations and infrastructure installation for the project site

SITE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
Geolabs performed observation and field density testing services during the site
grading and utility installation work from to The
earthwork commenced with the 54 48 and 24 inch drain lines which traversed up
Alahee Drive to Market Street and extended along the right side of the baseline for Market
Street from Kapoai Place to Mokuhau Road The depth of the HDPE drain lines varied
from 4 to 14 feet below the existing ground surface During the excavation many boulders
were encountered The boulders ranged in size from 12 inches to approximately
72 inches in diameter As the depth of the excavation increased the contractor began to
use trench shoring that was approximately 14 feet high by 8 feet wide interior width by
20 feet long to protect against sidewall cave in of the loose boulders as well as potential
undermining of the existing utility lines
Due to the abundance of boulders the contractor did not have sufficient quantity of
backfill material over the drain line Therefore the contractor mobilized a crusher to
produce crushed 3 inch minus fill material for use as Type B trench backfill The 3 inch
minus material was tested for gradation sand equivalent and Maximum Density and
Moisture Relations of Soils to comply with the project specifications It was determined that
the 3 inch minus material contained a quantity of gravel that is retained on the 4 inch
sieve exceeding 30 percent by weight This quantity is in excess of the AASHTO T180
requirements for an accurate determination of maximum density Therefore the material
was evaluated by the County of Maui Engineering Division and the project designers The
result was a conditional approval of the material for use as trench backfill Type B up to
the roadway subgrade elevation provided that Geolabs Inc provide full time backfill
observations inspections to verify that adequate compaction efforts are being performed
and in turn adequate compaction is achieved Therefore our services during the drainage
installations included the establishment of a compaction control chart for the equipment
that was used during the backfill operations This control chart was used to establish the
minimum number of passes necessary to achieve the required compacted density of the
3 inch minus material In addition hand probe observations were performed to support the
number of passes with the compaction equipment In some cases the material varied in
gradation Therefore it was recommended to perform additional passes with the
compacting equipment to pass the hand probe penetration observations
Prior to the start of the roadway rehabilitation work samples were collected to
determine the on site soil s characteristics at various locations during the utility installation
phase of work At Stations 19 50 and 21 05 highly saturated soils were encountered at
the subgrade elevation As requested by the County of Maui a California Bearing Ratio
test was performed on the sample from the subgrade soil at Station 21 05 The result was
1 7 percent which is very low and considered to be unsuitable for the current pavement
design As a result the subgrade investigation continued
Since Station 21 05 was not accessible during the investigation locations of similar
soil conditions were selected On October 28 and small test sections

were excavated at Stations 50 and 20 80 in the roadway to perform a trial
exercise to verify if the remedial plan to replace the unsuitable soils at the new subgrade
elevation would be successful The test sections were excavated to approximately 12 feet
wide by 20 feet long At Stations 19 35 and 19 50 the soil at the subgrade elevation
consisted of light gray clay material in a highly saturated condition Hand probe
observations yielded an average penetration depth of 15 inches Therefore the remedial
plan using geotextile fabric and 12 inches of untreated base course material was placed
and compacted in a single lift with a 10 ton vibratory drum roller During the compaction
efforts with the drum roller the untreated base began to show signs of soil pumping and
the density was showing a downward trend with each pass of the drum roller Therefore it
was recommended to remove the top 6 inches of base place a previously proposed
RX 7 triaxial geogrid in a tight wrinkle free mat replace the top 6 inches of base and
recompact with the drum roller The density tests showed an increasing trend with each
pass of the roller until 96 percent compaction was achieved Therefore the addition of the
geogrid material appeared to have assisted the remedial plan within these excessively soft
subgrade areas After reviewing the results with the County of Maui Engineering Division
it was decided to include the RX 7 triaxial geogrid material into the roadway remedial plan
for the excessively soft areas of the roadway
The majority of the roadway rehabilitation work was conducted during the Veterans
Holiday weekend with 16 hour operations from November 11 through 14 2010 The
remaining roadway tie in sections at the lao Bridge and Mokuhau intersections were
completed by The intent of the roadway replacement was to remove
the top 2 feet of asphalt and road base compact the soil at the new subgrade elevation of
minus 2 feet from the existing grade and place the new pavement section consisting
8 5 inches of Asphalt Treated Base and 3 inches of Mix V Hot Mix Asphalt material The
design life of the new pavement section is 30 years However should unstable saturated
soils be encountered at the new subgrade elevation then a remedial plan would be
implemented consisting of the over excavation of 12 inches of unstable soil placement of
a non woven geotextile fabric and 12 inches of untreated base course material In addition
the placement of the RX 7 Triaxial Geogrid material would be placed within the 12 inches
of untreated base to assist with stabilizing the excessively soft soil areas The compaction
requirement was to achieve 95 percent compacted density relative to the material s
maximum density as determined by AASHTO T 180 Maximum density and moisture
relations of soils However if subgrade failure such as soil pumping is observed prior to
achieving 95 percent compaction a variance was allowed to a minimum of 90 percent
compacted density
As the removal of the existing pavement material progressed the soil conditions at
the new subgrade elevations were observed to be highly saturated and hand probe
observations varied from 8 to 28 inches of penetration Therefore it was recommended to
follow the remedial plan and in the areas of penetration greater than 8 inches it was
recommended to include the triaxial geogrid to aid in achieving the required 95 percent
relative compacted density The RX 7 triaxial geogrid was placed at approximately

The site grading work consisted of the placement and compaction of fills from the
native ground to achieve the design finished grades Goodfellow Brothers Inc performed
the site grading and utility installation work During the site grading operations we
obtained samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials
and transported them to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
AASHTO T180 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to the optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with AASHTO T180 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method
Results of the compaction tests are summarized on the attached Plates 5 1 through 5 9
These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and
backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to
be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of on call field testing of the contractor s fill placement
subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil
conditions exposed during the above grading operations
