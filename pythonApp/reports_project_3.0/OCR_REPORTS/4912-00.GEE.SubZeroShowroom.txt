SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Sub Zero Showroom located at 11 Parcels 20 and 03 in the
Honolulu area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design of
building foundations slabs on grade site preparation and pavements only and may not
be appropriate to form the basis for construction cost estimates nor for construction
bidding purposes
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is located at 11 Parcels 20 and 03 in the Honolulu area
of Oahu Hawaii The site is along Piikoi Street between Alohi Way and King Street The
two lots are approximately rectangular in shape and have a combined plan area of
approximately 10 797 square feet
Based on the information provided it is planned to construct a two story building
for use as a showroom We understand that the lower level of the structure will cover
an area of approximately 3 372 square feet and the upper level of the structure will
cover an area of approximately 3 113 square feet Details for the structure were not
available during the preparation of this report
In addition we understand that it is planned to construct a parking area that will
consist of 15 parking stalls and 1 stall for loading We envision that the parking area will
consist of asphaltic concrete pavements Based on the existing grades we anticipate
that minimal site grading work will be required at the project site to achieve the design
finished grades

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which time erosion
incised deep valleys into the Koolau shield volcanic activity returned with a series of lava
flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These series are referred to as the
Honolulu Volcanic Series
The project site is located near the southern flank of Punchbowl Hill The
Punchbowl Hill Puowaina is a tuff cone near the center of Honolulu built against the end
of a spur of the Koolau Range The tuff consists of mostly brown palagonitized vitric ash
and lapili with scattered fragments of coral limestone and Koolau basalt
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was
available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the
sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such
that the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The process of erosion and deposition were affected by these glacio eustatic sea
level fluctuations When the sea level was low the erosional base level was
correspondingly lower and valleys were carved to depths below the present sea level
When the sea level was high the erosional base level was raised such that sediments and
coral reefs accumulated at higher elevations

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we
recommend that the showroom structure be supported on a shallow foundation system
consisting of spread and or continuous footings Our field exploration disclosed loose
soils near the ground surface Therefore if loose and or soft soils are encountered at
the bottom of footing elevation the loose and or soft soils should be over excavated to
the underlying dense or firm materials The resulting over excavation should be
backfilled with select granular fill materials compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction The footings may extend down to bear on the dense or firm
materials in lieu of backfilling the over excavation
Our laboratory tests indicate that the on site silty soils have a low expansion
potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations To reduce the potential for future
distress to the lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from shrinking and swelling of the
clayey and sandy silts due to changes in the moisture content we recommend that the
subgrade soils be properly prepared and capped with at least 6 inches of non expansive
select granular fill capping fill material below the slab cushion As _ previously
mentioned loose soils were encountered near the ground surface To provide a firm
base for the slabs the subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches
moisture conditioned to 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering
recommendations for design of building foundations slabs on grade site preparation
and pavements are presented in the following sections of this report

3 2 Slabs On Grade
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by
near surface clayey and sandy silts with a low expansion potential when subjected to
moisture fluctuations To reduce the potential for future distress to the lightly loaded
slabs on grade resulting from shrinking and swelling of the clayey and sandy silts due to
changes in the moisture content we recommend that the subgrade soils be properly
prepared and capped with at least 6 inches of non expansive select granular fill
capping fill material below the slab cushion
Based on our field exploration loose soils were encountered near the ground
surface Therefore the slab subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of about
8 inches moisture conditioned to 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The capping fill should be
placed as soon as possible after the subgrade soils are prepared The prepared capping
fill and the underlying subgrade soils should be wetted and kept moist prior to the final
placement of concrete Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the
subgrade we recommend that the soils be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather

After clearing and demolition the areas within the future slab on grade should be

3 6 Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away from
foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter systems
be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from the
foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and
slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of the concrete to
reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundations

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
four borings to depths of about 6 5 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using
truck mounted drilling equipment
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and
drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Boring at the
appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation
of soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
One one inch ring swell test was performed on a selected soil sample to evaluate
the swelling potential of the near surface soils Results of the tests are summarized on
Plate B 1
Two sieve analysis tests ASTM C 117 and C 136 were performed on selected
samples of the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is provided on
Plate B 2
One direct shear test ASTM D 3080 was performed on a selected sample to
evaluate the shear strength characteristics of the materials tested Results of the direct
shear test are presented on Plate B 3
One California Bearing Ratio Test ASTM D 1883 was performed on a bulk sample
of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the soils The
results of the test are presented on Plate B 4
