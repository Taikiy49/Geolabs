This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing 
services performed during site grading, utility installation, and foundation preparation for the 
New Coffee Shop, Aikahi Park Shopping Center Renovation project from October 8, 2020 to 
March 2, 2021 in accordance with our fee proposal dated September 28, 2020. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located at Aikahi Park Shopping Center, near the intersection of 
Kaneohe Bay Drive and Mokapu Boulevard in the Kaneohe area on the Island of Oahu, 
Hawaii. The project generally involves the renovation of an existing building for the new coffee 
shop, installation of new driveways and concrete curbs, and trash enclosure structure. The 
location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1, and Site Plan, 
Plate 2. 
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services 
pertaining to the site grading, utility installation, and foundation preparation for the project site. 
SITE GRADING, UTILITY INSTALLATION & FOUNDATION PREPARATION 
The site grading, utility installation, and foundation preparation work for the New Coffee 
Shop, Aikahi Park Shopping Center Renovation project were observed by Geolabs, Inc. from 
October 8, 2020 to March 2, 2021. The site grading, utility installation and foundation 
preparation consisted of clearing and grubbing, and placement and compaction of fills and 
backfills up to about 4 feet to achieve the design finished grades for the project. The site

grading, utility installation and foundation preparation work were performed by CJ Peterson 
Services Inc., who was the subcontractor for the prime contractor, Maryl Construction. During 
the site grading and backfilling operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing. The 
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in 
general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are 
summarized on Plate 3. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture 
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as 
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D1557. In the roadway areas, the fill and 
backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 
95 percent relative density. 
During the site grading and backfilling operations, periodic field density tests were 
performed in general accordance with ASTM D2922 (Nuclear Gauge Method). Results of the 
compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.9 of this report. These 
tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and constructed fills and backfills at 
the site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site 
survey stakes. These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the 
degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked 
until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and/or approved by our field 
representative based on visual observation and hand probing. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill and backfill 
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures during the above grading 
operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the referenced 
tests, it is our opinion that the site grading, backfill installation, and foundation preparation 
work for the New Coffee Shop, Aikahi Park Shopping Center Renovation project, observed 
and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report, were performed in conformance
