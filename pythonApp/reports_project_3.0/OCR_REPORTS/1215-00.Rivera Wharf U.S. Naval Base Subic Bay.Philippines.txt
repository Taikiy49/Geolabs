Site Visit and Preliminary Research Findings
Rivera Wharf U S Naval Base
Subic Bay Republic of the Philippines
As requested a site visit was made to the above referenced site on
by Mr Bob Wong from our office to observe and
gather preliminary information for evaluation of the reported damage
to the above referenced wharf This report summarizes the results of
his site visit observation and finding
SITE DESCRIPTION AND GEOLOGY
The project site is located in the eastern portion of the U S Naval
Station Subic Bay The Naval Station and Olongapo City to the north
are situated on the delta of the Kalaklan River see Plate 1 Project
Location Map
W O 1215 00 Page 2
Examination of the geomorphology of the area leads to the conjecture
that this delta is located in a valley which was cut during a lower
stand or stands of the sea by older streams originating primarily in
the Mount Balakibok Volcanic area As sea level rose to its current
stand this valley became infilled with terrigenous sediments origina
ting predominant ly From the Mount Balakibok volcanics
Owing to the low energy estuarine or deltaic environment of deposition
these soft and compressible sediments would tend to be poorly consoli
dated clays and silts underlain at depths probably 165 feet or more
below MLIW by more granular sediments of gravels and sands resulting
from the higher energy youthful stage of the valley
Examination of maps and plans in the OICC files at Subic Bay indicates
that large areas of the Naval Station are also underlain by man made
fills which were most likely placed over the older tidal marsh
deltaic deposits
FIELD OBSERVATIONS
Signs of settlement were evident along the wharf structure particu
larly at the boundary between the pile supported wharf structure and
the bulkhead supporting the fill It appears that portions of the
pile supported wharf structure as well as portions of the fill areas
have settled
W O 1215 00 Page 3
A condition survey of the wharf structure by PWC disclosed that many
cracked and spalled portions of the piles caps and deck are present
A distinct pattern or location of settlements was not readily evident
from the brief review of the previous visual observation reports It
is believed that previous repair projects may have masked the true
displacements of the structure and pavement
PRELIMINARY FILE RESEARCH
As stated above visual observations alone are not sufficient to detect
and to evaluate the condition and past performance of the wharf An
attempt was made to research into the files at Bay and
PACDIV library in Honolulu to obtain pertinent design and as built
construction drawings previous soil reports and PWC lateral and
vertical survey measurements of the area Based on our recent brief
file research due to time and budget constraint the following
information was obtained
Structure
The eastern half of the docking area consists of a pile supported
wharf structure approximately 60 feet wide The slope bank below the
wharf slopes upward from the estimated dredge depth of about elevation
minus 30 feet 30 ft to about elevation 1 5 feet at a slope ratio
grade separation up to about elevation 9 to 10 feet which is the
finished pavement level
W Page 4
The drawings indicate that the piles consist of composite timber
sections on the bottom with concrete sections above The depth of the
piles is uncertain and may vary from elevation minus 55 to minus 145
feet It appears that a vertical design load of 35 tons per pile may
have been used A schematic sketch of the wharf structure is shown on
Plate 2
The remaining portions of the area consist of a steel sheetpile
bulkhead supporting the required landfill or backfill
Plate 3 in the appendix of this report indicates the location of the
pile supported wharf structure and sheetpile bulkhead structure

survey Readings
Survey readings of vertical movements along the northeastern portion
of the wharf structure bents 90 to 135 from 1960 to 1970 measured
settlements of up to 2 8 feet and indicated that the inboard edge of
the wharf was settling more than the outboard
Horizontal measurements from 1960 to 1970 along the outboard edge of
the wharf from bents 120 to 135 and a portion of the sheetpile bulk
head indicated outward lateral movements as much as 1 4 feet near
bent 135
The general shape of the total deflections measured up to 1970 is also

POSSIBLE CAUSES OF MOVEMENT
Based on our preliminary research and analysis of previous boring
logs crack surveys and wharf settlement profiles it is our opinion
that the observed movements may be attributed to the following two
major causes

It appears that the piles may not have been driven sufficiently
deep to reach the dense sand or rock layer The site filling
has caused settlement of the soft clay subsoils If the pile
tips are located within the compressible soils that are
settling we believe that they too would settle
As a result the settling of the soft soils around the piles
creates a high downdrag load on the pile which greatly increases
the vertical load on the pile and induces additional foundation
settlements and or possibly failure
Instability Due to Lateral Movements
ee ec an
Up to 40 feet of fill has been placed over the soft soils for
the construction of the wharf facilities The weight of this
high 111 behind the wharf structure could be sufficient to
create a slope failure through the soft soils low shear
strength sensitive marine clays which could have developed a
deep seated rotation or progressive creep type of movements To
better illustrate this loading condition a schematic sketch of
the possible mode of failure is shown on Plate 4

RECOMMENDATIONS
It should be realized that the above observations and findings are
based on available data that we were able to gather to date In order
to further evaluate the future performance and more accurately define
the magnitude of the problems exist at the Rivera Wharf we recommend
the following
1
Monitor Movements Periodic surveys should be made or continued
to monitor both the vertical and horizontal movements at the
wharf The survey bench marks should be carefully chosen to
assure that they are located in a stable area unaffected by
ground or structural movements The same bench marks should be
used for all the surveys and a high level survey accuracy should
be provided
File Research A comprehensive research of the available data
soil borings survey readings construction drawings pile
driving records etc should be attempted to compile the
information already available
Field Exploration A program of exploratory borings and
laboratory testing should be developed at the completion of the
aforementioned research to supplement the existing and available
information It is our opinion that a number of borings should
be drilled in areas with good performance records as well as
distressed areas to compare varying soil conditions
W O 1215 00 Page 8
O critical concern would be the present in situ shear strength
and the settlement characteristics of the soft soils after many
years of loading by the fills and subsequent consolidation as
this would determine the present slope stability and future
performance of the area Deep borings to determine the depth to
rock or competent layers would be important in the future design
of new pile foundation
The cost for the investigation is difficult to estimate at this time
since the amount of field work borings laboratory and field
testing instrumentation etc would depend on the amount and
quality of available data and the results of the movement surveys
We will be pleased to provide an estimate when the scope of our
services are better defined
