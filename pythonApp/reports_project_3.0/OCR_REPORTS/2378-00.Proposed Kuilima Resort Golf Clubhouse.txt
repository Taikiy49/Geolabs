INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Kuilima Resort Golf Clubhouse in Kahuku on the island of
Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our exploration and our geotechnical
engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the data obtained during the
exploration These recommendations are intended for design input and are not intended
for construction estimation or bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to improve the existing Kuilima Resort Golf Course in Kahuku by
constructing a clubhouse and a cart storage building Approximately 14 to 18 feet of fills
are planned for the proposed development to raise the site above the design flood level
It is anticipated that the proposed clubhouse will be a two story wooden structure
with concrete or masonry components The main structure of the clubhouse will measure
approximately 105 feet by 250 feet in plan and will have a finish ground floor grade at
approximately 18 feet above Mean Sea Level MSL A port cochere with the floor grade
at approximately 22 5 feet elevation is planned in front of the clubhouse
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2378 00 Page 3
The proposed cart storage building will be a one story structure constructed at
approximately the same elevation as the clubhouse
An on grade parking area is planned east of the clubhouse and a main driveway
is planned at the front of the building A smaller on grade parking area is planned west
of the cart storage building
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the development site to develop a generalized soil rock data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the foundation and
earthwork design of the proposed development
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated May 12 1989
to Kuilima Development Company The scope of work was limited to
1 Drilling and sampling of nine 9 borings at the site to depths ranging from
16 0 to 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface
2 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the drilled borings
3 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained from the field exploration
to classify the subsoils encountered and determine their pertinent engineer
ing properties
4 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the
proposed structures and related earthworks
GEOLABS HAWAII

5 Project management of our overall work and design team consultation on
an as needed basis
6 Preparation of this report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing are presented
in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Kuilima Resort development area at Kahuku on
the North Shore of the island of Oahu Hawaii It is situated between the 18th hole and
the future road C in the existing Kuilima Resort Golf Course
At the time of our exploration the site was primarily undeveloped land covered with
dense vegetation A small unlined drainage ditch was located at the southern portion of
the site where the proposed port cochere will be built
Except for several high mounds and the drainage ditch the site is generally flat
Ground elevations at the proposed building locations range from 4 to 6 feet MSL The
higher grounds are at approximately 9 to 15 feet elevations
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsoil Conditions
The subsoil conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling nine 9
borings to depths of 16 0 to 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approxi
mate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The boring locations
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 2378 00 Page 5
were based on the preliminary layout of the proposed structures Subsequent to the field
exploration the layout of the structures were revised
The borings encountered up to 12 feet of loose to medium dense beach marine
sands below a thin crust of stiff clayey silt and medium dense sand fill materials The
sand deposits were variable in thickness but generally grade thinner towards the
southwestern portion of the clubhouse site
Dense coral formation was encountered below the beach marine sands generally
at depths of 4 to 15 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater Level
During our field exploration groundwater levels were measured at approximately
2 5 to 4 9 feet below the existing ground surface in the drilled borings Due to the
proximity to Kuilima Bay groundwater level at the project site is expected to fluctuate with
tidal fluctuations and storm surge conditions
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In order to raise the building site above the design flood level approximately 14
to 18 feet of fill is required for the site development Several inches of settlement are
anticipated due to compression of the loose sandy soils from the weight of this new fill
Therefore new fills should be placed as soon as possible to allow the ground settlements
to take place prior to building construction
The potential scour from tsunami conditions also has a critical effect on the building
foundation design The design scour depth has been determined by others to be on the
order of about 12 to 15 feet or close to the existing ground level This criteria requires
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2378 00 Page 6
that the building foundations extend down through the new fill and bear on or below the
existing ground
From the above considerations it is apparent that the site grading and building
foundation design construction are intimately related
Based on the above considerations two 2 major site grading foundation
alternatives were evaluated
1 In alternative No 1 the site should be filled to finished grade as soon as
possible and allowed to settle prior to building construction After the
ground settlement is completed the building foundation construction may
commence Deep trenches will need to be excavated through the new fill
in order to place the footings below the design scour depth Slab on grade
may be used on properly compacted backfill The major disadvantage of
this method is that the excavation for the building foundation construction
will remove most of the fill placed for the mass grading of the site Then
after the foundation construction the fill will need to be replaced and
compacted around the footings and walls
2 In alternative No 2 the areas around the buildings would be filled however
the actual building footprint would be left unfilled After the settlement
waiting period the building perimeter wall and interior footings may be
constructed on or below the existing grades below the design scour
depth The perimeter walls may be backfilled after completion A structural
slab will be required to eliminate the need for backfill under the building
since the weight of the backfill would cause additional ground settlement
The alternative would eliminate the rehandling of the fill material and lessen
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2378 00 Page 7
the fill required for the site However the structural slab would cost more
than the slab on grade in Alternative No 1
Site grading and foundation recommendations applicable for both alternatives are
presented below
Site Grading
Although detailed grading concepts were not available during our exploration it is
our understanding that approximately 14 to 18 feet of fills are required to raise the project
site to the proposed building floor grades Due to the higher ground elevations at
localized areas in the site a minor cut into the existing ground could also be needed for
the project grading
Ground Settlements
Based on our analyses it appears that the weight of the proposed fill may induce
up to three 3 inches of ground settlements due to compression of the underlying
loose to medium dense sand deposits
Due to the elastic nature of the granular subsoils it appears that the filled ground
would settle relatively quickly within approximately one month Therefore we
recommend that the entire project site be filled first prior to building construction
If the two 2 building locations are not to be filled Alternative No 2 the other
project areas especially those surrounding the building sites should still be filled
prior to the building and pavement construction
GEOLABS HAWAIIi
W O 2378 00 Page 8
The site grading design should include placement of additional fill to compensate
for the anticipated ground settlements
Ground Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the project area within the contract grading limits
should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing vegetation debris rubbish
and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
in order to prevent contamination of the excavated materials
Any soft loose deposits in the existing drainage ditch crossing the site should be
removed completely prior to fill placement
After clearing the entire project site including the building sites should be scarified
at least 6 inches below the cleared finished grades and recompacted proof rolled
to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
Loose spots encountered at the subgrade level should be removed and replaced
with select on site imported soils compacted to the same density
Fill Material
Since the project grading will involve minimal cut but significant fill imported fill
materials will be needed The imported fill materials should consist of non expan
sive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum size with less than
25 passing the 200 sieve
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2378 00 Page 9
Fill materials should be placed in maximum 9 inch thick loose level lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density per ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78
Fill Slope
Permanent fill slope consisting of the in situ soils or imported select materials may
be constructed to a maximum slope gradient of two horizontal to one vertical
2H 1V Temporary fill slope consisting of the same materials may be constructed
to a maximum slope gradient of one and one half horizontal to one vertical
Footing Foundation
If the entire project site is filled prior to footing construction we believe that the
proposed buildings may be supported by deep spread and or continuous footing
foundations bearing on the compacted structural fill below the design scour depth of 12
feet
Alternately if the two 2 building sites are left unfilled spread and or continuous
footing foundations bearing below the existing grades may be used to support the
proposed buildings with an elevated structural ground floor slab
An allowable bearing capacity of 2500 p s f may be used for both of the footing
foundation design schemes The bearing value is for total dead plus live loads only and
may be increased by up to one third 1 3 for short duration wind and seismic load
considerations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2378 00 Page 10
A minimum embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
should be used for the footing foundations bearing below the existing grades at the
unfilled building sites
The bottoms of the footing excavations should be recompacted to at least 90 of
its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Provided the above design guidelines are followed and proper construction
techniques are used footing settlements under the anticipated building loadings are
estimated to be on the order of less than one 1 inch total and a one half 1 2 inch
differential
Resistance to lateral loads acting on the proposed structures may be provided by
passive soil pressure and frictional resistance We recommend that an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth be used to calculate the passive soil pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the footing foundations and a frictional
coefficient of 0 4 be used to calculate the frictional resistance developed between the
footing bottoms and the recompacted footing subgrade
Perimeter Retaining Walls
If the building sites are not to be filled retaining walls should be constructed along
the entire perimeters of the two 2 proposed buildings to enclose the sites The following
recommendations may be used for the design and construction of the retaining walls
1 The surrounding site behind the perimeter walls should be filled prior to the
wall construction and the retaining walls should be backfilled prior to
construction of the building superstructure to reduce the settlements of the
structure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2378 00 Page 11
Granular backfill should be used to reduce the compaction effort required
and also to reduce the lateral earth loads on the retaining walls
A lateral earth load of 50 p c f equivalent fluid pressure may be used for the
design of retaining walls with level granular backfill if the wall is restrained
at the wall top or the backfill is under an at rest condition A lower lateral
earth load of 35 p c f equivalent fluid pressure may be used for design if
the wall is free to move at the top or the level backfill is under an active
condition
Footing bearing values of 2500 p s f on compacted new structural fill or
recompacted in situ subsoils may be used for the wall foundation design
The footing bearing values may be increased by one third 1 3 for short
duration load considerations
A passive earth resistance of 300 p c f equivalent fluid pressure and a
friction coefficient of 0 4 for wall footing on compacted fill may be used for
the wall design
Weepholes should be provided in the retaining walls to reduce the build up
of hydrostatic pressures
Driveway and Parking Pavements
Asphaltic concrete A C pavements may be used for the proposed driveways and
parking areas to serve the new buildings
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2378 00 Page 12

pavement section may be used for preliminary design purpose

Laboratory CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil samples
during construction to verify the above design section The recommended pavement
section assumes that good drainage will be provided for areas adjacent to the pavements

Construction Observation
It is recommended that the project geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during site grading and the other foundation
construction phases of the project This is to observe compliance with the design
concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event
that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the project geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the project site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between
the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re
evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings were approximately determined by taping from
the existing structures features on an undated existing topographic survey of the project
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2378 00 Page 14
site provided by Geoffrey Paterson Associates Inc AIA on May 4 1989 Elevations of
the borings were estimated from the topographic survey The physical locations and
elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition The strata lines shown on profiles or cross sections are based upon
interpolation between borings and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the borings were collected at the times given on the graphic
representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed and
interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors

sampler with a 140 pound free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler
the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
Rock core samples were obtained by a NX core barrel The core recovery and
rock quality designation RQD values are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
