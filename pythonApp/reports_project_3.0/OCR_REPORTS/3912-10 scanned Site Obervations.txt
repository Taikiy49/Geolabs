Dear Mr Hayashida
In accordance with your request a site visit was made to observe the re drilling of
the pilot hole for the HDD pipe installation under Ala Naio Stream It was reported by the
drilling contractor that previous drill runs encountered boulders below the stream which
would prevent successful back reaming and pulling of the HDPE outer casing through the
pilot hole However the contractor did not present physical evidence of the presence of
the boulders and drilling difficulty Therefore the purpose of the test drilling today was to
allow us to observe the drilling operations and obtain cuttings of the material drilled to
attempt to confirm or deny the presence of the boulders
The drill stem was inserted into the previously drilled pilot hole with a drili bit slightly
smaller than originally used It was believed that the previously drilled hole had probably
caved in within the boulder zone and a smaller bit would reduce the chance of getting
stuck It appeared that the hole had not caved in so a larger bit was used to re drill the
pilot hole The depth and alignment of the drill bit was monitored with a beacon from the
ground surface It was reported that this system has an accuracy of plus or minus 1
The following summarizes our observations
e Thedrilling though the coral in the initial portion of the hole was relatively smooth
e The drilling was noticeably rough when the drill bit approached the stream
e The color of the drilling fluid changed from milky white to grayish white during the
rough drilling under the stream
e Cuttings were obtained from the drilling fluid and brought back to our laboratory for
examination Visual observations after washing disclosed that most of the cuttings
were gray basalt
e The beacon did not function properly at the stream crossing probably due to
interference from the overhead electrical lines However readings prior to reaching
the stream crossing indicated that the depth of the drill bit was about 12 feet below the
road surface
