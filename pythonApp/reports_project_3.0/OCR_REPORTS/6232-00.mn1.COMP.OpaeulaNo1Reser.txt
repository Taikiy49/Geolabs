PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Opaeula No 1 Reservoir Dam at Waialua was constructed in 1910 for
irrigation purposes The dam is approximately 71 feet high from toe downstream to crest
and has a maximum impounding volume of approximately 400 acre feet It is the highest
and largest of the Opaeula dams 1 2 and 15 The reservoir receives water from the
Opaeula ditch tunnel which is fed by the Opaeula and Kawaiiki Streams in the Kawailoa
Forest Reserve
Opaeula No 1 Reservoir Dam is located approximately 5 5 miles east southeast of
Haleiwa Town on the Island of 34 4 N W The reservoir is
located on the Leeward side of the Koolau Mountain range in the northwest portion of
Oahu as shown on the attached Site Plan Plate 1

The objective of the upgrades on Opaeula No 1 Reservoir Dam is to improve its
operational and safety characteristics Additional features include
1
A new primary emergency spillway constructed on the north side of the dam
as shown in the Site Plan Plate 1
2
A flip bucket constructed at the base of the dam face to mitigate high exit
velocities at the new spillway outlet including a rubble apron at the
discharge point into the existing streambed
3
Upgrading the hydraulic controls to provide safer operation and improved
control of water flowing into and out of the reservoir
4
Construction of an earth buttress on the downstream face of the dam
35 feet high by 120 feet long consisting of compacted earth from on site
materials excavated from the new spillway construction
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the site grading operations consisting of fills and backfills associated with the above dam
improvements and infrastructure installation
SITE GRADING
The grading works consisted of fills and backfills using on site soils consisting
primarily of clayey silts At the onset of grading the areas that received fill were cleared
and grubbed prior to filling Hawaiian Dredging and Construction Company performed the
site grading and backfilling work During the site grading operations samples of the
imported materials utilized as fill materials were obtained and transported to our soils
laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were
established in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor The test results
are summarized on Plate 2
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 At the crest of the dam where it is also
used as a service road to get across the channel between the north and south abutments
the fill and backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative density
During the grading and structure backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method
Compaction test results are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 6 These tests were
performed on compacted surfaces of constructed fills and backfills Test locations and
elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These
locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified
minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on
visual observation and hand probing
