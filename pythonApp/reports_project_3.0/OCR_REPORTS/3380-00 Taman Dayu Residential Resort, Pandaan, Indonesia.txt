Compaction and Density Test Data
Based on the laboratory test data the average in situ dry density and moisture
content are approximately m and 40 percent respectively Laboratory test
results for the one proctor test conducted on the near surface soils indicate that the
maximum dry density is about 1 68 ton m with an optimum moisture content of about
21 percent
Based on the field exploration data and laboratory test results the soils at the
project site generally have high natural moisture contents due to the relatively wet climatic
conditions in the area The moisture contents are usually significantly in excess of the
optimum moisture about 15 to 25 percent above the optimum moisture content The
optimum moisture content is based on one proctor test conducted on the near surface soils
presented in the report We suggest that two or three additional proctor tests ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91 be conducted on the near surface materials to further evaluate the
maximum dry density and optimum moisture contents
In general we believe that the embankment material should be moisture conditioned
to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of
soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined

The high natural moisture contents could result in increased handling of the on site

Shrinkage Factors
Based on the results of the one proctor test and average in situ dry density we
estimate that the shrinkage factors for the general fill 90 percent relative compaction to
be approximately 17 percent 0 83 factor The shrinkage for the golf course fill
85 percent relative compaction is estimated to be about 11 percent 0 89 factor These
average shrinkage values take into consideration the shrinkage due to compaction only
Shrinkage due to handling and other causes were not included in the factors above
Grading Requirements
Where required import material should consist of non expansive granular materials
and should not contain organic or other deleterious materials In general the import fill
should be well graded with no particles greater than 3 inches in largest dimension and
should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR swell value not exceeding
1 percent when prepared near the optimum moisture Import fill should be moisture
conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than
90 percent relative compaction Import fill materials should be tested and approved for its
intended use prior to hauling to the project site
In general cut slope ratios of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter may
be used Cut slopes exposing expansive clayey soils should be protected from drying to
reduce the potential for instability due to shrinkage cracks Therefore the slope face
should be adequately protected from development of shrinkage cracks It may be prudent
to flatten cut slopes to 2 H 1V or flatter in some cases
In the event that over cutting a cut slope occurs consideration should be given to
leaving the slope rather than attempting to backfill the slope to the design grade with sliver
fills If backfilling of an over cut slope is necessary keying and benching requirements

Embankment Settlements
Since the grading concept is still being finalized at this time site specific settlements
due to embankment fill placement have not been evaluated at this time Based on our
preliminary estimates we believe that settlements on the order of about 0 25 meters may
be anticipated due to consolidation of the existing soils under the 10 meters of new fills
Based on preliminary estimates a large portion of the estimated settlements should occur
within 2 to 4 months after placement or during construction However it should be
recognized that it is very difficult to accurately predict the exact time required for the filled
ground to settle since the settlement rate are affected by variations of the subsoil
structure
To monitor the settlements and the rates of settlement we suggest that settlement
gauges be installed prior to the start of the grading work in the fill areas A typical
settlement gauge is shown on Plate 3 The gauges should be read optically by profes
sionals and the readings should be transmitted to the geotechnical engineer for review
The readings should be taken on a weekly basis during the first 3 months of construction
and once every 2 weeks during the remainder of the anticipated settlement period and or
until the settlement rate has stabilized

In accordance with your request we have reviewed the soil investigation report

review of the above referenced report we have the following comments
In general the report describes the field work performed and presents the
information from the field exploration and laboratory testing programs The report also
provides general recommendations with respect to construction compaction equipment
settlements of fills and buildings on fills slope stability of cuts and fills and allowable
bearing pressures The report also briefly discusses numerous other items including
the design California Bearing Ratio CBR boulder hazard retaining walls and
subdrains among others
The recommendations presented in the report are general in nature Because
the terrain and the soil conditions at the site are quite complex special attention should
be given to the geotechnical considerations for development of the site Therefore we
believe that it would be difficult for the design team to interpret the soil conditions from
the report and use the appropriate recommendations for a cost effective and practical
design As a result more specific recommendations will be needed to assist the civil
and structural consultants in the design to avoid misinterpretation of the intent of the
general recommendations provided in the report In addition the subsurface conditions
at some areas consist of soft to medium stiff soils therefore the planned cut fill
conditions and structures may significantly affect the geotechnical analyses

investigation performed by Testana Engineering Inc we envision a laboratory testing
program consisting of the following tests Additional tests may be needed depending
on the materials encountered during the drilling and sampling operations

undisturbed soil sample obtained
