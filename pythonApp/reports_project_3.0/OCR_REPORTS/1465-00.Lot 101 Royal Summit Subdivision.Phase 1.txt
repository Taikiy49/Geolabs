In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to
provide foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The
study consisted of drilling three shallow borings laboratory testing
of the swelling potential of the soils and reviewing of the
subdivision site grading and preliminary house foundation plans This
report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
Three borings were drilled within the proposed building location to
depths ranging from 5 0 to 10 0 feet below the existing grade at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
In general the borings encountered very stiff reddish brown clayey
silt to the full depth of our borings
A review of the mass grading plan prepared by Community Planning Inc
dated for Royal Summit Phase 1 indicated that this
lot has received up to about 10 feet of compacted fill over the
natural grade during the initial subdivision development
No groundwater was encountered during our investigation
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The stiff reddish brown clayey silt encountered can generally provide
adequate foundation support for the proposed house provided that some
care is exercised during construction to minimize the swelling
potential of the soil
Laboratory tests performed on the stiff on site clayey silt soil
indicates that this material generally has low to moderate swelling
potential Therefore to minimize effects due to soil swelling under
on grade slabs we recommend that the on site soil be capped with at

Lot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 18 inches minimum below the
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soil such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral Where minimal fill is anticipated
such as the garage driveway and the lower house areas the on site
oil should be over excavated to provide an 18 inch minimum thickness
of non expansive select fill capping
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the soils
engineer prior to being used in the site
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be cleared
of vegetation and debris
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8 inches
in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78

Fills placed on existing slopes should start from the bottom up and
where the slope is steeper than 5 1 H V and should be keyed and
benched to bond the new fill to the existing slope
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away
from all foundations
House Foundations
It is anticipated that the entire house will be of slab on grade
construction
If the on site soils are capped with at least 18 inches of
non expansive select fill as recommended in the LOT GRADING
section then spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the
proposed residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be
used on the compacted soil
Interior footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 8
inches below the finished grade Perimeter wall footings should be
embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the adjacent finished grade
Surface water should be diverted away from foundations

Slab On Grade
It should be noted that some of the on site clayey silt soils are
moderately expansive and may be unsuitable to provide direct slab
support Therefore the on site soil should be over excavated down to
a depth of 18 inches below the slab bottom first wetted and then
replaced with non expansive imported soils as previously discussed
The thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the lowest adjacent finish grade and bear on the above
select borrow backfill
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the in place clayey silt below the
upper capping fill pad be kept moist for several days prior to
concrete placement
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture
infiltration impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below
the slab The on grade slabs should be structurally separated from
wall foundations

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid
ponding condition Also it is advised that gutter systems should be
installed around the house and that gutter water should be diverted
far away from the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape
watering near the foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss
of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled tight against slab footing immediately after setting of
the concrete to prevent ponding condition

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill operations
be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
