From March 17 1987 to we observed the earthwork and grading
operations performed for the above referenced project This report summarizes our
observations and compaction tests performed at the above referenced site
The above project site is located adjacent to Honokai Hale Subdivision across the
Waimanalo Gulch Landfill
The grading operations at the above referenced site consisted of cuts up to 8 feet
and fills up to 25 feet The material on site generally consisted of dark brown clayey silt
mixed with coral or basalt gravel excavated basalt rock and coral material

Prior to fill placement the existing vegetation was removed and the existing ground
scarified and recompacted The fill was then placed in horizontal lifts and compacted to
a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
During the fill placement operations periodic field density tests were done in
accordance with the American Society for Testing and Materials ASTM Test Designation
D 1556 Sand Cone Method D 2922 Nuclear Method The results of the compaction
test are summarized in the attachments of this report
During the grading period various on site and borrow soils were tested prior to
use in the field The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were
established in our laboratory in accordance with ASTM test designation D 1557 modified
proctor and the test results included in the attachments
Cavities in Coral Formation
Numerous cavities were observed in the surface of the natural coral formations in
the southern portions of the Grading Area No 3 and the Lagoon Grading and Marina
Areas Following discussion of the various methods which could be used it was decided
to utilize a test section in the proposed park area to assess the effectiveness of ripping
and flooding the cavity area In general the method consisted of the following
1 Rip open the upper 2 to 3 feet of coral to expose the cavities
2 Stockpile the excavated material
GEOLABS HAWAII
KG Hawaii Corporation Page 3

3 Flood the area to attempt to wash the finer material into the deep portion
of the cavities
4 Over excavate the areas where larger cavities are indicated by the ripping
and or flooding to expose the cavities
5 Backfill the cavities exposed with sandy soils
The above procedures indicated potential for adequately filling the cavities and was
used for the remaining areas
Since we do not know details of the future structures and their influence depths
methods such as the above described ripping and flooding may not extend to a sufficient
depth to adequately cover the depth of concern The designers of structures on the
affected lots should consider the possible presence of cavities in the coral formation in
their foundation design
Boulder Fill
Many coralline and basalt boulders were encountered in the excavations at the
project Some of the boulders were used in the fills for Lot 25
In general the boulders were spread out to allow placement of
finer material between them then watered and compacted to form a dense mass
During field observations of the mass grading it was noted that Lot
were not filled to the planned grade elevations according to the mass grading plans It
is noted that the as built elevations were filled and compacted properly

Site Grading
Subsequent to completion of lot grading and utility trenches within the lot pads any
