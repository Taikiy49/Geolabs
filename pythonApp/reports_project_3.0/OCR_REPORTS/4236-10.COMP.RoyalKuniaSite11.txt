Earthwork Observation and Compaction Testing Report
Royal Kunia Site 11
Waipahu Oahu Hawaii
Dear Mr Gaston
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed during
the mass grading and utility backfilling operations at the Royal Kunia Site 11 development
from August 1999 through November 1999
The subject site is located in the vicinity of the northern intersection of Ano iki and
Anonui Streets in Waipahu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii A copy of the grading and utility
plan is attached for your reference
The mass grading and utility backfilling work was performed by Royal Contracting
Company Inc The mass grading work consisted of fills on the order of 3 to 8 feet in height
and cuts up to 4 feet in depth The fill materials were generally imported from the Royal Kunia
Sites 4 7 and 13 The available fill materials generally consisted of reddish brown silty clay
The on site subgrade materials generally consisted of reddish brown silty clay
As recommended in our geotechnical engineering exploration report the previously
placed fill soils under the recreation center building locker and exercise building and pool
locations were over excavated by 6 feet prior to additional filling The over excavation was
extended at least 10 feet beyond the proposed limits of the buildings and pool
The fill and backfill materials were brought to near optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557
During the grading period various on site soils were tested prior to use in the field The
maximum dry density and optimum moisture content relationships were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with the American Society of Testing and Materials ASTM
Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this
report

During the fill placement operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with the ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method
Results of the compaction tests are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 11 of this report
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill placement
and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil conditions exposed during the
above grading operations
Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site
survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used Where low test results were noted the areas were
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Field observations by our field technician during the utility backfilling work for the project
indicate that the work was performed in accordance with the general intent of the
recommendations in the following references
