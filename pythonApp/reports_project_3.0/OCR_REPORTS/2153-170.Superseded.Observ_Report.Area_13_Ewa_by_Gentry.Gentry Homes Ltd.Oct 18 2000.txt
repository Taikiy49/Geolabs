This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing
services performed during fine grading and roadway and utility construction for the Ewa by
Gentry Area 13 development from to May 10 2000 in accordance with our
revised fee proposal dated
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the western portion of the Ewa by Gentry Residential
Development Area in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is
bounded by North South Road on the east and Coral Creek Golf Course on the west and
Geiger Road on the north The location of the project site is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The project site encompasses an area of approximately 4 9 acres and includes a total of
40 residential lots
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the fine grading operations and infrastructure installation for the project site
MASS GRADING
The mass grading operations for the Ewa by Gentry Area 13 site were performed by
RHS Lee Inc under a separate contract In general the mass grading operations consisted
of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills The depths of excavations
and thickness of embankments at the site were on the order of about 2 to 5 feet Much of
the fills needed at the site to raise the pre existing grades to the design mass grading grades
generally consisted of fill materials generated from Area G3 and the western end of Area G2
Field monitoring work for the mass grading operations were provided by our office and were
documented in our grading report entitled Earthwork Observation and Compaction
Testing Report Areas F and G of Ewa by Gentry Makai West dated
2006 Kalihi Street Honolulu Hawaii 96819

FINE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
The fine grading and utility installation work for the Area 13 project were observed by
Geolabs Inc from to May 10 2000 The fine grading work consisted of
placement and compaction of fills on the order of about 1 to 2 feet thick to achieve the design
finished grades for the project The fine grading and utility backfilling work were performed
by Delta Construction Company Ltd During the fine grading operations samples of the
on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and
transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture
contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 2
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill and
backfill materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
During the fine grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge
Method Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1 through
3 11 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed
to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Compaction testing was also performed on the asphalt concrete pavement for Roads
A B and B2 Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1
through 3 11
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill placement
subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil
conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the fine grading and utility installation work for the
above referenced project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this
report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the
following references
