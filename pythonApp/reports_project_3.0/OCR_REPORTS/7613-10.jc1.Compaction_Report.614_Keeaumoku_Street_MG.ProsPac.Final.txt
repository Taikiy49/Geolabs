We are pleased to submit our geotechnical engineering earthwork observations and 
compaction testing report for work performed during the mass grading operation of the Azure 
Ala Moana high-rise building development at 641 Keeaumoku Street in general

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The proposed development is located along Keeaumoku Street in the Ala Moana 
area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The property consists of two parcels 
(designated as TMK: 2-3-021: 037 & 039) bounded approximately by Keeaumoku Street to 
the west, Makaloa Street to the south, an existing golf academy facility to the east, and the 
Samsung Plaza to the north. 
The mass grading (a.k.a. the pre-construction site grading) consisted of demolition 
basement parking garage, backfilling and grading the site to near finish pad grade level 
and preparing for auger-cast-in-place pile construction access. Albert C. Kobayashi, Inc. 
is the project general contractor, while Jayar Construction, Inc. performed the earthwork 
operations as a sub-contractor.

SITE GRADING 
At the onset of earthwork, areas within the contract grading limits were generally 
cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials. The existing basement 
parking garage was demolished. Materials generated from the clearing operations were 
disposed of off-site. 
Soft and yielding areas encountered during site grading were generally 
over-excavated to expose firm natural material, and the resulting excavation was backfilled 
with well-compacted engineering fill. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density 
as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. Some areas, where are subjected to the 
crane loads and presented pumping and/or yield conditions, were over-excavated about 
12 inches deep, replaced with engineering fill and geogrid reinforcement as stabilization, 
and compacted to a minimum 95 percent of the maximum dry density. 
During the site grading operations, samples of the materials utilized as fill were 
obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing.  The maximum dry densities and 
optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 
(Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 3. 
Periodic in-situ moisture and density tests were conducted in general accordance 
with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method) to determine the adequacy of compaction of 
fills and backfills during the mass grading operation.  
The test results are summarized on Plates 4.1 and 4.2. These tests were 
performed on moisture-conditioned and compacted lifts of fills and backfills. Test locations 
and elevations were estimated using on-site survey stakes for reference. These locations 
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods 
used. Areas deemed unsatisfactory were reworked until the measured relative 
compaction conformed to or exceeded the minimum specified for the project. 
This report summarizes our field observations and compaction testing 
services during the site grading work for the period covering March 18 through 
April 25, 2019.
