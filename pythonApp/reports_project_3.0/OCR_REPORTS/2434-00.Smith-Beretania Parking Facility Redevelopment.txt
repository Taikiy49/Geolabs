INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Smith Beretania Parking Facility Redevelopment
in downtown Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site
and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings resulting from the exploration and presents our
preliminary geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses based on
the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are intended for
design input and are not intended for construction bidding or estimation purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The existing Smith Beretania Parking Lot is an open lot located along Smith Street
between Beretania and Pauahi Streets in downtown Honolulu Based on the current
scheme it is proposed to redevelop the site into a multi use complex including the
following elements
An underground structure providing two levels of parking
A landscaped park at the existing street level
Commercial retail and office spaces one 1 to three 8 floors high and
sO NM
A child care facility
Based on the plans provided to us we anticipate that construction of the
underground parking structure will involve about 21 feet of excavation below the existing
parking lot grade The excavation will abut the existing elderly housing highrise structure
and the Calvary Chapel on Nuuanu Avenue as well as Smith Beretania and Pauahi
Streets

Support of the open excavation during construction of the underground structure
will be a major concern particularly for the portion of the excavation adjacent to the
existing highrise building

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the downtown area of Honolulu on the island of Oahu
The site is bounded by Beretania Street to the east Smith Street to the north and Pauahi
Street to the west On the south along Nuuanu Avenue the site is bounded by existing
buildings including Calvary Chapel some one story structures and a 22 story highrise
tower There is a covered walkway about 12 feet wide between the tower structure and
the existing parking lot
Presently the site is an on grade municipal parking lot paved with asphaltic
concrete Patches cracks and pot holes were observed on the existing pavement The
terrain of the site is nearly level with a slight slope towards Pauahi Street southwest
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling eight 8 borings to
depths of 26 0 to 71 5 feet below the existing parking lot The approximate boring
locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2434 00 Page 5
Below the asphalt surface of the parking lot our borings generally encountered a
surface fill layer about five 5 to six 6 feet thick consisting of materials ranging from
loose silty sand to medium stiff clayey to sandy silts In Boring No 4 thin layers of cinder
sand and brown silt were encountered at the base of the surface fill
Underlying the surface fill the borings encountered coral formations which
generally extended to a depth of about 25 feet below the existing ground surface Based
on the exploration results the upper five 5 to seven 7 feet of the coral below the fill is
well cemented and competent However with increasing depth the degree of
cementation decreases and the coral grades generally to a medium dense to dense and
fragmented condition
Below the coral formation the borings generally encountered stiff to very stiff
alluvial deposits consisting of sandy silt to silty sand with some weathered basalt gravel
to the maximum depth drilled 71 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings at depths ranging between 24
and 27 feet below the existing grade at the time of our exploration It is believed that the
groundwater exists as a basal water table and that the groundwater levels will fluctuate
in response to precipitation and tidal influences
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered are presented on the Boring

Foundations
Based on the exploration data it appears that the coral formation and underlying
very stiff alluvium encountered below the proposed excavation depth of about 21 feet
below the existing ground surface can provide adequate foundation support for the
structure
It is our opinion that shallow spread and continuous footing foundations may be
utilized to support the proposed parking structure An allowable bearing value of up to
4500 p s f may be used for the design of footing foundations bearing on the in situ coral
formation The recommended bearing value is for total dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered A
minimum footing embedment of 24 inches is recommended
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2434 00 Page 7
The foundations should bear directly on the undisturbed coral formation If
fragmental coral material is encountered in the foundation subgrade the material should
be moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum
of 95 of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Estimated settlements for foundations designed and constructed in accordance with the
above recommendations are anticipated to be on the order of less than one inch total and
one half of an inch differential
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent
fluid pressure of 350 p s f per foot of depth of the buried vertical faces This assumes
that the soil around the foundation is well compacted Unless covered by pavements or
slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soils should be neglected A
coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be used between the footing bottom and subgrade soils
If foundations are located next to utility trenches they should be embedded below
a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the
bottom edge of the trenches or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines
All prepared foundation excavations should be observed by a qualified geotechnical
engineer prior to the placement of reinforcement and concrete to determine if adequate
bearing has been obtained

Excavation
Based on the current plans the anticipated depth of excavation below existing
grade is 21 feet for the two level underground parking structure
Very hard excavation requiring hoerams or chipping is anticipated for cuts into
the coral formation below the fill This is particularly true for the upper five 5 to seven
7 feet of the coral formation which based on our boring data is well cemented and
relatively hard
The method and equipment used for excavation should be the choice of the
contractor within practical limits and safety considerations
Excavation Support
The excavation for the underground structure will abut existing improvements along
the boundaries of the site An adequately designed and properly constructed excavation
support system will be needed to reduce the potential of damage to the surrounding utility
lines streets and buildings by earth movement into or towards the open excavation
Based on the available plans and information the existing 22 story building
adjacent to the site is supported by a mat foundation approximately 4 feet thick bearing
on the coral formation A covered walkway runs along the perimeter of the tower
adjacent to the existing parking lot It appears that the proposed deep excavation
boundary will be about eight 8 feet from the edge of the existing mat foundation
In addition the proposed excavation will abut the Calvary Chapel and one story
commercial buildings along Nuuanu Avenue Plans for the foundations of these buildings
were not available for use in preparation of this report It is our belief that the foundations
of these existing structures also bear on the upper fill material at shallow depths
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2434 00 Page 9
Therefore particular care should be exercised to reduce the potential of lateral
ground movements along the building line particularly near the 22 story highrise tower
It is our opinion that at least two 2 different structural retaining support designs will be
required for the excavation one for that adjacent to the existing buildings and one for that
adjacent to the streets
Since the hard coral may stop sheet pile penetration it is our opinion that a
support system consisting of soldier piles and lagging with temporary tiebacks or rakers
could be adopted to provide excavation support of the project It appears that the use
of horizontal bracing or struts inside the excavation will not be practical since the
anticipated width of the excavation is over 120 feet Generally the soldier piles should
consist of wide flange steel beams and heavy timbers should be used for the lagging
However the actual design of the soldier pile and lagging system or other appropriate
type of excavation support system should be left to the contractor
The soldier piles should generally be installed at no more than seven 7 feet on
centers in predrilled holes extending a minimum of ten 10 feet below the bottom of the
excavation The bottom 10 feet of the predrilled holes should be backfilled with tremie
concrete after placement of the soldier piles
For preliminary design of the excavation support system the apparent lateral earth
pressure diagram presented on Plate 3 may be used as a guide
Due to the anticipated heavy lateral loads derived from the 22 story highrise
buildings adjacent to the excavation lateral supports such as temporary tiebacks or
rakers should be utilized to reduce the potential ground movement towards the open
excavation Temporary tiebacks may also be required for the remainder of the
excavation

A tieback is a structural system which uses an anchor embedded in the ground
to secure a tendon which applies a lateral force on a retaining structure Tiebacks
have been employed primarily for temporary excavations generally utilizing soldier
piles with lagging for soil retention
Tiebacks are advantageous since they are external to the excavation and
permanent construction may proceed without hinderance from internal bracing
A schematic sketch of the tieback support system is presented on Plate 4
Excavations supported by tiebacks usually experience less movement than those
supported by raker braced walls This is because the tiebacks are installed from
the temporary ground surface and excavation does not proceed below a given
anchor level until the anchors for that level are installed
At this time there are a number of different tieback systems available for
consideration including drilled and grouted anchors deadman anchors and helical
anchors It is recommended that the contractor retain a specialty subcontractor
or structural engineering consultant to aid in selecting an appropriate tieback
system and to design the overall excavation support for the project
Alternatively a raker supported soldier pile and lagging system may be considered
to provide temporary support for the excavation sidewalls It should be
understood that rakers are acceptable but are less preferable than tiebacks This
is because the excavation in front of the temporary lagging wall will have to
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2434 00 Page 11
proceed prior to positioning the rakers As a result raker braced excavations
usually experience greater lateral movements at the top than tieback support
systems In addition the rakers project into the excavation and cause interference
with construction of the permanent structure
if the raker system is selected for excavation support it is recommended that the
following raker installation procedures be followed and extreme care be exercised
to reduce the ground movements during excavation
In the raker support system excavations in the central portion of the site may
proceed first leaving temporary berms around the anticipated excavation
boundary see Plate 5 In order to reduce potential ground movements the
berms should then be trenched for the installation of rakers The first set of rakers
should be installed as early as practicable As the berms are removed gradually
the second set of rakers should then be placed by trenching in Additional sets
of rakers as necessary may be installed by following the same procedure
We strongly recommend that the contractor retain a competent structural
engineering consultant to design and to coordinate the implementation of the excavation
support system
Construction Precautions
The soldier piles and timber lagging with either tieback or raker systems must
comply with all applicable safety requirements The adequacy and safety of the
excavation support should be made the sole responsibility of the contractor His
representative who should be continuously present on site will have the best opportunity
to observe any changing conditions during excavation and construction such as
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2434 00 Page 12
unforeseen subsoils unsuitable construction sequences or techniques etc which might
affect the excavation stability
Since lateral supports are installed as excavation proceeds movements will
inevitably occur due to changes in stress on the excavation sidewalls during excavation
Even with the best construction procedures the excavation will proceed ahead of support
installation The further excavation proceeds before the next level of support is installed
the greater is the potential for lateral ground movements
Therefore it is important to realize that the excavation supports should be installed
as early as practicable particularly if rakers are to be adopted The vertical and
horizontal ground movements outside the excavation should be monitored periodically
It is also recommended that a damage survey of all the existing buildings and
structures adjacent to the project site be conducted The survey should include
photographs and records of the cracks and existing conditions prior to the
commencement of construction
Underpinning
It is suspected that some or all of the foundations of the low rise structures
surrounding the 22 story tower may bear on the upper medium dense fill material at
shallow depths below the existing ground surface
It is recommended that these footings where they are situated near the face of the
excavation be underpinned to transfer loads to the underlying well cemented coral
formation

Fill Material
Based on the field exploration results the on site coral material wnen excavated
will be a suitable source of fill material provided that it is processed to 3 inch minus
sizes
If required imported fill material should consist of non expansive select granular
materials less than 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20 passing the 200
sieve All imported fill material should be tested and approved by the geotechnical
engineer prior to transportation to the site
Backfill behind basement walls should consist of self compacting clean granular
material such as ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel 3B Fine or clean coral gravel of
similar gradation
Fill Placement
Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78
Waterproofing
It is anticipated that the lowest basement level will be close to groundwater level
therefore proper waterproofing should be provided during construction
Special attention should be given to the details of waterproofing between horizontal
and vertical members to reduce potential of leakage through the joints between the walls
and foundations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the
field borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction If variations then appear evident it may be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The approximate location of the field borings were determined by taping from
objects indicated on the schematic drawings transmitted on March 9 1990 by Kajioka
Okada Partners Actual elevations of the field borings were not available at the
time of report preparation The physical location of the field boring should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field borings
depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a
gradual transition
Water level data from the field borings was collected at the times given on the
graphic representations and in the text of this report This data has been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted
that fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors
