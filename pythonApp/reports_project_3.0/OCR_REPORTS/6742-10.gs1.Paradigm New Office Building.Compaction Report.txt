Dear Mr. Kwon: 
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and compaction 
testing services performed during foundation preparation, pavement and utility installation 
for the Paradigm New Office Building project from June 25, 2014 to December 31, 2014 in 
accordance with our fee proposal dated July 30, 2014. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located near the old Waipahu Sugar Mill in Waipahu on the 
Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  The site is situated between Akoki Street and Malakeke Street.  
The location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1.  The project 
involves the construction of an office building and parking area.  The layout of the project 
is shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
Site development involved up to about 2 feet of fill and some minor cut sections; 
installation of utility lines; and construction of parking stalls.  This report addresses our field 
observations and compaction testing services for the foundation preparation, parking area 
and utility construction at the project site. 
FOUNDATION PREPARATION AND PARKING AREA & UTILITY CONSTRUCTION 
The earthwork construction at the Paradigm New Office Building project consisted 
of foundation preparation, and parking area and utility construction.  The footing 
construction, and pavement and utility backfilling work were performed by Paradigm 
Construction LLC.  During the site grading operations, samples of the on-site and imported 
materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office

for testing.  The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established 
in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor).   The 
compaction test results are summarized on Plate 3. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to near optimum moisture 
content, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as 
determined in accordance with ASTM D1557.  In the parking areas, the fill and backfill 
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 
95 percent relative density.  In addition, the base course material below the pavement 
areas was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative density. 
During the footing preparation and utility backfilling operations, periodic field density 
tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method).  
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.4 of 
this report.  These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on 
constructed fills and backfills at the site.  Test locations and elevations were estimated by 
pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes.  These locations and elevations 
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used.  Areas 
assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was 
obtained and/or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and 
hand probing. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill 
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures and confirmation of 
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the footing preparation, and pavement and utility 
construction work for the above-referenced project observed and tested by Geolabs in the 
period covered by this report were performed in conformance with the general intent of the 
recommendations in the following references. 
1. 
Chapter 14 of the Revised Ordinances of Honolulu 1990, as amended. 
2. 
Report by Geolabs, Inc. entitled, “Geotechnical Engineering Exploration, 
New Office Building, Waipahu Mill Town, Waipahu, Oahu, Hawaii", dated 
March 12, 2013.
