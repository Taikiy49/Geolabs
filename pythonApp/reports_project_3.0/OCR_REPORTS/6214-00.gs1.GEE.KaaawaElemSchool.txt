PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is within Kaaawa Elementary School on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
We understand that as part of the cesspool conversion project an 8 foot diameter by
23 feet long Aerobic Treatment Unit will be installed about 10 feet below the existing
ground surface
Temporary shoring will be required for the new tank installation We understand
that a 4 foot thick tremie plug will be constructed at the bottom of the excavation
Therefore the total depth of excavation will be about 14 feet below the existing ground
surface We understand that 30 or 50 foot long steel sheeting are being considered for the
project In addition we understand that the existing ground elevation at the tank site is
about 6 Mean Sea Level MSL

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling
two borings designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 to depths of about 50 5 and 51 5 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate boring locations are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration generally encountered a surface fill layer about 0 5 to 1 foot
thick underlain by beach deposit to about 12 5 to 15 feet below the existing ground
surface Below the beach deposit coralline detritus and lagoonal deposits were
encountered to depths of about 42 5 to 44 5 feet followed by alluvial deposits extending to
the maximum explored depth of 51 5 feet below the existing ground surface
The fill layer consisted of sandy silt and the beach deposit was composed of loose
to medium dense sand with silt and gravel The coralline detritus was comprised of very
loose to medium dense sands and gravels The lagoonal deposits consisted of very soft
to medium stiff clayey silt and sandy silt and medium dense silty gravel The alluvial
deposits were composed of soft to very stiff silty clay
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at about 4 4 and 5 feet below the
existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration Based on an existing ground
elevation of 6 MSL these measured groundwater levels correspond to approximately
1 6 and 1 MSL respectively It should be noted that groundwater levels may fluctuate
depending on tidal fluctuation seasonal precipitation and other factors Detailed
descriptions of the materials encountered in the borings are presented on the Logs of
performed on selected soil samples are presented in Appendix B
LATERAL EARTH PRESSURES
As indicated above we understand that an excavation depth of 10 feet below the
existing ground surface is required for the temporary shoring during tank installation In
addition we understand that a 4 foot thick tremie plug will be constructed below the
10 foot excavation
Based on the results of our field exploration and information provided above the
lateral earth pressure diagrams for the two temporary conditions are provided on Plates 3
and 4 The first temporary condition is before the tremie plug is poured and the depth of
excavation will be about 14 feet below the existing ground surface It should be noted that
the earth pressures provided on Plate 3 assumes that the excavation will be performed in
the wet prior to tremie plug construction The second temporary condition is after the
tremie plug has been poured and the excavation is dewatered to provide a dry excavation

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of subsurface conditions between and
beyond the field borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident Geolabs
Inc should be contacted to re evaluate the recommendations presented herein
This letter report has been prepared for the exclusive use of Jas W Glover Ltd for
specific application to the temporary shoring for the Kaaawa Elementary School Hawaii
DOE Cesspool Conversion project in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical
engineering principles and practices No warranty is expressed or implied This report has
been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the client and design engineers in the
design of the temporary shoring for the installation of the Aerobic Treatment tank
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are commonly
encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as perched groundwater soft deposits
hard layers or cavities may occur in localized areas and may require additional probing or
corrections in the field which may result in construction delays to attain a properly
constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to
accommodate these possible extra costs
