INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soils and foundation
investigation for the proposed development located in Kahaluu
Oahu Hawaii The scope of this study was to investigate geologic
and subsurface soil conditions evaluate overall site stability
and determine foundation requirements for the proposed residences
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is presently planned to subdivide the existing parcel into 19
single family residential lots A road approximately 500 feet
long will be constructed to gain access to the property Cuts on

SITE CONDITIONS
Surface
The proposed development site is located south of Ahuimanu Road
Kahaluu Stream follows the easterly boundary of the property then
traverses across the site through Lot 10 just below the retention
basin site The slopes adjacent to the stream are fairly steep
ranging from approximately 20 to 50 percent in grade Basalt
boulders are found along the stream bottom Away from the stream
the remainder of the property has slopes of approximately 10 to
15 percent The topography ranges from Elevation 190 on the
northeastern corner to Elevation 290 at Lot 9 The vegetation
cover along the stream gorge is extremely heavy the more level
portions are covered with a heavy growth of grass Very wet
ground conditions were found in the vicinity of Lots 3 4 and
5 a low and generally level area A cut slope approximately
12 feet in height standing nearly vertical is found adjacent
to Ahuimanu Road near Lot 1 At the retention basin area the
slopes are averaging 20 to 25 grade with a heavy growth of trees
and undergrowth

Subsurface
To explore the subsurface conditions at the site 6 borings were
drilled at the locations shown on the Site Plan and Boring Location
Plate 1 A more detailed description of the field investigation
and laboratory testing is contained in the Appendix which also
includes a log of each boring and the results of our laboratory
tests
Geologically the site is located on the northeast side of the
elongated Koolau Volcanic Range which forms the southeast portion
of Oahu After the formation of the Koolau Volcanic Range the
volcanic flows were deeply dissected by stream erosion Due partly
to high rainfall chemical weathering has altered the basalt rock
to residual laterized soil deposits
Our borings revealed that the site is immediately underlain by a
clayey silt that was encountered to the depths explored by our
borings This clayey silt is in a medium stiff to stiff condition
and contains numerous basalt fragments This residual soil is
found throughout the site except in the stream channel where some
alluvium was found
Groundwater was encountered in Boring 1 at an elevation of approx
imately 230 feet MSL This may be a localized perched water table
because no groundwater was found in Boring 6 which is at a lower
elevation

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the site is suitable for the construction
of the roadway and proposed residences It is our opinion that
no stability problems will be encountered if the residences are
constructed within the building setback lines provided this
setback line is beyond the peak flow of the stream The site
soils are slightly expansive and certain precautions should be
taken if slab on grade construction is used
Footings should be founded a minimum of 12 inches below final
adjacent grade A bearing value of 2500 pounds per square foot
should be used for foundations bottomed in natural soils This
value may be increased by one third when considering wind and
seismic loads A minimum horizontal setback distance of 4 feet
should be maintained from the outer edge of footings measured
horizontally to the slope face
Slabs on Grade
The site soils are slightly expansive It is therefore recommended
that slabs on grade be placed on a minimum of 4 inches of a granular
type material In areas where dampness in the floor would be un
desirable or where moisture susceptible floor coverings are to be

Site Preparation
Within the grading limits the site shall be cleared of all déle
terious and organic matter and removed from the site In areas
to receive fill the ground should be scarified to a minimum depth
of 6 inches The scarified surface shall then be brought to near
optimum water content and compacted to a minimum density of 90
of maximum dry density as determined by Fill shall
then be placed in a horizontal 6 inch maximum lifts and compacted
to a minimum of 90 of maximum density Where the natural ground
slope is 5 horizontal to 1 vertical or steeper the fill should be
keyed and benched into firm soils The benching and keying will
be an extremely critical operation for the retention basis This
should be done under the direct observation of the soils engineer
to insure stability of the basin on the relatively steep sloping
site A minimum 10 foot wide key should be constructed in the
natural slope extended to firm natural ground
In Lots 3 4 and 5 the ground surface was very wet during our
field investigation However during the dry season this may
not present a problem for grading operations Surface drainage
should be designed to prevent ponding of water in this area

APPENDIX
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
6 borings utilizing truck mounted for 5 borings and portable
rotary auger equipment for Boring 6 The depths of the borings
ranged from 10 to 20 5 feet below the existing ground surface
The locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan in the
text of this report The soils were classified by visual and
textural examinations in the field by our geologist who continu
ously supervised the drilling operations The classifications
were verified by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory
All soils were classified in accordance with the unified Soil
Classification System A graphical representation of the soils
encountered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1A through
A 1F Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch
I D diameter or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound
hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to
drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Log of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths

LABORATORY TESTING
To provide data for evaluating the strength characteristics of the
encountered soils Direct Shear Tests were performed on two
selected samples The results of these tests are shown on Plate
A 2 Direct Shear Data
In place moisture and density determinations were made on all
obtained samples Atterberg limits were performed to aid in
the classifications of the site soils The results of these
tests are presented on the Log of Borings at the appropriate
sample location
To test the expansiveness of the site soils an expansion test
was performed on the sample with a surcharge of 60 pounds per
square foot The results of this test showed an expansion of
2 4 which may be classified as slightly expansive
