Geolabs conducted a geotechnical engineering exploration for the HRT Medical
Office Building project and our recommendations are presented in the referenced report
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering observation during construction was to
provide engineering consultation and assist in problem resolutions during the construction
of the drilled shaft foundation In addition we provided Special Inspection services by
observing the drilled shaft foundation installation at the Hale Pawa a HRT Medical Office
Building project for general conformance with the project plans and specifications
Project Considerations
The HRT Medical Office Building project site is at the southeast corner of the
intersection of South Beretania and Keeaumoku Streets in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The medical office building project

Subsurface Conditions
Based on the information generated from our field explorations the project site
generally is blanketed by a surface fill layer of very stiff silty clay soils extending to depths
of about 3 to 5 feet below the existing ground surface Below the surface fill layer volcanic
ash deposits and weathered volcanic tuff locally known as mudrock consisting of very
stiff to hard silty clays and clayey silts were encountered to depths ranging from about
15 to 45 feet below the existing ground surface In the northern portion Zone A of the
project site alluvial deposits consisting of very stiff clayey silts inter bedded with medium
dense to dense cinder sands then were encountered to depths of about 90 to 110 feet
below the ground surface The alluvial deposits were underlain by hard basalt rock
formation extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately 130 feet below the
existing ground surface
The thick alluvial and cinder sand deposits were generally absent in the southern
portion Zone B of the project site Below the volcanic ash deposits and weathered
volcanic tuff our borings in this area generally encountered coral formation and or
coralline detritus between depths of about 25 and 70 feet below the existing ground
surface The coral formation and or coralline detritus is underlain by hard basalt rock
formation extending to depths of over 100 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at depths varying from approximately
17 7 to 19 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of our field explorations

Summary of the Drilled Shaft Installation
As part of the project requirements one sacrificial load test shaft was constructed
and tested Following completion of the load test the drilled shaft lengths for the project
were confirmed based on the results of the instrumented load test The following
subsections provide descriptions of the load test shaft construction and discussions on the
production drilled shaft installation
Load Test Shaft
As part of the project specifications one sacrificial 42 inch diameter load test shaft
was installed at the approximate location indicated on the Foundation Plan Plate 2 The
final location was relocated several times from its previously proposed locations due to
significant cave ins of the drilled holes The load test shaft was excavated by the drilled
shaft contractor on July 14 and 15 2008 The 42 inch diameter load test shaft was
extended to a depth of about 89 5 feet below the existing ground surface The drilled shaft
contractor lowered the reinforcing cage with an Osterberg Load Cell and embedment
strain gage instrumentation into the drilled hole and placed concrete in the load test shaft
on
Temporary casing with a diameter of about 46 inches also was installed to about
71 6 feet below the ground surface for the 42 inch diameter load test shaft A copy of the
drilled shaft record for the load test shaft construction is presented in Appendix A
After the load test shaft was installed and cured for at least 7 days Load Test Inc
performed the load test The 42 inch diameter load test shaft was loaded to approximately
4 145 kips Complete details of the load test program are presented in the report prepared
by Load Test Inc

Production Drilled Shafts
The drilled shaft contractor installed 44 production shafts from July 24 through
The as built diameters and the lengths of the production drilled
shafts installed for the project are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 10
Representatives from Geolabs observed the drilled shaft excavation placement of
reinforcing cage and concrete operations during the installation of the production drilled
shafts
In general the production drilled shafts were excavated using a Bauer 28 drill rig
equipped with 30 and 42 inch diameter auger core barrel and clean out bucket similar to
the load test shafts Temporary steel casings were installed to depths ranging from about
35 to 84 feet below the ground surface in the production drilled shafts In general 36 and
46 inch diameter temporary casings were used for 30 and 42 inch diameter drilled shafts
respectively Because the drilled holes stayed open without significant caving in of the
sidewalls polymer slurry was not used during excavation of some of the production drilled
shafts After excavation of the production shafts to their designated tip elevations the
reinforcing cages were lowered into the drilled holes to the appropriate elevations with the
use of a crane After the reinforcing cage was installed in the drilled hole concrete was

placed through a 4 inch steel tremie pipe starting from the bottom During concrete
placement we observed that at least 5 feet of concrete head was maintained above the
bottom of the tremie pipe In addition sand content measurements were performed on an
as needed basis prior to the concrete placement Where the measurements indicated that
the sand content exceeded one percent Insta Floc slurry flocculent was used to remove
the fine grained solids that were suspended in the slurry
Due to the presence of groundwater within the drilled shaft holes concrete was
placed in the drilled holes using the tremie method Concrete with slump ranging from 7 to
9 inches was used for the drilled shafts Mid Pacific Testing Services Inc also monitored
the concrete used for the drilled shafts including the performance of slump tests and
compression tests Logs of the individual drilled shafts installed and the materials
encountered in each of the drilled shafts during shaft excavation are presented in
Appendix A
During drilling for Shaft and 24 hydrocarbon layer was
encountered in the subsurface As a result the drilled shaft lengths for these shafts were
increased by an additional amount of 3 to 5 feet
In addition the rebar cage or tremie concrete inside some of the drilled shafts have
settled significantly after the temporary casing was removed As a result these drilled
shafts would need to be built up to the desired elevations
Based on the observations of our field representatives it is our professional opinion
that the production drilled shafts supporting the Hale Pawa a HRT Medical Office
Building project were installed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans
and specifications and the geotechnical recommendations presented in our geotechnical
engineering report for the project dated and subsequent
recommendations In addition it is our professional opinion that the drilled shafts have
been installed to sufficient depths to provide the allowable design load capacities
