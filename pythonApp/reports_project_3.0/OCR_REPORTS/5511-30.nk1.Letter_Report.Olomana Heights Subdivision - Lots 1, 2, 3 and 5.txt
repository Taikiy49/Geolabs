subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located along Old Kalanianaole Highway, on the southern side 
of Kalanianaole Highway, in the Kailua/Waimanalo area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. 
The project site is bounded by Waimanalo Ridge towards the south and Mount Olomana 
towards the west. We understand that the project development was divided into 
24 residential, agricultural lots ranging in size from two to eight acres. The roadway site 
grading and construction was performed from 2013 to 2016 and observed by Geolabs. 
We understand that the project site encompasses Lot Nos. 1, 2, 3 and 5. The 
remaining lots and portions of Lot Nos. 2, 3 and 5 for the agricultural subdivision were 
previously designed and observed during mass grading by others. A rockfall impact 
barrier was installed on the western boundaries of Lot Nos. 1 and 2.

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS 
The project site is located at Lot Nos. 1, 2, 3 and 5 at the southwestern cul-de-sac 
of the Olomana Heights subdivision in Waimanalo on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The 
project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. The 
site layout and lot boundaries are presented on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
A visual site reconnaissance was conducted by two of our engineers on 
January 26, 2023. The purpose of our field reconnaissance was to observe the existing 
grading conditions within Lot Nos. 1, 2, 3 and 5 to note any inconsistencies with the 
original mass grading plans. On-the-ground pictures and observations were collected. In 
addition, a drone was used to obtain overall aerial photographs and to observe harder to 
reach areas. Site reconnaissance photographs depicting the observed conditions at the 
project site are presented on Plates 3.1 through 3.8.

Our site reconnaissance began at the eastern end of Lot No. 2. The original mass 
grading plans show that two adjacent driveways were to be constructed near Driveway D. 
We observed that the northern driveway in Lot No. 3 was constructed. However, the 
southern driveway in Lot No. 2 was not constructed (Photograph No. 14, Plate 3.7). This 
area was observed to be graded flat with short land grass on the existing ground surface. 
The graded, benched slopes on the eastern side of Lot No. 2 generally appeared 
to agree with the original mass grading plans. The slopes and benches exposed 
near-surface reddish brown silty soil. A steel probe was used to gauge the stiffness of the 
material. Probe penetrations of about 7 to 8 inches was observed, which is generally 
consistent with soft to medium stiff cohesive material. Deteriorating erosion control 
matting (ECM) was observed on the uppermost slope. Photographs depicting the existing 
site conditions in this area are presented in Photograph Nos. 1 through 3, Plates 3.1 and 
3.2. 
The slopes and benches continued into Lot No. 5 to the east. The slopes and 
benches were generally lightly vegetated with maintained land grass. Steel probe 
penetrations of the near-surface material were measured at about 2 inches which is 
generally consistent with stiff cohesive material. Note that numerous “potholes” were 
observed on the slope surfaces in a regular pattern. The potholes do not appear to be 
caused by erosion and may have previously contained landscape plants. At the top of the 
slope (southern end of Lot No. 5), a relatively flat graded pad was observed. Steel probe 
penetrations in this area were also about 2 inches deep. A stockpile of boulders from the 
mass grading operations was observed on the western side of the pad. The graded 
slopes, benches, and pad generally appeared to agree with the original mass grading 
plans. Photographs depicting the existing site conditions at Lot No. 5 are presented in 
Photograph Nos. 4 through 9, Plates 3.2 through 3.5. 
Based on the original mass grading plans, grading operations barely extended into 
Lot Nos. 1 and 3. The western end of Driveway D extends into Lot No. 1, and the top of 
a small cut slope extends into Lot No. 3. Based on our field observations, these small 
areas generally appeared to agree with the original mass grading plans. An overall view 
that includes these two areas is presented in Photograph No. 14, Plate 3.7.  
The previously mentioned areas encompass all the areas in Lots Nos. 1, 2, 3 and 
5 that were affected by the original mass grading plans. However, we noticed there were 
graded areas on Lot Nos. 1 and 2 that were not shown on the original mass grading plans. 
Photographs depicting the existing conditions of these graded areas are presented in 
Photograph Nos. 10 through 16, Plates 3.5 through 3.8. 
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
Based on our field observations and the information provided, it appears that the 
existing conditions of the areas previously affected by the mass grading operations in 
Lot Nos. 1, 2, 3 and 5 generally agree with the original mass grading plans. The only 
inconsistency observed was that the southern driveway in Lot No. 2 adjacent to

Driveway D was not constructed. In addition, soft to medium stiff soils were encountered 
in the previously mass graded area in Lot No. 2. 
We recommend that the geotechnical engineer responsible for the mass grading 
of the lots be retained to review and evaluate the current conditions of the previously 
mass graded areas and provide remedial recommendations if needed.  
The undocumented, graded areas in Lot Nos. 1 and 2 were outside the scope of 
work of this letter report. However, we recommend that an evaluation of the cut and/or fill 
in these areas be evaluated. The graded areas should be compared to previous 
topographic survey maps to determine the heights and extents of the cut and/or fill. In 
addition, the compaction of the fill materials should be evaluated. Based on previous 
conversations, we understand that slope stability concerns are present in these areas. 
We recommend that a field exploration program and engineering analyses be performed 
to address these areas of concern.
