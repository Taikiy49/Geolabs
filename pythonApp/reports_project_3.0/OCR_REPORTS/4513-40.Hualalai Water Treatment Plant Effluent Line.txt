SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering reconnaissance
performed for the proposed Water Treatment Plant Effluent Line project for the Hualalai
Resort located in the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii The general location
and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field reconnaissance and presents our
geotechnical recommendations derived from our analyses for the proposed Water
Treatment Plant Effluent Line project only These recommendations are intended for the
design of site grading and thrust blocks only The findings and recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The Hualalai Resort project site is located about 8 5 miles northeast of the Keahole
International Airport in the Kaupulehu area within the District of North Kona on the Island
of Hawaii As part of the Phase Il project of the Hualalai Resort we understand that
various infrastructure development and additional residential parcels will be constructed
This report covers only the geotechnical aspects of the design for the Water Treatment
Plant Effluent Line project The water treatment plant and the new water treatment plant
effluent line are located on the mauka side of Queen Kaahumanu Highway
We understand that it is proposed to construct an 8 inch diameter effluent line to
serve the existing Hualalai Resort The new effluent line will start from the new water
treatment plant site to near the existing 2 0 million gallon MG irrigation lake
Approximately one half of the new effluent line alignment will traverse generally
undisturbed land The remaining portion of the new effluent line alignment will run along
the existing unpaved roads in the other areas Where the new effluent line traverses
across undisturbed land a new 20 foot wide access road will be constructed We
understand that the new roadway will be used for temporary access only and surface
pavement or treatment will not be provided

SECTION 2 0 SITE CHARACTERIZATION
2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii the largest in the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five shield
voicanoes which include the following Kohala which is jong extinct Mauna Kea which
has had activity during recent geologic time Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and
Mauna Loa and Kilauea both of which are still active
The project site is situated on the northwestern flank of Hualalai Mountain which
makes up the west central portion of the island The lava formation encountered at the
site appears to be of both a a and pahoehoe type flows which spread and ponded as they
approached the ocean Aja lava is typically characterized by a porous rough and
irregular flow surface resembling a jagged accumulation of rock fragments including
cobbles and boulders Pahoehoe is characterized by a smooth rope like or billowy
surface and an internal structure of vesicular porous rock
Cavities are commonly encountered in basaltic lavas especially pahoehoe type
lava flows These cavities were formed when the lava was still in a molten state and they
represent both lava tubes intra flow cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow cavities
Lava tubes are formed when molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow
tube like structure which may extend for a large distance longitudinally along the flow
Blisters and pockets inter flow cavities are generally smaller in horizontal extent
Basalt is considered to be a relatively permeable rock and can transmit water quite
readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions In general water is transmitted
through the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities and
along clinker layers The permeability of the clinker and cavities is high therefore they
serve as the primary water transmission features

2 2 Surface and Anticipated Subsurface Conditions
The project site is located toward the southeast of the Hualalai Resort in the
Kaupulehu area of the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii The proposed
Hualalai Water Treatment Plant Effluent Line project site is located on the mauka side of
Queen Kaahumanu Highway as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The new
effluent line will start from the new water treatment plant site and extend to near the
existing irrigation lake as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The terrain at the project site is generally rugged reflecting the original surface of
the thick basaltic lava flows of the a a and pahoehoe form Based on the topographic map
provided the project site is gently sloping downward from south to north The majority of
the project site is underlain by a a and pahoehoe lavas at the ground surface An existing
Jeep trail heads mauka upslope from the irrigation lake The existing Jeep trail is
roughly leveled to the contour of the natural ground The majority of the surface materials
adjacent to the existing Jeep trail consist of a a lava The portion of the project site that
traverses along the conservation district CDUA boundary the surface materials generally
consist of pahoehoe basalt Vegetation at the project site is generally sparse and consists
of some wild grasses growing through the crevices of the basaltic lava flows
It should be noted that numerous collapsed lava tube features and cavities were
observed along the proposed effluent line alignment Based on our experience in the
vicinity of the project site lava tubes and cavities are commonly encountered in this area
Therefore the presence of lava tubes and cavities should be anticipated along the
proposed effluent line alignment In addition groundwater is not anticipated within the
depths of the new effluent line inverts However groundwater levels are expected to
change due to seasonal precipitation surface water runoff and other factors

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site to reduce the
potential for contamination of the excavated materials to be used as embankment
fill materials
Following clearing and grubbing or excavation to the roadway subgrades the
ground surface should be proof rolled with a minimum 15 ton vibratory drum roller
for a minimum of six passes to assist in detecting and possibly collapsing
near surface cavities We wish to point out that caution should be exercised by the
contractor while clearing and grading near the collapsed features which may
represent potential lava tubes or cavities that may further collapse under the load of
heavy equipment The vibratory drum roller used for the proof rolling operations
should be operated at a speed of about 300 feet per minute
As mentioned previously the new effluent line will be installed within the new
roadway corridor Therefore the proof rolling operations should be conducted prior

The proof rolling operations should be performed in the presence of a

Fills and Backfills
In general we recommend that structural fill materials be used to fill the areas from
the existing ground surface to achieve the design subgrades Structural fill
materials should consist of well graded granular materials of 6 inches in largest
dimension with sufficient fines to prevent the occurrence of voids in the compacted
mass The excavated on site materials may be re used as a source of structural fill
materials provided that the materials are processed to meet the above gradation
requirements
If the excavated on site materials do not contain sufficient fines to produce a
fragments or boulders should be considered to provide the required gradation and
particle sizes to develop a well graded material Imported material if necessary
should be observed and or tested by Geolabs for its suitability prior to being
transported to the site for the intended use
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Structural fill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in
loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers
to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry

density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density Compaction should be accomplished
by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers or other types of acceptable compaction
equipment
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practical in fills composed of
rocks boulders and or cobbles Instead a testing program to evaluate the number
of passes by a compactor needed to achieve the desired level of compaction
should be conducted at the start of the grading phase of the project under the
observation of a representative from Geolabs Based on this testing program the
number of passes may then be used as the field criterion for adequate compaction
For preliminary estimating purposes compaction may be accomplished by using a
large vibratory drum roller having a static weight of at least 15 tons traveling at a
speed of about 300 feet per minute The roller should pass over the fill surface at
least six times for structural fill
Excavation
Based on our reconnaissance the project site is generally underlain by clinker a a
lava flow materials and fractured pahoehoe lavas ranging from medium hard to
hard It is anticipated that the near surface materials may be readily excavated
with normal heavy excavation equipment such as excavators and ripping with
large bulldozers However some of the deeper excavations into the dense
pahoehoe formations may require the use of hoe rams chipping or blasting
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are based
on our visual observation of the existing basalt formation and experience with
similar type conditions on the site We recommend that contractors be encouraged
to examine the site conditions to make their own interpretation

Cutand Fill Slopes
Based on our reconnaissance it appears that the cut slopes along the new
roadway alignment would generally expose basalt formation In general we
believe that the cut slopes may be designed with a slope inclination of one
horizontal to one vertical 1 H 1V or flatter In areas where highly fractured basalt
and or loose clinker layers are encountered the cut slopes should be flattened to
2H 1V or flatter Large cavities that may be exposed on the cut slope face should
be backfilled and or grouted if necessary A representative from Geolabs should
observe the cut slopes during construction to evaluate whether the exposed cut
slope materials are consistent with our assumptions
Permanent fill slopes constructed of the structural fill or general fill materials
recommended herein may be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter
Fills that are to be placed on existing ground steeper than 5H 1V should be
benched The fill slope face should be free of large boulders greater than
18 inches in largest dimension and should also be free of voids which would
allow erosion and migration of fines to occur In addition slope planting or other
means of slope protection should be provided as soon as possible to reduce the
potential for significant erosion of the finished slopes

3 3 Thrust Blocks
We envision that thrust blocks will be required at the bends of the new effluent line
The following recommendations may be used for the preliminary design of thrust blocks
Lateral loads acting on the thrust blocks may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the thrust block and the bearing material and by
passive earth pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the thrust block A
coefficient of friction of 0 45 may be used for thrust block bearing on the basalt formation
or new structural fill materials Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be
estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 450 pounds per square foot per cubic foot
of depth pcf This assumes that the soils around the thrust blocks are well compacted
Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of
soil rock should be neglected
3 4 Effluent Lines
In general a new 8 inch effluent line will be installed along the roadway corridor It
is anticipated that most of the trenches for the effluent lines will be excavated in the
medium hard to hard basalt rock and clinker materials observed at the site In general
granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation is recommended for support below the pipes
Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation should be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the

pipes It is critical to use this free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of
voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the
pipes Improper backfill material around the pipes and improper placement of the backfill
could result in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
As an alternative the granular bedding and the initial trench backfill up to about
12 inches above the top of the pipes may consist of 1 inch minus well graded granular
materials aggregate base course The granular bedding and the initial trench backfill
should be moisture conditioned and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of well graded granular materials
less than 6 inches in maximum size The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above
the optimum moisture placed in about 12 inch loose lifts and mechanically compacted to
at least 90 percent relative compaction
