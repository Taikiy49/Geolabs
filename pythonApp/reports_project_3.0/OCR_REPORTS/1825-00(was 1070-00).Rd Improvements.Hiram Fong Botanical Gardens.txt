This report summarizes the findings of our soil investigation
and presents our recommendations for the design of the above
referenced project
The soil investigation consisted of drilling ten borings
excavating seven test pits along the proposed road alignment
and parking area location visual observations at the site
and laboratory testing of selected surface and subsurface
soil samples
PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
The proposed plan is to construct a 20 foot wide roadway
along the alignment of the existing Pulama Road in order to
provide access to the Hiram Fong Botanical Gardens This
W
P 2
roadway will contour along the ridge above Kaalaea Stream and
will involve approximately 2130 lineal feet of pavement To
widen the existing road deep cuts or walls in excess of 20
feet in some areas may be required Fills are expected to
be minimal
In addition to the above referenced roadway it is also pro
posed to construct a parking area and comfort station at the
botanical gardens This construction will involve expansion
of an existing level area in the botanical gardens with cuts
of up to 15 feet deep Some shallow fill placement is antici
pated in this area also
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the southern slope of the ridge
separating Kaalaea and Waiahole Valleys The existing Pulama
Road generally contours along this ridge above Kaalaea Stream
The existing road was cut into the ridge leaving steep slopes
above and below the road The road was originally paved how
ever the asphalt surface has broken down and only some patches
of pavement remain
The proposed parking area is a generally level manicured grassy
area which was previously graded Adjacent to this level area
are ridges of residual soil which will be cut into to expand
the level area for use as a parking area

The areas surrounding the existing road and the proposed park
ing area are densely vegetated with semi tropical trees shrubs
and grasses

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by six test borings
and seven test pits along the alignment of the proposed road
way improvement and four test borings in the proposed park
ing area
Two percolation tests were performed in the proposed parking
area in order to provide data for possible effluent disposal
from a proposed comfort station

the logs of borings are included in Appendix A A descrip

The subsurface explorations disclosed that the road alignment
is underlain by residual soils weathered in place from basic
igneous rocks Some areas are thinly mantled by transported
soils such as alluvium and colluvium The soils generaily
consist of medium plasticity clayey silts
Basaltic rock was encountered in some areas however these
appear to be predominantly isolated weathering resistant dikes
rather than continuous strata of bedrock
Exploration in the proposed parking area revealed that this
site is underlain by stiff alluvial silts with gravel
No groundwater was encountered in any of the drilled borings
or test pits at the time of our field exploration

the proposed road and parking area construction provided our
recommendations are closely followed

appurtenances

Site Grading
Prior to the commencement of grading surface vegetation and
organic material should be removed and wasted off site
Along the alignment of the existing Pulama Road the surface
should be stripped of old asphaltic concrete and other dele
terious materials
The existing ground surface should be compacted to 90 of its
maximum density as established by ASTM D 1557
Any loose or soft soils encountered in the grading area should
be undercut to firm natural soil prior to fill placement
Boulders encountered in excavation and grading should be
wasted off site
Selected on site material may be used for fill below the road
prism and in the proposed parking area All fills should be
compacted to 90 maximum density as determined by ASTM D 1557
The fill material should be placed in horizontal 10 inch loose
lifts and compacted within 4 of optimum moisture
Slopes
