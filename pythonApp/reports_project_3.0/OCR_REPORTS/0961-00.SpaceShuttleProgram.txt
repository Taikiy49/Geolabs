SUBSURFACE EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 6 borings at the site utilizing a truck mounted
Mobile B 34 drilling equipment The borings were drilled
to depths of 21 0 to 28 5 feet
The soils were classified by visual and textural examinations
in the field by our engineer who continuously monitored the
drilling operations The classifications were reviewed by
visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in accordance with the Unified Soil Classi
fication System A graphic presentation of the soils
encountered in the borings is presented on the Boring Logs
Plates A 1l through A 12
Soil samples were obtained by driving a standard penetration
sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of
30 inches ASTM D 1586 The blow counts to drive the sampler
0 5 feet are shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate
sample depth Undisturbed inches Shelly tube samples
were taken where possible by hydraulic push with the maximum
push pressure recorded at the appropriate sample depth ASTM
D 1587 Core samples of the rock were obtained by using a
diamond bit core barrel with the core recovery and RQD
recorded for each run ASTM D 2113

LABORATORY TESTING
Eight sieve analysis were performed on representative silty
sand soils and broken up volcanic tuff samples encountered
The test methods used were that of ASTM C 136 and C 117
combined The results of these tests are presented on
Tables 1 and 2 Summary of Laboratory Test Results
Three remolded direct shear tests were performed on selec
ted samples of the silty sand and coral soils The test
results are plotted on Plates B 3 B 4 and B 5 The test
method used is that of ASTM D 3080
Eight hardness tests Scratch Hardness of Coarse Aggregate
Particles ASTM D 851 were performed on selected samples
of the coral and volcanic tuff The test results are pre
sented on Plate B 6
