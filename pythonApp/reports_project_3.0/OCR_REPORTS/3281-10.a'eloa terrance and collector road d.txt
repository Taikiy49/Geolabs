SITE GRADING
The A eloa Terrace and Collector Road D is part of the Kapolei Village 2
development in the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The project site has
been previously mass graded as part of the Villages of Kapolei mass grading work by
Oahu Construction with our firm providing the grading and compaction monitoring
services A general layout of the project site is provided on the Site Plan Plate 1
The fine grading operations roadway and utility construction for A eloa Terrace
and Collector Road D under the current scope of work were performed by Royal
Contracting Company The site grading consisted of clearing and grubbing placement
and compaction of engineered fills and utility trench excavation and backfill

Following the clearing and grubbing operations the exposed subgrades were
scarified to a depth of about 10 inches moisture conditioned to between 2 and
5 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to about 85 to 90
percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with American
Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for
testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established
in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified
Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
During the fill and backfill operations the fill and backfill materials were generally
placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor The upper 12 inches below the
building pad subgrades generally consist of non expansive materials compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction

Periodic field density tests were taken during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil
conditions were noted A total of 292 field density tests including retests of failed
areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 2922 The results of the compaction tests taken are summarized on
Plates 3 1 through 3 24 of this report These tests were taken on the prepared ground
surfaces and on constructed engineered fills and backfills at the site Test locations
and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until
the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field
representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the mass grading
and roadway and utility construction for the above referenced project observed and
tested by Geolabs Hawaii in the period covered by this report were performed in
general accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the above referenced report
dated and subsequent addendum and recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously ona construction site Therefore some of the earthwork were
performed without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotech
nical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not
insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain
responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications
and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
