EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
The site grading and utility line installation work for the Maui Research and 
Technology Park, Phase 1, Increment 1 project was observed by Geolabs, Inc. from 
July 27, 2021, through January 18, 2022. The site grading and utility line installation 
consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills and backfills 
to achieve the design finished grades for the project. Goodfellow Bros., Inc. performed 
the site grading and utility line installation work. During the site grading and backfilling 
operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill 
materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing. The maximum dry 
densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general 
accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results for the materials 
used at the project site are summarized in the attached Plate 3. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to near the optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 or 95 percent of the maximum dry 
density determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. During the site grading and 
backfilling operations, periodic field density tests were performed in general accordance 
with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). Results of the compaction tests performed 
at the project site are summarized in the attached Plates 4.1 through 4.6. These tests 
were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and constructed fills and backfills at 
the site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from 
on-site survey stakes. These locations and elevations should be considered accurate 
only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were 
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and/or approved by our 
field representative based on visual observation and hand probing. 
Select granular fills with a maximum aggregate dimension of 3 inches were used 
as backfill at the project site. Some of the 3-inch minus material placed at the project 
site consisted of greater than 30 percent rock larger than ¾-inch in size, which is 
outside of the parameters of ASTM D1557. Conventional compaction testing generally 
is not practical in the select granular fills that meet this criterion; therefore, the number 
of passes by a specific compactor needed to achieve the desired level of compaction 
was performed. Hand probe and visual observations were used during compaction of 
the select granular fills in this category to confirm a firm and unyielding compacted 
surface.

grading operations. Based on the observations of our field representatives and the

and backfill placement for the Maui Research and Technology Park, Phase 1, Increment
