and rock conditions below the above referenced lots The
purpose of the study was to evaluate the Suitability of the

proposed residential structures
PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
The present plan is to construct an architect designed house
with swimming pool on each of the subject lots along with
the necessary driveways and parking areas Access will be
from Puu Ikena Place
Minor site grading is anticipated

SITE DESCRIPTION
The subject lots consist of three adjacent lots fronting the
south side of Puu Ikena Place The lots slope and drain
towards the south
At the time of our field exploration the lots were sparsely
vegetated by Haole Koa Kiawe and grasses There are piles
of loose boulders and areas of thin fill left from previous
grading and sewer line construction In addition there are
numerous outcrops of basaltic bedrock The ground surface
elevations vary from approximately 295 to 245 feet
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The test pits encountered basaltic bedrock at very shallow
depths Overlying this bedrock is a thin layer of native
soil or old fill from previous grading Both soils consist
of a dark brown clay with cobbles and boulders This clay
soil is highly expansive
The basaltic bedrock consisted primarily of weathered Pahoe
hoe lava flows
Detailed descriptions of the soils and rock encountered in
the test pits are presented in Appendix A Plates A 1 and
A 2 The approximate locations of the test pits are shown
on the attached Test Pit Location Plan Plate 1

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration and reconnaissance disclosed basically
competent bearing materials on the subject lots From a
soils engineering point of view the subject lots are suit
able for the proposed development
Shallow foundations can be used for the proposed development
The near surface soils have high swell potential and should
be wasted off site
Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundations are recommended for
the structures If slab on grade construction is desired
the slab should bear entirely on rock Alternately contin
uous stem wall footings backfilled with non expansive imported
material underlying a separate slab could be used
This backfill should be placed in 8 inch horizontal lifts
and compacted to 90 of its maximum density as determined by
test method ASTM D 1557

Slabs should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of gravel
size In order to protect against moisture infiltration
a polyethylene moisture barrier should be incorporated below
the slab
The spread or continuous footings should have a minimum
embedment of 12 inches into weathered basalt to reduce slid
ing potential Footings located close to the edge of slopes
should be embedded a sufficient depth to obtain a minimum of
4 foot set back from the slope face
The on site expansive soil pockets should be stripped and
removed prior to fill placement This expansive clay mater
ial should be wasted off site
For the light loadings anticipated an allowable bearing
value of 4000 p s f is recommended for footings bearing on
in situ weathered rock For slabs on compacted fill a bear
ing value of 2000 p s f may be used The above values are
for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by one
third when temporary seismic and wind loads are considered
A minimum footing width of 12 inches should be utilized
Surface water should be diverted away from the building
foundations

Site Grading
Fills up to 8 feet thick are anticipated for the driveways
and parking areas
Prior to fill placement the area should be cleared of vege
tation and the expansive soils stripped removed and wasted
off site
Fill and backfill materials should consist of non expansive
select borrow and they should be placed in 8 inch horizontal
loose lifts and compacted to 90 maximum density as determined
by test method ASTM D 1557
Any fills placed on slopes steeper than 9 1 H V should be
keyed and benched into the existing ground
Fill slopes of 2 1 H V are recommended
Design Review
The plans for foundations site grading and utility construc
tion should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included in the project plans and specifi
cations
