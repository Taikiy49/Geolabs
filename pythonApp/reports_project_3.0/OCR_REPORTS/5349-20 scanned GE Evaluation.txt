Geotechnical Engineering Evaluation
Excavation at New Ward Village Shops
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
Dear Mr Lee
As requested we evaluated the soil conditions of the exposed excavations at the
New Ward Village Shops project site located to the northeast of the intersection of
Auahi and Kamakee Streets in the Kaka ako area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii This letter summarizes our findings and presents our recommendations for soil
types as it pertains to the shoring requirements of the planned excavations
A site visit was conducted on May 1 2006 to evaluate the exposed soil conditions
at some of the column footing and tower crane foundation excavations At the time of our
site visit the column footing excavations measured at depths of approximately 5 to 6 feet
below the existing ground surface with near vertical cut faces The tower crane foundation
excavation measured at a depth of approximately 15 feet below the existing ground
surface with multiple benches of near vertical cut faces each bench is approximately 4 to
5 feet in vertical height
Based on our field observations the upper 5 to 6 feet of the exposed faces of the
excavations generally consisted of medium dense sands and gravel and stiff clayey silts
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed at selected locations around the excavations to
provide an indication of the unconfined compressive strength of the upper cohesive fill
soils Based on the field test results the upper fill soils appear to exhibit very high
unconfined compressive strengths of over 4 tons per square foot tsf or 8 000 pounds per
square foot psf
Below the upper fill soils lagoona deposits consisting of loose clayey gravel were
generally encountered and extended to the bottom of the excavation of about 15 feet
below the existing ground surface Pocket penetrometer tests could not be performed on
the lagoonal deposits due to the soft and or loose consistency of the subsoils exposed at
the sides of the excavations

Based on our field observations the estimated unconfined compressive strength of
the soils the available soil boring information for the project and our engineering
analyses we believe that the upper 6 feet of subsoils may be considered as Type A Soil
while the lagoonal deposits encountered below the upper 6 feet of subsoils may be
classified as Type C Soil in accordance with the guidelines provided under
Title 12 Department of Labor and Industrial Relations Subtitle 8 Division of Occupational
Safety and Health Chapter 132 Excavations Trenching and Shoring
It should be noted that the recommendations submitted in this letter are based in
part upon information obtained from a brief site visit Variations of the subsurface
conditions between and beyond the areas of field observations may occur and the nature
and extent of these variations may not become evident until further excavations are
underway and exposed If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the recommendations presented in this letter This letter has been prepared
for the exclusive use of Jayar Construction Inc only for specific application to the New
Ward Village Shops project as described herein in accordance with generally accepted
geotechnical engineering principles and practices No warranty is expressed or implied
