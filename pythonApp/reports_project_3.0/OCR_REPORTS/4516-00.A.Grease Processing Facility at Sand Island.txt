PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at 1003 Makepono Street on Sand Island in the Honolulu
area of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided it is proposed to construct a grease processing
facility at the project site We understand that six grease tanks of approximately 12 feet in
diameter will be constructed at the southern portion of the project site We understand that
the loads imposed by each tank on the foundations will be approximately 250 kips The
finished floor elevation of the tanks is set at approximately 9 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
Based on the information provided the existing ground surface elevation at the project site
is at about 8 feet MSL

and or loose lagoonal deposits The relative density of the undocumented fill is generally

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration was to obtain an overview
of the surface and subsurface conditions at the site The subsurface information obtained
was utilized to develop a soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertaining to the design of foundations pavements and site grading only
To accomplish this we conducted an exploration program consisting of the following tasks
and work efforts
1
Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and two operators
to and from the project site
Drilling and sampling of three borings extending to depths of about 26 5 to
43 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a field
engineer from our firm
Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration as
an aid in classifying the materials encountered and evaluating their
engineering properties
Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of foundations
pavements and site grading for the proposed project
Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
Coordination of our overall work on the project by an engineer from our firm
Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
principal engineer from our firm
Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and Logs of Borings are presented on
Plates 3 through 6 of this letter report The results of the laboratory tests performed on
selected soil samples are presented on Plates 7 and 8 of this report

FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 extending to depths of about 26 5 to 43 5 feet below
the existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped
with continuous flight augers The approximate locations of the borings drilled at the site are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic representations
of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates 4 through 6
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch
OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition
some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as
the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
LABORATORY TESTING
Selected soil samples from the field exploration were tested in the laboratory with an
emphasis on evaluating the moisture density and gradation The following laboratory tests
were performed on selected soil samples obtained from the field exploration
One sieve analysis test was performed on a selected sample of the soils encountered
to evaluate the gradation characteristics and to aid in soil classification The test was
performed in accordance with ASTM C 136 and D 1140 Graphic presentation of the grain
size distribution is provided on Plate 7
One Modified Proctor compaction test was performed on the near surface soils to
determine the dry density and moisture content relationship The test was performed in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Results of the test are presented on Plate 8

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration generally encountered undocumented fills consisting of medium
dense to dense sandy gravel with asphaltic concrete fragments extending to depths of about
3 to 4 feet below the existing ground surface The undocumented fills were underlain by
older fills consisting of medium dense sandy coralline gravel extending to a depth of about
6 feet below the existing ground surface The older fills were underlain by loose lagoonal
deposits consisting of silty coralline sands and gravel extending to the maximum depths
explored of about 41 5 to 43 5 feet below the existing ground surface Detailed descriptions
of the materials encountered from our field exploration are presented on the Logs of Borings
Plates 4 through 6
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at depths ranging from about 6 4 to
8 6 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of our field exploration However
groundwater levels are subject to change due to tidal fluctuations seasonal precipitation
surface water runoff and other factors
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The project site is generally underlain by a surface layer of medium dense to dense
undocumented fills of about 3 to 4 feet thick The undocumented fills are underlain by older
fills medium dense extending to depths of about 6 feet below the existing ground surface
The older fills are underlain by loose lagoonal deposits extending to the maximum depths
explored of about 41 5 to 43 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered we recommend that the six grease
tanks be supported on a shallow mat foundation Because the site is underlain by
undocumented fill it is likely that the relative density of the undocumented fills will vary
across the project site Therefore we recommend that the undocumented fills within the
footprint of the mat foundation be over excavated to a minimum depth of about 36 inches
and be replaced with compacted fills The over excavation should extend a minimum of
24 inches laterally beyond the limits of the mat foundation
In addition the concrete driveway and flexible pavement areas should be
over excavated to a minimum depth of about 18 inches below the aggregate subbase and
aggregate base course respectively The over excavation should extend a minimum of
12 inches laterally beyond the limits of the driveway and pavement areas The resulting
over excavation should be backfilled with compacted fills Detailed discussion of these items
and our geotechnical engineering recommendations for design are presented in the following
sections of this letter report
Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we recommend
that the six grease tanks be supported a shallow mat foundation Based on our

reconnaissance and our borings undocumented fills of about 3 to 4 feet thick were
encountered at the project site We anticipate that the relative density of the undocumented
fills will likely vary across the project site Therefore we recommend that the undocumented
fills be over excavated to a minimum depth of 36 inches below the bottom of the mat
foundation The over excavation should also extend a minimum of 24 inches laterally
beyond the limits of the mat foundation The resulting over excavation should be backfilled
with granular materials compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction up to
about 6 inches below the bottom of the mat foundation The excavated on site soils may be
re used as compacted fill material provided that the materials are properly compacted and
that deleterious materials are removed
In addition we recommend that a 6 inch thick layer of aggregate subbase compacted
to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction be provided below the mat foundation to
provide for uniform support The aggregate subbase should also extend a minimum of about
24 inches laterally beyond the limits of the mat foundation
An allowable bearing pressure of up to about 2 000 pounds per square foot psf may
be used for design of the mat foundations bearing on the compacted aggregate subbase
transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces For the mat foundation
design a modulus of subgrade reaction of 10 pounds per square inch per inch of deflection
pci may be used for the mat foundation bearing on the aggregate subbase
The bottom of the mat foundation should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade The bottom of the mat footing excavation should
be firm and unyielding prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete If soft and or loose
materials are encountered at the bottom of the mat foundation excavation the material s
should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm and or dense materials The
resulting over excavation should be backfilled with the on site soil placed and compacted in
accordance with the recommendations presented in the Site Grading section of this report
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be embedded
below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trench or extended to a depth as deep as the inverts of the utility lines
We estimate the total foundation settlements under the anticipated structural loads
to be on the order of about 2 inches Differential settlements between the center and the
corner of the mat are estimated to be on the order of about 1 inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure acting
against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil A coefficient of friction
of 0 40 may be used between the mat and the aggregate subbase Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds
per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the soils around the mat are well

Concrete Pavements
We understand that concrete pavements are planned for the driveway areas leading
to the grease processing facility Based on the information provided we understand that the
truck loading for this site is equivalent to a truck designation of H20 with some passenger
vehicles We understand that the frequency of truck traffic was given at five trucks per day
for six days a week with a design life of 20 years We have made our preliminary pavement
design assuming the pavement subgrade soils will be similar to the sandy gravel soils
encountered in our borings The following rigid pavement section may be used for
preliminary design and budget estimating purposes
Rigid Pavement
6 0 Inch Portland Cement Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Subbase 95 Percent Relative Compaction
12 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Subgrade
We recommend that the existing soils at the pavement subgrades be over excavated
to a minimum depth of about 18 inches below the bottom of the aggregate subbase The
over excavation should also extend a minimum of 12 inches laterally beyond the limits of the
concrete pavements The resulting over excavation should be backfilled with granular fill
materials compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that concrete walkways be supported on 4 inches aggregate subbase The
aggregate subbase should be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Crack control joints should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkways with
expansion joints at right angle intersections
The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slab should
be backfilled tightly against the edges of the slab with the on site soils and graded to divert
water away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding around the slab edges

Site Grading
In general the area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared at
the on set of earthwork Unsuitable materials within the grading limits should be removed
and disposed of properly off site Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing should
be over excavated to expose firm or dense material and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with well compacted fill The excavated soft and or organic soils should be
properly disposed of off site Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit
prices for over excavation and compacted fill placement to account for variation in the
over excavation quantities
Existing underground utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the resulting
excavation should be properly backfilled with on site fill materials The on site fill materials
should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in 6 to 8 inch
loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Utilities to be
abandoned in place especially under buildings may be backfilled by pumping lean concrete
under low pressure
After clearing the soils at the subgrade areas of the mat foundation should be
over excavated to a depth of about 36 inches The over excavation should also extend a
minimum of 24 inches laterally beyond the limits of the mat foundation In addition the
pavement subgrades concrete and flexible pavements should be over excavated to a depth

of about 18 inches below the pavement section The over excavation should also extend a
minimum of 12 inches laterally beyond the limits of the pavements
The soils encountered at the over excavated and finished subgrade areas should be
scarified to a minimum depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test
procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
Fill and backfill materials up to the foundation or pavement subgrade elevations
should consist of well graded granular materials less than 3 inches in largest dimension In
general we envision that the excavated on site granular materials may be re used as fill or
backfill materials provided that deleterious materials are removed and that they are
processed to a relatively well graded select granular material with a maximum size of about
3 inches Fill and backfill materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in level lifts of 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
If needed imported fill should consist of non expansive select granular fill material
such as crushed coral basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from
coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should contain
between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have
a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less
when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883 Imported fill material should be tested by
Geolabs for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the site for the
intended use
Drainage
The finished grades outside the structures should be sloped to shed water away from
foundations and slabs to reduce the potential for ponding The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the walls or slab edges immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce the potential for significant water infiltration into the subsurface In
addition drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be maintained
to drain surface water runoff away from the foundations and slabs

Construction Monitoring
Considering the variable nature of the subsurface conditions encountered at the site
it is recommended that Geolabs be retained to provide geotechnical engineering services
during construction of the proposed project The items of construction monitoring that are
critical requiring Special Inspection include observation of the over excavation of the
undocumented fills subgrade preparation fill and backfill placement and foundation
construction
Other aspects of earthwork construction should also be observed by a representative
from Geolabs This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may
be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time
this report was prepared The recommendations provided in this report are contingent upon
such observations If the actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate
modifications to the design should be made
