SOIL ENGINEERING REPORT
LOT 132 ROYAL SUMMIT SUBDIVISION PHASE I
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of three shallow drilled borings laboratory testing of the
swelling potential of the soils and a review of the subdivision site
grading and preliminary house plot plan This report summarizes our
findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a one story wooden structure
approximately 48 by 80 feet in plan dimension with a 2 car garage
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the driveway garage and the entire
house area
CRM retaining walls are planned along the western and rear property
lines
The proposed layout of the house and the garage is shown on the Site
Plan Plate 1

SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the southern side of Aloalii Street in
the Royal Summit Subdivision Phase 1 in Aiea on the island of Oahu
Hawaii
The lot has two generally level pads with a transitional slope
measuring about 5 1 H V between them The grade seperation between the
upper pad and lower pad is approximately 4 feet in height The rear
portion of the lot slopes down at a grade of 2 1 H V
SUBSURFACE SOTL CONDITIONS
A review of the mass grading plan for Royal Summit Phase I dated
prepared by Community Planning Inc indicated that
Lot 132 received up to 8 feet of compacted fills over the natural grade
at the rear portion of the lot and cuts up to 12 feet deep at the front
of the site
Three borings were drilled within the proposed building location to
depths ranging from 5 0 to 10 0 feet below the existing grade at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
All three borings encountered hard reddish brown clayey silt to the
full depth of the borings
No groundwater was encountered in the borings at the time of our
field exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The laboratory tests disclosed that the existing near surface soils
generally have low swelling potential however could exhibit moderate
swelling potential if they are allowed to dry out excessively and then
wetted Therefore some special foundation design and subgrade
preparation are recommended in order to provide suitable foundation
support for the proposed residence
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2 500 p s f may be used on the
in situ soil or compacted fill
Individual footings on level areas should be embedded a minimm of
12 inches below the adjacent finished grade Continuous wall footings
should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent finished
grade
If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down an
additional amount to obtain a 5 foot minimum horizontal set back
distance measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope
face This additional embedment depth is required to help resist the
downhill slope creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing

It should be noted that some of the on site clayey silt soils are
moderately expansive Therefore we recommend that the on site soil

Lot Grading
Structural fills and backfills should consist of non expansive soil
such as select borrow or crusher waste and be placed in maximum 8 inch
loose lifts and then compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density established by ASIM D 1557 78 The on site soils in the Royal
Summit area generally have low to moderate swelling potential and would
require special preparation prior to use as structural fill Therefore
the on site soil should only be used for yard fill
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be
cleared of vegetation and debris
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 6
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
The site should be so graded to provide drainage of surface runoff
away from all foundations

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems should be installed
around the house and that gutter water should be diverted far away from
the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to prevent
ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
Our scope of work did not include foundation recommendations for the
proposed future swimming pool We do recommend that the subsurface soils
be investigated in this area before construction

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our subsurface
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
