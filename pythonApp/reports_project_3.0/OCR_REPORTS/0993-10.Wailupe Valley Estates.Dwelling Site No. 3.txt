PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to
explore the subsurface soil and groundwater conditions at
the proposed Dwelling Site No 3 and to provide soil data
for use in the design of the site grading for construction
of a portion of the private access roadway and building on
the proposed estate development
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 993 10 P 2
A previous soil engineering investigation report for Dwelling
Sites 1 and 2 was issued by our office in February 1981
Our scope of work was done in accordance with our proposal
dated for SITE C
Our study consisted of seven 7 test pit excavations dis
turbed sampling laboratory testing and site reconnaissance
of the area The results of our field exploration and la
boratory tests which were used in the engineering analysis
and formulated our conclusions and recommendations are
presented in the appendix of this report
The approximate test pit locations are shown on Plate 1
Site Plan and the logs are presented in Appendix A
Detailed information regarding the field exploration and
laboratory testing are presented in the appendices
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed dwelling Site C is the last of the proposed
Wailupe Valley Estates development which includes three 3
estate type residential lots
It is presently planned to construct a dwelling site about
5950 feet up valley from Dwelling Site No 2 The total
distance from Hao Street to the dwelling site is approxi
mately 2250 feet A private access road leading to the
dwelling site will be a continuation of the roadway giving
access to Dwelling Site Nos 1 and 2
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 993 10 P 3
Cuts generally less than 10 feet high would be required
for the roadway construction from Dwelling Site No 2 to
No 3
Cuts up to 20 feet and fills up to 10 feet high are planned
to create a level dwelling site pad at about elevation 425
feet
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated on the western valley side
Slopes in upper Wailupe Valley approximately 4400 feet
up valley from the existing end of Hao Street
Ground elevations at the site range from 410 to 470 feet
with overall surface slopes of about 4 1 H V sloping
towards the southeast Locally flatter areas are present
throughout the site
The proposed access roadway is a continuation of the access
road for Dwelling Site No 2 The proposed alignment runs
along the natural hillside which slopes in a southeast
direction at 4 1 H V ratio
Small drainageways were noted along the access roadway and
within the development site These drainageways extended
down from higher elevations emptying runoff into Wailupe
Stream These small drainageways were noticeable but not
very prominent
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4
Vegetation leading to and within the site consisted of a
heavy growth of hao mango haole koa and other trees and
shrubs
GEOLOGY
Geologically the site is similar to Dwelling Site Nos
l and 2
An older alluvium is mapped to exist in higher elevations
closer to the valley walls and probably at greater depths
beneath the younger alluvium which exists closer to the
valley floor
Clayey soil deposits encountered at all the sites represent
a portion of the younger alluvium that tends to mantle the
older alluvium in higher elevations
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The field exploration revealed basically a brown clayey soil
in all the test pits extending down from the ground surface
to 10 to 17 feet below the surface
Within the clayey soil various amounts of cobbles boulders
and smaller aggregates were encountered The percentage of
aggregates ranged from 10 to 50 percent in the clayey soils
Test Pit Nos 2 and 4 encountered a gravel and cobble collu
vium layer at 10 and 13 feet respectively extending to the
bottom of the pits
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 993 10 P 5
Test Pit Nos 3 and 5 encountered boulders at the bottom
of the pits preventing any further advance
Boulders in the test pits ranged in size from 1 foot to
greater than 5 feet in diameter
Test Pit No 7 revealed a gray clay CH Adobe layer
from 11 feet to the bottom of the pit at 17 feet
Slickensides were present within this clay layer
Groundwater level was not encountered in the test pits
For more detailed description of the soil conditions refer
to the test pit logs in the appendix
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration and site reconnaissance disclosed
that the site is situated on a talus deposit along the
lower hillside slopes of Wailupe Valley similar to Sites
1 and 2
The soils encountered were quite variable and generally
consisted of clays and silty clays with increasing boulder
content up to 50 with depth The general site area
appears to be underlain by older alluvium deposits which
are generally stiff and relatively stable
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 6
Extensive deposits of thick adobe clay were generally
not encountered in our test pit exploration
Site Grading
Slickensided clay which may indicate some previous move
ments of the deposit was encountered at 11 to 17 foot
depth in Test Pit No 7 Slope cuts of about 8 feet high
are planned for the access road in this area Assuming
a low residual friction angle of 14 degrees and a cohesion
of 100 p s f due to the slickensided zone computed factor
of safety against slope movement for a 2 1 H V slope are
about 1 2 In order to increase the calculated factor of
safety to more than 1 5 we recommend 1 either a flatter
3 1 H V cut slope ratio be used or 2 the road grade be
raised to reduce the cut slope height to about 5 feet
Relatively high slope cuts up to about 45 feet top to toe
high are planned for the proposed house pad Test Pit No 3
located near the top of the proposed cut encountered about
7 feet of stiff clay over silty clay and boulder deposits
We recommend that a flatter slope ratio of 3 1 H V be
used for the upper 7 feet more or less of this slope cut
in clay soil The remainder of the cut in stiff silty
clay and boulders below can be cut to a steeper 2 1 H V
ratio
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 7
Fills up to about 10 feet high are planned for the lower
portion of the proposed pad area Test pits 2 and 5
in this area encountered silty clay with clay pockets
and boulders The existing ground here is about 4 1 H V
In order to prevent downhill slope creep of the new fill
the toe of the new fill should be well keyed into the
existing ground a minimum of 5 feet see Generalized
Cross Section Plate 2 A subdrain system should be
incorporated in the fill to intercept possible subsurface
seepage
If encountered surface adobe should be removed and
disposed of outside the development area prior to filling
Adobe clay should not be utilized in the new fill and
roadway construction
Additional grading guidelines are presented below
The field exploration and laboratory test results
disclosed that the on site soils are heterogeneous
and vary from low to highly expansive We recommend
that non adobe brown color on site materials generally
less than 12 inches in size can be utilized to con
struct the proposed fills with the understanding that
the future house foundations slab on grade driveway
and walkway areas may have to be designed for expansive
soil condition
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 8
If encountered adobe clay soils should not be
used in the embankment fills
Prior to filling the site should be cleared of
vegetation and debris then scarified moisture
conditioned and recompacted to 90 of its maximum
dry density established by ASTM D 1557
Fills should be placed in 10 inch maximum horizontal
lifts compacted to the above specified density
Sufficient water should be incorporated in the
fill material to obtain the required optimum
moisture content
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than
5 horizontal to 1 vertical should be keyed and
benched into the existing firm ground For details
see Plate 2 in the Appendix
Slopes
Slope ratios of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical can be used
for cut and fill slopes in stiff natural colluvial
material Flatter slope ratio of 3 1 H V should
be used for cut slopes in clay less than 6 feet high
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 993 19 P 9
Should thick deposits of adobe clay be encountered
in the cut slopes possibly in old buried drainageways
additional slope flattening or stabilization such as
buttress fill would be required A certain amount of
contingency budget should be provided for to take care
of these unforeseen soil conditions
For cut and fill slopes greater than 15 feet in vertical
height 8 foot wide drainage benches should be placed
at mid height of the slope
The drainage benches should be designed to intercept
and divert surface water into suitable outlets
Surface water should be diverted away from the top
of slope and slope planting utilized to reduce
erosion as soon as practicable
Many surface boulders were noted around the site In
addition a large quantity of boulders will be generated
from the cutting operation If necessary these boulders
could be used in the deeper fill areas The recommended
rock disposal procedures should be as follows
1 The rocks and boulders should be scattered
2 Sandy material with less than 15 percent fines
should be used to fill the voids between boulders
by flooding The boulder fill layer should be
located a minimum of 8 feet below the finished
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 993 10 P 10
pad and roadway grades and below future swimming
pool and utility line invert elevations
3 After adequate filling of the voids between
boulders are completed the upper eight 8
feet of the compacted fill should consist of
select on site soils non adobe materials from
the upper cut areas
The above rock disposal procedures should be monitored
by the soils engineer and areas with sufficient fill
for disposal should be delineated by the project sur
veyor
Future homeowners shoud be advised of this fact and
that any future improvements consisting of excavation
such as swimming pool may encounter such rock and
boulders at shallow depths
Alternate boulder disposal methods such as off site
disposal areas rip rap or rock wall utilization could
also be used
We would be happy to provide you with additional
recommendations on the above alternatives should
they become feasible
Foundations
Due to the heterogeneous soil deposits and moderately to
highly expansive soil conditions at the site we strongly
recommend that post and beam construction be utilized for
the proposed residence
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P Ii
Individual post footings which consist of poured in
place concrete should be embedded a minimum of 18
inches below level ground surface to resist erosion
and heaving of the on site soils
Where foundations are situated on sloping terrain
deeper poured in place concrete pier foundations
which extend downward below the ground surface a
sufficient depth to obtain a minimum 6 foot horizon
tal set back distance measured from the outer edge
of the pier footing out to the slope face are
recommended
Por the light loads anticipated an allowable bearing
value of 3000 p s f on stiff natural boulder colluviun
and 2500 p s f on compacted fill can be used Generally
footings on adobe soil are not recommended Where
adobe soil is encountered at the footing bottom they
should be removed and replaced with lean concrete It is
critical that all surface water should be diverted away
from the house slab and footing foundations
Slab On Grade
Where little or no cut is planned some amount of adobe
clay may be encountered at finished grade Garage slabs
and walkways should not bear on these expansive soils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 993 10 P 12
Where encountered below slabs and walkways the adobe
soil should be overexcavated for a maximum depth of 3
feet and then replaced with either quarry waste or
acceptable non expansive select on site soils
From a swelling potential point of view the brownish
on site fill material is anticipated to be better than
the dark brownish gray adobe clay However slab on
grade driveway and concrete walkways or slabs should
not bear on this material without having an adequate
select borrow layer in between The amount of select
borrow required will have to be determined by the soil
engineer after the site grading is completed For budget
estimate a 12 inch layer should be provided for
Road Pavement
Asphaltic concrete is planned for the access road Due
to the heterogeneous nature of the boulder talus deposit
large variations in the subgrade soils encountered is
anticipated
The recommended pavement thickness will depend on the
amount of cut type of fill utilized and the amount of
boulder and cobbles encountered at subgrade level
Most of the roadway will be in cut and would encounter
the underlying stable boulder colluvium layer The
following preliminary pavement design is recommended
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 993 00 P 13
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
_ inch Select Borrow
14 inch Total Pavement Thickness
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual sub
grade soil encountered during construction and the above
design section can be revised if necessary The recommended
section considers medium traffic and that good drainage will
be provided adjacent to paved areas
In order to reduce accumulation of water around the utility
lines from seepage leakage etc which could saturate
and weaken the adobe soils we recommend that clean gravel
be used as cushion material below the pipes The trench and
gravel cushion layer should be shaped to drain water into
catch basins or manholes Subdrains are required at various
locations to allow drainage and relief of potential water
seepage into the utility trenches The locations of the
subdrain will be staked out in the field by the soil engineer
during the time of construction Flexible joints should be
provided for all utility connections
Boulder Protection
The upper hillside lots may be subject to boulders rolling
down the hillside
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 993 10 P 14
The falling boulders are a general phenomena occurring
in all ridge slopes on Oahu Various methods of boulder
barriers or catchments can be used to reduce the hazard
of falling boulders The degree of safety of preventing
falling boulder hazards is a function of economics For
this site various methods of boulder prevention can be
utilized such as
1 A boulder catchment area about 10 to
15 feet wide with a fence or wall
could be utilized near the property
boundary to catch rolling boulders
The concrete interceptor ditch along
the upper boundaries of the site could
be used in this capacity
2 Boulder sweeps along the upper boundaries
of the site could be performed to remove
the loose boulders that appear likely to
roll down
3 The natural trees and vegetation along the
upper portions of the site should be retained
to act as a natural barrier against the boulders
and to reduce erosion
4 Where practicable the houses should be set
back away from the toe of the hillside slope
to create an additional boulder catchment area
It must be pointed out that whatever methods are used an
inherent risk of falling boulders could still exist for this
hillside lot
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 993 10 P 15
The state of the art of soil engineering practice has not
advanced to a point that a solution is available for this
area It is our opinion that no matter what one does
there is always the risk of boulders rolling down the
hill if not from areas near the house it will come from
areas high up on the slope Whatever measure is chosen
it only reduces the risk It should be pointed out that
no guarantee against future falling boulders can be made
due to changes in soil and rock conditions with time
weathering erosion earthquake and construction vibration
and improvements and construction activities at and around
the site
The owner of this hillside lot should be made aware of the
aforementioned limitations and cautioned against future
improvements in the upper areas of the lot which may disturb
the stability of the existing boulders

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that stripping excavation benching
and filling operations be observed by the soils engineer
during construction This is to see that adequate compac
tion has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investi
gation
The soils engineer s observation should also include
inspections of foundation subgrades and density tests to
record compaction of fill The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon the above inspection
