PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The planned Kukui ula Development is a resort community that encompasses
about 1 045 acres in Koloa on the south shore of Kauai and includes a hotel vacation
ownership commercial park golf course and residential uses The Parcel CC West
Phase 2 Subdivision project is part of the improvements for the Kukui ula Development
The Parcel CC West Phase 2 Subdivision project is located in the central portion
of the Kukui ula Resort Community bordered to the north by Ala Kukui ula the tee boxes
of Hole 9 of the Kukui ula Golf Course to the west and Ike Kai Street to the East The
project consisted of grading Lot Nos A through H and J through L and the construction
of Kaimalino Place A Project Location Map and Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2
respectively
Site grading generally consisted of cuts and fills up to about 8 feet thick On site
soils generated from the cut areas were the primary source of fill material for the project
The project also included installation of new infrastructure including roadways
drainage sewer water and other utilities

SITE GRADING AND UTILITY AND ROADWAY INSTALLATION
We observed the mass and pad grading underground utility construction and
roadway construction of Parcel CC West Phase 2 Subdivision project from
through The mass and site grading work consisted of clearing and
grubbing and placement and compaction of fills up to about 8 feet thick to achieve the
finished grades Prior to filling the existing ground surface was cleared and grubbed of
vegetation and deleterious material then proof rolled with a compactor Earthworks
Pacific Inc performed the mass and pad grading and utility and roadway construction
work
During the site grading operations samples of the materials utilized as fill were
obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill materials were moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM D1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill materials at the
finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
During the mass and pad grading and utility and roadway construction operations
periodic field density tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938
Nuclear Gauge Method Compaction test results are summarized on Plates 4 1 through
4 9 These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills
and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed as
unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained and or
approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading utility and roadway installation work
for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report
were performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the
