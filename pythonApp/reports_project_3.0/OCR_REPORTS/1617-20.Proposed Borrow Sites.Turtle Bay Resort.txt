In accordance with your request we have performed a geotechnical
engineering exploration of the above referenced sites Our work was done
in general accordance with our proposal of
The scope of our investigation included the drilling and sampling of
twenty eight 28 borings geologic reconnaissance of the existing site
conditions laboratory testing and engineering analyses
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusion and
recomendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The purpose of our exploration was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the sites to provide soils rock data pertinent to the use of
the subsoils and or rock as potential borrow material in the site grading
W O 1617 20 Page 2
and construction of the proposed golf course and other project
developments planned for the nearby Turtle Bay resort area
The main objectives of the exploration were
1 To determine the suitability of the underlying soils for use in
the site grading and construction of the proposed new golf
course development
2 To determine the potential excavation difficulties of the
materials within the proposed borrow sites
3 To explore the potential occurrence of seepage and groundwater
within the horizontal and vertical bounds of the proposed borrow
sites and to determine depths to groundwater if encountered
4 To determine allowable side slope cut ratios for slope design
Preliminary grading plans prepared for the borrow sites indicate
planned cuts of up to about 40 feet in depth Final grading plans for the
planned golf course development were being prepared at the time this
report was prepared
SITE DESCRIPTION
The borrow sites are located about one half mile mauka or south of
Kamehameha Highway The Turtle Bay Resort Hotel is located about
one and one half miles northwest of the site
Access to the sites is from Kamehameha Highway along the paved Gate
tc access road then along dirt roads
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1617 20 Page 3
The sites are situated on the northermmost end of the Koolau Range
where the high central axis of the range descends towards the ocean at the
north The topography is typical of deeply weathered volcanic terrain
that has been eroded by stream and wind action showing low ridges with
knolls standing above portions off the ridges The ground surface at the
sites ranges from nearly level to fairly steep with slope gradients
locally as steep as 20 or more Surface elevations vary from about 75 to
300 feet above Mean Sea Level
The proposed borrow sites had formerly been used for the cultivation
of sugar until the old Kahuku Plantation ceased operations The now
fallow cane fields have become overgrown with haole koa christmas berry
guava and other semitropical vegetation There are a few remaining
isolated canebreaks scattered through the sites
Some large boulders were noted on the ground surface in a few
areas Portions of proposed Borrow Sites Areas 1 and 2 were formerly
used as borrow sites prior to our exploration It appears that several
thousand cubic yards of material have been removed
The locations of the sites are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The borrow site is located in a deeply weathered and eroded lower
gentle slope of the Koolau Volcano The weathering has altered the basalt
rocks to an iron rich mass of reddish brown colored soils Drainageways
have cut gullies with fairly steep side slopes into the residual soils
The subsoils encountered in our exploration consisted of stiff to hard
clayey silts and silty clays with varying amounts of sand gravel and
boulders These soils grade with depth to weathered basalt and then
relatively unweathered rock

The soils encountered at the borrow sites may be generalized into
three 3 categories as follows

stiff to hard low to moderately expansive clayey silts and silty

sites

and fabric of the original parent rock but may be mechanically
reduced during excavation to soil materials ranging from clayey
silts to silty gravels The saprolite also grades with depth to
basaltic bedrock formation which ranges from extremely to
moderately weathered

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our findings disclosed that the on site soils generally consist of
twenty 20 feet or more of very stiff to hard fine grained residual
soils and saprolites with some boulders which grade with depth to
weathered basalt formation These residual soils have a moderate to high
shrink swell potential when used as compacted fill
It is our opinion that the soils at the proposed borrow sites are
suitable for use as general fill in the mass grading of the proposed golf
course grading and for use in the lower portions of the structural fill
areas for the Turtle Bay project Careful design of structural fill
grading and foundations will be required to minimize the shrink swell
potential of the compacted borrow soils
Due to the moderate swelling potential in general the clayey borrow
soils should be kept a minimum of three 3 feet below structures and a
minimum of two 2 feet below paved areas
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1617 20 Page 6
The soils encountered in our exploration have relatively high clay
contents and therefore have relatively low permeability Site grading
design for the golf course and structural areas should allow for proper
surface drainage and subdrainage
Based on our exploration we estimate that the soils will experience
a compaction loss shrinkage of about 4 at 80 maximum dry density and
of about 15 at 90 fill compaction Excavated rock formation will
probably experience a volume increase of about 5 to 10 per cent
Borrow Site Grading
Clearing Grubbing and Stripping
Existing vegetation should be cleared and stripped to a minimum depth
of about 12 inches below the existing ground surface Tree roots in
excess of 1 5 inches in diameter should be grubbed
The stripped surface soil containing organic matter may be stockpiled
and reused as topsoil in planting areas but should not be used as
structural fill Care should be exercised to avoid inclusion in the
borrow material and subsequent entrapment of deleterious materials
such as mats of grasses or piles of wood etc in the fill being
constructed of the borrow
The stripping operation should be continuously monitored by the
geotechnical engineer to verify removal of unsuitable materials
Some boulders were encountered in some of the borings and also noted
during the clearing of trails at the sites Therefore a significant
quantity of boulders could be generated from the site grading
Because the borrow site is an excavation type construction boulders
should be hauled off site or stockpiled for use in the deeper
portions of fills in the golf course grading development areas

Alternately the boulders could be sorted and picked over to generate
material for stone wall construction Also a portable crushing
plant could be mobilized to the project area and used to reduce the
boulders to material of a suitable size for use as granular select
borrow fill It is our opinion that the boulders and or any
excavated rock formation will not be able to produce aggregate
quality material However with sufficient processing a material
that is roughly equivalent to select borrow could be produced
Excavation
It is our opinion that the upper clayey silts and silty clays may be
excavated with conventional earth moving equipment In the deeper
parts of the planned cuts some moderately hard ripping may be
required to excavate the weathered rock material
Field adjustment of the site grading design of the borrow sites may
be required to reduce the quantity of hard rock excavation However
if sufficient quantities of select borrow or similar material are
required it may be economically feasible to excavate the rock by
blasting and to crush and screen the material to generate the
granular fill Otherwise the depth of the proposed cuts should be
limited to about 25 to 30 feet below the existing ground surface
Slope Construction
Based on our exploration it appears that the upper portions of the
cut slopes for the proposed borrow sites will be primarily in soil
The deeper portions of the cuts will encounter firm weathered rock
formation A cut slope ratio of 2 1 H V is recommended for slopes
in soil Where firm weathered rock is encountered slope cut ratios
up to 1 1 H V may be used

Groundwater and seepage were encountered in some of the drilled
borings However the exploration was conducted during very wet
weather and the water encountered may have been isolated perched

Slope Planting
Upon completion of the borrow site grading the exposed cut slopes
should be planted to minimize erosion of the soils in the slope faces
Past experience indicates that it may be difficult to establish grasses or
other vegetative cover on slopes cut in residual soils and saprolites If
the hydromilch method is used it may be necessary to reapply the spray
mulch several times in order to establish an adequate vegetative cover
We suggest that consideration be given to sodding or use of a
vegetation erosion control mat such as Miramat or Enkamat If the cost
of three 3 spray mulch applications exceeds the cost of the mat then
the mat would probably be preferable
Experience indicates that planting of rock cut slopes is generally
neither necessary nor practical If it is required to plant the rock
slopes for aesthetic purposes we suggest that a landscape architect be
consulted for a suitable spreading ground cover
Golf Course Site Grading
Special grading design is contemplated for the golf course fills due
to landscaping concerns Treated sewage effluent will be used for
landscape watering as well as a means of effluent disposal Therefore
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1617 20 Page 9
there are concerns for adequate drainage in the surface soils Ponding of
the irrigation water could hinder the growth of the vegetation and due to
the source of the irrigation water could be a potential health problem
In order to provide good drainage in the near surface soils the site
grading design should allow for good surface drainage and the fill soils
should be only moderately compacted to aid in the drainage of the
landscape water Since the soils in the golf courses will not be required
to support structural loads it is our opinion that a lower level of
compaction effort may be used
Final grading plans for the golf course grading were being prepared
at the time this report was prepared General recommendations for the
design of the golf course grading are as follows
Fill Material Placement
We believe that most of the borrow site natural soils may be suitable
for use as general fill provided that they are not contaminated with
unsuitable materials or oversized particles
Highly expansive soils if encountered should be used in the deeper
fill areas
In the golf course areas the top four 4 feet of fill should be
compacted to a minimum of 75 but not greater than 85 per ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78 This lower level of compaction as opposed to the
normal 90 required for general fills will maintain a degree of
porosity in the relatively low permeability borrow soils This
porosity will aid in the drainage
Due to the reduced level of compaction fill slope ratios should be
limited to 3H 1V or flatter

Fill Material and Placement
Based on the field exploration and laboratory test results in
general the on site materials are suitable for use in compacted fill
and backfill The laboratory swell tests indicated that most of the
on site soils have moderately high swelling potential depending on
the initial compaction density and molding moisture content prior to
saturation At or below the optimm moisture content the soil
exhibits moderate or slightly higher swell potential However it
was noted at a molding moisture content slightly above the optimum
moisture content the wet side of the optimum moisture the swell
potential is substantially reduced Therefore in order to reduce
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O
1617 20 Page 11
the swelling potential of the soil fill materials should be placed
at moisture contents slightly above the optimum moisture content
Materials used for fills placed within the top three 3 feet of
finished grade should be non expansive with no individual particles
exceeding three 3 inches in maximum dimension and a plasticity
index PI per ASTM Test Method D 4318 84 not exceeding 20
Boulders less than two 2 feet in diameter may be used in the fill
provided they are not allowed to nest together and the voids between
boulders are filled Boulders should not be placed in the upper
three 3 feet of the fill or where future utilities will be located
The fills should in general be placed in horizontal lifts not
exceeding 10 inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum
of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V fill slopes are recommended
for fills constructed from the borrow soils
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling past the design fill
slope and then cutting back
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope
planting utilized to reduce erosion
Slope benches are generally recommended for slope heights in excess
of 20 feet
Modifications to the above recommendations could be made on an
individual basis subject to review and written comment by the geotechnical
engineer

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork recommendations given herein If this review is not made
the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and earthwork phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required from sources other than the subject
borrow sites should be non expansive tested and approved by the
geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

borings probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary for the geotechnical
engineer to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
se
Moisture content unit weight and Atterberg Limit determinations
were performed on selected samples as an aid in the evaluation and
classification of soil properties The results of these tests are
presented on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
Two 2 moisture density relationships ASTM D 1557 78 for
compacted samples were performed on bulk samples of the near surface soils
to evaluate the compaction characteristics of the soils The test results
are summarized on Plate B 1
Eleven 11 Atterberg limits and one 1 sieve analysis tests were
performed on selected samples of the soils to evaluate their plasticity
The test results are presented on Plates B 2 through B 5
Eighteen 18 ring swell tests were performed on remolded and
undisturbed samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils in
both natural and compacted conditions The test results are summarized on
Plates B 6 thru B 8
One 1 direct shear test was performed on a selected undisturbed
sample to evaluate the shear strength parameters of the natural soils
The test results are summarized on Plate B 9
