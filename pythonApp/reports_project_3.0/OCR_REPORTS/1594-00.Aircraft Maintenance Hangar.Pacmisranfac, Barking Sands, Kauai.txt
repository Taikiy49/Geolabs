This report presents the results of our geotechnical investigation
performed for the proposed aircraft maintenance hangar and the support
facilities at Pacific Missile Range Facility at Barking Sands on the
island of Kauai Hawaii The general location of the project site and
its vicinity is shown on the Project Location map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed project is to construct an Organizational Maintenance
Hangar to provide weather protected shelter for servicing and repair of
aircraft emergency shelter for operable aircraft and headquarters and
office space for personnel The facility will consist of hangar space
shop and equipment spaces and administrative spaces required to support
the aircraft and mission of the Pacific Missile Range Facility
W O 1594 00
I D 1702 Page 2
The project includes the construction of the following
1 A 37 298 square foot hangar of steel frame construction with
concrete floor CMU walls and partitions Maximum column and
wall loads are anticipated to be 102 kips and 4 5 kips per
linear foot respectively
2 A bridge crane will be required for the repair maintenance
facility
3 Access apron and parking areas
4 An A C taxiway connection
It is our understanding that the finished floor elevation for the
proposed hangar is about 13 feet above MSL Therefore site grading
will be required to provide building area for the hangar and other
support facilities
PRUPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation was to evaluate the subsurface
soil conditions at the site to provide soil data for the design of the
foundation for the maintenance hangar and pavement thickness required
for the new access apron and parking area
The scope of our investigation which was performed in general
accordance with our proposal of May 3 1985 included drilling and
sampling of nine 9 borings five 5 field CBR tests laboratory soil
testing engineering analysis utilizing the field and laboratory data
and report preparation

GENERAL SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed site is located approximately 100 feet to the
southwest of the triangular road intersection formed by the North Nohili
and Waiway at the Pacific Missile Range Facility at Barking Sands on the
island of Kauai Hawaii
The site is an open and unoccupied area measured at about 190 by
200 feet The topography consisted of a slightly elevated zone located
roughly to the north of the proposed hangar The surface elevation
within the site varies from approximately 12 to 17 feet above MSL
Ground cover consists of some dry grass and loose silty fine sand
A section of an abandoned A C paved access road was encountered at the
mid western part of the site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the proposed site for the hangar and
the access apron and parking area were explored by drilling and sampling
eleven 11 borings to depths of 8 5 to 20 5 feet below the existing
ground surface In order to evaluate the subgrade soil strength below
the aircraft apron areas 5 field CBR tests were also performed

W
I D 1702 Page 4
In general all our borings encountered sand from the ground
surface down to the full depths The grain size of the sand deposit was
found to increase gradually from a silty fine condition at the surface
to a medium condition at the lower portion of the boring The
consistency of the sand deposit was also found to vary from a loose to
medium dense condition at the top 2 to 5 feet to very dense and cemented
condition below
Groundwater was not encountered at the shallow borings However
at Boring and 4 groundwater was encountered at depths varying
from 10 0 to 14 5 feet from the ground surface approximately at
elevation 2 5 feet above MSL
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our subsurface exploration encountered dense to slightly cemented
sand at the proposed foundation elevation for the proposed maintenance
hangar Shallow spread or continuous wall footing can be used for
foundation support of the structure
Site grading will consist mainly of cutting with limited amount of
filling The on site sandy material can be reused as fill
The underlying dense to very dense sandy material can provide good
subgrade support for the new pavement
Organizational Maintenance Hangar
The proposed hangar will be approximately 140 by 200 feet in plan
dimension Maximum column and wall loads are anticipated to be 102 kips
and 4 5 kips per foot respectively
GEOLABS HAWAII
I D 1702 Page 5
Foundation Support
Based on the anticipated soil conditions and column loads spread
or continuous footing foundation bearing on the dense slightly
cemented sand are recommended for the proposed structure
Footings should be placed a minimum of 30 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Uplift wind or other lateral loading
condition may further increase the embedment of the footing
foundation
An allowable bearing capacity of 3500 p s f may be used for the
foundation design The bearing capacity value is for total dead
plus live load only and may be increased by one third for short
duration wind loading Settlements under the design loading are
anticipated to be on the order of less than one half of an inch
total and one fourth of an inch differential
Resistance to lateral loads for the hangar structure can be
obtained by passive pressure and frictional resistance A
frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the bottom of the
footing and the dense soil In addition an equivalent fluid
pressure of 350 p s f per foot of depth may be used to determine
the passive resistance

In the office area where light floor loads are anticipated a
4 inch concrete slab over 4 inches of well graded aggregate base

course could be used should a capillary break be deemed necessary
to protect floor covering
Pavement Design
Both the hangar floor and access apron will be paved with Portland
cement concrete to resist fuel and hydraulic fluid spillage while the
proposed taxiway connection will be constructed of asphaltic concrete
The pavement design for this project was carried out in accordance with
NAVFAC Design Manual DM 21 Airfield Pavements June 1973 and DM 21 3
Flexible Pavement Design for Airfields August 1978
The hangar floor access apron and taxiway connection will be
subject to aircraft without cargo fuel or armament loads Therefore
these pavements are designed in the same manner as the aircraft aprons
or taxiways as specified in DM 21 except using only the empty weight
of the aircraft or approximately 60 percent of the maximum gross
aircraft weights

Design Subgrade California Bearing Ratio CBR
CBR tests for both in situ materials and laboratory compacted soils
were performed The field CBR tests were carried out in accordance
with ASTM D 4429 84 test method while those performed in the
laboratory followed the procedure as stated in ASTM D 1883 test
method The results of these tests are summarized in the
appendices
In general the subgrade material consists of fine to medium
grained sand with good drainage characteristics and very low
compressibility and expansion Based on our field and laboratory
tests we believe that a design CBR value of 8 can be used

Pavement Drainage
Despite the very dry climate at Barking Sands we would recommend
that subdrainage system be installed at the subgrade level around and
within the hangar and access apron area to collect and drain any
surface runoff during heavy rainstorms Also the site should be
graded to provide positive drainage away from the pavement sections
Vehicle Parking Area
A vehicle parking area is planned on the eastern side of the proposed
hangar Based on the field exploration dense to very dense sand is
anticipated at finished grade Assuming a typical CBR of 8 and light
traffic conditions the following is the recommended pavement section
2 0 Inch Aphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
Site Grading
Site grading consists of cutting down the existing elevated area to
the finished grade level Excavation should be easy and hence the use
of conventional equipment is anticipated

At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing pavements
vegetation structures debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site in order to prevent
contamination of the excavated material
The areas which are to receive foundation footing should be
thoroughly tamped prior to placement of the footing if they are loosened
during the excavation The areas which are to receive pavement should be
scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches below the subgrade elevation
moisture conditioned and recompacted to 100 of its maximum density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78 test method
Loose spots encountered at the footing or subgrade level should be
removed and replaced with on site soil compacted to the specifications
mentioned above
Corrosion Tests
To examine the corrosive characteristics of the natural soil against
utility pipes and other underground structures soil resistivity and pH
tests were conducted
The results of the tests indicate that the underlying soils have
resistivity values from 3800 to 13000 ohm cm and pH values of 6 0 to 7 0
indicating low to moderate corrosion potential
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundations should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior t
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading subgrade preparation and
footing excavations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering
principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other
warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such as

additional probing or corrections in the field Therefore additional
expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a properly
constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended to
accommodate these possible costs
