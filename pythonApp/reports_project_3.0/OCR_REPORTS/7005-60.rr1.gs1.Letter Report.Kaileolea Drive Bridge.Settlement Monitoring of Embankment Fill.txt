Dear Mr. Baginski: 
In accordance with our fee proposal dated May 24, 2018, this letter report presents 
the results of the settlement monitoring of the embankment fill for the bridge girder support 
for the Kaileolea Drive Bridge project in Ewa on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  The project 
location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
We had previously provided our geotechnical recommendations for the embankment 
fill settlement monitoring in our memorandum entitled “Revised Memorandum, Change in 
Subsoil Conditions, Embankment Fill Design for Bridge Girder Support, Kaileolea Drive 
Bridge, Ewa, Oahu, Hawaii,” dated November 13, 2017. 
This report summarizes the measurements taken by the contractor and presents our 
geotechnical engineering recommendations resulting from our engineering analyses of the 
collected settlement monitoring data. These recommendations are intended for the 
embankment fill placed for the bridge girder support only. The findings and 
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this 
report. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project involves the construction of a new bridge to extend Kaileolea Drive over 
the planned water feature at the Ocean Pointe development.  The project is within the 
south-central portion of the development and south of the existing golf course. The new 
bridge location is shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
We understand that two embankments will be used as the falsework support for 
the placement and splicing of the bridge girders. A settlement monitoring plan for the

SETTLEMENT MONITORING PLAN 
The settlement monitoring program consisted of the installation of four settlement 
gauges at the bottom of the embankment and six settlement points on the top of the 
embankment for each of the fill embankments. The settlement gauges measured the 
settlement that occurred beneath the gauges. The settlement points measured the 
settlement of the embankment and subsoil beneath the embankment. It should be noted 
that the installation of the settlement points was performed at the completion of the 
embankment when a significant amount of settlement had occurred. The location of 
settlement gauges and settlement points are shown on the Embankment Settlement 
Monitoring Plan, Plate 3.  
Settlement gauges (SGs) were installed on December 8, 2017. Construction of the 
embankment fill started on December 11, 2017 and completed on December 15, 2017. 
Surface settlement monitoring points (SMPs) were installed on December 19, 2017. The 
last measurements of the settlement gauges and settlement points were taken on 
May 23, 2018. 
It was recommended that a surcharge load of about 2,300 pounds per square foot 
(psf) on 6 feet by 6 feet square pad be placed to try and simulate the bridge girders. The 
contractor decided to place the actual girders as the surcharge load. The girders were 
placed on April 19, 2018. 
SETTLEMENT MONITORING RESULTS 
Embankment No. 1 
The settlement gauges and settlement points for Embankment No. 1 are shown on 
the Embankment No. 1 – Maximum Measured Settlements, Plate 4. The maximum 
measured settlements for each settlement gauge and settlement point are shown on 
Plate 4. 
The settlement gauges indicated that the total ground settlements measured beneath 
Embankment No. 1 ranged from 1.08 inches on the makai side and 6.0 inches on the mauka 
side of the embankment. The settlements at the top of the embankment, measured with the 
settlement points, ranged from 0.72 inches to 1.8 inches. It should be noted that the majority 
of the measured settlements occurred during embankment construction. Therefore, the 
measured settlements of the settlement points are less than the settlement gauges. It 
appears that the majority of the post-embankment construction settlement occurred during 
the frequent dewatering that occurred at the site during construction of the wingwalls at both 
abutments, which can be distinguished by the steps in the time-settlement curves. The 
measured settlements are shown in the Settlement Monitoring Reading Summary and Plots 
– Embankment No. 1, Plates 7.1.1 through 7.10.2.

Ground settlement of about 0.48 inches (maximum) was measured at the center of 
the embankment. This settlement reading was taken after the girders were placed. In 
general, a sharp drop in time-settlement curve was observed immediately after the girder 
placement and was stabilized thereafter. 
Embankment No. 2 
The settlement gauges and settlement points for Embankment No. 2 are shown on 
the Embankment No. 2 – Maximum Measured Settlements, Plate 5. The maximum 
measured settlements for each settlement gauge and settlement point are shown on 
Plate 5. 
The settlement gauges indicated that the total ground settlements measured beneath 
Embankment No. 2 ranged from 0.96 inches on the makai side and 2.76 inches on the 
mauka side of the embankment. The settlements at the top of the embankment, measured 
with the settlement points, ranged from 0.6 inches to 1.44 inches. It should be noted that the 
majority of the measured settlements occurred during embankment construction. Therefore, 
the measured settlements of the settlement points are less than the settlement gauges. It 
appears that the majority of the post-embankment construction settlement occurred during 
the frequent dewatering that occurred at the site during construction of the wingwalls at both 
abutments, which can be distinguished by the steps in the time-settlement curves. The 
measured settlements are shown in the Settlement Monitoring Reading Summary and Plots 
– Embankment No. 2, Plates 8.1.1 through 8.10.2. 
Ground settlement of about 0.48 inches (maximum) was measured at the center of 
the embankment. This settlement reading was taken after the girders were placed. In 
general, a sharp drop in time-settlement curve was observed immediately after the girder 
placement and was stabilized thereafter. 
RECOMMENDATIONS 
Based on the results of the settlement monitoring program, we believe that the 
settlement of the embankment fill and underlying subsoil has stabilized. Therefore, we 
believe that girder splicing may proceed. 
Based on the readings, some additional settlements occurred after dewatering 
operations at the project site. The weight of the underlying material was increased with the 
dewatering inducing additional settlements. Therefore, we recommend avoiding dewatering 
operations at the project site until completion of the bridge girder splicing operation. 
LIMITATIONS 
It should be noted that our analysis and recommendations were performed in 
accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice; no 
warranty is expressed or implied.
