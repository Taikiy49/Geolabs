In accordance with your request we have performed a subsurface
investigation for the above referenced project
Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal of
except that six auger borings were carried out instead
of the resistivity survey initially proposed since sand coral formation
under submerged condition can be better delineated by auger borings
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

GENERAL SITE CONDITION
The project site is part of the Kaluanui Agricultural Subdivision
situated on the western side of Kamehameha Highway about 1 100 feet
south of the intersection of Kaluanui Stream and Kamehameha Highway
The study area is approximately 900 feet wide by 2 000 feet long
located immediately west of the north south trending major drainage
ditch Access to the area is provided by the paved Puhuli Street and
unpaved roads between unfilled shrimp ponds
The ground elevation in this area is about 5 to 10 feet
FIELD EXPLORATION
Previous report on the caprock aquifer at the Punaluu Kaluanui
region suggested that a wedge of coarse sand and coral reef gravel
underlies the project area The purpose of this investigation is to
delineate this sand gravel deposit in order to provide guidance for
locating pump wells The wells are planned to provide aquaculture
agriculture grade water with a projected yield of about 75 gpm per well

Six auger probes to between 35 foot and 50 foot depths were drilled
at selected locations The approximate thickness of geologic deposits
were estimated based on sand gravel chips brought up by and silt clay
adhering to the 4 inch diameter auger
Three split spoon samples were obtained in critical horizons to
allow visual identification of the underlying deposits encountered
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
During our field exploration the borings indicated that
1
Beneath a surface layer 9 to 15 feet of brown silty clay to
clayey silt a wedge of sand gravel deposit exists beneath
portions of the site In most boring locations this
sand gravel caprock aquifer is underlain first by a 10 to
15 foot thick layer of brown to gray silty clay and then by a
much thicker layer of brown alluvial silty clay
The wedge of sand gravel deposit appears to be thickest at the
ocean makai side of the site and is estimated to be
approximately 8 to 20 feet thick at distances of 500 to 700
feet from the coast Beyond 900 to 1 000 feet inland this
sandy layer appears to have thinned out

W O 1621 00 Page 4
In two locations A 2 and A 6 about 500 to 600 feet from the
coast it was noted that within the sand gravel layer
interbedded medium to fine sand occurs on top of silty sandy
coral gravels
Within the sand gravel wedge the coral gravel is likely to be
the most porous portion of the caprock aquifer The
permeability of the silty coarse to fine sand layer is
anticipated to be low because of the dominating fine sand and
silt components Towards the coast in certain location such
as A 4 the permeability of the sand is expected to be
further reduced because of the increased fine sand and silt
content probably due to the change to a lagoon type of
deposit
Groundwater was encountered at approximately 1 to 2 feet
elevation in most borings but at 5 feet in Boring A 5
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
Findings obtained by the subsurface investigation indicate that the
most suitable area within the site for pump well location is about 500
feet to 700 feet inland from the coast along the 2 000 feet length of
the site
At this general location the sand coral gravel layer is
approximately 10 to 15 feet thick and is about 10 to 20 feet beneath the
existing ground surface
GEOLABS HAWAIli
Boring A 2 about 500 feet from west encountered probably the
thicker and more porous area of the sand gravel deposit within the site
Groundwater yield in this general location may represent the maximum that
a pump well can extract from the upper caprock aquifer
As the subsurface occurrence of coral materials can be variable it
will be prudent to use suitably qualified personnel to record ground
conditions actually encountered during drilling of the pump wells Such
information can be used to better locate the remaining wells
