INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the sub
surface conditions at the site to provide soil data for
the design and construction of the proposed reservoir and
service roadway
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed site is located in Kawela Molokai Access
to the site is from Kamehameha V Highway The proposed
reservoirs are part of the on site improvements for the
Kawela Platations Unit I development The proposed reser
voirs are located along the eastern ridge of the project
development
The upper 550 reservoir site is located near the center
of the eastern ridge which is sloping at about 5 1 H V
down toward the south The area is covered with some
brush and a few Kiawe trees The elevation generally varies
from about 530 to 560 feet The reservoir site had been
cut down close to finished grade at the time of our field
explorations
An existing dirt road which was recently constructed by the
project contractor provides access between the Kamehameha V

generally will follow the existing dirt road The existing
dirt road is approximately 44 foot wide and has been con

had been cut down about 3 feet at the time of our field
exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the reservoir sites are
underlain by competent weathered rock formation which can
provide adequate support for the proposed tank structures
Foundations
Spread and continuous footings can be used for the reservoir
Structure Based on the borings and field observations
it appears that volcanic clinker material would be encoun
tered near the reservoir footing level
An allowable bearing value up to 4000 p s f can be used
for footings resting on the dense clinker material
Based on the boring data and estimated reservoir loads
small settlements less than of an inch are anticipated
for the footing loads
The footings should bear a minimum of 12 inches below the
adjacent finish grade The ground surface around the footings
should not be excavated or allowed to erode

Slab on grade should bear entirely on the very dense natural
soil
Surface water should be diverted away from the reservoir
foundations
Lateral Resistance
A coefficient of friction between concrete and soil of 0 5
can be utilized to determine the lateral resistance of
the structure and foundation Additionally a passive soil
resistance of 300 p c f may also be used
Site Grading
The grading plans for both reservoirs indicated cuts of a
bout 1 to 14 feet for each site Low fills may be required
for the access road construction near the tank site
At the time of our field exploration the 550 reservoir
site had been cut down approximately to grade and cutting
of the 250 reservoir had commenced Cut slope ratios of
s horizontal to 1 vertical were utilized for both sites
A thin layer of residual soil over weathered rock formation
was exposed in the slope cuts
Based on our field observations and boring data the cut
Slopes in weathered rock could be steepened up to 1 horizon
tal to 1 vertical if desired however this does not seem
to be necessary at this time

Fill slope ratios of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical are recom
mended Slope planting would be required on all slopes
The site should be cleared of all vegetation and loose
topsoil prior to filling or concrete placement
Select on site material can be used for fill The fill
should be placed in horizontal loose lifts of 10 inches
and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density established
by ASTM Test Method D 1557
Fills placed on slopes having grades steeper than 5 1 H V
should be keyed and benched into the existing natural ground
Sliver fills should be avoided if practical
Surface runoff should be diverted away from the tops of
slopes and slope planting should be utilized to reduce
slope erosion
Service Roadway Pavement
Based on field observations of the near surface soils at
the site and light traffic conditions the following pavement
design is recommended for the service roadways
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
4 inch Base Course
6 inch Total Pavement Thickness

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the subgrade preparation
for the reservoir sites
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be ob
served by a soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our subsurface
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections
