INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed New Office Building project located at the corner of Kalihi and
Colburn Streets in Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommen
dations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses
These recommendations are intended for design input only and are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located at the corner of Kalihi and Colburn Streets in
the Kalihi area in Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The subject lot is
approximately square in shape with an area of approximately 10 000 square feet The
site is currently occupied by a two story and four one story residences
It is proposed to demolish the existing houses and to construct a new office
building at the site Based on the information provided to us the new office building will
be a two story structure with parking at ground floor level Based on the information
provided by the project architect we understand that the proposed building will generally
be of CMU wall construction with wooden roof framing In addition based on the
information provided by the project structural engineer we anticipate that column loads
will be on the order of 150 kips per column

the time this report was prepared However we anticipate that approximately 1 to 2 feet

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of
the western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3677 00 Page 5
eastern two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while Koolau
was still active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lavas in central
Oahu
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to fill
the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea When
the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea
levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of accu
mulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits at
correspondingly higher elevations and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters
protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys in the
sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and calcareous
materials caused consolidation of the soft deltaic materials and lagoonal deposits and
induration of the calcareous reef materials Land development and reclamation projects
within the last century have brought the project site to its present form

Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the site we believe that a
shallow foundation system may be used to support the proposed building structure An
allowable bearing pressure of up to 4 000 pounds per square foot p s f may be used for
design of isolated and continuous footings bearing on the recompacted in situ granular
soils and or dense coral formation This allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live
loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as wind and or
seismic forces In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below
the lowest adjacent finish grades However in order to found the footings on dense
recompacted granular material or coral formation we recommend that special provisions
be made to allow for the flexibility of lowering the footings in the field during construction
Because of the potential for presence of voids and or cavities in the coral formation
below the foundations a program of probing and grouting the foundation locations is
recommended Detailed recommendations for probing and grouting the foundations are
discussed in the Foundation Probing section of this report
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3677 00 Page 8
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on the recompacted in situ granular soils coral or dense hard coral
formation as recommended herein to be less than one inch total with differential
settlements on the order of 1 2 inch
For footings bearing on the in situ granular soils the bottom of the footing
excavations should be recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction prior
to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete For footings bearing on the dense hard
coral the bottom of the coral should be clean and free of loose material If soft and or
loose materials are encountered during footing excavations they should be over
excavated to expose the underlying firm dense materials The over excavation should be
backfilled with lean concrete or select granular material compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction
In general lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil A
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3677 00 Page 9
coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted in situ
soils or new engineered fills Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated
using an equivalent fluid pressure of 350 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f
This assumes that the soil around the footings is well compacted Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be
neglected
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the foundation bearing
conditions and the required embedment depths
Foundation Probin
To reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of
cavities and or voids below the foundations we recommend that a program of cavity
probing and grouting be conducted below the footings locations
For estimating purposes we recommend that probe holes be drilled at each
spread footing location and on 15 foot centers for continuous footings The probe holes
should be at least 3 inches in diameter and should extend to at least 10 feet below the
planned bottom of footing If cavities and or voids are encountered or suspected during
the probing operation additional probe holes should be drilled at closer spacings to help
delineate the vertical and lateral extent of the cavity and or void Following the drilling of
each probe hole a 2 inch PVC pipe should be inserted into the probe holes to preserve
the open hole integrity for grouting A field engineer or geologist from Geolabs Hawaii
should be present to log the probe holes and to determine the locations of any additional
probe holes needed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3677 00 Page 10
The probe holes and any cavities discovered should be backfilled with sand
cement grout We recommend that a low strength sand cement grout mixture with an
approximately 6 to 9 inch slump range pumped at low to moderate pressures be utilized
for the grouting operation
Because of the potential for encountering large cavities and or voids at the site we
recommend that unit prices for additional probing and grouting be obtained during
bidding In addition the probe drill should be available on site until the probing and
grouting operation is complete In addition the contractor should be made aware that a
longer lag time between probing grouting operations and foundation construction may be
required in the construction schedule
The probing and grouting program should be conducted under the observation of a
representative from Geolabs Hawaii This would allow our firm to monitor the presence of
cavities and to allow additional recommendations to be made if excess grout take and or
changed conditions are observed
Slabs On Grade
Based on the current design concept we anticipate that concrete slabs on grade
will be used at a small portion of the proposed building In general for interior building
slabs not subjected to vehicular traffic we believe that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of
cushion fill consisting of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should be
placed below the slab to provide more uniform support of the slab and to serve as a
capillary moisture break To reduce future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage
to floor coverings an impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3677 00 Page 11
recommended on top of the gravel cushion layer Two inches of moist fine sand should
be placed above the plastic membrane to provide protection of the membrane and to aid
in curing of the slab concrete
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that a minimum 6 inch thick layer of select granular material or select borrow
subbase be provided below the concrete walkway slab Construction joints should be
provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkways with expansion joints at right
angle intersections
The area adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab with
non expansive impervious soil and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce
the potential of water ponding around the foundation
Site Gradin
We envision that site grading for the proposed project will involve fills on the order
of 1 to 2 feet relative to the existing ground surface to attain the design grade for the
proposed construction Site grading operations should be observed by a representative
of Geolabs Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office observe the site
grading to evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered during site grading
and whether the exposed soil conditions are similar to those encountered in our field
exploration
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3677 00 Page 12
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing and grubbing operations
should be over excavated to expose firm dense ground and the resulting excavation
should be backfilled with compacted select fill moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Prior to
placement of the backfill fill material the over excavated subgrade should be scarified to
a depth of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The excavated soft loose soils
should be properly disposed of off site
Areas designated to receive fills and the finished subgrades should be scarified to
a minimum depth of 8 inches where possible moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction The compaction requirement for pavement subgrade should be increased to
a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum
dry density
In general the excavated on site soils may be reused as a source of general fill
materials provided that they contain no lumps or fragments greater than 3 inches in
maximum dimension In addition because of the slight expansive potential of the near
surface silty soils encountered at the site the on site silty soils should not be used as fill
or backfill within the upper 6 inches below the finish subgrade in the pavement and slab
areas
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3677 00 Page 13
We envision that imported fill materials will be required to raise the grade at the
site In general the imported material should consist of non expansive material such as
crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from
coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and should
contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material
should have a laboratory CBR value of 12 or more and should have a maximum swell of
1 percent or less Imported fill material should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for
conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its
intended use
Generally the fill and backfill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding
8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to 2 percent above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The
fill and backfill material within the upper 2 feet below the finish subgrade in pavement
areas should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Pavements
We understand that the ground floor will be used for parking We anticipate that
the vehicle loading for this project will consist primarily of passenger cars and light trucks
Our pavement design assumes that the pavement subgrade soils will be imported non
expansive fills with a CBR value of at least 12 and a swell value of less than 1 percent as
specified in the Site Grading section Based on the above assumptions we recommend
that the following pavement section be used for preliminary design
Asphaltic Concrete Pavement
2 0 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 0 Inch Basalt Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on a Moist Compacted Subgrade

The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches
moisture conditioned to 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted
to not less than 95 percent relative compaction The base course should consist of
crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
We recommend that field density tests and laboratory CBR tests be performed on
samples of the actual subgrade soils encountered during construction to confirm the
above recommended design section The recommended pavement section is based on
the assumption that good drainage will be provided for areas adjacent to pavements
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry all
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed anywhere on
the site during or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate
landscaping in or adjacent to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs
extend a minimum of 2 inches into the subgrade below the aggregate base course to
reduce migration of landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively a
subdrain system could be constructed to collect excessive water from landscaping
irrigation For long term performance we recommend that a subdrain system be
constructed adjacent to paved landscape areas
Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines will be required for the proposed project In
general a granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials
such as No 3B Fine gravel should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about
12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3677 00 Page 15
the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the
pipes It is critical that the free draining material be used to reduce the potential for
formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support around
the sides of the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the on site soils or select fill
material The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than
90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence
Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below
the pavement subgrade should be compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of the concrete to
reduce water infiltration

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties
The results of these tests are presented on the Log of Boring at the appropriate sample
depths
Two ring swell tests were performed on selected samples to evaluate the swelling
potential of the near surface soils The test results are summarized on Plate B 1
One sieve analysis test ASTM D 422 was performed on a selected sample of
the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 2
