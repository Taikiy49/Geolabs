PALAMA ELDERLY HOUSING
This report summarizes the results of our geotechnical engineering services
performed during the foundation excavation and grading operations at the above
referenced project from through in general
accordance with our revised fee proposal dated
SITE GRADING
The project site was graded for the development of a 5 story elderly housing
building with 1 level basement parking The site grading and building construction work
was performed by Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company A general layout of the
project site is presented on the Site Plan Plate 1
Based on the laboratory and field tests conducted by representatives from our
firm it is our opinion that the grading was performed in general accordance with the
intent of the recommendations presented in the Project Soils Report prepared by Soils
International for the project dated and the approved Plans and
Specifications Detailed observations and discussions are presented in the following
sections of this report
Over Excavation of Slab Subgrade
During the course of site grading a laboratory testing program was conducted
to evaluate the properties and behavior of the clayey sands encountered at the slab
subgrade elevations Particular attention was given to the potential swelling
characteristics of the clayey sand materials when subjected to moisture fluctuations

Our laboratory tests included four 1 inch ring swell tests with different surcharge loads
an Atterberg Limits test a sieve analysis a California Bearing Ratio CBR test and
a Modified Proctor compaction test Results of these tests have been transmitted to
your office in our letter dated
Based on the laboratory test results the clayey sands encountered at the
subgrade elevations are considered to be highly expansive when subjected to moisture
fluctuations Future cracking of the garage slab would probably occur if the subgrade
soils were not properly prepared The laboratory testing program conducted confirmed
the requirement for over excavating a minimum of 3 feet of the expansive soils below
the subgrade level in accordance with the recommendations presented in the Project
Soils Report and Item E of the Foundation Notes in the project Structural Plan Sheet
S 15 The 3 foot over excavation was replaced with structural fill materials placed in
general accordance with the project specifications
Fill Placement
During the site grading operations a sample of the imported soils utilized as fill
materials was obtained and returned to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry
density and optimum moisture content relationship was determined in our laboratory in
accordance with the ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Results of our laboratory test are
presented on Plate 2
Observation and testing of the fill placement in the field were performed by
representatives from our office on an on call basis intermittent to observe that the
grading was performed in general conformance with the intent of the Project Soils
Report and Specifications pertaining to the geotechnical aspects of the development
In place density tests were performed periodically in general accordance with ASTM
Test Designations D 2922 Nuclear Method The test results are presented on the
attached Summary of Field Density Test Results Plates 3 1 through 3 6
FOUNDATION EXCAVATIONS
Foundation excavations for the subject building were observed by our field
representatives on an on call basis Based on the Project Soils Report prepared for
the subject project and further discussions with the project structural engineer we
understand that the footings for the building should be embedded a minimum of 6
inches into the underlying basalt formation in order to develop the necessary bearing
support for the proposed building Therefore in areas where basalt formation was not
encountered at the design footing subgrade elevations the footing subgrades were

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site especially since our services were
retained on an on call basis Therefore some of the earthwork were performed without
our observation However the level of earthwork observation and testing services
provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical risks to the
project It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical
repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and
materials Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted
standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied
Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it
guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the
quality of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing
defects regardless of when they are found
