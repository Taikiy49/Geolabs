Cut Slopes
The proposed site is mostly in relatively hard slightly
weathered tuff and is well suited to cut slopes
Aa and Pahoehoe Basalt
These rocks are generally dry hard to very hard
and sound Cuts at slopes of up to 1 2 horizon
tal to 1 vertical could be utilized however the
basalt was only noted in localized areas
Volcanic Tuff
These rocks are generally dry moderately hard and
sound The recommended cut slopes would depend on the
amount of cementation in the formation
Generally cuts at slopes of 1 horizontal to 1 verti
are recommended Some adversely dipping
rock layers slanting into the cut may be encoun
tered and cause localized sloughing
The tuff formation may be susceptible to erosion
and therefore should be vegetated soon after
cutting Generally slope benches should be
utilized for slope heights greater than 20 feet
Due to the complex nature and variability of the soil and
rock formations at the site it appears that a general
design cut slope ratio of 1 1 H V would be suitable for
the proposed cut
All cuts should be inspected by a qualified representative
of the soils engineer
Some variations in the soil conditions should be anticipated
and some slope adjustments may be required in the field
during construction
Soils
The materials generated from the cutting operations gen
erally less than about 8 inches in size should be suitable
for borrow material The volcanic tuff generally breaks
down to a sandy silt or silty sand with gravel and is non
expansive
