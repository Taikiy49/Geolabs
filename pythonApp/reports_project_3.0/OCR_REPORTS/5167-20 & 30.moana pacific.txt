PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Moana Pacific high rise condominium project is in the northwestern corner of
the intersection of Piikoi Street and Kapiolani Boulevard in the District of Honolulu on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The development consists of a five level above grade parking
garage and apartment storage encompassing the majority of the project site
Two oval shaped residential towers rise above the parking garage at the northeastern and
southwestern portions of the property Each residential tower consists of 46 stories of
dwelling units and two levels of penthouse units comprising a total of 416 living units for
each tower The layout of the residential development is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Site grading work generally consisted of minimal cuts and fills of up to about 2 feet
thick The scope of work discussed in this letter report includes observation and
compaction testing during the earthwork and infrastructure installation for the residential
development

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
JJS Construction performed the site grading operations pavement and _ utility
construction for the Moana Pacific project The site grading work consisted of subgrade
preparation placement and compaction of fills and backfills pavement construction and
utility trench excavation and backfill
Following the demolition clearing and foundation installation operations the
excavated subgrades underneath future slab and pavement areas were scarified to a
depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
proof rolled with a minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller to a minimum of 90 and
95 percent relative compaction under the building slab and pavement areas respectively
of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with American Society of
Testing and Material ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
Fill and backfill materials required for the project were generally placed in near level
lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 or
95 percent under the building slab and pavement areas respectively of the maximum dry
density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 664 field density tests including retests of failed
areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 2922
and D 3017 Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1
through 3 26 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces
and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading
pavement and utility construction for the above project observed and tested by Geolabs in
the period covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of
the project plans and specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations
presented in the referenced report dated and subsequent
recommendations

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some
of the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials
