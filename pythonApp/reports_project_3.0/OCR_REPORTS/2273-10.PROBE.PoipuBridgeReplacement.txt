In accordance with our proposal dated we have completed the
foundation probing and grouting services for the Poipu Bridge Replacement project This
report summarizes our field observations during the probing and grouting operations
performed at the subject site
The project site is located along Hoonani Road near the town of Poipu on the Island
of Kauai Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project involves the
construction of a two span cast in place concrete bridge structure over Waikomo Stream
The north and south abutments and the center pier will be supported on spread footings
bearing on the basalt formation encountered at relatively shallow depths at the site
The scope of work discussed in this letter report describes our probing and grouting
services along the footing alignments for the abutments and center pier The footings for the
abutments are 12 feet wide and the footing for the center pier is 10 feet wide as shown on the
Site Plan and Probe Location Layout Plate 2 While the elevations along the bottom of the
footings vary the footings are to bear on basalt rock embedded 6 inches below the top of
basalt rock surface As recommended in the geotechnical engineering report the area below
the footings should be probed and grouted as part of the foundation preparation The
foundation probing and grouting operation is an attempt to locate and grout potential
subsurface cavities and or voids beneath the abutment and pier footings
The bridge footings were located in the field by Kaikor Construction Associates prior
to our arrival at the job site Along the northern abutment existing grades at the probe

Geolabs performed the probing and grouting operations over a three day period The
probe holes were drilled with a Mobile B 40 drill rig equipped with a down the hole percussion
hammer The probe holes drilled were about 3 inches in diameter and extended from about
11 to 13 feet below the existing grade or about 10 feet below the bottom of footing elevations
A total of 15 probe holes were drilled along the pier and abutment lines as shown on Plate 2
The materials encountered during the probe drilling operations generally consisted of
medium hard to very hard basalt formations interbedded with layers of clinker material
Overlying the bedrock along the two abutment lines were approximately 2 to 5 feet of silty clay
fill materials Along the center pier alignment approximately 2 to 5 feet of alluvial soil deposits
basalt gravel in a silty clay matrix were observed overlying the bedrock Based on our
observations of the probing operation small cavities and or voids were encountered in 5 of the
15 probe holes drilled A summary of our foundation probe drilling and grouting observations
is provided on Plate 3
After completion of the probe drilling operations the probe holes were filled by injecting
grout into the probe holes The grout used to fill the probe holes consisted of a sand cement
grout mix with an unconfined compressive strength of about 2 500 psi Approximately
0 5 cubic yards of grout was used to fill the 15 probe holes and minor cavities encountered
The maximum grout intake was located at Probe No 13
In summary the foundation probing and grouting operations have been conducted in
general conformance with the intent of the specifications and the geotechnical engineering
exploration report Based on our observations it is our professional opinion that the minor
cavities encountered beneath the abutment and pier footings have been grouted and that the
subgrade area should provide the recommended foundation support for the bridge
Limitations
Elevations of the probes were estimated from the field measurements relative to the
on site survey stakes The elevations of the probe holes should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used Our services were performed in
