This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering services performed during
the construction for the Makalei Fire Station project on the Island of Hawaii
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project is in the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii The project
generally consisted of constructing a new fire station on an approximate 2 acre area at
two stories high New access roadways and parking areas consisting of flexible and
rigid pavements were also constructed In addition an individual wastewater system
was constructed for the restroom facility
EARTHWORK OPERATION
The general contractor for the project was Maryl Group Inc The earthwork for the
project was performed by CTS Earthmoving Inc Our observation and testing services
during the construction of the project occurred between June 2011 and March 2012
In general the subgrade preparation in fill areas consisted of ripping the original
ground to a depth of about 3 feet or until refusal using a ripper tooth of a Caterpillar
D 11 bulldozer to assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or voids
contained in the porous near surface materials After the fill surfaces have been ripped
the ground generally was leveled with the use of a Caterpillar D 11 bulldozer working the
surface for about four passes to provide a relatively level surface After leveling of the

Fill materials used for the project consisted of structural fills general fills and base
course materials Structural fills consisted of well graded granular materials generally
smaller than 6 inches in dimension In general the fill materials within the top 2 feet of the
lots within the fill areas of the pad consisted of structural fill materials It should be noted
that general fills consisting of boulders of up to about 18 inches in dimension generally
were placed about 2 feet below the finished grades within the pad areas of the lots and
choked off with finer sized materials
Structural fills required to raise the grades were generally placed in level lifts of
about 12 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned and compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction General fills were generally placed in level lifts of about
18 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned and compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction Base course materials were generally placed in level lifts
of about 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned and compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative compaction
During the earthwork operations samples of the imported materials utilized as fill
and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory
in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are
summarized on the attached Plate 2
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 131 field density tests were performed using
the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 6938 Compaction test results
are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 8 These tests were performed on the prepared
ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills Test locations and elevations
were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes and topographic
features These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were
reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the construction
of the Makalei Fire Station project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period
covered by this report were performed in general conformance with the intent of the
project plans and specifications and our geotechnical engineering recommendations

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance
nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for
the quality of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing
defects regardless of when they are found
