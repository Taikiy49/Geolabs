PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project involves the installation of a new fabric structure at the existing 
amphitheater courtyard area within the Ewa Makai Middle School in the District of Ewa 
on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii.  The project location and general vicinity are shown on 
the Project Location Map, Plate 1.  Based on the information provided, we understand 
that it is desired to support the new fabric structure, measuring approximately 135 feet 
by 85 feet in plan dimension, on drilled shaft foundations at the four corners.  A layout of 
the new fabric structure is shown on the Drilled Shaft Identification Plan, Plate 2. 
The structural loads were provided for the foundation design of the new fabric 
structure planned for the project.  The loads are applied at the bottom of the base

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS 
Based on the observed subsurface conditions encountered in the exploratory and 
drilled shaft borings, the project area is generally underlain by surface fill soils, 
consisting of very stiff clayey silts with coralline gravel, extending to depths of about 2 to 
3 feet below the existing ground surface.   
Below the surface fill soils, medium dense to dense coralline detritus interbedded 
with medium hard to hard sandstone and coral formation were encountered and 
extended to the maximum depth explored of approximately 32.5 feet below the existing 
ground surface. 
An underground power line encapsulated in the concrete jacket was encountered 
at a depth between about 6 to 9 feet during the excavation for drilled shaft P1 at the 
southwestern corner of the structure. 
Groundwater was generally encountered at depths of about 21.2 and 22.7 feet 
below the ground surface within the borings drilled at the time of our field exploration.  
The groundwater levels encountered generally correspond to about Elevations +0.3 and 
+0.8 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL). In general, groundwater was encountered at the 
bottom of the drilled shaft excavations at a depth greater than 21 feet.  
DRILLED SHAFT FOUNDATION INSTALLATION 
Kelikai Inc. performed the drilling and S & M Sakamoto, Inc. performed the 
concreting of the drilled shafts.  Four 60-inch diameter by 22 feet long shafts were 
installed at each corner of the New Fabric Structure at the Ewa Makai Middle School 
amphitheatre site from July 17 to 21, 2017. The approximate drilled shaft locations are 
shown on the Drilled Shaft Identification Plan, Plate 2. A Geolabs representative 
observed the shaft excavation, placement of the steel reinforcement, and concrete 
placement operations during the installation of the drilled shafts. 
The drilled shafts were excavated using a Bauer BG-15 rig equipped with 60-inch 
diameter tools that include an auger and a core barrel. A 57-inch clean out bucket was 
used to remove the cuttings and loose materials providing a relatively smooth and flat

surface at the bottom of the shaft excavations. The drilled shafts were excavated to 
elevations ranging from about +1.8 to + 2.5 feet MSL.   
After the shaft holes were drilled to their designated tip elevations, the reinforcing 
bars (rebar) cages were placed into the drilled holes. Rebar cage spacers were used to 
provide the clearance associated with the minimum concrete cover between the 
sidewalls of the drilled hole and the sides of the rebar cage. 
The shaft concrete was poured after placement of the rebar cage using a tremie 
pipe extending to near the bottom of the shaft. The concrete was placed in two stages in 
all of the shafts. The first pour filled the shafts up to an elevation of about +19.5 feet 
MSL. After the initial pour of concrete was allowed to set for at least one day, the anchor 
bolts and base plate templates were fitted into the exposed upper end of the rebar cage 
forming the pedestal.  Prior to placement of the second pour of concrete, incidental 
construction debris at the surface of the initial pour was removed with vacuum cleaners 
(“Shop Vacs”) to provide a clean interface between the first and second layer of 
concrete.  The second pour was deposited by directing the flow of the fresh concrete 
from the delivery chute of the transit mixers allowing it to drop into the upper shaft and 
pedestal assembly. A vibrator was utilized to reduce the formation of voids in the 
concrete during the pour which continued until the forms were filled to the top at +24.5 
feet MSL.   
Based on the above and our field observations, we believe that the drilled shafts 
for the New Fabric  Structure at the Ewa Makai Middle School project have been 
installed in general conformance with our recommendations and within the limits of 
generally accepted construction practices. The top of the shafts should be measured 
using optical survey equipment to verify that the shaft position is within the tolerance of 
the project requirements. Based on our observations of the drilled shaft installation, it is 
our professional opinion that the drilled shafts have been installed to sufficient depths to 
provide the design load capacity.
