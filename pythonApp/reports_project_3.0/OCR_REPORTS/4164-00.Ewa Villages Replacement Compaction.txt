Background Information
The project site is located in the Ewa Villages area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The project consisted of reconstruction of road prisms at 20 locations along Renton Road
Park Row and other minor roads The repair work generally consisted of removal of the
existing pavement section over excavation of subgrade materials backfilling and
compaction of the excavation and reconstruction of the pavement section
Because the quality of the subgrade conditions generally determines the
over excavation depths Geolabs Hawaii was retained to provide construction observation
services during the pavement repair operations A project engineer from our office observed
the excavation of repair sites to evaluate the subgrade conditions and to determine the
over excavation depth based on the subgrade conditions at each repair location
Earthwork Observation
The earthwork operations for the project were performed by Delta Construction
Corporation At the on set of earthwork asphalt concrete and asphalt treated base materials
within the contract repair limits were generally excavated and disposed of off site Following

the asphalt removal operations the existing base course and subbase materials were
excavated and taken to a designated stockpile area for future re use The base course and
subbase materials were generally crushed coral consisting of sandy gravel with some silt
The majority of the excavated coralline base materials were stockpiled and later re used as
select granular fill materials for backfilling
After excavation of the coralline base materials the subgrade was evaluated by our
project engineer The subgrade generally consisted of silty clay fills with varying amounts
of coral fragments The fills ranged from very soft to very stiff and damp to very moist Based
on visual observations and probe rod tests the depths of over excavation were determined
by our project engineer The over excavated subgrade materials were generally taken
off site for disposal
The over excavated subgrades were proof rolled with a Rammax sheepsfoot vibratory
compactor prior to placement of backfills In some areas based on the compacted
conditions of the subgrades bi axial geogrids were placed to improve the poor subgrade
conditions as recommended by our project engineer In areas where the compacted
subgrades were very stiff or hard the placement of geogrids was not required
The backfill materials for the earthwork generally consisted of the on site coralline
base materials generated from the excavations of the pavement prism and imported coralline
base course materials Samples of the backfill materials utilized during construction were
obtained and transported to our office for laboratory testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified
Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 1 of this report
During the fill placement operations the fill materials were generally placed in thin lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor The compaction
requirement for backfills within 3 feet of the finished pavement grades was increased to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Periodic field density tests were generally
performed for each lift during the fill placement and subgrade preparation operations to
confirm the degree of compaction and moisture content of the fill material achieved during
construction The backfills were compacted using a sheepsfoot roller attached to a large
backhoe excavator and or Rammax vibratory sheepsfoot roller
A total of 184 field density tests were performed using the nuclear probe method in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designations D 2922 and D 3017 The results of the
compaction tests performed during construction of the proposed project are summarized on
Plates 2 1 through 2 9 of this report Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory based on the
compaction tests were generally reworked and in most cases re tested until the specified
minimum compaction was obtained Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing

In some instances the contractor did not have the geogrids on site during excavation
and backfilling operations Therefore a non woven filter fabric was placed in some areas
as indicated in the table above In addition a small void was discovered within the repair
area during the excavation process at location SP 1C In order to remove the void and
soft loose materials underlying the void an area of approximately 4 feet by 4 feet was
over excavated to about 48 inches below the pavement section as opposed to 24 inches for
most of the SP 7C location
After excavation of the pavement and subgrade materials localized depressions and
voids were encountered in many locations Based on our field observations ground
subsidence occurred in some areas which appeared to have been subsequently patched
gutter
Generally these voids were about 4 to 10 inches in height and extended about 2 to
4 feet into the sidewalk The voids generally extended along the roadway up to about 20 feet
in length Because a portion of the existing curbs and or gutters were suspended the voids
were generally filled using controlled low strength material flowable fill In some cases the
contractor was reconstructing the sidewalks and or curbs gutters at the same time the
voids were being filled Therefore in these areas the voids were filled using Class A
concrete
Several small voids were also encountered within the repair areas at locations SP 1C
and SP 1F At location SP 1F the voids were detected during removal of the asphalt
concrete and asphalt concrete base however the surface subsequently collapsed during the
excavation process At location SP 1C the over excavation was extended until firm
subgrade materials were encountered
Based on the results of the compaction tests and observations by our representatives
during our site visits it is our professional opinion that the earthwork operations tested and
observed by Geolabs Hawaii for the Ewa Villages Pavement Repair project were performed
in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications
It should be noted that numerous voids were encountered under the curbs gutters
and or pavement areas In general the voids encountered were excavated and or filled with
concrete mix However based on our observations there are some indications that other
voids may be currently present along Renton Road Park Row and other minor roads in the
vicinity of the area that were not included as part of this roadway repair project
