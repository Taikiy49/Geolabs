INTRODUCTION
In accordance with your request we have conducted percolation testing at the
project site The approximate location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general accordance
with our proposal dated
This report summarizes our findings for the installation of a septic system at
the project site
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is situated in the Kahuku area of the island of Oahu Hawaii
It is proposed to construct a single family residence at the site Sanitary domestic
wastes will be generated by the residential development The estimated design flows
for the development are about 800 gallons per day Since the project is situated in
an unsewered area the wastes will be treated and disposed of by a septic system
The disposal of the treated effluent will be by the shallow leaching field method
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our testing at the site was to provide engineering and
geohydrologic data for the preparation of an engineering report of the design and
installation of the leaching field for the disposal of treated effluent

The scope of our work on the project included but was not limited to drilling
and sampling of two 2 shallow borings at the project site performance of
percolation testing in the borings analyses of the percolation test data and
preparation of this report
GEOHYDROLOGIC CONSIDERATIONS
The ground surface elevation at the site at the time of percolation testing was
approximately 10 feet above Mean Sea Level
The logs of the drilled borings are presented in Appendix A Generally the
borings encountered surface soils consisting of fine calcareous sand to the maximum
depths drilled of 6 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings pit at the time of our
drilling or testing
PERMEABILITY TESTING
The borings were drilled and sampled by a truck mounted rotary drilling rig
by the borings were taken for classification purposes The samples were obtained
by driving a 2 inch O D split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches When the borings had been drilled to a depth of approximately 5 feet below
the existing ground surface the subsurface permeability was tested by the falling
head method

FINDINGS AND CONCLUSIONS
Based on our field testing the average rate of fall for the last six 6 inches of
water ranged from about 5 minutes per inch to approximately 9 minutes per inch
Our testing indicates that the fine sand formation encountered at the site has sufficient
permeability for disposal of treated effluent by the leaching field method
Using the methods presented in U S Public Health Service Publication No
526 Manual of Septic Tank Practice it appears that the fine sand soils encountered
at the site are capable of accepting effluent from a septic tank at the rate of about 2
gallons per square foot of leach field trench bottom per day
For the disposal of 800 gallons per day this would require a net leaching field
area of 400 square feet Using a standard leach field trench design with a 2 foot wide
trench and six 6 inches of gravel below the leaching pipes this would require 200
lineal feet of trench The required length of trench may be reduced by placing
additional gravel below the pipe The percentage of the required trench length may

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from field data points such as drilled borings and
percolation testing Variations of conditions may occur and the nature and extent
of these variations may not become evident until construction and operation of the
septic system If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate
the findings presented in this report

APPENDIX A
Boring Logs Percolation Testing
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by the drilling and sampling
of two 2 borings to depths of about five 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled by a
truck mounted rotary drill rig advancing continuous flight augers
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Standard Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a standard split spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches
The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at
the appropriate sample depths
The materials encountered in the borings were examined and classified in general
accordance with ASTM Test Method D 2488 84 Description and Identification of Soils
Visual Manual Procedure by a geologist from our firm The logs of the borings are
shown on Plates A 1 and A 2
Falling head percolation tests were conducted in the borings to provide data
pertaining to the suitability of the on site soils for disposal of primary treated sewage
effluent by shallow leach fields The drilled borings were filled with clear fresh water and
the rate of drop in the water level was recorded with respect to time These tests are
presented on Plates A 3 and A 4

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our
field exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to schedule
appropriate tests to aid in determining pertinent engineering properties of the soil
and or rock materials sampled
Classification Tests
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Moisture Content ASTM Test Method D 2216 80 are used as an aid to soil
classification and to aid in determining certain properties such as relative saturation
The moisture content expressed as a percentage of the dry weight of the tested
sample is shown at the appropriate location on pilates summarizing test results and
at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs
Sieve Analysis tests ASTM Test Method D 422 63 are performed to determine
the grain size distribution of the soil The grain size distribution is used for
classification in the Unified Soil Classification System and to provide indices on other
engineering properties The results of these tests are summarized on Plates B 1
