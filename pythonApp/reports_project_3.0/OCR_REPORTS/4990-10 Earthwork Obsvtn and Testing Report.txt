BACKGROUND INFORMATION
The project site is located north of Hawaii Kai Drive between about Keahole
Place and Wailua Street in the Hawaii Kai area of eastern Honolulu on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii The site is generally bounded by the Nanea Kai Phase 1 project and the
Hale Alii at Hawaii Kai senior living development to the south the United States Post
Office Hawaii Kai Station to the east Kaluanui Road to the north and vacant land to
the west
The multi family housing development project consists of thirteen buildings
designated as Buildings 1 through 13 as shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 The
buildings are three stories high and constructed of light gauge metal framing with wood
trusses and asphalt shingle roofing Based on the grading plan provided cuts and fills
of up to about 10 feet thick were performed to create relatively level pads for the
building construction In addition retaining walls ranging from about 4 to 8 feet high

Asphalt concrete paved access road and parking lots were also constructed to serve the

Since the project site is located along the lower eastern flank of Kaluanui Ridge

EARTHWORK OPERATION
The site grading work and infrastructure installation were performed by Royal
Contracting Company The earthwork operations consisted of clearing and grubbing of
the existing ground over excavation of unsuitable materials preparation of slab and
pavement subgrades placement and compaction of non expansive capping fill select
borrow and base course and utility trench excavation and backfill It should be noted that
field observations of the building foundation excavation and preparation work were
performed by others and were specifically excluded from our scope of work for this project
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the existing ground was generally
over excavated where necessary to provide 24 inches of non expansive select granular
fill material capping fill at the building pad footprints and extending 2 feet beyond The
24 inch non expansive select granular capping fill was generally reduced to about
12 inches for the south side of Building 4 due to the presence of cemented sand and
volcanic tuff formation encountered at the slab subgrade level In general the
over excavated subgrades were moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent of the maximum dry
density During placement of the non expansive select granular fill layer the fill materials
were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density The maximum
dry density of the materials referred to in this report were determined in accordance with
American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor
Based on our field observations undocumented fills mixed with decomposing tree
branches and tree stumps were encountered near the existing ground surface within the
footprint of Building 3 at the project site We recommended that the undocumented fills be
removed to expose the natural in situ materials Additional probing with three excavated
test pits suggested that the extent of the undocumented fills measured approximately
75 feet long 25 feet wide and 6 feet deep Subsequent to the removal of the
undocumented fills the bottom of the over excavation was recompacted to a minimum of

90 percent relative compaction prior to backfilling with general fill materials up to the
bottom of the 2 foot non expansive capping layer General fill materials consisted of the
on site excavated soils with a maximum particle size of 6 inches and free of vegetation
adobe clays and other deleterious materials General fill materials were
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts
not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction The excavated test pits resulting from the probing operations were
also backfilled in accordance with the guidelines presented above
New sewer water and drain lines as well as catch basins and manholes were
installed to serve the new residential development In general a bedding layer
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel was provided below the piping and
manholes for uniform support Free draining granular materials consisting of
No 3B Fine gravel was also used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the
pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort
on the initial trench backfill The trench backfill was placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction Where
trenches are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the
pavement grade was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
During the earthwork operation samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 568 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the Nuclear Probe Method in accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 2922 The results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1
through 3 28 of this report These tests were taken on the prepared ground surfaces and
on constructed engineered fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes and construction plans
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the
specified minimum compaction was obtained

observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in
general accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications our geotechnical
engineering recommendations presented in the referenced report dated

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in
the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not
insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain
responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications and
for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
