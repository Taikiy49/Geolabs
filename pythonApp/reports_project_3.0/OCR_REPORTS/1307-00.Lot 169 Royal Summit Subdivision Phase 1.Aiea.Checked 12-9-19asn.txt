In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to
provide foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The
study consisted of drilling three shallow borings performing
laboratory testing and reviewing the subdivision site grading and
preliminary house foundation plans This report summarizes our
findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
Based on the preliminary house plans the proposed residence will be a
two story structure approximately 37 by 86 feet in plan dimensions
with a small partial basement at the southwest corner of the house
The general layout of the house foundation is shown on the Site Plan
Plate l

Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage driveway and the
entire house except for the wooden sun deck at the rear of the
residence The house will be of wood frame construction
For the proposed residence CMU retaining walls up to 8 feet high are
planned extending along the house perimeter from the southwest to the
southeast
Fills and backfills up to 5 5 feet thick are anticipated under the
main floor slab of the house in order to retain the existing grade at
the front of the site
Cuts generally less than 8 feet deep are anticipated for the partial
basement floor
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the southwestern end of Papalealii
Street in the Royal Summit Phase 1 Subdivision in Aiea Oahu Hawaii
A relatively level pad about 8 feet wide is situated along the front
portion of the site The ground surface then slopes downward at about
5 1 H V to a second level pad at the rear of the lot The ground
surface then slopes downward at a 2 1 H V slope for a vertical
height of about 30 feet The 2 1 slope is bisected at mid height by
an 8 foot wide bench

SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
Three borings were drilled within Lot 169 to depths ranging from 5 0
to 10 0 feet below the existing grade at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
In general the borings encountered very stiff reddish brow clayey
silt to the full depth of our borings A review of the mass grading
plan for Royal Summit Phase 1 dated prepared by
Community Planning Inc indicated that lot 169 received up to ll
feet of compacted fills over the natural grade
No groundwater was encountered at the time of our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The stiff reddish brown clayey silt soil encountered will generally be
suitable for foundation support of the proposed house provided that
some care is exercised during construction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 4
Laboratory tests performed on the stiff clayey silt soil indicates
that this material generally has low swelling potential if the
moisture of the soil is maintained over 30 Based on the findings
from our field exploration the present moisture content is quite dry
with moisture contents ranging from 22 to 27 Thus the swelling
potential could increase to a moderate range Therefore to minimize
the swelling potential the subgrade soil should be moistened and
protected from drying from the start of construction until final
concrete placement
Select non expansive soils such as select borrow crusher waste or
coral should be used for fill below the slab and backfill behind the
retaining walls to minimize the swelling potential of the natural
soil
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on
the compacted soil
Interior footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the finished grade Perimeter wall footings should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent finished grade to
provide a barrier against future moisture infiltration under the slab
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1307 00 Page 5
If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down an
additional amount to obtain a 6 foot minimum horizontal set back
distance measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope
face This additional embedment depth is required to help resist the
downhill slope creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing
Capacity of the soil
The southeastern corner of the house partial basement is located
about 2 5 feet from the existing sewer easement To minimize the
possibility of footing settlement due to settlement of the trench
backfill or due to sewer trench excavation at a later date for repair
purposes we recommend that the footings in this area be embedded
easement
Slab On Grade
The laboratory swell tests indicate that the on site soils have low
swelling potential if they are maintained at or above the optimum
moisture content 30 During the field exploration the near
surface soils were found to be a little drier than optimum moisture
content To reduce the soil swelling potential the subgrade soils
should be kept moist and not allowed to dry out from the start of
construction until the placement of concrete This would require
daily sprinkling of the subgrade soils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1307 00 Page 6
It is recommended that slabs on grade be placed over a 4 inch layer of
well graded base course aggregate as a cushion layer and should be
structurally separated from wall foundations
A plastic membrane should be placed over the cushion layer if a
moisture barrier is deemed necessary to protect the floor covering
Lot Grading
Structural fills and backfills should consist of hon expansive soil
such as select borrow or crusher waste and be placed in maximum
8 inch loose lifts and then compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density established by ASIM D 1557 78 The on site soils
in the Royal Summit area generally have low to moderate swelling
potential and would require special preparation prior to use as
structural fill Therefore the on site soil should only be used for
yard fill
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be cleared
of vegetation and debris
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 6 inches
in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
The site should be so graded to provide drainage of surface runoff
away from all foundations

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to
avoid ponding conditions Also it is advised that gutter
systems should be installed around the house and that gutter
water should be diverted far away from the foundations and
walkways Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and
Slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the
subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded
water could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled tight against the wall
or slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition

Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and
maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and
footing foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and
utility construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer
for review and comments prior to construction to see that the
intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
