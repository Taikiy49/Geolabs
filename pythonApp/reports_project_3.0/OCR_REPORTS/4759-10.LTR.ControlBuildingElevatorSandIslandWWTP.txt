PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The existing Sand Island Wastewater Treatment Plant is along Sand Island
Parkway in the Sand Island area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project
location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 As part of
the Primary Expansion Phase 1 development a new elevator will be constructed for the
existing Control Building at the location shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the subsurface conditions anticipated at the proposed elevator
location we envision that soft and or soft lagoonal deposits may be encountered at the
foundation subgrade level of the new elevator planned at the Control Building
Therefore we recommend using a deep foundation system to support the new elevator
planned The foundation system should penetrate through the highly compressible
lagoonal deposits and derive its load supporting capacity from the underlying hard coral
formation anticipated at depths between about 20 and 30 feet below the existing ground

Micropile Installation
The production micropiles installed at the site consisted of the cased micropile
system The components of the cased micropile system include an unbonded length
permanent steel casing a bonded length at the bottom to provide vertical load resistance
primarily from friction between the grout and surrounding soils and a steel reinforcement
bar extending the full depth of the drilled hole
The micropiles were installed by drilling 5 5 inch outside diameter steel casing with
a CASE 580L drill rig equipped with a Superdraw drill bit A steel casing with
Bitumastic 300M corrosion inhibiting coating was extended down to a depth of about
47 feet below the existing ground surface The steel casing was left in the drilled hole and
cleaned out by blowing air through the drill rods After the drilled hole was cleaned out a
single steel reinforcement with a nominal diameter of 1 27 inch was inserted into the drilled
hole down to near the bottom of the hole The micropile was grouted by tremie methods
until relatively clean grout was observed at the top of the steel casing The steel casing
was then extracted a length of about 16 feet to provide a minimum bonded length of
15 feet as required into the underlying hard coral formation to develop an allowable
compressive load capacity of 80 kips The micropile installation summary is presented on
the attached Plate 4
Micropile Proof Testing
In accordance with the project documents all production micropiles installed were
subjected to proof tests In general the proof tests consisted of loading the micropiles in
tension to a maximum load of 120 kips which is 150 percent of the allowable compressive
load capacity of 80 kips Geolabs Inc measured and recorded the associated
movements of the micropile head at each load increment up to the maximum load In
