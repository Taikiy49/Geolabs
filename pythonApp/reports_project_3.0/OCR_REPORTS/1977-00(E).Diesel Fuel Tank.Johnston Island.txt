INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Diesel Fuel Tank at Johnston
Island The general location of the site is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in general accordance with
our revised proposal dated
This report summarizes the findings from the field exploration and
our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses
based on data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction
bidding estimation purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct an above ground 13 500 barrel capacity
diesel fuel storage tank This storage tank is intended to replace an
existing facility which is approximately 25 years old
A concrete wall or a berm is planned to surround the fuel tank A
4 inch thick concrete lining is proposed between the tank and the berm to
certain potential product losses
Minimal site grading is anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface soil conditions at the site in order to develop a generalized
soil data set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertinent to the foundation design of the proposed diesel
fuel tank

The topography is generally level with ground elevations ranging from

Foundation Design
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
It is our understanding that a ring foundation may be used for the new
steel tank structure Our borings generally encountered dense silty sand
with gravel at the existing ground surface
Therefore it is our opinion
that a ring foundation bearing on the upper sandy fill material may be used
to support the tank
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1977 00 E Page 5
An allowable bearing value of up to 3500 p s f may be used for design
of a ring footing bearing on the dense sand and gravel fill material A
minimum footing embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished
grade is recommended
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth
pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the foundation
system and by friction resistance developed between the bottom of the
foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 350 p c f This assumes that the soil around
the foundation is well compacted A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may be
used
Settlement Characteristics
Based on the anticipated fuel loads and subsoil conditions settlements
are estimated to be on the order of 2 to 3 inches total at the center of the
tank and 1 to 2 inches along the tank perimeter
Based on soil data from Boring 3 it appears that a major portion of
the tank settlements at the center of the tank could be long term due to
consolidation of the underlying soft 10 foot thick silty clay layer Thus
it is recommended that the tank after fabrication and prior to
pipe drainage connections be loaded with water to initiate the ground
settlements
Incremental filling of the tank with water and concurrent settlement
monitoring is recommended The loading period required prior to normal
tank operation should be about three 3 months Potential leakage of the
tank may be checked during this program

Surcharge
Alternatively a surcharge program may be performed to initiate the
ground settlements prior to the tank construction
To induce the full estimated tank settlements under the anticipated
loads surcharge fills up to about 20 feet in thickness may be required If
this does not seem practical minimum 10 foot high surcharge fills may be
utilized instead We estimate that the 10 foot surcharge in a 3 month
period may induce about one half of the anticipated tank settlements due to
future fuel loads
The surcharge area should cover the general tank location and extend
about 10 feet beyond the perimeter of the tank area A settlement
monitoring program should be implemented to observe the rate and amount of
settlement This information may be used to determine when the surcharge
may be removed
After the initial surcharge fill program is completed it is still
recommended that the tank be incrementally filled and field tested with
water to detect potential leakage prior to diesel fuel storage
Berm Area
It is our understanding that a berm or a concrete wall may be planned
to surround the fuel tank The surcharge fill material could be
incorporated into the berm construction if feasible
For the berm the fill material should consist of on site sandy
material placed in maximum 10 inch loose level lifts and then compacted to a
minimum of 90 maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 If
required new imported fill material should consist of non expansive select
granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20
passing the 200 sieve
GEOLABS HAWAII
W E Page 7
A slope ratio of 2H 1V may be used for the fill slope The berm should
be over filled during construction and then trimmed back to the design
slope
If a concrete wall is planned a bearing value up to 3500 p s f with a
minimum footing embedment of 18 inches may be used for wall footings bearing
on the upper dense sandy fill material Estimated settlements are on the
order of less than one half of an inch total and a one fourth of an inch
differential
Site Preparation
Minimal site grading is anticipated A 4 inch thick concrete
containment lining is planned between the tank and berm or wall
Prior to the commencement of earthwork all areas within the grading
limits should be cleared and grubbed of all vegetation rubbish and
deleterious materials The resulting grub and spoil material should be
disposed of properly off site Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed
during the clearing operations should be over excavated to firm ground and
the resulting excavations should be backfilled with well compacted
engineered fill
Subsequent to clearing and grubbing the area within the grading limits
should be scarified to a depth of 6 inches moisture conditioned to
near optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of 90 maximum
dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 prior to the placement of concrete lining
The tank site should be graded to shed drainage water away from the
tank perimeter due to tank settlements
Concrete Lining
Construction of the concrete lining should be delayed until the
completion of the tank and after filling the tank with water for leakage
detection Minimal ground settlements are anticipated in the lining area
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1977 00 E Page 8
Due to the different settlement characteristics between the tank the
berm and the surrounding lining joints are recommended between the tank
structure the berm and the concrete lining in order to accommodate the
differential settlements
Joints are also recommended in the design of the concrete lining to
control potential cracks due to shrinkage or temperature changes
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior
to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to the
earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review is
not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during earthwork and construction phases
of the foundation work This is to observe compliance with the design
concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in
the event that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated prior to
start of construction The recommendations given in this report are
contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed soil rock conditions encountered during construction
are different from those assumed or considered in this report then
appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in
part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
Variations of conditions between the field data points may occur and the
nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined by
taping from the structures indicated on the plan undated provided to us
Elevations of the data points were approximately determined by estimation
from the plan The physical location and elevation of the field data points
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field
data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as
such may denote a gradual transition
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the times
given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this report
This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of
this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to
variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
