Soil Engineering Report
lot 3 Royal Summit Subdivision
Phase ITI
Aiea Cahu Hawaii
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling three shallow borings performing laboratory
testing and reviewing the subdivision site grading and preliminary house
foundation plans This report summarizes our findings and reconmenda
tions
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
Based on the preliminary house plans the proposed residence will
be a one story structure approximately 48 by 56 feet in plan dimensions

SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the western side of Ainanui Loop in
the Royal Summit Phase ITI Subdivision in Aiea on the island of Oahu
A relatively level pad about 30 feet wide is situated along the

along the rear lot boundary

SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
Three borings were drilled within Lot 3 to depths of 5 feet below
the existing grade at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 1
In general the borings encountered hard reddish brown clayey silt
to the full depth of our borings A review of the mass grading plan for
Royal Summit Phase III dated August 23 1984 prepared by Community
Planning Inc indicated that Lot 3 received up to 10 feet of compacted
fills over the natural grade
No groundwater was encountered at the time of our field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATT ONS
General
The hard reddish brown clayey silt soil encountered generally can
provide suitable foundation support for the proposed house provided that
some care is exercised during construction to minimize the swelling
potential of the soil
Laboratory tests performed on the hard clayey silt soil indicates
that this material generally has low to moderate swelling potential
Gepending on the moisture content of the soil
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 1555 00 Page 4
Therefore to minimize effects due to soil swelling under on grade
slabs we recommend that the on site soil be capped with at least 12
inches of non expansive select fill and that the subgrade soils
underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and protected from
drying from the start of construction until the slab concrete placement
Select non expansive soils should also be used as backfill behind
the retaining walls to minimize the additional pressure due to the
swelling potential of the natural soil
Lot_Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimum below
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive soil such as select
borrow crusher waste or coral Where cut is anticipated such as the
garage driveway and the lower house areas the on site soil should be
over excavated to provide a 12 inch minimum thickness of non expansive
select fill capping
All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being used in the site
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be
cleared of vegetation and debris

The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimm of 90 of its
maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Fills placed on existing slopes should start from the bottom up and
should be keyed and benched to bond the new fill to the existing slope
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff
away from all foundations
House Foundations
It is anticipated that about half of the house except its middle
portion will be of slab on grade construction The middle portion will
be supported by individual spread footing or retaining walls
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on the
compacted soil or stiff natural ground
Interior footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 8
inches below the finished grade Perimeter wall footings shovld be
embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the ad jacent finished grade
Surface water should be diverted away from foundations

Slab On Grade
It should be noted that some of the on site clayey silt soils are
moderately expansive and are marginally suitable to provide direct slab
support Therefore the on site soil should be over excavated down to a
depth of 12 inches below the slab bottom thoroughly moistened and then
replaced with non expansive imported soils as previously discussed The
thickened edge of the slab should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent finish grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the
underlying expansive soils we recommend that the in place clayey silt
below the upper capping fill pad be kept moist for one week prior to
concrete placement heavily soaked with water for the first two days and
sprinkled with water for the remainder of the week
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below the slab In order to protect against future moisture
infiltration an impervious moisture membrane may be incorporated below
the slab The on grade slabs should be structurally separated from wall
foundations
Concrete Driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving for
economy the 12 inch on site soil over excavation and replacement may be

eliminated if some small slab cracking is tolerable within the driveway
It is critical that the subgrade soils should be maintained in a moist
condition and prevented from drying out prior to concrete placement
Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot
intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some relative
movement of the concrete
Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that retaining walls generally less than 6
feet high may be required at the front of the proposed residence to
provide grade separation The guidelines for the retaining wall designs
are listed below
1 For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
Wall restrained at the top 55 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth
An allowable bearing value of up to 2500 p s f is
recommended for foundations bearing on imported select
borrow or on stiff on site soil

2 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth
may be used for the wall footing on level areas The passive
pressure must be reduced for walls on slopes
3 A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine
the resistance against sliding of the wall
4 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent
finished grade
5 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should
be utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall
Drainage
We reconmend that planters next to the foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from
entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving
or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly

backfilled tight against the wall or slab footing immediately after
setting of the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and main
tained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing
foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for lot grading foundation and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is reconmended that the lot grading footing excavations and
backfill operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been cbtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
