INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed 20 x 8 box culvert and northwest corner mass grading
project along Farrington Highway in the Villages of Kapolei on the island of Oahu
Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommenda
tions resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses
These recommendations are intended for preparation of design drawings and may not
be appropriate to form the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates nor for
construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the Villages of Kapolei development in the District
of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed to construct new concrete box
culverts and grade the project site located at the northwest corner of the Villages of
Kapolei
It is proposed to construct a new drainage system to convey surface water runoff
from Makakilo Gulch under Farrington Highway We understand that the drainage
system will consist of a partially lined inlet channel a twin 16 x 5 box culvert and a
20 x 8 box culvert The drainage system will be located across Farrington Highway
at about 240 feet southwest of the intersection of Farrington Highway and Keaianani
Avenue as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Flows into the new twin box culverts will
pass underneath Farrington Highway and through a transition section into the 20 x 8
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2820 10 Page 3
box culvert which will be connected to an existing box culvert traversing Kealanani
Avenue flowing towards Kapolei Golf Course
Based on the grading pian minor cuts and fills on the order of 2 to 3 feet are
anticipated at the project site It is our understanding that the existing Makakilo Gulch
located on the makai side of Farrington Highway will be backfilled during the mass
grading operation Fills on the order of 9 feet will be needed at Makakilo Gulch Based
on the earthwork summary shown on the grading plan excavation volumes will exceed
embankment volumes It is our understanding that underground utility construction is
not included in this phase of the construction

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from
two shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated
to be of middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be of late Pliocene
to early Pleistocene in age As volcanic activity in Waianae ceased lava flows from
Koolau banked against its eroded eastern slope forming a broad plateau now known
as the Schofield Plateau
Physical and chemical weathering followed by erosion of this plateau generated
sediments which were transported to the coast In the vicinity of the project site and
to the south these sediments accumulated and interbedded with marine sediments and
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2820 10 Page 6
coral algal reef formations to form a sedimentary wedge The thickness of the
sedimentary wedge ranges from zero in the area of the H 1 Freeway to over 1 000 feet
at Ewa Beach This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves as the confining formation
or caprock over the artesian basal aquifers of southern Oahu Deposition of
sediments has continued from earlier geologic time through the present

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
On the basis of our field exploration the project site is underlain by a surface
layer of very stiff to hard silty clays As previously indicated the surficial clays are
relatively dry and shrinkage cracks were noted during our exploration Due to the dry
and occasionally windy site conditions moisture conditioning of fill material will be
necessary Since the surficial clayey material contains substantial amount of silt it is
also advisable to institute an effective dust control program during grading operations
Special attention should be given to the recommended procedures for subgrade
preparation to reduce the swelling potential of the near surface clayey material
Based on our field exploration and laboratory testing we recommend that an
allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 p s f be used for the design of the box
culvert and inlet structure For lateral load resistance a passive earth pressure of
GEOLABS HAWAI

300 p s f per foot of depth may be used A frictional coefficient of 0 35 may be used
to evaluate sliding resistance developed between the base of the box culvert structure
and the underlying soil or cushion fill

grading and the box culvert are further discussed in the following sections
Site Grading
Based on the grading plan only minor cuts and fills on the order of about 2 to
3 feet or less would be needed at the project site to achieve the final design grades
We understand that the existing Makakilo Gulch located on the makai side of Farrington
Highway will be backfilled during the mass grading operation Fills on the order of
9 feet will be needed at Makakilo Gulch
Items of site grading that are addressed in the following subsections include the
following 1 Site Preparation 2 Fill Material 3 Fill Placement and Compaction and
4 Slopes
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the grading limits should be thoroughly
cleared of trees and other vegetation debris rubbish boulders and other
deleterious materials These materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site Soft sediments if encountered at the bottom of Makakilo
Gulch should be completely removed to expose firm soils
Any zones of wet saturated or yielding areas encountered during the clearing
process should be over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2820 10 Page 9
excavations should be backfilled with well compacted engineered fill Contract
documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation
and engineered fill placement to account for variations in the over excavation
quantities
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement
weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and
replacement with well compacted engineered fill especially at the temporary
runoff storage area
In areas to receive fill the ground surface should be scarified to a depth of 12
inches moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above optimum moisture
content and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Designa
tion D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content corresponding to the
maximum dry density
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after scarification we recommend the soil be
thoroughly moistened to close all cracks
Fill Material
All fill and backfill material should be evaluated by the project geotechnical
engineer prior to use In general the excavated soil materials should be suitable
for use as fill material provided that they are properly moisture conditioned as
recommended herein Any boulders or cobbles larger than 6 inches in maximum
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 2820 10 Page 10
dimension should be separated from the excavated material and disposed of off
site
imported material if required should be non expansive with no particles greater
than 3 inches in maximum dimension and no more than 15 percent passing the
No 200 sieve
If additional off site borrow soil is required it should be tested by the project
geotechnical engineer to evaluate its suitability as fill for the project prior to its
delivery to the project site
Fill Placement and Compaction
Fills should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above optimum
moisture content placed in maximum 8 inch thick loose level lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction unless specified
otherwise Each layer should be spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain
uniformity of the material and moisture content
Based on the grading plan fill thickness on the order of 9 feet will be required
to backfill Makakilo Gulch located on the eastern portion of the project site If
future improvements are planned above the existing Makakilo Gulch the
improvements may span partially over the approximately 9 feet of new fills
needed to backfill the gulch and partially over the existing stiff to hard ground
We anticipate some settlements of the new fills will occur with only small
settlements estimated for the portions of improvements over existing ground
Therefore larger than normal differential settlements may be expected Because
of this we recommend that fills required to backfill Makakilo Gulch be moisture
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2820 10 Page 11
conditioned to above optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than
95 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential differential settlements of
future improvement
Observations and soil density tests should be performed by the project
geotechnical engineer during grading to assist the contractor in obtaining the
required degree of compaction and the proper moisture content on each fill lift
Where compaction is less than required additional compactive effort should be
applied with adjustment of moisture content as necessary to obtain the specified
compaction Where moisture content is less than required additional scarifi
cation and moisture conditioning may be necessary
In general slope ratios of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V may be used to
design cut slopes and fill slopes which are compacted to 90 percent relative
compaction or more Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling beyond the
design slope limits and cutting back to the design slope to provide adequate
compaction at the edge of the slope In the event over cutting occurs
consideration should be given to leaving the slope rather than attempting to
backfill the slope to design grade If backfilling of an over cut slope is required
keying and benching requirements should be implemented
Where the existing ground is steeper than five horizontal to one vertical 5H 1V
keying and benching will be required during fill placement to properly bond the
new fill to the existing slope The filling operations should start at the lowest
point and continue up in level horizontal compacted layers in accordance with
the above recommendations for fill placement

The on site surficial soils are highly susceptible to erosion As a result water
should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope planting should be
done as soon as possible to reduce the potential for erosion We also
recommend that V ditches be provided at the top of slope especially in areas
around the inlet channel to reduce the potential for erosion In addition
placement of geotextile fabrics on the slope should be considered to reduce

Box Culvert
Based on the subsoil conditions encountered during the exploration it appears
that the box culvert will be founded on the underlying very dense silty sands and very
hard silty clays encountered in our drilled borings at the proposed base of structure
elevation
Box Culvert and Inlet Structure
Based on our field exploration and laboratory testing we recommend that an
allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 p s f be used for the design of the box
culvert and inlet structure bearing on the in situ silty sands and silty clays The
allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third for transient loadings such as wind or seismic forces
For a precast box culvert structure we recommend that the box culvert be
supported on a cushion fill consisting of 12 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM
C 33 No 67 gradation wrapped in a filter fabric such as Mirafi 140N or
equivalent to reduce the potential for migration of fines into the cushion fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2820 10 Page 13
For a cast in place box culvert structure the cushion fill and filter fabric may be
eliminated and the concrete structure should be cast neat against the in situ soil
Prior to concrete placement the subgrade should be scarified to a minimum of
6 inches moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture content and
compacted to 95 percent relative compaction to provide a more uniform bearing
surface
Lateral Earth Pressures
Assuming granular backfill is used the following lateral earth pressures are
recommended for the design of the box culvert and inlet structures
1 Level Backfill Condition
Walls unrestrained at the top K 35 p c f equivalent
active condition fluid pressure
Walls restrained at the top K 50 p c f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
Sloping Backfill Condition
Walls unrestrained at the top K 50 p c f equivalent
active condition fluid pressure
Walls restrained at the top K 65 p c f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
Surcharge stresses due to traffic areal surcharges line loads and point loads
within a horizontal distance equal to the depth to the bottom of the box culvert
or inlet structure should be considered in the design For uniform surcharge
stresses imposed on the loaded side of an unrestrained wall a rectangular
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2820 10 Page 14
distribution with uniform pressure equal to 30 percent of the vertical surcharge
pressure acting on the entire height of the wall may be used in design For walls
that are restrained at the top a rectangular distribution with uniform pressure
equal to 45 percent of the vertical surcharge pressure acting on the entire height
of the wall may be used
Box culvert and inlet structures should be well drained to reduce the build up of
hydrostatic pressures A typical drainage system would consist of a 1 to 2 foot
wide zone of permeable material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation immediately adjacent to the wall with a perforated pipe
perforations down at the base of the wall discharging to a discharge facility
A filter fabric such as Mirafi 140N or equivalent should be placed between the
drainage material and the compacted backfill material As an alternative a
prefabricated geocomposite drainage system such as MiraDrain or EnkaDrain
may be used instead of the drainage material with filter fabric The prefabricated
drainage product should also be hydraulically connected to a perforated pipe at
the base of the wall The geocomposite drainage system should meet the
requirements in Section 646 of the Department of Transportation State of
Hawaii Standard Special Provisions for Road and Bridge Construction
Lateral Load Resistance
Lateral loads may be resisted by a combination of passive earth pressure
exerted against the box culvert structures and soil friction between the base of
the box culvert structures and the supporting subgrade For lateral load
resistance a passive earth pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth may be used
Unless covered by pavement or slabs the passive resistance in the upper
12 inches of soil should be neglected A frictional coefficient of 0 35 may be
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 2820 10 Page 15
used to evaluate the sliding resistance developed between the base of the box
culvert structure and the underlying soil or cushion fill
Backfill Behind Box Culvert and Iniet Structure
The backfill at the box culvert and the inlet structure up to 1 foot below the finish
grade should consist of non expansive material such as the excavated on site
sand or select granular material the on site near surface silty clay is considered
too expansive and is not recommended for use as backfill material Unless
wrapped in a filter fabric free draining granular material such as No 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should not be used as backfill material to
reduce the potential for migration of fines into the gravels To reduce possible
future ground subsidence the fill should be placed in level horizontal lifts not
exceeding 8 inches in thickness moisture conditioned to above optimum
moisture content and mechanically compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction
The top 1 foot of backfill should consist of non expansive impervious material
to reduce infiltration of surface water behind the wall

Excavation Method
It is anticipated that conventional excavation methods using large equipment
may be used to excavate the very hard silty clays with boulders and very dense

for excavation should be determined by the contractor subject to practical limits
and safety considerations
Excavation Support
The excavation support and shoring system used must comply with all applicable
safety requirements It is recommended that a qualified geotechnical engineer
be retained by the contractor to design and evaluate the shoring system used
The adequacy and safety of the shoring installation should be made the sole
responsibility of the contractor His representative who should be required to
be continuously present on site during excavation and construction work will
have the best opportunity to promptly observe any changing conditions during
construction such as unforeseen subsoils unexpectedly high groundwater table
inappropriate construction sequences or techniques etc which may affect
shoring stability
However it must be noted that some movement of the shoring system and the
adjacent ground may still occur due to changes in earth stresses during
excavation Due to the complexity of the stress changes it is difficult to predict
accurately the magnitude of movement The magnitude also depends greatly
upon workmanship e g how quickly and tightly the shoring and bracing
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2820 10 Page 17
supports are installed the subsoil conditions the size of the excavation and the
rate of excavation
Therefore it is important to realize that the excavation shoring should be
installed properly and as early as practicable if necessary and that the adjacent
ground should be continuously monitored for cracks dips and or other
indications of movements with instruments
