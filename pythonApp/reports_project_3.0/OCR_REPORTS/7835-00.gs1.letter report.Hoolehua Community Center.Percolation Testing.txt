Dear Mr. Mow: 
In accordance with our fee proposal dated November 6, 2018, this letter report 
presents the results of Percolation Testing performed at the Hoolehua Community Center 
project on the Island of Molokai, Hawaii. The project location and general vicinity are shown 
on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
This report summarizes the results of the Percolation Testing performed for the 
proposed project. The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the 
limitations noted at the end of this report. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project site is located at the Hoolehua Community Center facility on the Island 
of Molokai, Hawaii.  We understand that it is desired to perform percolation testing at the 
perimeter of the existing leach field as shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS 
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and sampling 
three borings, designated as Boring Nos. 1 through 3, to a depth of about 5 feet below the 
existing ground surface.  The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan, 
Plate 2. 
Our field exploration generally encountered a surface fill layer about 2 to 2.5 feet 
thick.  The surface fill layer was underlain by residual soil extending to about 5 feet below 
the existing ground surface, the maximum depth explored.  The surface fill layer consisted 
of silty clay with little sand and gravel.  The residual soil was composed of silty clay with 
some sand.

Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field 
exploration.  It should be noted that groundwater levels at the project site may fluctuate 
depending on seasonal precipitation and other factors.  Detailed descriptions of the 
materials encountered in the borings are presented on the Logs of Borings, Plates A-1 
through A-3.  Results of the laboratory tests performed on selected soil samples are 
presented on Plate B-1. 
PERCOLATION TESTING 
Percolation testing was performed in each of the three borings to a depth of about 5 
feet below the existing ground surface.  The percolation testing was performed in general 
accordance with the provisions of Chapter 11-62, “Wastewater Systems”, State of Hawaii, 
Department of Health.  The measured percolation rates ranged from 9.23 to 20 minutes per 
inch.  The percolation test results are presented on Plates C-1 through C-3.  
LIMITATIONS 
The findings submitted in this report are based upon information obtained from the 
percolation testing performed in the borings.  Variations of conditions beyond the boring may 
occur and variations of flow conditions may occur over time. 
The boring locations were estimated by taping from reference points and visible 
features shown on the Utility Plan dated October 8, 1999.  The field boring location should 
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. 
The stratification breaks shown on graphic representations of the boring depict the 
approximate boundaries between soil/rock types and, as such, may denote a gradual 
transition.  Water level data from the boring was measured at the time shown on the graphic 
representations and/or presented in the text of this report.  These data have been reviewed 
and interpretations made to formulate this report.  However, it must be noted that fluctuation 
may occur due to variation in the seasonal rainfall and other factors.
