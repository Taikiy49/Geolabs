SUMMARY
Inclinometer and 4 indicated some increased movement during the last
12 months of the monitoring period with the total lateral movement of about 0 3 to
0 6 inches Some shallow depth subsurface ground movement and or creep at their
respective instrument locations were measured with average lateral movement rates of
about 0 2 to 0 3 inches per year However Inclinometer and 6 through 9 do
not indicate significant subsurface ground lateral movement within this monitoring
period It should be noted that Inclinometer No 5 used to indicate some ground
movement within the last monitoring period but less than 0 1 inch within this monitoring
period
Our piezometer data indicates that subsurface water seepage occurred at the
project site Based on our pavement deformation observation we believe that lateral
near surface earth movement and or creep at the site is still active
Based on inclinometer Nos 1 through 6 it appears that the sliding zone may be
relatively shallow approximately 10 to 20 feet below the ground surface We believe
that the ground movement slip circle may not extend to the existing slope toe as
evident by no significant ground movement measured by Inclinometer Nos 7 and 8
This may be partially due to the buttress installed at the slope toe in the previous
landslide remediation project in the 1970 s
Inclinometer No 9 indicated lateral creep movement at shallow depths We
believe that this creep movement may be related to traffic surcharge laterally acting on
the surface fill materials and medium stiff clayey soils encountered in the boring
Leaning angle of the existing electrical utility pole was measured with increasing from
about 7 degrees in 2015 to 11 degrees in 2016 from vertical axial which may be caused
by the creeping ground movement
Status of Earth Movements
The project road and adjacent slope continue to experience slow downhill creep
movements Based on the history of the earth movement monitoring sudden
catastrophic earth movements are not likely however cannot be completely ruled out
Extreme rainfall events and the increased subsurface groundwater seepage associated
with such rain events have the potential to accelerate the earth movement in the short
term and cause significant disruption to the existing pavements and fixed structures in
the area
The continued earth movements will continue to adversely affect road surfaces
and buried utilities Continued maintenance of the infrastructure is important to reduce
sources of water infiltrating into the subsurface which can accelerate movements

Long Term Repair Options
A long term repair option utilizing a drilled shaft wall to support the road fill has
been developed and is described in our report entitled Geotechnical Engineering
Services Mona Street Landslide Remediation Hawaii dated
In addition interim roadway stabilization is currently under design for a
short term mitigation The interim stabilization consists of reinforcing embankment fills
through the entire earth movement limits along Mona Street to reduce the earth
movement rate and to prolong the life of the infrastructure
Status of Earth Movements
We will continue monitoring the lateral ground movement through the installed
instrumentations and mapping the pavement conditions
