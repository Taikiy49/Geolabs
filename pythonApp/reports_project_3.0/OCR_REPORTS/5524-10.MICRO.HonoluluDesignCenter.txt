As requested Geolabs has provided geotechnical engineering observation and
consultation services during the micropile foundation installation for the Honolulu Design
Center project located in the Honolulu area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This report
summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed during the micropile
installation for the above project
Geotechnical recommendations pertaining to the micropile foundations were
presented in our geotechnical engineering report entitled Geotechnical Engineering
Exploration Honolulu Design Center Honolulu Oahu Hawaii dated
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering observation and consultation services was to
observe the micropile foundation installation for general conformance with our
recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Honolulu Design Center project site is located between the Moana Pacific
development currently under construction and Kapiolani Boulevard in the Honolulu area
on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 The project address is 1230 1250 and 1286 Kapiolani
Boulevard and the site is bounded by Piikoi Street on the east and Pensacola Street on
the west
Based on the information provided the proposed design center consists of a
three story building structure on the order of about 45 feet in height We envision the new
design center will be of concrete construction encompassing a total floor area of
approximately 26 300 square feet on the ground level Based on the information provided
by the project structural engineer we understand the maximum load for the interior
columns may be on the order of about 405 kips per column

The new design center will be supported by a deep foundation system consisting of
a total of approximately 256 planned micropiles The reinforcing bars for the micropiles
consisted of the Titan 73 53 bars with a minimum drill bit diameter of 5 inches for the
project Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings drilled we believe
the micropiles would need to extend to a minimum depth of about 40 feet below the
ground surface to develop an allowable compressive load capacity of 120 kips 60 tons
per micropile As part of the construction requirements one static load test and
approximately 25 proof tests about 10 percent of the micropiles were conducted on
selected micropiles to evaluate the adequacy of the foundation support system
MICROPILE INSTALLATION
As indicated previously the micropiles installed consisted of Titan
The Titan micropile system consists of hollow steel rods that are continuously advanced
into the ground These drill rods are abandoned in the hole and serve as the reinforcing
steel for the micropile The micropiles were drilled using a forklift mounted rotary
percussion drill rig Drilling fluid consisting of a neat cement grout mixture flushes soil
cuttings from the hole and creates a grout bulb around the drill rod The drilling of the hole
installing the reinforcing member and grouting of the micropiles generally occurs in a
single operation
Based on subsequent discussions with Con Tech Systems Ltd manufacturer of
the Titan micropiles during the production micropile installation we recommended that the
drilling grouting system be modified to provide a flushing grout with a water cement ratio of
for the grouting operation at the full depth of the micropile
Static Load Test
In accordance with the project specifications a compressive load test was
conducted on a selected micropile designated as Micropile No 166 on
The load test micropile was loaded to a test load of 240 kips 200 percent of the design
load of 120 kips in 15 kip load increments in general accordance with the Quick Load
Test Method ASTM D 1143 The static load test results were provided in the micropile load
test report dated prepared by Meta Engineering
Based on our field observations and the results of the static load test we believe
that the equipment and method of installation generally are acceptable for installing the
specified micropiles in general accordance with the project specifications In addition it is
our professional opinion that the load test micropile has been installed to sufficient depth
and or resistance to provide the design load capacity of 120 kips 60 tons per micropile
with a factor of safety of 2 0
Based on the results of the static load test we recommended the production
micropiles for the project extend a minimum of 35 feet below the bottom elevation of the

Production Micropiles
Installation of the production micropiles and the load test micropile was performed
by Pacific Ground Systems Inc A total of 265 micropiles including replacement micropile
micropiles installed at wrong locations and additional micropiles to compensate for the
failed proof tests were installed from February 8 through The approximate
locations of the 265 micropiles installed are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2
The micropiles generally extend to depths ranging from about 25 to 47 feet below
the bottom of the cap elevations The tip elevations of the installed micropiles
corresponded to about 22 2 to 44 2 feet Mean Sea Level MSL A Summary of Installed
Micropiles is presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 9 for reference

tests were conducted on a total of 29 micropiles including

micropile movement was measured with each load increment using two dial gauges and a
surveyor s level In addition the maximum load was held for 10 or 60 minutes to evaluate

tested micropiles

generally accepted construction practices Based on our observations of the micropile
