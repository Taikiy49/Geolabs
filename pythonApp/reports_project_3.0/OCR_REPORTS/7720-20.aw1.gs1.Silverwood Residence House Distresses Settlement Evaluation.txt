This letter report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering desktop study 
performed for the Silverwood Residence, House Distress and Settlement Evaluation 
Study, Kukui’ula Lot 58 (Subdivision Y) in the Koloa area on the Island of Kauai, Hawaii. 
This report summarizes our findings and presents our discussion as to the probable 
cause of the distress observed based on our recent site visits and floor level surveys 
provided by Tommy Oi Land Surveyor, LLC, dated between February 9, 2022 through 
April 27, 2022, and a desktop study for the project.  The findings and our discussion 
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The Silverwood Residence is a two-story structure with attached garages located 
at Lot 58 of Subdivision Y within the Kukui’ula Resort Community in Koloa on the Island 
of Kauai, Hawaii. 
Prior to construction of the home, Geolabs, Inc. conducted a geotechnical 
engineering exploration of the site and geotechnical recommendations were provided in 
our report entitled “Geotechnical Engineering Exploration, Silverwood Residence, 
Kukui’ula Lot 58 (Subdivision Y), Koloa, Kauai, Hawaii, TMK: (4) 2-6-016: 058,” dated 
June 28, 2018. 
The mass and site grading for the project were completed in September of 2019 
by Pac Build, the general contractor for the project.  The existing clayey subgrade soil 
was scarified and moisture-conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum water 
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent compaction prior to fill placement. 
The building is supported on shallow spread and continuous footings, and concrete

OBSERVED DISTRESSES DURING SITE VISIT ON FEBRUARY 23, 2022 
The distresses observed on our site visit on February 23, 2022 were located 
within the first floor entry room, master bedroom/bathroom/closet, east garage, and 
second floor terrace. The observed distresses generally consisted of cracks and 
separations ranging from hairline to about ½-inch wide.  The majority of the cracks were 
hairline to 1/8-inch wide. The widest crack was observed between the exterior tile and 
the sidewall outside of the master bathroom. Several of the observed cracks appears to 
be between adjoining gypsum boards. 
The observed distresses in the entry room consisted of a crack in the foundation 
slab from the front door to the hallway to the master bedroom, and separation of base 
boards from the walls at the east garage hallway. In addition, the crack within the entry 
room appears to continue out on the front lanai.  
Within the master bedroom, an uneven floor was noticed with the separation of 
base boards along the east wall, and drywall cracks at the ceiling were generally 
located at the corners and between adjoining gypsum boards. Cabinetry within the 
master closet showed severe distress with bowing of cabinetry woodwork and splitting 
of backing boards. In addition, the exterior tile outside of the master bathroom appears 
to have separated from the house.  
The east garage showed signs of distress with drywall cracks around the east 
window openings, ceiling corners, above the garage door, along the south wall and 
within the garage closet on the west wall. The door to the entry room was also

FLOOR LEVEL SURVEY 
Periodic floor level surveys were performed between February 9, 2022 and 
April 27, 2022 on the first floor levels of the house by licensed surveyor Tommy Oi Land 
Surveyor, LLC.  The floor level survey recorded the elevations of the floor utilizing 
survey equipment referencing a known stable elevation outside of the house. The 
measured readings ranged from +28.058 feet to +28.353 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL), 
or a difference of approximately 3.54 inches. The +28.058 feet reading was located 
within the master bathroom, and the +28.353 feet reading was located in the southwest 
corner of the first floor master bedroom.  A floor level survey of the second floor was not 
conducted. The survey locations are presented on the First Floor Level Survey Plan, 
Plate 3.1.  Cross-sections of the first floor surveys are presented on Cross-Sections 
A-A’ to L-L’, Plates 4.1 and 4.12, respectively. 
FINDINGS AND PROBABLE CAUSE OF DISTRESS 
Cracks and separations ranging from hairline to about ½-inch wide were 
observed on the walls, floors, baseboards, and exterior tile at the first floor entry room, 
master bedroom/bathroom/closet, east garage, and second floor outdoor terrace. 
The results of the floor level survey performed generally indicates that the first 
floor level appears to be settling from the entry room to the east side of the house 
affecting the entry room, front porch, master bedroom/bathroom/closet, and east 
garage. 
Based on the observed distresses, floor level survey results, and documented 
water leakage events, we believe the probable cause of the observed distresses is due 
to the excessive amount of water from the water leakage associated with the irrigation 
valves and koi pond that may have gotten into the underlying clayey subgrade soils 
below the concrete slabs-on-grade and footings. With the addition of water to the clayey 
subgrade soils, these clayey soils tend to swell and then soften and cause settlement of 
the structure bearing on these softened soils.
