INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of a subsurface investigation
undertaken at Fueling Pier No 149 at the Marine Corps Air Station
Kaneohe Bay Oahu Hawaii The purpose of the investigation was to
provide soils data for the repair design of the Fueling Pier No 149
The scope of this investigation includes the drilling of three
exploratory borings an evaluation of existing geologic and soils
information made available laboratory testing engineering analysis
and evaluation to develop information and recommendations for the
proposed design
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the south side of the U S Marine Corps
Air Station at Kaneohe Bay Oahu Hawaii The general area of the
project sites is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

The existing Fueling Pier No 149 consists of a reinforced concrete

square concrete piles There is a timber curtain fender system along
the perimeter of the pier The general layout of the pier is shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2

It is our understanding that the existing fender system has dete

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the Mokapu Peninsula of the Island of
Oahu The foundation of this peninsula was deposited by several vents
of the Honolulu Volcanic Series during the Kahipa low stand of the
sea approximately 300 feet below present sea level These vents laid
down extensive deposits of pyroclastic materials in the form of ash
and cinder with some associated lava flows The ash deposits have
consolidated to form a volcanic tuff
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1154 00 Page 3
Since the deposition of these materials during Kahipa time sea level
has risen and fallen several times These changes in sea level have
resulted in the deposition of coral algal materials over portions of
the peninsula Later stream activities deposited reworked cinder
material over coral
During the past century the efforts of man have shaped the site to
its present form by the placement of fill materials over the older
marine deposits
Field Exploration
Three exploratory borings were drilled at the site to depths ranging
from 60 5 to 68 0 feet from the top of pier deck to investigate the
subsurface conditions The approximate boring locations are presented
on the Site Plan Plate 2
The subsurface consist of several feet of very soft bay sediment a
moderately thick layer of stiff clayey silt and finally a coralline
sand and gravel deposit to the maximum depth explored
The very soft bay sediment is about 5 to 8 feet thick and consists
of light bluish gray silty clay with fine sand shell fragments and
occasional organic matter The layer of stiff clayey silt below the
bay sediment is about 10 to 15 feet thick and contains highly
weathered and decomposed sand and gravel size tuffaceous particles
GEOLABS HAWAII
Occasional gray stiff silty clay pockets are also present in this
layer which is probably decomposed old cinder deposit from Mokapu
vent This stiff clayey silt layer is underlain by a thick deposit
of dense coralline sand and gravel The coralline sand and gravel
deposit is moderately cemented to well cemented and forms a ledge
that contain numerous small cavities These cavities are partially
infilled with stiff gray clay within the depth explored
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
It is our understanding that for the proposed fender pile system the
piling may cosist of wooden or steel pile While steel piles would
not be subjected to biological attack wood piles would resist
corrosion and may have a lower initial cost
For wood piles they should be at least 62 feet long driven through
the stiff clayey silt and to coral ledge beneath It is assumed that
dredging will not extend 35 feet below MLW or about 40 feet below the
top of the pier deck The butt diameter should be at least 12 inches
The piles should be driven by a hammer delivering an energy not in
excess of lbs per blow to avoid overdriving The pile
should be stopped when it has penetrated at least 45 feet below the
top of the pier deck and encountered a penetration resistance of 2
blows or more per inch Over driving of wood pile should be avoided
to prevent blooming of pile tip or decreasing in lateral resistance
from excessive lateral flexture during hard driving
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1154 00 Page 5
For steel piles pipe or H shaped piles may be used For steel pile
driving a slight embedment of 6 inches into coral is recommended to
assure a better seating at the bottom Steel piles may be stopped
when encountering a penetration resistance of 3 blows per inch for the
final 3 inches
The pile installation should be observed by a soils engineer
We believe that Douglas fir wood piles installed as described above
may be assumed to develop a fixity about 4 feet below the top of stiff
clayey silt or about 46 feet below the top of deck For other pile
types the assumed fixity will depend on the stiffness and size of
pile It is noted that the above assumed fixity is simplified
approximation
In general it may be assumed that the gray brown stiff clayey silt
will provide a uniform lateral passive soil resistance of 1200 p s f
below the top of stiff clayey silt about 42 feet below top of deck

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 6 to 68 0 feet below the existing pier
deck surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 Truck mounted equipment was used to drill the
borings
The soil and rock encountered in the borings were
classified by visual and textural examination in the field by
our engineer and or geologist who continously monitored the
drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory
The soils were classified in general conformance with the
Unified Soil Classification System A graphic presentation
of the materials encountered is presented on the Log of
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving
with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts
to drive the sampler 12 inches are shown on the log of
borings at the appropriate sample depths
Rock core samples were obtained by using a rotary
wash NWD4 series double tube core barrel The core recovery
and rock quality designation RQD are presented on the log
of borings
