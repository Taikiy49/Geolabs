INTRODUCTION
Geolabs Hawaii under contract to Waste Management of has
performed quality assurance quality control observation and testing for the construction
of the Phase IV ash monofill base liner at the Waimanalo Gulch Sanitary Landfill The
general location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Figure 1
W O 2458 50 Page 2
The design of the ash monofill base liner section consists of bottom to top
1 A three 3 foot thick layer of compacted soil liner with design permeability
values equal to or lower than
2 A layer of 60 mil high density polyethylene HDPE geomembrane
3 A geonet drainage layer
4 A layer of 16 ounce per square yard non woven geotextile
This report provides a summary of 1 the observation and soil testing associated
with the construction of the soil liner 2 the results of the geosynthetic material
conformance testing and 3 the observation and testing of the geomembrane liner
installation

GEOMEMBRANE INSTALLATION
Approximately 235 425 square feet of 60 mil HDPE geomembrane comprising the
new shipment and left over from the previous phases of geosynthetic liner installation
were delivered to the site Approximately 229 000 square feet of the 60 mil HDPE
geomembrane were used for the present Phase IV lining system out of which 142 000

Geomembrane Deployment
Deployment of the 60 mil HDPE geomembrane began on and was
completed on Deployment and welding progressed continuously throughout
this period
The deployment of the geomembrane was observed by Geolabs Hawaii s
personnel Observation duties included measurement of the panel thickness confirmation
of panel overlap and a visual observation of the overall sheet quality A summary of the
deployment observations is included in Appendix F 1
Deployment of the liner began at the southeast corner of the slope and proceeded
to the northwest and northeast The slope panels extended a minimum of fifteen 15 feet
beyond the crest of the slopes into the anchor trench The floor panels extended a
minimum of eight 8 feet beyond the centerline of the berm A minimum of five 5 inches
of overlap was maintained between adjoining panels The geomembrane panel layout for
the ash monofill base liner is shown on Figure 2
W O 2458 50 Page 8
Each panel of deployed 60 mil HDPE geomembrane was given a unique panel
number which identified that panel within the installation The panels were numbered
sequentially as they were deployed
Each geomembrane panel was observed for overall sheet quality and measured
for thickness as they were being deployed The thickness measurements were made
using a micrometer Five 5 measurements were taken on each of the leading edge one
side and the trailing edge of each panel The reported thicknesses in Appendix F 1
represent the average of the five readings All panels had an average thickness equal to
or greater than 60 mils and all individual measurements were greater than 54 mils 90
percent of the specified 60 mils thickness
Weld Observation
The majority of the seaming of the HDPE panels was accomplished using fusion
welding apparatus Extrusion welding was used to make repairs and to weld the seams
at the junctions of three 3 or more panels overlapping each other The welding was
observed by Geolabs Hawaii s personnel on a procedural basis The entire length of all
seams patches or other repairs was observed either during welding or shortly after
completion of the welding
Each seam was numbered according to the two panels being welded together
For example seam P1 2 is the seam between panels P1 and P2 Each seam was
visually inspected by Geolabs Hawaii s personnel to ensure that the panels were properly
cleaned prior to welding and that the weld was continuous and free of defects A
summary of the weld inspection is presented in Appendix F 2
Trial test seams were required of all welders and seaming apparatuses on a regular
basis as specified in the QA manual The welding personnel each produced a test seam
W O 2458 50 Page 9
at the start of each seaming period normally twice a day Three 8 one inch wide test
strips were cut from each test sample for peel and shear testing using a field tensiometer
Two 2 strips were used to run peel test and one 1 strip was used to run shear test
All seam testing were performed in the presence of Geolabs Hawaii s personnel All
welders were required to produce a trial test seam that did not fail in the weld during peel
and shear testing before they were allowed to start welding on the liner If a specimen
fails the entire operation shall be repeated If the additional specimen fails the seaming
apparatus and seamer shall not be accepted and shall not be used for seaming until the
deficiencies are corrected and two consecutive successful trial welds are achieved A
summary of the test seams is presented in Appendix F 3
Destructive Testing
In accordance with the QA Manual and Specification Guidelines destructive test
samples of the welded seams were obtained at a minimum frequency of one destructive
test sample per every 500 linear feet of weld The test locations were selected by
Geolabs Hawaii s QA personnel based either on the completion of approximately 500 feet
of welding or on seaming observations
Once a destructive test location had been located a 12 inch wide by 48 inch long
sample centered lengthwise on the weld was cut Two strips were cut from each
destructive test sample for field testing in peel Both strips had to pass the field testing
before the sample was qualified for destructive test The sample was then cut into three
3 equal pieces One section was packaged and shipped to ECS Inc s laboratory at
Duluth Georgia for laboratory destructive testing one section taken for Northwest Linings
archives and the other section was taken for the owner s archives After the removal of
each destructive test sample the sampled area was patched and the patch seam was
non destructively tested
PrP A eae
W O 2458 50 Page 10
From each destructive test sample 10 specimens were removed for testing Five
5 specimens were tested for adhesion peel for both the inner weld track and the outer
weld track with the specified acceptance criterion being that four out of five test
specimens per weld track will not fail in the weld The peel strengths must be greater
than 90 pounds per inch and 78 pounds per inch for fusion and extrusion welds
respectively as specified in the QA manual Five 5 specimens were also tested for
bonded strength shear with the specified acceptance criteria being that four 4 out of
five 5 specimens must have yield strengths greater than 132 pounds per inch and that
failure cannot occur in the weld
Destructive test sample DT 31 failed the field testing by peeling off the seam
According to the QA manual the installer has to trace the welding path to an intermediate
location at 10 feet minimum from the point of the failed field or laboratory test in each
direction and take a one inch wide sample for additional field test at each location If
these additional samples pass the test then full laboratory samples were taken If these
laboratory samples pass the tests then the seam is repaired between these locations
DT 31B was obtained at 60 feet ahead sample DT 31 Since DT 31 is located near the
end of the last seam the installer could not successfully trace a passing field test location
until the end of the seam Therefore the installer decided to repair the seam from DT 31B
to the end of this seam
The seam between P1 and the last panel of Cell 3 also failed the field peel test
because of the very bad condition of the last panel of Cell 3 The extrusion weld was
used to repair the whole seam Destructive test sample DT 30 was obtained at the middle
of the repaired seam
A total of 31 destructive test samples were taken for laboratory testing The

Non Destructive Continuity Testing
Non desiructive continuity testing of the welded seams was performed using
vacuum box and air pressure testing apparatuses For the vacuum test a soapy solution
was mopped on the seam and the vacuum box was placed over the seam A vacuum
of approximately five 5 psi was applied in the box for a duration of ten 10 seconds
Detected leaks were repaired and retested using the same procedure The seams that
were not vacuum tested were non destructively tested using air pressure testing For this
test both ends of the seam being tested were sealed and a needle with an attached
pressure gauge and air valve was inserted into the air channel between the two welds
A pressure of approximately thirty 80 psi was then applied to the air channel and
monitored for five 5 minutes If the pressure drop over the five 5 minute period was
less than two 2 psi the seam was considered continuous and defect free If the loss
in pressure exceeded two 2 psi the seam was then pressure tested in sections until the
defect was found or the seam was tested using a vacuum box Leaks detected using the
air pressure method were repaired and retested using a vacuum box A summary of
non destructive tests is included on the weld inspection summary and repair summary of
Appendix F 2 and Appendix F 4 respectively
