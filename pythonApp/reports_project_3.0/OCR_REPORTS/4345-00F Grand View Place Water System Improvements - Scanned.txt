PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located along Grand View Place Nonohe Place and various side
streets in the Wahiawa area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity
of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Based on the information
provided we understand that it is proposed to construct new 8 inch and 4 inch water mains
and appurtenances along these streets for water system improvement The water system
improvement is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the plan and profile drawing provided the pipe inverts are not known at
this time although they are expected to be about 3 feet below ground surface In order to
evaluate the need for corrosion control protection field resistivity testing was conducted
along the water main alignment in general accordance with the Water System External
Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 1991 Detailed discussions and results of our
corrosion tests are presented in the following sections of this letter report

and the requirements provided in the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards
Volume 3 1991 The scope of our work consisted of the following tasks and work efforts

Fifty two soil resistivity surveys were conducted at a maximum of 300 foot intervals at
thirteen locations designated as R 1 through R 13 along the shoulder or sidewalk of the
streets using the in Pin Method and a Nilsson Model 400 resistance meter
manufactured by Nilsson Electrical Laboratory Inc The method utilizes four surface probes
or pins electrodes to evaluate the average resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the
underlying soil mass The average resistivity values of the soil mass extending from the
existing ground surface to the test depth was assessed by using this method at the
approximate survey locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Results of the resistivity
surveys are presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 3
The soil resistivity surveys were performed at the existing ground surface along the
shoulder or sidewalk of the existing streets of the proposed 8 inch and 4 inch water mains
The average soil resistance along the proposed alignment was evaluated at each survey

FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics with
regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In general the lower the
resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic structure Conversely
the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion of metallic objects
On the basis of the field resistivity test results a majority of the test locations indicate
the subsurface soils along the proposed water main of California Avenue to Haumalu Place
within the project site have corrosion ratings of 2 to 4 This indicates the subsurface soils have
very corrosive to moderately corrosive potential Isolated resistivity measurements indicating
corrosion ratings of 5 were noted A majority of the test locations indicate the subsurface soils
along the proposed water main of Grand View Place within the project site have corrosion
ratings of 3 to 4 This indicates the subsurface soils to have corrosive to moderately corrosive
potential A majority of the test locations indicate the subsurface soils along the proposed
water main of Nonohe Place within the project site have corrosion ratings of 4to 5 This
indicates the subsurface soils to have moderately corrosive to mildly corrosive potential
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from field
data points by means of geophysical surveys Variation of soil conditions between and beyond
the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations may not become
evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
reevaluate the ratings provided in this report
