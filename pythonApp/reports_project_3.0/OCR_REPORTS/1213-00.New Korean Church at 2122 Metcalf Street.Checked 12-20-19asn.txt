As requested we have performed a foundation engineering investigation
at the above referenced site in accordance with our proposal of
This report summarizes our findings and
recommendations
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions below the existing building subgrade excavation in order to
obtain soil data for recommendations regarding the placement of the
footings and to determine requirements for subgrade and retaining wall
backfill materials
W O 1213 00 Page 2
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which used
in the engineering analysis that formulated our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendix of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the University Moiliili area of
Honolulu Hawaii
The topography of the site was relatively level with a slight slope to
the west The site is bounded by several retaining walls of moderate
height Construction of the new building is currently in progress and
portions of the site have been excavated There is a large stockpile
in the western portion of the site
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
Construction of the new church building has already been started The
plans indicate that the building will be two stories and of concrete
and CMU construction The first story will be a semi basement and be
used as a classroom
Paved parking areas are planned for the areas adjacent to the building
Some retaining walls are planned for the project

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Field Exploration
Four borings were drilled with a truck mounted rotary drilling rig
between the two column lines to explore the subsurface soils at the
project site Accessibility for the drilling equipment to other
portions of the site was limited by the existing excavation and
stockpile
The borings were advanced to depths between 13 2 and 24 5 feet below
the building subgrade excavation
The approximate locations of the borings are presented on the Site
Plan Plate 2 and the logs of the borings are presented in the
appendix

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field explorations and observations revealed that the original
surficial soil at the site is an expansive silty clay Laboratory
testing shows that this material has swell potential on the order of
4 when saturated at natural moisture and 12 when air dried and
resaturated The borings indicate that in some areas this material
extends below the design footing grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1213 00 Page 5
Field observation also indicates that the bottom of the interior of
the existing footing forms is covered with loose material containing
some adobe Additionally it was observed that the existing
stockpile on the western side of the site is composed of a mixture of
materials such as concrete rubble demolition debris adobe clay
clayey silt and basalt fragments
Building Foundations
It is our opinion that the adobe clay material is unsuitable for
foundation support due to its expansion potential Therefore the
footings should be extended to the underlying clayey silt or clinker
material For the two southernmost column lines this would involve
additional excavation of approximately two to three feet below the
existing footing grade
In addition the northern two column lines should be deepened an
additional one foot to remove the loose material which is partially
mixed with adobe clay
The footings should bear on undisturbed natural material with no fill
placed below the foundation All footing bottoms should be tamped to
obtain a minimum 95 density per ASTM D 1557 78 test method There
should be no loose material in the excavation prior to the placement
of concrete
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1213 00 Page 6
Following the casting of the foundations the footing excavation
should be backfilled with non expansive off site select material and
compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78 test method
Building Subgrade
The proposed slab on grade should be separated from the adobe by a
minimum of 3 feet of non expansive select material such as crusher
run waste This material should be placed in 9 inch loose lifts and
compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density as referenced above
Retaining Wall
The retaining walls should bear on hon expansive undisturbed natural
material The backfill behind the walls should be free draining
granular material such as 3B Fine and the top 2 feet of the
backfill should be non expansive impermeable select material compacted
to 90 maximum dry density as referenced above
Stockpile
Due to the mixed nature of the stockpile it is our opinion that this
material is of too low a quality to provide assurance as a
non expansive select fill to be used as structural fill or backfill
Unless the stockpile is to be used for landscaping material or buried
in the parking area a minimum of 3 feet below the finished grade it
should be removed and disposed of properly off site

Pavement Section
Based on our observations and laboratory test results we recommend a
pavement section of
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
24 inch Borrow Non Expansive Select Material
52 inch Total Pavement ickness
The on site expansive soils should be removed to a depth of 24 inches
below the base course or to the underlying non expansive select
material quarry crusher waste coral or cinder compacted to 90 of
its maximum dry density The in situ clayey subgrade materials should
be kept moist prior to the placement of the base course The above
section considers medium traffic and that good drainage will be
provided for adjacent to paved areas
Design Review
Plans for site grading should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and written comments prior to construction to see that the
intent of our recommendations is included in the project plans and
specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the filling operations be observed by the soils
engineer during construction This is to see that adequate compaction
has been obtained and the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our subsurface investigation The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon the above inspections

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations made
in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering
principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other
warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based on
our site reconnaissance soil information from boring data and
laboratory tests obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by reconnaissance and soil borings These unforeseen
conditions such as pockets of organic soils trash fill or expansive
soils may occur in localized areas Therefore additional
expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a properly
constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended to
accommodate these possible costs
