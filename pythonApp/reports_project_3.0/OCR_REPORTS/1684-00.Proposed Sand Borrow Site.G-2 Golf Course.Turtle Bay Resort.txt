In accordance with your request we have performed a soil
reconnaissance investigation at the above referenced site Our work was
done in general accordance with our proposal of March 20 1986
The purpose of the investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil data for sand borrow material for
site grading for a proposed new golf course that is to be constructed near
the study area
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusion and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the soil conditions
at the site to obtain pertinent soil data to assist in the design of the
golf course development grading
The main objectives of the investigation were
1 To determine the suitability of the underlying soils for use in
the construction of fills for the proposed new golf course and
2 To determine the excavation difficulties and depths of water
over the site and
3 To determine the material requirements for restoring the site
for future developments
SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is relatively flat and encompasses an area of about 3500
feet long by 1000 feet wide 80 acres The site is located along the
old landing strips south of Kahuku Point Old A C pavements cover much of
the site Punahoolapa marsh is located south of the site The Turtle Bay
Resort Hotel is located about one mile west of the site The present
ground elevation is at about 5 to 6 feet elevation above MSL
The location of the site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate
l and the Site Plan Plate 2

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Fourteen 14 borings were drilled at the site to depths of 10 5 to
24 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 In general the borings
encountered a surface layer approximately 10 to 15 feet thick of tan
white silty coral sand with coral gravel Unified Soil Classification
SM SP Pockets of coral gravel Unified Soil Classification GM GP were
noted in some of the borings
At Borings 2 and 2A a 3 to 7 foot thick layer of organic peaty soil
OL was encountered about 3 to 5 feet below the existing surface
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at depths of about 3 5 to
5 9 feet below the existing ground surface
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the site is generally underlain
by 10 to 15 feet of silty coral sand mixed with coral gravel It appears
that the in situ sandy soil may be suitable for use in the golf course
grading Some unsuitable organic material was encountered at Borings 2
and 2A locations and may represent a low energy lagoonal deposit that has
been filled over
It is anticipated that the excavated sand from the site will be
sandwiched between less permeable soils to provide for better drainage
conditions
The golf course designer or landscape architect should review the
boring logs Grading Analysis forms and Sieve Analysis Plots to determine
if these soils meet the desired specifications for the golf course
construction
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 1684 00 Page 4
It should also be understood that the excavated sandy material will
have to be replaced with carefully controlled backfill for future
developments that are planned at the site
Excavation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing pavements
debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and
disposed of properly off site in order to prevent contamination of the
excavated sandy material
Excavated on site sandy material generally free of organic and
other expansive component may be used as a source of fill material for
the golf course construction
It appears that the sand can be excavated without difficulty by
conventional earth moving equipment
The practical depth of excavation is limited to about 3 5 to 5 foot
depths where the water level was encountered Excavation below the water
table would require special equipment and would be much more costly
Additionally granular material would be needed to backfill the underwater
excavation Therefore we recommend that the maximum depth of excavation
be kept about 1 foot above the high water level
Backfilling
All imported fill material to replace the excavated sand areas
should consist of low expansive clayey silt or granular materials less
than 6 inches in size All backfill materials should be placed in maximum
of 8 inch horizontal loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the
maximum density as established by the ASTM D 1557 78 test method

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading should be forwarded to the
soils engineer for review and written comments prior to construction to
see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the excavation and backfilling operations be
observed by a soils technician during construction to see that adequate
compaction been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should have low expansion tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspection

Field Exploration

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
fourteen 14 borings to depths of 10 5 to 24 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate
2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings

textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Boring Logs Plates A l through A 14
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
