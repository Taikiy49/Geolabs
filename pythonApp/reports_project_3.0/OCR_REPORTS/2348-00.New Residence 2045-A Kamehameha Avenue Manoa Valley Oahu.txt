PROJECT CONSTDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a single family residence on the site
It is our understanding that the proposed residence will be a two story
wood framed structure with slabs on grade to be used for the ground floor
of the house and for the garage floor
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the site to develop a shallow soils rock data set
for use in analyses for the formulation of geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertinent to the design and construction of the proposed
residence
Foundations and Slabs
Our exploration encountered low expansion potential silty soils at
the upper five 5 foot level on the project site These silty soils are
in a naturally moist state and should be protected from drying to reduce
the dehydrating during the construction phase of the project and then
subsequently wetted after construction
Spread foundations should be embedded a minimum of twelve 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent grade An allowable bearing capacity of 2 000
p s f may be used for the design of the house spread footing foundations
Slabs on grade should have exterior thickened edges extending a
minimum of twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Non expansive select borrow material should be used as the source of fill
to bring the finish subgrade under the slabs and basaltic termite barrier
if any A vapor barrier membrane should be incorporated between the slab
and cushion if necessary to protect floor coverings and or reduce the
potential of moisture infiltration into interior spaces

The concrete driveway and garage slab will function as vehicular

Exterior patio lanai and walkway slabs should be underlain by a

To minimize the compactive effort required for the above base course
and slab cushion fill 3B Fine gravel is highly recommended as a source
of base course or select borrow fill materials
Site Grading
It appears that the project will require some fill for the
construction of the proposed driveway and residence To facilitate the
placement of the non expansive select material below the slabs we
recommend that the building area be cut down to a uniform level grade
allowing for the required thickness of base course cushion fill The
excess material generated by this excavation may be used for landscaping
and yard fill
After the site has been cut down the in situ soils should be
scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture conditioned to about
three 3 percent above optimum moisture content and compacted to 85 of
its maximum dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78
The site should be brought to subgrade level by the placement of the
required non expansive select borrow material in eight 8 inch compacted
lifts at 90 of its maximum dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78
The select material should consist of a granular material such as coral
cinder or crusher waste materials and should be tested and approved by the
geotechnical engineer prior to its hauling to the site

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
sitework excavation and foundation construction phases of the work This
is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface
conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
and samples Variations of condition between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately determined
by taping from reference points along property boundaries shown on the
Foundation Plan by Kajioka Okada and Partners Elevation of the borings
