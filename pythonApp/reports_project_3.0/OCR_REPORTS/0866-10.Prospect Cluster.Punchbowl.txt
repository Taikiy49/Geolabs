This report presents the results of our geologic reconnais
sance and test pits at the subject development site located
on the northern slope of Punchbowl Crater adjacent to
Prospect Street
Our original reconnaissance study was performed in June and
July of 1979 for a previous development plan on the lot
Subsequently a new development plan has been developed for
the subject lot The purpose of this report is to review
the soil data at the site to evaluate the new development
plan from a soil engineering point of view

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is presently planned to construct four two and three
story residential structures on the subject parcel Each
building will include from 2 to 5 units The preliminary
development scheme is to locate the units around the perimeter
of the site with an access roadway from Prospect Street
through the center of the site
The preliminary grading plans dated and
architectural plans indicate that most of the access road and
building areas will be cut Fills are planned near the end
of the access road
Cut slopes generally less than 10 feet high are planned
Retaining and basement walls generally less than 10 feet
high are also planned
SITE CONDITIONS
Surface
The project site is located on the northern slope of Punch
bowl Crater above Prospect Street near the intersection of
Prospect and Puowaina Streets The site is presently
heavily overgrown with trees shrubs and grasses Located
in the northeast corner of the site is a burned out house
and associated concrete slab two loose rock walls and a
walk and stairway leading to Prospect Street

The site is fairly steep sloping up from the street level
at a gradient of 3 horizontal to 1 vertical Surveyed ele

Geology and Soil Conditions
Punchbowl Crater is composed of a vitric volcanic tuff
formed during a violent phreatomagmatic eruption of ash
Similar to Salt Lake Crater and Diamond Head Crater Vol
canic tuff forms a brown hard glassy rock known locally
as mudrock
The site is underlain entirely by the Punchbowl tuff Ex
posed rock outcrops can be found along Prospect Street in
a 10 to 20 foot nearby vertical cliff Outcrops are also
present in a 3 to 5 foot ledge behind the derelict dwelling
and on the ground surface around the dwelling The tuff is
highly fractured and jointed Bedding planes are evident
dipping approximately 40 degrees in Test Pit No 2 to 7
degrees in the rock wall at the street level
Over most of the site the tuff is highly weathered up to
a depth of about 8 5 feet Test Pit No 2 and easily
excavated with a backhoe The weathered tuff is very
highly fractured and breaks easily into a platy gravel
sand and silt mixture when excavated Overlying the tuff
over most of the site a relatively thin layer of recent

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the results of our test pits and geologic reconnais
sance the site appears to be underlain by competent subsoils
and it it our opinion that the site can be developed for the
proposed residential development provided the soil engineering
recommendations are followed
Site Grading
Based on the test pit data the soils at the site can be
generalized as follows
1 Brown clayey silt loose recent slope wash
one to three feet thick
2 Highly weathered mudrock sandy silt and gravel
three to six feet thick
3 Dense mudrock five to eight feet deep
The upper portions of the cuts less than five to eight feet
should generally be easily excavated however some hard
ripping may be required in the deeper cuts through the dense
mudrock formation
The proposed cut slopes are generally from about 5 to 10 feet
high and therefore would be in the highly weathered mudrock
and recent slope wash deposit Most of the highly weathered
mudrock has essentially decomposed to a stiff soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 866 10 P 5
Geologic reconnaissance indicated that the bedding planes
in the weathered mudrock may dip as steep as 40 degrees
approximately in portions of the site and there
fore cuts should be flatter than H V in order to
reduce the instability along the bedding planes For these
reasons cut slope ratios should be limited to 1 1 H V
maximum
Two horizontal to 1 vertical fill slopes are recommended
The site should be cleared of vegetation and debris then
scarified and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557
Fills should be placed in 9 inch loose lifts compacted to
the above specified density
Where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal to 1
vertical 5 1 benching and keying are required to properly
bond the new fill to the slope The filling operation should
start at the lowest point and continue up in level compacted
layers in accordance with the above structural fill require
ments For construction details refer to Plate 2
The on site excavated soils less than 8 inches can be used
as a source of fill material However they have moderate
swell potential and should be compacted wet of optimum moisture
content to reduce the swelling potential

Building Foundations
Relatively light structural loading is anticipated from the
proposed residential buildings Based on the preliminary
soil information we anticipate that conventional shallow
foundations bearing on the weathered mudrock formation or
compacted fill can be utilized
Some of the on site soil may be moderately expansive and
may require deeper perimeter footings around the slab on
grade Additional borings and swell tests should be conduc
ted during the design phase soil investigation to evaluate
the slab design
Retaining and Basement Wall Foundation
Retaining and basement walls are planned for the development
From the preliminary soil data it appears that the wall
foundations could be adequately supported on the underlying
weathered or dense mudrock formation
Additional Studies
Additional soil engineering and geologic studies as well as
borings drilled within the final locations of the building
and retaining wall should be made in order to obtain specific
engineering recommendations for the design of the foundations
and site grading
