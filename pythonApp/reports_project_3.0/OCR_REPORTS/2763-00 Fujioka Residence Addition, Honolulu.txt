This letter report presents the results of our field exploration performed at the
project site At the request of and after our telephone conversation
it was decided that only the field exploration portion of our proposed scope of work would
be performed
It is proposed to construct a one story addition of wood construction
approximately 22 feet by 30 feet in plan dimensions in front of the existing residence at
5420 Hoana Place in Aina Haina on the island of Oahu
The site is located along the lower slopes on the western side of Hawaii Loa Ridge
The field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling one 1 boring to a depth
of 31 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate location of the boring
is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The boring encountered stiff to very stiff gray brown adobe clay with cobbles and
boulders near the existing ground surface and to a depth of 31 5 feet the maximum
depth drilled

These adobe clay soils have high shrink swell potential and generally will exhibit
a high tendency for downhill creep movement when the clay soil is wetted due to water
infiltration seepage and utility leakage
Mr Fujioka was notified of the findings from our field exploration In the telephone
conversation we had expressed our current opinion that our preliminary recommendation
is to consider the use of drilled pier type of foundation extending through the adobe clay
deposit and into stable ground to reduce the downslope movement of the planned
addition
Considering the high cost of caisson systems Mr Fujioka requested that
subsequent work on the project be discontinued
