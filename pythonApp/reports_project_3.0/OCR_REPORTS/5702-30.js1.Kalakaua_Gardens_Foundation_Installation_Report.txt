PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at 1723 Kalakaua Avenue adjacent to the
Kapiolani Boulevard Corridor in the Ala Moana area of Honolulu The project site consists
of four parcels of land encompassing approximately 30 000 square feet of land area In
general the project involves the construction of a 17 story high rise building for senior
living with a lobby dining area kitchen management offices landscaped walking paths
and parking on the ground floor
The new high rise building structure is supported on a deep foundation system
consisting of 18 inch diameter augered cast in place piles with an allowable compressive
load capacity of up to 300 kips 150 tons per pile The project consisted of 10 indicator
piles test piles and 114 production piles for a total of 124 ACIP piles for the foundation
support of the high rise structure
Swinerton Builders was the general contractor for the project and Hawaiian
Dredging Construction Company Inc HDCC was the ACIP pile installation subcontractor
for the project
AUGERED CAST IN PLACE PILE INSTALLATION
Geotechnical recommendations for the ACIP pile foundations were presented in the
afore mentioned technical memorandum dated The purpose of our
geotechnical engineering observation and consultation services was to observe the ACIP
pile foundation installation for general conformance with our recommendations
The ACIP pile installation program for the high rise structure consisted of
performing one static load test installation of 10 indicator piles and 114 production piles
Detailed discussions are provided in the following subsections of this report
ACIP Pile Load Test Program
In order to evaluate the adequacy of the design length of the ACIP pile foundation
an ACIP Pile load test program consisting of one static load test was implemented The
load test pile program included the installation and observation of reaction piles and
one instrumented load test pile The static pile load test was conducted on the
instrumented ACIP pile on September 24 and 25 2014 The pile load test was conducted
on one 18 inch diameter ACIP piles with a length of approximately 72 feet relative to the
existing ground surface
The static pile load test results are summarized in the report entitled ACIP Pile
Load Test Report Kalakaua Gardens Honolulu Oahu Hawaii dated
Based on our field observations and the results of the static pile load test we confirmed
the design embedment of 60 feet below the existing ground surface to achieve the design
compressive load capacity of 300 kips for ACIP piles with a factor of safety of at least 2 0

ACIP Indicator Pile Program
An indicator pile also known as a probe pile or test pile program which consisted
of installing 10 ACIP piles was conducted for the project from September 15 through 17
2014 The indicator piles were selected from the future production pile locations and are
shown on the ACIP Pile Location Plan Plate 2 The purpose of the indicator pile program
was to evaluate the subsurface conditions across the project site with respect to the
selected ACIP equipment and the methods of ACIP pile installation
A summary of the indicator piles and our field observations is included in the ACIP
Pile Installation Summary Plates 3 1 through 3 4 of this report for reference Based on the
results of the indicator pile program the selected ACIP equipment and the methods of
ACIP pile installation are capable of installing the ACIP piles to the design length
Production ACIP Pile Installation
The production ACIP pile installation program consisting of installing 114 augered
cast in place piles 18 inch diameter was conducted from October 8 through 20 2014
under the near continuous full time observations by a Geolabs field representative
HDCC used the same drilling grouting equipment utilized for the indicator or test pile
program to install the production piles Automatic monitoring equipment was mounted on
the drilling pumping equipment with the intent of providing auger depth grout flow rate
grout pressure etc on a near continuous basis
During the pile installation operation logs were maintained for each ACIP pile
which included information pertaining to pile number date installed depth of pile grout
volume used grout return depth etc The identifications of the production ACIP piles
installed are shown on the ACIP Pile Location Plan Plate 2 The production ACIP pile
installation summary is presented by pile number on the attached Plates 3 1 through 3 4
As with most foundation installation projects one problem did arise during
construction Grouting of one ACIP Pile designated as Pile No 49 was aborted during
grouting due to a grout pump malfunction The grout pump was immediately repaired and
the pile was successfully re grouted and designated as Pile No 49R1
Based on our observation of the equipment performance and subsurface conditions
encountered it is our professional opinion that the production ACIP piles have been
installed to sufficient depths and or resistance to provide the design compressive load
capacity
