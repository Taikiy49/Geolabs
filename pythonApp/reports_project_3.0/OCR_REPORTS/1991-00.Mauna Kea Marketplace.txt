foundation loads we believe that spread footing

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soil engineering evaluation
performed for the proposed Maunakea Market Place Project in downtown
Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site
and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 This
report summarizes our findings and recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to redevelop Pauahi Block B area in downtown
Honolulu The planned development consists of a one to two story large
structure covering the entire Block B site The maximum column and wall
loads anticipated are 50 kips and 4 kips per foot length respectively
It is also our understanding that the burned Colusa Building next to
Yee Yee Tong Building located on the southern boundary of the site will
be demolished and the basement inside will be backfilled A new retaining
wall is planned on the Yee Yee Tong Building side prior to backfilling
since there is a basement inside Yee Yee Tong Building
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our evaluation was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions at the site to obtain pertinent soil data for the design of the
foundations of the structure particularly to evaluate if there would be
an adequate crust with sufficient thickness to support a spread footing
type of foundation
The scope of our investigation included drilling and sampling
fourteen 14 borings to depths of 11 0 to 30 8 feet below the existing
ground surface Also included were laboratory testing of selected samples
obtained engineering analysis and evaluation of the field and laboratory
data development of the preliminary design recommendations and report
preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report

SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located between Pauahi and Hotel Streets and
north of Maunakea Street in downtown Honolulu Currently the site is
used as a parking facility The existing ground elevation varies from
about 18 feet along Maunakea Street to about 12 feet near the northern
boundary
The site is surrounded by some existing buildings including Yee Yee
Tong Siu Lum Yip Kee Sumida and commercial stores along the
northwestern boundary see Site Plan Plate 2 It is our understanding
that the existing buildings probably have one level of basements
estimated to be about 9 feet below the current ground level
The burned Colusa Building next to Yee Yee Tong Building currently
blockaded for security reasons is located on the southern boundary of the
site The building will be demolished and the basement inside will be
backfilled The blockage and the burned down wreckage however prevent
closer inspection inside the building

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally indicated that a 3 to 4 foot thick
layer of hard coral formation exists below the upper 4 foot surficial fill
which consists primarily of medium dense silty sand Deposits of medium
dense reef detritus and lagoonal silty sand were then encountered to
depths in excess of 30 feet
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1991 00 Page 5
Dk No 49
We believe that the upper fill layer and the underlying coral
formation can provide adequate foundation support for the proposed
structures
Site Grading
Minimal site grading is anticipated
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable
materials such as debris rubbish vegetation and other deleterious
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
The ground surface below the slabs should be scarified to a minimum
depth of 6 inches and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density as per ASTM D 1557 78
It was noted that one of the drilled borings B 11 did encounter
some loose fill materials probably part of a utility trench backfill It
is probable that other previous on site utility trench backfill materials
may also exist but were not encountered during our field exploration
Thus any loose or soft spots encountered at the footing locations or
subgrade level should be over excavated down to in situ firm ground and
replaced with backfill compacted to a minimum of 90 of ASTM D 1557 78
maximum density
The upper sandy fill materials may be used as a source of backfill
material If required new imported fill materials should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum size
with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
Site grading should provide drainage of surface run off away from all
foundations

The proper basement backfilling procedures for to be demolished
building will be discussed later in the report
Foundation Support
Based on the anticipated soil conditions and foundation loads from
the proposed structure we believe that shallow spread footing foundations
bearing on the in situ fill material may be used
An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f with a minimum footing
embedment of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade is recommended for
foundation design The bearing value is for total dead plus live load
only and may be increased by one third for short duration wind seismic
loading
Estimated settlements under the design load are anticipated to be on
the order of less than one half 1 2 of an inch total and one quarter
1 4 of an inch differential
For footings located immediately adjacent to the existing buildings
with basement we recommend that the footings be lowered to bear on the
underlying coral formation to minimize lateral pressures on the adjacent
basement walls The required footing embedment may vary from 4 to 8 feet
below the existing ground level
For footings located along the northwestern boundary of Yee Yee Tong
Building where the old basement will be backfilled and a new retaining
wall is planned we recommend that the footings be embedded at the level
of the old basement slab which is probably about 9 feet below the existing
ground level The actual basement level should be verified at the time of
building demolition prior to backfilling of the old basement

If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench or the trench backfill must be well
compacted to reduce footing settlement due to settlement of the trench
backfill
Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed building can be obtained
by passive soil pressure and frictional resistance A frictional
coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the bottom of the footing and the
in situ soil In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per
foot of depth may be used against the buried footing of the structure to
determine the passive soil resistance
Slab On Grade
The on grade floor slabs should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer
of gravel size material such as base course rock to provide a uniform
support A moisture barrier membrane should be placed under the slabs to
protect against future moisture infiltration
The bottom of the slab areas including the thickened edges should
be kept moist until the concrete is poured The backfill against the
concrete slabs should be adequately compacted and graded to shed water
away from the slab perimeters
Basement Backfilling
In order to provide a firm footing subgrade for the proposed new
buildings the basement adjacent to Yee Yee Tong Building should be
backfilled with properly compacted structural fills as recommended below
1 The basement area should be cleared of wreckage and unsuitable
materials before filling
2 The existing basement slab may remain or be removed sufficiently
to accommodate the new retaining wall footing

Backfill materials should consist of off site borrow such as
crusher waste cinder sand or coral
The fill should be placed in about 8 inch thick loose level lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry
density
The top of the existing basement walls should be removed down to
a minimum depth of three 3 feet below the finished grade to
obtain a compacted fill cushion layer between new foundations and
the old basement walls This 3 foot fill may provide a more
uniform support for the new foundations and reduce differential
settlements
Retaining Wall
For the new retianing wall planned adjacent to Yee Yee Tong Building
the guidelines for design are as follows
1
Within a 5 foot distance behind the retaining wall backfill
material should consist of 3B Fine gravel The remaining part
of the basement may be backfilled with off site borrow as
recommended previously
For lateral earth pressures we recommend an equivalent fluid
pressure of 50 p s f per foot of depth for wall at restrained
condition
An allowable bearing value of up to 4000 p s f may be used for
the design of wall foundation

Pavement and Parking Areas
Based on the results of our exploration the preliminary pavement
design is as follows
2 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness

during construction and the above design section should be verified in
the field
Utilities
Existing utilities should be located and verified in the field It
may be necessary to move an existing line or adjust structural details to
accommodate the utility Should unanticipated condition s or buried
structure s be encountered during construction they should be evaluated
by the soils engineer and the design modified if necessary
Utility lines trench backfill should be properly compacted to obtain
90 compaction density to minimize future ground settlements and damage to
slabs
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earth work and foundation recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer can assume no responsibility
for misinterpretation of the recommendations

Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction and
foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
soil samples test pits and or visual observation Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the topographic survey dated
provided by Walter P Thompson Inc Elevations of the data points were
determined by interpolation from elevation points on the topographic
survey map The physical location and elevation of the field data points
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method
used

The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the

report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation

constructed project Therefore sufficient contingency fund is thus
recommended to accommodate these possible extra costs
