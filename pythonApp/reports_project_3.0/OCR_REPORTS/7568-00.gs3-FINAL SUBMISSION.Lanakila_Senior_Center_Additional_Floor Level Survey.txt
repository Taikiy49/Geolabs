This letter report presents the results of the additional floor level survey performed 
for the Lanakila Senior Center Structural Study project in the Honolulu area on the Island

This report summarizes our findings and presents our geotechnical engineering 
recommendations resulting from the additional floor level survey performed for the project.  
The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at 
the end of the report. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The Lanakila Senior Center is located adjacent to Lanakila Avenue in the 
Honolulu District on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The Lanakila Senior Center is located 
north of Lanakila Elementary School and west of the Lanakila Health Center. The 
project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
The Lanakila Senior Center is a single-story structure supported on spread 
footings with concrete slabs-on-grade. Vertical movements of up to about 1.5 inches 
were observed at the building concrete slabs-on-grade. The most pronounced vertical 
movements were observed in the Arts and Crafts Room. We understand that the 
concrete floor slabs in the Lounge and Library Rooms have been previously repaired. 
The building layout of the center is shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
In our letter report entitled “Preliminary Findings, Lanakila Senior Center, 
Structural Study, Honolulu, Oahu, Hawaii,” dated September 22, 2017, the results of our 
field exploration for the project were presented.  Our field exploration included the

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS 
Based on the results of our borings and laboratory testing provided in our report 
dated September 22, 2017, the underlying clay soils were observed to have a high to 
very high shrink/swell potential when subjected to variations in moisture content. Due to 
poor drainage conditions at the site, it appeared that the underlying clay soils have 
experienced swelling and shrinkage causing the movement and distress observed. 
Differential movement was observed throughout the Senior Center with the most severe 
movements occurring in the west and southwest portions of the facility. These areas 
included the existing Lounge Room, Library, offices, and Arts and Crafts Room. 
OBSERVED DISTRESSES 
During our initial study, building distresses were observed throughout most of the 
Lanakila Senior Center structure and appeared to be more severe around the perimeter of 
the property. The observed distresses included cracks through the slabs-on-grade, cracks 
on the floor tiles, separation between the slabs-on-grade from the wall base moldings, 
uneven finished floor surfaces, and cracks in the outside walls. Additional building distress 
was not observed by Geolabs during the additional floor level survey. 
In the courtyard area, we understand that rainwater ponded adjacent to the side of 
the building where the offices and air conditioner concrete pads are located.  In this area, 
cracking and settling of the exterior slabs adjacent to the building walls were observed. 
Since our initial survey, we understand that the storm drain inlets on the northern edge of 
the facility had been cleaned of debris blockage and sandbags were placed around the 
drainage swale. Based on the observations of the Lanakila Senior Center employees, 
these drainage improvements have reduced rainwater ponding near the offices and air 
conditioner concrete pads.  
ADDITIONAL FLOOR LEVEL SURVEY 
As mentioned previously, the initial floor level survey was performed on 
August 18, 2017 for the Lanakila Senior Center facility, including the main facility building, 
outdoor concrete slab, multi-purpose room, and kitchen areas. An additional floor level 
survey was conducted on January 3, 2018 after heavy rains occurred during the week of 
December 25, 2017.  The additional level survey was performed at generally the same 
locations as the initial survey. The floor level survey equipment consists of a pressurized 
hydrostatic altimeter that utilizes a pressurized system that measures changes in elevation 
between a determined datum (zero location) and a point of interest.  The zero-datum 
location was selected at the north corner of the Inner Court near the center of the property.

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
Based on the results of our borings and laboratory testing, the underlying clay soils 
have a high to very high shrink/swell potential when subjected to variations in moisture 
content.  Due to poor drainage conditions at the site, it appears that the underlying clay 
soils have experienced swelling and shrinkage causing the movement and distress 
observed. 
The results of the floor level surveys performed generally indicate that there are 
differential movements throughout the building complex.  The most severe areas appear to 
be along the perimeter of the structure.  
The initial floor level survey was performed during a dry period with little rainfall 
while the additional survey was done after a period of heavy rainfall. The measured 
differential vertical movements ranged from swell of 3 inches and shrinkage of 1.7 inches 
in the Office and Courtyard, respectively.  Based on the readings, the average swell was 
about ⅜-inch and the average shrinkage was about ¼-inch. Significant differential 
movements (greater than ½-inch) were measured mainly in the Lounge, Library, Offices, 
Courtyard and Covered Activity Lanai.  The significant differential movements mostly 
consisted of swelling.

Based on the results of our field exploration and floor level surveys, significant 
shrinkage and swelling occurred after the heavy rainfall event.  We believe that continued 
distress to the structure will continue to occur with the wetting and drying of the underlying 
clay soils. Therefore, we believe that repairs to the existing structure will be on-going and 
reconstruction of the building slabs for the Lanakila Senior Center building should be 
considered.  Reconstruction of the building slabs should consist of removal of the concrete 
slab and 3 feet of soil below the slab and replacement with compacted structural fill 
material.     
Should reconstruction of the building slabs for the Lanakila Senior Center not be 
performed in the near future, we recommend implementing drainage improvements to 
minimize water infiltration into the underlying expansive clay soils. Drainage improvements 
may include, but are not limited to, the following examples: 
1. 
Provide positive drainage away from the building and into drainage 
structures such as drain pipes and inlets. 
2. 
Extending the roof downspout outlets farther away from the building or 
connecting them to a drain pipe. 
3. 
Converting landscaped areas adjacent to the building to paved surfaces that 
slopes away from the building. 
Due to the potential tripping hazard from the cracks and differential movements in 
the existing concrete slabs, we recommend that reconstruction of the building slabs be 
performed.
