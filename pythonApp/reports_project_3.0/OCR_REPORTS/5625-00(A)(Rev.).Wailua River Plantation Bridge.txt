Dear Mr Miyahara
As requested we have conducted three static load tests on the existing pier
structures at the Wailua River Plantation Bridge in the Kapaa Area on the Island of Kauai
Hawaii This letter report presents the results of our field observations and the test data
collected during the static pile load tests conducted on three weekends during the month
of June 2006 for the above project The load tests were performed in accordance with our
revised fee proposal dated The project location is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
Project Considerations
Currently the Wailua River Plantation Bridge carries one lane of traffic in the Kapaa
direction It is proposed to widen the bridge to a width of about 30 feet to accommodate
two lanes of traffic An Acrow panel system will be used for the new temporary bridge
structure
The available drawings of the Wailua River Plantation Bridge were redrawn in 1993
from the original plans dated November 1920 Based on the drawings the existing
eight span bridge is about 10 feet wide and 390 feet long with span lengths of 30 and
60 feet The concrete bridge structure is supported on driven piles with a design pile
capacity of 15 tons The Lihue abutment Abutment No 1 is supported on a spread
footing foundation bearing on the underlying basalt rock formation The number of driven
piles at each pier footing and abutment varies from 8 to 20 piles Information on pile type
and pile tip elevations was not available However it is anticipated that the piles are likely
timber piles similar to some of the other bridges of the same era in the area We
understand that the original bridge structure was modified by the State of Hawaii
Department of Transportation The modification consisted of replacing the bridge deck with
a reinforced concrete deck

Compression Load Tests
Three static load tests were performed during three weekends of June 2006
Description and results of the load tests are summarized below
Pier No 7
The load test for Pier No 7 was conducted on June 10 and 11 2006 Concrete
blocks about 2 feet by 1 foot 4 inches by 8 feet 2 feet by 2 feet by 6 feet and 2 feet by 2
feet by 4 feet were used to apply the test load The test loads were applied at the top of
the roadway over the pier structure The pier was loaded in general accordance with
ASTM D 1143 Quick Load Test Method The maximum test load on the pier was 111 2
tons The load test was loaded in about 10 ton load increments In addition a 12 hour hold
was maintained at 111 2 tons to evaluate the creep effects of the pile After loading the
pier structure to the maximum test load 111 2 tons the test load was unloaded in
approximately five equal decrements and held for at least 10 minutes at each decrement
The results of the load test are presented on the attached Load Test Data Sheets Plates

On the weekend of June 17 and 18 2006 the load test for Pier No 6 was
conducted The same concrete blocks used for the load test at Pier No 7 were used to

A plot of the compressive load test measurements on the pier structure is provided
on the attached Load Displacement Curve Plate 4 4 Dial gages supported on steel
beams and optical survey levels were used to measure the displacement during the load
test In addition tarps were erected to shade the steel beams from the sun Because of the
strong wind condition and the potential for the tarps to hit the steel beams and dial gages
during the test the tarps were removed Although variations in the dial gage readings were
measured compared to the optical survey level readings these variations were less
pronounced than the Pier 7 load test The reduced variations in the readings were
probably caused by a smaller change in steel beam temperature due to the cooler and
partly cloudy conditions during the load test

Summary and Conclusions
In the three load tests that were performed the measured maximum displacements
were very small and ranged from 0 03 to 0 12 inch The load test results show that the
existing pier structures can carry a static load of 111 2 tons without failure
