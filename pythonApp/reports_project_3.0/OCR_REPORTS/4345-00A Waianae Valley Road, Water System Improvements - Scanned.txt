PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located on private roads off of Waianae Valley Road in the
Waianae area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 Based on the information
provided we understand that it is proposed to construct new 8 inch water mains and
appurtenances along two private roads for water system improvement The private roads
are labeled as Private Road A and Private Road B as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the plan and profile drawing provided the pipe inverts are not known
at this time but they are expected to be about 3 feet below ground surface In order to
evaluate the need for corrosion control protection a field resistivity testing was
conducted along the water main alignment in general accordance with the Water
System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 1991 Detailed discussions
and results of our corrosion tests are presented in the following sections of this letter
report
PURPOSE AND SCOPE

characteristics of the subsurface soils along the proposed 8 inch water mains planned at
the project site Our work was performed in general conformance with ASTM Test
Designation G 57 95 and the requirements provided in the Water System External
Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 1991 The scope of our work consisted of the
following tasks and work efforts

FIELD SURVEY
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site on
for the field survey Our field work was performed under sunny
conditions with generally dry surface conditions
Twenty eight soil resistivity surveys were conducted at a maximum of 300 foot
intervals at seven locations designated as R 1 through R 7 along the shoulder of the
existing roads using the in Pin Method and a resistance meter Nilsson
Model 400 manufactured by Nilsson Electrical Laboratory Inc The method utilizes four
surface probes or pins electrodes to evaluate the average resistivity an indication of
corrosivity of the underlying soil mass The average resistivity values of the soil mass
extending from the existing ground surface to the test depth was assessed by using this
method at the approximate survey locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Results of
the resistivity surveys are presented on Plates 3 1 and 3 2
The soil resistivity surveys were performed at the existing ground surface along the
shoulder of the existing roads of the proposed 8 inch water mains The average soil
resistance along the proposed alignment was evaluated at each survey location with the

electrodes aligned at equal spacing of generally 3 to 6 feet at the existing ground surface
The depth of penetration of a resistivity sounding is a function of the electrode spacing
Since the depth of the water main invert was not available at the time of our field
exploration a spacing of 3 to 6 feet was used for the electrode spacing for each soil
resistivity survey location
The resistance meter Nilsson Model 400 impresses a voltage between the outer
electrodes causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between
the inner electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the
soil resistance in ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity in ohm cm
which is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The
evaluated soil resistances the computed resistivity values and the corresponding
corrosion ratings are presented on Plates 3 1 and 3 2 of this report Field Resistivity
Testing Summaries are presented on Plates 4 1 and 4 2 It should be noted that the soil
resistivity may be influenced by temperature weather conditions existing underground
facilities and other factors
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics
with regard to the corrosivity of the soil to buried metallic objects In general the lower
the resistivity the greater the potential for corrosion of the buried metallic structure
Conversely the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion of
metallic objects
On the basis of the field resistivity test results a majority of the test locations
indicate the subsurface soils along Private Road A within the project site have corrosion
ratings of 2 to 4 This indicates the subsurface soils to have very corrosive to moderately
corrosive potential Isolated resistivity measurements indicating corrosion ratings of 1 and
5 were noted A majority of the test locations indicate the subsurface soils along Private
Road B within the project site have corrosion ratings of 1 This indicates the subsurface
soils to have extremely corrosive potential
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from
field data points by means of geophysical surveys Variation of soil conditions between
and beyond the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations
may not become evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to reevaluate the ratings provided in this report
