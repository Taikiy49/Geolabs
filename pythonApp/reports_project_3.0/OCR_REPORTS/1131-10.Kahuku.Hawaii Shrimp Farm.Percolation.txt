INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to provide subsurface soil
permeability data for use in the design of the proposed injection and
salt water supply wells for the proposed shrimp farm
The results of our field exploration and tests which were used in
the analyses that formulated our conclusions and recommendations are
presented in the appendices of this report
The scope of this investigation included drilling development
and pump testing of two wells one for salt water supply and one for
injection The locations of the test wells are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct an aquacultural facility at the
project site for the purpose of raising marine shrimp Detailed
description of this facility are contained in our previous Soils
Engineering Report dated
In order to support the aquacultural activities it will be
necessary to supply saline water at a production rate of about 7000
the water through the aquacultural system it will be necessary to
dispose of the water at approximately the same rate To this end
it is proposed to drill and develop a series of supply wells on the
northeastern end of the site and a series of injection wells on the
southwestern end of the site The general project layout is shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is located on the north flank of the Koolau
Volcano This volcanic shield formed a salient for the growth of
coral algal reefs on the seaward fringes of northern Oahu

During the Pleistocene sea level fluctuated widely as the result
of glacio eustatic changes When the sea stood at higher levels than
the present the coral algal reefs grew to elevations above present
sea level The coral algal formations encountered at the project site
have been assigned to the Waimanalo stand of the sea at elevation 25
Stearns 1978

After the drilling of the test wells they were developed by air
lifting using a compressor As the water being developed cleared the
supply of compressed air was closed off and then opened to create a
surging effect which produced additional flow of sand This process
was continued for approximately 1 5 hours per well at which time
only minimal amounts of sand were developed with each surge
Following the development the wells were tested using a 3 inch
centrifugal pump to eject or inject water as applicable Flow rates
were measured by the use of an in line paddle wheel flow sensor The
pump test data is shown on Plates B 1 through B 6
Upon completion of the pump testing all of the drilled holes were
backfilled to avoid presenting a safety hazard to people or livestock
in the area
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The test wells which were drilled at the project site encountered
coral algal reef formation In general coral algal materials are
quite permeable and can readily transmit clear water for both supply
and injection wells
Supply Well Permeability
The pump test performed at the supply well site indicated very
good permeability in the coral algal materials The field data showed
a drawdown of 0 6 foot for a discharge rate of 220 G P M
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1131 10 Page 7
This data leads to a calculated coefficient of permeability k of
0 6 foot per minute
It should be noted that this calculated value was obtained by
pumping from an open well with no casing or screen therefore entry
head losses are minimal For schematic diagram see Plate B 2
In the drilling and testing of our test wells it was noted that
there was a substantial increase in permeability at a depth of about
50 feet This change may be due to an unconformity between two
coral algal formations or the limit of the solution reworking of the
limestone which occurred when the Waimanalo age reef was exposed
during the Makai Range 60 foot stand of the sea
It is our understanding that the existing supply wells at the
nearby aquacultural developments have been pump tested to discharge
rates up to 7000 G P M with drawdowns averaging between 0 and 4
feet The best performance was obtained from wells on the order of
100 feet deep This appears to be in general agreement with our test
data
Injection Well Permeability
The pump test performed for the injection well area also indicated
good permeability The field data for the gravity injection test

It should be noted that this value was obtained from testing an

Conversations regarding existing disposal wells at nearby develop
ments indicate that they could be handling up to 12 M G D with small
head build up for gravity injection Once again an increase in per

Supply Well Design
Based on the pump test data the anticipated discharge for various
well sizes depths and drawdown are summarized on Plate B 3 The
calculations were based on uniform soil conditions from the water
level to 100 feet deep
We recommend that the supply wells should be constructed with
screens to protect against formation caving and to minimize sand
pumping It is preferable that removable type screens be used to
allow for cleaning of possible incrustation
Injection Well Design
Based on the field test data our calculation indicated that a
disposal well with a diameter of 3 feet and a depth of 100 feet could
accommodate a gravity injection of about 6 M G D with a 2 foot head
build up However it should be stressed that this assumption is
based on injection of clear water without air entrainment The
calculated relationship between discharge and head build up for
different size and depth of wells is presented on Plate B 6
GEOLABS HAWAII

Typically water injected into disposal wells contains suspended
solids micro organisms and other matter which may reduce the
potential permeability Here in Hawaii there have been numerous
failures of disposal wells injecting into coral algal formations
These failures have been attributed to numerous causes e g lack of
maintenance accummulation of solids and formation caving
In view of this past history of failures we recommend that the
injection system be overdesigned to allow for reduction in capacity or
well failure In addition provisions should be made in the facility
design for a maintenance program to extend the useful life of the
wells
The injection wells could be constructed with a removable screen
or perforated casing to protect against formation caving and to prevent
migration of accummulated solids into the injection formation The
screens could be removed at regular intervals to be cleaned and emptied
of accummulated solids Upon completion of this maintenance procedure
the screens should be replaced and the wells redeveloped
It may be desirable to include at least one stand by well that can
be used in the event of reduced injection capacity peak flows during
maintenance or construction of other wells

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil engineering principles
and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other warranties
either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are
based on our site reconnaissance information from boring data and
laboratory tests obtained from this investigation
Unanticipated conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by reconnaissance and borings These unforeseen
conditions such as pockets of caving formation impermeable strata or
artesian conditions may occur in localized areas Therefore addi
tional expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a
properly constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recom
mended to accommodate these possible costs

the preparation of cost estimates and preliminary sizing of the wells
for this project

drilling 0 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

presentation of the materials encountered is presented on the
Log of Borings Plates A 1l through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving
a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler 12 inches are
shown on the log of borings at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of the coral were obtained by using a
rotary wash NWD4 series double tube core barrel The core
recovery and rock quality designation RQD are presented on
the logs of the boring

APPENDIX B
Injection well tests were performed in Boring IW 1
to evaluate the percolation quality of the subsoils for use
in injection well disposal of treated effluent
Pump out or drawdown tests were performed in Boring SW 1
to evaluate the permeability of the subsoils for use in the
water supply source well design
Six inch diameter exploratory borings were drilled
to a depth of 50 feet for both test wells Following the
construction of the test wells preliminary testing was
performed on the wells The wells were first developed by
airlifting with an air compressor After development pump
tests were performed using a 3 inch centrifugal pump delivering
water from a nearby supply well Flow rates were measured
using an in line paddle wheel flow sensor A sketch of the
drawdown and injection tests are shown on Plates B 2 and B 5
The test data are presented on Plates B 1 and B 4
