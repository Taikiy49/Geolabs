INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical engineering
evaluation performed for the proposed Mademoiselle Spa Lady and Kalauao
Springs Ditch Bridge Crossing at the Pearl Kai Center at Aiea Oahu
Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1

PROJECT CONSTDERATTONS
The proposed project will include the construction of a two story
Spa building which is 50 by 100 feet in plan dimensions with an
adjacent parking area It is our understanding that a wooden structure
rather than a concrete bridge is planned for the crossing of Kalauao
Stream Ditch Structural loads for the building and bridge were not
available at the time of our study
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil data for use in the foundation
design of the proposed structures
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located across Kamehameha Highway from the
existing Pearl Ridge Shopping Center in Aiea on the island of Oahu
Hawaii To the east of the site across the Kalauao Springs Ditch is
the Pearl Kai Center development presently under construction

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The geologic conditions underlying the site are the results of a
complex series of depositional and erosional events brought on by
glaciceustatic sea level changes and post erosional volcanic activities
during the Pleistocene Following the cessation of activity of the
Koolau Volcano alluvial materials were deposited in a deltaic or
estuarine environment in the area of Pearl Harbor
The sea level fluctuated many times receding perhaps as much as 300
feet below and rising up to 95 feet above the present sea level
Concurrent with a rise in sea level during the late Pleistocene
era eruption of the Salt Lake Makalapa member of the Honolulu Volcanic
series mantled the Older Alluvium with thick deposits of volcanic ash
which consolidated to form volcanic tuff locally called mudrock
layers Subsequent erosion dissected and thinned those mantles of
tuff During the Recent epoch as sea level rose to its current stand
the area was covered with lagoonal marine deposits

Groundwater Condition
The project site is located within an area which serves as one of
the main discharge points for the Pearl Harbor Aquifer As a result
artesian conditions were encounteed at this site
Groundwater conditions were continuously monitored during the soil
exploration Furthermore one shallow and one deep piezometer have been
placed to record the piezometer heads and provide a more detailed record
of the change of water head elevations The measured water levels
during the drilling operation and those of the installed piezometers are
detailed in Groundwater Conditions Summary Plate 3 It should he
understood that the water head elevations indicated might not reflect
the actual regional artesian head because the borings and piezometers do
not extend completely through the caprock into the basaltic formation

The regional heads in the vicinity of the site are in a state of
change According to the hydraulic head contour maps published by the
Board of Water Supply 1980 regional heads in the site vicinity were
approximately 33 feet MSL in 1879 23 feet in 1958 and 17 feet in
1978 Therefore we feel that further head declines with time are
expected
The groundwater conditions summary show the variation observed both
between borings and within each individual boring location in depth to
water measurements
The piezometer readings indicate that an average piezometric head
of 4 5 feet elevation exists at an approximate elevation of 33 feet
within the soft organic sandy silt The average head of 1 75 feet was
measured from approximately 1 2 feet elevation within the stiff fill
Soil Characteristics
Four consolidation tests have been performed on the soft
compressable soils which underlie this site the results of which are
presented on Plates B 1 to B 4 These test results indicate that these
soils are normally consolidated
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain by a 5
to 8 foot thick stiff surface crust over soft compressible soils
It appears that the existing crust is suitable for foundation
support of the proposed relatively light building and bridge structures
Significant fills would initiate long tem ground settlements
Therefore fills should be minimized or avoided

Spa _ Building
The two borings which were drilled at the Spa site encountered soft
compressible soils underlying an 8 foot thick surface crust The stiff
to hard clayey silt crust can provide suitable foundation support for
the proposed Spa Building utilizing spread or continuous wall footings
The recommended minimm footing width is 2 feet with a minimm
embedment of 2 feet The allowable bearing capacity at this site for
this footing configuration is 3000 p s f The bearing capacity is for
total dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for
temporary wind and seismic loads
The bottom of all footings should be free of loose soils and
observed by the soils engineer prior to concrete placement
Due to the compressible soils underlying the site it is
inches would occur under a building colum load of 20 and 30 kips
respectively provided no additional fill is placed at the site
If additional fill is placed for the site development the building
construction may have to be delayed until the ground settlement from the
new fill weight has stopped
Bridge
The two borings which were placed on each side of the Kalauao
Springs Ditch encountered a crust which is thinner and slightly less
firm than that encountered at the Spa site The crust along the ditch
is only 5 feet thick and is composed of medium stiff to stiff clayey
silt Soft compressible soils underlie the crust

Spread or continuous footing foundations may be used for the
proposed bridge A minimm footing embedment of 2 feet is recommended
The allowable bearing capacity for this site is 2000 p s f The bearing
capacity is for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third for temporary wind and seismic loads The design abutment
loads were not available however it is anticipated that settlements on
the order of 1 to 2 and 2 5 to 3 5 inches may occur with loads of 5 and
10 kips per linear foot respectively The settlements may occur
slowly therefore provisions to relevel the structure may be necessary
The bottom of the footing excavations should be overexcavated 1
foot and recompacted to 90 of its maximm dry density as established by
ASTM D 1557 78
The bridge footings should be protected from erosion and placed a
minimum of 5 feet back from the ditch slope
Pavement and Parking Areas
Based on the results of our field study the preliminary pavement
design is as follows
2 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil samples
during construction and the above design section can be revised
accordingly
Site Grading
Site grading plans for the Mademoiselle Spa Lady were not available
for our review We recommend that fills be limited to less than 1 foot
to reduce ground settlement due to the weight of the new fills

Site Preparation
Prior to placing the compacted fill the site should first be
Cleared of vegetation rubbish debris and other unsuitable
materials
Clearing and grubbing should consist of removing the brush above
the ground surface Roots larger than about 1 inch should also be
removed It is estimated that the clearing and grubbing of the
surface vegetation and organic material will average about 6
inches
Fill Material
Excavated on site material free of any organic and other expansive
component may be used as a source of fill material All imported
fill material should consist of selected non expansive silty sandy
or granular materials less than 3 inches in size
Fill Placement
All fill materials should be placed in maximum of 10 inch
horizontal loose lifts and compacted to a minimm of 90 of the
specified maximum density
Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and pavement
design should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included

Construction Monitoring
We recommend that all footing excavations be observed by the soils
engineer to check that adequate bearing and compaction have been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistant with the
findings of our investigation
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in
this report then appropriate modifications to the design should he
made
LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this soil exploration
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such
as pockets of weak organic soils trash fill or expansive soils may
occur in localized areas and may require additional probing or
corrections in the field Therefore additional expenditures may be
needed during construction to attain a properly constructed project
Some contingency fund is thus recommended to accommodate these possible
costs
This report has been prepared in order to assist the engineer in
the preparation of the foundation design of this project

Field Exploration

four test borings to depth of 41 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by one of our engineers who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory A graphic
presentation of the soils encountered is presented on the Logs of
accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a standard
penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel ring sampler with a
140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler for 12 inches are shown on the Iogs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
