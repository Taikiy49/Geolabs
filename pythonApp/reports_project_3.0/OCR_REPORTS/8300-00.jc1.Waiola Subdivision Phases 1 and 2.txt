PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The proposed Waiola Subdivision project is on the southern side of Nawiliwili Road 
in the Puhi area on the Island of Kauai, Hawaii, as shown on the Project Location Map, 
Plate 1. The Waiola Subdivision development is divided into three phases of construction. 
This report covers our observation and testing during the earthwork construction between 
July 17 and December 16, 2021 for Phase 1 and Phase 2 construction. 
The Phase 1 development includes the construction of 87 lots with roadways and 
underground utility installation, encompassing approximately 47.3 acres, as shown on the 
Site Plan, Plate 2.1. The Phase 2 development includes the continuation of Phase 1 for 19 
lots and roadways within approximately 0.59 acres, as shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2.2. 
The grading and construction of the subdivision generally consisted of subgrade cuts 
and fills, trench excavations for utility installation and backfills, and pavement base course 
and/or subbase preparations. Earthworks Pacific, Inc. performed the earthwork operations 
for the project.

SITE GRADING 
At the onset of earthwork, areas within the contract grading limits were generally 
cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials. Materials generated from 
the clearing operations were disposed of off-site. Following the clearing and grubbing 
operations, the areas to be cut were excavated. Finished grades were compacted using 
a sheepsfoot and/or smooth vibratory drum roller. 
Soft and yielding areas encountered during site grading were generally 
over-excavated to expose firm natural material, and the resulting excavation was backfilled 
with on-site general fill. 
In general, heavy construction equipment was utilized in the site grading operation, 
including Deere grader, Caterpillar scraper, bulldozer, and sheepsfoot wheel compactor. A 
water truck provided moisture-conditioning while the grading equipment moved/compacted 
the in-situ soils during the excavating and filling mass grading operation. 
Considering the heavy Caterpillar 815B sheepsfoot wheel compactor used in the fill 
operation, loose lift thickness was recommended to be increased from 8 to 12 inches, 
providing better compacted lift, with moisture-conditioning within 3 percent of the optimum 
moisture content. We believe the above loose lift thickness was appropriated using a 
Caterpillar 815B sheepsfoot compactor and would likely provide sufficient compaction 
meeting the project specifications. 
The fill and backfill materials were generally moisture-conditioned to above the 
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum 
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. In the roadway areas, the 
subgrade was compacted to a minimum 95 percent of the maximum dry density. 
During the site grading operations, samples of the materials utilized as fill were 
obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing.  The maximum dry densities and 
optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 
(Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 3. 
Periodic in-situ moisture and density tests were conducted in general accordance 
with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method), to determine the adequacy of compaction of 
fills and backfills for infrastructure and utility installations.  
The test results are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.30. These tests were 
performed on moisture-conditioned and compacted lifts of fills and backfills. Test locations 
and elevations were estimated using on-site survey stakes for reference. These locations 
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods 
used. Areas deemed unsatisfactory were reworked until the measured relative 
compaction conformed to or exceeded the minimum specified for the project.
