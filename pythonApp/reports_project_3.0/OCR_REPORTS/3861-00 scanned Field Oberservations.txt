Field Observations
Excavation and Shoring AFFF Storage Tank Pit
Continental Airlines Maintenance Hangar
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with your request site visits were made to observe the reported
ground cracking adjacent to the excavation and to provide recommendations for the
removal of the shoring This letter summarizes our observations and
recommendations
A site visit was made in the morning to observe the reported ground cracking
adjacent to the excavation The following were observed
e The excavation was down to the final depth of about 17 feet below the
existing ground One row of struts was installed near the top of the
excavation at spacings of about 5 to 10 feet
e Five dewatering wells deep sumps were installed along the southern side
of the excavation next to the existing building Discharge from the
dewatering pumps was directed into excavated ponds on site Estimated
discharge was less than 50 gallons per minute gpm The bottom of the
excavation was relatively dry
e A 5 foot wide sidewalk was situated about 6 feet away from the
excavation between the excavation and the existing building The ground
adjacent to the sidewalk had pulled away from the sidewalk toward the
excavation leaving gaps of 0 5 to 1 5 inches wide

Based on the initial observations it appeared that the ground cracking may have
been caused by ground settlement due to lowering of the ground water level or
movement of the sheet pile supports
Following a review of the previous boring data as built drawings of the existing
building and the construction sequence a site visit was made to discuss possible
courses of action Present were Eric of Jayar Rusty of KSF and Kirk of Mortensen
A second discharge pit was being used for the disposal of effluent from the
dewatering The estimated discharge was still less than 50 gom The excavation was
generally dry with isolated areas of slight seepage through the sheet piles at a level of
5 to 6 feet above the bottom of excavation A portion of the mud slab had been poured
and the bottom waterproofing was being installed
Based on the observed water seepage through the sheet piles at a level well
above the bottom of the excavation and the penetration depth of the sheet piles it was
our opinion that the dewatering did not lower the ground water level outside of the
excavation appreciably Therefore the observed ground movement was probably due to
deflection of the sheet piles
Due to the proximity of the existing building which was found to be supported on
spread footing foundations about 3 feet deep there was a concern that additional large
lateral ground movements may affect the building foundations Therefore several
alternatives to reduce additional movements were discussed These alternatives are
summarized below
1 Leave struts and walers in place until permanent walls are constructed
This would limit the sheet pile wall deflections and corresponding ground
movements However this would not leave sufficient space for Mortensen
to install their forms and construct the permanent walls
2 Reinstall struts with longer walers to allow less struts to interfere with forms
This would maintain lateral support of the excavation with more space
between the struts However this would take a significant amount of time
and delay the concrete pour
2 Monitor the movements of the ground and building Reference points could
be established on the ground between the excavation and building to allow
monitoring of the ground movement during and after incremental removal of
the struts If the movements were too large the strut removal could be
stopped and some of the struts could be reinstalled if necessary
It was agreed to follow alternative number 3

It was reported that during the initial removal of the supports at the northern end
toward airport of the excavation some ground movements were noted A site visit was
made to evaluate the magnitude of movements
The remaining supports at the northern end of the excavation were cut off by the
welders They estimated lateral ground movements of about 1 inch inward due to the
removal of the struts along this 30 foot length of excavation Since the length of
excavation adjacent to the building was about 190 feet long several inches of movement
was projected for this larger area In order to limit the movements to about 1 inch we
recommended that some of the struts be left in place to maintain a maximum spacing
between struts of about 25 to 30 feet
The existing struts that were to remain were identified Removal of the struts
would be done incrementally with monitoring of the ground movements It was also
suggested that the ground cracks be filled with sand or soil to reduce water infiltration and
erosion
Recommendations
The existing building is located about 14 feet away from the excavation and
supported on spread footing foundations 3 feet below the ground surface therefore the
building foundations are situated at about a 45 degree plane from the bottom of the
excavation Generally at this location the building foundations should not be affected by
normal movements of the sheet pile wall However as a precaution we recommend that
some of the struts be left in place and the ground movements monitored during the
removal of the struts
