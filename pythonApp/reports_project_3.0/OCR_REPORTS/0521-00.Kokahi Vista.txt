This report presents the results of our geological reconnaissance
for the proposed cuts at Units E and F at the subject site The
purpose of our study was to investigate the site soil and geologic
conditions to only evaluate stability of proposed cuts for the
upper four units
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is presently planned to construct eight 2 story townhouse
residences of wood frame post and beam construction Slabs on
grade will be used for a portion of the residence Maximum cuts
on the order of 8 feet are anticipated in the upper portion of
the site for Units C D E and F

SITE CONDITIONS
_ LS
Surface
The site is wooded and densely vegetated The site slopes from
about 10 percent on the lower portion to as steep as 40 percent
on the upper portion Highly fractured basalt is exposed on
the steep slopes of the adjacent gully Some exposed basalt was
found near Unit C No surface instabilities of the site soils
were in evidence No groundwater water seepage was observed
however the site soils were in a wet condition
Geology
The island of Oahu was formed by two major volcanoes the Waianae
Shield Volcano and the Koolau Shield Volcano Ulumawao Peak and
associated ridge line on which the site is located is the cal
dera of the old Koolau Shield Volcano Dikes are numerous
throughout the area as are breccias associated with caldera
collapse The dikes and breccias are more resistant to erosion
compared to the thin lava flows Thus an erosional resistant
ridge line has resulted after extensive weathering of the less
resistant surrounding lava flows The exposed basalt has a
jointing pattern of striking N 802 W and dips almost vertically
The soils noted on the surface of the site are residual reddish
brown to light brown clayey silts Weathered basalt boulders
were observed in the gully adjacent to the site and may be
located within the cut areas

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Bee ee ae
Based on our surface reconnaissance it appears that the site
will be suitable for the proposed cuts of the rear lots which
compose Buildings C D E and F Cuts should be designed for
slopes 1 horizontal to 1 vertical It is anticipated that
the full depth of the cut will be through residual clayey silt
and some occasional weathered basalt It is recommended that
the cut slopes be inspected by a geologist or engineer from
our firm to verify that no geologic planes of weakness or
groundwater seepage exist below the present surface and sub
surface conditions are as anticipated
Retaining walls may be designed to resist active lateral pres
sures developed by a fluid with an equivalent pressure of 45
pounds per square foot The backfill immediately against the
retaining wall should be an open graded compacted granular
materials Weepholes or a subsurface drainage system should
be provided to prevent the build up of excessive pore pressures
behind walls
Erosion control measures should be provided to prevent surface
water from running over exposed slopes Also slopes should
be planted to reduce the rate of erosion
