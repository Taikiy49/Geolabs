Dear Ms Yanagawa
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing
services performed during the site grading of the Makai Drainage Channel project at
Mehana from through in accordance with our fee proposal
dated
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project included construction of the Makai Drainage Channel extending from
Fort Barrette Road to Kamokila Boulevard at Mehana development Site grading primarily
consisted of cuts to extend the channel from the mass grading elevations to the final
channel elevations with the connections of the drains from the Mehana subdivision to the
channel and channel lining in certain areas The project location and general site vicinity
are shown on the Project Location Map and Site Plan Plates 1 and 2 respectively
Earthwork involved partial excavation for the channel with approximately 6 000 feet
in length and base widths ranging between about 22 and 50 feet The earthwork also
included placement of fill for portions of the channel walls to create levees
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for the
site grading at the project site
SITE GRADING
We observed the site grading work for the project from through
At the on set of earthwork the area within the limits of grading was
cleared and grubbed of unsuitable surface materials Royal Contracting Co Ltd was the
Site Contractor for the project and performed the site grading During the site grading
operations samples of the on site soils and or imported fill materials were obtained and
D R Horton Schuler Division
Page 2

Hawaii California
transported to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture
contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557
Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D1557 Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
During the site grading operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D6938 Nuclear Gauge Method Compaction test results
are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 8 Test locations and elevations were estimated
using on site survey stakes for reference These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas that did not
meet the required moisture and density conditions were reworked until the required
minimum compaction were obtained or exceeded
Our services consisted of part time as requested field observation of the
contractor s grading activities and confirmation of subgrade soil conditions exposed during
the grading operations
Based on our field representative s observations and the referenced test results it is
our opinion that the site grading work for the Makai Drainage Channel at Mehana project
observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in
