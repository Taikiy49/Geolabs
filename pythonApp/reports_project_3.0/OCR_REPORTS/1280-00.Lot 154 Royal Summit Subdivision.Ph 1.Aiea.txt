In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to
provide foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The
study consisted of drilling two shallow borings laboratory testing of
the swelling potential of the soils reviewing of the subdivision site
grading plans and the preliminary house foundation plans This report
summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a two story wooden structure approxi
mately 36 by 56 feet in plan dimensions A basement level with CMU
walls up to 8 feet high will be included

Concrete on grade slabs are planned for the garage driveway open
lanai basement level and a portion of the main level of the house
Cuts up to 5 feet and fills generally less than 6 feet thick are
anticipated
CMU retaining walls 2 to 8 feet high are also planned to retain the
proposed 111 and to provide grade separation
The layout plan for the house is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the western side of Kuini Street in the
Royal Summit Phase 1 Subdivision located in Aiea Oahu Hawaii
Lot 154 is terraced and slopes downward from the front towards the
back A relatively level pad is located adjacent to the roadway The
site then slopes downward at about a 5 1 H V ratio to a lower second
level pad The ground surface then continues down towards a 2 1 H V
bank along the western boundary
There is an existing concrete lined drainage ditch located at the rear
of the site and runs parallel to the western boundary

SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
Two borings were drilled within the proposed building location to
depths ranging from 5 5 to 6 25 feet below the existing grade at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The borings encountered up to 5 5 feet of very stiff reddish brown
clayey silt underlain with saprolite highly decomposed rock in
Boring 2 to the full depth drilled
Our plan review indicated that this lot has received up to about 11
feet of compacted fill during the initial subdivision development
No groundwater was encountered during our investigation
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The stiff reddish brown clayey silt will generally be suitable for
foundation support of the proposed house provided that some care is
exercised during construction
The field exploration and laboratory testing performed on the stiff
clayey silt soil indicates that this material generally has low
swelling potential if it is kept moist Previous experience
indicates that its swelling potential will increase if allowed to dry
out Therefore to minimize the swelling potential the subgrade soil
should be kept moist after excavation and protected fron drying during
the period of time required for preparation prior to final concrete
placement

It is anticipated that up to 6 feet of new fill will be required
behind the basement wall to match the existing grade at the front
portion of the lot
Ideally select non expansive soils such as select borrow crusher
waste or coral should be used for fill within 2 feet of the house
finished grade This would provide a competent bearing layer for the
foundations in this portion of the house
The on site stockpile of excess material from the Royal Summit
Subdivision grading work may be available for fill at a lower costs
Although this material generally has low to moderate swelling
potential it could have been contaminated with other expansive soil
during handling Therefore it is recommended that these soils be
only used up to 2 feet below the slab subgrade and the remaining upper
2 feet should consist of select borrow non expansive granular soil
The garage driveway and basement floor will probably bear on the
in situ existing soils The foundation and subgrade preparation
recommendations are listed in the following sections
Foundations on Imported Select Borrow
Foundations bearing on the minimum 2 foot cap of compacted
non expansive select borrow material may use an allowable bearing
value of 3000 p s f

Foundation in On Site Soil
Special precautionary measures are recommended for foundations placed
on the existing soils to minimize the swelling potential The
recommended subgrade preparation prior to final concrete placement
described under the SLAB ON GRADE section of this report should be
followed
We further recommend that a deeper footing embedment of 12 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade for individual footings and
18 inches for perimeter slab footings be used for the stiff on site
soils An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used
The bottom of all footing excavations should be cleaned of loose
material and bear on firm soil The excavation should be observed by
a soils engineer to see that adequate bearing soil has been reached

The ground surface should be shaped to drain water away from the

Site Grading
Prior to filling the area to receive any fill should be cleared of
vegetation and debris
Fills and backfills should consist of non expansive soil such as
select borrow and be placed in maximum 8 inch loose lifts and then
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established
by ASTM D 1557 78
If the on site soil from Royal Summit is used for fill it should
first be approved by a qualified soils engineer and should generally
be placed wet of optimum moisture content 30 to reduce the
swelling potential

majority of the house foundation Where a minimum of 2 feet of
imported non expansive soil such as crusher waste is used for the
111 conventional slab on grade design with an 8 inch deep thickened
edge may be used

procedures listed hereunder for lime saturation be followed

footing excavations prior to water saturating of the subgrade soil
We believe that about ten 50 1b bags of hydrated lime would be

2 days with perforated hoses by constant sprinkling prior to
concrete placement to reduce the swelling potential of the
underlying subsoil It is important that the soils engineer should
be notified to inspect the lime preparation and check the moisture
contents of the underlying soils prior to pouring of the slab and
perimeter footing
The bottom of the finished subgrade and Footing excavations should
be neat and free of loose soils It is anticipated that the very
soft silt and lime mud deposited at the footing after saturation
would be removed just prior to concrete placement

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should be
shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems should be
installed around the house and that gutter water should be diverted
far away from the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape
watering near the foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition

Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to
drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill operations
be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
