PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project involves improvements to the existing Anahola Farm Lots Water 
System located in Anahola on the island of Kaua’i. The project generally consists of the 
following: 
• Replacement of the existing 0.5 million-gallon (MG) reservoir tank with 
a new 0.44 MG concrete tank  
• Installation and removal of a Temporary 0.1 MG Steel Reservoir Tank 
• Installation of an in-line booster pump assembly 
• Installation of a new emergency generator and fuel tank 
• Addition to the existing Control Building 
• Installation of new retaining walls

SITE GRADING & FOUNDATION CONSTRUCTION 
We observed the site grading work for the project from December 1, 2020 through 
January 12, 2023. Site grading for the project generally consisted of excavations and fills 
up to about 10 feet thick to achieve the finished design grades for the project.  
At the on-set of earthwork, the area within the limits of grading was cleared and 
grubbed of unsuitable surface materials. Kiewit Infrastructure West Co. was the general 
contractor and performed the site grading. During the site grading operations, samples of 
the on-site soils and imported fill materials were obtained and transported to our laboratory 
for testing. The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established 
in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test 
results are summarized on Plate 3.  
The fill and backfill materials we observed were moisture-conditioned to above the 
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative 
compaction. The imported select granular fill material used below the water tank 
foundations, utility trench crossings, and access roads was moisture-conditioned to near 
the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative 
compaction. Relative compaction refers to the in-place dry density of soil expressed as a 
percentage of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D1557. Optimum 
moisture is the water content (percentage by dry weight) corresponding to the maximum 
dry density. 
During the site grading operations, periodic field density tests were performed in 
general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). Compaction test results 
are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.9. Test locations and elevations were estimated 
using on-site survey stakes for reference. These locations and elevations should be 
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas that did not 
meet the required moisture and density conditions were reworked until the required 
minimum compaction was obtained or exceeded. 
Our services consisted of part-time, as-requested field observation and testing of 
the contractor’s grading activities which included foundation subgrade preparation, fill and 
backfill placement, and confirmation of subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above 
grading operations.
