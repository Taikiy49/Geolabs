This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the grading and infrastructure installation work for the Kalakaua Gardens project
from through March 22 2016
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at 1723 Kalakaua Avenue adjacent to the
Kapiolani Boulevard Corridor in the Ala Moana area of Honolulu The project site consists
of four parcels of land encompassing approximately 30 000 square feet of land area In
general the project involves the construction of a 17 story high rise building for
senior living with a lobby dining area kitchen management offices landscaped walking
paths and parking on the ground floor
The new high rise building structure is supported on a deep foundation system
ACIP piles The two story NUHC building at the northern corner of the project site is
supported on a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous
footings The layout of the new senior living community project is shown on the Site Plan

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Swinerton Builders is the general contractor for the project Jennings Pacific LLC
and Rons Construction Corporation performed the site grading operations pavement
construction and utility line installation The site grading work consisted of subgrade
preparation placement and compaction of fills and backfills pavement construction and
utility trench excavation and backfill
Following the demolition clearing and foundation installation operations the
excavated subgrades underneath future slab and pavement areas were scarified to a
depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 and 95 percent relative compaction under the building slab
and pavement areas respectively of the maximum dry density determined in accordance
with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor
Due to the presence of soft and or loose lagoonal deposits the footing subgrades
at the NUHC building were generally over excavated to a depth of about 3 feet below the
bottom of footing elevations The over excavation was then lined with a layer of woven
geotextile fabric Mirafi 500X and backfilled with 18 inches of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C33 No 67 gradation at the bottom The remaining backfill consisted of structural
fill material placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction A layer of non woven geotextile fabric Mirafi 180N was provided
between the upper structural fill material and the underlying gravel backfill The woven
geotextile fabric was wrapped around the entire gravel and structural backfill to increase
the supporting capability of the structural fill material To increase the rigidity of the
structural backfill for uniform bearing support a layer of biaxial geogrid reinforcement
was provided at about the mid depth of the structural backfill
Fill and backfill materials required for the project generally were placed in near level
lifts brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 or
95 percent under the building slab and pavement areas respectively of the maximum dry
density determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 310 field density tests were performed using the

LIMITATIONS

performed without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the
geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor
earthwork construction and materials
