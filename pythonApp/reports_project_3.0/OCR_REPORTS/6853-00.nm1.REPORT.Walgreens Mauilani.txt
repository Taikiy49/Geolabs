Dear Mr. Kaiwikuamoo: 
This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed 
during the grading and infrastructure installation work for the Walgreens at Mauilani Village 
Center project from May 14, 2013 through February 14, 2014. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The Walgreens at Mauilani Village Center project is located at the northeastern 
corner of the intersection of Kuikahi Drive and Waiale Road in Wailuku on the Island of 
Maui, Hawaii.  The new structure will consist of an approximately 14,550 square foot 
building with a slab-on-grade foundation design. The layout of the new Walgreens building 
is shown on the Site Plan, Plate 1. 
Based on the site grading plan, minimal cuts and fills over the general pad were 
required to achieve the design grades. However, there will also be new concrete retaining 
walls along the north and east sides of the property, which involved the placement of fill 
material up to 13 feet in height. In addition, a new subterranean drainage system was 
installed along with other service utilities including electric, sewer and water lines. The 
scope of work discussed in this letter report includes observation and compaction testing 
during the earthwork and infrastructure installation for the Walgreens structure 
development. 
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
Arita Poulson General Contracting, LLC was the general contractor for the project. 
T.J. Gomes Trucking Company, Inc. performed the site grading operations, pavement 
construction, and utility line installation for the Walgreens at Mauilani Village Center 
project. The site grading work consisted of subgrade preparation, placement and 
compaction of fills and backfills, pavement construction, and utility trench excavation and 
backfill.

Following the site clearing, the project site was raised to the design finish grades 
with approximately 1 to 4 feet of fill materials and up to 13 feet of fills at the retaining walls 
along the north and east facing sides of the project site.  
Subsequent to the foundation installation work, the density and moisture content of 
the subgrade soil below future slab, retaining wall footings and pavement areas were 
verified. In areas where corrections were required, the soils were scarified to a depth of 
about 8 inches, moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture content, and 
proof-rolled with a 10-ton vibratory drum roller to obtain a minimum of 90 percent relative 
compaction under the building slab, retaining wall footings and backfills and 95 percent 
under pavement areas, respectively, of the maximum dry density determined in 
accordance with American Society of Testing and Material (ASTM) D 1557 (Modified 
Proctor).  
Fill and backfill materials required for the project generally were placed in near-level 
lifts, brought to above the optimum moisture, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent 
under the building slab, retaining wall backfills, various utility line backfills and 95 percent 
under pavement areas, respectively, of the maximum dry density determined in 
accordance with ASTM D 1557 (Modified Proctor). 
During the site grading operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing 
for compliance to project specifications and local County requirements. The maximum dry 
densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general 
accordance with ASTM D 1557 (Modified Proctor). 
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an 
as-need (on-call to full-time) basis to confirm our field observations as variations in 
construction procedures and soil conditions were noted. A total of 533 field density tests, 
including retests of failed areas, were performed using the nuclear method in accordance 
with ASTM D 6938. These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on 
constructed fills and backfills at the site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by 
pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These locations and elevations should 
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed 
to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained. 
The test results and our observations were documented in our daily field reports that were 
transmitted to you. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading, 
pavement and utility line construction for the above project observed and tested by 
Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in general accordance with 
the intent of the project plans and specifications.

LIMITATIONS
lt should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site. Therefore, some of the earthwork were
performed without our observation. However, the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the
geotechnical risks to the project. It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor
earthwork construction and materials.
