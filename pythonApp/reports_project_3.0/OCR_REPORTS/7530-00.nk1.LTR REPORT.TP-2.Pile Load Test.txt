Dear Mr. Montalvo: 
In accordance with the project specifications, a compressive pile load test was 
conducted on a selected test pile, designated as Test Pile No. 2 (Pile No. 73) on 
June 2, 2017 for the Bachelor Enlisted Quarters project.  This letter report presents the 
results of our field observations and the test data obtained during the pile load test. 
The compressive pile load test was conducted on a 16.5-inch octagonal precast, 
prestressed concrete pile.  Based on the project shop drawings, Test Pile No. 2 was cast 
with a length of 60 feet. The test pile was driven to a depth of about 48.5 feet below the 
existing ground surface, which corresponds to a pile tip elevation of about -39.2 feet Mean 
Sea Level (MSL).  The approximate location of the load test pile is shown on the Test Pile 
Location Plan, Plate 1. 
The test pile was driven using a Delmag D30-23 diesel hammer capable of 
delivering a maximum rated energy of 66,100 foot-pounds. Test Pile No. 2 was advanced 
to an elevation of -39.2 feet MSL on May 22, 2017 using Pump Setting 2.  The test pile 
was restruck on May 30, 2017, advancing the pile approximately 1/16th of an inch to an 
elevation of -39.2 feet MSL using Pump Setting 2. During the test pile driving, a 
Geotechnical QC Specialist maintained a record of driving resistance (blow counts) and 
field observations of the driving behavior.  The test pile driving record and restrike record 
are presented on Plates 2.1 and 2.2 of this report. 
The maximum test load was specified at 700 kips.  The test pile was loaded in 
35-kip load increments (5% of the maximum test load) in general accordance with 
ASTM D1143/1143M Procedure A – Quick Test.  Each load increment was held for at 
least 8 minutes.  The pile was loaded to the maximum planned test load of 700 kips and
