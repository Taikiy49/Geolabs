The stucco finish outside of the house shows several cracks some of
which had been previously patched and since have reopenned These
reopened cracks although relatively small indicate continued
deformation of the slab and the wall
We believe that the crack pattern of the southern wall Plate 3
indicates that there had been moderate settlement of the house in the
southern side and some shrinkage of the stucco itself around the
dining room window The crack along the top of the slab and tilting
of the fence wall next the kitchen suggest that there had been settle
ment of the ground This settlement most probably is due to the
densification of the poorly compacted backfill in the storm drain
trench that runs along the southern side of the house This backfill
densification may have also caused the cracks in the fence wall near
the southwestern corner of the lot

The cracks in the southern side of the garage floor Plate 2 and the

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Although some possible causes of cracks are discussed in the above
paragraphs we consider that the evidences themselves i e the crack
patterns are not adequate enough to positively identify any one cause
or causes at this time Further identification of the cause may be
made after reviewing the previous construction activities the repair
history and the actual subsurface conditions by obtaining and testing
subsoil samples at selected locations
Theoretically speaking the rates of swelling or settlement from
various causes tend to diminish with the passage of time Therefore
we believe that the future crack potential would be relatively small

In general if all cracks are patched the magnitude of future
openings should not exceed those already occurred Therefore it is
felt that the cracks should be patched and their movements monitored
As much as cracks on both the northern and southern walls can be
attributed in part to heaving of subsoils buried cut off walls along
a portion of these wall may be effective The depth of any cut off
wall should be a minimum of 2 feet below the ground There should be
water tight sealing between the house walls and these cut off walls
buried along the edge of the house The length of cut off wall should
cover at least the dining room kitchen and the lanai areas on the
southern stucco wall and should cover from the master bedroom bath to
the western edge of the northern stucco wall
We suggest that photographic records of the cracks be made on a
periodical basis to provide visual comparisons with time The time
interval of these photographic recording should not be more than 4
months
There may be cracks that are significantly large that further deforma
tion will not be structurally tolerable The existence of the cracks
although not identified at on site visit should be judged by a struc
tural engineer

There may also be cracks that are caused by loss of soil mass that
leaked through cracks or joint opennings in the buried storm drain
beneath The cracks or joint opennings of the storm drain can be
inspected from inside the drain after a precautionary safety steps
were taken
If underpinning or recompaction of the material beneath of the house
is desired it would be necessary to excavate test pits or drill test
borings to determine the extent and degree of soil mass that may need
to be densified We will be pleased to provide advices on such
matters This work may be done concurrently with the construction of
the buried cut off walls mentioned above if so desired
