REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be of
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be of late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age The project site is located on the seaward fringe of the Honolulu
Coastal Plain The coastal plain is composed of interbedded alluvial marine and post
erosional volcanic deposits Extensive development of the plain and reclamation of the
lowlying areas have given the land its present shape
There is evidence that a basaltic flow of the Honolulu Volcanic Series possibly
originating from the Makuku vent in Nuuanu or from Punchbowl reached the eastern
portion of what is now Sand Island During the Waipio stand of the sea at elevation
60 feet Mean Sea Level MSL this layer of basalt would have provided a resistant
surface which could have diverted the flows of Nuuanu and Kapalama Streams to the
west The diversion of these two streams would have resulted in deposition of extensive
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3398 00 Page 8
subaerial sediments consisting of alluvial clays and silts These exposed alluvial deposits
would have become consolidated due to seasonal moisture changes
Subsequently as the sea level rose to the Waimanalo stand at elevation 25 feet
MSL and then fell to its present level the stream laid alluvium was buried beneath
backreef deposits of sands as a fringing coral reef grew and emerged on the seaward
margin of the island Land reclamation projects during this century have built Sand Island
to its current topography

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration at the proposed sites for Scheme A indicated that the area
is generally underlain by a thin surficial fill crust overlying loose lagoonal deposits
extending to a depth of approximately 35 to 40 feet below the existing ground surface
Therefore we recommend that deep foundation systems consisting of driven piles or
drilled shafts be used to support the heavily loaded 3 story concrete building proposed for
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3398 00 Page 11
Scheme A The driven piles or drilled shafts would derive support primarily from skin
friction and or end bearing on the coralline deposits underlying the site as encountered in
our borings drilled for the project
in general driven pile foundation is preferred over drilled shaft foundation due to
the ease of installation and higher degree of quality control and workmanship The
performance of drilled shaft foundations will depend significantly upon the contractor s
method of construction construction procedures and workmanship Because of the
presence of loose sandy soils and high groundwater table anticipated at the project site
temporary casing and concrete placement by tremie methods will be required for the
drilled shaft construction Due to the relatively loose corailine structure underlying the
site there is a high potential for tremie concrete migration or spreading during placement
This could result in placement of concrete volume well in excess of the theoretical
volume Therefore we strongly recommend that driven pile foundations be used for the
proposed annex building or Scheme B be selected for the proposed expansion We
believe that vibrations generated from the pile driving operations may be reduced by
implementing the following measures during the foundation construction 1 predrilling
through the surficial dense fill crust at the proposed pile locations 2 use of a small pile
driving hammer and equipment and 3 temporary suspension of any sensitive laboratory
testing during the pile driving operations Based on the smail footprint of the proposed
addition and the relatively short pile lengths anticipated for the project we believe that the
pile installation process should be relatively short and any interruptions caused could be
minimized
For the 2 story building located at the northeast corner of Sand Island WWTP
under Scheme B we recommend that driven piles also be used for foundation support

of the proposed structure Similar measures and construction practices as described
above may be implemented to reduce the vibration impacts on the existing WQL building
during the pile driving operations if necessary
Concrete slabs on grade may be used for the proposed structures under both
schemes Because of the soft loose soils underlying the site we recommend that any
site filling required be initiated as early as possible to allow the existing ground to adjust to

and column foundations be used

Pile Foundation Settlements
Settlements of pile foundations will result primarily from the elastic compression of
the pile member and subgrade response We estimate the total settlement of the
pile supported foundation to be less than inch with differential settlements
between piles not exceeding about one half that amount We believe that these

Pile Hammer Selection
The piles should be driven with a hammer capable of delivering a rated energy
between 15 000 and 25 000 foot pounds The hammer should be equipped with
an energy level control such that driving within the loose soft soil may be controlled
at a lower energy to reduce the tensile stress development within the piles which
could damage or cause cracking to the piles
Determination of driving equipment to be used for this project should take into
consideration the matching of the pile hammer with the weight of the piles to be
driven This will result in the pile hammer efficiently delivering the required energy
to the top of the pile and also reducing damage to the piles Geolabs Hawaii
should review the pile driving equipment proposed to evaluate if the equipment is
capable of installing the piles to the recommended capacity The contractor
should forward pile and driving equipment data forms to Geolabs Hawaii prior to
construction for evaluation Geolabs Hawaii should review the submittal and

Pile Driving Criteria
For preliminary design purposes pile driving may be terminated when aggregated
blow counts are at least 50 blows per foot for the last foot or 5 blows per inch for
the last 3 inches This preliminary driving criteria should be confirmed after the
selection of the pile driving equipment
Each pile should be driven continuously without interruption A pile may be
rejected or its design capacity may be reduced when the driving resistance is
interrupted for more than four hours and the pile cannot be driven to the required
depth During soft driving i e when the pile tip is within soft loose soils the
hammer stroke or energy should be carefully controlled such that significant
tensile stresses do not develop within the pile
Because variations in soil strength characteristics are likely to exist in the bearing
soils the supporting capacity of each pile must be confirmed in the field by a
representative from our office during driving It should be noted that the pile
design was developed from our pile analysis using the field exploration data A
test pile program should be carried out to confirm our preliminary pile design and
establish a driving criteria The production pile driving criteria may be revised
based on the test pile program

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling

The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural

Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with

sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to drive

Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice
D 2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in the
hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt such as if 3 7 feet of material
is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and would be
shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 74

APPENDIX
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on selected soil
samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The results of
these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
One 1 inch ring swell test was performed on a selected sample to evaluate the
swelling potential of the subsoils The test results are summarized on Plate B 1
Four sieve analyses with hydrometer ASTM D 422 test were performed on
selected samples of the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to
aid in soil classification Graphic presentation of the grain size distribution is presented on
Plate B 2
Two direct shear tests were performed on selected samples to evaluate their shear
strength parameters The test results are presented on Plates B 3 and B 4
One California Bearing Ratio CBR ASTM D 1883 test was performed on a bulk
sample collected for pavement design Results of the CBR test are presented on
Plate B 5

Guide Specifications for Drilled Shafts
GEOLABS HAWAII
GUIDE FOR DRILLED SHAFT
Locations And Dimensions
Dilled shaft foundations shall be constructed at the locations to the lengths and in
accordance with the details and notes shown on the plans As built shaft lengths
shall extend to the depths shown on the plans and shall not be deeper by more
than 5 feet without prior written permission from Geolabs Hawaii Shaft diameters
shall be as shown on the plans for each individual drilled shaft The diameter
shown shall be the diameter of the drilling bit or bucket
Allowable Tolerances On Location Straightness and Plumbness
The center of the top of the shaft and reinforcing cage shall not deviate from the
location shown on the plans by more than 2 inches in any direction
The drilled shaft shall not deviate from a plumb line referenced to the center of the
drilied shaft as described above by more than 3 percent of the drilled shaft length
from the top to the point of interest
The clear distance between reinforcing steel and the drilled shaft hole sidewall
shall not be Jess than 3 inches
Equipment And Operation
Drilling casing setting of reinforcing cages pumping bailing concrete placement
pulling casing and all other phases of the shaft construction operation shall be
performed with equipment and tools capable of meeting the specifications
All loose material shall be removed from the hole prior to placing concrete
Cleaning shall be by machine or hand operated equipment Drilling and
placement of concrete shall be scheduled such that concrete is placed in each
drilled shaft hole on the same day in which drilling is completed Holes spaced
closer than three shaft diameters center to center shall not be drilled on the same
day unless approved by Geolabs Hawaii
Casing
Casing shall be used where needed to maintain the integrity of the drilled shaft
hole during drilling installation of reinforcing steel and placement of concrete
Where casing is used the hole shall not be drilled ahead of the casing any further
than the hole will stand without sloughing At any time sloughing occurs the hole

Inside casing diameter shall not be less than the required drilled shaft diameter

Concrete
Concrete shall comply with all provisions of the project plans At the time of
placement the concrete shall have a slump necessary to produce the specified
strength and should be determined by the cone slump test ASTM D 143 Unless
a higher compressive strength is specified elsewhere the concrete shall have a
minimum compressive strength of 3 000 p s i at 28 days as determined by the
concrete cylinder compression tests ASTM C 39 9
Reinforcing
The reinforcing cages shall be braced and tied in such a way that the cages are
not deformed or distorted during fabrication placement of the cage in hole and
concrete placement
Any reinforcing cage which is bent collapsed or distorted or which varies from a
straight line by more than 3 inches at any point shall be rejected
Reinforcing cages shall be set in the hole in such a way that they do not rake the
sidewall and cause sloughing Reinforcing cages shall not be spliced in the drilled
shaft holes instead they should be fabricated to the full length prior to placement
The reinforcing cage shall be hung from a solid support at the top of the hole so
that it does not bear on the bottom of the hole and hangs free in the hole The
reinforcing cage shall be set at the required location and elevation prior to
beginning placing concrete and shall be held and supported in such a way that it
does not move during concrete placement
Reinforcing dowels which are to be set in the concrete after completion of
placement if required are to be installed immediately following placement of
concrete and prior to the initial set of the concrete The concrete shall be vibrated
after setting the dowels The dowels shall be securely fixed with templates or by
other means in order that they are not displaced during vibration of the concrete

Driving dowels into the concrete after the initial set is forbidden In the event
dowels are not placed prior to the initial set of the concrete they shall be placed by
drilling and grouting as directed by the Structural Engineer
Placement Of Concrete
lf the hole is filled in the dry concrete shall be placed through a hopper and
elephant trunk positioned in the center of the reinforcing cage The elephant trunk
shall be long enough and be of sufficient diameter to prevent segregation The
hopper shall be of sufficient size to prevent spilling of concrete outside of the
elephant trunk
For placing concrete in a cased hole a minimum head of 5 feet of concrete shall
be maintained in the casing at all times Where staged casing is used the casing
shall be pulled and removed in units in such a way that after pulling one unit of
casing there is at least 5 feet of concrete in the remaining casing until the hole is
topped out at the cut off grade
Unless otherwise noted vibration of the concrete is not required until the concrete
reaches an elevation of approximately 10 feet below the top of the drilled shaft
After this elevation is reached the concrete shall be vibrated continuously to the
top of the drilled shaft and until good densification of the concrete has been
achieved Concrete shall be added as required to compensate for consolidation
and expansion into the hole The concrete vibrator and the vibration operation
shall conform to all requirements for internal concrete vibrators and vibration of
concrete given in the job specifications
The elapsed time from beginning the placement of concrete until completion shall
not exceed 1 hour Placement of concrete shall not commence until sufficient
concrete to complete the drilled shaft is on the job site waiting to be placed
Disposal Of Soil From Drilling
Soil obtained from drilling shall not be used as fill unless otherwise approved by
Geolabs Hawaii and it shall be disposed of off the site or used in landscaped
areas as appropriate as designated by the Architect or Civil Engineer

IX Observation
The drilling of holes setting of reinforcing setting and pulling of casing
dewatering placing concrete and any other operation involved in the construction
of the drilled shafts must be performed under the continuous observation by a
representative from Geolabs Hawaii The contractor shall give notice at least 48
hours in advance of his performing such work He shall do no such work in the
absence of a representative from Geolabs Hawaii
