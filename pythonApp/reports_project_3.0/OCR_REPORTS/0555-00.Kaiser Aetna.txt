Subject Evaluation of Proposed Kaluanui 2 Borrow Site
Marina 10 Area

Gentlemen
At your request we have obtained three soil samples from the pro
posed Kaluanui 2 borrow site located in the Marina 10 area
The borrow site is below Kaluanui Road and north of Hawaii Kai
Drive near its intersection with Wailua Street It consists of a
15 to 25 feet high stockpile approximately 300 by 500 feet in
plan dimensions
To determine the soil types within the stockpile seven backhoe
test pits were dug at various locations to obtain soil samples
for visual examination and laboratory testing The laboratory
tests include CBR expansion grain size sieve analysis and atter
berg limits The logs of the test pits are enclosed
The soils were grouped into three samples which represent the
predominate soils within the stockpile The soil types are des
cribed in the following paragraphs
Sample No 1
Sample No 1 is a light gray to tan silty sand with broken finger
coral The finger coral and sand sample was obtained from the
Slope surface located at the makai perimeter of the stockpile
Generally broken finger coral fragments were present within the
upper 2 to 3 feet layer along the makai perimeter Below the
finger coral layer brown sandy clay with gravel and fine coral
sand was encountered

Sample No 2
Sample No 2 is a gray silty sand apparently resulting from
dredging of Kuapa Pond some years ago Because of the nature
of the alluvial marine deposits alternating layers of silts
and clays can be expected intermixed with the silty sand portions
It is estimated that the majority of the stockpile consists of
the marine dredged material
Sample No 3
Sample No 3 is a dark brown sandy clay with gravel adobe
and approximately 50 per cent of the sample passing through
the 200 sieve
This sample was obtained from the mauka perimeter of the stock
pile area where large basalt boulders were also noted Because
of the presence of the expansive adobe clay it is our opinion
that this part of the stockpile originated from hillside talus
excavations
The CBR value of this material is low and the soil has a high
expansion potential due to high contents of adobe clay in
the sample
Conclusions
The results of the laboratory CBR expansion test grain size
analysis and atterberg limits are summarized on the enclosed
Plates 1 2
The test results show that the soils generally have 40 or more
per cent passing the 200 sieve and that the CBR values vary
from 4 to 36
While the material with a CBR value of 36 would provide a good
source of select borrow the high content of fines and the diffi
culties of separating the low CBR value soil limits the suita
bility of the fill
It is our opinion that the soils within the stockpile can be
utilized as general fill providing the expansive soils Sample
No 3 are not concentrated in any one area These materials
should also not be used immediately beneath pavements or slabs
on grade

Subject Evaluation of Proposed Borrow Material
Freeway Reserve adjacent to Mariner Business
Center No 2
Hawaii
Gentlemen
At your request we have obtained a representative soil sample
of the proposed borrow material located at the Freeway Reserve
adjacent to Business Center No 2
The sample was a tan white well graded gravelly coral sand with
approximately seven percent passing the 200 Sieve The sand
is non expansive and the laboratory test indicated a CBR value
of 68 at 0 1 inch penetration Therefore this material is
suitable as select borrow for the proposed roadway prism along
Hawaii Kai Drive
The results of the laboratory CBR expansion test and the grain
size analysis for the above sample is attached
Should you have any questions concerning our testing methods or
others please do not hesitate to contact us
