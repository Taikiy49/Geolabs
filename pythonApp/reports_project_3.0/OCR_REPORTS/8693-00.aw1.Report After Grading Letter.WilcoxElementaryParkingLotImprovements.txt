Dear Mr. Teixeira: 
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation and testing 
services performed during the site grading of the Wilcox Elementary School Parking Lot 
Improvements project in the town of Lihue on the Island of Kauai from June 7, 2023 
through July 20, 2023 in accordance with our fee proposal dated May 31, 2023.  
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project involves improvements to the existing Wilcox Elementary School 
Parking Lot located in the town of Lihue on the Island of Kauai, Hawaii. The parking lot 
improvements project consists of the installation of a new covered concrete walkway 
and drainage improvements, including the installation of new catch basins and a 
detention basin located within the school play yard. A Project Location Map and Site 
Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2, respectively. 
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services 
performed during the site grading at the project site. 
SITE GRADING 
We observed the site grading work for the project from June 7, 2023 through 
July 20, 2023. Site grading for the project generally consisted of excavations up to about 
9 feet for utilities and foundations and fills up to about 1 foot thick for a new covered 
sidewalk to achieve the finished design grades. 
At the on-set of earthwork, the area within the limits of grading was cleared and 
grubbed of unsuitable surface materials. Close Construction, Inc. was the general

contractor and self-performed the site grading. During the site grading operations, samples 
of the on-site soils and imported fill materials were obtained and transported to our 
laboratory for testing. The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were 
established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), 
and the test results are summarized on Plate 3.  
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction. For the 
pavement restoration areas, the existing subgrade materials were recompacted to a 
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction. Imported granular fill materials from Kauai 
Concrete and Rock utilized below slabs-on-grade and roadways were compacted to a 
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction. Relative compaction refers to the in-place dry 
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density as determined by 
ASTM D1557. Optimum moisture is the water content (percentage by dry weight) 
corresponding to the maximum dry density. 
During the site grading operations, periodic field density tests were performed in 
general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). Compaction test results 
are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.3. Test locations and elevations were estimated 
using on-site survey stakes for reference. These locations and elevations should be 
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas that did not 
meet the required moisture and density conditions were reworked until the required 
minimum compaction was obtained or exceeded. 
Our services consisted of part-time, as-requested field observation and testing of 
the contractor’s grading activities which included foundation subgrade preparation, fill and 
backfill placement, and confirmation of subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above 
grading operations. 
Based on our field representative’s observations and the referenced test results, it 
is our opinion that the site grading work for the Wilcox Elementary School Parking Lot 
Improvements project, observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this 
report, was performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in
