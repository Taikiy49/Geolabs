to provide updated design input for the proposed housing project in the district of Manoa

SITE DESCRIPTION
The overall project site is situated at the toe of the easterly Manoa hillside
bounded on the northwest by Woodlawn Drive and on the southeast by Kalawao Street
in the Manoa Valley on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The two 2 parcels to be developed are located on the opposite sides of Lowrey
Avenue
The southwestern parcel is situated between Manoa Stream and Lowrey

The easterly Manoa hillside adjacent to the site is a relatively steep terrain and the
up slope area between Woolsey Place and Kalawao Street is known to have undergone
downhill ground movements The project site at the toe of the hillside is in general a
gentle terrain rolling up from the south towards the north with local depressions and hills

approximately 155 to 158 feet above MSL
An existing drainage ditch up to approximately 11 feet deep extends along the
southeasterly project boundary and traverses below Lowrey Avenue through a box
culvert The ditch bottom is at the elevations of approximately 135 to 152 feet above
MSL

Subsoil Conditions

seventeen 17 borings to depths ranging between 28 and 63 5 feet below the existing
feet below the existing ground surface at the southwestern parcel Boring Nos B 24 and

In general the borings encountered a thick deposit of highly compressible marshy
subsoils below a surface crust of fill materials and recent alluvium in most areas of the
subject parcels It appears that the underlying soft compressible layer generally
decreases in thickness towards the easterly hillside of Manoa Valley
The upper fill materials and alluvial deposits in the surface crust have a generally
stiff to hard consistency However the crust materials generally grade softer below
GEOLABS HAWAII

groundwater level which was measured at approximately 5 to 20 feet below the existing
ground surface at the site
In Manoa Valley and other valleys on the island of Oahu the near surface and also
deeper alluvial deposits are known to exhibit high expansion potential low strength and
creep characteristics when saturated Based on our laboratory swell test results the
surface near surface fill materials and alluvium at the site exhibit similar expansion
characteristics when re wetted from dry conditions
In most of the borings the marshy deposits below the surface crust are underlain
by a relatively thin layer of generally medium dense cinder sand Further below the dense
sand is a lower layer of alluvial soils or colluvial cobbles and boulders generally very stiff
to hard
The previous borings near Manoa Stream did not encounter the soft marshy
deposits indicating that the stream had removed the soft soils and redeposited alluvium
in its place

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
General
Based on our updated evaluation of the hillside slope stability it appears that the
project site may not be adversely affected by the landslide movement of the adjacent
hillside as previously reported in our first phase exploration report dated January 23
1992 However the project areas in the site which are to be filled about four 4 to eight
8 feet as proposed in the current grading concept are susceptible to fill ground
settlements and it will require surcharging over a period of about six 6 months prior to
construction of surface structures utilities and pavements
Since there is a generally competent surface crust we believe that shallow footing
foundations may subsequently be used to support the proposed lightly loaded wooden
buildings after the expansive clay soil is adequately capped with an imported non
expansive borrow fill layer The building footings should be embedded deeper adjacent
to the proposed box culvert
Site Stabilit
As previously reported our slope stability analyses of the adjacent Manoa hillside
indicate that the critical imaginary slope circle extending into the easterly portion of the
project parcels may have a long term safety factor of 1 3 which may be considered
acceptable The additional borings on the hillside that were drilled during this second
phase exploration encountered subsurface conditions that are generally more competent
than those previously assumed in our stability analyses Based on these updated boring
data we now believe that the long term safety factor may be greater than 1 3 and that
in general we are of the current opinion that the likelihood of the existing landslide
affecting the project site is relatively low

Site Grading
Our current understanding is that the project site will be graded to form level areas
at different elevations stepping up from the southwesterly area adjacent to Manoa Stream
Fill Design
The present grading concept calls for the southeasterly half of the parcel on the
west side of Lowrey Avenue to be filled on the average by four 4 to eight 8 feet
high above the existing ground surface For the remainder of the site minimal fills
and or cuts may be required
At the southwesterly portion of the site where competent colluvial subsoils were
encountered it appears that the proposed fill up to eight 8 feet high may be
placed without causing significant ground settlements It also appears that up to
two 2 feet of new fill may be allowed at the other areas without causing significant
ground settlements due to a generally thick and competent crust above the
compressible marshy deposits
In view of the variable nature of the subsoils and subsurface conditions at the site
as well as the varying amount of fills to be placed it is anticipated that the ground
settlements will vary from location to location
In general filled ground settlements on the order of three 8 to seven 7 inches
occurring over a period of approximately 18 months may be expected
We recommend overloading this area with a surcharge with minimum height equal
to the designed height of the fill To simplify we recommend a surcharge four 4

Site Preparation
The recommendations given in the previous report dated
remain valid For clarity these recommendations are herein repeated
The expansive surface soils at the site are considered unsuitable for
providing direct building slab and or parking pavement support Therefore
we recommend that the site be specially prepared by placing a non
expansive granular capping over the on site expansive surface soils within
the entire building parking and landscaping areas This will avoid later
contamination of the critical building and pavement areas subsequent to
the initial removal of the on site expansive surface soils by the mass grading
contractor
At the onset of earthwork the project area within the contract grading limits
should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing vegetation rubble
debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and
disposed of properly off site prior to initiation of grading However the
above grubbed contaminated top soils could be stockpiled as an additional
source of surcharge fill materials and they may be removed off site after
surcharging The anticipated cut area may be excavated after clearing and
grubbing
Prior to filling the cleared grade it should be scarified a minimum of six 6
inches properly moisture conditioned and recompacted to a minimum of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2784 10 Page 12
90 of its maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78 hereinafter referred to as the specified density Any
soft loose spots in the cleared grade should be over excavated to firm
ground and backfilled with select non expansive granular material
compacted to the specified density Filling and backfilling should be in
loose level lifts not exceeding ten 10 inches in thickness
In areas where two 2 feet or more of fills would be needed we recommend
that a minimum 2 foot thick layer of select non expansive granular borrow
fill material such as coral select borrow or base course be used to form the
granular capping
Where less than two 2 feet of new site fills is desired the cleared grade

In cut areas or where the proposed improvements are to be built on the

Settlement Monitoring
It should be recognized that it is very difficult to accurately predict the exact
time required for the filled ground to settle since the settlement rate will be
affected by variations of the subsoil structures and the history of the subsoil
deposition
For a thick deposit of highly compressible subsoils such as the marshy
deposits encountered at the site we believe that the estimated waiting time
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2784 10 Page 14
could vary as much as 50 to 100 percent from the actual waiting time
required Therefore the actual settlement rate should be monitored and
provisions be made for potential delay in the project construction schedule
if a longer waiting period is required
To monitor the actual settlement rate we recommend that a minimum of
fifteen 15 settlement gauges as shown on Plate 4 be installed at the future
fill areas The gauges should be read optically by a professional land
surveyor and the readings should be transmitted immediately to the project
geotechnical engineer for review The readings should be transmitted on
a weekly basis in the first three 3 moriths and subsequently on a semi
monthly basis during the rest of the anticipated waiting period and or until
the geotechnical engineer determines that the settlement rate has stabilized
We will be happy to identify the locations of the recommended fifteen 15
settlement gauges on the final surcharge fill location plan
A proper contingency plan should be devised to accommodate the potential
delay in the construction schedule and the possible extra costs that may be
involved

Box Culvert
It is our understanding that an underground box culvert approximately eight 8
feet high will need to be constructed first prior to site grading to replace an existing
drainage ditch along the southeasterly boundary of the site along Kalawao Street The
invert elevation of the proposed culvert taken from the preliminary cross sections varies
from eight 8 to twelve 12 feet below the finish grade The culvert structure will also be
covered with about two to four feet of new fill to bring it up to the finish grade proposed
It appears that the box culvert structure itself would be embedded below the
existing ground surface such that it will not impose additional loading on the soft subsoils
underlying the excavated ground However the overlying fill and temporary surcharged
fill will impose stresses on the underlying soft subsoils underlying the box culvert invert
and the requirement that the box culvert structure be constructed first prior to the site
filling and surcharge fill placement could induce as much as four 4 inches of settlement
This condition has been brought to the attention of the project civil engineer who
indicated that such magnitude of settlements of the box culvert would be within tolerable
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2784 10 Page 17
limits Ideally to minimize later structural settlements the site grading and surcharge fill
should be initiated first and the culvert construction be delayed until the fill ground
settlements have decreased to within lower limits However the current project will
require that the drainage culvert structure be put in place first prior to subsequent site
grading
A review of the boring data indicates that portions of the culvert may bear on wet
and soft loose subgrade materials below the groundwater level while other portions may
bear on the stiff surface crust In addition it appears that the present alignment of the
proposed culvert may traverse through different soil conditions from soft marshy deposits
to anticipated at the southwesterly project site
Therefore we recommend that provision be made in the plan and specifications
to allow a minimum of two 2 feet of select bedding material such as No 3B Fine gravel
be provided below the bottom of the proposed culvert where soft subgrade soils are
encountered to function as a working platform as well as to provide uniform subgrade
support along the alignment of the culvert The 3B Fine gravel bedding material shall
be wrapped around with filter fabric such as Mirafi 140N or its equivalent overlying the
remaining soft compressible deposits
Due to their soil swelling and expansive nature the on site surface materials should
not be used as a source of backfill material behind the box culvert walls We recommend
that imported select granular material such as No 3B Fine ASTM C 33 No 67 gravel
be used as backfill behind the culvert walls to reduce the compactive effort required and
the resulting lateral earth load on the walls

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
In our first phase exploration subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling and sampling fourteen 14 borings to depths ranging between 30 0 and 56 5 feet
below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan 1
The subsurface conditions at the site were further explored in the current
exploration by the drilling and sampling of seventeen 17 borings to depths ranging
between 28 0 and 63 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 and 3 The borings were drilled by a truck
mounted rotary drill rig advancing continuous flight augers or rotary core drilling tools as
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches or a shorter penetration when refusal was encountered are
shown on the boring logs at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of boulder materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2113 83
Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling method which
uses a hollow bit to cut into the hard layer formation The hard material left in the annulus
of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination classification and testing
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of hard materials such as rock masses and boulders Recovery is defined as the
actual length of material recovered from a coring attempt vs the length of the core attempt
e g if 3 7 feet of material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be
74 and would be shown on the Boring Logs as REC 74
The materials encountered in the borings were examined and classified in general
accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84 Description and Identification of Soils
Visual Manual Procedure by a geologist from our firm The logs of the borings are shown
