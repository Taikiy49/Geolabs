DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our findings from a brief site visit a review of original
grading plan for the subdivision at the City Hall it appears that the
eastern half of the site is undisturbed natural ground surface
underlain by several feet of stiff brown clayey silt with some
boulders underlain by weathered basalt The western half of the site
is covered with rocky fill several feet thick over the natural ground
The soil condition should be verified by a soils engineer during the
house site grading and foundation excavation work to see if the actual
conditions disclosed are consistent with that assumed and if any
modification will be necessary should conditions differ
The on site stiff clayey silt soil probably has low to moderate
swelling potential if it is kept moist Previous experience in the
similar soils indicates that its swelling potential may increase to
moderate if allowed to dry out Therefore to minimize the swelling
potential the subgrade soil should be kept moist after excavation and
protected from drying during the period of time required for
preparation prior to final concrete placement
Based on the preliminary house plan it appears that some cut and
fill including a swimming pool excavation will be required along the
sewer easement on the western side of the lot We wish to point out
that cuts below the sewer invert may require special shoring to

Site Grading
For the proposed house construction the existing 3 1 H V slope will
require cuts up to 12 feet deep and fills up to 6 feet thick
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be cleared
of vegetation and debris
It is anticipated that the on site low to oderately expansive clayey
silt soil would be encountered at localized areas under the on grade
Slab areas Where encountered such clayey silt soil should be
removed to at least 18 inches below the bottom of the slab cushion to
provide a more uniform subgrade support
Structural fills and backfills should consist of non expansive soil
such as Select Borrow City and County of Honolulu or the on site
crushed rock fill as approved by the soils engineer All fill or
backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch loose lifts and compacted
to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM

Foundations
Spread footings may be used for the proposed residence provided that
the footings are founded on the compacted rock fragment fill or on the
weathered rock The bottom of the footings should be embedded a
minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade An
allowable bearing value of 4000 p s f may be used
The recommended subgrade preparation prior to final concrete placement
described under Slab On Grade section is also required The bottom
of the footing excavations should be cleaned of loose material and
bear on firm soil and should be examined by a soils engineer to see
that adequate bearing soil has been reached and that the loose soils
are removed prior to concrete placement

Slab On Grade
On grade slabs are planned for the house and are anticipated to bear
on compacted rocky fill at least 12 inches and a 4 inch cushion
layer Therefore where expansive of clayey soils is encountered it
should be situated at least 16 inchess below the bottom of the slabs
It is recommended that the slab clayey subgrade soil be water
saturated for at least 24 hours prior to the final concrete placement
The concreting of footing and slab should be coordinated in such a way
that the time period between the initial excavation and the final
concrete placement should be kept to a minimum in order to prevent
excessive subgrade drying
Tne above recommendations for saturating the subgrade should also be
followed for the sidewalk and patio slabs

Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should be
shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems should be
installed around the house and that gutter water should be diverted
far away from the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape
watering near the foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters and garden courts next to the foundations should be avoided
or have concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the
subsoils

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to
drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill operations
be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of this investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
