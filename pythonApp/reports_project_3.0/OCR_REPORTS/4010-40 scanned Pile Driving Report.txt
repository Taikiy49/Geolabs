PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project is located to the west of the existing Pier 1C extension within Kahului
Harbor on the Island of Maui Hawaii The project will involve the construction of a new
mooring dolphin and a connecting catwalk structure to the Pier 1C extension that has
completed construction
Based on the findings and recommendations presented in our geotechnical
engineering report entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Mooring Dolphin and
Catwalk Pier 1C Kahului Harbor Maui Hawaii dated August 25 2003 prepared for the
above development the proposed structures will be supported by 24 inch octagonal
precast prestressed concrete vertical piles
The mooring dolphin and catwalk will have a finished deck elevation set at
approximately 10 04 feet Mean Lower Low Water MLLW Based on the foundation
design concept 16 piles with an estimated pile tip elevation of about 90 feet MLLW are
planned for foundation support of the mooring dolphin The catwalk structure will be
supported by 8 piles with an estimated pile tip elevation of about 75 feet MLLW The
location and designation of the pile foundation support system are shown on the attached
Pile Foundation Plan Plate 1

PILE INSTALLATION PROGRAM
The pile installation program was conducted between August 5 and 17 2004 under
the near continuous observations by a field representative from our office Prior to the
commencement of the pile driving operations predrilling was performed to a depth of
approximately 40 feet below the water surface to facilitate the pile driving operations
through the obstructions at the proposed pile locations
American Marine Corporation used a single acting diesel hammer
for the pile driving operations Our field observations indicated that fuel pump setting 10
was used for the pile hammer with a maximum rated energy of approximately
66 100 foot pounds The actual energy delivered by the hammer during the pile driving
operations generally ranged between about 50 000 and 54 000 foot pounds
The production piles consisted of 24 inch octagonal precast prestressed concrete
piles with lengths of 100 and 85 feet for the mooring dolphin and catwalk respectively A
total of 16 production piles were driven to a tip elevation ranging between about 61 3 and
71 9 feet MLLW for the mooring dolphin In addition a total of 8 piles were driven to a tip
elevation ranging between about 62 9 and for the catwalk structure The
pile driving summary is presented by pile number on Plates 2 1 and 2 2 Individual pile
driving records have been attached to this report for your reference
It should be noted that the actual pile tip elevations for the mooring dolphin were
approximately 18 to 29 feet above our estimated pile tip elevation As a result we have
performed additional lateral load analyses during the pile driving operation to confirm the
adequacy of the foundation design based on the actual pile tip elevations and subsurface
conditions encountered during the pile driving operations
Based on our observation of the hammer performance pile driving behavior and
additional engineering analyses it is our professional opinion that the production piles
have been driven to sufficient depths and or resistance to provide the design load capacity
of 100 tons mooring dolphin and 40 tons catwalk per pile with a factor of safety of 2
