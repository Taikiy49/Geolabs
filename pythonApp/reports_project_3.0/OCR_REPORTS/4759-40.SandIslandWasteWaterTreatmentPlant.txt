Project Description
The existing Sand Island Wastewater Treatment Plant is along Sand Island
Parkway in the Sand Island area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project
location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 As part of the
Primary Expansion Phase 1 development approximately 75 lineal feet of 54 inch
underground foul air piping will be installed for the project We understand that the new
underground piping will be supported on a deep foundation system consisting of 16 5 inch
octagonal precast prestressed concrete piles with an allowable design load capacity of
60 kips 30 tons per pile in compression

Pile Driving Observations
The pile driving program consisting of a total of inch octagonal precast
pre stressed concrete piles designated as Pile through FAP 10 was
conducted on October 20 and 21 2008 under the near continuous observations by our
field representative HDCC used a Delmag Model D19 32 diesel hammer with a fuel
setting of No 2 delivering a rated energy of approximately lb during the pile
driving operations Prior to the pile driving operations the pile locations were predrilled up
to a depth of about 20 feet below the existing ground surface to facilitate the pile driving
operations through the surface fill crust During the pile driving operation logs were
maintained for each pile which included information pertaining to pile number date driven
predrilled depth blow counts observations size and length of the piles The approximate
locations of the driven piles are shown on the Pile Location Plan Plate 2 The pile driving
summary is presented by pile number on Plate 3
Based on our observation of the hammer performance and pile driving behavior it
is our professional opinion that the piles have been driven to sufficient depths and or
resistance to provide the design load capacity of 60 kips 30 tons per pile with a safety
factor of 2
