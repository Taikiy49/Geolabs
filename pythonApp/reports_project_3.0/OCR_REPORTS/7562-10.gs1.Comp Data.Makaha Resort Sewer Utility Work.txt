This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing 
services performed during sewer line and manhole installation for the Makaha Resort Sewer 
Utility Work from November 3, 2017 to June 22, 2018 in accordance with our fee proposal 
dated September 6, 2017. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The sewer utility work project is in Makaha Valley in the Waianae District of the Island 
of Oahu, Hawaii. Based on project drawings, the existing 10-inch sewer line will be replaced 
with a new 12-inch PVC sewer line. In addition, new sewer manhole structures will be 
installed. 
The sewer replacement project extends along Jade Street and crosses the golf course 
to the southeast corner of the Makaha Valley Resort. The sewer system will be about 2,000 
linear feet in length. In general, the inverts of the new sewer line will be about 10 feet below 
the existing ground surface. However, we understand that near Makaha Valley Resort, the 
sewer line invert will be up to about 30 feet below the existing ground surface. The location of 
the project site is shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1, and the new sewer structures 
are shown on the Site Plans, Plates 2.1 through 2.4. 
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services 
pertaining to the sewer line and manhole structure installation for the project site.

SEWER LINE & MANHOLE STRUCTURE INSTALLATION 
The sewer line and manhole structure installation work for the Makaha Resort Sewer 
Utility Work project was observed by Geolabs, Inc. from November 3, 2017 to June 22, 2018. 
The sewer line and manhole structure installation and backfill work was performed by RHS 
Lee Inc. During the backfilling operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing. 
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our 
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results 
are summarized on Plate 3. 
The backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above optimum moisture content 
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in 
accordance with ASTM Test Method D1557. In the driveway areas, the backfill materials 
within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum or 95 percent relative 
density. 
During the backfilling operations, periodic field density tests were performed in general 
accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). Results of the compaction tests 
performed are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.12 of this report. These tests were 
performed on the constructed backfills at the site. Test locations and elevations were 
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These locations and 
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. 
Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction 
was obtained and/or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and 
hand probing. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s backfill 
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures during the above grading 
operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the backfill installation work for the Makaha Resort 
Sewer Utility Work project, observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this 
report, were performed in conformance with the general intent of the following references.
