EARTHWORK OBSERVATION AND TESTING REPORT
AREA BY GENTRY
This report presents the results of our geotechnical services performed during
the earthwork and grading operations for the above referenced project from November
1993 to January 1994
The subject site is located within the Ewa Gentry Development in the district of
Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is bounded by Area 8 to the north
and west Fort Weaver Road to the east and Geiger Road to the south
The mass grading operations at the above referenced site was performed by
R H S Lee Inc and consisted of fills up to 6 feet The mass grading work consisted
of scarifying and compacting the existing subgrade prior to the placement of fills The
available on site fill materials generally consisted of sandy coral gravel and reddish
brown silty clay

Prior to fill placement the existing vegetation was removed and the exposed
subgrades were scarified to a depth of approximately 12 to 18 inches brought to near
optimum moisture and compacted to 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 Modified Proctor The fill
material was then placed in near level lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
of its maximum dry density
During the fill placement operations periodic field density tests were done in
general accordance with the American Society for Testing and Material ASTM Test
Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests are
summarized in the appendicies of this report
During the grading period various on site and borrow soils were tested prior to
use in the filed The maximum dry density and optimum moisture content relationships
were established in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are also summarized in the appendicies
of this report
Our services consisted of periodic field observation of the contractor s fill
placement and compaction procedures and observation of subgrade soil conditions
exposed during the above grading operations
Field density test locations were selected to confirm field observations as
variations in construction procedures and soil conditions were noted Test locations
and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
