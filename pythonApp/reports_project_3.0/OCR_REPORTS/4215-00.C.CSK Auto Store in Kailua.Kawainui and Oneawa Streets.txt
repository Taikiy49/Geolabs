INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration performed
for the proposed CSK Auto Store in Kailua at the intersection between Kawainui and
Oneawa Streets in the Kailua area on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and
vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations
derived from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses The
recommendations presented herein are intended for the design of foundations
slabs on grade site preparation and pavements only The findings and recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located at the southeast corner of the intersection
between Kawainui and Oneawa Streets in the Kailua area on the island of Oahu Hawaii It
is proposed to construct a new one story auto parts store with associated parking areas
around the building Based on the information provided the project site encompasses an
area of approximately 26 505 square feet which includes the new one story store and
parking areas as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The site is currently occupied by a
drive in restaurant with an asphalt concrete paved parking area
The new store building will have a footprint of about 7 000 square feet Based on the
surface conditions at the site we anticipate that the finished floor of the new store building
will be at or near the existing grades Therefore we envision that only minor site grading
consisting of cuts and fills on the order of about 1 foot or less would be needed to achieve
the design finished grades Further details pertaining to the site grading were not available
at the time this report was prepared
Based on our experience in the vicinity of the project site we anticipate that the
subsoil conditions at the proposed project site may consist of some surficial fill underlain by
soft and or loose lagoonal deposits over unconsolidated calcareous marine sediments

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of the
western third of the island while the younger shield volcano Koolau forms the majority of the
eastern two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while Koolau was
still active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lavas in central Oahu
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to fill the
oceanic basins such that the sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea When the
glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea levels
rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of accumulated
terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits at
correspondingly higher elevations and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters
protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys in the
sediments and reef deposits The project site is situated adjacent to Kawainui Swamp where
the subsurface soil deposits are dominated by lagoonal deposits and coralline soil deposits
Land development and reclamation projects within the last century have brought the project
area to its present form

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
As previously mentioned our field exploration at the subject site generally
encountered medium dense near surface granular fills overlying medium dense sandy soil
deposits coralline beach sands The coralline beach sands were underlain by marine
sediments consisting of loose to dense silty coral gravel extending to the maximum explored
depth of about 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface A void of approximately 1 foot
in vertical height was encountered in the near surface fills in Boring No 2 We believe that
the void is likely the result of an improperly backfilled abandoned cesspool located near
Boring No 2 or may be the result of poorly placed surficial fills
Based on our field exploration and laboratory tests we recommend that a shallow
foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings bearing on new
compacted fills be used to support the proposed auto store building Since the new store
building will be located over the area of Boring No 2 where the void was encountered we
recommend that the foundations for the proposed store building be supported on a minimum
of 2 feet of new compacted fills Considering the relative size of the building footprint it may
be prudent to over excavate the existing fills within the building footprint to a minimum depth
of 2 feet below the bottom of footing elevation and be replaced with compacted fills The

Foundations
Based on the subsurface soil conditions encountered at the site we recommend that
shallow spread and or continuous footings bearing on new compacted fills be used for
support of the proposed store building An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds
per square foot psf may be used for the design of foundations bearing on 2 feet of new
compacted fills This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces
As previously indicated we believe that it may be prudent to over excavate the
near surface fills within the entire footprint of the proposed building and be replaced with
compacted fills for support of the building The depth of the over excavation should extend
to a minimum depth of 2 feet below the bottom of footing elevation in order to provide the
2 feet of compacted fills below the footings The over excavation should also extend a
minimum distance of 2 feet laterally beyond the edges of the building structure
If it is elected to over excavate only the footing locations and not to over excavate the
surficial fills within the entire building footprint as recommended above to provide the 2 feet
of compacted fills below the foundations we recommend that the over excavation for the
footings extend a minimum of 1 foot beyond the edges of the footings on each side of the
foundation
The over excavated surficial fill soils granular materials may be reused as the new
compacted fills The new compacted fills should be moisture conditioned to above the
optinum moisture content placed in 8 inch maximum loose lifts and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
W O 4215 00 C Page 8
established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the
water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent grade If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should
be embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility tench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines
This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with
additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation settlement
If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of the footing
over excavations the soft and or loose materials should be removed to expose the
underlying firm materials The over excavation should be backfilled with the on site granular
materials compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the footing may be
extended down deeper to the underlying competent material
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for footings
bearing on the new compacted fills to be less than 1 inch total with differential settlements
on the order of about 0 5 inches
Lateral loads acting on the building may be resisted by frictional resistance developed
between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of friction of
0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the new compacted fills Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds
per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the soils around the footings are
well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive pressure resistance
in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
We recommend that foundation material over excavation backfill and footing
excavations be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii prior to placement of

Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed auto
store building In general we recommend that the subgrade soils below the concrete
slabs on grade be prepared in accordance with the recommendations presented in the Site
Preparation section of this report Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of
the subgrade we recommend that the soils be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks or
prepared again Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to
inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and
replacement with well compacted fill at no additional cost to the owner
For interior slabs we recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer consisting
of free draining materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be
placed below the slab to provide more uniform support of the slab To reduce the potential
for future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious
moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic membrane is recommended on top of the cushion
layer Two inches of moist fine sand may be considered above the plastic membrane to
provide protection of the membrane and to aid in curing of the slab concrete
The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at
least 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slab should
be backfilled tightly against the slab with relatively impervious soils on site soils and graded
to divert water away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding around the
foundations

Corrosion Evaluation
One of the subsurface soil samples retrieved from our field exploration at the site was
subjected to chemical analyses for soluble chloride and sulfate concentrations The chemical
analysis tests were performed by Environmental Laboratory of the Pacific The results of
these tests are presented on Plate B 4 of Appendix B
Based on the test results the soluble chloride and sulfate concentrations in the soil
sample tested were below the reporting limit as shown on Plate B 4 Therefore the on site
soils tested may be considered to be generally non corrosive to reinforced concrete in
contact with the soils However normal precautions to reduce the potential effects of
corrosion such as minimum concrete cover should be observed for reinforced concrete
substructures
Pavement Design
We understand that asphalt concrete pavements will be required for the parking areas
around the proposed building In general we anticipate that the vehicle loading for the
proposed store will consist primarily of passenger vehicles pick up trucks and occasional
moderately loaded trucks Our laboratory tests performed on the near surface silty sands
encountered beneath the existing pavement appears to have a CBR value of 8 5 with a swell
value of less than 1 percent Considering the generally light to medium traffic anticipated for
the proposed project we recommend that the following pavement design be used for
preliminary budgeting purposes

The subgrade soils for the new pavement areas should be scarified to a depth of
about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and

Utility Trench
We envision that some new utility lines and utility connections may be required for the
proposed project In general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes for support Free draining
granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation should also
be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide
adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill in
order to reduce the possibility of damaging the pipes It is critical to use this free draining
material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to
provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper trench backfill could result in
settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 4215 00 C Page 14
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to the
top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the excavated on site soils The backfill
should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in about
8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative
compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are below
pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement grade should be
compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away from
the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter systems
be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from the foundation
and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should
be avoided Planters next to foundations should also be avoided or have concrete bottoms
and drains to reduce the potential for water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled against the walls or slab edges immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce
the potential for water infiitration
In addition drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain surface water runoff away from the foundations and slabs

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling six
borings extending to depths of about 6 5 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a
truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight hollow stem augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a near
continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing in the
laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials encountered are
presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 6
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with ASTM
Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving
either a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler or a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler
with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler
the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties The
results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Two sieve analysis tests ASTM C 136 were performed on selected samples to
determine the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil classification Graphic
presentations of the grain size distribution are presented on Plate B 1
One Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D 1557 was performed on a bulk
sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the moisture content and dry density
relationships The results of the tests are presented on Plate B 2
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR test ASTM D 1883 was performed
on a bulk sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics
of the soils The results of the tests are presented on Plate B 3
One set of chemical analysis tests including Chlorides EPA Test Method 325 2 and
Sulfates EPA Test Method 375 2 was performed on a selected sample of the soils to
evaluate their corrosion potential The chemical analysis tests were performed by
Environmental Laboratory of the Pacific Results of the tests are summarized on Plate B 4
