PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this soil engineering investigation was to
explore the subsurface soil and groundwater conditions at
the site and to provide data for use in design and construc
tion of the building foundations The work was performed
generally in accordance with our proposal of
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis that formulated
our conclusions and recommendations are presented in the
appendix of this report

testing are presented in the Appendices
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located north of the intersec
tion of Ward Avenue and Prospect Street in Punchbowl Honolulu
Hawaii
The site property consists of an area of approximately 8 800
square feet and is rectangular in configuration It is bor
dered on the north and west by adjacent single family and
apartment dwellings on the east by an existing common drive
way and on the south by Prospect Street

Ground elevations at the site slope up from a low point of
180 feet in the south to 208 feet in the north with an aver
age ground surface slope of about 6 5 to l H V
A 7 to 9 foot high retaining wall fronts the property along
Prospect Street Another retaining wall about 4 to 6 feet
in height runs across the property near the north boundary
During the time of the investigation an abandoned single
story wood frame house was situated on the property along
with several large mango trees and a few other fruit trees

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
Located on the south outer slope of Punchbowl Crater the pro
ject site s ground formation is geologically mapped to be a
consolidated gray to brown color bedded volcanic tuff mud
rock The volcanic tuff is generally thicker and closer to
the surface near the crater and then becoming thinner and
located at deeper depths away from the crater
Subsurface Conditions
Three borings were drilled at the site to depths of 25 35
and 45 feet to explore the subsurface soil conditions and to
obtain samples for laboratory testing Based on the infor
mation obtained from these borings the site is generally
underlain by the following materials
1 A surficial layer consisting of brown clayey soils
with some sand covering the entire site ranging
in thickness from 4 feet in the north to 12 5 feet
in the south

at Boring No 3 location between a depth of 3 5 to

Soil samples recovered within this upper soil layer
revealed the in situ moisture contents to be rela
tively low
Beneath the brown surface soil is a black sand
layer which was encountered in all three drilled
borings ranging in thickness from 6 5 feet in the

Underlying the black sand Punchbowl volcanic tuff

The tuff mudrock extended to the bottom of all the
borings The density of the tuff generally increased
with depth reflecting a lesser amount of weathering
at deeper depths

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the project site is
underlain by competent soil formations suitable for founda
tion support of the proposed 5 story structure
Foundations
The borings encountered three 3 distinct soil layers at
relatively shallow depths below the Sloping building site
The 3 layers appear to generally follow the Sloping surface
of existing ground
Due to the existing sloping ground surface cuts up to about
12 feet planned for a semi basement level in the higher
northern portion of the building minimal cuts are planned
in the lower southern portion As a result of this site
cutting requirement and the sloping subsoil layers three
different soil formations are to be encountered at the
building foundation level
Weathered volcanic tuff may be encountered in the upper end
section of the proposed building excavation deeper cut
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 981 00 P 7
black sand formation is anticipated in the northern half
of the lower level parking level and stiff clayey silt soil
is anticipated in the southern half of the building excava
tion Refer to the Generalized Cross Section Plate 2 for
our estimated subsoil conditions
Ideally all of the building footings should bear on similar
formation soils to minimize differential settlements between
footings Since dense tuff formation may be encountered in
the deeper building excavations preferably all the footings
should bear on this dense weathered tuff formation and higher
bearing values could be utilized However since minimum
cut is planned at the southern portion of the building
footing excavation to about 15 foot depth would be required
in order to bear the southern footings on the dense tuff
also
In order to reduce the above required footing excavation
an alternate foundation scheme utilizing a System of spread
footings bearing on the various materials encountered close
to the finished grade was evaluated and found feasible
provided additional differential settlement can be tolerated
by the structure
The following allowable bearing values can be used for the
footing design
1 Stiff clayey silt 3500 psf
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 981 00 P 8
2 Medium density black sand 3500 pst
3 Dense weathered tuff 5000 psf
The above values may be increased by one third when considering
temporary loads wind and seismic loads
Minimal settlements are anticipated for the footings bearing
on the dense weathered tuff Estimated total settlements
for the footings bearing on the medium density black sand
and stiff clayey silt are on the order of about three fourths
of an inch However we anticipate that the footing settle
ments should occur relatively rapidly as the building load
is placed on the footings It is our Opinion that the
majority of the settlement should occur during building
construction
The black sand layer has little or no cementation and was
found to be medium density in its natural state Therefore
we recommend that the bottoms of the footing excavations
in the black sand should be recompacted to obtain a more
uniform bearing layer
All subsequent loose or muddy materials falling into the
excavation should be removed from the bottoms of the footing
excavation prior to concrete placement
The footings should be placed a minimum of 24 inches below
the adjacent finished grade

Retaining and Basement Walls
Retaining and basement walls up to 12 feet high are planned
for the northern portion of the structure Retaining walls
generally less than 8 feet high are planned for an entrance
Stairway from Prospect Street
Well graded granular material such as on site black sand
should be used as backfill material behind the walls Imper
vious material should be used for the top 2 feet of the back
fill
The recommendations given under foundations Should be followed
for the design of the wall foundations
For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 40 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Walls restrained at the top 35 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Additional load due to traffic or surcharge
should be included where applicable
A friction factor of 0 40 can be utilized to determine the
sliding resistance of the wall foundation Additionally a
passive soil resistance of 300 p c f may be used
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the
build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls

Cuts up to about 12 feet will be required for the northern
portion of the foundation excavation Minimal fills are
planned for the site
It is anticipated that conventional earthmoving equipment
could be used for the basement and foundation excavations
Based on Boring No 1 it appears that much of the basement
excavation would be into the medium density black sand layer
The field exploration indicates that the black sand has
little cementation and would probably require excavation
Support to prevent ravelling and sloughing
The site should be cleared of all vegetation debris and
old foundations and sidewalks prior to filling
Select on site black sand material should be used for fill
and footing backfill material The fill and backfill should
be placed in horizontal loose lifts of about 8 inches and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density
established by ASTM Test Method D 1557
Slab on Grade
Based on the field exploration the basement level slab
would probably bear on either black sand in the rear half
or clayey silt in the front portion generally both of these
materials are low to non expansive soils However in

Boring No 3 a layer of expansive clay was encountered at
about 3 to 7 foot depths If encountered at subgrade level
the expansive clay should be removed for a depth of 3 feet
below the finished elevation and replaced with select on site

Slab on grade bearing on the clayey silt subgrade soil should
be placed over a 4 inch layer of base course or on site black
sand compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density The sub

Where the slab is located below the outside ground surface
a subdrainage system around the perimeter will help to pre

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
3 borings to depths of 25 35 and 45 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1 Truck mounted drilling equipment was used
to drill borings No 1 and No 2 Portable drilling equipment
was utilized for Boring No 3 where truck mounted equipment
was not possible

classified by visual and textural examination in the field by
our engineering geologist who continuously monitored the
drilling operations The classifications were reviewed by
visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classfication System A graphic presentation of the soils is
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained by driving a standard penetration
sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12
inches are shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate
sample depth
