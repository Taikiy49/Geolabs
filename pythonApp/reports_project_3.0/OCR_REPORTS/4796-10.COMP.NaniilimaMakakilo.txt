This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed during
the mass grading pad grading utility installation and road construction operations from
April 2002 to January 2003 for the above project in accordance with our fee proposal dated
August 1 2001 A geotechnical engineering exploration report dated was
prepared for the project by Geolabs Inc
Project Considerations
The project site is located east of Makakilo Drive north of the existing Makakilo
Gardens II development and south of an existing BWS reservoir in the District of Kapolei on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The project consists of the construction of 73 single family homes and three
asphalt paved roads and associated utilities including water sewer electrical telephone
cable and storm drain lines The mass grading work generally consisted of cuts less than
10 feet and fills less than about 15 feet high relative to the existing ground surface
Based on our field reconnaissance and laboratory testing most of the surface soils
at the site range from slightly to moderately expansive with isolated pockets of highly
expansive soil Based on our laboratory swell test results we have categorized the on site
soils into three general types as described below
Type 1 Low expansion soils with a swell value of 0 to 5
Type 2 Moderate expansion soils with a swell value greater than 5 to 8
Type 3 High expansion soils with a swell value of over 8 0
The house foundation recommendations for concrete slabs on grade corresponding
to the above soil categories are as follows

Site Grading
The earthwork operations for the project were performed by Delta Construction At
the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits were generally cleared and
grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials Materials generated from the clearing
operations were disposed of off site Following the clearing and grubbing operations the
areas to be cut were excavated Finish grades were compacted using a smooth drum and or
sheepsfoot vibratory roller

During construction representative soil samples were collected from near the lot
finished grades for swell testing Generally samples tested were remolded in 2 4 inch
diameter by 1 inch high rings They were air dried overnight and then saturated for 24 hours
under a surcharge pressure of 110 psf Based on the swell test results lots were categorized
as Types 1 2 or 3 to determine recommended foundation types
The fills and backfills needed for the earthwork generally consisted of on site materials
generated from the excavations The on site fill materials generally consisted of silty and
clayey soils with some gravel and cobbles and boulders An on site crusher was used to
crush the boulders to produce select borrow and select granular fill Samples of the fill and
backfill materials utilized during construction were obtained and transported to our office for
laboratory testing The maximum dry densities and optinum moisture contents were
established in our laboratory in general accordance with American Society of Testing and
Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor The test results are
summarized on Plate 5 of this report
In general fill materials consisting of 6 inch minus material were placed for the fills
and utility tench backfills Field density tests were performed with the nuclear density gauge
ASTM Test Designations D 2922 and D 3017 During the fill and backfill operations the
materials were generally placed in near level lifts brought to near the optimum moisture and
compacted to obtain the specified degree of compaction based on visual observations and or
field density tests Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill
operations to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil
conditions were noted

A limited amount of boulder fill was used in the deeper fill areas in the eastern edge of
the project In general boulder less than 18 inches in diameter generated during site

A total of 529 field density tests including re tests of failed areas were performed by
our firm using the nuclear density gauge method in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designations D 2922 and D 3017 The results of the compaction tests performed during
construction of the subject project are summarized on Plates 6 1 through 6 27 of this report
On a few occasions the density test indicated a compaction level slightly less than specified

implied by the methods used

HOUSE CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
The finished grades adjacent to the house structures should be sloped to shed water
away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that
gutter systems be installed around the house structures and that the discharge be diverted
away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering within 3 feet
of the foundations and slabs should be avoided Gravel strips adjacent to slabs and
foundations should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the house
foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent loss
of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water
infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain surface run off away from foundations Landscaping of the house lots by

therefore reduce the potential for swelling shrinking and softening of the subgrade soils

Geolabs assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or finished
grades on the subject project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
knowledge and written acknowledgment Lot owners should be advised not to block the

construction of any future improvements
