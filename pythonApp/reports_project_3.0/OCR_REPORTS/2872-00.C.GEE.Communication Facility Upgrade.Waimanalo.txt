SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located approximately three 3 miles southeast of Waimanalo
It is at the crest of the Koolau Range near the intersection of the Eastern Koolaus and the
Kamehameha Ridge The associated property bears Tax Map Key 001 Access
to the site was from the south via an extension of Kamehameha Drive
The terrain drops off very steeply to the north Highly to moderately weathered
basalt and saprolite are exposed in outcroppings and road cuts near the site The site
is Currently occupied by an existing building a tower a fuel storage tank and concrete
paved areas all enclosed by a fence
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration encountered 6 5 to 13 5 feet of brown and gray mixture of
sand and gravel from the ground surface These granular soils were in a very dense
condition Underlying the sand and gravel a very dense light brown and reddish brown
saprolitic material was encountered to a depth of twenty five 25 feet the maximum depth
drilled The saprolitic material was derived from extremely weathered basalt and retained
the structure of the parent rock The material of which the saprolite was composed of
may be described as a silty sand with gravel
Boulders were encountered in both borings at various depths in both of the drilled
borings
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings at the time of our exploration
However a temporary rise of ground water level is possible especially during periods of
heavy rain

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Tower Foundations
Based on the available subsurface information we recommend that a mat
foundation be used for the support of the proposed new communication tower The
communication tower is relatively light in weight but will be subjected to significant
moment and tension forces at the foundation level as indicated above For the given
loading conditions a fifteen by fifteen foot 15 x 15 mat may be used for support of the
tower The bottom of the mat foundation should be a minimum of six 6 feet below the
finished grade The design relies on the weight of soil on top of the mat to offset the
moment and uplifting forces
If it is decided that the 50 foot high tower is to be obtained by extension of an
existing tower tiedown anchors may be used to provide additional capacity against uplift
and moment reactions at the base of the tower
Excavation and Backfilling
Excavation and backfilling will be required for the construction of the tower
foundation The bottom of the excavations should be cleared of loose material prior to
the placement of concrete
The material with particle sizes less than six 6 inches resulting from excavation
for the mat foundation may be used as backfill The backfill should be placed in level lifts
not exceeding eight 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to 95 percent of its
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with procedures described in ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed facility upgrade should be forwarded to
the project geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction
This review is needed to determine adherence to the recommendations given herein If
this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot considered to be responsible
for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the project geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the project construction to observe that critical
items of work such as excavation backfilling and concrete placement are properly
performed This is to identify possible deviations from the design concepts specifications
or recommendations and to expedite recommendations for design changes in the event
that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time of preparation of this
report The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the project geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the project site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered during preparation of this report then appropriate
modifications to the design should be made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by the drilling and sampling
of two 2 borings to depths of approximately 25 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled
by a truck mounted rotary drill rig advancing hollow stem continuous flight augers or
rotary core drilling tools as appropriate
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate sample
depths
The materials encountered in the borings were examined and classified in general
accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84 Description and Identification of
Soils Visual Manual Procedure by a geologist from our firm The logs of the brings are
shown on Plates A 1 and A 2

engineering properties of the material
