Geotechnical Engineering Evaluation
Excavation at New Elevator for Makiki Library
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
Dear Mr Koki
In accordance with your request we have evaluated the soil conditions at the
planned excavation for the new elevator at the existing Makiki Library within the Makiki
District Park in the Makiki area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Based on the
anticipated foundation level of the elevator pit we envision that excavation depths of up
to about 15 feet below the existing ground surface may be required for the project
construction This letter summarizes our findings and presents our recommendations
pertaining to the shoring requirements of the planned excavation
Site visits were conducted on February 2 and 3 2006 to evaluate the exposed soil
conditions at the new elevator pit excavation At the time of our site visits the excavation
measured approximately 13 to 15 feet deep below the existing ground surface with the
majority of the exposed cut faces excavated to a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V
slope In addition we understand that the excavation will be left open for up to about one
month for the project construction Based on our field observations the exposed faces of
the excavation generally consisted of medium dense silty sands with slight cementation
which is consistent with the anticipated subsurface conditions presented in the soil boring
log prepared for the project A copy of the soil boring log is attached with this letter for
reference
Based on our field observations the soil boring information provided and our
engineering analyses it is our opinion that the exposed soil conditions at the elevator pit
excavation may be classified as Type B Soil in accordance with the guidelines provided
under Title 12 Department of Labor and Industrial Relations Subtitle 8 Division of
Occupational Safety and Health Chapter 132 Excavations Trenching and Shoring
Therefore we recommend that the following measures and precautions be observed and
implemented during the project construction
e The in situ silty sand soils should be sloped back at a slope inclination of
1H 1V or flatter
e Excavated soils should not be stockpiled within a horizontal distance closer
than the depth of the excavation measured from the edge of the excavation
