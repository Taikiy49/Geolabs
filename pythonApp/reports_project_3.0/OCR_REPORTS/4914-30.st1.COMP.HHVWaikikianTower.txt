the construction for the Grand Waikikian Tower project at Hilton Hawaiian Village in the
Waikiki area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii

The project site is adjacent to the existing Hilton Hawaiian Village complex in the
Waikiki area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The Grand Waikikian Tower is situated to the
north of the parking garage within the Hilton Hawaiian Village complex and adjacent to

EARTHWORK OPERATION
The earthwork for the project was performed by Koga Engineering and
Construction Inc The general contractor for the project was Dick Pacific Construction
Company Inc Our observation and testing services during the construction of the project
occurred between June 2006 and February 2009
After clearing and grubbing areas to be filled and subgrades in cuts were generally
scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor The compaction
requirement for the finished subgrades in areas subjected to vehicular traffic was
increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Fills and backfills required to raise the grades were generally placed in level lifts of
about 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction The compaction requirement for the fill materials required
under the pavement areas was increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
During the earthwork operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on the attached Plates 2 1 and 2 2
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 154 field density tests were performed using
the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 6938 Compaction test results
are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 14 These tests were performed on the
prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills Test locations and
elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes and
topographic features These locations and elevations should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory
were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of
the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with
generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is
expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance
nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for
the quality of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing
defects regardless of when they are found
