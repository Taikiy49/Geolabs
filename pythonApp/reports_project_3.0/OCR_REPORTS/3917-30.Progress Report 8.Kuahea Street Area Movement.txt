REPORT SUMMARY
The results of the instrumentation monitoring during the past six month monitoring
period indicate that the recorded earth movement rates are not extraordinary in relation to
previously recorded earth movement data for the project However it appears that the
subsurface earth movement located within Area A is noteworthy in that the recorded
movements remain elevated despite a general reduction of earth movement rates
encountered at the Waiomao Landslide and within Area B at the project site The
continued elevated rate of movement at Area A may be related to the higher than normal
precipitation and subsurface seepage experienced at the project site during the past year
Based on our review of the inclinometer data recorded during the past six month
monitoring period spanning between February and August 2004 it appears that
presence of subsurface ground movement at their respective instrument locations The
earth movements indicated by the deflection of the inclinometer casing show well defined
basal movement boundaries below which limited earth movement is apparent
Furthermore of the inclinometers showing well defined movement the earth movement
Landslide and Area B appear to have leveled off during the past several months
However the earth movement rates at Inclinometer Nos I 5 and I 12 located within
Area A appear to have maintained their previous movement pattern during the past
six months

Based on our review of the rainfall information and monthly precipitation summaries
for the current monitoring period February through August 2004 the months of March
May and August experienced above average rainfall totals associated with significant
storm events The summer months of 2004 although generally drier than the previous
winter months continued to experience elevated rainfall amounts Therefore the rainfall
amounts may be considered above average for the normally drier summer months in the
Palolo area
Based on our review of the inclinometer data it appears that Inclinometer Nos I 2
decrease in the previous rates of earth movement during the past six months especially
from about May through August In contrast Inclinometer Nos I 4 I 5 and 12 located
within Area A appear to have recorded movement that is generally unaffected by the
decrease in rainfall experienced over the summer months Finally Inclinometer No I 8
appears to have experienced a relative increase in movement over the drier summer
months followed by a decline in movement experienced between about June and August
In general our interpretation of the earth movement response to elevated rainfall is
that if a period of increased or above normal rainfall is experienced following a prolonged
period of normal or lower than normal rainfall the earth movement rates at some of the
inclinometers tend to increase abruptly following the period of elevated precipitation and
then settle back to normal levels
