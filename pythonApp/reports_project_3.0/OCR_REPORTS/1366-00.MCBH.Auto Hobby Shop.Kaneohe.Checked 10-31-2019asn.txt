PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed Auto Hobby Shop will be a one story steel framed
building approximately 74 by 97 feet in plan dimensions CMU exterior
walls and concrete on grade slabs are planned for the building Inside
the shop will be a 3 ton hoist with overhead monorail hydraulic and
electric lifts and other facilities Minor site grading is anticipated
The general layout of the proposed construction is shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
The anticipated foundation loadings are up to 1 9 kips per linear
foot under exterior CMU walls and up to 37 kips for the heavier
interior columns Average column loadings will range about 20 kips per
column
A new concrete paved strip of area 20 feet wide and about 143 feet
long will be constructed along the eastern side of the project area
abutting the southern side of Building 1307 This area will be used as
an open work area for auto repair and maintenance

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in a relatively level area near
existing Building 130 off the eastern end of 2nd Street inside Kaneohe
Marine Corps Air Station At the time of our exploration the majority
of the area was paved with asphalt concrete and fenced The area
outside the fence was covered with grass As indicated on the as built
plans two small hot water lines traversed diagonally across the
proposed building area
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
Geologically the site is located on the southern shore of Mokapu
Peninsula formed by several vents of the Honolulu Volcanic Series that
laid down extensive ash and cinder tuff deposits with some lava
flows A more recent rise in the sea level resulted in the deposition
of loose coral materials in a lagoonal environment in the portion of the
peninsula where the project site is located During the past century
the efforts of man have shaped the site to its present form by the
placement of fill materials on this older lagoonal coral deposits
Field Exploration
To explore the subsurface conditions five exploratory borings were
drilled and sampled to depths ranging from 10 to 18 feet below the
existing grades Three of the borings were further probed to depths
ranging from 20 to 22 3 feet The approximate boring locations are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1366 00 Page 4
The subsurface conditions were generally consistent under both
paved and unpaved areas The pavements generally consisted of 2 inches
of asphalt concrete and 3 to 8 inches of crushed basalt rock base
course The grassed area was underlain by about 12 inches of silty
coral gravel fill Below the pavements or coral gravel fill was a layer
of stiff reddish brown clayey silt 6 to 18 inches thick overlying a
thick deposit of lagoonal coral deposits The coral deposits consisted
of loose to medium dense tan and brown silty coral gravel with some
sand This coral gravel was generally dense within the top few feet
Groundwater was encountered and the measured depths below the
existing grades are shown on the boring logs The groundwater levels
appear to be about 1 to 2 feet above Mean Sea Level and may fluctuate
due to seasonal and tidal influences
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our borings indicate the site is underlain by medium dense coral
deposits with loose zones near the water level To minimize future
ground settlements due to site grading fill should be placed as soon as
possible The magnitudes of additional foundation settlements are
anticipated to be relatively small under the recommended spread footing
foundation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1366 00 Page 5
The existing asphalt concrete pavement appeared to be in a fair to
good condition at the time of our exploration Thus the AC may remain
under the building provided that it is situated below the subgrade level
to allow for the construction of concrete slab and or cushion fill In
general the new floor slab should be a minimum of about 4 inches thick
and where it is required it may bear directly on the existing AC
surface If cushion rock is required to bring the subgrade up to the
bottom of the slabs it should consist of No 3 Fine crushed rock or
equivalent and be a minimum of 3 inches thick over the existing AC
surf ace
Site Grading
For the proposed auto hobby shop construction it is anticipated
that only about one foot of new fill will be required to the subgrade
level at the southwestern portion of the area
Any vegetation organic or other deleterious materials should be
removed from unpaved areas Part of the existing asphalt concrete
pavement may need to be removed to accommodate the slab or cushion
fill
Imported fill material should consist of select non expansive
granular material less than 3 inches in size with less than 20 passing
the 200 sieve The minimum CBR value should be 15 when compacted to
95 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1366 00 Page 6
Fill should be placed in 6 inch thick horizontal lifts and
compacted to 90 of the maximum dry density The top 6 inches of the
fill below the subgrade should be compacted to 95 minimum under the
asphalt concrete or Portland cement concrete slabs
Foundation Support
It is our understanding that potential settlements will be among
the major concerns for the planned exterior CMU walls design In view
of the moderate compressibility of the underlying thick lagoonal coral
deposits we anticipate that some settlement should be expected
For the design wall foundation loading of 1 9 kips per linear foot
the anticipated footing settlements may range up to about one half of an
inch and differential settlements may range up to one fourth of an inch
over a distance of 10 feet The differential settlements can be
expected to be more pronounced near the end segments of the long walls
Differential settlements will also be higher where loading vary
Significantly such as at the locations where large wall openings doors
or gates are located and where loose soils are next to dense soils
Therefore we recommend that the wall footings be designed as continuous
grade beams that can span over a distance of 10 feet
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1366 00 Page 7
For the interior column loading of 37 kips per column the
anticipated spread footing settlement may range up to three fourths of
an inch For average 20 kip columns the anticipated settlements may
range up to one half of an inch The differential settlements May range
up to one fourth to one half of an inch between columns It is our
understanding that the above magnitudes of settlements will be
acceptable for the proposed construction
An allowable soil bearing value of up to 2000 p s f may be used
for footings bearing on the stiff silt or dense coral A one third
increase may be allowed for soil bearing under wind or seismic loads
All footings should have a minimum dimension of 18 inches and the
footing bottom should be embedded at least 2 feet below the lowest
adjacent finished grade
Any soft or weak soils encountered at the bottom of the foot ing
excavations should be removed to firm soils and backfilled with
compacted fill or lean concrete as appropriate The footing
excavations should be inspected by a soils engineer to verify that they
are located in suitable soil
Footings adjacent to any utility trench should be deepened so that
their bottom would be at or below the level of the adjacent trench
bottom If the wall footings are designed to span at least 10 feet it
may not be necessary to deepen the wall footing at the existing hot
water line crossings
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1366 00 Page 8
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundations and the bearing soil
Resistance utilizing passive earth pressure may be calculated with
an equivalent fluid weighing of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil
backfill around the foundations is well compacted Due to the loose
nature of the underlying coral deposits the lateral soil resistance
should not exceed 1200 p s f below a depth of 4 feet The frictional
resistance may be calculated by using a coefficient of friction of 0 4
The above resistance values are maximum values and do not contain a
factor of safety

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundation
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading spread footing
excavations subgrade preparations and cushion layer constructions for
slabs and pavements be observed by the soils engineer during
construction to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site

Field Exploration

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling 5
test borings at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by one of our engineers who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory A graphic
presentation of the materials encountered in the borings are presented
in the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 5 All soils were classified
in accordance with the Unified Soil Classification System
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler
with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts used to
drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the
appropriate sample depths
In and 5 a continuous penetration probe was made by
driving a 2 inch diameter blunt point attached to a string of A size
drill rods driven with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow
counts required to drive the probe every 12 inches are shown o the
boring logs
