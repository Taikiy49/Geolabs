PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our survey was to obtain an overview of the electrical resistivity of
the subsurface soils at the site Our work on this project was performed in general
conformance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 78 and with the requirements provided
in the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991 and
subsequent amendments and additions The scope of our work consisted of the
following work efforts
1 Performance of 31 in situ soil resistivity tests at nine locations along Olive
Avenue These locations generally correspond to the 300 foot spacing
maximum required in the Water System External Corrosion Control
Standards Volume 3 1991
2 Coordination of the field work by an Engineer from our firm
3 Preparation of six copies of this letter report summarizing our work on the
project
4 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
Principal Engineer of our firm
5 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

FIELD EXPLORATION
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site on
February 25th and our field work was performed in sunny conditions with generally moist
surface condition
Thirty one soil resistivity surveys were conducted at nine locations at the above
referenced area using the Wenner 4 Pin Method and a resistance meter Strata Scout
R 40C manufactured by SoilTest Inc with 120 volts output and 24 watts output into the
ground This method utilizes four surface probes or pins electrodes to evaluate the
average resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the underlying soil mass The average
resistivities of the soil mass extending from the existing ground surface to the planned
invert elevations of the water main were assessed by using this method at the test
locations listed on Plates 4 1 and 4 2
The average soil resistance was evaluated at each test location with the electrodes
aligned at equal spacings of generally 3 to 6 feet at the existing ground surface Among
these spacings used one may have more significance than the others for allowing more
contribution to the evaluated soil resistivity from the closest mass of soil to the proposed
pipe invert Such spacings are flagged with an asterisk on Plates 4 1 and 4 2
Based on the current design concepts the water main alignment will be located
closer to the south side of the road In general soil resistivity surveys were conducted
at selected locations along the shoulders of the road Tests performed along the south
side of the road are flagged with 2 asterisks on Plates 4 1 and 4 2

The resistance meter R 40C impresses a voltage between the outer electrodes
causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between the inner
electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the soil
resistance in Ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity in Ohm cm
which is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The
evaluated soil resistances the computed resistivities and the corresponding corrosion
ratings are presented on Plates 4 1 and 4 2
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics
with regard to the corrosivity of the soil In general the lower the resistivity the greater
the potential for corrosion of buried metallic structures Conversely the higher the
resistivity the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from
field data points by means of geophysical surveys Variations of soil conditions between
the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations may not
become evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to reevaluate the ratings provided in this report
Locations and elevations of the data presented in this report are approximate
having been estimated from features and reference points shown on the undated
construction plan prepared by Okahara Associates Inc All elevations were rounded
to the nearest foot to emphasize that they may not be accurate enough for other
purposes Thus elevations and locations of the data presented should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
