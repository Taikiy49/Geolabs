PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is in the Kapolei Village 8 subdivision, known as Kaupe’‘a, within
the existing Villages of Kapolei development in the District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu,
Hawaii. The Kaupe’a subdivision development is generally bounded by Kapolei Parkway
to the north, Kapolei Middle School to the east, Kapolei High School to the west, and
Kapolei Lower Channel to the south.
Based on the development plans, the Kaupe’a housing development has been
broken down into three phases, designated as Phases 1 through 3. This report addresses
our field observations and compaction testing services for the house construction portion
of Phase 1, which includes Lot Nos. 217 through 292, as shown on the Site Plan, Plate 1.

EARTHWORK OPERATION AND FOUNDATION EXCAVATION
Shioi Construction, Inc. performed the house construction portion of the Kaupe‘a -
Phase 1. The work generally involved the removal of topsoil placed during the mass
grading operations at the house pads and driveway aprons. The exposed select granular
fill (capping fill) at the house pads and driveway aprons was then recompacted with a
10-ton vibratory drum roller to at least 90 and 95 percent, respectively, of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 (Modified Proctor).
The thickened-edge footings for the house structures were generally excavated
to a depth of about 14 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grades. The bottom of
the footing excavations were recompacted to at least 95 percent of the maximum dry
density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 (Modified Proctor).
Periodic field density tests were performed at selected locations during the
recompaction of the house pads and driveway aprons and also at the bottom of the footing
excavations to confirm our field observations and to determine the compaction level. A
total of 466 field density tests, including retests of failed areas, were performed using the
Nuclear Probe Method in accordance with ASTM D 2922. The results of the compaction
tests performed are summarized on the attached Plates 3.1 through 3.22. These tests
were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed engineered fills at
the site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from
on-site survey stakes and construction plans. These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed to
be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
and/or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing.
Based on the observations of our field representative and the results of the
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the earthwork operation and foundation
excavation during the house construction portion of the Kaupe’a - Phase 1 project
observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in
substantial conformance with the project plans and _ specifications and the
recommendations contained in the referenced geotechnical exploration report. |
