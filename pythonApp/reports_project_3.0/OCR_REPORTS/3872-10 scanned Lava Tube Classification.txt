Dear Mr Yamashiro
This letter presents a summary of the discussions regarding lava tube occurrences
at the above referenced project in the project meeting on March 24 1998 The purpose
of the meeting was to come to a consensus regarding the method of documentation
classification and ultimately payment to the contractor for additional work performed for
the lava tubes encountered at the project site
Based on our observations of the earthwork operations to date the project site is
underlain by numerous lava tubes blisters and pockets of cavities Due to the presence
of a large number of these features we have been documenting the lava tubes
encountered during the mass grading operations To date we have documented
approximately 23 lava tube occurrences at the project site Our documentation includes
the date of first encounter approximate location approximate dimensions and status of
the lava tube
Based on the discussions during the project meeting lava tubes encountered at
the project site will be classified into two major groups 1 incidental lava tubes and
2 lava tubes requiring additional work For the lava tubes requiring additional work
classification it is further sub classified into two categories The following summarizes
the proposed tentative agreement regarding the classification of lava tubes

It is common in the West Hawaii area to encounter some z amount of lava tubes and
cavities during the mass grading operations which may be considered incidental to the
mass grading operations For the purpose of this project incidental lava tubes will consist
of those blisters voids and cavities exposed above the finish cut subgrades encountered
during the proof rolling operations six passes of a Caterpillar D 9 bulldozer or similar
heavy equipment The lava tubes classified as incidental will be removed or collapsed
and backfilled by the Contractor at no additional cost to the Owner
Because lava tubes encountered above the finish cut subgrades will be removed
during the excavation operations during mass grading no additional cost will be incurred
by the Owner In addition of Oahu Construction Company Ltd indicated
that lava tubes encountered during the proof rolling of finish subgrades will also be
considered incidental The Contractor will absorb the cost to remove and or collapse and
backfill those lava tubes encountered during the proof rolling operations The
above referenced letter by Geolabs Hawaii regarding collapsing and backfilling of lava
tubes should be followed for the backfilling operations
Our field representative will continue to document these lava tubes as they are
encountered in the field for information purposes only
2 Lava Tubes Requiring Additional Work
Lava tubes encountered or discovered after the proof rolling operations will not be
considered incidental and will require additional work by the Contractor These lava tubes
will be collapsed and backfilled as recommended in the above referenced letter by
Geolabs Hawaii dated Backfilling of these lava tubes may require the
use of sandbags to build a wall to confine the backfill and or mapping of the lava tubes
beyond the area of interest The Contractor will be compensated for the additional work
performed to remove these lava tubes encountered or discovered after the proof rolling
operations and the subsequent backfilling
For this classification of lava tubes there is further distinction between whether the
lava tubes may be backfilled using mass grading equipment or if the lava tubes are too
small in lateral extent such that the backfilling operations will require the use of small size
equipment The following discussions further sub classify those lava tubes requiring
additional work
a Some of the lava tubes discovered are quite large in lateral extent where
the backfilling operations may be performed using the larger mass grading
equipment These lava tubes typically have widths of about 15 feet or
more allowing the large bulldozers to work within the confines of the lava
tubes For those lava tubes slightly narrower than the width of the bulldozer
about 15 feet wide it may be advantageous to widen the lava tube to
allow a path for the bulldozer to access the lava tube If a lava tube is
