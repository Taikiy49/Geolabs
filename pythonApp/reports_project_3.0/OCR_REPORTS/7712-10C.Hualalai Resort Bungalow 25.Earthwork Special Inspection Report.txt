Dear Mr. Pang: 
This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed 
by Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company, Inc. for the Bungalow 25 project at Four 
Seasons Hualalai Resort on the Island of Hawaii. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project generally involved renovation of the existing Bungalow 25 and surrounding 
areas within Four Seasons Hualalai Resort. The project generally involved renovation of the 
existing bungalow and construction of new footings, swimming pool, walkways, lanai, and 
underground utility lines.  The general vicinity of the project site is shown on the Project 
Location Map, Plate 1. 
EARTHWORK OPERATION 
Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company, Inc. was the site work contractor for the 
project covered by this report.  Our observation and testing services during the site work 
performed by Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company, Inc. for the project occurred 
between December 2020 and June 2021. 
In general, fill and backfill materials generally consisted of excavated materials from 
the project site and some imported materials. Fills and backfills were generally placed in 
level lifts of about 8 inches in loose thickness, moisture-conditioned and compacted to a 
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction.

During the earthwork operations, samples of the on-site and imported materials 
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing. 
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our 
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results 
are summarized on the attached Plate 2. 
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an 
as-need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures 
and soil conditions were noted. A total of 44 field density tests were performed using the 
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D6938. Compaction test results are 
summarized on Plates 3.1 and 3.2. These tests were performed on the prepared ground 
surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills. Test locations and elevations were 
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes and topographic 
features. These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the 
degree implied by the methods used. Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked 
until the specified minimum compaction was obtained. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the 
referenced tests, it is our opinion that the earthwork operations performed by Hawaiian 
Dredging Construction Company, Inc. for the project observed and tested by Geolabs in 
the period covered by this report were performed in general accordance with the intent of 
the project plans and specifications and subsequent recommendations. 
LIMITATIONS 
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities occurring 
simultaneously on a construction site. Therefore, some of the earthwork may have been 
performed without our observation. However, the level of earthwork observation and testing 
services provided appears reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical risks to the 
project. It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical 
repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and 
materials. Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards 
of geotechnical engineering practice; no warranty is expressed or implied. Provision of 
construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance, nor does it guarantee construction 
of any type. The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the work, for adhering 
to the plans and specifications, and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found.
