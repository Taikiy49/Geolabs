Moisture Infiltration Through Slab
West Loch Fairways Townhomes
In accordance with your request during our meeting on August 10th we have
conducted a preliminary investigation and soil testing at the above referenced site
Project Background
We understand that the owners at several units of the West Loch Fairways
Townhomes have reported damage of the vinyl flooring in the kitchen It is believed
that the damage was due to moisture infiltration through the concrete slab
Our telephone conversation with of Island Flooring Co Inc
indicated that moisture readings in excess of 26 percent were recorded from the
concrete slab in units where floor covering damage were reported
Based on the construction plan the concrete slab is underlain by about 1 inch
of fine sand over a vapor barrier and 4 inches of cushion base Approximately 2 feet
of capping fill material underlies the cushion base In general our records indicated
that the 2 feet of capping fill material consisted of reddish brown sandy silt material
from Kunia
During the construction period the capping fill material at 34 test locations
generally had an average in place moisture content of about 22 percent

Field and Laboratory Findings
Following our meeting eight hand augered borings were made to retrieve the
underlying soil samples at selected units which reported moisture infiltration through the
slab The borings were generally drilled adjacent to the building slab and to a depth
of about 3 feet below the existing ground surface At Boring No 8 the boring was
drilled to a depth of about 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
location of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the boring data the existing ground surface adjacent to the units is
underlain by approximately 2 to 3 feet of reddish brown sandy silt capping fill
material overlying brown silty clay At Boring No 8 which is located about 18 feet from
the building slab brown silty clay was encountered from the ground surface to the
maximum depth drilled of about 5 feet
Laboratory test results on the capping fill material indicated that the average
moisture content for the 14 samples is about 31 percent which is about 9 percent
higher than the in place moisture during construction We also noted that the lower
portion of the capping fill generally has higher moisture content which may be an
indication of water ponding at the bottom of the capping fill
Random moisture tests were also conducted using a nuclear gauge on the
concrete sidewalk near the units which reported moisture infiltration through the slab
In general moisture content readings of 8 to 12 percent were measured on the
concrete sidewalk
Summary and Recommendations
Based on our findings and research to date the following is a summary of our
preliminary findings
1 In general the existing moisture content of the capping fill material
adjacent to the exterior of the building slab has about 9 percent higher
moisture content than those tested during construction This increase is
likely due to moisture infiltration
2 It was noted that the sprinkler system is located close to the house slabs
This may likely increase the potential of moisture infiltration into the
capping fill material especially if the drainage pattern is altered and or
impeded

A summary of the moisture tests conducted is presented on Plate 1 Based on
our preliminary findings we recommend the following be conducted
1 Moisture content tests be conducted on the floor slabs of selected units
to confirm the moisture readings obtained by Island Flooring We propose
the test be performed by non destructive method using a nuclear gauge
machine
2 Borings be drilled through the floor slabs within selected units to confirm
the high moisture content of the capping fill material underlying the slab
3 The water usage volume of the sprinkler system should be obtained The
volume should be compared with other similar scale of multi family
development to evaluate if there is an excessive water usage
Based on our preliminary findings we believe that excessive moisture may have
been introduced into the capping fill material underlying the building slab In general
the capping material is more permeable than the Surrounding and underlying clayey
material Therefore excessive water may have infiltrated and may be trapped in the
capping material Capillary vapor pressure underlying the slab may cause moisture to
migrate through the slab and damage the flooring system
Preliminary Solution Options
In general we feel that additional work will need to be performed to confirm our
preliminary findings and to recommend any subsequent remedial work needed to
reduce the moisture infiltration At this time we believe that the following options may
be considered
