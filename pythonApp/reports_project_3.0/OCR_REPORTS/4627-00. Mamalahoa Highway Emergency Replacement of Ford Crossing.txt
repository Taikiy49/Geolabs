Dear Mr Haymore
This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed
during the foundation probing and grouting operations for the Emergency Replacement of
Ford Crossing Makakupu Bridge project between September 4 and
The project site is located approximately 2 miles north of the Town of Pahala in the
District of Kau on the Island of Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The project consists of constructing a replacement bridge of approximately 42 3 feet in
width and 120 feet in length The replacement bridge structure will consist of three piers
and two abutments supported on a shallow foundation system bearing on basalt rock
formation The intent of the foundation probing and grouting operation is an attempt to
locate and grout potential subsurface cavities and or voids within the basalt formation
beneath the bridge abutment and pier foundations
The bridge abutment and pier foundation locations and the probe hole locations
were surveyed and marked in the field by the general contractor Hawaiian Dredging
Construction Company prior to the probing operations The probe holes were spaced at
about 10 foot on center along the abutment and pier foundations A total of 60 scheduled
probe holes were located at the bridge abutment and pier foundations The approximate

The probing and grouting work for the replacement bridge was performed prior to
excavation for the bridge abutment and pier foundations The probing work was
performed by Isemoto Contracting Company The probe holes were drilled with a track
mounted drill rig equipped with an air percussion hammer The probe holes drilled were
about 3 5 inches in diameter and extended to about 14 to 28 feet below the existing
ground surface The probe holes drilled were extended to a depth of at least 10 feet below
the boitom of footing elevations A summary of our foundation probe drilling and grouting
observations is provided on Plates 3 1 and 3 2
Materials encountered in most of the probe holes during the probe drilling
operations generally consisted of a layer of volcanic ash sandwiched between two layers
of gray medium hard to hard basalt formation However the volcanic ash layer was not
encountered in some of the probe holes mosily located at Abutment No 1 and Pier No 1
Occasional clinker layers were encountered in a few of the probe holes The field probing
and grouting records are provided in Appendix A of this letter report
After completion of the probe drilling operations the contractor filled the probe
holes with fine aggregate masonry grout specified in the project specifications The grout
used to fill the probe holes was supplied by Jas W Glover Ltd Additional probe holes
were drilled adjacent to the scheduled probe holés that generally had grout intakes of 0 5
cubic yards or greater Of the 60 scheduled probe holes one of ithe scheduled probe
holes located at Wing Wall No 3 had grout intakes greater than 0 5 cubic yards
Therefore four additional probe holes were drilled at Wing Wall No 3 The approximate
locations of the additional probe holes are shown on the Probe Hole Identification Plan
Plate 2 Approximately 7 8 cubic yards of fine aggregate masonry grout was used to fill
both the scheduled and additional probe holes drilled for the bridge foundations
In summary the foundation probing and grouting operations have been conducted
in general conformance with the intent of the project specifications and our geotechnical
engineering exploration report Based on our observations it is our professional opinion
that the minor voids and or cavities encountered at the bridge abutment and pier
foundation locations have been grouted and that the subgrade area should provide the
recommended foundation support for the bridge abutment and pier foundation
