Subject Soil Engineering Report
Proposed Residence
Lot 44 Ahuimanu Hills Phase II
Gentlemen
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near surface
soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling two borings at the flat upper portion of the lot
two hand auger probes along the rear lot slope laboratory testing of the
swelling potential of the soils and a review of the subdivision site
grading and preliminary house plans This report summarizes our findings
and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a one to two story wood frame structure
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the front portion of the house on
the level area while post and beam construction is planned for the rear
portion
The layout of the proposed structure is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located in the Ahuimanu Hills Subdivision Phase Il
to the west of Halemanu Street The eastern front portion of the site
is a relatively level pad area West of the pad area the ground surface
slopes at about 2H 1V towards a drainage easement which covers the
western end of the lot Ground surface elevations range from 200 feet in
the lower western portion of the property to 218 feet at the upper level
pad area
A review of the Ahuimanu Hills Phase II grading plans indicates that
the lot was cut up to 20 feet
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
The borings and hand auger probes were drilled at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 The borings and probes
encountered medium stiff to stiff brown clayey silt to 15 0 feet the
maximum depth explored
Laboratory swell tests performed on the near surface fill material
indicated that the soil generally has low swelling potential if the
moisture content of the soil is maintained near the optimum condition
For more detailed information regarding the soils encountered refer to
the boring and hand auger probe logs
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our field explorations the proposed slab on grade and
post and beam construction may be used to support the house
Deeper footing embedment is recommended for the steep slope 2 1 area to
minimize movements due to slope creep

House Foundation
For the proposed light residential structure spread footing foundations
and or slab on grade may be used To minimize the effects of Slope creep
and erosion post footings located close to the tops of slopes or on the
slopes should be embedded a sufficient depth to obtain a minimum
horizontal set back distance of 6 feet measured from the outer edge of
the footing to the slope face Post footings 6 feet or more away from
the tops of slopes should be placed 12 inches below the finish grade
see Plate 2
For the light loads anticipated an allowable bearing pressure of 2500
p s f can be used for footings resting on the stiff compacted fill
If a footing is located next to a utility trench the footing should
extend to the bottom of the utility trench or be designed to span over
the trench to reduce footing settlement due to settlement of the trench
backfill
Site Grading
Minimal site grading is planned for the lot development Foot ing
backfills should be placed in maximum 6 inch lifts and compacted to a
minimum of 85 of its maximum dry density established by ASIM D 1557 78
Surface water should be diverted away from the top of the slope and Slope
planting utilized as soon as practicable to reduce erosion
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for portions of the house garage and
driveway In order to provide a barrier against future water
infiltration under the slab we recommend that the slab perimeter footing
be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the outside finished ground
surface

Lot Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should be
shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also the owners should be advised to install a gutter
system around the house and that the gutter water should be diverted far
away from the footings walkways and the steep slope at the rear of the
house Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slab
should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage considerations are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to
prevent ponding condition

Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill operations
be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
