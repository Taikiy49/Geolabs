INTRODUCTION
This letter report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering site
reconnaissance for the proposed 6 foot high CMU makai perimeter wall to be located at
the PVT Land Company Landfill in the Waianae area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field reconnaissance and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses for the proposed
project These recommendations are intended for design input only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a 6 foot high CMU wall at the makai or southwest side
of the PVT Land Company Landfill The wall will extend from Maia Street to near the
Ulehawa Stream It is our understanding the wall will be constructed near the existing
grade and backfilling behind the wall will be less than 6 inches The exception is by
Kipahele Street where a gate retaining wall and a retaining return wall are proposed It
is anticipated that these retaining walls will retain about 2 feet of backfill soil

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed wall site is slightly sloping about ten horizontal to one vertical
10H 1V down toward the southwest The existing grades range between about 15 and
34 feet Mean Sea Level MSL The site contains some grass and weed vegetation with
bare coral outcrops Currently a perimeter chain link fence is located within 2 feet of the
PVT Land Company s property The site is bounded by residential houselots to the
southwest
A site reconnaissance conducted on revealed a wall foundation footing
trench extending from about Maia Street to about Auyong Homested Road The footing
trench was found to be about 2 to 3 feet in widths with embedment depths of about 12 to
18 inches The bottoms were found to be firm except that many areas had a thin layer of
loose disturbed soil
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were previously explored on
The boring was drilled by Geolabs Hawaii and logged by Clayton
Environmental Consultants Inc The subsurface conditions at the project site were
explored by drilling one boring to a depth of about 40 feet below the existing ground
elevation The approximate location of the boring is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The previous field exploration generally encountered 6 inches of medium dense
light tan silty sand underlain by 2 feet of very dense yellowish tan cemented calcareous

boring
Groundwater was encountered in the boring at a depth of about 30 5 feet below the
existing grade at the time of the field exploration However water level at the project site
may be influenced by tidal influences seasonal precipitation and storm surge conditions
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of our field reconnaissance the proposed 6 foot high CMU wall
may be supported on shallow foundations bearing on the on site very dense calcareous
and coral sand materials
Fence Wall Foundations
An allowable bearing pressure of may be utilized for the design of shallow
foundations bearing on the on site very dense calcareous and coral sand materials This
bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for
transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces Footings should be
embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade and have a minimum

embedded to the same elevation as the adjacent existing wall footing
For footings constructed near tops of slopes or on sloping ground the footings
should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal set back distance of
8 feet measured from the outside edge of footings to the slope face
If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of footing excavations
i e material disturbed during the excavation process they should be over excavated to
expose the underlying firm materials The over excavation should be backfilled with
structural fill material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction or
concrete In lieu of filling the over excavation the footing bottom may be extended down
to the underlying competent material
Lateral loads acting on the wall may be resisted by frictional resistance developed
below the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due to friction may
be estimated using a coefficient of friction of 0 35 This value assumes that the concrete
for footings is placed directly against the footing excavations Rotational moments acting
on the wall may be resisted by the weight of the soil above the footing and the weight of
the wall itself Due to the shallow embedment and the uncertainty of potential future
grading outside of the PVT Land Company s property passive earth pressure should be
neglected in design
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii prior to placement of reinforcing steel and concrete to confirm the
foundation bearing conditions and the required embedment depths
Retaining Walls
Based on the planned development retaining walls and return retaining walls will
be constructed at the intersection with Kipahele Street It is estimated that the retaining
walls will be retaining up to 2 feet of backfill
Wall Foundations
In general retaining wall foundations should be designed in accordance with the
recommendations presented in the Fence Wall Foundations section of this report
The exception is that the embedment depth should be increased to 20 inches below
the lowest adjacent grade Also wall footings parallel to the direction of the slope
should be constructed in stepped footings

Lateral Earth Pressures
For design of retaining structures an equivalent fluid earth pressure of 35 pcf may
be used for free standing walls This pressure applies for relatively permeable
free draining backfill material such as the on site calcareous and coral sand
material placed behind the retaining wall Also note that free standing walls are
defined as walls that are allowed to rotate about 0 5 percent of the wall height
Surcharge and hydrostatic loads are not accounted for in the above equivalent fluid
earth pressure
Backfill behind retaining walls should consist of the on site calcareous or coral sand
material compacted to between 90 and 95 percent relative compaction
Over compaction of the retaining wall backfill should be avoided
We recommend that footing excavations and retaining wall backfill compaction be
observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
Drainage
The finished grades next to the wall should be sloped to shed water away from the
wall and to reduce the potential for ponding This is especially important for the proposed
retaining walls at Kipahele Street which will not likely have a drainage system as disposal
of water to neighboring properties is unlawful
Proper drainage is essential for the proper performance of the above foundation
recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
against the walls immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce the potential for water
infiltration In addition drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should
be maintained to drain surface run off away from the wall
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to
Geolabs Hawaii for review and written comments prior to advertisement for bid This review
is necessary to evaluate adherence of the plans and specifications to the intent of the
foundation and earthwork recommendations provided herein If this review is not made
Geolabs Hawaii cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
presented

It is recommended that Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide geotechnical

earthwork construction should also be observed by a representative from Geolabs Hawaii
This is to observe general conformance with the intent of the design concepts specifications

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the previous field boring and our field reconnaissance Variations
of conditions beyond the field boring may occur and the nature and extent of these variations
may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident
it will be necessary to reevaluate the recommendations presented in this report
The boring locations in the field were determined by taping from structures indicated
on the site map by Clayton Environmental Consultants Inc dated July 1992 The location
of the field boring should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
methods used
The stratification breaks implied by the written descriptions of the previous boring
depict the approximate boundaries between soil rock types and as such may denote a
gradual transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown
on the graphic representations and or presented in the text of this report These data have
been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must
be noted that fluctuation may occur due to tidal influences seasonal precipitation and
storm surge conditions
