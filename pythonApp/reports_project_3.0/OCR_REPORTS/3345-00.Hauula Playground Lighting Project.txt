INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Hauula Playground Lighting Project in the Hauula area on
the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory
testing and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our
analyses for the proposed project These recommendations are intended for design
input only and may not be appropriate to form the basis for construction cost estimates
nor for construction bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located within the Hauula Playground adjacent to
Hauula Elementary School in the Hauula area on the island of Oahu Hawaii It is
proposed to install six light poles along the two baselines of the new softball field at the
Hauula Playground as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The new light poles will be of
steel construction approximately 60 feet in height and 14 inches in diameter at the
base The following information pertinent to the foundation design of the new light
poles was provided for our analysis by the project structural engineer
Allowable Vertical Load per pole 2 2 Kips
Vertical Seismic Load per pole Not Applicable
Ultimate Lateral Load at the base per pole 1 5 Kips
Ultimate Bending Moment at the base per pole 59 Kip Feet

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed project site is located inside the existing Hauula Playground in the
Hauula area on the island of Oahu Hawaii The playground is situated to the mauka
side of Kamehameha Highway and adjacent to Hauula Elementary School
The existing playground generally consist of a softball field to the south a paved
playcourt to the north and a park building to the northeast The playground is
generally grassed with some shrubs scattered across the site The project site is
relatively level with existing ground elevations at about 6 to 7 feet Mean Sea Level
MSL
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program at the Hauula Playground site consisted of drilling
and sampling three borings at the softball field area and two borings at the playcourt
area The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration disclosed that the proposed new light pole locations at the
softball field are generally underlain by a surficial fill layer consisting of stiff gravelly silts
and clayey silts of approximately 2 to 4 feet thick In Boring No 1 the surficial fill was
underlain by a thin layer of loose silty sand and medium stiff silty clay to a depth of
approximately 6 feet below the existing ground surface In Boring No 3 the surficial
fill layer is underlain by a lagoonal deposit consisting of very soft silty clays of
approximately 3 feet thick The borings then encountered a medium dense sandy
deposit extending to a depth of approximately 10 to 12 feet below the existing ground

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration indicates that the proposed light pole locations are underlain
by a surficial fill layer consisting of stiff silty soils overlying medium dense sandy
deposits at shallow depths Very loose coralline deposits underlie the near surface soil
deposits at a depth of approximately 15 feet below the existing ground surface In
consideration of the lateral loads acting on the foundations of the new light pole
structures we recommend that drilled pier foundations embedded in the upper fill crust
and underlying sandy deposits be used to support the proposed lighting system Due
to the presence of loose soft subsoils with shallow groundwater table temporary casing
during the drilled pier construction will be required to reduce the potential for caving in
of the drilled holes In addition placement of concrete by wet construction methods will
be necessary Minor site grading of 1 foot or less is anticipated for the foundation
construction of the proposed light pole structures Detailed recommendations are
presented in the following subsections

Drilled Pier Foundations
In order to develop the required bearing and lateral load capacities the proposed
new light pole structures may be supported by a foundation system consisting of cast
in place concrete drilled piers Based on the anticipated structural loads and the
subsoil conditions encountered at the project site we recommend that drilled piers with
a minimum diameter of 24 inches and a length not less than 15 feet be used to support
the proposed light pole structures
The cast in place concrete drilled piers would derive vertical support principally
from skin friction between the pier shafts and the surrounding soils An allowable
compressive load capacity of up to 12 kips per pier may be used for dead plus live
loads and may be increased by one third for transient loads such as wind or seismic
forces The end bearing component of the drilled piers should be discounted due to
the presence of groundwater and potential cave in of the loose sandy gravel layer near
the anticipated tip of the drilled piers that would preclude obtaining a clean bottom
during drilled pier installation
Lateral loads imposed on the foundations can be resisted by a combination of
the following 1 resistance of the pier soil system to deflect and 2 passive resistance
on the vertical faces of pier caps where applicable Lateral load resistance of the pier
is a function of the stiffness of the surrounding soil the stiffness of the pier shaft
allowable deflection at the top of pier and induced moment in the pier Based on the
information provided each pier will be subjected to a lateral load of 1 5 kips and a
bending moment of 59 kip feet at the ground surface Our lateral load analyses
indicated that the estimated deflection at the top of the pier should be less than inch
for a fixed head condition

Pier settlements are estimated to be on the order of less than 1 inch We
believe that a significant portion of the settlements is elastic and should occur as the
loads are applied
Construction Considerations
Drilled piers are desirable to the subject project because of the significant
increase in lateral and uplift load capacities when compared to shallow foundations
However the performance of the drilled piers will depend significantly upon the
contractor s method of construction and construction procedures In our opinion the
following conditions would have a significant effect on the effectiveness and cost of the
drilled pier foundations
Loose Soft Subsoils
Based on our field exploration some of the proposed new light pole locations are
underlain by loose sandy material and soft lagoonal deposit in the upper 5 feet
with shallow groundwater table To reduce the potential for caving in of the
drilled holes temporary casing will be needed Care should be exercised during
removal of the temporary casing to reduce the potential for necking of the
concrete pier Therefore a minimum 5 foot head of concrete should be
maintained above the bottom of the casing during removal of the casing The
anticipated high groundwater level may also pose further construction difficulties
since proper observation of the sides and bottoms of the drilled shaft may not
be possible
The load bearing capacities of drilled piers depend to a large extent on the
contact between the pier shafts and the surrounding soils Therefore proper
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3345 00 Page 9
construction techniques are important The contractor should exercise care in
drilling the pier holes and in placing concrete into the holes
Drilling by methods utilizing drilling fluids is not recommended Placement of
concrete by wet construction methods using a tremie pipe will be required due
to the high groundwater table anticipated at the site The concrete should be
placed in a suitable manner by displacing the water in an upward fashion from
the bottom of the drilled hole A representative of Geolabs Hawaii should be
present to observe the installation of drilled piers during construction
A low shrink concrete mix with high slump should be used to provide close
contact between the pier shafts and the surrounding soil The concrete should
be placed in a suitable manner to reduce segregation of the aggregates from the
concrete mix
General guidelines for the drilled pier construction are presented in Appendix C
of this report

APPENDIX _A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling five
borings to depths ranging between 16 and 26 feet below the existing ground surface
Boring No 2 was drilled to a depth of about 26 feet and was further advanced by
continuous penetration without sampling to a depth of about 54 feet below the existing
ground surface The borings were drilled using truck mounted drilling equipment The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our field geologist who monitored the drilling operations on
a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual
observation and testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance
with the Unified Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic presentation
of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 1
through A 5 1
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an
18 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

GUIDE SPECIFICATIONS FOR DRILLED PIERS
I LOCATIONS AND DIMENSIONS
Drilled pier foundations shall be constructed at the locations to the lengths and in
accordance with the details and notes shown on the plans As built pier lengths shall
extend to the depths shown on the plans and shall not be deeper by more than 5 feet
without prior written permission from the Soils Engineer Pier diameters shall be as
shown on the plans for each individual pier The diameter shown shall be the diameter
of the drilling bit or bucket
ll ALLOWABLE TOLERANCES ON LOCATION STRAIGHTNESS AND
PLUMBNESS
The center of the top of the pier and reinforcing cage and built as the bottom
of the column cap wall or grade beam shall not deviate from the location shown on
the plans by more than 2 inches in any direction
The drilled pier shall not deviate from a plumb line referenced to the center of
the drilled pier as described above by more than 2 percent of the drilled pier length
from the top to the point of interest
The clear distance between reinforcing steel and the drilled pier hole sidewall
shall not be less than 3 inches
li EQUIPMENT AND OPERATION
Drilling casing setting of reinforcing cages pumping bailing concrete
placement pulling casing and all other phases of the pier construction operation shall
be performed with equipment and tools capable of meeting the specifications
All loose material shall be removed from the hole prior to placing concrete
Cleaning shall be by machine or hand operated equipment Drilling and placement of
concrete shall be scheduled such that concrete is placed in each pier hole on the same
day in which drilling is completed Holes spaced closer than three pier diameters
center to center shall not be drilled on the same day unless approved by the Soils
Engineer
IV CASING
Casing shall be used where needed to maintain the integrity of the pier hole
during drilling installation of reinforcing steel and placement of concrete

Where casing is used the hole shall not be drilled ahead of the casing any
further than the hole will stand without sloughing At any time sloughing occurs the
hole shail be cased to the bottom of the sloughing area immediately before proceeding
to drill the hole any deeper
Inside casing diameter shail not be less than the required drilled pier diameter
The hole shall be filled with concrete and the casing removed as specified hereafter
All casing must be removed Failure to remove ail casing will result in rejection of the
drilled pier
V CONCRETE
Concrete shall comply with all provisions of the project plans At the time of
placement the concrete shall have a siump necessary to produce the specified strength
and should be determined by the cone siump test ASTM D 143 Unless a higher
compressive strength is specified elsewhere the concrete shall have a minimum
compressive strength of 3 000 p s i at 28 days as determined by the concrete cylinder
compression tests ASTM C 39 9
Vi REINFORCING
The reinforcing cages shall be braced and tied in such a way that the cages are
not deformed or distorted during fabrication placement of the cage in hole and
concrete placement
Any reinforcing cage which is bent collapsed or distorted or which varies from
a Straight line by more than 3 inches at any point shall be rejected
Reinforcing cages shall be set in the hole in such a way that they do not rake
the sidewall and cause sloughing Reinforcing cages shail not be spliced in the drilled
pier holes instead they should be fabricated to the full length prior to placement
The reinforcing cage shall be hung from a solid support at the top of the hole
so that it does not bear on the bottom of the hole and hangs free in the hole The
reinforcing cage shall be set at the required location and elevation prior to beginning
placing concrete and shail be held and supported in such a way that it does not move
during concrete placement
Reinforcing dowels which are to be set in the concrete after completion of
placement if required are to be installed immediately following placement of concrete

Vil PLACEMENT OF CONCRETE
lf the hole is filled in the dry concrete shall be placed through a hopper and
elephant trunk positioned in the center of the reinforcing cage The elephant trunk
shall be long enough and be of sufficient diameter to prevent segregation The hopper
shall be of sufficient size to prevent spilling of concrete outside of the elephant trunk
For placing concrete in a cased hole a minimum head of 5 feet of concrete shall
be maintained in the casing at all times Where staged casing is used the casing shall
be pulled and removed in units in such a way that after pulling one unit of casing there
is at least 5 feet of concrete in the remaining casing until the hole is topped out at the
cut off grade
Unless otherwise noted vibration of the concrete is not required until the
concrete reaches an elevation of approximately 10 feet below the top of the drilled pier
After this elevation is reached the concrete shall be vibrated continuously to the top of
the pier and until good densification of the concrete has been achieved Concrete shall
be added as required to compensate for consolidation and expansion into the hole
The concrete vibrator and the vibration operation shall conform to all requirements for
internal concrete vibrators and vibration of concrete given in the job specifications
The elapsed time from beginning the placement of concrete until completion shall
not exceed 1 hour Placement of concrete shall not commence until sufficient concrete
to complete the pier is on the job site waiting to be placed
Vill DISPOSAL OF SOIL FROM DRILLING
Soil obtained from drilling shall not be used as fill unless otherwise approved by
the Soils Engineer and it shall be disposed of off the site or used in landscaped areas
as appropriate as designated by the Architect or Civil Engineer

IX OBSERVATION
The drilling of holes setting of reinforcing setting and pulling of casing
dewatering placing concrete and any other operation involved in the construction of
the drilled piers must be performed under continuous observation of the Soils
Engineer The contractor shall give notice at least 48 hours in advance of his
performing such work He shall do no such work in the absence of the Soils Engineer
