PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project is located within the property limits of the Makena Resort in
Makena on the island of Maui Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided the proposed construction where dewatering may
occur will consist of construction of a wetwell structure and drywell structure within the
proposed Pump Station ML Based on the preliminary site plan provided the proposed
wetwell structure and drywell structure will have a finished floor elevation of about Mean
Sea Level MSL
Based on the information provided we anticipate that site grading of the ground
surface at Pump Station ML will consist of fills of about 1 foot and cuts of about 3 feet to
attain a finish ground surface elevation of about 13 feet MSL Excavations for the
various structures other than the proposed wetwell and drywell at the site are
anticipated to be less than 8 feet below the finish ground surface elevation Groundwater
was encountered at a depth of about 10 8 feet below the ground surface during our field
exploration corresponding to an elevation of 1 7 feet MSL The bottom of the proposed
wetwell and drywell will be approximately 2 feet below the groundwater table Therefore
temporary shoring and dewatering of the excavation will be required for the proposed
construction

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our work on this phase of the project was to provide the
groundwater sampling and testing services necessary to obtain baseline groundwater
quality data in support of the preparation of an NPDES permit application for construction
dewatering activities Our scope of work generally consisted of the following tasks and
work efforts
1 Collection of groundwater samples from the monitoring well at the site by a
Project Engineer from our firm The chain of custody documentation
handling and transportation of the water samples to the laboratory were
also performed by our Project Engineer
2 Analytical laboratory testing of the collected samples in general accordance
with the requirements of the Department of Health NOI Form A
3 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings

The project site is located adjacent and east of an existing comfort station and
parking lot that is enclosed by a chain link fence on the east side of Makena Keoneoio
Road The project site is bounded by Makena Alanui Road to the east
Makena Keoneoio Road to the west and Honoiki Street to the north as shown on the
Site Plan 1 Plate 2 The proposed project is located within the property limits of the
Makena Resort in Makena on the island of Maui Hawaii

Subsurface Conditions
During our field exploration subsurface materials were examined and classified by
an Engineer from our firm A soil sample was obtained from the drilled boring s cuttings
The soil was classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System
Core samples of rock materials were obtained using diamond core drilling techniques in
general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for
Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling method which uses a hollow bit to cut
into the rock formation The rock material left in the hollow core of the bit is mechanically
recovered for examination and description
In general the boring encountered a 2 inch thick asphaltic concrete layer
overlying dense gravel to a depth of about 5 feet below the existing ground surface
followed by soft to medium hard basalt extending to the maximum depth explored of
about 26 feet below the existing ground surface A loose clinker zone was encountered
from 19 to 21 feet below the existing ground surface

Groundwater Conditions
Relatively shallow groundwater conditions were encountered during the drilling and
monitoring well installation Due to the close proximity of the project site to the shoreline
it is likely that the groundwater levels measured in the boring during our field exploration
will be affected by tidal fluctuation of the sea level In addition the groundwater levels at
the project site may be influenced by seasonal precipitation and storm surge conditions
The analytical laboratory results indicate that no significant adverse water quality
conditions were detected in the water samples that were tested The water sample test
results are provided in Appendix A and include the completed Department of Health
NPDES NOI Form A and the official laboratory report
The test results for the NOI Form A Part A Water Quality Constituents in
general appear to be within normal values for typical fresh to brackish groundwater In
addition the test results for the NOI Form A Part B Toxic Constituents including
Benzene Toluene Ethylbenzene Total Xylenes and metals including Lead and
Cadmium all revealed levels Jess than the reporting limit In addition purgeable
hydrocarbons and oil grease all revealed levels less than the reporting limit
In general the test results indicate the reporting limit which refers to the detection
limit of the laboratory equipment The reporting limit is dependent on the testing
equipment and the statistical control applied by the laboratory to the test results
Therefore laboratories adopting the same standard test method may have different
reporting limits
