Test Pile Report
Kakaako Elderly Housing Project
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
As requested we have observed the test pile driving of the above referenced
project A total of 32 test piles were driven at pre determined locations on the site The
piles consisted of 14 inch square precast prestressed concrete piles The pile driving
hammer used was a delivering a maximum rated energy of about 40 200
ft lbs
The test pile numbers reflect the actual pile locations in the field and we have
followed the pile numbering system provided by the contractor Predrilling of 14 and 25
feet below the existing grade at an approximate ground elevation of 5 5 feet MSL was
adopted for the parking and tower areas respectively
A sketch showing the areas with three 3 different production pile casting lengths
is shown on Plate 1
At the parking garage area the shorter piles were driven to depths of 13 to 20 feet
below the ground surface It appeared that a production pile length of 20 feet may be
used The predrilling would help socket the short piles into the underlying coral ledge
formation
For area near TP 52 see Plate 1 the predrill depth should be increased to 20 feet
below the existing surface and longer 34 foot piles would be used In general a shorter
predrill depth of 14 feet below the existing surface is recommended for the remaining
