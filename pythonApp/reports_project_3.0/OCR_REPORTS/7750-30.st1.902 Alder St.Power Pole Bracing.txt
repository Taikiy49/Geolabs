In accordance with your request, we provided geotechnical engineering 
consultation services in support of the evaluation of the existing power pole bracing at the

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The development is located at 902 Alder Street in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, 
Hawaii. The project will generally consist of a 20-story high-rise residential building with 
a separate five-story parking structure. 
During construction of the project, we understand that a temporary power source 
will need to be installed. Installation of the temporary power line will require trenching 
close to an existing power pole and its guy wire anchor. Therefore, it is desired to evaluate 
the potential impact of planned excavation in the proximity of the existing guy wire anchor. 
FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS 
Based on the information provided, the temporary underground power line will be 
installed along the Alder Street sidewalk at the northeastern corner of the project site. 
Currently, there is an existing power pole and guy wire anchor near the proximity of the 
planned power line trench excavation. We understand that the guy wire is attached to a 
deadman anchor located about 5 feet 10 inches below the ground surface. Based on the 
angle of the guy wire, the deadman anchor is estimated to be about 4 feet 8 inches 
beyond the location where guy wire penetrates the ground. 
The planned excavation for the temporary power line will be about 6 feet from the 
guy wire at the closest location. We understand that the excavation depth will be about 
3 feet below the ground surface. A Sketch of Existing Conditions and Planned Excavation 
is provided on Plate 1. A Photograph of Existing Conditions and Planned Excavation is 
provided on Plate 2.

Based on our borings drilled for the 902 Alder Street development, we envision 
that the near-surface materials at the site generally consist of stiff silts and clays. 
Groundwater is not anticipated to be encountered within the temporary power line trench 
excavations. In general, we believe that the trench excavations may be achieved with 
near-vertical sidewalls. 
Deadman anchors generally achieve anchoring resistance from the weight of soil 
above the deadman anchor and passive resistance of the soil surrounding the deadman 
anchor in the direction of the guy wire. The planned trench excavation is located behind 
the deadman anchor (away from the guy wire) and will occur outside of the area where 
the deadman anchor will achieve passive resistance from the surrounding soils. 
Therefore, we believe that the planned excavations for the temporary power line as 
described herein would likely not affect the resistive capacity of the existing deadman 
anchor.  
The evaluation of the existing power pole anchor contained in this letter is based 
in part upon information provided by the client. We recommend Geolabs observe and 
monitor the excavation, installation, and backfilling of the temporary power line 
installation.
