INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the sub
surface conditions at the proposed site to provide soil data
for the site grading design of the proposed subdivision at
Pupukea Oahu Hawaii
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the eastern or mauka side
of Kamehameha Highway about 900 feet south of the inter
section of Kamehameha Highway and Pupukea Road Access
to the site is by a dirt road from Kamehameha Highway
The site is bordered by houses on the north west and
south The area to the east is undeveloped and covered
with trees and grass
The site generally slopes up to the east at about 5 percent
grades The southern corner of the lot is on a terrace
about 10 feet higher than the rest of the site
Most of the site is covered with trees and grass
There is an existing concrete drainage swale along the western
border of the site
The elevation of the site generally varies from about 20 to
52 feet
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed plan is to clear and grade the site for a single
family residential development consisting of 14 lots
The preliminary grading plan indicates that the higher sou
thern portion of the site will be cut and the central portion
will be filled Cuts up to about 10 feet and fills up to
about 6 feet are planned

Subsurface Exploration
Three borings were drilled to depths of 16 5 to 26 5 feet
and seven test pits were dug with a backhoe to depths of
2 to 6 feet to explore the subsurface conditions and to
obtain samples for laboratory testing
The approximate locations of the borings and test pits
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Detailed information on the field exploration and labora
tory testing is covered in the Appendix along with the
boring and test pit logs and laboratory test results
Soil Conditions
The field exploration disclosed variable soil conditions
at the site It appears that the subsoils were formed by
a variety of processes
In the higher southern and eastern portions of the site
2 to 6 feet of adobe clay was probably washed down from
the upper hillsides and covered the underlying rock for
mation
GEOCLABS HAWAII
W P 4
In the western portion of the site it appears that the rock
formation has been cut down by stream or wave action to depths
of 18 feet or more Variable alluvial deposits cover the under
lying rock
In the northeastern porticn of the site near Kamehameha High
way the rock has been cut down even further and is covered
by beach sand and coral deposited during higher stages of
the sea level
Water was encountered in one of the borings at 22 foot depths

In general competent bearing soils were encountered at the
site However expansive adobe clay was noted in the eastern
two thirds of the site
Due to the adobe soils shallow pier type foundations are
recommended

that most of the cut will be in the adobe clay and lava
rock The adobe clay should not be used wrehin the top

by drilling 3 borings and excavating 7 test pits with a

reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory

Unified Soil Classification System

STANDARD GRADING SPECIFICATIONS
PROPOSED SUBDIVISION AT PUPUKEA BEACH
The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing
preparation of fill areas placement and compaction of fill
and removal and backfilling of underground structures These
specifications are of a general nature and may be superseded
by specific requirements or recommendations contained within
the soils report
Ie Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of
trash debris organic matter and all other deleterious
materials These materials shall be removed and disposed
offsite
II Preparation of Fill Areas
A Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose compe
tent natural soils Localized soft pockets shall be
undercut and backfilled with compacted select mate
rials
B All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the
soils engineer priior to the placement of any fill
C After approval the surface shall be scarified to a
depth of six inches brought to near optimum moisture
content and compacted as herein specified
3 De
D Where the existing surface exceeds a slope of 5
horizontal to 1 vertical 5 1 the ground surface
shall be keyed and benched into competent soil to
bond the fill to the existing ground surface
E Subsurface drains may be required as directed by the
soils engineer where springs or zones of seepage are
encountered
III Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or
approved import material Import material shall be
tested and approved prior to transportation to the site
Fill material shall contain less than 5 percent organic
matter and other deleterious material and shall not
contain rocks or lumps in excess of eight inches 8
in diameter
Vi Placement and Compaction of Fill
A The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers ait
when compacted will not exceed nine inches 9 in
thickness Each layer shall be spread uniformly and
blade mixed to attain np Gorman of material and
water content within each layer
GEOLABS HAWAII
ae
Rock fragments or boulders less than eight inches
8 in diameter may be utilized provided they are
not concentrated in any one area and that there is
sufficient fine grained material to fill the voids
between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter
shall be removed from the fill unless specifically
approved for placement in the fill by the soils
engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90
percent of the maximum dry density established by
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 unless otherwise
specified in the soils report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibra
tory or pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil
type and capable of producing the specified results
in an expedient and efficient manner The equipment
shall be in good working condition fully ballasted
and self cleaning
The compaction shall be accomplished ina systematic
and uniform manner over the entire fill area to pro
duce a uniformly compacted fill

seasonal Requirements
