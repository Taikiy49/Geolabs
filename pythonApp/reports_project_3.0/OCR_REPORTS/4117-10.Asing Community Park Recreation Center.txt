INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Asing Community Park Recreation Center located in the Ewa
District on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The site is located east of the existing West Loch
Elderly Housing Complex which is located at the northeastern corner of Renton Road
and Fort Weaver Road The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and _ geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended to assist in the design of the mass
grading building structure foundations slabs utilities excavation and fill placement only
The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted
at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a one story building to the east of the existing West
Loch Elderly Housing Complex The one story building will cover approximately
15 000 square feet in area Future improvements include basketball and volleyball courts
and other recreational facilities to the north of the existing West Loch Elderly Housing
Complex These future improvements are not included in our current scope of work

geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the proposed recreation center
building Our work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
August 31 1999 The scope of our work included the following tasks and work efforts

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be middle
to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene in
age As volcanic activity in Waianae ceased lava flows from Koolau banked against its
eroded eastern slope forming a broad plateau now known as the Schofield Plateau or
Central Oahu
GEOLABS INC
W O 4117 10 Page 4
The forces of weathering and erosion attacked this plateau generating sediments
which were transported to the coast In the vicinity of the project site and further south
these alluvial sediments accumulated and interbedded with marine sediments and
coral algal reef formations to form a sedimentary wedge ranging in thickness from
zero feet in the area of H 1 Freeway to over 1 000 feet at Ewa Beach This wedge forms
the Ewa Plain and serves as the confining formation or caprock over the artesian basal
aquifers of southern Oahu Deposition of these sediments has continued from earlier
geologic time through the present The surface clayey soils represent these alluvial
sediments
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed project is located east of the existing West Loch Elderly Housing
Complex which is located at the northeastern corner of Renton Road and Fort Weaver
Road The proposed project is located in the Ewa District on the Island of Oahu Hawaii
The site is bounded by a parking lot to the west an extension of Renton Road to the
west Arizona Road to the south and a drainage channel to the east and north
The site is slightly sloping to the east with ground elevations ranging from 36 5 to
40 5 feet mean sea level MSL The project site was covered with manicured grass
throughout the majority of the project site

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of our exploration we believe that the proposed recreation
center building may be supported on shallow spread or continuous wall footings The
footings should bear on properly re compacted on site silty clay material or properly
compacted import fill material The footings should be embedded a minimum of
24 inches below the lowest adjacent grade An allowable bearing value of 2 500 pounds
per square foot psf may be used for footing foundations on recompacted on site
material or imported fill
Our laboratory testing on the silty clay material indicated a moderate to high
expansion potential and a very high shrinkage potential To reduce the potential for
distress to slabs on grade resulting from swelling and shrinking of the clayey materials
we recommend that 18 inches of non expansive select granular fill material be used
below the interior and exterior slabs on grade
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report

Site Grading
Site grading will generally consist of fills up to about 3 feet and minimal cuts Items
of grading that are addressed in the following subsections include 1 Site Preparation
2 Fills and Backfills 3 Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements and 4 Cut and
Fill Slopes
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Inc It
is important that a representative from our office observe the site grading operations to
evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered and to confirm that the exposed
soil rock conditions are similar to those encountered in our field exploration
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site or stockpiled in a
designated area to reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated
materials Within the limits of the slabs on grade clayey materials encountered
should be removed to provide the recommended non expansive fill thickness The
excavated clayey material should not be used as structural fill material
Existing utilities to be abandoned should be removed and the resulting excavation
should be properly backfilled with fill material placed in 6 to 8 inch level loose lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Utilities to be
abandoned in place under the proposed structure should be backfilled by pumping
lean concrete under low pressure
Soft and yielding areas encountered should be over excavated to expose firm
natural material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
well compacted engineered fill The excavated soft soils should not be used as fill
material
GEOLABS INC
W O 4117 10 Page 7
Finished subgrades and areas designated to receive fills should be scarified to a
depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to a minimum of 2 percent above the
optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close ail cracks or prepared
again as recommended above Saturation and subsequent yielding of the
exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require
over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted
engineered fill The excavated soft soils should not be used as structural fill
material
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
Fills and Backfills
Structural fill should consist of non expansive select granular fill material such
as crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material should be free
of vegetation and deleterious materials The material should be well graded
from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest dimension and
should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve
The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a
maximum swell of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1883 The on site clayey material is not suitable for structural fill
material but may be used in landscape and other non structural areas Imported
fill materials should be tested by Geolabs Inc for conformance with these
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use
GEOLABS INC

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to at
least 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance
with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers vibratory rollers
multiple wheel pneumatic tired rollers or other types of acceptable compaction
equipment
Because moisture conditioning and compaction of the clayey subgrade soils are
critical elements of earthwork observations and soil density tests should be
performed by Geolabs Inc during site grading to assist the contractor in obtaining
the required degree of compaction and the proper moisture content Where
compaction is less than required additional compactive effort should be applied
with adjustment of moisture content as necessary to obtain the specified
compaction It should be noted that the moisture requirement of subgrades
a minimum of 2 percent above the optimum moisture is an important requirement
for the on site clayey soils
Cut and Fill Slopes
Cut and fill slopes should be designed with a slope inclination of two horizontal to
one vertical 2H 1V or flatter Fills placed on slopes steeper than 5H 1V should be
keyed and benched into the existing slope to provide stability of the new fill against
sliding The filling operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in
GEOLABS INC
W O 4117 10 Page 9
level horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the above fill placement
recommendations
Fill slopes in excess of 5 feet in height should be constructed by overfilling and
cutting back to the design slope ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face
Track rolling of slopes in excess of 5 feet in height should not be accepted In the
event that over cutting of a slope occurs keying and benching requirements
should be implemented instead of backfilling the slope to the design grade with
sliver fills Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope
planting should be provided as soon as possible to reduce the potential for erosion
of the finished slopes
Foundations
Based on the generally competent subsoil conditions encountered at the site we
believe that shallow spread and or continuous footings may be used for support of the
proposed structures
An allowable bearing pressure of may be utilized for the design of
foundations bearing on properly re compacted on site silty clay material or properly
compacted imported fill material This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may
be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or
seismic forces
Footings should have a minimum width of 12 inches and should be embedded a
minimum of 24 inches below the existing grade For footings constructed near tops of
slopes or on sloping ground the footings should be embedded deep enough to provide a
minimum horizontal set back distance of 5 feet measured from the outside edge of the
footings to the slope face
GEOLABS INC
W O 4117 10 Page 10
We estimate that settlements for footings bearing on properly re compacted on site
silty clay material or properly compacted imported fill material as recommended herein to
be less than one 1 inch total Differential settlement is estimated to be less than
one half 1 inch
Footing bottoms should be moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction ASTM D 1557 91 If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the
bottom of footing excavations they should be over excavated to expose the underlying
firm materials The over excavation should be backfilled with select granular material
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction or the footing bottom may be
deepened to the underlying firm materials
Footings located adjacent to retaining walls should be embedded deep enough to
avoid surcharging the retaining wall foundations or the retaining walls should be
designed to resist the additional structural loads
lf foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the
utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to
avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to
reduce the potential for foundation settlement
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 psf
This assumes that the soil around the footings is relatively undisturbed or
well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the

Slabs On Grade
Our laboratory testing on the silty clay material indicate a moderate to high
expansion potential and a very high shrinkage potential Unless floor slabs are properly
designed and constructed there is a potential for future distress to the lightly loaded
slabs on grade resulting from shrinking and swelling of these soils due to changes in the
moisture content To reduce the potential for distress to slabs on grade resulting from
swelling and shrinking of the clayey materials we recommend that 18 inches of
non expansive select granular fill material be used below the interior and exterior
slabs on grade
In addition prior to placement of the select granular fill the subgrade should be
scarified to a depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the
optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
ASTM D 1557 91 Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade
we recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close all cracks or prepared
again as recommended above Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed
subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the
soft areas and replacement with well compacted engineered fill
Consideration should be given to construction of a 3 foot wide sidewalk adjacent to
and surrounding the entire building structure A sidewalk should help stabilize the
GEOLABS INC

subgrade soil moisture below the building structure and therefore reduce the potential for
swelling shrinking and softening of the subgrade soils
The area adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab and
graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce the potential of water ponding around
the foundation
We recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill be placed below
the slab The cushion fill consisting of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
or Basaltic Termite Barrier BTB should provide more uniform support of the slabs and
should serve as a capillary break To reduce the potential for future moisture infiltration
and subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture vapor barrier such
as a plastic membrane is recommended on top of the gravel or BTB cushion layer
Flexible floor coverings such as carpet or sheet vinyl should be considered since they
can better mask minor slab cracking It is also recommended that interior walls be
designed to incorporate some flexibility to accommodate small amount of possible ground
movements
cushion layer and the vapor barrier may be omitted For the design of structural slabs a
modulus of subgrade reaction of 200 pounds per square inch per inch of deflection pci
may be used for the compacted select granular fill subgrade material A minimum slab
thickness of 6 inches may be used for preliminary design purposes Provisions should be
made for proper load transfer across the slab joints that are subjected to vehicular traffic
The thickened edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at least
12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Exterior concrete walkways are currently planned for this project For these
concrete slabs we recommend a minimum 4 inch thick slab thickness The 4 inch

Utilities
It is anticipated that some utilities may be constructed for the proposed project
The methods and equipment to be used for excavation should be determined by the
contractor subject to practical limits and safety considerations The excavation should
comply with all applicable local state and federal safety requirements Trench shoring
design and installation should be the responsibility of the contractor Trench shoring and
bracing should conform to the appropriate health and safety requirements
In general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used
under the pipes The initial backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of
free draining backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging
the pipes from compaction of the backfill It is critical that a free draining granular material
be used to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to
provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes The use of on site soils as backfill
immediately around utility pipes is not recommended
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to the
finished subgrade should consist of approved on site soils or select granular material
The backfill material should be moisture conditioned to a minimum of 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness

for future ground subsidence The upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement
subgrade should be compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
Mechanical compaction equipment should be used to compact the materials at the project
site Water tamping jetting or ponding should not be used to compact the backfill
material
Drainage
The finished grades adjacent to the structures should be sloped to shed water
away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that
gutter systems be installed around the structures and that the discharge be diverted away
from the foundation and pavement areas We recommend that the discharge be at least
3 feet away from the building Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and
slabs should be avoided Planters next to foundations should also be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for water infiltration into the subsoils
Gravel strips adjacent to the building should be avoided
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry surface
water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during or after
construction Due to the fairly level grade of the area construction of drainage inlets to a
subsurface drainage system may be necessary to accommodate proper drainage of
surface water
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of
the concrete to reduce the potential for water infiltration Drainage swales should be

from walls and foundations

and therefore reduce the potential for swelling shrinking and softening of the subgrade

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
five borings designated as Boring Nos 1 to 5 to depths of about 15 9 to 21 5 feet below
the existing ground elevation at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with auger
equipment
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations ASTM D 2937
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
Three one inch ring compression collapse tests were performed on relatively
undisturbed and or remolded samples to evaluate the compression potential of
compacted fill under surcharge loads with no saturation and to evaluate the collapse
potential under surcharge loads with saturation Results of these compression collapse
tests are presented on Plate B 1
Seven one inch ring swell tests were performed on relatively undisturbed and or
remolded samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils under surcharge loads
These tests were run on air dried samples Results of these swell tests are presented on
Plates B 2 and B 3
Two one inch ring shrinkage tests were performed on remolded samples to
evaluate the shrinkage potential of the soils Results of these shrinkage tests are
presented on Plate B 4
