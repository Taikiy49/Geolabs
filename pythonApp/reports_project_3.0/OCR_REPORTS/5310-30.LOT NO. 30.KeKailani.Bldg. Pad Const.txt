Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii the largest island in the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an
area of approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of
five shield volcanoes which include the following Kohala which is long extinct
Mauna Kea which has had activity during recent geologic time Hualalai which last
erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa and Kilauea both of which are still active
The project site is situated on the northwestern flank of Hualalai Mountain which
makes up the west central portion of the island The lava formation encountered at the
project site appears to consist of a a type lava flows at the ground surface which spread
and ponded as they approached the ocean Pahoehoe lavas may be encountered at
greater depths below the ground surface A a lava typically is characterized by a porous
rough and irregular flow surface resembling a jagged accumulation of rock fragments
including cobbles and boulders Pahoehoe is characterized by a smooth rope like or
billowy surface and an internal structure of vesicular porous rock
Cavities are encountered commonly in basaltic lavas especially pahoehoe type
lava These cavities are formed when the lava is still in a molten state and they
represent both lava tubes intra flow cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow
cavities Lava tubes are formed when molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a
hollow tube like structure which may extend for a large distance longitudinally along the
flow Blisters and pockets are generally smaller in horizontal extent
Basalt is considered to be a relatively permeable rock and can transmit water quite
readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions In general water is transmitted
through the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities
and along clinker layers The permeability of the clinker and cavities is high therefore
they serve as the primary water transmission features
Subsurface Conditions
As mentioned previously the Ke Kailani project site is located in the Anaehoomalu
area in the District of South Kohala on the Island of Hawaii In general the original
topography of the project site prior to site grading activities consisted of irregular
or hummocky lava flow terrain with frequent topographic depressions separated by
adjacent higher relief mounds and low ridge features
Based on observations during the mass grading operations the project site
generally is underlain by a a lavas over medium hard to hard basalt formation It should
be noted that lava tubes cavities and blisters are encountered commonly in basaltic lava
flows in the vicinity of the project site In addition numerous voids and cavities were
observed at the project site during the mass grading operations It should be noted that
near surface lava tubes and cavities detected within the limits of generally were
collapsed and the loose materials from within were removed The resulting excavation

Discussion and Recommendations
Based on the grading plan for the project the original ground surface elevations
ranged from about 46 to 52 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at Lot No 30 Lot No 30
generally was graded to a finished pad elevation of about 58 5 feet MSL Mass grading
at Lot No 30 generally consisted of placement of up to about 12 5 feet of new fills to
achieve the design grades
Extensive subgrade preparation procedures were employed within the limits of
grading at the commencement of the mass grading operations for the project to reduce
the potential for collapse of near surface cavities and or voids within the lots The intent of
the subgrade preparation procedures is to assist in detecting and collapsing near surface
cavities and or voids contained in the porous near surface materials It should be noted
that the subgrade preparation procedures employed for this project are consistent with the
standard of practice for similar type projects and similar subsurface conditions along the
Kohala Coast on the Island of Hawaii
In general the subgrade preparation in fill areas consisted of ripping the original
ground to a depth of about 3 feet or until refusal using a ripper tooth of a large bulldozer to
assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or voids contained in the
porous near surface materials After the fill surfaces have been ripped the ground
generally was leveled with the use of a Caterpillar D8N D9 and D10R bulldozer working
the surface for about four passes to provide a relatively level surface After leveling of the
rugged surfaces with the bulldozer the fill subgrades generally were proof rolled with an
Ingersoll Rand 175 Pro Pac roller compactor 20 ton grid drum roller for about
eight passes to help detect and collapse near surface cavities and or voids
Fill materials consisting of some cobbles and boulders and structural fills were
placed in the fill areas of the project site Structural fills consisted of well graded granular
materials generally smaller than 6 inches in dimension In general the fill materials within
the top 1 foot of the lot in the fill areas within the pad consisted of structural fill materials
General fills consisting of materials generally smaller than about 12 to 18 inches in
dimension were placed below structural fills Boulders larger than 18 inches in diameter
generally were placed about 4 feet below the finished pad elevation
