Cracking of Garage Slab at Lot No 29

monitoring survey program for the existing cracks observed at the subject garage slab at the

Crack Monitoring Program
Two major cracks labelled as Cracks A and B were observed in the subject garage
slab at the approximate locations shown on the Crack Location Plan Plate 1 Two minor cracks
labelled as Cracks C and D were also observed at the approximate locations shown on the
same plan The tip positions of these cracks were marked on the concrete slabs to monitor their
growth or elongation during the monitoring period Their initial positions as recorded on
March 23rd were referenced as zero as shown on the Crack Monitoring Record Plate 2
Six monitoring lines were set up along selected locations of Cracks A and B to monitor
the widening of these cracks Their initial lengths as measured on March 23rd are shown on
Plate 4
Subsequent monitoring readings were taken ona periodic basis at approximately 2 week
intervals and the readings obtained are summarized on Plates 2 through 5

Findings and Recommendations
Based on the readings obtained from the crack monitoring program it appears that the
observed cracks Cracks A B and D have experienced some elongation during the initial
2 to 3 months but appeared to have stabilized for the 5 month period thereafter Crack C has
essentially remained unchanged during the entire monitoring period The readings also indicate
that the widths of the observed cracks did not appear to have widened during the monitoring
period
Based on the readings obtained from the slab elevation settlement monitoring program
it appears that the difference between the highest and lowest elevations surveyed at each control
point is less than one hundredth of a foot Our discussion with Imata Associates Inc indicated
that such elevation difference is within the normal instrumental and personal errors that can result
from the surveying operations It is also our belief that this magnitude of slab elevation difference
did not indicate any on going settlement or heaving of the garage slab

The other distress observed at the site do not appear to be causing structural damage
to the subject residence The hollow sound noted underneath the garage and sidewalk slabs
may be a result of the existence of potential voids between the concrete slab and the underlying
soils We recommend that core holes be drilled in the suspected areas for further evaluation
These voids if found to be present may be backfilled by pumping grout through the core holes
drilled through the subject slab
Based on our field observations and the above discussions it is our current opinion that
the distress observed at the subject garage slab do not appear to be caused by the
heaving shrinkage of subgrade soils We believe that there is a high possibility that the observed
cracks may be common shrinkage cracks encountered or developed during the hardening or
setting of the concrete Since these cracks did not appear to be expanding in length and width
for the past 5 months it is our opinion that the existing cracks does not appear to be a structural
distress However it may be desired to fix them for aesthetic purpose
