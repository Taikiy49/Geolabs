Dear Mr Uemura
The Ewa Beach Sewers Section 4 D project site is on the eastern and northern
sides of Fort Weaver Road within an existing residential subdivision in the Ewa Beach
area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Currently the existing subdivision is serviced by
cesspool systems It is proposed to replace the cesspool system with a new sewer
system
During our previous exploration at the site conducted in 2009 soft or loose subsoil
conditions were encountered in Boring No 14 in the corner of Section 4 1 D This soft or
loose material was encountered at a depth of about 12 5 feet below the existing ground
surface and extended to a depth of about 21 0 feet below the existing ground surface the
maximum depth explored in that boring All of our other borings for the Section 4 D
project encountered competent coral formation and coralline detritus
Therefore it was desired to obtain additional subsurface information in the vicinity
of Boring No 14 to determine the relative extent of the soft or loose material
Scope of Additional Exploration
To further explore the subsurface conditions in the vicinity of Boring No 14 we
have drilled and sampled two additional borings designated as Boring Nos 14A and
14B in the vicinity of our previous Boring No 14 Boring No 14A was drilled and

Subsurface Conditions
Both Boring Nos 14A and 14B encountered coralline deposits consisting of
medium hard to hard coral formation and medium dense to dense coralline detritus to
depths of 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface the maximum depth drilled It was
noted that the consistency of the detritus graded to loose to medium depth at about
20 feet below the ground surface However these subsoils are still considered to be
relatively competent and would provide adequate support of the proposed sewer lines
Groundwater was encountered at a depth of 12 0 feet below the ground surface
in Boring No 14A and at a depth of about 15 0 feet below the ground surface in
Boring No 14B at the time of our exploration These groundwater depths correspond to
elevations ranging of about 4 feet and 1 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL in
Boring Nos 14A and 14B respectively It should be noted that groundwater levels at the
project site may fluctuate depending on tides seasonal precipitation and other factors
Discussion and Recommendations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our two additional borings it
appears that the soft subsoil conditions encountered in Boring No 14 are mostly likely an
isolated occurrence However allowances should be provided in the contract documents
for the occurrence of soft soil conditions at sewer invert elevations or under manhole
structures
The stress distribution against the bottom of a pipe has a great effect upon the
load supporting capacity of the pipe Therefore pipe bedding is an important design
consideration For the majority of the Ewa Beach Sewers 4 D area a granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation or similar
free draining material has been recommended under the bottoms of pipes and manhole
structures located over medium stiff medium dense soil conditions This recommendation
remains in effect for areas where competent materials are encountered
If soft and or loose soils are encountered at the invert elevations of the sewer lines
and manhole structures we recommend providing an additional 24 inches of No 3B Fine
gravel wrapped in a filter fabric Mirafi 180N or equivalent below the new pipe and
manhole structures for more uniform support under such conditions The granular
bedding should extend beyond the sides of the pipe a minimum width of one fourth 1 4

ROCK FRACTURE CHARACTERISTICS
The following terms describe general fracture spacing of a rock
Massive Greater than 24 inches apart
Slightly Fractured 12 to 24 inches apart
Moderately Fractured 6 to 12 inches apart
Closely Fractured 3 to 6 inches apart
Severely Fractured Less than 3 inches apart
DEGREE OF WEATHERING
The following terms describe the chemical weathering of a rock
Unweathered Rock shows no sign of discoloration or loss of strength
Slightly Weathered Slight discoloration inwards from open fractures
Moderately Weathered Discoloration throughout and noticeably weakened though not able to break by hand
Highly Weathered Most minerals decomposed with some corestones present in residual soil mass Can be broken by hand
Extremely Weathered Saprolite Mineral residue completely decomposed to soil but fabric and structure preserved
