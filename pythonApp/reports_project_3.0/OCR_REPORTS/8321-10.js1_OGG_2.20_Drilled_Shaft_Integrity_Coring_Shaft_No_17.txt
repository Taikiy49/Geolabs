This technical memorandum presents the concrete integrity coring results for 
Drilled Shaft No. 17 at the Holdroom and Gate Improvements project at Kahului Airport 
on the Island of Maui, Hawaii. In accordance with the project specifications, concrete 
integrity coring on a minimum of one production drilled shaft at each of the three 
passenger boarding bridge (PBB) locations is required. In addition, the core hole should 
be a minimum of 3 inches in diameter, cored vertically, centrally located, and extend 
throughout the full depth of the drilled shaft. 
Drilled Shaft Installation 
Drilled Shaft No. 17 was installed at the proposed Gate No. 7 rotunda column for 
the PBB fronting the new Holdroom AB. The location of drilled shaft No. 17 is shown on 
the Integrity Coring Location Plan, Plate 1. Generally, we observed the installation of 
Drilled Shaft No. 17 from April 20 through 22, 2022. The following provides a summary of 
the drilled shaft installation details for your information and use. 
1. 
Date Drilling Completed = April 21, 2022 
2. 
Specified Shaft Diameter = 18 inches (OK) 
3. 
Actual Shaft Length = 15.4 feet (15 feet required) 
4. 
As-Built Tip Elevation = +24.9 feet MSL (+25.3 feet MSL or lower specified) 
5. 
Theoretical Concrete Volume = 1.8 Cubic Yards 
6. 
Actual Concrete Volume Used = 2.2 Cubic Yards 
Concrete Integrity Coring 
The concrete integrity coring was performed by Geolabs, Inc. using a Central Mine 
Equipment model CME-45 truck-mounted drill equipped with PQ coring tools. The PQ 
coring tool cuts a hole measuring 4.8 inches (Outside Diameter) and extracts a 3.3-inch 
diameter core (Inside Diameter). All drilling fluid generated during coring was contained 
within the pile cap excavation. 
After extraction of the concrete cores from the core barrel, measurements and 
photographs of the cores were taken. The cores were then wrapped in plastic wrap to 
preserve the natural moisture per ASTM C42. All coring depths were measured with

A detailed log of the concrete coring is presented on the Log of Coring, Plate 2. It 
should be noted that coring was terminated at 14 feet below the top of concrete 
(approximately 1.1 feet less than the total drilled shaft length) due to interference with the 
steel reinforcing cross-bracing present near the bottom of the drilled shaft. Photographs 
of the core samples recovered from the drilled shaft coring operation is presented on 
Plate 3. Fractures observed in the core photograph are mechanical breaks due to the 
drilling operations. 
In general, based on the concrete cores retrieved during the coring operation and 
our field observations during the concrete placement, we believe the concrete within 
Drilled Shaft No. 17 should be considered of “good” quality.
