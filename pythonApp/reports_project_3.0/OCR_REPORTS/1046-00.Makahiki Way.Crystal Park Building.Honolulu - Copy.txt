SITE DESCRIPTION
The site for the proposed building is located east of the
intersection of Waiola Street and Makahiki Way It is bor
dered on the west by Makahiki Way on the north by residen
tial structures on the south by a high rise condominium
and on the east by Moiliili Park formerly Honolulu Stadium
Access to the site is by Makahiki Way off of South King
Street
The site consists of three adjacent residential lots pres
ently occupied by 1 and 2 story residential structures with
large trees and shrubs In general the site is flat and
Slopes toward the south The ground elevation ranges from
about 5 to 7 feet above sea level
PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
The proposed building will consist of a basement level below
the entire site with a 6 story structure approximately 50
feet by 155 feet in plan
Maximum column loads of up to about 480 kips are anticipated

Geology
The project site is located on the Honolulu coastal plain
which consists of a thick series of interbedded marine and
nonmarine sediments capping the Koolau Basalt The near
subsurface structure at the site consists of a broad ele
vated coral reef about 30 feet thick partially covered by
nonmarine alluvium and directly overlying another extensive
nonmarine alluvium These conditions are considered to be
the result of a complex series of Pleistocene sea level
changes The present reef platform which stands approxi
mately 5 feet above sea level is apparently a wave cut ter
race formed by wave erosion of an older reef which origin
ally stood over 25 feet above sea level This widespread
reef structure is known locally as the Waimanalo Reef or
the plus 25 foot reef The Waimanalo Reef directly over
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1046 00
P 5
lies a basically nonmarine deposit of sediment which formed
during a period of time when the sea level was 40 feet lower
than at present
Although commonly referred to as coral reefs Hawaii s reef
structures are predominately calcareous algae skeletons which
Support a small percentage of coral growth The reef environ
ment is varied and complex but consists basically of zones
of growing coral and algae deposits of coarse coral debris
on the forereef and deposits of sand silts and clays on
the backreef or lagoon With changes in sea level these
basically calcareous deposits usually become cemented or
even recrystalized by the action of percolating fresh water
Most of the coral reef as typically seen along the Hawaiian
shores consists of beachrock cemented sand or beds of
cemented sand and coral algal gravel known as reefrock All
reefs also have channels of deeper water leading through them
Normally these channels are opposite the mouths of rivers
and are typically filled with nonmarine sediments

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Foundations
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain
by a coral reef formation at shallow depths At the proposed
basement level at about 8 feet deep dense coral was encoun
tered below most of the site except for a few pockets along
the perimeter of the building Boring 4 5
Boring Nos 1 and 5 encountered a layer of very stiff clayey
silt with gravel alluvium over medium to dense coral at
about 11 to 15 foot depths Boring Nos 2 and 4 encountered
medium dense sand and coral near the proposed basement level
Ideally spread footing foundations bearing on the very dense
coral formation could be used for the proposed structure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1046 00
P 7
However the field exploration indicates that the depth to
the underlying dense coral formation could vary and be as
much as 15 to 20 feet down below the existing grade In
order to avoid deep footing excavations below the water
level we recommend that the footing foundations be designed
to bear on the upper medium dense coral sand or very stiff
alluvium layer utilizing a lower allowable bearing value
of 4000 p s f Due to the variable nature of the coral for
mation at the site it does not appear practicable to accur
ately delineate which footings could be designed to bear on
very dense coral therefore being able to use a higher bear
ing value of 6000 p s f and which could bear on the medium
dense coral or very stiff alluvium Therefore we recommend
that all the footings be designed with an allowable bearing
value of 4000 p s f
The bearing values are for dead plus live loads and may be
increased one third when temporary lateral loads wind and
seismic are considered
The footings should be placed a minimum of 2 feet below the
adjacent floor level
Estimated settlements for the footings are on the order of
one half to three fourths inch total Differential settle
ments are anticipated to be about one half of the total
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1046 00
P 8
Although large cavities were not encountered in the borings
solution cavities may be present in coral formations There
fore to reduce the possibility of footing settlement due
to possible cavities in the dense coral formation a minimum
of one probe hole per column location should be drilled
below the building foundations The probe holes should have
a minimum diameter of 2 inches and should extend below the
bottom of footings a minimum of 10 feet The probe hole
should be properly grouted from the bottom of the drilled
hole using low pressures prior to basement excavation and
dewatering operations
If a cavity is suspected below a footing additional probe
holes should be made to delineate the horizontal extent of
the cavity The probing and grouting operations should be
monitored by the soils engineer to see that these recommen
dations are carried out properly
The footing excavations should be properly dewatered allow
ing the excavations to proceed in a dry condition in order
to reduce the disturbance and subsequent softening of the
foundation sugrade
The footing excavations should be observed by a soils engin
eer to see that adequate bearing material is encountered

Excavation
Excavations up to about 8 feet will be required for the
proposed basement level and up to about 15 feet for the
elevator pit
Some relatively hard ripping may be required in the dense
coral formation The upper soil layers should be easy to
excavate
Due to the proximity of the surrounding existing structures
sheeting and bracing is recommended for the excavation in
the upper soil layer in order to reduce settlement and dam
age to the adjacent structures
Heavy equipment and stockpiles should be kept away from the
tops of the excavation
Dewatering
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at about 4 to 7
foot depths
Dewatering will be required for the foundation excavations
and elevator pit The quantity of water pumped through coral
formations varies considerably and depends on the character
istics of the coral structure
For shallow excavations perimeter drains can be used to
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1046 00
P 10
lower the water level For deeper excavations wellpoint
or deep wells are recommended
The dewatering operations should be performed without pump
ing out the soil fines creating a quick condition or
softening the bottom of the excavation
Site Grading
Minor fill is planned for the project
Select granular material such as the on site coral should
be used for fill and footing backfill material The fill
and backfill should be placed in 8 inch maximum horizontal
loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum
dry density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
course generally less than 3 4 inch and greater than 1 4
inch in size
Basement and Retaining Walls
Basement and retaining walls up to 8 feet high are planned
for the proposed structure
Well graded granular material such as base course rock or
on site coral sand should be used as backfill material behind
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1046 00
the walls Impervious material should be used for the top
2 feet of the backfill
The recommendations given under foundations should be fol
lowed for the design of the wall foundations
The following lateral earth pressures are recommended for
the wall design
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Walls restrained at the top 50 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
A friction factor of 0 5 can be utilized to determine the
sliding resistance of the wall foundation on the coral for
mation
The basement walls and slabs should be waterproofed
Existing Cesspools
Existing cesspools may be located on the site Where exist
ing cesspools are located below or adjacent to footings and
slabs the sludge should be removed and the cesspool filled
with compacted granular material or concrete The cesspool
walls within 5 feet of the finish grade should be removed

The plans for foundations site grading and utility construc
tion should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review

It is recommended that the site filling footing excavations

ing construction to see that adequate bearing has been obtained
and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the

Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the
