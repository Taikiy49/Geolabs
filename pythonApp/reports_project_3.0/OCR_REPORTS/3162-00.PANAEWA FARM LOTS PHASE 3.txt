EARTHWORK OBSERVATION AND TESTING REPORT
PANAEWA FARM LOTS PHASE 3
PANAEWA WAIAKEA SOUTH HILO HAWAII

This report summarizes the results of our geotechnical engineering services
performed during the roadway and utility construction at the above referenced project
from September 1993 through February 1995 The subject site is located 1 miles east
of Kanoelehua Avenue State of Hawaii Highway No 11 off the junction of Auwae
Road and Mamaki Street in the District of South Hilo on the island of Hawaii
The project consists of the construction of two adjoining roadways namely
Mamaki Street Extension and Road A as shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 The
Mamaki Street Extension begins near the intersection with Auwae Road at
and extends east to about Road A extends from to
in a north south direction
The roadway and utility construction were performed by Willocks Construction
Corporation and generally consisted of cuts on the order of about 6 feet and fills on the
order of 15 feet Fill materials generally consist of rock boulders and or cobbles
generated from the excavation Therefore conventional compaction testing was
generally not practicable Instead visual observations and the number of passes
needed to achieve the desired level of compaction was evaluated and used as the field
criterion for adequate compaction During the fill operation materials were generally
placed in near level lifts brought to near optimum moisture content and compacted
using heavy construction equipment such as a Caterpillar DON or equivalent

During the grading work various imported soils were tested prior to their use in
the field The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established
in our laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified
Proctor Laboratory tests were also performed to determine the maximum specific
gravity of the asphalt concrete in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2041
Rice Method A summary of these test results are presented in the attachments of
this report
Periodic field density tests were taken during the roadway preparation
operations in accordance with the ASTM Test Designation D 2922 Nuclear Gauge
Method The results of the compaction tests are summarized in the attachments of
this report In general the subbase and base course layers were tested to have at
least 95 percent relative compaction For the asphalt concrete the pavement was
generally compacted to a minimum of 91 percent of its theoretical maximum specific
gravity
Field density test locations were selected to confirm field observations as
variations in construction procedures and soil conditions were noted Test locations
and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until
the specified minimum compaction were obtained
Our services consisted of periodic field observations of the contractor s backfill
placement and compaction procedures during the above roadway and_ utility
constructions Field observations made by our field technician during the utility backfill
operation for the project indicated that this work was performed in general accordance
with the project plans and specifications Based on the field density test results the
subbase base course and asphalt concrete were compacted in general conformance
with the project specifications and the County of Hawaii Standard Specifications for
Public Work Construction
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some work have occurred
without our observation since our services were on a periodic basis However we
believe that the level of observation and testing services provided is reasonable in
reducing the geotechnical risks to the project It should also be recognized that there
