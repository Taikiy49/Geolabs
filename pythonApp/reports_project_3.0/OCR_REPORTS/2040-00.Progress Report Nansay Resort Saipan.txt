Based on the preliminary plans supplied to us site grading for the
project consists of extensive fills up to about 25 feet in the
southeastern portion and minor cuts in the western portion of the site A
marshy area is located in the southern portion of the site The
approximate location of this marsh area is Shown on the Site Plan Plate
2 Special site preparation and stabilization procedures are recommended
for filling the marshy area Additionally several inches of ground
settlements may occur due to compression of the soft mud caused by the
weight of the new fill

Clearing and grubbing should consist of removing vegetation and

The existing surface soil below the dense rootmat in the marshy
area is soft and wet and does not have sufficient shear strength
to support heavy construction equipment Therefore after
clearing and grubbing of a section of the existing ground a
working platform or drainage blanket should be established prior
to the major site filling operation The working platform should
consist of a combination of a synthetic filter fabric overlain by
a gravel layer The required thickness of the gravel layer will
be affected by the water level conditions at the time of
placement Under normal conditions with the water level at or
below the ground surface an 18 inch thickness is anticipated
The gravel should consist of material generally between
one fourth and three fourths of an inch in size
We believe the filter fabric will reduce the required gravel
thickness and the gradation requirements of the drainage blanket
since it will act as a filter medium to prevent the mud from
contaminating the granular blanket material It also provides
tensile reinforcement to the gravel layer and permits use of
heavy equipment on a minimum thickness of compacted aggregate

Remaining Area
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of trees vegetation debris rubbish and
other deleterious materials These materials should be removed
and disposed of properly off site Top soil should be segregated
and stockpiled for future landscaping uses if appropriate
The fill areas should then be scarified to a depth of six 6
inches moisture conditioned and recompacted to 90 of ASTM
D 1557 78 maximum dry density

should not contain rocks or lumps in excess of eight 8 inches in
diameter Off site borrow should be tested and approved by the

Fill Placement and Compaction
The fill should be placed in 12 inch thick loose level lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density Each
layer should be spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain uniformity of
the material and water content
Additional fill material should not be placed on any fill layer which
has not been properly compacted
Surcharge
The use of surcharge loads i e loads from a temporary stockpile of
earth fill in excess of that applied by the permanent fill can accelerate
the settlement and reduce long term settlement We recommend that the
portions of the proposed building over the marsh area be surcharged with a
minimum 5 feet of fill for about one month
In areas where deep fills are planned e g Station Line 4 00 at the
the lobby area the fill should extend full depth into the building area
for a distance approximately equal to the fill thickness and then decrease
on a 2H 1V slope to the 5 foot minimun A typical section for
fill surcharge in the marshy area is shown on Plate 4
Settlement Monitoring
We recommend that an adequate number of settlement gauges be
installed both within and outside the marsh area both prior to filling
and after completion of filling to monitor the actual amount of fill
subsidence fill settlement rates and to determine when the construction
of buildings and utilities can commence
Settlement gauges could consist of conventional settlement platforms
see Plate 5 which are optically monitored by the surveyor s level
readings Since the gauges are highly susceptible to damage by the
contractor s equipment during filling operation a greater number of
gauges should be provided

Building Foundations
Based on the two 2 borings the top 20 feet of the subsoils at the
site consist of loose fine to medium grained sands Since the project
site is in a generally high seismic zone the underlying loose sandy layer
is believed to be highly susceptible to liquefaction which could cause
complete loss of strength during an earthquake loading resulting in large
ground settlements
Although there are methods to reduce the liquefaction potential by
densification of the sand these procedures are somewhat complicated and
very costly
For these reasons pile foundations are recommended for all of the
buildings
It is our opinion that with the use of pile foundations the
buildings would probably remain intact even if liquefaction occurs
however there may be disruption to the utility lines servicing the
building Therefore repair and maintenance may be required after
earthquake shaking
Based on the present information piles penetrating through the upper
fill crust loose sand and stiff clay layers and extending into the
severely fractured and weathered limestone formation may be used The
piles may develop its Capacity partially from the side friction and
partially from end bearing
For sixteen and one half inch octagonal concrete piles with a total
pile length of 50 feet an allowable vertical pile capacity of 50 tons may
be used The capacity may be increased if our exploration encounters an
extensive limestone formation

Retaining Walls
Retaining and basement walls are planned for the proposed hotel
complex Well graded granular material such as base course rock sand or
equivalent should be used as backfill material directly behind the
retaining walls This would minimize the compactive effort required for
the backfill and reduce the build up of additional lateral pressures on
the walls The granular backfill should be placed in the area within a
line drawn at a 45 degree angle from the heel of the retaining wall
structure
We recommend that the walls should be designed to resist the
following lateral earth pressures
Unrestrained at top ky 35 p c f equivalent
active condition fluid pressure
Restrained at top ky 50 p c f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
Additional loads due to surcharge or traffic should be included where
applicable
The above recommendation does not include additional lateral
pressures during earthquake in accordance to the Uniform Building Code
However if alternative design which includes the influence of earthquake
is require then we would recommend that the Mononobe Okabe analysis be
used The alternative design lateral earth pressures under seismic
conditions are as follows
Unrestrained at top ky 55 p c f equivalent
active condition fluid pressure
Restrained at top ky 75 p c f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
A coefficient of friction of 0 4 between the wall and the backfill
material can be used

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earth work and foundation recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility
for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during site grading and
construction phases of the foundation work This is to observe compliance
with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow
design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this progress report
are based in part upon the two 2 borings and three 3 hand auger probes
obtained from the field exploration Variations of conditions between the
field data points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations
may not become evident until further exploration and construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
