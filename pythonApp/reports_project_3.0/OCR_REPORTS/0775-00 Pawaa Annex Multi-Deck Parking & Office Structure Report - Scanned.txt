INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to evaluate the sub
surface conditions at the site and to provide recommendations
for the design and construction of the foundations of the
proposed parking structure
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The site for the proposed structure is located between
Beretania and Young Streets adjacent to the existing
Pawaa Annex Building
The site is bordered by existing one story buildings on
the east Most of the buildings are one story structures
constructed at grade however one of the adjacent buildings
along Young Street has a full basement
The area is presently paved with asphaltic concrete and is
being used as a parking lot
The site slopes down toward Young Street south at about
2 percent gradients
The elevation at the site generally varies from about 28
feet near Beretania Street to 22 feet near Young Street
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed structure will be a 5 story concrete parking
and office structure A basement level is planned for the
western half of the structure The basement level will
vary from 0 feet at Young Street to about 10 feet near
Beretania Street
Column loads of 700 and 1400 kips are anticipated

W P 3
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located over a large alluvial fan created
by weathered igneous rock being washed down from the upper
valleys
During the Tantalus eruptions about 5000 years ago cinders
and ash were deposited over the area Subsequently these
deposits were washed down and mixed with additional alluvial
deposits to form the silty sandy subsoils beneath the site
subsurface Exploration
Eight borings were drilled at the site to depths of about
35 to 55 feet below the existing ground surface to explore
the subsurface conditions and to obtain samples for labora
tory testing The approximate locations of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Detailed information on the field exploration and laboratory
testing is covered in the appendix along with the boring logs
and laboratory test results
Based on the borings the site appears to be underlain by
alluvial deposits consisting of stiff clayey silts sandy
silts and silty fine sand Near the water level at about
20 to 25 feet depths the subsoils grade to a medium to
coarse cinder sand of loose to medium density

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed fairly competent bearing
soils at the site Shallow foundations can be considered
for the proposed structure However due to the relatively
high column loads and varying load conditions some settle
ments and differential settlements are anticipated
Foundations
For the proposed structure spread or continuous footing
foundations are recommended An allowable bearing value
up to 4000 p s f can be used for footings resting on the
stiff natural ground The allowable bearing value may be
increased up to one third for temporary loading conditions
such as wind toads
In the basement area the footings should extend a minimum
of 2 feet below the finish grades Where basements are not
utilized the footings should extend a minimum of 4 feet
below the existing ground surface Where existing structures
are located adjacent to the proposed building the new foot
ings should extend down to the level of the existing building
foundations
The bottom of the footing excavations should be compacted
to 95 of its maximum density established by ASTM Test
Method D 1557
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 5
Footings located next to utility trenches should extend
to the bottom of the trench
Due to the relatively heavy column loads and varying load
conditions varying basement depths some settlements and
differential settlements are anticipated For the above
recommendations estimated settlements are 2 to 3 inches
total and up to 1 to 1 inches differential Due to the
silty sandy composition of the subsoils most of the settle
ments should occur during construction of the structure
Earthwork Compaction Requirements
Cuts up to 10 feet for the basement level are planned Some
minor filling may be required
The site should be cleared of all vegetation debris and old
foundations and sidewalks prior to filling
Select on site material should be used for fill and footing
backfill material The fill and backfill should be placed
in horizontal loose lifts of about 8 inches and compacted
to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density established
by ASTM Test Method D 1557
Slab On Grade
Due to the anticipated settlements the ground floor slab
should not be poured until the superstructure is completed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 6
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base
course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density The
subgrade should be kept moist and recompacted prior to
placing the base course
Where the slab is located below the outside ground surface
a subdrainage system around the perimeter will help to pre
vent seepage water
Retaining and Basement Walls
Retaining and basement walls up to 10 feet high runing
the length of the building are planned for the western half
of the structure
Well graded granular material such as base course rock
should be used as backfill material behind the walls
Impervious material should be used for the top 2 feet
of the backfill
The recommendations given under foundations should be
followed for the design of the wall foundations
For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 45 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Walls restrained at the top 60 p c f equivalent
fluid pressure
Additional load due to traffic or surcharge
should be included where applicable
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 7
A friction factor of 0 45 can be utilized to determine
the sliding resistance of the wall foundation Addi
tionally a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f may
be used
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent
the build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
Due to the different loading conditions a joint or
separation should be placed between retaining and base
ment walls
The plans for foundations site grading and utility con
struction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and comments prior to construction to see that the
intent of our recommendations is included in the project
plans and specifications
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by a soils engineer during construc
tion to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the
site

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored
by drilling 8 borings to depths of 35 to 55 feet below the
existing ground surface at the locations shown on the Site
Plan Plate 1
The soils encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our en
gineer who continuously monitored the drilling operations
The classifications were reviewed by visual inspection and
testing in the laboratory The soils were classified in
general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the soils is presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 13
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving
split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30
inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
depths
