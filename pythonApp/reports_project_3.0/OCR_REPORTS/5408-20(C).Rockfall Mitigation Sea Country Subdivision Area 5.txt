PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Sea Country Subdivision Area 5 project site is located in the
Lualualei Waianae area on the western side of the Island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1 The residential subdivision development consists of
87 new housing units located on the valley floor adjacent to a hillside containing potential
rockfall hazards As a result of a hillslope assessment and rockfall hazard evaluation
which was required by the City and County of Honolulu rockfall protection improvements
have been constructed at the project site to reduce the potential for rockfall activity to
affect the subdivision development
In general the rockfall mitigation project consisted of the construction of a rockfall
impact barrier system across the hillslope located on the upslope side of the existing
concrete drainage ditch The approximate location of the rockfall barrier construction is
shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2
This report addresses our field observations for the construction of the rockfall
barrier system and provides a summary of the ground anchor proof testing data which
was performed by the contractor at the project site

ROCKFALL MITIGATION CONSTRUCTION
Between and Geolabs Inc observed the
rockfall mitigation work on a part time basis for the Sea Country Subdivision Area 5
project Janod Contractors Entrepreneur performed the rockfall mitigation contract work
The major work tasks observed by Geolabs Inc included the following activities
Construction of the barrier column footings
Concrete placement for the column foundations
Construction of the wire rope ground lateral and upslope restraining anchors
Installation of the rockfall barrier support ropes nets and wire mesh
Proof testing of the rockfall barrier ground anchors
Based on our observations the completed rockfall barrier system located at the
project site consists of approximately 700 lineal feet of Type Barrier 74 foot ton
capacity approximately 225 lineal feet of Type Il Barrier 184 foot ton capacity and
approximately 375 lineal feet of Type III Barrier 295 foot ton capacity Therefore the
overall length of the completed rockfall barrier system is approximately 1 300 lineal feet as
shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2
As mentioned previously our services during construction consisted of part time field
observation of the contractor s work performed at the project site However Geolabs Inc
observed the ground anchor proof testing which was performed by Janod Contractors
Entrepreneur on a full time basis The approximate locations of the 47 wire rope ground
constructed at the project site are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2 Thirty five
ground anchors were selected for proof testing in general accordance with the contract
documents The 35 anchors tested include 100 percent of the 22 lateral anchors and
50 percent of the 25 upslope restraining anchors
The results of the anchor proof testing observed by our field representatives are
summarized and presented on Plates 3 through 79 It should be noted that the
requirement for acceptable anchor performance as indicated by the proof testing results
Therefore the contractor using new adjacent anchor installations which were also proof
tested replaced the five anchors The five replacement anchors were tested and found to
have acceptable anchor performance
SUMMARY
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the rockfall mitigation work for the Sea Country
Subdivision Area 5 project observed by Geolabs Inc in the period covered by this report

Geolabs Inc assumes no responsibility for alterations made to slopes or finished
grades on the project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
knowledge and written acknowledgment In addition Geolabs Inc assumes no
responsibility for the accumulation of various earth deposits and debris which may be
intercepted and retained by the rockfall barrier system
Rockfall activity may be generally classified as a natural event which cannot be
calculated or predicted Therefore it is impossible for Geolabs Inc to guarantee absolute
safety for persons and property with scientific engineering methods Thus it is imperative
to maintain and service the rockfall barrier system on a regular appropriate basis
Furthermore the degree of rockfall protection may be diminished by rockfall events that
exceed the absorption capacity of the system as calculated to good engineering practice
failure to use original parts or by environmental corrosion
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site therefore some work may have occurred
without our observation However we believe that the level of construction observation
services provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project It should
