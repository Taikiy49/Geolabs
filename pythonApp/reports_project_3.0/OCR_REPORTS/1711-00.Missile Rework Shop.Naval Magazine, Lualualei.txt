SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated to the west of G Avenue and in the
vicinity of the existing Buildings 489 490 and 501 within the Naval
Ammunition Depot which is located along the southern shores of the West
Loch branch of Pearl Harbor Hawaii
The topography at the site is generally flat with a slight gradient
down towards the south
The area is presently overgrown with grass A concrete slab was
noted around portions of Building 489 within the site
The existing ground elevations vary from about 11 to 15 feet
above Mean Sea Level
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
On the island of Oahu the main shield building activity ended in
the late Pliocene about 25 million years ago Since then the island has
subsided up to 1200 feet As Oahu sank coral reef grew around the
shorelines keeping pace with the subsidence
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1711 00 Page 4
DK No 16
The site is underlain at a depth of about 13 feet by coral reef
which indicates that this area was once in a shallow marine environment
During the pleistocene fluctuations of sea level were caused by
accumulation and recession of the polar glaciers
Contemporaneous with these sea level fluctuations were post
erosional pyroclastic eruptions from the Salt Lake and Makalapa vents
The volcanic ash consolidated to form tuff locally called mudrock over
the coral formation
In more recent times as the sea level rose again to the Waimanalo
High 25 feet stand of the sea the mudrock was overlain by coral
detritus which was later covered locally by man made fills
Subsoil Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
twelve 12 borings to depths of 4 9 to 21 0 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plans
Plates 2 and 3 Soil samples obtained from the borings were selectively
tested in our laboratory to further examine their geotechnical engineering
characteristics Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and
laboratory test results are presented in the appendices
The borings encountered a surface layer generally less than 5 feet
thick of dense to hard silty coral sand and gravel over a layer of hard
mudrock tuff about 4 5 to 7 5 feet thick The mudrock layer was
generally weathered and occasionally found to be decomposed completely to
clayey silt and dense silty sand in some of the borings
The mudrock was underlain by a generally hard coral formation
with some small cavities or soft spots

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
In general our borings at the proposed site encountered competent
subsoils at shallow depths which can provide adequate foundation support
for the proposed structures and pavements
Foundation Design
Spread or continuous footing foundation bearing on the dense in situ
coral sand and gravel may be used to support the proposed buildings
An allowable bearing value of up to 4000 p s f may be used for
footings bearing on the dense in situ soil The bearing value is for
total dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for
temporary wind or seismic loads The allowable bearing value contains a
factor of safety of about 3 Under very short term blasting overpressure
loads a much lower factor of safety is often used in the design
Considering the probability of occurrence short loading time and assuming
that some damage is tolerable the allowable bearing value may be
increased up to 2 5 times for blast overpressure design This would
provide a factor of safety against bearing failure of about 1 2
The footings should be embedded a minimm of 24 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade and bear on the dense in situ material
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1711 00 Page 6
Dk No 16
Any loose pockets encountered at the bottom of the footing
excavation should be recompacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry
density established by ASTM D 1557 78 If a footing is situated adjacent
to a utility trench the trench backfill must be well compacted to prevent
potential footing settlement due to settlement of the loose trench
backfill
Minimal footing settlements are anticipated for the design loads if
the above recommendations are followed
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by frictional resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundations and the bearing soil Resistance due to passive
earth pressure may be calculated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 350
p s f This assumes that the soil around the foundation is well
compacted The frictional resistance may be calculated by using a
coefficient of friction of 0 4 between the foundation concrete and coral
sand The above resistance values are maximum values and do not contain a
factor of safety

Fill Material and Placement
The on site coral sandy and gravelly soils are suitable for use as
both earth cover to the proposed missile magazine and fill material
for the proposed buildings If required new imported fill material
should be non expansive soil free of organic matter and form a
dense stable soil mass when compacted to the required density
Generally the particle size should be less than 3 inches in size
The pavement designs presented in the following sections of this
report were based on a subgrade CBR value of 17 and therefore the
material placed within the top 3 feet of the fill under the
pavements should have a soaked CBR of at least 17 per ASTM
D 1883 73 when compacted to 95 of the maximum densities determined
according to ASTM D 1557 78 The total expansion of the sample
during the soaking for the CBR test should be less than 1
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 of
its maximum dry density

Field Exploration

drilling 12 borings at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan

Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 12

sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
sample depths Core samples were obtained by using 2 3 inch I D Triefus
