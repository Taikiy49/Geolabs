Geotechnical Engineering Investigation
FY86 Special Project C74 85
Public Works Building
Pacific Missile Range Facility
Barking Sands Kauai Hawaii
This report presents the results of our geotechnical investigation
performed for the proposed Public Works Building at the Pacific Missile
Range Facility at Barking Sands on the island of Kauai Hawaii The
general location of the project site and vicinity are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate l
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The Public Works Building includes an air conditioned
pre engineered metal building approximately 3 000 square feet in area
Total load on each column is approximately 20 kips Minimal site grading
is anticipated for the construction of the building
W O Page 2
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation was to evaluate the subsurface
soil conditions at the proposed site to provide soil data for the design
of the foundation for the proposed building and to determine the bearing
and settlement characteristics of the underlying soil
The scope of our investigation which was performed in general
accordance with our proposal of included drilling and
sampling two 2 borings laboratory soil testing engineering analysis
utilizing the field and laboratory data and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration laboratory testing
and boring logs are presented in the appendices The approximate
location of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
GENERAL SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed site is located to the south of Olo Kamoi Road in the
adjacent lot located to the north of Building 288 at the Pacific Missile
Range Facility at Barking Sands on the island of Kauai Hawaii

The 3 000 square foot site is an open area currently used as a
temporary parking lot for heavy construction equipments Ground level at
the site is generally flat However the ground surface elevation of the
site is not available at this time
Ground cover consists primarily of deteriorating asphaltic concrete
pavement This condition may be due to the heavy construction equipment
traffic on the site
Subsoil Condition
Two borings were drilled to depth of 20 feet below the ground
surface Both borings encountered a top soil layer of loose to medium
dense dark brown silty fine beach sand underlain by medium dense light
brown slightly cemented beach sand with fine to medium grain size at a
depth of about 2 5 feet At a depth of about 7 feet an approximately
6 foot thick hard white tan coral layer was noted and it was further
underlain by medium to very dense brown beach sand with medium grain
size
Groundwater was encountered at 7 to 8 feet below ground surface at
the time of our field exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
Foundation
Based on the anticipated soil conditions and the light column load
we believe that shallow spread or continuous wall footing bearing on the
recompacted in situ loose to medium dense sand may be used
For footings bearing on sand we recommend they should be placed a
minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Uplift
wind blast or other lateral loading condition may further increase the
embedment of the footing foundation
An allowable bearing capacity of 2 000 p s f may be used for the
foundation design The bearing capacity value is for total dead plus
live load only and may be increased by one third for short duration wind
loading Settlements under the design loading are anticipated to be on
the order of less than one half of an inch total and one fourth of an
inch differential
Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed building can be
obtained by passive soil pressure and frictional resistance A
frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the bottom of the
footing and the dense soil In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of
300 p s f per foot of depth may be used for the buried footing structure
to determine the passive soil resistance

Site Grading
Due to the relatively flat ground minimal cut and or fill is
anticipated for the site development The finished grade level is not
available at this stage
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing pavements
debris and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of
properly off site in order to prevent contamination of the excavated
material
The areas which are to receive foundation footing should be kept
moist and tamped prior to placement of the footing reinforcement steel if
they are loosened during the excavation The areas which are to receive
pavement should be scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches below the
cleared ground surface moisture conditioned and recompacted to 95 ASTM
D 1557 78 maximum density Any loose or soft spots encountered at the
footing locations or subgrade level should be recompacted if it is sandy
or removed if it is clayey or organic and replaced with select on site
sandy soil or with other suitable imported material compacted to the
above mentioned standard
GEOLABS HAWAII
After clearing and grubbing the area to receive fill should be
scarified at least 6 inches below the cleared grade moisture conditioned
and recompacted to 95 of its maximum density as established by ASTM
D 1557 78 test method Any loose spots encountered should be removed and
replaced with select on site soil or with suitable imported material
compacted to the above mentioned standard
The on site clean sandy soil may be used as a source of trench
backfill and fill material If required new imported fill material
should consist generally of non expansive select granular materials less
than 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
Structural fill should be placed in 8 inch thick compacted
horizontal lifts and compacted to 95 ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density

Slab On Grade
It is anticipated that concrete slab on grade will be used to
support the proposed metal building We believe that the slab on grade
may be designed according to the same recommendations used for Rigid
Pavement shown above under the Pavement Section of the soil report We
believe the slab thickened edges should be embedded at least eighteen
18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundations should be
forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to
construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading subgrade preparation and
footing excavations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling two
2 borings to depth of 20 feet below the existing ground surface A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Log of Borings Plates A l and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Log of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
