INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical engineering evaluation performed
for the proposed residence at 7220 Pulehu Street in the Hawaii Kai district on the island
of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is Our understanding that the proposed project will consist of a two story single
family residence
It is our understanding that the existing concrete driveway and garage slab will be
utilized as the future driveway and garage of the proposed residence
A new concrete slab on grade is planned for the house area of the residence
Building foundation loadings are anticipated to be moderate to light and typical for the
proposed structure
Minimal site grading is anticipated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this study was to provide recommendations for the building
foundation of the subject house The recommendations are based on the soil information
obtained from borings drilled for this exploration
Our work consisted of drilling three 3 borings The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Soil sampling was performed by one of our engineers The results of our field
exploration and laboratory tests which were utilized in the engineering analysis to
formulate our recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the Hawaii Kai district of Honolulu This approximately
rectangular shaped 6 594 square foot lot is located at 7220 Pulehu Street near the
intersection with Aipo Street
The existing lot is generally flat and is bounded by existing perimeter walls along
the southern and western property lines There is an existing fence along the eastern
property line
At the time of the exploration the original house was demolished and the site was
cleared
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling three 3 borings B 1 through B 3
to depths of 6 5 and 11 5 feet The borings were drilled with a truck mounted drilling rig
The locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings disclosed that the site is underlain by very stiff clayey silt
with fine sand to the depths of the borings This clayey silt also graded with gravel and
cinder sand at depths below the upper 5 feet
Groundwater was not encountered in our borings at the time of the drilling
exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by light orange to brown
clayey silt with fine sand The laboratory test results and previous experience indicate that
this material has low expansion potential
Foundation
A spread or continuous footing foundation may be used to support the proposed
structure
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for footings bearing on the
in situ soil A minimum footing embedment of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
is also recommended
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to the bottom of the
utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundation This is to avoid
ponding of surface run off water that may affect the swelling potential and bearing
capacity of the soils around the foundation
Slab On Grade
In order to reduce the potential expansive effects of the soils we recommend that
the exposed subgrade consisting of clayey silt with fine sand below the slab should be
moistened by water sprinkling and then maintained in a moist condition prior to the slab
concrete placement
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2416 00 Page 5
A 4 inch layer of 3 Fine cushion fill should be provided below the slab In order
to protect against moisture infiltration a vapor barrier may be incorporated below the
slab
The thickened edge slab footings can bear on the in situ soil The thickened edges
should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
Lot Grading
Minimal site grading is anticipated for the proposed residence
The fills and backfills should consist of non expansive soil such as select borrow
crusher waste or coral All imported fill materials should be tested and approved by the
soils engineer prior to being used in the site
Prior to construction the area to receive fill should be cleared of vegetation and
debris
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8 inches in compacted
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface run off away from all
foundations
Drainage
The finished lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should be shaped
to shed water away from foundations and to avoid ponding condition Also it is advised
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2416 00 Page 6
that a gutter system be installed around the residence and that gutter water be diverted
far away from the house foundation and driveway Excessive landscape watering near
foundations should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundation should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and
drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
recommended foundations since ponded water could cause excessive subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavation should
be properly backfilled tight against the slab footing immediately after setting of the
concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slab foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineering for review and written comments prior to construction This
review is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and_ foundation
recommendations given herein If this review is not made the geotechnical engineer
cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and construction of the

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of conditions between the field data
points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The location of the field borings were approximately determined by taping from
structures indicated on the plans provided The physical location of the field data points
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field data points
depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a
gradual transition The strata lines shown on profiles or cross sections are based upon

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling three 8 borings
ranging between 6 5 to 11 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig using 4 inch diameter
continuous flight auger was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered were classified by visual and textural examination in the field
by one of our engineers who continuously monitored the drilling operations The
classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory
A graphic presentation of the materials encountered in the borings are presented in the
Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 3 All soils were classified in accordance with the
Unified Soil Classification System
In the borings soil samples were obtained by driving a 2 inch standard split spoon
The blow counts used to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring
Logs at the appropriate sample depths
