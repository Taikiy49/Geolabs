In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to
provide foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The
study consisted of drilling two shallow borings laboratory testing of
the swelling potential of the soils and reviewing of the subdivision
site grading and preliminary house foundation plans This report
summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a one story wooden structure approxi
mately 46 by 76 feet in plan dimensions with a 2 car garage Concrete
on grade slab is planned for the driveway and garage area with
post and beam construction for the entire house foundation

MU retaining walls are anticipated to provide grade separation
between the garage and the remainder of the house
The proposed layout of the house and the garage is shown on the Site
Plan Plate 1
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the southern side of Ka ahele Street in
the Royal Summit Phase 1 Subdivision in Aiea Oahu Hawaii
The lot has two generally level pads with a transitional slope
measuring about 5 1 H V between them The lower pad is located at
the rear of the site There is an existing concrete lined drainage
ditch located at the rear of the site and running parallel to the
southern boundary
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
Two borings were drilled within the proposed building location to
depths ranging from 5 to 5 5 feet below the existing grade at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Both borings encountered about 4 feet of reddish brown clayey silt
It grades to silty clay in Boring 1 at about 4 feet and mottled silty
soil in Boring 2 at about 3 5 feet to the full depth of both borings

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Laboratory test further disclosed that the existing near surface soils
are quite dry and could exhibit moderate to high swelling potential if
allowed to dry out excessively in the beginning and then wetted
Therefore some special foundation design and subgrade preparation are
recommended in order to provide suitable foundation support for the
proposed residence
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used on
the residual soil
Post footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches
below the adjacent finished grade Continuous wall footings should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent finished grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down an
additional amount to obtain a 5 foot minimum horizontal set back
distance measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope
face This additional embedment depth is required to help resist the
downhill slope creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing
capacity of the soil
Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage and driveway and will
bear on the in situ soil Due to the moderate swelling potential of
the natural soil it is recommended that a deeper 18 inch perimeter
footing embedment be utilized to provide a barrier against future
water intrusion under the slab area
To further minimize the swelling potential of the soil special
subgrade preparation with lime saturation is recommended The general
procedure listed hereunder should be followed
1 The slab subgrade soils should be water saturated with perforated
hose by constant sprinkling at least 2 days prior to concrete
placement in order to reduce the swelling potential of the
underlying subsoil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1276 00 Page 5
2 Hydrated lime should be placed on the subgrade soils and the footing
excavations prior to water saturating of the subgrade soil We
believe that about ten 50 1b bags of hydrated lime would be
adequate to cover these areas It is important that the soils
engineer should be notified to inspect the above subgrade prepara
tion and check the moisture contents of the underlying soils prior
to pouring of the slab and perimeter footing
The bottom of the finished subgrade and footing excavations should
be neat and free of loose soils It is anticipated that some very
soft silt and lime mud will be deposited at the footing after
saturation These soft mud should be removed just prior to concrete
placement
The concreting of footing and slab should be coordinated in such a
way that the time period between the initial excavation and the
final concrete placement should be kept to a minimum in order to
prevent excessive subgrade drying
The above recommendations for saturating the subgrade with hydrated
lime should also be followed for the sidewalk driveway and patio
Slabs
It is recommended that on grade slabs be placed over a 4 inch layer of
well graded aggregate base course and separated from wall foundations

A vapor barrier membrane should be placed over the base course layer if
a moisture barrier is deemed necessary to protect the floor covering
Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that short retaining walls generally less than
5 feet high are planned around the front portion of the house to
provide grade separation The guidelines listed below for wall design
should be followed
1 For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
Wall restrained at the top 45 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth
2 An allowable bearing value of up to 3000 p s f is
recommended for foundations bearing on the stiff soil
3 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth
may be used for the wall footing on level areas The passive
pressure does not apply for walls on slopes
4 A base friction factor of 0 4 can also be used against
sliding of the wall

5 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the outside finished
grade
Wall situated on slopes should be embedded an additional
amount to maintain a minimum 8 foot horizontal set back
distance measured from the outside slope face to the edge of
the wall footing
6 A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should
be utilized behind the walls Any subdrain should be
properly constructed and drain freely by gravity onto lower
open space
Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should be
shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems should be
installed around the house and that gutter water should be diverted
far away from the foundations and walkways Excessive landscape
watering near the foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
GEOLABS HAWAII

These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to
drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill operations
be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
