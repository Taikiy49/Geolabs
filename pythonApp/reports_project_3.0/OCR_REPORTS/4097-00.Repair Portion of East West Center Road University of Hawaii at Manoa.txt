In accordance with our proposal dated September 4 we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration at the proposed walkway repair across East West
Center Road at the University of Hawaii at Manoa on the island of Oahu Hawaii This letter
report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory testing and presents
our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses for the proposed
project These recommendations are intended for design input only and are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is desired to repair the existing walkway across East West Center Road near the
Intersection with Dole Street The walkway was constructed with concrete paving blocks
presumably over a base course or cushion layer The concrete paving blocks have
experienced deterioration and settlement possibly due to base or subgrade failure It is
planned to replace the paving blocks with a decorative concrete pavement
The field exploration for the above consideration consisted of drilling four shallow
borings below the concrete paving blocks and asphaltic concrete pavement to determine the
thickness of the base course or cushion and sample the subgrade soils to evaluate the bearing
support characteristics
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration is to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site to develop a generalized soil rock data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the proposed walkway

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling four borings designated
as Boring Nos 1 through 4 to a depth of about 2 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Two of the borings were drilled within the concrete walkway paving while the other two were
drilled within the adjacent asphaltic concrete pavement to compare the subgrade support soil
conditions The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The deterioration of the concrete paving block walkway appears to be due to lack of
confinement and interlocking of the paving blocks The individual paving blocks could be lifted
out by hand It appears that vehicle traffic could cause rocking and loosening of the individual
paving blocks
The field exploration indicated that the subgrade soil conditions are suitable for
pavement support It appears that there is about 18 inches of relatively well graded sand and
gravel below the walkway and the adjacent asphaltic concrete pavement
lt is our opinion that the concrete paving blocks may be replaced with a concrete
pavement placed over the existing sand and gravel soil
Pavement Design
The concrete paving blocks and sand leveling course should be removed to expose the
base course select borrow layer Considering normal traffic anticipated it is our opinion that
reconstruction with the following new pavement section may be used for the walkway
6 0 inches Portland Cement Concrete over existing base course select
borrow material compacted to 95 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage
of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91
test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density

Site Preparation
We anticipate that minimal site grading work will be required for the proposed pavement
reconstruction project At the on set of earthwork the areas within the contract grading limits
should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed The existing concrete pavement under the Ewa
side of the walkway should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and yielding
areas encountered below pavement areas should be over excavated to expose firm natural
material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with base course The excavated
soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site or used in landscaping areas if
appropriate
After clearing and demolition the pavement subgrades should be moisture conditioned
to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as
a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with
ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry
weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for
over excavation and engineered fill placement to account for variations in the over excavation
quantities
Additional select borrow and base course materials required for the new pavements
should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in 8 inch level loose
lifts and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Site grading operations
should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii It is important that a representative
from our office observe the site grading operation to evaluate whether undesirable materials
are encountered during the excavation process and whether the exposed soil rock conditions
are similar to those encountered during our field exploration

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions from the boring
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate
the recommendations provided in this report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having been
estimated by taping from the features at the site The physical location and elevation of the
boring should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the boring depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition
Waiter level data from the borings was measured at the times shown on the graphic
representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed and
interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
