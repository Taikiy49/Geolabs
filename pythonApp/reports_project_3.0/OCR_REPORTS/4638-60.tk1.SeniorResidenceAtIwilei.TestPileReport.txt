Dear Ms Arakawa
In accordance with the project specifications a test pile program was undertaken
for the Senior Residence at Iwilei project from September 6 to 18 2012 The test pile
program generally included field observations during the test pile driving operations and
performing a static compressive pile load test on a selected test pile This report
summarizes our field observations made and the results of the test pile program and
presents our geotechnical recommendations pertaining to the production pile installation
program
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The senior housing project is near the northwestern corner of the intersection of
Iwilei Road and North King Street in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location and vicinity of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
project site is approximately bounded by Kaaahi Place to the north North King Street to
the east and Iwilei Road to the south Based on the information provided it is proposed to
construct a thirteen story senior residential building and a four story parking garage
Based on the findings and recommendations presented in our geotechnical
engineering report entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Senior Residence at
Iwilei Intersection of Iwilei Road and North King Street Honolulu Oahu Hawaii dated
prepared for the development the new buildings planned at the site will
be supported on a deep foundation system consisting of precast prestressed concrete
driven piles The location and designation of the pile foundation support system are
shown on the Pile Foundation Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2

TEST PILE PROGRAM
The test pile driving program was conducted from September 6 through 14 2012
under the near continuous observations by a field representative from our office The
purpose of the test pile driving program was to establish the driveability of the piles
driving equipment and methods of pile installation In addition the program also
provided information on the driving resistance and contours of the load bearing stratum
across the project site which was used to estimate the lengths of the production piles
Healy Tibbitts Builders Inc the piling subcontractor used a Junttan HHK5a
hydraulic impact hammer for the test pile driving operations Our field observations
indicated that a stroke height of about 2 feet was generally used for the pile hammer
during the test pile driving operation with a rated energy of approximately
prestressed concrete piles with a pile length of about 70 and 80 feet long for the parking
garage and residential tower respectively Prior to the pile driving operations the pile
locations were predrilled up to a depth of about 15 feet below the existing ground
surface to facilitate the pile driving operations through the surface fill crust During the
test pile driving operation logs were maintained for each pile which included
information pertaining to pile number date driven predrilled depth blow counts
observations size and length of the piles A total of 49 test piles were driven to a tip
elevation ranging between about 47 0 and 77 2 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at the
locations shown on the Pile Foundation Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2 Information collected
during the test pile driving observations are presented on the Test Pile Driving
Summary Plate 3
Static Pile Load Test
In accordance with the project requirements a compressive pile load test was
conducted on a selected test pile designated as Pile No 075 on September 17 and 18
2012 The test pile was loaded to a test load of 300 kips 200 percent of the design
load of 150 kips in 18 75 kip load increments in general accordance with the Quick
Load Test Method of ASTM Test Designation D 1143 Detailed discussions and results
of the static pile load test are presented in our report entitled Compressive Pile Load
Test Senior Residence at Iwilei Honolulu Oahu Hawaii dated
transmitted under separate cover
PRODUCTION PILE INSTALLATION PROGRAM
Based on our observations and the results of the test pile program we believe
that the driving equipment and methods of installation are acceptable for installing the
specified production piles in general accordance with the project requirements Based
on our observation of the hammer performance pile driving behavior and static load
test it is our professional opinion that the test piles have been driven to sufficient depths
and or resistance to provide the design load capacity of 75 tons with a safety factor of 2

Geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the production pile installation
program are presented below
Predrilling
Based on our field exploration a surface fill crust was encountered in the shallow
subsurface across the project site Therefore a predrilling depth of 10 feet below the
ground surface was recommended for the pile installation program We understand that
Healy Tibbitts Builders Inc has increased the predrilling depth to about 15 feet during the
test pile program to facilitate the pile driving operations through the existing fills and
potential obstructions at the project site Based on the test pile program it is our opinion
that a predrilling depth on the order of about 15 feet below the existing ground surface may
be used for the remaining production piles provided there is no additional cost to the
owner The drill operator should note if the bottom of the predrilled hole is still in hard
material These locations should be brought to our attention for supplemental
recommendations
Pile Driving Criteria
Based on the results of the test pile program we believe that the following
production pile driving criteria may be used for the above project The production piles for
the project should be driven with the same type of hammer Junttan HHK5a hydraulic
impact hammer with a stroke height of 2 feet The pile driving operation may be
terminated when the following driving criteria is satisfied
