PURPOSE AND SCOPE
A soil engineering investigation was conducted at the site in 1981 for a
previously proposed single family residential development Subsequently
the development concept was revised to a multi family cluster
development However the site grading design has remained essentially
the same
The purpose of this soil report was to evaluate the previous subsurface
data in order to update the soil engineering investigation to reflect the
revised development scheme
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1026 10 Page 2
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we used
in the engineering analysis that formulated our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendix of this report
The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 and
the boring logs are presented on Appendix A Information regarding the
field exploration and laboratory testing are also presented in the
appendices
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the southern side of Waimalu Valley
adjacent to the valley side slopes The site is situated approximately
3 500 feet northeast of Moanalua Road
The site is bordered by steep valley side slopes on the south an
existing residential subdivision on the west portions of Waimalu Stream
and Kilinoe Street on the north and similar undeveloped land on the east
Access into the site is presently limited to a small dirt trail which
crosses Waimalu Stream
GEOLABS HAWAII
The terrain at about three fourths of the site is gently sloping toward
the stream This level area contains abandoned residences animal coops
stables shacks and a large 85 by 65 foot pump house structure The pump
house walls extended down more than 30 feet below the existing ground
surface Water was noted in the bottom of the structure The ground
elevation in this area generally varied from about 50 to 60 feet
There is a high steep sided knoll on the eastern end of the site The
top of the knoll is about 80 feet higher than the remainder of the site
at an elevation of about 140 feet The ground slopes upward toward the
portion of the site was covered with Haole Koa trees and brush
Surface rubbish piles were noted throughout the project site
The Waimalu Stream channel meanders through the site Although its
dimensions vary it is roughly 30 feet wide and 8 to 15 feet deep The
stream channel contains abundant large to about 5 feet diameter and
hard basalt boulders
The remains concrete abutments and steel girders of an old bridge lie
in the channel about 50 feet downstream of the existing access road to
the site The stream is unlined Rock walls were noted in several areas
along the stream There is an existing 2 1 H V concrete lining in the
area above the northern side of the stream adjacent to and below the
existing Kilinoe Street The stream is generally quite dry except during
rainy weather
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1026 10 Page 4
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed plan is to clear and grade the site for a multi family
residential development consisting of about 150 units and access roads
through the site
To develop the site the high knoll in the eastern portion of the site
will be cut down to generate fill material in order to raise the lower
portion Cuts up to 40 feet and fills up to about 25 feet are planned
for the development
Portions of the existing Waimalu Stream will be realigned to flow
adjacent to the existing Kilinoe Street Stream improvements will
consist of constructing a trapezoidal concrete channel for the portion of
the stream east of the proposed bridge crossing West of the bridge
crossing the channel will consist of a H V concrete slope lining
on the north and a vertical concrete wall on the south The channel
walls are anticipated to be about 13 to 15 feet high
SUBSURFACE COND ITIONS
Geology
The site is located on the extreme south southwestern flank of the Koolau
Volcanic Range adjacent to Pearl Harbor The volcanic range was formed
during the Tertiary Period by pahoehoe and aa basalt flows The flows
erupted from rift zones roughly paralleling the existing mountain crest
trend
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1026 10 Page 5
During the development of Pearl Harbor late Tertiary and Pleistocene
the surrounding valleys which emptied into the harbor area were
continuously being submerged and re emerged as the sea level flunctuated
This caused deposits of mud silt sand and marine clays to accumulate in
the drowned valley areas and also coral reefs to develop at a time when
the sea level was at a higher elevation than its present state When the
sea level dropped erosion of these deposits took place until another
change of sea level altered the situation The numerous changes of sea
level accompanied by the continuous deposition and erosion of material
gives Pearl Harbor and the surrounding coastal and valley mouth areas a
very complex geologic history
Waimalu Valley where the project site is located is one such valley
that was affected in its lower reaches by the events that developed Pearl
Harbor Currently it appears that the area above the project site is
being eroded or downcut by the ephemeral flows when raining Waimalu
Stream
The generally level alluvial terrace upon which much of the deve lopment
is planned as well as the sloping knoll area consists mainly of older
alluvium overlain by recent alluvial colluvial deposits
The older alluvium which was formed during the Pleistocene generally
consists of clayey silt and highly weathered gravel well consolidated by
overburden pressure and rock expansion during weathering
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1026 10 Page 6
The recent alluvial colluvial deposits consist of less consolidated sand
silts and river gravel
Localized deposits of surface Adobe clay were noted over portions of
the top of the knoll Test Pit 6 and along the side slopes Test Pit
5 but did not appear to extend near the stream It appears that the
Adobe was deposited after the silty sand alluvial deposits and both of
these covered the valley floor from wall to wall Then with the
subsequent downcutting by the stream the Adobe along the valley floor
was removed exposing the silty sand below leaving the Adobe to remain
near some of the valley walls

conditions and to obtain samples for laboratory testing
Four borings were drilled to 10 to 36 5 feet at the proposed bridge and
drainage channel locations The approximate locations of the test pits
and borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

Soil Conditions
Based on the boring test pits and field observations the site appears
to be underlain by alluvium generally consisting of stiff silty clay with
sand gravel and boulders
In general the upper 5 to 10 feet consists of more recent alluvial
deposits which have higher clay content and therefore are moderately
expansive The underlying alluvium generally varies from low to
moderately expansive
Fill was encountered in Test Pit and 11 to depths of and
14 feet respectively The fill consisted of clayey silt with some wood
and metal
Adobe clay was encountered in Test Pits and 5 11 in the
sloping knoll area
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed relatively conpetent soils at the site
Most of the excavated material from the knoll area should be suitable as
fill Soft deposits were not encountered in the lowlying level area and
therefore major settlement problems are not anticipated fron the proposed
high fills
The borings also indicated suitable spread footing foundation bearing
soils for the proposed bridge and channel walls

the knoll Test Pit 5 It appears that the slope cutting in this area
will remove the Adobe clay deposit The Adobe clay should be used in

Fill slope ratios of 2 1 H V are recommended If a concrete slope

For cut and fill slopes greater than 20 feet in vertical height

House Foundations
Most of the building pads will be filled to finished grade For the
light residential structures spread footing foundations or slab on grade
may be used Slabs should be set back a minimum distance of 5 feet away
from the tops of slopes to minimize the effects of slope creep and
erosion Also post footings located close to the tops of slopes should
be embedded a sufficient depth to obtain a minimum horizontal set back
distance of 5 feet from the slope face For post footings located 5 feet
or more away from the tops of slopes the footing embedment should be 12
inches below the finish grade
For the lots situated next to the fill slope adjacent to Waimalu Stream
foundations should be extended down to provide a minimum horizontal
set back distance of 10 feet from the slope face to the outer edge of
footing
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1026 10 Page 12
For the light loads anticipated an allowable bearing pressure of 2500
p s f can be used for footings resting on very stiff in situ soil or
compacted fill
If a footing is located next to a utility trench the footing should
extend to the bottom of the utility trench or be designed to span over
the trench to reduce footing settlement due to settlement of the trench
backfill
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of base course
compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density The thickened edge around
the slab on grade should extend a minimum of 8 inches below the adjacent
ground surface
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material a
polyethylene moisture barrier should be considered
Isolated pockets of expansive clay soil may be encountered at or near
finished house pad subgrade in the cut areas When they are encountered
the clay soil should be overexcavated for a maximum depth of 3 feet and
replaced with select on site material
The slab subgrade soils should be kept moist and not be allowed to dry
out prior to concrete placement

The lots should be graded to drain water away from the house foundations
Bridge and Drainage Channel Wall Foundations
A 50 foot long single span bridge structure is planned across Waimalu
Stream for access into the site A concrete channel wall is planned for
the realigned stream west of the bridge
The invert of the realigned stream will be about 5 to 15 feet below the
existing ground surface The borings indicate dense alluvial deposits
consisting of clayey silts and gravel at this level An allowable
bearing value of 4000 p s f is recommended for footings bearing on the
dense deposit
The recommended lateral earth pressures are as follows
Walls unrestrained at top 35 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
active case
Walls restrained at top 50 p c f equivalent fluid pressure
at rest case
Additional load due traffic or surcharge should be included where
applicable
A coefficient of friction of 0 45 between the concrete and dense alluvium
is recommended Additionally a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f
could be utilized

Boulder Protection
The southern lots situated at the base of the valley side slope may be
subject to boulders rolling down the hillside
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1026 10 Page 15
Rock falls are a general phenomena occurring in all ridge slopes in
Oahu
Various methods of boulder barriers or catchments can be used to
reduce the hazard of falling boulders For this site some of the
methods which may be used to reduce the boulder hazard are as follows
1
Boulders sweeps along the upper boundaries of the site could be
performed to remove the loose boulders that appear likely to roll
down
A boulder catchment area with a fence or wall could be utilized to
catch and arrest the rolling boulders The interceptor ditches
along the upper boundaries of the site could be utilized for this
purpose
The natural trees and vegetation along the upper portions of the
site should be retained to act as a natural barrier against the
boulders and to reduce erosion
Where practicable the houses should be set back away from the toe
of the hillside slope to create an additional boulder catchment
area
It should understood that whatever methods are used an inherent risk of
falling boulders could still exist for these hillside lots
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1026 10 Page 16
The state of the art of soil engineering practice has not advanced to a
point that a solution is available for this area It is our opinion that
no matter what one does there is always the risk of boulders rolling
down the hill if not from areas near the house it could come from areas
higher up the slope Whatever measure is chosen it only reduces the
risk It should be pointed out that no guarantee against future falling
boulders can be made due to changes in soil and rock conditions with
time weathering erosion earthquake construction vibration and
improvements and construction activities at and around the site
The owners of these hillside lots should be made aware of the
aforementioned limitations and cautioned against future improvements in
the upper areas of the lots which may disturb the stability of the
existing boulders

W O 1986
The work under this section includes clearing and grubbing preparation
of fill areas placement and compaction of fill and removal and
backfilling of underground structures These specifications are of a
general nature and may be superseded by specific requirements or
recommendations contained within the soils report
I Clearing
All areas within contract limit lines shall be cleared of trash
debris organic matter and all other deleterious materials These
materials shall be removed and disposed offsite
II Preparation of Fill Areas
A Loose surface soils shall be removed to expose competent
natural soils Localized soft pockets shall be undercut and
backfilled with compacted select materials
B All surfaces shall be inspected and approved by the soils
engineer prior to the placement of any fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O
Appendix
C After approval the surface shall be scarified to a depth of
six inches brought to near optimum moisture content and
compacted as herein specified
D Where the existing surface exceeds a slope of 5 horizontal to 1
vertical 5 1 the ground surface shall be keyed and benched
into competent soil to bond the fill to the existing ground
surface
E Subsurface drains may be required as directed by the soils
engineer where springs or zones of seepage are encountered
TIt Fill Materials
Material for fill shall consist of on site soils or approved import
material Import material shall be tested and approved prior to
transportation to the site Fill material shall contain less than
5 percent organic matter and other deleterious material and shall
not contain rocks or lumps in excess of eight inches 8 in
diameter

W O

IV Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
Be
The fill shall be placed in loose horizontal layers which will
not exceed ten inches 10 in thickness Each layer shall be
spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain uniformity of
material and water content within each layer
Rock fragments or boulders less than eight inches 8 in
diameter may be utilized provided they are not concentrated in
any one area and that there is sufficient fine grained material
to fill the voids between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter shall be
removed from the fill unless specifically approved for
placement in the fill by the soils engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Designation
D 1557 unless otherwise specified in the soils report
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O
Appendix C
E The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibratory or
pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil type and capable of
producing the specified results in an expedient and efficient
manner The equipment shall be in good working condition
fully ballasted and self cleaning
F The compactioin shall be accomplished in a systematic and
uniform manner over the entire fill area to produce a uniformly
compacted fill
G Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition and not in
accordance with these specifications shall not be approved and
shall be removed or reworked until the required density is
obtained
H The soils engineers shall be provided unrestricted access to
the fill to perform the compaction tests
V Removal and Backfilling of Underground Structures
Underground structures such as cesspools cisterns septic tanks
wells pipe lines etc shall be removed as directed by the soils
engineer Backfilling of the excavations shall be in accordance
with these specifications

VI Seasonal Requirements
A No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather condition
When interruption of work is due to heavy rain the previous
fill surface shall be approved before resumption or earthmoving
operations
B The contractor shall maintain the surface of the fill so that
it will be free draining and will not pond the surface water
