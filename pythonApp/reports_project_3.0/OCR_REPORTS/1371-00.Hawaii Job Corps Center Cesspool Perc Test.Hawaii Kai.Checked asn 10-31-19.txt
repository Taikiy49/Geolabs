This report presents the results of our boring exploration and
percolation tests performed at the Hawaii Job Corps Center in Hawaii
It is our understanding that additional and
replacement cesspools may be required as one of the alternatives to
alleviate operational difficulty encountered with the existing sewage
system The general location of the project site and its vicinity is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate l

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
For the design and feasibility study of cesspool construction the
subsurface soil and groundwater conditions at the project site were
investigated by drilling four borings at various site areas A series
of percolation tests was then performed in the drilled borings to
evaluate the permeability characteristics of the upper 30 foot soil
layer The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
According to the 90 Review Submittal for Upgrade Of Sewage
Treatment System For Hawaii Job Corps Center Honolulu Hawaii
prepared by Belt Collins Associates a series of new cesspools would
be required to upgrade the sewage treatment system at the project site
For this purpose four areas are generally being considered where groups
of cesspools would be constructed These areas are generally shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2 as indicated by our boring
According to the above referenced submittal it is our
understanding that the existing cesspools are generally 6 feet in
diameter and 20 to 30 feet in depths It is assumed that the new
cesspools would be of similar dimensions It was further stipulated
that a cesspool should be designed to accept no more than 800 gpd
Paragraph Thus such a design capacity is being assumed

SUBSURFACE EXPLORATION
Four 4 inch diameter auger test borings were drilled each to a
depth of 32 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The drilling operations were monitored by our engineer who also
logged the borings and performed the percolation tests The boring logs
are presented on Plates A 1 through A 4
Subsurface Conditions
The materials encountered in the borings consisted mainly of dry to
moist moderate to highly weathered volcanic ash and cinder sand
generally classified as medium dense silty sand according to the Unified
Soil Classification
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings within the depth
explored
Detailed description of materials encountered in the borings
together with the results of moisture and or density tests are shown on
the Log of Borings Plates A 1 through A 4
Several laboratory mechanical sieve analysis was performed on the
soil samples recovered in the borings to examine their grain size
distribution The test results are presented on Plates B 1 and B 2

PERCOLATION TESTS
After completion of drilling the borings were saturated with water
overnight as presaturation and a field percoaltion test was perform in
each boring A constant flow of water was introduced into the boring to
develop a constant head water level within the hole A constant head
was maintained in and 4 at various discharge rates
However the materials in Boring 1 were less permeable and a falling
head test was performed

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field percolation test results it appears that the
soil permeabilities would be fair to good at the site This is further
confirmed by visual examinations of the soils encounteded in the borings
and the laboratory grain size analysis results
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1371 00 Page 5
Therefore it appears that the underlying natural weathered
volcanic ash and cinder sand deposits can provide some percolation rates
for sewage effluent disposal Based on the percolation test data
obtained in the drilled holes a 6 foot damter cesspool extednding a
minimum of 30 feet below the gournd could provide a more than adequate
rate of seepage for a cesspoool with an 800 gpd design capacity
It should be pointed out however that the permeability of the
soils can be reduced considerably in the future by suspended solids in
the effluent including oil or grease that clog up the pores in the
soil mass Dissolved air in the effluent may become air bubbles and can
also be trapped in the pores and reduce permeability Bacteria growth
will also clog the soil pores and reduce permeability Chemical
reaction between the effluent and soil may result in solid precipitation
which may also reduce permeability Other factors such as rainfall
rise in groundwater level etc may also affect the permeability to
some extent Therefore cesspools will require periodical maintenance
The test borings which were left uncased after presaturation
showed little signs of caving in during our testing However for
drilling larger diameter holes provisions should be made to case the
holes as necessary

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this Teport are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such
as well cemented soils or clays and silts of very low permeability may
occur in localized areas and may require additional probing or
corrections in the field Therefore additional expenditures may be
needed during construction to attain a properly constructed project
Some contingency fund is thus recommended to accommodate these possible
costs
