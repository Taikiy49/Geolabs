Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our survey was to obtain in situ resistivity data in general
accordance with Standard Test Method for Field Measurement of Soil Resistivity Using the
Wenner Four Electrode Method ASTM G 57 The scope of our work consisted of the
following tasks and work efforts
1 Coordination of the field exploration and performance of 40 field resistivity
tests by a field engineer and helper from our firm at 10 test locations with pin
spacing up to 20 feet apart along the requested five testing reference lines
shown on the Resistivity Survey Plan Plate 2
2 Analyses of the field data for the evaluation of resistivity values
3 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations
4 Coordination of our overall work on the project by a project engineer from
our firm
5 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
principal engineer from our firm
6 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
Field Survey
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the project
site on May 14 2004 for the field survey
Forty soil resistivity surveys were conducted at ten locations designated as R 1
through R 10 using the in Pin Method and a Nilsson Model 400 resistance
meter manufactured by Nilsson Electrical Laboratory Inc The method utilizes four
surface probes or pins electrodes to evaluate the average resistivity of the underlying soil
mass The average resistivity values of the soil mass extending from the existing ground
surface to the test depth was assessed by using this method at the approximate survey

The soil resistivity surveys were performed at the existing ground surface along the
reference lines shown on the site plan The average soil resistance along the proposed
alignment was evaluated at each survey location with the electrodes aligned at equal
spacings of generally and 20 feet The depth of penetration of a resistivity
sounding is a function of the electrode spacing
The resistance meter Nilsson Model 400 provides a voltage between the outer
electrodes causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between
the inner electrodes is then measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the
soil resistance in ohms is obtained from the voltmeter dial reading The soil resistivity
inohm cm which is directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be
computed The evaluated soil resistances and computed resistivity values are presented
on Plate A 1 The computed resistivity values with pin spacings at the test locations along
through A 5 The computed resistivity values for locations R 3 and R 5 were projected on
the reference line E E of the graph on the Plate A 5 At the time of resistivity surveying
the temperature was about 85 F The existing ground surface was damp to moist It
should be noted that the soil resistivity may be influenced by temperature weather
conditions existing underground facilities and other factors
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics
with regard to the conductivity of the soil to buried metallic structures In general the lower
the resistivity the greater the potential for conduction of electric power away from the
buried metallic structures Conversely the higher the resistivity the less likely the soil will
contribute to conduct electric power away from the metallic structures
Four laboratory resistivity tests ASTM G 57 were performed using the soil
samples obtained from our previous geotechnical exploration The test results are
presented on Plate B 1 The Minimum Resistivity was the lowest resistivity measured
with the sample saturated with water The High Range Resistivity was measured with
as is moisture condition and slightly remolded Since the in situ density of the soil is
higher than the slightly remolded sample this High Range Resistivity represented in situ
resistivity close to the highest value These results provide the range of resistivity of soil
mass at the particular depths
On the basis of the field resistivity test results a majority of the test results for the
subsurface soils within the project site have resistivity ranging from 5 000 to
20 000 ohm cm

Limitations
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from our field borings and resistivity survey The field resistivity
surveys are based upon information obtained from field data points by means of
geophysical surveys Variations of subsurface conditions between and beyond the
borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident
until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the recommendations provided in this report
The locations of the field borings and field resistivity tests indicated in this report are
approximate having been taped from features shown on the site plan transmitted by
Ronald N S Ho Associates Inc on Elevations of the borings were
estimated based on interpolation between the contour lines shown on the same plan The
physical locations and elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used
