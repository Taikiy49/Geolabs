Subsurface Exploration
Underground Fuel Oil Seepage
Millican Field and NSC
Pearl Reclamation Plant
Pearl Harbor Oahu Hawaii
In accordance with the scope of services outlined in our proposal of
we have performed a subsurface exploration for the
above referenced project
The purpose of the exploration was to conduct a boring program to
help delineate the location and extent of the fuel oil seepage into the
storm drainage system within the limits of the site
The exploration was performed along the existing storm drain
alignment which extends from Manhole No Q6 located on the east side of
Makalapa Gate Road to the discharge outlet located at the southern corner
of Quarry Loch The aerial extent of the exploration was limited to 100
feet on each side of the alignment In addition exploration was
performed along the western edge of the NSC Pearl Reclamation Plant
The scope of work consisted of the following
1 Grid mark the drill hole spacing based on 25 feet interval along
the storm drain alignment and 50 feet grids laterally outward on
both sides of the storm drain alignment The invert elevation of
the storm drain at the discharge end and the drainage Manhole Q1
was also taken as part of this effort

The invert elevations at Manhole No Q1 and the discharge outlet of
the drainline were surveyed at 99 6 and 97 6 feet elevation Navy datum
respectively
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling fifty two 52
borings along the existing storm drain alignment and one 1 boring at the
NSC Pearl Reclamation Plant The depth of the borings ranged between 8 0
and 11 0 feet below the existing ground surface A truck mounted arill
rig utilizing 4 inch diameter continuous flight augers was used in the
exploration
The presence of fuel oil was determined by visual examination of the
Grill augers immediately following the drilling of each boring In
borings where fuel oil was not encountered on the augers a one hour
waiting period was observed for each boring Following the waiting
period groundwater samples were obtained from each of the borings
presence of fuel oil was then determined by visual examination of the
groundwater samples
The locations of the borings drilled along with identification of the
borings which encountered fuel oil are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2
