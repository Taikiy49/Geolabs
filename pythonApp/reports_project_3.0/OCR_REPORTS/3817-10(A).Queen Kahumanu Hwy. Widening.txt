New pavements will be constructed as part of the Queen Kaahumanu Highway
Widening Henry Street to Kealakehe Parkway project Based on the field exploration
and project plans we anticipate that the majority of the pavement subgrade soils will
consist of basalt clinker basalt rock formation and compacted fill materials We
understand that a permeable drainage base layer will not be required in the pavement
design section for the new roadway based on the requirements stipulated in the
Request for Proposal RFP documents In addition we understand that the pavement
edge drain system will not be required for the project
Based on the traffic data provided the R Value of the subgrade soils and the
economic analyses performed we recommend that a flexible pavement structural
section be used for the new pavements of the highway The recommended pavement
structural section consisting of 4 0 inches of AC on 8 0 inches of ACB over 6 0 inches
of ASB should be used for the design of new pavements for the Queen Kaahumanu
Highway Widening Henry Street to Kealakehe Parkway project
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due
to saturation of the base subbase and or subgrade soils Therefore the pavement
surface should be sloped adequately and positive drainage gradients should be
maintained to carry surface water off the pavement to appropriate drainage structures
The text of this pavement justification report should be referred to for detailed
discussion and specific recommendations for design of pavements for the project

SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This pavement justification report presents the results of our analyses performed
for the design of new pavements for the Queen Kaahumanu Highway Widening Henry
Street to Kealakehe Parkway project located in the North Kona area on the Island of
Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and presents our
geotechnical recommendations derived from our analyses for the design of pavements
for the project only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The highway widening project is located along Queen Kaahumanu Highway in
the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii The project will begin in the vicinity of
the Henry Street intersection with Queen Kaahumanu Highway and will terminate in the
vicinity of the Kealakehe Parkway intersection with Queen Kaahumanu Highway
covering approximately 2 8 miles of highway widening
The project involves widening the existing two lane highway to a four lane
highway with the potential for future widening to a six lane highway We understand
that drainage facilities and utility line installations also are planned as part of the project
Based on the existing site conditions we envision that site grading generally will consist
of cuts and fills up to about 10 to 20 feet in vertical height
It should be noted that a separate geotechnical engineering report has been
prepared in support of the design of retaining walls drainage structures and other
elements of the project Detailed discussion and geotechnical recommendations for
design of the other elements of the project are presented in the Geotechnical
Engineering Report for the project prepared by our office

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii the largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an
area of approximately 4 000 square miles This island was formed by the eruptive
activity of five major shield volcanoes Kohala long been extinct Mauna Kea activity
during recent geologic time Hualalai last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa and
Kilauea both still active
The project site is located on the western flank of Hualalai Mountain which
makes up the west central portion of the island Most of the near surface basaltic lava
formation at the site appears to be composed of the pahoehoe type lava flow which
spread and ponded as it approached the ocean Pahoehoe is characterized by a
smooth rope like or billowy surface with an internal structure of vesicular porous rock
In addition to the pahoehoe type lava some land area of the project site appears to be
locally covered by a a lava flows The a a lavas are typified by the appearance of loose
rubbly clinker layers interspersed with seams of very dense massive basalt rock The
dense basalt rock layers represent the hard interior core of the individual lava flow
Cavities or voids are commonly encountered in pahoehoe lava especially the
more fluid pahoehoe lava Cavities are formed when the lava was still in a molten state
and represent both lava tubes intra flow cavities and blisters and pockets
inter flow cavities Lava tubes are formed when molten lava drains from the cooling
flow leaving a hollow tube like structure which may extend for a large longitudinally
distance along the flow Inter flow cavities generally are closed cavity features that are
usually smaller in horizontal extent
Basalt is considered to be a relatively permeable rock and can transmit water
quite readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions In general water is
transmitted through the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and _inter flow

2 2 Site Description
The project site is located approximately between the intersections of Henry
Street and Kealakehe Parkway with the Queen Kaahumanu Highway in the District of
North Kona on the Island of Hawaii The limits of the project are shown on the Site
Plan Plates 2 1 through 2 5
Based on our site reconnaissance the ground surface along the existing highway
is generally irregular and exhibits the typical surface characteristics of a pahoehoe lava
flow Several collapsed near surface lava tube features were observed at various
locations along the project alignment In addition many small voids and blisters were
observed on the cut slope faces adjacent to the existing highway Based on these
observations the presence of other near surface lava tubes cavities and blisters should
be anticipated and taken into consideration in the design of the proposed highway
widening project
Based on our field exploration and the topographic survey map provided the
project site is gently sloping with elevations ranging from about 46 to 187 feet Mean
Sea Level MSL along the highway alignment Vegetation adjacent to the existing
highway generally consists of wild grasses growing from the cracks of the surface basalt
formation

3 1 Methodology of Pavement Design
Two types of pavement structural sections flexible and rigid pavements were
considered in the pavement analyses for this project The flexible pavement sections
presented in this report generally were determined based on the methodology described
in Chapter 3 of the revised Pavement Design Manual dated March 2002 The
Pavement Design Manual was prepared by the State of Hawaii Department of
Transportation Highways Division Material Testing and Research Branch The
pavement design methodology is based on the Hveem Stabilometer method developed
and used by the California Department of Transportation Caltrans
The design procedures for the rigid pavement section generally are based on the
design procedures described in the Portland Cement Association Thickness Design for
Concrete Highway and Street Pavements

Preparation Below Pavement Section
We anticipate that the majority of the new highway pavements will be constructed
at or near the existing grades or in a cut or fill condition In general the area within the
contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed at the on set of
earthwork Vegetation debris deleterious material and other unsuitable materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site or disposed of in a designated area
to reduce the potential for contamination of the excavated materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas
designated to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to expose
stiff and or dense materials The resulting excavation should be backfilled with
aggregate subbase materials The excavated soft and or organic soils should be
properly disposed of off site
After clearing and grubbing the future pavement areas should be excavated
where necessary to the pavement subgrade level i e bottom of the aggregate
subbase layer In areas where the pavement subgrades expose dense clinker materials
or basalt rock formation the subgrades should be proof rolled with a minimum 15 ton
vibratory roller for a minimum of eight passes to help detect and collapse near surface
cavities Loose areas or cavities disclosed during clearing and proof rolling operations
should be backfilled with well compacted granular fill materials The proof rolling
operation also should be performed at the finished subgrades in cut areas The
vibratory drum roller should travel at a speed of about 200 to 300 feet per minute Due
to the potential for presence of cavities lava tubes blisters pockets etc some
variation in the over excavation and backfill quantities should be anticipated

3 6 Fill Materials
As mentioned previously we have assumed that the recommended pavement
section will be underlain by structural fill materials 6 inch minus granular fills in the fill
areas In general structural fill materials should consist of well graded granular
materials generally less than 6 inches in maximum dimension with sufficient fines to
prevent the occurrence of voids in the compacted mass
Imported fill materials where required should consist of non expansive granular
materials such as crushed basalt or cinder sand The materials should be well graded
from coarse to fine with no particles greater than 6 inches in largest dimension The
materials should have a CBR value of 25 or more and should have a maximum swell
value of 1 percent or less In addition the material should have a minimum R value of
60 or more Imported fill materials should be tested for conformance with these
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use
3 7 Compaction Requirements
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practical in fills composed of
rocks boulders and or cobbles Instead a testing program to evaluate the number of
passes by a compactor needed to achieve the desired level of compaction should be
conducted at the start of the grading phase of the project under the observation of a
representative from Geolabs Based on this testing program the number of passes
may then be used as the field criterion for adequate compaction For preliminary
estimating purposes compaction may be accomplished by using a large vibratory drum
roller having a static weight of at least 15 tons traveling at a speed of about 200 to 300
feet per minute The roller should pass over the fill surface at least eight times for
structural fill
Field density tests should be performed on fills and backfills in accordance with
