INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the design of the proposed Electrical
Distribution System Improvements at Berth B 14 Navy Public Works Center
Pearl Harbor Oahu Hawaii The approximate location of the project site
is shown on the Project Location Map Plate
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the exploration was to study the subsurface
conditions below the site to provide pertinent soil parameters for the
foundation design of the proposed substation F 9A at Berth B 14
The scope of our work included drilling and sampling three 3
borings laboratory testing of samples cbtained engineering evaluation
of the field and laboratory data development of design recommendations
and report preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration boring logs and
laboratory test results are presented in the appendices of this report
The approximate boring locations at the new substation site are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the southwestern side of Southeast
Ioch at the end of Berth B 14 in the Navy Public Works Center of Pearl
Harbor on the island of Oahu Hawaii

The proposed structure location is partially occupied by an existing
snack shop and an existing dining canopy It is our understanding that
these existing structures and other minor facilities will be relocated
for the proposed construction
It is also our understanding that the new structure site is located
over an existing anchored sheetpile bulkhead and several existing utility
lines The existing tiebacks of the bulkhead pass directly under the

Existing ground cover consists of asphaltic concrete pavement above

site are also covered by asphaltic concrete pavement
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
A two story substation F 9A is proposed to improve the electrical
distribution system at Berth B 14
The proposed structure will be about 36 by 66 feet in plan with
metal grating for the second floor The anticipated wall load is about
8 5 kips per lineal foot
A 45 foot long concrete jacket will be constructed to encase some of
the existing utility lines under the proposed structure
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The geologic conditions underlying the site are the results of a
complex series of depositional and erosional events brought on by
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1697 00 Page 4
Dk No 41
glacioeustatic sea level changes and post erosional volcanic activities
during the Pleistocene Following the cessation of activity of the
Koolau Volcano alluvial materials were deposited in estuarine and
deltaic environments in the area of Pearl Harbor
The sea level fluctuated several times receding perhaps 300 feet
below and rising about 95 feet above the present sea level
As Oahu sank coral reef grew around the shorelines keeping pace
with the subsidence
Concurrent with a rise in sea level during late Pleistocene era
eruption of the Salt Lake Makalapa member of the Honolulu Volcanic Series
mantled the coral reefs and alluvium with deposits of volcanic ash which
consolidated to form volcanic tuff locally called mudrock
Subsequent erosion has dissected and thinned the mantles of tuff at the
site
During the Recent epoch as the sea level rose to its current stand
the area was covered with loose lagoonal marine deposits These deposits
were subsequently covered by man made fills placed for land reclamation
projects during the construction of the existing Navy Public Works Center
in the middle portion of this century
Subsoil Conditions
The subsurface soil conditions at the site were explored by drilling
and sampling three 3 borings at three corners of the proposed structure
to depths from about 51 5 to 84 4 feet below the existing ground surface
The borings generally encountered loose surface fills and lagoonal
deposits of sand silt coral and tuff gravel over generally medium dense
gravel and sand size coral detritus The loose layers increase in
thickness towards the northwest

The depth of the medium dense layer below ground surface varies from
about 23 feet in the southeastern side of the site to more than 62 feet
in the northwestern side Alluvial deposits of stiff clayey silt and
very dense silty tuff gravel were encountered beneath the coral detritus
to the drilled depth of Boring No 2
Scattered tuff boulders were encountered at depths varying depths

Boring No 2
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings at depths of
about 10 6 to 11 5 feet below the existing ground surface during our
field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Shallow foundations had been considered to support the proposed
light structure However shallow foundations may impose additional
loads on the adjacent sheetpile seawalls and tierods of the existing
anchored bulkhead Also future repair of the sheetpiles or tieback
anchors would jeopardize the new building foundation Moreover the
loose upper subsoils could cause significant ground settlements under the
anticipated footing bearing pressures
Deep foundations are recommended in order to bypass the loose fd
existing underground tieback rods and near surface underground utilities
Pile foundations together with a structural slab should be used to
Support the proposed structure Due to the varying depths to the bearing
layer varying pile lengths are anticipated
Predrilling prior to pile driving is anticipated to penetrate
through the scattered boulders in order to achieve the required embedment
depths

Pile Foundations
Based on our findings we recommend that pile foundations bearing
into the medium dense gravel and sand layer be used to support the
proposed structure
The piles should be used in conjunction with a structural slab
However if the floor will not support heavy equipment or storage loads
and the risk of floor damage due to future tierod or utility repair is
acceptable then a slab on grade may be considered
The piles would develop their vertical load capacity primarily from
end bearing into the medium dense gravel and sand layer and partially
from adhesion between the piles and the adjacent soils
Twelve inch square prestressed concrete piles are recommended
Due to the varying depths to the bearing layer from about 23 feet
to more than 62 feet below the existing ground surface pile embedment
depths will vary accordingly We recommend that the piles in the
southeastern half of the proposed structure be driven to a minimm depth
of about 50 feet below the existing ground surface and the piles in the
northwestern half of the structure be driven to a minimm depth of abou
70 feet
The piles should be driven to the recommended depths with a hammer
delivering a minimm of 24 000 foot pounds of energy and to a resistance
of about 5 blows per inch for the last 2 inches or 60 blows per foot for
the last foot
Allowable vertical pile capacity of 45 tons per pile may be used
Pile settlements on the order of less than one half of an inch are
anticipated

The pile driving should be monitored by a qualified geotechnical
engineer to see that the piles are driven to the required depth and that
sufficient capacity has been developed
A test pile program should be undertaken to help evaluate and
modify if necessary the driving criteria and to more accurately
determine the production pile order lengths
To achieve these objectives we recommend that a minimm of five
piles be test driven at the site These test piles may later be
incorporated into the actual foundations
As a part of the test pile program a minimm of one 1 pile load
test should be budgeted The test pile s should be load tested to two
times the total allowable vertical capacity or 90 tons in accordance
with the ASTM D 1143 test procedures
To resist the lateral loads on the structure passive earth pressure
around the pile caps and tie beams may be used We recommend that an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth
against the buried vertical faces of the pile caps and tie beams be
used The backfill against the pile caps or tie beams should be select
material compacted to a minimum of 95 maximum dry density determined by
ASTM D 1557 78
Site filling should be avoided or minimized to less than one foot to
reduce potential ground settlements which would impose downdrag loads on
the piles
Predrilling
Boulders were encountered at various depths in two of the three
borings drilled Piles may not be able to penetrate the hard boulders
which may also exist in other areas of the site

Therefore we recommend that 10 feet of predrilling be used for
piles to be driven in the southeastern half of the proposed structure and
25 feet of predrilling be used for the northwestern half of the proposed
structure
Existing Anchored Bulkhead
To reduce the impact of pile driving on the existing adjacent
bulkhead and anchors below the proposed structure the piles should be
set back a minimm distance of 3 feet from the existing tierods and
sheetpile walls of the bulkhead
The exact locations of the existing tierods and sheetpile walls
should be determined prior to pile driving to establish the proper
locations of piles at the recommended minimm set back The piles to be
driven in close proximity to these structures should be carefully aligned
during driving to avoid damage to the tierods and sheetpiles
Site Grading
Based on the present plans minimal cuts and or fills are
anticipated for the proposed construction It is our opinion that major
site filling should be avoided to reduce ground settlements
At the onset of earthwork the project area within the grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing demolition
waste debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed
and disposed of properly off site to prevent contamination of the
excavated materials
After clearing the cut or fill area should be scarified to a depth
of at least 6 inches below the cleared or finished grade and recompacted
to a minimm of 95 of its maximm dry density per ASTM D 1557 78

3 borings to depths of 51 5 to 84 4 feet below the existing ground

textural examination in the field by our geologist who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials

inches or the hydraulic pressure to push the sampler the first 24 inches
are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths

recovery and RQD values are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
