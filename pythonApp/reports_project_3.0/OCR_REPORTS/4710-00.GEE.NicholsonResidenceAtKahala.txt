SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Nicholson residence at 727 Honua Street 33 36 in the Kahala
area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the
project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings and recommendations resulting from our field
exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses These recommendations are
intended for the design of foundation site grading and pavements only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is located at 727 Honua Street in the Kahala area of Honolulu on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii The subject lot has an existing one story house with an
attached garage and a swimming pool
It is proposed to demolish the existing one story structure and construct a new
two story residence on the subject lot We anticipate that the proposed new residence will
be a two story slab on grade structure Based on the preliminary grading plan we envision
minimal site grading for the proposed construction that will involve minor cuts and fills
1 3 Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site to develop an idealized soil rock data set for the formulation
of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of foundations site
grading and pavements for the proposed new residence The work was performed in
general accordance with our fee proposal dated Our scope of work
included the following tasks and work efforts

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of
the western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of the
eastern two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while Koolau
was still active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lavas in central Oahu
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was
available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the
sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such
that the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of accumulated
terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits at
correspondingly higher elevations and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters
protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys in the
sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and calcareous
materials caused consolidation of the soft deltaic materials and lagoonal deposits and
induration of the calcareous reef materials

2 3 Subsurface Conditions
Our exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two borings to depths
of approximately 15 and 17 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Pian Plate 2

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site is underlain by dense to very dense
fill consisting of silty clays to silty calcareous sands over very dense sandstone extending
to the maximum depths drilled of about 15 and 17 feet below the existing ground surface
Based on our field exploration and laboratory testing we believe that a shallow foundation
system consisting of spread and or continuous footings bearing on the recompacted in situ
soils and or compacted engineered fills may be used to support the proposed new
residence We anticipate that site grading will be minimal with the demolition of the existing
one story house and the construction of a new two story residence We anticipate minor
cuts and fills may be required to accomplish design grade Detailed discussion and
recommendations for design of foundation site grading and other geotechnical aspects
of the project are presented in the following sections of this report
3 1 Foundations
Based on the subsoil conditions encountered at the site we recommend that a
shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings bearing on the
recompacted in situ soils and or compacted engineered fills be used for support of the
proposed new residence An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square
foot psf may be used for the design of foundations bearing on the compacted subgrade
transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic forces The bottom of footings
should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent ground level The
allowable bearing value may be increased to if the foundations are extended
down to bear on the dense coral or cemented sand layer at about 3 feet below the existing
ground surface
If loose sand lenses or pockets are encountered at the bottoms of footing
excavations they should be over excavated to dense in situ sand The resulting
depression should then be backfilled with select granular material compacted to at least
90 percent relative compaction

We understand that new fills may be required to attain the design grade at the site
Due to the granular nature of the subsoils we believe that ground settlements would be
small and would occur rapidly Where new structures and pavements are located on fills
greater than 2 feet above the existing ground surface Geolabs Inc should be consulted

Foundations located next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded
below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the

requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with
additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation settlement
lf the foundation is designed in accordance with the recommendations presented
herein total footing settlements under the anticipated design loads are estimated to be on
the order of less than 1 inch with differential settlements of approximately inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
developed against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the soil
around the footings is well compacted The passive pressure should be reduced for
foundations located on slopes Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive
resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected A coefficient of friction of
0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the compacted subgrade soils
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative from
Geolabs Inc prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the

3 2 Cavity Probing and Grouting
Voids and cavities may be present in coral formations However due to the low
structural loads from the two story residence we believe that the potential for loss of
foundation support resulting from collapse of the cavities below the formations is small If
it is desired to further reduce the potential for loss of foundations support then a program
of cavity probing and grouting at the foundation locations should be conducted
The probes should be drilled at each column location and on 10 foot centers for line
footings The probe holes should be a minimum of 3 inches in diameter and should extend
a minimum of 10 feet below the footing bearing elevations or about 12 feet below existing
ground elevations whichever is deeper
lf a cavity or void is encountered at the footing location additional probes should be
drilled to further delineate the areal extent of the void
3 3 Slabs On Grade
We anticipate that concrete slabs on grade will be used for the proposed new
residence Based on the preliminary grading plan provided we anticipate that minimal fills
may be required to attain the design grade for the proposed structure
Prior to any filling operation the subgrade to receive fill should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction In general the fill material
needed to raise the grades to the finish subgrade may consist of the on site sandy soils
and or imported non expansive granular material The fill material should be placed in
level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage
of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density

For more uniform support of interior slabs we recommend that the concrete
slab on grade be supported on a minimum 4 inch layer of cushion fill The cushion fill layer
should consist of open graded gravels such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation or basalt termite barrier BTB The slab cushion fill should be placed
as soon as possible after the scarification and recompaction of the existing subgrade soils
as recommended above For slabs on grade with moisture sensitive surfacing we
recommend that an impervious moisture barrier be placed over the cushion fill to reduce
potential migration of moisture through the concrete slab In order to promote more
uniform curing of the slab concrete and to provide protection of the moisture barrier we
recommend that a 2 inch layer of moist fine sand or BTB be placed above the membrane
prior to placing the slab concrete The sand should be moistened slightly prior to placing
concrete
Where concrete slabs will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as the garage slab
we recommend that the slab be supported on a 6 inch layer of base course compacted to
a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction in lieu of the 4 inch cushion fill and 2 inch
sand layers The base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate The subgrade
should also be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 95 percent
relative compaction For design of structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of
200 pounds per cubic inch pci may be used for the compacted base course The
thickened edges of slabs adjacent to any unpaved area should be embedded at least
12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
If exterior concrete walkways are planned for the proposed project we recommend
that a minimum 4 inch thick cushion layer of select borrow be provided below the concrete
walkway slab for more uniform support The subgrade below the concrete walkway should
be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Expansion joints should
be provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkways with construction joints at
right angle intersections

3 4 Site Grading
Based on the preliminary grading plan provided we understand that site grading will
involve minor cuts and fills with the demolition of the existing one story structure and the
construction of the new proposed two story structure Site preparation should be observed
by a representative from Geolabs Inc It is important that a representative from our office
observe whether the exposed soil conditions during excavation are similar to those
encountered in our exploration The following recommendations are intended to provide
guidelines for the design of site preparation work
At on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials generated from demolition should be removed and disposed of off site After
clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrade should be scarified to a minimum depth of
8 inches properly moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction For subgrades of
improvements subjected to vehicular traffic such as garage slab and pavements the
compaction requirement should be increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage
by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
A swimming pool currently exists on the western portion of the subject property and
is proposed to be backfilled up to finished grade Based on the preliminary grading plan
a portion of the swimming pool is located within the future building area Holes should be
punched through the pool bottom to allow water to drain It is important that the swimming
pool be properly backfilled to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence We
envision the swimming pool will be backfilled with imported granular material The backfill
material should consist of approved non expansive material such as select borrow placed
in 8 inch loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The
side walls of the pool should be removed for a minimum depth of 3 feet below the existing

anticipate the vehicle loading for this project will consist primarily of passenger vehicles and

3 6 Utility Trench
We envision that new utility connections may be needed for the proposed project
In general a granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials such
as No 3B Fine gravel should also be used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches
above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the
compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of on site sandy soils or imported
granular material The backfill should be placed in about 8 inch loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the
potential for future ground subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the
upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to
not less than 95 percent relative compaction
3 7 Drainage
The finished grades outside the residence should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the structure and that the discharge be diverted away from the
foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and
slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling two
borings to depths of approximately 15 and 17 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a
portable drill rig equipped with solid augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer geologist who monitored the drilling operations
on a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials

Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with

ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
One sieve analysis test ASTM C 136 D 1140 was performed on a selected
sample of the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1
Two Unconfined Compression Tests ASTM Test Method D 2938 were performed
on core samples to evaluate the unconfined strength of the rock for preliminary design
Results of the unconfined compression tests are presented on Plate B 2
