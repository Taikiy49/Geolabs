PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our supplemental geotechnical engineering exploration was to obtain
an overview of the surface and subsurface conditions at the site The subsurface information
obtained was utilized to assess the thickness of the undocumented fill and to confirm the
subsurface conditions encountered in the 1995 geotechnical engineering exploration To
accomplish this we conducted an exploration program consisting of the following tasks and
work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a rubber tire backhoe with an operator to
and from the project site
2 Excavation and sampling of seven test pits extending to depths of about 1 5 to
7 0 feet below the existing ground surface
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the test pits by a field
geologist from our firm
4 Review and analyses of the field data for the formulation of professional
opinions regarding the thickness of the undocumented fill and whether the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the conditions encountered in the
1995 geoiechnical engineering exploration
5 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and recommendations

Our field exploration program consisted of excavating seven test pits designated as
Test Pit Nos 1 through 7 extending to depths of about 1 5 to 7 feet below the existing
ground surface The test pits were excavated at locations close to the boreholes drilled in
1995 using a rubber tire backhoe The approximate locations of the test pits excavated at
the site are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The test pit numbers excavated in 2001
generally follow the boring numbers drilled in 1995 For example Test Pit No

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our test pit exploration the project site is underlain by fill materials extending
from the ground surface to depths of about 1 5 to 7 feet below the existing ground surface
The fill materials encountered generally consisted of silty sand and or silty clay fill materials
with gravel and occasional reinforcing bars at the ground surface The fill materials graded
with more concrete pieces asphaltic concrete pieces and reinforcing bars in a matrix of soils
with increasing depth below the ground level The soil matrix in the fill materials generally
ranged from medium dense to medium stiff in consistency Some of the soil matrix below
the concrete pieces appeared to be loose and the voids between the concrete pieces were
not completely filled and or were not filled with well compacted soils The test pits were
terminated upon refusal to further excavation on the concrete pieces and or boulders
encountered Groundwater was not encountered in the test pits at the time of our field
exploration
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the test pits excavated we
believe that there is a distinct difference in the nature and character of the fill materials
encountered in the test pits excavated in the southern portion of the site TP 6
and TP 7 Based on the materials encountered in these four test pits the fill materials
contained abundant construction debris such as large concrete pieces asphaltic concrete
pieces and reinforcing bars starting at the existing ground surface The presence of these
deleterious materials inhibited further excavation and halted the progress of our test pit
exploration at depths of about 3 5 to 6 feet below ground level
located at the southern portion of the site generally did not encounter the obstructions until
a depth of about 12 to 18 feet below the ground Jevel in 1995 Based on this contrast we
believe that the nature and character of the subsurface materials in the southern portion of

Based on the materials encountered in the test pits excavated at the site we believe
that there is a distinct change in the nature and character of the fill materials at the site
between the 1995 field exploration and the current field exploration The fill materials
encountered near the existing ground surface contained a significant amount of deleterious
materials that will need to be removed and replaced with well compacted materials in order
to consiruct the proposed residential dwellings on the site Therefore we recommend that
the vertical and lateral extent of the undocumented fills containing deleterious materials be
further evaluated and that the undocumented fills be removed and replaced with
well compacted fills minimum 90 percent relative compaction The removal of the
undocumented fill and replacement with well compacted fill shall be performed under the
near continuous observation of qualified geotechnical engineer licensed in the State of
Hawaii with a minimum of 10 years of experience in Geotechnical Engineering This item of
work should be designated as a Special Inspection item in general accordance with Section
1701 of the Uniform Building Code 1997

development proposed at this site Follow up work should be performed and written
confirmation should be obtained from Geolabs Inc prior io using the recommendations
contained in the report for detailed design and construction of the development on this site

Construction Monitoring
Considering the uncertainty in the nature and distribution of the undocumented fill
materials at the site it is strongly recommended that Geolabs be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction of the project Items of construction
monitoring that are critical requiring Special Inspection include over excavation and
replacement of the undocumented fill observation of subgrade preparation fill and backfill
placement and foundation construction
Other aspects of earthwork construction should also be observed by a representative
from Geolabs This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may
be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time
this report was prepared The recommendations provided in this report are contingent upon
such observations If the actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate
modifications to the design should be made
