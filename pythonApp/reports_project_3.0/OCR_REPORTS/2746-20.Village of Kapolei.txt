Subject Observation of Percolation Tests of Injection Wells and Testing Report
Village of Kapolei

Dear Mr Sakanashi
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering services performed during our
observation of the percolation tests of eighteen 18 injection wells at the subject site along
the Kapolei Village Lower Channel in the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The approximate location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is situated in the Ewa district on the island of Oahu Hawaii and is
part of the Villages of Kapolei development project The proposed Villages of Kapolei
development consists of a planned residential community with related facilities The entire
project will be developed in several phases
As part of the overall infrastructure of the development a drainage channel has
been constructed along the southerly and easterly boundaries of the development area
This channel is used to intercept runoff originating within and above the development area
The drainage channel has aL shaped alignment The shorter leg of the L or the
upper leg begins near the south side of Waimanalo Road and extends southward to the
longer lower leg of the channel which parallels the north side of Franklin D Roosevelt
W O 2746 20 Page 2
Road The drainage channel terminates adjacent to the Barbers Point Naval Air Station
Coral Pit a former borrow site for coral materials In general the lower channel bottom is
relatively flat and slopes gently down in the northern direction with an existing ground
surface elevation of about 30 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
The channel serves as both a detention basin and an infiltration basin To
supplement the capacity of the channel a series of 18 injection wells have been
constructed along the south portion of the drainage channel in 1993 The purpose of
injection wells is to increase the design capacity of the drainage channel by 50 percent
during a 100 year storm with a duration of 24 to 48 hours The general locations of the
injection wells are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 and 3
SYSTEM OPERATION
Kapolei Village Lower Channel forms a part of the drainage system for the
development of Villages of Kapolei Based on the current design concepts the storm
water run off from Kapolei Village 3 Makakilo Gulch and Makalapa Gulch flows into
Kapolei Golf Course currently under construction The golf course functions as a detention
basin while allowing some of the run off to infiltrate through surface percolation After
passing through the golf course the run off discharges into the drainage channel The
storm water run off from other development areas travels overland and through drainage
pipes to the drainage channel which will collect the run off
The drainage channel is also designed as a detention structure Run off entering
the channel is disposed of by infiltration through the sides and bottom of the channel In
addition 18 injection wells are constructed along the Lower Channel to increase the run off
disposal capacity of the drainage channel Based on the design criteria provided the
injection well system is intended to increase the design capacity of the drainage channel by
50 percent during a 100 year storm with a duration of 24 to 48 hours which means that the
injection well system should have a capacity of 24 445 gallons per minute

The ground surface elevation at the site at the time of injection well testing was
approximately 30 feet MSL Groundwater in the area was encountered at about 28 feet
below the existing channel bottom surface at the time of the injection well testing The
groundwater appears to consist of brackish water in an unconfined coralline formation
aquifer Based on area topography and the subsurface conditions encountered the down
gradient direction for groundwater appears to be toward the south
PERCOLATION TESTING METHODOLOGY
Before the subcontractor Roscoe Moss Hawaii Inc performed the tests they
cleaned the 18 injection wells to remove debris and silt accumulated at the bottom of the
wells due to the previous storms As we understood dry ice and sand pump were used to
perform cleaning The depths of the wells were measured before and after cleaning by the
subcontractor The results were summarized on Plate 5
Percolation testing was performed from April 15th to 30th 1998 Each injection well
was tested by injection of relatively clear water to confirm the subsurface permeability
conditions at the site The injected water was pumped from another injection well with a
submersible pump at a minimum of 200 feet from the injection well being percolation tested
The water was transported through a 6 inch diameter hose to the injection well being
tested The general design of the injection wells was shown on Plate 3 The galvanized
steel grating was removed from the top of the injection well A 6 inch diameter PVC pipe
was put into the well with angled well head connection at the top
A straight metal pipe with a paddle wheel flow sensor attached was connected
between the transporting hose and well head connection The flow sensor was used to
monitor the volume of water flow discharged in gallon By monitoring volume of water flow
and time elapsed average flow rate was estimated in gallon per minute The tests were
performed at a minimum of three different flow rates at which the water level stabilized for
approximately 5 minutes The fiow rate was increased to the maximum which the well could
discharge Otherwise the final flow rate of the test corresponded to the maximum
achievable flow rate from the submersible pump and the withdrawn well The maximum
flow rate attainable was taken to the flow at which the maximum achievable water level was
W O 2746 20 Page 4
held constant for a period of time steady state condition The maximum flow rates
attainable for the percolation tests were typically above 500 gallons per minute gom and
the maximum flow rate used was about 933 gpm Each test lasted about four hours
During percolation testing of the injection well number 6 the injection well capacity
appeared to be low Therefore water was discharged to the top of the well and was
stopped Then water depth from the top of the well was measured with time presented on
Plate 6 6
Before the injection of water the depth of the well and water below the top of the
well were measured A Geokon vibrating wire piezometers with datalogger system
installed about 40 feet below the top of the well and well sounder were used to measure the
depth of water below the top of the well The readings were recorded with respect to time
by dataloggers The depth of water was referenced from the top of the
injection wells

Background Information
The project location is within the existing Villages of Kapolei site in the Kapolei area on
the island of Oahu Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The Housing
Community Development Corporation of Hawaii is repairing the damaged area of lower channel
The subject project consists of the construction of repairing and restoration of the channel slope
approximately 780 linear feet 2 853 cubic yard excavation and 954 cubic yard embankment
The total area to be graded is approximately 1 8 acres The approximate subdivision layout is
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by a surficial layer of
weathered coral reef formation consisting of coral sand and gravel locally mixed with reddish
brown sandy silt or reddish brown clayey silt which are believed to be alluvium deposited in old
stream channels These materials generally have low permeability characteristics The
Housing Community Development Corporation of Hawaii
W O 2746 20 Page 2
earthwork for the repairing construction generally included necessary cuts and fills the low
channel to the original finished grades compaction and coordination of the grading and slope
protection
Earthwork Observation and Testing
performed the earthwork operations for the project At the on
site of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits were generally cleared and grubbed of
channel invert including the removal of soil geoweb and geotextile fabric which have collapsed
from the failed channel slopes Materials generated from the clearing operations were disposed
of at a designated area Following the clearing and grubbing operations the excavated
subgrades of the channel were moisture conditioned and compacted by a backhoe with a
compaction wheel boom attachment All graded areas within the slope repair area were
stabilized with grouted rip rap underlain with geotextile fabric and 3 inch diameter PVC weep
holes
The fills and backfills needed for the earthwork generally consisted of on site materials
generated from the excavations Samples of the fill and backfill materials utilized during
construction were obtained and transported to our office for laboratory testing The maximum
dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor The test results are summarized on Plate 3 of this report
During the fill placement operations the fill materials were generally placed in thin lifts
ranged from 4 to 12 inches brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor The channel slope subgrades were also compacted to
90 percent relative compaction Periodic field density tests were generally taken at every station
about 100 foot intervals during the fill placement and subgrade preparation operations to
confirm the degree of compaction and moisture content of the fill material achieved during
construction
A total of 84 field density tests were performed using the nuclear probe method in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designations D 2922 and D 3017 The results of the
compaction tests performed during the fill placement operations are summarized on Plates 4 1
through 4 5 of this report These tests were performed on the compacted subgrades and
engineered fills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes where available These locations and elevations should
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be
unsatisfactory were generally reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the results of the referenced tests and observations of our field representatives
during our site visits it is our professional opinion that the earthwork operations tested and
observed by Geolabs Hawaii for the channel repairing were performed in general accordance
with the intent of the project plans specifications and the above referenced report
