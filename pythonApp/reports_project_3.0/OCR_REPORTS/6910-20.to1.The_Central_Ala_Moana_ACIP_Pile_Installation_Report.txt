In accordance with the scope of services outlined in our revised fee proposal 
dated May 6, 2019, Geolabs observed the ACIP pile foundation installation operations 
for The Central Ala Moana project in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. This letter 
report summarizes our geotechnical observations during the ACIP pile foundation 
installation for the project. The deep foundation support system of the new high-rise 
building consisted of 209 24-inch diameter ACIP piles, and the parking garage 
consisted of 173 18-inch diameter ACIP piles. 
Geolabs provided geotechnical consultation for The Central Ala Moana project, 
and the findings and recommendations are presented in the referenced report. The 
purpose of our geotechnical observations was to monitor the geotechnical engineering 
aspects of the ACIP pile foundation installation for conformance with the intent of the 
recommendations presented in our report. In addition, we provided “Special Inspection” 
services (as stipulated in Section 1704 of the 2006 International Building Code) by 
observing the ACIP pile foundation installation for general conformance with the project 
plans and specifications and the geotechnical engineering report.

PROJECT DESCRIPTION 
The project site is situated at the southeast corner of the Kapiolani Boulevard 
and Kona Iki Street intersection in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. The project 
involves the development of a new 43-story high-rise building with about 513 residential 
units. The project also will include a parking structure with 11 parking levels on the 
makai side of the residential tower. The project area was formerly occupied by existing 
single-story structures, and the site was an on-grade parking lot covered by asphaltic 
concrete pavement. The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project 
Location Map, Plate 1. 
Based on the geotechnical engineering report referenced above for the project, 
we recommended supporting The Central Ala Moana project on a deep foundation 
system consisting of a combination of 18-inch and 24-inch diameter augered 
cast-in-place (ACIP) piles with allowable compressive load capacities ranging from 400 
to 1,200 kips (design load) per pile. The ACIP piles would extend through the fill 
material, soft to medium stiff silty clays and loose to medium dense clayey gravel, and 
loose to dense sandy and gravelly coralline detritus and soft to medium hard coral 
formation with design pile lengths ranging from about 60 to 110 feet below the bottom of 
pile cap elevation.  
BI-DIRECTIONAL STATIC PILE LOAD TEST 
In order to evaluate the adequacy of the recommended design lengths of the 
ACIP pile foundation, bi-directional static pile load tests were conducted on two 
dedicated ACIP load test piles, one 18-inch diameter and one 24-inch diameter load test 
piles, designated as Test Pile No. TP-18R2 and TP-24R1, respectively. To better 
understand the load transfer mechanism at the pile-soil interface, each load test pile 
was instrumented with vibrating wire embedment strain gages installed at various 
depths along the length of the load test pile. The 18-inch diameter load test pile was 
installed to a depth of about 63 feet and the 24-inch diameter load test pile was installed 
to a depth of about 114 feet below the existing ground surface. Each load test pile was 
loaded in general accordance with ASTM D8169M “Deep Foundations Under Bi-
Directional Static Axial Compressive Load” Procedure A Quick Test. The bi-directional 
static pile load test results are summarized in the following reports. 
“Final Report of AFT-Cell Bi-Directional Static Load Testing Test Pile 
TP-18, The Central at Ala Moana, Honolulu, Oahu, Hawaii” dated 
August 8, 2019. 
“Final Report of AFT-Cell Bi-Directional Static Load Testing Test Pile 
TP-24, The Central at Ala Moana, Honolulu, Oahu, Hawaii” dated 
August 6, 2019. 
Based on the static pile load test results, the 18-inch diameter load test pile was 
loaded to a maximum test load of 708 kips and the 24-inch diameter load test pile was

PRODUCTION PILE INSTALLATION 
The General Contractor for the project and the ACIP pile installation 
subcontractor was Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company. The production ACIP pile 
installation program, consisting of installing 209 18-inch and 173 24-inch diameter ACIP 
piles, was conducted from July 9 through October, 4, 2019 under the full-time 
observations by our field engineer. A Manitowoc 999 crane equipped with a hanging 
lead rotary drill with hollow-stem augers was used to install the production piles. 
Automatic monitoring equipment was mounted on both drilling/pumping equipment with 
the intent of providing auger depth, grout volume, grout pressure, etc., on a 
near-continuous basis. 
During the pile installation operation, logs were maintained for each ACIP pile, 
which included information pertaining to pile number, date installed, depth of pile, grout 
volume used, grout return depth, etc. The locations of the production ACIP piles 
installed are shown on the ACIP Pile Identification Plan, Plates 2.1 and 2.2. The 
production ACIP pile installation summary is presented by pile number on Plates 3.1 
through 3.13 of this letter report. 
Based on our observations of the equipment performance and subsurface 
conditions encountered, it is our professional opinion that the production ACIP piles 
have been installed to sufficient depths and/or resistance to provide the design load 
capacities of 400 and 1,200 kips per pile with a factor of safety of 2.0.
