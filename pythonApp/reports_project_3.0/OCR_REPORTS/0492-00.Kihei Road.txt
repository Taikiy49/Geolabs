General Geology
The island of Maui was formed by the flows of two volcanos that
eventually joined and gives the island its present configura
tion The site is underlain by flows from Haleakala Volcano
which was later covered with flows from the Kula Series during
the Pleistocene Period This later flow contains many inter
stratified ash soil layers During the late Pleistocene Period
the land was undergoing an emergent period where the sea stood
about 60 feet lower Extensive lithified calcareous marine
sands were blown inland during this time
Subsurface Conditions
To explore the subsurface conditions at the site nine borings
were drilled at the locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
A more detailed description of the field exploration and lab
oratory testing is contained in the Appendix which also
includes the log of each boring and the results of our labora
tory tests
Our borings revealed that the Site is immediately underlain by
silty sands that vary from 2 to 5 feet in depth The deposition
of these marine sands are fairly uniform on the lower half of
the site It appears that after a massive volcanic flow a
small depression eroded in the vicinity of Boring 2 This was
filled with volcanic sands which were then in turn partially

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the site is suitable for the proposed
construction with conventional foundations supported on the
Site s silty sands decomposed rock or basalt rock Due to
the variation of subsurface conditions adjacent footings may
be supported on different materials The multi story structures
should be supported on the decomposed rock or basalt while the
two story structures could be supported on the surface silty
sands

Foundation
The proposed buildings may be supported on spread or continuous
footings bearing in undisturbed Site soils The bearing value
will vary depending upon the material the footings are founded
upon Footings for the multi story structures should be founded
in the decomposed rock and extend a minimum of three feet below
the lowest adjacent finished grade in natural undisturbed soils
or properly compacted fill and have a minimum width of 24
inches Footings for the two story high or less structures
could be founded in the upper silty sands and can extend a mini
mum of two feet below the lowest adjacent finished grade and
have a minimum width of 18 inches Footings bearing in the
basalt rock should be placed a minimum of one foot into this
competent material Bearing capacity curves have been deve
loped for footings with various depths bearing on the different
site soils and are presented on Plate 3

As the depth of the footings in sand and decomposed rock are
increased the bearing values may be increased to the maximum
recommended value
A one third increase in the values shown on Plate 3 may be used
when considering wind or seismic loadings
When footings are founded behind retaining walls the influ
ence of footing pressure can be assumed to be on a 45 degree
angle from the bottom edge of the footing If this influence
pressure should fall above the base of the retaining wall
then the wall should be designed to resist the horizontal com
ponent of this pressure If the influence pressures should
be over the wall footing then the footing should be designed
to withstand the vertical component of the footing pressure
where it meets the wall footing
Because of the variation in subsurface conditions it is re
commended that a qualified soils engineer inspect the footing
excavations to confirm the conditions on which our recommen
dations are based
Lateral Loads
The lateral wind or seismic loads on the buildings may be re
sisted by either passive pressures or by friction along the
base of footings Passive pressures may be assumed to be equal

Settlements
Maximum ultimate settlements on the order of one quarter inch
may be anticipated for footings bearing in the silty sand or
decomposed rock Settlements on footings bearing in competent
basalt rock will be negligible Therefore maximum differen
tial settlements between adjacent footings bearing on sand and
rock will be one quarter inch Differential settlements be
tween footings bearing in similar material will be minimal
Floor Slabs
The slab on grade floor can be supported on natural undisturbed
soil or engineered fill or on a combination of both In any
areas where dampness in the floor slab would be undesirable or
where moisture susceptible floor coverings are to be used a
minimum of four inches of granular type material should be
placed to act as a capillary break In addition a moisture
proof membrane should be placed over the granular material to
act as a vapor barrier The membrane should be covered by one
inch of sand to protect it during construction and to aid in
curing the concrete

Site Preparation
After all debris has been removed the ground surface should
be stripped to remove all vegetation All fill areas exposed
should be scarified to a minimum depth of six inches and brought
to near optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 of
maximum density as determined by ASTM D 1557 70 Fill may then
be placed in maximum nine inch lifts and also compacted to a
minimum of 90 of maximum density In cut areas at grade the
exposed surface should also be compacted before building con
struction We recommend that the site preparation and grading
on this project be done under supervision of the soils engineer
to ascertain the fills are placed and compacted properly

APPENDIX
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface soil conditions at the site were explored by
drilling nine test borings utilizing trailer mounted rotary
auger equipment with rock coring attachments The depths of
the borings ranged from 9 to 20 feet below the existing
ground surface The locations of the borings are shown on
the Site Plan Plate 1
The soils and rock were classified by visual and textural
examinations in the field by our engineer who continuously
supervised the drilling operations The classifications
were verified by inspection and testing in the laboratory
All soils were classified in accordance with the Unified
Soil Classification System The description of rocks is based
on local geologic terminology Graphical representations of
the soils and rocks encountered are presented on the Boring
Logs Plates A 1A through A 1I Soil samples were obtained
by driving a sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a
distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler
and the percent of recovery of rock cores are also shown on
the Log of Borings at the appropriate sample depth
