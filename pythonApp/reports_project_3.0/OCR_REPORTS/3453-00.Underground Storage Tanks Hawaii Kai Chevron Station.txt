Backfilling Observation and Compaction Testing Report
Underground Storage Tanks
Hawaii Kai Chevron Station
Honolulu Oahu Hawaii
Presented herewith is our report on the backfilling observation and compaction
testing services rendered in conjunction with the removal of the underground storage tanks
at the Hawaii Kai Chevron Station in Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The above
work was performed from May 16th to June 30th
Excavation and Backfilling Observation
The excavation backfilling and compaction works were performed by Kagami
Incorporated the general contractor for the subject project Equipment utilized for these
operations included a backhoe a front end loader a bulldozer two vibratory rollers and
a plate tamper
Excavation was performed at two sites 1 former driveway and planter area along
northwest edge of site and 2 former fuel islands service station building and UST
locations
Site 1
An underground storage tank site approximately 20 by 60 feet in plan dimension
was excavated to a depth of approximately 6 feet below the existing ground surface In
areas where unsuitable materials were encountered the excavation was extended deeper
by an additional 2 feet Groundwater was encountered at a depth of approximately 6 feet
below the existing ground surface A 1 to 2 foot thick layer of 3 inch minus rocks was
placed at the bottom of the excavation and capped with a 12 inch thick layer of 3B crushed
rocks Compaction of the 3 inch minus rocks and 3B crushed rocks which serve as a
stabilization layer was performed by tamping with the excavator bucket Gravel fill was
then placed on top of the rock layer in 6 to 12 inch increments and compacted lightly by

Site 2
An area approximately 70 by 90 feet in plan dimension originally containing various
structures was excavated to a depth of approximately 6 feet below the existing ground
surface in areas where unsuitable materials were encountered the excavation was
extended deeper by an additional 2 feet
Backfilling of this area was performed in two increments The first increment
consisted of an area approximately 53 by 70 feet in plan dimension The second
increment approximately 37 by 70 feet in plan dimension was backfilled after a period of
approximately 4 weeks following completion of the first increment During the first
increment an area of approximately 32 by 34 feet in plan dimension was backfilled
temporarily for future installation of another fuel storage tank The bottom of the
excavation was observed to be approximately 6 feet below the existing ground surface with
the groundwater level at a depth of about 5 feet A 12 inch layer of 6 inch surge rocks
and 12 inch layer of 3B crushed rocks was placed at the bottom of the excavation for
stabilization purposes Compaction was performed by tamping the stabilization layer with
the excavator bucket Gravel fill was placed on top of the rock layer in 6 to 12 inch
increments and compacted lightly by a 15 ton roller without the vibratory action The
gravel fill appeared to be dense and unyielding with no pumping or wave like action
observed during the course of compaction The rock and gravel fill served as a
stabilization pad and working platform prior to backfilling with the excavated on site
materials
During the second increment excavation and backfilling were performed in a similar
manner as the first increment However in addition to using the untreated native materials
for backfilling excavated on site materials treated at Hawaii Remediation and Recycling
Center at Campbell Industrial Park were used to backfill this side of the excavation
Compaction Testing
Prior to the backfilling operations representative samples of the on site excavated
materials both treated and untreated were obtained and returned to our laboratory for
testing to evaluate their physical properties and suitability for backfilling purposes Sieve
analyses plasticity index moisture density relations and California Bearing Ratio tests
were performed and the test results are presented on Plate A 1

Untreated on site excavated materials consisting of silty sands with gravels were
used in the backfilling of the excavations except for the top 2 feet of backfill at the second
increment at Site 2 The backfill was generally placed in 9 inch maximum loose lifts from
a depth of approximately 5 feet below the existing ground surface Rocks greater than 6
inches in maximum dimension plastic sheets debris and other unsuitable materials were
removed prior to compaction works utilizing a 10 ton vibratory roller In place density tests
of the backfills were performed on each lift using a Troxler nuclear density test gauge in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922 81 Standard Test Methods for Density
of Soil and Soil Aggregate In Place by Nuclear Methods Shallow Depth The backfill
was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction except for the first lift
where the minimum compaction requirement is 90 percent The backfilling compaction
and testing procedures were repeated for each lift until the excavation was backfilled to a
depth of approximately 12 inches below the final grade Base course materials for the
wearing course of the pavement section will be placed by the contractor at a later date
Based on our visual observations and test results presented on Plates B 1 through B 3
the backfill materials appeared to be dense and unyielding Copies of the daily field report
Plates C 1 through C 9 are also attached for your reference
