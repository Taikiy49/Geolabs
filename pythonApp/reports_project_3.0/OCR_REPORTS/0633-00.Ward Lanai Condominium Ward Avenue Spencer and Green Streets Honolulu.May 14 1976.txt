INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a foundation investigation
for the Ward Lanai Condominium a 7 story condominium deveiop
ment located on Ward Avenue between Green and Spencer Streets
in the Punchbowl area in Honolulu Hawaii The purpose of the
subsurface soil investigation was to identify existing soii con
ditions in order to formulate data for design and construction
of foundations
SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is approximately 164 feet long along Ward Avenue and
100 feet wide along the Spencer and Green Streets frontage
W
Page 2
The site is presently occupied by three residential homes
Blockwalls about 2 to 3 feet high divide the site into
three parcels The mauka Spencer Street parcel is about
4 feet below street level and it gradually slopes toward
the Green Street direction from elevation 129 to elevation
122 The two lower makai parcels are 2 to 34 feet lower
The site may have been partly graded in the past Cut was
made to provide level pads for the existing structures
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The present plan is to construct a 7 story high apartment
building 60 by 103 feet in plan Columns will be used to
support the building C M U retaining walls will be uti
lized for the lower Tevel parking decks The plans indicated
that the parking structure will have an entry driveway to the
second deck from Spencer Street and Green Street will provide
access to the lower deck
Site grading will involve a maximum of 4 feet of fill under
the Spencer Street entrance ramp and about 6 feet of cut in
the lower parking deck area along Spencer Street The lower
parking slab subgrade of elevation 118 96 would be at existing
grades
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
Three test borings were drilled to depths of 10 to 20 feet in
order to explore subsurface conditions and to obtain samples
for laboratory testing Three additional hand augered borings

were dug to further identify soil conditions between existing
structures Detailed information on field exploration and
laboratory testing is covered in the appendix along with
boring logs and laboratory test results Locations of borings

Soil Conditions
The site is blanketed by volcanic soils originating from the
most recent eruption of Punchbowl Crater Ash and cinder
exploded into the air during the eruption and they have de
posited on the Punchbowl fissure surface Upper surface
material has been weathered down to form part of the existing
clayey layer
Beneath this 3 to 4 foot surface soil are the ash and cinder
deposits of silts and sands sizes This 5 to 9 feet thick
layer is underlain by deep deposit of dense Black Sand or
cinder Generally the Black Sand is slightly cemented The
Clay so1ls are stiff and contain traces of coral Sand che
upper ash cinder soils found over the entire site are of medium
density In Boring 2 the upper brown cinder was very loose
which probably indicated previous lot fill or utility line
backfilling The Black Sand at the site is medium dense to
very dense depending on the amount of cementation
Groundwater was not noted in the bore holes at the time of
field exploration

DISCUSSION RECOMMENDATIONS
The subsurface conditions encountered during the exploratory
soil investigation were relatively good from a soils and foun
dation engineering viewpoint Spread and wall footings bearing
on the undisturbed in situ sandy soil will provide adequate
support for the proposed apartment parking structures
Foundations
Spread and wall footing foundation bearing below the in situ
surface clay soil on the underlying dense and undisturbed sandy
soil can be designed for an allowable bearing pressure of 6 000
pounds per square foot The bearing value can therefore be
000 pounds per square foot when considering
seismic and wind loadings Footing foundations should bear
a minimum of four feet below the adjacent final grade
It is estimated that the total settlements for the highrise
structure should be less than one inch with a differential
settlement of less than one half of an inch
Where existing or abandoned utility lines are located under
the foundation the footing should either step down or be
designed to bridge over the loose backfill areas
During construction if any abandoned cesspool or loose utiiity
backfill is located below the footing foundation the sludge
and soft materials should be removed down to firm ground and
replaced with lean concrete up to its design footing bottom
elevation

Slab On Grade
Slightly expansive soils were encountered during the subsurface
explorations Swell tests on the near surface clay soil indi
cated about 3 swell with a surcharge load of 60 p s f This
percentage is generally considered slightly expansive
Four inch layer of base course is recommended below the lower
level parking concrete slab Where the existing surface clay
soil is encountered at the subgrade level within 12 inches
below the base course layer it should be removed from the
slab area and replaced by non expansive compacted select
backfill The on site sandy soil from the footing excavation
can be used for the above select backfill All structural
backfill should be placed in 9 inch layers and mechanically
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density esta
blished by ASTM Designation D1557 72T Prior to placing this
granular backfill the clayey subgrade soil in the undercut
area should be wetted to above its optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum 90 compaction
Retaining Wall
The retaining wall at the northern portion of the lower lJevel
parking area should be designed to receive active lateral pre
ssure equivalent to a fluid density of 40 pounds per cubic
foot if on site sandy soils are used as backfill material A
friction factor of 0 45 can be utilized to determine sliding
resistance of the retaining wall foundations

Page 6
For the lower retaining wall along Spencer Street additional
lateral forces on the retaining wall should be considered
according to the anticipated street traffic load from above
Weepholes or a subsurface drainage system should be incorporated
to prevent the build up of excessive hydrostatic pressure behind
the wall
Earthwork and Grading
The proposed site grading will entail some cuts at the northern
portion of the lower level parking area In general the high
rise structure will be constructed essentially at grade Dis
turbed soil due to the removal of existing trees utility lines
and structures should be replaced with compacted backfill
New fill varying from 1 to 4 feet in thickness will be required
at the northern portion of the site The on site soils are
acceptable fill materials However the non expansive on site
sandy soil should be stockpiled for backfill within the upper
12 inches below the base course layer in the slab and pavement
areas
All areas to receive fill should be undercut to remove the
surface vegetation topsoil Prior to the placement of new
fill the exposed surface should be wetted to near optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 maximum
dry density Additional fill should then be placed in 9 inch
horizontal lifts and compacted to the above specified density

Slopes
Fill slope of two horizontal to one vertical 2 1 ratio
or flater may be used The on site clayey and sandy soils
can be cut at essentially vertical slopes for short term
construction conditions up to 6 feet deep Beyond that
depth unsupported excavation should be sloped back at one
horizontal to one vertical slope ratio
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the foundation excavations for the
apartment be inspected by the soil engineer prior to the
placement of concrete or reinforcing to assure that adequate
bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
During construction if any loose utility backfill is located
below the building construction area the soft material should
be removed and replaced with compacted fill under the observation
of a soil engineer
The utility trench backfilling and earthwork operations should
be monitored by the soil engineer Construction inspection
should include the necessary testing to assure that the proper
degree of compaction is consistently achieved
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections
