PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is in the Kailua Kona area in the District of North Kona on the
Island of Hawaii The project site is an irregularly shaped parcel south of
Queen Kaahumanu Highway north of Henry Street and east of the existing Lanihau
Shopping Center Phase development A Site Plan is provided on Plate 2
We understand that it is desired to expand the existing Lanihau Shopping Center
Phase by constructing additional commercial buildings and associated infrastructure
at the project site We also understand that about 695 parking spaces will be provided
at the site In addition access roadways underground utilities and drywells for storm
water disposal will be constructed at the site
Based on the existing topography and the design finished floor elevations
anticipated for the new buildings we envision that grading consisting of cuts and fills on
the order of about 15 to 25 feet thick will likely be required to achieve the design
finished grades

The other areas of the project site generally consist of undisturbed natural ground
Based on our field observations the project site generally is underlain by pahoehoe lavas
at the ground surface However it should be noted that piles of concrete tires and other
deleterious material were scattered around the project site The undisturbed areas at the
site were generally moderately vegetated with dense shrubs and some trees
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by a surface
layer of volcanic ash over dense basalt formations pahoehoe basalt with interbedded
clinker layers The volcanic ash layer generally consist of sandy silts with some gravel and
cobbles and was generally less than about 3 feet in thickness In some areas volcanic ash
was not observed at the ground surface
The volcanic ash where present was generally underlain by dense basalt
formation pahoehoe basalt with interbedded clinker layers extending down to the
maximum depth explored of about 32 feet below the ground level In general the dense
basalt formations encountered were hard to very hard and relatively unweathered to
moderately weathered
Fill materials were previously placed at portions of the project site At these areas
our borings encountered cobbles and boulders extending down to about 17 feet below the
ground surface In addition we encountered numerous voids of about 30 inches in vertical
height at various depths in the borings It should be noted that cavities and or voids are
commonly present in the basaltic lava flows in the vicinity and therefore should be
expected during construction of the project
We did not encounter groundwater in the borings drilled at the time of our field
exploration However groundwater levels may change due to seasonal precipitation
surface water runoff and other factors

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be

Over Excavation of Basalt Formation
Based on the preliminary plans provided we envision that the subgrades of
Offices A and B will straddle across cut fill transition Based on the information provided
and our analyses we envision that the majority of the subgrade materials under the new
building structures will likely consist of compacted fills placed during mass grading The
cut areas of the building subgrades will likely consist of dense basalt formation
In general the new structures should be constructed on similar foundation
subgrade materials in order to provide for uniform support across the structure and to
reduce the potential for appreciable differential settlements across the buildings Where
the new structures will straddle varying subgrade conditions special provisions should be

Subgrade Preparation
After the required over excavation of undocumented fills and basalt formation we
recommend ripping the fill areas of the project site to a depth of about 3 feet below the
existing ground surface at the commencement of grading The purpose of ripping the fill
subgrades is to assist in detecting and collapsing cavities and or voids contained in the
porous near surface materials encountered at the project site As a minimum ripping of
the ground surface should extend at least 3 feet laterally beyond the fill areas cut fill
transition areas
After the fill surfaces have been ripped to a depth of about 3 feet below the ground
surface the ground should be leveled with the use of a Caterpillar D 10 bulldozer or
similar size equipment working the surface a minimum of four passes to provide a
relatively level surface After leveling of the rugged surfaces with a bulldozer we
recommend proof rolling the fill subgrades with a large vibratory drum roller minimum
20 tons static weight for a minimum of eight passes to help detect and collapse
near surface cavities and or voids The vibratory drum roller should be operated at a
speed of about 300 feet per minute Yielding areas loose areas or cavities disclosed
during the clearing and proof rolling operations should be over excavated and backfilled
with compacted fill materials The depth of over excavation should be extended until
dense underlying materials are exposed and should be evaluated in the field by our
representative
As for the finished subgrades in cut areas we recommend proof rolling the cut
subgrades with a minimum 20 ton static weight vibratory drum roller for a minimum of
eight passes to help detect and collapse near surface cavities and or voids The vibratory
drum roller should also be operated at a speed of about 300 feet per minute The
requirement for ripping the ground surface by a depth of about 3 feet in fill areas may be
omitted for the cut subgrades In addition proof rolling of the subgrade with a Caterpillar
D 10 bulldozer for a minimum of four passes may be omitted in the cut subgrades
provided that the finished cut subgrades are relatively level to operate the vibratory drum
roller
The proof rolling operations should be performed in the presence of a Geolabs
representative Yielding areas loose areas or cavities disclosed during clearing and
proof rolling operations should be over excavated and backfilled with compacted fill
materials Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for
over excavation of the collapsed lava tube features and other features and backfilling
with compacted fill to account for variations in the over excavation and backfill quantities
It should be noted that ripping and proof rolling of subgrades in the basalt
formation will likely cause some compression of the porous near surface materials
Based on our observations we estimate that the prepared subgrades may end up about
6 to 18inches lower in elevation than the pre existing ground surface due to
compression of the porous near surface materials following the ripping and proof rolling

Fills and Backfills
In general we recommend capping the fill areas of the project site with a minimum
2 foot thick layer of structural fill materials Structural fill materials should consist of
well graded granular materials generally less than 6 inches in maximum dimension with
sufficient fines to prevent the occurrence of voids in the compacted mass In addition
structural fill materials should contain less than 30 percent particles passing the No 200
sieve by weight
Fill and backfill materials below the minimum 2 foot thick structural fill layer may
consist of general fill materials General fill and backfill materials should consist of
well graded granular materials the majority of which are less than 12 inches in size with
an absolute maximum dimension of 18 inches Materials between 12 and 18 inches in
particle size should be limited to about 15 percent or less of the total volume of general fill
materials
Where over cutting of the finished cut subgrades occur during the mass grading
operations backfilling of the over cut area should be treated as fill areas Therefore the
above recommendations pertaining to fill materials should apply to the over cut areas and
the fill materials required for backfilling will depend on the depth of over cut below the
finished subgrade
The excavated on site materials including undocumented fills may be used as a
source of fill materials structural or general fill provided that they are free of vegetation
debris deleterious materials and other unsuitable materials and processed to meet the
above gradation requirements for structural and general fill as appropriate If the
excavated materials do not contain sufficient fines to produce a well graded material
off site borrow or on site rock crushing of larger rock fragments or boulders should be
considered to provide the required gradation and particle sizes to develop a well graded
material
Imported fill materials if required should be free of organics and deleterious
materials and should be suitable for the intended use based on the recommendations for
fill materials provided in this report structural or general fill In addition a Geolabs
representative should observe and or test the imported fill materials a minimum of
seven days prior to being transported to the project site for the intended use
Excavated on site materials including undocumented fills consisting of boulders
less than 3 feet in maximum dimension may be used as rock fill provided that the top of
the rock fill layer is placed at least 4 feet below the finished subgrades as shown on
Plate 3 In general the placement of boulders in fills should be performed in accordance
with the procedures recommended in the following Boulder Disposal subsection

Boulder Disposal
Boulders i e rock fragments larger than 12 inches in maximum size but less
than 3 feet in largest dimension encountered during site grading may be used in rock
fills provided that the following recommendations are followed
1 Boulders less than 3 feet in largest dimension may be utilized as rock fills
provided that the top of the rock fill layer is placed at least 4 feet below the
finished subgrades Boulders larger than 5 feet in maximum dimension
should not be used as rock fill unless reduced in size
2 Boulders greater than 18 inches in largest dimension should not be
exposed at the fill slope face Boulder placement should be set back a
lateral distance of at least 6 feet from the edge of the fill slope face
3 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in the proposed
utility line alignment roadways and within the depths of the proposed
utility lines to reduce the potential for encountering the boulders during
excavation for the utility trenches
Compaction of the boulders in the rock fills should be performed in accordance
with the recommendations presented in the following Fill Placement and Compaction
Requirements subsection It should be noted that disposal of boulders in the general fill
material will require that the entire fill mass be compacted and properly choked off at the
surface and each layer of rock fill to reduce the potential for migration of fines through
the voids between boulders
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
Structural fill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in
loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and compacted to
at least 95 percent relative compaction General fills and backfills should be placed in level
lifts not exceeding 18 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of
the maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density Compaction should be accomplished by sheepsfoot rollers
vibratory rollers or other types of acceptable compaction equipment
Rock fills should be placed in lifts not exceeding the maximum dimension of the
majority of the rocks or 5 feet whichever is less Placement should be by haul trucks
traversing previously placed lifts and dumping at the edge of the currently placed lift
Spreading of the rock fill should be by a large bulldozer to facilitate seating of the rocks
The rock fill should be watered heavily during placement by water trucks traversing in front

Excavation
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by surface
volcanic ash layer over basalt formation We anticipate that the volcanic ash and porous
basalt formation near the ground surface may be readily excavated with normal heavy
excavation equipment such as excavators and ripped with large bulldozers However
some of the deeper excavations into the dense pahoehoe lava will likely require the use of
hoerams or chipping
The above discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are based
on our visual observation of the existing basalt formation and field data from the borings
performed at the project site Contractors should be encouraged to examine the site
conditions and the subsurface data to make their own reasonable and prudent
interpretation

Steepened Cut Slopes
Based on the preliminary grading plans provided we understand that cut slopes of
up to about 25 feet may be required to achieve design grades In areas requiring major
cuts we understand that it is planned to terrace the slopes in 10 foot height increments
with a steeper slope inclination In general we believe cut slopes that expose dense basalt
formation may be designed with a steeper vertical slope inclination of or flatter
provided that the following provisions are followed
The steepened cut slopes should be excavated with care to not overcut the slopes
and to provide a relatively smooth cut slope face A fiber reinforced shotcrete facing of at
least 4 inches thick should be provided on the face of the steepened cut slope face to
reduce the potential for raveling and erosion of surface materials Consideration should be
given to draping a wire mesh over the face of steepened cut slopes to help assist in
placement of the shotcrete facing
The steepened cut slopes should be well drained to reduce the build up of
hydrostatic pressures behind the shotcreted face In general strips of prefabricated
drainage product such as MiraDrain or EnkaDrain should be placed on the steepened cut
slope face prior to placement of shotcrete The prefabricated drainage product should be
placed for the full height of the steepened cut slope and have a minimum width of 2 feet
placed at a maximum 8 foot center to center spacing In addition weepholes should also
be provided along the bottoms of the steepened cut slopes at a maximum 8 foot
center to center spacing to allow for drainage of water trapped behind the shotcrete facing
The prefabricated drainage product should also be hydraulically connected to a perforated
pipe placed at the base of the wall
The performance of the steepened cut slopes depends significantly on the type
and condition of rock formations encountered Due to the potential presence of loose
clinker and highly fractured pahoehoe basalt it is imperative that our representative
monitor and map the steepened cut slopes during construction to observe whether the
exposed cut slope materials are consistent with our assumptions or if remedial
measures would be required Where loose clinker or highly fractured pahoehoe basalt is
encountered the cut slopes may need to be flattened Therefore provisions to flatten

Fill Slopes
In general permanent fill slopes may be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V
or flatter This assumes that the fill slopes will be constructed using well graded granular
materials structural and general fills In addition fills placed on slopes steeper than
5H 1V should be benched A typical keying and benching detail is provided on Slope
Detail Plate 4
In general the filling operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in
level horizontal compacted layers in accordance with the above fill placement
recommendations Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the
design slope ratio to obtain a well compacted slope face The fill slope face should be
finished to a relatively smooth and well compacted surface The fill slope face should be
free of voids which would allow erosion and migration of fines to occur In addition
surface water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope planting should
be provided as soon as possible to reduce the potential for significant erosion of the
finished slopes
For fill slopes steeper than 3H 1V with a vertical height greater than 30 feet we
recommend that a minimum 8 foot wide bench be provided at the mid point of the slope to
reduce the potential for significant erosion of the slope due to surface water runoff Where
slope benches are provided concrete lined swales should be provided on the benches to
collect and divert surface water from the slopes to appropriate discharge outlets

Based on our field percolation tests the subsurface materials generally have
coefficient of permeability k of about 6 3 x 10 and 4 8 x 10 feet per minute which
correspond to about 3 2 x 10 and 2 5 x 10 centimeters per second at the locations
tested Based on the results of our field percolation tests drywell capacities may range
from about 1 cfs to over 10 cfs for a 6 foot diameter 20 foot deep drywell
It should be emphasized that the hydraulic conductivity of the on site materials is
highly variable due to the presence or absence of significant voids in the lava or the
presence of embedded clinker seams which greatly affect the disposal capacity of a
drywell In general the greater the number of clinker seams and or voids encountered by
the drywell excavation the higher the overall permeability and disposal capacity
We understand that the County of Hawaii allows drywell capacity of up to 6 cfs for
design purposes For drywells designed with relatively high capacities provisions to
extend the depths of the proposed drywells and or to construct additional drywells should
be incorporated in the contract documents and UIC permit application
