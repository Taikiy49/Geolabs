CONCLUSIONS RECOMMENDATIONS
Based upon the results of field investigations laboratory
testing engineering analyses and office research the
following conclusions and recommendations are presented
Due to non uniform materials including large boulders and
relatively loose condition of fill it is our opinion that
it would_not be prudent to build on Lots 133 through 143 as
presently graded We further recommended that existing
surface materials beneath Lots 133 through 143 be partially
excavated and removed This will enable construction of
shallow raft or layer of compacted fill immediately beneath
building areas The possibility of scraping vegetation off
present fill and adding fill without any stabilization was
considered Due to the marginal condition of existing fill
materials we believe that it is in the ultimate best interests
of the project to partially remove and place surface compacted
layers for more uniform support Deep excavations and complete
removal of fill would be slow and very difficult note refusal
of test pits 10 and 11 and are believed to be unwarranted
On site boulders are so large that they should generally be
wasted below the toe of the slope boulders less than 18 inches
may be reused only in backfilling key or in deep interior
layers of fill Imported new fill consisting of select
predominantly fine grained soils properly compacted keyed
and benched into existing slope subgrade should be used for
