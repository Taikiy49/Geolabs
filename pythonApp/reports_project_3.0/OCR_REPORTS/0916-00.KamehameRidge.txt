GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
Field Exploration
A geologic reconnaissance was made of the project area and
the approximate limits of the exposed geologic features
and materials exposed on the existing slopes were determined
These delineations and their explanations are shown on the
attached Site Plan Plate 1
The seismic profiles and depths to subsurface layers were
first developed by calculating the change in shear wave
velocity with depth The computed shear wave velocities
in the upper materials were also correlated with test boring
data to establish the relative density and character of the
subsurface materials

Geology
The site is located just north of Koko Crater along a
ridge spur of the Koolau range The site marks the approx
imate northern extent of the extensive deposition of
volcanic tuff during the series of eruptions of Koko Crater
The eroded ridge line of Koolau basalt was covered by the
Koko tuff which basically retained the original ridge con
tours and completely filled some of the smaller depressions
and gullies At least two distinct episodes of Koko Head
volcanic activity can be distinguished
Covering nearly the entire area where it has not been
removed by excavation or erosion is a blanket of thin
bedded gray brown to black unconsolidated volcanic ash
The ash beds are generally thin varying from a few inches
to several feet thick and they dip generally in the direc
tion of the original topography Although generally map
ped as part of the Koko Crater volcanics these ash and
cinder formations may also be derived from the Kalama
Cinder cone to the east
Underlying the ash and cinder in the southern half of the
site is the tan Koko tuff The Koko tuff is very dense
and outcrops in several cuts and eroded surfaces see Site
Plan
W O 916 00 P 4
The Koolau basalt series underlie the Koko volcanics at
varying depths Outcrops of the basalt were mapped in the
cuts and along the top of the ridge as shown on the
Site Plan
General Soil Conditions
Based on the information obtained from the field exploration
three 3 different soil and rock types can be identified
and are described below
1 Near Surface Ash and Cinder Our borings disclosed
that near surface materials consisted of thinly bedded
volcanic ash and cinder which blanket most of site
The volcanic ash is basically uncemented gray brown
to black silt and fine sand A single two to four
feet thick bed of partially cemented cinders appears
to have covered the site about five to ten feet be
low the original ground surface Generally the vol
canic ash and cinder formation varies in thickness
from 7 0 to 10 0 feet below the existing ground sur
face The site grading excavation is believed to be
relatively easy in this upper material
2 Koko Crater Tuff Underlying the above near surface
volcanic ash and cinder material a tan volcanic tuff
was encountered The volcanic tuff is partially to
highly cemented ranging in thickness from two to
five feet and is rippable The volcanic tuff appears

3 Koolau Basalt All the borings drilled at the job

The vesicular basalt formation varies from moderate
to highly weathered rock with occasional few feet
thick hard basalt strata It is felt that these
relatively thin hard lava rock strata can be ripped
with heavy equipment However some localized hard
strata may require special treatment such as minor
blasting or jack hammering

Rippability
Generally our borings encountered up to about 15 feet
thick of ash cinder and volcanic tuff This upper 15 feet
of material could generally be ripped easily with heavy
equipment
The vesicular basalt underlying the above materials is
moderately to highly weathered with only an occasional
thin layer of hard basalt rock It is our opinion that
most of this basalt formation can be ripped with a HD 41
machine and possibly even with a D 9 caterpillar tractor
equipment or similar However due to the variability of
volcanic rocks local harder rock layers should be antici
pated and may require minor blasting to excavate
The above discussion regarding the rippability of underlying
basalt materials at the site is based on our visual exami
nation of NX size rock cores obtained from drilled borings
and interpretation of the seismic refraction survey data
We recommend that all prospective contractors bidding on
this site should examine the rock cores recovered from
our borings and make his own interpretation The recover
