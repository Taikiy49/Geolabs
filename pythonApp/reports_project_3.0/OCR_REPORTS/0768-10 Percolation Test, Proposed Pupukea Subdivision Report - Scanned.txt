This report presents the results of our percolation tests
at the preposed subdivision development site on Kamehameha
Highway Pupukea Oahu Hawaii
The purpose of this investigation is to obtain preliminary
subsurface soil information and to evaluate the in situ
Seepage characteristics of the site as required by the
Department of Health
Field Exploration
Two 4 inch diameter auger borings were drilled down to 21 5
feet and 26 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locetions shown on Plate 1
W P 2
The drilling operation was monitored by our engineer who also
logged the borings The boring logs are presented on Plates
A 1 and A 2
Subsurface Conditions
The two borings encountered different subsurface conditions
Boring No 1
This boring encountered a surface layer about 4 feet
thick of brown silty clay with sand and gravel fi11
over dense tan silty sand and coral to 21 5 feet the
depth drilled
Groundwater was not encountered in the boring
Boring No 3
This boring encountered about 18 feet of stiff silty
clay with some clay pockets and boulders alluvium
over decomposed rock and weathered rock to 26 5 feet
the depth drilled
Groundwater was encountered at 22 feet below the existing
ground surface

Percolation Test
A constant head percolation test was performed in both drill
holes The groundwater level below the existing ground was
first measured A constant fiow of water was then introduced
into the hole The change in water level with time was observed
and recorded When a constant head of water water level was
maintained for about 7 hour the test was terminated The
test results are summarized on Plate A 3
For Boring No 1 a constant head of water was developed at
16 feet below the existing ground surface with an inflow rate
of 7 5 gallons per minute
For Boring Neo 3 a constant head of water was developed at
18 2 feet below the existing ground surface with an inflow
rate of 3 gallons per minute
Ra
Based on the test results it is our opinion that the subsoils
at the project site can provide adequate percolation rates for
cesspool sewage disposal systems
The cesspools should extend into the silty sand and coral or
weathered rock layers
