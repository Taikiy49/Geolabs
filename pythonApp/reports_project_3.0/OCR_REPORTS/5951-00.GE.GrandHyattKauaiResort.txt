PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves the construction of a new solar system within the existing
parking lot of the Grand Hyatt Kauai Resort Spa in Koloa on the Island of Kauai Hawaii
Based on the current design concept the new solar system will be installed between the
parking stalls and covering will be installed at the existing parking lot The new solar
system consists of 37 5 foot long solar panels supported on posts The panels will be
installed at about 8 5 feet above the existing parking lot pavement surface We anticipate
that footing excavation and minimal site grading will be required for the construction The
project location is shown on the attached Site Plan Plate 2

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the relatively competent subsoil conditions encountered at the site it is
our opinion that shallow footing foundations may be used to support the proposed solar
system structures planned An allowable bearing pressure of up to 4 000 pounds per
square foot psf may be used for design of footing foundations bearing on the properly
on site materials Detailed discussions and recommendations for the project design are
presented in the following sections

Foundations
Generally we encountered stiff dense fill over highly weathered medium hard
basalt formation near the surface during our field exploration Based on the current
design concept and the generally competent subsoil condition encountered we believe
that spread footings may be used to support the solar system planned An allowable
bearing pressure of up to may be used for design of footing foundations
bearing on the stiff dense fill This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by seismic forces
A higher bearing capacity of may be used if the footings are deepened to bear
on the basalt formation
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grades Foundations next to utility trenches or easements should be
embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of
the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary
to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and
to reduce the potential for foundation settlement
The bottom of the footing excavations should be recompacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative compaction prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 We
estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for footings bearing on
the recompacted in situ soils or new compacted fills as recommended herein to be less
than 1 inch total with differential settlements on the order of one half 72 inch
Soft and or loose materials encountered at the bottom of the footing excavations
should be over excavated and the over excavation should be backfilled with concrete
or lean concrete or the bottom of footing may extend deeper to bear on the more
competent material In addition concrete for the footings should be placed neat against
the sides of the foundation excavations
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by friction developed between
the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth pressure acting
against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of friction of 0 4 may
be used for footing bearing on properly compacted in situ materials Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 350 pounds
per square foot per foot of depth pcf assuming that the soils around the footings are well
compacted The passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of the soil should be neglected
unless covered by pavements or slabs

Site Grading
We anticipate that the site grading will involve minimal cuts and fills in order to
achieve the design grades In general a Geolabs representative should monitor site
grading operations to observe whether the exposed soil conditions during excavation
are similar to those encountered in our exploration
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation debris deleterious material and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly off site Materials
generated from the demolition should also be removed and disposed properly off site
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing should be
over excavated to expose firm material The excavated soft and or organic soils should be
disposed properly off site and may not be used for fill materials The resulting
over excavated areas should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches
moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The over excavation should
then be backfilled with general fill materials The fill should be moisture conditioned to at
least 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of
soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by
dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
In the event shrinkage cracks are observed after compaction the soil should be
thoroughly moistened to close cracks or prepared again as recommended above The
excavated soft soils may be used as fill provided that it meets fill material requirements
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement
weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement
with well compacted fill A Geolabs representative should evaluate the need for
over excavation in the field
General fill materials required to raise the existing ground surface to the proposed
finished subgrades may consist of the excavated on site soils provided that they are free
of vegetation deleterious materials and rock fragments greater than 3 inches in maximum
dimension Structural fill should consist of granular material less than 3 inches in maximum
dimension with 10 to 30 percent fines passing No 200 sieve a CBR value greater than
20 and a CBR swell less than 1 percent Aggregate base course and subbase materials

Excavation
Based on our field exploration the project site is paved The pavement sections
are underlain by fill consisting of very stiff clay medium dense gravel and cobbles and
boulders overlying weathered basalt formation and clinker In general we believe that
conventional excavation equipment may be used to excavate the pavement sections fill
and underlying weathered basalt formation and clinker However excavations into
localized areas consisting of less weathered and hard cobbles and boulders and basalt
formation may require the use of hoe rams or chipping
The contractor should determine the method and equipment to be used for
excavation subject to practical limits and safety considerations The excavated soils should
not be stockpiled closer than a horizontal distance equal to the depth of the excavation
measured from the edge of the excavation in order to reduce the potential for appreciable
ground movement

Site Drainage
The finished grades outside the structures should be sloped to shed water away
from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Blockage or clogging of
drainage system could cause ponding of water at the structure and foundations
resulting in foundation and structure distress
Excessive landscape watering near the foundations should also be avoided
Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to
reduce water infiltration into the subsoil Gravel strips adjacent to slabs and foundations
should be avoided
Because ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
loss of strength of the near surface soils we believe these drainage requirements are
very important for the proper performance of the proposed structures Immediate backfill
of the foundation excavations after setting of concrete is strongly recommended to
reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and
should be maintained to drain surface run off away from foundations
