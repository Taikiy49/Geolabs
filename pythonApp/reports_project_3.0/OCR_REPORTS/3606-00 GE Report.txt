PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed vault is located adjacent to the Bank of Hawaii office in the
Princeville Shopping Center complex The existing building is a wood frame structure
with the finished floor level about 3 feet above the existing ground surface
The proposed vault will be approximately 10 by 25 feet in plan one story high and
consist of 12 inch thick concrete walls The estimated loading from the vault including
the weight of 3 feet of fill to raise the grade is 1000 pounds per square foot p s f
Subsurface Conditions
New borings were not drilled for this project due to budget constraints Instead
previous borings drilled in the vicinity of the site for the shopping center development
were used in the geotechnical engineering analyses Based on the previous borings the
subsurface conditions were generalized as follows

0 to 5 feet Stiff brown clayey silt fill and or residual soil
5 to 20 feet Medium stiff to soft brown clayey silt residual soil
Below 20 feet Stiff brown clayey silt residual soil saprolite

layer of fill over residual soil and saprolite derived from weathering of basalt The upper

On Grade Foundations
The existing bank building is a wood framed structure with a raised floor
approximately 3 feet above the ground surface In order to construct a conventional
on grade vault structure adjacent to the bank building approximately 3 feet of fill would be
required along with retaining walls to contain the fills Due to the soft wet residual soils
below the site estimated settlements due to 3 feet of fill is on the order of 1 to 3 inches
Most of the settlements would occur relatively rapidly however some long term
settlements should be expected The settlements would also affect adjacent structures
as the settlement limits would extend beyond the lateral limits of the new fill Additional
settlements would occur due to structural loads from the vault and storage Due to these
settlement concerns alternative foundation systems were evaluated
Raised Structural Slab on Footin
To eliminate the settlements due to the weight of the fill a structural slab system
was evaluated The structural slab would be supported by wall footings along the
perimeter of the vault Although the settlements from the fill would be eliminated
settlements would occur due to the structural loads on the wall footings Estimated
footing settlements for wall loads of about 3 kips per foot are on the order of 0 5 to
2 inches depending on the proximity of the underlying soft soil layer Site specific boring
information would be required to obtain a better estimate of the structural foundation
settlement
Drilled Pier Foundations
A raised structural slab supported by drilled pier foundations would eliminate the
settlement from the fill and reduce the settlement from the structural loads The drilled
piers would derive support from the friction between the soil and concrete shaft of the
drilled pier A 12 inch diameter pier 20 feet long could provide an allowable compressive
capacity of 11 kips each The drilled piers should consist of a 12 inch diameter hole
drilled with an auger a 4 inch diameter steel pipe placed in the hole for reinforcement and
filled with structural concrete The piers should be spaced a minimum of 3 feet apart
center to center

Design Review
Preliminary and final drawings and specifications for the proposed Bank of Hawaii
Vault project should be forwarded to Geolabs Hawaii for review and written comments
prior to advertisement for bidding This review is necessary to evaluate conformance of
the plans and specifications with the intent of the foundation recommendations provided
herein If this review is not made Geolabs Hawaii cannot be responsible for misinterpre

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs Hawaii be retained to provide geotechnical
engineering services during construction of the vault foundation This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to expedite
suggestions for design changes that may be required in the event that subsurface
conditions differ from those anticipated at the time this letter report was prepared The
recommendations provided in this report are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications
to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from field borings previously drilled in the vicinity of the project
Variations of subsoil conditions between the borings may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction is underway If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the recommendations
provided in this report
