In accordance with your request we have performed a soil and
foundation investigation for the subject project The approximate
location of the proposed site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general accordance with
our proposal of August 22 1986
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the subsurface soil
and water conditions at the site to obtain pertinent soil data for design
of the foundation for the proposed structures

The scope of our investigation included drilling and sampling four

Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a two story L shaped warehouse
structure approximately 90 by 250 feet in plan It will bea
steel framed structure with concrete masonry walls and concrete on grade
slabs Asphaltic concrete pavement will be utilized along the eastern
portion of the site for the on grade parking areas
The proposed layout of the building and the parking area is shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is situated on the Ewa side of Puuhale Road between Hue
Street and Nimitz Highway
The area is generally flat and presently paved with asphaltic
concrete There are no adjacent structures at both the Ewa side and mauka
side of the site There is an existing warehouse on the makai or
southwestern side of the site

SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling
four 4 borings to depths of 10 0 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground
surface
The borings disclosed that the site was mantled by a dense layer of
sandy gravel and cobble fill about 2 to 4 feet thick beneath the
existing 3 inch asphaltic topping Below the 2 feet of fill on the Ewa
side of the site was a dense coral layer down to depth of 15 5 feet below
ground surface where our boring terminated
The Diamond Head side of the site had about 4 feet of fill underlain
by about 5 feet of stiff brown silty clay which was in turn underlain by
the coral layer
Groundwater was encountered in two of our borings The observed
water levels varied from 11 feet 3 inches to 11 feet 6 inches below the
ground surface
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
General
Our subsurface exploration encountered a generally competent upper
crust of fill at the anticipated foundation elevation for the proposed
building Shallow spread or continuous wall footings bearing directly on
this existing fill may be used for foundation support of the structure
Minimal site grading is anticipated for the construction of the
proposed building

Foundation Support
Based on the anticipated soil conditions and light design loading
we believe that an allowable bearing capacity of 2500 p s f may be used
for spread or continuous footing bearing on the existing fill materials
Footing should be placed a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Uplift wind or other lateral loading condition
may further increase the embedment of the footing foundation
The bearing capacity value is for total dead plus live loads only
and may be increased by one third for short duration wind loading
Estimated settlements under the design loading are anticipated to be on
the order of less than one inch total and one half of an inch
differential
Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed structure can be
obtained by frictional resistance and passive pressure A frictional
coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the bottom of the footing and the
recompacted existing fill In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of
300 p s f per foot of depth may be used to determine the passive
resistance
Site Grading
No major site grading is anticipated
At the on set of earthwork the areas within the building grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed It is recommended to
remove the existing asphaltic concrete slab in order to provide a
competent subgrade for the on grade slabs and pavements Any unsuitable
materials such as debris rubbish and broken up pavements should be
removed and disposed of properly off site in order to prevent
contamination of the excavated material

After clearing an grubbing the areas to receive footing and
pavement should be scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches below the
bottom of footing or subgrade elevation moisture conditioned and
recompacted to 95 of its maximum density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
test method Any loose or wet spots encountered at the footing or
subgrade level should be removed and replaced with compacted select
on site granular soil or with suitable imported material compacted to the
above mentioned standard
The on site gravelly soil may be reused as backfill material If
required new imported fill material should consist of non expansive
select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximm size with less
than 20 passing the 200 sieve

Joints in concrete pavements subjected to traffic should be properly

The base course should consist of crushed rock compacted to a dry

CBR test should be performed on the actual exposed subgrade soil

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading foundation and pavement
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the grubbing site grading foundation
excavation backfilling and subgrade preparation be observed by the soils
engineer during construction to see that adequate compaction has been
obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site for use
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling four
4 borings to depths of 10 0 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were Classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches or a 3 inch diameter thin wall
tube hydraulically pushed The blow counts or the hydraulic pressure to
drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the
appropriate sample depths
