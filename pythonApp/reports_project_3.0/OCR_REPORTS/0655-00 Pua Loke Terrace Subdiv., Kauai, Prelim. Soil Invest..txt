INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary soil inves
tigation and percolation testing for the design and construction
of Pua Loke Subdivision located on Pua Nani Street in Pua Loke
in the Lihue district on the Island of Kauai Hawaii
W
Page 2
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed site will be developed in two increments The
first increment will consist of 25 single family cluster units
along the north western boundary The second increment is to
develop the remaining area into 90 residential lots
Final grading plans and building construction have not been
established as of the writing of this report The anticipated
residential structures will be one and two story wood framed
single family houses The building loads are expected to be
relatively light
Site grading will involve filling the lowlying areas and obtain
ing the fill material from their adjacent higher ground Road
ways for the subdivision will extend from those already existed
in the adjacent area
SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in Lihue town along Nawiliwili Stream
on Pua Nani Street in the Pua Loke District
The site had previously been graded Recent cut slopes are noted
along the southern boundary southwest property corner adjacent
to the Ulu Mahi Subdivision and along existing houses in the
northern most section of the site
W
Page 3
The cut slope along the southern portion of the site is almost
vertical The 30 to 40 foot high bank is sparsely vegetated
The width of this cut bank is about 150 feet Location of this
vertical slope bank is outlined in the Site Plan
A stream is located in the southern half of the site starting
from the west end and heading south toward the west boundary
and then running along the eastern boundary until it empties
into Nawiliwili Stream
The site gradually slopes down from west to east with open
recently graded areas north of the on site stream Vegetation
is dense along the stream and in the north corner of the site
In the south corner of the site a 4 foot thick fill pad had
been constructed between the stream and the vertical cut slope

Subsurface Exploration Percolation Test
Subsurface exploration for the proposed project consisted of
drilling 3 test borings between 12 0 to 25 5 feet deep Boring
locations are shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 1
The 44 inch diameter bore holes were used as test holes for

Soil Conditions
Subsurface soil conditions of this site vary between the northern
upper part of the site and those areas along the lowlying stream
areas
Boring No 1 located in the upper part of the site showed Sitatet
silty clay surface layer underlain by stiff sandy silts down to _
a depth of 20 feet where water level was encountered Below the
water table is the gray clayey silt
Boring No 2 which was drilled at the northern side of the lower
stream areas encountered 6 feet of fill at the ground surface
Below the surface fill layer there was a 5 foot thick soft to
medium stiff organic silt layer underlain by stiff clayey silt
Boring No 3 located at a lower ground elevation than Boring
No 2 has 8 foot thick fill underlain by in situ stiff clayey
silt Some organic material was noted in the stiff clayey silt
formation
Groundwater was encountered in all three borings and varied
from 8 to 21 feet below the ground surface

CONCLUSION RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface condition encountered during this preliminary
investigation indicated that the proposed site development
by means of cut and fill is feasible from a geology and soils
engineering viewpoint
Spread and wall footings bearing on the stiff in situ soils
or compacted fill will provide adequate support for the pro
posed light residential construction without excessive settle
ment
In the lowlying gully area where organic layer is encountered
buildings and utility construction in the new fill area should
be delayed until the ground has settled to a tolerable limit
The settlement period should be shortened by surcharge fill
Low expansive soils were encountered at the project site and
slab on grade can be utilized with a 4 inch base course layer
Site Grading
All areas to receive fill should be undercut to remove the
surface vegetations topsoils and any previously placed loose
fills
Prior to placement of compacted fill in the lowlying and gully
portions of the site local soft spots encountered within the
building areas should be removed and replaced with compacted
PAdAs

Page 6
The ground surface should then be keyed and benched to pro
perly bond the fills to the existing slope The upper six
inches of the existing ground surface should be scarified
brought to near optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM
D1557 72T
Additional fills should then be placed in 9 inch loose horizon
tal lifts compacted to the above specified density
Subdrain will be required at the bottom of the stream and gully
areas where surface water was encountered
Existing Vertical Cut Bank
The existing vertical cut bank located at the southern boundary
if left unprotected will present potential slide hazard to the
upper and lower residential construction Therefore we recom
mend that immediate correction measures should be made to minimize
this unstable condition
The remedial measures include reducing the height of the cut bank
by a stabilization fill against the existing toe slope or raising
the site grading in front of the bank area A shorter retaining
wall could then be constructed for the balance of the exposed cut
bank
Based on our previous experience in this area cut slope ratio of
about 1 horizontal to 1 vertical may be used for preliminary design
purpose Fill slope should be constructed at a 2 horizontal to 1

Future Slope Stability Analysis
It is recommended that additional specific subsurface boring
data to be obtained from large proposed cut and fill areas to
determine the overall stability of these slopes at planned slope
gradients This data should be obtained in conjunction with the
design level soil investigation and grading plan review prior to
construction
Fill Over Organic Layer
Because of the non uniform nature of the existing uncontrolled
fills and the underlying soft alluvial and organic deposits
fill over this area will initiate the consolidation process of
the underlying compressible layers In the preliminary soil
investigation compressible organic layer was only found at
Boring No 2 location Additional subsurface exploration should
be conducted to better define the extent of this area
In general building and utility construction in the new fill
areas over the organic layer should be delayed as much as prac
ticable after site filling To minimize future differential
settlements and to shorten the consolidation period surcharge
fill should be placed over the building sites The surcharging
period will be available in the final investigation soil report
when more data are available

Cesspool
The soils encountered in our borings consist of clayey and
silty soils
The seepage rates of these soil types were reflected in the
field percolation tests which varied from 1 1 to 10 min in
drop in the 44 inch diameter hole in the final 10 minute period
For details of the percolation tests see the attached Subdivi
sion Sewage Disposal Report These percolation rates will be
affected by the groundwater condition in the area
Additional percolation tests were also conducted in the way of
establishing a constant head near the ground surface for 30
minutes by pumping water into the 4 inch diameter boring holes
The rates of water pumped into the boring holes were recorded
ranging from 0 83 to 1 09 gallons per minute
Based on the above percolation rates it is our opinion that the
proposed cesspool construction is feasible for this site

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
3 borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The depths of all
the borings terminated at 12 0 to 25 5 feet betow the existing
ground surface The locations of the borings are shown on the
enclosed Site Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination in
the field by our engineer who continuously supervised the drilling
operations The classifications were verified by visual inspection
and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified in accor
dance with the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic pre
sentation of the soils encountered is presented on the Boring Logs
Plates A 1 through A 3
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch I D ring
sampler or a standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer
free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the logs of borings
at the appropriate sample depth
