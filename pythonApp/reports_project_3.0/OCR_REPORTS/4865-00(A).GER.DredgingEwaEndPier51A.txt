PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the Ewa western end of the existing Pier 51A of
Honolulu Harbor on the north side of Sand Island in Honolulu Oahu Hawaii The
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
At present the site is submerged land adjacent to the pier and is being used for
the berthing of container cargo vessels Based on the hydrographic survey dated
prepared by Sea Engineering Inc the existing depth of water
ranges from about 35 to about 43 feet below Mean Lower Low Water MLLW
Based on the information provided we understand that the project will involve
the dredging of an area starting at the Ewa end of the pier and extending to the west to
a minimum depth of 40 feet MLLW The proposed dredging area is rectangular in plan
view and measures 100 by 250 feet as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 No other site
improvements are anticipated in conjunction with this project

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two
extinct shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau Waianae became extinct while Koolau
was still active
The site is located on the southern flank of Koolau and its geomorphology and
subsurface conditions are directly related to glacio eustatic fluctuations of sea level
during the Pleistocene and the genesis of Honolulu Harbor The base of the
stratigraphic section in this area is Koolau Basalt
During the Pleistocene Ice Age there were many sea level changes as a result
of widespread glaciation in the continental areas of the world The higher sea level
stands caused the accumulation of sediments in the heads of the old bays
accumulation of reef deposits at corresponding higher elevations and lagoonal marine
sediments in the quiet waters protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused
streams to carve valleys in the sediments and reef deposits Renewed erosion
deposited alluvial soils in the lagoons and bays
The site is situated on the north side of Sand Island At the beginning of the
development of Honolulu Harbor the area now known as Sand Island was a shallow
reef flat and sand spit on the land side of a fringing reef forming a barrier across the
estuary created by the confluence of Nuuanu Kapalama and Kalihi Streams During
first dredging of Honolulu Harbor this area was used for the disposal of the dredge
spoils forming fast land that was used as an agricultural quarantine station Quarantine
Island Later dredging and fill projects built Sand Island to its present form

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our review of the available information and the subsurface soil
conditions anticipated at the project site we believe that conventional dredging
methods such as clamshell or rotary dredging may be used for the proposed dredging
of the Ewa end of Pier 51A If properly segregated and dewatered the dredged
material may have the potential for re use as fill material at an off site location
Detailed discussion of these items and our geotechnical engineering design
recommendations are presented in the following sections of this report
Site Grading
It is understood that grading for this project consists of dredging of up to about
5 feet below the existing harbor bottom Based on our reconnaissance it is anticipated
that the material to be removed generally consists of sands and gravels with some silt
It is also anticipated that there is a layer of variable thickness of soft silty sediment on
the bottom overlying these sands and gravels
Our reconnaissance indicates that in general it is anticipated that conventional
dredging methods such as clamshell excavation or rotary dredging may be utilized for
the dredging work Some areas of harder coral algal limestone may be present
however it is anticipated that these could most likely be broken and removed with a
clamshell excavator
Cut slopes should be designed and constructed at a maximum of two horizontal
to one vertical 2H 1V Care should be taken to not undercut or over steepen existing
shoreline slopes adjacent to the proposed dredging area such as to the west of the
existing pier

When considering the difficulties of maintaining grade control while working
underwater and the tendency for material to migrate while an area is being dredged
consideration should be given to over dredging slightly and allowing material to settle at
the contract dredge depth
All dredging work should be done in accordance with the applicable state and
federal requirements including permits water quality certifications and environmental
protection
Dredged Material
Based on the information obtained from our reconnaissance it is anticipated that
the dredged material will generally consist of sand and gravel with some silt If this
material is properly handled and dewatered it may have some potential for re use as fill
at an off site location
Care should be exercised to segregate the granular dredge spoils from the silty
sediment which is anticipated to be present at the mudline on the harbor bottom If this
sediment is mixed with the granular spoils it may render the dredged material unusable
as fill
If the dredged material is to be disposed of at an off shore location this disposal
should be done in accordance with the applicable state and federal requirements
including the performance of bio assay and toxicity testing on the material prior to
dredging and disposal of the material
