INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary foundation
investigation for the proposed Kamaole Sand Condominium
development to be located in
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 886 00
P 2
The purpose of this investigation was to investigate the
subsurface conditions at the site in relation to the founda
tion requirements and behavior of the underlying materials
under anticipated loading conditions
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed condominium complex will consist of ten buildings
varying in height up to four stories on grade A C parking
and a tennis court The buildings will be of reinforced
concrete construction with continuous wall footings and shear
walls at about 29 0 feet span Precast concrete planks will
be used for the upper floors Average loading on shear walls
is anticipated to be 29 kips foot
Site grading would require fills on the order of eight feet
in some structural areas in order to achieve the planned grades
SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed development site is located on Kihei Road Tax
Map Key 4 and is bounded by Haleakala Shores
Condominium on the north and Hale Kamaole on the south of the
property in
The site is presently being used for growing fruit trees for
commercial use by the tenants The tenants reside in two

a network of pipes from the existing well located at the mid
dle of the proposed Building 5 location to water the fruit
plants A reservoir is located at the proposed Building 8

Dry tall grass and other sparce vegetation exists all over the
remaining portion of the site Debris and few abandoned auto

Elevations at the site slopes up from approximately 20 0 feet

on the west Some isolated depressed and raised areas exist

The island of Maui was formed by the flows of two volcanos that
eventually joined and gives the island its present configura
tion The site is underlain by flows from Haleakala Volcano
which was later covered with flows from the Kula Series during
the Pleistocene Period This later flow contains many inter
stratified ash soil layers During the late Pleistocene Period
the land was undergoing an emergent period where the sea stood
about 60 feet lower Extensive lithified calcareous marine
sands were blown inland during this time

Subsurface Conditions
Subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling a
total of 14 borings ranging in depth from 5 0 feet to 20 0 feet
Additionally 4 test pits were excavated Boring and test pit
locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
Based on the information obtained from the borings and the
test pits the site is generally underlain by the following
materials
Brown silty sand ranging in depths from 1 5 feet in
Boring No 12 to 9 0 feet in Boring No 2 Upper
6 to 10 inches of this material is in loose condition
and contains grass roots Varying degree of cementation
was noted to exist in this formation
Underlying the sandy materials highly weathered basalt
with clayey silt was encountered extending to the depths
of borings and test pits
Brown clayey silt was encountered in Boring No s 3 and 10
Test Pit No s 2 and 3 sandwiched between the upper silty
sand and the underlying highly decomposed basalt formations
No ground water was encountered in our drilled borings and
test pits during our exploration

Above allowable bearing pressures could be increased by
one third to allow for temporary applied loads such as wind
and earthquake
All footings and slab on grade should be set back at least
five feet away from the top of fill slopes
Building loads and weight of the new structural fill used to
raise the site grades as indicated on the preliminary grading
plan would cause the underlying soil to settle However
most of the settlement would occur during site and building
construction
Our preliminary estimates indicate that the total settlement
on the order of half an inch and differential settlement
equal to about half the total settlement should be anticipated
Differential settlement of footings bearing on similar
material would be minimal
Prior to receiving any fill the ground surface should be
stripped to remove vegetation and debris It is estimated that
at least 6 inch of the upper sand crust consisting of organic
material would have to be wasted or stockpiled for possible
future landscaping use All the fill areas exposed should
then be scarified to a minimum depth of 6 inches brought to
near optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent

On site sandy materials could be used as a source of structural

dry density
The existing well under Building No 5 location will require
proper sealing and backfilling Sealing of the well should be
conducted under the direction of the Board of Water Supply
County of Maui
The existing reservoir at the proposed Building No 8 location
should be drained and graded All the loose materials should
be excavated and replaced with sandy structural fill compacted
as described above

the intent of the above recommendations are adequately met

Floor Slabs
The slab on grade floor can be supported on natural undisturbed
soil or engineered fill or on a combination of both In any
areas where dampness in the floor slab would be undesirable or
where moisture susceptible floor coverings are to be used a
minimum of four inches of granular type material should be
placed to act as a capillary break In addition a moisture
proof membrane should be placed over the granular material to
act as a vapor barrier The membrane should be covered by one
inch of sand to protect it during construction and to aid in
curing the concrete

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface soil conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 14 test borings utilizing trailer mounted rotary
auger equipment with rock coring attachments Additionally
4 test pits were excavated The depths of the borings ranged
from 5 to 20 feet below the existing ground surface The
locations of the borings and test pits are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 1
The soils and rock were classified by visual and textural
examinations in the field by our engineer who continuously
supervised the drilling and test pit excavation operations
The classifications were verified by inspection and testing in
the laboratory All soils were classified in accordance with
the Unified Soil Classification System The description of
rocks is based on local geologic terminology Graphical
representations of the soils and rocks encountered are
presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 18 Soil
samples were obtained by driving a sampler with a 140 pound
hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts
to drive the sampler and the percent of recovery of rock cores
are also shown on the Log of Borings at the appropriate sample
depth
