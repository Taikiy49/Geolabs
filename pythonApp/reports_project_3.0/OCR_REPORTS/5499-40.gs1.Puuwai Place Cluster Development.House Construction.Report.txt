This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations and testing
services performed during house construction for the Pu uwai Place Cluster Development
project from March 15 2018 to in accordance with our fee proposal
dated
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Pu uwai Place Cluster Development project site is located in a ravine
bounded by Interstate Route H 1 to the north Paiwa Street to the east Koaki Street to
the south and Kahaulua Street to the west in the Waipahu area on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii A Project Location Map and Site Plans are shown on Plates 1 and 2 1
through 2 4 respectively The project involves the construction of 21 house structures for
a total of 40 residential units
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the house grading and utility installation within the house lot areas for the
project site
HOUSE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
We observed the house grading and utility installation work for the Pu uwai Place
Cluster Development project from March 15 2018 to The house
grading work consisted of footing excavation and footing and slab on grade subgrade
preparation for the project The utility installation work consisted of trench backfill of
plumbing and sewer lines within the house lot The house grading and utility backfilling

work were performed by Reef Development of Hawaii Inc During the house grading
operations we obtained samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and
backfill materials and transported them to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry
densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on
Plate 3
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density
as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557
During the house grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density
tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938 Nuclear Gauge Method
Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 13 of
this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on
constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by
pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should
be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed
as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
and or approved by our field representative based on visual observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the house grading and utility installation work for the
above project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were
performed in conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the following

LIMITATIONS
Geolabs assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or finished
grades on the above project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
knowledge and written acknowledgment
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site therefore some work may have occurred
without our observation However we believe that the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project It
should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability
of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials Our
services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical
engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction
monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type
The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to the
plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
