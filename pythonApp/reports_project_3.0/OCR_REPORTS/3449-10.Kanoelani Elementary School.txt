This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation and testing
services performed during the building pad preparation foundation excavation utility
installation and pavement construction for the project of Kanoelani Elementary School
Administration Building and Library construction from March 1997 through August 1997 in
general accordance with our fee proposal dated
SITE GRADING
The existing Kanoelani Elementary School is located in the Waipio area within the
District of Waipahu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The improvements project consists of
constructing a one story administration building and library Based on information
provided we understand that the buildings consist of concrete and masonry bearing walls
with concrete slab on grade construction
The project site is located within the existing Kanoelani elementary school In
general the earthwork at the site consisted of clearing and grubbing placement and
compaction of fills trench excavation and backfill and subgrade preparation and
compaction for the foundation of the building Following the clearing and grubbing
operations the exposed subgrades were generally scarified brought to near the optimum

During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and tested The maximum dry densities
and optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 1 of
this report During the fill and backfill operations the materials were generally placed in
near level lifts brought to near the optimum moisture and compacted to obtain the
specified degree of compaction based on visual observations and or field density tests
Periodic field density tests were taken during the fill and backfill operations to confirm our
field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions were noted
A total of 51 field density tests including re tests of failed areas were performed
using the nuclear density gauge method in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designations D 2922 and D 3017 The results of the compaction tests taken during
construction of the subject project are summarized on Plates 2 1 through 2 3of this report
These tests were taken on the prepared subgrade and on constructed fills and backfills at
the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from
on site survey stakes where available These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to
be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
and or approved by our field representatives based on visual observation and hand
probing with a pointed steel rod
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the building pad
preparation foundation excavation and utility installation for the above referenced
project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were
performed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and
our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the referenced report dated
and subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that our field observations and testing services were conducted
on a periodic basis Therefore some of the earthworks may have been performed without
our observation However the level of earthwork observation and testing services
provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical risks to the
project It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical
repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and
materials Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted
