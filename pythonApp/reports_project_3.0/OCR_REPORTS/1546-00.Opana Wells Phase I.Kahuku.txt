PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF SERVICES
The purpose of our soil engineering investigation was to explore
the subsurface conditions at the proposed control building site and the
access road in order to provide recommendations for use in the design of
the site grading and foundations for the proposed structures The
general location of the project area is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
The scope of work was performed in general accordance with our
proposal dated March 25 1985
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which
we used in the engineering analysis to formulate cur conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices cf this report
The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2 The log of borings and information regarding the field
exploration and laboratory testing are also presented in the appendices
of this report

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project consists of the following
1 A cne story control building approximately 15 by 55 feet in
plan dimension It will be located partly in a cut section
at the toe of the existing slope and constructed of concrete
and CMU walls
2 A 12 foot wide A C paved access road that will connect with
the existing Opana paved road located east of the site The
road is about 250 feet long
3 A future water well will be lecated about 50 feet east of
the control building
SITE DESCRIFTION
The project site is located near the base of the Pali that
parallels the ocean near Kawela Bay on the North Shore of Oahu The site
is about one half mile south of Kamehameha Highway and about three
fourth mile southwest of the Turtle Bay Hilton Hotel

The topography of this site is fairly flat at the proposed
building site A fairly steep natural hillside slope is located south of
the site that slopes to the south at a slope ratio of approximately
to 4 1 H V North of the flat area the ground slopes in a northerly
direction at about 2 1 H V then flattens to about 6 1
An erosional drainageway is located on the steep slope west of the
control building location
An existing deep water well that is capped is located about 75
feet west of the control building
A U S Army Signal Corp cable crosses through the site in an east
to west direction
Some large boulders are located along the toe of the fill slope
northwest of the control building
The ground elevations at the site range from approximately 120 to
150 feet
The site is currently heavily vegetated with a growth of grasses
and scattered trees

SUBSURFACE_CONDI TIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the Kahuku Coastal Plain that is
essentially a narrow flat windward fringe area on Cahu that fronts a
steep pali that is probably an ocean wave cut feature The pali is about
250 feet high south of the site
The site lies on a gently sloping upland slope that consists of
coalesced fans of alluvium and colluvial sediments derived from the
salient volcanic cliffs of the Koolau Range to the south of the site
Soil Conditions
The borings encountered a surface layer about 5 to 7 feet thick of
stiff brown clay with decomposed gravel over boulders and cobbles in a
matrix of clayey silt Older Alluvium to depths of 16 to 19 feet the
depth of the borings drilled Rock formation was encountered in Boring
1 the uppermost boring location at a depth of 6 5 feet A profile
depicting the general soil conditions is shewn on Cross Section A A
Plate 3
The upper clay layer was found to be moderately to highly
expansive
Groundwater was not encountered in any of the borings during the
time of our investigation

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface exploration generally indicates that after the
proposed site grading the subgrade soils may consist of stiff to hard
alluvial colluvial deposits which could in our opinion provide adequate
foundation bearing support for the proposed structure provided that some
care is exercised during construction to minimize the swelling potential
of the soil
Laboratory tests performed on the stiff on site clayey soil
indicates that this material generally has moderate to high swelling
potential Therefore to minimize effects due to soil swelling under
on grade slabs we recommend that the on site soil be overexcavated and
replaced with non expansive select fill and that the subgrade soils
underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and protected fran
drying from the start of construction until the slab concrete placement
Control Building Foundation
For the proposed one story structure spread and continuous wall
footings bearing on the alluvial soil are recommended An allowable
bearing value of 3000 p s f may be used for footings bearing on these
soils
The allowable bearing value may be increased by one third for
temporary wind and seismic loads
GEOLABS HAWAII

Footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the
lowest adjacent finished grade Footings located next to utility
trenches should extend down to the bottom of the utility trench and bear
on stiff undisturbed soil or the trench backfill should be well compacted
to minimize footing settlement
Prior to concrete placement the bottcm of all footing excavations
should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78 prior to concrete placement
The footing excavations should be kept moist from the time of the
initial excavation up to concrete placement
Slab on grade is planned for the structure To minimize the
swelling effects of the expansive soils the expansive soils below 3 feet
of the finished slab grade should be removed and replaced with compacted
non expansive soil such as select borrow crusher waste or coral
as OS Ses
Based on the borings and laboratory test data and estimated
building loads minimal settlements are anticipated

Site Grading
In general the site grading will consist of cutting the hillside
about 2 to 6 feet at the control building site Filling is anticipated
at the northern side of the site Fills up to about 8 feet are
contemplated
Site Preparation
The site to be graded should be cleared of all vegetation organic
matter debris and soft material Prior to filling the existing
ground should be scarified to a minimum of 6 inches moisture
conditioned and then recompacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum Cry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 test methcd
Fill Placement Compaction Requirements
It is anticipated that no filling will be required at the building
site Some filling for the access roadway will be necessary
Where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal to 1
vertical fill placed should be keyed and benched into the
existing ground to properly bond the new fill to the slope For
details see Plate 4 This detail is essential for the stability
of the fills particularly any large fills The filling operation
should start at the lowest point and continue up in level
compacted layers

For the proposed control building it is anticipated that the
existing hillside to the south of the structure will be cut about
2 to 6 feet Since the surface clay soil is relatively thin cut
slope ratios up to 2 1 H V may be used with slope benches
provided at 15 foot maximum vertical height intervals
It appears that most of the material generated frem the cutting
will consist of expansive clay These clay soils are generally
weak when saturated and have a high potential for downhill slope
creep when placed on steep slopes In order tc reduce the
possibility of slope movement we recommend that the fill slopes
be placed at a slope ratio of 3 1 H V or flatter and the slope
height should be limited to 10 feet
Alternatively a 2 1 H V slope ratio may be used if select
granular material such as coral or select borrow is used within
the outer 3 feet of the slope face
Surface water from areas above the slope should be diverted away
and prevented frem flowing over the slope face The slope should
be planted as soon as practical te prevent unravelling due to
erosion

Interceptor Ditch
A concrete interceptor ditch about 2 feet deep is planned along
the cut slope bench Where expansive clay is encountered at the bottom
of the ditch excavation a 6 inch cushion of select non expansive
material should be placed belew the ditch Additionally the subgrade
soils should be saturated several days prior to concrete Placement
Roadway Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the project service
access roadway
Based on the results cf our laboratory CBR tests on the on site
soils the preliminary pavement design is as follows
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
12 Inch Non sive Soil _
20 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade
soil samples during construction and the above design section can be
revised if necessary The recommended section considers light traffic
and that good drainage will be provided adjacent to paved areas

Plans for the site grading and foundation should be forwarded to
the soils engineer for review and written comments prior to construction

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the fill placement slope cuts and
to monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by
a scils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
