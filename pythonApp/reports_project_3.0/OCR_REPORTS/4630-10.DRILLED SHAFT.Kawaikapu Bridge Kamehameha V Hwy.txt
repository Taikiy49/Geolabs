PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The replacement bridge project site is along Kamehameha V Highway across
Kainalu Gulch on the Island of Molokai Hawaii It was proposed to demolish the existing
bridge and construct a single span concrete girder bridge structure approximately 61
feet long across the stream The general contractor was Brown s Trucking Inc and the
drilled shaft contractor was CMZ of Hawaii Inc
To allow local and tourist traffic across the stream during construction the new
bridge structure was constructed in two phases In the first phase the northwestern half
of the existing bridge structure was retained to allow for vehicular access while the
southeastern half of the new bridge structure was constructed In the second phase
vehicular access was routed onto the newly constructed southeastern half of the new
bridge structure while the northwestern half of the new bridge was constructed

Based on the subsurface conditions encountered and the structural loads
imposed on the foundations 48 inch diameter cast in place concrete drilled shafts were
designed to support the new single span concrete bridge structure To accommodate
the structural loading demands the drilled shaft foundation was designed to extend to
elevation 84 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL with a length of 82 feet to achieve
compressive load capacities of 680 and 375 kips for extreme event limit state and
strength limit state respectively
It should be noted that the cut off elevation of the drilled shaft was designed at
about elevation 2 5 feet MSL with bottom of cap elevation about 2 98 feet MSL
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on the borings drilled the project site is underlain by fills and or alluvial
soils extending to depths of about 4 5 to 5 feet below the existing ground surface The
fills and or alluvial soils are underlain by very soft and very loose alluvial and or lagoonal
soil deposits extending to depths of about 67 5 to 73 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The very soft and very loose alluvial and or lagoonal soil deposits are underlain
by colluvial and or alluvial deposits consisting of hard to very hard basaltic boulders and
cobbles with some gravel extending to the maximum depths explored of about 70 to
88 feet below the existing ground surface
We encountered groundwater in our borings at depths of about 4 to 7 5 feet
below the existing ground surface Due to the proximity of the project site to the
Pacific Ocean groundwater levels can fluctuate depending on tidal fluctuations
seasonal precipitation groundwater withdrawal and or injection and other factors
PRE PRODUCTION TESTING PROGRAM
As part of the specification requirements a sacrificial trial shaft 48 inch
diameter was constructed to examine the adequacy of the methods and equipment
proposed by the contractor for actual subsurface conditions Following satisfactory
completion of the trial shafts static load test utilizing a bi directional load cell in the shaft
along with instrumentation by embedment strain gages was performed to confirm or
modify the production drilled shaft length
An engineer from LoadTest Inc installed the load cell in the shaft and performed
the load test utilizing the bi directional load cell The load test results are presented in
the reports entitled Report on Drilled Shaft Load Testing Osterberg Method dated
as previously referenced A representative from our office observed the
installation and testing of the load test shaft
Based on the load test results the original design drilled shaft length was
maintained

PRODUCTION DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION
A total of 12 production drilled shafts excluding two abandoned shafts were
installed between May 10 2005 and for the project The locations of the
production drilled shafts are presented on the Drilled Shaft Location Plan Plate 1 A
Geolabs engineer observed the installation operations
The production drilled shafts were excavated using a _ crane mounted
drill rig equipped with auger digging bucket clean out bucket
and or core barrel Temporary 50 inch D Inside Diameter steel casing about 42 feet
long was used during excavation to prevent the drilled hole from cave in in the soft
and or loose soil layers A Summary of Drilled Shaft Installation is presented on Plates
2 Details of the shaft installation are summarized in Plate 2 Summary of Drilled Shaft
Installation and presented in Appendix Drilled Shaft Installation Logs
The drilled shaft contractor used polymer slurry to keep the drilled uncased hole
open during excavation The polymer slurry was prepared in the hole by
sprinkling sifting the dry polymer Shore Pac onto flowing water and further mixed by
rotation of the drilling tool in the hole in accordance with the polymer manufacturer s
recommendations The polymer slurry was removed from the hole by centrifugal pump
during concrete placement into a holding tank for recycling
Concrete was placed through a 10 inch O D steel gravity tremie pipe starting
from the bottom of the hole During concrete placement a minimum of 5 feet of
concrete head was maintained above the bottom of the tremie pipe In order to provide
good quality concrete at the top of the drilled shaft additional concrete was placed in
the drilled shaft to allow some over pouring at the top In general concrete was
over poured until sound concrete was evident at the top of the drilled shaft
The approximate volume of concrete placed in the shaft was monitored and is
presented in the attached Appendix The theoretical and actual concrete volume placed
in the shafts is summarized on Plate 2
Non destructive Integrity testing by Cross hole Sonic Logging CSL was
performed on all the production drilled shafts and concrete coring was conducted on
selected production shafts The results of the integrity testing indicated that poor
concrete and soil intrusion occurred at depths between 56 and 62 for Shaft No 4 and
depths between 58 and 76 feet for Shaft No 8 Extensive repair effort was carried out
by coring pressure washing and pressure grouting However the repairs for Shaft Nos
4 and 8 were not successful based on the CSL tests Consequently Shaft Nos 4 and 8
were abandoned and Shaft Nos 4R and 8R were designed and _ installed for
replacement
