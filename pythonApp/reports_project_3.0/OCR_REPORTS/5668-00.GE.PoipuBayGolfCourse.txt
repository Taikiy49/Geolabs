This letter report presents the findings from our preliminary slope stability

Project Considerations
The project site is located at the existing Poipu Bay Golf Course which is situated
in the coastal Poipu area on the Island of Kauai Hawaii A large block type slope failure
occurred along a steep section of sea cliffs bordering the golf course The portion that
failed had experienced undercutting by wave action Similar undercut conditions exist
along other sections of the sea cliff westerly of the slope failure area
An elongated portion of the coralline sea cliff experienced an unanticipated collapse
failure following an extended period of heavy rains which affected the Island of Kauai
during March 2006 The sub vertically inclined cliff ranges between about 30 and 50 feet
high The collapsed portion of the cliff is within the existing shoreline conservation zone
The collapse area involved the full height of the cliff and extended approximately 300 feet
long by about 25 feet wide
The cliff failure is in close proximity to the existing golf course 15 Green and
16 Tees and resulted in the loss of a section of the shoreline access trail As a result of
the collapse an evaluation of the cliff slope stability in this area was desired to develop a
safe use setback for the golf course and shoreline trail from the new edge of the cliff

Regional Geology
The Island of Kauai is essentially a dissected basaltic dome that was initially formed
by a single large shield volcano which has subsequently been deeply eroded and partially
covered with later younger volcanic materials The Island of Kauai is the oldest of the
main Hawaiian Islands and today encompasses approximately 555 square miles in area

The Island of Kauai was built mainly by the extrusion of lava representing the
Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series during the late Pliocene Epoch more than 2 3 million
years before present Following the cessation of this main volcanic shield building phase
and a long period of erosion about 1 4 million years before present there was
rejuvenated volcanic activity involving the extrusion of basaltic lavas representing the
post erosional Koloa Volcanic Series along with the concurrent deposition of alluvial
sediments representing the Palikea Formation
During the Pleistocene Epoch many sea level changes occurred as a result of
widespread worldwide glaciation As the great continental glaciers accumulated the global
sea levels fell because there was less water available to fill the oceanic basins
Conversely as the glaciers receded or melted global sea levels rose because more
water was available The land mass of Kauai remained essentially stable during these
sea level changes therefore the fluctuations were eustatic in nature These
glacio eustatic fluctuations resulted in stands of the sea which were both higher and lower
relative to the present sea level on Kauai
During lower stands of the sea which occurred during the Pleistocene Epoch large
calcareous sand dune deposits were blown inland in some coastal areas Percolating
rainwater caused lithification into calcareous rock sandstone and over the passage of
time the inland sand dune deposits became cemented and compacted
The Poipu Bay Golf Course project site is located along a section of the
southeast facing coastline in the Poipu area The coastline fronting the golf course is
comprised of tall sea cliffs composed of lithified dune sand sandstone overlying basaltic
rock formation of the Koloa Volcanic Series The geologic contact between the basalt rock
formation and overlying sandstone is sharp and is near the elevation of the present day
mean sea level The coastline is exposed to ocean swells that continually erode the
exposed weathered basalt sandstone at the geologic contact The ocean wave action
created an erosional notch undercut eroding the weaker highly weathered basaltic rock
material below the sandstone cliffs
Evidence of this continual undercutting along with the resulting block type failure at
portions of the sandstone cliff is visibly apparent by the many previously fallen blocks lining
the coastal shoreline In general it appears that the previously fallen blocks provide
additional armoring of the shoreline which reduced the effects of the ocean wave erosion
In addition our opinion is that the overall stability following a typical block type failure
event generally increases because the previous undercut condition and loading of the rock
mass is reduced after the release of the overhanging block s of rock

Background
On March 27 2006 after an extended period of heavy flooding a large slope failure
involving a substantial span of sea cliff approximately 300 lineal feet and about 20 to
30 feet wide fell into the ocean The slope failure released several very large blocks of
coralline sandstone rock that are presently along the toe of the cliff on the shoreline As a
result of the slope failure the top edge of the cliff is currently about 10 to 30 feet inland
from the cliff line The mapped location of the landslide is shown on the General Site Plan
Plate 2 A photograph of the slope failure area is provided on Plate 5
The slope failure resulted in the loss of a portion of the existing shoreline access
trail within the existing shoreline conservation zone The slope failure occurred adjacent to
the area occupied by the existing 15 Green 16 Tees and cart path A portion of an
existing cart path traverses within approximately 18 lineal feet inland from the new top of
cliff After the landslide temporary fencing was erected in an effort to keep public access
to a minimum
The slope failure eliminated most of the unstable sandstone rock overhang that was
above the basalt sandstone geologic contact near the toe of the cliff The landslide also
created a new cliff slope that appears to stand at an inclination of about a
one half horizontal to one vertical Based on our observation of the landslide
area the new slope appears to be generally stable within the limits of the landslide zone
as shown on the General Site Plan Plate 2
However along the brow of the cliff we observed some smaller loose blocks
containing mixed rock soil and remnant vegetation that appear to be unstable and at
risk for separation from the top of the slope These blocks ranging between about 1 and
4 feet in dimension are occasionally bounded by some open fractures Although the loose
rocks could fall toward the shoreline below foot traffic appears inaccessible there due to
the very rugged shoreline terrain strewn with fallen boulders and large blocks of rock
Due to the close proximity of the existing landslide zone to the 16 Tees and the
presence of additional cliff undercut features below the 16 Tees it is desired to evaluate
the short term stability of the remaining cliff immediately west of the landslide zone below
the 16 Tees A long term slope stability assessment is recommended to study the
stability of the length of the sea cliff to develop recommendations pertaining to the
long term safe use of the golf course including possible re alignment of portions of the
golf course away from the cliff instability

Based on our observations the coralline sandstone sea cliffs below the 16 Tees
are about 30 to 35 feet in height The cliffs consist of sub vertical to slightly overhanging
coralline sandstone rock containing few horizontal bedding discontinuities At
approximately 5 to 10 feet Mean Sea Level MSL a sharp near horizontal geologic
contact exists between the overlying sandstone and the underlying highly weathered
basaltic rock The geologic contact between the differing geologic materials appears to be
the focus of the observed wave erosion which has produced variable degrees of erosional
undercutting at the basalt sandstone interface
The base of the cliff below the 16 Gold and Blue Tees contains relatively few
fallen blocks of rock capable of providing natural protection against direct wave action and
surge As a result higher energy wave surge is able to make contact and affect the base
of the cliff It appears that undercutting is active in this area and is anticipated to continue
Based on our observations it appears that the previous block type collapse failures
occurred as a result of the sudden release of the cantilevered rock mass above the
erosional undercut The resultant failure surfaces have produced new cliff slopes ranging
from about vertical to inclinations
Based on our reconnaissance along the base of the cliff a maximum undercut
depth of about 22 feet was measured approximately below the 16 Red and White Tees
The average height of the 22 foot deep wedge shaped undercut was measured to be
4 feet Based on our observations other cliff undercuts measured toward the west of the
16 Tees average about 5 to 10 feet in depth by about 5 to 10 feet in height In general
we observed that the deeper horizontal undercuts greater than about 10 feet deep exhibit
lower vertical height dimensions than the shallower depth undercuts Thus the observed
horizontal undercuts of 5 to 10 feet deep generally exhibit vertical heights on the order of
about 5 to 10 feet
The erosional undercuts further toward the east below the 16 Gold and Blue
Tees were not directly measured because the coastline was inaccessible due to rugged
terrain and active wave surge However based on the undercut conditions visible from a
distance from below the 16 Red and White Tees it is estimated that the taller undercuts
in the vicinity of the 16 Blue and Gold Tees may be approximately 10 to 15 feet in depth
and about 10 feet in height
Based on our observations it appears that the cliff adjacent to the 16 Blue and
Gold Tees has an elevated potential for slope failure due to the existing cantilevered
condition of the rock mass and the relatively open exposure to direct wave action along
with the associated erosion occurring at the sandstone basalt interface Other areas along
the cliff westerly of the 16 Tees appear to have a lower risk for slope failure due to lower

Preliminary Findings and Recommendations
Based on our evaluation of the short term slope stability using the available
topographic survey map we estimated the approximate limits of potential slope
instability in the vicinity of the 16 Hole Blue and Gold Tees Our estimate is based on
an assumed maximum cliff undercut depth of 25 feet below the Gold 16 Tee as
depicted on the Slope Profile A A Plate 4 Furthermore our evaluation conservatively
estimated the probable slope inclination resultant failure surface that may be produced
by a block type failure occurring in the coralline sandstone encompassing the 25 foot
undercut depth The probable collapse slope inclination used is Based on the
estimated limits of the potential slope instability it appears that the safe zone would be
about 10 feet seaward from the southern edge of the existing Gold 16 Tee play
surface as shown on the Site Plan Plate 3 and the Slope Profile Plate 4
Less conservative estimates were also evaluated by assuming a 25 foot deep
undercut failure producing a new failure surface inclined at steeper and
vertical failure surface inclinations The estimated distance of the probable safe zone
extending seaward from the edge of the 16 Gold Tee toward the edge of the
hypothetical failure surface would appear to increase to about 15 feet and about 20 feet
for the steeper and vertical slope inclinations respectively
It should be noted that the topographic survey map provided for our analysis only
covers the ground surrounding the 16 Gold Tee and the coastline extending toward the
east from the 16 Gold Tee Topographic coverage of the remaining Blue White and
Red 16 Tees extending toward the west from the Gold Tee was not available for our
use However based on our field observations it appears that the other 16 Tees
Blue White and Red Tees are aligned approximately parallel with the existing top
edge of the cliff Therefore similar safe zone designations may be assumed for the
remaining tee locations using the 25 foot undercut depth and failure surface inclined at
Based on our preliminary evaluation the estimated limits for potential slope
instability do not encroach upon the 16 Gold and Blue Tee play areas However the
estimated limits are located in close proximity to the existing Blue and Gold Tee
locations approximately 10 feet away
A collapse of the coastal cliff as a result of the undercut condition is considered
to be a generally unpredictable sudden and natural evolutionary slope failure event

Because of the close proximity of the estimated instability zone to the 16 Blue and
Gold Tees our opinion is that the 16 Tees should be relocated further inland to provide
long term low risk safe use of the area
For the short term on the order of less than 1 year we recommend installing an
appropriate safety barrier such as a chain link fence with appropriate warning signage
extending continuously along the southern margin of the existing 16 Blue and Gold
Tees to limit public access within the zone of potential slope instability The chain link
safety fence should extend from the Gold Tee toward the westerly direction along the
southern side of the remaining 16 Hole Tees Blue White and Red to limit public
access seaward of the tee locations The fence installation extending alongside the
Blue White and Red Tees is considered an appropriate short term safety measure until
additional slope stability studies are completed or the 16 Hole Tees are relocated
further inland
In addition several ground survey reference points could be established in an
effort to monitor possible developing ground subsidence or movement within the zone of
potential instability The routine monitoring should continue until a long term mitigation
such as realignment of the tees is completed The selected monitoring survey points
should be maintained by a licensed professional surveyor The results of the monitoring
survey including both horizontal and vertical control should be forwarded to an engineer
or geologist for interpretation of the results and to provide additional recommendations
if needed
A geotechnical exploration is recommended to determine appropriate long term
safe use setbacks from the cliff face for portions of the golf course located in close
proximity to the steep potentially unstable cliffs
15 Hole Cart Path
Based on our preliminary visual assessment the existing cart path traversing
areas adjacent to the collapsed slope segment appears to be located on relatively
stable ground with a generally low risk for short term large block slope failure Along the
tope edge of the cliff some minor on going erosion and failure of isolated earth should
be anticipated However it appears that the new slope face created by the cliff collapse
stands at approximately and is substantially armored from direct wave action
due to the fallen large block debris on the shoreline and in the near shore waters In
addition the potential hazard from the pre existing cliff undercut and cantilevered
coralline sandstone within the collapse area appears greatly reduced as a result of the
collapse
Based on our observations the existing cart path is within about 18 feet of the
top edge of the cliff at the closest approach in some locations Although the cart path
appears to be on generally stable ground the close proximity of the cliff to the public

Limitations
This report is based in part on a visual assessment of the exposed and accessible
areas of the project site It may not be possible to observe all of the existing site conditions
during a brief reconnaissance and literature review The preliminary findings and
discussion submitted in this report are based in part upon information obtained from the
available topographic survey prepared by others our site reconnaissance and literature
review Variations of surface and subsurface conditions from those described in this report
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until a
geotechnical engineering exploration is conducted at the site Our services were
performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering
practice no warranty is expressed or implied
The findings presented in this report are considered preliminary and should be used
only for the short term less than about 1 year evaluation of the slope stability at the
project site Geological processes such as erosion and weathering are natural on going
evolutionary processes that may be dependent upon other significant external natural
factors such as seismic disturbance storm events and tsunami activity As a result the
site conditions may change with the passage of time and the preliminary findings
discussed in this report may require re evaluation
