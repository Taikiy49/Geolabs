2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae Volcano forms
the bulk of the western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau Volcano
forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae
Volcano became extinct while Koolau Volcano was still active and its eastern flank is
partially buried below Koolau lavas in Central Oahu
The project site is located on the southern flank of the Koolau Volcano The
geomorphology and subsurface conditions at the project site are directly related to the
glacio eustatic fluctuations of the sea level during the Pleistocene Epoch and the
genesis of the Honolulu Coastal Plain The subsurface conditions in the Waikiki area
are dominated by fossil Pleistocene Age coral algal reef deposits and related deposits
laid during high stands of the sea which are intercalated with terrigenous sediments
The base of the stratigraphic section in this area is Koolau Basalt
During the later Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age there were many sea level
changes as a result of widespread glaciation in the continental areas of the world
These glacio eustatic fluctuations resulted in stands of the sea that were both higher
and lower relative to the present sea level of Oahu About 15 000 years ago a
relatively rapid rise in sea level occurred During that rise the valleys in the project area
were drowned In the last 10 000 years or so the sea level has adjusted to its present
stand Terrigenous and marine sediments have continued to accumulate in low energy
estuarine or lagoonal environments resulting in thick deposits of soft and or loose
sediments along the coast in the areas that were formerly valleys and drainageways
In the early part of this century the Waikiki area consisted of low marshy
wetlands As the City of Honolulu grew and the Waikiki area was urbanized man made
fills were placed to reclaim the marshy areas and lagoons The fill was placed upon
lagoonal deposits consisting of silty coralline sands with coral fragments The lower

Earthquakes and Seismicity
In general earthquakes that occur throughout the world are caused solely by shifts
in the tectonic plates In contrast earthquake activity in Hawaii is linked primarily to
volcanic activity Therefore earthquake activity in Hawaii generally occurs before
or during volcanic eruptions In addition earthquakes may result from the
underground movement of magma that comes close to the surface but does not
erupt The Island of Hawaii experiences thousands of earthquakes each year but
most of the earthquakes are so small that only sensitive instruments could detect
the small tremors However some of the earthquakes are strong enough to be felt
and a few cause minor to moderate damage
In general earthquakes associated with volcanic activity are most common on the
Island of Hawaii Earthquakes that are directly associated with the movement of
magma are concentrated beneath the active Kilauea and Mauna Loa Volcanoes on
the Island of Hawaii Because the majority of the earthquakes in Hawaii over
90 percent of earthquakes are related to volcanic activity the risk of high seismic
activity and degree of ground shaking diminishes with increased distance from the
Island of Hawaii The Island of Hawaii has experienced numerous earthquakes
greater than Magnitude 5 M5 however earthquakes are not confined only to the
Island of Hawaii

should be evaluated for the potential for soil liquefaction Soil liquefaction is a
condition where saturated cohesionless soils located near the ground surface
undergo a substantial loss of strength due to the build up of excess pore water
pressures resulting from cyclic stress applications induced by earthquakes In this
process when the loose saturated sand deposit is subjected to vibration such as
during an earthquake the soil tends to densify and decrease in volume causing an
increase in pore water pressure
If drainage is unable to occur rapidly enough to dissipate the build up of pore water
pressure the effective stress internal strength of the soil is reduced Under
sustained vibrations the pore water pressure build up could equal the overburden
pressure essentially reducing the soil shear strength to zero and causing it to
behave as a viscous fluid During liquefaction the soil acquires a mobility sufficient
to permit both horizontal and vertical movements and if not confined will result in
significant deformations
Soils most susceptible to liquefaction are loose uniformly graded fine grained
sands and loose silts with little cohesion It is generally acknowledged that
liquefaction will not occur if the deposit is greater than 40 to 50 feet below the
ground surface In deeper deposits the greater overburden pressure generally is
sufficient to prevent liquefaction from occurring The major factors affecting the
liquefaction characteristics of a soil deposit are as follows
1 Grain Size Distribution Fine and uniform sands and silts are more
susceptible to liquefaction than coarse or well graded sands
2 Initial Relative Density Loose sands and silts are most susceptible
to liquefaction Liquefaction potential is inversely proportional to
relative density
3 Magnitude _and Duration of Vibration Liquefaction potential is
directly proportional to the magnitude and duration of the
earthquake

As mentioned previously finished floor elevations for the new basement
expansion will range from about 1 to 3 25 feet MSL We understand that the new
basement structure will need to be designed to resist buoyant uplift forces for a high
flood water elevation of 10 feet MSL In order to resist the buoyant uplift forces we
recommend that the new basement be supported on micropiles Micropiles should be
installed to resist the uplift loads by side friction in the dense to very dense coralline
deposits encountered at the project site
In general the micropile foundation system consists of a small diameter usually
less than 12 inches drilled and grouted pile with steel reinforcing The micropile
foundation typically is constructed by drilling a borehole placing reinforcing steel in the
hole and grouting the borehole Micropiles are desirable because they can be readily
installed in access restrictive environments and in numerous soil types and ground
conditions In addition installation of the micropiles generally causes minimal
disturbance to adjacent structures the adjacent soils and the environment
Based on our experience we believe that a micropile system with a minimum
4 inch diameter drill bit resulting in a grout bulb diameter of 6 inches may be used to
support the new basement structure Based on the typical column loads and the
available reinforcing bars we recommend that each micropile be designed based on an
allowable compressive load capacity of 75 kips The allowable compressive load
capacity for the piles is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by 33 percent for
transient loads such as wind or seismic forces The capacity of the micropile
foundation is derived primarily from skin friction between the micropile shaft and the
medium dense to dense coralline materials underlying the lagoonal deposits
encountered in the borings

Uplift Load Capacity
For preliminary estimating proposes we believe that an uplift capacity of 40 kips
may be used for a minimum 6 inch diameter micropile extending down to a
minimum tip elevation of at least 50 feet MSL The uplift load capacity for the
micropile is intended to resist transient loads such as the uplift forces due to the

In general resistance to uplift loads imposed on the basement foundation also may
be mobilized by the dead weight of the structure and the foundation In addition
the weight of soils placed above the foundation system also may be used to resist
uplift loads Contribution of dead weight from compacted soils above the
foundation may be estimated using a unit weight of 110 pounds per cubic foot pcf
Micropile Load Test Program

dependent on the drilling procedures and the grouting methods employed by the
contractor to install the micropile Therefore the bond stress between the grout

foundation systems In order to evaluate whether the contractor s methods of

stress we recommend that at least one pre production compressive load test be
performed on a sacrificial micropile In general the purpose of the pre production

To examine the adequacy of the methods and equipment proposed

To confirm or modify the estimated minimum depths of the
micropiles by determining the ultimate grout to soil bond stress
To assess the contractor s method of drilling and grout injection

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs
of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected samples
of the soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The
results of the tests are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1
Four Sieve Analysis tests ASTM C 117 and C 136 were performed on selected
samples of the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in
soil classification Graphic presentations of the grain size distributions are provided on

APPENDIX C
Percolation Testing
APPENDIX C
Percolation Testing
The permeability of the in situ soils was evaluated by performing two Constant
Head Permeability tests at selected depths within Boring No 4 The tests were
performed at depths ranging from about 17 5 to 30 5 feet below the existing ground
surface
The Constant Head Permeability tests were performed by pumping relatively
clean water into the borehole until the water surface stabilized and remained at
equilibrium for a sufficient period of time The water height and the rate of pumping
were then measured to provide data upon which to base the calculation of the subsoil
permeability The field permeability test results are presented on Plates C 1 and C 2
