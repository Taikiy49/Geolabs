Dear Mr Hibler
In accordance with your request and our fee proposal dated May 17 we have
conducted an evaluation of the existing coal reserve stockpile at the AES Facility located
at Campbell Industrial Park This report is presented to summarize the density and
moisture content data obtained from our laboratory testing of samples collected from the
coal stockpile by drilling exploration
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located at the AES Barbers Point Cogeneration Plant at
Campbell Industrial Park in the Kapolei area of the Island of Oahu Hawaii We
understand that it is desired to obtain information pertaining to the density unit weight
and moisture content of the coal materials stockpiled at the subject site The density data
will be used by AES to calculate the tonnage of material based on the known volume of
the materials Based on the information provided by AES a similar study was last
performed in 1992
Based on the available topographic plan dated the existing
coal pile is approximately 35 to 40 feet in height at the highest point The highest point
has been graded flat with steep side slopes falling to the north east and south The
slopes along the western perimeter are more gradual and were used for drill rig access
to the top of the stockpile
DISCUSSION OF FINDINGS
The field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling six borings extending to
depths of about 40 feet below the existing coal pile surface The approximate location of
the borings is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1

Based on the boring data the materials encountered in the borings generally
consisted of black colored loose to medium dense sandy gravel with cobble fragments
that represent coal materials The logs of the borings are provided on Plates A 1 1
through A 6 2
Of the samples collected from the drilled borings forty two relatively undisturbed
samples were tested in the laboratory to determine the moisture content ASTM D 2216
wet density and dry density ASTM D 2937 of the coal materials The laboratory data is
presented on the logs of borings corresponding to the depth at which the sample was
collected
In general the laboratory test results indicate a range of coal dry densities
spanning between a low of 52 pounds per cubic foot pcf and a high of 62 pcf The
arithmetic mean of the 42 dry densities obtained from the laboratory data is 58 pcf
Therefore it appears that a dry density value of 58 pcf should represent the average dry
density of the coal materials tested from the site Based on similar calculations the mean
total density wet density of the 42 samples was calculated to be 67 pcf
