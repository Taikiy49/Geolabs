to perform the slope stability analyses The program PCSTABLSM is able to calculate
the factor of safety for slope stability using different methods including simplified Janbu
simplified Bishop and Spencer s Method of Slices The Simplified Bishop Method was
used in this case for both undrained and drained conditions under static and seismic
loading conditions Based on our engineering analyses and calculations the factor of
safety for short term and long term stabilities of the MSERW at both locations were
computed to be at least 1 5 For short term stability of MSERW under seismic loading
the factor of safety was computed to be greater than 1 1 the minimum

2 SETTLEMENT CALCULATIONS
The computer program SETTLG was used to calculate the stress at the
midpoint of each layer under the existing embankment and the new MSERW The
program SETTLG utilizes elastic theory which includes Boussinesq Method Mindlin
Method and Westergaard Method to calculate the stress distribution at the desired
points in a half space For our analyses the Westergaard Method of stress distribution
was used The settlement considered is the result of consolidation of the medium stiff
soils Conventional one dimensional consolidation theory is used to estimate the
settlement at MSERW No 7 Based on our engineering analyses and calculations the
settlement at points and 4 are estimated to be on the order of about
and 5 inches respectively The cross section with the idealized soil profile stress
calculations and settlement calculations are attached for reference

3 BEARING CAPACITY CALCULATION
A Short term condition end of construction
The foundation material for MSERW No 5 is a composite foundation material
consisting of the improved soil removal and replacement with rock fill zone and
the silty clay subgrade soils Because of the composite foundation system it is
difficult to calculate the bearing capacity of the composite foundation material
To evaluate the short term bearing capacity of the composite foundation
material a slope stability approach was used
It was assumed that the existing embankment located on the makai side is
generally self stable since a soil nail retaining structure was constructed to for
the temporary excavation Therefore the probable bearing failure of the
MSERW will be forced under the removed and replacement with rock fill zone
As a result the replaced rock fill zone has to be failed in order to have a bearing
failure It is our opinion that the bearing failure would be a small scale slope
failure in this case Therefore the slope stability program was also used to
evaluate the bearing failure of the MSERW To simulate the actual bearing
failure mechanism MSERW founded on the composite foundation material

location is at with a height of about 41 feet and a width of 34 feet Based

existing embankment extending to about 16 to 20 feet below the existing ground The
soft soils grade to medium stiff at depths of about 10 to 11 feet below the ground
surface Therefore the foundation soil is divided into two layers The idealized cross

2 SETTLEMENT CALCULATIONS
The computer program SETTLG was used to calculate the stress at the
midpoint of each layer under the existing embankment and new MSERW The
program SETTLG utilizes elastic theory which includes Boussinesq Method Mindlin
Method and Westergaard Method to calculate the stress distribution at the desired
points in a half space For our analyses the Westergaard Method of stress distribution
was used The settlement considered is the result of consolidation of the soft to
medium stiff soils The conventional one dimensional consolidation theory is used to
estimate the settlement at MSERW No 5 The settlements at points 1 and 2 are
negligible since these points are above the removal and replacement with rock fill zone
Based on our engineering analyses and calculations the settlement at points 3 4
and 5 are estimated to be on the order of 10 inches 8 inches and 6 inches
respectively However because point 3 is located at the limit of the removal and
replacement zone we believe that the actual stress concentration is only about one half
the amount calculated due to boundary conditions Based on the rock fill MSERW
structure and subsurface soil conditions the calculated settlement of 10 inches at
point 3 should be neglected We estimate that the settlement at point 3 to be on the
order of about 4 to 6 inches The cross section with soil profile input file output file

A Short term condition end of construction
The foundation material for MSERW No 5 is a composite foundation material
consisting of the improved soil removal and replacement with rock fill zone and
the silty clay subgrade soils Because of the composite foundation system it is
difficult to calculate the bearing capacity of the composite foundation material
To evaluate the short term bearing capacity of the composite foundation
material a slope stability approach was used
It was assumed that the existing embankment located on the makai side is
generally self stable since a soil nail retaining structure was constructed to for
the temporary excavation Therefore the probable bearing failure of the
MSERW will be forced under the removed and replacement with rock fill zone
As a result the replaced rock fill zone has to be failed in order to have a bearing
failure It is our opinion that the bearing failure would be a small scale slope
failure in this case Therefore the slope stability program was also used to
evaluate the bearing failure of the MSERW To simulate the actual bearing
failure mechanism MSERW founded on the composite foundation material

2 SETTLEMENT CALCULATIONS
The computer program SETTLG was used to calculate the stress at the
midpoint of each layer under the existing embankment and new MSERW The
program SETTLG utilizes elastic theory which includes Boussinesq Method Mindlin
Method and Westergaard Method to calculate the stress distribution at the desired
points in a half space For our analyses the Westergaard Method of stress distribution
was used The settlement considered is the result of consolidation of the soft to
medium stiff soils The conventional one dimensional consolidation theory is used to
estimate the settlement at MSERW No 5 The settlements at points 1 and 2 are
negligible since these points are above the removal and replacement with rock fill zone
Based on our engineering analyses and calculations the settlement at points 3 4
and 5 are estimated to be on the order of 10 inches 8 inches and 6 inches
respectively However because point 3 is located at the limit of the removal and
replacement zone we believe that the actual stress concentration is only about one half
the amount calculated due to boundary conditions Based on the rock fill MSERW
structure and subsurface soil conditions the calculated settlement of 10 inches at
point 3 should be neglected We estimate that the settlement at point 3 to be on the
order of about 4 to 6 inches The cross section with soil profile input file output file
