INTRODUCTION
The additional geotechnical engineering exploration was performed at the updated
dolphin locations for the proposed electrical distribution system improvements at the
Naval Inactive Ship Maintenance Facility NISMF in Middle Loch of Pearl Harbor on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site and its vicinity is shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in Middle Loch of Pearl Harbor on the island of Oahu
Hawaii The loch is bounded by Pearl City Peninsula to the east and Waipio Peninsula
to the west Fleet moorings are provided in the loch for use by the Naval Inactive Ship
Maintenance Facility Pearl Harbor
To supply electricity to the naval ships at moorings concrete and wooden dolphins
are provided throughout the loch Four 4 existing wooden dolphins D 7 D 8 D 10 and
D 11 are located approximately between the center and the southern outlet of the loch
These wooden dolphins were built more than 40 years ago and are in progressively
deteriorating conditions
Based on a May 1987 bathymetric survey by Pelagos Corporation the water depth
in Middie Loch generally varied from about 20 feet at the northern end to about 40 feet
towards the southern outlet
The new and replacement dolphins will be located in relatively shallow water As
measured in the probes the mudline appears to be at approximately 22 5 to 25 feet
below MLLW at the updated dolphin locations

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The geologic conditions underlying the Pearl Harbor lochs were the results of a
complex series of depositional and erosional events due to glacioeustatic sea level
changes and post erosional volcanic activities during the Pleistocene
Following the cessation of activity of the Koolau Volcano alluvial materials were
deposited in an eustarine and deltaic environment in the area of Pear Harbor
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2667 10 Page 7
The sea level fluctuated several times receding perhaps 300 feet below and rising
about 95 feet above the present sea level
As Oahu sank coral reef grew around the shorelines keeping pace with the
subsidence of the island
Concurrent with a rise in sea level during late Pleistocene era eruption of the Salt
Lake Makalapa member of the Honolulu Volcanic Series mantled the coral reefs and
alluvium with deposits of volcanic ash which consolidated to form volcanic tuff layers
Subsequent erosion has dissected and thinned the mantles of coral reefs and tuff at the
lochs
During the recent epoch as the sea level rose to its current stand the Middle Loch
area was covered with a thick deposit of highly compressible harbor sediments
comprising primarily cohesive soils and locally sand and gravel over the underlying
alluvium
Subsoil Conditions
The anticipated pile bearing layer was delineated by drilling four 4 probes one
1 each at the updated dolphin locations to approximately 55 5 to 115 0 feet below
MLLW The approximate locations of the probes are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The updated dolphin sites are generally in shallow water with mudline at
approximately 22 5 to 25 0 feet below MLLW as measured in the probes
During probing easy drilling was experienced to various depths below mudline
indicating a thick layer of highly compressible harbor sediments An attempt had been
made to sample the soft sediments in Probe No 4 Similar to the previous initial
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 2667 10 Page 8
exploration the probing indicates that the thickness of the highly compressible layer
appears to increase from the northern end and the southern outlet of the loch towards
its central area
Previous laboratory consolidation tests indicated that the upper harbor sediments
were very soft and generally normally consolidated
The probing encountered stiff to very stiff clays at the approximate depths of 41 5
to 101 0 feet below MLLW at the updated dolphin locations The probe holes were
extended approximately 10 5 to 14 0 feet into the stiff materials Two 2 samples were
taken in each of the probe holes to verify the materials of the pile bearing layer It
appears that the stiff alluvial clays are heavily over consolidated as encountered in the
previous exploration

General
As determined from our initial geotechnical exploration pile foundations should be
used for the proposed dolphins at their updated locations

the dolphin platforms at the elevation of 117 feet MLLW are recommended to support
the dolphins The updated pile lengths for the relocated dolphins will depend on the
depths of the bearing layer as delineated in the probes drilled at the updated dolphin
locations

Field Exploration

four 4 probes to depths of 55 5 to 115 0 feet below the Mean Lower Low Water Level
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A raft mounted drill rig was
used to drill the probes

examination in the field by our geologist who continuously monitored the driiling
operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in
the laboratory The soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials encountered are presented
Soil samples were obtained from the Probes by driving a standard penetration split
spoon sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches where available are shown on the Probing Logs at the
appropriate sample depths
