HAWAII
As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil
conditions below the above referenced development The purpose of the
study was to evaluate the suitability of the near surface soils as
foundation support for the proposed townhouses and the access roadway
The study which was performed in general accordance with our proposal of
May 8 1984 consisted of excavating seven 7 test pits within the
proposed building sites performing laboratory soil tests and
engineering analysis The general location of the proposed site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 This report
summarizes our findings and recommendations
W O 1412 00 Page 2
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The townhouse project site is about 2 5 acres in size and bounded
by Luinakoa Street to the south general directions Kapakahi Gulch to
the east the existing rocky hillside to the west and the upper gulch
undeveloped area to the north
Preliminary plans were not available at the time this investigation
was done
We anticipate that some amount of cuts and fills is required to
grade the site for townhouse development and related access roadway
construction
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The site is located northerly from the end of Luinakoa Street for
about 800 feet on the westerly side of Kapakahi drainage channel which is
lined with grouted rock and is about 30 feet wide with 1 1 H V side
slopes
An old topographic map indicated a 10 foot wide sewer easement is
parallel to and runs along the top of the channel The site generally
slopes down towards the stream at about 5 to 25 percent gradients from
the central portion of the site The westerly side of the site is a
rocky hillside with gradients of about 40 to 80 percent with steeper
gradients in localized areas The existing elevation at the easterly
portion along the drainage channel varies from about 240 to 165 feet

An existing boulder stockpile is located along the central portion
of the site The boulders may have been excavated for construction of
the drainage channel
The area is partly overgrown with small trees and brush
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A review of the recent test pits excavated at the site indicates
that most of the site is underlain by a layer of dark gray expansive
adobe clay of at least 10 feet thick Rock exposures were noted in the
steeper portions above the proposed development Generalized soil rock
profiles across the site in the east west directions are shown in the
Cross Section A A B B Plate 3

samples and the results are summarized on Plate B 1

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the site is underlain with
relatively deep deposits of dark gray adobe clay Laboratory tests on
the near surface material indicate that the soil has a high shrink swell
potential and can be classified as clay CH according to the Unified
Soil Classification System
Where the adobe clay is fairly thick about 8 to 10 feet or more
and extends up slope beyond the proposed Townhouse sites development
utilizing cuts into the adobe natural ground should be avoided due to
the difficulty of stabilizing the large mass of adobe soil above the
cut slope
Slickensided zones were noted in some of the test pits which
indicate previous shear creep movement of the deposits These deep
sloping adobe clay deposits have high shrink swell downhill creep as
well as landslide potential particularly when steepened by cuts
The general grading concept of removing the upper adobe clay
deposits down to rock or dense competent boulder colluvium does not
appear to be feasible for this area due to the thickness and large extent
of the adobe deposits Extensive grading into the hillside may be
required to remove considerable amount of adobe deposits beyond the
proposed cut limits If the deep upper adobe deposits are not removed
further steepening of these natural slopes by site grading cutting
would create an unstable condition and could trigger eventual downhill
movement of the remaining upper adobe soils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1412 00 Page 5
The adobe clay soils could become very weak when they are
affected by an increase in moisture content water infiltration and
seepage and are subject to slope creep or landslide movement during
extended wet periods
The following general grading concepts for this type of terrain and
soil conditions are recommended
1 Avoid large cuts into the hillside but instead consider
filling up the lower areas with non expansive soils to create a
relatively flat slope and provide a buttress effect against the
toe of the existing adobe clay slope
2 A part of the adobe soil below the uphill area on the natural
slope may be over excavated about 3 feet below the finished
grade and replaced with select material to provide resistance
against the downhill slope movements of the adobe deposits if
the uphill area is to be graded for the project access roadway
construction If possible the alignment should avoid major
cuts into the adobe clay deposits on the existing slopes
Site Grading
The preliminary grading plan was not available at the time of our
investigation We anticipate only minor grading cuts and fills less
than 10 to 15 feet in height for this site

Site Preparation
Prior to filling the site should first be cleared of vegetation
debris and other unsuitable materials The area to receive fill
should then be scarified and recompacted to 90 of its maximum
density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 test method
The general grading concept of removing the adobe soils from the
upper cut area and bury it in the lower deep fill area appears to
be the most economical However there might not be sufficient
future fill to be utilized with this approach since the adobe
soils would still have to be capped off with a minimum of another 3
feet of non expansive imported material Where less than 3 feet of
fill will be placed over the adobe clay the adobe clay should
also need to be over excavated to 3 feet below finished grade and
then capped with select on site rocky material from the excavation
or imported soil
Imported fill capping material if required should generally be
low to non expansive soils and a sample submitted to the project
soils engineer for laboratory testing and approval obtained prior
to its intended delivery to the project site
Boulder Fill
Many surface boulders and a boulder stockpile were noted around the
site In addition we believe a large quantity of boulders will be
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1412 00 Page 7
generated in cut areas If feasible some of these boulders could
be used or buried in the deeper fill area The recommended rock
disposal procedures if required for this site should be as
follows
1 The rocks and boulders should be scattered
2 Sandy material with less than 15 percent fines should be used
to fill the voids between boulders by flooding
3 After adequate filling of the voids between boulders are
completed a 3 foot thick compacted fill using the on site
soils may be used so that the rock and boulders are at least 3
feet below the final pad grade
4 The upper 3 feet of the compacted fill should consist of
on site or imported low to non expansive soils
The rock disposal procedures recommended above should be monitored
by the soils engineer and areas with sufficient fill for disposal
should be delineated by the project surveyor
Cut Slopes
Various soil types are anticipated for low cut slopes In the rock
formation not adobe clay area cut slopes as steep as
H V can be used in dense unweathered basalt However due to
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1412 00 Page 8
the presence of weathered zones and clinker layers which are
typically encountered and require flatter slopes an average cut
slope ratio of H V is recommended for cut into competent
rock formation Based on the existing visible rock outcrops rock
formation is anticipated at shallow depths near the southern
portion end of Luinakoa Street of the site However the test
pits could not penetrate significantly into the formation to verify
the existence or competence of the rock formation Therefore the
rock formation will have to be verified in the field later during
additional final boring soil investigation phase should the
project become feasible
Our present rock area delineations which are shown on the Site
Plan are approximate since natural soil and rock formations are
typically variable
Although the layers of adobe clay encountered in the test pits
are generally less than 10 feet in thickness slope cut into the
adobe clay is generally not recommended unless the height of the
cut is less than 4 to 5 feet or heavy retaining wall with over
excavation behind the wall face should be considered
We recommend that the final design cut slopes in rock and clayey
soils be evaluated during the additional final soil investigation
phase when the grading concept is developed

Foundations
Filled Site
If low expansive select fill imported is available from site
grading and used for the top 3 feet of fill then slab on grade or
conventional footing foundations embedded a minimum of 12 inches
below the outside finished grade can be used on fairly level areas
For the anticipated light loads an allowable bearing value of up
to 2000 p s f can be used
Surface water should be diverted away from the building foundations
In areas where shallow fill of less than 3 feet thick are planned
the underlying clayey soil should be over excavated and backfilled
with select borrow fill if slab on grade and driveway are utilized
Foundation on Natural Ground Post And Beam
Foundation construction would require special design procedures in
order to reduce the shrink swell effect of the subsoils For this
condition post and beam construction utilizing pier type
foundations would be highly desirable due to the sloping terrain
and expansive soils conditions The individual footings can be
readily adjusted should the need occur If a concrete
on grade slab is used it should also be supported over a minimum 3
feet of non expansive backfill over the underlying expansive soil
conditions
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1412 00 Page 10
For post and beam construction we recommend that the expansive
clays under the entire footing plus an extra 12 inches around its
perimeter be over excavated down for a minimum depth of 3 feet
below the finished grade and then replaced with non expansive
borrow soils such as crusher waste select borrow or coral
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM
D 1557 78 The spread footings can then be placed on the compacted
non expansive soil
An allowable bearing value of up to 2000 p s f can be used for
post footings bearing on the above specified over excavated
compacted backfill The bottom of the footing excavation should be
neat and free of loose soils or debris
The ground surface should be shaped to divert surface water away
from building foundations
It is important that surface water be diverted away from the
footings as well as under the structures Tie beams should be
buried sufficiently so that they would not form a basin to pond
water under the house or around the footings Recommendations
discussed under the DRAINAGE section should be followed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1412 00 Page 11
Foundation on Natural Ground On Grade Slab
To reduce the effects of soil heaving for concrete on grade slab
the expansive on site soil should be over excavated down to a depth
of 3 feet below the slab bottom and then replaced with
non expansive borrow soils such as select borrow crusher waste or
coral The over excavation and backfilling should extend a minimum
of 1 5 feet beyond the edge of the slabs
The non expansive borrow soil should be compacted to a minimum of
90 of its maximum dry density as per ASTM D 1557 78 Similar
over excavation and replacement of the expansive adobe clay soils
are recommended for the lanai slabs if constructed on grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the
underlying expansive soils we recommend that the adobe clay
below the capping fill pad be kept moist
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab and
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to protect
against moisture infiltration an impervious moisture barrier may
be incorporated below the slab
The slabs should be independent and free to move from the walls
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1412 00 Page 12
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving the on site
adobe clay over excavation and replacement may be reduced to 2 feet
minimum below the bottom of the driveway slab After moisture
conditioning of the exposed adobe clay it should then be immediately
backfilled with a select non expansive borrow material which would act as
a capping over the underlying adobe clay
Where more than 2 feet of fill is planned select non expansive
borrow material should be used to cap over the expansive clay Where
less than 2 feet of new fill is planned over excavation and replacement
of the adobe clay with select non expansive material is recommended to
provide a minimum 2 feet of capping The above capping requirements
should be followed for a minimum distance of 1 foot beyond the driveway
edge
Joints are recommended in the driveway slab at about 8 to 10 foot
intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some amount of
relative movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay by means of the
over excavation and replacement method similar to the driveway is
recommended to reduce heaving caused by the expansive clay soil
Over excavation beyond the edge of the walkway may not be necessary
GEOLABS HAWAII

Drainage
The finished grade outside the slab pavement and footings should
be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed around
the structure and that gutter water be diverted far away from the
foundations and walkways Excessive landscape watering near the
foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponding water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving
or loss of strength The site drainage should be maintained throughout
the life of the structure
The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete to prevent
ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation

engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all

conformance with the Unified Soil Classification System
