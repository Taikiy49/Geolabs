INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the City of Joy Community Center Building project located in Nanakuli on
the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These recommendations are intended for design input only and are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project site is located adjacent to the Ulehawa Channel in Nanakuli
on the island of Oahu Hawaii The subject lot is approximately rectangular in shape with
an area of about 16 000 square feet The site is currently vacant
It is proposed to construct a new community center building for the City of Joy
Assembly of God Based on the information provided the community center will be a
three story structure with parking at the ground level Based on the information provided
by the project architect we understand that the proposed building will generally be of
concrete column and beam construction with CMU or concrete wall construction with
wooden roof framing In addition based on the information provided by the project
structural engineer we anticipate that maximum loads will be on the order of 300 kips per
column and 12 kips per foot for wall footings
We understand that the finish floor elevation for the ground level parking will be at
approximately 9 5 feet Mean Sea Level MSL which is near the existing grade
Therefore we anticipate minimal grading with cuts and fills of less than about 1 foot will
be required to level the site and attain the design grade However we understand that
the elevation of Nanaikeola Street is at approximately 12 feet MSL and a ramped

subsurface conditions at the project site The subsurface information obtained at the site

pavements at the subject site The work was performed in general accordance with our
revised fee proposal dated Our scope of work included the following
tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and
two operators to and from the project site

approximately 20 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface and

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of two extinct
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae forms the bulk of
the western third of the island while the younger shield Koolau forms the majority of the
eastern two thirds of the island It is believed that Waianae became extinct while Koolau
was still active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lavas in central
Oahu
The project site is located on the western side of the Waianae Mountain During
the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of continental
glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to fill the oceanic
basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea When the glaciers
melted and receded an excess of water became available such that the sea levels rose
to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of
accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits
at correspondingly higher elevations and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters
protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys in the
sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and calcareous
materials caused consolidation of the soft deltaic materials and lagoonal deposits and
induration of the calcareous reef materials Land development and reclamation projects
within the last century have brought the project site to its present form

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our drilled borings and probing operations at the site the proposed
project site is underlain by near surface clayey and silty fill materials up to about 7 feet
thick underlain by dense to very dense clayey silty and sandy gravels coral detritus
extending to the maximum depth drilled of about 21 5 feet below the existing ground
surface Based on the subsoils encountered at the site we believe that a shallow
foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings may be used for
support of the proposed building
Cavities were noted in two of the fifteen probes drilled at the proposed footing
locations We recommend that a program of probing and grouting be implemented during
construction at the footing locations where cavities were noted and at the columns that
were not probed A representative from Geolabs Hawaii should observe the probing and
grouting operation to evaluate the extent of cavities where encountered Special
attention should be given to the Foundation Probing section of this report Detailed
discussions and geotechnical engineering design recommendations pertaining to the
proposed project are presented in the following sections of this report
Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the site we believe that a
shallow foundation system may be used to support the proposed building structure An
allowable bearing pressure of up to 4 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for
design of isolated spread and continuous footings bearing on the recompacted
near surface soils This allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4040 00 Page 8
be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as wind and or seismic forces In
general footings should be embedded to a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent finish grades
Because of the cavities noted in the coral detritus below the foundations a
program of probing and grouting the foundation locations is recommended Detailed
recommendations for probing and grouting the foundations are discussed in the
Foundation Probing section of this report
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the
utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to
avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to
reduce the potential for foundation settlement
We estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for
footings bearing on the recompacted near surface soils as recommended herein to be
less than one inch total with differential settlements on the order of 1 2 inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive
earth pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system
A coefficient of friction of 0 40 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted
near surface soils Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This
assumes that the soil around footings is well compacted Unless covered by pavements
or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
Footing subgrades should be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction prior to placement of reinforcing steel and concrete Relative compaction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4040 00 Page 9
refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil established in accordance with test
procedures In addition the footing subgrade should be firm and unyielding
Where soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of the footing
excavations they should be over excavated to expose the underlying firm materials
The over excavation should be backfilled with lean concrete or select granular material
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the footing should be
extended down to the underlying competent material
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to evaluate the competency of the
bearing materials and embedment depths
Foundation Probing
To reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of
cavities below the foundations we recommend that a program of cavity probing and
grouting be conducted below the footings locations where cavities were noted in the
probes and below footing locations which were not probed We estimate that about 7 to
10 holes may be required The probe holes should be at least 3 inches in diameter and
should extend to at least 20 feet below the planned bottom of footing If cavities and or
voids are encountered or suspected during the probing operation additional probe holes
should be drilled at closer spacings to help delineate the vertical and lateral extent of the
cavity and or void Following the drilling of each probe hole a 2 inch PVC pipe should be
inserted into the probe holes to preserve the open hole integrity for grouting A field
engineer or geologist from Geolabs Hawaii should be present to og the probe holes and
to determine the locations of any additional probe holes if needed
The probe holes and any cavities discovered should be backfilled with
sand cement grout We recommend that a low strength sand cement grout mixture with
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 4040 00 Page 10
an approximately 6 to 9 inch slump range pumped at low to moderate pressures be
utilized for the grouting operation
Because of the potential for encountering large cavities at the site we recommend
that unit prices for additional probing and grouting be obtained during bidding In addition
the probe drill should be available on site until the probing and grouting operation is
complete In addition the contractor should be made aware that a longer lag time
between probing grouting operations and foundation construction may be required in the
construction schedule
The probing and grouting program should be conducted under the observation of a
representative from Geolabs Hawaii This would allow our firm to monitor the presence of
cavities and to allow additional recommendations to be made if excess grout take and or
changed conditions are observed
Slabs On Grade
Based on the current design concept we anticipate that concrete slabs on grade
will be used in the waiting area and stairwells of the proposed building In general for
building slabs not subjected to vehicular traffic we believe that a minimum 4 inch thick
layer of cushion fill consisting of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation
should be placed below the slab to provide more uniform support of the slab and to serve
as a capillary moisture break To reduce future moisture infiltration and subsequent
damage to floor coverings an impervious moisture vapor barrier such as a plastic
membrane is recommended on top of the gravel cushion layer Two inches of moist fine
sand may be considered on top of the plastic membrane to provide protection of the
membrane and to aid in curing of the slab concrete Flexible floor coverings such as
carpet or sheet vinyl should be considered since they can better mask minor slab
cracking

In general the exposed slab subgrade should be scarified to a depth of about
8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Details of subgrade
preparation are presented in the Site Grading section of this report
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that a minimum 6 inch thick layer of select borrow subbase be provided

The area adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab with
non expansive impervious soil and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce
the potential of water ponding around the foundation

At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
Soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing and grubbing operations
should be over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with compacted select fill moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Prior to placement of
the backfill fill material the over excavated subgrade should be scarified to a depth of at
least 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The excavated soft loose soils
should be properly disposed of off site
Areas designated to receive fills and the finished subgrades should be scarified to
a minimum depth of 8 inches where possible moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent
above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction The compaction requirement for pavement subgrade should be increased to
a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum
dry density
In general the excavated on site soils may be reused as a source of general fill
materials provided that they contain no lumps or fragments greater than 3 inches in
maximum dimension In addition because of the slight expansive potential of the
near surface silty and clayey soils encountered at the site the on site silty and clayey
soils should not be used as fill or backfill within the upper 6 inches below the finish
subgrade in the pavement and slab areas

Pavements
We understand that the ground floor will be used for parking We anticipate that
the vehicle loading for this project will consist primarily of passenger cars and light trucks
Based on the laboratory test results the near surface fill soils at the site have a
CBR value of about 18 7 with a swell value of about 1 5 percent We have made our
preliminary pavement design assuming the pavement subgrade soil will be similar to the
material tested Based on the above assumptions we recommend that the following
pavement section be used for preliminary design purposes
Flexible Pavement
2 5 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Basalt Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 5 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on a Moist Compacted Subgrade
The pavement subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches
moisture conditioned to 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted

to not less than 95 percent relative compaction The base course should consist of
crushed basalt aggregate compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
We recommend that field density tests and laboratory CBR tests be performed on
samples of the actual subgrade soils encountered during construction to confirm the
above recommended design section The recommended pavement section is based on
the assumption that good drainage will be provided for areas adjacent to pavements
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry

constructed to collect excessive water from landscaping irrigation For long term
performance we recommend that a subdrain system be constructed adjacent to
paved landscape areas
Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines will be required for the proposed project In
general for utility trenches a granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining
granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel should also be used for the trench backfill
up to about 12 inches above the pipes or about 12 inches above the groundwater level to
provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compactive effort of the
backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is critical that the
free draining material be used to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the
haunches of pipes and to provide adequate support around the sides of the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the excavated on site soils or
GEOLABS HAWAII

select fill material The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not
less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench
backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to at least 95 percent relative
compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of the concrete to
reduce water infiltration

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
three borings to depths ranging from 20 1 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface
and fifteen probe holes to depths of approximately 15 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings and
probes were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with solid stem augers
The maierials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our Field Engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the
Unified Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the
materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch
drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
The probe holes were drilled in an attempt to locate cavities or soft zones in the
subsurface material The probing operations were monitored by our Field Engineer on a
near continuous basis Soft zones and or cavities were noted by changes in drilling
pressure sound and other characteristics of the materials Graphic representations of
the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Probes Plates A 4 through A 18

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties
The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths
One sieve analysis test was performed to evaluate the gradation characteristics
and to aid in soil classification The test was performed on a selected soil sample in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation C 136 Graphic presentation of the test results
is provided on Plate B 1
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected sample of
the soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification Results of
the test are summarized on the Logs of Boring and are graphically presented on
Plate B 2
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio Test ASTM D 1883 was performed on a
near surface soil sample to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the soils
Results of the test are presented on Plate B 3
