INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical engineering
exploration for the design of the borrow sites of the proposed Village Pokai Bay
Phase residential development The general location of the project site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Our work on the project was performed in general accordance with our
proposal to RYM Inc dated This report summarizes our
preliminary findings and recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed development consists of clearing and grading the approximately
32 acre site to construct 198 single family residential lots Substantial borrow fill
material will be required for the mass grading of the site The two 2 borrow sites
are situated adjacent to the Phase residential development site
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our preliminary exploration was to obtain soil rock data to
evaluate the borrow material quality and to aid in the design of the mass grading for
the proposed borrow site
The scope of our exploration consisted of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted backhoe and
operator to and from the project site
2 Excavation and sampling of eight 8 test pits at the project site to
depths of 10 to 16 feet below the existing ground surface
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the test pits by an
Engineering Geologist from our firm
4 Reconnaissance of the existing ground surface conditions exposed
5 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration as an aid to classifying the materials encountered and
determining their engineering properties

6 Preparation of six 6 copies of this report summarizing our work on the
project and presenting our findings and recommendations
7 Coordination of our overall work on the project and client design team
consultation by a Principal Engineer of our firm
8 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and
clerical support
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing are
presented in the appendices of the report
GENERAL SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the base of Puu Mailiili in the Waianae district on
the leeward coast of the island of Oahu Hawaii The lower portion of the site was
formerly used as a coral limestone quarry The remainder of the site is raw land that
may have been used as pasture but is now covered with dryland vegetation such as
grasses kiawe trees and haole koa
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Borrow Site Dirt
Five 5 test pits were excavated in this area The test pits encountered a
surface layer of adobe clay with boulders over stiff silty clay with boulders older
alluvium colluvium The adobe clay layer was found to be about 10 to 12 feet
thick within the proposed borrow site but thinned to less than 5 feet thick in the
upslope areas
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2294 00 A Page 4
Borrow Site Coral
Three 3 test pits were excavated in this area and supplemented with visual
observations of the exposed cut slopes along the lower portions of the site
The test pits encountered about 2 to 4 feet of adobe clay with boulders over
stiff silty clay older alluvium colluvium Coralline deposits were visible in the
existing cut slopes along the lower portions of this area Basalt rock formation may
be encountered at deeper depths in the southwestern portion of the borrow site
Groundwater was not encountered in the pits at the time of our exploration
Detailed descriptions of the soil conditions encountered in the test pits are
presented on the test pit logs
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our findings disclosed that the borrow site soils generally consist of stiff
adobe clay and silty clay with boulders Coralline soils may be available from the
lower portions of the site It appears that the soils at the site may be suitable for use
in the mass grading of the proposed subdivision grading
Due to the moderately to high swelling potential the clayey borrow soils should
be kept a minimum of 3 feet below structures
Non expansive coralline soils should be used to cap over the expansive soils
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2294 00 A Page 5
Borrow Site Grading
Clearing Grubbing and Stripping
Existing vegetation should be cleared and stripped three roots in excess of
1 5 inches in diameter should be grubbed
The stripped surface soil containing organic matter should not be used as fill
Entrapped deleterious materials such as mats of grasses or piles of wood
etc under the fill should be avoided
Boulders were encountered in the test pits and a significant quantity of
boulders could be generated from the site grading Because the borrow site
is an excavation type construction boulders should be hauled off site or
stockpiled for use in fills at other locations
The stripping operation should be continuously monitored by the soils
engineer to verify removal of unsuitable materials
Slope Construction
Based on our findings it appears that the required cut slope at the borrow site
would be made in clayey soil A maximum cut slope ratio of two horizontal to
one vertical 2H 1V is recommended for the slope cuts in soil
Relatively easy excavation and conventional site grading procedures are
anticipated for earthwork in the on site soil
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2294 00 A Page 6
Slope benches are recommended where the vertical height of slope exceeds
30 feet
Boulder Fills
Many boulders will be generated from the excavation Boulders encountered
in the excavation may be used in the deep fills provided that the following
recommendations are followed
1 Boulders must not be nested together They should be placed so that
compaction equipment would be able to compact around them
2 Large boulders should not be used within three 3 feet from finish
grade
3 No boulder larger than five 5 feet in its largest dimension should be
placed in the fill it may be necessary to reduce the size of larger
boulders or dispose of them off site
4 Care must be exercised to avoid placement of boulders in the proposed
utility alignments to prevent difficulty in the later excavation of the utility
trenches
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comment prior to construction
This review is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineer services during site grading and foundation phases of the
work This to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface
conditions differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate
modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from visual observations and test pits Variations of
conditions may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re
evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The location and elevation of the field data points were approximately
determined from reference points shown on the Grading Plan prepared by
Community Planning Inc The physical location and elevation of the field data points
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
