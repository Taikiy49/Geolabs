INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to evaluate the
subsurface conditions at the proposed site and to
provide soil data for the design and construction of
the proposed Shower Tree Estate Subdivision at Waiakea
South Hilo Island of Hawaii
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SHOWER TREE ESTATE SUBDIVISION P 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in Waiakea South Hilo about
3 miles south of Hilo Bay The site is bounded on the south
and west by undeveloped land on the east by Ahualani Subdi
vision and on the north by Kawailani Street
The total area of the site is 19 5 acres consisting of unde
veloped formerly cultivated sugarcane land The terrain is
irregular and hilly sloping generally to the northeast A
dry stream channel trending southwest to northeast transects
the northern section of the site
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed plan is to clear and grade the site for a single
family residential development consisting of about 53 lots with
access roadways to the lots on both sides of the existing dry
stream
Preliminary grading plan for the site was not available during
our exploration It is estimated that site grading for the
project would be minimum with cuts and fills less than 10 feet
high
The existing dry stream channel on the site will generally
remain at its present alignment

Subsurface Exploration
Five test pits were dug with a backhoe to depths of 3 0 to
7 5 feet below the existing ground surface to explore the
subsurface conditions and to obtain soil samples for labo
ratory testing The test pit locations are shown on the
enclosed Site Plan Plate 1
Test pit No 5 was utilized for percolation testing A
description of the field exploration test pit logs labo
ratory and percolation test results are given in the Appendix
Geology
The proposed site located on the northeast flank of Moana
Loa is underlain by a prehistoric lava flow of approximately
10 feet thick The flow is a dense Aa flow predominately
olivine basalt of the Kau Volcanic Series This area is geo
logically young with stony silty clay loam soils on recent
Tava flows
The upper surface clayey soils which was formed in volcanic
ash havehigh in place moisture contents exceeding 100 percent
Soil Conditions
Based on the test pits and field observations the site is
covered by a layer of dark brown silty clay weathered ash
and gravel layer about 1 to 1 5 feet thick This layer is
underlain by a brown silty clay weathered ash and large

Boulders ranged in size from 1 to 3 feet Several small

Percolation Test
A percolation test was performed in test pit 5 for use
in the design of cesspool system
The percolation test was performed by cleaning the pit of
loose material filling the pit with 250 gallons of water
from a water tank then measuring the dissipation rate
The results of the percolation test indicated a rate of
0 15 gallon per square foot per minute in the upper silty
clay loam material Much higher percolation rate would
be obtained in the unerlying lava flows

From the topography of the project site it appears that

Based on the field exploration and laboratory tests no
major settlement problems are anticipated for fills up to

Slopes
Slope ratio of 2 horizontal to 1 vertical can be used for
fill slopes
For cut slope in fractured lava rock design slope ratios
of 3 4 horizontal to 1 vertical can be used
For rock cut less than 6 feet high steeper slope ratios
up to horizontal to 1 vertical may be used in the lava
rock formation
Where ash or stony clay formation is encountered at the
upper surface layer the cut slope should be pulled back
to a 2 horizontal to 1 vertical 2 1 ratio
Foundations
For the proposed light residential structures spread
footing foundations or slab on grade may be used
Slab should be set back from the top of the fill slopes
a minimum distance of 10 feet This also includes the
areas along the two sides of the existing dry stream channel
Uniess bearing on rock all post footings away from the tops
of slopes should be placed 12 inches below the finish grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
SHOWER TREE ESTATE SUBDIVISION P 7
Post footings located close to the tops of slopes should
have a minimum horizontal set back distance of 5 feet
from the slope face
For light loads anticipated an allowable bearing pressure
of 2000 p s f can be used for footings resting on the stiff
natural soil or compacted fill Higher bearing value up to
8000 p s f may be used for footing bearing on dense lava
rock formation
If a footing is located next to a utility trench it should
extend down to the bottom of the utility trench Surface
and gutter water should be diverted away from the building
foundations
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should bear either entirely on compacted fill
Or on cut areas Where hard lava rock is encountered at
part of the slab subgrade level the hard rock should be
over excavated a minimum depth of 12 inches and then replaced
with compacted fill to match the adjacent ground density
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of Aa
base course compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density
To protect against moisture damage of the flooring material
a polyethylene moisture barrier should be considered

Roadway Pavement
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the development
For pavement section on lava rock formation or on well compacted
granular fill the following design criteria may be used
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
Based on the results of the laboratory CBR test it is estimated
that the on site brown volcanic ash silty clay soil can provide
a CBR value of 15 The preliminary pavement design may be as
follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
6 inch Select Borrow
14 inch Total Pavement Thickness
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the subgrade soils
as they are encountered in the field during construction and
the design sections can be revised based on these tests

rtals
