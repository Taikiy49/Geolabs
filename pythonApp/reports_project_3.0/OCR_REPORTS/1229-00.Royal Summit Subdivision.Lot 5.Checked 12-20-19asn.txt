As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil
conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The
study consisted of drilling two shallow borings laboratory
testing of the swelling potential of the soils a review of the
subdivision site grading plans and the preliminary house
foundation plans This report summarizes our observations and
recommendations

PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a one story structure approxi
mately 48 by 47 feet in plan dimension with a 2 car garage and
lanai Concrete on grade slab is planned for the entire
structure
The foundation plan for the structure is shown on the Site Plan
Plate l
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is on the western side of Nohoalii Street in the
Royal Summit Subdivision located in Aiea Oahu Hawaii The
lot is generally level with a slight slope down toward the west
A review of the site grading plans for the subdivision indicates
that Lot 5 required cut up to 10 feet deep
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
The borings encountered 0 to 1 5 feet of recently placed fill
over 1 5 to 2 5 feet of reddish brown clay residual soil which
was underlain by weathered rock and saprolite
Laboratory swell tests indicated that the upper fill was com
pacted dry 22 moisture contents and had moderate swelling
potential

The underlying residual soil was very moist 40 moisture
contents and exhibited low expansion potential in its natural
State However previous experience indicates that this type of
material could have high swelling potential if allowed to dry
out Therefore measures should be taken to prevent excessive
drying of the soil
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the existing near surface
soils have moderate swelling potential therefore special
foundation design and subgrade preparation is recommended
House Foundation
The recently placed fill soil and underlying residual soil are
moderately expansive Ideally the soils below 3 feet of
finished grade should be removed and replaced with compacted
non expansive soil such as select borrow crusher waste or
coral The house slabs and foundations could then be placed on
this non expansive fill
An alternate method of partial removal and subgrade saturation
could also be used in order to reduce costs This method would
be less costly than the above but would involve a higher risk
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1229 00 Page 4
For the alternate method the general procedure listed hereunder
should be followed
1 The recently placed fill should be removed for a minimum
depth of 12 inches below the slab cushion layer
2 Hydrated lime should be placed at the bottom of the 12 inch
deep excavation We believe that ten 50 1b bags of hydrated
lime would be adequate to cover the slab subgrade
3 The subgrade soils should be saturated by sprinkling for at
least 1 day prior to placing the upper 12 inch layer It is
important that the soils engineer should inspect the above
subgrade preparation and check the moisture contents of the
underlying soils prior to pouring of the slab and perimeter
footing
4 Non expansive soil such as crusher waste should be used to
backfill the upper 12 inches to finished subgrade level
The fill should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of ASTM
D 1557 78
5 In order to provide a barrier against future water infiltra
tion under the slab we recommend that the slab perimeter
footing be deepened another 6 inches from 12 to 18 inches
below the outside finished ground surface
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1229 00 Page 5
6 Hydrated lime should also be placed at the bottom of the slab
perimeter footing excavation About one pound of lime per
lineal foot of excavation should be applied The footing
excavation should then be saturated
The bottom of the footing excavation should be neat and free
of loose soils It is anticipated that very soft silt and
lime mud deposited at the footing after saturation will need
to be removed just prior to concrete placement
The concreting of footing and slab should be coordinated in
such a way that the time period between the initial excava
tion and final concrete placement should be kept to a minimum
in order to prevent excessive subgrade drying
7 We recommend that the slab on grade be placed over a 4 inch
layer of base course as cushion material
Lot Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings
should be shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to
avoid ponding condition Also the owners should be advised to
install gutter around the house and they should also be made
aware that gutter water should be diverted far away from the
perimeter footings and walkways
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1229 00 Page 6
Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slab
should also be avoided Planters next to the foundations should
be avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to prevent water
from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper perfor
mance of the above recommended foundations since ponded water
could cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
loss of strength or heaving The foundation excavations should
be properly backfilled tight against slab footing immediately
after setting of the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and main
tained to drain all surface run off away from the slab and
footing foundation
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
