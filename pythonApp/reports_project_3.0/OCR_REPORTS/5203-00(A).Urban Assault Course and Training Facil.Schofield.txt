2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lava from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and the younger shield Koolau is estimated to be late
Pliocene to early Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during
which time erosion incised deep valleys into the Koolau shield volcanic activity returned
with a series of lava flows followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These series are
referred to as the Honolulu Volcanic Series
Schofield Plateau was formed when lavas from the Koolau Volcano ponded against
the already eroded slopes of the Waianae Volcano in the late Pleistocene Epoch The
dips of the lava beds generally range from 3 to 5 degrees The lava flows on the plateau
have weathered to depths of 15 to 30 meters and are characterized by the red colors of
the soil The deep narrow canyons such as Waikakalaua Kipapa and Kaukonahua are
a result of this weathering The brown and reddish brown soils generally consist of ancient
alluvial deposits overlying the weathered rock

2 4 Earthquakes and Seismicity
In general earthquakes throughout the world are caused solely by shifts in the
tectonic plates In contrast earthquake activity in Hawaii is linked primarily to volcanic
activity Therefore earthquake activity in Hawaii generally occurs before or during
volcanic eruptions In addition earthquakes may result from the underground
movement of magma that comes close to the surface but does not erupt The Island of
Hawaii experiences thousands of earthquakes each year but most of the earthquakes
are so small that they can only be detected by instruments However some of the
earthquakes are strong enough to be felt and a few cause minor to moderate damage
In general earthquakes associated with volcanic activity are most common on
the Island of Hawaii Earthquakes that are directly associated with the movement of
magma are concentrated beneath the active Kilauea and Mauna Loa Volcanoes on the
Island of Hawaii Because the majority of the earthquakes in Hawaii over 90 percent of
earthquakes are related to volcanism the risk of seismic activity and degree of ground
shaking diminishes with increased distance from the Island of Hawaii The Island of
Hawaii has experienced numerous earthquakes greater than Magnitude 5 M5
however earthquakes are not confined only to the Island of Hawaii
To a lesser degree the Island of Maui has experienced several earthquakes
greater than Magnitude 5 Therefore moderate to strong earthquakes have occurred in
the County of Maui The effects of earthquakes occurring on the Islands of Hawaii and
Maui may be felt on the Island of Oahu For example small landslides occurred on the
Island of Oahu as a result of the Maui Earthquake of Some houses on the
Island of Oahu were reportedly damaged as a result of the Lanai Earthquake of 1871
M7
In the last 150 years of recorded history we are not aware of earthquakes
occurring on the Island of Oahu that is greater than Magnitude 6 An earthquake of
Magnitude 4 8 to 5 0 occurred along the Diamond Head Fault in 1948 on the Island of
Oahu The moderate tremor resulted in broken store windows ruptured building walls
and broken underground water mains

3 1 Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we
recommend that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous
footings be used to support the new structures planned for the project An allowable
bearing pressure of up to may be used for the design of shallow
footings bearing on the stiff to hard clayey soils This bearing value is for dead plus live
loads and may be increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused
by wind or seismic forces
In general footings should have a minimum width of 300 millimeters In addition
footings should be embedded a minimum of 450 millimeters below the lowest adjacent
finished grade Foundations located next to utility trenches and or easements should be
embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of
the utility trench or embedded as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement
is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional
structural loads and to reduce the potential for appreciable foundation settlement
The soils encountered at the footing subgrade level should be recompacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Soft and or loose materials that are
encountered at the footing subgrade level should be over excavated to expose the
underlying firm or dense materials The over excavation should be backfilled with select
granular fill material compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the
footings may be extended down to the firm or dense materials
If the foundations are designed and constructed in accordance with our
recommendations we estimate that total settlements of footings bearing on the stiff to hard
clayey soils may be on the order of about 25 millimeters We estimate that differential
settlements between adjacent footings supported on the stiff to hard clayey soils to be on
the order of about 15 millimeters

3 2 Slabs On Grade
Based on our laboratory test results the clayey soils encountered at the site
exhibit relatively low to moderately high potential for shrinking and swelling when
subjected to moisture fluctuations To reduce the potential for future distress to the
lightly loaded slabs on grade resulting from shrinking and swelling of the clayey soils

depth of about 200 millimeters moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction

3 7 Corrosion Protection
Four sets of pH and minimum resistivity tests were performed to evaluate the
corrosivity of the on site soils clayey silt and silty clay materials generally encountered
at the site The results of these tests are presented on Plate B 2 of Appendix B The
minimum resistivity values of the near surface soil samples testes ranged from 4 000 to
13 000 ohm cm which correspond to Corrosion Ratings of 3 to 5 corrosive to mildly
corrosive based on the guidelines provided by the City County of Honolulu Board of
Water Supply Therefore we recommend that all metallic substructures such as ductile
iron water lines in contact with the soils be properly designed to be protected against
potential corrosion A corrosion engineer should be consulted for detailed
recommendations on corrosion protection

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs
of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Seven 50 mm Ring Swell tests were performed on relatively undisturbed and
remolded samples of the surface soils to evaluate the swelling potential of the
near surface soils The test results are presented on Plate B 1
Four sets of Corrosion tests including pH ASTM G 51 and Minimum Resistivity
ASTM G 57 were performed on selected soil samples obtained from our field
exploration The results of these tests are summarized on Plate B 2
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected samples
of the clayey soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification
Results of the tests are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 3
One Direct Shear test ASTM D 3080 was performed on a selected sample to
evaluate the shear strength characteristics of the materials tested Results of the direct
shear test are presented on Plate B 4
Two laboratory California Bearing Ratio tests ASTM D 1883 were performed on
bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics
of the soils The test results are presented on Plates B 5 and B 6
Two Modified Proctor Compaction tests ASTM D 1557 were performed on bulk
samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density and moisture content
relationships The test results are presented on Plates B 7 and B 8
