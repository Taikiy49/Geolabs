This report presents the results of our design level investigation
on the subject property The purpose of this investigation was
to obtain additional necessary subsurface data upon which to
base specific engineering recommendations for design of the
structure foundations
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The investigation site is located on the north side of Ohai
Street between Wainuku Street and Puneo Street The property
is approximately 145 feet wide along Ohai Street 190 feet wide
along Waipilopilo Stream and approximately 345 feet deep

Based upon presently available data the proposed structures
will be 3 story wood frame buildings varying between 120 and
170 feet long by approximately 35 feet wide Three 3
large structures and one of limited area are presently planned
on the site The buildings are planned to be constructed at
or near existing grade Maximum column loads will be on the
order of 50 kips
FIELD EXPLORATION
In addition to the original two borings drilled on the site
in September 1972 three additional borings were excavated on
November 30 and Boring logs for the original
two borings B 1 and B 2 have been included again in this
report for continuity The three additional borings desig
nated B 1A through B 3A were drilled through the surface ash
layer and into underlying basalt bedrock The location of all
borings are shown in Plate 1 and the boring logs for all
borings are included with this report as Appendix A
The explorations were undertaken with a minuteman portable
rotary drill rig and continuous flight augers
In ail borings Standard Penetration Tests were made at regular
intervals The Standard Penetration Test consists of driving

hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The number of
blows required to drive the samples the middle 12 inches is
termed the Standard Penetration Resistance N and is an

a soil These resistance values are plotted on the boring logs

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
_ Late phase Mauna Kea ash eruptions blanketed the eastern half
of Hawaii This ash overlies the Mauna Kea basalts to 20 to 30
foot depths north of the Wailuku River in Hilo Tropical
weathering of the Pahala Ash rainfall over 100 inches a year
and average temperatures over 70 F has formed a volcanic clay
soil with moisture contents exceeding 100 to 200 in many areas
Cd
1972
Soils
The very soft red clayey silt found on the site is charac
terized by a moisture content exceeding 2003 thixotropic
properties and irreversible change from highly plastic clay
or clayey silt to a non plastic sand silt upon drying
Natural drying seldom extends more than 1 to 2 feet below the
ground surface This soil will stand only slight working
2 or 3 passes with a cat by heavy equipment before it loses
all cohesive strength The depth of this soil varies from
20 feet in boring B 3A to 33 feet in boring B 2A
Groundwater
Although the materials encountered have high moisture contents
no free groundwater was encountered during drilling However
groundwater was measured in boring B 2A at a depth below grade
of 30 feet t approximately 16 hours after drilling No
caving occurred during the drilling however because of the
wet and soft nature of the subsurface materials some caving
could occur during construction since the caisson diameters
will greatly exceed that of the exploration equipment 3 inch
diameter

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based upon the results of our investigations which included
field exploratién laboratory testing and engineering analyses
the following conclusions and recommendations are applicable
A Foundations
1 Due to the thixotropic characteristics of the subsurface
materials encountered and their susceptibility to high
settlements under loading it is recommended that the
proposed structures be founded on drilled cast in
place end bearing caissons or piers founded at a mini
mum depth of 12 inches into hard basalt bedrock As
indicated above the bedrock on the site varies in
depth from 20 to 33 feet below existing grade The
allowable bearing value for the end bearing caissons
in basalt is 10 kips per square foot Lateral loads
must primarily be resisted by friction and passive
resistance in the bedrock as opposed to the overlying
ash Settlements of structures founded on basalt will
be negligible
B Grading
Due to the unique and extremely difficult working charac
teristics of the onsite materials site grading should be
m7
Mr
kept to an absolute minimum Any grading performed on the
site will require that the underlying Pahala Ash be protec
ted and stabilized by special procedures To accommodate
construction equipment the clayey silt below the topsoil
Should be covered by a blanket of 18 inch thick layer of
select material Aa lava material is Satisfactory as
the select protection material
Fills over the onsite materials should generally be avoid
ed due to their consolidation characteristics The onsite
materials are not considered adequate for fill If struc
tures are planned for the rear portion of the site the
structures should be cantilevered over the existing slopé
and founded in bedrock as indicated above
Slope Stability
The existing slope towards the rear of the property should
be safe if not excessively disturbed or oversteepened
during construction
Retaining Walls
If retaining walls are planned they should also be founded
in the basalt bedrock It is recommended that retaining walls
be avoided where possible

E Exterior Concrete Walkways Decking etc
Exterior concrete walkways decking etc may be placed
on the stabilized soils Large areas of concrete should
be reinforced with a minimum of 6x6 10x10 welded wire
fabric Sidewalks and other small concrete latwork
should contain an adequate amount of saw cuts or construc
tion joints
F Drainage
Surface and roof drainage should be collected and ade
quately conveyed off site in non erodible drainage devices
CESSPOOLS
No cesspools were detected during the exploration however
it is probable that they do exist If cesspools are encoun
tered during construction they should be cut off 5 feet
below grade the excavations filled with sand flooded into
place No mechanical compaction should be attempted
INSPECTION
It is recommended that the excavation of all caissons be in
spected by the soils engineer or his representative to deter
mine that the structures will be founded in the recommended
bearing stratum
