This second quarterly report is presented to summarize our progress on the Kuahea
Street Area Movement project This report provides summary information pertaining to the
status of the Phase II field exploration and installation of instrumentation In addition a
summary of the earth movements and subsurface water levels detected to date during the
Phase III instrument monitoring effort is provided
PHASE II FIELD EXPLORATION
The completed field exploration consisted of drilling 15 borings ranging in depths from
about 40 to 100 feet below the existing ground surface Of the 15 borings drilled
inclinometer casings identified as Boring Nos I 1 through I 8 were installed in eight of the
borings The inclinometer casing installations ranged in depths from about 37 to 92 feet
below the existing ground surface
Seven of the 15 borings were converted to nested piezometer installations identified
as Boring Nos P 1 through P 7 The nested piezometers consisted of screened pipe
casings set to monitor the potential for groundwater in discrete 10 to 15 foot length intervals
in the subsurface The piezometer installations ranged in depths from about 37 to 69 feet
below ground level
The field exploration drilling and instrument installation commenced in August 1999
and was completed in June 2000 The attached Instrument Location Plan Plate 2 shows
the locations as built of the borings and instruments installed at the project site
The official start date of the 12 month instrument monitoring program began in
February 2000 A few instrument readings were taken beginning in October 1999 as the
instruments were installed to provide some baseline survey data

P 7 the records show that the location and amount of subsurface water seepage may vary
encountered some significant subsurface water seepage at their respective screen interval
well points The water level build up in the piezometers was measured generally ranging

OCCURRENCE OF
MOVEMENT
During the past quarterly monitoring period the water level in some of the standpipes
was raised and lowered in an effort to evaluate the recharge and discharge capacity of the
screened well points The evaluation is intended to determine whether recharge or discharge
of water is freely occurring at the well point and whether the surrounding strata is
hydraulically connected with the well point The manipulation of the water level in the wells
was achieved by either adding or removing a known volume of water from the well and
monitoring the discharge or recharge of water within the well over time Based on the data
evaluated thus far it appears that the piezometer well points are in proper functioning order

DISCUSSION
Based on our monitoring program to date the following discussion is provided to
record our progress and some noteworthy observations of the existing conditions at the
project site
Rainfall Summary
A review of the National Weather Service NWS rainfall data for the Palolo Valley
area between May and July 2000 was performed Based on our review of the available data
the rainfall recorded at the Palolo Valley gauge was generally light and below normal for the
Palolo Valley area The month of May received about 0 59 inches of rainfall The months
of June and July received about 1 75 inches and 1 60 inches of rainfall respectively The
rainfall was the result of normal trade wind showers at times being enhanced by the
intermittent close proximity of upper level low pressure systems with respect to the Hawaiian
Island location Abnormally large rain producing storm systems or associated storm water
runoff was not experienced since the installation of instruments had been completed
Pavement Structural Deformation
The conditions described in the first quarterly progress report remain essentially
unchanged from the conditions observed during our recent site visits No new crack pattems
or ground surface disturbance was observed over the past three months with the exception
of some possible new disturbance to the asphaltic concrete pavement at Kuahea Place
fronting the address of 2343 Kuahea Street
Some slight rippling and heave of the paved road surface was observed adjacent to
the previously noted shear crack pattern located in the vicinity of Boring No I 4 The rippling
has created a slightly uneven road surface that was not present in prior observations
In addition the ground surface located adjacent to the north side of Kuahea Street in
the vicinity of Boring No I 1 appears to be slowly subsiding creating a noticeable depression
adjacent to the paved road surface

SUMMARY
The seven piezometer and eight inclinometer installations have been completed as
of June 2000 As previously mentioned the 12 month instrument monitoring program
commenced in February 2000 The boring logs for Boring Nos I 1 through I 8 are included
as part of this progress report In addition a sketch of the typical inclinometer installation and

Based on the inclinometer data recorded to date the Waiomao Landslide and the
potential earth movements located at Area A bounded by Kuahea Place Helo Place and
Kuahea Street appear to be active Some slight movements have been recorded by the
inclinometers installed within Area A Observation of the ground surface in these earth
movement areas indicates that some surface disruption is occurring as evidenced by the
on going pavement deformation The activity observed at Area A appears to be most
pronounced at the southern and eastern portions of the area previously identified as
Area A We will continue to monitor the instruments installed at the site and provide you
with the quarterly updates
