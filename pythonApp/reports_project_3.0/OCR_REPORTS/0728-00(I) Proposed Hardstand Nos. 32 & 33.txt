INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to evaluate the sub
surface conditions and to provide soil data for the design
and construction of the proposed Hardstand and
related structures at Honolulu International Airport Hono
lulu Oahu Hawaii
Based on the boring data and the laboratory test results
engineering analysis was performed to evaluate the settle
ment and drainage characteristics of the underlying soft
clay layer due to site filling and aircraft loading and to
develop recommendations for pavement and structural design
drainage grading and general site development
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
SCOPE
The scope of this study includes field reconnaissance
geologic research review of available soil and geologic
literature and boring data from previous soil investigation
reports drilling 56 new exploratory borings laboratory
and field CBR testing analysis of field and laboratory
data engineering analysis and report preparation
This report is for the Ewa Hardstand areas only
and the scope of this study is performed generally in accor
dance with our proposals dated 1977

SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the western portion of the
airport terminal adjacent to Hardstand No 31 of the Ewa
Gull Wing
The area is bordered by Hardstand No 31 on the east Taxi
way A on the south Taxiway G on the west and northwest
and the Continental and Pan American Airline Cargo buildings
on the northeast
The site is generally level with gradients of about 1 to 5
percent down toward the south
A stockpile of miscellaneous rubble and soil up to 20 feet
high was noted in the north central portion of the project
site the characteristics of this stockpile are not included
in this study
An unlined drainage ditch about 7 feet deep and 30 feet
wide was noted in the southern portion of the site generally
running in an eastwesterly direction An existing 9 by 4 5
feet box culvert drains runoff from the existing pavement
areas into the drainage ditch About 1 to 2 feet of water
was noted in the drainage ditch during the field exploration

A portion of the central portion of the site is covered
with asphaltic concrete and the rest of the area is
covered with grass and weeds
Most of the northern and central portions of the site is
being used as a parking area for the airport ramp vehicles
The ground elevation at the site generally varies from about
7 to 12 feet
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed plan is to construct new hardstand and approach
apron areas adjacent to the existing Hardstand No 31
The construction will consist of both flexible and rigid
pavements designed to support large aircraft such as the
Boeing 747
A by pass taxiway for light aircraft as well as a parking
area for the airport ramp vehicles are also planned
Construction of two aircraft flood light structures and
five fuel spill collectors will also be required at this
Ewa site
The proposed reinforced concrete box culvert about 9 by
4 5 feet is to replace the existing open drainage ditch

In the hardstand area the concrete pavement should reduce
the aircraft wheel loads on the soft areas However due
to the non uniformity of the soft layer and very little
anticipated settlement in the apron area after removal of
the soft clay the concrete pavement could experience uneven
settlement particularly near the apron area Therefore if
some future maintenance is to be avoided or reduced it is
recommended that either the soft clay below the hardstand
area be removed and replaced with compacted granular material
or the area be surcharged with the excavated soil from the
adjacent approach apron
In general dredged coral materials often produce high CBR
test values under static test loading condition However
due to the long term dynamic loading effects of the large
aircraft and the tendency of coral materials to break down
under repeated dynamic loadings it is recommended that a
reduced CBR value be used in the pavement design For this
investigation the design CBR value equal to 50 percent of
the test values obtained with the laboratory and field CBR
tests was used

BUEL SPIEL COLEEGTOR

The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
56 test borings to depths of from 10 to 30 feet at the locations
shown on the Site Plans Plates 1 and 2 The borings were drilled
with 4 inch augers using Model Versa Drill rigs Drilling
operations were performed under the observation of our project soil
engineer or geologist who assisted in obtaining representative un
disturbed samples and bulk samples of the underlying materials
Our engineers maintained field boring logs and the soils were
classified by visual and textural examination in the field
The classifications were verified by additional visual inspec
tion and testing in the laboratory All soils were classified
in accordance with the Federal Aviation Agency FAA System and
the Unified Soil Classification System A graphic presentation
of the soils is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through
A 60 In the borings samples were obtained by driving either a
standard penetration or 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The
blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown
on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Three 3 field California Bearing Ratio CBR tests were carried
out to obtain data which would aid to evaluate the characteristics
of the on site soils for pavement design The results of the field
CBR tests are summarized on Plate A 0

Select samples were tested in our laboratory to aid in the
soil classification and to evaluate the soil engineering
properties Numerous moisture contents and Atterberg Limits
were determined on selected samples to aid in the correlation
of soil units The results of these tests are presented in the
respective boring logs at the appropriate sample depths
Particle size grading analyses were determined for 7 samples
to assist in soil classification and correlation The results
of the analyses are presented on the Summary of Laboratory Test
Results
Two consolidation tests were performed on undisturbed ring
samples to determine the consolidation characteristics of the
underlying soft clay materials The results were used to evaluate
the possible settlement behavior of the pavements and site filling
under the anticipated loads The results of the tests are pre
sented graphically on Plates B 1 and B 2
To obtain the moisture density relationship of the near surface
site soil as a potential source of fill a Modified Proctor Com
paction test was developed by compacting the silty coral sand
sample The results are summarized in the Summary of Laboratory
Test Results
