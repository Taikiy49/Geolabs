The purpose for our engineering evaluation was to obtain the necessary field data to
supplement the UIC permit application for the subject drywell In order to accomplish the
objectives our exploration program generally consisted of the following tasks and work
efforts
1 Research and review of the available information pertaining to the UIC permit
application The information was obtained from the Department of Heaith
Safe Drinking Water Branch located in Honolulu
2 Mobilization and demobilization of equipment and labor to assist in providing

of the well head for signs of damage or unsuitable conditions

FINDINGS DISCUSSION
The subject drywell facility is on the eastern side of Molokai Hema Street located
between Hina Avenue and Moali Street intersections in the town of Kahului on the
Island of Maui Hawaii The drywell evaluation was performed on
under generally fair weather conditions The steel grate covering the concrete catch
basin and drywell was removed for access to the drywell

Physical Charateristics
Our observation of the visible portion of the concrete catch basin and drywell
indicated that the system was in generally good condition with no overt signs of leakage
or deterioration The entire depth of the drywell was lined with precast perforated
concrete ring liners of about 8 feet in diameter Thus the lithology encompassing the
drywell could not be directly observed The drywell was observed in a dry condition with
no static groundwater table present at the time of our site visit
The depth of the weii was measured by sounding with a weighted tape to iocate
the bottom of the well The depth and diameter measurements including a sketch of the
drywell are presented on the attached Summary of Drywell Dimensions and Lithologic
Log Plate 1
Injection Testing
A short term injection test was conducted by our field engineer to evaluate the
disposal capacity of the drywell A 4 000 gallon capacity tanker equipped with a pump was
used to introduce clear fresh water into the drywell The usable volume of the tanker
source water to perform the test was estimated to be about 3 900 gallons A pressure
transducer and electronic data logger were utilized to collect and record the head level
response within the drywell
In general approximately 3 900 gallons of fresh water was pumped into the well
during the injection test The average flow rate was estimated at about 430 gallons per
minute gpm The test was terminated after pumping the usable volume of the tanker
source water into the well The duration of the test was approximately 9 minutes
Based on the injection test results the drywell appeared to be operating
satisfactorily at an average injection rate of about 430 gpm With an average injection rate
of about 430 gpm the head level rose to a maximum height of approximately 3 38 feet
above the static water level just prior to termination of the test The test was terminated
when the water source was depleted Based on the available head space contained in the
drywell the disposal capacity appears to be substantially greater than 430 gpm The
results of the short term injection test are presented on the attached Injection Test Results
Plates 2 and 3
LIMITATIONS
The evaluation presented in this report is based upon visual observations and
short term testing of the injection well Variation from our findings may occur and the
nature of such variations may not become evident until at a later point in time If
variations then appear evident it will become necessary to reevaluate the findings in
this report
