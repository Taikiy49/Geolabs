PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Mehana at Kapolei development site is bordered by a drainage easement and
Roosevelt Avenue to the south Ft Barrette Road to the east and the future James
Campbell Estate residential development to the north and west Mehana at Kapolei
Phase II Lots also known as Nanala at Mehana Parcel 2 is located within the
northeastern quadrant of the Mehana Development It is bordered by a parking strip to
the north and Roads M A and N on the west south and east respectively It
encompasses approximately 6 1 acres as shown on the Alternate Phasing Plan Plate 1
Our monitoring services during construction consisted of moisture and density
testing to determine adequacy of compaction of fills and backfills for utilility installations
including compaction testing for building pads garages walkways and driveways The
Grading Plans are shown on Plates 2 1 and 2 2
This report summarizes our field observations and compaction testing services for
the site grading operations and infrastructure installation for the above described parcel

MASS GRADING
Nanala at Kapolei encompasses the northwestern portion of Mehana at Kapolei
Phase I a previously mass graded portion of the Mehana at Kapolei Development The
mass grading for Phase I consisted entirely of filling the site up to about 12 feet Various
imported off site materials were used to fill up to about 4 feet below finished grade The
upper 4 feet of fill consisted primarily of crushed coral structural fill At the onset of grading
the area was cleared and grubbed prior to filling
The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of
95 percent relative density
SITE GRADING
In conducting our construction monitoring services during the site grading and utility
backfilling operations for Nanala at Mehana Parcel 2 samples of the materials utilized as
fill were obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities
and optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 4
Periodic field density tests were performed in general accordance with
ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method The test results are summarized on Plates 4 1
through 4 18 These tests were performed on prepared surfaces of constructed fills and
backfills Test locations and elevations were estimated by referencing from on site survey
stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the
measured relative compaction conformed to or exceeded the minimum specified for the
project
Our services consisted of full time and part time field observation of the contractor s
Delta Construction Corporation subgrade preparation fill and backfill placement and
compaction procedures and confirmation of subgrade soil conditions exposed during the
above site grading operations
Based on our field representative s observations and the referenced test results it
is our opinion that the utility installation work and site grading for the Nanala at Mehana
Parcel 2 of the Mehana at Kapolei Development Project observed and tested by Geolabs
in the period covered by this report were performed in accordance with the general intent

HOUSE CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
The finished grades adjacent to the structures should be sloped to shed water away
from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised to install
gutter systems around the house structures to collect and discharge water away from the
foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering within 3 feet of the
foundations and slabs should be avoided Gravel strips adjacent to slabs and foundations
should be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
building foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations should
be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the concrete to
reduce water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and
should be maintained to drain surface run off away from foundations Landscaping of the
house lots by the homeowners is recommended to reduce the potential for erosion and for
altering the drainage patterns
