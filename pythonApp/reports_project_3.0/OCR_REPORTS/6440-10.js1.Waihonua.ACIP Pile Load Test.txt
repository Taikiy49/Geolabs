In general the Waihonua at Kewalo project involves construction of a new high rise
condominium The project consists of a six level base parking structure with 37 stories of
residential units above a portion of the base parking structure The footprint of the high rise
portion is about 70 feet by 175 feet in plan dimension The design finish floor elevation is
set at approximately 8 1 feet Mean Sea Level MSL In addition a portion of the garage
will be about half a level below grade with the lowest elevation at around 3 5 feet MSL
In accordance with the project design requirements a static compressive pile
load test was conducted on a dedicated augered cast in place ACIP pile on
October 5 and 6 2012 This letter report presents the results of our engineering
analysis field observations and test data obtained from the static compressive pile load
test The project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
Previously we provided geotechnical engineering recommendations for the ACIP
foundation design and our recommendations were presented in the geotechnical
engineering report entitled Geotechnical Engineering Report Waihonua at Kewalo
Honolulu 017 dated March 12 2012
The pile load test was conducted on an 18 inch diameter ACIP pile with a length of
approximately 70 1 feet relative to the existing ground surface The load test pile was
installed by Hawaiian Dredging Construction Company HDCC using a crane mounted
drill rig equipped with automatic monitoring equipment to provide the drilling depth drilling
rate auger torque stroke count etc on a near continuous basis HDCC used continuous
flight augers to drill the pile As the augers were removed the hole was grouted from the
bottom upwards After sound quality grout based on visual observation appeared at the

To provide support for the load test two 18 inch ACIP reaction piles approximately

piles are also shown on the ACIP Test Pile Location Plan Plate 2
To better understand the load transfer mechanism at the pile soil interface the load
test pile was instrumented with seven vibrating wire embedment strain gages Model
No 4200A 2 manufactured by Geokon The strain gages were connected using a
multiplexer to a Geokon Micro 10 data acquisition system to record the strains during the
compressive load test The embedment strain gages were installed in the load test pile at
the following depths below the existing ground surface

The piling subcontractor HDCC provided load testing equipment including a
hydraulic jack reaction beam reference beams and other accessories Geolabs Inc
provided the load cell and hydraulic pump Based on the load cell calibration data an
equation relating the applied load kips to the load cell reading voltage was generated
and used during the load testing
As requested Geolabs provided the data acquisition system including a
multiplexer a data logger and data acquisition program to collect the strain gage readings
during the load testing The strain gage readings were further used in the analyses and
evaluation of the static pile load test In addition displacements at the top of the load test
pile were measured and recorded by Geolabs
The load test pile was loaded in general accordance with the Quick Load Test
Method ASTM D1143 and modified by our office to suit the subsurface conditions at the
project site In general the test pile was loaded to about 990 kips in approximately

approximately five equal decrements and held for at least 1 minute at each decrement
