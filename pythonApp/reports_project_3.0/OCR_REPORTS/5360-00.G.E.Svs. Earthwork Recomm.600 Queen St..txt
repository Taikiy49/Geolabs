SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site had been previously explored by
Dames Moore in 1991 Our current field exploration program at the project site
consisted of drilling and sampling six additional borings designated as Boring Nos 1
through 6 extending to depths of about 100 to 104 feet below the existing ground
surface The approximate locations of the borings drilled are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
Based on the previous study performed by Dames Moore and the information
from our current field exploration it appears that the project site generally is underlain by
surface fill materials consisting of stiff clayey silts and medium dense silty sands
extending to depths of about 3 to 8 feet below the existing ground surface The surface
fill materials generally were underlain by a layer of dense cemented cinder sands
overlying an upper coral ledge It should be noted that the upper coral ledge encountered
at the project site is highly variable erratic in terms of thickness and quality
Below the upper coral ledge the borings generally encountered coralline detritus
materials consisting of medium dense silty coralline sands and gravel with localized loose
and dense zones extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately 104 feet
below the existing ground surface in our current field exploration In some of the borings
drilled at the project site a 2 to 7 foot thick layer of slightly to completely weathered
voicanic tuff material was encountered at depths ranging between about 67 to 87 feet
below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was encountered in the borings drilled at depths varying from
approximately 5 3 to 6 8 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of our field
exploration The groundwater levels encountered generally correspond to about
Elevations 0 3 to 1 2 feet MSL Due to the proximity of the project site to the Pacific

SITE GRADING
In general we anticipate that grading work consisting of minor fills on the order of
about 1 foot thick will be required to achieve the finished grades for the project In
addition excavations extending to depths of about 6 to 8 feet deep are anticipated for
construction of the mat foundation planned for the project In addition excavations and
subsequent backfills of up to about 12 feet deep likely will be required for construction of
the planned elevator pits Items of site grading that are addressed in the subsequent
subsections include the following

Excavation
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative from Geolabs It
is important that a representative from Geolabs be present to observe the site preparation
operations to evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered during the
excavation and scarification process and whether the exposed soil conditions are similar
to those encountered in the field exploration
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared Vegetation debris deleterious materials existing structures to
be demolished including the asphaltic concrete surface and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site to reduce the
potential for contamination of the excavated materials
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas designated to
receive fill and or future improvements should be over excavated to expose firm
materials and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill
The excavated soft soils should not be re used as a source of fill materials and
should be properly disposed of off site
In general the over excavated subgrades and areas designated to receive fills
should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density as determined by
ASTM Test Designation D1557 Optimum moisture is the water content
percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density

Fills and Backfills
In general the near surface sandy and gravelly soils encountered during our field
exploration should be suitable for use as general fill and backfill materials provided
that the maximum particle size is less than 3 inches in largest dimension The
on site materials generated from the planned excavations may be used as a source
of general fill or backfill materials provided that they are screened of the over sized
materials and or processed to meet the above gradation requirements less than
3 inches in largest dimension
Imported materials to be used as select granular fill should be non expansive
granular materials such as crushed coral or basalt The select granular fill should
be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in largest
dimension The material should have a California Bearing Ratio CBR value of
20 or higher and a swell potential of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1883 The material also should contain between
10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve Imported fill materials
should be tested for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to
the project site for the intended use
Where groundwater is encountered within the excavations backfill materials
should consist of free draining granular materials such as open graded gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation wrapped on all sides with non woven filter fabric
The free draining granular materials should be used up to a level about 12 inches
above the groundwater level to facilitate compaction of the fill materials above the
open graded gravel
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
In general fills and backfills should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Compaction requirements
for the subgrades of improvements subjected to vehicular traffic such as the
ground surface of the parking structure and driveways should be increased to a
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the
in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density
as determined by ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
Compaction of the fill materials should be accomplished by utilizing sheepsfoot
rollers vibratory rollers or other types of acceptable compaction equipment Water
tamping jetting or ponding should not be allowed to densify the fills in lieu of
mechanical compaction effort

Excavation
As mentioned previously we anticipate that excavations down to about 12 feet
below the existing ground surface will be required for construction of the elevator
pits Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our field exploration we
believe that temporary cut slopes of about one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V or
flatter may be used for the upper 12 to 15 feet of materials below the existing
ground surface Where space constraints limit the use of temporary cut slopes
shoring of the temporary excavations will be required
In general the method and equipment to be used for making the excavations
should be determined by the contractor subject to practical limits and safety
considerations Based on the field explorations the surface fill materials and
underlying cinder sands encountered in the borings drilled may be excavated with
conventional earthmoving equipment However excavations into the upper coral
ledge and some localized areas of hard materials may require the use of hoerams
or chipping Therefore the contractor may encounter and should expect some
difficult excavation conditions at localized areas especially in the deeper
excavations
We also recommend that a monitoring program be developed and implemented by
the contractor to detect ground movement and or subsidence adjacent to the
excavations which may result in damage to nearby structures and pavements It
should be noted that minor settlements of the adjacent ground may occur during
and after installation of the shoring system Therefore we recommend that a
qualified geotechnical engineer be retained by the contractor to design and
evaluate the shoring system used
LIMITATIONS
The findings and recommendations submitted in this letter report are based in part
upon information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsurface conditions
between and beyond the boring locations may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then
appear evident Geolabs should be contacted to re evaluate the recommendations
presented in this letter report
