In accordance with our proposal dated August 20 we have performed a geotechnical
engineering exploration and field percolation testing program within the existing Hauula
Elementary School in the Hauula area on the island of Oahu Hawaii This letter report
summarizes the findings from our field exploration and percolation testing at the proposed
leach field sites and presents our discussion and evaluation for the proposed project These
findings and discussions are intended for design input only and are subject to the limitations
noted at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the existing Hauula Elementary School in the Hauula
area on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided we understand that on site sewage treatment and
disposal by septic tanks and leach fields are planned within the school site Therefore it was
desired to conduct field percolation tests at the proposed leach field locations to obtain data
pertaining to the preliminary design of the sewage disposal system The approximate
locations of the proposed septic tanks and leach fields are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Structural details for the proposed septic tanks and leach fields were not available at
the time this report was prepared In general we anticipate that the bottom of the absorption
trenches will be located at depths of approximately 3 to 5 feet below the existing ground
surface Based on our knowledge and past experience in the vicinity of the project site we
anticipate that the project site may be underlain by silty clays overlying coral sand and gravel
formation at greater depths Special attention was given to the depth of the natural

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our field exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the proposed septic tanks and leach field locations and to perform field
percolation tests to evaluate the subsoil infiltration characteristics for the formulation of
preliminary recommendations pertinent to the design of the disposal components of the
on site sewage disposal system The work was performed in general accordance with our
fee proposal dated August 20 1998 Our scope of work included the following tasks and
work efforts
1 Review of available in house soils and geologic information pertinent to the
project site and its vicinity
2 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and operators to
and from the project site
3 Drilling and sampling of two borings to a depth of about 11 5 feet below the
existing ground surface to evaluate the subsurface soil profiles at the proposed
leach field locations
4 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a Geologist
from our firm
5 Drilling of five boreholes to depths ranging from about 1 3 to 2 0 feet below the
existing ground surface for field percolation testing
6 Performance of five field percolation tests to evaluate the subsoil infiltration
characteristics for the formulation of preliminary recommendations pertinent
to the design of the disposal components of the on site sewage disposal
system
7 Laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained during the field exploration
as an aid in classifying the materials encountered and evaluating their
engineering properties
8 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and discussions

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 to a depth of about 11 5 feet below the existing ground
surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration in Boring No 1 encountered relatively permeable
materials consisting of medium dense sandy soils throughout the majority of the depth
explored A relatively thin layer of basalt gravel and fragments were encountered in the
drilled boring from about 1 5 to 4 0 feet below the existing ground surface Groundwater was
encountered in the drilled boring at depths ranging from about 2 2 to 4 3 feet below the
existing ground surface
Our field exploration in Boring No 2 generally encountered highly variable subsoil
conditions consisting of interbedded ijayers of very stiff silty clays and loose sandy soils
throughout the depth explored Groundwater was encountered in the drilled boring at depths
ranging from about 3 0 to 5 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Due to the proximity of the project site to the shoreline the water levels measured in
the borings during our field exploration were likely affected by tidal fluctuations In addition
the water levels at the project site may be influenced by seasonal precipitation and storm
surge conditions Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in our field exploration
are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates 4 and 5
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
We understand that on site sewage treatment and disposal by septic tank and leach
field method are planned within the Hauula Elementary School site Therefore we have
conducted field percolation tests to obtain data pertaining to the preliminary design of the
sewage disposal system A total of five field percolation tests were performed at selected
locations across the project site The approximate locations of the field percolation tests
designated as P 1 through P 5 are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

Field Percolation Testing
Field percolation testing was performed in general accordance with the State of
Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten States Standards to evaluate the permeability of the
subsurface soils for wastewater disposal The percolation tests were performed by drilling
existing ground surface The boreholes were terminated at these shallow depths in order to
provide approximately 12 inches of percolation distance between the bottom of the borehole
and the natural groundwater table
Approximately six inches of gravel were then placed at the bottom of the boreholes
to protect the bottom of the holes from scouring and sediments During testing the holes
were carefully filled with water to a minimum depth of 12 inches above the gravel pack The
time intervals and water drops were then recorded to provide data upon which caiculation
of the percolation tests was based
Test Results and Discussions
Based on the percolation tests conducted the percolation rates range from about
2 minutes per inch at test location P 1 to virtually impermeable at test location P 3 Results
of the individual percolation tests are presented on Plates 6 through 10 of this report Due
to the variability of the subsoil conditions the absorption capacity of the disposal system
should be confirmed by additional percolation tests during construction
According to the Manual of Septic Tank Practice prepared by the U S Department
of Health Education and Welfare proposed leach field locations with percolation rates
exceeding 60 minutes per inch would not be suitable for the siting of the absorption system
Based on the percolation test results obtained in test locations P 2 through P 4 we believe
that the subsoils in these areas are highly impermeable and may not be suitable for the siting
of the sewage disposal system
In addition based on the guidelines presented in the State of Hawaii Department of
absorption trenches should not be closer than 2 feet to the seasonal high groundwater level
Based on the shallow groundwater levels measured in the drilled borings it is our opinion
that the feasibility of using septic tank and leach field method for sewage disposal should be
carefully evaluated for the subject site

LIMITATIONS
The findings and discussions submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings and percolation tests Variations of subsoil
conditions from the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to reevaluate the findings and discussions provided in this report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having been
estimated by taping from the features shown on the Site Plan provided by Fujita
Associates Inc The physical locations of the borings should be considered accurate only
to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition
Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the graphic
representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed and
interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of Fujita Associates Inc their
client the Department of Accounting and General Services and their subconsultants for
specific application to the proposed septic tanks and leach fields project within the Hauula
Elementary School site in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical engineering
principles and practices No warranty is expressed or implied
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the architect and
engineer in the preliminary design evaluation of the proposed project Therefore this report
may not contain sufficient data or the proper information to serve as the basis for
preparation of construction cost estimates A contractor wishing to bid on this project is
urged to retain a competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation of this report
and or in the performance of additional site specific exploration for bid estimating purposes
The Owner Client should be aware that unanticipated soil conditions are commonly
encountered Unforeseen soil conditions such as perched groundwater soft deposits hard
layers or loose fills may occur in localized areas and may require additional probing or
corrections in the field which may result in construction delays to attain a properly
constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to
accommodate these possible extra costs
