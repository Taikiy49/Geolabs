Subsurface Water Monitoring
The monitoring of subsurface water levels groundwater is accomplished by
measuring the level of water head contained within the fifteen individual and nested
piezometers installed in the borings at the project site A list of the piezometer installations
for the project is provided in the preceding Table entitled Summary of Completed
Instrument Installations The fourteen operational piezometers are installed in the borings
identified as Boring Nos P 1 through P 6 and P 8 as shown on Plate 2
Three piezometers identified as P 2A P 4A and P 8 consist of dedicated vibrating
wire piezometer installations that provide a near continuous record of the water level
encountered in the weil casing The remaining 12 piezometers consist of simple standpipe
well casings which are monitored manually on about a bi monthly basis
It should be noted that the standpipe Piezometer P 2B has experienced a blockage
in the pipe at a depth of about 22 feet which has rendered the instrument as
non functional for further seepage monitoring In addition Piezometer P 7 has
experienced a blockage at a depth of about 29 feet rendering the instrument as
non functional for measurement of deeper groundwater seepage conditions

General reconnaissance of the Kuahea Street Area Movement study area was
conducted on to record observations pertaining to the existing site
conditions In general visible active disturbance to fixed structures generally involves the
cracked and undulating pavements and curbing along portions of Kuahea Street and
portions of Waiomao Road
Areas that appear to be experiencing ongoing slow ground surface deformation
include Kuahea Street Area A between Helo and Kuahea Place the Waiomao Landslide
head at Kuahea Street and portions of Waiomao Road within the Waiomao Landslide
footprint These areas show some visible evidence of compounding ground heave and or
subsidence within the paved roadway areas Surface groundwater seepages were not
observed within the study area at the time of our reconnaissance
Kuahea Street Area A between Helo Place and Kuahea Place
The primary area of ground surface deformation within Kuahea Street Area A was
observed to extend between about 2326 Kuahea Street and about

as evidenced by widening crack apertures and some vertical offsets at the joints

Kuahea Street Waiomao Landslide Head
Observation of the street area along Kuahea Street at the head of the

traverse the roadway Vehicular ride across the deformation zone is very rough and

Settlement within the southern half of the paved roadway has produced regions that

slopes on the northern side of Kuahea Street continue to show minimal visible
evidence of earth movement or ground surface deformation
Waiomao Road Central Waiomao Landslide
