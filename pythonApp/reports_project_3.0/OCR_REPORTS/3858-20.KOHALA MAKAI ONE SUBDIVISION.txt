This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed
during the probing and grouting operations for the 0 5 Million Gallon Reservoir for the
Kohala Makai One Subdivision
The project site is located mauka of the Akoni Pule Highway in the Kohala Ranch
Subdivision in the District of North Kohala on the Island of Hawaii The subject site is
situated about 600 feet east from one of the proposed subdivision cul de sacs Based on
the grading plans the topography at the subject site is generally sloping down to the south
at a slope inclination of about six horizontal to one vertical 6H 1V The existing ground
surface elevations at the reservoir project site vary from about 248 to 252 feet Mean Sea
Level MSL
Just prior to the probing and grouting operations the site was graded to provide a
relatively level pad at about the footing subgrade elevation which is designed to be at a
ground elevation of 250 feet MSL We understand that the 4 foot over excavation and
backfilling with structural fill recommended for the tank structure had been completed prior
to the probing and grouting operations The center of the reservoir structure and perimeter
footing were surveyed and marked in the field by Goodfellow Brothers Inc for our

The probing and grouting operations were performed by Isemoto Contracting
Company Ltd on In general probe holes were drilled at each of the
four isolated spread footing locations and at 10 foot lateral intervals on centers for the tank
perimeter foundation A pressurized air percussion hammer on a track drill rig was used
for the probing operations The probe holes were about 3 inches in diameter and
extended to a minimum depth of about 10 feet below the footing subgrade elevations
The probe holes were backfilled with sand cement grout injected at low to moderate
pressures The sand cement grout material was designed for a slump of 772 inches and
was supplied by West Hawaii Concrete A field engineer from Geolabs observed the
probing and grouting operations Cavities or loose zones were not encountered or
suspected based on easy drilling or high grout intake therefore additional probe holes
were not deemed necessary
In summary a total of 27 probe holes with an aggregate length of about 270 lineal
feet were drilled for the reservoir structure foundation Approximately 1 cubic yard of
sand cement grout was utilized to fill the probe holes Based on our field measurements it
appears the probe holes took about twice the amount of grout to fill the holes than the
theoretical volume of the probe hole The approximate locations of the probes are shown
on the Site Plan Plate 1 A summary of our observations during the probing and grouting
operations is provided on Plates 2 and 3 of this report
The probing and grouting operations were performed in general conformance with
the intent of the recommendations presented in our geotechnical engineering exploration
report dated The purpose of the probing and grouting operations was to
reduce the potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities or
compression of loose zones below the tank structure Based on our observations it is our
professional opinion that the probed and grouted foundation area should provide the
recommended foundation support for the reservoir structure
Limitations
Elevations of the probes were estimated from the field measurements relative to
the on site survey stakes The elevations of the probe holes should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Our services were performed
in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice
