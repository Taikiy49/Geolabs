PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the Makakilo area of the Island of Oahu Hawaii as
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The hillside project site is located adjacent to
Makakilo Drive within the Palehua East B Subdivision Phase development The project
site consists of an undeveloped hillside which had previously contained potential rockfall
hazards including large boulders and fractured rock outcrops located upslope from the
housing development and Makakilo Drive
As a result of a hillslope assessment and rockfall hazard evaluation which was
required by the City and County of Honolulu rockfall protection improvements have been
constructed at the project site to reduce the potential for rockfall activity to affect the
downslope development In general the rockfall mitigation project consisted of the
removal of existing surface boulders and rock outcrops from the hillside boulder sweep
and the construction of a rockfall impact barrier system along the upslope private property
boundary
This report addresses our field observations during construction of the rockfall
mitigation improvements and provides a summary of the rockfall barrier anchor proof
testing data The rockfall mitigation contractor at the project site Janod Contractors
Entrepreneur performed the anchor proof testing under our observation

The major work tasks observed by Geolabs Inc included the following activities

Construction of the rockfall barrier column footings
Construction of the wire rope lateral ground anchors

Proof testing of the rockfall barrier lateral ground anchors
Based on our observations the completed rockfall barrier system located at the
project site consists of approximately 748 lineal feet of Type Barrier 30 foot ton capacity
and approximately 320 lineal feet of Type II Barrier 74 foot ton capacity Therefore the
overall length of the completed rockfall barrier system is approximately 1 068 lineal feet
It should be noted that the planned Segment A Type Barrier was reduced by
approximately 66 lineal feet in length because a new retaining wall was constructed along
the private property boundary of Lot No 5897 which is located upslope of the barrier
alignment The retaining wall construction which was completed prior to the start of the
barrier construction effectively reduced the potential hazard of falling rock emanating from
the upslope private property Lot No 5897
The boulder sweep performed by Janod Contractors Entrepreneur consisted of the
demolition and removal of existing boulders and fractured rock outcroppings from the
surface of the hillslope located downslope from the rockfall barrier alignment The
contractor performed multiple passes over several months to remove the rock materials
toward the base of the hillside for later collection and disposal by Royal Contracting Ltd
As mentioned previously our services during construction consisted of part time
field observation of the contractor s work performed at the project site However the
ground anchor proof testing performed by Janod Contractors Entrepreneur was observed
on a full time basis The approximate locations of the 18 wire rope ground anchors
identified as Anchor Nos 1 through 18 constructed at the project site are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2 The results of the anchor proof testing observed by our field
representatives are summarized and presented on Plates 3 through 45 It should be noted
that ground anchors constructed at the project site were required to be load tested
because greater than 25 percent of the initial anchors tested failed to meet the criteria for
acceptable anchor performance The contractor using new adjacent anchor installations
which were also load proof tested replaced the six failed anchors The six replacement
anchors were subsequently tested and found to have acceptable anchor performance
