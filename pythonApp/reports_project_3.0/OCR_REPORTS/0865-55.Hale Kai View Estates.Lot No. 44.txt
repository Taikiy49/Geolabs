As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil conditions
below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study was to eva
luate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation support
for the proposed house The study consisted of three borings drilled
within the proposed building location laboratory soil tests and a
review of the grading and compaction test record on the building
site This report summarizes our observations and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed single family residence will consist of a two story wood
structure Due to the sloping terrain at the site a lower level
garage cutting into the hillside with an upper level living area are
planned

the remainder of the house
EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lot 44 is located in the central portion of the subdivision develop
ment on the southwestern side of Kahena Street The lot is bounded
by Kahena Street on the northeast subdivision lots on the east and
west and the natural valley side slope on the south An existing con
crete drainage ditch is located on the southwestern upper portion of
the lot The lot generally slopes upward from Kahena Street toward
the southwest at about a 22 percent gradient The elevation of the
lot generally varies from about 193 to 222 feet

Prior to the recent project site grading operation the northeastern

drilled borings and site reconnaissance indicate that better less
expansive and gravelly colluvial deposits or highly weathered rock may
be encountered below the surface clay layer at about 2 foot depth or
more

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by 2 to more
than 5 feet of grayish brown clay The laboratory test results on
this material generally indicated that they have high expansion
potential Therefore for Lot 44 the slab on grade and foundation
construction would require special design procedures in order to
reduce the shrink swell effect of the subsoils

Foundation
For this sloping lot post and beam construction is highly desirable
and recommended due to the sloping terrain and expansive soil condi
tions The post and beam construction generally results in lower
foundation costs and the individual footings can be readily adjusted
should the need occur Where utilized concrete slab on grade should
also be properly designed for expansive soil conditions
Due to the expansive soil and sloping terrain the individual post
footing should extend a minimum depth of 36 inches below the ground
surf ace
In the rear portion of the house the footing embedment may be reduced
if better gravelly colluvial soil or weathered rock is encountered
All footing should have a minimum embedment of 12 inches below the
finished grade
The footings should be poured in place and the excavation backfilled
and compacted to provide an impervious layer over the footing
GEOLABS HAWAII
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the stiff natural ground or compacted fill The bottom of
the footing excavation should be neat and free of loose soils or
debris
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should be extended
to the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due
to settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations This is
especially important in the rear portion of the house to reduce
ponding of surface run off water and possible saturation and
settlement or heaving of the foundation soils

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage area It should be
noted that the on site clay soils are expansive and unsuitable as slab
subgrade soils In order to reduce the expansive effects of the clay
the expansive clays should be overexcavated down for a depth of 3 feet
below the slab subgrade and then replaced with non expansive borrow
soils such as select borrow crusher waste or coral compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab and
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to protect
against moisture infiltration a polyethylene moisture barrier may be
incorporated below the slab The membrane should be protected by a
thin layer of sand to aid in the concrete curing
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
A layer of more than 5 feet of expansive soils was encountered in the
front portion of the lot Since the driveway can tolerate some amount
of heaving the clay overexcavation and replacement can be reduced to
2 feet below the driveway subgrade level Where 2 or more feet of
fill is planned select non expansive borrow material should be used
to cap over the expansive clay Where less than 2 feet of fill is
planned overexcavation and replacement of the expansive clay with
select non expansive material is required The above capping
requirements should be followed for a minimum distance of 3 feet
beyond the driveway

For the relatively long driveway planned joints are recommended at
about 8 to 10 foot intervals in order to allow for some relative move
ment of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or overexcavation and
replacement similar to the driveway are recommended to reduce heaving
caused by the expansive clay soil

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during earthwork
construction particularly the fill placement slope cuts and to
monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the finding of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
