Dear Mr. Kaneko: 
At your request, Geolabs, Inc. provided geotechnical engineering observation and 
consultation services during the drilled shaft foundation installation for the new monopole 
at the above project. This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering 
observations during the installation of the drilled shaft foundations for the SPSP Maritime 
Wireless Network System project located in the Kawaihae area on the Island of Hawaii. 
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering observation during construction was 
to provide engineering consultation and assist in problem resolutions during the 
construction of the drilled shaft foundation. 
Project Considerations 
The project consists of constructing new communication monopoles at various 
locations throughout the State of Hawaii. As part of the project, a new monopole of about 
80 feet in height will be constructed at Kawaihae Harbor. 
Based on the project plans, the new monopole was designed to be supported on a 
deep foundation system consisting of a 72-inch diameter cast-in-place concrete drilled 
shaft.  The project plans required that the drilled shaft extend down at least 40 feet below 
the existing ground surface.

The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the 
drilled shaft foundation construction for the monopole mentioned above only. The 
general contractor for the project is Elite Pacific Construction, Inc., and the drilled shaft 
installation subcontractor is Healy Tibbits Builders, Inc. 
Summary of Drilled Shaft Installation 
Installation of test shafts and load testing were not required by the contract 
documents for this project.  The drilled shaft contractor installed the drilled shaft from 
May 29, 2013 through May 31, 2013. The as-built diameters and the lengths of the 
production drilled shafts installed for the project are summarized on Plate 1.1 of this letter 
report. Detailed installation logs for the production shafts are provided on Plates 1.2 
through 1.4. Representatives from Geolabs observed the drilled shaft excavation, 
placement of reinforcing cage, and concrete operations during the installation of the drilled 
shaft. 
In general, the drilled shaft was excavated using a Casagrande drill rig equipped 
with 72-inch diameter augers and 60-inch diameter clean-out bucket. No drilling slurry was 
utilized for the production shafts. However, 72 and 78-inch diameter temporary casings 
were used during excavation of the drilled shaft to avoid the potential cave-in condition of 
the lagoonal deposits near the ground surface and also to provide a safer working 
environment. The drilled shaft was generally extended to about 40.5 feet below the 
existing ground surface. 
After excavation of the shaft to its designated tip elevation, the reinforcing cage was 
lowered into the drilled hole to the appropriate elevation using a crane. After the reinforcing 
cage was installed in the drilled hole, concrete was placed and pumped through a 4-inch 
I.D. steel tremie pipe starting from near the bottom of the excavation. During concrete 
placement, we observed that at least 5 feet of concrete head was maintained above the 
bottom of the tremie pipe. 
The concrete for the shaft was supplied by West Hawaii Concrete. A special design 
concrete mix was utilized for the production shafts, which maintains a high slump 
(7 to 9 inches) throughout the concrete pour. Log of the drilled shaft installed and the 
materials encountered in the drilled shaft during shaft excavation are presented on the 
attached Drilled Shaft Record. 
Crosshole sonic logging (CSL) testing was conducted on the completed drilled shaft 
to evaluate the integrity of the drilled shaft.  The details of the CSL testing and test results 
are provided on Plates 2 through 4.8. 
Based on the results of the CSL tests conducted, first arrival times (FATs) 
increased greater than 25% between about 11.2 to 13.7 feet below the ground surface. 
The other portions of the drilled shaft generally had increased first arrival times (FATs) that 
were less than 10%, and the wave energy was relatively uniform in the profiles for the 
shaft tested.

In general, increases in FATs of 10% or less may be considered insignificant. 
Increases in FATs between 10% and 25% usually are considered minor, but verified 
defects have been discovered in some cases, while increases in FATs greater than 25% 
usually indicate anomalies worthy of consideration and further evaluation. 
Due to the apparent presence of anomalies at about 11.2 to 13.7 feet below the 
ground surface based on the CSL test results, we recommended coring of the drilled shaft 
to further evaluate its condition.  The core holes were drilled using the triple tube method 
and extended down to about 20 feet below the ground surface.  The core samples 
recovered were generally intact and did not show signs of soil intrusion or compromised 
concrete. After coring, the holes were pressure grouted with non-shrink grout.  A 
representative from Geolabs was present to monitor the coring and pressure grouting 
operations. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives, it is our professional opinion 
that the drilled shaft for the new monopole at Kawaihae Harbor for the SPSP Maritime 
Wireless Network System project was installed in general accordance with the intent of the 
project plans and specifications and the geotechnical recommendations presented in the 
above-referenced geotechnical engineering report. 
Limitations 
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities 
occurring simultaneously on a construction site. Our observation and consultation services 
were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical 
engineering practice; no warranty is expressed or implied. Provision of construction 
monitoring by an engineer is not insurance, nor does it guarantee construction of any type. 
The contractor shall retain primary responsibility for the quality of the work, for adhering to 
plans and specifications, and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found.
