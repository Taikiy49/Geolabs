SITE CONDITIONS AND PROPOSED DEVELOPMENT
The site is located mauka of Puako Beach Road about 500 feet
west of Hoku Loa Church The site is nearly level but somewhat
hummocky due to ridges of exposed pahoehoe basalt The low
areas have accumulated mud deposits generally less than 2 feet
deep Two small dwellings are located near the southwest
portion of the property A few palms several milo trees and
a number of keawe trees are scattered over the site Some
trash has accumulated near the houses The houses are serviced
by low pressure water from the County Water District A cement
covered cesspool is located at the northwest corner of the
southwest dwelling There was no cesspool evident near the
other dwelling
The existing road embankment is about 3 feet above the low areas

FIELD EXPLORATION
Five 5 exploratory borings were drilled to depths of about
10 feet to 20 feet with a Mobile B 52 rotary auger drilling
rig adapted for rock coring Standard Penetration split spoon
samples were obtained in Boring No 2 Rock coring started
at the surface for the other 4 holes Boring No 1 was not
drilled since it falls within one of the existing houses
The soil material encountered in the borings does not neces
sarily represent subsurface soil conditions at other points on
the site however the soils and rocks encountered are considered
to be representative of the subsurface conditions across the
site The soil and rock materials obtained in the borings were
classified visually in the field and taken to the Laboratory for
a more detailed analyses

DISCUSSION
The entire area is underlain by pahoehoe basalt lava which out
crops as low ridges interspaced with thin soil cover The soil
is generally only a few inches thick with a maximum depth
of 2 5 feet in Boring No 2 The upper one foot of the
basalt represents the top of the original lava flow which
generally is frothy at the time the hot magma cools so the
rock is soft and porous Below this layer the rock is hard
and dense with a few small pukas
Foundations for the planned 3 to 4 story building may be conven
tional spread footings and should be founded in the moderately
hard to hard basalt All soils and soft rock should be removed
from the building site and stock piled for landscaping purposes

RECOMMENDATIONS
Foundations
1 The bottom of the foundations should be placed in the basalt
a minimum depth of one foot below the rock surface An allowable
bearing of may be utilized for the basalt
2 All footing excavations should be inspected by a soils
engineer prior to placing concrete and steel to assure that the _
footings are supported in hard basalt
3 All footing backfill and utility backfill should be properly
compacted
Grading
_ Prior to the placement of any fill all grass weeds and loose
soil should be removed All organic matter brush trash etc
should be removed and wasted Fill should then be placed
under the supervision of a soils engineer to a minimum relative
compaction of 90 percent and in accordance with the enclosed
