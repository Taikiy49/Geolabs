This letter report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Distribution Pipeline Phase 6 Upcountry Maui Watershed project
located in the Upcountry Maui area on the Island of Maui Hawaii The general location
and project vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This letter report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering

analyses These recommendations are intended to assist in the design of the concrete
reaction blocks for the new waterline The findings and recommendations presented
herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project is in the Upcountry Maui Watershed area on the Island of Maui Hawaii
We understand that approximately 3 100 linear feet of waterline consisting of 8 inch
diameter ductile iron will be installed The waterline alignment starts at about 1 100 feet
northeast of Waipoli Road and then travels along Waipoli Road towards Kekaulike Avenue
as shown on the General Site Plan Plate 2
The waterline invert is about 5 to 6 feet below the existing ground surface In
addition the installation of horizontal and vertical concrete reaction blocks will be required
We understand that it is desired to obtain the allowable bearing pressures for the reaction
blocks

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Maui is comprised of two major volcanoes the older West Maui
Tertiary Epoch and the more recent East Maui also known as Haleakala Pleistocene
Epoch The Isthmus of Maui is a narrow gently sloping plain located between these
two volcanoes
The older West Maui volcano is generally comprised of three lava flows The lower
unit was formed during the Pliocene to early Pleistocene Epoch and is identified as the
Wailuku Volcanic Series The lavas of the Wailuku Volcanic Series generally consist of
thin bedded a a and pahoehoe lavas Overlying this unit are lavas of the Honolua Volcanic
Series late Pleistocene Epoch that consist of alkalic lavas and stiff trachyte Volcanic
inactivity occurred during the Middle Pleistocene Epoch where weathering of the lavas

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located in the Upcountry Maui Watershed area on the Island of
Maui Hawaii The new waterline alignment travels off of and along Waipoli Road for a
distance of about 3 100 linear feet
The existing Waipoli Road is an asphalt concrete paved roadway with one traffic
lane in each direction Low brush was observed along the sides of Waipoli Road Heavier
brush and trees were noted along the proposed waterline alignment off of Waipoli Road
In general the project site slopes down toward the west at an average slope
inclination of about five horizontal to one vertical 5H 1V The existing ground elevations
range from about 3 889 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at the eastern end to about
3 602 feet MSL near the western end of the project site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of excavating and sampling five test pits
designated as Test Pit Nos 1 through 5 extending to depths of about 2 to 6 feet below the
existing ground surface The approximate test pit locations are shown on the Site Plan
Plates 3 1 and 3 2 The test pits were excavated using hoptoe equipment
In general our field exploration disclosed that the project site is underlain by
volcanic ash extending down to the maximum depth explored of 6 feet below the existing
ground surface The volcanic ash generally consisted of stiff to very stiff silt with sand
gravel cobbles and boulders In one of the test pits basalt rock formation was
encountered at about 2 feet depth Groundwater was not encountered in the test pits at

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the project site generally is underlain by stiff to very
stiff volcanic ash extending down to the maximum depth explored 6 feet below the existing
ground surface In one of the drilled borings basalt rock formation was encountered at
about 2 feet depth Groundwater was not encountered in the test pits at the time of our
field exploration
Thrust blocks are anticipated at the vertical and horizontal bends along the pipe
alignment We anticipate that the thrust blocks will bear against stiff to very stiff silt with
sand gravel cobbles and boulders and basalt rock formation Detailed geotechnical
engineering recommendations regarding the thrust blocks are presented below
Thrust Blocks
We envision that thrust blocks will be required at vertical and horizontal bends
along the alignment to provide resistance to the thrust forces due to the bends in the
pressurized pipes The thrust force acting on the pipes may be resisted by passive earth
pressure acting against the face of the thrust block
We understand that the new water line invert is located about 5 to 6 feet below the
existing ground surface Based on our field exploration we anticipate that the thrust blocks
will bear against the stiff to very stiff volcanic ash deposit and basalt rock formation A
lateral bearing pressure of 1 500 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the design
of thrust blocks bearing on the stiff to very stiff volcanic ash soils We anticipate that the
volcanic ash deposit will be encountered from about Station to 25 00
From Station to 31 00 we anticipate that a basalt rock formation will
be relatively close to the existing ground surface A lateral bearing pressure of
4 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for thrust block design in this area The
thrust blocks should be poured directly in contact with the rock formation
Based on the proposed excavation depth and our field exploration we anticipate
that excavation into the cobbles and boulders and basalt rock formation will be required
We anticipate that the use of a hoeram may be required for excavations within the
boulders and rock formation
The above discussions regarding the excavation of the surface materials are based
on field data obtained from the test pits excavated at the project site We recommend that
contractors bidding on this project be encouraged to examine the site conditions and soil
data to make their own interpretation

appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from the test pits Variations of conditions between and beyond
the test pits may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report
The test pit locations were determined by taping from reference points indicated on
the Plan and Profile drawings received from Park Engineering on
The test pit locations should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the methods used
The stratification breaks shown on graphic representations of the test pits depict the
approximate boundaries between soil rock types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the test pits were measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or presented in the text of this report These data have been
reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be
noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in seasonal rainfall and other factors

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by excavating and
sampling five test pits designated as Test Pit Nos 1 through 5 extending to depths of
about 2 to 6 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate locations shown on
the Site Plan Plates 3 1 and 3 2 The test pits were excavated using a hoptoe equipment
The materials encountered in the test pits were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the test pit operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing
in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Test Pits Plates A 1 through A 5
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on the soils exposed on the sides of
the excavation The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined
compressive strength of the soil Pocket penetrometer test results are presented on the
Logs of Test Pits at the appropriate sample depths
Torvane shear tests were performed on the soils exposed on the sides of the
excavation The torvane shear test provides an indication of the shear strength of the soil
Torvane shear test results are provided on the Logs of Test Pits at the appropriate sample
depths
