PROJECT CONSIDERATION
It is our understanding that the existing service station is to be
demolished and replaced by a new service station The latter will
include the construction of a single story station building and possibly
5 pump islands New concrete pad measured at about 40 feet by 90 feet
will be constructed at the service area New AC pavement will be
constructed on the east and southeast parts of the service area Three
3 new gas storage tanks 8 feet in diameter will be installed
underground with minimum cover of 3 feet at the southern part of the
site
Site grading will include extending and improving the existing loose
overcast soil slope at the southern part of the site where the new gas
tanks will be buried below grade Part of the existing open drainage
ditch located at the bottom of the new slope will have to be backfilled
A new pipe culvert will be constructed to maintain the drainage flow At
the southwestern end of the new culvert a new headwall and wingwall
structure will be required

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the investigation was to explore the subsurface
condition at the site to develop soil parameters for site grading and
design of pavement and foundations for the proposed structure
Particular attention is also paid to delineate the possible loose old
fill over the southern portion of the site
The scope of our investigation included drilling and sampling six 6
borings three 3 hand auger probings laboratory testing of samples
obtained engineering evaluation of the field and laboratory data
development of design recommendations and report preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration log of borings and
laboratory test results are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located at the western corner of Rice Street and Waa Road
in Lihue on the Island of Kauai Hawaii At present it is being used as
a service station which consists of a main station building at the
center a storage shed to its east two pump islands in front an AC
paved parking area to its west and a yard at the back
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 4
Most of the area is relatively flat except for the southern corner
which slopes down at a gradient of 50 to 100 per cent The middle to
lower portion of the slope is densely covered with vegetation Along the
toe of the slope is an open drainage ditch which appeared to be dry
during our exploration
A water tunnel easement passes underneath the AC paved parking area
along the western boundary The alignment indicates that the tunnel is
close to the future excavation area for the fuel tank
SUBSURFACE CONDITION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and
sampling six 6 borings to a depth of 6 5 to 26 5 feet Three probe
holes were drilled on the slope by the use of a hand auger to determine
the depth of loose fill material The boring and hand auger probe
locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the level portion of the site is underlain by relatively
uniform materials consisting of 2 to 4 feet of fill overlying the in situ
residual soil At the southeastern part of the site where the fill
slope is located loose fill material was encountered with depth varying
from more than 8 feet at the top edge of the slope to about one foot at
the toe The approximate boundary of the loose overcast fill material is
shown on Plate 2
GEOLABS HAWAII

Both the fill and the underlying residual soil consist of reddish
brown clayey to sandy silt with generally a stiff consistency Close
to the edge of the slope broken glasses and metals were noted in the
fill material
No groundwater was encountered during our boring exploration
A generalized soil profile Section A A across the slope at the
southeastern part of the site is shown on Plate 3
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATION
In general our exploration discloses competent subsoil condition for
the construction of the proposed gas station However the large
quantity of loose fill material found at the southern part of the site
should be removed prior to construction of the new embankment 111
slope Detailed discussion and recommendation on site grading
foundation and pavement design are presented as follows
Site Grading
Most grading work will be concentrated at the southern part of the
site where the existing slope is located
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1477 00 Page 6
Site Preparation
The site to be graded should be cleared of all vegetation soft and
other unsuitable materials All the organic materials anticipated to
be found along the drainage ditch shovld be removed and disposed
off site
After the clearing operation the loose overcast material can be
excavated Based on the soil profile presented on Plate 3 and
assuming that the section continues for 45 feet a rough estimate of
the total volume of this loose material to be removed is about 250
cubic yards
Fill Material
We believe that the on site material free of any expansive or
organic components may be reused as a source of fill material We
believe most of loose on site fill material has a higher
susceptibility to erosion and therefore should be placed at the
inner part of the new embankment fill covered by at least 2 to 3
feet of the better imported granular material required for the
remaining fill slope We recommend the imported fill material
consist of non expansive soils such as quarry waste or other
granular materials less than 3 inches in size with fines less than
20 passing through the 200 sieve
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1477 00 Page 7
Fill Placement
Fills placed on the existing slope should be keyed and benched into
the firm ground to properly bond the new fill to the Slope For
grading details see Plate 3 Typical bench width and height of 6
and 4 feet respectively should be followed whenever appropriate
Also a minimum key of 2 feet should be used at the toe of the slope
The filling operation should start at the lowest point and continue
up in level compacted layers Horizontal loose lift thickness of
less than 10 inches should be used Campacted soil should achieve at
least 90 of the maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
test method
Slope Stability
Provided that the slope is filled with materials as recommended in
the Fill Material section and that proper keying and benching are
carried out it is our opinion that sufficient factor of safety can be
achieved against deep seated slope failure

New Culvert Installation
The new pipe culvert can be installed after the embankment is filled
up to at least 2 feet above the crown of the culvert structure A
six inch cushion layer of 3B Fine gravel is recommended below the pipe
culvert to provide uniform bedding support For backfilling around and
up to 1 foot above the pipe culvert granular material such as 3B Fine
gravel or sand should be utilized
Headwall and Wingwall Structure
A headwall and wingwall structure is required at the outlet end of
the culvert buried under the fill slope Due to the inaccessibility to
this area no boring was carried out However should soft organic soil
be encountered below the wall footing we recommend that it be removed
and replaced with compacted granular fill
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f is recommended for the
walls resting on granular backfill compacted to 95 of its maximum ary
density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 test method In addition a
passive soil resistance of 250 p c f and a friction factor of 0 4
between concrete and the compacted fill can be used The passive
resistance should be reduced by one third when both friction and passive
resistances are considered

reduce the compactive effort required for the backfill and reduce the
build up of additional lateral pressures on the walls

Foundation Design
From the interpretation based on the results of our soil exploration
the fuel tanks can be installed in such a way that they all bear on
the native residual soil with cover varying between 3 and 6 feet

Provided that site grading is properly carried out an allowable
bearing capacity of 2500 p s f can be used Settlement is
anticipated to be minimal
Our laboratory tests indicated that the soil surrounding the tank
excavation with undrained shear strength higher than 750 p s f was
classified as stable according to the standard of Chevron USA Inc
Therefore a 2 foot minimum clearance between the tank and the edge
of excavation can be applied However because of the uncertainty
about the alignment of the existing nearby water tunnel extra
clearance at the western side of the tank excavation is recommended
We also recommend that the tanks be set back a minimum distance of 15
feet away from the surface of the new fill slope During excavation
temporary slope ratio steeper than H V is not recommended
The specification for underground tank and piping installation
entitled 02 Excavation and Backfill Supplement to Construction
Specification M O S 243 prepared by Chevron USA Inc had been
reviewed In general it also reflects our recommendations on
similar items and therefore should be constantly referred to during
construction
Because there is a short supply of pea gravel in Kauai we recommend
the use of 3B Fine gravel be allowed as backfill material and for
use as the 12 inch cushion layer below the gas tank

Single Story Station Building
The building site is underlain by stiff sandy to clayey silt
material We believe that spread or continuous footing foundation
bearing on this in situ material may be used to support the building
An allowable bearing value of up to 3500 p s f may be used for the
stiff silty residual material The footings should be embedded a
minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade The foundation
soil should be kept moist and free of loose soil prior to concreting
of the footings
Minimal settlements are anticipated for the proposed building loads
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive
earth pressure developed against the buried vertical faces of the
foundation system and by friction resistance developed between the
bottom of the foundations and the bearing soil
Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p c f This assumes that the soil
around the foundation is undisturbed or the footing backfill is well
compacted The frictional resistance may be calculated by using a
coefficient of friction of 0 4 The above resistance values are
maximum values and do not contain a factor of safety

Pavement Design
A new rigid cement concrete pavement is planned for areas around the
new fuel service facility Also new AC pavement is planned for the east
and southeastern parts of the site For design purpose we have selected
a medium light traffic load which consists of 6 to 20 daily repetitions
of 18 kip equivalent axle load
Based on our borings and laboratory CBR tests the existing upper
fill will generally provide a good subgrade support However it is
recommended that the existing fill be compacted by roller to achieve a
minimum of 95 relative compaction per the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum
density

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundation construction
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading excavations for the spread
and wall footings subgrade preparations and cushion layer constructions
for slabs and pavements be observed by the soils engineer during
construction to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
