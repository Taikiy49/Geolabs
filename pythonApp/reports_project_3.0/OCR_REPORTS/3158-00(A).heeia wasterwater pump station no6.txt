SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed force main will extend west from the existing Heeia Wastewater
Pump Station No 6 along Yacht Club Drive to Nahiku Drive south on Nahiku Drive to
Lilijpuna Road and west on Lilipuna Road to Kamehameha Highway as shown on Plate 2
Heeia Wastewater Pump Station No 6 is located on the north side of Yacht Club Drive
at the intersection with Yacht Club Place The pump station adjoins Kaneohe Bay to the
north

The proposed force main will be approximately 1 850 feet long Site elevations
range from about 7 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at the pump station to about 91 feet
MSL at the intersection of Lilipuna Road and Kamehameha Highway
It is anticipated that groundwater will be encountered during excavation for the new
force main in the vicinity of the pump station Based on current design concepts
dewatering of the excavation will likely be needed to facilitate force main construction
Therefore one of the primary environmental concerns is the quality of the water that will
be encountered and whether discharge of the water to storm drainage systems will be
appropriate
The proposed force main will be located in an area of existing residential
development except for about 400 lineal feet of the alignment on Lilipuna Road near
Kamehameha Highway Kaneohe Bay Shopping Center is located to the southeast of the
Lilipuna Road segment approaching Kamehameha Highway Windward Mall is located to
the southwest and residential development exists to the north and northwest
Geologic Setting Geologic maps of the area Stearns 1966 indicate that the
study area is likely to be underlain by alluvium Alluvium is soil that has been deposited
by flowing water Most of the study area is mapped as older alluvium except for the area
near the pump station which is mapped as young alluvium Older alluvium is usually
more consolidated than the younger alluvium Alluvium in the study area has been
deposited in the Heeia Stream drainage basin The content of the alluvium can be highly
variable although alluvial deposits in sloping terrain such as the western portion of the
study area typically include coarser grained materials such as sands and gravels

Alluvium in flatter low lying terrain such as the eastern portion of the study area typically
includes finer grained sands silts and clays
A geotechnical engineering exploration along the subject sanitary force main
alignment was in progress at the time this report was prepared A series of ten borings
were drilled along the proposed alignment to depths ranging from about 10 to 21 feet
below the existing ground surface Predominantly clayey silt with occasional gravelly
layers were encountered to the maximum depths explored
Hydrogeologic Setting The alluvial soils in the low lying area near the pump
station are probably saturated to about near sea level Groundwater flow in this setting
usually occurs in response to tidal fluctuations Uphill of the pump station groundwater
in the alluvium is probably limited to localized zones of perched water within
discontinuous permeable beds of alluvium Groundwater flow in this setting is generally
in the downslope direction toward the northeast
Geotechnical borings near the pump station encountered groundwater at a depth
of about 6 to 7 feet below the existing ground surface corresponding to about 3 feet
MSL Groundwater was encountered at depths of about 14 to 17 feet below the existing
ground surface corresponding to approximately 65 to 73 feet MSL along Lilipuna
Road between Kamehameha Highway and Nahiku Street Along the central portion of
the alignment groundwater was not encountered in some of the borings which were
drilled to depths of about 21 feet
Indications of soil or groundwater contamination were not noted during the
geotechnical exploration Unusual soil discolorations odors or oily sheens were also not

RECOMMENDATION
It is our opinion that a Phase 2 environmental site assessment does not appear to
be warranted at this time Since indications of soil or groundwater contamination were
not detected by visual and olfactory screening of samples from the ten geotechnical
engineering exploration borings drilled along the force main alignment additional
environmental evaluation does not appear to be needed
