This report presents the results of our boring explorations and
percolation tests performed at Heeia Kai Boat Harbor Honolulu Oahu
Hawaii in order to provide subsurface data for use in the design of the
proposed sewage leaching field The general location of the project site
and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Our work was performed in general accordance with our proposal of
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
For the design and feasibility of the proposed sewage leaching field
the subsurface soil conditions at the project site were explored by
drilling two 2 borings at the site
A percolation test was performed in each of the drilled borings to
evaluate the permeability characteristics of the underlying sandy gravelly
formation The approximate location of the borings are shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2

SITE DESCRIPIION
The project site is located at the Heeia Kai Boat Harbor in Heeia
Oahu Hawaii
The proposed leaching field site is located within the Boat Harbor s
parking area adjacent to Kamehameha Highway and an existing comfort
station
SUBSURFACE SOTL CONDITIONS
To examine the area for a leaching field two 2 borings were
drilled at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The
borings generally encountered a 3 inch thick asphaltic concrete surface
layer underlain by medium dense to loose sands and gravels to about 6 5
feet below the existing ground surface the maximum depth drilled
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings from about 2 7 to
3 3 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of our field
exploration
Detailed description of the materials encountered in the borings are
shown on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
PERCOLATION TESTS
After completion of drilling water was discharged into the hole
through a hose then a percolation test was performed in the hole The
falling head percolation test procedure was used for the tests The test
data are presented on Plates B 1 and B 2

Table 11 and the curves in Figure 19 do not allow for wastes from
garbage grinders and automatic washing machines Discharge from
these appliances to an institutional septic tank system calls for extra
capacity of 20 and 40 percent respectively over the calculated absorp
tion area values If both of these appliances are installed the absorption
area should be increased by about 60 percent over the calculated value
Part I allowed for these types of waste in giving specifications for small
household systems but they may never occur in institutional effluents
such as those from factories and offices Other institutions such as a
country club or drive in theatre with eating facilities may contribute
wastes from garbage prinders but not from automatic washing ma
chines As previously emphasized all institutions should have their
systems designed by an engineer who is competent to place proper
evaluations on the kind of wastes to be contributed
