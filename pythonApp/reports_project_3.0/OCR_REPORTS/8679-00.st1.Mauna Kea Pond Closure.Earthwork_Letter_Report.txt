PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
We understand that it is proposed to drain and backfill the existing irrigation pond 
within Mauna Kea Resort in the District of South Kohala on the Island of Hawaii. The 
existing irrigation pond is located along Lanikeha Place.  Based on the information 
provided, materials from the surrounding areas will be used to backfill the irrigation pond.  
The finish grades will generally slope down from about +240 feet Mean Sea Level (MSL) 
at the south portion of the site to about +230 feet MSL at the northern portion of the site.  
Based on the preliminary plans provided, mass grading consisting of cuts of up to about 8 
feet in depth and fills of up to about 4 feet in height may be required to achieve design 
grades.

consisted of stiff sandy silts at the ground surface extending down to about 1 foot below

Site Preparation 
At the on-set of earthwork, areas within the contract grading limits should be 
thoroughly cleared. Vegetation, debris, deleterious materials, and other unsuitable 
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off-site to reduce the potential for 
contamination of the excavated materials.  Silty deposits accumulated at the bottom of 
the existing pond should be removed.  The pond liner should be punctured to provide 
positive drainage or be removed prior to placement of fills.  Where pond liners are left in 
place, a minimum 3” diameter holes should be provided every 5 feet on center to provide 
positive drainage.

Areas to receive fills and finished subgrades in cut areas should be proof-rolled 
with a minimum 20-ton (static weight) vibratory drum roller for a minimum of eight passes. 
The vibratory drum roller also should be operated at a speed of about 300 feet per minute.

Fills and Backfills 
In general, the fill materials required for the project should consist of structural fill 
materials. Structural fill materials should consist of well-graded granular materials 
generally less than 6 inches in maximum dimension with sufficient fines to prevent the 
occurrence of voids in the compacted mass. In addition, structural fill materials should 
contain less than 30 percent particles passing the No. 200 sieve by weight. In general, 
the excavated on-site materials may be reused as a source of structural fill materials, 
provided that they are processed to meet the maximum particle size and gradation 
requirements presented above.  Excavated silty soils should be blended with granular 
materials to produce a well-graded structural fill.  
Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements 
Structural fill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches in 
loose thickness, moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture content, and 
compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction. Relative compaction refers to the 
in-place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the 
same soil established in accordance with ASTM D1557. Optimum moisture is the water 
content (percentage by weight) corresponding to the maximum dry density. 
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practical in fills composed of 
rocks, boulders, and/or cobbles. Instead, a testing program should be conducted at the 
start of the grading phase of the project under the observation of a Geolabs representative 
to evaluate the number of passes needed to achieve the desired level of compaction. 
Based on this testing program, the number of passes may then be used as the field 
criterion for adequate compaction. 
For preliminary estimating purposes, compaction may be accomplished by using 
a large vibratory drum roller having a static weight of at least 20 tons and traveling at a 
speed of about 300 feet per minute (about 3½ miles per hour). The roller should pass 
over the fill surface at least eight times for structural fill.

LIMITATIONS 
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based, in part, upon 
information obtained from the excavated test pits. Variations of the subsurface conditions 
between and beyond the field data points may occur, and the nature and extent of these 
variations may not become evident until construction is underway. If variations then appear 
evident, it will be necessary to re-evaluate the recommendations provided herein. 
The test pit locations indicated in this report are approximate, having been estimated 
by taping from visible features shown on the Grading Plan transmitted by Bowers + Kubota 
on June 28, 2023. Elevations of the test pits were interpolated based on the contour lines 
shown on the same plan. The physical locations and elevations of the test pits should be 
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. 
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the test pits depict 
the approximate boundaries between the soil and/or rock types and, as such, may denote 
a gradual transition. Water level data from the test pits were measured at the times shown 
on the graphic representations and/or presented in the text herein. These data have been 
reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report. We did not encounter 
groundwater in our test pits at the time of our field exploration. However, it must be noted 
that fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall, temperature, and other factors.
