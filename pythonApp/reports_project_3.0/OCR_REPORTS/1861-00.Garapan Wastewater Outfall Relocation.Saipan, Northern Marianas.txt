INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a preliminary engineering field
reconnaissance performed for the subject project
The existing Garapan wastewater outfall is located in the vicinity of
the commercial port area of Tanapag Harbor on the west coast of the island
of Saipan The existing 850 foot long 12 inch diameter outfall passes
between Charlie and Baker Docks and terminates in a shallow depression in
the harbor bottom approximately 50 feet below MIIW The existing outfall
was broken by a ship s anchor in 1976 and presently discharges
approximately 100 feet from its original terminus The existing outfall
pipe is reportedly buried in mud and silt for virtually its entire length
Proposed improvements to the commercial port including construction
of a new dock on top of the existing outfall alignment requires
relocation and construction of a new outfall

SITE DESCRIPTION
The site is located in the southeastern shore of the Tanapag Lagoon
on the western coastal plain of Saipan The proposed new outfall
alignment is approximately 1100 feet southwest of the existing outfall
The on land portion of the proposed alignment is bounded by a refuse dump
on the southwest and a fenced area on the northeastern side From the
existing beach road the proposed outfall extends generally northwest
towards the ocean
Between the existing beach road and the shoreline the topography
slopes gently towards the lagoon The ground surface elevations range
from about 9 2 feet near the beach road to about 4 5 feet at the high
water mark
Off shore the topography drops fairly rapidly from 4 5 feet at the
shore to about 40 feet at a distance of about 400 feet out into the
lagoon Between 400 and 1200 feet off shore the topography generally
varies between 40 to 50 feet respectively
Rubble from the tilted reinforced concrete seawall and the corroded
sheetpiles of the collapsed old pier are found along the existing
shoreline

Regional Geologic Setting
The island of Saipan is the second largest of the Mariana Islands
It is 13 miles long north to south and averages less than 4 miles wide
Saipan and the other Mariana Islands occur along an eastwardly convex
oceanic ridge for approximately 400 miles north to south Parallel to
this ridge approximately 60 to 100 miles further east is a deep marine
trench The trench coincides with a zone of earthquake foci occurring at
increasing depth westward from it indicating the probable presence of a
tectonically active subduction zone beneath the island chain
In general the island of Saipan consists of a volcanic interior
surrounded by younger limestones and recent sedimentary deposits Major
northeast trending faults have displaced and locally folded the volcanic
rocks and limestone deposits on the island It is believed that movements
along some faults have been recurrent at intervals from probably
Pre Miocene to recent time

During the Pleistocene Ice Age there were many sea level changes
The high sea level stands have resulted in the occurrence of elevated

deposits and may have lead to the formation of presently submerged reef
limestone deposits

Trench Backfill Materials
It is recommended that select backfill material consisting of clean
sand sized material be used as pipe backfill from the pipe bottom
above the gravel cushion layer to 12 inches above the top of the
pipe However be advised that it has been our past experience that
coral or other calcareous materials can be corrosive to ferrous
metals in the presence of saltwater Therefore it is recommended
that a corrosion control consultant be contacted regarding protection
of the steel outfall pipe
A minimum of 6 inch thick layer of 3 4 inch gravel 3B Fine cushion
layer could be adequate to provide a uniform supporting layer beneath
the sewer pipe on firm ground However if soft ground conditions
are encountered below the pipe invert the soft materials should be
overexcavated and replaced with a thicker 18 inch gravel cushion
layer with geotextile fabric lining reinforcement
The remainder of backfill from 12 inches ve the top of the pipe
may consist of the on site excavated sand and gravel materials
except that the material should be free of vegetation or organic
matter and other deleterious substance and contain no particle
greater than 3 inches in size All on site or imported materials
should be inspected tested and approved by a geotechnical engineer
before their use

Settlements
Total settlements of the pipe may be minimal provided that favorable
ground conditions are encountered and good construction practices are
followed However flexibility in the pipe should be provided at the
junction of different ground condition such as rock and soil cut and
fill firm soil and ground underlain by soft materials pipes supported on
piles etc Where the pipe passes between two materials of differing
strength the bedding in rock should be increased to 10 inches thick
thimniing out to a thickness of 6 inches in a distance of about 25 feet
Settlements of ground adjacent to trenches may be avoided by preventing
the lowering of the groundwater table outside the trench by not
overloading the area and by providing sufficient shoring for trench
excavation
Seismicity
Saipan is located in an active seismic zone The possible presence
of underlying saturated sandy deposits at the project site may result in
the loss of foundation support for the sewer outfall pipe and or
liquefaction damage during an earthquake If this possibility is
considered unacceptable then a detailed study beyond the present scope
of work should be performed to evaluate and verify the liquefaction
potential of the subsoils and the ground response at the project site
under seismic loading
