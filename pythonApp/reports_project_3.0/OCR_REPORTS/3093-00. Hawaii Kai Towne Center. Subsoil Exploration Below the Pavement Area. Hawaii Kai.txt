SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
In general our field exploration at the pavement area encountered a two inch 2
thick layer of asphaltic concrete surfacing the site The pavement was underlain by
compacted base course ranging in thickness between four 4 to six inches 6 underlain
by dense coral sand and gravel fill ranging between about 5 to 7 feet in thickness
This was underlain by loose to medium dense fine sand ranging between 3 to 3 feet in
thickness underlain by a thin layer of medium stiff silty clay ranging between six inches
6 and 2 feet in thickness Generally loose alluvial sand and gravel deposits were
encountered below to the maximum depths explored of about 21 and 11 feet below
the ground surface respectively
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at depths ranging between 7 3 and
7 7 feet below the existing ground surface

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration to
confirm the subsoil conditions under the pavement area at Center
located at Hawaii The general location of the site and its vicinity are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The exploration was done in general conformance with our proposal
dated
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain soil samples of the subsurface
materials to confirm the soil conditions beneath the existing parking areas Our overall
efforts included the following work items
1 Mobilization and demobilization of truck mounted drilling equipment and
operators to and from the project site
2 Drilling and sampling of two 2 borings at locations directed by
representatives of R M Towill Corporation to depths of about 11 and 21
feet below the existing ground surface
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by a Field
Engineer Geologist from our firm
4 Preparation of six 6 copies of a letter report summarizing our work on the
project and presenting our findings
5 Coordination of our overall work on the project by a Project Manager from
our firm

6 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
Principal Engineer of our firm
7 _Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface soil conditions under the pavement area at the above referenced
site were explored by drilling and sampling two 2 borings to depths ranging between
11 and 21 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The field exploration at the site encountered a thin layer of compacted base course
ranging in thickness between four 4 to six inches 6 under the two inch 2 thick
asphaltic concrete pavement The base course is underlain by fill material consisting of
medium dense to dense coral sand and gravel with thickness ranging between about 5
to 7 feet The sand and gravel fill overlay loose to medium dense fine sand deposits
ranging from about 3 to 3 feet in thickness Below the sand deposits is a thin layer of
medium stiff silty clay ranging from about six inches 6 to 2 feet in thickness underlain
by loose gravelly sand alluvial deposits from 10 8 feet and 11 0 feet below the existing
ground surface to the maximum depths explored of 21 and 11 feet at Borings No 1
and 2 respectively Coral fingers were encountered from 15 to 211 feet at Boring No 1
Groundwater was encountered at 7 6 feet and 7 3 feet below the existing ground
surface at Borings No 1 and 2 respectively Due to the relative proximity of the ocean
to the site it is anticipated that this water level will fluctuate in response to tides storm
surges and other influences

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and obtaining samples from
two 2 borings The soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in accordance
with ASTM D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils and ASTM
D 1587 83 Standard Practice for Thin Walled Tube Sampling of Soils
The materials obtained from the borings were examined and classified in general
accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84 Description and Identification of
Soils Visual Manual Procedure by a geotechnical engineer from our firm The logs of
the borings are shown on Plates 3 and 4
The borings were backfilled with soil cuttings and the surface was sealed with cold
patch asphaltic concrete
LIMITATIONS
The findings submitted in this report are based in part upon information obtained
from field data points such as borings and visual observations Variations of conditions
between the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of these variations
may not become evident until a later point in time f variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of R M Towill Corporation
and their client to confirm the subsoil condition under the pavement area at the Hawaii
Kai Towne Center in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical engineering
principles and practices No warranty is expressed or implied
