INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soils and foundation
investigation for the proposed project The study consisted
of subsurface borings laboratory testing and engineering
analyses as outlined in our proposal dated
Based on the results of our engineering studies conclusions
and recommendations pertinent to site development are
presented
PROJECT DESCRIPTION
The property is located on the makai side of Hana Highway
between the Hana Airport and the town of Hana The building
sites are indicated on the attached site plan Plate 1 The
site drains towards the ocean with an estimated relief being
on the order of 110 feet The maximum wall loads are expec
ted to be on the order of 2 kips per lineal foot There
will be a concrete floor slab on grade
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface exploration consisted of fourteen 14 borings
ranging in depth from 10 4 to 22 7 feet The drilling was
accomplished using truck mounted rotary drilling equipment
with 4 inch diameter continuous flight auger and rock coring

classified in the field and representative material was
placed in air tight containers and returned to our lab
oratory for inspection and testing

Geology
The proposed building site is located on the lower slopes
of Haleakala Volcanoe approximately 2 miles northwest of
Subsequent to the formation of east Maui Haleakala by
Kula Volcanic Series lava flows there was an extended
period of volcanic quiescence During this period
erosion incised deep canyons in a radial pattern around
the Volcanic Shield A large alluvial fan was formed
at the mouth of Kaupo Valley site location at this time
The period of unloading of volcanic deposits from above
a still molten magma chamber may have induced gas release
and started the lava flows and cinder eruptions of the
Post Erosional period Hana Volcanic Series These flows
and cinder eruptions intercalated with alluvial debris
and residual soils formed on top of previous flows
subsequently buried the alluvial fan
Recent weathering has formed a thin residual soil over
the site s volcanic and alluvial deposits

the proposed structure The following design parameters

Footings for individual buildings should be all on rock
all on undisturbed soil or all on compacted structural
fills If different bearing materials are used for foot
ings within a building differential settlements may
occur at the transition There should be a minimum of
2 feet vertical of soil or structural fill beneath
all footings founded on the respective material

excavations should be free of large surface irregularities

ential settlements should be less than 1 2 inch over the

expansive soils are not required

Lateral Loads
Resistance to lateral loads may be provided by a combin
ation of passive pressure and friction Passive pressure
may be taken as that exerted by a fluid of density equal
to 200 pounds per cubic foot Surface one foot of soil
should be neglected for passive pressure unless it is
confined beneath a concrete floor slab Friction may be
taken at normal dead load times a coefficient of friction
equal to 0 3
Concrete Floor Stab On Grade
The surface one foot of soil in the building pad should
be scarified watered and compacted to a minimum of 90
of the maximum dry density A moisture barrier consis
ting of plastic membrane should be provided under the
slab areas which will receive covering such as carpeting
or tiles The concrete floor slab on grade should be a
minimum of 4 nominal thickness and be reinforced with
a minimum of 6 x 6 10 x 10 welded wire mesh

LABORATORY TESTING
Lf
A laboratory testing program was developed to establish the
engineering properties of the soils and rocks encountered at
the project site The tests where applicable to the subject
project are included in this Appendix
1 Classification Tests
a
b
c
Visual Classification All soil samples obtained from
the borings were brought to our laboratory where they
were visually reclassified to confirm or modify the
field classification prior to finalizing the Logs of
Borings
Water Content Determinations In addition to visual
Classification typical samples were tested for natural
water content as an aid in soil classification and in
evaluating soil properties The water content values
are based upon the dry weight of the soil
Atterberg Limits Atterberg limit tests were performed
on selected clay and silt samples obtained from the
borings for the purposes of identification and correla
tion of sails Standard procedures ASTM D 424 for the
plastic limit and ASTM D 423 for the liquid limit were
used in the performance of these tests
1
d Grain Size Analysis Sieve analysis were performed on
granular soils For fine grained soils the hydrometer
test was performed to determine the distribution of
the grain sizes beyond the No 200 sieve
2 Shear Strength Tests
a Unconfined Compression Tests Intact core and split
spoon soil samples which apparently exhibited high
plasticity were tested for unconfined strength Length
to diameter ratio of 2 was maintained throughout the
tests to avoid scatter of test results and to facilitate
evaluation of shear strength values for design purposes
b Direct Shear Test In general soil samples were tested
for shear strength using a 2 41 I D direct shear box
Each set consisted of at least two tests each test run
under different normal loads The results are plotted
as Normal Stress vs Shear Strength with a failure envelope
drawn to determine values of cohesion c and angle of
frictional resistance 9
3 Consolidation Test Consolidation tests were performed on
selected undisturbed ring samples of the compressible
soils to provide basic data for making settlement calculations
Porous stones were placed on the top and bottom of the samples
to allow drainage Vertical loads were applied in increments
with each load increment being allowed to consolidate prior
2
to adding the next increment Measurements of the time
and consolidation were obtained during each load increment
and rebound was measured during the unloading portion
Consolidation test results are plotted in terms of percent
settlement versus applied loads
Swell Test The ring sample was placed between porous
stones and a 60 psf load was applied to the sample for a
24 hour period The difference between the initial and
final sample heights is the amount of swell which is expressed
as a percentage of the initial height of the sample
Maximum Density Test Samples of soils are compacted in a
mold of given size with a 10 pound rammer dropped 18 inches
to determine the relationship between moisture content and
density of the soil At least three trial points at dif
ferent moisture content are run to determine the maximum
density and optimum moisture content The test is run in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 70
Resistivity and pH Testing The results of this testing
will be submitted under separate cover when completed
