INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Repair BEQ Building 1330 Phase 2 project located within the
Naval Submarine Base in Pearl Harbor on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location and vicinity of the site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering recommen
dations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses
These recommendations are intended for design input only and are subject to the
limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located near the intersection of Bole Street and North Road
within the Naval Submarine Base in Pearl Harbor on the island of Oahu Hawaii We
understand that it is proposed to repair and renovate the enlisted living spaces in the
Bachelor Enlisted Quarters BEQ Building 1330 to meet the current criteria for BEQs
and to comply with Navy and NFPA Fire Protection Requirements Based on the
information provided the proposed repair project will consist of new utility outlets
reroofing a new water line to support the new fire sprinkler system and _ interior
renovations including bathrooms closets and fire alarm system
The existing building is a three story structure constructed with concrete masonry
and reinforced concrete As part of the repair project we understand that some of the
existing shear walls will be removed and new shear walls will be installed Therefore
recommendations pertaining to the foundation design of new shear walls will be required
for the proposed project Based on the scope of the project we anticipate that site
grading for the proposed project site will only involve foundation excavation and trench
excavation and backfill of the water line

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early
Pleistocene in age After a long period of volcanic inactivity during which erosion incised
deep valleys into the Koolau shield volcanic activity returned with a series of lava flows
followed by cinder and tuff cone formations These series are referred to as the Honolulu
Volcanic Series Many of the eruptions were rather explosive in nature due to the
interaction of groundwater with the rising magma resulting in steam explosions which
expelled large quantities of pyroclastic material predominantly ash and cinder Some
explosive eruptions produced ash that cemented together rapidly to form a firm rock
known as tuff locally known as mudrock
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3629 20 Page 5
The proposed project site is located on the coastal plain of southern Oahu The
coastal plain was built on the eroded flanks of Koolau Volcano which forms the eastern
two thirds of Oahu The coastal plain was built by extensive accumulation of alluvium
derived from erosion of the volcano interbedded with coral reefs and associated
deposits During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to
the cycles of continental glaciation Most of the coastal plains developed during the
Pleistocene Epoch when the sea levels fluctuated significantly As the glaciers grew and
advanced less water was available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell
below the present stands of the sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess
of water became available such that the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The processes of erosion and deposition were affected by these glacio eustatic
sea level fluctuations When the sea level was low the erosional base level was
correspondingly lower and valleys were carved to depths below the present sea level
When the sea level was high the erosional base level was raised such that sediments
accumulated at higher elevations

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the proposed project site ts underlain by stiff to very
stiff silty and clayey soils over loose to medium dense gravel over hard mudrock
extending to about 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface Based on the subsoils
encountered at the site we believe that a shallow foundation system consisting of spread
and or continuous footings bearing on the recompacted in situ soils may be used for
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3629 20 Page 7
support of the new shear walls Detailed discussions and geotechnical engineering
design recommendations pertaining to the proposed project are presented in the following
sections of this report
Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our exploration we believe
that a shallow foundation system may be used to support the new shear walls An
allowable bearing pressure of 2 000 pounds per square foot p s f may be used for
design of isolated and continuous footings bearing on the recompacted in situ soils This
allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third
1 3 for transient loads such as wind and or seismic forces In general footings should
be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finish grades in
addition the bottoms of the new footings should extend to approximately the same
elevations as the bottoms of the existing adjacent footings to reduce the poteniial
settlements due to surcharge effects
lf foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation
settlement
For footings bearing on the in situ soils the bottom of the footing excavations
should be recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction prior to
placement of reinforcing steel or concrete Where soft and or loose materials are
encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they should be over excavated to
expose the underlying firm dense materials The over excavation should be backfilled
with select granular material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3629 20 Page 8
If the foundation is designed in accordance with the recommendations presented
herein total footing settlements under the anticipated design loads are estimated to be
on the order of less than 1 inch with differential settlements of approximately inch
In general lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil A
coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted in situ
soils Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent
fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This assumes that
the soil around the footings is well compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs
the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the foundation bearing
conditions and the required embedment depths
Slabs On Grade
Where new slabs are needed we recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of
cushion fill consisting of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be placed
below the slab to provide more uniform support of the slab Prior to placement of cushion
layer the slab subgrade bottom of cushion layer should be scarified to a depth of about
8 inches and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Where soft
and or loose materials are encountered at the slab subgrade level they should be over
excavated to expose the underlying firm dense materials and backfilled with select
granular material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction To reduce
future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious
moisture vapor barrier is recommended on top of the gravel cushion layer Two inches of
moist fine sand should be placed above the vapor barrier to provide protection of the
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3629 20 Page 9
barrier and to aid in curing of the slab concrete Flexible floor coverings such as carpet
or sheet vinyl should be considered since they can better mask minor slab cracking
Exterior concrete walkways may be required for the proposed project We
recommend that a minimum 6 inch thick layer of select granular material or select borrow
subbase be provided below the concrete walkway slab Contraction joints should be
provided at intervals equal to the width of the walkways with expansion joints at
right angle intersections
The area adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab with
non expansive impervious soil and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce
the potential of water ponding around the foundation
Site Grading
We envision that site grading for the proposed project will involve foundation
subgrade preparation for the new walls and trench excavation and backfill for a water line
It is recommended that site grading operations be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii to evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered during
foundation excavation and whether the exposed soil conditions are similar to those
encountered in our field exploration
At the on set of earthwork the structural elements to be replaced should be
removed as shown on the plans All concrete debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials generated from the demolition operation should be removed and disposed of
properly off site
Following the demolition operation the footing subgrades should be recompacted
to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction f soft and or loose materials are
encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they should be over excavated to
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3629 20 Page 10
expose the underlying firm dense materials The over excavation should be backfilled
with select granular material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage
of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry
weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
In general the excavated on site soils may be reused as a source of general fill
materials provided that they contain no lumps or fragments greater than 3 inches in
maximum dimension If imported fill materials are required as general fill and backfill
materials the imported fill material should consist of non expansive material such as
crushed coral mudrock basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from
coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in maximum dimension The material
should have a laboratory CBR value of 12 or more and should have a maximum swell of
1 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM Test Designation 1883
Imported fill material should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for conformance with our
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use
Generally the fill and backfill materials should be placed in level lifts not exceeding
8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The fill and backfill
material within the upper 2 feet below the finish subgrade in pavement areas should be
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Utility Trench
We understand that a new water line will be constructed for the proposed project
In general a granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation or S4C sand is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular
materials such as No 3B Fine gravel or S4C sand should also be used for the initial
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3629 20 Page 11
trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around
the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility
of damaging the pipes It is critical that the free draining material be used to reduce the
potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide adequate
support around the sides of the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the on site soils or select fill
material The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than
90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence
Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench backfill below
the pavement subgrade should be compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away
from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that gutter
systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of the concrete to
reduce water infiltration

Field Exploration

borings to a depth of about 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a

the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 3

ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils

Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations were performed
on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil properties
The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample

the soils to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plates B 1 and
B 2
Two unconfined compression tests ASTM D 2166 were performed on selected
samples of the soils to evaluate the strength characteristics of the soils The results of
the tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
