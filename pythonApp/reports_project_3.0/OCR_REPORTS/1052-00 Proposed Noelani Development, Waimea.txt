SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site consists of two adjoining 30 000 square
foot lots fronting on Opelo Road The site is bounded on
the north south and west by residential lots and to the
east by Opelo Road
The ground surface is rolling with drainage toward the
southwest
At the time of our field exploration there were two houses
and a concrete slab on the site Access to the site is by
an existing gravel driveway
The area is covered with thick Waimea grass The portion of
the site surrounding the existing house is planted with many
trees both fruit bearing and ornamental and various shrubs
and bushes
The ground elevation of the site varies from about 2 645 to
about 2 615 feet
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS

minimal with anticipated cuts and fills of 3 and 6 feet res

SUBSURFACE EXPLORATIONS
Four six inch borings were made on the project site The
borings were made by utilizing a truck mounted rotary hy
draulic drill rig advancing 6 inch diameter continuous
flight augers Soil samples were obtained from each boring
for visual classification and laboratory testing
A bulk surface sample was also obtained from the site for
plasticity index CBR and other laboratory tests
Subsurface Conditions
The borings encountered two sets of surface conditions
dividing the site into two areas These areas are desig
nated as the upper area in the northern half of the site
and the lower area in the southern half of the site
Upper Northern Area
In the upper area our test borings revealed a
surface soil layer about 7 feet thick consisting
of very stiff to hard gravelly silt This material
is derived from in situ weathering of volcanic ash and
clinker
Below this surface layer was a strata of decomposed
basalt approximately 3 feet thick which is underlain
by hard basalt to the depths drilled
Lower Southern Area
In the lower area our test borings encountered a
surface layer of medium stiff gravelly silt approxi
mately 4 feet thick This is probably transported
sediment from the upper area
Underlying this surface layer is a layer of very stiff
to hard gravelly silt approximately 5 feet thick
which is derived from in situ weathering of volcanic
ash and clinker
Below this layer hard gravelly siit or decomposed
basalt was encountered to the depths drilled

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed basically competent foun
dation bearing soils at the proposed project site Froma
soil engineering point of view the site is suitable for the
proposed residential development
Shallow foundations may be used for the proposed structures
The near surface soil have low swelling potential therefore
slab on grade construction may also be utilized
Foundations
Spread or continuous footing foundations are recommended for
the structures on level or gently sloping areas
An allowable bearing value of 3500 p s f is recommended for
footings bearing on the very stiff to hard in situ gravelly
silt soils For footings bearing on compacted structural
fill or medium stiff natural soils a bearing value of 2500
p s f may be used to design the footings The above values
are for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are con
sidered
A minimum footing width of 18 inches should be utilized
The in situ soils are very silty and susceptible to erosion
therefore the bottom of all footings should be placed a
minimum of 24 inches below the finished grade In addition
the outside heel of the footing should be set back a minimum
of 6 feet from the nearest outside slope face This setback
may require deepening of the footing embedment to maintain
the above specified minimum setback

The post footings should be cast in place and the concrete
should be poured neat against the excavation to eliminate

Surface water should be diverted away from the building Ss

Anticipated settlements are expected to be relatively small
on the order of less than half an inch for structures

Site Grading
Relatively shallow cuts and fills are anticipated for the
site
The site should be cleared of vegetation old foundations
and debris prior to commencement of earthwork There is a
possibility that one or more old cesspools may exist on the
site These cesspools should be located opened any sludge
cleaned out and backfilled with compacted granular material
at 90 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM
D 1557 societal _
Prior to placement of structural fill the existing ground
surface should be moisture conditioned scarified and re
compacted by a heavy vibratory roller to obtain a minimum of
95 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM
D 1557 This will serve to densify the loose or soft sur
face silts
Fill and backfill should be placed in 10 inch loose hori
zontal lifts compacted to obtain 95 of its maximum dry den
sity as determined by ASTM D 1557
Fills placed on slopes steeper than five horizontal to one
vertical 5 1 should be keyed and benched into firm natural
ground
Two horizontal to one vertical 2 1 cut and fill slopes are
recommended The finished slopes should be protected by
proper type of slope planting as soon as possible to reduce
erosion due to the silty nature of the on site soils

Lateral Resistance
To resist lateral loads a coefficient of friction of 0 40
between the foundations and the underlying soil is
recommended
In addition a passive soil resistance of 300 p c f can
also be used
Slab On Grade
Slabs should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of gravel
size A polyethylene moisture barrier should be used be
tween this cushion and the slab
The laboratory soil tests indicate that the on site soils
have low swelling potential Slab on grade should have
perimeter footings extending down a minimum of 12 inches
below the finish grade to reduce ground movement due to dry
ing and wetting below the slab
The slab subgrade should be kept moist and protected from
drying prior to concrete placement Subgrade should be con
pacted to a minimum of 95 of the maximum dry density esta
blished by ASTM D 1557
Parking Lot Pavement
The laboratory CBR test on the near surface on site soil
indicated low expansion potential and a CBR value of 14 when
it is used as compacted fill
Based upon the laboratory CBR test of the remolded on site
brown silt surface soil the preliminary pavement design is
as follows
2 inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 inch Base Course
8 inch Total Pavement Thickness
The subgrade soil should be scarified down to a depth of 6
inches moistened and compacted at optimum moisture content
to 95 of the maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557

PERCOLATION TESTING
A sewage treatment plant and injection well disposal system
is planned for the development
Two 4 inch diameter borings were drilled for the purpose of
percolation testing to evaluate the suitability of the un
derlying rock formation for injection well disposal
These borings encountered approximately ten feet of brown
gravelly silt which was underlain by a layer of hard massive
basalt between 6 and 14 feet in thickness Below this ledge
of massive basalt were interbedded layers of clinker and
massive basalt which forms the pervious zone for effluent
disposal
The percolation testing consisted of pumping water from a
fire hydrant into the drilled holes and observing the
increase in water level or head in the drilled hole Inflow
rates of about 200 g p em were utilized and the pump test
was run from a minimum of one hour in each boring The in
jected water dissipated without an appreciable build up of
the groundwater level
Based on our test results it is our opinion that injection
well disposal of effluent is feasible and the two per
collation test borings have been cased to allow for their
possible future use as injection wells
However it should be noted that periodic flushing and surge
pumping inside the injection wells is recommended to main
tain the effluent percolation
The logs of the percolation test borings and the percolation
test results are included in the Appendix

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the site grading footing excavations
and backfill operations be observed by a soils engineer
during construction to see that adequate bearing and con
paction have been obtained and that the subsurface coditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface inves
tigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive and
meet the approval of the soils engineer A sample of any
proposed import material should be submitted to the soils
engineer for testing and approval a minimum of 10 working
days prior to its intended delivery to the project site
