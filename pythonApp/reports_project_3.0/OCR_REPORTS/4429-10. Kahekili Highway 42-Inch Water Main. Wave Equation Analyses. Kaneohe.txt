In accordance with your request we have performed a wave equation analysis for the
proposed pile driving operations for the Kahekili Highway 42 inch Water Main project in the
Kaneohe area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The wave equation analysis was conducted
based on the information provided and the subsurface conditions contained in the
Geotechnical Exploration report mentioned above
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS

water main will be supported on a driven pile foundation system The proposed pile section
will consist of 12 inch square precast prestressed concrete piles The project alignment was
divided into six sections The details of the sections are summarized in the following table

The piles were designed with an allowable compression load capacity of 45 tons 90
kips A safety factor of 2 0 was used Therefore the required ultimate pile capacity is
90 tons or 180 kips
The piles were designed based on deriving support primarily from side resistance
within the stiff to dense alluvium deposits underlying the weaker alluvium deposits
Subsurface Conditions
Based on the information presented in the Geotechnical Exploration report the project
site is generally underlain by a surface fill layer consisting of medium stiff to very stiff silty
clays with sand gravel and boulders extending to depths of about 5 to 15 feet below the
existing ground surface The fill materials were underlain by alluvial deposits consisting of
soft to very soft clays and silts with organic materials and or loose to very ioose clayey sands
and gravel extending to depths of about 20 to 50 feet below the existing ground surface The
very soft and or loose alluvial deposits were underlain by stiffer and denser alluvial deposits
consisting of silty clays and sandy gravel extending to the maximum depth explored of about
81 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Hammer and Driving System
We understand that a single acting diesel hammer is proposed for
the pile driving operations by Healy Tibbits Builders Inc Based on the pile driving equipment
data form provided the hammer has a ram weight of 3 528 pounds and a
maximum stroke of 11 2 feet with a maximum rated energy of 39 400 foot pounds at the
maximum pump setting of 4 The hammer has a minimum rated energy of
14 100 foot pounds corresponding to a pump setting of 1
Based on the subsurface conditions and the driving conditions anticipated a pile
cushion consisting of 6 inches of plywood cushion blocks will be used for the pile driving
operations The plywood cushion blocks will have a cross section area of approximately
156 2 square inches
Pile Details
We understand that the specified piles will be 12 inch square precast prestressed
concrete piles with lengths of about 60 to 80 feet The piles were designed using an
allowable compression load of 45 tons with a factor of safety of 2 0 resulting in an ultimate
pile capacity of 90 tons 180 kips The piles will be cast and driven in one single piece in
Sections 2 5 and 6 The piles in Sections 1 3 and 4 will be cast in two pieces and
connected with splice cans
We understand that the piles will be cast from concrete with a minimum 7 day
compressive strength of 5 000 pounds per square inch psi The 28 day strength of the
concrete is at least 6 000 psi We anticipate that the effective prestress level of the

WAVE EQUATION ANALYSES
A wave equation analysis was performed based on the pile driving system proposed
by Healy Tibbitts Builders Inc and the subsurface conditions presented in the Geotechnical
Exploration report The wave equation analysis was performed using the computer program
GRLWEAP Wave Equation Analysis of Pile Foundations which is an extension of the
WEAP 87 program developed under the sponsorship of the Federal Highway Administration
