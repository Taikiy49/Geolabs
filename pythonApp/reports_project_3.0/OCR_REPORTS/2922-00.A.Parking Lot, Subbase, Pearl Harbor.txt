INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed parking lot at the Pearl Harbor Naval Shipyard on the island
of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site is shown on the Project Location
Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings resulting for our field exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based on the data
obtained from the field exploration and laboratory testing These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended to form the basis for preparation of
construction cost estimates for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the Subbase area in Pearl Harbor Naval Shipyard
on the island of Oahu Hawaii
It is planned to construct a new 99 stall parking lot at the demolished Building 678
location
An 1 inch thick asphaltic concrete overlay above the remaining concrete floor
slab from the demolished building is planned for the new parking lot
It is our understanding that approximately 50 feet of the existing concrete slab from
Pierce Street will be removed to provide a smoother transition to Pierce Street

materials encountered

6 Preparation of six 6 copies of a formal report summarizing our work on the

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located between Pierce Street and North Road and between
Millican Field and Building 679 within the Subbase area of Pearl Harbor Naval Shipyard
The project site is relatively level
Presently the site is covered with the remaining concrete floor slab from the
demolished building and is being used for parking The top of the existing concrete slab
is higher in the western portion of the site Some amount of cracks was noted on the
previous concrete floor slab

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration disclosed that the project site is underlain by loose to medium
dense and stiff fill deposits below the existing concrete floor slab Base course material
is not used under the concrete slab
Because of the potential for reflection cracks through the asphalt overlay by joints
and cracks in the existing concrete slab and an inadequate base course layer we
recommend that the existing concrete slab be removed and replaced with an asphaltic
concrete pavement section
If this above replacement is not feasible due to budget constraint a pavement
reinforcing fabric could be used below the asphalt overlay to reduce the potential for
GEOLABS HAWAII

reflection cracks Some amount of future reflection cracks should be anticipated to occur
in the future
Pavement
Based on our field exploration and observations the existing concrete slab varies
in thickness and in elevation across the project site Also it does not appear that there
is an adequate aggregate base course layer
Several joints and moderate amount of cracking was noted on the existing
concrete slab
It is our understanding that a portion of the existing concrete slab will be removed
at the western end of the site for a smoother transition to Pierce Street
New Flexible Pavement Design
Because of the tendency for joints and cracks in concrete slabs to work their way
through flexible material such as asphalt overlays which will eventually appear on
the surface as reflection cracks it is our first recommendation that the existing
concrete slab be removed disposed of properly off site and replaced with a new
asphaltic concrete pavement section
Based on the results of laboratory swell and field CBR test on the site materials
the following preliminary pavement design may be used on a recompacted sandy
subgrade
2 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Subgrade

CBR and density tests and or field observations should be performed on the
actual subgrade soil encountered during construction to verify the adequacy of the
section and the section may be revised if necessary The recommended section
considers medium to light traffic and that good drainage will be provided for areas
adjacent to paved areas
Asphalt Overlay
Assuming that the above new flexible pavement is not feasible due to current
budget limitations asphalt overlay could also be considered To reduce the
potential for reflection cracks through an asphalt overlay we believe that a
pavement reinforcing fabric such as Petromat Amopav or equivalent may be used
between the existing concrete slab and the new asphalt overlay Also we
recommend that the asphalt overlay thickness be increased to three 3 inches
The following are additional recommendations for an asphalt overlay
1 All significant cracks greater than inch in width should be filled prior to
placing the fabric The smaller cracks should be filled with hot liquid crack
filler while the larger cracks be filled with asphalt hot mix or cold patch
2 To reduce the potential for fabric tear sharp or rough edges on the surface
of existing concrete slab should be removed and made smooth
3 An asphaltic sealant such as AC 5 or AC 20 should be uniformly sprayed
over the existing concrete slab

W O 2922 00 A Page 8
4 The fabric should be placed on the sealant in accordance with the
manufacturer s specifications Stiff brooms should be used to smooth out
the surface as well as getting a good bond with the sealant Joint overlays
of about three 3 inches are generally used
5 The hot mix asphalt overlay should be placed directly on the fabric as soon
as possible
Site Grading
Minor site grading work is anticipated
For the asphalt overlay alternative the existing soil material in the depressed slab
area should be removed and replaced with base course material compacted to 95
percent compaction
Since the base material below the concrete slabs in the western portion of the site
contains clay we recommend that a six inch layer of base course material be provided
below the finished subgrade and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent compaction
The area within the contract grading limit should be cleared of vegetation debris
rubbish broken concrete slabs and other deleterious materials These materials should
be removed and disposed of properly off site
In areas to receive fill the ground should be scarified to a minimum depth of six
6 inches moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 95 percent of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78 Any loose or soft spots encountered should be over excavated down to the
firm subsoils and replaced with compacted non expansive select material

After site preparation the fill should be placed and compacted to a minimum of 95
percent of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 in
eight inch compacted lifts
It appears that off site borrow may be required The imported fill material should
be tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site to see
that it is suitable for its intended purpose The imported fill material should consist of non
expansive select granular materials less than three 3 inches in maximum size with less
than 20 percent passing the 200 sieve
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded
to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the site grading work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow
design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated prior
to the start of construction The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the geotechnical engineer prior to delivery to the site

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of conditions between the borings
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re
evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings are approximate having been determined by
taping from structures and features indicated on the parking lot plan prepared by Irie and
Nakamoto Architects Inc The physical locations of the field borings should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote gradual
transitions
Water level data from the field borings were collected at the times given on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulations of this report However it must be noted
that fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to schedule appropriate tests
to aid in determining pertinent engineering properties of the soil and or rock materials
sampled
Classification Tests
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Moisture Content ASTM Test Method D 2216 80 and Unit Weight Determinations
are used as an aid to soil classification and to aid in determining certain properties such
as relative saturation The moisture content expressed as a percentage of the dry weight
of the tested sample is shown at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs
Physical Property Tests
Tests are performed to develop a correlative index of certain physical parameters
of the soil These physical parameters may be used in engineering analyses pertaining
to the performance or suitability of a soil
Expansion tests are performed to evaluate the swelling potential of soils under
structural loads These tests aid in evaluating the materials for use as foundation support
and embankment These test results are summarized on Plate B 1
