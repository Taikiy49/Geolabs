In accordance with our proposal dated August 25 2004 we have performed a
geotechnical engineering exploration for the proposed interior renovation of the
Food Pantry Eaton Square Store 003 in the Waikiki area of Honolulu on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii Our exploration was limited to evaluating the potential impacts of ground
water and dewatering on the proposed renovation project This letter report summarizes
the findings from our field exploration at the project site and presents our findings and
recommendations for the proposed project These findings and recommendations are
intended for design input only and are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project involves interior renovation to the Food Pantry Store located
at 438 Hobron Lane in the Waikiki area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 As
part of the renovation trenches will be excavated to install new drain sewer and grease
lines and a new grease interceptor Approximately 150 feet of new drain line with a drop
of 3 feet will be installed The proposed grease interceptor will be installed within the
existing loading area and will be approximately 10 feet below the store s slab There is an
existing water proofing membrane underneath the existing slab to provide protection for
the store interior
Our geotechnical engineering exploration focused on the impact of the groundwater
table on the proposed new drain lines and grease interceptor Two boreholes were drilled
to depths of approximately 15 feet below the existing ground surface One of the
boreholes was drilled at the loading dock area and the other borehole was drilled at the
sidewalk area in front of the store at the northern end After drilling and sampling 2 inch
diameter PVC pipes and screen were installed in each borehole for groundwater
monitoring The groundwater table was monitored in each monitoring well for a period of
about four 4 weeks

FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling two borings designated as
Boring Nos 1 and 2 extending to depths of about 15 and 16 5 feet below the existing
ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig and a portable drill
rig equipped with continuous flight solid stem augers The approximate locations of the
borings drilled at the site are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings
Plates 4 and 5
Soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of
an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the information provided we anticipate that the invert of the new sewer
line will be on the order of about 3 to 4 feet below the existing ground surface and the
bottom of the new grease trap will be about 10 to 11 feet below the existing loading dock
pavement surface With respect to this exploration the primary consideration for the
project will be the impacts of groundwater on the new sewer and grease trap installation
Due to the shallow depths of the groundwater encountered in the borings and monitoring
wells it is anticipated that dewatering will be required for the installation of the grease trap
and possibly for the new utilities
Our field exploration at the site generally encountered silty materials which have
relatively moderate to low permeability Monitoring of the groundwater indicated that the
tidal efficiency of the soils underlying the site is on the order of about 25 percent of the
open ocean tide These data also indicate relatively moderate to low permeability in the
shallow subsoils at the site Therefore it is our opinion that limited dewatering is feasible
It is anticipated that the proposed sewer trench may be dewatered in a relatively
conventional manner by limiting the amount of open trench and pumping Due to the
depth of the grease trap it is anticipated that a cut off wall system will be required for
dewatering Alternatively since grease traps are pre fabricated the excavation could be
partially dewatered and the trap installed in the wet Since the excavation will involve
discharge of groundwater a National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System NPDES
permit may be necessary
Dewatering for construction is the responsibility of the contractor The selection of
equipment and methods of dewatering should be left up to the contractor and he she
should be aware that modifications to the dewatering system may be required during
construction depending on the conditions encountered The dewatering method selected
should have minimal impact on the groundwater level surrounding the proposed
excavation It is anticipated that the fill materials and lagoonal formation encountered at
the project site are moderately permeable Therefore dewatering of the excavation may
involve moderate to large volumes of water
As mentioned previously we believe that a cut off wall system such as sheeting or
interlocking steel sheet piles should be considered to aid in dewatering the grease trap
excavation However sumps may be needed to collect water that percolates up into the
base of the excavation or infiltrates through the sheet piles The sheet piles should be

driven to a sufficient depth to reduce the potential for areal ground subsidence and to

It is our opinion that the definition of Dewatering in the contract documents should
be written to include all works or systems required to lower the natural groundwater table
and or to exclude the water from the excavations to allow construction of the proposed
sewer line and grease trap under safe and dry conditions These works or systems may
include but are not limited to grouting cut off walls tremie concrete plugs or combination
of the above and or other possible methods
We suggest that the following three basic criteria be considered in selection of a
suitable method of dewatering
a The dewatering method should result in the least disturbance or damage to
existing structures roads and environment
b The dewatering method should maintain stability of and provide safe and
dry working conditions in the excavation
Cc The dewatering method should be sufficiently flexible to allow modifications
to accommodate various ground conditions
The potential impact of the dewatering system selected on depressing the natural
groundwater table must be carefully evaluated by the contractor prior to dewatering It is
recommended that a qualified geotechnical engineer be retained by the contractor to
design and evaluate the dewatering system used
The impact and safety of the dewatering operations should be made the sole
responsibility of the contractor His her qualified representative who should be required to
be continuously present on site during dewatering activities will have the best opportunity
to promptly observe the effects of dewatering during construction and to implement as
soon as possible necessary precautionary or remedial measures including but not limited
to stopping or slowing down the dewatering operations
Where encountered at the bottom of excavations permeable granular subsoils may
be susceptible to piping and quick conditions The dewatering operations should be
carried out without creating a quick condition or softening at the excavation bottoms
Therefore the project dewatering operations should be performed without pumping out
soil fines pumping clear water only and should be coordinated with shoring installation
such that the excavation stability is not adversely affected Excessive pumping which
removes soil fines may result in a blowing or heaving of the excavation bottom or sides
Special caution should also be taken to avoid dewatering utility trenches connected
to excavations If this occurs the granular bedding and or backfill in the utility trenches

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs be retained to provide geotechnical engineering
services during construction of the proposed project The items of construction monitoring
that are critical requiring Special Inspection include observation of subgrade preparation
and fill and backfill placement
Other aspects of earthwork construction should also be observed by a
representative from Geolabs This is to observe compliance with the intent of the design
concepts specifications or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design
changes that may be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from those
anticipated at the time this report was prepared The recommendations provided in this
report are contingent upon such observations If the actual exposed subsurface conditions
encountered during construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The findings and discussions submitted in this letter report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between and
beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the findings and discussions provided in this letter report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping from features shown on the Site Plan transmitted by
Food Pantry Ltd on August 23 2004 The approximate elevations of field borings were
not available for this exploration The physical locations and elevations of the borings
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
