SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our boring and laboratory test data
the findings indicate that the subsurface
conditions at the proposed food services
building site consist primarily of
approximately 2 to 4 feet of dense silty sandy
gravel fill overlying dense coral reef and
gravelly coralline deposits Spread or
continuous footing foundations bearing on the
dense coral or surface fill may be used
In an area adjoining the existing underground
fuel tanks loose gravel fill was encountered
to approximately 12 feet depth below the
existing ground surface The lateral extent of
this loose fill is not known
We believe that the site should be probed
during construction at and along footing
locations to delineate the existence of
possible cavity loose lens Once delineated
the probe holes and the cavity lens should be
backfilled with low pressure grout to ensure
sufficient support to the foundations above
The text of this report should be referred to
for detailed and special design
recommendations
INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soils and foundation
engineering evaluation performed for the subject project

It is our understanding that the proposed food services structure
will be a one story high reinforced concrete building with on grade slab

A general layout of the proposed construction is shown on the Site

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this evaluation was to explore the subsurface soil and
water conditions at the site to obtain pertinent soil data for the design
of the foundation for the proposed structure
The main objectives were to explore the thickness uniformity and
competency of the underlying soil for spread footing foundation design
The scope of our evaluation included drilling and sampling five 5
to 25 5 feet below the existing ground surface Also
included were laboratory testing of the samples obtained engineering
evaluation of the field and laboratory data development of design
recommendations and report preparation
The results of the field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our recommendations are
presented in the appendices of this report

SITE DESCRIPTION
The existing OCCC Facility is located at the southwestern corner of
Dillingham Boulevard and Puuhale Road
The proposed structure will be located in an open area southwest of
Blocks 12 and 16 of the existing Oahu Community Correctional Center
Presently the site is covered with asphalt concrete pavement
There are two existing underground fuel tanks located near the
southern portion of the proposed structure which will be relocated off the
project site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling 5
borings to depths of 10 0 to 25 5 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Core samples of cemented coral were obtained to identify the type of
coral formation degree of cementation and extent of cavities
In general four 4 of the five 5 borings drilled encountered
dense coral gravel or cemented coral reef deposits at shallow depths below
the upper 2 0 to 4 0 feet of dense to very dense silty sandy gravel
fill Locally near the existing fuel tank area up to about 10 feet of
loose fill was located approximately from 2 to 12 feet below the existing
ground surface Boring 5 The lateral extent of this loose fill is not
known but is suspected to be related to the underground fuel tanks
Cavities of approximately 1 foot height were noted in Borings 3 and 4
at approximately 14 and 16 feet depth respectively

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The drilled borings indicate that a surficial 2 to 4 foot thick layer
of dense to very dense fill consisting primarily of silty sandy gravel
overlies generally medium dense to very dense coralline gravels and hard
coral reefs at the proposed building site However locally loose gravel
fill was encountered to a depth of approximately 12 feet
Because the depth to the dense coralline deposits and hard coral reef
material is variable spread footing foundations bearing on either coral
formation or the existing dense fill may be used for foundation support
for the proposed building structure
The cavities encountered in Borings 3 and 4 indicate that other
cavities and or loose lens may also exist in the underlying soils across
the site These may induce excessive settlement if they are present at
shallow depths below the bottom of the footing Therefore we believe
that the footing locations should be probed during construction to
delineate possible cavity loose lens Once delineated the probe holes
and the cavity lens should be backfilled with low pressure grout to ensure
sufficient support to the foundations above
Shallow Spread or Continuous Footing Foundations
For the proposed reinforced concrete structure spread or continuous
footing foundations may be used The footings may bear on the hard coral
formation generally encountered at about 2 to 4 feet below the existing
ground surface An allowable bearing value of 5000 p s f may be used on
the hard coral formation However it should be noted that the surface of
coral formations are erratic and the depth to the top of the coral will
vary Additionally it appears that the coral has been excavated to a
significant depth in the vicinity of the existing underground fuel tanks

Due to the variable depth to the coral formation an alternative
would be to design spread or continuous footings bearing on the upper soil
layer using a lower bearing value
Based on the anticipated soil conditions site grading and design
loading we believe that an allowable bearing capacity of 3000 p s f may
be used for spread footing bearing on existing dense fill
The bottoms of the footing excavations should be compacted to a
minimm of 95 of the maximum density determined by ASTM D 1557
Footings should be placed a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Uplift wind or other lateral loading conditions
may further increase the embedment of the footing foundation
For the above recommendations estimated foundation settlements for a
column load of 100 kips are on the order of one quarter of an inch total
Cavity
During the field exploration 1 foot high cavities were encountered
in the dense to hard coral layer in Borings 3 and 4 which were drilled
near the southern and northern edges of the proposed building site
respectively The cavities were encountered at depths of 14 0 to 16 0
oy mde
Loecle
Cavities are formed in coral formation due to the presence of fresh
water streams during the growth stages of the coral which prevent the
growth of the coral Therefore it is possible that the cavities may be
part of an old stream and therefore other cavities may exist beneath the
site rather than the cavities encountered being isolated occurrences

To reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to possible
occurrence of cavities or loose lenses near the footing bottom elevation
in the underlying coral formation drilled probes are recommended below
each column footing and at 15 foot centers for continuous wall footings
The probe holes should be a minimm of 3 inch in diameter and extend below
the bottom of the footing to a depth equal to two times the footing width
or a minimm of 10 feet The probe holes should be grouted with a
sand water cement mixture under low pressures
If a cavity or loose lens is suspected below a footing additional
probe holes should be made to delineate the horizontal extent and
thickness of the cavity or loose lens and properly grouted The probing
and grouting operations should be monitored by the soils engineer to see
that the aforementioned recommendations are carried out properly and to
evaluate other solutions to properly backfill potential voids and densify
the loose soil which may occur near the footing elevations
Freezer Area
The depth to the water table is about 14 feet therefore the
potential for capillary rise and subsequent frost heaving under the
freezer is relatively small
However it may still be desirable to provide a cushion layer of
low frost susceptible material such as 3A Fine gravel under the freezer
slab to further reduce the potential for frost heave
Site Grading
It is our understanding that site grading work for the proposed
structure will be minimal
The ground surface below the slabs should be scarified to a minimm
depth of 6 inches and recompacted to a minimum of 95 of its maximum dry
density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 Soft pockets encountered in the
initial site preparation should be removed and backfilled with select
backfill material

The upper sandy to gravelly fill materials may be used as a source of
backfill material If required new imported fill material should consist
of non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum
size with less than 25 passing the 200 sieve
Site grading should provide drainage of surface runoff away from all
foundations
Existing Fuel Tank
The existing underground fuel tank will be excavated and removed
After removal of the tanks the excavation should be examined to
determine the extent and condition of the previous backfill Loose
backfill should be completely removed and recompacted to 95 of ASTM
D 1557 Based on the 11 feet of loose fill encountered in Boring 5
the extent of the tank backfill may extend beyond this boring

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the probing and grouting site grading and
footing excavation be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our evaluation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site for use
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation engineering
principles and practices This warranty is in liew of all other
warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this study
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot be
fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions may occur
in localized areas and require additional probing or corrections in the
field Therefore additional expenditures may be needed during
construction to attain a properly constructed project Some contingency
fund is thus recommended to accommodate these possible costs
This report has been prepared in order to assist the engineer in the
preparation of site grading plans and foundation design of this project
