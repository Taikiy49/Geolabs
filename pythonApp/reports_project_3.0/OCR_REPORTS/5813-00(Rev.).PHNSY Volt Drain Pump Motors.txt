In accordance with the scope of services outlined in our revised fee proposal dated
we have performed a geotechnical engineering exploration in support of
the foundation design for the new diesel engine generator pad for the proposed
design build project Replace 2300 Volt Drain Pump Motors at Pumpwell S 17 within
Dry Dock No 1 at the Pearl Harbor Naval Shipyard on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
We understand that subsurface soil information and a geotechnical report are not
included in the RFP documents The design build contractor is required to perform a
geotechnical subsurface investigation to support the foundation design of the new diesel
engine generator pad The RFP originally required the design build contractor to design
the foundation system for the future generator building under a separate contract
However we understand that the future generator building was recently deleted from the
scope of work
Our scope of work is in general accordance with the geotechnical requirements
outlined in Part 3 Project Program and Part 4 Performance Technical Specifications of
the Request for Proposal RFP documents This report summarizes the findings from
our field exploration and laboratory testing and presents our findings and
recommendations for the foundation design of the diesel engine generator pad only
The findings and recommendations are intended for design input only and are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the information provided the modifications planned for this design build
project will enable the continued operation of the lighting and low voltage systems at
Pumpwell S 17 as well as the operation of the drain pumps sewage pumps and sump
pumps in the event of a loss of normal utility power This will prevent flooding of Dry Dock
No 1 if there is a storm condition or leakage and simultaneous loss of utility power
protecting any vessels and equipment that may be in the dry dock

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
We explored the subsurface conditions at the proposed diesel engine generator
pad by drilling and sampling one boring designated as Boring No 1 extending to a depth
of about 32 5 feet below the existing pavement surface The approximate boring location is
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration generally encountered a 6 inch thick AC paving over 18 inches
of granular fill consisting of dense gravelly sand Stiff clayey silts on the order of about
3 feet thick overlying medium dense to loose sandy gravel were then encountered and
extended to the maximum depth explored of about 32 5 feet below the existing pavement
surface We encountered groundwater in the boring at a depth of about 7 feet below the
pavement surface at the time of our exploration It should be noted that the water level at
the site could be influenced by tidal fluctuations seasonal precipitation storm surge
conditions and other factors
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration methodology and the Log of Boring are
presented in Appendix A Results of the laboratory tests performed on selected soil
samples obtained from our field exploration are presented in Appendix B
SOIL PROFILE TYPE FOR SEISMIC DESIGN
Our field exploration generally encountered dense stiff surface fills overlying
medium dense to loose sandy gravel extending to the maximum depth explored of about
32 5 feet below the existing pavement surface
Based on the type of structure involved and in accordance with Section of
IBC 2003 we believe that the project site may be assumed as a Stiff Soil Profile
Therefore it is our opinion that seismic design of the generator pad may be designed
based on a Site Class D soil profile type based on the International Building Code
Table No 2003 Edition

Slab On Grade Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered and the relatively light
structural loads anticipated we believe that slab on grade foundations with
thickened edge footings may be used to support the proposed new diesel generator An
allowable bearing pressure of up to about may be used for the design of
shallow foundations bearing on the recompacted in situ soils This bearing value is for
those caused by wind or seismic forces
Since the concrete slab on grade of the generator pad will be subjected to
equipment operation a 6 inch layer of aggregate subbase is recommended in lieu of the
normal gravel cushion and moisture barrier between the slab concrete and the underlying
subgrade material The aggregate subbase should consist of crushed basalt aggregate
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction For the design of structural
slab a modulus of subgrade reaction of 200 pounds per cubic inch pci may be used for
the compacted aggregate subbase
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by friction developed between
the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth pressure acting
against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of friction of about
0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted in situ soils Resistance due to
passive earth pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of about
350 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the soils around the
footings are well compacted or undisturbed Unless covered by pavements or slabs the
passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be neglected

In addition it is our opinion that a lateral soil bearing pressure of 350 psf may be
used in the design of the electrical conduit support column footings which will be
embedded in the earth and are unrestrained at grade
The perimeter thickened edges of the slab should be embedded a minimum of
12 inches below the lowest adjacent exterior grade In addition the base of the thickened
edge should have a minimum width of 12 inches Foundations next to utility trenches or
easements should be embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward
from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This
requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with
additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for appreciable foundation
settlement If foundations are designed and constructed in accordance with our
recommendations we estimate total settlements to be less than 1 inch with differential
settlements on the order of about 0 5 inch
Areas adjacent to the slabs should be backfilled tightly against the slab with
low expansion relatively impervious soil and graded to divert water away from the slab to
reduce the potential for water ponding around the foundation and slab
A Geolabs representative should monitor subgrade preparation and footing
excavations prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the foundation
bearing conditions and the required embedment depth Proper subgrade preparation is
critical to the performance of the slab on grade foundation system
Site Preparation
In general the area within the contract grading limits should be cleared
thoroughly at the on set of earthwork Vegetation debris deleterious material and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly off site Soft and
yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas designated to receive fill or
future improvements should be over excavated to expose firm and or stiff material and
the resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted fill The excavated
soft and or organic soils should be disposed properly off site
After clearing the existing ground should be over excavated where necessary
to the bottom of the aggregate subbase layer Prior to placement of the 6 inch
aggregate subbase the over excavated subgrade should be scarified to a minimum
depth of 8inches properly moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content and recompacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density

If pumping conditions are encountered during compaction of the subgrade soils
the subgrade soils may be stabilized by cement treatment to reduce the potential for
pumping subgrade conditions As a guide one sack of cement may be used for every
50 square feet of subgrade area This preliminary mix should be adjusted in the field to
suit the exposed soil conditions Alternatively the subgrade may be proof rolled and a
woven geotextile fabric such as Mirafi 500X or equivalent should be placed on the
subgrade prior to placement of the aggregate subbase
Aggregate subbase required underneath the generator pad should conform to the
requirements stipulated in the Hawaii Standard Specifications for Road Bridge and Public
Works Construction 2005 Aggregate subbase should be moisture conditioned to above
the optimum moisture content placed in one level lift not exceeding 8 inches in loose
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Fill materials required for the project should consist of non expansive select
granular material such as crushed coral or basalt The material should be well graded
from coarse to fine with particles no larger than 3 inches in largest dimension In addition
the material should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200
sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a
maximum swell of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883 The
materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed
in 8 inch level loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Geolabs should test imported fill materials for conformance with these recommendations
prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use
Geolabs representative should monitor site grading operations to observe
whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation and scarification
process and whether the exposed soil conditions are similar to those encountered in our
field exploration
