extending to the maximum explored depth of 31 5 feet below the existing ground surface

slabs on grade may be used to support the new electrical substation structures and
equipment The existing subgrade soils below foundations should be scarified to a

the lowest adjacent exterior grade

moderate expansion potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations Slabs on grade

on site expansive soils we believe that non expansive structural fill material should be
provided for a minimum depth of 12 inches below the slab cushion

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Kauai is composed of a single basalt shield volcano built by the
extrusion of lavas of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series during the late Pliocene
Epoch more than million years before present Following the cessation of this
main shield building phase renewed volcanic activity occurred with the extrusion of
basaltic lavas of the post erosional Koloa Volcanic Series and the concurrent deposition
of alluvial sediments of the Palikea Formation
The majority of the Island of Kauai is covered by lavas of the Waimea Canyon
Volcanic Series These lavas consist of four distinct formations Napali Olokele
Haupu and Makaweli These formations are comprised of thin bedded a a and
pahoehoe flows to massive basalt flows that ponded in calderas and graben
Rocks of the Koloa Volcanic Series cover most of the eastern half of the Island of
Kauai These rocks are generally characterized as thick flows of dense basalt extruded
from groups of vents aligned in north south trends in various locales Associated with
the vents are pyroclastic materials which usually form low cinder cones at the vent
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age many sea level changes occurred as a
result of widespread continental glaciation As the great continental glaciers
accumulated the level of the ocean fell since there was less water available to fill the
oceanic basins Conversely as the glaciers receded or melted global sea levels rose
because more water was available The land mass of Kauai remained essentially stable
during these changes and the fluctuations were eustatic in nature These glacio eustatic
fluctuations resulted in stands of the sea that were both higher and lower relative to the
present sea level of Kauai
The higher sea level stands caused the accumulation of deltas and fans of
terrigenious sediments in the heads of the old bays accumulation of reef deposits at
correspondingly higher elevations and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters
protected by fringing reefs

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally underlain by
medium stiff to hard residual soil extending to the maximum depth explored of 31 5 feet
below the existing ground surface We did not encounter groundwater in the borings at
the time of the field exploration
Based on the subsoil conditions encountered at the project site and the relatively
light foundation loading anticipated we believe that shallow foundations consisting of
spread and or continuous footings and concrete slabs on grade bearing on the
recompacted in situ soils or new compacted fill materials may be used to support the
new electrical substation structures and equipment An allowable bearing pressure of up
to 3 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the design of footing foundations
bearing on the recompacted in situ soils or new compacted fills placed to achieve the
design finished grades The existing subgrade soils below foundations should be
scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches and recompacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction
Detailed discussions of our geotechnical engineering recommendations are
presented in the following sections herein

3 2 Slabs On Grade
New slabs on grade pad foundations are anticipated Based on our field
exploration the project site is generally underlain by near surface clayey soils with low
to moderate expansion potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations
Slabs on grade supported by the expansive soil should be designed properly to reduce
the potential for future distress to the structures resulting from wetting and subsequent
swelling of the clayey soils
To reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from swelling of the
on site expansive soils we believe that non expansive structural fill material should be
provided for a minimum depth of 12 inches below the slab cushion Prior to placement
of the structural fill we recommend scarifying the exposed subgrades after clearing
and grubbing to a minimum depth of 8 inches where possible moisture conditioned to
at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and properly re compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
The thickened edges of slabs should be embedded at least 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slab should be backfilled tightly

3 5 Utility Trench
New utility lines may be required In general for support of the utility lines we
recommend using granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel
ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation under the pipes The initial trench backfill up to about
1 foot above the pipes should consist of free draining backfills such as No 3B Fine
gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation to reduce the potential for pipe damage from
compaction of the backfill It is critical to use a free draining granular material to reduce
the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of pipes and to provide
adequate support for the sides of the pipes We do not recommend using the on site
clayey soils as initial trench backfill directly around the utility pipes
In general we recommend providing a minimum cover of 3 feet between the top
of the utility pipes and the finished grade to facilitate compaction of the trench backfill
The upper portion of the trench backfill from a level of 1 foot above the pipes to the
finished subgrade should consist of the approved on site soils or select granular
material The backfill material should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for
future ground subsidence Mechanical compaction equipment should be used to
compact the materials Water tamping jetting or ponding should not be used to
compact the backfill material considering the clayey nature of the on site soils

3 6 Drainage
The finished grades outside the foundations and slabs should be sloped to shed
water away from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Drainage swales
should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain surface run off away
from the slabs and foundations
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
foundation recommendations presented in this report since ponded water could cause
subsurface soil saturation and subsequent loss of strength and or heaving of the
near surface clayey soils The foundation excavations should be backfilled properly
against the footings immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce the potential for
water infiltration

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling two borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 each extending to a depth of about 31 5 feet below the
existing ground surface The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous
flight augers

examination in the field by our geologist who observed the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual observation
and testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 and A 2

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Two one inch Ring Swell tests were performed on relatively undisturbed samples to
evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils The tests results are summarized
on Plate B 1
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected samples
to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The test results
are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depth
Graphic representations of the test results are provided on Plate B 2
One Unconfined Compression test ASTM D 2166 was performed on a selected
in situ cohesive soil sample to evaluate the unconfined compressive strength of the
soils The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depth and graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 3
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio test ASTM D 1883 was performed on a
bulk sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics
of the soils The test results are presented on Plate B 4
