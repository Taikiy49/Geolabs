SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 extending to depths of about 11 5 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 3550 84 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a

sample depths

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration disclosed that the project site is generally underlain by medium
dense silty sands throughout the depths explored Based on the generally competent subsoil
conditions encountered at the project site we envision that slabs on grade with
thickened edge footings may be used to support the proposed new comfort station
anticipated for the subject project Based on the sandy soil conditions encountered in the
borings special attention should be given to the site preparation recommendations to
compact the subgrade prior to the construction of new improvements Detailed discussion
and our geotechnical engineering design recommendations are presented in the following
sections of this report
Slab On Grade Foundations
We envision that slab on grade foundations with thickened edge footings will likely be
used to support the proposed new comfort station anticipated for the subject project Based
on our field exploration an allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500 pounds per square foot
psf may be used for the design of shallow foundations bearing on the recompacted in situ
soils or select granular fill material This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may
be increased by one third for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces In general thickened edge footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent grade The thickened edges of concrete walkway slabs adjacent
to unpaved areas should be embedded at least 6 inches below the lowest adjacent grade
We recommend that the bottom of footings bear on the firm and unyielding
near surface soils The bottom of the footing excavations should be recompacted to at least
90 percent relative compaction to provide a relatively firm and unyielding bearing surface
prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete If soft and or loose materials are
encountered at the bottom of footing excavations they should be over excavated to expose
firm materials The over excavation should be backfilled with granular material compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the bottom of footing should be extended
down to the underlying competent material Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil
established in accordance with procedures described in ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of
the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary
to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to
reduce the potential for appreciable foundation settlement If foundations are designed and
constructed in accordance with our recommendations we estimate total settlements to be
less than 1 inch with differential settlements on the order of about 0 5 inches

Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted in situ soils or select
granular fill materials Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf This
assumes that the soil around the footings is well compacted or undisturbed Unless covered
by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be
neglected
In general we recommend that concrete slabs on grade be supported on a
minimum 4 inch layer of cushion fill The cushion fill should consist of open graded
gravels such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation for more uniform
support of the slab and to serve as a capillary break To reduce the potential for moisture
infiltration through the slab concrete a plastic vapor membrane should be provided below
the slab Two inches of sand may be considered above the plastic membrane to provide
for protection of the membrane and to aid in curing of the slab concrete Provision of the
2 inches of fine sand above the vapor barrier will be left up to the discretion of the project
structural engineer
The subgrade soils supporting the slabs on grade should be scarified to a depth of
about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The slab subgrades should be firm
and unyielding prior to the placement of the cushion fill Subgrade preparation and footing
excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs prior to placement of
reinforcing steel or concrete
Site Preparation
Based on our field observation we anticipate that minimal site grading work on the
order of 1 to 2 feet will be required for construction of the new comfort station at the subject
site In general the area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and
grubbed at the on set of earthwork Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of off site
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas
designated to receive fill should be over excavated to expose firm natural material and the
resulting excavation should be backfilled with compacted granular fill material The
excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site or used in
landscaping areas if appropriate
After clearing and grubbing areas designated to receive fills should be scarified to a
depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to
the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the
GEOLABS INC

same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
Slab subgrades should be kept moist and free of shrinkage cracks until covered by
slab cushion fill or concrete Where shrinkage cracks are noted after preparation of the
subgrade we recommend that the soil be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and
poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
compacted granular fill material
In general the on site sandy soils generated from the excavations may be used as
a source of fill materials At this time we do not anticipate that imported fill materials for
grading purposes will be required for this project However if imported fill materials will be
necessary the imported fill materials should consist of non expansive granular material such
as crushed coral basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from coarse to
fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in maximum dimension and should contain
between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve The material should have
a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should have a maximum swell of less than
1 percent when tested in accordance with ASTM D 1883
Imported fill materials should be tested by Geolabs for conformance with these
recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for the intended use Imported fill
materials should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in level lifts
not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent
relative compaction
Percolation Tests
We understand that a drainage structure may be constructed and installed at the
shower facilities adjacent to the proposed new comfort station Two percolation tests were
conducted in the borings at a depth of about 5 feet below the existing ground surface to
evaluate the permeability of the subsurface materials at the site The subsurface
percolation rates at the site were tested by the falling head method In general water was
added to the hole up to approximately the top of the casing The changes in water level
in the borings were then recorded with respect to time The results of the tests are
presented on Plates 7 and 8

Pavement Design
We anticipate that some new asphalt concrete paved access roads and or paths may
be constructed at the site In general we anticipate that the vehicle loading for this project
will consist primarily of passenger vehicles and light pick up trucks We have made our
preliminary pavement design assuming that the pavement subgrade will consist of the
recompacted on site granular materials Based on the above assumptions the following
pavement section may be used for preliminary budgeting purposes
Flexible Asphaltic Concrete Pavements
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 0 Inch Aggregate Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Moist Subgrade
The subgrade soils below the new pavement section should be scarified to a
minimum depth of 12 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction The aggregate base course
should consist of crushed basalt aggregates compacted to not less than 95 percent relative
compaction CBR and field density tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils
encountered during construction to confirm the adequacy of the above section
Paved areas should be sloped and drainage gradients should be maintained to carry
surface water off the site Surface water ponding should not be allowed on the site during
or after construction Where concrete curbs are used to isolate landscaping in or adjacent
to the pavement areas we recommend that the curbs be extended a minimum of 2 inches
into the subgrade soils below the aggregate base course to reduce the potential for migration
of landscape water into the pavement section Alternatively a subdrain system is
recommended to collect the excess water from landscaping irrigation For long term
performance we believe that a subdrain system should be considered adjacent to the
paved landscaped areas
Utility Trench
We envision that some new utility lines may be required for the proposed project In
general granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation is recommended under the pipes Free draining granular materials such as
No 3B Fine gravel should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches
above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction
effort of the backfill thus reducing the possibility of damaging the pipes It is critical to use
this free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches
of pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper trench backfill
could result in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes

The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to the
top of the subgrade or finished grade may consist of the excavated on site soils or select
granular fill material The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted
to not less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench
backfill below the pavement grade should be compacted to at least 95 percent relative
compaction
Drainage
The finished grades outside the new comfort station should be sloped to shed water
away from foundations and slabs to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised that
gutter systems be installed around the building and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and slab areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and
slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should also be avoided or have
concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and
subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled against the walls or slab edges immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce
the potential for water infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible
and should be maintained to drain surface water runoff away from the foundations and slabs

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs Inc be retained to provide geotechnical engineering
services during construction of the proposed comfort station project The items of
construction monitoring that are critical requiring Special Inspection include observation of
footing excavation and subgrade preparation Other aspects of earthwork construction
should also be observed by a representative from Geolabs This is to observe compliance

for design changes that may be required in the event that subsurface conditions differ from
those anticipated at the time this report was prepared The recommendations provided in
this report are contingent upon such observations If the actual exposed subsurface
conditions encountered during construction are different from those assumed or considered
in this report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between and
beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations provided in this report
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations on the logs of borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed and
interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
