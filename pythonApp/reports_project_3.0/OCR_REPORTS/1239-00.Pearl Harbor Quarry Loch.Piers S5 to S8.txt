INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a soils investigation
performed for the proposed wharf area repairs U S Navy Project No
P 088 at the Pearl Harbor Naval Submarine Base Pearl Harbor Oahu
Hawaii This project consists of two portions Project A located at
Quarry Loch and Project B located between Piers S5 and S8 The
approximate locations of the project sites are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that for Project A a new sheetpile wall
to retain fill is being considered Additional dredging in front of
the new sheetpile wall is not planned
GEOLABS HAWAII

For the Project B site the harbor bottom will be dredged to a
depth of 35 feet below Mean Lower Low Water or Elevation 65 U S Navy
Datum and to a line 35 feet in front of the quay wall between S5 and
S8 A line of new tieback sheetpile bulkhead will be installed about
10 feet behind the quay wall in this area The design also calls for
a uniform live load of 500 p s f over the wharf area

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The geologic conditions underlying both sites are the result of a
complex series of deposition and erosional events brought on by glacio
eustatic sea level changes and post erosional volcanic activities
during the Pleistocene Following the cessation of activity of the
Koolau Volcano alluvial materials were deposited in a deltaic or
estuarine environment in the area of Pearl Harbor
The sea level fluctuated several times receding perhaps 300 feet
below and rising about 95 feet above the present sea level
Concurrent with a rise in sea level during late Pleistocene era
eruption of the Salt Lake Makalapa member of the Honolulu Volcanic
Series mantled the older alluvium with thick deposits of volcanic ash
which consolidated to form volcanic tuff locally called mudrock
layers Subsequent erosion dissected and thinned those mantles of
tuff
GEOLABS HAWAII
During the Recent epoch as sea level rose to its current stand
the area was covered with soft and loose lagoonal marine deposits
Those deposits were subsequently covered by man made fill placed
during the middle portion of this century for land reclamation pro
jects during the construction of the new existing Naval Submarine Base
Quarry Loch Project A Site
The project site is probably on the southern bank of an ancient
burried stream that previously discharged into Qarry Loch
The subsurface conditions at the Quarry Loch was explored by
drilling 8 to 10 to depths varying from 30 to 70
feet below the existing ground surface
The site conditions along the proposed sheetpile wall alignment
consisted of steeply sloping unimproved shoreline The water depth
ranged from 2 to 4 feet along the sheetpile alignment
In general the subsurface conditions consisted of 2 to 4 feet of
stiff sandy silt fill and 2 to 4 feet of medium dense to dense silty
sand overlying 12 to 14 feet of dense moderately hard volcanic tuff

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
PROJECT A
General
It is our understanding that tieback sheetpiles to retain the
fill are planned for the shoreline improvement It is further
understood that the area fronting the new sheetpile will not be
deepened further except for minor maintenance dredging
Sheetpile Construction
Our borings indicate that the sheetpile site is underlain by 10
to 12 feet thick of moderately hard volcanic tuff at shallow water of
approximately 1 to 3 feet Sheetpile will have to penetrate some
distance into the tuff and driving of the sheetpile into the tuff
rock may be difficult We recommend that the sheetpile location be
pre excavated by a series of auger holes Sheetpiles may then be
installed through these holes driven and seated at the design depth
After the installation the annular space should be backfilled
with concrete grout to the top of the tuff on both the back and the
front sides of the sheetpile The remainder of the backfill should
consist of crushed rock such as No 3 Fine

Sheetpile Design Pressure
For the design of the sheetpiles we recommend that the design
pressure diagram presented on Plate 6 be used This assumes that the
sheetpiles are installed in predrilled holes and concrete grouted It
is also assumed that a granular backfill will be used behind the
sheetpile with a live load surcharge of 300 p s f during construction
It is our understanding that the top of the sheetpile will be at
Elevation 106 feet and tieback wale for sheetpile will be located at
about Elevation 102 feet For the tieback force design we recommend
that free earth support condition be used It is anticipated that the
required tieback force will be relatively small
Deadman Anchor Tieback System
For deadman anchor one of the restrictions at the site will be
the existing flammable fuel storage building which is located at about
18 feet from the sheetpile line on the southern portion of the project
site In addition there are several buried utility lines between the
new sheetpile system and this building To provide anchorage we
recommend installing drilled caisson deadman for the anchors The
caissons should be drilled to at least 15 feet below the existing
grade which currently is at about Elevation 108 feet For anchor
face located at 14 feet back from the sheetpile line only the portion
below Elevation 98 feet should be considered as effective for passive
resistance The net allowable passive pressure on the side of the
drilled shaft anchor should be assumed at 5000 p s f

The tie rods will be sloped at about H V where the area
is restricted due to the existing building Otherwise relatively
level tie rod may be used where the caisson deadman can be placed at
least 25 feet from the sheetpile In such a case the top of the
caisson deadman can be set at Elevation 102 feet The passive earth

Plate 7 presents the drilled caisson deadman anchors and tie rod
arrangments
The construction of these caisson deadman and the tie rod can be
relatively straight forward These may include drilling holes for
caissons trenching for precast tie rod member installation cleaning
out of the bottom of the caisson placing reinforcing steel in the
caisson holes and tremie concrete and backfilling both the trench and
the caisson It may be desirable to also tremie and backfill the tie
rod and then backfill the remainder with soil
The backfill over the tie rod and caisson deadman below water
should consist of clean crushed rock such as No 3 Fine The
backfill above water may consist of select borrow type material such
as coral cinder sand etc All backfill material should be examined
and approved by a soils engineer and should be compacted to a density
of at least 90 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum density

PROJECT B
Dredg ing
For the proposed dredging at Piers 5 to S8 an effort was made
to locate previous driving records for the quay wall piling between
Piers SS and S8 However we are unable to locate the as built pile
records and it is our belief that these old records may not likely be
available Therefore we were unable to fully evaluate the dredging
effect on the quay wall piling foundation
Judging from the pile driving records for the period from 1931
through 1933 for other piling work in the vicinity the quay wall
piles were probably of 16 inch square reinforced concrete piles and
most likely driven by a hammer with a rated energy of lbs
5000 1b ram with 3 feet stroke It is estimated that these piles
carry dead loads of up to about 20 tons on the front row and 35 tons
for the back row
On the Navy Drawing Nos Z N15 112 and 113 dated November 28
1932 only the estimated pile lengths for construction were shown
The estimated pile lengths indicated were all 40 feet except 35 feet
for the back row piles between Piers S5 and the now demolished old
Pier S6 Assuming that the subsurface conditions under the quay wall
were as depicted on Plate 5 we believe that previous piles were
probably driven to full penetration into the stiff to very stiff silt
and clay but did not reach the underlying volcanic tuff

Sheetpile Design Pressures
For the design of the bulkhead sheetpiles at the Project B site
we recommend that the pressure diagram presented on Plate 9 be used
These pressures include a uniform surcharge live load of 500 p s f on
the ground surface
As the existing quay wall appears to offer little lateral
Support its contribution is ignored in the design pressure diagram
For tieback force design we recommend that a free earth support
be considered For deeper sheetpile penetration say to about
Elevation 53 feet the bottom of sheetpile may be assumed to be
hinged at Elevation 56 feet and the passive earth pressure be assumed
to be zero 0 at that point assuming a triangular distribution with
maximum at 2000 p s f at Elevation 75 feet
The above design parameters are developed based on conditions at
Boring 1 where thick loose sand and gravel deposits were encountered
Conditions at Borings 3 and 4 are also analyzed and the results are
found to be less critical because the loose sand and gravel of the
lagoonal deposits are thinner The design pressure diagram may be
modified in accordance with the diagram shown on Plate 10 This
revised diagram only applies to the zone between Elevations 88 feet
and 75 feet to reflect a thinner lagoonal deposit between Piers S7
and S8

Sheetpile Bulkhead Construction
During our boring exploration numerous utilities were identified
These conditions will not only increase the cost of future sheetpile
and tieback anchor installations but will also limit the allowable
lateral bearing on deadman anchors
Based on our findings from the borings we believe that the new
sheetpiles can be driven through the alluvial soils and weak coral
under the lagoonal deposit We believe that the partly cemented tuff
fragments at Elevation 86 feet at Boring 2 and the tuff and beach
rock layer at Elevation 81 feet at Boring 4 could be driven through
with some effort Sheetpiles may be driven further into the volcanic
tuff layer at about Elevation 55 feet a depth of approximately 53
feet below the existing ground if so desired
Recommendations for sheetpile installation criteria should be
developed after the actual selections for sheetpile and driving hammer
are made
Deadman Anchor Tieback System
For sheetpile tieback at Project B site we recommend that
the buried deadman anchors be located a distance behind the
sheetpile and thread tie rods through the existing utilities

For deadman anchor design our recommended design pressure
diagram is shown on Plate 11 We believe that the face of the
deadman should be at least 35 feet away from the sheetpile
Generally this horizontal distance will place the passive earth
pressure wedge of the deadman anchor outside the active wedge
zone of the sheetpile
We recommend that the top of the deadman be embedded at

Other Considerations
Wave Protection
ee
After the dredging in front of the quay walls the loose
sand and gravel material underneath the quay walls may
erode and the new sheetpiles may be exposed Therefore
provisions should be made to protect the sheetpile against
wave action
Seal Under the Quay Wall and Sheetpiles
There will be a structural discontinuity between the
existing quay wall and the new sheetpile This may lead to
loss of fill through the widening of gaps or cracks We
believe that some protective measures such as filling the
discontinuity with lean concrete should be provided
Tying of the Quay Wall
Structurally the quay wall has little resistance to
horizontal force and or deformation because the lateral
resistance of piles are relatively small Some measures to
provide resistance to lateral forces in the outboard
direction may merit some consideration

Moisture content and unit weight determinations were
performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests
are presented on the boring logs at the appropriate sample
depths
To further identify soil characteristics additional
tests were performed These include 3 Atterberg limit tests
and 2 sieve analyses The resuits of these tests are presented
on Plate B 1
Two direct shear tests were performed on undisturbed
samples to evaluate the strength characteristics of near surface
granular fill materials on the sheetpile bulkhead site The
test results are presented on Plates B 2 and B 3
Five unconsolidated undrained UU triaxial compression
tests were performed on select undisturbed samples to evaluate
the soil strength characteristics of the soil Four of these
five triaxial compression tests were performed using a multi
stage procedure in which the next higher confining pressure was
applied after the sample approached the peak compressive strength
under the on going confining pressure One consolidated undrained
CU triaxial compression test with pore water measurement was
also performed The results of all triaxial compression tests
are summarized on Plate B 4 The plots of deviator stress and
pore water pressure vs strain for the CU test are shown on
Plate B 5
Nine unconfined compression tests including 3 compres
sion tests on NWX core samples were performed on slightly
disturbed and undisturbed samples to further evaluate the soil
strength characteristics The peak compressive strength values
for unconfined compression tests are summarized on Plate B 6
