In accordance with your request we have been providing field observations and
compaction testing services during the site grading and foundation excavations for the
above referenced project This letter is presented to provide clarification on the footing
subgrade preparation requirements for the subject project
As stated in the notes of the construction plans prepared for the subject project
where footing subgrades are located in cut areas footings bearing on the undisturbed
in situ soils the bottom of the footing excavations should be compacted to provide a
relatively firm and smooth bearing surface prior to placement of reinforcing steel or
concrete For footing subgrades in these conditions a visual observation and probing of
the footing subgrade should be performed by our field personnel to confirm the subgrade
conditions of the footings The footing subgrades should be firm and unyielding A
compaction test of the footing subgrade is not required for these conditions since the
subgrade exist in a relatively high moisture condition
For footings bearing on new engineered fills where the footing subgrades are
located in fill areas the bottom of footing excavations should be recompacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction prior to placement of reinforcing steel or
concrete In the fill conditions compaction tests should be performed in the footing
excavations to confirm whether that the compaction requirements of the subgrades are
met
It should be emphasized that if the footing subgrades are saturated or softened
after the visual observations probing or compaction testing due to inclement weather
the footing subgrades may need to be over excavated to expose firm ground and
