INTRUMENTATION MONITORING

Boring Nos 1 through 3 drilled in November 2001 as shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 to
depths of approximately 100 feet each in order to measure the lateral deflection of the soil
at various depths An additional inclinometer was installed in Boring No 4 drilled in
September 2003 as shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 to a depth of approximately 100 feet
This additional inclinometer was installed to better delineate the slide limits

It should be noted that the inclinometers recorded the lateral earth movement in
two directions perpendicular to Mona Street A Axis and parallel to Mona Street B Axis
as shown on Plates 2 through 5 2 Based on our review of the inclinometer data recorded
to date it appears that Inclinometer Nos I 1 3 and 4 indicated some shallow depth
subsurface ground movement at their respective instrument locations However
Inclinometer No 2 did not indicate a pattern that suggests significant subsurface ground
lateral movement is occurring at this time It should be recognized that one of the factors
that cause significant ground lateral movement is rainfall Based on our research it
appeared that Mona Street has not experienced significant rainfall in the short time during
our monitoring period

PAVEMENT DEFORMATION MAPPING
As mentioned in our previous reports ground surface observations at the project
site were recorded Approximately 1 to 2 inch wide pavement cracks on the surface of the
roadway were previously observed in the area between 1189 and 1199 Mona Street in
the period from May 2002 to September 2002 No significant continuous cracking
development was recorded in the period from September 2002 to January 2003
Pavement re surfacing was performed in localized areas of Mona Street during the
period from January 2003 to August 2003 Therefore a majority of the cracks previously
recorded was patched by asphaltic concrete No significant continuous cracking
development was recorded in the period from January 2004 to August 2004
Our pavement deformation mapping was resumed in the Year 2005 During the
resumed monitoring period majority of the cracks recorded prior to the pavement
re surfacing was again observed on the re surfaced pavement Ground surface

SUMMARY
Inclinometer Nos I 1 I 3 and I 4 indicated some shallow depth subsurface ground
movement at their respective instrument locations However Inclinometer No 2 did not
indicate a pattern that suggests significant subsurface ground lateral movement is
occurring at this time It may be too early to conclude based on the limited monitoring
period and locations Our piezometer data indicates that subsurface water seepage
occurred at the project site Based on our pavement deformation mapping we believe
that lateral earth movement at the site is still active
Based on the interpretation from Inclinometer Nos I 1 and I 3 it appears that the
sliding Zone may be relatively shallow approximately 11 to 15 feet below the ground
surface in the area near Boring Nos 1 and 3 Inclinometer No I 4 is located between
Inclinometer Nos 2 and I 3 It appears that the sliding zone near Boring No 4 may be
located slightly deeper than Inclinometer Nos I 1 and I 3 at a depth of about 17 feet
below the ground surface
It may be too early to conclude based on the limited monitoring period and
locations We will continue monitoring the lateral ground movement through the installed
instrumentations and mapping the pavement conditions
