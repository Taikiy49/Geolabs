INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our soils engineering
investigation conducted for the proposed construction of a
new station armory at Kaneohe Marine Corps Air Station
Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site
and its vicinity is shown on Plate 1 Project Location Map
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the subsurface investigation was to explore
the soil conditions at the site to provide data for use in
the design of the foundations of the proposed structure
The scope of our investigation included field reconnais
sance the drilling of three exploratory borings and
laboratory testing of selected soil samples
Results of our field exploration and laboratory tests
which were used in the engineering analysis that
formulated our conclusions and recommendations are
presented in the appendices of this report
GEOLABS HAWAII
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed station armory will be a new one story structure
about 20 x 35 feet in plan dimension
The planned armory will have concrete walls slab on grade and
a roof consisting of concrete girders with prestressed planks
Anticipated structural loading are 2 2 kips per lineal foot for
walls and 77 kips per column which includes dead plus live
loads
A concrete access driveway will also be constructed having an
approximate plan dimension of 13 5 x 25 feet
A review of the preliminary site grading plans indicate small
cuts and fills up to one half foot
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located within the Kaneohe Marine Corps Air
Station in Kaneohe Oahu Hawaii The proposed structure will
occupy the southwest corner at the intersection of C Street
and Third Street
The site is rectangular in shape and has a plan dimension of
about 95 x 145 feet The site is bordered by Third Street on

parking area to the south and the transportation services base

Ground surface elevations range from 109 to 112 feet U S Navy

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
GEOLOGY
The project site is located on the Mokapu Peninsula of the
Island of Oahu The foundation of this Peninsula was deposited
by six vents of the Honolulu Volcanic Series during the Kahipa
low stand of the sea 300 feet These vents laid down
extensive deposits of pyroclastic materials in the form of ash
and cinder with some associated lava flows The ash deposits
have consolidated to form a volcanic tuff locally known as
mud rock
Since the deposition of these materials during Kahipa time sea
level has risen and fallen several times as a result of
glacio eustatic fluctuations These changes in sea level have
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 5
caused a reworking of portions of the pyroclastic rocks and
deposition of coral algal materials over portions of the
Peninsula
The tuff encountered at the project site appears to be water
laid having a shale like structure and evidences of ripple
bedding It appears that this tuff was composed of pyro
clastic materials that were transported to a tidal flat
environment prior to their consolidation Thin layers of
indurated alluvial clays were found to be interbeded with the
pyroclastic materials
Overlying this tuff material are deposits of silty sands which
appear to be littoral deposits that were laid down as sea level
rose to an elevation higher than its present stand
During the past century the efforts of man have shaped the
site to its present form by the placement of fill materials
over the older littoral deposits

SOIL CONDITIONS
The three borings encountered a thin surface layer about 1 5
to 2 0 feet thick of stiff brown clayey silt over medium dense
to dense silty sand with gravel to depths of 17 5 to 24 5
feet Dense volcanic tuff was encountered below the Silty sand
and gravel to about 25 feet the depths of the borings
Ground water was encountered in all three borings ranging from
10 2 to 11 5 feet below the existing ground surface

generally exhibit a low swelling potential if maintained at or
above the optimum moisture content

SITE GRADING
A review of the preliminary site grading plan indicates that
shallow cuts and fills up to one half foot are planned
Site Preparation
Prior to grading all deleterious materials such as vegeta
tion debris and unsuitable fill should be removed and disposed
of properly off site
Prior to filling the existing ground below the proposed
structure should be scarified to a depth of 6 inches moisture
conditioned to near optimum moisture content and then compacted
according to the requirements for structural fill
Structural Fill Material and Placement
_ a ee me a a cee
Based on laboratory results and field exploration the on site
soils are generally suitable for use in structural fill
The soils should be compacted near the optimum moisture content
to minimize swelling potential
GEOLABS HAWAII
W P 8
If required imported fill material should be free of organic
material non expansive and generally less than 6 inches in
Size
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 6
inches in compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 95
of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 70
Site grading should provide drainage of surface runoff away
from all foundations
BUILDING FOUNDATIONS
Spread or continuous footing foundations can be used to support
the proposed structure
Footings should be placed a minimum of 24 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade and should bear on the dense silty sand
layer
An allowable bearing value of 3500 psf may be used for founda
tions on the dense silty sand The bearing capacity value is
for total dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third for short duration wind and or seismic loading
Minimum footing width of 18 inches for spread footings and 12
inches for continuous footings are recommended
GEOLABS HAWAII
The bottom of all footings should be compacted to a minimum of
95 of its maximum dry density as established by ASTM
D 1557 70 and inspected by the soils engineer prior to
concrete placement
If a footing is situated next to a utility trench the footing
should be extended to the bottom of the trench or the trench
backfill must be well compacted to prevent footing settlement
due to settlement of the trench backfill
Estimated settlements are anticipated to be on the order of
less than one half inch total and one quarter inch differential
SLAB ON GRADE
Generally the on site soils have a low swell potential
therefore slab on grade construction may be utilized
For the anticipated static floor leads a 4 inch slab over 4
inches of well graded aggregate base course is recommended
A plastic vapor barrier over 4 inches of 3 fine gravel may be
substituted for the base course should a capillary break be
deemed necessary to protect the floor covering A thin layer
of sand should be placed over the plastic barrier to aid in the
concrete curing and minimize shrinkage cracks
GEOLABS HAWAII
The subgrade soils should be kept moist prior to concrete
placement
DRIVEWAY
The access driveway is anticipated to have some heavier traffic
loading therefore a 6 inch concrete slab over 4 inches of
compacted aggregate base course is recommended
DESIGN REVIEW
We recommend that plans and specifications for grading and foun
dation construction be forwarded to the soils engineer for
review and written comments prior to construction to see that
the intent of our recommendations is included
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the filling footing excavation and back
filling operations be observed by a soils engineer during construc
tion This is to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and
the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
subsurface investigation The recommendations given in this report
are contingent upon the above inspections

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of
all other warranties either expressed or implied The analysis and
recommendations submitted in this report are based on our site
reconnaissance soil information from boring data and laboratory
tests obtained from this investigation
Although the subsurface conditions interpreted from the borings are
generally correct at the boring locations some variation may be
encountered during construction

fully determined by reconnaissance and soil borings Unforeseen

constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended to

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 0 to 25 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 Truck mounted drilling equipment was used to drill
the borings Two hand dug surface samples to depths of 2 0
to 2 5 feet below the existing ground were obtained at the
locations also shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The material encountered in the borings were classified
by visual and textural examination in the field by our
engineering geologist who continously monitored the drilling
operations The classifications were further reviewed by
visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1l
through A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving a
standard penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel
sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling at a height of
30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12
inches are shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Rock core samples were obtained by using a rotary wash
NXM series core barrel with a diamond bit The core recovery
and rock quality designation RQD are presented on the Logs of
Borings

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were
performed on selected samples to aid in the classification and
evaluation of soil properties The results of these tests are
presented on the log of borings at the appropriate sample
depths
A laboratory CBR test was performed on a bulk sample of
the near surface material obtained from the proposed subgrade
elevation to evaluate its swelling potential and subgrade
Support capacity The test result is summarized on Plate B l
One ASTM D 1557 test modified proctor was performed on
a bulk sample of the near surface existing fill material to
evaluate its compaction characteristics The test result is
summarized on Plate B l
Three sieve analyses were performed to determine the
gradation characteristics of the site materials The test
results are summarized on Plate B 1l
Two direct shear tests were performed on both undisturbed
and remolded samples to evaluate the strength characteristics
of the natural soil The test results are presented on Plates
B 2 and B 4
One one inch ring swell test was performed on an undis
turbed sample to evaluate the swelling potential of the exist
ing surface soil The test result is summarized on Plate B S
One triaxial shear test was performed to evaluate
Young s modulus for settlement calculations for the given
design loads The test result is summarized on Plate B 6
