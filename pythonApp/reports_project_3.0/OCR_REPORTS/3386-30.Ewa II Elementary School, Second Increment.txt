newly constructed Ewa Gentry Elementary School adjacent to the Summer Hill

MAY
The proposed site had previously been filled by about 4 to 5 feet during mass
grading operations for the first increment of the school by Tower Construction Inc TCI
However based on our records the filling operation at the Building E site was
performed without our field observations and testing for compaction Therefore prior to
the start of the second increment field density tests were performed on the existing fills
placed to evaluate the adequacy of the compaction of the fill material below the Building
E pad Based on the results of our field density tests the degree of compaction of the fill
material placed was much lower than the minimum requirement specified in the project
specifications Based on this TCI the Contractor for the first increment of the project was
called back for remedial measures
The existing fill placed at Building E during the mass grading operation for the
first increment was removed to expose the original ground by TCl The excavated
material was stockpiled and reused as backfill material at the site Following the removal
of the fill the materials were placed in level lifts of less than 8 inches in loose thickness
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1557 The fills within the upper 2 feet below the finish subgrade
in the Building E pad was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
A 6 inch layer of non expansive select fill consisting of well graded basalt sandy material
was placed below the bottom of the slab cushion fill The building pad was brought to the
elevations specified on the grading plans by TCI The Contractor for the second
increment Okada Trucking Company continued earthwork operation and_ utility
construction
A total of 56 field density tests including retests of the failed areas were
performed using the nuclear probe method in general accordance with ASTM Test
Designation D 2922 and D 3017 The results of the compaction tests taken during site
grading are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 3 of this report These tests were taken
on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed engineered fills and backfills at the
site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from
on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate
only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory
were reworked until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations during the site grading
observed and tested by Geolabs Hawaii in the period covered by this report were
performed in general accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications
and our geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the above referenced
report dated

LIMITATIONS
it should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork was
performed without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical
risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and
statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork
construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with generally
accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice No warranty is expressed or
implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it
guarantee construction of any type The Contractor shall retain responsibility for the
quality of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing defects
regardless of when they are found
