Dear Mr Kondo
This letter summarizes our preliminary findings regarding the observed distresses to
the family housing units based on the information obtained to date
Our study consisted of a review of the construction drawings geotechnical reports and
available correspondence related to the project Borings have been drilled at selected
locations and laboratory soil testing is in progress Visual observations of several typical
units have also been made to observe the reported damage
It appears that the affected units are generally limited to the three story structures that
have a garage and retaining wall at the first level and two levels of living area above The
typical grading at these units consisted of cuts at the garage level and up to about 6 feet of fill
under the rear half of the units
Three borings were drilled at various levels inside Unit 109A to obtain samples of the
soils underlying the concrete slab Borings were drilled in the garage kitchen and patio slab
This unit has experienced noticeable movements resulting in cracks in the garage retaining
wall and various portions of the unit Borings were also drilled outside the patios next to
Unit 113 A which has not experienced significant movement and Unit 65 A which has
experienced movement but is situated away from Unit 109A
Summary of Preliminary Findings
Level measurements inside the units disclosed that the concrete slab extending from
the top of the retaining wall to the rear patio is not level The rear portion of the slab adjacent
to the patio is generally higher than the opposite end of the slab at the retaining wall The
measured difference in elevation ranged from about 1 5 to 3 5 inches in the units where
damage was visible In Unit 113 A which has not experienced damage the elevation
difference was about 0 75 inches The level measurements and observations indicate that the
rear portions of the concrete slabs are lifting up

Dear Mr Kondo
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering borings and
laboratory tests performed at specific locations within the Family Housing Project site
located in the Marine Corps Base Hawaii in Kaneohe on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The subject family housing project was constructed under a design build contract
The grading work commenced in early 1997 with the turn over of the first group of houses
to the Navy in August 1998 Subsequent to the completion of the units apparent signs of
distress were noted in some of the units The signs of distress noted were cracks in the
garage retaining wall uplifting of slabs some cracking of drywall and sticking of doors in
the units
Supplementary forensic geotechnical and hydrological studies have been
performed by the design build contractor to evaluate possible cause s and remedial
measures pertaining to the distressed units
OBSERVATIONS
Most of the damage reported at the project site appeared to be confined to the
three story units located on the uphill lots These units consist of a two car garage and
entryway at the lowest level with a concrete retaining wall supporting a cut into the slope
at the interior end of the garage About one half of the second level living room has a
wood floor supported by the garage walls with the remainder kitchen dining supported on
a concrete slab on grade extending from the top of the retaining wall to the rear of the unit

The third level consists of a suspended wood floor above the second level It is our
understanding that the concrete slabs at the lanai and second level were constructed as
a single post tensioned concrete slab with 12 inch deep perimeter footings The garage
slab was also constructed as a post tensioned slab
The ground surface at these uphill lots generally have an elevation difference of
about 8 to 10 feet with a slope gradient of about three to four horizontal to one vertical
3 to 4H 1V Generally the site grading consisted of shallow cuts at the garage level and
fills up to about 6 feet to raise the ground up to the second floor level A generalized
section through the units illustrating the grading concept is shown on Plate 3
Each building consists of duplex units generally a mirror image of each other Each
building is located about 10 to 15 feet apart
Observations were first made in Unit 109A which was left vacant due to the extent
of the cracks in the concrete and drywalls The signs of distress noted are summarized
below
A horizontal crack in the garage retaining wall about one eighth of an inch
wide near the mid height of the wall
Cracks in the drywall particularly in the area between the garage retaining
wall and the rear wall of the units on both the second and third levels
The rear edge of the concrete slab in the family room was measured to be
3 5 inches above the opposite end at the retaining wall
e A horizontal separation between the rear lanai and adjacent sidewalk up to
about 1 inch
Subsequently observations were made inside Units 108A 108B
and 113A Most of the units had similar signs of movements as Unit 109A raised rear
edge of concrete slab and measured floor elevation differences of about 1 75 to
3 25 inches between the interior and rear edges of the second level concrete floor
Unit 113A had minimal signs of movement and a lower elevation difference of 0 75 inches
Visual observations and limited level surveys inside the units indicate that the rear
portions of the buildings have lifted up relative to the remainder of the units causing
distress in the second and third levels due to the differential movements
FIELD EXPLORATION
Following a visit to Unit 109A three borings were drilled inside the building to
evaluate the soil conditions under the concrete slabs Borings were drilled in the garage
kitchen and patio areas The borings were drilled with portable drilling equipment utilizing
continuous flight augers A pacometer was used to locate the post tensioning cables in the
slab prior to drilling

Boring No 1 was drilled through the garage slab and encountered stiff residual soil
consisting of mottled brown silty clay to 10 8 feet the depth of the boring
Boring No 2 was drilled through the patio slab and encountered about 5 5 feet of
medium stiff clay with basalt gravel fill over stiff residual soil to 9 8 feet the depth of the
boring
Boring No 3 was drilled in the kitchen area behind the retaining wall to determine
the condition of the granular backfill The boring encountered medium dense to dense
well graded silty gravel to about 8 feet the depth of the boring
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings during the exploration
Subsequent to the results of the first three borings and observations inside other
units Boring No 4 was drilled adjacent to Unit 65A which exhibited similar distress as
Unit 109A but was located in another part of the project Also Boring No 5 was drilled
adjacent to Unit 113A which did not exhibit significant distress
Boring No 6 was drilled adjacent to the drain manhole at the cul de sac for Kaawa
Drive The ground and sidewalk in this area has settled causing cracks in the sidewalk
The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The
borings were drilled using a portable drilling machine with continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our field engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by testing in
the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System Descriptions of the materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings
Plates A 1 through A 6
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches A tripod assembly was utilized to support
the 140 pound hammer The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third
6 inches of an 18 to 24 inch drive are shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample
depths
LABORATORY TESTING
Selected soil samples retrieved from the borings were tested for moisture content
density plasticity grain size analysis and swell potential

Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight ASTM D 2937 determinations were
performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Three Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected samples of
the soils to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits The test results are presented in tabular and
graphic form on Plate B 1
Two Sieve Analysis tests ASTM C136 and D 1140 were performed on selected
sampies to evaluate the grain size distribution of the soils The test results are plotted on
Plate B 2
Four one inch ring swell tests were performed on undisturbed and remolded samples
to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils under surcharge pressures These tests were
run on air dried samples Results of these swell tests are presented on Plate B 3
FINDINGS
Pertinent findings from the field exploration laboratory testing and review of
available construction information are summarized below
The soils encountered directly under the concrete slab and cushion BTB layer
can generally be considered moderately to highly expansive Laboratory
swell tests on natural and remolded samples ranged from about 5 to
13 percent
A minimum 24 inch thick capping layer of non expansive granular soil was
recommended below the foundations to reduce the expansive effects of the
soils The capping layer was not present in the borings drilled inside the
garage and patio of Unit 109A
e Boring No 3 which was drilled inside Unit 109A confirmed the presence of
granular backfill behind the garage retaining wall The samples from this
boring indicated that the backfill was generally dense but not over
compacted
Boring No 6 indicated that the backfill around the drain manhole at the end
of Kaawa Drive is loose
