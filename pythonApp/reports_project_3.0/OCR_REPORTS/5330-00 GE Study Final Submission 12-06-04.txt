1 1 Introduction
This letter report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering study
performed for the Waimano Home Road Moanalua Road Kuala Street Intersection
Improvements project in Pearl City on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location
and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and _ geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field reconnaissance desk top study and
engineering analyses The recommendations presented herein are intended for the
design of pavements fence wall foundations and site preparation only The findings
and recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of
this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is located at the intersection of Waimano Home Road Moanalua
Road and Kuala Road in Pearl City on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project consists
of improvements at the above intersection and includes the widening of Moanalua Road
on the east side of the intersection A new traffic lane will be added in the westbound
direction and additional right of way will be acquired for the traffic lanes in the
eastbound direction as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Approximately 180 linear feet
of the road will be widened and will require the relocation of approximately
100 linear feet of Concrete Rubble Masonry CRM wall
Based on available plans the existing asphalt concrete pavement at the
intersection of Moanalua Road Waimano Home Road and Kuala Street and on
Moanalua Road east of the above intersection consists of 2 5 inches of asphalt concrete
underlain by 8 inches of aggregate base course followed by 6 inches of aggregate
subbase material In addition the existing asphalt concrete pavement on Kuala Street
west of the above intersection consists of 3 5 inches of asphalt concrete underlain by
8 inches of glassphalt followed by 6 inches of untreated permeable base followed by
6 inches of aggregate subbase material

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island The
younger shield Koolau is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age
and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island Waianae became extinct
while Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially buried below Koolau
lavas banking against its eastern flank Both Waianae and Koolau Ranges have lost
most of their original shield outlines and are now long narrow ridges shaped largely by
erosion
The project area is generally composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of
lavas of the Koolau Volcanic Series These rocks are generally characterized by flows
of jointed dense vesicular basalt with interbedded thin clinker layers In situ chemical
weathering of the Koolau lava flows has occurred during the last 1 to 2 million years
The weathering process has formed a mantle of residual soils and saprolite In general
saprolite is composed mainly of silty material and is typical of the tropical weathering of
volcanic rocks The saprolite grades to basatic rock formation with depth

The project site is located in Pearl City on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The

Moanalua Road and Kuala Road

noted Recent asphalt surfacing of the far right lanes on Waimano Home Road in the

roadway widening The flexible pavement sections presented in this report were
generally determined based on the State of Hawaii Department of Transportation
Pavement Design Manual dated March 2002 The pavement design methodology is
based on the Hveem Stabilometer method developed and used by the California
Department of Transportation Caltrans
Design traffic parameters were developed based on recent traffic counts
performed by the City and County of Honolulu The traffic counts were taken on the far
right lane on Moanalua Road in the eastbound direction near the intersection with
Waimano Home Road The design traffic parameters used in our pavement analyses
are presented on Plate A 1 in Appendix A An annual growth rate of 1 percent was
used in the analysis

3 2 Concrete Fence Wall Foundations
Based on the generally competent subsurface conditions anticipated at the site
we believe that shallow footings may be used for support of the proposed concrete
fence wall An allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 pounds per square foot psf
may be used for the design of foundations bearing on the re compacted on site soils or
new compacted fills This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements
the foundations should be embedded below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or should be extended to a depth as
deep as the inverts of the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid
surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads and to
reduce the potential for appreciable foundation settlement

SECTION 3 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
As previously mentioned the bottom of the footing excavations should be
re compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction prior to the placement of
reinforcing steel or concrete Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of
soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established
in accordance with ASTM D 1557 test procedure Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density Where
shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the footing subgrade we recommend
that the soils be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks or prepared again Saturation
and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor
drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted fill at no additional cost to the owner
If soft and or loose materials are encountered at the bottom of the footing
excavations the soft and or loose materials should be over excavated to expose the
underlying firm materials The over excavation should be backfilled with select granular
fill materials compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction or the footing
may be extended deeper to bear directly on the underlying competent material
If foundations are designed and constructed in accordance with our
recommendations we estimate the total footing settlements under the anticipated
design loads to be less than 1 inch Differential settlements between adjacent footings
supported on the re compacted on site soils or new compacted fills should be on the
order of about 0 5 inches or less
Lateral loads acting on the fence structure may be resisted by frictional
resistance developed between the bottom of the fence foundation and the bearing soil
and by passive earth pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation
system A coefficient of friction of 0 35 may be used for footings bearing on the
re compacted on site soils or new compacted fills Resistance due to passive earth
pressure may be estimated using an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square
foot per foot of depth pcf This assumes that the soils around the footings are well

3 3 Site Preparation
Based on the surface conditions at the site we envision that minimal site grading
will be required for the proposed project construction In general we recommend that
the following guidelines be used for the minor site grading anticipated At the on set of
earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be thoroughly cleared
Vegetation debris deleterious materials and other unsuitable materials including the
lawn grass within the grading limits should be removed and disposed of properly
off site
Soft loose and yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas
designated to receive fill or future improvements should be over excavated to expose
firm or dense material and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
well compacted fill The excavated soft loose and or organic soils should be properly
disposed of off site Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit
prices for over excavation and compacted fill placement to account for variation in the
over excavation quantities
In general existing utilities that are to be abandoned within the limits of the
proposed improvements should be removed The resulting excavation should be
properly backfilled with on site fill materials moisture conditioned to above the optimum
moisture content placed in 6 to 8 inch loose lifts and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction
In areas to receive fills the subgrade soils should be scarified to a depth of about
8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted
to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The subgrades for areas subjected to
