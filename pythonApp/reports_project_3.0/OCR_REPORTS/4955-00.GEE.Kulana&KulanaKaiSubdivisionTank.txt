SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Kulana and Kulana Kai Subdivision Tank in the Kapaa area on
the Island of Kauai Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses for the proposed water tank project These recommendations are intended for
the design of foundations site grading and pavements only The findings and
recommendations presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this
report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project site is located along Hauiki Road in the Kapaa area on the Island of
Kauai Hawaii Based on the information provided we understand that it is proposed to
construct a new 0 25 Million Gallon MG potable water tank The tank will be located on
the west side of the project site
We understand that the new water tank will be a reinforced concrete
structure having a diameter of approximately 60 feet The bottom of tank elevation will be
set at 299 84 feet Mean Sea Level MSL and the spillway elevation will be set at
312 84 feet MSL An access and a service perimeter road are also planned for the water
tank project
Based on the available grading plans we understand that the new tank will be
situated in a cut condition We anticipate cuts on the order of about 1 to 4 feet will be
required to achieve the design grades

2 1 Regional Geology
The Island of Kauai is composed of a single basalt shield volcano built by the
extrusion of lavas of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series during the late Pliocene Epoch
more than 2 million years before present Following the cessation of this main shield
building phase there was renewed volcanic activity with the extrusion of basaltic lavas of
the post erosional Koloa Volcanic Series The project site is located on the eastern side of
the Island of Kauai
Rocks of the Koloa Volcanic Series are generally characterized as thick flows of
dense basalt extruded from groups of vents aligned in north south trends in various
locales Associated with the vents are pyroclastic materials which usually form low cinder
cones at the vent Rocks of the Koloa Volcanic Series cover most of the eastern half of
the Island of Kauai
This in situ weathering of the lava has formed a mantle of residual soils that grade
to saprolite with depth In general saprolite is composed mainly of silty material and is
typical of the tropical weathering of volcanic rocks The saprolite grades to basaltic rock
formation with depth

SECTION 3 0 DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our field exploration at the project site generally encountered stiff to hard silty clay
underlain by stiff to very stiff clayey silt extending to the maximum depths explored of
about 51 5 feet below the existing ground surface Groundwater was not encountered in
the borings during the time of our exploration
Based on the relatively stiff soil conditions encountered at the project site we
recommend that a shallow foundation system be used to support the water tank The soils
encountered at the bottom of footing elevation should be over excavated a minimum of
12 inches and replaced with select borrow compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction
Based on the laboratory test results the surface soils exhibit a low to moderate
potential for shrinking and swelling when subjected to moisture fluctuations Therefore to
reduce the potential for future distress to the slabs on grade we recommend that the slab
subgrade soils should be capped with a minimum of 12 inches of compacted select
borrow This will also provide more uniform support for the tank slab The slab subgrade
should be kept moist and free of shrinkage cracks prior to placement of select borrow and
slab concrete
Our geotechnical engineering recommendations pertaining to the design of
foundations site grading and pavements are further discussed in the following sections of
this report

3 2 Slab On Grade
As previously mentioned the project site is underlain by stiff to very stiff clayey silts
Based on our laboratory tests the surface silty clay soils are low to moderately expansive
when subjected to moisture fluctuations Therefore to reduce the potential for distress to
the tank floor due to shrinking and swelling of the surface soils we recommend that the
slab subgrade be over excavated a minimum of 12 inches The resulting over excavation
should be backfilled with the select borrow compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction
The select borrow should consist of crushed basalt aggregate For the design of
structural slabs a modulus of subgrade reaction of 200 pounds per square inch per inch of
deflection pci may be used for the compacted select borrow The area adjacent to the
water tank floor slab should be backfilled tightly against the slab with compacted on site
soils and graded to divert water away from the slab to reduce the potential for water
ponding around the slab and foundation
3 3 Site Grading
Based on the existing topography and the design finish floor elevation of the new
water tank we anticipate that the site grading work will consist of cuts and fills on the order
of about 1 to 4 feet to achieve the design finished grades
At the on set of earthwork the areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and yielding areas
encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas designated to receive fill should be
over excavated to expose firm natural material and the resulting excavation should be
backfilled with well compacted fill The excavated soft and or organic soils should be
properly disposed of off site or used in landscaping areas if appropriate
After clearing and grubbing areas beneath future tank floor and footings should be
over excavated where necessary to provide the minimum 12 inch thick compacted select

3 7 Drainage
The finished grades outside the water tank structure should be sloped to shed
water away from foundations and slabs and to reduce the potential for ponding Excessive
landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should also be _ avoided
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be
properly backfilled against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce water infiltration In addition drainage swales should be provided as
soon as possible and should be maintained to drain all surface run off away from slabs
and foundations

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content ASTM D 2216 and unit weight determinations ASTM D 2937
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Logs of Borings at the
appropriate sample depths
One one inch ring swell test was performed on a selected soil sample to evaluate
the swelling potential of the in situ soils The results of the test are summarized on
Plate B 1
One Atterberg Limits test ASTM D 4318 was performed on a selected soil sample
to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The test results are
presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths Graphic
representations of the test results are provided on Plate B 2
One direct shear test ASTM D 3080 was performed on a selected sample to
evaluate the shear strength characteristics of the materials tested Results of the direct
shear test are presented on Plate B 3
One California Bearing Ratio CBR test ASTM D 1883 was performed on a bulk
sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the strength characteristics for pavement
subgrade support Results of the CBR test are presented on Plate B 4
