SITE DESCRIPTION
The three disposal sites A B and C consist of relatively
steep sided depressions which were previously used as open
reservoir areas It appears that these areas were natural
depressions or drainageways which were bermed at the lower
end to create a reservoir
The sites are situated on the eastern Side of Makaha Valley
Road about 1 mile north of Farrington Highway Each of
the three sites are about 600 to 800 feet long and from 100
to 300 feet wide The depressions are from about 15 to
25 feet deep
Most of the sites are covered with grass and trees with
some surface water ponding at the bottom of the site
Rubbish and debris was encountered along the slope of Dis
posal Site A

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the field exploration it appears that the pro
posed disposal sites are underlain at shallow depth by
a dense boulder colluvium deposit similar to what was
encountered below the proposed Maunaolu Subdivision far
ther into the valley
It appears that the upper 2 to 4 feet of soil below the
disposal sites could be excavated and re used as a source
of borrow fill material for the subdivision development
However the boulder content of the deposit increases with
depth and exceeds about 50 below the upper 2 to 4 foot
layer Therefore it does not appear to be economical or
practical to excavate beyond this depth as additional large
amounts of boulders would be generated and would require
further disposal
The existing vegetation and debris which would rot with
time should be removed prior to borrow excavation site

A two 2 foot layer of Adobe clay should then be

moistened to above optimum moisture content and
placed in 1 foot lifts compacted with a large sheeps
foot roller Several passes of the sheeps foot
roller are required to attempt to knead the Adobe
into the upper boulder voids and create an impervious
moisture barrier to prevent later moisture seepage
