EARTHWORK OPERATION
The project site is located within the existing Villages of Kapolei development in the
District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is generally bounded by the
Kapolei Intermediate School to the north the future Makai Village 8 to the west and
Kapolei Lower Channel to the east and south as shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
The project site has been previously mass graded as part of the Villages of
Kapolei mass grading work by Oahu Construction Company Ltd The current site
grading work and infrastructure installation were performed by Albert C Kobayashi Inc
The earthwork operation consisted of clearing of the existing ground over excavation of
the surface expansive clays preparation of slab and pavement subgrades placement and
compaction of non expansive capping fill select borrow and base course and utility
trench excavation and backfill

Following the clearing operation the existing ground was generally over excavated
where necessary to approximately 12 inches below the building slab cushion to provide
the recommended 12 inches of non expansive select granular fill material capping fill
For the garage and driveway slabs the existing ground was over excavated where
necessary to provide 6 inches of select borrow over 6 inches of non expansive select
granular fill material In addition the capping fill also extended beyond the perimeter of the
house and garage slabs by a minimum of 2 feet In general the over excavated
subgrades were then moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum
moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent of the maximum dry density
During placement of the non expansive select granular fill layer the fill materials were
generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density The maximum dry
density of the materials referred to in this report were determined in accordance with
American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor
The majority of the sidewalk slabs were generally underlain by 6 inches of select
borrow subbase material as required in the plans Where expansive clayey soils were
exposed at the subgrade level the select borrow subbase material was increased to at
least 12 inches The over excavated subgrades at the sidewalk areas were
moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The select borrow subbase at
the sidewalk areas were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction
Laboratory CBR tests were performed on the exposed subgrade materials at
selected locations along the roadways to confirm the design pavement sections Based
on the test results the roadways were generally over excavated where necessary to
approximately 6 to 12 inches below the base course layer for placement of the select
borrow subbase material in accordance with the plans The over excavated subgrades
at the pavement areas were moisture conditioned to about 2 percent above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 95 percent relative compaction
The select borrow subbase and base course at the pavement areas were generally
placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
New sewer water and drain lines as well as catch basins and manholes were
installed to serve the new residential development In general a bedding layer
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel was provided below the piping and
manholes for uniform support Free draining granular materials consisting of No 3B
Fine gravel was also used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the pipes
to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort on
the initial trench backfill The trench backfill was placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and

During the earthwork operation samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor
and the test results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to
confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions
were noted A total of 1 978 field density tests including retests of failed areas were
performed using the Nuclear Probe Method in accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 2922 The results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1
through 3 100 of this report These tests were taken on the prepared ground surfaces and
on constructed engineered fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes and construction plans
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the
specified minimum compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative
based on visual observation and hand probing
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the earthwork operations for the above project
observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in
general accordance with the intent of the project plans and specifications and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations presented in the referenced report dated
August 12 1996 and subsequent recommendations
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork was
performed without our observation However the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of the geotechnical
risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and
statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork
construction and materials Our services were performed in accordance with generally
accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or
implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it
guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality
of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing defects
regardless of when they are found
