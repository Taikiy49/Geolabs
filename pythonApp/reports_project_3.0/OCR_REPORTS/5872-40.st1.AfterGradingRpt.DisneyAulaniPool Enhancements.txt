Dear Mr. Moore: 
As requested, we provided geotechnical engineering services during the site work 
operations of pool expansion and enhancement project at Aulani, A Disney Resort & Spa 
in the Ko Olina area of the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. This letter report summarizes our 
geotechnical engineering observation and consultation services performed for the site 
grading and backfilling portion of the project only. The discussions presented herein are 
subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report. 
Project Considerations 
Aulani, A Disney Resort & Spa (formerly Disney’s Grand Hawaiian Resort & Spa) 
project site is within the existing Ko Olina Resort development in the District of Ewa on the 
Island of Oahu, Hawaii (as shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1). The project site 
encompasses two parcels of land (Lot Nos. 13 and 15) fronting Lagoon No. 1 of the 
Ko Olina Resort. The project site is also adjacent to the existing JW Marriott Ihilani Resort 
and Spa (to the northwest). The two parcels are to the southwest of Ali`inui Drive, which is 
the main entry road into the resort. 
Our observation and testing services during the site work of the pool expansion 
and enhancement projects occurred between March 2013 through August 2013. Nordic 
PCL Construction, Inc. was the general contractor for the project, and Jayar Construction 
was the site work subcontractor for the project.

Discussion and Recommendations 
Earthwork for the pool expansion and enhancement projects generally consisted of 
cuts and fills of less than about 2 feet except for some areas where the excavation and 
backfills extended up to 5 feet thick. 
Fill and backfill materials generally consisted of excavated materials from the 
Aulani project site. In general, fill materials consisted of materials less than 3 inches in 
maximum particle size. Samples of the on-site materials were obtained and transported to 
our office for testing, where appropriate. The maximum dry densities and optimum 
moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with 
ASTM D 1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 2. 
The fill and backfill materials generally were moisture-conditioned to above 
optimum moisture content, placed in loose lifts, and compacted to a minimum of 
90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557. 
The compaction requirement generally was increased to a minimum of 95 percent of the 
maximum dry density for the last lift of fills under pavements (finished pavement 
subgrades). 
Subgrades in the cut areas generally were scarified and recompacted to obtain a 
minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with 
ASTM D 1557.  
During the earthwork operations, periodic field density tests were performed in 
general accordance with ASTM D 6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method), where appropriate. The 
compaction test results are summarized on Plates 3.1 through 3.4 presented herein. 
These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and 
backfills at the site. Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and 
interpolating from on-site survey stakes, where available. These locations and elevations 
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. Areas 
assessed to be unsatisfactory generally were reworked until the specified minimum 
compaction was obtained and/or accepted by our field representative based on visual 
observation and hand probing. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the referenced tests, it 
is our professional opinion that the filling and backfilling operations for the pool expansion 
and enhancement projects observed by Geolabs in the period covered in this report were 
performed in general conformance with the intent of the project plans and specifications 
and the geotechnical recommendations presented in the referenced report and 
subsequent recommendations and amendments. 
It should be noted the above statement of professional opinion is based on the 
grades existing at the completion of the mass grading operations of the project in 
August 2013. Geolabs assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to the slopes or

Limitations 
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities 
occurring simultaneously on a construction site. Therefore, some of the earthwork may 
have been performed without our observation. However, the level of earthwork 
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some of 
the geotechnical risks to the project.  
It should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical 
repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and 
materials. Our services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards 
of geotechnical engineering practice; no warranty is expressed or implied. Provision of 
construction monitoring by an engineer is not insurance, nor does it guarantee 
construction of any type. The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the 
work, for adhering to the plans and specifications, and for repairing defects regardless of 
when they are found.
