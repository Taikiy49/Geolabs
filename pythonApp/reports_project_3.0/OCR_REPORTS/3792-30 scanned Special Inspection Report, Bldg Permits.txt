PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The 2100 Kalakaua Avenue development is located in the middle of Waikiki in the
District of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is located at the
northeast corner of the intersection between Kalakaua Avenue and Kalaimoku Street The
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
The project consists of the development of the site into a 110 000 square foot retail
complex In general the commercial development consists of a three story steel framed
structure with some associated infrastructure The commercial development will comprise
of retail space units ranging in sizes from about 5 000 to 15 000 square feet

Due to the presence of loose and or soft subsurface soil conditions at the site we
recommended the use of a deep foundation system consisting of cast in place concrete
drilled shafts for support of the new building structure Based on the information provided
the structural column loads of the new building range from approximately 50 to 600 kips
compression load Therefore we recommended the use of 24 inch and 36 inch
diameter drilled shafts for foundation support The approximate locations of the drilled
shafts for the building foundation are shown on the Drilled Shaft Identification Plan
Plate 2
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Based on the borings drilled at the project site in 1997 and 2000 the project site is
underlain by loose and or soft lagoonal deposits below the surface fill materials The
lagoonal deposits consisted of sandy silts silty sands and silty gravel The loose and or
soft lagoonal deposits were generally underlain by stiff clayey silts and silty clays alluvial
soil deposits At the western portion of the site medium dense sandy gravel gravelly
sand deposits generally underlie the alluvial soil deposit
Groundwater was encountered in the borings during our field exploration at depths
of about 2 8 to 6 8 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of the
field exploration These measured groundwater levels generally correspond to about
Elevations 0 8 to 3 0 feet Mean Sea Level MSL Due to the proximity of the project
site to the Pacific Ocean and the permeable subsurface soils at the site we anticipate that
the groundwater levels might be affected by tidal fluctuations In addition groundwater
levels are also subject to change due to seasonal precipitation storm surge conditions
and other factors
DRILLED SHAFT INSTALLATION
The trial shaft and the production drilled shafts were installed by Hawaiian Dredging
Construction Company Based on the trial shaft program the contractor generally
demonstrated that the proposed excavation methods and equipment were acceptable for
installing the drilled shaft foundations in general accordance with the project specifications
Based on the results of the instrumented load test performed the lengths of the production
drilled shafts were not changed from the original design lengths
A total of 59 production drilled shafts and one replacement shaft were installed
between and March 15 2001 The approximate locations of the drilled
shafts installed are shown on the Drilled Shaft Identification Plan Plate 2 The drilled shaft
installation operations were conducted under the near continuous observation of an
engineer or geologist from our office

The production drilled shafts were excavated using Watson 5000
crane mounted and Watson 2500 crawler mounted drill rigs The bottoms of the
drilled shafts were excavated to tip elevations of about 42 to 74 feet MSL During the
production shaft excavation temporary steel casings were used for drill hole stabilization
Steel casing lengths of about 51 and 50 feet were used for the 2 and 3 foot diameter
shafts respectively The steel casings used were 0 5 inches thick with outside diameters
of 24 and 36 inches Logs of the production drilled shaft construction and subsurface
materials encountered in the drilled shafts are provided in the Appendix A summary of
the drilled shafts installed is presented on Plates 3 1 through 3 4
After the drilled shafts were excavated to the respective tip elevations the
reinforcing bar rebar cages were placed into the drilled holes using a crane Drilled shaft
spacers were used to provide the 4 inches of concrete cover over the rebar cages After
the rebar cages were installed concrete was placed through a 4 inch tremie steel pipe
starting from the bottom During concrete placement we observed that at least 5 feet of
concrete head was maintained above the bottom of the tremie pipe to counter the
hydrostatic pressures due to groundwater In order to provide good quality concrete at the
top of the drilled shafts additional concrete was placed in the drilled shafts to allow some
over pouring of the concrete at the top of the drilled shafts Concrete was over poured
until sound concrete was evident at the top of the drilled shafts
For Drilled Shaft No DS 56 the measured concrete slump during the drilled shaft
installation was 4 5 inches compared to the 7 to 9 inches required by the project
specifications Therefore Shaft No DS 56 was rejected due to concerns with proper
contact at the concrete soil interface and an additional shaft Shaft No DS 56R was
installed to replace the shaft in question
For Drilled Shaft No DS 20 one of the vertical No 8 bars of the rebar cage was
pulled up by about 12 feet out of the cage during shaft construction In addition the top
four spirals of the rebar cage at 3 inch pitch were damaged The remedial measure
recommended by the project structural engineer consisted of the construction of a
deepened cap for the drilled shaft
During the concrete placement for Drilled Shaft No DS 58 the rebar cage was
pushed to one side by the tremie pipe The measured concrete cover at the bottom of
footing level ranged from about 2 75 to 3 25 inches The remedial measure recommended
by the project structural engineer consisted of attaching Galvashield XP anode to each of
the vertical reinforcing bars
