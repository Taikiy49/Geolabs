As requested we have made a study of the near surface soil conditions
below the above referenced lot The purpose of the study was to eva
luate the suitability of the near surface soils as foundation support
for the proposed house The study consisted of one 1 hand auger
probe and three 3 drilled borings within the proposed building site
laboratory soil tests and a review of the grading and compaction test
record on the building site This report summarizes our observations
and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
House plans and anticipated finished floor elevations were not
available at the time of our investigation We anticipate that the
proposed single family residence will consist of a one story wood
structure

Due to the sloping terrain at the site a split level design may be
utilized Short retaining walls may also be required Based on the
preliminary layout plans the house will set back about 37 feet from
Kahena Street
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the first floor garage and
driveway Post and beam construction with wood floors are planned for

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Lot 46 is located in the central portion of the subdivision develop
ment on the southwestern side of Kahena Street The lot is bounded
by Kahena Street on the northeast subdivision lots on the east and
west and the natural valley side slope on the west An existing
concrete drainage ditch is located on the southwestern upper portion
of the lot The lot generally slopes upward from Kahena Street toward
the southwest at about 10 to 20 percent gradient The elevation of
the lot generally varies from about 182 to 250 feet
SUBDIVISION LOT GRADING
Prior to the recent project site grading operations the northeastern
front portion of Lot 46 was covered with a large stockpile of soil
and boulders left over from previous grading operations in the area

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
A review of the grading compaction test record and the site recon
naissance indicate that the front half of the lot where most of the
house is planned is underlain by a layer of grayish brown expansive
clay about 5 feet deep on the proposed garage area B 1 and 10 feet
deep on the rear portion of the proposed house area B 2 The hand
auger probes drilled borings and site reconnaissance indicate that
better less expansive and gravelly colluvial deposits is encountered
below the surface clay layer at about 5 to 10 foot depths
The remaining portion of the lot beyond the house area is underlain by
natural colluvial deposits transported from the upper hillside area
These deposits are typically heterogenous and vary from clayey silt
low expansion potential to clay high expansion potential

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that most of the lot is underlain by
more than 5 to 10 feet of grayish brown clay The laboratory test
results on this material generally indicated that they have high
expansion potential Therefore for Lot 46 the slab on grade and
foundation construction would require special design procedures in
order to reduce the shrink swell effect of the subsoils
Foundation
For this sloping lot post and beam construction is highly desirable
and recommended due to the sloping terrain and expansive soil condi
tions The post and beam construction generally results in lower
foundation costs and the individual footings can be readily adjusted
should the need occur Where utilized concrete slab on grade should
also be properly designed for expansive soil conditions
W O 865 37 Page 5
For post and beam construction we recommend that the expansive clays
be over excavated down for a depth of 3 feet below the bottom of the
individual post footings and then replaced with non expansive borrow
soils such as crusher waste select borrow or coral compacted to a
minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 The
spread footings can then be placed on the compacted non expansive soil
Alternatively the individual post footing can be extended a minimum
depth of 36 inches below the ground surface The footings should be
poured in place and the excavation backfilled and compacted to
provide an impervious layer over the footing
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for footings
bearing on the stiff natural ground or compacted fill
If a footing is located next to a utility line it should extend to
the bottom of the utility trench to reduce footing settlement due to
settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations This
is especially important in the rear portion of the house to reduce
ponding of surface run off water and possible saturation and settle
ment or heaving of the foundation soils

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage area The on site
clay soils are expansive and are unsuitable as slab subgrade soils
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the clay the expansive
clays should be overexcavated down to a depth of 3 feet below the slab
subgrade and then replaced with non expansive borrow soils such as
select borrow crusher waste or coral compacted to a mimimum of 90
of its maximum dry density as per ASTM D 1557 78
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab and
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to protect
against moisture infiltration a polyethylene moisture barrier may be
incorporated below the slab The membrane should be protected by a
thin layer of sand to aid in the concrete curing
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
A layer of expansive soils more than 5 feet thick was encountered in
the front portion of the lot driveway area Since the driveway can
tolerate some amount of heaving the clay overexcavation and replace
ment can be reduced to 2 feet below the driveway subgrade level
Where 2 or more feet of fill is planned select non expansive borrow
material should be used to cap over the expansive clay Where less
than 2 feet of fill is planned overexcavation and replacement of the
expansive clay with select non expansive material is required The
above capping requirements should be followed for a minimum distance
of 3 feet beyond the driveway
W Page 7
For the long driveway planned joints are recommended at about 8 to
10 foot intervals and at discontinuities in order to allow for some
relative movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or overexcavation and
replacement similar to the driveway is recommended to reduce heaving
caused by the expansive clay soil
Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down at about 10 to
20 percent gradient
Minor grading is anticipated for most of the area below the house
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and debris prior
to filling
Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and com
pacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established by
ASTM D 1557 78
The finished grade outside the slabs pavements and foundations
should be shaped to avoid ponding conditions and to shed water away
from the structures

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during earthwork
construction particularly the fill placement slope cuts and to
monitor the contractor s grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be observed by a
soils engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with
the findings of our subsurface investigation
