REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Hawaii the largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an
area of approximately 4 000 square miles This island was formed by the eruptive activity
of five major shield volcanoes Kohala which has long been extinct Mauna Kea which
has had some activity during recent geologic time Hualalai which last erupted in 1801
and Mauna Loa and Kilauea which are both considered to be active
The project site is located on the southeastern flank of the Mauna Kea Shield
Volcano which had been built up by the successive accumulation of basaltic lava flows
and pyroclastic materials The most recent lava flows that comprise the ground surface
and shallow subsurface at the project site are believed to be Pliocene to Pleistocene in
age approximately 12 million years to 2 million years before present time and belong to
the upper member of the Hamakua Volcanic Series
The lava formation at the site generally appears to be of pahoehoe type flow which
is characterized by a smooth rope like or billowy surface and an internal structure of
vesicular porous rock The basalt formations of the project area are typically layered with
thin flows of dense basalts and scoria basalts Clinker layers are also commonly found
interbedded between the pahoehoe flows In situ weathering of the basalt formation has
yielded layers of soft highly weathered basalts interbedded with more competent hard
basalt formation A geologic map showing the distribution of geologic materials and the
relationship between the different series of basaltic lava flows and the project location is
provided as Plate 2 of this report
FAULTING AND SEISMICITY
Based on our review of available geologic information the Kupapaulua Bridge
project site is not located in the immediate proximity of mapped geologic fault structures
It appears that there are no known faults within about 5 miles of the project site Due to
the close proximity of the project site to active volcanoes and mapped fault zones the
project site is considered to be located in a seismic zone of potential recurring activity
Earthquake activity associated with fault movement and underground magma movement

In general earthquakes that occur throughout the world are caused solely by shifts
in the tectonic plates In contrast earthquake activity in Hawaii is linked primarily to
volcanic activity Therefore earthquake activity in Hawaii generally occurs before or
during volcanic eruptions In addition earthquakes may result from the underground
movement of magma that comes close to the surface but does not erupt The Island of
Hawaii experiences thousands of earthquakes each year but most of the earthquakes are
so small that they can only be detected by instruments
The earthquakes on the Island of Hawaii are closely related to the volcanic activity
associated with the active volcanoes and Loihi that compose a
large portion of the Island of Hawaii Most of the smaller earthquakes volcanic
earthquakes may occur as a result of the underground movement of magma and surface
eruptions associated with the presently active volcanoes Other earthquakes including
the larger and more devastating ones tectonic earthquakes occur within the areas of
structural weakness located near the base of volcanoes or deeper within the earth s crust
beneath the Island of Hawaii
The Island of Hawaii has experienced several notable earthquakes in recorded
history It should be noted that the Honomu area about 13 miles to the southeast has
experienced a sizable earthquake event in 1973 In addition the Pahala area located
about 40 miles to the south has been the location of several notable earthquakes in

SEISMIC DESIGN CONSIDERATIONS
Based on the design criteria provided by the State of Hawaii Department of
Transportation Highways Division the project site will need to be evaluated based on a
seismic acceleration coefficient of 0 36g We believe that the seismic acceleration
coefficient of 0 36g has been developed based on a 10 percent probability of exceedance
in 50 years or a return period of approximately 475 years
The subsurface conditions at the project site generally consist of a relatively thin
layer of surface soils overlying highly weathered to moderately weathered basalt rock
formation Based on the subsurface materials described in the boring logs contained in
the contract documents and the geologic setting of the area the bridge site may be
classified from a seismic analysis standpoint as a stiff or dense soil site corresponding to
a Soil Profile Type in general accordance with the AASHTO LRFD Bridge Design
Specifications Second Edition 1998

Honomu Earthquake
The Honomu Earthquake of occurred on the Hamakua Coast in
North Hilo and was measured at a magnitude of 6 0 on the Richter scale USGS The
epicenter of the earthquake was located about 29 miles beneath the Town of Honomu
North Hilo The epicenter of the earthquake is located approximately 13 miles
southeast of the Kupapaulua Bridge project site The location of the epicenter in
relation to the project site is also shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
It appears that the Honomu Earthquake is the most significant
seismic event that is unrelated or indirectly related to volcanic activity occurring in the
Hawaii area USGS We understand that the Honomu Earthquake is an earthquake
generated by the slow crustal adjustment to the load or weight of the volcanoes This
type of earthquake deep seated earthquakes has no known recurrence interval and is
difficult to predict however this deep seated earthquake can produce damaging effects
over a wide area As a matter of fact the earthquake was felt throughout the entire
State of Hawaii and resulted in significant damage
Based on our review of available earthquake records recorded on the Island of
Hawaii we have selected the earthquake recorded by the strong motion instrument
placed at the Namakani Campground in the Hawaii Volcanoes National Park from this
event The Namakani Campground recording station was located approximately 31 miles
from the epicenter of the earthquake

Kaoiki Earthquake
The Kaoiki Earthquake of occurred beneath the Kaoiki Fault
Zone between the summits of Mauna Loa and Kilauea Volcanoes The earthquake was
measured at a moment magnitude of and the epicenter of the earthquake is
located approximately 40 miles south of the project site The location of the epicenter in
relation to the site is also shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The earthquake caused widespread damage throughout the southern and
eastern portions of the Island of Hawaii including Hilo Several types of damage were
observed near the epicenter including structural damage to buildings damage to water
tanks extensive breakage inside houses and disruption of electrical and telephone
service The earthquake also triggered numerous landslides within the Hawaii
Volcanoes National Park along the Hamakua Coast Highway 37 miles from the
epicenter and in Kealakekua Bay on the Kona Coast 31 miles from the epicenter
In the Hawaii Volcanoes National Park parts of the Crater Rim Drive collapsed
into the adjacent caldera Damage to structures also occurred in Hilo including several
houses that slid off their post and pier foundations Structural damage also occurred at
the Hilo Hospital and several buildings in the older part of Hilo suffered some damage
The mechanism of the Kaoiki Earthquake was directly related to volcano activity
occurring when magma enters a volcano The motion of the magma caused the flanks of
the volcano to move outward in order to accommodate the new magma This flank
movement is the cause of a number of large earthquakes We understand that five
earthquakes have occurred in this area between 1941 and 1983 with an average
recurrence interval of about 8 to 12 years
Due to the seismicity of the area numerous strong motion instruments recorded
this seismic event Based on our review of available earthquake records recorded on the
Island of Hawaii we have selected three ground motion records recorded at the
and Pahoa areas for use in our study The
and Pahoa recording stations were located about 45 11 and 33 miles from the epicenter
of the earthquake as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
ACCELERATION TIME HISTORIES
Ground motion time histories are numerical descriptions of how certain ground
motion parameters such as acceleration velocity or displacement varies with time
Ground motion time histories provide a full description of the earthquake motion unlike
response spectra as they show duration as well as amplitude and frequency content
The ground motion time histories are usually expressed as plots of the ground motion
parameter versus time

In order to evaluate the applicability of the selected earthquake records
acceleration time histories for use on this project the response spectrum for each of the
four pairs of earthquake records were computed and evaluated Both the horizontal
components of the four earthquake records were processed and the response spectrum
for each record was computed using the computer program SHAKE The SHAKE
program is a computer program for earthquake response analysis of horizontally layered
sites developed by the University of California at Berkeley The SHAKE program is
based on the one dimensional wave propagation method to compute the response of the
subsurface materials for a design motion The program also incorporates non linear soil
behavior and the effect of the elasticity of the base rock and systems with variable
damping
To review the selected pairs of earthquake records response spectra for 5 percent
damping were computed for the four pairs of earthquake records In order to match the
seismic acceleration coefficient of 0 36g required for seismic analysis of the bridge the
individual earthquake records were scaled to produce a peak ground acceleration of
0 36g by altering the amplitudes of the individual time histories of the recorded
earthquake motions The response spectra for 5 percent damping associated with the
modified ground motion records acceleration time histories are presented on
Plates A 1 through A 4 in the Appendix
The response spectra for 5 percent damping are also presented in tripartite form
on Plates A 5 through A 8 in the Appendix The tripartite plots show the relationship
between spectral acceleration Sa pseudo relative velocity Sy and pseudo
displacement Sp as a function of the undamped natural period In general spectral
acceleration is plotted along the 45 axis pseudo relative velocity is plotted along the
vertical axis and pseudo displacement is plotted along the 45 axis of the tripartite plot
The tripartite plot clearly shows the relationship between spectral acceleration Sa
pseudo relative velocity Sy and pseudo displacement Sp on a single curve The
tripartite plots take into consideration the dependence of spectral accelerations on the
amplitude frequency and duration of the earthquake motions For very short periods
high frequency which generally mean very stiff structures the spectral acceleration is
almost equal to the peak ground acceleration For very long periods low frequency
which generally mean very flexible structures the pseudo displacement is closely
represented by the peak ground displacement
FINDINGS AND CONCLUSIONS
Based on our review of the response spectrum for each of the four pairs of
earthquake records we believe that the earthquake ground motions recorded at the four
recording stations appear to have similar subsurface conditions to the Kupapaulua Bridge
site Therefore we believe that the selected earthquake ground motions do not need to
be significantly altered by the wave propagation method to be used as design ground
motion for the project site

earthquake records would be compatible for use in the seismic analyses for the

relative velocity Sy and pseudo displacement Sp as a function of the undamped

It should be noted that the computed response spectra for the modified Hawaii
earthquake records shown on Plates A 1 through A 8 indicate that there are numerous
peaks and troughs in the individual response spectrum for the selected ground motion
records Therefore care should be exercised when using the modified time histories in
the seismic analysis of the structure to avoid underestimating the design forces due to the
frequency content particular to a specific earthquake record As a check we recommend
that the forces developed from the seismic analysis using the modified time histories be
compared to the results from the multi modal analysis using the site specific response
spectrum developed for the Kupapaulua Bridge project site contained in this letter report
