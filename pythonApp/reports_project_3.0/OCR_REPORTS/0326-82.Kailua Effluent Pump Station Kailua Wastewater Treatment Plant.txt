I INTRODUCTION
General
This report présents the results of a soils investigation
for the proposed Kailua Pump Station The study consisted
of subsurface borings laboratory testing and engineering
analyses as outlined in our proposal dated
and in accordance with our contract Based on the results
obtained engineering recommendations concerning design
and construction are presented
Project Description
The site of the proposed pump station is at the northern
boundary of the existing Kailua Wastewater Treatment Plant
Kailua Oahu Hawaii The propsed pump station will cover
an area approximately 100 x 80 The pump station wet well
has an invert elevation of 10 feet approximate ground eleva
tion is 2 feet
II FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface exploration program consisted of three 3 borings
ranging in depth from 27 to 47 feet The drilling was accomplished
using truck mounted rotary drilling equipment with continuous
flight hollow stem auger The borings were continuously
inspected by an engineer or geologist from our firm The approxi
mate boring locations are shown on Plate 1 and the boring logs
are presented in Appendix A
In all borings either Standard Penetration Test or 2 5 inch O D
a ring samples were taken at selected depth intervals not exceeding
5 feet The Standard Penetration Test consists of driving a
2 inch 0 D split spoon sampler 18 inches with a 140 pound hammer
free falling a distance of 30 inches The number of blows required
to drive the sampler the last 12 inches is termed the Standard
Penetration Resistance N and is an approximate measure of the
relative density or consistency of a soil These resistance
values are plotted on the boring logs Samples obtained were
visually classified in the field and representative material was
placed in air tight containers and returned to our laboratory for
inspection and testing

IV ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the results of our field investigation laboratory test
ing and engineering analyses the following conclusions and
recommendations are presented
A General
Due to the relatively poor subsurface conditions encountered
at the site conventional spread or continuous footings are
not recommended for foundation support The structure should
be founded on driven friction or end bearing piles
B Pile Foundations
1 Friction Piles
A 12 inch square reinforced concrete pile driven 25 feet
below cutoff assumed to be approximately 10 feet elev
would have a design capacity of 25 kips per pile Pull
out capacity for these piles would bé approximately 10 kips
per pile
2 Point Bearing
A 12 inch square reinforced concrete pile driven into

45 approximately 35 feet of penetration below the
proposed wet well invert should be capable of supporting
design loads of 40 kips per pile A pullout capacity
of 12 kips per pile may be utilized for design
Higher capacities may be realized however a static
load test would be necessary to confirm higher loading
3 Pile Driving Criteria
All piles should be designed and driven in accordance with
governing building codes
Piles should be driven and load tests performed under
the supervision of a qualified soils engineer
Settlements
Accurate determination of settlements for pile foundation
Systems are not possible without a static pile load test
because of the complex pile soil interaction Based on the
available information we anticipate total settlements of
less than 14 inch with differential settlements less than
3 4 inch for the friction piles and total settlements of
less than 1 inch with differential settlements less than
1 2 inch for the end bearing piles

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
LABORATORY TESTING
The laboratory testing program was developed to establish the
engineering properties of the soils and rocks encountered at the
project site
1 Classification Tests
a Visual Classification All soil samples obtained from
the borings were brought to our laboratory where they were
visually reclassified to confirm or modify the field classi
fication prior to finalizing the Logs of Borings
b Water Content Determinations In addition to visual
classification typical samples were tested for natural
water content as an aid in soil classification and in
evaluating soil properties The water content values are
based upon the dry weight of the soil
c Atterberg Limits Atterberg limit tests were performed
on selected clay and silt samples obtained from the borings
for the purposes of identification and correlation of soils
Standard procedures ASTM D 424 for the plastic limit and
ASTM D 423 for the liquid limit were used in the performance
of these tests
d Grain Size Analysis Sieve analysis were performed on
granular soils For fine grained soils the hydrometer
test was performed to determine the distribution of the grain
sizes beyond the No 200 sieve
Shear Strength Tests
a Unconfined Compression Tests Intact core and split
spoon soil samples which apparently exhibited high plasticity
were tested for unconfined strength Length to diameter
ratio of 2 was maintained throughout the tests to avoid
scatter of test results and to facilitate evaluation of
shear strength values for design purposes
b Direct Shear Test In general soil samples were tested
for shear strength using a 2 41 I D direct shear box Each
set consisted of at least two tests each test run under
different normal loads The results are plotted as Normal
Stress vs Shear Strength with a failure envelope drawn to
determine values of cohesion c and angle of frictional
resistance
c Consolidation Tests Consolidation tests were performed
on selected undisturbed ring samples of the compressible
soils to provide basic data for making settlement calculations
Porous stones were placed on the top and bottom of the samples
to allow drainage Vertical loads were applied in increments
with each load increment being allowed to consolidate prior
to adding the next increment Measurements of the time and
consolidation were obtained during each load increment and
rebound was measured during the unloading portion Consolida
tion test results are plotted in terms of percent settlement
versus applied loads
