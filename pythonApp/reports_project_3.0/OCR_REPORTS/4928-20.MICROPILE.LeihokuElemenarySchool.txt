PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
Leihoku Elementary School is located off of Leihoku Street in the Waianae area on
the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general vicinity of the site is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 The existing Building C at Leihoku Elementary School is used as
a serving kitchen dining room building for the school This building was constructed about
15 years ago The building is a one story structure with masonry walls and concrete
slab on grade Cracks have developed in the concrete floor slab and some of the
masonry walls in one end of the building
As indicated in our previous geotechnical engineering reports the structural
distresses developed inside the school kitchen dining room was mainly due to the
expansion of the adobe clay underlying the building site We understand that the effects
of noise and vibrations to the existing adjacent building from the foundation installation
would be a concern Therefore the selected method of structural repair for Building C

MICROPILE INSTALLATION
The installation of the production micro piles was performed by Structural Systems
Inc A total of 28 micro piles were installed from through August 17 2004
The micro piles were designated as Micro Pile Nos 1 through 28 The approximate
locations of the 28 micro piles are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The micro pile foundation was constructed by drilling a 6 inch diameter hole
placing reinforcing steel in the hole and grouting the hole with sand cement mixture mixed
on site A representative from Geolabs observed the grouting operation of micro piles
during the installation of these piles It should be noted that drilling operation for the
micro pile installation was not observed However the holes drilled for micro pile
installation were observed and measured before grouting operation in order to verify the
lengths and quality of the drilled holes
Based on our observation Micro Pile Nos 1 through 21 generally extended into the
in situ clayey materials at 40 to 43 3 feet below the bottom of footing or achieved refusal
into the boulders which was encountered at about 26 5 to 36 feet below the bottom of
footing A total of seven micro piles achieved refusal in the boulders Micro Pile Nos 22
through 28 generally extended 15 to 16 8 feet into the coral formation The Micro Pile
Summary is presented on Plate 3
Based on our field observations we believe that the 28 micro piles installed for the
building have been installed in general conformance with the project plans and
specifications and within the limits of generally accepted construction practices Based on
our observations of the micro pile installation it is our opinion that the micropiles have
been installed to the required diameter and depth to provide the design load capacity
