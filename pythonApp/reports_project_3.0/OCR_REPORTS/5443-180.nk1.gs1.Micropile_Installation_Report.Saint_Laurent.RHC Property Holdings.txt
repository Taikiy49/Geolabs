MICROPILE INSTALLATION AND TESTING
Geotechnical recommendations for the micropile foundations were presented in our
referenced letter report dated The purpose of our geotechnical
engineering observation and consultation services was to observe the micropile foundation
installation and testing for general conformance with our recommendations
Based on the anticipated subsurface conditions at the site the micropiles were
specified to extend through the loose lagoonal deposits and derive load bearing support
from the underlying medium dense to dense coralline deposits
Kelikai Inc installed a total of six production micropiles from April 6 through
10 2020 at the approximate locations shown on the Micropile Location Plan Plate 2 The
micropile locations were provided by Kelikai Inc Details of the micropile installation are
presented in the Micropile Records in Appendix A
In general the micropiles were installed using a 75 drill rig equipped
casing to the specified depth of the micropile to create a drilled hole of at least 5 5 inches
Advancing steel casing to the bottom of the specified depth was performed in order to
facilitate the micropile installation
After the drilled holes were extended to the specified depth and cleaned out the
micropiles were initially grouted Grout was batched on site utilizing a colloidal high shear
and high speed mixer equipped with a grout pump The grout mix
consisted of one sack of 94 pound Portland Cement Type I and or II 87 pounds of
masonry sand and 5 gallons of water
The grout was pumped into the cased hole through a PVC tremie pipe starting from
the bottom of the cased hole After clean grout appeared at the top of the cased hole a
reinforcing bar with centralizers was placed in the cased hole
The temporary steel casing was removed one section at a time and re grouted as
necessary to maintain the grout level near the top of the casing This process continued
until the bottom of the steel casing reached the top of the bond zone The permanent
casing was then plunged to a final depth of approximately 3 feet into the bearing layer
It should be noted that Micropile No 104 was not installed properly and was
abandoned in place A void was encountered during the micropile installation causing

Micropile Testing
In order to evaluate the load carrying capacities of the installed micropiles one
proof test was conducted on a production micropile The proof test was conducted on
Micropile No 103 on The micropile was loaded to a maximum test load of
about 80 kips The maximum test load of about 80 kips was held for 10 minutes to
evaluate creep effects Subsequently the micropile was unloaded to zero load in about
four equal load decrements
In general the proof tested production micropile met the acceptance criteria
specified in the project requirements and our letter report dated
At the maximum test load the creep movement during the 10 minute holding period
for Micropile No 103 was about 0 009 inches which is less than 0 04 inches typically
allowed for the micropile foundation system At the maximum test load the vertical
displacement of Micropile No 103 was about 0 199 inches The results of the proof test
are presented in Appendix B
CONCLUSIONS
Based on our field observations of the micropile installation and micropile proof test
performed by the subcontractor it is our professional opinion that the specified micropiles
except for Micropile No 104 for the new Saint Laurent have been installed to provide the
design load capacity of 60 kips with tolerable movements
