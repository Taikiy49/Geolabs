INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation is to evaluate the
subsurface conditions and provide recommendations for
the design and construction of the proposed Matsuda
residence at 3264 Pinaoula Street Manoa Valley Honolulu
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the eastern side of Pinaoula
Street near the intersection of Pinaoula Street and Pinaoula
Place
The site is bounded by houses on the north and south and
an empty lot on the east
The lot is trapezoidal in shape with a frontage of about
115 feet and depths of 92 and 66 feet
The site generally slopes down to the west at about 13 1
H V to 2 1 H V ratios
Large trees and brush cover the site
At the time of our soil investigation there were no
existing structures on the site
The elevation at the site generally varies from about
310 to 345 feet
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The present plan is to construct a 2 story residence
approximately 30 by 45 feet in plan
Cuts into the existing slope for the garage and backyard
construction are contemplated Fills are not planned for
the site development

Basement walls are planned for the garage and retaining
walls up to about 6 feet may be required for the backyard
construction
Spread footing foundations resting on the surface soils
are planned
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Subsurface Exploration
Two test borings were drilled to depths of 14 and 15
feet in order to explore the subsurface conditions and
obtain samples for laboratory testing Detailed infor

are covered in the appendix along with the boring logs
and laboratory test results

Soil Conditions
Based on the two borings drilled the soils at the site
appear to be stiff clayey silt with some decomposed rock
No groundwater was noted in the drill holes during the
field exploration

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The subsurface exploration indicates that the soils at
the site are probably residual soils developed from the
weathering of the original lava rock formation The
stiff subsoil should provide adequate bearing support
for the proposed structure However due to the sloping
terrain the foundation and site grading should be designed
with care to reduce slope creep and prevent slope insta
bility
Foundations
For the garage and backyard area where the existing slope
will be cut to form level areas spread and wall footing
foundation resting on the stiff natural soils may be used
On sloping areas short pier type foundations should be
used The piers should extend below the ground surface
such that the outer edge of the pier has a minimum hori
zontal set back distance of 5 feet from the slope face
For foundations bearing on the stiff in situ soil bearing
values of 3 000 p s f may be used
If a footing or pier is located next to a utility line
the footing or pier should extend to the bottom of the
utility trench or be designed to bridge over it
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations

Slopes
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down
backyard development of up to about 15 feet is planned
From the laboratory soil test results and observations
of existing slopes around the site it appears that cut
slopes up to 14 1 H V can be used for the site develop
ment This recommendation is based on the assumption that
no seepage water is encountered and slope planting is uti
lized to reduce erosion
Surface runoff should be diverted away from the toe of
the slope
Retaining and Basement Watls
A basement wall about 11 feet high is planned for the
garage A short retaining wall should be utilized for
the backyard development
Well graded granular material such as base course rock
should be used as backfill material behind the walls
Bearing values of 3 000 p s f can be used for footings
resting on the stiff in situ soil
For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls restrained at the top 60 p c f equivalent
basement wall fluid pressure
Walls unrestrained at the top 70 p c f equivalent
backyard retaining wall fluid pressure

W 6
A friction factor of 0 35 can be utilized to determine
the sliding resistance of the wall foundation For
additional resistance a cohesion value of 400 p s f
can be used
Weepholes or a subsurface drainage system should be
used to prevent the build up of hydrostatic pressure
behind the wall
Steep cuts for wall construction should be supported as
soon as practicable to reduce sloughing and weakening
of the existing slopes
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade may be utilized for the garage A 4 inch
layer of base course should be provided below the slab
and compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density
Since the slab will be below the ground surface a sub
drainage system around the perimeter will help to reduce
seepage water
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the inspection of the cut slopes
and foundation excavations be carried out by a soils
engineer to verify that the subsurface conditions encoun
tered during construction are consistent with the prelim
inary soil data obtained during this study

APPENDIX A
FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by
drilling 2 borings with portable drilling equipment The
borings were terminated at 14 5 and 15 0 feet below the
existing ground surface The locations of the borings
are shown on the enclosed Site Plan Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examina
tion in the field by our engineer who continuously monitored
the drilling operations The classifications were verified
by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The
soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System A graphic presentation of the
soils is presented on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
In the borings samples were obtained by driving either a
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches
The blow counts to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are
shown on the boring logs at the appropriate sample depth
Rock samples were obtained with a BX double tube core
barrel with a diamond coring bit
