SOIL ENGINEERING REPORT
LOT 11 ROYAL SUMMIT SUBDIVISION PHASE III
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to provide
foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The study
consisted of drilling three shallow borings performing laboratory testing
and reviewing the subdivision site grading and preliminary house
foundation plans This report summarizes our findings and
recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
Based on the preliminary house plans the proposed residence will be
a one story structure approximately 48 by 80 feet in plan dimensions
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage driveway and the
rear lower house area while elevated wood floor is planned for the middle
portion of the house The house will be of wood framed construction

SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the western side of Ainanui Loop in
the Royal Sunmit Subdivision Phase III in Aiea on the island of Oahu
A relatively level pad about 30 feet wide is situated along the
front portion of the site The ground surface then slopes downward at

at the rear of the lot There is a 5 foot wide drainage easement along

DISCUSSTON AND RECOMMENDATTONS
General
The very stiff reddish brown clayey silt soil encountered generally
can provide suitable foundation support for the proposed residential
structure provided that some care is exercised during construction to
minimize the swelling potential of the underlying soil due to later
wetting
Laboratory tests performed on the very stiff clayey silt soil
indicates that this material generally has low swelling potential
depending on the in situ moisture content of the soil prior to subsequent
subgrade saturation
Therefore to minimize effects due to soil swelling under on grade
Slabs we recommend that the on site soil be capped with at least 12
inches of non expansive select fill and that the subgrade soils
underneath the select fill and slab be moistened and protected from drying
from the start of construction until the slab concrete placement
Select non expansive granular soils should also be used as backfill
behind the retaining walls to minimize the additional pressure due to the
swelling potential of the natural soil
Iot Grading
The fills and backfills within the top 12 inches minimum below
on grade slabs should consist of non expansive granular soils such as
select borrow crusher waste or coral Where cut is anticipated such as
the garage driveway and the lower house areas the on site soil should be
over excavated to provide a 12 inch minimum thickness of the above
specified non expansive select fill capping

portion will be of slab on grade construction The middle portion will

Spread or continuous wall footings should be used to support the

Interior spread footings on level areas should be poured in place
and be embedded a mininum of ten 10 inches below the finished grade

Slab On Grade
It should be noted that some of the on site clayey silt soils are
moderately expansive and are marginally suitable to provide direct slab
support Therefore we recommend the on site soil should be
over excavated down to a depth of twelve 12 inches below the slab
bottom The exposed subgrade should thoroughly be moistened and then
replaced with eight 8 inches of non expansive select borrow imported
soils below the 4 inch cushion fill The thickened edge of the slab
should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finish
grade
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the
underlying expansive soils we recommend that the in place clayey silt
subgrade soil below the upper capping fill pad be kept moist for one week
prior to concrete placement heavily soaked with water for the first two
days and sprinkled with water for the remainder of the week
We recommend a 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel should be provided as
the cushion fill below the slab The 3B Fine gravel layer will also act
as a capillary break To protect against future moisture infiltration an
impervious moisture membrane may be incorporated below the slab
The on grade slabs should be structurally separated from wall
foundations
Concrete Driveway
Since the driveway can tolerate some amount of heaving for economy
the 12 inch on site soil over excavation and replacement may be replaced
with a 4 inch base course layer and an 8 inch thick perimeter thicken
edges It is critical that the subgrade soils should be maintained ina
moist condition to prevent from drying out prior to concrete placement
Joints at about 8 to 10 foot intervals are recommended in the
driveway slab in order to allow for some relative movement of the
concrete

Retaining Walls
It is our understanding that retaining walls generally less than 6
feet high may be required at the front of the proposed residence and along
two lot boundary lines to provide grade separation The guidelines for
the retaining wall designs are listed below
For lateral earth pressures the following can be used
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
Wall restrained at the top 55 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth
An allowable bearing value of up to 2500 p s f is recommended for
foundations bearing on imported select borrow or on stiff on site
soil
A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth may
be used for the wall footing on level areas The passive
pressure must be reduced for walls on slopes
A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine the
resistance against sliding of the wall
The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be embedded
a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
A subsurface drainage system subdrains or weepholes should be
utilized behind the walls Subdrains should be properly
constructed to drain water away from the wall

Drainage
We recommend that planters next to the foundations should be avoided
or have concrete bottoms and drains to prevent excessive water from
entering the foundation slab subsoils
We believe these drainage requirements are essential for the proper
performance of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could
cause excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss
of strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of the
concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible after footing
backfill and maintained to drain all surface run off away from the slab
and footing foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for lot grading foundation and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the lot grading footing excavations and
backfill operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections

principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all other
