INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this investigation was to define subsurface
conditions and to provide recommendations for the design and
construction of the proposed improvements for Kaiser High
School This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
W 1975
Site Description
Kaiser High School is located on the west facing slope of
Koko Crater in Hawaii The school
athletic fields are at the base of the slope adjacent to
Lunalilo Home Road and Kapala Street The two areas of the
campus covered by this investigation are 1 the proposed
athletic field bleacher expansions adjacent to the existing
bleachers located on the existing slope above Athletic Field
1 and 2 the proposed swimming pool and varsity locker
facility mauka of the gymnasium under construction
The existing bleachers on the slope are in good condition
There is no visible sign of slope movements or instability
at the existing bleachers
However a depression was noted following the drain line
parallel to the existing bleachers Several utilities are
located in the area of the proposed upper elevated bleachers
Another small depression appears over the 6 inch water line
The proposed swimming pool and varsity locker facility site
is essentially level and presently being used as a storage
area for the construction of the Gymnasium
W 1975
Geology
The school site is at the base slope of the Koko Crater
the geologic history of which is rather recent Collect
ively the cones and lava flows of the Hawaii Kai area are
known as the volcanics of the Koko fissure All eruptions
were hydromagnetic and built cones of vitric ash now
consolidated into tuff Ocean level changes resulted in
the alluviation of the Kuapa Pond now Hawaii Kai and
subsequent weathering caused the forming of the surface soils
Subsurface Explorations
The subsurface conditions at the building sites were explored
utilizing nine test borings The borings were drilled at
the locations shown on the enclosed Boring Location Plate 1
A detailed description of the field exploration methods and
accompanying laboratory testing is given in the appendix
along with the boring logs and laboratory test results
The following two sections describe our findings and re
commendations in detail
IMPROVEMENTS FOR ATHLETIC FIELD
W 1975
Proposed Construction
The proposed athletic field improvements will consist of
a 130 foot extension mauka of the existing bleachers and
additional elevated bleachers between the existing bleachers
and the gymnasium
The proposed 130 foot bleacher extension will be constructed
similar to the existing slab on grade bleachers located on
the existing cut slope Design live loads of 100 pounds
per square foot shall be used
The additional elevated bleachers will be about 355 feet
long and will be of precast concrete bearing upon pre
stressed planks supported on columns and bearing walls
Maximum column loads of 200 kips are anticipated
The elevated bleachers will have concession and toilet
facilities below and a press box above Site grading in
volving cut will be minimum
Soil Conditions
The explorations disclosed essentially uniform soil con
ditions within the proposed bleachers construction area
W 1975
The grade site is generally blanketed by a thin layer
2 to 18 inches of gravelly fill Under the fill layers
of stiff to very stiff silt and clayey silt were encountered
to depths of 17 or more feet The silt and clayey silt
were weathered in situ tuff materials In Boring Nos
6 and 7 a sand layer was encountered below these silty
04 S
No ground water was encountered during the subsurface
explorations
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface exploration and laboratory test results in
dicated the site is underlain by seventeen or more feet of
stirf to very to very stiff clayey silt and silt soils
Spread or line footing bearing on the clayey silt and silt
layers will provide adequate support for the proposed
construction without excessive settlement
Foundation
Spread or line footing foundations may be used for the
elevated bleachers Foundation bearing within the stiff
in site silty soils can be designed for an allowable bearing
pressure of 4 000 pounds per square foot
W O
The bottom of all footings should be a minimum of 36
inches below the existing ground surface Where existing
or abandoned utility lines are located under the foundation
footings should either step down or be designed to bridge
over the loose backfill areas
During construction if any abandoned cesspool or loose
utility backfill is located below the footing foundation
it should be cleared out and sludge and soft materials
removed before being backfilled with lean concrete up to
its design bottom elevation
Slab on grade bleachers should be placed on a 4 inch layer
of S4C compacted to 95 of its maximum density established
by ASTM Designation D 1557 72T
The on site surface soils under the slab on grade foundation
should be scarified to a minimum of 12 inches wetted to
its optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum density
Floor Slabs Walkway Pavement
Expansive soils were not encountered during the investigation
Slab on grade under the bleachers and other walkway pavements
W 1975
can be utilized without special provisions for expansive
SOs
It is recommended that the subgrade be compacted to a min
imum of 90 of maximum dry density as previously specified
This will provide relatively uniform support
Slab on grade with floor coverings should be protected by
a plastic vapor barrier The plastic should be covered by
a thin layer of sand for protection and to avoid punctures
Groundwater was not encountered within a depth of 20 feet
below the surface and it is not necessary to install a
granular base for the slab
Earthwork and Compaction Requirements
Where fill will be placed upon existing slopes slopes
should be keyed and benched for the new fill Fill
should then be placed in 8 inch thick horizontal lifts and
compacted to the specified 90 of its maximum density
The on site clayey silt and silt soils are non expansive
and they are acceptable as fill material
PROPOSED SWIMMING POOL VARSITY LOCKER FACILITY
W 1975
Proposed Construction
The proposed varsity locker facility is adjoining to the
proposed swimming pool The locker facility will utilize
columns and bearing walls for support of a laminated
wood roof and concrete bleachers
Soil Conditions
The test boring data indicated uniform layers of stiff
clayey silt and silt soils immediately below 12 to 18 inches
of loose fill and top soils Underlying the clayey silt
and silt layers at a depth approximately 19 feet below the
existing ground surface is black sand
No ground water was intercepted within 20 feet of the sur
face in all borings
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface explorations indicated that generally competent
soil will be encountered beneath the proposed foundations
W 1975
The stiff clayey silt and silt soils will provide adequate
support for the locker facility without excessive settle
ments Both the surface soils and the clayey silts are non
expansive materials The swimming pool and slab on grade
can be designed without special provisions for expansive
soil conditions
Foundations
It is recommended that spread and line footings be designed
for allowable bearing pressures of 4000 psf bearing upon
the stiff clayey silt soils The bottom of all footing
foundations should penetrate through the fill and other
disturbed surface materials and should be placed 36 inches
below finished grade
For the 4000 psf bearing pressure it is estimated that
settlements will not exceed 3 inch total and inch diff
erential
A continuous footing should be stepped down to avoid utility
lines under the foundation if possible If the footing is
located in cesspool or loosely backfilled areas the loose
materials should be removed down to stiff undisturbed soil
W 1975
and backfilled with lean concrete
Swimming Pool
For the gunite swimming pool to be excavated the walls should
be designed to resist an equivalent fluid pressure of 35 pcf
The bottom of the swimming pool should be compacted to a
minimum of 95 of its maximum density A 4 inch layer of
3 4 inch minus crushed rock drainage base should be added
before the pool slab is constructed Positive drainage should
be provided below the pool
The proposed concrete pool deck should be placed on a
compacted subgrade and a 4 inch layer of S4C cushion material
Block Wall
The block wall located around the pool area can be supported
on line footing upon the compacted surface soils The foot
ing should be a minimum of 18 inches below finish grade
Allowable bearing pressure of 3000 psf shall be used on
compacted fill soils
Slab on Grade and Walkways
Slab on grade and walkways can be constructed without designing
for expansive soil conditions Slab subgrade close to existing
grade should be free of topsoil The subgrade should be scari
W 1975
fied watered and recompacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum density established by ASTM D 1557 72T
Earthwork and Compaction Requirements
Footing excavation backfills should be compacted in 8 inch
lifts to a minimum of 90 maximum density within the slab
areas
All areas to receive fill should be undercut to remove the
surface vegetation and any previously placed top soil fills
The ground surface should then be keyed and benched to properly
bond the newly placed fill to the existing soil Prior to the
placement of any new fill the upper six inches of the exist
ing ground surface should be scarified brought to near
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90
of the maximum dry density established by ASTM Test D 1557 72T
Additional fills should be placed in horizontal lifts and
compacted to the specified density
CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the foundation excavations be in
spected prior to the placement of the concrete or rein
W 29 1975
forcing to assure that adequate bearing has been obtained
and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the
findings of our subsurface investigation
As previously discussed the backfilling and earthwork oper
ations should be inspected on a full time basis This inspec
tion should include the necessary testing to assure that the
proper degree of compaction is consistently achieved Ex
cessive compaction of the backfill can increase the lateral
earth pressures above the design values
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon
the above inspections
Should you have any questions concerning our findings or
conclusions please feel free to contact us

FIELD EXPLORATION
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling
9 borings utilizing truck mounted equipment The depths of all
the borings terminated at 20 0 feet below the existing ground
surface The locations of the borings are shown on the enclosed
Site Plan and Boring Location Plate 1
The soils were classified by visual and textural examination
in the field by our geologist who continuously supervised the
drilling operations The classifications were verified by visual
inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils were classi
fied in accordance with the Unifield Soil Classification System
A graphic presentation of the soils encountered is presented on
the Boring Logs Plate A 1 through A 9
Soil samples were obtained by driving either a 2 4 inch 1 D ring
samplers or a Standard penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer
free falling a distance of 30 inches The blow counts to drive
the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the logs of borings
at the appropriate sample depth
Appendix 2
W
LABORATORY TESTING
Moisture density determinations and Atterberg Limits were
made on selected samples to aid in the classification of
the soils The results of these tests are presented on the
Log of Borings at the appropriate sample location
A California Bearing Ratio CBR test and a modified proctor
compaction test were performed on a surface sample of the on
site soil to evaluate the suitability of the soil as fill and
pavement support The results are given on Plate A 10
Two swell tests were conducted on near surface samples to
measure the swell potential The swell test results are
Summarized on Plate A ll1
A total of three direct shear tests were performed on selected
samples to evaluate the strength characteristics of the en
countered soils The test results are shown on Plate A l2
