We have also reviewed the preliminary plans titled Foundation
and Ground Floor Plan 1 Drawing No S 2 and Site Layout Grading
Utilities Plan Drawing No C 2 prepared by your office and were
made available to us by Hawaii Structural Engineers Ltd
It is our understanding that the building layouts are now in the
process of being revised and that the general approach to the
foundation design will remain essentially the same We were advised
that the column loads will range up to 150 kips dead and live loads
per column with column loads averaging between 50 to 120 kips Wall
footings will also be used and the wall loads will range about 4 to 8
kips per foot for dead and live loads There will be a significant

varies from 5 to 15 feet thick A medium dense zone with standard

is 5 to 35 feet thick underlain by a dense zone

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
It appears that additional effort to over excavate and recompact
the top few feet of loose sand below the finished grade would result
in a significant reduction in potential differential settlement at a
relatively small additional costs Therefore a set of more specific
recommendations are developed to supplement and expand the initial
recommendations presented in the referenced report
Site Grading
For the 2 story building area we recommend that the existing
areas that are below Elevation 714 feet be over excavated to Elevation
710 50 feet or at least feet below the existing grades which
ever is the lowest It is noted that the referenced report recom
mended stripping an average of 12 inches of topsoil from the site A
schematic cross section presenting such a recommendations is shown on

For the one story building the areas recommended for additional
over excavation should be those under the existing grades at or below
Elevation 718 The depth of the over excavation should be feet
below the existing grade or to Elevation 714 50 whichever is the
lowest
The areal over excavation should extend at least 5 feet beyond
the outside edge of the perimeter footings Where a major interior
footing such as column footing or shearwall footing is located along
the over excavation line Contour Lines Elevation 714 for the 2 story
building and Elevation 718 for the l story building over excavation
should include an area at least feet beyond the footing
All over excavated footing bottoms and other areas excavated to
design subgrade should be thoroughly wetted to a moisture content of 5
percentage points or more above the optimum moisture content and then
compacted to a dry density of at least 95 of the maximum density as
determined in accordance with the ASTM D 698 78 test procedure
Although the original Technosphere report recommended a minimum of
90 we believe that a higher densification should be achieved even in
the site grading work The compaction should be made by a medium to
large vibratory compactor that will deliver at least of
total dynamic impact force

The over excavation may then be backfilled up to subgrade level
with the on site sandy material in 8 inch horizontal lifts compacted
to 95 of the maximum density ASTM D 698 78
Fills outside the building areas should be compacted to 95 of
the ASTM D 698 78 maximum density
The cut and fill slope should not be steeper than 3 1 H V
Filling on a slope steeper than 5 1 H V including those result from
areal over excavation should be properly keyed during filling or
backfilling operations Any fill Slope over the existing slope should
be properly keyed and benched by over excavating the loose sand below
the existing slope Sketches showing the recommended keying and

Footing Design
The allowable soil bearing pressures may be increased to a
higher value if the on site loose silty sand is densified at least 3
feet below the footing bottom The areas of over excavation under the
footing should extend at least feet horizontally beyond the
perimeter of the footing at the bottom of the over excavation The
over excavation along the perimeter building footing should extend at
least 5 feet beyond the outer edge of the footings Sketches showing
the footing over excavations are also presented on Plate 1

The bottom of the over excavation should be wetted and recom
pacted to 95 of the maximum density It should then be backfilled
with a clean on site silty sand placed in 8 inch thick lifts and
compacted to 95 of the maximum density ASTM D 698 78
For the footings located on the areas that are already over
excavated and recompacted under the site grading work the special
footing over excavation discussed above will not be necessary
The footings resting on the recompacted silty sand as discussed
above may be designed to a soil bearing pressure of 4500 p s f for
dead and live loads The Navy Design Manual 7 1 will allow a factor
of safety of 2 for the allowable pressure under transient loads such
as wind or seismic loads versus the normal minimum factor of safety
requirement of 3 Thus an increase of 50 of the allowable soils
bearing pressure is implied under the wind or seismic loads However
we recommend that the allowable soil bearing pressure be limited to
6000 p s f under wind or seismic loads
Resistance to lateral loads along the bases of the footings may
be computed by assuming a friction factor of 0 5 Passive resistance
to lateral loads may also be provided by soils adjacent to the footings
and other structureal elements below grade Passive earth pressures
may be assumed to be 200 p s f per foot of depth However the
passive resistance in the upper one foot of soil should be neg lected
unless the soil is confined by pavements or slabs

We further recommend that continuous footings be at least 24
inches wide and isolated footings be at least 30 inches in the minimun

The footing bottom should be embedded at least feet below

In addition the outer edge of the base of the footing should be
