Dear Mr. Kaneko: 
At your request, Geolabs, Inc. provided geotechnical engineering observation and 
consultation services during the drilled shaft foundation installation for the new monopole 
at the above project site. This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering 
observations during installation of the drilled shaft foundations for the SPSP Maritime 
Wireless Network System project located in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. 
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering observation during construction was 
to provide engineering consultation and assist in problem resolutions during the 
construction of the drilled shaft foundation. 
Project Considerations 
The project consists of constructing new communication monopoles at various 
locations throughout the State of Hawaii. As part of the project, a new monopole of about 
80 feet in height was constructed at Pier 2 of Honolulu Harbor. 
Based on the project plans, the new monopole was designed to be supported on a 
deep foundation system consisting of a 66-inch diameter cast-in-place concrete drilled 
shaft. The project plans required the drilled shaft extend down at least 40 feet below the 
existing ground surface.

Summary of Drilled Shaft Installation 
Installation of test shafts and load testing were not required by the contract 
documents for this project. The drilled shaft contractor installed the drilled shaft on 
May 7, 2013 and June 6, 2013. The as-built diameter and the length of the drilled shaft 
installed for the project are summarized on Plate 1.1 of this letter report. Detailed 
installation logs for the production shaft are provided on Plates 1.2 through 1.4. 
Representatives from Geolabs observed the drilled shaft excavation, placement of 
reinforcing cage, and concrete placement operations during the drilled shaft installation. 
In general, the drilled shaft was excavated using a Casagrande drill rig equipped 
with 66-inch diameter augers and a 60-inch diameter clean-out bucket. No chemical 
additives were mixed in the drilling slurry for the production shaft. However, 66-inch and 
72-inch diameter temporary casings were used during excavation of the drilled shaft to 
avoid the potential cave-in condition of the lagoonal deposits near the ground surface. The 
drilled shaft generally extended to about 40.1 feet below the existing ground surface. 
During the excavation, significant undermining of the paved areas surrounding the 
temporary casing was observed. The undermined area extended down to about 11 feet 
below the ground surface and extended laterally about 2 to 4 feet beyond the outside of 
the drilled shaft. About 10 cubic yards of controlled low strength material (CLSM) was 
placed to fill the undermined areas outside of the temporary casing. In order to protect the 
integrity of the drilled shaft, the temporary casing was abandoned in place. 
In order to evaluate the condition of the areas surrounding the drilled shaft 
foundation, additional subsurface exploration consisting of exploratory probing was 
conducted. Based on the probing results, it appears that significant cavities and/or voids 
affecting the performance of the monopole structure foundations are not present in the 
areas surrounding the drilled shaft. The exploratory probing report dated July 9, 2013 was 
transmitted under separate cover. 
After excavation of the shaft to its designated tip elevation, the reinforcing cage was 
lowered into the drilled hole to the appropriate elevation using a crane. After the reinforcing 
cage was installed in the drilled hole, concrete was placed and pumped through a 4-inch 
ID (inside diameter) steel tremie pipe starting from near the bottom of the excavation. 
During concrete placement, we observed at least 5 feet of concrete head was maintained 
above the bottom of the tremie pipe. 
Concrete for the shaft was supplied by Hawaiian Cement. A special design tremie 
concrete mix, which maintains a high slump (7 to 9 inches) throughout the concrete 
placement, was utilized for the production shaft. Log of the drilled shaft installed and the

Crosshole sonic logging (CSL) testing was conducted on the completed drilled shaft 
to evaluate the integrity of the drilled shaft concrete. Details of the CSL test and the test 
results are provided on Plates 2 through 4.5. Generally, the CSL test results did not 
indicate significant anomalies of concern in the drilled shaft concrete. 
Based on the observations of our field representatives, it is our professional opinion 
that the drilled shaft for the new monopole at Pier 2 Honolulu Harbor for the SPSP 
Maritime Wireless Network System project was installed in general accordance with the 
intent of the project plans and specifications and the geotechnical recommendations 
presented in the above-referenced geotechnical engineering report. 
Limitations 
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities 
occurring simultaneously on a construction site. Our observation and consultation services 
were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical 
engineering practice; no warranty is expressed or implied. Provision of construction 
monitoring by an engineer is not insurance, nor does it guarantee construction of any type. 
The contractor shall retain primary responsibility for the quality of the work, for adhering to 
plans and specifications, and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found.
