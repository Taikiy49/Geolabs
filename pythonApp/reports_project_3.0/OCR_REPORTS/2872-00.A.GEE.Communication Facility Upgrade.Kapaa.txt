INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed communication facility upgrade in Kapaa on the island of
Oahu Hawaii The general project area is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings of our field exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of data obtained during
the exploration These recommendations are intended for preliminary design input and
are not intended to form the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates or for
bidding purposes

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the Kapaa 272 Reservoir situated near Kaneohe in
the District of Koolaupoko on the island of Oahu Hawaii It is located just west of the
intersection of Mokapu Saddle Road and the H 3 Highway The owner of the reservoir
is the Honolulu Board of Water Supply The associated properties bear Tax Map Keys
The proposed tower location is adjacent to an existing 20 million gallon water tank
At the time of our subsurface exploration the area was paved and the proposed
construction site was used as a storage yard by the Honolulu Board of Water Supply

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The site was explored by drilling and sampling a total of three 3 borings to depths
of approximately 10 0 to 17 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate
locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Approximately 2 5 to 3 0 feet of sandy silt with variable amounts of clay and gravel
was encountered at the ground surface in all three 3 borings It appears that this soil
was brought in and placed as a fill for the construction of the water tank facility The fill
was in a dense to very dense condition Underlying the fill the borings encountered gray
and green basalt to a depth of 17 5 feet the maximum depth drilled The basalt was
highly to moderately weathered at depths from three 3 to seven 7 feet with Rock
Quality Designation RQD ranging from 30 to 64 The Basalt became more intact from
below the seven foot depth with RQD values in excess of 90 Please refer to Appendix
A for an explanation of RQD
Groundwater level was not encountered in the borings at the time of our
exploration However a temporary rise of ground water level is possible especially during
rainy seasons
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Tower Foundations
Based on the available subsurface information we recommend that drilled shaft
foundations socketed in the basalt formation be used for the support of the proposed
communication tower The communication tower is relatively light in weight and will be
subjected to significant tension force at the foundation level as indicated above For the
design of the drilled shaft foundation the following embedment lengths into the basalt

Drilled Shaft Construction
The drilled shaft foundation construction should be closely monitored by the
geotechnical engineer in order to verify if adequate foundation support is achieved Since
a portion of the loading capacities and resistance to tension forces comes from the
bonding between the shaft concrete and the in situ rock extra care should be applied to
the drilling of the shaft holes selection of the concrete mix design to avoid shrinkage
and the placement of concrete in the drilled shafts
Prompt placement of concrete in the prepared holes is recommended to reduce
caving in of the holes It is critical that the concrete mix be designed to minimize
shrinkage in order to maintain intimate bonding between the shaft concrete and the in situ
rock The concrete should be placed in a suitable manner to avoid segregation of the
aggregates
Radio Equipment Building Foundations
Based on the anticipated light loading conditions we believe that the proposed
radio equipment building may be supported on a concrete mat The mat should be
placed on the dense to very dense in situ fill The edges of the mat should be thickened
if necessary to assure a minimum of 18 inches of embedment below the nearest adjacent
finished grade The mat with a minimum thickness of six 6 inches will serve as the

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed communication facility upgrade should
be forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior
to advertisement for bid This review is needed to confirm adherence to the

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the project geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during the project construction to observe that critical
items of work such as drilling and concrete placement for the drilled shafts and mat
foundation excavations are properly performed This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the
event that subsurface conditions differ from those anticipated at the time of preparation
of this report The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by the drilling and sampling
of three 3 borings to depths ranging between 10 0 and 17 5 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings
were drilled by a portable or truck mounted rotary drill rig advancing solid stem
continuous flight augers or rotary core drilling tools as appropriate
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving either a standard split spoon sampler or a three inch O D Modified California
sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate sample
depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice
D 2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left in
the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination description and
testing
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt vs the length of the core attempt e g if 3 7 feet of material is
recovered from a five foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and would be
shown on the Boring Logs as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core material that is in
excess Of four 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any drilling
induced fractures or breaks if two and one half 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered
from a five foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the
Boring Logs as REC 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative
quality based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and Minerals
Rock Quality RQD
Very Poor 0 25
Poor 25 50
Fair 50 75
Good 75 90
Excellent 90 100
