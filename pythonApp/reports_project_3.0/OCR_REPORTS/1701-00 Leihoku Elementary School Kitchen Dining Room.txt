Project Consideration
It is proposed to construct a serving kitchen dining room on the
existing campus of Leihoku Elementary School located in the Waianae
district on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The proposed building will be approximately 60 by 120 feet in plan
and consist of a one story steel framed structure The proposed building
will be located west of the existing Building D
Anticipated maximum column loads for the structure are 50 kips
Fill up to about 1 foot is anticipated for the proposed
construction

Site Description
The proposed site is located on the existing campus of Leihoku
Elementary School to the east of Leihoku Street and south of Lualualei
Homestead Road in Waianae
The site is presently an existing grass lawn fronted by the existing
Building D An existing L shaped parking area is located to the west
of the proposed structure
Presently the grass lawn is used as a recreational area by the
school The ground surface at the site slopes down slightly from Building
D towards the parking lot
Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling
five borings to depths of 19 3 to 20 0 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings encountered two different types of soil at
the proposed building site Generally the northern end was underlain by
coral while the southern two thirds was underlain by expansive clay
Borings 1 and 4 encountered a thin fill layer about 1 4 to 1 6 feet
thick of silty sand with some gravel over stiff highly expansive adobe
clay to their drilled depths of about 20 feet Several coral gravel
pockets were noted at the depths of about 15 to 20 feet in these two
borings In Boring 3 the adobe clay was underlain by medium dense
coral gravel at a depth of about 15 feet At Borings 2 and 5 coral
formation was encountered at the surface and consisted primarily of
cemented coral sand with gravel and silt

exploration Groundwater was only encountered in Boring No 4 at a depth

Discussion and Recommendations
The field exploration disclosed expansive adobe clay under most of
the proposed building site Therefore the slab on grade and building
foundation would require special design and construction procedures in
order to reduce the shrink swell effect of the subsoils
Foundation Support
Spread or continuous footing foundation may be used for the proposed
building provided special procedures are utilized to reduce the
shrink swell effects of the clay soils The clay subgrade soils below the
foundations are expansive and can heave considerably if saturated in the
future Therefore we recommend that the expansive clays below the
foundations be over excavated down to non expansive coral or to a maximum
depth of 2 feet below the bottom of the footings whichever is the
shallower The over excavation should extend a minimum of 5 feet beyond
the edge of the footings
The over excavation should be backfilled with non expansive borrow
materials such as select borrow crusher waste or coral placed in
horizontal lifts not exceeding 8 inches thick and compacted to a minimum
of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78 In the areas
outside of the building the top one foot of backfill should consist of
impervious non expansive soil to minimize water infiltration
Based on the anticipated soil conditions site grading and design
loading we believe that an allowable bearing capacity of 3000 p s f may
be used for spread or continuous footing bearing on compacted structural
fill or recompacted in situ coral sand with gravel and silt

Footings should be placed a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Uplift wind or other lateral loading condition
may further increase the embedment of the footing foundation
The bearing capacity value is for total dead plus live load only and
may be increased by one third for short duration wind loading Estimated
settlements under the design loading are anticipated to be on the order of
less than one half of an inch total and one fourth of an inch
differential
Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed structure can be
obtained by passive pressure and frictional resistance A frictional
coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the bottom of the footing and the
compacted structural fill or the recompacted in situ coral sand with
gravel and silt In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f
per foot of depth may be used to determine the passive soil resistance
Slab On Grade
The expansive on site clay soil under the slab areas should be
completely removed or over excavated to a depth of 3 feet below the slab
bottom and replaced with non expansive borrow soils in order to reduce the
effects of soil heaving on the slabs
Similar over excavation and replacement of the expansive clay soils
are recommended for the walkway and slabs if required
To reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the on site soils if any below the
over excavation level be kept continuously moist prior to concrete
placement

A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the slab and
compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to protect against
moisture infiltration an impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated
below the slab
Site Grading
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limit should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable materials
such as rubbish and vegetation should be removed and disposed of properly
off site in order to prevent contamination of the excavation material
Prior to site filling and backfilling the expansive clay or coral
sand subgrade below the non expansive structural fill should be scarified
to a depth of at least 6 inches below the cleared grade elevation
moisture conditioned to optimum moisture content and recompacted to a
minimum of 90 of its ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density
Loose spots encountered at subgrade level should be removed and
replaced with imported fill material compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density
After scarification and recompaction of the subgrade the structural
fill should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 8 inches in
compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its ASTM
D 1557 78 maximum dry density
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away
from the building
Drainage
The finished grade outside the slabs and footings should be shaped
to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding condition

Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed around the
structures and that gutter water be diverted far away from the
foundations and walkways including the existing structures Excessive
landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above mentioned foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength
The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the footing immediately after setting of the concrete to prevent
ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading foundation and pavement
design should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading and footing excavation be
observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investigation

Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site for use
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration

5 borings to depths of 19 3 to 20 0 feet below the existing ground

monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further

were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Boring Logs Plates A l through A 5

a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Boring Logs
at the appropriate sample depths
Six one inch ring swell tests were performed on undisturbed and
remolded smaples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils in both
natural and compacted conditions The test results are summarized on
Plate B l
One Atterberg Limit was performed on a selected sample of the soil
The test results are shown on Plate B 2
Two sieve analysis tests were performed on selected samples of the
soils to evaluate the grain size distribution The test results are
presented on Plate B 2
One laboratory CBR test was performed on a bulk sample to evaluate
the subgrade support characteristics of the soil for pavement design The
test result is summarized on Plate B 2
One consolidation test was performed on a selected undisturbed
sample to evaluate the settlement characteristics of the subsoil The
curve is presented on Plate B 3
One unconsolidated undrained triaxial shear test was performed on a
selected undisturbed sample to evaluate the shear strength parameter of
the soil The test result is plotted on Plate B 4
