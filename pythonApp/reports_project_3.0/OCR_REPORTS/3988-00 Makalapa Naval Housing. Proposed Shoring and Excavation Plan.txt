Shoring System
Based on our evaluation of the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings
we believe that a temporary shoring system would be required for the proposed exit
stairway construction Excavations exposing the surface sandy gravel may be sloped at
0 5 horizontal to 1 vertical or flatter with some reinforcement of the sandy
gravel fill material Excavations exposing the underlying mudrock may be excavated to
near vertical A minimum 2 foot wide bench measured horizontally from the exposed
mudrock face should be provided at the interface of the sandy gravel and mudrock The
depth of the bench will vary depending on the actual depth to the mudrock layer
As the excavation progresses two rows of 10 foot long No 5 deformed bars
should be driven into the sandy gravel material at a horizontal spacing of 5 feet
on center The deformed bars should be installed at depths of about 3 and 7 feet from
the existing ground surface as shown on Plate 1 Once the deformed bars have been
installed a 6 inch square steel plate with a thickness of about inch should be welded
to its exposed end A minimum of 4 inches of fiber reinforced shotcrete should be
applied over the exposed sandy gravel surface and the deformed bar steel plate as
shown on Plate 1
A drainage system consisting of 12 inch wide prefabricated drainage boards
such as MiraDrain 6000 or equivalent should be placed between the sandy gravel face
and the shotcrete The drainage boards should be located vertically between the
deformed bars and should be spaced no further than 5 feet on center Weep holes
consisting of 2 inch diameter Schedule 40 PVC pipes and filter fabric should be provided
at the bottom of each drainage board to reduce the potential for build up of hydrostatic
pressure behind the shotcrete face A typical section is presented on Plate 1
Determination of the necessary temporary shoring system for the excavation
exposing mudrock will need to be made during construction In general if the mudrock
is observed to be in a highly fractured condition it is recommended that 2 to 3 inches of
fiber reinforced shotcrete be applied over the exposed surface Prior to applying the
shotcrete the exposed mudrock surface should be free of loose materials A drainage
system similar to that presented above should be placed between the mudrock face
and the shotcrete The drainage boards should also be spaced no further than 5 feet
on center Weep holes will not be necessary for the lower drainage boards in the
mudrock however the drainage boards should extend about 6 inches beyond the
bottom of the excavation to reduce the build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the
shotcrete face as shown on Plate 1 Care should be taken to prevent blockage of the
outlet portion during construction of the stairway

Instrumentation and Monitoring
Considering the proximity of the proposed shoring system to the existing office
structure retaining wall roadway and utility duct we recommend that instrumentation
and monitoring of the shoring system be provided The extent of construction monitoring
may vary however we believe that the following items of work should be considered
We also recommend that a representative from Geolabs Hawaii observe the excavated
shoring face for mudrock formation prior to application of the shotcrete to confirm and or
modify our recommendations
Prior to Shoring
Al Damage survey of the existing buildings and structures adjacent to the
proposed shoring alignment should be conducted including photographs
and crack surveys prior to the commencement of construction
2 Settlement reference points should also be installed as much as possible
in a straight line pattern near the outside edge of the shotcrete berm The
reference points should be installed at approximately 20 foot intervals
along the edge of the excavation
A reference point should also be established at the cover of the existing
utility manhole located to the south of the excavation
During Construction
Frequently once a day observe the conditions of the existing adjacent structures
and shoring system for signs of distress The information should be recorded
written on a log and any unusual findings should be immediately reported for
further evaluation or action
The settlement reference points should be monitored generally on a weekly basis
and should distresses be observed on a more frequent basis
Site Visit Observations
A site visit was made on when the excavation had been completed
The surface and near surface soils were excavated at a slope gradient which appeared
to be on the order of or flatter The soils were generally covered with shotcrete
although a portion of the soil in the northern corner of the excavation exposed fill
consisting of granular material It is our understanding that the portions of the excavation
that were covered with shotcrete had exposed sandy gravel granular fill similar to that

Modifications to Recommendations
Based on our observations during our site visit it is our opinion that the shoring
system described above should be adequate for the project However since the
mudrock exposed at the face of the excavation is better than that anticipated it is our
opinion that temporary shoring using the shotcrete would not be required for the
excavation exposing the massive mudrock
In a few small areas the observed conditions were not as anticipated and
therefore the following modifications are recommended The attached Plate 2 presents
our revised recommendations
1k The exposed fill material in the northern corner of the excavation should be
covered with shotcrete Number 5 deformed bars welded to steel plates
should also be installed prior to shotcrete application in a similar manner as
the other excavation areas
2 The area where the shotcrete was damaged should be removed and
replaced with new shotcrete All shotcrete damaged during construction
should also be removed and new shotcrete should be applied All loose
material exposed at the surface should be removed prior to application of
new shotcrete
3 The areas adjacent to the sewer tunnel structure that exposed granular
backfill should also be covered by shotcrete For these areas the shoring
system should consist of welded wire fabric covered with a minimum of
2to 3 inches of fiber reinforced shotcrete Prior to placement of the
welded wire fabric loose material exposed at the surface should be
removed The welded wire fabric should be fastened to nailed into the fill
to provide a more stable bonding surface for the shotcrete
