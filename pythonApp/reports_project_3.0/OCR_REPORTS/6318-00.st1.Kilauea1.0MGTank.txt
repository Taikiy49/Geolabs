As requested we provided geotechnical engineering exploration for the Kilauea
Water Storage Tank project on the Island of Kauai This letter report summarizes
our geotechnical engineering exploration performed at the site and preliminary findings for
the project The discussions presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the
end of this letter report
Project Considerations
The proposed project is on a property adjacent to the Puu Pane Tank site at T M K
131 in the Kilauea area on the Island of Kauai Hawaii The project location and
general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
We understand that the proposed project involves the construction of a 1 0 million
gallon MG water storage tank and associated appurtenances A preliminary site layout
and grading plan was not available at the time this letter report was prepared In addition
preliminary structural loads were not available at the time this letter report was prepared
However we understand that the new reservoir will have a diameter of about 98
feet and a height of about 18 feet We also understand that the finish floor elevation of
the new tank will be at about 448 feet MSL
Subsurface Conditions
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three borings
extending to depths ranging from about 50 4 to 51 5 feet below the existing ground
surface Approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Based on our
field exploration program conducted for the project the project site appears to be underlain
by residual soils and saprolite
It should be noted that the consistency of the residual soils encountered varied
significantly from soft to hard In general residual soils consisting of very stiff to hard
clayey silts were encountered from the ground surface to a depth of about 10 feet below

Ground Settlement
As previously mentioned a preliminary site layout and grading plan was not
available at the time this letter report was prepared In addition preliminary structural
loads were also not available at the time this letter report was prepared However we
understand that the new reservoir will have a diameter of about 98 feet and a
height of about 18 feet We also understand that the finish floor elevation of the new tank
will be at about 448 feet MSL
Based on the presence of soft soils at the project site ground settlements may
occur when new loads are placed over the potentially compressible soil deposits Based
on our preliminary analysis ground settlements of up to about 4 to 8 inches may be
anticipated for the new reservoir placed over soft soil deposits Based on the
topographic survey map we envision that the new reservoir footprint will be graded in a
cut condition In general grading the site in a cut condition will offset some of the
structural loading of the new reservoir resulting in a lower net loading on the subgrade
soils However the settlements will still be significant Also due to the sloping
topography of the site we envision that the southern portion of the new reservoir will
require significantly more cuts than the northern portion Therefore significant differential
ground settlements may occur due to the differential net loading conditions
It should be noted that the preliminary settlement analysis was conducted based
on the anticipated reservoir location and site grading conditions In addition structural
loads were estimated based on dimensions of the new reservoir Detailed grading plans
and structural loads should be forwarded to Geolabs when they become available for a
more detailed evaluation of potential ground settlement at the site

Reservoir Foundations
Based on the preliminary evaluation of the potential ground settlements we
recommend that an alternative site be considered or the new reservoir should be
supported by a deep foundation system such as cast in place concrete drilled shafts
Based on the anticipated structural load demands we envision that the drilled shaft
foundations for the new reservoir may consist of 2 to 3 foot diameter drilled shafts
extending to depths of about 50 to 60 feet below the ground surface In general the
cast in place concrete drilled shafts would derive vertical support primarily from skin
friction The end bearing component of the drilled shafts should be discounted due to
difficulties associated with obtaining a clean bottom during construction in the relatively
deep drilled shafts
A detailed analysis of the foundation system should be conducted when grading
plans and structural loads become available

Field Exploration
We explored the subsurface conditions by drilling and sampling three borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 3 extending to a maximum depth of about 51 5 feet
below the existing ground surface using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous
flight augers The approximate boring locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual and
textural examination in the field and monitored the drilling operations on a near continuous
basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System as shown on the Soil Log Legend Plate A Graphic representations of the
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings in general
accordance with ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch
OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition
some samples were obtained from the borings in general accordance with ASTM D 1586
Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard
penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive
the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the
Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples in
the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined
compressive strength of the sample Pocket penetrometer tests results are summarized on
the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

Laboratory Tests
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Three Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and for soil classification The test results
are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
The test results are provided on Plate B 1
Two Consolidation tests with time rates ASTM D 2435 were performed on
samples of the potentially compressible soils to evaluate the compressibility characteristics
of the materials encountered The test results are presented on Plates B 2 and B 3
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio test ASTM D 1883 was performed on a
bulk sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of
the soils The test results are presented on Plate B 4
One Modified Proctor Compaction test ASTM D 1557 was performed on a bulk
sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density and moisture content
relationships The test results are presented on Plate B 5
