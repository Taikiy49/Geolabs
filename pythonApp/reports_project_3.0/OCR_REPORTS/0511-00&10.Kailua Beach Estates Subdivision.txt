INVESTIGATION
To explore the subsurface conditions at the site a total of
5 borings were drilled to depths of 10 to 15 feet below the
existing surface The locations of the borings are shown on
the Site Plan Plate 1 The borings were drilled with a truck
mounted rotary auger with coring attachments All borings were
continuously supervised by our field engineer who maintained a
log of borings obtained samples of the site soils and corals
and performed percolations tests Soil samples were obtained
with a 2 inch 0 D Sampler The sampler was driven with a
140 pound hammer free falling a distance of 30 inches The
blows required to drive the sampler the last 12 inches were
recorded The coral was cored with a double tube NX core
barrel and the percent recovery was measured for each core run
The subsurface conditions are more clearly detailed on our Log
of Borings Plates 2A through 2E The blow counts and core
recovery are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate depths

SITE CONDITIONS
Surface
The site comprising approximately 15 acres is located on the
eastern side of Kailua Bay and is a portion of Kapoho Point
The site is very level with a slight slope to the south The
site varies from elevation 2 to 11 feet The central portion
of the site is covered with dense growth of grass and brush
along the low lying western boundary adjacent to the existing
residential subdivision
There is a small white coral sand ridge 4 to 5 feet high which
forms the boundary between the inner plain from the wave cutting
platform
Standing water was found on the low spots after heavy rain The
southern tip has gone through more erosion and as a result has
hanging cliffs 6 to 8 feet in height The main wave cut platform
consists of laminated coral beds Underlying these coral beds
appear to be volcanic rocks
Subsurface
Generally the site is immediately underlain by about 6 inches
of coral sand throughout most of the site except for several
J V Development Corporation 1974
depressions where up to 5 feet of loose sandy silts and soft
clayey silts were found These conditions were encountered in
Borings 3 and 4 Below these upper materials a coral reef
structure was encountered which extended to the depth of our
borings The reef structure contained a coralline algae coral reef
with cemented coral sands and debris The coral is medium dense
to dense
Groundwater was encountered in all our borings from depths of
2 to 4 feet below the existing ground surface This groundwater
surface will be subjected to tidal influences
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the site is suitable for the proposed
development with structures supported on shallow footings
bearing on coral or compacted fill Sewage disposal will be
possible with cesspool systems
Foundations
The proposed residences may be supported on shallow spread or
continuous footings bearing on the coral or compacted fill The
footings should not be founded in loose clays and silts that
exist on the site Footings should be bottomed a minimum of
J V Development Corporation 1974
12 inches below final adjacent grade and designed for a safe
soil bearing value of 2000 pounds per square foot dead plus
real live loads Continuous footings should have a minimum
width of 12 inches
A one third increase may be used when considering wind and
seismic forces These footing recommendations are based on
non expansive import fill
Slabs on grade
Slabs on grade should be underlain by a minimum of 4 inches of
granular compacted material In areas where dampness in the
floor slab would be undesirable or where moisture susceptible
floor coverings are to be used a waterproof membrane should be
placed over the granular material If the imported fill is
expansive in nature then wire mesh may have to be placed in the
slab
Site Preparation
The site shall be cleared of all deleterious and organic matter
Several areas of soft and loose deposits were observed during our
field investigation These materials should be removed in building
and roadway areas and replaced with compacted fill Fills should
be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 6 inches in compacted
thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of maximum density per
ASTM D 1557 62T
J V Development Corporation 1974
Fill Material
All imported fill should be non expansive free from debris
and rocks larger than 8 inches in diameter and approved by
the soils engineer prior to placing on the site
Generally the tannish and reddish brown clayey silt soil
available in the Kaneohe Kailua area may be considered as
borrow fill material for the proposed project site However
if this type of soil is brought to the site when its natural
moisture content greatly exceeds its optimum moisture it
should be dried prior to placing and compacting
Utility Trench Excavations
The depth of trench excavation for the proposed drain pipe is
approximately 5 feet below existing ground elevation The
coral reef formation whtch was encountered in our borings
at about 1 to 4 foot depths can be excavated with conventional
backhoe excavation equipment for the most cases However the
coral reef formation may contain hard localized areas that could
require hard ripping or jack hammering Harder material can be
expected near the Kailua Bay southern end of project as indicated
by larger percentage of core recoveries in Boring 1 and 2
J V Development Corporation 1974
Water was encountered in all our borings 3 to 5 feet below
existing grade Trench dewatering to lower the water level for
drain pipe installation may be difficult due to the porous
nature of coral and proximity to the ocean Therefore utility
pipes may have to be placed under water
Temporary unsurcharged trench embankments in the surface sand
and silt layers may be sloped back without shoring Unsurcharged
embankments should be cut back to 1 horizontal to 1 vertical
The trench excavation should be inspected during construction
by the soils engineer
Percolation
It is our opinion that the coral materials provide excellent
percolation rates and that private disposal systems may be used
on the site It is our recommendation that the disposal systems
be designed for a percolation rate of one gallon per square foot
per minute
Due to the presence of sand pockets in the oral reef sidewalls
may have to be used for cesspools

The earthwork for this site was accomplished by utilizing various
borrow fill soils wetted and compacted with vibratory rollers
