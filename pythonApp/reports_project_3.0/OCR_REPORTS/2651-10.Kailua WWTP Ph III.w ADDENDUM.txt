performed for the proposed Maintenance Building and Operators Building at Kailua
Wastewater Treatment Plant on the island of Oahu Hawaii as a portion of the proposed
Phase Ill modifications to the treatment plant Also included in the project is an Elevated
Walkway connecting the existing adjacent Administration Building and the proposed
Operators Building The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
The exploration was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated
to GMP Associates Inc The proposal covered work only for the
Maintenance Building The efforts related to the Operators Building and the Elevated
Walkway were subsequently included at the client s request

General
It is our opinion that construction of the proposed facilities is feasible from a
geotechnical engineering point of view provided the recommendations presented in this
report are properly followed
Different foundation systems and site grading methods wiil be needed for each of
the three 3 proposed structures Shallow spread and or continuous footings may be
used for the Maintenance Building and the Elevated Walkway Piles will be required due
to the less competent subsurface materials to support the Operators Building even
though it is anticipated to have lighter yet still heavy column loads Piles may also be
considered for use as an alternative foundation support to the Elevated Walkway

The highly expansive surface clays anticipated at the sites of the Operators Building
and the Elevated Walkway will require special construction procedures to minimize the
potential for slab pavement distress that might be caused by swelling of these soils
Foundation Design
Maintenance Building
According to the project structural engineer four 4 columns in this structure will
be heavily loaded up to about 200 kips each The other columns and bearing
walls will impose lighter loads on the foundations
It is our opinion that shallow spread and continuous wall footings bearing on the
generally competent coral formation may be used to support the proposed
Maintenance Building despite its heavy column loads Continuous wall footings
should be designed with a minimum width of eighteen 18 inches
A minimum footing embedment of two 2 feet below the lowest adjacent finish
grade is recommended Deeper footing embedment should be used where
necessary to assure that the footings bear directly on the underlying competent
coral formation
We recommend that an allowable bearing capacity of up to 4 000 p s f be used
for the footing design This bearing value considers dead and live loads only and
may be increased by up to one third 1 3 when short term loads such as those
caused by wind or seismic activity are considered
Any loose material encountered at the bottoms of footing excavations should be
removed and the resultant depression backfilled with select granular material
GEOLABS HAWAIlI
W O 2651 10 Page 12
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent of its maximum dry density as determined
in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Foundations located adjacent
to utility lines should be extended down to an elevation equal to or deeper than
that of the utility trench bottom
A total footing settlement of up to one half inch and a differential footing
settlement of up to one quarter 4 inch should be anticipated
A passive earth pressure of 300 p s f per foot of depth and a frictional coefficient
of 0 4 may be used to calculate the lateral resistance that may be developed by
the embedded footings
Footing excavations should be observed by the project geotechnical engineer prior
to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to allow verification of the bearing
material and the embedment depths
Operators Building
According to the project structural engineer loading on the order of 100 kips per
column should be anticipated for the Operators Building
It is our opinion that shallow spread footing foundations will not provide satisfactory
support for this building due to the poorer and widely variable subsurface
conditions anticipated at the site The near surface materials upon which such
foundations would bear are underlain by relatively thick deposits of highly
compressible material Our calculations indicate that settlements on the order
of two 2 inches or more might be experienced for a structure supported by

shallow foundations over these highly compressible subsoils Pile foundations are
thus recommended for this structure
The compressible subsoils at each end of the site exhibit significantly different

subsurface conditions To obtain a prudent design we believe that the building
should be supported by a single foundation system designed for the worst
conditions i e those encountered in Boring No 4 drilled at the eastern end
Based on the conditions encountered in Boring No 4 we believe that sixteen and
one half 161 inch octagonal concrete piles with an allowable vertical capacity of
50 tons per pile may be used to support the structure The indicated pile capacity
includes dead plus live loads only and may be increased by up to one thirds 1 3
when short term loads such as those caused by wind or seismic activity are
considered
An allowable lateral pile capacity of up to eight 8 kips per pile for a single pile and
of up to five 5 kips per pile in a pile group may be used in the design If
predrilling is found to be required the predrilled holes should be properly
backfilled The potential need for predrilling is discussed in the subsequent section

Based on the worst soil conditions encountered at the eastern end of the building

vertical compression capacity indicated The pile lengths required for the western

A minimum pile spacing of four and one half 4 feet center to center is

The precast prestressed concrete piles should be driven with a pile hammer
capable of delivering a minimum of 24 000 foot pounds of energy The hammer
should be equipped with an energy level control device so that driving within the
loose deposits may be controlled at a reduced energy to minimize the
development of tensile stresses within the piles which could cause damage
cracking to the piles
Additional pile driving criteria are as follows
1 The driving process should be continuous without interruption for each pile
A pile may be rejected or its design capacity modified when the driving
process is interrupted for more than four 4 hours
2 During easy driving when the pile tip is within the loose sand gravel or soft
clays the hammer stroke should be carefully controlled reduced energy
so that excessive tensile stresses are not induced in the pile
3 The pile driving may be terminated when the blow counts steadily increase
with depth and reach three 3 blows per inch for the last six 6 inches or
thirty six 36 blows per foot for the last foot of pile penetration
