PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the existing Paia Elementary School in the Paia
area on the Island of Maui Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided we understand that there are six existing
cesspools at the school site Due to regulatory requirements we understand that the
existing cesspools will need to be removed and replaced with a septic tank and leach field
system for the disposal of wastewater We previously conducted percolation tests for
three potential sites at the school Results of the previous geotechnical engineering
exploration are contained in the report entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration
Septic Tank and Leach Field System Paia Elementary School
dated Since then the leach field sites have been consolidated into
one new location Therefore it was desired to perform additional percolation tests at
the new leach field location

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our field exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the proposed new leach field site and to perform field percolation tests to
evaluate the subsoil infiltration characteristics for the formulation of preliminary
recommendations for the design of the disposal components of the on site sewage
disposal system The work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal
dated Our scope of work included the following tasks and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and operators to
and from the project site
2 Drilling and sampling of three borings to depths of about 5 and 10 feet below
the existing ground surface to evaluate the subsurface soil profiles at the
proposed new leach field location
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by our
geologist
4 Performance of three field percolation tests at selected locations within the
proposed new leach field site to evaluate the subsoil infiltration
characteristics for the formulation of preliminary recommendations for the
design of the disposal components of the on site sewage disposal system
5 Laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained during the field
exploration as an aid in classifying the materials and evaluating their
engineering properties
6 Preparation of this letter report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and discussions
7 Coordination of our overall work on the project and client design team
consultation by our principal engineer
8 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program at the proposed new leach field location consisted of
drilling and sampling three borings designated as Boring Nos 101 through 103 to depths
of about 5 and 10 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate boring
locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration encountered surface fill materials on the order of
about 2 5 to 3 feet thick consisting of very hard sandy silt soils The surface sandy silt
soils were generally underlain by very stiff to hard clayey silts extending to the maximum
depth explored of approximately 10 feet below the existing ground surface We did not
encounter groundwater in the borings at the time of our field exploration
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered during our field exploration are
presented on the Soil Log Legend and Logs of Borings Plates 3 through 6
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
We understand that on site sewage treatment and disposal by septic tank and
leach field method are planned within the Paia Elementary School site Therefore we
have conducted additional field percolation tests to obtain data for the preliminary design
of the sewage disposal system at the proposed new leach field site A total of three field
percolation tests were performed at selected locations across the new site The
approximate locations of the field percolation tests designated as P 1 through P 3 are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Field Percolation Testing
Field percolation testing was performed in general accordance with the State of
Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten States Standards to evaluate the permeability of the
subsurface soils for wastewater disposal The percolation tests were performed by drilling
ground surface The boreholes were presoaked for a minimum of 4 hours prior to the
percolation testing
Approximately one inch of gravel was then placed at the bottom of the boreholes to
protect the bottom of the holes from scouring and sediments During testing the holes
were carefully filled with water to a minimum depth of 12 inches above the gravel pack
The time intervals and water drops were then recorded to provide data upon which
calculation of the percolation tests were based

Relatively good percolation rates were obtained for the tests conducted at the new
leach field site with percolation rates generally between 2 and 20 minutes per inch Based
on the generally favorable test results obtained we believe that consideration may be
given to construction of the new leach field at this location Due to the variability of the
subsoil conditions the absorption capacity of the disposal system should be confirmed by
additional percolation tests during construction
