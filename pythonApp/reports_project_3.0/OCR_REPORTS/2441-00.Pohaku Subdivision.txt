INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our preliminary geotechnical
exploration performed for the design of the site grading and preliminary
house foundation evaluation of the proposed Pohakapu Subdivision
devel ornt in the Kailua area of Oahu Hawaii
The purpose of this exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of design
recommendations pertaining to the design of the proposed site grading
infrastructure access roads and preliminary house foundations

provide adequate foundation support for the proposed residential
structures provided that some care is exercised during construction to
reduce the swelling potential of the soil as recommended herein

select fill and slab be moistened and protected from drying from the start

Foundation Design
it is our opinion that shallow spread and continuous footing foundations
may be utilized to support the proposed structures Any soft wet and or
yielding areas encountered at footing subgrade level should be
over excavated to firm natural material and the resulting excavation
should be backfilled with non expansive material compacted in thin lifts
to 90 of its maximm dry density per ASIM Test Method D 1557 78
4n allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for footings
bearin on compacted fill soils
Individual footings and continuous wall footings should be embedded a
minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade
Based on our exploration and laboratory testing estimated foundation
settlement are on the order of less than one half of an inch total ard
one fourt of an inch differential
base friction coefficient of 0 4 may be used to compute the
frictional resistance between the bottoms of the footings and the
recompacted on site soils or the select backfill In addition a passive
resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth of the buried vertical faces of
the footings may be used to calculate resistance to lateral loads if the
backfill around the footings is compacted to 90 relative density

Slab On Grade
It should be noted that the on site clay soils may be moderately
expansive if allowed to dry then saturated Therefore the on site soils
be over excavated down to a depth of 12 inches below the slab
am thoroughly moistened and then replaced with non expansive
imported soils as previously discussed
To further reduce post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the in place silty clay below the upper
capping fill pad be kept moist prior to concrete placement
A 4 inch layer of 3B Fine gravel cushion fill should be provided
below slab In order to protect against future moisture infiltration
ervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and
construction of the foundations This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which when
compacted will not exceed ten inches 10 in thickness Each
layer shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain
uniformity of material and water content within each layer
Additional fill material shall not be placed on any fill layer
which has not been properly compacted Subsequent removal of
any unacceptable layer shall be done at the Contractor s
expense
Rock fragments or boulders less than twelve inches 12 in
diameter may be utilized provided they are not concentrated in
any one area and that there is sufficient fine grained material
to fill the voids between the rocks
Rocks greater than six inches 6 in diameter shall be removed
from the fill unless specifically approved for placement in the
fill by the Geotechnical Engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimm of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
unless otherwise specified in the Geotechnical Engineer s
Report
The compaction equipment may be sheepsfoot vibratory or
pneumatic tire rollers suited to the soil type and capable of
producing the specified results in an expedient and efficient
manner The equipment shall be in good working condition fully
ballasted and self cleaning
GEOLABS HAWAII
Appendix C Continued
The compaction shall be accomplished in a systematic and uniform
manner over the entire fill area to produce a uniformly
compacted fill
The Contractor shall be required to obtain the above specified
levels of compaction out to the finish slope of fill slopes
This may be achieved by either overbuilding the slope and
cutting back to the compacted core or by direct compaction of
the slope face with suitable equipment
Fill placed against previously graded cut or fill slopes shall
be properly keyed through topsoil or loose slope material into
firm material and the transition stripped of loose material
prior to fill placement Fill shall be placed and compacted
from the low side up
Fill slopes should be planted or protected from erosion by
methods indicated on the plans
Fill material placed in an unsatisfactory condition and not in
accordance with these specifications shall not be approved and
shall be removed or reworked until the required density is
obtained
The Geotechnical Engineer shall be provided unrestricted access
to the fill to perform the compaction tests
No fill shall be placed during unfavorable weather conditions
When interruption of work is due to heavy rain the previous
fill surface shall be approved before resumption of earthmoving
operations
