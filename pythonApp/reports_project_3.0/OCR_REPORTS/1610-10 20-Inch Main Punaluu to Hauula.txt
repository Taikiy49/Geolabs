INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of a geotechnical exploration performed to
determine criteria for design of reaction blocks that may be required at bends of a 20 inch
water main at Punaluu Oahu Hawaii The overall general location of the project and the
approximate location of the current exploration are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 The exploration was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated
August 22 1990
This report summarizes the findings resulting from the field exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based on the data
obtained during the exploration These recommendations are intended for design input
and are not intended for construction estimation or bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
A few 20 inch water main is planned on the makai side of Kamehameha Highway
The proposed project comprises approximately 6 382 feet of 20 inch force main with
laterals at each end passing under Kamehameha Highway and connecting to existing
water mains Nine 9 fire hydrant installations are also included in the project In
addition to the bridge crossing site which is the subject of this report the 20 inch main
passes under four concrete box culverts The approximate locations of these culverts are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Concrete reaction blocks are to be installed at abrupt changes of direction of the
main at fire hydrant connections at the ends of the main and temporarily at test points
Exceptions to these general provisions occur at the abrupt vertical bends at the bridge
and culvert crossings At these locations it appears that reliance was placed on the
concrete pipe jacketing that is provided
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1610 10 Page 3
At the bridge crossing concrete piles are required in addition to the concrete
jacketing This construction is shown schematically on Plate 2 This is in accordance
with one of the recommendations in our report and is intended to preclude
damage to the water main that could result from scour action
During construction residents of property adjacent to the bridge crossing objected
to the pile driving operations and it was decided to review the design to determine if some
of the piles could be deleted with provision of reaction blocks in lieu thereof
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this exploration was to supplement previously obtained subsurface
information and to thus develop a generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations regarding the feasibility of deleting the
approach piles and the basis of design for the reaction blocks if needed
The work was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated August
22 1990 and comprised the following items of work
1 Review of information available from previously accomplished geotechnical
studies at the site
2 Field reconnaissance to develop an overview of general site conditions as
they relate to the proposed construction and determining the optimal
locations of the borings
3 Drilling and sampling of borings at each approach to the bridge

4 Laboratory testing of selected samples for determination of the classification
and engineering properties of the subsurface materials
5 Technical evaluation of the field and laboratory data
Gb Preparation of this report summarizing the information obtained and
providing engineering recommendations for the geotechnical aspects of the
project
Detailed description of the field exploration and laboratory testing are presented
in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The bridge is supported by six 6 pile bents consisting of 14 inch square concrete
piles The total length of the bridge is approximately 125 feet The depth from the top
of the bridge to the existing stream level is approximately 4 5 feet During our previous
investigation February 1986 minor scouring was noted around the piles In general
the depth of scour was less than one foot
The abutments of the bridge are comprised of concrete rubble masonry The new
pipeline will penetrate these abutments requiring their partial removal and reconstruction
The new 20 inch water main is to be supported on fifteen pile bents each
comprising two 12 inch square concrete piles The water main is jacketed with reinforced
concrete all around A partial typical representation of the design is shown on Plate 2

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling borings at the north
and south approaches of the bridge The approximate locations of the recent borings as
well as those we performed in February 1986 are shown on Plate 3
At the north approach the boring was advanced to a depth of 11 1 feet below the
ground surface encountering tan coralline sand with some black basaltic sand intermixed
The material was loose to medium dense Groundwater was encountered at a depth of
5 8 feet below the surface The material is generally consistent with that encountered in
Boring Nos 5 and 6 of our 1986 program the logs of which are provided as Plates A 1
and A 2 of this report
At the south approach seven attempts were made to determine subsurface
conditions All of these attempts encountered refusal at 2 5 to 3 0 feet below the surface
due to a very hard layer of basaltic cobbles and boulders Groundwater was not
encountered in this area
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The subsurface exploration of February 1986 discussed in our report dated April
10 1986 and the additional exploration of November 1990 indicate similar soil conditions
at the four culvert sites and at the bridge crossing The only exception is at the south
approach to the bridge crossing where an impenetrable strata of cobbles and boulders
were encountered
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1610 10 Page 6
Pile Foundations
Our previous report suggested that the 20 inch water main be supported on pile
foundations at the bridge crossing to provide protection against scour action This being
the case we feel that the three pile bents behind the bridge abutments Stations 54 39
54 54 and 54 68 and Stations 23 and 56 36 may be eliminated without
jeopardizing the safety of the structure If this course of action is adopted the test piles
currently indicated at Stations 54 39 and 56 36 should be relocated to Stations 54 82
and 55 97 respectively
Reaction Blocks
It is our understanding that provision of reaction blocks at Stations 54 54 and
56 23 in lieu of the pile bents which may be deleted at these stations If it is decided that
reaction blocks are required an ultimate lateral bearing value of 4 000 p s f is
recommended for design A suitable factor of safety should be used in the reaction block
design
The soil around the reaction blocks should be well compacted to develop the
lateral bearing capacity of the soils
Culvert Crossings
There are four culvert crossings in the scope of construction Although additional
geotechnical exploration was not included in the scope of the current effort it was noted
during preparation of this report that no reaction blocks are provided in the current
design This is cited because the design of the 20 inch main at these crossings is similar
to the design at the bridge crossing except that pile foundations are not provided If
deletion of the pile foundations outside the bridge abutments as described hereinbefore

W O 1610 10 Page 7
results in a requirement for reaction blocks it may be prudent to review the requirement
for reaction blocks at the four culvert crossings
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comment prior to construction This review
is needed to determine adherence to the foundation recommendations given herein If
this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot be responsible for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during construction of the foundation phase of the
work This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based upon
information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions between the borings may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1610 10 Page 8
construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report
The locations of the borings were determined by taping from structures indicated
on the topographic survey and construction drawings Elevations of the borings were
approximately determined by interpolation from elevation points on the topographic survey
map and construction drawings The physical location and elevation of the borings
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the boring points depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
Water level data at the boring locations were collected at the times given on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the
architect engineer in the preliminary design evaluation of the proposed project
Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the proper information for use
in contract bid estimation A contractor wishing to bid on this project is urged to retain
a competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the interpretation of this report and or in
the performance of additional site specific exploration for bid estimating purposes

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling borings at the
northerly and southerly approaches to the bridge The boring at the north approach was
advanced to a depth of 11 1 feet At the south approach seven 7 attempts were made
all encountering refusal at depths of 2 5 to 3 0 feet on a very hard layer of basalt cobbles
and boulders The logs of two previously drilled borings drilled in February 1986 are
included in this appendix for reference The approximate locations of all the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A truck mounted drill rig mobilized from our Honolulu
office was used to drill the borings
The material encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who continuously monitored the drilling
operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in
the laboratory The soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the material encountered are presented
on the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 4
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a standard penetration
sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample
depths
