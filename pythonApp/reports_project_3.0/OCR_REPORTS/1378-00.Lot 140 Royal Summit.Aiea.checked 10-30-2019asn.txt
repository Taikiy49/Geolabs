Dear Mr Tanaka
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to
provide foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The
study consisted of sampling the near surface soils from a test pit
excavated by the contractor and the exposed cut slope performing
laboratory testing and reviewing the subdivision site grading and
preliminary house foundation plans This report summarizes our
findings and recommendations

SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
One test pit was excavated within the lower portion of Lot 140 to a
depth of 3 feet below the existing grade at the approximate location
shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 Visual observations of the cut slope
for the basement were also made
In general the near surface soils consist of very stiff reddish brown
clayey silt A review of the mass grading plan for the Royal Summit
Subdivision Phase 1 dated prepared by Community
Planning Inc indicated that the lower three fourths of Lot 140
received about 2 feet of compacted fills over the natural grade while
the upper portion was cut up to 8 feet
No groundwater was encountered at the time of our field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The stiff reddish brown clayey silt soil encountered generally can
provide suitable foundation support for the proposed house provided
that some care is exercised during construction to minimize the
swelling potential of the soil
Laboratory tests performed on the stiff clayey silt soil indicates
that this material generally has low swelling potential if the
moisture content of the soil is maintained over 30 Based on the
findings from our field exploration the present moisture content is
quite dry with natural moisture contents ranging from 23 to 27

Thus the swelling potential could increase to a moderate range
Therefore to minimize the swelling potential the subgrade soil
should be moistened and protected from drying from the start of
construction until final concrete placement
Select non expansive soils such as select borrow crusher waste or
coral should be used for fill below the slab and backfill behind the
retaining walls to minimize the swelling potential of the natural
soil
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used on
the compacted soil
Interior footings on level areas should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the finished grade Perimeter wall footings should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the adjacent finished grade to
provide a barrier against future moisture infiltration under the slab
If located on a sloping area the footing should be embedded down an
additional amount to obtain a 6 foot minimum horizontal set back
distance measured from the outer edge of the footing out to the slope
face This additional embedment depth is required to help resist the
downhill slope creep movement and to develop the recommended bearing
capacity of the soil

Slab On Grade
It is our understanding that concrete slab on grade is planned for the
garage driveway and the entire house It is recommended that a
minimum 18 inch perimeter slab footing embedment be utilized to
provide a barrier against future water intrusion under the slab area
Where on site soil is encountered at subgrade level garage driveway
and basement level constant subgrade saturation is recommended to
minimize the swelling potential of the soils The general procedures
listed hereunder for saturation should be followed
1 The subgrade soils should be water saturated by sprinkling daily
up until concrete placement to reduce the swelling potential of
the underlying subsoil It is important that the soils engineer
be notified to inspect the above subgrade preparation and check
the moisture contents of the underlying soils prior to pouring of
the slab and perimeter footing
2 The bottom of the finished subgrade and footing excavations should
be neat and free of loose soils The very soft silt deposited at
the footing after saturation should be removed just prior to
concrete placement

3 The concreting of footing and slab should be coordinated in such a
way that the time period between the initial excavation and the
final concrete placement should be kept to a minimum in order to
prevent excessive subgrade drying
4 The above recommendations for saturating the subgrade should also
be followed for the sidewalk driveway and patio slabs
It is recommended that slabs on grade be placed over a 4 inch layer of
well graded base course aggregate as a cushion layer and should be
structurally separated from wall foundations
A plastic membrane should be placed over the cushion layer if a
moisture barrier is deemed necessary to protect the floor covering

Lot Grading
Structural fills and backfills should consist of non expansive soil such
as select borrow or crusher waste and be placed in maximum 8 inch loose
lifts and then compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 78 The on site soils in the Royal Summit
area generally have low to moderate swelling potential and would require
special preparation prior to being used as structural fill Therefore
the on site soil should only be used for yard fill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W Page 8
Prior to construction the area to receive any fill should be cleared of
vegetation and debris
The fills should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 6 inches in
compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density per ASTM D 1557 78
The site should be so graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away
from all foundations
Drainage
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against the wall or slab footing immediately after setting of
the concrete to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained to
drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing foundation

Design Review
Plans and specifications for foundations site grading and utility
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill operations
be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that
adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions
are consistent with the findings of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
inspections
