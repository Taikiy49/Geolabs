Project Considerations
The new GSA Office Building project site is at 91 1300 Enterprise Avenue Parcel
12B in the former Naval Air Station Barbers Point in the Kapolei area of the District of
Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii It is approximately bounded by Franklin D Roosevelt
Avenue to the north Enterprise Avenue to the east and open land along the south and
west sides The project site is approximately rectangular in shape encompassing
approximately 10 acres and presently consists of vacant land with moderate vegetation
toward the western portion of the site We understand the new GSA Office Building
project plan calls for the development of the following structures
A four story office building on the east side of the site
A one story link annex on the west side of the site
A one level parking garage along the south side of the site
A vehicle screening facility at the entrance along Enterprise Avenue

Probing and Grouting Operations
A foundation probing and grouting program was implemented for the Office
Building Link Annex Parking Garage and Vehicle Screening Facility for the project
Pacific Ground Systems Inc performed the probing and grouting operations in general
conformance with the recommendations presented in our report dated
for the project The purpose of the probing and grouting operations was to reduce the
potential for loss of foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities or compression
of loose zones below these building structures
Archer Western Charles Pankow Builders Joint Venture surveyed the footing layout
and marked the probe hole locations prior to the probing and grouting operations In
general the probe holes were located at the column footings and spaced at approximately
10 foot intervals along the wall footings The approximate probe hole locations are shown
on the Probe Hole Location Plans Plates 1 through 4 In general the probe holes at the
new building structures were drilled to a depth ranging from about 11 to 19 feet below the
existing ground surface corresponding to approximately 10 feet below the design footing
bottom elevations
The probing and grouting operations were accomplished using hollow drill rods with
3 or 3 5 inch diameter drill bits with side discharge holes for water and grout discharge
Each probe hole was advanced by rotary percussion using a TEI 300HT drill mounted on
a Komatsu PC75 excavator Water was injected continuously through the hollow rods
while advancing to the target probe depth Evidence of cavities voids was observed by the
loss of water circulation with rapid receding of water into the drill hole For the probe hole
where cavities voids were not encountered the probe hole was tremie grouted through the
drill rod while the rod is being retracted The grout used for these probe holes generally
consisted of one sack of cement and 7 5 gallons of water with a water cement ratio of
about 0 70
For the probe hole where cavities voids were encountered or suspected a 1 5 inch
diameter PVC pipe was inserted into the probe hole to maintain the integrity of the
open hole and to reduce the potential for cave in prior to the grouting operation The
probe hole was subsequently backfilled with sand cement grout batched by
Pacific Ground Systems Inc on site and injected at low pressures Additional probe holes
were also drilled in the proximity to better delineate the lateral extent of these potential
voids and or cavities
Our field representative observed the probing and grouting operations and recorded

an aggregate length of about 7 710 lineal feet below the existing ground surface were
drilled for the four structures as summarized in the following table The probing and

seven cubic yards of sand cement grout was injected into these probe holes with the top

recommended excavating down to about 4 67 feet below the wall footing bottom i e

laterally a minimum of 5 feet from the edge of the wall footing The bottom of the
excavation should be lined with a layer of woven geotextile fabric Mirafi 500X overlain by
a layer of The excavated coralline materials may be re used to backfill
the excavation in 8 inch compacted lifts The bottom 2 17 feet should be compacted to a
minimum 90 percent relative compaction A second layer of should then

relative compaction Considerations should be given to strengthening the steel
