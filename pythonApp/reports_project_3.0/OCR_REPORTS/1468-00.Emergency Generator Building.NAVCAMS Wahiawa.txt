In accordance with your request we have performed a soil and
foundation investigation for the subject project The approximate
location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general accordance with
our proposal of the 4th
The purpose of the investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil data for use in the foundation
design of the proposed structure
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analyses to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

Site Description
The project site is located about 1200 feet north of the gate to the
Naval Communications Facility in Wahiawa Oahu Hawaii
The proposed structure will be located on the southern side of Pump
House No 2 at the facility sewage treatment plant
Project Consideration
It is proposed to build a 12 by 14 feet concrete tiled structure to
house a 4000 1lb generator unit for the sewage pump station
Subsurface Conditions
Field Exploration
One boring was drilled at the site to a depth of 19 5 feet below the
existing ground surface at the approximate location shown on the Site
Plan Plate 2
Soil Conditions
The boring encountered residual soil consisting of very stiff red to
brown clayey silt
Groundwater was not encountered in the boring during the field
exploration
Detailed description of the soils encountered are presented on the
Boring Log Plate A 1l
The subsoil condition at the project site consisting of stiff to
very stiff clayey silt appears to be suitable for the proposed generator
building foundation

Foundation
Based on our findings we believe that shallow spread or continuous
footing may be considered for the foundation support of the proposed
building It is recommended that the footing bottom be embedded at least
12 inches below the lowest adjacent grade A maximum net allowable soil
bearing pressure of 3500 p s f may be used for the design
The anticipated footing settlement under the building and the
4000 1b generator should be minimal less than one fourth of an inch
Slab On Grade
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch layer of compacted base
course The thickened edge around the slab on grade should extend a
minimum of 12 inches below the adjacent ground surface The slab
subgrade soils should also be kept moist and not be allowed to dry out
prior to concrete placement
Site Preparation
The overall site preparation will include removal of any vegetation
debris and other organic matter After clearing and grubbing the
existing ground should be scarified to a depth of 6 inches moisture
conditioned and proof rolled to achieve at least 90 of the ASTM
D 1557 78 maximum density
Fill Material
Excavated on site material free of any expansive component may be
used as a source of fill material All imported fill material should
consist of selected non expansive silty sandy or granular material less
than 3 inches in size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve
All fills and backfills should be placed in 10 inch maximum loose
lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry
density

Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and house foundations
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction to see that the intent of our recommendations is
included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that site grading spread footing excavations
subgrade preparations and cushion layer construction for slabs be
observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
