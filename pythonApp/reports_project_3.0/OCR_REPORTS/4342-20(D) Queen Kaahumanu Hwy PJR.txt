INTRODUCTION
This pavement justification report presents the results of our analyses for the design
of new pavements for the Queen Kaahumanu Highway Intersection Improvements project at
the Kukio Bay Resort development located in the Kukio area in the District of North Kona on
the Island of Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our literature research and site
reconnaissance and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from
our analyses for the design of pavements only The findings and recommendations presented
herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Kukio Bay Resort project site is located about 8 miles northeast of the Keahole
International Airport in Kukio in the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii The Kukio
Bay Resort project site is generally rectangular in shape measuring approximately 0 5 miles
wide by about 2 miles long The project site encompasses an area of approximately 675 acres
of land bounded by the shoreline on the northwest and extends inland by about 2 miles The
Kukio Bay Resort development will consist of resort residential subdivisions a guest
cottage hotel complex consisting of bungalows a championship 18 hole golf course and a
clubhouse with associated roadways and infrastructure Queen Kaahumanu Highway
trending in a northeast southwest direction traverses the project site at about the mid point
We understand that various highway improvements will be required for the
development of this project Based on the available plans the improvements to the existing
Queen Kaahumanu Highway will begin at about Station 527 17 and end at about
Station 560 27 as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A new intersection will be constructed at
about Station 543 50 to provide access to the Kukio Bay Resort In general the existing
highway will be widened to four lanes near the intersection In addition we envision that
several pipe culverts will be installed and extended to accommodate the highway widening
GEOLABS INC
Based on the guidelines provided in the Pavement Design Manual prepared by the
State of Hawaii Department of Transportation Highways Division Material Testing and
Research Branch dated May 1995 we understand that a detailed economic comparison of
pavement types is not required if the area of new pavement is less than 4 lane miles and or
a short highway to highway connection is being constructed between pavements of the same
type Since the new pavements for the intersection improvements of the project will be on the
order of about 2 5 lane miles we believe that recommendations for new pavements may be
limited to pavement sections with an asphaltic concrete riding surface
REGIONAL GEOLOGY
Hawaii the largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of approximately
4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five shield volcanoes These are
Kohala which is long extinct Mauna Kea which has had activity during recent geologic time
Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa and Kilauea which are both still active
The project site is situated on the western flank of Hualalai Mountain which makes up
the west central part of the island The lava formation encountered at the site appears to be
of pahoehoe flow which spread and ponded as it approached the ocean Pahoehoe is
characterized by a smooth rope like or billowy surface and an internal structure of vesicular
porous rock Cavities are commonly encountered in pahoehoe lavas These cavities were
formed when the lava was still in a molten state and represent both lava tubes intra flow
cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow cavities Lava tubes intra flow cavities are
formed when molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like structure
which may extend for a large distance longitudinally along the flow Blisters and pockets
inter flow cavities are generally smaller in horizontal extent
Basalts are considered to be relatively permeable rock and can transmit water quite
readily in both horizontal and vertical directions Water is transmitted through the porous rock
matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities and along clinker layers
GEOLABS INC
The permeability of the clinker and cavities is high therefore they serve as the major water
transmission features

Methodology of Pavement Design
The pavement structural section presented in this report was generally determined
based on the methodology described in Chapter 3 of the Pavement Design Manual dated
May 1995 The Pavement Design Manual was prepared by the State of Hawaii Department
of Transportation Highways Division Material Testing and Research Branch The pavement
design methodology is based on the Hveem Stabilometer method developed and used by the
California Department of Transportation Caltrans

Design Subgrade Conditions
Based on our site reconnaissance experience in the area and the current design
concept we anticipate the pavement subgrade soils will likely consist of compacted 6 inch
minus granular fill materials imported and or in situ basalt rock formation for the proposed
highway intersection improvements project Based on our experience with similar subgrade
materials we believe that the 6 inch minus granular fill materials and the in situ basalt rock
formation would exhibit a Resistance R value equivalent to that of an aggregate subbase
GEOLABS INC
material Therefore a design R value of 60 was adopted in our pavement analyses for the
subgrade materials

Subgrade Preparation Below Pavement Section
Based on the preliminary plans and profiles we understand that cuts of up to about
7 feet deep and fills of up to approximately 13 feet thick will be needed for the proposed
highway intersection improvement construction In general the area within the contract
grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed at the on set of earthwork
Vegetation debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed
of properly off site or disposed of in a designated area to reduce the potential for
contamination of the excavated materials Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing
and grubbing below areas designated to receive fill or future improvements should be
over excavated to expose dense materials The resulting excavation should be backfilled with
well compacted granular fill The excavated soft and or organic soils should be properly
disposed of off site
After clearing and grubbing the future pavement areas should be excavated where
necessary to the pavement subgrade level i e bottom of the asphalt concrete base layer
GEOLABS INC
In areas where the pavement subgrades expose dense clinker materials or basalt rock
formation the subgrades should be proof rolled with a minimum 15 ton vibratory roller for a
minimum of eight passes to help detect and collapse near surface cavities Loose areas or
cavities disclosed during clearing and proof rolling operations should be backfilled with
well compacted granular fill materials The proof rolling operation should also be performed
at the finished subgrades in cut areas The vibratory drum roller should travel at a speed of
about 200 to 300 feet per minute Due to the potential for presence of cavities lava tubes
blisters pockets etc and soft and or wet soils contract documents should include additive
and deductive unit prices for over excavation and structural fill placement to account for
variations in the over excavation and backfill quantities
If needed a leveling course consisting of aggregate base course 1 inch minus may
be provided on the prepared pavement subgrade prior to the placement of the ACB The
leveling course should be proof rolled to a dense and unyielding condition prior to placement
of the ACB The ACB material should consist of asphalt treated basalt aggregate placed in
two layers of equal thickness and compacted to a density of at least 91 percent of the
maximum theoretical specific gravity determined in accordance with AASHTO T 209 or
ASTM D 2041
Fill Materials
As previously mentioned we have assumed that the recommended pavement section
will be underlain by structural fill materials 6 inch minus granular fills in the fill areas For this
project we recommend that the upper 2 feet of fill below the pavement areas located above
a fill section should consist of structural fill materials 6 inch minus granular fills In general
structural fill materials should consist of well graded granular materials generally less than
6 inches in maximum dimension with sufficient fines to prevent the occurrence of voids in the
compacted mass
Imported fill material where required should consist of non expansive granular
material such as crushed basalt or cinder sand The material should be well graded from
GEOLABS INC
coarse to fine with no particles greater than 6 inches in largest dimension The material should
have a CBR value of 25 or more and should have a maximum swell value of 1 percent or less
In addition the material should have a minimum R value of 60 or more Imported fill materials
should be tested for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the project
site for the intended use
Compaction Requirements
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practical in fills composed of rocks
boulders and or cobbles Instead a testing program to evaluate the number of passes by a
compactor needed to achieve the desired level of compaction should be conducted at the start
of the grading phase of the project under the observation of a representative from Geolabs
Based on this testing program the number of passes may then be used as the field criterion
for adequate compaction For preliminary estimating purposes compaction may be
accomplished by using a large vibratory drum roller having a static weight of at least 15 tons
traveling at a speed of about 200 to 300 feet per minute The roller should pass over the fill
surface at least eight times for structural fill
Field density tests should be performed on fills and backfills in accordance with
ASTM D 1556 or ASTM D 2922 ASTM D 2922 results in a wet unit weight of soil and when
using this method ASTM D 3017 should be used to determine the moisture content of the soil
Calibration curves furnished along with the density gauge should be checked as described in
ASTM D 3017 Calibration checks of the density gauge should be performed at the beginning
of a job on each type of material encountered If ASTM D 2922 is used in place densities
should be checked by ASTM D 1556 at least once per day and one for every 10 density tests
performed in accordance with ASTM D 2922 test method In general field density tests should
be performed at the frequencies presented in the following table

Pavement Drainage
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due to
saturation of the aggregate subbase and or subgrade soils The basalt rock formation in this
area generally consists of vesicular porous pahoehoe lava The basalt rock formation in this
area of the Island of Hawaii drains well and may be considered to be highly permeable
Considering the permeable nature of the subgrade materials granular fill materials or basalt
rock pavement drainage for this project is not as critical As a minimum the pavement
surface should be sloped and drainage gradients should be maintained to carry surface water
off the pavement to appropriate drainage structures Surface water ponding should not be
allowed on the site during or after construction Where landscaping is planned adjacent to the
pavement areas we recommend that a subdrain system be constructed to collect excess
water from landscaping irrigation and to reduce the potential for migration of excessive
landscape water into the pavement section
GEOLABS INC
Subdrains should be provided in areas where seepage is observed during and after
grading or as recommended by the Engineer during construction Subdrains should conform
to the requirements of Section 605 Underdrains of the State of Hawaii Standard
Specifications for Road Bridge and Public Works Construction 1994 HSS As a minimum
the subdrain pipes should consist of 6 inch diameter perforated pipes conforming to
Subsection 706 12 of the HSS with the perforations facing down The subdrains should be
daylighted into appropriate drainage structures for proper discharge

to the design should be made
