This letter report presents the results of the field percolation testing performed in
support of the design of the Renovate Buildings 139 140 and 188 at Pohakuloa Training
Area project in the Pohakuloa area on the Island of Hawaii The project location and
general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The findings presented herein are intended to assist in the design of the storm
water runoff disposal and septic disposal systems only The findings presented herein are
subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project involves the renovation of three existing buildings Building Nos 139
140 and 188 at the U S Army Pohakuloa Training Area As part of the renovation it is
proposed to install a new drywell for disposal of storm water runoff and two new
leachfields for the disposal of wastewater effluent from new septic systems
To evaluate the subsurface percolation characteristics we drilled three boreholes to
depths ranging between about 11 5 and 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface and
conducted constant head and falling head infiltration tests in the borings In Boring Nos
101 and 103 which were tested in accordance with Hawaii Department of Health
procedures the percolation tests were conducted at a depth of about 5 feet below the
existing ground surface In Boring No 102 for the proposed drywell the test was
conducted at a depth of about 20 feet below the existing ground surface as a constant
head test The results from these tests are presented in Appendix C

In general the borings generally encountered a surface layer of loose to medium

FIELD INFILTRATION TESTING
Three field percolation tests were conducted at the site to evaluate the infiltration
characteristics of the subsurface materials encountered at the boring locations The tests
performed in Boring Nos 101 and 103 were conducted in accordance with Hawaii
Department of Health procedures and are intended to provide information for the design
of the proposed septic system leachfields The test performed in Boring No 102 was a
constant head permeability test and was intended to provide information for the design of
a drywell for the disposal of storm water runoff Detailed information of the tests is
presented in Appendix C

FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based upon the results of our field testing it is our opinion that the subsurface
materials encountered at the site are sufficiently permeable for the disposal of both
wastewater effluent and storm water runoff The falling head tests conducted for the septic
systems indicated a very rapid rate of infiltration on the order of about one minute per
inch While this rate is favorable for subsurface disposal the Hawaii DOH may require soil
replacement to reduce the rate of infiltration as a measure to protect ground water
However it is believed that ground water beneath the site is sufficiently deep on the order
of thousands of feet that contamination from septic systems should not be a
consideration

With respect to the proposed drywell for storm water management at the site our
constant head test indicates that the subsurface permeability is moderate and that a well
constructed drywell should be able to accept approximately 10 to 15 gallons per minute
per square foot of wall surface area in the well neglecting the bottom area
For both the septic system and well design a minimum factor of safety of 2 should
be applied to allow for clogging and variations in subsurface soil conditions
