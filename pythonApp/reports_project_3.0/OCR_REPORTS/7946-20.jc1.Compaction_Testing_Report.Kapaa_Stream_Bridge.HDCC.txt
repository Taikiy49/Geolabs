This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation and testing 
services performed during the earthwork construction of the Kapa’a Stream Bridge project. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project is located at MP 9.89 of Kuhio Highway (Route 56), in the District of 
Kawaihau on the Island of Kauai, Hawaii, approximately 300 feet north of the 
Mailihuna Road and Kuhio Highway intersection, as shown on Plate 1. The project 
generally consisted of removing and replacing the existing two span concrete bridge (150 
feet long and 38.5 feet wide). 
Construction of the new bridge involved installing a temporary bypass crossing 
using a Geosynthetic Reinforced Soil (GRS) wall system prior to demolition of the existing 
bridge. The replacement bridge consists of a single span concrete bridge about 190 feet 
long and 42 feet wide, supported on a single row of drilled shafts at the abutments.  
The project also involves roundabout improvements at the intersection of 
Kuhio Highway and Mailihuna Road, including relocating a private driveway and widening 
Mailihuna Road. Geosynthetic Reinforced Soil wall system is also used for the relocated 
private driveway and north side of Mailihuna Road for the widening. Locations of the 
replacement bridge and roundabout improvements are shown on the Site Plan, Plate 2. 
Geolabs provided geotechnical engineering services for the design of the 
temporary bypass bridge, construction of the drilled shaft foundation installation and 
testing, observation and testing of the site grading operation, including the temporary 
bypass bridge, the replacement bridge, installation of GRS walls, and construction of the 
roundabout improvements. This report summarizes our field observations and

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS 
Site grading for the project generally consisted of excavations up to about 10 to 
15 feet deep for removal of the existing bridge, construction of the new bridge structure, 
head walls, placement and compaction of fills and backfills, and pavement construction.  
At the onset of earthwork, the area within the limits of grading was cleared and 
grubbed of unsuitable surface materials.  During the site grading operations, samples of 
the on-site soils and imported fill materials were obtained and transported to our 
laboratory for testing.  The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were 
established in general accordance with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test 
results are summarized on Plate 3. 
The on-site fill and backfill materials generally consisted of on-site soils 
moisture-conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a 
minimum of 95 percent relative compaction. 
The imported borrow material was moisture-conditioned to near the optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction. Base 
Course Aggregate material imported from Kauai Aggregates used below the bridge, 
wingwalls, approach slabs, and roadways was moisture-conditioned to near the optimum 
moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction. 
Relative compaction refers to the in-place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage 
of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D1557. Optimum moisture is the 
water content (percentage by dry weight) corresponding to the maximum dry density. 
During the site grading operations, periodic field density tests were performed in 
general accordance with ASTM D6938 (Nuclear Gauge Method). Compaction test results 
are summarized on Plates 4.1 through 4.33.  Test locations and elevations were 
estimated using on-site survey stakes for reference.  These locations and elevations 
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used.  Areas 
that did not meet the required moisture and density conditions were reworked until the 
required minimum compaction was obtained or exceeded. 
Our services consisted of part-time, as-requested field observation and testing of 
the contractor’s grading activities, which included foundation subgrade preparation, fill 
and backfill placement, and confirmation of subgrade soil conditions exposed during the 
above grading operations. 
Based on our field representative’s observations and the referenced test results, it 
is our opinion that the site grading construction for the Kapaa Stream Bridge project, 
observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report, were performed in 
conformance with the general intent of the recommendations in the following references.
