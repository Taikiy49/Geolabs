INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our updated geotechnical
engineering evaluation performed for the proposed Kamooalii Cluster in
Kaneohe on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site
and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 A Soils
Engineering Report was issued in December 1980 for the subject site
Subsequently the proposed development has been changed and the current
evaluation was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated
March 25 1988

This report summarizes the updated geotechnical engineering
recommendations derived from our analysis based on the information
supplied to us a recent field exploration by hand augering and the
previous exploration results performed in December 1980 These
recommendations are intended for design input and are not intended for
construction estimation or bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is our understanding that the proposed development has been
revised from a subdivision project to the current cluster development
consisting of 27 single family residences
Based on a comparison between the recent and old topographic survey
maps the northeastern portion of the site was graded subsequent to 1980
The quality of the earthwork and fill compaction however is
questionable It is also our understanding that the current site grading
scheme for the northeastern portion of the site may require minor cuts and
fills along the present topography
Near the Kamooalii Stream additional fills up to about 5 to 6 feet
are anticipated to reach the finished grade Retaining walls are
anticipated for grade separation

conditions which have changed somewhat since December 1980 and to review
the subsurface conditions at the project site in order to update our

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the ends of Apuakea Street and Apiki
Street in Kaneohe area The central portion of the site slopes down
gently toward the northwest at a grade of approximately 18 horizontal to 1
vertical Near the western and southwestern boundaries of the site the
terrain slopes down steeply at a ratio up to 2 horizontal to 1 vertical
toward the Kamooalii Stream which is about 20 feet lower than the project
site

In the northeastern portion of the site a fill area from previous
grading with thickness up to about 6 feet exists A new townhouse
development was recently completed immediately outside the northern
boundary of the site A retaining wall with chain link fence on top
separates the two properties Across the Kamooalii Stream housing
development was under construction at the time of our field exploration
The current ground elevations vary from about 120 feet western
portion to 170 feet eastern portion above Mean Sea Level MSL The
project site is covered with grass and some vegetation
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The subject site is located roughly midway between the Likelike and
Pali Highways on the windward slope of the Koolau Range The area is part
of a deeply eroded and weathered section typified by thick alluvial
deposits overlying volcanic dike complex
Subsoil Conditions
Based on our exploration performed in December 1980 the subsoils
generally consist of stiff brown to reddish brown clay silt with some
partially decomposed boulders at various depths Groundwater was not
encountered during our field exploration
Our recent probing along the existing fill area in the northeastern
portion of the site disclosed that the fill material consisting primarily
of clayey silt was not properly compacted
The approximate locations for previous borings and current probings
are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our recent field exploration disclosed that the existing fill area in
the northeastern portion of the site was not properly compacted and is not
suitable to serve as a foundation material The existing fill thickness
ranges from 1 foot northern portion to 6 feet northeastern portion
In addition it appeared that previous vegetation and rootlets were not
cleared during earlier filling and were encountered in our current field
probing The approximate general extent of the fill area is delineated on
the Site Plan Plate 2
Over excavation and recompaction of the existing fill material are
recommended so that a properly engineered fill may be obtained for the
cluster development The part of the material contaminated with previous
vegetation should be disposed of properly off site
Site Grading
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared and grubbed of all vegetation rubbish
and deleterious materials The resulting grub and spoil material should
be disposed of properly off site Any soft weak or yielding areas
disclosed during the clearing operations should be over excavated to firm
ground and the resulting excavations should be backfilled with
well compacted engineered fill
Subsequent to clearing and grubbing the area within the grading
limits should be scarified to a depth of 6 inches moisture conditioned to
near optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimum of 90 maximum
dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Fills and backfills should be placed in 10 inch maximum loose lifts
and compacted to a minimum of 90 of the ASTM D 1557 maximum dry density

The existing fill area along the northeastern portion as
approximately delineated on Site Plan Plate 2 should be over excavated
down to the original firm ground first The material contaminated with
previous vegetation should be disposed of properly off site The material
should then be placed in 10 inch thick loose level lifts and compacted to
a minimum of 90 maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Any rubble or boulders encountered during the over excavation and
scarification which exceed eight 8 inches in maximum dimension should be
removed and discarded Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during
the recompaction operations should be over excavated and backfilled with
select material placed in 10 inch thick loose level lifts compacted to a
minimum of 90 maximum dry density as described above
Cut and fill slope ratio of 2H 1V are recommended Fill slopes
should be constructed by overfilling and cutting back to the design grade
Care should be exercised in the grading of cut slopes to prevent
overcutting of the slopes
Imported select fill material should be non expansive less than 3
inches in size and have a minimum CBR value of 15 when tested in
accordance with ASTM D 1883
Specifications for standard earthwork and grading are attached in
Appendix C of this report
Building Foundations
It is anticipated that spread footing foundations will be used for
the proposed residential structures
For the design of foundations bearing on the in situ stiff soils or
fill compacted in accordance with the recommendations presented herein an
allowable bearing capacity of 2500 p s f may be used The value is for
total dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third for temporary
wind and seismic loads

The recommended minimum footing width is 12 inches with a minimum
embedment of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade on
sloping areas the footings should be embedded an additional depth to
provide a 6 foot minimum set back distance measured from the outer edge
of the footings to the slope face
Minimal settlements are anticipated from the light structural loads
Slab On Grade
The on grade floor slabs should be placed over a 4 inch thick cushion
layer of gravel size material such as 3B Fine crushed rock to provide
uniform support A moisture barrier membrane is recommended between the
slab and the cushion layer to reduce the possibility of moisture
infiltration to the floor coverings
The thickened edges of the on grade slabs should be embedded a
minimum of 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade The bottom
of all thickened edge excavations should be wetted and kept moist until
concrete placement The backfill against the concrete slab should be
adequately compacted and graded to shed water away from the slab
perimeter
Retaining Walls
It is anticipated that retaining walls will be used to provide grade
separation near the Kamooalii Stream The guidelines for design of
retaining walls are as follows

Walls unrestrained at the top 40 p s f equivalent
active condition level fluid pressure
backfill per foot of depth
Walls restrained at the top 60 p s f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
level backfill per foot of depth

2 An allowable bearing capacity of up to 2500 p s f is
recommended for foundations bearing on the stiff on site
alluvial soils or compacted fill
3 A passive earth resistance of 300 p s f per foot of depth
may be used for the wall footing on level areas The passive
pressure must be reduced for walls located on slopes
4 A base friction factor of 0 4 may also be used to determine
the resistance against sliding of the wall
5 The wall foundations on relatively flat areas should be
embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest adjacent
grade For sloping ground the footing should be extended
down to obtain a minimum 8 foot set back measured from the
outside edge of the footing measured horizontally to the slope
face
6 A subsurface drainage system subdrains and or weephole
should be utilized behind the walls to minimize the build up
of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls Subdrains should be
constructed properly to drain water away from the wall
Select granular backfill material is recommended behind the
retaining wall to minimize excessive compaction required

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are
based in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction
If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate
the recommendations given in this report
The location of the field data points were approximately
determined by taping from the structures indicated on the plan
undated provided to us Elevations of the data points were
approximately determined by estimation from the plan The physical
location and elevation of the field data points should be considered
accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown
on profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between
field data points and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
times given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this
report This data has been reviewed and interpretations made in the
formulation of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation
may occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other
factors

VI Placement and Compaction of Fill
A
The fill shall be placed in horizontal layers which when
loose will not exceed ten inches 10 in thickness Each
layer shall be spread uniformly and blade mixed to attain
uniformity of material and water content within each layer
Additional fill material shall not be placed on any fill
layer which has not been properly compacted Subsequent
removal of any unacceptable layer shall be done at the
Contractor s expense
Rock fragments or boulders less than eight inches 8 in
diameter may be utilized provided they are not concentrated
in any one area and that there is sufficient fine grained
material to fill the voids between the rocks
Rocks greater than eight inches 8 in diameter shall be
removed from the fill unless specifically approved for
placement in the fill by the Geotechnical Engineer
All fill shall be compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of
the maximum dry density established by ASTM Test Method
D 1557 78 unless otherwise specified in the Geotechnical
Engineer s Report
