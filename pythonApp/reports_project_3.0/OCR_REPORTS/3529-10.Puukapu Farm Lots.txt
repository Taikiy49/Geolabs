Background Information
The project site is located to the south of Kahilu Road near the town of Waimea in the
District of South Kohala on the island of Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 The Department of Hawaiian Home Lands is developing the site for
agricultural residential use The subject project consists of the construction of approximately
25 000 lineal feet of roadways and infrastructure such as water drainage and irrigation lines
The approximate subdivision layout is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on our field exploration the project site is generally underlain by a surficial layer
of volcanic ash soils consisting of stiff silts and medium dense silty sands in a relatively friable
state The ash materials are underlain by highly weathered basalt materials saprolites

Earthwork Observation and Testing
The earthwork operations for the project were performed by Oahu Construction Co
Ltd At the on set of earthwork areas within the contract grading limits were generally cleared
and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious materials Materials generated from the clearing
operations were disposed of at a designated area near the site Following the clearing and
grubbing operations the excavated subgrades of the roadways were moisture conditioned
and compacted with a vibratory drum roller
The fills and backfills needed for the earthwork generally consisted of on site materials
generated from the excavations and subbase and base course materials imported from West
Hawaii Concrete Samples of the fill and backfill materials utilized during construction were
obtained and transported to our office for laboratory testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
American Society of Testing and Material ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified
Proctor The test results are summarized on Plate 3 of this report
During the fill placement operations the fill materials were generally placed in thin lifts
brought to above the optimum moisture and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the
maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557
Modified Proctor Roadway subgrades were also compacted to 90 percent relative
compaction The subbase and base course materials were compacted to at least 95 percent
relative compaction Periodic field density tests were generally taken at every station about
100 foot intervals during the fill placement and subgrade preparation operations to confirm
the degree of compaction and moisture content of the fill material achieved during roadway
construction
A total of 1 702 field density tests were performed using the nuclear probe method in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designations D 2922 and D 3017 The results of the
compaction tests performed during the fill placement operations are summarized on Plates 4 1
through 4 78 of this report These tests were performed on the compacted subgrades and
engineered fills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes where available These locations and elevations
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas
assessed to be unsatisfactory were generally reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained
Based on the results of the referenced tests and observations of our field
representatives during our site visits it is our professional opinion that the earthwork
operations tested and observed by Geolabs Hawaii for the roadways were performed in

Limitations
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities occurring
simultaneously on a construction site Therefore portions of the earthwork at the site
occurred without our observation It should be recognized that there are variations in the
accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork
construction and materiais Our services were performed in accordance with generally
accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied

guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of
the work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of
when they are found
