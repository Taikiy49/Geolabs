PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the existing Kahana Valley State Park in the Kahana
area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided we understand that three existing comfort stations
are experiencing problems with their leach fields which provide disposal of sewage effluents
from the comfort stations Several remedial measures have been considered to mitigate and
remedy the current deficiencies with the leach fields for sewage effluent disposal In lieu of
rehabilitating the existing leach fields we understand that it was decided to construct a new
leach field for the disposal of sewage effluent from the comfort stations The existing leach
fields will be used as a backup measure once the new leach field is constructed Therefore
it was desired to conduct field percolation tests at the proposed leach field location in order
to obtain data pertaining to the preliminary design of the sewage disposal system The
approximate limits of the proposed leach field are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling one boring designated as
Boring No 1 to a depth of about 8 5 feet below the existing ground surface to evaluate the
subsurface conditions at the proposed leach field location Three shallow borings
designated as Boring Nos 2 through 4 were drilled to depths of about 3 feet below the
existing ground surface for percolation testing The approximate locations of the borings are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration across the proposed leach field area encountered a
thin surface layer of granular materials consisting of loose to medium dense silty gravel with
sand on the order of about 3 feet thick This surface layer appears to be absent in
Boring No 4 which was drilled towards the north side of the proposed leach field site Below
the surface granular soils our deep boring generally encountered stiff clayey silts with sands
and gravel extending to a depth of about 7 feet below the ground surface Auger refusal was
encountered at this depth suggesting the presence of boulders or basalt rock formation
Groundwater was not encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field
exploration Due to the proximity of the project site to the shoreline the water levels
measured in the borings during our field exploration were likely affected by tidal fluctuations
In addition the water levels at the project site may be influenced by seasonal precipitation
and storm surge conditions Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in our field
exploration are presented on the Boring Log Legend and Log of Boring Plates 3 and 4

sewage disposal system A total of three field percolation tests were performed at selected

Field Percolation Testing
Field percolation testing was performed in general accordance with the State of
Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten States Standards to evaluate the permeability of the
subsurface soils for wastewater disposal The percolation tests were performed by augering
4 0 inch diameter boreholes to depths of about 3 feet below the existing ground surface
Approximately one inch of gravel was then placed at the bottom of the boreholes to
protect the bottom of the holes from scouring and sediments The holes were filled with
water and allowed to soak overnight prior to the commencement of testing During testing
the holes were carefully filled with water to a minimum depth of 6 inches above the gravel
pack The time intervals and water drops were then recorded to provide data upon which the
calculation of the percolation tests was based
Test Results and Discussions
Based on the percolation tests conducted the percolation rates range from about 2 to
4 minutes per inch at test locations B 2 and B 3 to 20 minutes per inch at test location B 4
Results of the individual percolation tests are presented on Plates 5 through 7 of this report
Based on our field exploration it appears that the proposed leach field location is
generally underlain by clayey silt soils extending from 3 to 7 feet below the existing ground
surface Based on the subsoil conditions encountered and the percolation test results
obtained we believe that the slower percolation rate of 20 minutes per inch should be used
in the design of the proposed leach field if desired Due to the potential variability of the
subsoil conditions the absorption capacity of the disposal system should be confirmed by
additional percolation tests during construction
LIMITATIONS
The findings and discussions submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings and percolation tests Variations of subsoil
conditions between and beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear
evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the findings and discussions provided in this report
