INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration for the
proposed Olai Street Improvements in the Campbell Industrial Park on the island of Oahu
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2700 00 Page 2
Hawaii The general location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and engineering recommendations resulting
from the field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analysis These
recommendations are intended for foundation design input and are not intended to form
the basis for preparation of construction cost estimates or for bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to improve the existing Olai Street in Campbell Industrial Park by
widening the pavement from eighteen 18 to forty four 44 feet and installing
underground utilities The portion to be improved extends westerly and southwesterly
from the intersection with Hanua Street to the end of the roadway a distance of
approximately 1 400 linear feet
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this study was to provide geotechnical engineering
recommendations pertinent to the design of the pavement section and utility placements
for the roadway improvements The recommendations presented herein are based on
the geotechnical information obtained from borings drilled and laboratory testing
performed for this exploration
The work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
to Hawaii Pacific Engineers Inc The scope of work included but was
not limited to the following

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The project site is located in the Campbell Industrial Park and presently is a paved
roadway 20 feet in width and approximately 1400 feet in length running westerly to
southwesterly from its intersection with Hanua Street The ground surface adjacent to the
pavement on either side of the roadway where the expansion is to occur is sandy to silty
coral covered with grass The general location of the project site is shown on the
attached Project Location Map Plate 1
The topography of the project site is generally level with current ground elevations
ranging from about seven to nine feet above Mean Sea Level MSL Presently there are
no utilities in the area with the exception of overhead electric lines and a subsurface PVC
irrigation line which runs along and outside of the pavement and services shrubbery along
the roadway
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling seven 7 borings to
depths of between six 6 and sixteen and one half 16 5 feet below the existing ground
surface and the collection of three surface samples The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings disclosed that the site is underlain by coral formations with
some previously placed surface fills consisting of sandy to silty coral gravel
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2700 00 Page 5
The roadway between Station 0 00 the intersection with Hanua Street and Station
5 00 is underlain in some areas by localized soft areas and small cavities Cavities were
encountered in Boring Nos 1A and 2A at depths of two 2 five 5 and five and one half
5 5 feet below the existing ground surface These cavities were roughly one 1 to two
2 feet thick with loose debris at their bottoms Below this depth in this portion of the
roadway the subsurface conditions appear to be dense to very dense coral algal
formations
The roadway between Stations 5 00 and 14 00 appears to be underlain by dense
to very dense coral algal formations to the maximum depths drilled Cavities or soft areas
were not encountered in the borings in this portion of the project
Groundwater was encountered in the drilled borings at depths of 7 0 to 9 2 feet
below the existing ground surface during our field exploration Based on the estimated
elevations at the boring locations the measured groundwater level appears to be at or
Close to Mean Sea Level MSL Due to the proximity of the site to the ocean it is
anticipated that the groundwater level will fluctuate in response to tides and storm surges
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering point of view provided that the following recommendations are followed
Site Preparations
The area adjacent to the roadway to be paved should be cleared of all vegetation
and deleterious materials The exposed subgrade should be scarified to a depth of twelve
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2700 00 Page 6
inches and recompacted to 95 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78
Any additional fills if needed should consist of on site well graded coral gravel with
a maximum particle dimension of one inch or select borrow materials approved in
advance by the geotechnical engineer The materials should be placed in maximum loose
lift heights of six inches and compacted to 95 of its maximum dry density as determined
by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Excavation
It is Our Opinion that excavation for infrastructure installation in the in situ coral
formation encountered at the site will require hard excavation such as ripping and or
hoerams We anticipated that the material will have a volume increase on the order of
5 to 10 per cent when excavated and reused as fill material The excavated material may
require some processing e g removal of breaking of fragments greater than twelve 12
inches in maximum diameter prior to its use as fill material The material will require
further processing to removal or reduce the fragments to less than three 3 inches in size
prior to its being used as backfill
Shoring and or bracing of the excavations should conform to the applicable safety
requirements The design of the shoring bracing should be left to the discretion of the
contractor performing the work However excavations in coral formation will most likely
stand unsupported for temporary construction purposes
Utilities
Excavations for the proposed water and drain lines are anticipated to be in
materials ranging from localized soft to loose coralline materials to very dense coral
formations Due to the possibility that portions of the utilities will be founded on soft or
GEOLABS HAWAIi
W O 2700 00 Page 7
loose materials it is recommended that a geotechnical engineer observe the excavations
at the invert elevation to determine if the materials encountered at the proposed bearing
surfaces are suitable to support the proposed structures In addition the exposed
bearing surfaces for the utilities should be probed by the excavation equipment backhoe
bucket in the presence of the geotechnical engineer to determine the competency of the
materials encountered The bearing surfaces should be struck with force by the backhoe
bucket in suspect areas to collapse and or expose underlying cavities
If the materials encountered at the invert elevations for the utilities are found to be
inadequate to support the proposed structures the area should be overexcavated to
competent in situ soils rock The resulting depression should then be backfilled with
select granular materials compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density
as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The utilities should be placed and backfilled in conformance with the Standard
Specifications for Public Works Construction for the City and County of Honolulu
Pavement Sections
The anticipated traffic volume for the roadway was not available however it was
observed that buses and trucks utilize this portion of the road
Based on our laboratory CBR testing and assumed traffic of 40 trucks and buses
per day the following pavement section is recommended
3 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
9 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
on Compacted Subgrade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2700 00 Page 8
The above recommended pavement design is based on the assumption that the
roadway sections will be adequately drained
Drainage
The finished grade outside the pavements should be shaped to shed water away
from the roadway and to avoid ponding Excessive landscape watering near pavement
areas should also be avoided
Design Review
Drawings and specifications for site grading utility and roadway construction
should be forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for review and written
comments prior to advertisement for bids This review is needed to determine adherence
to the earthwork recommendations given herein If this review is not made the
geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the
recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the utility excavations and backfilling be observed by a
competent geotechnical engineer during construction to see that adequate bearing has
been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
exploration
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved by
the soils engineer prior to being transported to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the above
observations

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from field borings Variations of subsoil conditions as indicated by
the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings are approximate having been determined by
taping from objects indicated on the topographic survey maps by Walter P Thompson
dated Elevations of the data points were estimated from the same plan
Their accuracy should be considered only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between different soil strata and may also denote a gradual
transition

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling seven 7 borings
to depths between six 6 and sixteen and one half 16 5 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were
drilled with a truck mounted drill rig
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who continuously monitored the drilling
operations The classifications were further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in
the laboratory All soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are presented on the
Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 7
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch standard split
spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch D split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling
30 inches The blow counts used to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on
the Boring Logs at the appropriate sample depths
Surface soil samples were collected at three 3 locations along the roadway and
returned to our laboratory where CBR testing was performed to obtain data for
recommendations pertinent to the pavement design
