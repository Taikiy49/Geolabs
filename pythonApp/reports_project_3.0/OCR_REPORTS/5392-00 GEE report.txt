PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the existing Paia Elementary School in the Paia
area on the Island of Maui Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided we understand that there are six existing
cesspools at the school site Due to regulatory requirements we understand that the
existing cesspools will need to be removed and replaced with a septic tank and leach field
system for the disposal of wastewater We understand that an assessment has been
made and three leach fields designated as Site Nos 1 through 3 are proposed at the
locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general we anticipate that the project site is underlain by fill materials
overlying alluvial deposits at shallow depths Based on previous project experience in
the vicinity the infiltration rates of the near surface soils may not be favorable for the
disposal of wastewater using a leach field In order to evaluate the permeability of the
subsurface soils and the disposal capacities of the proposed leach field sites for

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program at the proposed leach field sites consisted of drilling
and sampling six borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 6 to depths ranging from
about 11 5 to 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of
the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration encountered surface fill materials on the order of
about 1 to 2 feet thick consisting of stiff to very stiff clayey silt soils The surface fills were
generally underlain by alluvial deposits consisting of very stiff to hard clays with varying
amounts of gravel and cobbles extending to the maximum depth explored of
approximately 16 5 feet below the existing ground surface Groundwater was not
encountered in the drilled borings at the time of our field exploration
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered during our field exploration are
presented on the Boring Log Legend and Logs of Borings Plates 3 through 9
FINDINGS AND DISCUSSIONS
We understand that on site sewage treatment and disposal by septic tank and
leach field method are planned within the Paia Elementary School site Therefore we
have conducted field percolation tests to obtain data pertaining to the preliminary design of
the sewage disposal system at the proposed leach field sites A total of nine field
percolation tests were performed at selected locations across the project site The
approximate locations of the field percolation tests designated as P 1 through P 9 are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Field Percolation Testing
Field percolation testing was performed in general accordance with the State of
Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten States Standards to evaluate the permeability of the
subsurface soils for wastewater disposal The percolation tests were performed by drilling
4 0 inch diameter boreholes to depths ranging from about 5 to 15 feet below the existing
ground surface The boreholes were presoaked for a minimum of 4 hours prior to the
percolation testing
Approximately one inch of gravel was then placed at the bottom of the boreholes to
protect the bottom of the holes from scouring and sediments During testing the holes
were carefully filled with water to a minimum depth of 12 inches above the gravel pack
The time intervals and water drops were then recorded to provide data upon which
calculation of the percolation tests were based

Based on the test results obtained it appears that Site No 1 generally is
underlain by relatively low permeability soils with percolation rates in excess of
120 minutes per inch Therefore the percolation characteristics of the subsurface
materials at Site No 1 may not be favorable for the construction of the new leach field
due to the extremely slow percolation rates of the subsurface materials encountered at
the Site No 1 location As an alternative considerations may be given to the use of
seepage pits for wastewater disposal at this location
Conversely relatively good percolation rates were obtained for the tests conducted
at Site Nos 2 and 3 with percolation rates generally between 5 and 20 minutes per inch
Based on the generally favorable test results obtained we believe that consideration may
be given to construction of the new leach fields in these two locations The new leach
fields at these two areas should be embedded a minimum of 5 feet below the existing
ground surface A percolation rate of 10 and 20 minutes per inch may be used to size the
new leach fields planned at Site Nos 2 and 3 respectively Due to the variability of the

LIMITATIONS
The findings and discussions submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings and percolation tests Variations of subsoil
conditions between and beyond the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to reevaluate the findings and discussions provided in
this report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping from the features shown on the General Plan provided by
Wilson Okamoto Corporation on The physical locations of the borings
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition
Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the graphic
representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed and
interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall tides temperature and other factors

constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is recommended to
accommodate these possible extra costs
