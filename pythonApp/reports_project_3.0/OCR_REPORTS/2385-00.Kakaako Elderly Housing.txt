INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Kakaako Elderly Housing project in
the Kakaako area of metropolitan Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1 The exploration was performed in general
accordance with our proposal dated August 7 1989
This report summarizes our findings from the field exploration and
our geotechnical engineering recommendations were derived fron our
analyses based on the preliminary data obtained during the exploration
These recommendations are intended for foundation design input and may not
be accurate for final construction cost estimation or for construction
bidding purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to develop the site as a mixed use facility with
commercial and residential spaces The present plans are to construct a
25 story high rise for residential purposes and four 4 levels of parking
and commercial space It is our understanding that the lower parking
level will be partially below grade and it has been set at about elevation
2 5 feet MSL
Site grading involving about 3 5 feet of excavation below the
existing grade for the parking structure is anticipated Based on the
project structural engineer the anticipated maximum column loads are on
the order of 1000 kips and 400 kips for the proposed high rise and parking
structure respectively
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface soil conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading basement
excavation retaining walls and foundation design of the project

W O 2385 00 Page 3
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal to Housing
Finance and Development Corporation dated August 7 1989 The scope of
work is limited to the following
Drilling and sampling six 6 borings to depths of 48 5 to 139 0
feet below the existing ground surface
Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual
observations of the surrounding areas buildings
Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity
Laboratory testing of selected samples cbtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered
Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the
purpose of developing site grading basement excavation

Preparation of this report presenting the findings and design

Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Exploration Program
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling six 6
borings to depths ranging from 48 5 to 139 0 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
Due to the existing structures at the project site our field borings
were generally located along the outside of the proposed building
footprint We believe that these borings are representative of the
general subsurface conditions at the site and are valid to develop
preliminary geotechnical engineering recommendations for the project
However it is recommended that additional borings be drilled inside the
proposed high rise building footprint after demolition of the existing
structures to verify more accurately the complex coral subsoil conditions
Subsoil Conditions
Based on the available borings the subsoils at the project site
generally consist of about five feet of an upper sandy fill material
overlying medium dense silty sand deposits In the mauka portion of the
site a coral ledge was encountered at about 10 feet below the existing
grade In the makai portion of the site however the coral ledge was
encountered at a lower depth of about 20 feet It appears that this upper
coral ledge has an erratic thickness and it generally extends to about 20
to 25 foot depth below the existing ground surface

It appears that a second lowlying coral ledge was generally
encountered at about 30 to 40 foot depth at the site Based on the few
borings this ledge appears to be thicker than the upper ledge moderately
to well cemented and generally extends to about 47 foot depth
Below about 50 foot depth the borings generally encountered medium
dense to loose coral fragments and silty sand extending down to a depth of
about 90 feet where another thin ledge was encountered The borings then
encountered primarily silty sand and coral fragments to the maximum depths
drilled

Groundwater Level
Groundwater was encountered at depths ranging from 5 0 to 7 0 feet
below the existing ground surface during our field exploration Based on
the topographic map provided to us the groundwater level fluctuated
between elevations 1 2 and 1 1 feet MSL during the time of our field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on the exploration results it appears that pile foundations
should be considered for the proposed Kakaako Elderly Housing project
because of the anticipated heavy structural loads Due to the erratic
nature of the upper coral ledge encountered in the borings it is our
opinion that for the 25 story high rise structure the concrete piles
should be end bearing on the underlying more competent coral ledge
generally encountered at about 30 to 40 feet below the existing ground

surface For the four 4 level parking and commercial structure it
appears that there is a possibility that shorter piles may bear on the
upper shallow coral formation depending on the results of the addition

Additional Exploration Program
The current exploration program consisted of borings located along
the outside of the proposed building footprint Although the available
borings generally encountered an upper coral ledge at depth of about 10
feet in the mauka portion and of about 20 feet in the makai portion of the

We believe there is a high possibility that the mauka portion of the

Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be cleared of vegetation rubbish demolished footings
slabs and pavements and other deleterious materials The resulting
grub and spoil material should be disposed of properly off site
Prior to pile driving and foundation work it is anticipated that the
site will be cleared and excavated with all necessary excavation
support systems such as sheetpile shoring bracing properly installed
Basement Excavation
Basement excavation up to about 3 5 feet below the existing grade is
anticipated for the parking structure area The excavation in the
upper pavement and medium dense sandy fill layer is anticipated to be
relatively easy

interlocking sheetpile shoring bracing systems to protect the

Pile Driving Criteria
The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering a rated energy of
at least 32 000 foot pounds The hammer should be equipped with an
energy level control so that driving within the loose deposits may be
controlled at a reduced energy to minimize the tensile stresses
development within the piles which could cause damage cracking to
the piles
Additional pile driving criteria are as follows
1 The driving process should be continuous without interruption for
each pile A pile may be rejected or its design capacity
modified when the driving process is interrupted for more than
four 4 hours
2 During easy driving when the pile tip is still within the loose
sandy soil the hammer stroke should be carefully controlled
reduced energy so that excessive tensile stresses are not
induced in the pile

objectives
1
To examine the drivability of piles with the use of one or more
selected hammers
To more accurately determine production pile order lengths in the
generally variable coralline deposits and to delineate different
areas requiring different production pile lengths

actual foundation system later
When the final foundation plan is available the project geotechnical
engineer should be consulted for selection of the test pile locations
The test piles should be casted ten 10 feet longer than the estimated

APPENDIX
Wave Equation Analysis of Pile Driving
A A A SR A A Et kt tae EH
The wave equation analysis of pile driving was performed using
Program WEAPG which is a microcomputer adaption of the program WEAP
originally written by Goble and Associates for the Federal Highway
Administration to solve pile drivability problems
The program models hammer pile soil system by representing the pile
and driving system by a series of masses and springs The soil is modeled
by a spring and dashpot attached to each mass The program output
provides ultimate resistance and maximum compressive and tensile stresses
in the piles as a function of hammer blowcount
The primary objectives of the analysis are
1 to analyze the driving stresses in the pile
2 to determine the suitable driving system to be used for the pile
driving and
3 to predict the ultimate pile capacity of the pile
The analysis was performed for driving 14 inch square prestressed
concrete piles with a DEIMAG D 30 hammer The relationships of ultimate
capacity and hammer blowcounts commonly known as the bearing graph are
presented on Plate C 1 together with input information The maximum
compressive stresses are presented on Plate C 2
