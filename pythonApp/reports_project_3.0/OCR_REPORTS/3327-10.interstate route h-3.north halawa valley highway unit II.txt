SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed North Halawa Highway of the new Interstate Route H 3 highway
system will be located in North Halawa Valley Halawa Valley is located northeast of
Pearl Harbor between Aiea and Aliamanu Crater Halawa Stream flows through the
valley and drains into Pearl Harbor
The Unit II portion of the new highway system between Stations 480 00 and
540 00 is one of the middle sections of the proposed alignment between the Halawa
Interchange and Trans Koolau Tunnel The sides of the valley are relatively steep and
the valley floor is narrow North Halawa Stream takes a winding path along the valley
floor The rugged terrain is generally covered by dense vegetation
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
In general the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings drilled for the
North Halawa Valley Highway may be categorized into four groups
Koolau Basalt
Koolau Basalts consist of thinly bedded basaltic flows with an average flow unit
thickness of 10 feet or less The flows occur both in the a a and pahoehoe forms with
transitions between the two forms The lavas are generally vesicular with jointing
resulting from contraction during cooling and solidification of the molten rock The
Koolau Basalts grade from extremely weathered saprolite to relatively unweathered
rock with increasing depth
Saprolite consists of soil materials derived from in situ chemical weathering of
the parent basaltic rocks These soils range from structureless residual clayey silts or
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3327 10 Page 7
silty clays to saprolite extremely weathered basalt which are soils that retain the relict
structure of the parent rock such as joints vesicles and or flow structures
Older Alluvium
These materials are deposited during the later Pleistocene and are poorly sorted
alluvium with significant amounts of boulders In situ weathering and induration have
altered some of the materials into a highly weathered poorly consolidated
conglomerate Core samples of these older alluvium are typified by slightly to highly
weathered boulders cobbles and gravel in a matrix of cemented sandy silt
Recent Alluvium
Recent alluvium is generally composed of terrestrial sediments deposited by the
movement of water and consists mostly of silts and clays with gravel cobbles and
boulders The deposits are poorly sorted highly variable in consistency density and
were encountered in the upper portion of our borings
Man Made Fill
This unit consists of miscellaneous materials placed for site grading and part of
the construction of the pioneer and access roads for the North Halawa Valley Highway
project

modulus of subgrade reaction in the direction of loading A factor of 0 25 times the
horizontal modulus of subgrade reaction was used based on a drilled shaft spacing of

Proximity to Halawa Stream Wet Environment
eee eet ere tete Det et ete
Some of the pier locations are located adjacent to the Halawa Stream and nearly
at the bottom of Halawa Stream The general area is wet with frequent rains
With heavy rainstorms the water level of the Halawa Stream rises rapidly and
could flood the construction site causing delays We recommend that some
protection measures be implemented by the contractor to reduce the potential
of flooding of the construction areas due to heavy rain water runoff
The weather conditions may also pose a potential severe impact on the
construction scheduling and the quality of the shaft construction The possibility
of a heavy storm and high water levels in Halawa Stream during or immediately
after construction of a drilled shaft will need to be considered
Excavation
Based on the existing topography and the anticipated bottom elevations of the
pier caps excavations up to approximately 20 feet below the existing ground
surface may be required for the construction of the pier caps An open cut
excavation may be feasible subject to archaeological and environmental
requirements If an open cut excavation is elected temporary side slopes of one
horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V or flatter may be used The side slopes should
be adequately protected against erosion especially during heavy rains which
may flood the excavation to reduce potential instability of the side slopes The
contractor should retain a qualified geotechnical engineer to evaluate the method
of excavation and or shoring of the excavation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3327 10 Oo Page 14
It should be emphasized that it is the responsibility of the contractor to provide
a safe and stable excavation for the construction workers It should be noted
that the abundant cobbles and boulders in the stream bed and in the excavated
materials may pose a falling boulder hazard within the excavation Therefore
shoring of the sides of the excavation may be prudent
The excavation shoring system used must comply with all applicable safety
requirements The adequacy and safety of the shoring installation should be
made the sole responsibility of the contractor His representative who should
be required to be present on site during excavation and construction work will
have the best opportunity to promptly observe any changing conditions during
construction such as unforeseen subsoils unexpectedly high groundwater table
inappropriate construction sequences or techniques etc which may affect
shoring stability
However it must be noted that some movement of the shoring system and the
adjacent ground may still occur due to changes in earth stresses during
excavation The magnitude of movement is difficult to accurately predict due to
the complexity of the stress changes The magnitude depends greatly upon
workmanship and also on the subsoil conditions the size of the excavation and
the rate of excavation
Therefore it is important to realize that the excavation shoring should be
installed properly and as early as practical if necessary and that the adjacent
ground should be continuously monitored for cracks dips and or other
indications of movements with instruments such as settlement markers and
inclinometers Such monitoring is particularly critical in areas adjacent to existing
facilities and utilities

The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer geologist who monitored the drilling operations
on a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and

Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
fornia sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed
to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18 inch drive are shown on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of rock materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM Standard Practice
D 2113 83 Diamond Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling
method which uses a hollow bit to cut into the rock formation The rock material left
in the hollow core of the bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt such as if 3 7 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and
would be shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 74
The Rock Quality Designation RQD is also a subjective guide to the relative
quality of rock masses RQD is defined as the percentage of the core run that is sound
material in excess of 4 inches in length without any discontinuities discounting any
drilling induced fractures or breaks If 2 5 feet of sound material is recovered from a
5 0 foot core run the RQD would be 50 percent and would be shown on the Logs of
Borings as RQD 50 Generally the following is used to describe the relative quality
of the rock based on the Practical Handbook of Physical Properties of Rocks and
Minerals

Laboratory Testing

Two Atterberg Limits tests were performed on selected samples of the soils to

basalt deposit The test results and stress strain curves are presented on Plates B 2
