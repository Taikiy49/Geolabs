In accordance with your request we have performed a field

This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATION
It is proposed to relocate an existing 10 feet diameter by 12 feet
high sugar tank at the site The approximate weight of the tank is 80
kips It is also planned to construct a new milk tank at the present
location of the sugar tank which will be relocated The milk tank will be
approximately 12 feet in diameter and weigh about 186 kips

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this exploration was to obtain generalized subsurface
data for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations
pertinent to the design of the foundation for the proposed tanks
The scope of the exploration included drilling and sampling two 2
borings each to a depth of 51 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Also included in the scope were laboratory testing of samples obtained
engineering evaluation of the field and laboratory data development of
design recommendations and report preparation
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The Foremost Dairies is located on the makai side of Kamehameha
Highway west of the Oahu Community Correctional Center
The area is generally flat and presently paved with concrete The
new sugar tank location is situated near the southeastern corner of the
existing main office building The existing sugar tank location of new
milk tank is located near the northeastern corner of the main building
There is an existing drainage easement which extends below the existing
sugar tank site It is believed that the drain line within the easement
is about 6 feet deep

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration encountered poor subsoil conditions below both
tank sites Excessive settlements would occur if the tanks were supported
on shallow footings or a mat foundation Therefore we recommend that
pile foundations be used to support the new tanks
Pile Foundation
The piles would develop their load capacity primarily from frictional
resistance or adhesion between the piles and the stiff alluvial soil
encountered below about 35 0 feet at the new milk tank and 26 5 feet at
the new sugar tank site
GEOLABS HAWAII

Dk 501
Twelve inch square pre stressed concrete piles extending into the
stiff alluvium are recommended Pile lengths of 40 feet are recommended
for both tanks The piles should be driven with a hammer delivering a
minimum of about 24 000 foot pounds of energy and to a resistance of 2
blows per inch for the last 3 inches
For the above recommendations an allowable pile capacity of 20 tons
per pile may be used Pile settlements on the order of less than
one quarter of an inch are anticipated
To resist the lateral loads the passive earth pressure around the
pile caps and tie beams may be used We recommend an equivalent fluid
pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth The backfill
against the pile caps or tie beams should be properly backfilled with
select materials compacted to a dry density of at least 95 of the maximm
density as determined by ASTM D 1557 78 test method
The piles for the new milk tank should be set back a minimm of 2
feet away from the existing drain pipe below the site
The pile driving should be monitored by a geotechnical engineer to
see that they are driven into competent bearing formation
It should be noted that the friction piles could lose some of their
load carrying capacity if the adjacent properties are filled thereby
causing ground settlements and downdrag on the piles
Design Review

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the
design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
upon information obtained from the borings Variations of conditions
between the borings may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the borings were determined by taping from
structures indicated on the topographic survey Elevations of the borings
were approximately determined by interpolation from elevation points on
the topographic survey map The physical location and elevation of the
borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
boring points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and
as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings
and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
