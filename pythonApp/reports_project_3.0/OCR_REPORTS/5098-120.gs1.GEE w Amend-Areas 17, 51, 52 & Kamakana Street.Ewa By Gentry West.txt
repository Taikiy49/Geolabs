SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
In general the site is underlain by moderately expansive silty clay soils over
coralline materials From a geotechnical engineering standpoint our opinion is that the site
is suitable for the residential development provided our recommendations are followed
Particular attention should be given to the procedures to reduce the effects of the
expansive soils
Based on our laboratory tests the near surface clayey soils exhibit moderate
shrink swell potential when subjected to fluctuations in the soil moisture content
Therefore special attention should be given to the subgrade preparation and
slab on grade design To reduce the potential for distress to the future residential
structures as a result of swelling of the on site clayey soils we recommend capping the
expansive clayey soils with a minimum of 24 inches of non expansive select granular fill
material below the building slab cushion The 24 inches of select granular fill should also
be provided below the walkway and lanai slabs The capping fill should also extend
beyond the new slabs on grade footprint a minimum of 24 inches The capping fill may be
omitted where a minimum of 24 inches of existing coralline material is present below the
building slab cushion
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered we believe that the proposed
residential structures may be supported on shallow foundations consisting of spread
and or continuous footings An allowable bearing pressure of may be utilized
for the design of residential building foundations bearing on the on site soils or properly
compacted fills The shallow foundations should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent grade and bear on properly compacted fill or in situ material
We anticipate that asphaltic concrete pavements will be used for the roadways
and parking areas within the subdivision areas and along Kamakana Street In addition
we understand that the pavement section will be underlain by 24 inches of
non expansive select granular material The following preliminary pavement section
may be used

Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Areas 17 51 and 52 and Kamakana Street Ewa by Gentry
West project in the Ewa District on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and
general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This
report
summarizes
our
findings
and
geotechnical
engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and
engineering analyses These recommendations are intended to assist in the design of
the residential structure foundations retaining wall foundations pavements utilities and
site grading only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2
Project Considerations
The project sites are south of Geiger Road in the Ewa District on the Island of
Oahu Hawaii The project sites consist of Area 17 Area 51 Area 52 and
Kamakana Street and are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The project sites are
described below
Area 17
The project site is at the southern end of Area 16A where a future business park
is planned at the intersection of Geiger Road and the proposed Kamakana
Street The project site has an estimated area of approximately 7 5 acres We
understand that the planned development will consist of single family residences
with public roads that will be dedicated to the City
Areas 51 and 52
The project site is west of Ewa Makai Middle School and will be bisected by the
proposed Kamakana Street The project site has an estimated area of
approximately 43 acres Planned development will consist of single family and
multi family residences with both private and public roads The public roads will

Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering exploration was to obtain a general
overview of the surface and subsurface conditions to develop an idealized soil data set
to formulate geotechnical engineering recommendations for the design of the
foundations slabs on grade retaining structures site grading utilities and pavements
Our work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
The scope of work for our exploration included the following tasks
and work efforts
1
Obtain utility clearance to drill at the project sites
2
Staking of the borehole locations and clearing of trails for our drilling
equipment
3
Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and operators
to and from the project site
4
Drilling and sampling of 23 borings extending to depths of approximately
15 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface Bulk soil samples were
collected for CBR analyses
5
Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by our field
engineer
6
Laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained during the field
exploration as an aid in classifying the materials and evaluating their
engineering properties

Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from the Waianae
and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to be middle to
late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western one third of the island The
younger Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Ice Age
in age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island As volcanic activity
in the Waianae Volcano ceased lava flows from the Koolau Volcano banked against its
eroded eastern slope forming a broad plateau now known as the Schofield Plateau
The project site is on the southwestern flank of the Waianae Volcano
Physical and chemical weathering followed by erosion of Schofield Plateau
generated sediments which were transported to the coast In the vicinity of the project
site and to the south these sediments accumulated and are interbedded with marine
sediments and coral algal reef formations to form a sedimentary wedge The thickness
of the sedimentary wedge ranges from zero in the area of the Interstate Route H 1
Highway to over 1 000 feet at Ewa Beach This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves
as the confining formation or caprock over the artesian basal aquifers of Southern
Oahu Deposition of sediments has continued from earlier geologic time through the
present
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to
fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea
When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that
the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of
accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits
at correspondingly higher elevations and deposited lagoonal marine sediments in the
quiet waters protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve
valleys in the sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling 23 borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 through 6 101 through 105 and 201 through 212 to
depths of about 15 to 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the approximate
locations shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2 The borings were drilled using a
truck mounted drill rig equipped with auger and coring equipment
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our engineer who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic representations of the
materials encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 23
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM D 3550 and D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
penetration sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are
shown on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Core samples of coral materials encountered at the site were obtained using
diamond core drilling techniques in general accordance with ASTM D 2113 Diamond
Core Drilling for Site Investigation Core drilling is a rotary drilling method that uses a
hollow bit to cut into the coral formation The coral material left in the hollow core of the
bit is mechanically recovered for examination and description
Recovery REC is used as a subjective guide to the interpretation of the relative
quality of rock masses Recovery is defined as the actual length of material recovered
from a coring attempt versus the length of the core attempt For example if 3 7 feet of
material is recovered from a 5 0 foot core run the recovery would be 74 percent and
would be shown on the Logs of Borings as REC 74

ASTM D 2937 were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings
at the appropriate sample depths
Twelve one inch Ring Swell tests were performed on selected samples to
evaluate the swelling potential of the soil under surcharge pressures The tests were run
on natural and remolded samples The swell test results are presented on Plate B 1
Seven Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate their liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The test
results are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths Graphic
presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 2
Two Sieve Analysis tests ASTM C 117 C 136 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the gradation characteristics of the soils and to aid in soil
classification Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 3
Four Direct Shear tests ASTM D 3080 were performed on selected soil samples
to evaluate the shear strength parameters of the soils The test results are presented on
Plates B 4 through B 7
Three Unconfined Compression tests ASTM D 2166 were performed on
selected soil samples to evaluate the unconfined compressive strength of the soils The
test results are presented on Plates B 8 through B 10
Nine laboratory California Bearing Ratio tests ASTM D 1883 were performed on
bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics
of the soils The samples were remolded to near the optimum moisture content The test
results are presented on Plates B 11 through B 19
Three Modified Proctor compaction tests ASTM D 1557 C were performed on
bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density and moisture content
relationships The test results are presented on Plates B 20 through B 22
Four pH tests ASTM G 51 were performed on selected soil samples obtained
from our field exploration to evaluate the corrosion potential of the soils The test results
are presented on Plate B 23
Four minimum soil resistivity tests ASTM G 57 were performed on selected soil
samples obtained from our field exploration to evaluate the corrosion potential of the soils
The results of the tests are presented on Plate B 23

Permeability Tests
The permeability of the in situ soils was evaluated by performing two Falling
Head Permeability tests at selected depths within two of the borings The tests were
performed at depths of about 9 and 10 feet below the existing ground surface
The Falling Head Permeability tests were performed by pumping water into the
borehole up to a reference level where the pumping of water was stopped The drop in
water height with time was then measured and used in the calculation of subsoil
permeability The field permeability test results are presented on Plates C 1 and C 2
