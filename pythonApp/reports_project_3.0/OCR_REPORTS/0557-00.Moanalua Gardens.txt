SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
The subsurface explorations along the proposed drain
alignment indicates that hard mudrock underlies its
entire length The mudrock will provide good sup
port for the proposed pipe but must be undercut six
inches and backfilled with granular material to
assure uniform bedding
The trench excavations should stand at essentially
vertical slopes without additional support The
trench walls should be inspected for pockets of
soft soil or areas of broken rock which may require
shoring
W 1975
Proposed Construction
The proposed storm drain consist of approximately 1250 feet
of 48 to 54 inch reinforced concrete pipe to be installed at
the locations shown on the enclosed plan Plate 1 The storm
drain begins at approximately elevation 180 feet on Krukowski
Road and extends to the northern bank of Moanalua Stream near
elevation 100 It is anticipated that the drain invert will
not extend more than 10 feet below the present ground surface
Site Description
The proposed drain alignment begins on the north side of
Krukowski Road within the Tripler Army Medical Center This
portion of the site is relatively level with a general slope
to the north
The drain crosses Krukowski Road and continues to the west for
approximately 250 feet The alignment then turns to the south
and crosses between Lots 33 and 34 of the subdivision to the
cul de sac on the north side of Ala Mahamoe Place The ground
surface there is at approximately elevation 170
A 15 to 20 foot section of mudrock is exposed in the cliff
behind the houses north of Ala Mahamoe Street
W 1975
The alignment continues through the paved and concrete streets
to the south side of the street at Lot 61 where it again turns
to the south and extends a distance of approximately 350 feet
to the north bank of Moanalua Stream
The ground surface throughout this section is relatively steep
dropping at a 30 to 40 percent grade between elevations 140
and 110 where the proposed drain will empty into the concrete
lined stream Reddish brown silty clay is exposed along the
slope surface
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The mudrock encountered along the proposed alignment was
developed from thick layers of ash spread over this area by
the eruptions of the Salt Lake and Makalapa Craters Follow
ing consolidation and cementation the erosion removed some
of the mudrock resulting in an irregular surface which may
vary considerably within short distances
In the higher portions the mudrock is blanketed by reddish
brown soil a weathered mudrock It appears that the mudrock
is underlain by older alluvial deposits consisting of consoli
dated and decomposed materials blanketing the lower basalt
W 1975
Subsurface Explorations
Four test borings were drilled at the locations shown on the
enclosed Boring Location Plan to delineate general subsurface
conditions along the proposed drain alignment
The borings were drilled utilizing 3 inch diameter continuous
flight augers and extended to between 11 and 12 feet below the
existing surface
The soils and rocks encountered were classified by visual and
textural examinations in the field by our engineer who conti
nuously supervised the drilling operations The classifica
tions were verified by visual inspection and testing in the
laboratory All soils were classified in accordance with the
Unified Soil Classification System
A graphic presentation of the soils encountered is presented
on the enclosed boring logs Plates A 1 through A 4
The soil samples were obtained by driving a standard penetra
tion sampler with a 140 pound hammer free falling a distance
of 30 inches The blow counts to drive the sampler the last
12 inches are shown on the boring logs at the appropriate
sample depth
W 1975
Soil Conditions
The subsurface explorations and laboratory testing disclosed
that the site is generally underlain by hard volcanic mudrock
In the upper areas near Boring 1 the mudrock has changed to
a brown decomposed rock with rock fragments varying from hard
to dense
Throughout the lower portion of the site the hard mudrock was
encountered essentially at the ground surface It was blan
keted with less than two feet of surface soils
In Boring 4 the mudrock extended to a depth of seven feet
where reddish brown decomposed rock with sandy silt seams was
encountered This material also varies between dense and hard
No groundwater was encountered during the subsurface explora
tions
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Pipe Support
The subsurface explorations indicate that the proposed storm
drain will bear within the mudrock or decomposed rock for its
entire length While these materials will provide excellent
bearing a cushion of granular materials is required to pro
vide uniform pipe support
W 1975
The pipe cushion should consist of a minimum of six inches of
sand graded crushed aggregate or other select granular
materials having a l inch maximum diameter and less than 5
percent finer than a No 200 sieve
Trench Excavations
It is anticipated that special excavation methods such as
jackhammers or other percussion equipment will be required
to excavate the drain trench to the proposed invert levels
Due to the close proximity of the residential dwellings and
the probable nearness of their foundations to the rock surface
it may not be advisable to utilize blasting methods for
trench excavations except under the strictest controls
The mudrock and decomposed rock will stand at essentially
vertical Slopes without additional support The trench exca
vations should be inspected for localized weak zones of
fractured rock or soft soils These areas may require tempo
rary shoring to provide stable trench sidewalls
Groundwater was generally not encountered during the subsur
face explorations and it is not anticipated that it will create
problems during the construction operations Some inflow of
water can be expected in seepage zones or limited areas of
fractured rock
W 1975
Construction Inspections
The construction operations should be in accordance with the
City and County of Honolulu s Standard Specifications for
Public Works Construction
The trench excavation should be inspected during the construc
tion operations to verify that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investigation If unfore
seen or undetected soil conditions such as concrete rubble
pockets of soft cohesive soils cesspools or unusual water
conditions are encountered corrective measures should be
made in the field as they are detected
