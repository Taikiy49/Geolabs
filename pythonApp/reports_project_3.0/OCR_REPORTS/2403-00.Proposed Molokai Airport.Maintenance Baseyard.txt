INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed maintenance baseyard development at
Molokai Airport on the island of Molokai Hawaii The general location of
the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2403 00 Page 2
This report summarizes findings of the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from analyses of the
exploration data obtained These recommendations are intended for design
input and not intended for construction estimate or bidding
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a light one story maintenance building
with a baseyard for vehicles at the Molokai airport
Other detailed project data about the proposed structure was not
available at the time of our exploration However slab on grade is
anticipated for the proposed building and asphaltic concrete pavement is
anticipated for the proposed baseyard
Minimal site grading is anticipated due to the existing flat grade in
the project site
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the site grading foundation
slab and pavement design of the proposed maintenance building and
baseyard
The work was done in general accordance with our proposal dated
to Mitsunaga Associates Inc The scope of work
included
1 excavation of two 2 shallow test pits at the project site
2 reconnaissance of the project site and its vicinity to make
visual observations of the surficial soil conditions

3 review of available soil and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity
4 laboratory testing of selected samples obtained from the field
exploration to determine the pertinent engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered
5 engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the
purpose of developing site grading foundation slab and pavement
design recommendations and
6 preparation of this report summarizing our work on the project
and presenting our findings and geotechnical engineering design
recommendations
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory testing
are presented in the appendices of this report
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located southerly of the existing car rental
lease lots and in the vicinity of the existing FAA facility future RIR
facility in the airport on the island of Molokai Hawaii
The site is presently an open grass area with a generally flat grade
at about elevations 444 to 445 feet above Mean Sea Level
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the proposed development site were
explored by excavating two 2 test pits to depths of approximately 5 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of these
test pits are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2403 00 Page 4
In general the project site is underlain by a 3 0 to 3 5 feet thick
layer of stiff sandy silt over very stiff silty clays to the maximum
excavated depth of the test pits
Laboratory swell tests indicate that the surface sandy silt layer has
low to moderate expansion potential
No groundwater was encountered in the test pits during our field
exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATTONS
General
The field exploration encountered generally competent subsoils that
can provide shallow footing foundation support for the proposed light
one story building
However the surface soil is low to moderately expensive when
saturated from a dry state and should be properly moisture conditioned
prior to placement of structures and pavement
Footing Foundation
Based on the generally competent subsoil conditions encountered in
the test pits we believe that shallow spread and or continuous wall
footing foundations may be used to support the proposed light structure
An allowable bearing capacity of 3500 p s f may be used for the
footing foundation design This value is for total dead plus live loads
only and may be increased by up to one third 1 3 for short duration
uplift wind or seismic load considerations
The footing foundations should be embedded a minimm of 18 inches
below the lowest adjacent finish grade
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2403 00 Page 5
Minimal footing settlements on the order of less than one half 1 2
of an inch total and one quarter 1 4 of an inch differential are
anticipated under the design loadings
Resistance to lateral loads acting on the proposed structure may be
provided by passive soil pressure and frictional resistance We recommend
that an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per foot depth be used to
calculate the passive soil pressure which will be developed against the
buried vertical faces of the foundations and a frictional coefficient of
0 3 be used to calculate the frictional resistance which will be developed
between the footing bottoms and the footing subgrade
Site Grading
A site grading plan for the subject project was not available at this
stage of geotechnical study however it is anticipated that minimal site
grading will be used due to the existing flat grade at the project site
At the onset of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable
materials such as rubbish vegetation and other deleterious materials
should be removed and disposed of properly off site
Any loose soft or yielding spots encountered at the footing or
slab pavement subgrade level should be over excavated down to in situ firm
ground and replaced with in situ or imported backfill compacted in 8 inch
loose lifts to a minimm of 95 of its maximm dry density per ASIM Test
Method D 1557 78
New imported backfill materials if needed should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum size
with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve

Slab On Grade
Based on the laboratory swell and CBR test results on the surface
soil we believe that the following slab on grade section may be used
provided the subgrade is properly moisture conditioned
5 Inch Portland Cement Concrete Slab
6 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
11 Inch Total Slab Thickness
Baseyard Pavement
It is our understanding that the proposed baseyard will normally serve
pick up truck traffic and also a maintenance type bulldozer
Based on the strength characteristics and swelling potential of the
surface soil it is our opinion that the following flexible pavement
section may be used
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course 95 Compaction
10 Inch Select Borrow 95 Compaction
18 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
The above pavement section considers that the pavement subgrade will
also be moisture conditioned to approximately 3 above its optimum moisture
content and recompacted to at least 95 of its maximum dry density per ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the project geotechnical engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction This review is needed to determine
adherence to the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If
this review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume
responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations

Construction Observation
It is recommended that the project geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during the site grading and
foundation construction phases of the project This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from
that anticipated prior to the start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Imported fill material if required should consist of select
non expansive material such as select borrow coral or cinder and should
be tested for suitability as fill material by the project geotechnical
engineer prior to its delivery to the project site
If actual rock soil conditions encountered during construction are
different from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate
modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in
part upon information obtained from test pits Variations of conditions
between the test pits may occur and the nature and extent of these
variations may not become evident until construction If variations then
appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations
given in this report
The locations of the test pits in the project site were detemnined by
taping from the existing dirt roads indicated on an undated copy of the
topographic survey provided by Mitsunaga Associates Inc Elevations of
the test pits were determined by interpolation from elevation contours on
the plan The physical locations and elevations of the test pits should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used

The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the test
pits depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may
denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on any profiles or
cross sections are based upon interpolation between test pits and may not
represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the test pits were collected at the times given
on the graphic representations and or in the text of this report These
data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this
report However it must be noted that fluctuation in water level may
occur due to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors

Field Exploration

2 test pits to depths of 5 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A hoptoe was used
to excavate the test pits

monitored the excavation operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Test Pit Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
Soil samples were obtained from the test pits by hand trimming on the
walls of the pits In addition pocket penetrometer tests were performed
on the exposed subsoils at different depths on the walls of the pits The
results of these tests are shown on the Test Pit Iogs at the approximate
depths
