Scope of Work
Project Considerations
The proposed Hali i Kai project site is located within the existing Waikoloa Beach Resort in the
Anaehoomalu area in the District of South Kohala on the Island of Hawaii The project site was graded
previously for single family homes by others It is now desired to construct 29 multi family residential
structures with two recreation areas at the project site
Some additional grading and utility line modifications will be made for the proposed multi family
development Therefore geotechnical engineering support is desired for the new development In general
services will be separated into two parts 1 Geotechnical Engineering Services and 2 Construction
Observation and Testing Services
Geotechnical engineering services generally would consist of the excavation of test pits and conducting
compaction tests on the compacted fills to evaluate the on site fills placed at the project site
In addition observation and testing services will be provided during construction of the proposed project
Based on the limited amount of site grading required only part time observation would be needed for the
proposed development The construction duration of the project will be about 8 months For budgeting
purposes the following is a breakdown of the assumed construction duration and anticipated working hours
for the construction of this project

Scope of Services
The purpose of the geotechnical engineering services is to obtain an overview of the surface and subsurface
conditions at the project site The subsurface information obtained would be utilized to develop a soil rock
data set for the formulation of geotechnical recommendations pertaining to the design of the proposed
project To accomplish this the geotechnical engineer will execute an exploration program consisting of
the following tasks and work effort
1 Review of the existing grading plans grading performed by others and the proposed grading for
the new multi family development
2 Mobilization and demobilization of a backhoe excavator and an operator to the project site and
back for excavation of test pits
3 Excavation of about 12 to 14 test pits extending to depths of about 5 to 10 feet below the ground
surface for 2 days of test pit excavation
4 Performance of field density tests on the existing fill materials by a field engineer technician
5 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the test pits by an engineer geologist
6 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration as an aid in
classifying the materials encountered and evaluating their engineering properties
7 Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of geotechnical recommendations
pertaining to the design of building foundations slabs on grade retaining structures site grading
and pavements for the proposed project
8 Preparation of a formal report 8 copies summarizing the geotechnical engineer s work on the
project and presenting our findings and recommendations
9 Coordination of geotechnical work on the project by a project engineer
10 Quality assurance of geotechnical engineer s work and client design team consultation by a
principal engineer
11 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical support
12 Review of plans and specifications for general conformance with the geotechnical engineering
report
13 Post design consultation during bidding of the proposed development
Construction Observation and Testing Services
The geotechnical engineering observation and testing services anticipated during construction of the
proposed development generally would consist of the following parts and work efforts
1 Provide a project engineer to attend the pre construction meeting
2 Review contractor submittals as appropriate
3 Provide a field engineer technician at the project site for the following
e Observe and document the removal of unsuitable materials including undocumented fills
as required and backfill of the resulting excavation
e Observe the excavation placement and compaction of fill materials
e Observe installation of underground utility lines
Observe the foundation excavation and compaction at footing subgrades
e Sample on site and or imported soils for laboratory testing and perform field density tests on
the fill materials as appropriate
4 Perform laboratory CBR maximum dry density and sieve analysis on the soil samples and submit the
test results on an as needed basis
5 Provide geotechnical engineering consultation to coordinate and support the above construction
observation and testing services including periodic site visits by an engineer
6 Submit a field report for each trip and a summary report at the end of the project including a grading
report for each building pad
