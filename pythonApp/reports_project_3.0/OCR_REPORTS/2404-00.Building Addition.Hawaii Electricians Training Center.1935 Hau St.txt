Foundation Recommendations
Based on the encountered soil conditions it is our recommendations
that spread continuous footing foundations can be used to support the
proposed building addition An allowable bearing capacity of up to 6 500

over excavated a minimum depth of another 18 inches and replaced with
compacted granular select borrow material Alternatively a lower
allowable bearing value of 3 000 p s f should be used for footings
bearing on the in situ clayey silt without the provision of 18 inches
over excavation and replacement by compacted select borrow beneath the
footing bottom elevation

INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed building addition at the Hawaii
Electricians Training Center at Kalihi on the island of Oahu Hawaii
general location of the site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate
The exploration was performed in general accordance with our revised
proposal dated
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2404 00 Page 2
This report summarizes the findings of the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation
or bidding purposes
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the foundation and pavement
design of the project
The work was done in general accordance with our revised proposal
dated The scope of work consisted of the following
1 The drilling and sampling of four 4 borings to depths between
15 3 and 21 5 feet below the existing ground surface
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual
observations of the surficial soil conditions
3 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to
the project site and its vicinity
4 laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field
exploration to determine the engineering properties and
classification of the materials encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the
purpose of developing foundation and pavement recommendations
6 Preparation of this report presenting our findings and design
recommendations

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct an addition to the existing four
4 story structure at the Hawaii Electricians Training Center The
proposed addition will consist of a three 3 story building supported by
columns with parking on the ground floor level High column loads of 400
kips and wall loads of 6 kips per linear foot are anticipated
It is our understanding that concrete pavement is planned for the
parking area underneath the proposed building addition and new asphaltic
concrete pavement will be constructed for the existing parking area
located on the Ewa side of the building addition
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at 1935 Hau Street in the Kalihi district
on the island of Oahu Hawaii
The existing four 4 story structure at the Hawaii Electricians
Training Center is located on the makai side of the project site The
proposed building addition is to be located immediately on the mauka side
of the existing building and is approximately 78 by 88 feet in plan
dimension
At present the existing training center site has an asphaltic
concrete parking area approximately 184 by 88 feet in plan dimension
The asphaltic concrete pavement area appears generally level and it is in
good service condition
The project site is bounded by approximately 6 foot high hollow block
fence wall along the property lines in the Waikiki Ewa and portion of the
makai boundary directions

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface exploration at the project site was performed by
drilling and sampling four 4 borings to depths ranging from 15 3 to 21 5
feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field exploration encountered a 2 foot thick surface
fill layer of dense sandy gravel with silt overlying coral formation A
thin layer of approximately 1 to 3 foot thick of stiff clayey silt
fill alluvium was encountered underneath the surface fill layer in
Boring Nos 2 and 4 Pockets or lenses of dense black cinder sand were
also encountered in Boring Nos 1 and 2 at approximate depths of 5 5 and
10 5 feet below the existing ground surface respectively
Groundwater level was encountered at an approximate depth of 18 5
feet below the existing ground surface during our field exploration
Detailed description of the subsoil and groundwater conditions are
presented on the Boring Logs in Appendix A
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered coral formation generally at
shallow depths of about 2 to 5 feet below the existing ground surface
Spread continuous footing foundations can be used to support the proposed
building addition Details of our recommendations are discussed in the
following sections
Foundations
The field exploration disclosed that the site appears to be underlain
by a thick coral limestone formation generally at shallow depths of about
2 to 5 feet below the existing ground surface The upper coral formation
coral algal limestone is generally hard near the ground surface When
approaching groundwater level the lower coral formation grades to
gravelly in nature
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2404 00 Page 5
We recommend that spread or continuous footing foundations be used to
support the proposed building addition An allowable bearing capacity of
coral formation with a minimum footing embedment of 2 feet Where clayey
silt soil were encountered at the bottom of footing excavation it should
be over excavated a minimum depth of another 18 inches and replaced with
granular select borrow material compacted to a minimm of 95 of its
maximum dry density as per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Alternatively all
spread footings should be embedded deep enough to bear on the in situ
coral formation at various levels ranging from 2 feet below the existing
ground surface up to about 5 feet or more
For footings bearing on the underlying coral formation settlement is
negligible For footings bearing on the above specified compacted
granular fill footing settlement is estimated to be less than one half of
an inch
An allowable bearing value of 3 000 p s f is recommended for
continucus wall footings bearing on either the coral formation or in situ
stiff clayey silt soil with a minimum embedment of 2 feet Differential
settlements for line footings bearing on coral and stiff clayey silt soil
are estimated to be less than one quarter of an inch
The bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be increased by
one third when temporary loads wind and seismic are considered
Cavity Probing
Coral formation often contain voids or cavities Therefore to
reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to possible
occurrence of cavities or loose lenses near the footing bottom
elevation drilled probes are recommended below each column footing
and at 15 foot centers for continuous wall footings The probe holes
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 2404 00 Page 6
should be a minimm of 3 inch in diameter and extend below the bottom
of the footing to a depth equal to two times the footing width or a
minimm of 10 feet The probe holes should be grouted with a
sand water cement mixture under low pressures
If a cavity or loose lens is suspected below a footing additional
probe holes should be made to delineate the horizontal extent and
thickness of the cavity or loose lens Then the cavity may be filled
by grouting through the probe holes Alternatively if a large
cavity is encountered near the ground surface it may be more
economical to expose the cavity and backfill with select fill The
probing and grouting operations should be monitored by the soils
engineer to see that the aforementioned recommendations are carried
out properly and to evaluate other field solutions which may be
required to properly backfill potential voids and densify the loose
soil which may occur near the footing elevations
Site Grading
Minimal site grading with generally less than one 1 foot cut or
fill is anticipated for the proposed project
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the
grading limits should be cleared and grubbed of all rubbish and
deleterious materials old foundations and asphaltic concrete pavements
The resulting grub and spoil material should be disposed of properly
off site Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during the clearing
operations should be over excavated to firm ground and the resulting
excavations should be backfilled with well compacted engineering fill
Subsequent to clearing and grubbing the area within the grading
limits should be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture
conditioned to near optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimm
of 90 maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78

Select on site material such as the excavated granular fill and
coral material could be stockpiled and reused as fill or footing backfill
material
The fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose
level lifts and compacted to a minimum of 90 maximm dry density as
established by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
Imported select fill material should be non expansive and have less
than 20 fines passing the 200 sieve
The finished grade should be shaped to shed water away from
foundations and the avoid ponding condition

located on the Ewa side of the building addition

Design Review
Final construction plans and specifications for the proposed
construction should be forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for updated
review evaluation and written comments prior to construction This
review is needed to determine adherence to the earthwork and foundation
recommendations given herein and to provide additional recommendations for
future design changes If this review is not made the geotechnical
engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the
recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during earthwork construction
cavity probe drilling grouting and footing excavation to monitor the
contractor s geotechnical operations This is to observe compliance with
the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design
changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that
anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations given in
this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to its delivery to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
Variations of conditions between the field data points may occur and the
nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations and elevations of the field data points were determined
by estimation from the topographic survey by Sam O Hirota Inc dated
Elevations of the borings were approximately
determined by interpolation from elevation points on the topographic
survey map The physical location and elevation of the borings should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
boring points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and
as such may denote a gradual transition The strata lines shown on
profiles or cross sections are based upon interpolation between borings
and may not represent actual subsurface conditions
Water level data from the boring points were collected at the times
given on the graphic representations and or in the text of this report
These data have been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation
of this report However it must be noted that fluctuation may occur due
to variations in rainfall tides temperature and other factors

Electricians Trust Fund and their architects engineer for specific
application to the proposed building addition project in accordance with
generally accepted geotechnical engineering principles and practices No
other warranty expressed or implied is made
