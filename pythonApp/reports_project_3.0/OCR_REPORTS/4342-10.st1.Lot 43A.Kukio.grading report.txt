Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii is the largest in the Hawaiian Archipelago covering an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of the following
five shield volcanoes Kohala long extinct Mauna Kea activity during recent geologic
time Hualalai last erupted in 1801 1803 and Mauna Loa and Kilauea both still active
The project site is situated on the northwestern flank of Hualalai Mountain which
makes up the west central portion of the island The lava formation observed at the
project site prior to site grading activities appears to consist of both a a and pahoehoe
type lava flows which spread and ponded as they approached the ocean A a lava is
typically characterized by a porous rough and irregular flow surface resembling a jagged
accumulation of rock fragments cobbles and boulders Pahoehoe lava is characterized
by a smooth rope like or billowy surface and an internal structure of vesicular porous
rock
Cavities are commonly encountered in basaltic lavas especially the pahoehoe type
lava Cavities form when the lava is in a molten state and they represent both lava tubes
intra flow cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow cavities Lava tubes form when
molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like structure which may
extend for a large longitudinal distance along the flow Blisters and pockets are generally
smaller in horizontal extent
Basalt is considered to be a relatively permeable rock and can transmit water quite
readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions In general water is transmitted
through the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities and
along clinker layers Clinker and cavities serve as primary water transmission features
because of their high permeability
Subsurface Conditions
Kukio is in the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii In general the
original site topography prior to site grading activities consisted of irregular
or hummocky lava flow terrain with frequent topographic depressions separated by
adjacent higher relief mounds and low ridge features
Based on our observations during the mass grading operations the project site is
generally underlain by medium hard to hard basalt formation and clinker materials It
should be noted that lava tubes cavities and blisters are commonly encountered in
basaltic lava flows in the project vicinity In addition numerous lava tube features were
observed at the project site during the mass grading operations It should be noted that

Discussion and Recommendations
Based on the grading plan the original ground surface elevations within the
building envelope boundary ranged from about 100 to 112 feet Mean Sea Level MSL
at the majority of Lot No 43A Lot No 43A was generally graded to a finished pad
elevation of about 114 feet MSL as shown on the Site Plan Plate 3 Mass grading at
Lot No 43A generally consisted of placement of up to about 14 feet of new fills to achieve
the design grades
During the mass grading operations extensive subgrade preparation procedures
were employed within the limits of grading at the commencement of the mass grading
operations for the project to reduce the potential for collapse of near surface cavities
and or voids within the lots The intent of the subgrade preparation procedures is to assist
in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or voids contained in the porous
near surface materials It should be noted that the subgrade preparation procedures
employed for this project are consistent with the standard of practice for similar type
projects and similar subsurface conditions along the Kohala Coast on the Island of Hawaii
In general the subgrade preparation in fill areas consisted of ripping the original
ground to a depth of about 3 feet or until refusal using a ripper tooth of a large bulldozer to
assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or voids contained in the
porous near surface materials After the fill surfaces have been ripped the ground
generally was leveled with the use of a Komatsu 375 bulldozer working the surface for
about four passes to provide a relatively level surface After leveling of the rugged surfaces
with the bulldozer the fill subgrades generally were proof rolled with a Caterpillar 825
sheepsfoot roller compactor minimum 20 tons for about eight passes to help detect and
collapse near surface cavities and or voids
Fill materials consisting of some cobbles and boulders and structural fills were
placed in the fill areas of the project site Structural fills consisted of well graded granular
materials generally smaller than 6 inches in dimension In general the fill materials within
the top 2 feet of the lots within the pad areas consisted of structural fill materials It
should be noted that general fills consisting of cobbles and boulders of up to about 2 feet
in dimension generally were placed at least about 2 feet below the finished grades within
the pad areas in fill areas and choked off with finer sized materials In addition boulders
of up to about 3 to 5 feet in dimension generally were placed at least about 4 feet below
the finished grades within the pad areas and choked off with finer sized materials
Boulders larger than 18 inches in dimension generally were placed about 6 feet or more
horizontally from the face of the fill slopes
