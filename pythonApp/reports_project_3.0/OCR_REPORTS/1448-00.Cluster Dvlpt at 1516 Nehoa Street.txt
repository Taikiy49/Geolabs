In accordance with your request we have performed a soil and
foundation investigation for the above referenced project The
approximate location of the project site and its vicinity is shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1 Our work was performed in general
accordance with our proposal dated March 20 1984
The purpose of the investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the site to provide soil data for use in the foundation
design of the proposed structures
W O 1448 00 Page 2
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our findings and recom
mendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed development will consist of six 6 2 story
wood frame residential structures Slab on grade construction is
planned for the driveway house and garage
Minimal site grading with fills and cuts generally less than 3 feet
thick is anticipated
The general layout of the proposed construction is shown on the
Site Plan Plate 2
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located along the northeast mauka side of
Nehoa Street about 150 feet southeast of the Nehoa and Makiki Street
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1448 00 Page 3
intersection The site is bounded by Nehoa Street to the southwest a
10 foot deep drainage channel to the northwest and private land parcels
to the northeast and southeast
The site is generally rectangular in shape and measures about 120
by 280 feet in plan dimension The ground slopes gently upward towards
the northeast mauka at about a 4 gradient with ground surface
elevations range from 104 to 114 feet Mean Sea Level Datum
A review of the existing Topographic Survey Plans prepared by R M
Towill Corporation indicated the site was previously occupied by several
houses At the time of our field investigation the site was vacant and
it appeared that previous superstructures had been removed from the
site However portions of the foundations CRM retaining walls
asphalt concrete driveways and some surface boulders remained on the
site
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling 5
borings to depths ranging from 6 5 to 16 5 feet below the existing
ground surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
GEOLABS HAWAII
Soil samples obtained from the borings were selectively tested in
our laboratory to further examine their geotechnical engineering
characteristics Detailed descriptions of our field exploration log
of borings laboratory testing and results are presented in the
appendices
Soil Conditions
In general Borings 1 to 4 encountered a thin mantle of stiff brown
clayey silt up to 5 feet thick below the existing ground surface and
about 3 feet of medium dense dark brown silty sand in Boring 5
Underlying the thin mantle the borings encountered dense brown
silty sand to sandy silt with some basalt cobbles and boulders to the
full depth of our borings
No groundwater was encountered in the borings at the time of our
field exploration
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our borings indicate that the site is mantled with up to 5 feet of
stiff clayey silt over dense silty sand which we believe can generally
provide adequate foundation support for the proposed 2 story wood frame
structures provided our recommendations are followed
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1448 00 Page 5
Shallow spread or continuous footing foundations may bear on the
upper stiff clayey silt However footings for the proposed structures
located next to the lined drainage channel should be lowered below a 1 1
H V plane projected up from the channel wall footing bottom to mini
mize imposing additional load against the existing channel wall and to
avoid potential movements of the new structures due to lateral wall
movements
The laboratory tests disclose that some of the near surface silty
soil when re worked and compacted may exhibit moderate swelling
potential Therefore we recommend that slab on grade be placed over a
minimum of 12 inches of select non expansive granular soil to minimize
the swelling potential of the subgrade soil
The field exploration indicates that some boulders may be
encountered in deeper utility trench excavations
Site Preparation
The preliminary grading plan indicates minimal site grading will be
necessary with cuts and fills generally less than 3 feet thick
At the time of our field exploration many large trees with
extensive root systems and portions of abandoned foundations were
observed covering a majority of the site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1448 00 Page 6
Prior to grading the site should be cleared of old foundations
vegetation large roots and other deleterious materials and properly
disposed of off site
The site should then be adequately moisture conditioned and
proof rolled to form a uniform crust Localized soft areas detected
should be removed backfilled with approved structural fill and
recompacted to the specified density presented below
Filling and Compaction Requirements
Imported fill material should be non expansive and generally less
than 3 inches in size tested and approved by a qualified soils engineer
prior to being used as fill
After the above site preparation fill should be moistened to
optimum moisture content placed in 9 inch maximum loose horizontal
lifts and compacted to obtain a minimum 90 of the maximum dry density
as established by ASTM D 1557 78
The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff
away from all foundations
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1448 00 Page 7
House Foundations
The field exploration and laboratory testing indicate that the
stiff in situ soils are adequate to support spread or continuous footing
foundations
A review of the Topographic Survey Map prepared by R M Towill
Corporation dated August 6 1979 indicates the drainage channel is
about 10 feet deep The preliminary Site and Utility Plan provided by
Fukunaga Associates Inc indicates the proposed structures along the
northeastern side of the site may be located as close as 5 feet from the
channel wall
The close proximity of the proposed structures and the use of
shallow foundations would increase the lateral earth pressures on the
channel wall
Additionally lateral movements of the channel wall would directly
affect the proposed structure foundations located next to them
Therefore it is recommended that the foundation for the buildings next
to the channel be lowered to extend below a 1 1 H V plane projected
upward from the heel of the channel wall which is believed to be located
at a depth of 10 to 12 feet below the existing surface
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1448 00 Page 8
Except for the above footings next to the channel wall the
remaining footings should be embedded a minimum of 12 inches below the
finished grade
For the light loads anticipated an allowable bearing pressure of
3000 p s f may be used for footings resting on stiff in situ soil or
compacted fill
Tf a footing is located next to a utility trench it should extend
to the bottom of the utility trench or the trench backfill must be well
compacted to reduce the possibility of footing settlement due to
settlement of the backfill
Slab On Grade
The laboratory swell tests indicate that some of the near surface
soils are moderately expansive Therefore in the slab on grade areas
the ground should either be filled over or overexcavated down below the
slab bottom to provide a minimum 12 inch capping layer of non expansive
imported soils between the slab and the natural ground
To further reduce future post construction swelling of the
underlying soils we recommend that the subgrade soil below the capping
fill pad be kept moist continuously
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1448 00 Page 9
Slab on grade should be placed over a 4 inch cushion layer of
gravel size crushed rock material such as 3 Fine rock to act asa
capillary break and to provide uniform support
In order to protect against future moisture infiltration an
impervious membrane may be incorporated below the slab to act as a vapor
barrier
Utility Trenches
The borings and visual observations indicate that some amount of
basalt cobbles and boulders may be encountered in the deeper excava
tions about 5 feet below the existing surface
Proper backfilling of the excavated trenches is important to
minimize ground settlement along the utility alignment On site silty
and sandy materials generally less than 6 inches in size may be
utilized as backfill All backfill should be compacted to a minimum of
90 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D 1557 78 Most of
the on site material is fine grained and would not be suitable for
compaction by water jetting or ponding alone and therefore would
require mechanical compaction
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 1448 00 Page 10
Pavements
The preliminary plans provided by Fukunaga Associates Inc
indicates rigid pavement sections are planned for the driveway
Based on the laboratory CBR tests the following pavement section
may be utilized
6 inches Portland Cement
6 inches Aggregate Base course
12 inches Total Pavement Thickness
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade
soils at the time of construction to confirm the above design
The recommended section also considers light traffic and that good
drainage will be provided for adjacent to paved areas
Existing Cesspools
Existing cesspools may be located at the site If encountered the
cesspool locations should be flagged in the field To reduce future
settlement of structures where existing cesspools are located the
sludge should be removed and filled with compacted granular backfill
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1448 00 Page 11
such as broken up coral or cinder The cesspool walls within 5 feet of
the finished grade should be removed to prevent hard spots and
subsequent differential settlement at the finished grade
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading and foundation
construction should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and
written comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site preparation proof rolling and
building foundation preparation be inspected by a soils enaineer to
assure adequate compaction of the in place materials and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to being used at the site
I conditions encountered during construction are different fron
those presented in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made

INVESTIGATION LIMITATIONS
Our services consist of professional opinions and recommendations
made in accordance with generally accepted soil and foundation
engineering principles and practices This warranty is in lieu of all
other warranties either expressed or implied
The analysis and recommendations submitted in this report are based
on our site reconnaissance soil information from boring and laboratory
test data obtained from this soil investigation
Unanticipated soil conditions are commonly encountered and cannot
be fully determined by taking soil samples Unforeseen conditions such
as expansive soil and soft spots may occur in localized areas and may
require additional probing or corrections in the field Therefore
additional expenditures may be needed during construction to attain a
properly constructed project Some contingency fund is thus recommended
to accommodate these possible costs

the preparation of site grading and foundation plans of the proposed
construction
