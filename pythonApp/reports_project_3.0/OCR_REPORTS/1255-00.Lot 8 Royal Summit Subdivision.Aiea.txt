Dear Mr Lum
In accordance with your request we have made a study of the near
surface soil conditions at the above referenced lot in order to
provide foundation recommendations for the proposed residence The
study consisted of drilling two shallow borings laboratory testing of
the swelling potential of the soils a review of the subdivision site
grading plans and the preliminary house foundation plans This report
summarizes our findings and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a one story structure approximately 60
by 70 feet in plan dimension with a 2 car garage three open lanais
and a covered lanai Concrete slab on grade is planned for the
garage house and lanai

SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the western end of Nohoali i Street in
the Royal Summit Phase 1 Subdivision in Aiea Oahu Hawaii Lot 8 is
a generally level pad which slopes downward at a 2 1 H V grade along
the western boundary of the site
A review of the site grading plans for the subdivision indicates that
this lot required cuts that ranged from 3 to 8 feet deep below the
natural ground surface
SUBSURFACE SOIL CONDITIONS
The borings which were drilled at the approximate location shown on
the Site Plan Plate 1 encountered 1 5 to 5 feet of mottled red gray
and brown clayey silt residual soil underlain by saprolite and
highly weathered basalt rock
Laboratory swell tests performed on the near surface material
indicated that the underlying residual soil is generally very moist
35 to 45 moisture content and exhibited low to moderate expansion
potential in its natural state However previous experience indicates
that this type of material could have high swelling potential if
allowed to dry out Therefore measures should be taken to prevent
excessive drying of the underlying soil

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
As mentioned above the field exploration disclosed that the existing
hear surface soils have low to moderate swelling potential if they are
allowed to dry out Therefore in order to minimize the swelling
potential the soils should be kept moist after excavation and pro
tected from drying during the several days required for preparation
prior to final concrete placement
Based on a review of the house foundation plan it is our understanding
that about 1 foot of cut will be required along the western portion of
the site at the top of the existing 2 1 H V slope
A soil engineer should observe the grading operation and inspect the
bottom of all footing excavations to see that adequate footing
embedment and bearing soil have been reached
An allowable bearing value up to 2500 p s could be used on the
stiff residual soil
Site Grading
It is anticipated that small cuts of about 1 foot will be necessary
along the existing 2 1 H V slope located along the western
boundary The following recommendations should be followed
Prior to new filling the area should be cleared of vegetation and
debris

If the on site soil from Royal Summit is used for fill or backfill it
Should be placed wet of optimum moisture content 30 to reduce the
swelling potential
Fills and backfills should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density established
by ASTM D 1557 78
Slope ratio of 2 1 H V for compacted fill is recommended
Fill slope should be constructed by overfilling and then cutting back
to the design slope to obtain a well compacted slope face
Surface water should be diverted away from the top of the slope and
Slope planting utilized as soon as practicable to reduce erosion
Slab On Grade
Concrete on grade slab is planned for the entire house garage
driveway and lanais To reduce the swelling effects of the residual
Soils special lime saturation is recommended The general procedure
is listed below
1 The slab subgrade soils should be saturated with hoses with
small holes by constant sprinkling at least 2 days prior to
concrete placement to reduce the swelling potential of the
underlying subsoil

2 Hydrated lime should be placed at the bottom of the slab
perimeter footing excavation and thickened edge of the
driveway prior to the above water saturating of the subgrade
soil We believe that about ten to fifteen 50 1b bags of
hydrated lime would be adequate to cover the slab subgrade
It is important that the soils engineer should inspect the
above subgrade preparation and check the moisture contents of
the underlying soils prior to pouring of the slab and
perimeter footing
3 The bottom of the footing excavation should be neat and free
of loose soils It is anticipated that the very soft silt
and lime mud deposited at the footing after saturation should
be removed just prior to concrete placement
4 The concreting of footing and slab should be coordinated in
such a way that the time period between the initial excavation
and final concrete placement at each lot should be kept to a
minimum in order to prevent excessive subgrade drying
5 The above recommendations for saturating with lime on the
subgrade should also be followed for the sidewalk driveway
and patio slabs

Lot Drainage
The finish lot grade outside the slab pavement and footings should be
shaped to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding
condition Also the owners should be advised to install a gutter
system around the house and that the gutter water should be diverted
far away from the footings and walkways Excessive landscape watering
near the foundations and slab should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage considerations are essential for the proper performance
of the above recommended foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled
tight against slab footing immediately after setting of the concrete
to prevent ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and footing
foundation

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction to
see that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurface
conditions are consistent with the findings of our subsurface
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
