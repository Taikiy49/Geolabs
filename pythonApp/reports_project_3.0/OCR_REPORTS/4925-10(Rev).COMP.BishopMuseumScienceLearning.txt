This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observation and testing
services performed during the mass grading and road construction for the Bishop Museum
Science Learning Center from June 2004 through August 16 2005 in general accordance
with our fee proposal dated
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the Bishop Museum facility in the Kalihi area of
Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The location of the project site is shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the site grading operations for the project site
SITE GRADING
The site grading work for the project was observed by Geolabs Inc from
June 2004 through August 2005 on an on call intermittent basis The site grading work
consisted of clearing and grubbing and placement and compaction of fills up to about
3 feet in thickness to achieve the design finished grades for the project The site grading
work was performed by Koga Engineering and Construction During the site grading
operations samples of the on site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill

materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry
density and optimum moisture content were established in our laboratory in general

are summarized on the Summary of Optimum Moisture Maximum Dry Density

The fill and backfill materials were moisture conditioned to above optimum moisture
content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as
determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1557
During the site grading and utility backfilling operations periodic field density tests
were performed in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922
Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on
Plates 2 1 through 2 3 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground
surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations
were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were re worked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained and or approved by our field representative based on visual
observation and hand probing
Our services consisted of on call intermittent field observation of the contractor s fill
placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the grading operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our opinion that the site grading work for the project observed and
tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in conformance
with the general intent of the recommendations in the following references
