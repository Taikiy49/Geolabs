We understand that the size of the bridge crossing the open channel has been changed
from 210 feet to about 35 feet in width We previously recommended precast
prestressed concrete piles for support of the bridge structure in the above referenced
report Because of the limited number of piles required for the smaller bridge and the
need for predrilling the pile locations it is our opinion that a drilled pier caisson
foundation system may be more cost effective for the smaller bridge structure
Bridge Foundation
Several options for the bridge foundation were considered in our analyses The
use of H piles was considered for the bridge foundation However because of the
presence of boulders encountered in our borings it is our opinion that it may be difficult
to drive the H piles through boulders into the underlying basalt formation without
predrilling the pile locations Predrilling the H pile locations will reduce the lateral load
capacity and lateral stability of the H piles and will likely require grouting of the
predrilled holes

Construction Considerations
Casing of the drilled piers will likely be required during construction due to the
presence of sandy soils and boulders encountered in our borings Casing will be
needed to keep the drilled pier holes open without significant sloughing and or caving
in of the sandy soils and boulders into the pier holes In addition coring through the
boulders and into the underlying basalt formation will likely be needed to embed the
drilled piers into the basalt formation
The load bearing capacities of drilled piers depend to a large extent on the
contact between the pier shaft and the surrounding soils Therefore proper
construction techniques are important The contractor should exercise care in drilling
the pier holes and in placing concrete into the holes Drilling by methods utilizing
drilling fluids is not recommended because the fluids could saturate and weaken the
surrounding soil above the groundwater level Since these are end bearing piers the
bottom of the piers should be clean and free and loose materials prior to placement of
concrete A representative of Geolabs Hawaii should be present to observe the
installation of drilled piers including drilling rebar cage placement and concrete
placement during construction
A low shrink high slump concrete mix should be used to provide close contact
between the pier shafts and the surrounding soil Since groundwater was encountered
above the basalt formation we recommend that the concrete for the drilled piers be
placed by wet construction method such as the use of a tremie pipe A minimum of
5 feet of concrete head should be maintained above the bottom of the tremie pipe at
all times during the concrete placement to facilitate displacement of the groundwater
in the pier hole The concrete should also be placed in a suitable manner to reduce
segregation of the aggregates from the concrete mix

Wet and Pumping Subgrade Conditions
Costco Store at Maui
Based on our geotechnical observation services during construction at the
above referenced project some of the footing subgrades are relatively wet and
pumping conditions were noted during compaction at the bottom of the over excavation
of some footing locations As a result the grading contractor will have difficulty in
achieving the required compaction of 95 percent relative compaction
To facilitate the earthwork construction in achieving the required compaction we
recommend that a geotextile fabric such as Mirafi 500X or equivalent be placed on the
proof rolled subgrade prior to placement of the engineered fills The geotextile fabric
would only be needed at footing excavations which encounter wet subgrade soils and
where pumping of the subgrades are noted
Engineered fill placement may then commence directly on the geotextile fabric
and should be compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction The
compaction requirement of the first lift of backfill above the geotextile fabric may be
reduced to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction to enable the bridging effect
to take place

Excavation of the Existing Fill
and Backfill with Engineered Fill
Costco Store at Maui
We have been providing geotechnical construction services at the above
referenced project site In general it is recommended that the existing fill materials below
the bottom of the footings and other structural improvements be over excavated a
minimum depth of 2 feet and replaced with compacted engineered fills If the bottom of
excavations expose soft soils additional over excavation of the soft material will be
required to the maximum depth of 4 feet below the bottom of the footings
During our on going field observations at the above referenced site it was
observed that pumping were occurred during compaction at the bottom of over
excavation at some footing locations Therefore it is not possible to achieve the required
compaction of 95 percent relative compaction In the event that subgrade soils during
over excavation are observed to be pumping during compaction a filter fabric such as
Mirafi 500X or equivalent should be placed on the bottom of the excavation prior to
placement of the engineered fills The filter fabric should be also be provided if the
compaction of the subgrade at the over excavation bottom cannot attain 95 percent
relative compaction This will be evaluated in the field After placement of the filter fabric
the resulting over excavation should be backfilled with engineered fills compacted to not
less than 95 percent relative compaction
During our field observation it was also observed that it is very difficult to achieve
95 percent relative compaction if the moisture contents of the backfill material are above
