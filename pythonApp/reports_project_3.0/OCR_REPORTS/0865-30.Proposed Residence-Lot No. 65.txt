conditions below the above referenced lot The purpose of the
study was to evaluate the suitability of near surface soils as

of four 4 hand auger probes within the proposed building
site laboratory soil tests and a review of the grading and
compaction test record on the building site This letter sun
marizes our observations and recommendations

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain by 4
to 9 feet of grayish brown clay fill The laboratory test
results on this material generally indicated that they have
high expansion potential Therefore for Lot 65 the slab on
grade and foundation construction would require special design
procedures in order to reduce the shrink swell effect of the
subsoils
House Foundation
For this lot post and beam construction is highly desirable
and recommended due to the sloping terrain and expansive soil
conditions The post and beam construction generally results
in lower foundation costs and the individual footings can be
readily adjusted should the need occur Concrete slab on
grade and driveway should also be properly designed for
expansive soil conditions
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 865 30 Page 5
For post and beam construction the individual post footings
should extend to a minimum depth of 36 inches below the ground
surface The footings should be poured in place and the
excavation backfilled and compacted to provide an impervious
layer over the footing
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f can be used for
footings bearing on the stiff natural ground
If a footing is located next to a utility line the footing
should extend to the bottom of the utility trench to reduce
footing settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations
Retaining Wall Foundation
Short retaining walls generally less than 3 feet high are
planned along the front and northern portion of the house
In order to reduce the expansive effects of the existing clay
soils on the wall we recommend that the expansive clays should
be overexcavated down for a depth of 2 feet below the bottom of
the footing and then replaced with non expansive borrow soils
such as crusher waste or coral compacted to a minimum of 90

and replacement should extend a minimum distance of 12 inches

The wall footing should be embedded a minimum depth of 18

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage It appears
that the garage area would require fill from about one to 3
feet in order to obtain sufficient level area for the garage
The on site clay soils are expansive and are unsuitable as slab
subgrade soils In order to reduce the expansive effects of
the clay the expansive clays should be overexcavated down for
a depth of 3 feet below the slab subgrade and replaced with
non expansive borrow soil such as select borrow crusher waste
or coral compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry
density per ASTM D 1557
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
Expansive clay soils were encountered in the driveway area In
order to reduce the expansive effects of the clay it should be
kept a minimum of 2 feet below the driveway subgrade level
Where 2 or more feet of fill is planned select non expansive
borrow material should be used to cap over the expansive clay
Where less than 2 feet of fill is planned overexcavation and
replacement of the expansive clay with select non expansive
material is required The above requirements should be
followed for a minimum distance of 3 feet beyond the driveway

Joints are recommended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals in the
driveway in order to allow for some relative movement of the
concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the clay or overexcavation and
replacement similar to the above driveway design is recommended
to reduce heaving caused by the expansive clay soil
Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down at about
9 1 H V ratio or flatter
Minor site grading is planned for the lot development
Fill and backfill material should be select non expansive
borrow material The on site clay soil should not be used for
fill material
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation and
debris prior to filling
Fill and backfill should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557
