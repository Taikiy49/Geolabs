INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the site grading and foundation design of the
proposed Makaha Wells Water System Development at Makaha Valley
The proposed water system development consists of construction of
three wells and 4 at three 3 separate sites The
approximate locations of the individual well sites are shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the exploration was to study the subsurface
conditions at the three sites to obtain pertinent soil parameters for the
site grading and foundation design of the new tanks buildings and
retaining walls Particular attention was directed to the northern upper
portion of Well Site No 4 where a high retaining wall is planned to
provide grade separation for the proposed construction
The scope of this exploration included field geological
reconnaissance drilling and sampling seven 7 borings laboratory
testing of samples obtained engineering evaluation of the field and
laboratory data development of design recommendations and report
preparation
Due to access difficulties only a preliminary field reconnaissance
had been performed previously at Well Site No 4 Based on the limited
data we had presented our preliminary recommendations in our report
entitled Soil Engineering and Geological Reconnaissance Report Proposed

SITE DESCRIPTION
General Area of Three Well Sites
The project sites consist of narrow strips of land located north of
Makaha Stream along separate stream terraces in Makaha Valley at about
11 000 to 13 000 feet northerly of the Sheraton Makaha Hotel
Access to the sites was by means of 4 wheel drive vehicles over
unpaved dirt roads that at times may be impassable
Well Site No 2 Lower Site
The site is a narrow strip of land about 80 by 200 feet located
about 150 feet north of Makaha Stream on a stream terrace The north and
the west hillsides slope down at about 4H 1V to 8H 1V towards the

steeper northern hillside
The site is generally flat with a slight gradient down towards the
south The ground elevations vary from about 966 to 976 feet above Mean
Sea level The area is primarily covered with thick grasses and small
shrubs

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The general areas of the project sites are located in the upper
reaches of Makaha Valley which is essentially an amphitheater headed
valley
After the Waianae Volcano ceased its activity it was deeply incised
by amphitheater headed valleys and partly filled with sediments during
Pliocene time Later during the glacioeustatic sea level changes during
the Pleistocene hundreds of feet of marine and non marine sediments were
accumulated
The three well sites lie on gently sloping terraces between the
stream channel and toe of the steep hillside The terraces consist of
coalesced fans of alluvial and colluvial sediments derived from the
salient volcanic cliffs of the Waianae Range

Subsoil Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the sites were explored by drilling and
sampling seven 7 borings to depths of about 16 to 21 feet below the
existing ground surface
From the subsoils encountered in the drilled borings and visual
observations of soil rock exposures at the sites it appears that most of
the areas are covered with a fairly thick surface soil layer of older
alluvium or colluviun
The alluvium generally consists of stiff Clayey silt with some
decomposed cobbles and boulders The decomposed cobbles and boulders may
be crushed to dense silty sand and gravel or stiff sandy and clayey silt
The colluvium is slope wash or previous landslide debris which may
be locally inter mixed with the older alluvium It generally consists of
unweathered to extremely weathered or decomposed cobbles and boulders in
a medium stiff clayey silt matrix

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our findings we believe that the alluvial colluvial soils
at the sites could provide adequate footing foundation support for the
proposed structures Where structural fill will be used for site
grading the structures may also be supported by shallow footing
foundations bearing directly on the compacted fill
Reservoir Foundation
The borings disclosed that the project sites are underlain by stiff
brown clayey silt with decomposed gravel cobbles and boulders which
could provide adequate footing foundation support for the proposed tank
and building structures The proposed grading plans indicate that the
new structures will be both on cut and fill
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f is recommended for the
structure footing foundations bearing on the stiff surface soil or
compacted structural fill The allowable bearing value may be increased
by one third for temporary wind and or seismic loads
Footings should be embedded a minimm of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Footings located next to utility trenches
should extend down to the bottom of the utility trench and bear on stiff
undisturbed soil or designed structurally to span over the trench
backfill to minimize footing settlement
A 6 inch cushion layer of gravel such as 3B Fine is recommended
below the structures to provide drainage and uniform support

For the tank structure at Well Site No 4 which will be located
partly in cut and partly on fill the area in cut should be overexcavated
1 foot and backfilled to reduce differential settlement The backfill
should be compacted to match the density of the new fill The fill
materials within the top 12 inches below the reservoir on grade slabs
should consist of select non expansive on site soil less than 3 inches
in size or off site borrow such as crusher waste material The
structural fill should be placed in 8 inch loose lifts and compacted to
obtain a minimm 95 of its maximum dry density established by ASTM
D 1557 78 test method
Prior to concrete placement the bottom of all footing excavations
should be compacted to a minimm of 95 of its maximm dry density as
established by ASTM D 1557 78
The site grading should be designed to divert surface water away
from the structure foundations
Based on the anticipated structure loads it is our opinion that
footing settlements would generally be less than one half of an inch
total and one fourth of an inch differential
Passive earth pressure against the buried vertical faces of the
footings may be taken as an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per
foot of depth A friction factor of 0 45 may be used to determine the
sliding resistance between the footing foundations and the subsoils

Footings at High Groundwater Level Area
Based on the proposed site grading and water level measured in the
borings the footing subgrade at the Boring 4 area in Well Site No 4 may
be very close to or below the groundwater level if the probable
groundwater seepage or water leakage is not intercepted or stopped before
footing excavation The high groundwater level may cause future footing
settlement problem and will also create construction difficulties
Therefore the cause of the high water level should be determined during
construction and remedied
If the high water level is due to seepage from the hillside the
groundwater level may be lowered by the subdrain system installed behind
the proposed retaining wall along the hillside toe area If it is caused
by well leakage the high groundwater level may be lowered by sealing the
leak s
Site Grading
In general minor cutting is planned for all the well sites At
Well Site No 4 the site grading will also include filling along the
existing terrace
At the onset of earthwork the sites should he cleared of
vegetation debris and soft materials Prior to filling or footing
excavation the existing ground should be scarified to a minimm depth of
6 inches and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximm dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 78 test method
Based on the field reconnaissance and laboratory test results most
of the on site soils are in general suitable for use as fill

Fills should be placed in 8 inch maximm lifts and compacted to a
minimum of 90 of ASTM D 1557 maximum dry density
Boulders less than 2 feet in diameter may be used as non structural
fill provided they are not allowed to nest together and the voids between
boulders are filled
Iarge boulders in excess of 2 feet in diameter may be used to
construct riprap or buttress or should be reduced to smaller sizes
If additional off site borrow is required the material should be
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to transportation to the
site The material should generally be of low expansion potential and
should not contain large percentages of boulders organic or deleterious
material
Since the project sites are located in a fairly high rainfall area
50 to 75 inches year the on site subsoils may have in place moisture
contents above their optimum moisture contents Therefore drying of the
excavated material may be required before using it as fill material
Any local soft spots and soft material encountered within the
construction areas should be removed and replaced with compacted fill
Fills placed on existing ground steeper than 5H 1V should be keyed
and benched into the existing firm ground to properly bond the new fill
to the slope as shown on Plate 5 This detail is essential for the
stability of the fills particularly to reduce the potential of sliding
along the interface between the fill and in situ ground The filling
operations should start at the lowest point and continue up in level
compacted layers
Proper drainage is essential for satisfactory performance of the
cuts and fills as excessive water will weaken the clayey silt soils

Retaining Wall
Retaining walls are required as part of the construction for the
site grading structures and roadway These walls will be located
against the existing hillside slopes to create level pads for siting
structures and along the existing stream banks to align the roadway
We recommend that the retaining wall excavation and construction be
made in sections and backfilled as soon as practicable For the
temporary cut slope behind portions of the wall a gradient of up to
may be used
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f may be used for the wall
footing foundations bearing on compacted fill or stiff natural soils
Wall footings located on level ground should be embedded a minimm
of 2 feet below the lowest adjacent finished grade For walls along the
stream banks the wall footings should be embedded a minimum of 3 feet or
a sufficient depth to obtain a minimum horizontal set back distance of
about 10 feet as measured from the outer edge of the footing to the
stream edge
Well graded granular material such as base course rock sand or
equivalent should be used as backfill material directly behind the
retaining walls This would minimize the compactive effort required for
the backfill and reduce the build up of additional lateral pressures on
the walls
For the material outside the zone for granular backfill we believe
that the on site excavated material which consists of alluvial or
colluvial soils can be used Notwithstanding the recommended use of
on site material all backfills used should be non expansive soil free
of organic matter and form a dense stable soil mass when compacted to the
required density Generally the particle size should be less than 6
inches in size

To minimize the build up of hydrostatic pressures behind the walls

Drainage Ditch Drainage ditches should be provided immediately

ditches will direct the surface run off away from the wall

Retaining Wall at Well Site No 4
The toe of the northern hillside at Well Site No 4 slopes locally
at a steep ratio of about Both our field reconnaissance and
on slope boring results and 4 indicate that the steep
Slope could probably remain generally stable for a relatively long period
of time In addition it appears that the risk of some amount of damage
to the down slope steel water tank by slope instability may be relatively
small and generally acceptable
Therefore we believe that a relatively short retaining wall as
currently proposed may be adequate for providing the required grade
separation However it is suggested that the wall height be increased
by an extra 3 feet to attempt to trap anticipated minor slope sloughing
Riprap Embankment
The section of retaining wall in close proximity to the Makaha
Stream at Well Site No 4 may be protected by a grouted riprap as
proposed We recommend that the side slopes be constructed at a slope
ratio of 1H 1V or flatter A cut off wall about 3 feet deep is
recommended at the end of the riprap
A filter fabric should be placed over the slope prior to placement
of the riprap to reduce piping and erosion The riprap should be
carefully placed to minimize voids between rocks The riprap should have
sufficient openings left in the facing to allow free drainage of water
through the grouted riprap

Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected soil samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of
soil properties The results of these tests are presented on the Boring
Logs at the appropriate sample depths
Six 6 one inch ring swell tests were performed on selected natural
or remolded soil samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the
existing near surface soils The test results are summarized on Plate
B 1
Two 2 Atterberg Limits tests were performed on selected soil
samples to further aid in their classification The test results are
summarized on Plates B 1 and B 2
One 1 sieve analysis test was performed on a selected sample of the
underlying soil to evaluate its grain size distribution The test results
are summarized on Plate B 2
Two 2 California Bearing Ratio CBR tests were performed on
selected surface soil samples to evaluate their subgrade support
characteristics The test results are summarized on Plate B 2
Two 2 direct shear tests were performed on selected soil samples to
evaluate their shear strength parameters The test results are presented
on Plates B 3 and B 4
