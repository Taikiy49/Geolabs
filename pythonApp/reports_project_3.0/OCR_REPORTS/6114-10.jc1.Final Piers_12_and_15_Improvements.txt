SUMMARY OF FINDINGS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Our on land field exploration generally encountered a thin surface fill layer and
relatively thick soft loose harbor sediment and or lagoonal deposits overlying coralline
detritus and alluvium materials extending to depths of about 119 to 123 feet below the
existing ground surface We encountered groundwater at depths varying from
approximately 4 5 to 6 feet below the existing ground surface at the time of our field
exploration
Our off shore exploration generally encountered thick soft loose harbor sediment
and or lagoonal deposits and medium dense coralline deposit overlying river deposits and
basalt formation extending to depths of about 100 to 150 feet below the water surface The
river deposits consist of soft estuarine stiff alluvium and dense river cobble and boulder
Mudline was encountered at about 15 to 24 feet below the water surface
Based on the results of the off shore exploration and higher structural lateral load
demands we believe that the proposed waterfront loading platforms and berthing mooring
dolphins may be supported on 20 inch octagonal prestressed precast concrete piles with
various batter inclinations between one horizontal to twelve vertical 1H 12V and
three horizontal to twelve vertical 3H 12V Based on the lateral load analysis we
recommend extending 20 inch octagonal concrete piles to tip elevations of 90 and
110 feet MSL at Piers 12 and 15 respectively to provide sufficient load resistance
We recommend installing 16 5 inch octagonal prestressed precast concrete piles to
support the parking and delivery structural slab at Pier 15 It is our opinion that 16 5 inch
octagonal concrete piles may be extended to a depth of about 100 feet below bottom of
pile cap to provide adequate load capacity
We recommend using a micropile system with 7 inch bulb diameter extending to a
depth of about 60 and 85 feet below pile cap at Piers 12 and 15 respectively The
micropile system consists of 35 foot and 60 foot load bearing grout bulb at Piers 12 and
15 respectively developed from competent soils and a 25 foot unbounded grout zone
neglected within the upper soft loose harbor and lagoonal deposits
The text of this report should be referred to for detailed discussions and specific
recommendations for design of the project

Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Piers 12 and 15 improvements project in Honolulu Harbor
on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are shown on
the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings and geotechnical recommendations derived
from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering analyses for the project
The recommendations presented herein are intended for the design of foundations and
pavements only The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to
the limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2
Project Considerations
The proposed waterfront improvements project is at Piers 12 and 15 along Nimitz
Highway in Honolulu Harbor on the Island of Oahu Hawaii It is proposed to increase
the berthing capacity of Piers 12 and 15 for the Marine Spill Response Corporation
MSRC and Clean Island Council CIC primary spill response vessels and barges as
part of the Honolulu Harbor Redevelopment We understand that the waterfront
improvements consist of design and construction of new berthing and mooring
structures to create approximately 300 and 500 linear feet of berthing spaces adjacent
to the existing Piers 12 and 15 respectively
We understand that the existing bulkhead at both piers is cracked in many areas
and in poor condition Therefore the waterfront improvements are required to be
supported independently without being connected to the existing bulkheads In
addition new structural slabs are planned to provide parking and delivery access for
service vehicles used to supply the vessels
As part of the initial development study Geolabs was hired by American Marine
Corporation AMC to conduct a subsurface investigation for the improvements project
at Piers 12 and 15 The results of the subsurface investigation were reflected in the

Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu is composed largely of the weathered remnants of the extinct
Waianae and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano forms the bulk of
the western third of the island while the younger Koolau Volcano forms the majority of
the eastern two thirds of the island It is believed that Wainae became extinct while
Koolau was still active and its eastern flank is partially buried below Koolau lavas in
central Oahu
The project site is on the coastal plain of southern Oahu The coastal plain was
built on the eroded flanks of the Koolau Volcano where extensive accumulation of
alluvium derived from erosion of the volcano interbedded with coral reefs and
associated deposits
During the later Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age many sea level changes occurred as
a result of widespread glaciation in the continental areas of the world As the great
continental glaciers advanced the level of the ocean fell since less water was available to
fill the oceanic basins Conversely as the glaciers melted global sea levels rose
because more water was available The landmass of Oahu remained essentially stable
during these changes and the fluctuations were eustatic in nature These glacio eustatic
fluctuations resulted in stands of the sea which were both higher and lower relative to
present sea level on Oahu
The higher sea level stands caused the accumulation of deltas and fans of
terrigenous sediments in the heads of the old bays accumulation of reef deposits at
corresponding higher elevations and lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters
protected by fringing reefs The concurrent growth of reefs and lagoonal sediments
may have also resulted in deposits of coral algal limestone and marls within the
predominantly lagoonal sedimentary unit
The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys in the sediments and reef
deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and calcareous materials caused
desiccation of the soft deltaic materials and lagoonal deposits and induration of the

Site Description
The project site is located at Piers 12 and 15 of Honolulu Harbor along Nimitz
Highway on the Island of Oahu Hawaii Pier 12 is vacant and is currently being used
as a parking lot while Pier 15 is currently being used as a base for the Honolulu Fire
Department Waterfront Station as shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2
The existing Pier 12 is bounded by the Honolulu Cruise Terminal at Aloha Tower
Marketplace to the south and Nimitz Highway to the east The rectangular shaped pier
structure extends about 120 feet into Honolulu Harbor with concrete bulkhead along
three sides of the waterfront pier A chain link fence was installed along the waterfront
for protection Abandoned concrete piles were cut off near the water level in front of the
northern pier Stacked coral blocks with some brick mortar were placed in front of the
western pier for wave surge protection
The existing Pier 15 is in a triangular shaped waterfront structure bounded by
Nimitz Highway to the east and adjacent to Nuuanu Stream to the north The entire
southern and partial western waterfronts were previously reconstructed supported by
16 5 inch octagonal concrete piles Dumped rock was placed in front of the concrete
bulkhead along the western pier for wave surge protection A shed was observed at the
northern end of the pier
Both Piers 12 and 15 are paved with asphalt concrete Based on our field
observations and the topographic survey map provided the topography at the project

Driven Pile Foundations
Based on the high lateral load demands and thick soft loose soils encountered in
the off shore borings drilled we recommend installing a driven pile foundation
consisting of 20 inch octagonal precast prestressed concrete piles to support the
loading platform and berthing mooring waterfront structures at Piers 12 and 15
We understand that the structural loads applied on the waterfront structures
including loading platforms and berthing mooring dolphins consist of dead live loads
berthing mooring forces and seismic loads acting at various locations The structural
loads were factored in eight load combinations and modeled to apply on each waterfront
structure
Based on the results of the lateral load analysis we recommend installing
20 inch octagonal concrete piles with various batter configurations from 1H 12V to
3H 12H It is our opinion that the driven concrete pile foundation supporting the
waterfront structures at Piers 12 and 15 should extend to tip elevations of approximately
90 and 110 feet MSL respectively
Based on the thick soft loose soils encountered at shallow depth in the land
borings drilled at Pier 15 we believe that appreciable settlements would occur if a
shallow foundation system is used to withstand the new structural slab supporting the
parking and delivery area of Pier 15 Therefore we recommend using driven pile
foundation consisting of 16 5 inch octagonal precast prestressed concrete piles
extending to a depth of about 100 feet below bottom of pile cap
In general the driven concrete piles would derive support from end bearing on
the dense coralline deposit or stiff alluvium materials and from skin friction between the
pile and the surrounding soils Based on our analyses we recommend designing the
driven pile compressive and uplift capacities to support the proposed structures as
summarized in the following table

Lateral Load Resistance of Pile Group Foundation
Due to high lateral load demand we envision that a pile group with a combination
of vertical and batter piles may be designed to resist the structural loads of the
waterfront loading platforms and berthing mooring dolphins planned at Piers 12
and 15 The pile group configuration would have to limit the pile battering angle
along the waterfront side to avoid potential vessel mooring impact Based on our
analysis we believe that pile group configuration consisting of batter piles ranging
from 1H 12V to 3H 12V with vertical piles may be efficient to provide axial and
lateral reaction resistance

Micropile Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered on the land side of Pier 12 we
believe that a micropile foundation system may be installed to support the structural
slab The micropiles may be installed effectively to a desired depth through the hard
layer of coralline deposits This hard coral layer may prevent adequate penetration of a
driven concrete pile without predrilling efforts
Construction of a column supporting the new shed roof at Pier 15 is also required
as part of Pier 15 Modification Based on the subsurface conditions encountered on the
land side of Pier 15 and magnitude of the structural loads provided we recommend
installation of micropiles to support the new shed rood column
A micropile consists of a small diameter usually less than 12 inches drilled and
grouted pile with reinforcing steel The micropile foundation typically is constructed by
drilling a borehole placing reinforcing steel in the hole and grouting the borehole
Micropiles are desirable because they can be installed readily in access restrictive
environments and in numerous soil types and ground conditions In addition
installation of the micropiles generally causes minimal disturbance to adjacent
structures the adjacent soils and the environment
Based on the results of the lateral load analysis and in situ bearing
characterization we believe that the micropile system with a minimum grout bulb
diameter of 7 inches may provide sufficient load capacity to support the concrete
structural slab for Pier 12 parking and delivery and the new shed roof column at Pier 15
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered we recommend extending the
micropiles at Pier 12 through the loose soft harbor and or lagoonal deposits to derive
load bearing support from the medium dense to dense coral and coralline materials
encountered at greater depths Due to relatively light structural load and thick soft loose
subsurface conditions we recommend extending the micropiles at Pier 15 through the

Based on the existing topography at the project site we envision that minimal
site grading work will be required to achieve the finished grades In general the
following recommendations are intended to provide guidelines for the site preparation

At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared and grubbed thoroughly Vegetation construction debris and other deleterious
materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site Soft and or yielding
areas encountered during clearing and grubbing below areas designated to receive fills
should be over excavated to expose firm dense materials and the resulting excavation
should be backfilled with well compacted fills The excavated soft and or organic soils
should be properly disposed of off site and or used in landscape areas where
appropriate Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for
over excavation and compacted fill placement to account for variations in the
over excavation quantities

After clearing and grubbing areas at grade or areas designated to receive fills
should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as
a percentage of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM D 1557 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
Imported fill materials required for site filling should consist of select granular fill
materials such as crushed coral and basaltic gravel The material should be
well graded from coarse to fine with particles no greater than 3 inches in largest
dimension The material should have a California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 20 or
higher and a swell value of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with
ASTM D 1883 The material should also contain between 10 and 30 percent particles
passing the No 200 sieve
Fill and backfill materials required for the project construction should be placed in
level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Compaction requirements for the subgrades of improvements subjected to
vehicular traffic should be increased to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Fill or backfill below the groundwater level should consist of free draining
granular materials such as open graded gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation up to a
minimum of 12 inches above the groundwater level If fill or backfill below the water
level is placed on clayey materials we recommend wrapping the open graded gravel in
a non woven filter fabric such as Mirafi 180N or equivalent
Aggregate base course and aggregate subbase required for the project should
consist of crushed basaltic aggregate and should conform to Subsections 703 06 and
703 17 of the State of Hawaii Standard Specifications for Road and Bridge
Construction 2005 Aggregate base course and aggregate subbase materials should

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and
sampling two off shore boreholes as designated as Boring Nos 101 and 102 and
three land boreholes designated as Boring Nos 103 through 105 The off shore
boreholes were extended to depths of about 115 5 and 120 feet below the water
surface while the land boreholes were advanced to depths of about 119 5 to 122 5 feet
below the existing ground surface The approximate boring locations are shown on the
Site Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2 The borings were drilled using a skid mounted for
off shore borings or a truck mounted for land borings drill rigs equipped with
continuous flight augers and rotary wash drilling coring tools
Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual and
textural examination in the field in general accordance with ASTM D 2488 Standard
Practice for Description and Identification of Soils and monitored the drilling operations
on a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general accordance with ASTM D 2487
Standard Practice for Classification of Soils for Engineering Purposes Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Soil Log Legend Plate A Graphic
representations of the materials encountered and other observations such as
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified
California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition some
samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch
OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are
shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Pocket penetrometer and or torvane tests were performed on selected cohesive
soil samples in the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the
unconfined compressive strength of the sample The torvane test provides an indication
of the undrained shear strength of the sample Pocket penetrometer test and torvane
test results are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

Laboratory Tests
Moisture Content and Unit Weight tests were performed on selected soil samples
to aid in defining the interface between the various soil layers encountered and
correlating the layers between the borings The moisture content tests were performed
in general accordance with ASTM D 2216 The unit weight tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 2937 The test results are presented on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths in Appendix A
Ten Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The test
results are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 1
Thirteen Sieve Analysis tests were performed on selected soil samples to
evaluate the gradation characteristics and to aid in soil classification The tests were
performed in accordance with ASTM C 117 and C 136 Graphic presentation of the test
results are provided on Plates B 2 through B 4
Four
Unconsolidated
Undrained
Triaxial
Compression
TXUU
tests
ASTM D 2850 were performed on selected soil samples to evaluate the undrained
shear strength of the silty and clayey soils encountered The approximate in situ
effective overburden pressure was used as the applied confining pressure for the
relatively undisturbed soil sample The test results and the stress strain curves are
presented on Plates B 5 through B 8
Three strained controlled
consolidated drained
Direct
Shear
tests
were
performed on the in situ soil samples in accordance with ASTM D 3080 The test
results are presented on Plates B 9 and B 11
One Unconfined Compression test ASTM D 7012 Method C was performed on
selected rock core to evaluate the unconfined compressive strength of the rock
formation encountered Unconfined compression test results are presented on
Plate B 12
One Modified Proctor compaction test ASTM D 1557 C was performed on a bulk
sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density and moisture content
relationships The test results are presented on Plate B 13
One laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR test ASTM D 1883 was performed
on a bulk sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support
characteristics of the soils The results of the test are presented on Plate B 14

Previous Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and
sampling five off shore boreholes designated as Boring Nos 1 through 5 extending to
depths of about 107 to 156 5 feet below the drilling platform The approximate boring
locations are shown on the Site Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2 The borings were drilled
using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers and rotary wash
drilling coring tools
Our geologist classified the materials encountered in the borings by visual and
textural examination in the field in general accordance with ASTM D 2488 Standard
Practice for Description and Identification of Soils and monitored the drilling operations
on a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general accordance with ASTM D 2487
Standard Practice for Classification of Soils for Engineering Purposes Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Soil Log Legend Plate C Graphic
representations of the materials encountered and other observations such as
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained in general accordance with
ASTM D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified
California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In addition some
samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch
OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The blow counts
needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive are
shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate
sample depths
Pocket penetrometer and or torvane tests were performed on selected cohesive
soil samples in the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the
unconfined compressive strength of the sample The torvane test provides an indication
of the undrained shear strength of the sample Pocket penetrometer test and torvane
test results are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths

Previous Laboratory Tests
Moisture Content and Unit Weight tests were performed on selected soil samples
to aid in defining the interface between the various soil layers encountered and
correlating the layers between the borings The moisture content tests were performed
in general accordance with ASTM D 2216 The unit weight tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 2937 The test results are presented on the Logs of
Borings at the appropriate sample depths in Appendix D
Seven Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The test
results are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate D 1
Four Sieve Analysis tests were performed on selected soil samples to evaluate
the gradation characteristics and to aid in soil classification The tests were performed in
accordance with ASTM C 117 and C 136 Graphic presentation of the test results are
provided on Plate D 2

effective overburden pressure was used as the applied confining pressure for the
relatively undisturbed soil sample The test results and the stress strain curves are
presented on Plates D 1 through D 10
