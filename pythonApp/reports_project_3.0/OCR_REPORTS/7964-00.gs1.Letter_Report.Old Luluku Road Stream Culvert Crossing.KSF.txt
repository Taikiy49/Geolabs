Geolabs, Inc. is pleased to submit our letter report entitled "Geotechnical 
Engineering Services, Old Luluku Road Stream Culvert Crossing, Kaneohe, Oahu,

shown on the Project Location Map, Plate 1. 
This letter report summarizes our findings and geotechnical recommendations 
derived from a site reconnaissance and our review of available drawings. These 
geotechnical recommendations are intended to assist the client in the design of a 
temporary crossing at the project site. The findings and recommendations presented 
herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this letter report. 
Project Considerations 
The Old Luluku Road Stream Culvert Crossing project is located adjacent to Likelike 
Highway near the Interstate Route H-3 overhead crossing.  A 108-inch diameter steel 
corrugated metal pipe (CMP) culvert crosses an existing access road from Likelike Highway.  
The existing culvert has been damaged on the outlet end of the culvert. 
We understand that Hawaiian Electric Company (HECO) wants to use the existing 
access road to gain access for their equipment.  Therefore, HECO would need to cross the 
damaged culvert with their equipment. Furthermore, we understand that repairing the 
damaged culvert is not desired. 
We understand that performing borings is not desired.  Instead, a site 
reconnaissance was performed for the development of foundation recommendations for the 
temporary culvert crossing.

108-Inch Diameter CMP Culvert 
Based on as-built drawings provided, the 108-inch diameter steel CMP pipe is 
about 101 feet in length.  A head wall inlet structure with wingwalls are provided on the 
upstream end.  At the downstream end, a head wall outlet structure with wingwalls, a 
skewed extension, energy dissipating protrusions and grouted rip-rap (GRP) lining are 
shown on the drawings.   
The bottom of the culvert is sloped at about 2.72 percent.  The cover height above 
the top of the culvert to the existing access road is about 15 feet.  The culvert is shown 
on the Site Plan, Plate 2, and the 108-Inch Diameter Culvert, Plate 3. 
Site Reconnaissance 
A site reconnaissance was performed on June 24, 2019 to observe the existing 
conditions at the culvert.  The downstream portion of the culvert has failed and broken off 
near the top of the embankment slope of the access road.  We understand that the failed 
portion extended to about 8 feet beyond the original top of the embankment slope. 
A near vertical face was observed at the downstream end.  It appears that the 
vertical face material consisted of fill material composed of medium stiff clayey silt with 
gravel and cobbles.  Damaged culvert section was observed further downstream from the 
vertical face. 
The condition of the upstream end of the culvert appears intact with no visible signs 
of failure.  Water was flowing through the culvert during our site visit. 
Recommendations 
We understand that a 12-foot wide roadway would be needed for anticipated 
HECO’s vehicles.  In addition, the installation of steel plates on the ground surface is 
planned to reduce the concentrated loads to the existing culvert. 
To reduce the potential for additional slope failure, we recommend that the edge 
of the steel plates be located behind a one horizontal to one vertical (1H:1V) imaginary 
line extending upwards from the bottom of the culvert at the vertical failure face.  If 
needed, the steel plates can extend beyond the top of the slope at the upstream end.  
Temporary shallow support structures or footings would be needed to support the 
cantilevered steel plates on the slope. 
During the period when construction vehicles are traversing across the existing 
culvert, it is recommended that the condition of the vertical failed face be monitored on a 
daily basis for signs of distress (cracks, fallen soil, etc.).

Design Review 
Preliminary and final drawings for the project should be forwarded to Geolabs for 
review and written comments prior to construction. This review is necessary to evaluate 
the conformance of the plans with the intent of the recommendations provided herein. If 
this review is not made, Geolabs cannot be responsible for the misinterpretation of our

Limitations 
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based, in part, upon 
information obtained from a site visit and our review of available drawings.  Field borings 
were not performed for this project. Variations of subsoil conditions may occur, and the 
nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until construction is 
underway. If variations then appear evident, it will be necessary to re-evaluate the 
recommendations provided herein.

accommodate these possible extra costs.
