performed for the proposed Westwood Gardens subdivision development at Waipio on

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located off Waipahu and Hiapo Streets in the Waipio area on
the island of Oahu Hawaii The project site covers an area of approximately 5 acres
along an existing cane haul road and is located to the south of Interstate Highway H 1
as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
It is proposed to construct approximately 25 two story single family housing units
Based on the current design concept wood frame construction with slabs on grade
foundations are planned for the majority of the houses For houses located on slope
spread and or continuous footings are anticipated In addition retaining walls access
roads and utilities are planned to serve the proposed subdivision development We
envision that site grading involving cuts and fills on the order of about 15 feet would be
needed to develop the site

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basaltic lavas from two shield
volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be of
the middle to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western third of the island
The younger shield Koolau is estimated to be of the late Pliocene to early Pleistocene
Ice Age in age and forms the majority of the eastern two thirds of the island
Waianae became extinct while Koolau was still active and its eastern flank was partially
buried below Koolau lavas banking against its eastern flank
The project site is composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of the lavas
of Koolau Volcanic Series These rocks are generally characterized by flows of jointed
dense vesicular basalt with interbedded thin clinker layers In situ chemical weathering
of the Koolau lava flows has occurred for the last 1 to 2 million years The weathering
process has formed a mantle of residual soils which grade with depth to saprolite The
saprolite grades with depth to basaltic rock formation In general saprolite is
composed mainly of silty material and is typical of the tropical weathering of volcanic
rocks

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Based on our field exploration and engineering analyses the proposed
subdivision site is underlain by stiff to hard clayey silts and silty clays extending to the
maximum depth explored of about 16 feet below the existing ground surface
Laboratory tests performed on the near surface silty and clayey soils indicate that
the materials generally have a slight to low shrink swell potential when subjected to
fluctuations in the moisture content Based on our field exploration and laboratory
testing we believe that a shallow foundation system bearing on the stiff soils and or
compacted engineered fills may be used to support the proposed houses
Detailed discussions and recommendations for foundation design site grading
pavements and other geotechnical aspects of the project are presented in the following
sections of this report
Slabs On Grade
In general the proposed houses may be supported on concrete slabs on grade
foundation bearing on the near surface stiff silty and clayey soils and or compacted
engineered fill materials We envision that slabs on grade will be supported on the
existing near surface soils and or on non expansive fills needed to raise the existing
grades where applicable Based on the laboratory test results the near surface soils
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3375 00 Page 7
encountered at the site generally consist of silty clays and clayey silts which exhibit
slight to low expansion potential
Therefore concrete slabs on grade may be constructed on grade with proper
subgrade preparation The subgrades for slabs on grade should be scarified to a depth
of at least 6 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers
to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry
density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation
D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density
For more uniform support of the slabs we recommend that concrete slabs on
grade be supported on a minimum 4 inch cushion fill The cushion fill layer should
consist of open graded gravels such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 grada
tion which will also serve as a capillary moisture break The slab cushion fill layer
should be placed as soon as possible after scarification and recompaction of the
existing subgrade soils For slabs on grade with moisture sensitive surfacing we
recommend that an impermeable membrane be placed over the cushion fill to reduce
potential migration of moisture vapor through the concrete slab In order to promote
uniform curing of the slab concrete and to provide protection of the vapor membrane
we recommend that a 2 inch layer of moist fine sand be placed above the membrane
prior to placing the slab concrete The sand should be moistened slightly prior to
placing concrete
Where concrete slab will be subjected to vehicular traffic such as the driveways
and garage slabs the slabs should be supported on a 6 inch layer of base course
GEOLABS HAWAIl
W O 3375 00 Page 8
compacted to 95 percent relative compaction in lieu of the 4 inch cushion fill layer and
2 inch sand The base course should consist of crushed basalt aggregate
For design of walkways we recommend that 4 inch thick concrete bearing on the
silty and clayey subgrade be considered Construction joints should be provided at
intervals equal to the width of the walkways with expansion joints at right angle
intersections Subgrade soils should be prepared in accordance with the recommenda
tions presented in the Pavements section of this report
Spread Footing Foundations
In lieu of concrete slabs on grade the proposed houses may be supported on
a spread continuous footing foundation bearing on the near surface stiff silty and clayey
soils and or compacted engineered fill materials The footings may be designed using
an allowable bearing pressure of 3 000 p s f The allowable bearing value is for dead
plus live loads and may be increased by one third 1 for transient loading conditions
such as wind or seismic forces The base of the foundation excavation should be firm
and unyielding It should be noted that the bearing value provided above assumes that
the proposed houses are not located across cut and fill transitions with more than 5 feet
of differential fill thickness across the structure If this condition exists Geolabs Hawaii
should be consulted for supplemental recommendations
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent finish grades For footings constructed near top of slope or on sloping
ground the footings should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum
horizontal set back distance of 8 feet measured from the outside edge of the footings
to the slope face
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3375 00 Page 9
Where footings will be located adjacent to below grade structures or utilities the
footings should extend to a depth below an imaginary 45 degree plane projected
upward from the bottom of the below grade structure or utility line trench This
requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade structures with
additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for foundation settlement
Lateral loads may be resisted by a combination of passive earth pressures
exerted against the near vertical foundation faces and soil friction between the base of
footings and the supporting subgrade A frictional coefficient of 0 35 may be used
between the base of footings and the supporting subgrade A passive earth pressure
with an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per cubic foot p c f may be used in
design Unless covered by slabs or pavements passive resistance from the upper 12
inches should be neglected
All footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs
Hawaii prior to placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to evaluate the bearing
materials and the embedment depths
If footing foundations are designed in accordance with our recommendations we
estimate total settlements to be less than 1 inch with differential settlements on the
order of inch

Site Preparation
a
At the on set of earthwork all areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site to
reduce potential contamination of the excavated materials
After clearing and grubbing the exposed subgrade should be scarified to a depth
of at least 6 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture and
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction In cut areas the
subgrade should also be proof rolled to attain a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil ex
pressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil deter
mined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum
moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the
maximum dry density
Any soft weak or yielding areas disclosed during clearing operations should be
over excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation should be
filled with engineered fill moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture

and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction The excavated
soft and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site

8 a a

Utility Trench Backfill
The stress distribution against the bottom of a pipe has a significant effect on the
load supporting capacity of the pipe Therefore pipe bedding is an important design
consideration For pipes bearing on the stiff silty and clayey soils a minimum of
6 inches of granular bedding material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation or equivalent should be provided below the pipe
Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation or equivalent should be used for the trench backfill up to about
12 inches above the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce
compaction of the backfill thus reducing the potential for damaging the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes
to the top of the subgrade or finish grade should consist of the on site soils The
backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture placed in
maximum 8 inch loose level lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than
90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground subsidence
Where trenches are below pavements and other improvements such as sidewalks the
upper 1 foot of the trench backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted
to 95 percent relative compaction

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
ik
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling
seven borings designated as Boring Nos 1 through 7 at the approximate locations
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The depths of our borings ranged from about 11 to
16 feet below the existing ground surface The borings were drilled using truck
mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our Field Geologist who monitored the drilling operations on
a near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed visually and by
testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified
Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic presentation of the materials
encountered are provided on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 7
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils
by driving a 3 inch O D Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling
30 inches The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the last 12 inches of an 18
inch drive are shown on the logs of borings at the appropriate sample depths

sample of the near surface soils to evaluate the moisture content and dry density
relationship The test results are summarized on Plate B 1 Graphic presentation of
the test results are presented on Plate B 3

of internal friction and cohesion of the soil sample tested The test results are
graphically presented on Plate B 4
