PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located within the western portion of the Royal Kunia
Development Area in the District of Waipahu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project
site is located east of Kunia Road west of Kupuohi Street and north of the Wal Mart
Store The project site includes a total of 61 residential lots and access roadways A
Project Location Map and a Site Plan are shown on Plates 1 and 2 respectively
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services
pertaining to the house grading and utility installation within the house lot areas for the
project site
HOUSE GRADING AND UTILITY INSTALLATION
The house grading and utility installation work for the Royal Kunia Site 4B project
were observed by Geolabs Inc from March 25 2002 to August 30 2002 The house
grading work consisted of footing excavation and footing and slab on grade subgrade
preparation for the project The utility installation work consisted of trench backfill of
plumbing lines within the house and garage pad areas and the backfill of the sewer
clean out areas The house grading and utility backfilling work were performed by Quality
General During the house grading operations samples of the on site and imported
materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office
for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established

HOUSE CONSTRUCTION CONSIDERATIONS
The finished grades adjacent to the house structures should be sloped to shed
water away from the foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding It is also advised
that gutter systems be installed around the house structures and that the discharge be
diverted away from the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering
within 3 feet of the foundations and slabs should be avoided Gravel strips adjacent to

slabs and foundations should be avoided Planiers next to foundations should be avoided
or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the house
foundations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation and subsequent
loss of strength of the near surface soils The foundation excavations should be properly
backfilled against the footings immediately after setting of the concrete to reduce water
infiltration Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be
maintained to drain surface run off away from foundations Landscaping of the house lots
by the homeowners is recommended to reduce the potential for erosion and for altering
the drainage patterns
Consideration should be given to the construction of a 3 foot wide sidewalk
adjacent to and surrounding the entire house structure A sidewalk with an 8 inch deep
thickened edge should help stabilize the subgrade soil moisture below the house
structure and therefore reduce the potential for swelling shrinking and softening of the
subgrade soils
LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site therefore some work may have occurred
without our observation However we believe that the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project It
should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability
of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials Our
services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical
engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction
monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type
The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to the
plans and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
