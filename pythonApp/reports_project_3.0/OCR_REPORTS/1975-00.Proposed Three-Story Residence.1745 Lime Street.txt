SUMMARY OF RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our boring and laboratory test data the
subsurface conditions consist primarily of a
surficial 2 foot fill layer over 2 to 3 feet of
highly compressible clayey silt Beneath the
highly compressible layer are loose lagoonal
silty gravel deposits
We believe that the thin fill crust and the
highly compressible deposits cannot provide
adequate foundation support and excessive
settlement may occur under the new building
loads
In order to provide adequate bearing support for
spread footing foundations of the three story
structure it is our opinion that the soft
highly compressible clayey silt under the footing
foundation should be over excavated and
backfilled with compacted structural fill prior
to footing construction
The text of this report should be referred to for
detailed and special design recommendations
This report presents the results of our soil engineering evaluation
performed for the proposed three story residence at 1745 Lime Street in
the Moiliili District of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the site
and its vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 This
report summarizes our findings and recommendations

PROJECT CONS TDERATTONS
It is proposed to demolish the existing house at the site and
construct a new three story residence The proposed building will have
CMU walls on the ground floor with wood construction above Anticipated
wall and column loads are 1400 p s f per foot length and 16 kips
respectively
Site filling up to about 1 foot is anticipated to facilitate
drainage
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our evaluation was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions at the site to obtain pertinent soil data for the design of the
foundations of the structure particularly to evaluate if there would be
an adequate crust with sufficient thickness to support a spread footing
type of foundation
The scope of our investigation included drilling and sampling two 2
borings to depths of 27 feet below the existing ground surface Also
included were laboratory testing of selected samples obtained engineering
analysis and evaluation of the field and laboratory data development of
the preliminary design recommendations and report preparation
tailed description of our field exploration and laboratory test

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration generally indicated that a 2 to 3 foot thick
highly compressible layer of clayey silt exists below the upper 2 foot
surficial fill at the subject site Loose lagoonal silty gravel was then
encountered to depths in excess of 20 feet
We believe that the thin upper fill layer and the underlying highly
compressible deposit cannot provide adequate foundation support for the
proposed building and significant settlements may occur under the new
building loads In order to provide adequate bearing for the proposed
building structure it is our opinion that either the soft clayey silt
deposit at the footing foundation locations be over excavated and replaced
with select compacted structural fill or pile foundations should be
considered
Shallow Spread or Continuous Footing Foundations
For the proposed wooden structure spread or continuous footing
foundations may be used However to reduce the potential excessive
settlements and the possibility of ground bearing failure we recommend
that the footing excavations extend down to a minimum depth of about 5
feet below the ground surface to remove the soft marsh deposit The
over excavation should also extend a minimm of 1 5 feet beyond the edge
of the footings See Plate 3 The excavation should first be lined with
a layer of geotextile fabric and then backfilled with 3B Fine gravel to
raise the backfill a minimm 12 inches above the groundwater level Then
non expansive borrow materials such as crusher waste or coral should be

placed in horizontal loose lifts not exceeding 8 inches thick and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximm dry density per ASTM
D 1557 78 prior to footing construction The top 1 foot below finish
subgrade level should be backfilled with impervious non expansive soil
compacted to the above density See Plate 3
Based on the anticipated soil conditions site grading and design
loading we believe that an allowable bearing capacity of 1500 p s f may
be used for spread footing bearing on compacted structural fill
Footings should be placed a minimum of 12 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Uplift wind or other lateral loading conditions
may further increase the embedment of the footing foundation
For the above recommendations estimated foundation settlements are
on the order of less than one inch total and one half of an inch
differential
All footings should be tied together with grade beams to improve the
rigidity of the foundation hence reducing the differential settlement
Pile Foundation
Because of the adverse subsoil conditions pile foundations were
initially considered However due to the stringent budget limit the
borings were not deep enough to locate a suitable bearing layer for piles
If pile foundations are to be considered for the project or the
current design changes in the future require pile foundation we recommend
that some contingent fees be available for a second phase exploration to
find the suitable bearing layer

Site Grading
Site filling should be avoided or at least minimized to less than one
foot since the weight of the new fill would initiate long term ground
settlements for spread footing
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable
materials such as old footings old slabs and pavement vegetation and
rubbish should be removed and disposed of property off site
The ground surface below the slabs should be scarified to a minimm
depth of 6 inches and recompacted to a minimm of 90 of its maximm dry
density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 Soft pockets encountered in the
initial site preparation should be removed and backfilled with select
backfill material
The upper sandy fill materials may be used as a source of backfill
material If required new imported fill material should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum size
with less than 25 passing the 200 sieve
Site grading should provide drainage of surface runoff away from all
foundations

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earthwork and foundation recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings probes test pits and or observation wells Variations of
conditions between the field data points may occur and the nature and
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction If
variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations given in this report

presented in the Boring Logs Plates A 1 and A 2
