This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering evaluation for the 
proposed project near the H-1 and H-2 Merge on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. This report 
summarizes the findings from our engineering evaluation based on previous boring drilled 
at the vicinity and presents our geotechnical recommendations derived from our analyses 
for the project. These recommendations are intended for the design of concrete barrier 
wall foundation only. The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject to 
the limitations noted at the end of this report. 
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The project involves design and construction of the new concrete barrier wall near 
H-1 and H-2 Merge. Based on the information provided, we understand that the new 
concrete barrier wall requires to support vehicle impact force. Details of the structural 
design were not available at the time this report was prepared. 
New boring was not planned. The foundation design parameters are evaluated 
based on the available boring information and subjected to be verified by the construction 
observation. 
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS 
One boring was previously drilled near the project site, designated as Boring Nos. 
112, extending to a depth of about 121 feet below the ground surface. The approximately 
locations of the boring drilled are shown on the Google Earth Map for reference. 
Based on review of boring logs drilled previously, we anticipate the project site is 
generally underlain by very hard/dense residual/saprolite materials over weathered basalt 
formation extending to a depth of about 121 feet below the ground surface.

SEISMIC DESIGN CONSIDERATIONS 
Based on the subsurface materials anticipated at the project site, the project site 
may be classified from a seismic analysis standpoint as being a “Very Dense Soil and 
Soft Rock” site corresponding to a Site Class C soil profile type based on the 2006 IBC 
(Table No. 1613.5.2). 
SHALLOW FOUNDATIONS 
Based on the generally competent subsurface conditions anticipated at the project 
site, we recommend that a shallow foundation system consisting of strip footing bearing on 
the recompacted in-situ residual and/or saprolite soil materials for support of the new 
concrete barrier wall. 
The ultimate bearing capacity of the barrier wall structure supported on strip footing 
is estimated based on the subsurface conditions as described above.  The ultimate 
bearing pressure for the barrier wall footing may be limited to about 7 kips per square foot 
(ksf), as presented in the table below.

The at-rest condition should be used for retaining structures where the top of the 
structure is restrained from movement prior to backfilling of the wall.  The active condition 
should be used only for gravity retaining walls and retaining structures that are free to deflect 
by as much as 0.5 percent of the wall height. 
Based on Site Class “C” at the project site, a design peak ground acceleration was 
computed to be 0.20g in accordance with the International Building Code (2006 Edition).  
Dynamic lateral earth forces due to seismic loading (a = 0.20g) may be estimated by using 
3.3H2 pounds per lineal foot of wall length for level backfill conditions, where H is the height 
of the wall in feet.  It should be noted that the dynamic lateral earth forces provided assume 
that the wall will be allowed to move laterally by up to about 2 inches in the event of an 
earthquake.  For restrained condition (less than 0.5 inches of lateral movement), forces 
caused by dynamic lateral earth pressures due to seismic loading may be estimated using 
9.5H2 pounds per linear foot of wall length for level backfill conditions.  The resultant force 
should be assumed to act through the mid-height of the wall.  An appropriately reduced 
factor of safety may be used when dynamic lateral earth forces are accounted for in the 
design of the retaining structures.

LIMITATIONS 
The analyses and recommendations submitted herein are based, in part, upon 
information obtained from available boring information drilled previously. Variations of 
subsurface conditions between and beyond the borings may occur, and the nature and 
extent of these variations may not become evident until construction is underway. If 
variations then appear evident, it will be necessary to re-evaluate the recommendations 
provided herein. 
The boring location indicated herein are approximate, having been taped from visible 
features at the project site.  Elevations noted on the borings were based on interpolation 
between the spot elevations and contour lines shown on the topographic survey plan 
provided to us previously.  The physical locations and elevations of the borings should be 
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used. 
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict 
the approximate boundaries between soil types and, as such, may denote a gradual 
transition. Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the 
graphic representations and/or presented in the text herein. These data have been 
reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report. However, it must be 
noted that fluctuation may occur due to variation in rainfall, tides, temperature, and other 
factors.
