PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS 
The Kamehameha Highway Access Improvements, Koa Ridge Development 
project site is along Kamehameha Highway from the intersection of Kamehameha 
Highway and Ka Uka Boulevard, and extends about 1,400 linear feet north from the 
intersection in the Waipio area on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. A Project Location Map  
and Site Plans are shown on Plates 1, and 2.1 through 2.3, respectively. 
The project consists of roadway improvements along Kamehameha Highway to 
accommodate a new right-in-right-out only intersection about 900 linear feet north of the 
intersection of Kamehameha Highway and Ka Uka Boulevard.  The roadway 
improvements include widening the existing roadway, re-striping the traffic lanes, 
constructing sidewalks and median, constructing a retaining wall, extending the drainage 
system, installing an anchored wire mesh along an existing slope, and installing traffic 
signage and street lighting.

SITE GRADING AND UTILITY & ROADWAY CONSTRUCTION 
We observed the site grading and utility and roadway construction work for the 
Kamehameha Highway Access Improvements project from November 26, 2019 to 
October 6, 2020.  The site grading work consisted of excavations up to about 30 feet in 
height and placement and compaction of fills on the order of up to about 2 feet thick to 
achieve the finished grades for the project. Royal Contracting Company Ltd. performed the 
site grading and utility and roadway construction work. During the site grading operations, 
we obtained samples of the on-site and imported materials utilized as fill and backfill 
materials and transported them to our laboratory for testing. The maximum dry densities 
and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance 
with ASTM D1557 (Modified Proctor), and the test results are summarized on Plate 3. 
The fill and backfill materials were moisture-conditioned to above the optimum 
moisture content, and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density 
as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557. In the roadway areas, the fill and backfill 
materials within 2 feet of the finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 
95 percent relative density. 
During the site grading and utility and roadway construction operations, periodic 
field density tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938 
(Nuclear Gauge Method). Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on 
Plates 4.1 through 4.10 of this report. These tests were performed on the prepared ground 
surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills at the site. Test locations and elevations 
were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on-site survey stakes. These locations 
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods 
used. Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum 
compaction was obtained and/or approved by our field representative based on visual 
observation and hand probing. 
Our services consisted of part-time field observation of the contractor’s fill 
placement, subgrade preparation, and compaction procedures, and confirmation of 
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above grading operations.
