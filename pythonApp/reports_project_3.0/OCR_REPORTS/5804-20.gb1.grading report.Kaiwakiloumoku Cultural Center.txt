Dear Mr Nishioku
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations performed
during the site grading operations from to August 1 2012 for the
above project in accordance with our fee proposal dated and
subsequent addendums
Project Considerations
We understand that a one story building and one level covered parking structure
are planned at the site The building will house classrooms an assembly area a dining
area and meeting areas The building will be situated in an undeveloped area north of
the Chapel parking lot The parking structure will be located at the existing Chapel
parking lot and will be a covered structure The roof of the parking structure will consist
of landscaped areas and pedestrian walkways
This report addresses our field observations and compaction testing services for
the foundation preparation parking area and utility construction at the project site
Site Grading
Geolabs Inc observed the earthwork construction for the Ka iwakiloumoku
Hawaiian Cultural Center project from to August 1 2012 The
earthwork construction generally consisted of compaction of the existing subgrade
placement and compaction of fills to achieve the finished subgrades shallow foundation
subgrade preparation compaction of the finished subgrades placement and compaction
of select borrow subbase and aggregate base course Delta Construction Corporation
performed the earthwork operations
Kamehameha Schools
Page 2
GEOLABS INC
Hawaii California
Samples of the on site materials utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained
and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and optimum
moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plates 2 1 and
2 2
The general fill and backfill materials were generally moisture conditioned to near
the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 and 95 percent of the
maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
During the earthwork operations periodic field density tests were performed in
general accordance with ASTM D 6938 Nuclear Gauge Method The compaction test
results are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 36 of this report These tests were
performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed fills and backfills Test
locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey
stakes These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used Areas assessed as unsatisfactory were generally reworked
until the specified minimum compaction was obtained
It should be noted that the current grading above the Diamond Head Wall does
not follow grading shown on the Grading Plan Sheet C 8 1A of PCD No 1 Revision 3
Based on our observations the slope inclination is steeper than what is shown on the
Grading Plan Due to the steep inclination of the slope we believe that there is a high
potential for slope instability
Due to budgetary constraints we understand that it was decided to place a turf
reinforcement mat on the face of the slope and vegetate the face of the slope to reduce
slope instability We were informed that a future detailed study on the overall slope
condition will be conducted in the future to determine the type of remedial measures
needed to permanently reduce the potential for slope instability We believe that the
currently proposed methods should be acceptable to temporarily reduce the potential for
slope instability provided that remedial measures are implemented in the future
Our services consisted of part time field observation of the contractor s fill and
backfill placement subgrade preparation and compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above earthwork operations
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the
referenced tests it is our professional opinion that the earthwork operations for the
Ka iwakiloumoku Hawaiian Cultural Center project observed and tested by Geolabs in the
period covered by this report were performed in general conformance with the intent of
the plans and specifications and our recommendations
Kamehameha Schools Page 3
Limitations
This office assumes no responsibility for any alterations made to slopes or finished
grades on the project subsequent to our observation and testing period without our
knowledge and written acknowledgment
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site therefore some work may have occurred
without our observation However we believe that the level of earthwork observation and
testing services provided is reasonable in reducing the geotechnical risks to the project It
should be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability
of the tests and methods used to monitor the earthwork construction and materials Our
services were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical
engineering practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction
monitoring by an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type
The contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to the
plans and specifications and for repairing defects
