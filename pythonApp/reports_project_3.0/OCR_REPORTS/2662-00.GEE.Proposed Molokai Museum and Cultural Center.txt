INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the design of the site grading building foundations and support facilities
for the proposed Molokai Museum and Cultural Center

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized soils data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering design recommendations pertinent to the design
of site grading foundations and support facilities for the proposed project development
In addition the potential for on site disposal of domestic sewage was explored

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The project site is located on the Hoolehua Plain between the West and East
Molokai Domes The Hoolehua Plain or isthmus of Molokai is composed of lava flows
from the east Molokai Volcano banked against the older West Molokai Volcano Most of
the plain is covered with ten to thirty feet of lateritic soil
The surface and subsurface soils consist of reddish brown to grey brown firm to
friable sandy to clayey silts which grade with weathered gravel at increasing depths The
substratum is soft weathered basic igneous rock

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
It is Our opinion that the proposed project is feasible from a geotechnical
engineering standpoint provided that our recommendations are followed
Sewage Disposal
The project site is located in an unsewered area and there is no known regional
facilities plan scheduled for implementation in the near future Therefore it will be
necessary to use on site treatment and disposal for sanitary wastes generated within the
development area
Our percolation testing performed in the field indicated that at the location and
depth of the test a percolation rate of 1 5 gallons per square foot per day may be
anticipated The results of our percolation test is presented in Appendix C of this report

Site Grading
Prior to the commencement of any earthwork all areas within the grading limits
should be cleared of all vegetation and deleterious materials The resulting grubbed and
spoil material should be disposed of properly off site or stockpiled for use in landscaping
Any soft weak or yielding areas encountered during the clearing operations within the
portions of the grading limits designated to receive foundations or embankment should
be over excavated to firm ground and the resulting depression should be backfilled with
well compacted engineered fill
Generally the on site soils should be suitable for use as engineered fill If
additional imported materials are required the material should generally be non
expansive able to be compacted in a dense stable mass and have a minimum California
Bearing Ratio of 15 when tested in the laboratory in accordance with ASTM Test Method
D 1883
All fill material should be placed in level lifts not exceeding ten inches 10 in loose
thickness Fill placement should commence at the lowest point and continue upward in
level compacted layers The embankments should be compacted to a minimum of 90
of the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM Test Method D
1557 78
in areas to receive fill where the existing ground surface is steeper than five
horizontal to one vertical 56H 1V the new fill should be keyed and benched into the
existing slope to aid in the proper bonding of the new fill into the in situ soils
Slopes
Slope ratios of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V for cut and fill slopes are
recommended at the site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2662 00 Page 6
Fill slopes should be constructed by overfilling past the design slope then cutting
back
Water should be diverted away from the tops of slopes and slope planting provided
to reduce erosion
Foundation Support
Conventional spread footings with slab on grade and or post and beam footings
may be used to support the structures For the design of the structure footings an
allowable bearing capacity of 2500 p s f may be utilized for footings bearing on in situ
soils or on fills compacted in accordance with the above recommendations This value
may be increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are considered
The footings should be embedded a minimum of eighteen inches 18 below the lowest
adjacent grade
If any soft or weak areas are encountered at the bottom of the footing excavation
they should be remedied by over excavation of the soft and or weak materials down to
very stiff natural alluvial soils and backfilling of the resultant depression with fill material
compacted in accordance with the above recommendations The site should be graded
to divert water away from foundations
Retaining Walis
A retaining wall is proposed in conjunction with the proposed building construction
for grade separation The wail will support fills to be placed along the existing slope and
building

The wall footing should be embedded to a depth such that the bottom of the
footing will be set back a minimum of five feet from the face of the slope or to a depth of
twenty four 24 inches whichever is greater
An allowable bearing value of 2500 p s f on compacted fill or natural soil may be
used for the design of the retaining wall foundations
Well graded granular material such as crushed stone base course sand or
equivalent should be used as backfill material directly behind the retaining wall This will
reduce the compactive effort required for the backfill and reduce the build up of additional
lateral pressures on the wall
The backfill material should be placed in eight 8 inch maximum lifts and
compacted to at least 90 of its maximum dry density as determined by ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78
To minimize the build up of hydrostatic pressures behind the wail a subsurface
drainage system should be provided

Pavements
It is anticipated that the proposed parking area and reconstructed access road will
be surfaced with ashaltic concrete Based upon our laboratory analysis the following
pavement section is recommended
2 Inches Asphaltic Concrete
4 Inches Base Course
6 Inches Total pavement Thickness
on a Compacted Moist Subgrade
The subgrade and base course should be compacted to a minimum of 95 of their
maximum dry densities as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
CBR and density tests and or field observations should be performed on the actual
subgrade soils encountered during construction and the above section should be revised
if necessary The recommended section considers light traffic and assumes that
adequate drainage will be provided for the paved areas Weepholes should be provided
at the base course level in drainage inlet structures in order to relieve seepage of water
which may otherwise accumulate in the subgrade

It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during site grading construction of the embankments
and foundation phases of the work This is to observe compliance with the design
concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow design changes in the event
that subsurface conditions differ from anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such observations
If actual exposed soil conditions encountered during construction are different from
those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to schedule appropriate tests
to aid in determining pertinent engineering properties of the soil and or rock materials
sampled
Classification Tests
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Moisture Content ASTM Test Method D 2216 80 and Unit Weight Determinations
are used as an aid to soil classification and to aid in determining certain properties such
as relative saturation The moisture content expressed as a percentage of the dry weight
of the tested sample is shown at the appropriate location on plates summarizing test
results and at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs
Atterberg Limits tests ASTM Test Method D 4318 84 are used to determine the
plasticity of the fine grained portion of soils The Plasticity Index of the material is used
to classify the soil under the Unified Soil Classification System The results of these tests
are shown at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs
Physicai Property Tests
Tests are performed to develop a correiative index of certain physical parameters
of a soil These physical parameters may be used in engineering analyses pertaining to
the performance or suitability of a soil
Expansion tests are performed to evaluate the swelling potential of soils under light
structural loads These tests aid in evaluating the materials for use in embankment
construction and to provide on grade slab support These test results are summarized
on Plate B 1
