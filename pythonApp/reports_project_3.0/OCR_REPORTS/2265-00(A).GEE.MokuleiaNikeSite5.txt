GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEERING EVALUATION
PROPOSED NEW ANTENNA AT
MOKULEIA NIKE SITE 5
In accordance with your request we have performed a geotechnical
engineering evaluation at the above referenced site Our work was done in
general accordance with our proposal of
The purpose of the evaluation was to obtain the subsurface conditions
at the site to provide soil data for use in the foundation design of the
proposed antenna
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
One 1 boring was originally planned at the site however due to
the relatively poor subsoil conditions encountered two 2 additional
borings were drilled to attempt to locate more favorable conditions
Boring No 1 was drilled at the original site for the antenna and
encountered loose gravel with soil in excess of 25 feet deep It appears
that this area was previously excavated and improperly backfilled Boring
No 2 was drilled about 10 feet away from Boring No 1 in an attempt to
locate better subsoil conditions for foundation support However similar
loose backfill conditions were also encountered Therefore Boring No 3
was drilled in another nearby area in order to locate a possible
alternate antenna location

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration encountered poorly compacted backfill at the
proposed antenna location For the proposed 30 foot high monopole
antenna we recommend that the top 5 feet of the backfill be removed and
recompacted then a drilled pier embedded into the fill may be used to
support the structure
Excavation and Backfilling
Excavation and backfilling would be required for the reconstruction
of the poorly compacted fill
The existing fill for a depth of 5 feet below the existing ground and
a horizontal distance of 5 feet around the antenna should be excavated
The bottom of the excavation should be compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximm dry density per ASTM D 1557 78
Excavated on site material free of any organic and other expansive
component may be used as a source of fill material If required all
imported fill material should consist of selected non expansive silty
sandy or granular materials less than 3 inches in size with less than 20
passing the 200 sieve All backfill materials should be placed in
maximum 8 inch thick horizontal loose lifts and compacted to 90 of the
maximum density as established by the ASIM D 1567 78 Test Method

Monopole Foundation
Based on the design information supplied to us a drilled pier may be
used for the monopole
Since the pier would be subjected to repeated wind loads a critical
consideration would be to limit permanent lateral deflections of the
pier This may be achieved by sufficient embedment of the pier
The vertical compressive load on the pole is relatively light and may
be supported by the frictional and end bearing resistance between the pier
and the subsoils
A laterally loaded pier analysis was performed for the pier
configuration used in the preliminary design Based on the analysis we
feel that a 48 inch diameter drilled pier embedded 12 feet into the
existing ground is adequate for the anticipated loading
Alternate Antenna Location
If the antenna location can be moved to the Boring No 3 area where
stiff residual soils were encountered the over excavation and
recompaction of the fill soils could be eliminated Additionally the
pier foundation depth could be reduced to 8 feet
Drilled Pier Construction
The drilled pier construction should be closely monitored by the
geotechnical engineer in order to see that adequate foundation support is
achieved Since a portion of the loading capacities come from the
frictional resistance between the shaft and the in situ soils extra care
should be applied to the drilling of the pier hole selection of concrete
mix design and the placement of concrete Some caving in of the drill
hole should be anticipated below the upper recompacted layer

Prompt placement of concrete in the prepared hole as soon as it is
completed is recommended to reduce caving in of the hole The concrete
mix should be designed to minimize shrinkage in order to maintain high
frictional contact between the in situ soils and pier concrete The
concrete should be placed in a suitable manner to avoid segregation of the
aggregates
Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the foundation recommendations given herein If this review is not made
the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for
misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during drilling of the pier hole
prior to placement of concrete or reinforcing steel This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive granular
material tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to
hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as borings
Variations of conditions away from the field data points may occur and
the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field data points were determined by tape
measurement on the site with reference to the Topographic Survey Map by
ParEn Inc dated The physical location of the field data
points should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the
method used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the
field data points depict the approximate boundaries between soil types
and as such may denote a gradual transition
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of the project
designers for specific application to the foundation design of the project
in accordance with generally accepted geotechnical engineering principles
and practices No other warranty expressed or implied is made
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the
architect engineer in the preliminary design evaluation of the proposed
project Therefore this report may not contain sufficient data or the
proper information for use in contract bid estimation A contractor
wishing to bid on this project is urged to retain a competent geotechnical
engineer to assist in the interpretation of this report and or in the
performance of additional site specific exploration for bid estimating
purposes

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling three
3 borings to depths of 10 to 25 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted rotary drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soil and rock encountered in the borings were classified by
visual and textural examination in the field by our geologist who
continuously monitored the drilling operations The classifications were
further reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory The
soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A graphic presentation of the materials
encountered is presented on the Boring Log Plates A 1 through A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the boring by driving a 2 inch
standard split spoon sampler or a 2 4 inch I D split barrel sampler with
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring logs at the appropriate
sample depths
