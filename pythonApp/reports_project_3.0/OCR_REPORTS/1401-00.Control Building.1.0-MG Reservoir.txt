PURPOSE AND SCOPE OF SERVICES
The purpose of our investigation was to explore the subsurface
conditions at the proposed reservoir site in order to provide recommen
dations for use in the design of the foundations for the proposed Control
Building and the 0 1M G Reservoir structures The general location of
the project area is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The results of our field exploration and laboratory tests which we
used in the engineering analysis to formulate our conclusions and
recommendations are presented in the appendices of this report The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed project consists of the following
1 A 1 0 M G concrete reservoir structure approximately 98
feet in diameter and 20 feet high Cuts into the
hillside of about 10 feet eastern and western sides up
to about 40 feet high southern side are planned to
create a level pad area for the reservoir
2 A one story control building approximately 15 by 55 feet
in plan dimension It will be located in a cut section
between the existing reservoir and the proposed new
reservoir and constructed of concrete and CMU walls
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located near the base of the Koolau Mountain
Range below Puu O Kona in the Waimanalo District of the island of Oahu
Hawaii The proposed development is approximately 1 5 miles south
mauka of Waimanalo Town An existing small Board of Water Supply
concrete tank is located about 100 feet east of the site
The topography of this site is fairly steep sloping to the north
at a slope ratio of approximately 5 1 H V The elevations at the site
range from approximately 340 to 390 feet

The site is currently lightly vegetated with growth of small ferns
and scattered trees
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Geology
The project site is located on the Waimanalo Coastal Plain that is
essentially a broad flat windward island fringe on Oahu that fronts a
drowned Amphitheater Headed Valley
After the Koolau Volcano ceased its activity it was deeply incised
by Amphitheater Headed valleys and subsequently drowned and partly filled
with sediments during Pliocene time and later when the glacio eustatic
sea level changes during the Pleistocene resulted in the accumulation of
hundreds of feet of marine and non marine sediments
The site lies on a gently sloping upland slope that consists of
coalesced fans of alluvium and colluvial sediments derived from the
salient volcanic cliffs of the Koolau Range to the south of the site
Soil Conditions
Relatively uniform soil conditions were encountered in our
investigation at the proposed tank site The borings encountered stiff
to hard brown clayey silt and silty clay with decomposed gravel boulders
and cobbles Older Alluvium to depths of 26 5 to 51 5 feet the depth of
the borings drilled A profile depicting the soil conditions is shown on

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The subsurface exploration generally indicates that after the
proposed site grading the subgrade might be underlain by stiff to hard
alluvial colluvial deposits which could in our opinion provide adequate
foundation support for the proposed structures
1 0 M G Reservoir Foundation
The test borings disclosed that the proposed site is underlain by a
stiff to hard brown clayey silt which could provide adequate foundation
support for the proposed reservoir
Foundations
Spread and continuous wall footings bearing on the older alluvial
soil is recommended for the reservoir An allowable bearing value
of 3000 p s f may be used for footings bearing on this type of
soil
The allowable bearing value may be increased by one third for
temporary wind and seismic loads
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O
1401 00 Page 5
Footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Footings located next to utility trenches
should extend down to the bottom of the utility trench and bear on
stiff undisturbed soil or structurally designed to span over the
well compacted trench backfill to minimize footing settlement
Additional footing embedment may be required for a portion of the
tank structure located within 10 feet away from the edge of the
slope For footing located close to the edge of the slope area
the horizontal set back distance measured from the outer edge of
the footing to the slope face should be a minimum of 15 feet or
more
Prior to concrete placement the bottom of all footing excavations
and trench backfill should be compacted to a minimum of 95 of its
maximum dry density as established by ASTM D 1557 78
The site grading should be designed to divert surface water away
from the reservoir foundation

would generally be less than one half of an inch total and
one fourth of an inch differential

Control Building
Foundations
For the proposed one story structure spread or continuous footing
foundations are recommended An allowable bearing value of up to
5000 p s f may be used on the stiff silty soils Footings should
be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the outside finished grade
The bottom of the footing excavations should be free of loose soil
and recompacted to 90 of its maximum dry density prior to concrete
placement
Retaining Wall
A retaining wall is planned to retain the cut slope on the southern
side of the reservoir and the control building
To develop its bearing capacity the wall foundations should be
embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the finished grade

Site Grading
In general the site grading will consist of cutting the ridge
about 10 to 40 feet at the reservoir site No filling is anticipated at
Site
Site Preparation
ae me ie ae em ek ee me tam i oe
The site to be graded should be cleared of all vegetation organic
matter debris and soft material Prior to footing excavation the
subgrade should be scarified to a minimum of 6 inches moisture
conditioned and then recompacted to a minimum of 90 of its
maximum dry density established by ASTM D 1557 78 test method
For the proposed reservoir the hillside behind the tank will be
cut about 40 feet We recommend that a cut Slope of 2 1 H V may
be used for the lower half and H V used for the upper half
with a 6 foot wide slope bench provided at about the mid point of
the slope
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1401 00 Page 9
Near the finished grade the slopes should be cut with extreme care
by the contractor We believe a backhoe should be used to provide
the final finishing of the cut slope surface to minimize
over cutting by a dozer Over cutting beyond the design slope
should be avoided since backfilling and recompacting would create a
very loose surface layer that would erode easily
Surface water from areas above the slope should be diverted away
and prevented from flowing over the Slope face The slope should
be planted as soon as practical to prevent unravelling due to
erosion
Service Road Pavements
For the light automobile traffic anticipated for the service access
roadway the following pavement thickness may be used based on the
results of our laboratory CBR tests on the on site soils
2 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
Additional CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade
soil samples during construction and the above design section can be
revised if necessary The recommended section considers light traffic
and that good drainage will be provided adjacent to paved areas

Design Review
Plans for site grading and foundation should be forwarded to the
soils engineer for review and written comments prior to construction to
see that the intent of our recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present during
earthwork construction particularly the slope cuts and to monitor the
contractor s grading operations It is also recommended that the footing
excavations be observed by the soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the subsurf ace
conditions are consistent with the findings of our investigation
If conditions encountered during construction are different from
those presented in this report such as pocket of expansive soil soft
spots appropriate modifications to the design should be made in the
field by the project soils engineer
Any imported materials backfill and base course required should
be tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site

APPENDIX A

by drilling 5 to 51 5 feet below the existing

general conformance with the Unified Soil Classification
System A graphic presentation of the materials encountered
and a cross section down through the proposed tank site is
presented on Plate 3

driving a standard penetration sampler or a 2 4 inch I D
split barrel sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches
The blow counts to drive the sampler 12 inches are shown on
the log of borings at the appropriate sample depths

ring sample to evaluate the consolidation characteristics
