Probing and Grouting Operations
A foundation probing and grouting program was implemented for the Building M
The Grove project The location of the building is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1 Mort
Drilling Services Inc performed the probing and grouting operations The purpose of the
probing and grouting operations was to reduce the potential for loss of foundation support
resulting from collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones below the building
structure
Swinerton Builders surveyed the footing layout and marked the probe hole locations
prior to the probing and grouting operations In general the probe holes were located at
the column footings one probe hole for every footing The approximate probe hole
locations are shown on the Probe Hole Location Plans Plates 2 1 and 2 2 In general the
probe holes at the new building structure were drilled to depths of about 12 5 to 13 feet
below the existing ground surface corresponding to approximately 10 feet below the
design footing bottom elevations
The probing and grouting operations were accomplished using hollow drill rods with
3 inch diameter drill bits with side discharge holes for water and grout discharge Each
probe hole was advanced by rotary percussion using a TEI 300HT drill mounted on a
Komatsu PC75 excavator Water was injected continuously through the hollow rods while
advancing to the target probe depth Evidence of cavities voids was observed by the loss
of water circulation with rapid receding of water into the drill hole For the probe hole where
cavities voids were not encountered the probe hole was tremie grouted through the drill
rod while the rod was being retracted The grout used for these probe holes generally
consisted of one sack of cement mixed with 8 gallons of water with a water cement ratio of
about 0 70
Several probe holes did not return grout during the initial grouting For these probe
hole locations that did not return grout during the initial grout application Mort Drilling
Services Inc placed a 1 5 inch diameter PVC pipe in the probe hole to maintain the
integrity of the open hole and to reduce the potential for cave in prior to additional grout
application These probe holes were subsequently filled with sand cement grout batched
by Mort Drilling Services Inc on site and injected at low pressures
Our field representatives observed the probing and grouting operations and
recorded the probe time grout time and the approximate grout take A total of 104 probe
holes with an aggregate length of about lineal feet below the existing ground
surface were drilled for the building as summarized in the following table and on Plate 3 1
