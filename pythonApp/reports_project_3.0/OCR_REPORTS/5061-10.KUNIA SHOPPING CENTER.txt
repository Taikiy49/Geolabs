This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the site grading operations foundation excavation pavement construction and
utility installation for the Kunia Shopping Center project from through
in accordance with our services agreement dated
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the vicinity of Kupuohi Loop in the Kunia area in the
District of Ewa on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project involved the construction of
three retail buildings Buildings A B and C associated parking areas three building
pads for future development and retaining walls around each of the three building pads In
general the three retail buildings consist of two story structures with a partial basement on
the ground floor of the buildings The ground floors of the buildings consisted of concrete
slabs on grades Site grading work for the construction generally consisted of cuts and fills
of up to about 9 feet relative to the existing grades The layout of the commercial retail
development is shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Nordic Construction Ltd is the general contractor for the Kunia Shopping Center
project Jayar Construction Inc performed the site grading operations foundation
excavation pavement construction and utility The site grading consisted of clearing and
grubbing footing excavation subgrade preparation placement and compaction of fills and
backfills pavement construction and utility trench excavation and backfill
2006 Kalihi Street Honolulu Hawaii 96819
Hawaii California

Following the clearing and grubbing operations the existing ground was generally
over excavated where necessary to approximately 12 inches below the slab cushion
within the building limits to provide the recommended 12 inch thick non expansive select
granular fill layer The over excavated subgrades were moisture conditioned to at least
2 percent above the optimum moisture content and compacted to at least 90 percent of
the maximum dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor
During placement of the non expansive select granular fill layer the fill materials
were generally placed in near level lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor During the foundation excavation and
preparation the excavated footing bottom was generally recompacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction
New sewer water and drain lines were installed to serve the new development In
general a bedding layer consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel was provided below
the piping and manholes for uniform support Free draining granular materials consisting
of No 3B Fine gravel was also used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the
pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort
on the initial trench backfill The trench backfill was placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to no less than 90 percent relative compaction Where trenches
are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement
grade was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and transported to our office for testing
The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our
laboratory in general accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test
results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 423 field density tests including retests of failed
areas were performed using the nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM D 2922
and D 3017 Results of the compaction tests performed are summarized on Plates 3 1
through 3 19 of this report These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces
and on constructed fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were
estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and
elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until the specified minimum
compaction was obtained

in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
