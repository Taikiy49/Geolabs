Regional Geology
The Island of Hawaii the largest in the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4 000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of the following
five shield volcanoes Kohala long extinct Mauna Kea activity during recent geologic
time Hualalai erupted in 1801 1803 and Mauna Loa and Kilauea both still active
The project site is situated on the northwestern flank of Hualalai Mountain which
makes up the west central portion of the island The lava formations observed at the
project site prior to site grading activities appear to consist of both a a and pahoehoe type
lava flows which spread and ponded as they approached the ocean A a lava is typically
characterized by a porous rough and irregular flow surface resembling a jagged
accumulation of rock fragments including cobbles and boulders Pahoehoe lava is
characterized by a smooth rope like or billowy surface and an internal structure of
vesicular porous rock
Cavities are commonly encountered in basaltic lavas especially the pahoehoe type
lava Cavities form when lavas are still in a molten state and represent both lava tubes
intra flow cavities and blisters and pockets inter flow cavities Lava tubes form when
molten lava drains from the cooling flow leaving a hollow tube like structure which may
extend for a large longitudinal distance along the flow Blisters and pockets are generally
smaller in horizontal extent
Basalt is considered to be a relatively permeable rock and can transmit water quite
readily in both the horizontal and vertical directions In general water is transmitted
through the porous rock matrix along joints fractures and inter flow contacts cavities and
along clinker layers Clinker and cavities serve as primary water transmission features
because of their high permeability
Subsurface Conditions
As previously mentioned the Maniniowali Development project site is in the
Kaupulehu area in the District of North Kona on the Island of Hawaii In general the
original topography of the project site prior to site grading activities consisted of irregular
or hummocky lava flow terrain with frequent topographic depressions separated by
adjacent higher relief mounds and low ridge features
Based on observations during the mass grading operations the project site is
generally underlain by medium hard to hard basalt formation with interbedded layers of
clinker materials It should be noted that lava tubes cavities and blisters are commonly
encountered in basaltic lava flows in the project vicinity In addition numerous lava tube
features were observed at the project site during the mass grading operations It should
be noted that near surface lava tubes detected within the limits of grading for the mass
grading operations were generally collapsed and the loose materials from within the

Discussion and Recommendations
Based on the grading plan for the project the original ground surface elevations
ranged from about 90 to 102 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at Lot No 9 Lot No 9
generally was graded to a finished pad elevation of about 92 to 96 feet MSL Mass
grading at Lot No 9 generally consisted of placing up to about 3 feet of new fills and
excavating up to about 6 feet to achieve the design grades The majority of the project
site was graded with less than about 2 feet of cuts and fills
At the commencement of the mass grading operations extensive subgrade
preparation procedures were employed within the limits of grading to reduce the potential
for collapse of near surface cavities and or voids within the lots The intent of the
subgrade preparation procedures was to assist in detecting and collapsing near surface
cavities and or voids contained in the porous near surface materials It should be noted
that the subgrade preparation procedures employed for this project are consistent with the
standard of practice for similar type projects and similar subsurface conditions along the
Kohala Coast on the Island of Hawaii
In general the subgrade preparation in fill areas consisted of ripping the original
ground to a depth of about 3 feet or until refusal using a ripper tooth of a large bulldozer to
assist in detecting and collapsing near surface cavities and or voids contained in the
porous near surface materials After the fill surfaces have been ripped the ground was
generally leveled with the use of a Komatsu 375 bulldozer working the surface for about
four passes to provide a relatively level surface After leveling of the rugged surfaces with
the bulldozer the fill subgrades generally were proof rolled with a Caterpillar 825
sheepsfoot roller compactor minimum 20 tons for about eight passes to help detect and
collapse near surface cavities and or voids
In general fill materials consisting of structural fills were placed in the fill areas of
the project site Structural fills consisted of well graded granular materials generally
smaller than 6 inches in dimension
Subgrades in cut areas were generally proof rolled with a Caierpillar 825
sheepsfoot roller compactor minimum 20 tons for about eight passes to help detect and
collapse near surface cavities and or voids It should be noted that the mauka portions of
Lot No 9 were generally graded to a cut condition Therefore the presence of basalt
formation and or a a basalt should be anticipated at or near the existing pad elevation in
this area
