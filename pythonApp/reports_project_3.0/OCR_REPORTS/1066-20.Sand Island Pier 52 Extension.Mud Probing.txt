Harbor Mud Probing Exploration
Thirteen 13 probes were made utilizing a 2 0 D standard split
spoon sampler driven by a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches to
determine the thickness of soft sediments below the mudline in the
proposed fill area The depth to the mudline below the water surface

Page 2
was first determined by sounding with an 8 inch diameter disc
All depths were corrected and refer to mean lower low water level
according to tide chart
The probes encountered soft sediments below the mudline ranging from
6 inches in Probe 12 to 15 feet in Probe 8 Much of the soft sedi
ments were so soft that the sampler sank through them under the weight
of the sampler and the rod Where the soft sediments were thick the
probes were extended until the underlying firmer material mostly of
medium dense sand was encountered Generally the probes extended to
depths between 5 feet and 16 feet below the mud line
The probing indicate that the harbor bottom is underlain by a thick
deposit of soft sediments therefore the procedure for filling the
site will be very critical for the project If the soft deposits are
trapped below the new fill substantial settlements would occur due to
the consolidation of the soft sediments The soft mud may work its
way up pump and weaken the subgrade support Furthermore lateral
loading on the sheetpile bulkhead would be greatly increased due to
the low strength of the sediments and would require much stronger
bulkhead system Therefore we recommend that the soft sediments be
removed Two alternatives to remove the soft sediments are suggested
below

To minimize the entrapment of the soft materials we
recommend that the area be dredged to the medium dense
sands prior to the placement of the new fill It is our
opinion that clamshell or dragline dredging would not be
practical in soft sediments of the type encountered at
the project site The materials are so soft that a large
portion of it would run out of the dredge buckets and the
surrounding soft material would tend to Slough back into
the excavation Additionally it is anticipated that
clamshell or dragline bucket would tend to bite deep into
the underlying soil during the dredging work Thus we
believe that these types of dredging would result in over
dredging and be quite ineffective The dredging opera
tions would require very close monitoring and sounding
during the work progresses
It is our opinion that a more thorough dredging method
would be to use a hydraulic dredge and progress across
the site in an orderly manner The dredging should start
in the shallow southern end of the site and progress to
the deeper northern end of the site

Since the soft materials would not readily settle and
most likely drain through the bottom of a mud scow we
recommend that the dredged material be pumped to a large
Settling basin on land After the water has drained from
the dredged material it should be properly disposed of
off site
Close monitoring by a qualified engineer familiar with
this type of dredging operation is recommended This
would require continuous monitoring of the dredge dis
charge to observe the changes from the silt sediments to
the underlying sands
It is felt that this method would provide the best
assurance that the soft sediments have been removed
However it is anticipated that the hydraulic dredging
would be quite expensive and require a substantial
ponding area and a long settling period for the dredge
spoil to become firm enough to be rehandled for disposal
The second alternative would be to use the displacement
method This is accomplished by placing sufficient fill
material to displace the soft mud creating a mudwave in
front of the fill As the fill is advancing the

tudwave will be pushed ahead of the fill and may need to
be clamshelled from time to time to assure its movement
and hence displacement of mud The embankment should
begin at one corner and progress across the site to
prevent trapping the soft sediments underneath Careful
coor dination of the placement of the embankment
material and mudwave excavation will be required
This method would most likely be cheaper than the suction
dredging However the following disadvantages should
be noted
a There is a greater possibility that some soft sedi
ments could be trapped below the fill caus ing
differential settlements Additional settlement
gauges are recommended to monitor the settlements
b The effect of the mudwave on the harbor area should be
evaluated It may be desirable to first create a dike
along the bulkhead line then proceed with the filling
and pushing mudwave toward the landside This will
also minimize silting of the harbor waters
