INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed Shoreline Protection Project at Johnston Island Johnston
Atoll The general location of the site is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The
exploration was performed in general accordance with our revised fee proposal dated
March 19 1990
This report summarizes the findings from the exploration and presents our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses based on the data
obtained during the exploration These recommendations are intended for design input
and are not intended for final construction cost estimation or for construction bidding
purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project area Reach T is situated along the shoreline in the northwestern
corner of Johnston Island The area has experienced continued erosion of the shoreline
which threatens to encroach upon and damage an existing road along the shore To
repair the area and to provide protection for the shoreline it is proposed to construct a
new cantilevered steel sheetpile bulknead with a concrete revetment behind the sheetpile
bulkhead
It is our understanding that the project will involve the installation of two separate
areas of sheetpile bulkhead and revetment One area will be about 1 200 L F in length
and the other area will be about 800 L F in length These two ends are separated by the
fenced off Agent Orange Spill Area

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the project site in order to develop a generalized soil rock data set for the
formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the proposed
shoreline protection project
The work was performed in general accordance with our revised proposal to Belt
Collins Associates dated March 19 1990 The scope of work was limited to
1 Drilling and sampling three 3 borings and three 3 probes to depths
between 30 0 and 46 0 feet below the existing ground surface
2 Reconnaissance of the project site and vicinity to make visual observations
of the surface soil rock conditions
3 Review of available soils and geologic information pertinent to the project
site and its vicinity
4 Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
to determine the classification and engineering properties of the materials
encountered
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data for the purpose of
developing geotechnical engineering design recommendations and
6 Preparation of this report summarizing the findings of our field exploration
and presenting our recommendations

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project shoreline area is situated at the northwestern corner of Johnston
Island The proposed shoreline protection construction area Reach T will connect
Reach S area to the east and Reach U area to the west Based on our field
observation the Reach S area is protected with cast in place concrete revetment while
Reach U area is protected with precast shingles revetment
Along the perimeter road the terrain is generally level with existing ground
elevations at 7 0 to 9 0 feet Approaching the beach the terrain dropped to about
elevation 4 0 feet then sloped down towards the ocean at an approximate ratio of 5
horizontal to 1 vertical 6H 1V
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
In general our borings encountered a surface layer of dredged fill material
consisting of medium dense coral sand and gravel Below the surface layer the borings
encountered in situ detrital coral materials consisting of loose coral sand and gravel with
weak cementation
At depths approximately corresponding to elevations 1 5 to about 23 0 feet our
borings encountered very weak or soft zones which may be voids in the coral formation
that are partially filled with loose sand silt and clay soils Below about elevation 23 0
feet relatively competent coral material was encountered to the maximum depth explored

Groundwater level was encountered at about six 6 to eight 8 feet below the
existing perimeter roadway ground surface It is anticipated that the groundwater level

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
The field exploration generally encountered detrital coralline materials to the
maximum depth explored At depths approximately corresponding to elevations 1 5 to
about 23 0 feet our borings encountered very weak or soft zones which may be voids
in the coral formation that are partially filled with loose sand silt and clay soils Below
about elevation 23 0 feet relatively competent coral material was encountered to the
maximum depth explored
Based on the data from our field exploration we recommend that the sheetpiles
be placed to a tip elevation of about 22 0 for an average sheetpile length of
approximately twenty five 25 feet
Laboratory testing indicates that the soils encountered at the site have relatively low
resistivity values In general low resistivity values indicate a relatively high corrosion
potential Proper corrosion protection is recommended for the sheetpiles

Site Grading
Details of the proposed site grading were not available for our exploration
However we anticipate minimal site grading consisting of the placement of some backfill
behind the sheetpile bulkhead and preparation of the shoreline slope for placement of the
concrete revetment The following discussions and recommendations are presented to
provide general guidelines for the design of the project site grading
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
cleared of vegetation debris rubbish and other deleterious materials These
materials should be removed and disposed properly off site
Fill areas should be scarified to a depth of six 6 inches moisture conditioned to
about 3 to 5 above optimum moisture content and recompacted to a minimum
of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test Method D 1557 78 Any loose
pumping yielding or soft spots encountered during the recompaction operation
should be over excavated to firm ground and then replaced with select granular
material such as the dredged coral material available on Johnston Island
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78
Fill Material and Placement
The locally available sources of potential fill material consist of dredged coral
materials Based on previous experience with similar materials it is our opinion
that these coral sands and gravels will be suitable for any fill required for the
proposed shoreline protection project
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2435 10 Page 7
Any fills required should be placed in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM Test
Method D 1557 78 Due to the granular nature of the on site soils and the
available fill materials the use of vibratory compaction equipment is recommended
Slope ratios of two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V or flatter may be used for the
design of the project site grading
Sheetpile Length
It is our understanding that the top of the proposed steel sheetpile bulkhead has
been set at elevation 3 0 feet Based on the data from our field exploration we
recommend that the sheetpiles be placed to a tip elevation of about 22 0 feet for a total
sheetpile length of approximately twenty five 25 feet
Corrosion Control
To provide information for the design of a corrosion protection system for the
proposed sheetpiles laboratory resistivity tests were performed on three 3 of the
samples obtained from the field exploration
Based on these laboratory test results a resistivity value of about 200 ohm cm may
be assumed for the coralline materials at the project site The resistivity value may be as
low as 70 ohm cm for the silty clay material encountered Although we did not test the
resistivity of the sea water at the site it should be noted that salt water is highly
conductive and therefore has a low resistivity value
We believe that the low resistivity values of the soils encountered in our exploration
and the low resistivity of sea water may result in a high corrosion potential for the steel
sheetpile bulkheads if proper corrosion protection is not installed as part of the project

A qualified corrosion control consultant should be retained to assist in the design of the
corrosion protection system
Lateral Earth Pressures
As an aid to the structural analysis of the sheetpile bulkhead an Apparent Earth
Pressure Diagram showing the distribution of lateral earth pressures against the bulkhead
is presented on Plate 3 of this report The lateral pressures and the recommended
sheetpile length is based on the assumption that the maximum scour or dredge depth at
the face of the bulkhead will not be deeper that elevation 5 0 feet
A drainage system behind the bulkhead is recommended to protect against the
build up of hydrostatic pressures Underwater backfill material behind the bulkhead
should consist of clean gravel such as 3B Fine ASTM C 3B Fine gravel
is not available select on site coral gravel between 3 8 and 3 4 of an inch in size may
be used
Concrete Revetment
It is our understanding that both cast in place and precast concrete plank shingles
may be considered for the construction of the proposed concrete revetment
A cushion layer should be provided below the concrete revetment The cushion
material should consist of a minimum of four 4 inches of 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
67B If 3B Fine gravel is not available select on site coral gravel between 3 8 and 3 4
of an inch in size may be used instead
In addition to reduce the potential of loss of fines from the subsoils underlying the
revetment we recommend that a layer of geosynthetic filter fabric be installed below the
gravel cushion and concrete revetment The filter fabric should extend below the Mean
Low Low Water level MLLW behind the bulkhead

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to construction This review
is needed to determine adherence to the recommendations given herein If this review
is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation
of the recommendations
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that a geotechnical engineer be retained to provide
geotechnical engineering services during earthwork and construction phases of the
project This is to observe compliance with the design concepts specifications or
recommendations and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and approved
prior to hauling to the site
lf actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the
design should be made
