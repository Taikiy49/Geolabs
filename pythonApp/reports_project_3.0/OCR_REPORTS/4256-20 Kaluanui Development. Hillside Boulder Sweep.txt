Project Considerations
The Kaluanui Development project is located on the western side of Hawaii Kai
Drive near the intersection of Onohi Street in the Hawaii Kai area on the Island of Oahu
Hawaii The subject parcel is approximately 2 acres in size and is situated on sloping
ground located at the base of the western hillside The undeveloped upper portion of the
parcel is covered with a relatively moderate growth of Kiawe trees and tall grass
containing scattered surface deposits of basalt boulders
The project site is presently under construction for the planned multi family
residential units A geotechnical engineering exploration was conducted by our office for
the design of this project Our findings and recommendations were presented in our
report entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Kaluanui Development Hawaii Kai
Oahu Hawaii dated
The project site is situated at the base of a hillside that is composed of exposed
basalt rock outcrops The in situ basalt rock outcrops are generally located further
upslope and outside of the property limits These basalt rock outcrops are believed to be
the source of loose boulders encountered within the project limits To reduce the
potential for these loose boulders to roll down the hillside and encroach into the
residential units a boulder sweep was conducted to remove loose boulders from the
hillside located within the property limits

Discussion
A site visit was made on January 5 in advance of the boulder sweep to perform a
reconnaissance of the hillside conditions and to identify and mark the boulders for
removal by the site grading contractor The general criteria employed to select the
individual boulders for removal were loose round shaped non embedded boulders larger
than about 18 inches in size located on sloping ground or at the head of existing surface
scarps
Approximately 40 boulders meeting the above criteria were marked for removal by
the contractor Based on our field observations the boulders that were marked were
removed using manual labor and some mechanized equipment Upon the completion of
the boulder removal observation of the site indicated that the potentially hazardous
boulders were satisfactorily removed
It should be noted that other boulder deposits remain on the hillside of the
property however the remaining boulders located within the property limits were visually
assessed and judged to be generally stable and at lower risk for rolling onto the
residential units However a segment of the steeper hillside located at the southwest
corner of the site was observed to contain some surface boulder deposits which were
intentionally left undisturbed due to the potential for creating slope instability by their
removal An existing flattened bench surface was located at the foot of the undisturbed
hillslope which may serve as a catchment feature for possible rolling boulders
Limitations
It must be pointed out that regardless of the methods used an inherent risk of
falling boulders could still exist for the buildings located adjacent to the sloping hillside
The existing rock outcroppings that compose the steep hillside located westerly of the
project site may produce some falling rocks that would contribute to future boulder
accumulation on the hillslope located within the property limits Therefore there is a
future potential for boulders to loosen and roll down the hillside onto the development
The state of the art of geotechnical engineering practice has not advanced to a point that
a solution is available for this area In addition it should be pointed out that no guarantee
against future falling boulders can be made due to changes in soil and rock conditions
with time weathering erosion earthquake and construction vibration and improvements
and construction activities at and around the site Some existing boulder deposits that
were observed to be generally stable at the time of our evaluation may become unstable
as a result of the afore mentioned activities and events
