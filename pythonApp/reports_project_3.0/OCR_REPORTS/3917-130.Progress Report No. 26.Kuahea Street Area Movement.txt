Status of Earth Movements
The earth movements currently being monitored at Areas A and B have not
stabilized and continue to experience slow downhill creep movements Based on the
history of the earth movement monitoring sudden catastrophic earth movements are not
likely but cannot be completely ruled Earth movements detected after completion of the
subdivision were severe and occurred at a higher rate than the movements recorded
during our site monitoring Our records of the earth movements indicate that the
movements continue at a typically slow and relatively steady rate with some inclinometers
showing incremental patterns of accelerated and regressive movement Extreme rainfall
events and the increased subsurface groundwater seepage associated with such rain
events have the potential to accelerate the movement in the short term and cause
significant disruption to the existing pavements and fixed structures in the area
The continued earth movements will continue to adversely affect road surfaces and
buried utilities Continued maintenance of the infrastructure is important to reduce sources
of water infiltrating into the subsurface which can accelerate movements
Short Term and Long Term Repair Options
We understand that engineered mitigation efforts to slow the movements within the
public right of way will be performed as funds become available Retaining structures
such as drilled shaft walls and tie back retaining walls may be designed to restrain the
hillside areas adjacent to the public right of way
Short term solutions may include reinforced earth structures to stiffen the upper
portions of the slide to reduce differential movements at the surface Such a structure is
scheduled for construction in Kuahea Street at the upper portion of the Waiomao
Landslide

Subsurface Water Monitoring
The monitoring of subsurface water levels groundwater is accomplished by
measuring the level of water head contained within the individual and nested
piezometers installed in the borings at the project site Sixteen piezometers were initially
installed at the project site A list of the piezometer installations for the project is provided
in the Table entitled Summary of Completed Instrument Installations on Pages 5 and 6 of
this report Of the sixteen piezometers initially installed thirteen piezometers remain
functional for current water level measurement The thirteen functional piezometers are
shown on the Current Operational Instrument Plan Plate 2
Three of the thirteen functional piezometers identified as P 2A P 4A and P 8
contain dedicated vibrating wire piezometer installations that provide a near continuous
record of the water level encountered in the well casing The other piezometers consist of
simple standpipe well casings which are monitored manually on about a bi monthly basis
Based on our monitoring of the groundwater levels from the thirteen functional
piezometers over the past six month monitoring period the records indicate that
volumes greater than about 1 foot head of subsurface water seepage at their respective
screened well points
lt should be noted that manual water level readings from Piezometer No P 1A
P 1B and P 2A were not collected during the past 6 month monitoring period However
Piezometer No P 2A has a dedicated vibrating wire piezometer installed in the standpipe

Based on our review and interpretation of the groundwater levels recorded
near continuously by the vibrating wire piezometers it appears that periodic groundwater
influxes resulting from normal rainfall events head increase spikes are relatively abrupt
followed by a gradual dissipation of the head level recorded by the piezometer The
gradual head dissipation typically occurs over a period on the order of several days to
several weeks Very high spikes in head level as a result of extreme rainfall and or
prolonged heavy rain events typically take several months to fully dissipate the elevated
groundwater head level

Observations Pavement Structural Deformation
General reconnaissance of the Kuahea Street Area Movement study area was
conducted on August 16 2013 to record observations pertaining to the existing site
conditions
Areas that appear to be experiencing ongoing slow ground surface deformation
include Kuahea Street Area A between Helo and Kuahea Place and the
Waiomao Landslide head at Kuahea Street These areas show visible evidence of
compounding ground heave and or subsidence within the paved roadway areas Surface
groundwater seepages were not observed within the study area at the time of our
reconnaissance
Kuahea Street Area A between Helo Place and Kuahea Place
The primary area of ground surface deformation within the public right of way at
Kuahea Street Area A was observed to extend between about
2326 Kuahea Street and about 2221 Kuahea Place Based on our observations
the temporary pavement repairs patches on Kuahea Street are in generally fair
condition but show signs of stress as evidenced by widening crack apertures and
some vertical offsets at the joints between adjacent concrete pavement slabs By
visual observation the undulatory condition of the existing concrete pavement and
deflections in the street curbing on Kuahea Street appears to be gradually
increasing in magnitude although no pavement surface rupture or dangerous
pavement irregularity was observed The ride over the undulating and patched
pavement is rough and irregular and should be negotiated at low travel speed
Other observations are as follows
We observed compression heave involving the concrete drainage inlet structure
adjacent to the 2326 Kuahea Street address The distress appears to be slowly
increasing in magnitude The buckled concrete has created a void in the curbing
and the concrete deformation is beginning to encroach on the adjacent concrete
driveway at the 2326 Kuahea Street address The buckled concrete may pose a
potential hazard for vehicles using the driveway
The once unoccupied house at 2334 Kuahea Street appears to have been
renovated and is now occupied The house had sustained significant structural
distress resulting from the earth movements
Based on our observations conducted from the street the existing residence at
2331 Kuahea Street continues to sustain moderate to severe house foundation
distress and damage to the existing high CRM retaining wall bounding the property

Kipona Place
Based on our observations the existing conditions along Kipona Place at the toe of
the Waiomao Landslide appear to be generally static with respect to new pavement
deformation and earth movement on the majority of slope abutting the roadway
However the localized earth slump previously reported along the slope toe at
