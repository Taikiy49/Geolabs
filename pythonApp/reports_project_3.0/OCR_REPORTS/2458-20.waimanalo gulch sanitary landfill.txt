INTRODUCTION
Geolabs Hawaii under contract to Waste Management of has
performed quality assurance quality control observation and testing for the construction
of the Phase III ash monofill base liner at the Waimanalo Gulch Sanitary Landfill The
general location of the project site is shown on the Project Location Map Figure 1
The design of the ash monofill base liner section consists of bottom to top
1 A three 3 foot thick layer of compacted soil liner with design permeability
values equal to or lower than
2 A layer of 60 mil high density polyethylene HDPE geomembrane
3 A geonet drainage layer
4 A layer of 16 ounce per square yard non woven geotextile
This report provides a summary of 1 the observation and soil testing associated
with the construction of the soil liner 2 the results of the geosynthetic material
conformance testing and 3 the observation and testing of the geomembrane liner
installation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2458 20 Page 2

The scope of Geolabs Hawaii s services included construction quality assurance

GEOMEMBRANE INSTALLATION
Approximately 77 400 square feet of 60 mil HDPE high density polyethylene
geomembrane was left over from the previous phases of geosynthetic liner installation and
66 600 square feet 4 rolls of HDPE geomembrane manufactured by National Seal
Company of Galesburg Illinois was delivered to the site Approximately 89 910 square
feet of the 60 mil HDPE geomembrane was used for the present Phase III lining system
Geomembrane Deployment
Deployment of the 60 mil HDPE geomembrane began on and was
completed on Deployment and welding progressed continuously
throughout this period
The deployment of the geomembrane was observed by Geolabs Hawaii s
personnel Observation duties included measurement of the panel thickness confirmation
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2458 20 Page 7
of panel overlap and a visual observation of the overall sheet quality A summary of the
deployment observations is included in Appendix F 1
Deployment of the liner began at the southeast corner of the pad and proceeded
to the northwest and northeast The slope panels extended a minimum of about fifteen
feet beyond the crest of the slopes into the anchor trench The floor panels extended a
minimum of five feet beyond the centerline of the swale A minimum of five inches of
overlap was maintained between adjoining panels The geomembrane panel layout for
the ash monofill base liner is shown on Figure 2
Each panel of the deployed 60 mil HDPE geomembrane was given a unique panel
number which identified that panel within the installation The panels were numbered
sequentially as they were deployed
Each geomembrane panel was observed for overall sheet quality and measured
for thickness as they were being deployed The thickness measurements were made
using a micrometer Five measurements were taken on each of the leading edge one
side and the trailing edge of each panel The reported thicknesses in Appendix F 1
represent the average of the five readings All panels had an average thickness equal to
or greater than 60 mils and all individual measurements were greater than 54 mils 90
of the specified 60 mils thickness
Weld Observation
The majority of the seaming of the HDPE panels was accomplished using fusion
welding apparatus Extrusion welding was used to make repairs and to weld the seams
at the T junctions of three panels overlapping each other The welding was observed by
Geolabs Hawaii s personnel on a procedural basis The entire length of all seams
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2458 20 Page 8
patches or other repairs were observed either during welding or shortly after completion
of the welding
Each seam was numbered according to the two panels being welded together
For example seam P1 2 is the seam between panels P1 and P2 Each seam was
visually inspected by Geolabs Hawaii s personnel to determine that the panels were
properly cleaned prior to welding and that the weld was continuous and free of defects
A summary of the weld inspection is presented in Appendix F 2
Trial test seams were required of all welders and seaming apparatuses on a regular
basis as specified in the QA manual The welding personnel each produced a test seam
at the start of each seaming period normally twice a day Two 2 one inch wide test
strips were cut from each test sample for peel testing using a field tensiometer All test
seams were performed in the presence of Geolabs Hawaii s personnel All welders were
required to produce a trial test seam that did not fail in the weld during peel testing before
they were allowed to start welding on the liner
On the first day of geomembrane deployment a field tensiometer was being
shipped to the project site and was not available for peel testing of the trial seams
Therefore the two 2 strips of each test sample were hand peeled to failure using a
clamp A field tensiometer was available on the second day of geomembrane
deployment The trial seam samples tested using a clamp on the first day were tested
again using the field tensiometer upon its availability A summary of the test seams is
presented in Appendix F 3
Destructive Testing
In accordance with the QA Manual and Specification Guidelines destructive test
samples of the welded seams were obtained at a minimum frequency of one destructive
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2458 20 Page 9
test sample per every 500 linear feet of weld The test locations were selected by
Geolabs Hawaii s QA personnel based either on the completion of approximately 500 feet
of welding or on seaming observations
Once a destructive test location had been located a 12 inch wide by 48 inch long
sample centered lengthwise on the weld was cut Two strips were cut from each
destructive test sample for field testing in peel Both strips had to pass the field testing
before the sample was qualified for destructive test The sample was then cut into three
equal pieces One section was packaged and shipped to ECS Inc s laboratory at Duluth
Georgia for laboratory destructive testing one section taken for National Seal s archives
and the other section was taken for the owner s archives After the removal of each
destructive test sample the sampled area was patched and the patch seam was
non destructively tested
From each destructive test sample 10 specimens were removed for testing Five
5 specimens were tested for adhesion peel for both the inner weld track and the outer
weld track with the specified acceptance criterion being that four out of five test
specimens per weld track will not fail in the weld The peel strengths must be greater
than 90 pounds per inch and 78 pounds per inch for fusion and extrusion welds
respectively as specified in the QA manual Five specimens were also tested for bonded
strength shear with the specified acceptance criteria being that four out of five
specimens must have yield strengths greater than 132 pounds per inch and that failure
cannot occur in the weld
Laboratory destructive test results indicated that sample DT 2 failed the destructive
test with two specimens failing in peel in the inner weld track According to the QA
manual the Installer has to trace the welding path to an intermediate location at 10 feet
minimum from the point of the failed field or laboratory test in each direction and take a
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2458 20 Page 10
1 inch wide sample for additional field test at each location If these additional samples
pass the test then full laboratory samples were taken If these laboratory samples pass
the tests then the seam is repaired between these locations Therefore two additional
laboratory destructive test samples DT 2A and DT 2B were obtained and the seam
bounded by DT 2A and DT 2B was repaired
Destructive test sample DT 7 failed the field testing in peeling off the seam Since
DT 7 is located near the end of the seam 7B will have to be located on the
next seam P12 13 welded by the same seamer and welding machine However the
Installer could not successfully trace a passing field test location along P12 13
Therefore the Installer decided to repair the whole seam of P12 13 by extrusion weld
The additional sample DT 7B was obtained at the middle of the repaired seam P12 13
It was decided that an additional sample DT 7C would be obtained by extrusion welding
two fragment pieces of geomembrane during the repairing process for additional
destructive test
A total of 15 destructive test samples were taken for laboratory testing The
destructive test locations are shown in Figure 2 A summary of the destructive test results
is included in Appendix F 5
Non Destructive Continuity Testing
Non destructive continuity testing of the welded seams were performed using
vacuum box and air pressure testing apparatuses For the vacuum test a soapy solution
was mopped on the seam and the vacuum box was placed over the seam A vacuum
of approximately 5 psi was applied in the box for a duration of 10 seconds Detected
leaks were repaired and retested using the same procedure The seams that were not
vacuum tested were non destructively tested using air pressure testing For this test both
ends of the seam being tested were sealed and a needle with an attached pressure
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2458 20 Page 11
gauge and air valve was inserted into the air channel between the two welds A pressure
of approximately 30 psi was then applied to the air channel and monitored for five
minutes If the pressure drop over the five minute period was less than 2 psi the seam
was considered continuous and defect free If the loss in pressure exceeded 2 psi the
seam was then pressure tested in sections until the defect was found or the seam was
tested using a vacuum box Leaks detected using the air pressure method were repaired
and retested using a vacuum box A summary of non destructive tests is included on the
weld inspection summary and repair summary of Appendix F 2 and Appendix F 4
respectively
Repairs
Each repair to the HDPE liner was given a unique number For each repair the
number the repair location the reason for the repair the date repaired and the date the
repair was non destructively tested were logged A summary of the repairs is presented
in Appendix F 4 The repair locations are also shown on Figure 2
GEONET INSTALLATION
Approximately 10 946 square feet of geonet was left over from the previous phase
of geosynthetic liner installation and 113 100 square feet of geonet manufactured by
National Seal Company was delivered to the project site for the present Phase Ill
installation Approximately 91 500 square feet of geonet was used for the lining system
One layer of geonet was placed over the geomembrane except along the centerline
of the leachate collection swale where an additional layer of one roll width was placed
The geonet panels were bond together using white plastic ties spaced approximately
every five to six feet End to end panels were joined by double rows of plastic ties
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 2458 20 Page 12
spaced approximately every six inches The geomembrane was cleaned by sweeping
prior to placement of the geonet
GEOTEXTILE INSTALLATION
The geotextile delivered to the site is a 16 ounce non woven needle punched
continuous filament polypropylene fabric manufactured by Trevira Inc Approximately
23 325 square feet of geotextile was left over from the previous phase of geosynthetic
liner installation and 90 000 square feet of geotextile was delivered to the site for the
present Phase Ill installation A total of 97 500 square feet of geotextile was used in this
project
One layer of geotextile was placed over the geonet The geotextile was
continuously sewn on the slopes and on the pad floor areas The sewing of the geotextile
began on However both of the sewing machines on site broke down
The placement and seaming of the geotextile was delayed until the sewing machine was
repaired The placement and sewing of the geotextile was completed on
CONCLUSIONS
The activities described above associated with the construction and the installation
of the geosynthetic liner of the Phase Ill ash monofill base were observed by Geolabs
Hawaii s personnel Based upon our observations and the results of the testing reported
herein it is our opinion that the above mentioned liner system has been installed in
substantial compliance with the design drawings and specifications and Waste
Management s Quality Assurance Manual and Specification Guidelines except as noted
otherwise
