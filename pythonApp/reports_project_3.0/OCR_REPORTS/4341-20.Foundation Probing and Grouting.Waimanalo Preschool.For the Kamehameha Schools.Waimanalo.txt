This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering observations during
the probing and grouting operations for the Waimanalo Preschool for the Kamehameha
Schools in the Waimanalo area from August 8 through 10 2000
Project Considerations
The project site is located on the makai side of llauhole Street near the intersection
with lupe Street in the Waimanalo area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The site is
approximately a trapezoidal shaped vacant parcel covering approximately 1 6 acres in
area The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided it is proposed to develop the site into a
preschool facility with the construction of an L shaped single story building We envision
that the new building will be of CMU wall and slab on grade construction Based on the
information provided by the project structural engineer maximum column loads on the
order of about 25 kips and wall loads up to about 2 kips per lineal foot are anticipated for
the proposed building The design finished floor elevation of the new building is set at
33 feet Mean Sea Level MSL Based on the existing topography we anticipate that
minor cuts and fills on the order of about 2 feet will be required to achieve the building
pad elevation

As part of the site development an asphalt concrete paved parking lot is planned
at the southwest corner of the site to serve the new facility In addition new retaining
walls will be required along the makai boundary to provide the necessary grade
separation between the new building pad and the adjacent property
Based on our literature research the project site is located at or near a quarry site
which was abandoned approximately 15 years ago Sinkholes were observed in the
adjacent Waimanalo Residence Lots prior to the site development The sinkholes may
be the result of the caving in of the surface material covering the underlying voids
between the boulders that were reportedly used to backfill the quarry Therefore special
attention was given to the potential presence of near surface cavities and or sinkholes
which may collapse under structural loads
Probing and Grouting Operations
The probing and grouting operations were performed in general conformance with
the recommendations presented in our report for the project dated
The purpose of the probing and grouting operation was to reduce the potential for loss of
foundation support resulting from collapse of cavities or compression of loose zones
below the new building
The probe hole locations were marked by Allied Builders System prior to the
probing operation The approximate locations of the probe holes at the new preschool
building are shown on the Probe Hole Location Plan Plate 2 In general the probe holes
at the footing locations of the new building were drilled to depths of about 12 feet below
the existing ground surface The probe holes about 4 inches in diameter were drilled by
Geolabs using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers Following
the probing operation a 2 inch diameter PVC pipe was inserted into each of the probe
holes to maintain the integrity of the open hole and to reduce the potential for cave in
prior to the grouting operation A total of 54 probe holes with an aggregate length of
about 648 lineal feet were drilled The approximate locations of the probe holes are
shown on the Probe Hole Location Plan Plate 2
The probe holes were backfilled with 500 pounds per square inch psi flowable fill
supplied by Hawaiian Cement injected at low to moderate pressures The grouting work
was performed by American Standard Concrete Pumping Hawaii Inc and observed bya
field representative from Geolabs The grouting operation generally proceeded in the
following manner
Immediately prior to grouting of a particular hole the temporary PVC pipe
was removed A 2 inch diameter grouting probe was inserted into the open
hole and lowered to approximately 2 feet from the bottom of the hole Grout
injection then commenced at the direction of our field representative who
recorded the approximate grout take and provided direction as to when the
injection was complete or whether periodic injection was required

Each probe hole was grouted from the desired depth upward by low to
moderate pressure injection of grout through a PVC conduit which was
retracted from the probe hole as the grout filled the hole Several injection
passes were needed to grout those probe holes where high grout take was
observed The grouting operation and the approximate grout take were
observed and recorded by our field representative
Approximately 4 cubic yards of flowable fill was utilized to fill the probe holes In
general the probe holes took about twice the theoretical volume of grout to fill the probe
holes The probing and grouting summary is provided on Plates 3 1 through 3 3
Limitations
The elevations of the probe holes were provided by Allied Builders System The
elevations of the probe holes should be considered accurate only to the degree implied
by the method used
