INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the design and construction of the foundations
and pavements of the proposed warehouse addition at Kalihi Kai The
approximate location of the project site is shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1

PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of the exploration was to study the subsurface
conditions below the site to obtain geotechnical data for the
formulation of engineering recommendations for the foundation and
pavement design and construction of the warehouse addition
The scope of our work included drilling and sampling five 5
borings laboratory testing of samples obtained engineering evaluation
of the field and laboratory data development of design recommendations
and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration boring logs and
laboratory test results are presented in the appendices of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct an addition to the existing ITO EN
USA Inc Warehouse Facility The new building addition will be a
lightly loaded structure with concrete floors and a 4 foot deep loading
ramp adjacent to the existing warehouse
The existing shed and parking area of the warehouse will be
demolished for the new construction
In addition excavation of the proposed loading ramp and
underpinning of the adjacent foundations of the existing warehouse
building will be required

SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located at the ITO EN USA Inc Warehouse
Facility on Puuhale Road in Kalihi Kai on the island of Oahu Hawaii
Currently the site is partially used as a parking area and
partially occupied by an existing shed
Ground level at the site is generally flat Ground cover consists
of about 3 inch asphaltic concrete at the existing parking area and
about 14 inch concrete slab for the existing shed

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATTONS
General
The borings indicate that the project site is underlain at shallow
depths by highly weathered coral formation with an undulating surface
We believe that the proposed building addition may be supported by
shallow spread or continuous footing foundations bearing directly on the
generally dense weathered coral At locations where the dense
weathered coral may be deeper footing foundations bearing directly on
the overlying dense sandy fill or stiff clayey silt alluvium may be
used
For the proposed 4 foot deep loading ramp construction the
excavation adjacent to the existing warehouse may require dewatering
prior to foundation placement because of the proximity to the
groundwater table Due to the excavation the adjacent foundations of
the existing warehouse should be underpinned below the excavation bottom
in order to maintain the stability of the warehouse
Building Foundation
Based on the anticipated soil conditions and light building loads
we recommend that shallow spread or continuous wall footings be used to
support the proposed building addition
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1960 00 Page 5
Dk No 44
Footings should be placed a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Uplift wind or other lateral loading
conditions may further increase the embedment of the footing
foundations
An allowable bearing capacity of 4000 p s f may be used for the
footing foundations bearing on the dense weathered coral For footings
bearing on the dense sandy fill or stiff clayey silt alluvium an
allowable bearing capacity of 2500 p s f may be used The bearing
capacity values are for total dead plus live load only and may be
increased by one third for short duration wind or seismic loading
Estimated footing settlements under the anticipated design loadings are
on the order of less than one half of an inch total and one fourth of an
inch differential
Resistance to lateral loads for the building addition can be
obtained by frictional resistance and passive earth pressure A
frictional coefficient of 0 4 may be used to compute the frictional
resistance between the bottoms of the footings and the footing
subgrade In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of 300 p s f per
foot of depth may be used to determine the passive earth resistance
against the buried vertical faces of the footings
Loading Ramp and Adjacent Underpinning Foundation
The proposed loading ramp construction will require about 4 feet
excavation below the existing pavement level The bottom of the
excavation is anticipated to be very close to the existing groundwater
level measured at about 4 5 to 5 5 feet below the existing pavement
level Therefore dewatering of the excavation may be required prior to
footing foundation placement
To maintain the stability of the existing warehouse adjacent to the
excavation the adjacent column wall loads of the warehouse should be
transferred from the existing footing foundations down to a depth below
the excavation bottom

colum while the footing is lowered Alternatively micro piles or

Site Grading
It is anticipated that the finished grade level of the proposed
building addition will be at the same elevation as the existing adjacent
warehouse Therefore minimal site grading is anticipated for the
generally flat site except the minimm 4 feet deep excavation for the
proposed loading ramp
At the onset of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed All existing slab
pavement debris rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be
removed and disposed properly off site in order to prevent contamination
of the excavated material
The excavated footing and pavement areas should be scarified to a
depth of at least 6 inches below the footing or pavement subgrade
elevation moisture conditioned to optimum moisture content and
recompacted to obtain 95 of ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density
Any loose soft wet or yielding spots encountered at the footing or
pavement subgrade level should be removed and replaced with compacted
on site sandy soil
The on site sandy soil may be used as a source of backfill and fill
material If required new imported fill material should consist of
non expansive select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum
size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve

Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations
and to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions
differ from that anticipated prior to start of construction The
recommendations given in this report are contingent upon such
observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based
in part upon information obtained from field data points such as
borings Variations of conditions between the field data points may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become
evident until construction If variations then appear evident it will
be necessary to re evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field data points were approximately determined
by scaling from the Site Plan Elevations of the data points were not
available at this stage of report preparation The physical locations of
the field data points should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the method used

The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the

Water level data from the field data points was collected at the
