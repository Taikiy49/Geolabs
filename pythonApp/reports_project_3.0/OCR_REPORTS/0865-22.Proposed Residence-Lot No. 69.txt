conditions below the above referenced lot The purpose of

building site laboratory soil tests and a review of the
grading and compaction test record on the building site
The letter summarizes our observations and recommendations

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the lot is underlain
by a surface layer of gray highly to critically expansive
Adobe clay about 1 5 to 2 0 feet thcik over dense
weathered rock Foundations should extend through the
thin surface Adobe clay layer and bear on the dense
rock formation Concrete slab and driveways should bear
on a minimum of 3 and 2 feet respectively of select non
expansive soil or on weathered rock formation
Foundations
Individual spread footings for the house should extend
through the surface Adobe clay and bear on the dense
weathered rock formation The footings should extend a
minimum of 6 inches into the weathered rock formation
An allowable bearing value of 4000 psf can be used on the
dense weathered rock
If a footing is located next to a utility line the footing
should extend to the bottom of the utility trench to reduce
footing settlement due to settlement of the trench backfill
Surface water should be diverted away from the foundations
This is especially important in the rear portion of the
house to reduce ponding of surface run off water and possible
saturation and settlement or heave of the foundation soils
W O 865 22 P 5
Slab on Grade
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage
The preliminary plans indicate that the rear portion of
the garage area will be cut in order to obtain the design
finished floor elevation of 81 feet Therefore it appears
that dense weathered rock would be encountered in the
rear cut half of the garage excavation while Adobe
clay would be encountered in the front half where little
or no cuts are planned The Adobe clay soils are highly
expansive and are unsuitable as slab subgrade soils
Where encountered the Adobe clays should be over exca
vated down to dense weathered rock or for a maximum depth
of 3 feet below the slab subgrade and replaced with non
expansive borrow soils compacted to a minimum of 90 of
its maximum dry density per ASTM D 1557 This over excava
tion and replacement should be extended for a distance of
3 feet beyond the perimeter of the slab
A 4 inch layer of base course should be provided below the
slab and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In
order to protect against moisture infiltration a poly
ethylene moisture barrier may be incorporated below the
slab The membrane should be protected by a thin layer
of sand to aid in the concrete curing
W O 865 22 P 6
Concrete Driveway and Sidewalks
Expansive Adobe clay soils were encountered in the pro
posed driveway area From 0 to 3 feet of fill is planned
for the driveway In order to reduce the expansive effects
of the Adobe clay the Adobe clay should be kept a
minimum of 2 feet below the driveway subgrade level
Where 2 or more feet of fill is planned select non expan
sive borrow material should be used to cap over the Adobe
clay Where less than 2 feet of fill is planned over
excavation and replacement of the Adobe clay with select
non expansive material is required The above requirements
should be followed for a minimum distance of 3 feet beyond
the driveway
Due to the relatively long driveway planned joints are
recommended at about 8 to 10 foot intervals in order to
allow for some relative movement of the concrete
For future sidewalks capping of the Adobe clay or over
excavation and replacement similar to the driveway is
recommended to reduce heaving caused by the expansive
Adobe clay soil
Site Grading
The existing terrain at the site generally slopes down
at about 4 1 H V ratio or flatter A cut slope for the
backyard development of up to about 5 feet is planned
W O 865 22 P 7
Based on the field exploration we anticipate that the
upper 1 to 2 feet of the cut would be in Adobe clay
while the lower portion would encounter weathered rock
The thin Adobe clay layer should be stripped off or
flattened as much as possible to reduce later sloughing
or downhill slope creep Cuts in dense weathered rock
could be made at slope ratios of 1 1 H V
Up to about 3 feet of fill is planned for portions of
the driveway Fill material should be select non expan
sive borrow material Some of the weathered rock excavated
material smaller than 8 inches could be used in the fill
if it is not contaminated with the Adobe clay The
Adobe clay soil should not be used for fill material
The area to be filled should be cleared of vegetation
and debris prior to filling
Fills should be placed in maximum 8 inch lifts and compac
ted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry denstiy
established by ASTM D 1557

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the soils engineer be present
during earthwork construction particularly the fill
placement slope cuts and to monitor the contractor s
grading operations
It is also recommended that the footing excavations be
observed by a soils engineer during construction to see
that adequate bearing has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings
of our subsurface investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive
tested and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling
to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon the above inspections
