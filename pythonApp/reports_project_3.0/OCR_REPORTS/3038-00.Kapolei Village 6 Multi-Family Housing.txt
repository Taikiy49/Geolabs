Regional Geology
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from two
2 shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older voicano Waianae is estimated to
be of middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be of late Pliocene to
early Pleistocene in age As volcanic activity in Waianae ceased lava flows from Koolau
banked against its eroded eastern slope forming a broad plateau now known as the
Schofield Plateau
Physical and chemical weathering followed by erosion of this plateau generated
sediments which were transported to the coast In the vicinity of the project site and to
the south these sediments accumulated and interbedded with marine sediments and
coral algal reef formations to form a sedimentary wedge The thickness of the
sedimentary wedge ranges from Zero in the area of the H 1 Freeway to over 1 000 feet

Slab On Grade Foundations
We understand that the structures proposed for the residential development will be
supported by slabs on grade with thickened edge footings Our exploration indicates that
the site is underlain by moderately to highly expansive clay soils Unless foundations
supported by these soils are properly designed there is potential for future distress to
structures resulting from wetting and subsequent swelling of these expansive clay soils
The following recommendations present alternatives for reducing the potential of such
structural distress
Generally the alternatives may be considered to be equivalent in their capability
to reduce the effects of the expansive soils Selection of either alternate will depend upon
economics site grading preparation requirements and ease of construction
Alternate No 1
To reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from the swelling of the on
site expansive soils we recommend that non expansive import select fil material
be used for a minimum depth of 24 inches below the slab subgrade as a capping
layer The capping layer should extend at least two feet 2 beyond the perimeter
of the proposed structures
The select fill material should consist of non expansive granular material such as
crushed coral mudrock or cinder Select fill material should be well graded with
no particles greater than three inches 3 in size should have a laboratory CBR
swell value not exceeding one percent 1 and should have a CBR value of 20
or more Select fill material should not contain large percentages of boulders
organic or other deleterious material The select capping fill should be moisture
conditioned to slightly above the optimum moisture content and compacted to not
less than 90 percent relative compaction
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3038 00 Page 11
The capping layer of select fill should be placed as soon as possible after the
scarification and compaction of the subgrade soils The select fill material should
provide a capping layer of moist soil above the underlying potentially expansive
subgrade soil thereby reducing the loss of moisture from the subgrade soils in
our opinion the reduction in potential drying and wetting of the subgrade soils
should reduce the impact on the slab due potential swelling of the underlying
expansive clayey soils when wetted
Conventional slab on grade construction may then be utilized for proposed
structures constructed on the 24 inch thick capping layer consisting of select fill
material However we recommend that flexible floor coverings such as carpet or
sheet vinyl be considered since they can better mask any minor slab cracking
it is also recommended that interior walls be designed to incorporate some
flexibility to accommodate small amount of possible ground movements
For minor unoccupied structures such as bus stop shelters and electrical
transformer pads the thickness of the select fill capping layer may be reduced to
18 inches
The select fill capping layer should be wetted and kept moist until final placement
of concrete A minimum six inch 6 layer of 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No
67 gradation should be placed over the capping fill to serve as a slab cushion and
capillary break A moisture barrier overlying the cushion fill should be incorporated
below the concrete slab to reduce the potential of damage to the floor coverings
by infiltration of moisture One inch 1 of moist fine sand may be placed over the
moisture barrier membrane to aid in curing of the concrete

Subdrainage
It has been our experience with projects in the Ewa area and with projects having
similar soil conditions to find that when a granular select material is used as subbase
course or as a capping over clay soils there is a tendency for water to accumulate at the
interface between the select material and the underlying clay soils _
It appears that the granular select material is relatively permeable and that the clay _
soils are relatively impermeable Precipitation and landscape irrigation water tend to
infiltrate through the topsoil into the select material and form a perched water table onthe
underlying clay horizon
Premature pavement failures have occurred when this water reaches the subgrade
of pavement section and when the select material daylights on top of the clay When the
water seeps into road subgrades it can soften the subgrade soils and weaken the
pavement section Where the seepage daylights continuously wet areas can develop
To reduce the potential of accumulation of water along the select material and clay
soil interface we recommend that subdrains be installed to intercept water and divert it
to appropriate drainage locations such as drain inlets Generally the worst cases have
occurred where the water moves into road subgrades Therefore we recommend that
subdrains be installed along the sides of the road prisms The subdrains could either be
of the conventional type such as a perforated pipe embedded in free draining gravel or
one of the newer synthetic types such as MiraDrain or ConTech 100

Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling five
5 borings to depths of about 15 feet below the existing ground surface at the
approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled using a
truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers
Soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in general accordance with
ASTM Test Method D 1586 84 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches The blow counts needed
_to drive the sampler the last twelve inches 12 of an 18 inch drive are shown on the
boring logs at the appropriate sample depths
The materials encountered in the borings were examined and classified in general
accordance with ASTM Standard Practice D 2488 84 Description and Identification of
Soils Visual Manual Procedure by an engineer or geologist from our firm The logs of
the borings are shown on Plates A 1 through A 5

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was formulated to aid in evaluating pertinent
engineering properties of the soil and materials sampled
Classification Tests
Moisture content ASTM Test Method D 2216 80 and unit weight determinations
are used as an aid to soil classification and to aid in evaluating certain properties such
as relative saturation The moisture content expressed as a percentage of the dry weight
_of the tested sample and dry unit weight are shown at the appropriate location on plates
summarizing test results and at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs Plates
A 1 through A 5
Atterberg Limits tests ASTM Test Method D 4318 84 are used to evaluate the
plasticity of the fine grained portion of soils The Plasticity Index of the material is used
to classify fine grained soils under the Unified Soil Classification System Two Atterberg
Limits test were performed The results of the tests are shown on Boring Logs of B 1 and
B 2 Plates A 1 and A 2
Physical Property Tests 5
Expansion tests were performed to evaluate the swelling potential of soils These
tests aid in evaluating the materials for use as foundation support and fill Twelve 12
one inch ring swell tests were performed on relatively undisturbed or remolded samples
of the clay soils to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils in natural and remolded
conditions respectively The test results are summarized on Plate B 2
Two 2 CBR tests ASTM Test Method D 1883 73 were performed on near
surface bulk samples to evaluate the soil strength for preliminary pavement design The
test results are summarized on Plate B 1
Two 2 soil resistivity tests ASTM Test Method G 57 were performed to evaluate
the corrosion rating of soils The test results are summarized on Plate B 1
