Seven test pits were dug in the scarp area outlined on the Site
Plan Plate 1 This aréa was first designated as a scarp by
Walter Lum Associates in their report dated
The test pits were dug on in the scarp area to
determine the depth and extent of the critical clay layer This
clay layer was originally indicated in the boring logs made by
Walter Lum Associates The field exploration work was co
ordinated and inspected by F H A Site Engieer

The critical clay layer was easily identifiable by its red
lavender gray color The atterberg tests on this material
showed a Unified Soil Classification of MH Geologically this
material is highly decomposed basalt which has been hydrothermally
altered at the time of origin within the center of the Koolau
Crater In addition the test pits revedled the fact that the
critical clay layer was not continuous as originally assumed by
other soil consultant This finding reduced the depth and extent
of excavation within the scarp area The tog of test pits and
their Location are shown on Plate 1b The locatien of test pits
were surveyed and determined by William Hee And Associates
The laboratory test results are shown on the test pit logs and
Plate 2
Cross sections were then drawn to outline the depth and extent
of excavation necessary to remove the clay layer to stabilize
the proposéd fill slopes
The logs of the test pits and the laboratory test data together
with the cross sections of proposed excavation and fill were
approved by the F H A office prior to excavation The excava
tion of the clay layer was done under our supervision and the
fill compacted according to F H A Data Sheet 79G Results
