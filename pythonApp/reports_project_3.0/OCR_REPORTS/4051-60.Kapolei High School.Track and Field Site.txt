PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The future track and field project site is located within the existing Kapolei High
School premises in the Kapolei area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Based on the information provided we understand that the proposed track and
field project will be developed under Phase IIC of the campus construction A
geotechnical engineering exploration has been conducted by our office for the design of
the high school campus and our findings and recommendations are contained in the
report entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Kapolei High School Kapolei
Oahu Hawaii dated March 24 1999
Based on the information provided we understand that a synthetic surface may be
provided at the top of the new track planned for the high school campus at the time our fee
proposal for this geotechnical engineering evaluation was prepared We understand that
the synthetic coating at the top of the new track may be highly sensitive to ground
movements Because the Kapolei High School site and the surrounding areas consist of
filled ground with the fill materials obtained from numerous sources throughout the Island
of Oahu we believe that the ground conditions across the high school campus and the
track and field project site may be highly variable and unpredictable Therefore there

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Our field exploration program at the track and field project site consisted of drilling
and sampling five additional borings designated as Boring Nos 101 through 105
extending to depths of about 20 feet below the existing ground surface The subsurface
conditions at the track and field site were explored previously by our office in 1999 as part
of the overall high school development The applicable subsurface boring logs pertaining
to the track and field project site from our previous field exploration are included in
Appendix B of this report for reference The approximate locations of the drilled borings
from our current and previous field explorations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general our field explorations disclosed that the track and field site generally is
underlain by surface fill materials of variable thickness extending to depths of about 4 to 14
feet below the existing ground surface It should be noted that the fill materials
encountered at the project site are highly variable in nature as the fill materials were
obtained from numerous sources throughout the Island of Oahu
The fill materials encountered in our field explorations ranged from very stiff to very
hard silty clays clayey silts and sandy clays as well as medium dense to dense clayey
sands and silty sands overlying cobbles and boulders in localized areas across the project
site In addition laboratory swell test results indicate that the shrink swell characteristics of
the near surface materials were highly variable ranging from slight to low expansion
potential 1 8 percent to high expansion potential 18 4 percent Below the surface fill
materials medium dense to dense coralline sands and gravel coralline detritus were
encountered and extended to the maximum depths explored of approximately 26 5 feet
below the existing ground surface
Groundwater was not encountered in the borings drilled at the time of our field
explorations In general the site is at an elevation several tens of feet above the highest
basal water table in the area However groundwater levels can vary significantly
depending on seasonal precipitation tidal fluctuation and other factors occurring off site
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration methodology and the Logs of Borings
are presented on Plates A 1 through A 5 of Appendix A The applicable subsurface boring
logs pertaining to the track and field project site from our previous field exploration are
included in Appendix B of this report for reference Results of laboratory tests performed
on selected soil samples retrieved from our current field exploration are presented in

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the field explorations and laboratory tests conducted on the soils in the
Kapolei area the clayey soils in the Kapolei area generally exhibit moderate to high
shrink swell potential when subjected to fluctuations in the soil moisture content This
phenomenon is further accentuated by the prolonged dry weather conditions that are
typical of the Kapolei area In order to reduce the potentially adverse effects of the
shrink swell behavior of the near surface soils on the planned improvements such as
the buildings and pavements resulting from the shrinking and swelling of the expansive
clay soils we generally recommend that the clayey soils be capped with an adequate
thickness of non expansive select granular fill materials
It should be noted that even with an adequate thickness of select granular fill
materials the ground surface will still move in response to the drying and wetting of the
near surface soils due to changes in the weather conditions and the moisture content in
the ground In general the movements at the ground surface of the improvements are
manageable on the order of about 1 to 1 5 inches The improvements such as the
buildings and pavements generally would be able to tolerate this amount of ground
movement without significant adverse effects on the improvements
Based on this type of movement and prior experience in the area we have
recommended the use of a minimum of 12 inches of select granular fill below the
ground floor slabs and pavements for the Kapolei High School development In addition
to the minimum 12 inches of select granular fill we have recommended special
subgrade preparation requirements to be observed during construction Special
attention has been given to the subgrade preparation during construction to reduce the
potential for appreciable adverse effects on the improvements
We understand that it is proposed to provide a synthetic coating at the top of the
new track We are not certain if the synthetic coating at the top of the new track is
sensitive to ground movements If the synthetic coating at the top of the new track is
highly sensitive to ground movements then the thickness of select granular fill capping
recommended previously for the building support and pavements would need to be
increased Depending on the amount of ground movements that the synthetic coating at
the top of the new track would be able to tolerate without cracking the thickness of select
granular fill capping would need to be increased accordingly
Because some cracking of the synthetic surfacing was experienced at the tennis
court area there have been concerns raised as to the potential for similar cracking at the
new track area due to the use of a synthetic coating on the top of the new track It should
be noted that we have observed cracks in numerous playcourts with synthetic surfacing at
various locations on the Island of Oahu with different types of ground conditions At the
same locations of the playcourts with surface cracks we have also noted that the parking
areas constructed of asphaltic concrete without a synthetic surfacing do not have
substantial cracks One hypothesis for the observed cracking in the playcourts is to

Based on our knowledge of the Kapolei area the ground conditions at the Kapolei
High School site and the surrounding areas consist of filled ground The fill materials used
to raise the ground in the Kapolei area were obtained from numerous sources throughout
the Island of Oahu Therefore the ground conditions can be highly variable and
somewhat less predictable than natural ground or filled ground from one or two specific
sources only
As mentioned previously in the Subsurface Conditions section of this letter report
the surface fill materials encountered at the track and field site are highly variable in
thickness ranging from 4 to 14 feet nature ranging from clays to sands and
characteristics ranging from slightly to highly expansive Given these ground conditions
and circumstances it is very difficult for us as geotechnical engineers to predict the
behavior of the ground conditions at the site based on our field explorations and laboratory
tests We have employed sound engineering practice in providing the recommendations
for design of the buildings and pavements however we cannot possibly predict the
unpredictable
Therefore there exists the potential for presence of highly expansive clayey soils in
localized areas of the site that are not identified and or recognized based on a finite
amount of field explorations and laboratory tests As mentioned previously the site is
underlain by fill materials of variable nature which could lead to unpredictable ground
behavior Therefore we believe that representatives of the high school and the
Department of Education should expect some amount of ground movements at the new
track area that might lead to distress in localized areas of the new track especially if the
synthetic coating at the top of the new track is sensitive to ground movements
Based on the explanation provided above we believe that representatives of the
high school and the Department of Education should be advised of the ground conditions
and the difficulty in eliminating all the geotechnical related risks and ground movements at
the project site As a result we believe that a natural tuff surfacing should be strongly
considered for the new track and field site if the potential for some cracking of the synthetic
coating is not acceptable by the appropriate agencies
In addition to the consideration of a natural tuff surfacing as discussed above we
recommend that the existing ground across the track and field site be over excavated by
about 3 feet to remove the highly variable fill materials placed during the mass grading
operations of the high school campus The over excavation should be backfilled with
select granular material placed in level lifts of about 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction per ASTM D 1557 We
believe that the 3 foot over excavation and replacement with select granular fill material
would serve to reduce the potential for appreciable differential soil movements across the
track and field site

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsurface conditions between
and beyond the field borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may
not become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident
Geolabs should be contacted to re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report
The locations of the field borings indicated in this report are approximate having
been estimated by taping from the features shown on the General Site Plan transmitted by
R M Towill Corporation on August 8 2003 The elevations of the borings were estimated
based on the contour lines and spot elevations shown on the same plan The physical
locations and elevations of the field borings should be considered accurate only to the
degree implied by the method used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between the soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the
graphic representations and or presented in the text of this report These data have been
reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be
noted that fluctuations may occur due to tidal fluctuation variation in rainfall temperatures
and other factors
This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of Makai Village Partnership for
specific application to the Track and Field Site within the Kapolei High School campus in
accordance with generally accepted geotechnical engineering principles and practices
No warranty is expressed or implied
This report has been prepared solely for the purpose of assisting the architect and
design engineers in the design evaluation of the proposed project Therefore this report
may not contain sufficient data or the proper information to serve as the basis for
preparation of construction cost estimates nor for bidding purposes A contractor wishing
to bid on this project should retain a competent geotechnical engineer to assist in the
interpretation of this report and or in the performance of additional site specific exploration
for bid estimating purposes
The owner client should be aware that unanticipated subsurface conditions are
commonly encountered Unforeseen subsurface conditions such as perched
groundwater soft deposits hard layers or cavities may occur in localized areas and may
require additional probing or corrections in the field which may result in construction
delays to attain a properly constructed project Therefore a sufficient contingency fund is
recommended to accommodate these possible extra costs

APPENDIX A

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the track and field project site were explored
previously by our office in 1999 as part of the overall high school development Our
current field exploration consisted of drilling and sampling five borings designated as
Boring Nos 101 through 105 extending to depths of about 20 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate locations of the drilled borings from our previous and
current field explorations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 The borings were drilled
using a truck mounted drill rig equipped with continuous flight augers
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who observed the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual
observation and testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance
with the Unified Soil Classification System as shown on Plate A Graphic
representations of the materials encountered are presented on the Logs of Borings
Plates A 1 through A 5
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the drilled borings in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling
of Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer
falling 30 inches In addition some samples were obtained from the drilled borings in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and
Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler
using the same hammer and drop The blow counts needed to drive the sampler the
second and third 6inches of an 18 inch drive are shown as the Penetration
Resistance on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
