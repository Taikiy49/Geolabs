PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The planned Kukui ula Development is a resort community that encompasses
about 1 045 acres in Koloa on the south shore of Kauai and includes a hotel vacation
ownership commercial park golf course and residential uses The Parcel E Makani
Kai Subdivision project is part of the improvements for the Kukui ula Development
The Parcel E Subdivision project is located on the northwest side of the Kukui ula
Resort Community development below agricultural parcels and west of Ala Makani Kai
Road overlooking the 11th hole of the Kukui ula Golf Course and the Pacific Ocean The
parcel encompasses approximately 12 4 acres with 11 individual house lots designated
as Lot Nos 1 through 11 A Project Location Map and Site Plan are shown on Plates 1
and 2 respectively
Site grading generally consisted of cuts up to about 14 feet deep and fills up to
about 14 feet thick On site soils generated from the cut areas were the primary source
of fill material for the project However fill materials consisting of reddish brown clayey
silts and silty clays were imported from the Manuhonuhonu Borrow site north of the

PARCEL E EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
We observed the mass and pad grading underground utility construction and Hiki
Moe Place roadway construction work for the Parcel E Makani Kai Subdivision project
from to The mass and site grading work consisted of
clearing and grubbing cuts of up to about 14 feet deep and placement and compaction of
fills up to about 14 feet thick to achieve the finished grades Prior to filling the existing
ground surface was cleared and grubbed of vegetation and deleterious material then
proof rolled with a compactor Earthworks Pacific Inc performed the mass and pad
grading and utility and roadway construction work
On site boulder fills were placed within the southeast portion of Lot and
11 The cobble and boulder fills were seated with a dozer then compacted with a large
sheepsfoot wheel compactor
The mass grading was completed for the Ala Kukui ula Extension road and Ala
Kukui ula Extension To Future Parcel as previously described However it is understood
that the road construction for the road extensions will be completed at a future date
During the site grading operations samples of the materials utilized as fill were
obtained and transported to our office for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general accordance with
ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
The fill materials were moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture content and
compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum dry density as determined in
accordance with ASTM D1557 In the roadway areas the fill and backfill materials at the
finished subgrade were compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
During the mass and pad grading and utility and roadway construction operations
periodic field density tests were performed in general accordance with ASTM D6938
Nuclear Gauge Method Compaction test results are summarized on Plates 4 1 through
4 10 These tests were performed on the prepared ground surfaces and on constructed
fills and backfills at the site Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and
interpolating from on site survey stakes These locations and elevations should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used Areas assessed as
