This report summarizes our earthwork observation and testing services performed
during the site grading operations foundation excavation pavement construction and
utility installation for the NFL Youth Education Town YET project from March 14 through
in accordance with the scope of services outlined in our fee proposal
dated March 14 2008
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site designated as Tax Map Key 67 is adjacent to the existing
Nanaikapono Elementary School off Mano Avenue in Nanakuli in the leeward coast area
on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project site is a trapezoidal shaped parcel
encompassing approximately 1 61 acres
Based on the information provided we understand that a new NFL Youth
Education Town YET facility for the Boys Girls Club of Hawaii is planned We
understand that the new building will be a single story structure with perimeter Concrete
Masonry Unit CMU walls and isolated CMU and steel columns as well as fiberglass
shingle roofing and a concrete slab on grade floor

EARTHWORK OPERATIONS
Lindemann Construction Inc is the general contractor for the NFL Youth Education
Town YET at Nanakuli project Lindemann Construction Inc also performed the site
grading operations foundation excavation pavement construction and utility installation
The site grading consisted of clearing and grubbing footing excavation subgrade
proof rolling placement and compaction of fills and backfills pavement construction and
utility trench excavation and backfill
Following the clearing and grubbing operations the existing ground was generally
over excavated where necessary to the subgrade elevations within the building and
pavement limits The coral formation exposed at the over excavated subgrades was then
proof rolled with a minimum 10 ton vibratory drum roller to detect the presence of potential
near surface cavities sinkholes or loose zones New fills required for the project
consisting of the excavated in situ coralline materials were generally placed in near level
lifts brought to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent of the maximum dry density determined in accordance with ASTM D 1557
Modified Proctor
New sewer water and drain lines were installed to serve the new development In
general a bedding layer consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel was provided below
the piping and manholes for uniform support Free draining granular materials consisting
of No 3B Fine gravel was also used for the trench backfill up to about 12 inches above the
pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes and to reduce the compaction effort
on the initial trench backfill The trench backfill was placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and
mechanically compacted to no less than 90 percent relative compaction Where trenches
are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below the pavement
grade was compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
During the site grading operations sample of the on site soils utilized as fill
materials was obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry
densities and optimum moisture contents were established in our laboratory in general
accordance with ASTM D 1557 Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on
the attached Plate 2
Field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations on an
as need basis to confirm our field observations as variations in construction procedures
and soil conditions were noted A total of 4 field density tests were performed using the
nuclear probe method in accordance with ASTM 6938 Results of the compaction tests

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some of the earthwork may
have been performed without our observation However the level of earthwork
observation and testing services provided appears to be reasonable in reducing some
of the geotechnical risks to the project It should be recognized that there are variations
in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods used to monitor the
earthwork construction and materials
