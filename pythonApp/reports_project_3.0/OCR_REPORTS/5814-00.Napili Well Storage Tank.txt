SECTION 1 0 GENERAL
1 1 Introduction
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the proposed 300 000 gallon storage tank project within the existing
Napili Well and Tank site in the District of Napili on the Island of Maui Hawaii The
project location and general vicinity are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes our findings and geotechnical engineering
recommendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and
engineering analyses for the new water tank project These recommendations are
intended for the design of water tank and control building foundations slabs on grade
and site preparation only The findings and recommendations presented herein are
subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
1 2 Project Considerations
The project involves the construction of an additional 300 000 gallon tank at the
existing Napili Well and Tank site situated in the upper slopes of the Napili area on the
Island of Maui Hawaii The proposed tank will be built west of the existing
100 000 gallon tank
Our experience in the area suggested that the project site may be underlain by
residual soils at shallow depths The main objectives of the exploration program
included the following
e Provision of design recommendations for the foundation of the proposed
water tank particularly the bearing pressure and footing embedment depth
e Provision of estimates of foundation settlements
e Provision of recommendations for site preparation
Details for the proposed tank and grading plans were not available at the time
this report was prepared However we anticipate that minor site grading work consisting
of fills less than 2 feet thick may be required to achieve the design finish grades for the
project construction

1 3 Purpose and Scope
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the surface and
subsurface conditions to develop a generalized soil rock data set to formulate
geotechnical engineering recommendations for the design of water tank and control
building foundations slabs on grade and site preparation for the new water tank
project Our work was performed in general accordance with our fee proposal dated
The scope of work for this exploration included the following tasks
and work efforts
1 Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig presently on
the Island of Maui and operators from Honolulu to the project site and
back
2 Drilling and sampling of one boring extending to a depth of about 41 5 feet
below the existing ground surface for one day of field exploration
3 Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the boring by our
geologist
4 Laboratory testing of selected soil samples obtained during the field
exploration as an aid in classifying the materials and evaluating their
engineering properties
5 Engineering analyses of the field and laboratory data to formulate
geotechnical engineering recommendations for the design of water tank
and control building foundations slabs on grade and site preparation
6 Preparation of this report summarizing our work on the project and
presenting our findings and geotechnical engineering recommendations
7 Coordination of our overall work on the project by our senior engineer
8 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by our
principal engineer
9 Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support

SECTION 2 0 SITE CHARACTERIZATION
2 1 Site Description
The project site is within the existing Napili Well and Tank site situated in the
upper slopes of the Napili area on the Island of Maui Hawaii The new water tank will
be built southwest of the existing 100 000 gallon tank as shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
Based on our field observations the project site lies on old pineapple fields and
was slightly vegetated with wild grass at the time of our exploration A fence exists to
the east of the new water tank site Based on the field observation the topography of
the area slopes very gently upward from west to east
2 2 Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling one boring
designated as Boring No 1 extending to a depth of about 41 5 feet below the existing
ground surface The approximate boring location is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration generally encountered thin fill overlying residual soils
extending to the maximum depth explored of approximately 41 5 feet below the existing
ground surface The residual soils consist of medium stiff to hard clayey silt In addition
extremely weathered basalt was encountered within the residual soil layer We did not
encounter groundwater in the boring at the time of our field exploration However it
should be noted that the groundwater level may be affected by seasonal precipitation
run off storm surge conditions and other factors
Detailed descriptions of the field exploration methodology are presented in

the drilled boring are provided on the Logs of Boring in Appendix A Laboratory tests
were performed on selected soil samples and the test results are presented in

3 4 Slabs On Grade for Control Building
In general we envision that slab on grade may be planned for the project As
previously indicated the project site is generally underlain by medium stiff to stiff clayey
silt Based on our laboratory test results the on site clayey silt exhibits slight to
moderate expansion potential when subjected to moisture fluctuations Slabs on grade
supported by the expansive soil should be designed properly to reduce the potential for
future distress to the structures resulting from wetting and subsequent swelling of the
clayey soils
To reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from swelling of the
on site expansive soils we believe that non expansive structural fill material should be
provided for a minimum depth of 6 inches below the slab cushion
Prior to placement of the structural fill we recommend scarifying the exposed
subgrades after clearing and grubbing to a minimum depth of 8 inches where
possible moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above the optimum moisture and
properly re compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction
The subgrade soils should be maintained in a moist condition until placement of
the structural fill The structural fill material should provide a capping layer of moist soil
above the underlying potentially expansive subgrade soil thereby reducing moisture
loss from the clayey subgrade soils In our opinion the reduction of drying and
subsequent wetting of the subgrade soils should reduce the impact of shrink swell
effects on the slab caused by potential heaving of the underlying expansive clayey soils
when wetted The structural fill should be moisture conditioned placed in level loose
lifts and compacted to no less than 90 percent relative compaction
In addition new interior slabs should be supported on a minimum 4 inch thick
layer of cushion fill consisting of No 3 Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation or
Basaltic Termite Barrier BTB To reduce the potential for moisture infiltration a
moisture barrier should be placed above the cushion fill Flexible floor coverings such
as carpet or sheet vinyl should be considered since they can better mask any minor

3 8 Drainage
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered at the project site we believe
that a perimeter drain may be required around the new water tank structure for surface
run off disposal In addition the finished grades outside the water tank structure should
be sloped to shed water away from foundations and slab and to reduce the potential for
ponding Excessive landscape watering near the foundations and slab should also be
avoided These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent loss of strength The foundation excavations should be
backfilled properly against the walls or slab footings immediately after setting of the
concrete to reduce the potential for water infiltration Furthermore drainage swales
should be provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to drain all surface
runoff away from slab and foundations

The subsurface conditions at the new water tank site were explored by drilling and
sampling one boring designated as Boring No 1 extending to a depth of about 41 5 feet
below the existing ground surface at the approximate location shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2 The boring was drilled using truck mounted drilling equipment equipped with
continuous flight augers

examination in the field by our geologist who observed the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis These classifications were further reviewed by visual observation
and testing in the laboratory Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937
determinations were performed on selected soil samples as an aid in the classification
and evaluation of soil properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings
at the appropriate sample depths
Two one inch Ring Swell tests were performed on relatively undisturbed and
remolded samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the near surface soils under
different surcharge loads The test results are summarized on Plate B 1
Two Atterberg Limits tests ASTM D 4318 were performed on a selected soil
samples to evaluate the liquid and plastic limits and to aid in soil classification The test
results are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
Graphic presentation of the test results is provided on Plate B 2
One Direct Shear test ASTM D 3080 was performed on one selected sample to
evaluate the shear strength characteristics of the materials tested The test results are
presented on Plate B 3
