PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the subsurface soil
and water conditions at the lot to obtain pertinent near surface soil data
in order to provide foundation and slab on grade recommendations for the
proposed house and driveway

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed a surface layer of expansive adobe
clay underlain by compressible soft and wet clay and loose coralline
sand gravel under the proposed building site Since minimal fill is
planned and considering the proposed light wood framed structure the main
concern in designing the slab on grade for the driveway and building
footing foundation would be to adopt special procedures in order to reduce
the shrink swell effect of the subsoils
Future filling at the site should be avoided since this additional
load may cause ground settlements

Foundation Support
Spread footing foundation may be used for the proposed building
provided special procedures are utilized to reduce the shrinkage swelling
effects of the clay soils
The adobe clay soils below the foundations have high volume change
potential and can shrink when dried or heave considerably if saturated in
the future Therefore we recommend that the expansive clays below the
foundations be over excavated down to a minimum depth of 3 feet below the
bottom of the footings The over excavation should also extend a minimum
of 18 inches beyond the edge of the footings
The bottom 2 feet of the over excavation should be backfilled with
non expansive borrow materials such as select borrow crusher waste or
coral placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 9 inches thick and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of its maximum dry density per ASTM
D 1557 78 The top 1 foot should be backfilled with impervious
non expansive soil compacted to the above density refer to Plate 3
Based on the anticipated soil conditions site grading and design
loading we believe that an allowable bearing capacity of 2000 p s f may
be used for spread footing bearing on compacted structural fill
Footing should be placed a minimum of 12 inches below the Lowest
adjacent finished grade Uplift wind or other lateral loading conditions
may further increase the embedment of the footing foundation
Driveway Slab On Grade
The expansive on site clay soil under the driveway slab areas should
be over excavated to a depth of 3 feet below the slab bottom and replaced
with non expansive borrow soils in order to reduce the effects of soil
heaving on the slabs see Plate 4

Similar over excavation and replacement of the expansive clay soils
are recommended for the walkway and slabs if required
To reduce future post construction swelling of the underlying
expansive soils we recommend that the on site soils below the
over excavation level be kept continuously moist prior to fill concrete
placement
A 4 inch layer of 3B fine gravel should be provided below the slab
and compacted to 90 of its maximum dry density In order to protect
against moisture infiltration an impervious moisture barrier may be
incorporated below the slab
Site Grading
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading
limit should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable materials
such as rubbish and vegetation should be removed and disposed of properly
off site
Prior to backfilling hydrated Lime should be sprinkled to cover the
adobe subgrade which should then be moistened using a sprinkler for 24
hours
Loose spots encountered at subgrade level should be removed and
replaced with imported fill material compacted to a minimum of 90 of its
ASTM D 1557 78 maximum dry density
After scarification and recompaction of the subgrade the structural
fill should be placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding 9 inches in
compacted thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 of its ASTM
D 1557 78 maximum dry density

The site should be graded to provide drainage of surface runoff away
from the building
Drainage
The finished grade outside the slabs and footings should be shaped
to shed water away from the foundations and to avoid ponding condition
Also it is advised that gutter systems be installed around the
structures and that gutter water be diverted far away from the
foundations and walkways including the existing structures Excessive
landscape watering near the foundations and slabs should also be avoided
Planters next to the foundations should be avoided or have concrete
bottoms and drains to prevent water from entering the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance
of the above mentioned foundations since ponded water could cause
excessive subsurface soil saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of
strength
The foundation excavations should be properly backfilled tight
against the footing immediately after setting of the concrete to prevent
ponding condition
Drainage swales must be provided as soon as possible and maintained
to drain all surface run off away from the slab and fooing foundation
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading foundation and pavement
design should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendation is included

Construction Monitoring
It is reommend that the site grading and footing excavation be
observed by the soils engineer during construction to see that adequate
compaction has been obtained and that the subsurface conditions are
consistent with the findings of our investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site for use
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
