PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our survey was to obtain an overview of the electrical resistivity of
the subsurface soils at the site Our work on this project was performed in general
conformance with ASTM Test Designation G 57 78 and with the requirements provided
in the Water System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 dated 1991 and
subsequent amendments and additions The scope of our work consisted of the
following work efforts
1 Mobilization Demobilization of equipment and engineers to the project site
2 Performance of 32 in situ soil resistivity surveys at eight locations on Roads
K L M and N in Phases F and G of Area 7 These locations
generally correspond to the 300 foot spacing maximum required in the
Water System External Corrosion Control Standards Volume 3 1991
3 Reduction and analyses of the data pertaining to the corrosion
characteristics of the soils at the site
4 Preparation of four copies of this brief report summarizing our work on the
project and presenting our findings
5 Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
Principal Engineer of our firm

FIELD EXPLORATION
Geophysical survey equipment i e resistance meter was mobilized to the site on
March 3 1994 and our field work was performed in sunny conditions with generally dry
surface conditions
Thirty two soil resistivity surveys were conducted at eight locations at the above
referenced area using the Wenner four pin method and a resistance meter Strata Scout
R 40C manufactured by SoilTest Inc with 120 volts output and 24 watts output into the
ground This method utilizes four surface probes or pins electrodes to evaluate the
average resistivity an indication of corrosivity of the underlying soil mass The average
resistivities of the soil mass extending from the existing ground surface to the planned
invert elevations of the water lines were assessed by using this method at test locations
staked out for this purpose by Walter P Thompson Inc The existing surface elevations
of the test locations were surveyed and provided by Walter P Thompson Inc
The average soil resistance was evaluated at each test location with the electrodes
aligned at equal spacings of generally 2 to 5 feet at the existing ground surface Among
these spacings used one spacing may have more significance than the others for
allowing more contribution to the evaluated soil resistivity from the closest mass of soil
to the proposed pipe invert Such spacings are flagged with an asterisk on Plates 3 1
and 3 2 At one test location the pipe invert is less than 1 foot below the existing ground
surface At another test location the pipe invert is above the existing ground surface
Therefore fill material will influence the corrosion potential of the water lines at these
locations In general we believe that the electrodes aligned at equal spacing of less than
2 feet may not be acceptable for this method due to the loose surface condition and
limited penetration depth of the electrodes into the ground These test locations are
flagged with 2 asterisks on the attached plates

The resistance meter R 40C impresses a voltage between the outer electrodes
causing an electrical current to flow between them The voltage drop between the inner
electrodes is measured by a sensitive built in voltmeter As a result the soil resistance
in Ohms can be found directly on the dial The soil resistivity in Ohm cm which is
directly related to both resistance and spacing can then be computed The evaluated
soil resistances computed resistivities and corresponding corrosion ratings are
presented on Plates 3 1 and 3 2 of this report
Resistivity is generally recognized as one of the most significant soil characteristics
with regard to corrosivity of the soil In general the lower the resistivity the greater the
potential for corrosion of buried metallic structures Conversely the higher the resistivity
the less likely the soil will contribute to corrosion
LIMITATIONS
The evaluations presented in this report are based upon information obtained from
field data points by means of geophysical surveys Variations of soil conditions between
the field data points may occur and the nature and extent of such variations may not
become evident until a later point in time If variations then appear evident it will be
necessary to reevaluate the ratings provided in this report
Locations and existing surface elevations of the data presented in this report were
surveyed by Walter P Thompson Inc Pipe invert elevations of the data presented are
approximate having been estimated from reference points shown on the undated
construction plans prepared by Gentry Hawaii Ltd All elevations were rounded to the
nearest 4 foot to emphasize that they may not be accurate enough for other purposes
Thus elevations and locations of the presented data should be considered accurate only
to the degree implied by the methods used
