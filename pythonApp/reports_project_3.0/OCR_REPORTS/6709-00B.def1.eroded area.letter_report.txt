PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is situated in the lower portion of Papakolea in Honolulu on the Island
of Oahu Hawaii It is our understanding that your firm has been retained by the Department
of Hawaiian Home Lands to design a rehabilitation project for the sewers in the area
During the initial site visits for the project some issues were observed at and in the
vicinity of 2103 Tantalus Drive The first issue is an eroded area in the backyard of
2103 Tantalus Drive that is encroaching on the steep slope leading down to
Auwaiolimu Street Initial observations at the site tend to indicate that this erosion may be
related to an abandoned sewer lateral in the backyard
The second issue is distresses to a retaining wall immediately adjacent to the
Tantalus Drive overpass bridge crossing Auwaiolimu Street This wall has large open vertical
cracks and shows some signs of movement
The final issue related to the project is the steep near vertical slope between the
Papakolea subdivision area and Auwaiolimu Street This slope was cut in both Punchbowl
volcanic tuff and Koolau basalt The portion in the tuff formation appears to be closely jointed
and fragments spall from the slope face and fall to the right of way below Since portions of

RETAINING WALL DISTRESS
At the front side of the property 2103 Tantalus Drive there is a relatively low
retaining wall along Tantalus Drive The wall is about 6 to 7 feet high with about 5 feet
of retaining The wall was constructed of quarried basalt blocks as is typical in the
Papakolea area Details of the wall construction are not available
Currently the wall is showing vertical cracks about 2 inches in width along joints
between the blocks i e separation of the mortar from the blocks There is about
1 inch maximum displacement of the wall face at the cracks A ficus tree has become
established at the southwestern end of the wall and it appears that the roots may have
extended behind the wall A portion of the wall supports the suspended driveway slab
but this portion does not appear to be distressed
At this location on the property our field exploration consisted of
hand excavating a test pit near the base of the retaining wall to evaluate the
approximate depth to volcanic tuff rock formation The test pit penetrated about 2 feet
of stiff clay overlying 2 feet of stiff clayey silt Volcanic tuff was encountered at a depth
of about 4 feet below the existing ground surface
Based on the conditions encountered at the site it is our opinion that a practical
solutions to the wall distresses would be to
1
To remove the ficus tree and reconstruct the wall or
2
To remove the tree and install a horizontal beam on the wall facing with
drilled tie backs extending under Tantalus Drive to transfer the wall loads
to the soil and rock behind the wall Preliminary analyses indicate that a
single row of tie backs on about 10 foot centers would stabilize the wall
Note that City approval would be required to install the anchors within the
right of way
