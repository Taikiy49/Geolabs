PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Kailua Road Improvements project is located along the northern boundary of
the Ka Malanai Phase 2 apartment complex in Kailua The improvements consisted of the
installation of new water and sewer lines drain connections manholes subsurface
electrical and communication lines road widening and a percolation field for storm water
disposal
The project included the demolition and replacement of the road infrastructure and
upgrading the aging utility lines along the Kailua Road corridor in support of the new
apartment complex project The segment of the Kailua Road improvements began from
Station 11 35 near the intersection with Aoloa Street Station to approximately
Station 16 23 to the east as shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 and the Site Plan
and Profile Plate 2
This report summarizes our geotechnical field observation and testing services
during site grading construction from to March 16 2017

SITE GRADING
During the site grading operations samples of the materials utilized as fill were
obtained and transported to our laboratory for testing The maximum dry densities and
optimum moisture contents were established in general accordance with ASTM D1557
Modified Proctor and the test results are summarized on Plate 3
Periodic in situ moisture and density tests were conducted in general accordance
with ASTM D6938 Nuclear Gauge Method to determine the adequacy of compaction of
fills and backfills for infrastructure and utility installations including utility trench manhole
structures and roadway
The fill and backfill materials were generally moisture conditioned to near or above
optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent of the maximum
dry density as determined in accordance with ASTM D1557 In the roadway areas the
fill and backfill materials within 3 feet of the finished grade were compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent of the maximum dry density
The test results are summarized on Plates 4 1 through 4 12 These tests were
performed on moisture conditioned and compacted lifts of fills and backfills Test locations
and elevations were estimated using on site survey stakes for reference These locations
and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods
used Areas deemed unsatisfactory were reworked until the measured relative
compaction conformed to or exceeded the minimum specified for the project
This report summarizes our field observations and compaction testing
services during the site grading work for the period covering to
March 16 2017 Our services consisted of part time field observation of subgrade
preparation fill and backfill placement compaction procedures and confirmation of
subgrade soil conditions exposed during the above site grading operations
Based on our field representative s observations and the referenced test results it
is our opinion that the site grading for the Kailua Road Improvements project observed
and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were performed in accordance
with the general intent of the recommendations in the following references
