REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lavas from two
shield volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older volcano Waianae is estimated to be
of the middle to late Pliocene in age and Koolau is estimated to be of the late Pliocene
to early Pleistocene in age When the building of the Waianae and Koolau volcanoes
came to an end only a slight embayment existed on the southern coast of Oahu
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was
available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the
sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such
that the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The processes of erosion and deposition caused the top of the basaltic lava flows
to be highly weathered and mantled by residual soils During the Pleistocene Epoch the
Kaena stand of the sea level at approximately 90 feet MSL a delta of silt sand and
gravel built out into the embayment near the project site Agricultural land development
and reclamation projects within the last century have brought the project site to its present
form

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
The proposed mini mart project is at the northern corner of the existing UNOCAL
gasoline station located at the intersection of Puuhale Road and Nimitz Highway in
Honolulu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The mini mart site is bounded by existing
concrete walls to the northwest and northeast and by existing driveways to the southwest
and southeast as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the preliminary site plan we understand that the mini mart site will be
located at the northern corner of the existing station where it is presently vacant Based
on our field observations and a review of the topographic survey map prepared by M

with existing ground surface elevations of approximately 16 to 18 feet Mean Sea Level
MSL The site slopes down gently toward the southwest

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on our field exploration the proposed mini mart site is generally underlain
by dense sands and gravels and silty clays extending to a depth of about 26 feet below
the existing ground surface Groundwater was encountered in the two borings at a depth
of about 141 feet below the ground surface at the time of our field exploration
Based on our field exploration and laboratory testing we believe that a shallow
foundation system bearing on the near surface dense sandy soils may be used to support
the proposed structure Slabs may be supported on grade provided that the subgrades
are prepared as recommended herein
We envision that the proposed mini mart will be constructed near existing grades
to match the existing pavements Based on this information we anticipate minor site
grading consisting of cuts and fills on the order of less than 1 to 2 feet will be needed to
provide a level building pad
Detailed discussions and recommendations for design of foundation site grading
and other geotechnical aspects of the project are presented in the following sections of
this report

Foundations
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in our exploration we believe that
a shallow foundation system consisting of spread and or continuous footings bearing on
the near surface dense sandy soils may be used to support the proposed mini mart An
allowable bearing pressure of up to 3 000 p s f may be used for the design of footings
bearing on the dense sandy soils This allowable bearing pressure is for dead plus live
loads and may be increased by one third 14 for transient loads such as temporary wind
and or seismic forces Footings should be embedded a minimum of 18 inches below the
lowest adjacent finished grade We estimate that the total footing settlements under the
anticipated design loads to be on the order of less than 1 inch with differential settlements
of approximately inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by frictional resistance
between the base of the spread footing and the bearing materials and by passive earth
pressure developed against the near vertical faces of the embedded portion of footings
A coefficient of friction of 0 45 may be used between the base of the footings and the
supporting materials Resistance due to passive pressure may be calculated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f for
footings embedded in the sandy soils These values assume that the concrete for
footings is placed directly against the footing excavations Unless covered by pavements
or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches below finished grade should be
neglected
Uplift loads if any may be resisted by a combination of the following 1 dead
weight of the structure and 2 weight of backfill material above the footing where
applicable Contribution to uplift resistance due to the weight of the backfill above the
footing may be estimated using a unit weight of 100 p c f

Where footings are to be located adjacent to below grade structures or utilities the
new footings should be extended to a depth below an imaginary 45 degree plane
projected upward from the bottom of the below grade structure or utility to avoid
surcharging adjacent below grade structures with additional structural loads
Footing excavations should not be allowed to dry before placing concrete If
shrinkage cracks appear in the footing excavations the soil should be thoroughly
moistened to close all cracks and recompacted to provide a firm base prior to concrete
placement
Footing excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm the competency of the
bearing materials and embedment depths If unsuitable materials are encountered in the
footing excavations these materials should be over excavated to expose firm soils and
replaced with compacted engineered fill
Slabs On Grade
We envision that the slabs on grade will bear on the dense sandy soils near the
ground surface We recommend that the subgrade be properly prepared and kept moist
prior to placement of cushion fill and slab concrete We envision that the slabs on grade
will bear on the dense sandy soils near the ground surface As previously noted a thin
clayey silt layer which is potentially expansive was encountered in the two borings
Therefore we recommend that the clayey silt layer where encountered be removed and
be replaced with compacted granular soils In general the subgrades for slabs on grades
should be scarified to a depth of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the
optinum moisture and compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction
Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage
of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test
GEOLABS HAWAI
W O 3405 00 F Page 9
Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry
weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
For more uniform support of interior slabs we recommend that the concrete slab
on grade be supported on a minimum 4 inch cushion fill The cushion fill layer should
consist of open graded gravels such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation which will also serve as a capillary moisture break The slab cushion fill
should be placed as soon as possible after the scarification and recompaction of the
subgrade soils as recommended above For slabs on grade with moisture sensitive
surfacing we recommend that an impermeable membrane be placed over the cushion
fill to reduce potential migration of moisture vapor through the concrete slab In order to
promote more uniform curing of the slab concrete and to provide protection of the vapor
membrane we recommend that a 2 inch layer of moist fine sand be placed above the
membrane prior to placing the slab concrete The sand should be moistened slightly
prior to placing concrete
Site Grading
Final grading plans were not available at the time this report was prepared
However we anticipate minimal grading with cuts and fills on the order of 1 to 2 feet
relative to the existing ground surface may be expected for the proposed construction
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs Hawaii
It is important that a representative from our office observe whether the exposed soil
conditions during excavation are similar to those encountered in our exploration The
following recommendations are intended to provide guidelines for the design of site
grading work

After demolition of the existing pavements and other structures within the project
limits all debris generated from the demolition should be removed and properly disposed
of off site The subgrade should be scarified to a minimum depth of 8 inches properly
moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a
minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil determined in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 Optimum moisture
is the water content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry
density
Any soft weak or yielding areas encountered during site grading should be over
excavated to expose firm ground and the resulting excavation should be backfilled with
engineered fill compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Where
encountered the clayey silt layer should be removed and be replaced with granular soils
Prior to placement of the backfill the over excavated subgrade should be scarified to a
depth of at least 8 inches moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction The material resulting from
the over excavation should be removed and properly disposed of off site
Generally the on site soils may be reused as a source of fill and backfill material
It should be emphasized that the materials generated from the demolition should not be
used as fill and backfill materials The on site soils should be properly moisture
conditioned to above the optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding
8 inches in loose thickness and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative
compaction
If imported fill materials are required the imported fill and or backfill material should
consist of well graded granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum dimension The
GEOLABS HAWAII

material should have a laboratory California Bearing Ratio CBR value of 15 or more and
should have a maximum swell of less than 1 percent when tested in accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1883 in addition it should contain between 10 and 30 percent
passing the No 200 sieve Imported fill materials should be tested by Geolabs Hawaii for
conformance with our recommendations prior to delivery to the project site for its intended
use Imported materials should be brought to above the optimum moisture content and
compacted to not less than 90 percent relative compaction
Utility Trench
We envision that new utility lines would be required for the proposed construction
In general for support of the utility lines we recommend that a granular bedding
consisting of 6 inches of No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used
under the pipes The backfill up to about 1 foot above the pipes should consist of free
draining backfills such as No 3B Fine gravel to reduce the potential for damaging the
pipes from compaction of the backfill
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 1 foot above the pipes to the
top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of select granular material or on site
soils The backfill should be moisture conditioned to above the optimum moisture
content placed in maximum 8 inch level loose lifis and mechanically compacted to not
less than 90 percent relative compaction to reduce the potential for future ground
subsidence Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 2 feet of the trench
backfill below the pavement subgrade should be compacted to not less than 95 percent
relative compaction

Drainage
The finished grades outside the building should be sloped to shed water away from
foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Also it is advised that gutter
systems be installed around the buildings and that the discharge be diverted away from
the foundation and pavement areas Excessive landscape watering near the foundations
and slabs should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be avoided or
have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce water infiltration into the subsoils
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent loss of strength and or heaving of the near surface soils The foundation
excavations should be properly backfilled against the footings immediately after setting
of the concrete to reduce water infiltration
Drainage swales should be provided as soon as possible and maintained to drain
all surface run off away from the slabs and footing foundations
