PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Parish Elements project is located within the existing Star of the Sea Church
and School premises between Malia Street and Kalanianaole Highway in the Kahala area
of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project
site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
We understand that Kahala Nui a senior living community development is planned
at the existing Star of the Sea Church and School premises A geotechnical engineering
exploration was conducted by our office and the findings and recommendations are
presented in our report entitled Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Kahala Nui
Kahala Oahu Hawaii dated August 6 2001
Based on the information provided we understand that approximately
55 000 square feet of Parish Elements will be constructed to serve the Star of the Sea
Church and School as part of the Kahala Nui development agreement plan Details for the
proposed Parish Elements were not available at the time this letter report was prepared
However we envision that the new construction will consist of lightly loaded slabs on grade
structures with a finished floor elevation of approximately 46 feet Mean Sea Level MSL

SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by Dames Moore in
1992 for the previously proposed development The subsurface information from the
previous exploration that is relevant to the subject project Boring and 18 has
been included in Appendix A of this letter report for reference The approximate locations
of these borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the available information disclosed that the project site is generally
underlain by a relatively thick deposit of mixed alluvial and colluvial materials consisting of
rock fragments boulders and clay with sand extending to the maximum depth explored of
approximately 61 5 feet below the existing ground surface These alluvial and colluvial
deposits are generally older and appear to be more consolidated with increasing depth
Groundwater was encountered in Boring Nos 14 and 18 at depths of about 36 and
34 feet below the existing ground surface respectively at the time of the field exploration
in 1992 These depths generally correspond to about Elevations 10 and 9 feet MSL
respectively Our experience in the area suggests that subterranean seepage is common
in the area due to the sloping terrain and the proximity of the two drainage canals
bordering the site It should be noted that groundwater levels are also subject to change
due to rainfall time of year seasonal precipitation surface water runoff storm surge
conditions and other factors
Our field exploration at the site generally encountered a relatively thick deposit of
stiff dense alluvial and colluvial materials consisting of rock fragments boulders and clay
with sand extending to depths of about 60 feet below the existing ground surface Based
on the average penetration resistance N values of the subsurface materials encountered
below the foundation subgrade level and the geology of the area we believe that the
project site may be classified as a Stiff Soil Profile Therefore we believe that the
seismic design of the building structures at the project site may be designed based on a
Soil Profile Type Sp in accordance with Table 16 J of the Uniform Building Code 1997

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on the subsurface conditions encountered in the borings we recommend
that shallow foundations consisting of isolated spread and or continuous footings be used
to support the new buildings planned for the project An allowable bearing pressure of up
to 3 000 pounds per square foot psf may be used for the design of foundations of the
new structures bearing on the recompacted in situ soils or new compacted fills
Our laboratory test results indicated that the clayey soils soil matrix between the
boulders encountered at the site exhibit moderate expansion potential when subjected to
moisture fluctuations To reduce the potential for cracking of the slabs on grade resulting
from the near surface expansive soils we recommend that the subgrade soils below
concrete slabs on grade be properly prepared and capped with select granular fill In
general the select granular fill should extend a minimum of 12 inches below the slab
cushion Provisions should be made to increase the select granular fill layer underneath
the slabs on grade in areas where highly expansive clayey soils are exposed at the slab
subgrade level during construction Detailed discussions and recommendations for design
of foundations slabs on grade retaining structures site preparation pavements and
other geotechnical aspects of the project are presented in the following sections of this
letter report
Foundations
Based on the anticipated subsurface soil conditions at the project site we
recommend that shallow foundations consisting of isolated spread and or continuous
footings be used to support the proposed new structures planned at the project site An
allowable bearing pressure of up to may be used for the design of foundations of
the new structures bearing on the recompacted in situ soils or new compacted fills placed
to achieve the finished grades This bearing value is for dead plus live loads and may be
increased by one third 1 3 for transient loads such as those caused by wind or seismic
forces
In general the bottom of the footing over excavations should be compacted where
possible to not less than 90 percent relative compaction Soft and or loose materials
encountered at the bottom of the footing excavation should be over excavated to expose
the underlying firm materials The over excavation should be backfilled with structural fill
materials compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
In general footings should be embedded a minimum of 24 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grades Footings constructed near tops of slopes or on sloping ground
conditions should be embedded deep enough to provide a minimum horizontal setback
distance of 8 feet measured from the outside edge of the footings to the face of the slope
Footings located adjacent to planned or existing retaining walls should be embedded deep
enough to avoid surcharging the retaining wall foundations Special attention should be

also given to the foundation depths of the new buildings to avoid surcharging the
basement wall of the adjacent new underground parking structure
Foundations located next to utility trenches or easements should be embedded
below a 45 degree imaginary plane extending upward from the bottom edge of the utility
trench As an alternative the footings should extend to a depth as deep as the inverts of
the utility lines This requirement is necessary to avoid surcharging adjacent below grade
structures with additional structural loads and to reduce the potential for appreciable
foundation settlement Special attention should be given to the location and depth of the
relocated sewer line due to its proximity to the new buildings planned for the project
Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by frictional resistance
developed between the bottom of the foundation and the bearing soil and by passive earth
pressure acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system A coefficient of
friction of 0 30 may be used for footings bearing on the recompacted in situ soils or new
compacted fills Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth pcf The
passive pressure resistances assume that the soils around the footings are well
compacted Unless covered by pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper
12 inches of soil should be neglected
If the foundations are designed and constructed as recommended herein we
estimate that footing settlements under the anticipated design loads for footings bearing on
the recompacted in situ soils or new compacted fills to be on the order of about 1 inch or
less Differential settlements between adjacent footings bearing on similar materials
should be on the order of about 0 5 inches or less
We recommend that footing excavations be observed by a representative from
Geolabs prior to the placement of reinforcing steel and concrete to confirm the foundation
bearing conditions and the required embedment depths
Slabs On Grade
Our laboratory test results Plate 4 indicated that the clayey soils soil matrix
between the boulders encountered at the site exhibit moderate expansion potential when
subjected to moisture fluctuations To reduce the potential for future distress to the lightly
loaded slabs on grade we recommend that the subgrade soils be properly prepared and
capped with select granular fill The select granular fill material should extend to a depth of
at least 12 inches below the slab cushion elevation The select granular fill should also
extend beyond the footprint of the new slab on grade by a minimum of 24 inches
Provisions should be made in the construction documents to increase the select granular
fill layer underneath the slabs on grade from the minimum of 12 inches to 18 or 24 inches
in areas where highly expansive clayey soils are exposed at the slab subgrade level

In general we recommend that a minimum 4 inch thick layer of cushion fill be
placed below the interior slabs to provide the slabs with more uniform support The
cushion fill should consist of open graded gravel such as No 3 Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation which would also serve as a capillary break To reduce the potential for
future moisture infiltration and subsequent damage to floor coverings an impervious
moisture barrier is recommended on top of the cushion layer Two inches of moist fine
sand may be considered above the moisture barrier to provide protection of the moisture
barrier and to aid in curing of the slab concrete Provision of the 2 inches of moist fine
sand above the moisture barrier is optional and will be left up to the discretion of the
project structural engineer Flexible floor coverings should be considered on top of the
slab since they can better mask minor slab cracking We recommend that the building
slabs on grades be reinforced with reinforcing bars spaced at least 18 inches in each
direction in lieu of the standard welded wire fabric This recommendation is provided to
reduce the potential for structural distress resulting from differential movements of the
slabs due to the expansive nature of the on site soils
The non expansive select granular fill should be placed as soon as possible after
the subgrade soils are properly prepared The prepared select granular fill and the
underlying subgrade soils should be wetted and kept moist until the final placement of
concrete Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we
recommend that the soils be thoroughly moistened to close the cracks or prepared again
Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather
and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with
well compacted fill
Exterior concrete walkways will likely be required for the proposed project We also
recommend that concrete walkways be supported on 12 inches or 18 to 24 inches in
areas underlain by highly expansive clays of non expansive select granular fill The
select granular fill should be compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction In
addition crack control joints should be provided at intervals equal to the width of the
walkways with expansion joints at right angle intersections
Thickened edges of slabs adjacent to unpaved areas should be embedded at least
18 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade Areas adjacent to the slab should be
backfilled tightly against the slab edges with the on site soil and graded to divert water
away from the slab to reduce the potential for water ponding around the foundations and
slabs

Drainage
In general retaining walls should be well drained to reduce the build up of
hydrostatic pressures A typical drainage system would consist of a 12 inch wide
zone of permeable material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation immediately around a perforated pipe perforations facing down at the
base of the walls discharging to an appropriate outlet or weepholes As an
alternative a prefabricated drainage product such as MiraDrain or EnkaDrain may
be used instead of the drainage material The prefabricated drainage product
should also be hydraulically connected to a perforated pipe at the base of the wall
As indicated previously the backfill behind the permeable drainage zone may
consist of the on site soils particles less than 3 inches in largest dimension or
select granular fill material However the highly expansive soils at the site should
not be used as backfill directly behind the retaining walls Unless covered by
concrete slabs the upper 12 inches of backfill should consist of relatively
impervious material such as the on site clayey soils to reduce the potential for
significant water infiltration behind the retaining walls

Utility Trenches
We anticipate that the utilities for the proposed Parish Elements project will consist
primarily of water sewer and drain lines In general good construction practices should
be utilized for the installation and backfilling of the trenches for the new utilities at the site
The method and equipment to be used for trench excavation should be determined by the
contractor subject to practical limits and safety considerations In addition the
excavations should comply with the applicable federal state and local safety
requirements Trench shoring design and installation should be the responsibility of the
contractor
Based on the results of our field exploration it appears that conventional
excavation methods may generally be utilized for utility line excavation at the site The
upper clayey soils encountered at the site are generally stiff to very stiff However some
of the utility trench excavations will likely encounter the dense to very dense cobbles and
boulders as described in the boring logs which may pose some excavation difficulties
during the trench excavation In addition the contractor should anticipate significant
over break of the sides of the trench excavations considering the nature of the materials at
the site
In general we recommend that granular bedding consisting of 6 inches of
No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67 gradation be used for support under the pipes
Free draining granular materials such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33 No 67
gradation should also be used for the initial trench backfill up to about 12 inches above
the pipes to provide adequate support around the pipes It is critical to use this
free draining material to reduce the potential for formation of voids below the haunches of
pipes and to provide adequate support for the sides of the pipes Improper trench backfill
could result in settlement of the backfill and damage to the pipes
The upper portion of the trench backfill from the level 12 inches above the pipes to
the top of the subgrade or finished grade should consist of on site soils with a maximum
particle size of 6 inches or select granular fill material The backfill should be placed in
maximum 8 inch level loose lifts and mechanically compacted to not less than 90 percent
relative compaction to reduce the potential for appreciable future ground subsidence
Where trenches are below pavement areas the upper 3 feet of the trench backfill below
the pavement finished grade should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction

Drainage
It should be noted that surface and subsurface drainage at the site are especially
critical considering the expansive nature of the soils at the site Therefore the finished
grades outside the new buildings should be sloped to shed water away from the slabs and
foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding around the structures It is also
advised that gutter systems be installed around the buildings and that the discharge be
diverted away from the slab and foundation areas Excessive landscape watering near the
slabs and foundations should also be avoided Planters next to foundations should be
avoided or have concrete bottoms and drains to reduce the potential for excessive water
infiltration into the subsurface
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the above
foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil saturation
and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations should be
properly backfilled against the walls or slab edges immediately after setting of the concrete
to reduce the potential for excessive water infiltration into the subsurface Drainage
swales should be provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to drain surface
water runoff away from the slabs and foundations
