INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for the Upolu VORTAC Main Antenna Tower located in Upolu North Kohala
on the island of Hawaii The general location and vicinity of the project site are shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1

This report summarizes the findings and geotechnical engineering recom
mendations resulting from our field exploration laboratory testing and engineering
analyses These geotechnical engineering recommendations are intended for the
design of foundations and site preparation only The findings and recommendations
presented herein are subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The Upolu VORTAC main antenna tower site is located in the District of North
Kohala near the northern tip of the island of Hawaii It is proposed to construct a 20
foot high self supporting tower for the main antenna in the parking lot adjacent to the
existing equipment building We understand that a specific model for the proposed
antenna tower has not been selected at the time this report was prepared Based on
the information provided we anticipate that the antenna tower will be relatively light in
weight but will be subjected to significant uplift forces and moment at the foundation
level Minimal site grading work is anticipated for the project construction

REGIONAL GEOLOGY
Hawaii the largest island of the Hawaiian Archipelago covers an area of
approximately 4000 square miles The island was formed by the activity of five shield
volcanoes Kohala which is long extinct Mauna Kea which has had activity during
recent geologic times Hualalai which last erupted in 1801 and Mauna Loa and
Kilauea which are both still active
The Kohala mountains which make up the northern part of the island consist
largely of the Pololu Volcanic Series that are mostly olivine basalt with intercalated beds
of ash After this series erupted the mountains were deeply eroded on its windward
side and a deep soil layer covered its other slopes Later andesite and trachyte lava
flows known as the Hawi Volcanic Series erupted
The late activity of the younger Pleistocene age Hawi Volcanics resulted in the
rock formation at the site consisting of fractured vesicular andesite with thick beds of
clinker that did not travel far from their source The eruptions were moderately
explosive and formed large cinder cones that dot the surface of the volcano The flows
developed from a a type lavas are mostly mugearite with some thin beds of ash or
lenticular deposits of gravel laid down by streams Andesites in Hawaii are not the
same as the common andesites of continental areas which are richer in silica and
sodium Macdonald and Abboit 1970

DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
On the basis of our geotechnical engineering exploration we believe that the
proposed antenna tower may be supported on a mat foundation bearing on the in situ
stiff soils encountered at shallow depths We estimate the total foundation settlements
under the anticipated light structural loads to be less than 1 inch with differential
settlements between the center and the corner of the mat to be on the order of inch
Detailed discussion of our geotechnical engineering design recommendations are
presented in the following sections of this report
Mat Foundations
Based on the generally competent subsoil conditions encountered at the site we
believe that a mat foundation bearing on the in situ stiff soils may be used to support
the proposed antenna tower structure An allowable bearing pressure of up to 2 500
pounds per square foot p s f may be used for design of shallow mat foundations
bearing on the in situ medium stiff to stiff clayey silt soils This bearing value is for
dead plus live loads and may be increased by one third 14 for transient loads such as
those caused by wind or seismic forces For mat foundation design a modulus of
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3396 00 A Page 7
subgrade reaction of 150 pounds per square inch per inch of deflection p c i may be
used for the mat foundation bearing on the in situ stiff soils The subgrade modulus is
the modulus of vertical subgrade reaction for a 1 foot square test plate
The bottom of the mat foundation should be embedded a minimum of 2 feet
below the lowest adjacent finish grade Deeper embedment may be required to provide
the required capacity against uplift and moment reactions at the base of the tower
Resistance to uplift loads may be provided by the dead weight of the structure and the
mat foundation Additional resistance to uplift loads may be provided by the weight of
the backfill material placed above the footings if applicable A unit weight of 110
pounds per cubic foot p c f may be used for the on site compacted clayey silt
materials
The bottom of the mat footing excavation should be firm and unyielding prior to
the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete If soft and or loose materials are
encountered at the bottom of the footing excavation they should be over excavated to
expose the underlying firm materials The resulting over excavation should be
backfilled with the on site soil placed and compacted in accordance with the
recommendations presented in the Site Preparation section of this report or the
footing bottom may be extended down to the underlying competent material
If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements they should be
embedded below a one horizontal to one vertical 1H 1V imaginary plane extending
upward from the bottom edge of the utility trench or as deep as the inverts of the utility
lines
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3396 00 A Page 8
We estimate the total foundation settlements under the anticipated light structural
loads to be less than 1 inch with differential settlements between the center and the
corner of the mat to be on the order of inch
Lateral loads acting on the structure may be resisted by passive earth pressure
acting against the near vertical faces of the foundation system and by frictional
resistance and adhesion developed between the bottom of the foundation and the
bearing soil Resistance due to passive earth pressure may be estimated using an
equivalent fluid pressure of 300 pounds per square foot per foot of depth p c f This
assumes that the soils around the mat are well compacted or undisturbed Where
needed a shear key may be considered to provide additional passive resistance to
lateral loads A coefficient of friction of 0 3 may be used between the mat and the in
situ medium stiff to stiff soils In addition an adhesion value of 100 p s f between the
mat foundation and the bearing soils may be used where needed
Foundation excavations should be observed by a representative of Geolabs
Hawaii prior to the placement of reinforcing steel or concrete to confirm foundation
bearing conditions and the embedment depths
Site Preparation
Based on the proposed location of the main antenna tower minimal site grading
work is anticipated for the project construction
At the on set of earthwork the area within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All debris unsuitable materials and existing surface
pavements that are to be demolished should be removed and disposed of off site
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 3396 00 A Page 9
All areas beneath the future tower mat should be over excavated to the future
foundation subgrade level If zones of soft loose or saturated soils are encountered
at the over excavated subgrade level deeper over excavation may be required to
expose firm material
Due to the relatively high natural moisture contents the mat subgrade should be
proof rolled with a smooth drum roller in lieu of scarification and recompaction In
addition heavy or rubber tired equipment are not recommended to reduce the potential
for pumping subgrade conditions
Generally the on site silty soils should be suitable for use as engineered fill
materials provided that they are properly moisture conditioned and compacted It
should be noted that the on site soils have moderate to high in situ moisture contents
Therefore it may be difficult to achieve the required compaction without some aeration
of the on site soils If imported material is required the material should conform to the
requirements for Select Borrow for Subbase Course stipulated in Section 30 of the
Standard Specifications for Public Works Construction dated September 1986 Any
imported fill material should be tested and approved for its intended use by our firm
prior to delivery to the project site
All fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in
loose thickness moisture conditioned to at least 2 percent above optimum moisture
content and compacted to at least 90 percent relative compaction Relative
compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the
maximum dry density of the same soil established in accordance with ASTM D 1557 91
test procedures Optimum moisture is the water content percentage by dry weight
corresponding to the maximum dry density

Drainage
The finished grades outside the antenna tower should be sloped to shed water
away from foundations and to reduce the potential for ponding Excessive landscape
watering near the foundations should also be avoided Drainage swales should be
provided as soon as possible and should be maintained to drain all surface run off
away from the foundation slab
These drainage requirements are essential for the proper performance of the
above foundation recommendations since ponded water could cause subsurface soil
saturation and subsequent heaving or loss of strength The foundation excavations
should be properly backfilled against the foundation slab immediately after setting of
the concrete to reduce water infiltration

Purpose and Scope
The purpose of the field exploration program is to obtain an overview of the surface
and subsurface conditions at the proposed antenna tower site to develop a soil rock data
set for the formulation of geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the
foundation design and site grading of the new facilities In order to accomplish this we
propose an exploration program consisting of the following tasks and work efforts
1
Mobilization and demobilization of a truck mounted drill rig and operators to
the project site
Drilling and sampling of one boring at each antenna tower location to depths
of about 25 to 30 feet below the existing ground surface for a total of 50 to
60 lineal feet of exploration We understand that rock coring if required will
be an additional item of work that will require prior approval by the Alcatel
representative in the field
Coordination of the field exploration and logging of the borings by an
Engineer Geologist from our firm
Laboratory testing of selected samples obtained during the field exploration
as an aid to classifying the materials encountered and evaluating their
engineering properties
Analyses of the field and laboratory data for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to the proposed antenna tower
project
Preparation of six copies of a formal report summarizing our work on the
project and presenting our findings and recommendations
Coordination of our overall work on the project by a Project Engineer from
our firm
Quality assurance of our work and client design team consultation by a
Principal Engineer of our firm
Miscellaneous work efforts such as drafting word processing and clerical
support
