INTRODUCTION
This report represents the results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the foundation design of the proposed 1 0 M G
reservoir at
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our study was to explore the subsurface soil
conditions at the proposed reservoir site and along a portion of the
alignment of the proposed waterline in order to provide recommendations
for use in the design of the site grading reservoir tank foundations and
trench excavation of the proposed facility

STTE DESCRIPTION
The project site is situated on Kalepa Ridge in Hanamaulu Kauai
The general location of the site and its vicinity is shown on the Project
Eocation Map Plate 1
The reservoir site is located adjacent to and west of a paved access
road used by Lihue Plantation Co Hawaiian Telephone Co and the Kauai
Department of Water The site slopes steeply towards the east and is
covered with dense vegetation e g trees shrubs vines and grasses
There is evidence that the site was previously used for residential
purposes and there are the remnants of a dirt road or driveway leading
from the paved access road towards the reservoir site Exposed sewer
pipes were also noted on the site indicating the possible presence of
abandoned buried cesspools A complete topographic survey of the site was

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a 1 0 million gallon capacity reinforced
concrete reservoir structure on the site The reservoir will be
approximately 100 feet in diameter and 17 feet in height
The exact details of the proposed site grading were not known at the
time of our exploration However it is anticipated that cuts of
approximately 30 feet will be required to create a level pad for the
reservoir and that minimal fills will be required for the portion of the
access road to the east of the reservoir structure
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
Regional Geology
The island of Kauai is composed of a single basalt shield volcano
built by the extrusion of the lavas of the Waimea Canyon Volcanic Series
during the late Pliocene more than 2 3 million years before present
Following the cessation of this main shield building phase there was
renewed volcanic activity with the extrusion of the basaltic lavas of the
post erosional Koloa Volcanic Series Preliminary reconnaissance of the
site and its vicinity indicates that the area is underlain by deep
residual soils derived from the in situ weathering of Waimea lavas

Subsurface Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the reservoir site were explored by
drilling and sampling three 3 borings to depths between 25 9 and 47 0
feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general the borings at the reservoir encountered a surficial
layer of very stiff brown clayey silts ranging in thickness from 0 to 3 5
feet which represents the transported soil or slopewash commonly found
on hillside sites Below this stratum the borings encountered very stiff
brown mottled clayey silts ranging in thickness between 5 0 and 10 0 feet
which represent residual soils derived from the in situ weathering of the
Waimea Basalts Underlying the residual soils the borings encountered
10 0 to 20 0 feet of hard brown and tannish brown silts with sand and
gravel These silts are saprolite soils which are soils derived from the
in situ chemical weathering of rock and retain some or all of the original
structures of the parent rock Below the saprolites the borings
encountered extremely weathered basalt to the maximm depths drilled
Although this extremely weathered horizon is correctly described as a
rock for the purposes of this report and for design of the proposed
project including constructability it is considered to be a soil e g
gray to reddish brown silty sand Boulders were encountered in two 2 of
the three 3 borings so it is anticipated that boulders will be
encountered during the site grading for the proposed reservoir
The borings along the portion of the proposed waterline alignment
which was explored generally encountered very stiff clayey silts and silts
to their maximum depths These soils are residual soils and saprolites
similar to those encountered in the reservoir site Therefore some
boulders and isolated ledges of rock may be encountered during excavation
No groundwater was encountered in any of the borings during the time
of our exploration

DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATTONS
General
The subsurface exploration at the reservoir site encountered slightly
to moderately compressible soils To minimize the foundation settlements
cuts ranging from 2 to 30 feet in depth are planned to compensate for the
weight of the reservoir structure and the fluid loads
Based upon the limited exploration for the proposed waterline major
problems are not anticipated for the design and construction of the line
However due to the nature of the soils encountered in the exploration it
is anticipated that some boulders and or isolated ledges of rock may be
encountered during excavation
Reservoir Foundations
The borings indicated that at the proposed finished grade the site
is underlain by very stiff to hard brown mottled silts saprolites which
could provide adequate foundation support for the proposed reservoir
An allowable bearing capacity of 3500 p s f may be used for the
design of foundations bearing on the undisturbed in situ soils This
Value may be increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads
are considered
Exterior wall footings should be embedded a minimm of 24 inches
below the lowest adjacent finished grade Interior spread footings should
be embedded a minimm of 18 inches below the reservoir floor Footings
located next to utility trenches should extend down to a grade equal to or
deeper than the trench Alternatively the trench backfill could be
backfilled with concrete or compacted to 95 of its maximm dry density
as established by ASTM D 1557 78 to minimize footing settlement
The site grading should be designed to divert surface water away from
the reservoir foundations

Settlements
Based upon the boring data laboratory test data and the anticipated
reservoir loads the estimated settlements would generally be on the
order of less than one inch total and one half inch differential
lateral Resistance
For passive earth pressure an equivalent fluid pressure of 300
p s f per foot of depth may be used A friction factor of 0 3 may
be utilized to determine the sliding resistance of the structure and
the foundations
Site Grading
The details of the proposed grading of the reservoir site were not
available at the time of our exploration However based upon the
proposed finished floor elevation it is anticipated that the grading will
consist of cutting into Kalepa Ridge to approximate depths between 2 and
30 feet Also anticipated are small amounts of fill and cut for the
access roadway
AS previously mentioned there is a possibility that one or more
abandoned cesspools may be present on the site One suspected area was
noted while clearing trails for drill rig access This area is shown on
the Site Plan Plate 2
Site Preparation
Prior to the commencement of earthwork the area within the grading
limits should be cleared and grubbed of all vegetation organic
material debris and other deleterious material Existing cesspools
should be located opened and cleaned to firm native soils The
bottoms of any cesspools should be located with reference to proposed

Fill Placement Compaction Requiremen
It is anticipated that no fill placement will be required at the site
of the reservoir structure However some minor amounts of fill may
be required for the access road
Where the existing ground is steeper than 5 horizontal to 1 vertical
any fill placed should be keyed and benched into firm existing ground
to properly bond the new fill to the slope For details refer to
Plate 4 The fill placement should begin at the lowest point and
continue upwards in level compacted lifts
Fills should be moisture conditioned to near optimm moisture
content placed in horizontal lifts not exceeding ten 10 inches in
loose thickness and compacted to 95 of its maximm dry density as
determined by ASTM D 1557 78
It is anticipated that the site grading for the reservoir structure
will require cuts of up to approximately 30 feet in depth cut slope
ratios up to 2 horizontal to 1 vertical may be used Slope benches
should be provided at maximum vertical height intervals of 20 feet

Near the finished grade of the slope faces cutting should be done
with extreme care Overcutting beyond the design slope should be
avoided as attempts to backfill and recompact overcut areas would
most likely result in loose areas which would be subject to damage by
erosion If boulders or small ledges of rock are encountered near
the design slope it may be desirable to carefully cut or break these
material to match the design slope rather than risking damage to the
Slope by loosening the soils adjacent to the boulders or ledges by
hard excavation
Surface water from areas above the slope should be diverted away from
the cut and prevented from flowing across the slope face The slope
should be planted or otherwise protected as soon as possible to
reduce damage and unravelling by erosion
Due to the relatively high rainfall occurring in the vicinity of the
project site and the nature of the soils encountered in the borings
it would not be wholly unexpected to encounter zones of seepage
particularly intermittent seepage in the cut faces If encountered
special provisions such as horizontal drains or toe drains may be
required to stabilize the slopes
Waterline Trench
Based upon the limited exploration performed between the Lihue
Plantation Co cane haul road and the reservoir site it is anticipated
that the excavation of the proposed waterline trench will be in very stiff
to hard clayey silts and silts Some boulders and or isolated ledges of
rock may be encountered and may require hard excavation techniques e g
hoerams or ripping Shoring should conform to the applicable health and
safety regulations and statutes

Trench Backfill
A suitable pipe cushion material such as sand or 3B Fine should be
used to backfill the trench to a height of 12 inches above the top of
the pipe This cushion material should be tamped as it is placed so
that it is in full contact with the periphery of the pipe
Between the top of the cushion and 24 inches below finished subgrade
the on site soils excavated from the trench may be used as backfill
The soil used should be non expansive and free of vegetation organic
matter and any other deleterious material The soils should be
moisture conditioned to near optimm moisture content placed in
level lifts not exceeding six 6 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to 90 of the maximm dry density established by ASIM
D 1557 78
From 24 inches below to finished subgrade the backfill should be
placed in the same manner except that it should be compacted to 95
maximum dry density
Roadway Pavements
Asphaltic concrete pavements are planned for the access roads and
replacement of the pavement in the currently paved portions of the
waterline alignment Based upon the results of our exploration the
preliminary pavement design is as follows
2 Inches Asphaltic Concrete
6 Inches Base Course
8 Inches Total Pavement Thickness
CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soils
encountered during construction and the above design section revised if
necessary The preliminary recommended section considers light traffic
and is based upon the assumption that good drainage will be provided
adjacent to paved areas

Design Review
Plans for the proposed construction should be forwarded to the
geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the reservoir foundation excavations trench
excavation and backfill placement be observed by the geotechnical engineer
during construction to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and
that the subsurface conditions are consistent with the design assumptions
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the goetechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made
