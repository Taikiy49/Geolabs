Dear Mr. Kaneko: 
At your request, Geolabs, Inc. provided geotechnical engineering observation and 
consultation services during the drilled shaft foundation installation for the new monopole 
at the above project. This letter report summarizes our geotechnical engineering 
observations during the installation of the drilled shaft foundations for the SPSP Maritime 
Wireless Network System project located in Honolulu on the Island of Oahu, Hawaii. 
The purpose of our geotechnical engineering observation during construction was 
to provide engineering consultation and assist in problem resolutions during the 
construction of the drilled shaft foundation. 
Project Considerations 
The project consists of constructing new communication monopoles at various 
locations throughout the State of Hawaii. As part of the project, a new monopole of about 
80 feet in height will be constructed at Pier 1 of Honolulu Harbor. 
Based on the project plans, the new monopole was designed to be supported on a 
deep foundation system consisting of a 60-inch diameter cast-in-place concrete drilled 
shaft.  The project plans required that the drilled shaft extend down at least 60 feet below 
the existing ground surface.

The scope of our work discussed in this letter report includes observation of the 
drilled shaft foundation construction for the monopole mentioned above only. The

installation subcontractor is Healy Tibbits Builders, Inc. 
Summary of Drilled Shaft Installation 
Installation of test shafts and load testing were not required by the contract 
documents for this project.  The drilled shaft contractor installed the drilled shaft on 
April 27, 2013 and April 30, 2013. The as-built diameters and the lengths of the production 
drilled shafts installed for the project are summarized on Plate 1.1 of this letter report. 
Detailed installation logs for the production shafts are provided on Plates 1.2 through 1.4. 
Representatives from Geolabs observed the drilled shaft excavation, placement of 
reinforcing cage, and concrete operations during the installation of the drilled shaft. 
In general, the drilled shaft was excavated using a Casagrande drill rig equipped 
with 60-inch diameter augers and clean-out bucket. No drilling slurry was utilized for the 
production shafts. However, 60 and 66-inch diameter temporary casings were used during 
excavation of the drilled shaft to avoid the potential cave-in condition of the lagoonal 
deposits near the ground surface and also to provide a safer working environment. The 
drilled shaft was generally extended to about 62 feet below the existing ground surface.  
After excavation of the shaft to its designated tip elevation, the reinforcing cage was 
lowered into the drilled hole to the appropriate elevation using a crane. After the reinforcing 
cage was installed in the drilled hole, concrete was placed and pumped through a 4-inch 
I.D. steel tremie pipe starting from near the bottom of the excavation. During concrete 
placement, we observed that at least 5 feet of concrete head was maintained above the 
bottom of the tremie pipe. 
The concrete for the shaft was supplied by Ameron Hawaii. A special design 
concrete mix was utilized for the production shafts, which maintains a high slump 
(7 to 9 inches) throughout the concrete pour. Log of the drilled shaft installed and the 
materials encountered in the drilled shaft during shaft excavation are presented on the 
attached Drilled Shaft Record. 
It should be noted that the contractor could not remove the 60-inch diameter 
temporary casing.  Therefore, the 60-inch diameter casing was abandoned in place.  In 
addition, the contractor initially poured the concrete to only about 2.5 feet below the finish 
cut-off elevation to provide space for electrical conduits.  Because the concrete laitance 
was not removed during the initial concrete pour, the concrete was chipped down to 
expose clean, durable concrete prior to making the second concrete pour. 
The contractor also removed the outer 65-inch diameter casing without filling in the 
annular space between the 65-inch and 60-inch diameter casings.  Therefore, after 
pouring of the concrete, perimeter of the 60-inch diameter drilled shaft was excavated 
down to about 6 feet below the ground surface and recompacted to at least 90 percent 
relative compaction.

Crosshole sonic logging (CSL) testing was conducted on the completed drilled shaft
to evaluate the integrity of the drilled shaft. The details of the CSL testing and test results
are provided on Plates 2 through 4.5.
Based on the observations of our field representatives, it is our professional opinion
that the drilled shaft for the new monopole at Pier 1 Honolulu Harbor for the SPSP
Maritime Wireless Network System project was installed in general accordance with the
intent of the project plans and specifications and the geotechnical recommendations
presented in the above-referenced geotechnical engineering report.
Limitations
lt should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site. Our observation and consultation services
were performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical
engineering practice; no warranty is expressed or implied. Provision of construction
monitoring by an engineer is not insurance, nor does it guarantee construction of any type.
The contractor shall retain primary responsibility for the quality of the work, for adhering to
plans and specifications, and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found.
