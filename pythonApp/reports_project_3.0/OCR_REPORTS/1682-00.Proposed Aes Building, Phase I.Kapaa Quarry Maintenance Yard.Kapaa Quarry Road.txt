In accordance with your request we have performed a soil and
foundation investigation for the subject project The approximate
location of the proposed site and its vicinity is shown on the Project

With our proposal of
This report summarizes our findings and recommendations
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of this investigation was to explore the subsurface
soil and water conditions at the site to obtain pertinent soil data for
design of the foundation for the proposed structure
The main objectives were to explore the thickness and competency of
the upper crust of fill and the underlying soil deposit for spread
footing design

The scope of our investigation included drilling and sampling three
5 to 30 5 feet below the existing ground surface Also
included were laboratory testing of the samples obtained engineering
evaluation of the field and laboratory data development of design
recommendations and report preparation
Detailed description of our field exploration and laboratory test
results are presented in the appendices of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project includes the construction of a one story steel frame
structure with a mezzanine level approximately 60 by 140 feet in plan
The finished grade of the proposed building was not available at
this time however minimal site grading is anticipated for the
construction of the proposed building
Tt is our understanding that an existing slope to the northeast of
the proposed building will be filled to expand the existing yard area
SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed building site is located in the existing Kapaa Quarry
Maintenance Yard of the City County of Honolulu Building Department
Presently the site is an open area partially occupied by an
existing building about 70 by 150 feet in plan several fuel and
propane tanks The existing building will be demolished and the tanks
relocated to provide space for the proposed construction
An existing cesspool at a distance of about 48 feet to the south of
the existing building is now serving the existing building

Ground level at the proposed building site and its surrounding area
in the existing maintenance yard is generally flat It appears that the
yard was probably formed by previous site filling
Ground cover at the proposed building area primarily consists of
asphaltic concrete of various thicknesses in the open area and concrete
on grade slab within the existing building The pavements are generally
deteriorating with worn out surface courses and cracks
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions were explored by drilling and sampling 3
borings to depths of 25 5 to 30 5 feet below the existing ground surface
at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general all borings encountered an upper crust of fill material
which varied from about 13 feet of medium dense gray brown sandy to
silty gravel in Boring 1 to about 12 feet of stiff to medium stiff gray
brown clayey silt with gravel and cobbles in Boring 3 Underlying the
crust was a thick alluvium layer of medium stiff to soft clayey silt
with traces of sand and some gravel which grades to stiff at the depth
of about 30 feet in both Borings 1 and 3
Groundwater level in the borings was monitored during our field
exploration A high of about 17 feet to a low of 19 5 feet below the
existing ground surface were recorded
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
General
Our subsurface exploration encountered a generally competent upper
crust of fill at the anticipated foundation elevation for the proposed
building Shallow spread or continuous wall footing bearing directly on
this existing fill may be used for foundation support of the structure
GEOLABS HAWAII
Design Partners Inc
W O 1682 00 I D 1956 Page 4
Minimal site grading is anticipated for the construction of the
proposed building and the on site sandy to silty gravel may be reused
as backfill materials
The existing pavements are worn out and cracked probably due to
pavement aging and regular heavy truck traffic on the yard We believe
that new replacement pavement sections should be used
Both the upper fill soil and the underlying alluvium near the
existing cesspool are cohesive materials which have low permeability
We believe that these cohesive soils are only marginally suitable for
the installation of a new cesspool to serve the proposed building
Soft and moderately compressible clayey silt soils are anticipated
at the proposed fill area on the existing slope to the northeast of the
proposed building Since the filling is likely to cause settlements of
the subsoils we recommend that a settlement gauge be installed in this
area to monitor the actual settlement prior to final pavement
installation
Foundation Support
Based on the anticipated soil conditions and light design loading
we believe that an allowable bearing capacity of 3000 p s f may be used
for spread or continuous footing bearing on the existing fill materials
Footing should be placed a minimum of 18 inches below the lowest
adjacent finished grade Uplift wind or other lateral loading
condition may further increase the embedment of the footing foundation
The bearing capacity value is for total dead plus live loads only
and may be increased by one third for short duration wind loading
Estimated settlements under the design loading are anticipated to be on
the order of less than one inch total and one half of an inch
differential
GEOLABS HAWAII
Design Partners Inc

Resistance to lateral loads for the proposed structure can be
obtained by frictional resistance and passive pressure A frictional
coefficient of 0 4 may be used between the bottom of the footing and the
recompacted existing fill In addition an equivalent fluid pressure of
300 p s f per foot of depth may be used to determine the passive
resistance
Site Grading
The proposed finished grade level was not available at this time
however no major grading is anticipated
At the on set of earthwork the areas within the building grading
limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed Any unsuitable
materials such as debris rubbish and broken up pavements should be
removed and disposed of properly off site in order to prevent
contamination of the excavated material
After clearing and grubbing the areas to receive footing and
pavement should be scarified to a depth of at least 6 inches below the
subgrade elevation moisture conditioned and recompacted to 95 of its
maximum density as established by ASTM D 1557 78 test method Any loose
or wet spots encountered at the footing or subgrade level should be
removed and replaced with compacted select on site granular soil or with
suitable imported material compacted to the abovementioned standard
The on site gravelly soil may be reused as backfill material If
required new imported fill material should consist of non expansive
select granular materials less than 3 inches in maximum size with less
than 20 passing the 200 sieve
Abandoned foundations and fuel tanks should be removed and the
resulting excavations backfilled in maximum 10 inch loose lifts to a
minimum of 90 of ASTM D 1557 78

Slope Filling
The slope filling to the northeast of the proposed building was not
included in the original scope of our work and therefore no boring was
drilled in this area
However Borings 1 and 3 drilled in the proposed building areas
near the slope disclosed the presence of soft and moderately
compressible clayey silt soils at the depths of about 20 to 25 feet
which may also exist at the slope area It is anticipated that the
slope filling may cause settlements of these subsoils Therefore we
recommend the installation of a settlement gauge at the slope filling
area and periodic survey readings of the gauges should be used to
monitor the actual settlement The readings should be taken by the
project surveyor and transmitted to the soils engineer to determine
whether the settlements have reduced to tolerable levels and when the
final grading and pavement can commence
Pavement
The existing pavements within the proposed building site are
worn out and cracked probably due to pavement aging and regular heavy
truck traffic The design traffic for the new building is not known at
this time but it is anticipated to remain the same
Based on a CBR value of 8 and 30 trips two way of heavy garbage
truck traffic the following pavement slab sections may be
used as replacement new pavement for preliminary design purpose

Cesspool
It is our understanding that a new cesspool may be planned near the
existing one to serve the proposed building
Based on our findings the clayey soils in the upper fill and the
underlying alluvium near the existing cesspool are cohesive in nature
and have low permeability Therefore we believe that cesspools in
these soils would probably perform poorly and require regular pumping
Design Review
Plans and specifications for site grading foundation and pavement
design should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included
Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that the site grading subsoil preparation and
footing excavation be observed by the soils engineer during construction
to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and that the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site for use
If conditions encountered are different from those presented in
this report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

Field Exploration
Big

presented in the Boring Logs Plates A l through A 3

with a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches or a 3 inch diameter
thin wall tube hydraulically pushed The blow counts or the hydraulic
pressure to drive the sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring
Logs at the appropriate sample depths

APPENDIX B
Laboratory Testing
Moisture content and unit weight determinations were performed on
selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The results of these tests are presented on the Boring Logs
at the appropriate sample depths
Three one inch ring swell tests were performed on undisturbed and
remolded samples to evaluate the swelling potential of the soils in both
natural and compacted conditions The test results are summarized on
Plate B l
One Atterberg Limit was performed on a selected sample of the soil
to evaluate liquid and plastic limits The test results are shown on
Plate B 2
One direct shear test was performed on a selected undisturbed
sample to evaluate the shear strength parameter of the soil The test
results are plotted on Plate B 3
Two consolidation tests were performed on selected undisturbed
samples to evaluate the settlement characteristics of the subsoil The
curves are presented on Plates B 4 and B 5
