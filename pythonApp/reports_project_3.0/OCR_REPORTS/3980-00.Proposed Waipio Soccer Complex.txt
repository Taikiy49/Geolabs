Preliminary Geotechnical Engineering Exploration Report
Proposed Waipio Soccer Complex
Waipahu Oahu Hawaii
Dear Mr Wallrabenstein
This report presents the preliminary results of our geotechnical engineering
exploration performed for the proposed Waipio Soccer Complex located in the Waipio
Peninsula area in the District of Waipahu on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general
location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
Project Considerations
It is proposed to construct a soccer complex at the project site The soccer
complex will consist of 30 soccer fields separated by earth berms benches detention
basins restrooms light poles paved roads gravel parking lots and an earthen stadium
facility In general fill heights are anticipated to be less than 10 feet in height However
in isolated areas fill heights in excess of 10 feet may be constructed
Existing Site Conditions
The proposed soccer complex is located in the Waipio Peninsula area south of
the Waipahu Incinerator and Makalena Golf Course Access roads to the site is via
Waipahu Depot Road and Waipio Point Access Road while access within the site is by
existing dirt roads Ground elevations at the site range from about 7 to 32 feet above
Mean Sea Level MSL
There is an existing landfill beyond the western edge of the project site which
was used primarily for the disposal of the incinerator waste There are existing mud
ponds and stilling ponds beyond the southwestern edge of the project site
The majority of the site is covered by abandoned sugar cane growth

Subsurface Conditions
The subsurface conditions at the project site were explored by drilling and
sampling 8 borings to depths of about 21 3 to 101 5 feet below the existing ground
elevation The approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration generally encountered three soil profiles Descriptions of
these soil profiles are as follows
1 The northern half of the site is separated from the southern half by a
well defined dirt road The northern half of the site represented by Boring
Nos 1 to 3 consisted of a 3 to 5 foot layer of dry medium stiff brown clayey
soil underlain by dense to very dense cemented coralline silty sand to
depths of about 8 to 18 feet Below the cemented coralline silty sand
medium stiff to very stiff clayey soils were encountered to the final depths of
the borings
2 The northeast corner of the site near the Waipio Point Access Road
represented by Boring No 8 consisted of a 5 foot layer of medium stiff light
orange brown clayey silt underlain by loose light brown silty coralline sand
to a depth of about 11 feet Below the silty coralline sand medium stiff to
very stiff clayey silt was encountered to the final depth of the boring
3 The southern half of the site represented by Boring Nos 4 to 7 consisted
of a 3 to 11 foot layer of stiff brown clayey soil material underlain by very
soft dark gray clay to depths of about 15 to 30 feet Below the very soft
_ dark gray clay the soil grades from medium stiff to very stiff to the final
depths of the borings
Groundwater was encountered at various elevations within the borings at the time
of our field exploration Groundwater elevations ranged from 4 3 to 16 4 feet MSL
The great difference in water level elevations could likely be attributed to perched water
conditions that may have been caused by water infiltration at isolated areas previously
containing unlined ponds It should be noted that water levels at the project site may be
influenced by tidal influences seasonal precipitation and storm surge conditions
Detailed descriptions of the materials encountered in the borings are presented in
Appendix A on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 8 Results of the laboratory
tests performed on selected soil samples are presented in Appendix B

Discussion and Recommendations Summary
Based on the results of our exploration at the southern half of the site fill heights
in excess of 10 feet may require geogrid reinforcement or staged filling to resist slope
instability caused by surcharge loads imposed on the soft subsurface soil
Fills placed on the soft soils in the southern half of the site will result in settlement
on the order of to inch for every foot of fill placed It is estimated that 90 percent
consolidation may take 2 years to occur The settlement time may be reduced by
surcharging and or the use of wick drains
Detention basins should be constructed with maximum slope inclinations of
three horizontal to one vertical 3H 1V for slope heights less than 5 feet Fill slopes
should be designed with a slope inclination of 2H 1V or flatter Within the southern half of
the site cut slopes should be designed with a slope inclination of 3H 1V or flatter Within
the northern half of the site cut slopes should be designed with a slope inclination of
2H 1V or flatter
_ Prior to filling the existing ground should be scarified to a depth of 6 inches
moisture conditioned between about 2 to 5 percent above the optimum moisture content
and compacted to a minimum of 90 percent relative compaction Where shrinkage cracks
are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend that the soil be thoroughly
moistened to close the cracks or the subgrade be scarified moisture conditioned and
recompacted Saturation and subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to
inclement weather and poor drainage may require over excavation of the soft areas and
replaced with properly compacted fill material Relative compaction refers to the in place
dry density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91
Fill materials should consist of on site coralline soils or brown clayey soils
The on site dark gray clayey soils should not be used as fill Import materials should be
well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 6 inches in largest dimension
and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the No 200 sieve
The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 12 or more and should have a
maximum swell value of 1 percent or less Fill materials should be tested by
Geolabs Hawaii for conformance with these recommendations prior to delivery to the
project site for its intended use
Fills should be moisture conditioned to near optimum moisture placed in level
lifts not exceeding 10 inches in loose thickness and compacted to at least 90 percent
relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil
expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil established in
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91

Stadium and restroom slabs on grade should be designed with a minimum of
12 inches of select granular fill beneath the slab Sidewalks should be designed with a
minimum of 6 inches of select granular fill beneath the slab
Asphaltic concrete pavements should be designed with 2 5 inches of asphaltic
concrete over 6 inches of base course and 12 inches of select borrow subbase material
If soft soil conditions are encountered at the subgrade the subgrade should be
over excavated by 30 inches and replaced with a 24 inch thick layer of No 3B Fine gravel
wrapped with geotextile fabric AMOCO 4557 or similar and a 6 inch thick layer of select
borrow subbase material Base course select borrow and the upper 6 inches of
subgrade should be compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Gravel parking lots should be designed with a minimum of 6 inches of base course
material If soft soil conditions are encountered at the subgrade the subgrade should be
over excavated by 30 inches and replaced with a 24 inch thick layer of No 3B Fine gravel
wrapped with geotextile fabric AMOCO 4557 or similar and a 6 inch thick layer of select
borrow subbase material Base course select borrow and the upper 6 inches of
subgrade should be compacted to a minimum 95 percent relative compaction
Restrooms and other small structures should be designed with shallow
foundations An allowable bearing pressure of up to may be utilized for the
design of shallow foundations If soft soil conditions are encountered footings should
bear on 12 inches of recompacted on site material underlain by a 24 inch thick layer of
No 3B Fine gravel wrapped with geotextile fabric AMOCO 4557 or similar
The No 3B Fine gravel wrapped with the geotextile fabric should extend a minimum of
3 feet beyond the edges of the footings Footings should be embedded to a minimum of
18 inches below the lowest adjacent grade and embedded deep enough to provide a
minimum horizontal set back distance of 5 feet measured from the outside edge of the
footings to the slope face If foundations are located next to utility trenches or easements
they should be embedded below a 1H 1V imaginary plane extending upward from the
bottom edge of the utility tench or as deep as the inverts of the utility lines Settlement is
estimated at about inch Lateral loads acting on the structures may be resisted by a
passive earth pressure of 200 pcf and a coefficient of friction of 0 25 Unless covered by
pavements or slabs the passive resistance in the upper 12 inches of soil should be
neglected
Light pole structures should be designed with drilled shaft foundations Based on
typical standard street light structures exposed to 100 mph wind loads we recommend
that 36 inch diameter drilled shafts of 10 feet in length be used in the southern half of the
project site For the northern half of the project site we recommend that 24 inch diameter
drilled shafts of 8 feet in length be used The actual foundation loading conditions will be
needed prior to determining the final foundation design of the poles

Limitations
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part
upon information obtained from field test borings Variations of subsoil conditions beyond
and between the test borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations
may not become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident
it will be necessary to reevaluate the recommendations provided in this report
The location of the field test borings indicated in this report were surveyed and
plotted onto the Topographic Survey Map by ControlPoint Surveying Inc Elevations of
the test borings were also surveyed by ControlPoint Surveying Inc
The stratification lines shown on the Logs of Borings Plates A 1 through A 8
depict the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a
gradual transition Water level data from the test borings were measured at the times
shown on the graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have
been reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it
must be noted that fluctuation may occur due to tidal influences seasonal precipitation
and storm surge conditions
