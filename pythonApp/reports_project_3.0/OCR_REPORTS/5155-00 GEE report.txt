FIELD EXPLORATION
Our field exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling two borings
designated as Boring Nos 1 and 2 extending to depths of about 21 5 feet below the
existing ground surface The borings were drilled using a truck mounted drill rig equipped
with continuous flight solid stem augers In addition two field percolation tests were
conducted to evaluate the permeability of the soils The approximate locations of the
borings drilled and field percolation tests conducted at the site are shown on the Site Plan
Plate 2
The materials encountered in the borings were classified by visual and textural
examination in the field by our geologist who monitored the drilling operations on a
near continuous basis Soils were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System as shown on the Boring Log Legend Plate 3 Graphic

Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the borings drilled in
general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of
Soils by driving a 3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling

Pocket penetrometer tests were performed on selected cohesive soil samples in
the field The pocket penetrometer test provides an indication of the unconfined
compressive strength of the sample Results of the pocket penetrometer tests are
summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths
LABORATORY TESTING
Selected soil samples from the field exploration were tested in the laboratory with
an emphasis on evaluating the moisture density and plasticity characteristics The
following laboratory tests were performed on selected soil samples obtained from the field
exploration
Moisture Content ASTM D 2216 and Unit Weight ASTM D 2937 determinations
were performed on selected samples as an aid in the classification and evaluation of soil
properties The test results are presented on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate

are summarized on the Logs of Borings at the appropriate sample depths Graphic

ON SITE SEWAGE DISPOSAL SYSTEM
Based on the information provided we understand that the existing waste
treatment facility consists of three cesspools at the Waiahole Elementary School It is
planned to replace the existing cesspools at the campus with a septic tank leach field
system Therefore it was desired to conduct field percolation tests at the project site in
order to obtain data for design of the new sewage disposal system
Field percolation testing was performed in general accordance with the State of
Systems and Chapter 10 of the Ten States Standards to evaluate the permeability of
the subsurface soils for wastewater disposal The percolation tests were performed by
augering two 4 inch diameter boreholes to depths of about 5 feet below the existing
ground surface
Approximately 1 inch of gravel was then placed at the bottom of the borehole to
protect the bottom of the hole from scouring and sediments Based on the results of the
trial runs the hole was presoaked with 12 inches of water for at least 4 hours and then
permitted to swell for at least 12 hours before testing During testing the hole was
carefully filled with water to a minimum depth of 6 inches above the gravel pack The
time intervals and water drops were then recorded to provide data upon which the
calculation of the percolation tests was based
The percolation tests conducted resulted in a wide range of percolation rates
ranging from 20 to 300 minutes per inch According to the Manual of Septic Tank
Practice prepared by the U S Department of Health Education and Welfare proposed
location with a percolation rate of more than 60 minutes per inch is generally considered
unsuitable for the siting of the absorption system such as leach field The approximate
test locations are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 Results of the individual percolation
tests are presented on Plates 7 and 8 of this letter report Due to the clayey nature of the
soils the low percolation rate may be a result of smearing of the sidewalls during drilling
In this type of formation it is generally advantageous to utilize a deeper disposal system
such as a seepage pit to intercept more permeable layers within the soil profile
Field observations were made to evaluate the existing cesspool condition
Two of three existing cesspools were observed The cesspools were measured as
34 feet and 31 feet deep and water depths in the cesspools were measured as 15 feet
and 0 3 feet respectively Concrete lining and concrete and brick lining were observed

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based in part upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of subsoil conditions between and
beyond the field borings may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not
become evident until construction is underway If variations then appear evident Geolabs
should be contacted to re evaluate the recommendations presented in this report
The location of the field boring indicated in this report are approximate having been
estimated by taping from the features shown on the Existing Condition Plan transmitted by
M E Pacific Inc on The physical locations of the field borings
should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown on the graphic representations of the borings depict
the approximate boundaries between the soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the borings was measured at the time shown on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this letter report These data have been
reviewed and interpretations made in the formulation of this report However it must be
noted that fluctuation may occur due to tidal action variation in rainfall temperatures and
other factors
