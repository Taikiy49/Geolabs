Dear Mr Lee
This report summarizes our geotechnical engineering services performed during
the roadway and utility construction for the above referenced project from
February 1994 through May 1995 The subject site is located in Hilo as shown on the
Project Location Map Plate 1 and is bounded by General Lyman Field Hilo Airport
Kalanianaole Avenue Hapai Avenue and Kuahane Avenue in the Keaukaha area in
the District of South Hilo on the island of Hawaii
The project consists of the widening of two roadways namely Kamehameha
Avenue and Pakele Avenue which are unpaved gravel roads except for Pakele
Avenue which is paved between Pua and Baker Avenues The Kamehameha Avenue
widening was from Road A to and the Pakele Avenue widening was from
Road B to
The roadway and utility construction were performed by Willocks Construction
Corporation and generally consisted of cuts on the order of up to 8 feet and fills on the
order of up to 7 feet Fill materials generally consisted of rocks boulders and or
cobbles generated from the excavations Therefore conventional compaction testing

was generally not practicable Instead visual observations and the number of passes
needed to achieve the desired level of compaction was evaluated and used as the field
criterion for adequate compaction During the grading operations fill materials were
generally placed in near level lifts moisture conditioned and compacted using heavy
construction equipment such as a Caterpillar DON or similar equipment
During the grading work imported materials such as base course were tested
prior to their use in the field The maximum dry densities and optimum moisture
contents of these materials were established in our laboratory in general accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor Laboratory tests were also
performed to determine the maximum specific gravity of the asphalt concrete in general
accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2041 Rice Method A summary of these
test results is presented on Plate 2 of this report
Periodic field density tests were taken on the base course layer during the
roadway construction in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 2922
Nuclear Gauge Method Results of the compaction tests are summarized on Plates
3 1 through 3 5 of this report In general the base course was tested to have at least
95 percent relative compaction For the pavements the asphalt concrete was generally
compacted to a minimum of 91 percent of its theoretical maximum specific gravity
Field density test locations were selected to confirm field observations as
variations in construction procedures and soil conditions were noted Test locations
and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey stakes
These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only to the degree
implied by the methods used Areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were reworked until
the specified minimum compaction were obtained Retests of failed areas were taken
as appropriate
Our services consisted of periodic field observations of the contractor s fill
placement and compaction procedures during the roadway construction Based on the
field density test results the base course and the asphalt concrete were compacted in
general conformance with the project specifications and the County of Hawaii Standard
Specifications for Public Works Construction

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities
occurring simultaneously on a construction site Therefore some work have occurred
without our observation since our services were on a periodic basis only It should also
be recognized that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of
the tests and methods used to observe and test the fill operations Our services were
performed in accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering
practice no warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction observation by
an engineer is not insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type The
contractor shall retain responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to the plans
and specifications and for repairing defects regardless of when they are found
