Dear Ms Rice
In accordance with your request we have been providing geotechnical engineering
services during the grading operations for the 49 Black Sand Beach Subdivision at the Mauna
Lani Resort in the Anaehoomalu area South Kohala Hawaii This report summarizes our
geotechnical engineering observation and testing services performed during the grading
operations for the period between March 22 1999 and May 25 2000 In general our services
were coordinated and requested through your office
SITE GRADING
The 49 Black Sand Beach Subdivision project site is located at the Mauna Lani Resort
in the Anaehoomalu area in the District of South Kohala on the Island of Hawaii as shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The project consisted of developing the project site into
a residential subdivision The residential subdivision included the construction of 49 lots with
associated infrastructure such as roadways and underground utilities
The earthwork operations for the project were performed by In
general the earthwork at the site consisted of clearing and grubbing proof rolling placement
and compaction of fills trench excavation and backfill and subgrade preparation and
compaction for the pavements and lot pads Following the clearing and grubbing operations
the exposed subgrades were generally proof rolled to a firm and unyielding base In general
the materials used as fills and backfills consisted of 6 inch minus granular materials

During the site grading operations samples of the on site and imported materials
utilized as fill and backfill materials were obtained and tested where appropriate It should be
noted that the majority of the fills at the project site consisted of imported materials The
maximum dry densities and optimum moisture contents of the fill materials were established
in general accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 Modified Proctor The test
results are summarized on Plate 2 of this report
In general fill materials consisting of some boulders and 6 inch minus structural fills
were placed for the lot and roadway fills and utility trench backfills Due to the nature of the
fill materials boulders and 6 inch minus fill materials the fills and backfills were evaluated
based on field observations during the fill placement Where applicable such as the base
course materials field density tests were taken with the nuclear density gauge ASTM Test
Designation D 2922 During the fill and backfill operations the materials were generally
placed in near level lifts brought to near the optimum moisture and compacted to obtain the
specified degree of compaction based on visual observations and or field density tests
Periodic field density tests were performed during the fill and backfill operations to confirm our
field observations as variations in construction procedures and soil conditions were noted
As indicated in our report the undocumented fills encountered at Lot 42
and 46 were about 4 to 10 feet thick The undocumented fills consisted of well graded
granular materials and boulders with localized areas containing waste materials The
undocumented fills were removed and replaced as compacted fill The fill materials were
moisture conditioned to near the optimum moisture content and recompacted to obtain the
specified degree of compaction based on visual observations
A total of 171 field density tests including re tests of failed areas were performed using
the nuclear density gauge method in general accordance with ASTM Test Designations D2922
and D 3017 The results of the compaction tests performed during construction of the subject
project are summarized on Plates 3 1 through 3 8 of this report These tests were performed
on the prepared subgrade and on constructed fills and backfills at the site where possible
Test locations and elevations were estimated by pacing and interpolating from on site survey
stakes where available These locations and elevations should be considered accurate only
to the degree implied by the methods used
Based on the observations of our field representatives and the results of the referenced
tests it is our professional opinion that the earthwork operations for the 49 Black Sand Beach
Subdivision project observed and tested by Geolabs in the period covered by this report were
performed in general conformance with the intent of the plans and specifications and our
recommendations In general areas assessed to be unsatisfactory were re worked to
achieve the specified minimum compaction Some of these areas were not retested by our
office however we understand that the contractor reworked the areas before placing the final
pavement surface

LIMITATIONS
It should be noted that it is not possible to continuously observe all activities occurring
simultaneously on a construction site In addition since our services were on an intermittent
basis some of the earthwork were performed without our observation It should be recognized
that there are variations in the accuracy and statistical repeatability of the tests and methods
used to monitor earthwork construction and materials Our services were performed in
accordance with generally accepted standards of geotechnical engineering practice no
warranty is expressed or implied Provision of construction monitoring by an engineer is not
insurance nor does it guarantee construction of any type The contractor shall retain
responsibility for the quality of the work for adhering to the plans and specifications and for
repairing defects regardless of when they are found

We appreciate the opportunity to be of continued service to you on this project If you
have questions or need additional information please contact our office
