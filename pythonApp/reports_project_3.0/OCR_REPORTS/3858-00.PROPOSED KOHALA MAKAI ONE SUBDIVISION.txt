As requested we have performed a site reconnaissance and reviewed the
geotechnical aspects of the grading plans for the proposed Kohala Makai One
Subdivision project in North Kohala on the island of Hawaii This letter report presents
our geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of site grading for
the proposed project
Project Considerations
Based on information provided to our office the proposed project will consist of the
construction of a residential subdivision located in the Kahua and Waika areas in the
District of North Kohala on the island of Hawaii as shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 Based on the construction plans the subdivision is approximately 33 acres in
area and is bounded by the Pacific Ocean to the west and Akoni Pule Highway to the
east We understand that the Kohala Makai One Subdivision will consist of 50 lots with
access roads In general site grading will involve cuts and fills up to about 20 feet thick
with slope inclinations of approximately two horizontal to one vertical 2H 1V
In addition we understand that improvements will be made to portions of Akoni
Pule Highway for construction of the intersection with the proposed new subdivision The
highway improvements will include widening the highway which will require cut slopes up
to about 36 feet high Based on reconnaissance of the site and review of the construction
plans we have formulated recommendations for the design of site grading and earthwork

Discussion and Recommendations
Based on our reconnaissance of the site and review of the construction plans site
grading will generally consist of cuts and fills for construction of the new access roads
within the Kohala Makai Subdivision Substantial cuts up to about 20 feet deep are
anticipated at Lot and 50 for the proposed Road A construction
Substantial fills up to about 18 feet thick are anticipated at Lot 32 34 and 40
and fills up to about 10 feet thick are anticipated at Lot 48 and 49 In
addition cut slopes up to approximately 36 feet high are anticipated for the proposed
Akoni Pule Highway Improvements associated with the subdivision development In
general the proposed cut and fill slopes within the subdivision may be constructed as
shown on the grading plans provided that the following recommendations are
implemented during construction However the proposed cut slopes along Akoni Pule
Highway should be flattened to 4H 1V from the proposed 72H 1V Detailed geotechnical
engineering recommendations are provided in the following sections
Site Grading
Based on the information provided we anticipate the proposed site grading will
consist of cuts up to about 20 feet deep and fills up to about 18 feet thick In general we
recommend that fill areas be capped with 2 feet of structural fill to reduce the potential for
migration of fines from the upper portion of the fill through the voids of the compacted
rock fill Items of grading that are addressed in the following subsections include the
following 1 Site Preparation 2 Fill and Backfill Materials 3 Boulder Disposal 4 Fill
Placement and Compaction Requirements 5 Excavation 6 Cut and Fill Slopes and
7 Subdrainage
Site grading operations should be observed by a representative of
Geolabs Hawaii It is important that a representative from our office observe the site
grading to evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation
and scarification process and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to
those anticipated
Site Preparation
At the on set of earthwork all areas within the contract grading limits should be
thoroughly cleared and grubbed All vegetation debris rubbish and other
unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site to reduce
potential contamination of the excavated materials Any scattered loose boulders
lying on the existing ground surface should be removed to expose firm ground
The boulders may be used as fill material provided that they are placed in
accordance with the Boulder Disposal subsection of this report

Finished subgrades and areas to receive fill should be proof rolled with a
Caterpillar D 9 bulldozer or similar heavy equipment minimum 6 passes to help
detect and collapse near surface cavities Loose areas or cavities disclosed
during clearing operations should be backfilled with compacted fill
Fill and Backfill Materials
The fill and backfill materials to be used as general fill up to 2 feet below the
pavement subgrade level should consist of relatively well graded granular
materials less than 12 inches in largest dimension The on site cut material if less
than 12 inches in maximum dimension may be used as general fill or backfill
materials Fill materials with boulders greater than 12 inches in diameter may also
be used as fill material provided that they are placed at least 3 feet below the finish
subgrades In general placement of boulders in fills should be in accordance with
the procedures recommended in the Boulder Disposal subsection of this letter
report
Structural fill should be used within the upper 2 feet of fill below the pavement
subgrade level or finished grade Structural fill should consist of well graded
granular material of 6 inches in maximum dimension with sufficient fines to prevent
the occurrence of voids in the compacted mass This is to facilitate construction of
the pavement structural section and sidewalks if provided
If the excavated rock material does not contain sufficient fines to produce a
well graded material off site cinders or non expansive soil may be mixed into the
excavated material to increase the percentage of fines to provide a well graded
material for compaction and increase the stability of the fills If imported fill will be
required for this project it should conform to the requirements above and should
be observed and or tested by Geolabs Hawaii for its suitability prior to being
transported to the site for its intended use

Fill Placement and Compaction Requirements
In general fills and backfills should be placed in level lifts not exceeding 12 inches
in loose thickness moisture conditioned and compacted to a minimum of
90 percent relative compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry
density of soil expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density of the same
soil established in accordance with ASTM Test Designation D 1557 91 The
finished subgrade of paved areas should be compacted to not less than
95 percent relative compaction The finished subgrade of fills should be
well graded free of voids not bony and unyielding
Conventional compaction testing is generally not practicable in fills composed of
rocks boulders and or cobbles Instead a testing program to evaluate the number
of passes of a compactor needed to achieve the desired level of compaction
should be conducted at the start of the grading phase of the project under the
observation of a representative from Geolabs Hawaii On the basis of this testing
program the number of passes may then be used as the field criterion for
adequate compaction

Excavation
Based on our reconnaissance we anticipate that the site is underlain by slightly to
severely fractured volcanic rock with ash materials ranging from medium hard to
very hard It is anticipated that the volcanic rock may be excavated with normal
heavy excavation equipment such as ripping with large bulldozers where the rock
is near the existing ground surface However excavations into the deeper
formations may require the use of hoerams chipping or blasting The above
discussions regarding the rippability of the surface materials are based on our
visual observation of the existing rock formation at the site We recommend that
contractors proposing to bid on this project be encouraged to examine the site
conditions to make their own interpretation

We understand that cut slopes up to about 40 feet high are planned for the
proposed subdivision project Based on our experience in the area we anticipate
that the cut slope would expose relatively dense basalt rock with frequent
fractures In general we believe that the cut slopes planned may be designed with
a slope inclination of 1H 1V or flatter For Akoni Pule Highway the proposed cut
slopes may be steepened to H 1V due to the 24 foot wide rockfall catchment
area provided at the base of the slope For cut slopes exposing basalt rock
formation we believe that slope benches may be omitted for cut slopes up to
40 feet in vertical height
Based on the subsurface conditions anticipated at the site cavities and or clinker
zones may be encountered at the cut slope face In the event that clinker zones
are exposed at the cut slope face the cut slope may need to be flattened Large
cavities that may be exposed on the cut slope face should be backfilled and or
grouted if necessary A representative from Geolabs Hawaii should observe and
map the cut slope during construction to evaluate if the cut slope needs to be
flattened and if the exposed materials are consistent with our assumptions
In the event that over cutting of a cut slope occurs consideration should be given
to leaving the slope as is Otherwise keying and benching the slope with
cement treated granular fills should be implemented instead of backfilling the slope
to the design grade with sliver fills Alternatively cut slopes may be buttressed
with fill slopes constructed of cement treated granular fills

Permanent fill slopes may be designed with a slope inclination of or flatter

Subdrainage
Based on our site reconnaissance and review of the construction plans seepage
and drainage conditions may be present at localized areas at the site such as
Lot and 21 Therefore we recommend that provisions be
incorporated into the construction documents to provide for subdrains in the
following areas
1 where new fills are placed within existing gullies and
2 in other areas of the site where seepage or drainage is observed during
and after grading or as recommended by the Engineer during
construction
In general subdrains should consist of 4 inch diameter perforated pipes with
perforations facing down All subdrains should be surrounded and underlain by
at least 6 inches of drainage material such as No 3B Fine gravel ASTM C 33
No 67 gradation or equivalent A non woven filter fabric such as Mirafi 180N or
equivalent should wrap around the drainage material In general subdrain
trenches should be at least 12 inches wide and at least 2 feet deep The actual
locations of the subdrains should be determined in the field depending on the
site conditions

Pavement Design
Based on the information provided asphalt concrete pavements will be used for
the proposed roadways We anticipate that the vehicle loading for the proposed
roadways for this project will consist primarily of passenger vehicles and light trucks
Therefore we have assumed medium traffic loading conditions for pavement design
purposes We understand that the following pavement section is required by the
Department of Public Works County of Hawaii
County of Hawaii Standard Detail R 34
2 0 Inch Asphaltic Concrete
4 0 Inch Base Course
6 0 Inch Select Borrow Subbase
12 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness over Subgrade
In general the purpose of the subbase course required in the County of Hawaii
Standard is to serve as a foundation or stabilizing course such that the subsequent base
and surfacing may be constructed on top of an inferior subgrade soil The standard
pavement section does not take into consideration good quality subgrade conditions
Since the subgrade soils along the proposed roadway alignment at the proposed Kohala
Makai One Subdivision will consist of compacted granular fill material or basalt rock
formation it is our opinion that a subbase course for stabilization foundation purpose is
not necessary due to the high quality of the subgrade materials anticipated at the subject
site If the roadway subgrade exposes the near surface ash soil at the site we
recommend that the ash soils be removed and be replaced with structural fill material
6 inch minus material compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction On
this basis we recommend the following preliminary pavement design be used for the
subject site
Flexible Pavement Design
2 0 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 0 Inch Base Course 95 Percent Relative Compaction
8 0 Inch Total Pavement Thickness on Compacted Subgrade
It is our opinion that the strength and performance of the proposed pavement
section of 2 inches asphalt concrete over 6 inches of base course is no less substantial
than the County of Hawaii Standard Section for this site

Other Considerations
Based on our review of the construction plans eleven drywells are proposed for
the Kohala Makai Subdivision project In general drywells on the island of Hawaii are
typically 5 feet in diameter and 20 feet in depth These typical drywells may generally be
designed for disposal capacities of up to 6 cubic feet per second cfs Based on our
experience in the area the drywell capacities have been noted to be lower than normally
expected for the island of Hawaii Therefore drywells in the areas have generally been
extended to greater depths below the 20 foot depth to improve their disposal capacities
If the proposed new drywells at the project site do not have sufficient capacity we
believe that it may be more economical to extend the drywell to greater depths than to
install additional drywells for disposal of stormwater runoff The Owner Client should be
made aware that the drywell extension may have a considerable cost impact to the
project as well as construction delays if unfavorable subsurface percolation rates are
encountered during construction We strongly recommend that unit prices for additional
drilling be obtained during bidding Provisions should be incorporated into the
construction documents to extend the drywell depths using a minimum 15 inch diameter
extension hole or 5 foot diameter drilling equipment The unit prices for drywell extension
should be obtained in 20 foot increments
In general the type of drilling operation may alter the hydraulic characteristics of
formation materials at the edges of the borehole and may result in significant reduction of
the hydraulic conductivity of the drilled drywell hole We envision that the drilling fluid may
seal the permeable surfaces of the drywell Therefore pressure washing or water jetting
to remove the seal should be included in the specifications if the drywells are to be
installed by drilling The contractor should forward the proposed excavation method and
equipment to Geolabs Hawaii for evaluation and approval Geolabs Hawaii should

For drywells located in fill areas we recommend that the surficial fill materials be
