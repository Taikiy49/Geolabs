Subject Soil Engineering Report
Lot 70 Mariners Ridge
Hawaii
Gentlemen
As requested we have made a study of the surface soil condi
tions at the above referenced lot in order to provide foundation
recommendations for the proposed residence The study consisted
of a site visit and a review of available field grading reports
and house foundation plan This report summarizes our observa
tions and recommendations
PROPOSED CONSTRUCTION
The proposed residence will be a 2 story structure approximately
45 by 53 feet in plan dimension CMU bearing walls are planned
around the entry area with wood construction planned for the
remaining portion of the house
Concrete slab on grade is planned for the garage and driveway
Minimal site grading is anticipated
The proposed layout of the residence is shown on the Site Plan
Plate l
SITE CONDITIONS
The subject lot is located on the eastern edge of the Mariners
Ridge Development at the end of Kaoopulu Street
W O 1221 00 I Page 2
The front portion of the lot 40 is relatively level From
this level portion the ground Slopes down at a steep
H V ratio for about 10 to 12 feet vertical height Below
this there is a level terrace about 15 feet wide then
followed by a near vertical 6 to 8 foot bank The ground
surface then slopes down the mountain side
A review of the subdivision grading plans indicate that the
front portion of the subject lot was cut up to 25 feet while the
lower portion of the lot was not graded
SOIL CONDITIONS
Visual observations at the site disclosed that weathered rock
formation is present near the existing ground surface The rock
formation is generally moderately hard to hard
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
A review of the grading records and visual observations at the
Site indicate that the existing weathered rock soils can provide
adequate support for the proposed residence
House Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed
residence An allowable bearing value of 6000 p s f may be
used on the weathered rock formation
For weathered rock condition footings on level areas should
be embedded a minimum of 6 inches into the weathered rock
formation If it is located on or next to a sloping area the
footing should be embedded down an additional amount to obtain a
2 foot minimum horizontal set back distance measured from the
outer edge of the footing out to the weathered rock Slope face
This additional embedment depth is required to help resist the
downhill slope creep and to develop the recommended bearing
Capacity of the soil
If required to resist uplift forces rebars can be extended from
the bottom of the footing into the weathered rock formation to
form an anchor For design purposes a 2 foot deep anchor using
a grouted No 4 bar embedded in a 2 inch drill hole could
provide 1 5 kips uplift resistance
The bottom of all foundation excavations should be cleaned of
loose material and bear on dense weathered rock The excava
tions should be observed by a soils engineer to see that
adequate bearing soil has been obtained
The ground surface should be shaped to drain water away from the
foundations

Garage and Driveway Slab On Grade
It is recommended that slab on grade be placed over a 4 inch
layer of base course and should be separated from foundations
The perimeter thickened edge should be embedded a minimum of 12
inches below the lowest adjacent ground or to dense rock
formation
Retaining Walls
Retaining walls generally less than 5 feet may be used for the
entry area of the residence
Well graded granular material such as 3 Fine aggregate should
be used as a granular drainage filter backfill material minimum
12 inches behind the walls Granular material such as select
borrow could be used as backfill behind the walls to minimize
the required compactive effort Otherwise the backfill should
be compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density
established by ASTM D 1557 78
The recommendations given under House Foundations should be
followed for the design of the wall foundations
The walls should be designed to resist lateral earth pressures
as follows
Walls unrestrained at the top 35 p c f equivalent
active condition fluid pressure
Walls restrained at the top 45 p c f equivalent
at rest condition fluid pressure
A friction factor of 0 5 can be utilized to determine the sliding
resistance of the wall
Minimal passive soil resistance would be available due to the
Steep slope in front of the wall
A subsurface drainage system should be used to prevent the
build up of hydrostatic pressure behind the walls
Design Review
The plans for foundation site grading and utility construction
should be forwarded to the soils engineer for review and written
comments prior to construction to see that the intent of our
recommendations is included in the project plans and specifica
tions

CONSTRUCTION MONITORING
It is recommended that the footing excavations and backfill
operations be observed by the soils engineer during construction
This is to see that adequate compaction has been obtained and the
subsurface conditions are consistent with the findings of our
investigation
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested
and approved by the soils engineer prior to hauling to the site
The recommendations given in this report are contingent upon the
above inspections
