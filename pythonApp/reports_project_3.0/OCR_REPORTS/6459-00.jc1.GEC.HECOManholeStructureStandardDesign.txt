REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Oahu is generally composed of the weathered and dissected
remnants of two extinct shield domed volcanoes Waianae and Koolau The older
Waianae Volcano forms the bulk of the western third of Oahu s landmass while the
younger Koolau Volcano forms a major portion of the eastern two thirds of Oahu s
landmass
Generally it is believed that the Waianae and Koolau Shield Volcanoes began
erupting on the order of about 20 to 25 million years ago as deep ocean submarine
volcanoes Subaerial volcanic eruptions on the Island of Oahu ended approximately

In addition to the primary volcanic composition of the Island of Oahu other
secondary and generally more localized geologic earth materials comprise the lowland
coastal and valley portions of the island The secondary earth materials include various
alluvial and colluvial deposits oceanic and marine sediments and submerged and
emerged coralline reef materials The following subsections present generalized
description of the major processes that contributed to the geomorphic evolution of the
condition in the immediate vicinity of the specified five areas
Kakaako
The Kakaako site is located on the coastal plain of Southeastern Oahu The coastal
plain was built on the eroded southwestern flanks of the Koolau Volcano which forms the
eastern two thirds of the Island of Oahu The coastal plain was built by extensive
accumulation of alluvium derived from erosion of the volcano interbedded with coral reefs
and associated deposits
During the Pleistocene Epoch Ice Age sea levels fluctuated in response to the
cycles of continental glaciation Most of the coastal plains were developed during the
Pleistocene Epoch when the sea levels fluctuated significantly As the glaciers grew and
advanced less water was available to fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below
the present stands of the sea When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water
became available such that the sea levels rose to above its present level
The processes of erosion and deposition were affected by these glacio eustatic sea
level fluctuations When the sea level was low the erosional base level was
correspondingly lower and valleys were carved to depths below the present sea level
When the sea level was high the erosional base level was raised such that sediments
accumulated at higher elevations
The Kakaako site generally is underlain by deposits consisting of calcareous
sediments and lagoonal deposits A surface layer of man made fill was placed over
these deposits for land development and reclamation of the Kaka ako area within the
last century The land development and reclamation have brought the Kakaako area to its
present form Many of the resulting fills are of poor quality in terms of supporting heavy
structural loads
Koa Ridge
The Koa Ridge area is composed of basaltic rock built by the extrusion of lava at
the western flank of the Koolau Volcano These rocks are generally characterized by
flows of jointed dense vesicular basalt rock formation with inter bedded thin clinker

1 to 2 million years The deep narrow canyons such as Kipapa and Panakauahi are a

The in situ chemical weathering process has formed a mantle of residual soils

to basaltic rock formation with increasing depth
Kapolei
The Kapolei site is located on the Ewa Coastal Plain on southeastern flank of the
Waianae Volcano and southwestern flank of Koolau Physical and chemical weathering
followed by erosion of Schofield Plateau generated sediments which were transported
to the coast In the site vicinity and to the south these sediments accumulated and are
interbedded with marine sediments and coral algal reef formations to form a
sedimentary wedge The thickness of the sedimentary wedge ranges from zero in the
area of the Interstate Route H 1 Highway to over 1 000 feet at Ewa Beach This wedge
forms the Ewa Plain and serves as the confining formation or caprock over the
artesian basal aquifers of Southern Oahu Deposition of sediments has continued from
earlier geologic time through the present
Similar to the Kakaako area sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation during the Pleistocene Epoch The higher sea level stands caused
the formation of deltas and fans of accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of
old bays accumulated reef deposits at correspondingly higher elevations and
deposited lagoonal marine sediments in the quiet waters protected by fringing reefs
The lower sea stands caused streams to carve valleys in the sediments and reef
deposits
Subaerial exposure of the sediments and calcareous materials caused
consolidation of the lagoonal deposits and induration of the calcareous reef materials
Agricultural development within the last 100 years and recent mass grading work have
brought the Kapolei area to its present form
Kalaeloa Former Barber s Point Naval Air Station
The Kalaeloa site is also situated on the Ewa Coastal Plain which extends
seaward off the southeastern flank of Waianae and the southwestern flank of Koolau
This plain was formed by the accumulation of a thick wedge of sediments and coral reef
formations on the flanks of the two volcanoes
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of reef deposits at
correspondingly higher elevations Subaerial exposure of the sediments and calcareous
materials caused consolidation of the lagoonal deposits and induration of the calcareous
reef materials

Ko Olina
The Ko Olina site is located on the southwestern flank of the Waianae Volcano at
the western margin of the Ewa Plain Physical and chemical weathering followed by
erosion of the Waianae Mountain Range generated sediments which were transported
to the coast In the vicinity of the site and to the south these sediments accumulated
and are inter bedded with marine sediments and coral algal reef formations to form a
sedimentary wedge
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and
fans of accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef
deposits at correspondingly higher elevations and deposited lagoonal marine
sediments in the quiet waters protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused
streams to carve valleys in the sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the
sediments and calcareous materials caused consolidation of the lagoonal deposits and
induration of the calcareous reef materials Placement of man made fills and
commercial development within the last 20 years have brought the Ko Olina area to its
present form
