INTRODUCTION
This pavement justification report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
analyses for the design of new pavements for the Replacement of Kaupakulua Bridge and
Approaches project located along Hana Highway in the District of Haiku on the Island of
Maui Hawaii The general location of the project site and its vicinity are shown on the Project
Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our field exploration and laboratory testing
and presents our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses for
the design of pavements The findings and recommendations presented herein are subject
to the limitations noted at the end of this report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The project site is located in the vicinity of Kaupakulua Gulch along Hana Highway
Route 36 in the District of Haiku on the Island of Maui Hawaii Based on the information
provided it is proposed to demolish the existing wooden bridge and construct a replacement
bridge on the mauka side of the existing bridge As a result new pavements for the
roadway approaches to the replacement bridge will be required for the project construction
Based on the plans and profiles provided the new roadway bridge approaches will have
a 12 foot travelway and a 10 foot shoulder in each direction The new roadway will
measure approximately 1 700 lineal feet in total length and will be designed for a service
life of 20 years
Based on the guidelines provided in the Pavement Design Manual prepared by the
State of Hawaii Department of Transportation Material Testing and Research Branch
dated May 1995 we understand that a detailed economic comparison of pavement types
is not required if the area of new pavement is less than 4 lane miles and or a short highway
to highway connection is being made between pavements of the same type Since the
new bridge approaches will be less than 1 lane mile and the existing highway on both
GEOLABS INC
W O 3758 00 Rev Page 3
ends of the new bridge approaches is currently paved with asphalt concrete we believe
that recommendations for new pavements may be limited to pavement sections with an
asphalt concrete riding surface
REGIONAL GEOLOGY
The Island of Maui is comprised of two major volcanoes the older West Maui Tertiary
Epoch and the more recent East Maui also known as Haleakala Pleistocene Epoch The
Isthmus of Maui is a narrow gently sloping plain located between these two volcanoes The
project site is located near the northern end of the island or on the lower northern slope of
the East Maui volcano Haleakala
Haleakala was generally built in three distinguishable series of lava flows The lower
unit the Honomanu Volcanic Series late Pliocene to early Pleistocene Epoch consists of
thinly bedded tholeiitic basaltic pahoehoe and a a lavas These lavas tend to be very
permeable and carry basal water Overlying this unit is the Kula Volcanic Series early
Pleistocene Epoch composed primarily of thicker alkalic a a flows which may contain many
inter stratified thin ash soil layers A long quiescent period followed during which time
canyons were carved in the volcano although a few eruptions may have occurred during this
erosion interval in addition to erosion due to eustatic changes higher and lower stands of the
sea level At the end of the rest period copious flows erupted along the southwest and east
rifts This is the third and upper rock unit Known as the Hana Voicanic Series Pleistocene
Epoch The lavas range in composition from ultra basic olivine porphyries to non porphyritic
alkalic basalts
The project site is located in an area associated with the Pauwela Haiku association
This association consists of well drained fine textured soils on low uplands on the
north facing slopes of East Maui These soils are gently sloping to moderately steep and
developed in material weathered from basic igneous rock

EXISTING SITE CONDITIONS
Based on our field observations the existing highway at both ends of the new bridge
approaches is currently paved with asphalt concrete AC The overall conditions of the
existing pavements appear to be generally fair to good At the time of our site visit traffic
was relatively light and consisted primarily of passenger vehicles with occasional school
buses and heavy trucks
Based on our field observations the proposed alignment of the new bridge
approaches is moderately vegetated with grasses bushes and some trees Based on the
plans provided the existing terrain along the new bridge approaches is generally sloping
toward Kaupakulua Gulch and the Pacific Ocean The existing ground surface elevations
range from about 554 feet Mean Sea Level MSL at the beginning of the bridge approach
on the Wailuku side to about 549 feet MSL at the end of the bridge approach on the Hana
side Based on the existing topography and the design finished grades of the new roadway
approaches we envision that cuts of up to about 25 feet deep on the Wailuku side and fills
of up to about 15 feet thick on the Hana side of the new replacement bridge will be
required for the roadway construction

Design Subgrade Conditions
Our field exploration program for the new bridge approaches consisted of drilling and
sampling four borings designated as Boring Nos 101 through 104 extending to depths of
about 11 5 to 15 4 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations of the
four borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Our field exploration generally encountered stiff to very stiff clayey silts and silty clays
residual soils over saprolite throughout the depths explored Detailed descriptions of the
materials encountered from our field exploration are presented on the Logs of Borings
Plates 4 through 7 Laboratory testing of the near surface soils resulted in modified
California Bearing Ratio CBR values of about 7 6 and 10 7 with an average CBR value
of about 9 The average CBR value of 9 corresponds to a design Resistance R value
based on correlations by the State of Kentucky of about 35

approaches may be designed using either of the following two pavement structural sections
Detailed calculations and analyses are presented on Plates A 3 and A 4 of Appendix A Due

pavement structural sections with a permeable drainage layer in our pavement analyses for
this project The conventional pavement section consisting of asphalt concrete over

Subgrade Preparation
Based on the existing topography and the design finished grades of the proposed
bridge approaches we anticipate that cuts and fills on the order of about 15 to 25 feet will be
required for the project construction In general the area within the contract grading limits
should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed at the on set of earthwork Vegetation debris
rubbish and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed of properly off site
Soft and yielding areas encountered during clearing below areas designated to receive fill
or future improvements should be over excavated to expose firm or stiff material and the
resulting excavation should be backfilled with well compacted general fill The excavated soft
and or organic soils should be properly disposed of off site
GEOLABS INC
W O 3758 00 Rev Page 8
After clearing and grubbing the future pavement areas should be excavated where
necessary to the pavement subgrade level i e bottom of the aggregate subbase layer The
pavement subgrades should be scarified to a depth of about 8 inches moisture conditioned
to above the optimum moisture content and compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative
compaction Relative compaction refers to the in place dry density of soil expressed as a
percentage of the maximum dry density of the same soil determined in accordance with
AASHTO T 180 or ASTM D 1557 91 test procedures Optimum moisture is the water
content percentage by dry weight corresponding to the maximum dry density
It should be noted that the in situ soils have relatively high moisture contents
Therefore if the subgrade soils are pumping during subgrade preparation the pumping
subgrade may be stabilized by cement treatment to reduce the potential for pumping
subgrade conditions As a guide one sack of cement may be used for approximately
50 square feet of subgrade area This preliminary mix should be adjusted in the field to suit
the exposed soil conditions
Where shrinkage cracks are noted after compaction of the subgrade we recommend
that the soils be scarified and prepared again as recommended above Saturation and
subsequent yielding of the exposed subgrade due to inclement weather and poor drainage
may require over excavation of the soft areas and replacement with well compacted general
fill Contract documents should include additive and deductive unit prices for over excavation
and compacted general fill placement to account for variations in the over excavation
quantities
Aggregate subbase materials required for the new pavements should conform to the
requirements stipulated in the Standard Specifications for Road Bridge and Public Works
Construction 1994 The aggregate subbase should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content placed in 8 inch level loose lifts and compacted to a minimum
of 95 percent relative compaction The asphalt concrete base ACB material should consist
of asphalt treated basalt aggregate placed in one layer and compacted to a density of at
least 91 percent of the maximum theoretical specific gravity determined in accordance with
GEOLABS INC
W O 3758 00 Rev Page 9
AASHTO T 209 or ASTM D 2041 The maximum compacted thickness of each ACB layer
should not exceed 6 inches
In general the excavated on site materials may be re used as a source of general fill
and backfill materials up to the bottom of the aggregate subbase layer provided that they are
free of organic materials and contain no lumps or rock fragments greater than 6 inches in
largest dimension The on site fill materials should be moisture conditioned to above the
optimum moisture content placed in level lifts not exceeding 8 inches in loose thickness and
compacted to a minimum of 95 percent relative compaction
Imported fill materials below the aggregate subbase layer if required should consist
of non expansive granular material such as crushed coral basalt or cinder sand The
material should be well graded from coarse to fine with no particles larger than 3 inches in
largest dimension and should contain between 10 and 30 percent particles passing the
No 200 sieve The material should have a laboratory CBR value of 20 or more and should
have a maximum swell of 1 percent or less when tested in accordance with AASHTO T 193
or ASTM D 1883 test procedures Imported fill materials should be brought to above the
optimum moisture content and compacted to not less than 95 percent relative compaction
Surfaces should be finished to create smooth unyielding subgrades and should be kept
moist until covered by concrete slabs or pavements Imported materials should be tested
and approved by Geolabs prior to delivery to the project site for its intended use
Pavement subgrade preparation operations should be observed by a representative
from Geolabs It is important that a representative from our office observe the subgrade
preparation to evaluate whether undesirable materials are encountered during the excavation
and scarification process and whether the exposed soil rock conditions are similar to those
encountered in our field exploration
Pavement Drainage
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due to
saturation of the base subbase and or subgrade soils Therefore the pavement surface
GEOLABS INC
W O 3758 00 Rev Page 10
should be sloped and drainage gradients should be maintained to carry surface water off
the pavement to appropriate drainage structures Surface water ponding should not be
allowed on the site during or after construction Development of good shoulder drainage
along with a program to prevent obstructions in the drainage structures is strongly
recommended to reduce the potential for pavement deterioration or premature failure of
the pavements
We envision that an edge drain will be needed to collect and daylight the infiltrated
water into the pavement structural section from the untreated permeable base layer
The edge drain should consist of a 6 inch PVC perforated pipe with perforations facing
down surrounded by untreated permeable base A permeable separator non woven filter
fabric conforming to Section 648 of the Hawaii Standard Specifications for Road Bridge
and Public Works Construction 1994 should be placed around the untreated permeable
base to reduce the potential for migration of fines into the drainage material as shown on
Plate 8 In addition a permeable separator should also be placed between the untreated
permeable base and aggregate subbase Care should be exercised when placing the
subbase base and or construction of the surface course to avoid damaging the edge drain
collector pipe A typical edge drain detail is provided on Plate 8 The edge drains should be
daylighted into appropriate drainage structures for proper discharge

Construction Monitoring
It is recommended that Geolabs be retained to provide geotechnical engineering
services during construction of the project The items of construction monitoring that are
critical include observation of subgrade preparation and placement and compaction testing
of the fill and base materials This is to observe compliance with the design concepts
specifications or recommendations and to expedite suggestions for design changes that may
be required in the event that the subsurface conditions differ from those considered in this
report The recommendations provided in this report are contingent upon such observations
If the actual exposed subsurface conditions encountered during construction are different
from those assumed or considered in this report then appropriate modifications to the design
should be made

information obtained from our field borings bulk samples and laboratory test data Variations
of subsoil conditions between and beyond our field borings and bulk samples may occur and
the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until construction is
underway Jf variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re evaluate the
recommendations provided in this report

and elevations of the borings should be considered accurate only to the degree implied by
the methods used
The stratification lines shown on graphic representations of the borings depict the
approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual transition
Water level data from the borings were measured at the times shown on the graphic
representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed and

APPENDIX C
Laboratory Testing
Two Standard Proctor compaction tests ASTM D 698 or AASHTO T 99 were
performed on bulk samples of the near surface soils to evaluate the dry density and
moisture content relationships The test results are presented on Plates C 1 and C 2
Two laboratory California Bearing Ratio Tests were performed on bulk samples of the
near surface soils to evaluate the pavement support characteristics of the soils Each
sample was remolded to near the optimum moisture content as determined by ASTM D 698
AASHTO T 99 Compaction of the samples was performed by dropping a 10 pound
hammer from a height of 18 inches The samples were compacted in 5 equal layers with
each layer receiving 10 blows of the hammer Each sample was soaked in water for 4 days
prior to the penetration test The results of the tests are presented on Plates C 3 and C 4
