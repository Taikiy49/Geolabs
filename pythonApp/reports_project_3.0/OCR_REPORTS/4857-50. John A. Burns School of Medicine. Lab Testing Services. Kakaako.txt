In accordance with our fee proposal dated we recently completed field
and laboratory work pertaining to the monitoring well installed at the John A Burns School
of Medicine site in the Kaka ako area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The monitoring well
was used to measure groundwater temperature and collect groundwater samples for
analytical laboratory testing This letter report summarizes our work for the project and
presents our findings pertaining to the groundwater temperature and analytical laboratory
testing The findings and test results presented herein are subject to the limitations noted
at the end of this letter report
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
We understand that the John A Burns School of Medicine for the University of
Hawaii is planned in the Kaka ako area of Honolulu on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The
general location and vicinity of the project site are shown on the Project Location Map
Plate 1 The subject property is approximately rectangular in shape and encompasses
approximately 10 acres in land area The site is bounded in a clockwise direction by
llalo Street to the north Cooke Street to the east Kelikoi Street to the south and
developed land to the west as shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the development plan provided we understand that the School of
Medicine will consist of the following construction 1 Educational Administration Building
Building No 1 2 Central Plant Building No 2 3 Research Building Building No 3
and 4 Ancillary Building Building No 4 In addition on grade parking areas are also
planned across the project site to serve the new School of Medicine

We understand that it is proposed to install a deep injection extraction well for the
removal of groundwater for cooling the air conditioning system of the new medical school
buildings The water will then be injected back into the groundwater system after use In
order to obtain general baseline groundwater quality data for the feasibility study of the
proposed injection extraction well system it was desired to sample and test the
groundwater underlying the subject site For this Purpose a monitoring well was installed
to a depth of approximately 128 feet below the existing ground surface for the collection of
groundwater temperature readings and groundwater sample for baseline quality data
testing
MONITORING WELL INSTALLATION
The drilling well installation and well development tasks were conducted from
July 7 through 11 2003 The monitoring well is identified as MW 1 and the approximate
location is shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
In general drilling of the monitoring well was accomplished by using a
truck mounted drill rig advancing 4 5 inch outside diameter OD PQ casing The
associated drilling and sampling tools were steam cleaned prior to drilling of the borehole
The groundwater monitoring well consisted of 2 inch diameter Schedule 40 PVC
pipe installed to a depth of about 128 feet below the existing ground surface The well
generally consisted of about 85 feet of screen with 0 020 inch slot opening and 42 feet of
solid casing The surface completion consisted of an 8 inch well cover that was
cemented flush with the existing ground surface A detail of the monitoring well
installation is provided as Plate 3 of this report
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The monitoring well was installed at the southwestern corner of the project site
Subsurface materials were examined and classified by an engineer from our office
Relatively undisturbed soil samples were obtained from the boring in general accordance
with ASTM Test Designation D 3550 Ring Lined Barrel Sampling of Soils by driving a
3 inch OD Modified California sampler with a 140 pound hammer falling 30 inches In
addition some samples were obtained from the boring drilled in general accordance with
ASTM Test Designation D 1586 Penetration Test and Split Barrel Sampling of Soils by
driving a 2 inch OD standard penetration sampler using the same hammer and drop The
blow counts needed to drive the sampler the second and third 6 inches of an 18 inch drive
are shown as the Penetration Resistance on the Logs of Boring at the appropriate
sample depths
In general the monitoring well location is underlain by granular surface fill
materials of medium dense relative density extending to a depth of about 10 feet below
the existing ground surface The surface fill materials were generally underlain by
lagoonal deposits consisting of loose to medium dense silty gravel and soft sandy silts

ABANDONMENT OF MONITORING WELL
Abandonment of the monitoring well is not included as part of our current scope of
work therefore we recommend that abandonment of the monitoring well be included in
the construction documents if appropriate In general the monitoring well should be
properly abandoned and sealed with cement grout
GROUNDWATER CONDITIONS
Relatively shallow groundwater conditions were measured at the project site Due
to the proximity of the project site to the Pacific Ocean it is likely that groundwater levels
measured in the monitoring well will be affected by tidal fluctuation of the sea level In
addition the groundwater levels at the project site may be influenced by seasonal
precipitation and storm surge conditions
Groundwater Temperature
The groundwater temperatures within the monitoring well were measured on
at 5 foot depth intervals using a Geokon piezometer Results of the
temperature readings are shown on Plate 6 of this report In addition a near continuous
profile of the groundwater temperature was obtained using Ocean Sensors
Model conducted on Results of the groundwater
temperature profile are shown on Plate 7 of this report Based on the data obtained by
both methods it appears that the groundwater temperature generally decreases from
about 84 degrees Fahrenheit near the existing ground surface to about 75 degrees
Fahrenheit at a depth of about 125 feet below the existing ground surface

LIMITATIONS
The findings submitted in this report are based in part upon information obtained
from the field monitoring well Variations of conditions beyond the monitoring well may
occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to
re evaluate the variations
The location of the monitoring well is approximate having been estimated by taping
from references indicated on the Partial Utility Plan dated March 31 2003 provided by
Architects Hawaii Limited The elevation of the monitoring well is also approximate having
been estimated by interpolation from elevation points on the available drawing The
physical location and elevation of the monitoring well should be considered accurate only
to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the logs of boring depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition Water level data from the boring were collected at the time shown on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the preparation of this report However it must be noted that
fluctuation may occur due to variations in tides rainfall temperature and other factors

In accordance with the project specifications one pile load test was conducted on a
selected test pile Pile No E A 28 for the University of Hawaii John A Burns School of
Medicine project in the Kaka ako area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii This letter report
presents the results of our field observations and the test data collected during the static pile
load test conducted on
The static pile load test was conducted on a 16 5 inch octagonal precast prestressed
concrete pile The test pile was driven on using a
hydraulic hammer delivering a maximum energy of up to about 44 23 kip feet During the
pile driving operations on the test pile a log of the blow counts needed to advance the test
pile was maintained by an engineer from our office The test pile driving record is provided
on Plate 1 for ease of reference
The test pile was loaded in general accordance with ASTM D 1148 Quick Load Test
Method and modified in the project specifications The maximum test load on the test pile
was planned at about 400 kips In general the load test was loaded to a test load of 320 kips
200 percent of the design load of 160 kips in 16 kip load increments Based on the load
settlement characteristics the load test was further advanced to a maximum test load of
400 kips in 20 kip load increments beyond the 160 kip test load
In general each load increment of the load test was held for at least 5 minutes or until
the rate of settlement was generally stable In addition a 4 hour hold was maintained a the
320 kip and 400 kip load increments to evaluate the creep effects of the test pile After
loading the test pile to the maximum test load the test load was unloaded in approximately
four equal decrements and held for at least 5 minutes at each decrement The results of the
load test are presented on Plates 2 1 through 2 3 and are summarized as follows
