Introduction
This pavement justification report presents the results of our geotechnical
engineering analyses performed for the design of new roadway pavements for the
Kamokila Boulevard Restoration Kapolei Parkway to Nau Place project in the Kapolei
area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The project location and general vicinity are shown
on the Project Location Map Plate 1
This report summarizes the findings from our literature research and presents
our geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analyses for the
design of new pavements only The findings and recommendations presented herein are
subject to the limitations noted at the end of this report

Project Considerations
Based on the information provided it is desired to restore the portion of pavements
along Kamokila Boulevard extending from Kapolei Parkway to Nau Place in the Kapolei
area on the Island of Oahu Hawaii The approximate limits of the roadway restoration
project are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on the drawings provided Kamokila Boulevard is a 100 foot right of way
roadway with a 20 foot median and a 30 foot wide travelway in each direction We

Regional Geology
The Island of Oahu was built by the extrusion of basalt and basaltic lava from the
Waianae and Koolau shield volcanoes The older Waianae Volcano is estimated to be
middle to late Pliocene in age and forms the bulk of the western one third of the island
The younger Koolau Volcano is estimated to be late Pliocene to early Pleistocene
Ice Age in age and forms the bulk of the western one third of the island As the
volcanic activity in Waianae Volcano ceased lava flows from Koolau Volcano banked
against its eroded eastern slope forming a broad plateau now known as the Schofield
Plateau The project site is located on the southwestern flank of the Waianae Volcano
Physical and chemical weathering followed by erosion of Schofield Plateau
generated sediments which were transported to the coast In the project vicinity and to
the south these sediments accumulated and are interbedded with marine sediments
and coral algal reef formations to form a sedimentary wedge The thickness of the
sedimentary wedge ranges from zero in the area of the Interstate Route H 1 Highway to
over 1 000 feet at Ewa Beach This wedge forms the Ewa Plain and serves as the
confining formation or caprock over the artesian basal aquifers of Southern Oahu
Deposition of sediments has continued from earlier geologic time through the present
During the Pleistocene Epoch sea levels fluctuated in response to the cycles of
continental glaciation As the glaciers grew and advanced less water was available to
fill the oceanic basins such that sea levels fell below the present stands of the sea
When the glaciers melted and receded an excess of water became available such that
the sea levels rose to above the present sea level
The higher sea level stands caused the formation of deltas and fans of
accumulated terrigenous sediments in the heads of old bays accumulated reef deposits
at correspondingly higher elevations and deposited lagoonal marine sediments in the
quiet waters protected by fringing reefs The lower sea stands caused streams to carve
valleys in the sediments and reef deposits Subaerial exposure of the sediments and
calcareous materials caused consolidation of the lagoonal deposits and induration of

Existing Site Conditions
The proposed project is along Kamokila Boulevard extending from the
intersection with Kapolei Parkway to the vicinity of Nau Place in the Kapolei area on the
Island of Oahu Hawaii The approximate limits of the roadway restoration project are
shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
Based on our field observations the existing roadway alignment within the
project limits is generally bordered by retail and or commercial developments A
reconnaissance of the existing roadway alignment indicates that the existing pavements
appear to be in generally poor condition with significant depressions rutting and
alligator cracking The ride characteristic of the pavement was generally fair
Based on the preliminary plans provided the terrain along the roadway alignment
within the project limits is relatively flat with existing ground surface elevations ranging
between about 65 and 75 feet Mean Sea Level MSL

Anticipated Subsurface Conditions
Research and review of available geologic information and soils boring data from
past projects in the project vicinity were performed in order to obtain an indication of the
subsurface conditions along the roadway restoration alignment
Our review of the available geotechnical data suggested that the project site may
be underlain by very stiff to hard silty clays and clayey silts in the shallow subsurface
However it should be noted that coralline materials could be encountered at the
pavement subgrade elevation in localized areas along the roadway alignment
especially towards the intersection with Kapolei Parkway at the southern end
Groundwater is not anticipated at shallow depths below the existing pavement
surface In general the site is at an elevation several tens of feet above the highest
basal water table in the area However groundwater levels can vary significantly

Subgrade Preparation Below Pavement Section
In general the area within the contract grading limits should be cleared
thoroughly at the on set of earthwork Vegetation debris deleterious materials existing
pavements and other unsuitable materials should be removed and disposed properly
off site
After clearing areas to receive the new pavements should be excavated to the
pavement subgrade level i e bottom of the untreated permeable base layer The
exposed subgrades consisting of the existing aggregate subbase material should be
recompacted in accordance with Section 305 Aggregate Subbase Course of the
Hawaii Standard Specifications for Road and Bridge Construction 2005
A Geolabs representative should monitor the pavement subgrade preparation to
observe whether undesirable materials are encountered at the pavement subgrade level
and to confirm that the thickness of the existing aggregate subbase is no less than that
assumed in our pavement analyses

Pavement Drainage
One of the primary distress mechanisms in pavement structures is pumping due to
saturation of the base subbase and or subgrade soils Therefore the pavement surface
should be sloped and drainage gradients maintained to carry surface water off the
pavement to appropriate drainage structures Surface water ponding should not be
allowed on the site during or after construction
We recommend providing a pavement edge drain system hydraulically connected
to the untreated permeable base along the edges of the new pavements to collect
infiltrated water in the pavement structural section We envision that an edge drain will be
needed to collect and daylight the infiltrated water from into the pavement structural
section from the untreated permeable base layer The edge drain should consist of a
6 inch PVC perforated pipe with perforations facing down surrounded by untreated
permeable base A permeable separator non woven filter fabric conforming to
Section 648 of the Hawaii Standard Specifications for Road and Bridge Construction 2005
HSS should be placed around the untreated permeable base to reduce the potential for

Subdrains should be provided in areas where seepage is observed during and or
