INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering
evaluation performed for the proposed Circle K store at Red Hill on the
island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site and its
vicinity is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1 The exploration
was performed in general accordance with our proposal dated May 10 1988
This report summarizes the findings from the exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based
on the data obtained during the exploration These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction
estimation or bidding purposes

PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
It is proposed to construct a Circle K convenience facility at this
site The facility will consist of a single story 40 by 67 foot store
and a 25 by 40 foot pump island with gasoline dispenser units To supply
the gas island an underground gasoline storage area consisting of three
storage tanks is planned adjacent to the pump island The convenience
facility will be surrounded by an asphaltic concrete parking lot
A five foot high retaining wall may be used on the northwestern side
of the store to support the side cut of the slope
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the
subsurface conditions at the project site in order to develop a
generalized soil rock data set for the formulation of geotechnical
engineering recommendations pertinent to site grading and foundation
design of the project
SITE DESCRIPTION
The project site is located on the northeastern corner of the
junction of Moanalua Road and Ala Kapuna Street in Honolulu Oahu
Hawaii The general location of the project site and its vicinity is
shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1
The site is bounded at the north by a 10 foot high slope at a ratio
of 2 1 H V The northeastern corner of the store will be partially
located on the slope Another slope at a ratio of about 1 1 bounds the
southwestern side of the site by Moanalua Road The southeastern and
northeastern sides are bounded by Ala Kapuna Street and Forward Avenue
respectively

The site measures about 175 by 135 feet in plan dimensions and is a
generally flat land created by previous cut and fill Presently the
site is used as a parking lot and is partially covered with vegetations
Scattered cobbles and boulders were observed on the ground surface
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The exploration program consisted of drilling and sampling three 3
borings to a depth about 10 feet below the existing ground surface The
approximate locations of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The boring locations were based on the preliminary plans
Topographic Survey Map dated made available to us
Two borings B 2 and B 3 are within the area of the proposed store and
one boring B 1 is within the area of the pump island and storage tanks
In general the borings encountered hard dark grayish brown
volcanic tuff
The proposed northern corner of the store is located on an existing
Slope Due to the limited accessibility of this area the slope subsoils
were explored by hand excavation Soft reddish brown clay was
encountered in the slope indicating that the soil in the Slope is fill
Groundwater was not encountered during our exploration Detailed
descriptions of the materials encountered are presented on the Boring
Logs Plates A 1 through A 3
DISCUSSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Site Grading
At the time of report preparation a grading plan was not available
However minimal site grading is anticipated The on site excavated tuff
materials less than 3 inches in size may be used as fill material If
imported fill material is needed it should be non expansive free of
GEOLABS HAWAII

organic and other deleterious matter and form a dense stable soil mass
when compacted to the required density Generally the soil particles
should be well graded and less than 3 inches in maximum dimension
Additionally the imported fill material should have a minimum CBR value
of 15
Prior to the commencement of fill placement the areas within the
grading limits should be thoroughly cleared and grubbed of vegetation and
deleterious material
The existing surface soils should be proofrolled with a heavy
roller Any soft yielding or pumping areas noted during the proofrolling
process should be excavated to firm material and backfilled with suitable
material placed in 9 inch thick loose level lifts and compacted to a
minimum of 90 maximum dry density as recommended above
Store Building and Pump Island
Our subsurface exploration generally encountered volcanic tuff which
can provide adequate support for the proposed Circle K Store building
and pump island Shallow spread or continuous wall footings bearing on
the dense tuff formation may be used
The recommended minimum footing width is 12 inches with a minimum
embedment of 8 inches below the lowest adjacent grade An allowable
bearing value of up to 4000 p s f may be used for foundations bearing on
the dense tuff The bearing capacity is for total dead plus live loads
and may be increased by one third for temporary wind and seismic loads
Minimal settlements are anticipated due to the light structural
loads

Slab On Grade
The on grade floor slab should be placed over a 4 inch thick cushion
layer of gravel size material such as 3B Fine rock to provide uniform
support A moisture barrier membrane should be placed under the slabs
The bottom of all thickened edge excavations should be wetted and
kept moist until the concrete is placed The backfill against the
concrete slab should be adequately compacted and graded to shed water away
from the slab perimeter
Underground Storage Tanks
It is planned to place three storage tanks underground in an area
adjacent to the Circle K store
The tanks will probably bear on the dense volcanic tuff formation
which has high bearing capacity and can provide adequate support
Relatively hard excavation is anticipated in the volcanic tuff
To reduce the compactive effort required for the backfill a clean
gravel such as ASTM C 33 No 67 is recommended
Pavement and Parking Areas
Based on the results of our field study the preliminary pavement
design is as follows
2 Inch Asphalt Concrete
6 Inch Base Course
8 Inch Total Pavement Thickness
CBR tests should be performed on the actual subgrade soil samples
during construction and the above design section should be verified in
the field

Retaining Wall
The retaining wall which will be located on the northern side of the
proposed store will vary from 1 to about 5 feet in height following the
outside cut slope
The embedded depth of the wall footing below the lowest adjacent
finished grade should be a minimum of 12 inches
An allowable bearing value of 4000 p s f on the volcanic tuff may be
used
Well graded granular material such as base course rock sand or
equivalent should be used as backfill material directly behind the
retaining walls This may minimize the compactive effort required for the
backfill and reduce the build up of additional lateral pressures on the
wall Generally the backfill particle size should be less than 6 inches
To minimize the build up of hydrostatic pressures behind the wall a
4 inch perforated pipe should be placed on the heel directly behind the
wall The subdrain should be placed and daylighted to allow proper
drainage
Drainage ditches should be provided behind the top of the wall to
direct the surface run off away from the wall

Design Review
Plans and specifications for the proposed construction should be
forwarded to the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments
prior to construction This review is needed to determine adherence to
the earth work and foundation recommendations given herein If this
review is not made the geotechnical engineer cannot assume responsibility
for misinterpretation of the recommendations
Construction Observation
It is recommended that the geotechnical engineer be retained to
provide geotechnical engineering services during construction of the
excavation and foundation phases of the work This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and
to allow design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ
from that anticipated prior to start of construction The recommendations
given in this report are contingent upon such observations
Any imported material required should be non expansive tested and
approved by the geotechnical engineer prior to hauling to the site
If actual exposed rock soil conditions encountered during
construction are different from those assumed or considered in this
report then appropriate modifications to the design should be made

APPENDIX A
Field Exploration
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling three
3 borings to depths of 9 7 to 10 2 feet below the existing ground
surface at the approximate locations shown on the Site Plan Plate 2 A
truck mounted drill rig was used to drill the borings
The soils encountered in the borings were classified by visual and
textural examination in the field by our engineer who continuously
monitored the drilling operations The classifications were further
reviewed by visual inspection and testing in the laboratory All soils
were classified in general conformance with the Unified Soil
Classification System A summary of the materials encountered are
presented in the Boring Logs Plates A 1 through A 3
Soil samples were obtained from the borings by driving a 2 inch
a 140 pound hammer free falling 30 inches The blow counts to drive the
sampler the last 12 inches are shown on the Boring Logs at the appropriate
sample depths
