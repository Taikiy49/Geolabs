Geophysical Survey Limits
The limits of the geophysical survey were established following our conversation
with the school custodial supervisor our point of contact at the site The survey limits
are shown on the attached general map of the site
During our on site meeting and discussion with the custodial supervisor portions
of the site were said to be County facilities and not part of the school campus We
understand the County facilities include the baseball field with associated parking lot
and open space to the west the tennis court and the basketball court The grounds
comprising these facilities were not surveyed by ground penetrating radar because they
were explained to us as not being part of the school campus

Clarification of Geophysical Survey Limits
Ka u High and Pahala Elementary School
Dear Mr Isa
In response to your facsimile of November 2 and our telephone conversation
yesterday this letter presents additional clarification of the geophysical survey limits at the
subject site
During our site visit to support the proposal preparation the fence line between the
campus and baseball field was judged to be the actual campus boundary However
because the baseball field was level and open it was envisioned that it could be included
in the survey and was therefore included in the proposed survey budget Other areas
due to restricted access and sloping ground conditions were not envisioned as being
included in the survey because of the impossibility of doing so
The attached School Site Plan shows the grounds that were not surveyed as
shaded area The areas were not surveyed due to the impracticality of doing so because
of obstructions and terrain In addition as mentioned in our report the on site
representative said that some areas did not need to be surveyed because they were not
part of the actual school campus Of the shaded areas shown the area shown by cloud
highlighting could have conceivably been surveyed by geophysical means A
geophysical survey could have been performed because of the open and level ground
condition of the baseball field However at the time of our field exploration it was
conveyed to us that the field was not part of the campus and did not have to be surveyed
The other shaded areas could not be surveyed by ground penetrating radar due to
ground slope vegetation building obstructions or restricted access to motor vehicle It
was never intended to include these areas in the survey
