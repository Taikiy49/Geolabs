PROJECT CONSIDERATION
The proposed Kapolei Reservoir is situated to the north of the H 1
Freeway at the base of Puu Makakilo in the District of Ewa on the island of Oahu
Hawaii The proposed construction consists of one 4 million gallon MG reinforced
concrete reservoir approximately 150 feet in diameter and 20 feet in height The
bottom of the reservoir is planned at an elevation of about 185 feet Mean Sea Level
MSL
Extensive excavation entailing cuts on the order of 50 to 60 feet were completed
to develop a level area on which to base the reservoir We have previously performed
a geotechnical engineering exploration for the subject project and the geotechnical
engineering recommendations were presented in our report entitled Geotechnical
Engineering Exploration Kapolei Storage Tanks dated December

Due to the possible occurrence of cavities and lava tubes in the underlying basalt

large voids below the reservoir foundations
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our work is to provide field observation and the necessary
geotechnical engineering consultation services for the probing and grouting operations
Our scope of work consisted of the following tasks and work efforts
1 Observation of the cavity probing and grouting operations by a Field
Engineer
2 Geotechnical engineering consultation by a Project Manager from our
firm
3 Quality assurance of our overall work on the project and consultation by
a Principal Engineer of our firm
PROBING AND GROUTING OPERATIONS
The probing operation was performed on March 25 and 26 1994 by California
Drilling and Blasting Company An air track drill rig with an air compressor was used
for probing Probe holes were drilled at each column footing location and on 10 foot
centers along the perimeter reservoir wall footing as shown on the Site Plan Plate 1
A total of 93 probe holes were drilled at the site The probe holes drilled were about
3 inches in diameter and extended down to about 13 feet below the existing ground
surface corresponding to at least 10 feet below the bottom of footings
Based on our observation of the probing generally basalt rock with varying
medium to dense clinker layers was generally encountered below the footing locations
Some loose clinker layers were also encountered and they ranged from 1 to 3 feet in
thickness at different depths Small voids of about 6 inches to 1 foot in vertical
dimension were encountered at depths ranging between 9 and 10 feet below the slab
subgrade level at the footings D3 E4 and F7 locations
At the location of Probe No 42 on the wall perimeter footing soils consisting of
reddish brown clayey silts with sand were encountered from the slab subgrade level to
the bottom of the probe about 13 feet below the slab subgrade

The probe hole backfill grouting operation was performed on March 28 1994
A concrete crane pump was used to pump sand cement grout into each probe hole
The sand cement grout was pumped into probes through a 2 inch PVC pipe until the
probe hole was filled The total sand cement grout used for the grouting operation was
about 10 cubic yards The theoretical volume of grout to fill up the 93 probe holes
would be 2 2 cubic yards
In summary the probe and grout operations did not detect significant cavities
which would affect foundation support of the tank at the locations of the probes
