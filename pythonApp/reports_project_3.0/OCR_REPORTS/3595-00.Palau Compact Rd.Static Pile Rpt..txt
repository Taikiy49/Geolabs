1 Introduction
This report presents the result of a static pile load test SPL T that was performed on
Pile No 21 at Bridge 7 Abutment 2 for the Compact Road Project in Babeldaob Island
Republic of Palau The test was performed in accordance with ASTM Test Method D 1143
using the quick load procedure Axial load was applied by single hydraulic jack acting
against a weighted platform
The static pile load test and data interpretation were carried out by Dong A Geological
Engineering Co Ltd under contract with DAEWOO E C Co Ltd
A professional
engineer from GeoEngineers Inc provided supervision as required by the project
specifications
This report includes the following
Pile fabrication and installation details
Test frame and hydraulic jack details
A description of the test procedure and graphical presentation of the measured
deflection
A discussion of pile capacity determined from analysis of the data
The test pile location soil conditions instrument calibration records and static pile load
test results are provided in Appendix A B C and D respectively

4 Measuring Procedure
For each increment time and settlement was recorded immediately on applying the load
and just before increasing to the next load increment The data logger automatically
recorded the L VDT output every 1 minute
In general the test proceeded in the following order
1 Pile Preparation
The pile installation left 1 7 m above the ground level which was appropriate for the
previous dynamic tests The exposed length was reduced to O m by cutting with a
grinder torching the reinforcing steel and breaking with a front end loader The pile
PALAU COMPACT ROAD SPLT B7
11
head was then prepared for the test by chipping the head slightly concave and capping
smooth and level with high strength cement mortar BASCON
2 Installing measuring devices
Two linear voltage differential transducers LVDT s were attached to the pile at equal
radial spacings The L VDT s were attached using a magnetic mounting arm supported
by two reference beams close to the pile The 500 ton jack was also placed on top of
the pile
3 Constructing the weighted platform
Six concrete blocks were placed around the pile in an 8 m square and were adjusted to
have equal elevation Steel beams were placed on the blocks and welded in place to
construct a platform Bundles of reinforcing steel sheetpiles and rejected PSC piles
were then placed on the platform to make a reaction load of 358 tons
4 Gage installation
The L VDT s and reference beams were placed near the pile The reference beams are
1 Om long and were supported on wood blocks at both ends
5 Load testing
Load was applied through the jack and pile deflections relative to the reference beams
were measured by the LVDT s Load increments of 16 tons 5 of the test load were
held for 5 minutes exceeding the 2 5 minute hold time required by Section 5 6 of the
test method The test load was held for 5 minutes and the reduced from 320 ton to 0
through three five minute decrements
6 Analysis
The collected deflection data were normalized to correct for eccentric deflection and
plotted as shown in Section 5 The data sheets are attached in Appendix D

A pile load test has been carried out as a part of the field investigation program and on
the basis of the results of this full scale test pile capacities have been estimated For
practical purposes the ultimate bearing capacity may be taken as the axial load that causes
the head of the pile to settle 10 of the pile diameter BS8004 1986 For a 600 mm
octagonal pile this failure settlement is 60 mm

7 Conclusions
Based on the static pile load test conducted on Pile No 21 at Bridge No 7 Abutment No
2 we offer the following conclusions
1
The SPLT successfully monitored pile head displacement under 20 loads increasing
to 320 tons The SPLT was performed according to ASTM Test Method D 1143
2
The yielding or ultimate bearing capacity of the pile was not observed from the test
The total settlement was and residual settlement was 1 345mm
3
The test results indicate an ultimate capacity higher than 320 tons 320 of the
Specified Design Load
4
The deflection behavior suggests that the tip did not deflect under the maximum
load
