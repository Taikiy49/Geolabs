INTRODUCTION
This report presents the results of our geotechnical engineering exploration
performed for two 2 compressed gas storage sheds at the Naval Supply Center in Pearl
Harbor Naval Base on the island of Oahu Hawaii The general location of the project site
is shown on the Project Location Map Plate 1

This report summarizes the findings resulting from our exploration and our
geotechnical engineering recommendations derived from our analysis based on the data
obtained from the field exploration and laboratory testing These recommendations are
intended for design input and are not intended for construction estimation or bidding
purposes
PROJECT CONSIDERATIONS
The proposed gas storage sheds consist of a 2 130 square foot Flammable Gas
Cylinder Storage Shed and a 8 584 square foot Non Flammable Gas Cylinder Storage
Shed The two storage sheds are part of the development of a Hazardous Materials
Storage Facility FY88 MCON Project P 106 The two storage sheds and the proposed
storage facility are located in the same lot situated on the southerly side of Neches
Avenue in the Naval Supply Center
The proposed storage sheds will consist of one story open sided steel structures
with sheet metal roofing and a concrete floor
PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The purpose of our exploration was to obtain an overview of the subsurface
conditions at the proposed shed sites to develop a soil data set for the formulation of
geotechnical engineering recommendations pertinent to the design of the building
foundations The main objectives of our exploration was to determine the thickness of
the existing stiff soil crust at the site and to evaluate the compressibility parameters of the
upper soft harbor mud for determination of the anticipated amount and time for
settlement

SITE DESCRIPTION
The proposed gas cylinder storage sheds are located on the southern side of the
existing Hazardous Materials Storage Facility and east of Necnes Avenue at the Naval
Supply Center in Pearl Harbor Naval Base
The ground level at the site is generally flat and the ground cover consist primarily
of compacted coral gravel fill
SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS
The subsurface conditions at the site were explored by drilling and sampling two
2 borings to depths of 71 5 to 72 0 feet below the existing ground surface The bottoms
of the drilled borings were further probed using continuous penetration testing to depths
of about 92 0 to 105 0 feet below the existing ground surface The approximate locations
of the borings are shown on the Site Plan Plate 2
The borings generally encountered a surface fill crust of about 11 0 to 14 0 feet
in thickness consisting of loose to medium dense silty sands and gravels underlain by
a thick layer of very soft and very loose lagoonal deposits to depths of about 82 0 to 89 0
feet below the ground surface The lagoonal deposits generally consisted of very soft
clayey to sandy silts and very loose silty fine sand to sand The lagoonal deposits
generally have high compressibilities when loaded and low soils shear strengths
GEOLABS HAWAII
W O 1720 50 Page 5
Below the overlying thick lagoonal deposit a stiff deposit was encountered in our
probings to depths of 92 0 and 105 0 feet the maximum depths probed Based on
previous borings performed in the area it appears that the stiff material encountered
could be an older alluvium deposit as encountered in the previous borings
Groundwater was encountered in the borings at depths of 8 5 to 11 2 feet below
the existing ground surface at the time of our exploration
Detailed descriptions of our field exploration and laboratory test results are
presented in the appendices of this report
DISCUSSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The field exploration disclosed that the proposed gas cylinder storage sheds are
underlain by thick highly compressible lagoonal deposit consisting of very soft and very
loose clayey silts and silty sands
Based on our field exploration and engineering analyses it is our opinion that the
Flammable Gas Cylinder Storage Shed with anticipated floor loading approximately 100
p s f may be located adjacent to the existing Hazardous Materials Storage Facility
without adversely affecting the storage facility foundations However we believe that
larger settlements will occur for the Non Flammable Gas Cylinder Storage Shed with
higher anticipated floor loadings up to 250 p s f
To reduce the anticipated settlements for the Non Fiammabie Gas Cylinder Storage
Shed a surcharge fill program is recommended
GEOLABS HAWAII

Flammable Gas Cylinder Shed
It is our understanding that the Flammable Gas Cylinder Storage Shed will be
loaded only one 1 pallet high According to the information provided by the Department
of the Navy one 1 pallet of cylinders at the storage shed will consist of 20 cylinders
banded together in the upright position and will weigh a total of approximately 1 500
pounds On the average it is estimated that the one 1 pallet high loading will impose
a loading pressure of approximately 100 p s f on the floor slab
Since the anticipated bearing pressure is relatively light we believe that the
Flammable Gas Cylinder Storage Shed may be located southerly of and adjacent to the
constructed Hazardous Materials Storage Facility without adversely affecting the storage
facility s foundations and the adjacent utilities and pavements
Non Flammable Gas Cylinder Shed
it is our understanding that the Non Flammable Gas Cylinder Storage Shed will be
loaded up to three 3 pallets high which will impose up to approximately 250 p s f of
storage loading on the ground floor slab Each pallet of cylinders will consist of 15
cylinders stacked horizontally to three 3 levels high
Based on this higher floor loading pressure our current calculations and settlement
analyses indicates that approximately four 4 to six 6 inches of ground floor slab
settlement could occur if the storage shed is built at the proposed site which location has
not been surcharged previously Therefore a new surcharge program is recommended
for the Non Flarnmable Gas Cylinder Storage Shed location in order to induce most of the
anticipated ground settlement to occur prior to construction of the shed foundation and
floor slab

Surcharge Monitoring
As recommended above a surcharge fill program is required for the proposed
Non Flammable Gas Cylinder Storage Shed area to induce most of the anticipated ground
settlements under the design current floor loading prior to construction of the structure
Itis our opinion that a minimum surcharge period of 4 to 6 months may be needed
for the 7 foot high surcharge fill
It should be recognized that it is very difficult to accurately predict the actual time
required for the surcharge settlement to take place since it will be affected by minute
details of the subsoil structures and also the history of the subsoil deposition For a thick
deposit of highly compressible subsoils such as those encountered at the site we believe
that the calculated surcharge time estimates could vary as much as 50 to 100 from the
actual surcharge time required Therefore the actual settlement rate should be field
monitored and provisions should be made in the construction contract and scheduling
to accommodate for delay in surface structure construction since a ionger surcharge
period may be required
For the proposed Non Flammable Gas Cylinder Storage Shed we recommend that
a minimum of six 6 settlement gauges be installed at the future surcharge area to
monitor the actual fill ground settlement rate We recommend that one 1 gauge each
be installed at the four corners of the surcharge area and that two 2 gauges be installed
at the center of the surcharge area as shown on Piate 3 A typical settlement gauge
detail is shown on Plate 4
The gauges should be read by a qualified professional surveyor and the readings
should be transmitted immediately to the project geotechnical engineer for review The
readings should be transmitted on a weekly basis in the first 8 months and subsequently

on a bi weekly basis during the rest of the anticipated surcharge period and or until the
geotechnical engineer determines that the surcharge fill may be removed
Foundations
Spread or continuous wall footings may be used for the proposed light one story
warehouse structures If loose or soft areas are encountered in the footing excavations
they should be over excavated down to firm subsoil and the resulting depression
backfilled with select material compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry
density as determined by ASTM Test Method D 1557 78
The backfill material should consist of non expansive select materials less than
three 3 inches in maximum size with less than 20 passing the 200 sieve Any
imported fill material should be tested and approved by the geotechnical engineer prior
to delivery to the site
The backfill should be piaced in maximum 10 inch thick loose level lifts and
compacted to a minimum of 90 of the maximum dry density as determined by ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78
An allowable bearing value up to 2 500 p s f may be used for spread footings
bearing on the upper stiff surface fill material The bearing value is for total dead plus live
loads and may be increased by one third when temporary wind and seismic loads are
considered
individual column footings and or continuous wall footings should be embedded
a minimum of twelve 12 inches below the lowest adjacent finished grade

Slab On Grade
Concrete slab on grade are planned for the proposed structures
The exposed subgrade soil should be scarified up to 10 inches deep moisture
conditioned and recompacted to a uniform 95 relative compaction density per ASTM
Test Method D 1557 78 The above compacted subgrade should be kept moist prior to
the placement of the overlying base course layer and concrete slab The thickened
edges of the slabs should be embedded a minimum of twelve 12 inches below the
lowest adjacent grade
A six 6 inch layer of compacted base course aggregate compacted to 95
relative density should be provided below the slab In order to protect against future
moisture infiltration an impervious moisture barrier may be incorporated below the slab
Design Review
Final construction plans and specifications for the project should be forwarded to
the geotechnical engineer for review and written comments prior to advertisement for

cannot assume responsibility for misinterpretation of the recommendations

It is recommended that the project geotechnical engineer be retained to review the
settlement gauge data during the recommended surcharge program This is to observe
compliance with the design concepts specifications or recommendations and to allow
design changes in the event that subsurface conditions differ from that anticipated at the
time that this report is written The recommendations given in this report are contingent
upon such review

LIMITATIONS
The analyses and recommendations submitted in this report are based upon
information obtained from the field borings Variations of conditions between the borings
may occur and the nature and extent of these variations may not become evident until
construction is underway If variations then appear evident it will be necessary to re
evaluate the recommendations given in this report
The locations of the field borings are approximate having been determined by
taping from structures indicated on the architectural site plan by Design Partners Inc
dated The physical locations of the field borings should be
considered accurate only to the degree implied by the methods used
The stratification lines shown in graphic representations of the field borings depict
the approximate boundaries between soil types and as such may denote a gradual
transition
Water level data from the field borings were collected at the times given on the
graphic representations and or in the text of this report These data have been reviewed
and interpretations made in the formulations of this report However it must be noted

Laboratory Testing
Laboratory testing was performed on selected samples obtained during our field
exploration A laboratory testing program was developed to schedule appropriate tests
to aid in determining pertinent engineering properties of the soil materials sampled
Classification Tests
Frequently the classification of a material provides a correlative index to certain
engineering properties of the material
Moisture Content ASTM Test Method D 2216 90 and Unit Weight Determinations
are used as an aid to soil classification and to aid in determining certain properties such
as relative saturation The moisture content expressed as a percentage of the dry weight
of the tested sample is shown at the appropriate location on plates summarizing test
results and at the appropriate sample depths on the Boring Logs
A Sieve Analysis test ASTM Test Method D 422 63 was performed to determine
the grain size distribution of the soil The grain size distribution is used for classification
in the Unified Soil Classification System and to provide indices on other engineering
properties The result of the test is summarized on Plate B 1
Physical Property Tests
Tests are performed to develop a correlative index of certain physical parameters
of a soil These physical parameters may be used in engineering analyses pertaining to
the performance or suitability of a soil
Consolidation tests ASTM Test Method D 2435 80 are used to evaluate the
consolidation characteristics of the soil The results of these tests are summarized on
Plates B 2 through B 9
